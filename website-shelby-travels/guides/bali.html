Getting There & Getting Around

Flights to Bali

Bali's international airport is Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) in Denpasar. Most international flights arrive from Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, Bangkok, or Sydney. From Australia, expect 4-6 hours. From Europe, 14-18 hours with connections.

Suggested Itineraries

5-Day Bali (Highlights)

  • Day 1-2: Ubud - temples, rice terraces, culture
  • Day 3-4: Canggu/Uluwatu - beaches, surfing, cliffs
  • Day 5: Nusa Penida day trip

7-Day Bali (Balanced)

  • Day 1-3: Ubud - culture, nature, cooking classes
  • Day 4-5: Seminyak/Canggu - beaches, nightlife, shopping
  • Day 6: Uluwatu - temples, sunsets, surfing
  • Day 7: Nusa Penida or waterfalls

10-14 Day Bali (Deep Dive)

Add East Bali (mountains, volcanoes), North Bali (beaches, diving), and multiple Nusa Islands. Perfect for digital nomads or those wanting a slower pace.

Essential Tips

Before You Go

  • Visa: Most countries get 30-day visa-free entry or visa on arrival ($35)
  • Vaccinations: Hepatitis A & B, typhoid recommended
  • Money: IDR (Indonesian Rupiah). ATMs widely available.
  • Connectivity: Buy eSIM before arrival or at airport

While You're There

  • Tips: 5-10% at restaurants, small amounts for drivers
  • Temple etiquette: Wear sarong, cover shoulders and knees
  • Water: Don't drink tap water. Use filtered water bottles.
  • Traffic: Expect heavy traffic, especially in Ubud and Canggu
  • Haggling: Expected at markets, not in malls or restaurants
