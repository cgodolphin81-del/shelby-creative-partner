Iceland

Duration: 17,100 words · Best for: 7-14 days

Budget: $100-250/day | Best time: June-September (summer), September-March (Northern Lights)

Getting There & Getting Around

Flights to Iceland

Reykjavik Keflavik Airport (KEF) is the main international airport. Many European cities have direct flights. From US: often with connections in Europe.

Getting Around

Car rental: Essential for Ring Road. 4x4 recommended for highlands.

Bus: Strætó for local Reykjavik. Gray Line for tours.

Ferries: Available to Westman Islands.

Where to Stay

Reykjavik

Budget: Hostels ($30-60/night)
Mid-Range: Central hotels ($100-200/night)
Luxury: Hotel Borg, Fosshotel ($250-500+/night)

South Coast

Self-catering cabins: $80-150/night
Guesthouses: $100-200/night

Suggested Itineraries

7-Day Ring Road (Highlights)

  • Day 1: Reykjavik arrival
  • Day 2: Golden Circle (Thingvellir, Geysir, Gullfoss)
  • Day 3: South Coast (Seljalandsfoss, Skogafoss, black sand beaches)
  • Day 4: Vatnajokull glacier, Jokulsarlon lagoon
  • Day 5: Eastfjords
  • Day 6: North (Akureyri)
  • Day 7: Return to Reykjavik

10-14 Days (Extended)

  • Add Snæfellsnes Peninsula
  • More time in Eastfjords
  • Highlands (F-roads, summer only)

Top Things to Do

  • Golden Circle: Thingvellir National Park, Geysir, Gullfoss
  • South Coast: Waterfalls, black sand beaches, glaciers
  • Jokulsarlon: Glacier lagoon, diamond beach
  • Blue Lagoon: Geothermal spa (book in advance)
  • Northern Lights: September-March, tour recommended
  • Midnight Sun: June-July, 24-hour daylight
  • Ice Caving: Winter only, guided tours
  • Whale Watching: Hvalfjordur, Husavik

Essential Tips

  • Weather: Changeable year-round. Pack layers, waterproof jacket.
  • Driving: Drive on right. Watch for sheep, wind, ice.
  • Water: Tap water is safe and delicious. Bring reusable bottle.
  • Money: Credit cards accepted everywhere. Bring some ISK for small vendors.
  • Book ahead: Popular hotels and Blue Lagoon book up months in advance.
  • Northern Lights: Check forecast, tour recommended for first-timers.
