Getting There & Getting Around

Flights to Japan

Major airports: Tokyo (Narita/Haneda), Osaka (Kansai). Direct flights from most major cities. From US West Coast: 10-12 hours. From Europe: 11-13 hours with connections.

Getting Around

Japan Rail Pass: 7-day pass costs ¥29,650 (~$200). Covers all JR trains including Shinkansen. Buy before arriving in Japan.

IC Cards: Suica/Pasmo for local trains in Tokyo. ICOCA for Osaka/Kyoto.

Where to Stay

Tokyo

Budget: Capsule hotels, hostels in Shinjuku/Shibuya ($30-60/night)
Mid-Range: Shinjuku, Shibuya, Asakusa ($100-200/night)
Luxury: Ginza, Roppongi ($300-800+/night)

Kyoto

Budget: Hostels near Kyoto Station ($25-50/night)
Mid-Range: Gion, Kawaramachi ($80-150/night)
Luxury: Traditional ryokan ($200-500+/night)

Osaka

Budget: Dotonbori hostels ($30-60/night)
Mid-Range: Namba, Umeda ($80-150/night)
Luxury: The St. Regis, Peninsula ($300+/night)

Suggested Itineraries

7-Day Japan (Classic)

  • Days 1-3: Tokyo - temples, shopping, culture
  • Days 4-5: Kyoto - temples, gardens, geisha districts
  • Day 6: Nara day trip (deer, giant Buddha)
  • Days 7: Osaka - food, nightlife

10-Day Japan (Extended)

  • Days 1-4: Tokyo + day trips (Hakone, Kamakura)
  • Days 5-7: Kyoto + Nara
  • Days 8-9: Osaka + Hiroshima/Miyajima
  • Day 10: Return to Tokyo, departure

Essential Tips

Before You Go

  • Visa: 90 days visa-free for most countries
  • Money: Japan is still cash-heavy. Get yen at airport or withdraw from ATMs (7-Eleven, Japan Post)
  • Connectivity: Pocket WiFi or eSIM recommended
  • Etiquette: No tipping, quiet on trains, remove shoes indoors

While You're There

  • Train etiquette: No phone calls, quiet conversations
  • Tipping: Not expected and can be offensive
  • Convenience stores: 7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart are amazing
  • Seasonal: Cherry blossoms (late March-early April), autumn foliage (November)
