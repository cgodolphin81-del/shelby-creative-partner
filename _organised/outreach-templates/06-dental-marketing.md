## Discovery Call Script

### Opening (2-3 minutes)

**You:** "Thanks for making time, Dr. [Last Name]. I know you're busy with patients. I want to make sure this is actually valuable for you.

Can I start by asking a few questions about your current new patient flow and what's working (or not)?"

**[Wait for yes]**

**You:** "Perfect. Two things upfront:
1. This is about you - I'll listen more than I talk
2. If I think we can help, I'll tell you. If not, I'll be honest. Fair?"

---

### Qualifying Questions (7-10 minutes)

**Current State:**
1. "Walk me through your current new patient flow - where do they come from?"
2. "How many new patients do you see per month currently?"
3. "What's your average case value for new patients?"

**Pain Points:**
4. "What's your biggest frustration with patient acquisition right now?"
5. "How much empty chair time do you have weekly?"
6. "How dependent are you on insurance panels vs. cash/premium patients?"

**Goals:**
7. "If you could wave a magic wand, how many ideal new patients per month would you want?"
8. "What would consistent patient flow mean for your practice?"

**Decision:**
9. "Have you tried other marketing before? What happened?"
10. "Who else is involved in decisions like this?"
11. "What would need to be true for this to be a no-brainer?"

---

### Presentation (5-8 minutes)

**You:** "Dr. [Last Name], thank you for sharing all that. Based on what you've told me, here's what I'm seeing..."

**[Present findings]**

1. **The Problem:** "You're getting [X] new patients/month, but they're mostly low-value PPO patients. You have [Y] empty chair hours/week, which is $[Z] in lost production."

2. **The Opportunity:** "There's significant demand in [City] for high-value services (implants, Invisalign, cosmetic). Your competitors are capturing most of it. But there's room for you to dominate."

3. **The Solution:** "We build patient acquisition systems that attract high-value patients. Our clients see 20-40 new patients/month, 70%+ show rate, $40k-$80k additional production."

4. **The Results:** "[Practice example] went from 12 to 34 new patients/month, added $47k production, reduced PPO dependence by 40%."

**[Share screen with case studies or simple deck]**

---

### Handling Objections

**"This is expensive"**
"I hear you. What number were you expecting? ... [Listen] ... That makes sense. Here's the framing: if you add [X] new patients at $[Y] average case value, that's $[Z] in monthly production. Our fee is a fraction of that. And you're currently losing $[W] in empty chair production. Does that change the equation?"

**"We've tried marketing companies before"**
"I appreciate you sharing that. What happened? ... [Listen] ... That's frustrating. Here's what we do differently: we specialize in dental only, we guarantee results, and we focus on high-value patients, not just any patients. We also offer a performance guarantee. Would that give you more confidence?"

**"I need to think about it"**
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think through? Is it the investment, the timing, or do you have questions about how it works?"

**"We're busy enough"**
"That's great - it means you're successful. But are you seeing the RIGHT patients? High-value, cash-pay, long-term? Or are you filled with low-reimbursement PPO patients? Our system helps you be selective about WHO fills your chairs. Would that be valuable?"

---

### Close (3-5 minutes)

**You:** "Dr. [Last Name], I think there's a real opportunity here. Here's what I'd recommend..."

**[Present specific recommendation]**

**Direct Close:**
"Does this feel like the right next step for [Practice Name]?"

**Trial Close:**
"How does this align with what you were hoping to accomplish?"

**Next Steps:**
"What would you need to see to move forward? Let's make a plan."

**[Confirm next steps, send agreement, schedule onboarding]**

## Objection Handlers

### Price Objections

**"This is more than we're spending on marketing"**
"I hear you. You're probably spending $[X]/month currently, right? ... [Listen] ... And you're getting [Y] new patients? With us, you'll get [Z] high-value patients. The ROI is 4-8x. One additional implant case covers our fee. Does that change the equation?"

**"We can't afford this right now"**
"I understand cash flow is tight. What if we started with the Starter package for 60 days? Lower investment, you see results, then we scale up. Or we could do performance-based pricing - you pay per seated patient. What would work better?"

---

### Trust Objections

**"We've tried marketing companies before"**
"I appreciate you sharing that. What happened? ... [Listen] ... That's frustrating. Here's what we do differently: we specialize in dental only, we guarantee results, and we focus on high-value patients. We're not a general marketing agency. Want to talk to one of our current clients?"

**"How do we know you can deliver?"**
"Great question. Here are 3 case studies from practices just like yours... [Share]. We also offer a performance guarantee - if we don't deliver [X] patients at [Y]% show rate, you don't pay. We're confident because we've done this [X] times."

---

### Timing Objections

**"We're too busy right now"**
"That's actually a great position to be in. But are you seeing the RIGHT patients? High-value, cash-pay, ideal cases? Our system helps you be selective. Also, what about when things slow down? Starting now builds momentum. When would be better?"

**"Let me think about it"**
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think through? Is it the investment, the timing, or do you have questions about how it works? I want to make sure you have what you need."

---

### Competition Objections

**"We get plenty of referrals"**
"That's great - referrals are the best. But they're unpredictable. What happens if they slow down? Our system gives you predictable, controllable patient flow ON TOP of referrals. It's insurance, not replacement. Would starting small make sense?"

**"We already have someone doing our marketing"**
"That's great. Are they guaranteeing [X] high-value patients per month with a show rate guarantee? ... [Listen] ... If not, we might be able to supplement or improve what you're getting. Would you be open to a comparison?"
