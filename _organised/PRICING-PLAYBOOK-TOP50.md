## 🛡️ GUARANTEE BEST PRACTICES

### Types of Guarantees

**1. Satisfaction Guarantee**
> "Not happy? Full refund within 30 days."
- Best for: Digital products, low-ticket
- Risk: Low (5-10% refund rate typical)

**2. Results Guarantee**
> "Don't see results? We work free until you do."
- Best for: Services, coaching
- Risk: Medium (requires client effort)

**3. Performance Guarantee**
> "Hit X metric or we refund [portion]."
- Best for: High-ticket, measurable outcomes
- Risk: Medium-High (requires clear metrics)

**4. Risk-Reversal Guarantee**
> "We take all the risk. You keep [bonus] even if you refund."
- Best for: Launch offers, new products
- Risk: High (but builds trust fast)

### Guarantee Wording That Converts

**❌ Weak:**
> "30-day money-back guarantee."

**✅ Strong:**
> "Try it risk-free for 30 days. If you don't see [specific result], email us and we'll refund every penny + you keep [bonus]. No hard feelings."

## 🎓 PRICING PSYCHOLOGY PRINCIPLES

### 1. Anchoring
**Always show highest price first.** The brain uses the first number as a reference point.

Example:
```
Partnership: $50,000
Mastery: $15,000 ← Looks reasonable now
Accelerator: $5,000 ← Looks like a steal
```

### 2. Decoy Pricing
**Make the middle tier irresistible** by adding a slightly worse option at a similar price.

Example:
```
Basic: $97 (limited features)
Pro: $147 (ALL features) ← Best value
Premium: $297 (same as Pro + minor bonus)
```

### 3. Charm Pricing
**End prices in 7 or 9** for consumer products. Use round numbers for premium/B2B.

- Consumer: $47, $97, $697
- Premium/B2B: $5,000, $15,000, $50,000

### 4. Framing
**Frame price as investment, not cost.**

❌ "This costs $5,000."
✅ "This is a $5,000 investment that typically returns $50,000+ in 90 days."

### 5. Pain of Paying
**Reduce payment pain with:**
- Payment plans (smaller chunks)
- Annual billing (discount for prepay)
- Value-first framing (show ROI before price)

## 📞 SALES SCRIPT: HANDLING PRICE OBJECTIONS

### Objection: "This is too expensive."

**Response Framework:**

1. **Acknowledge:** "I totally get that. This is a significant investment."

2. **Reframe:** "Can I ask—compared to what? Because if we're talking about cost, sure, there are cheaper options. But if we're talking about investment and ROI..."

3. **Show ROI:** "Our clients typically see [X result] within [Y timeframe]. At that rate, this pays for itself in [Z days]. Does that math make sense?"

4. **Offer Alternative:** "If cash flow is the concern, we do have payment plans. Would that help?"

5. **Close:** "Based on what you've told me about [their goal], this is the fastest path. Should we get you started?"
