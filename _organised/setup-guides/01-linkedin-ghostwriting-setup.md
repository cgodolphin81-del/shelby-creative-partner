## 2️⃣ Step-by-Step Setup (Click-by-Click)

### Step 1: Set Up Your Agency Infrastructure (Day 1)
**Time: 2 hours**

1. **Create Google Workspace Account**
   - Go to [workspace.google.com](https://workspace.google.com)
   - Click "Get Started"
   - Enter your business name
   - Choose "Yes, I have a domain" (buy one if needed via Google Domains)
   - Create your email: `yourname@youragency.com`
   - Complete verification

2. **Create LinkedIn Company Page**
   - Log into LinkedIn personal account
   - Click "Work" (top right, grid icon)
   - Scroll down → "Create a Company Page"
   - Select "Company"
   - Enter: Company name, LinkedIn URL, website
   - Upload logo (300x300px minimum)
   - Add tagline: "Executive LinkedIn Ghostwriting & Thought Leadership"
   - Click "Create page"

3. **Set Up Notion Workspace**
   - Go to [notion.so](https://notion.so)
   - Sign up with Google account
   - Click "+ New page" → Name it "Client Content Calendar"
   - Create database with columns: Client, Post Date, Topic, Status, Platform
   - Duplicate for each client

### Step 2: Find & Hire Your Team (Days 2-3)
**Time: 4-6 hours**

1. **Post Job on Upwork**
   - Go to [upwork.com](https://upwork.com)
   - Click "Post a job"
   - Title: "LinkedIn Content Writer for Executive Clients"
   - Description:
     ```
     Looking for experienced LinkedIn content writer to create thought leadership posts for C-level executives.
     
     Requirements:
     - Native-level English
     - Experience writing for LinkedIn (share samples)
     - Understands executive tone & B2B audience
     - Can write 12-16 posts/month per client
     
     Budget: $800-$1,500/month per client
     Long-term opportunity
     ```
   - Category: Writing → Content Writing
   - Experience Level: Intermediate
   - Click "Post Job Now"

2. **Screen Applicants**
   - Look for: 90%+ Job Success Score, 4.8+ rating
   - Request writing samples (specifically LinkedIn posts)
   - Test task: Ask for 2 sample posts ($20 paid trial)
   - Hire 1-2 writers for backup

3. **Hire an Editor**
   - Same process, search for "LinkedIn editor" or "B2B content editor"
   - Budget: $400-$600/month
   - Must have sharp grammar skills and understand LinkedIn best practices

### Step 3: Create Your Service Package (Day 4)
**Time: 3 hours**

1. **Define Your Offering**
   - Create a one-pager in Google Docs or Canva
   - Include:
     - 12-16 LinkedIn posts/month
     - Content strategy call (30 min/month)
     - Engagement management (respond to comments)
     - Monthly analytics report
     - 48-hour turnaround on revisions

2. **Set Your Pricing**
   - Starter: $3,500/month (12 posts, basic engagement)
   - Professional: $6,000/month (16 posts, full engagement, strategy)
   - Executive: $10,000/month (20 posts, priority support, video scripts)

3. **Create Contract Template**
   - Use HelloSign or DocuSign (free tiers)
   - Include: 3-month minimum, payment terms, revision policy, confidentiality

### Step 4: Set Up Content Workflow (Day 5)
**Time: 2 hours**

1. **Create Client Onboarding Form**
   - Use Google Forms or Typeform (free)
   - Questions:
     - What's your expertise/industry?
     - Who's your target audience?
     - What topics do you want to cover?
     - What's your voice/tone? (Share examples)
     - Any topics to avoid?
     - LinkedIn profile URL

2. **Build Content Calendar Template**
   - In Notion, create columns:
     - Date
     - Client
     - Post Type (Story, Insight, Opinion, Promotion)
     - Topic
     - Draft Status
     - Approved (Y/N)
     - Scheduled (Y/N)
     - Performance (views, likes, comments)

3. **Set Up Approval Process**
   - Draft posts in Google Docs (shared with client)
   - Client reviews & comments (48-hour window)
   - You revise & finalize
   - Schedule via LinkedIn native or Buffer

### Step 5: Find Your First Clients (Days 6-14)
**Time: 2-3 hours/day**

1. **Optimize Your Own LinkedIn Profile**
   - Headline: "Helping executives build thought leadership on LinkedIn | Ghostwriter & Content Strategist"
   - About section: Tell your story, show results
   - Post daily about LinkedIn growth tips

2. **Outreach Strategy**
   - Search: "CEO", "Founder", "VP" in your target industries
   - Connect with 20-30 prospects/day
   - Personalized message:
     ```
     Hi [Name], I noticed you're posting valuable insights on [topic]. I help executives like you scale their LinkedIn presence with done-for-you content (12-16 posts/month). Would you be open to a quick chat about your content goals?
     ```

3. **Alternative Channels**
   - Join CEO/founder Facebook groups
   - Post in LinkedIn groups for entrepreneurs
   - Partner with executive coaches (they have client lists)
   - Reach out to VC firms (offer portfolio company discounts)

## 5️⃣ Troubleshooting Guide

### Problem: Can't Find Quality Writers
**Solutions:**
- Increase budget to $1,200+/month for experienced writers
- Look for writers from Philippines, India, Eastern Europe (high quality, lower cost)
- Ask for LinkedIn-specific samples (not blog posts)
- Run paid test: $30 for 3 sample posts, hire the best

### Problem: Clients Want Revisions on Every Post
**Solutions:**
- Set clear expectations upfront: "2 rounds of revisions included"
- Create detailed onboarding form to capture voice upfront
- Provide style guide examples
- Charge extra for excessive revisions ($50/hour after 2 rounds)

### Problem: Posts Aren't Getting Engagement
**Solutions:**
- Review content: Is it too promotional? Shift to 80% value, 20% promo
- Post timing: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM or 12-1 PM (client's timezone)
- Engage first: Comment on 10 industry posts before posting your own
- Use hooks: First line must grab attention (story, question, bold statement)
- Add visuals: Posts with images get 2x engagement

### Problem: Client Wants to Cancel After Month 1
**Solutions:**
- 3-month minimum in contract (non-negotiable)
- Show quick wins: Track and report early engagement wins
- Set realistic expectations: "LinkedIn growth takes 90 days minimum"
- Offer bonus: "Sign for 6 months, get 4 free posts"

### Problem: You're Overwhelmed Managing Multiple Clients
**Solutions:**
- Hire a part-time account manager ($500-$800/month)
- Use templates for everything (emails, reports, onboarding)
- Batch work: Review all client content on same day
- Raise prices: Fewer clients, same revenue
