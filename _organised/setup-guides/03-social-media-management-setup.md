## 2️⃣ Step-by-Step Setup (Click-by-Click)

### Step 1: Set Up Your Agency Infrastructure (Day 1)
**Time: 2 hours**

1. **Create Google Workspace**
   - Go to [workspace.google.com](https://workspace.google.com)
   - Sign up with your business name
   - Create email: `hello@youragency.com`
   - Set up email signature with social links

2. **Set Up Meta Business Suite**
   - Go to [business.facebook.com](https://business.facebook.com)
   - Click "Create Account"
   - Add your agency's Facebook Page (create if needed)
   - Add Instagram account (connect to Facebook Page)
   - Complete business verification (optional but recommended)

3. **Create Agency Social Profiles**
   - Facebook Page: "Your Agency Name"
   - Instagram: @youragency
   - LinkedIn Company Page
   - TikTok: @youragency
   - Post consistently to show you practice what you preach

### Step 2: Choose & Set Up Scheduling Tool (Day 2)
**Time: 2 hours**

1. **Buffer Setup (Recommended for Starters)**
   - Go to [buffer.com](https://buffer.com)
   - Sign up for free plan (3 channels)
   - Connect client social accounts (you'll need client login or partner access)
   - Create posting schedule templates
   - Upgrade to Paid Plan ($15/month) for more channels

2. **Alternative: Hootsuite (For Larger Operations)**
   - Go to [hootsuite.com](https://hootsuite.com)
   - Start free trial
   - Connect unlimited social profiles
   - Set up content calendar
   - Plan: Professional ($99/month) for up to 10 profiles

3. **Create Content Calendar Template**
   - Use Google Sheets or Notion
   - Columns: Date, Platform, Content Type, Caption, Visual, Status, Posted, Performance

### Step 3: Hire Your Team (Days 3-5)
**Time: 6-8 hours**

1. **Post Job: Content Creator**
   - Platform: Upwork or Facebook Groups
   - Title: "Social Media Content Creator"
   - Description:
     ```
     Looking for creative social media content creator for small business clients.
     
     Requirements:
     - Experience creating content for Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn
     - Can write engaging captions
     - Basic graphic design skills (Canva)
     - Understands social media trends
     - Portfolio required
     
     Budget: $500-$1,000/month per client (part-time)
     ```

2. **Post Job: Graphic Designer**
   - Search for Canva specialists
   - Budget: $400-$800/month
   - Must have social media portfolio

3. **Post Job: Video Editor**
   - Search for Reels/TikTok editor
   - Must know CapCut or similar
   - Budget: $300-$600/month

4. **Post Job: Community Manager**
   - Respond to comments and DMs
   - Budget: $300-$500/month
   - Must have excellent English and customer service skills

5. **Screen & Hire**
   - Review portfolios carefully
   - Paid test: Create 3 posts for $30
   - Hire best 1-2 candidates per role

### Step 4: Create Service Packages (Day 6)
**Time: 3 hours**

1. **Define Your Offerings**

   **Starter - $2,000/month**
   - 3 platforms (IG, FB, LinkedIn)
   - 12 posts/month (3/week)
   - Basic graphics
   - Caption writing
   - Scheduling
   - Monthly analytics report

   **Growth - $4,500/month**
   - 4 platforms (add TikTok)
   - 20 posts/month (5/week)
   - Custom graphics + 4 Reels/month
   - Hashtag research
   - Community management (1 hour/day)
   - Bi-weekly strategy call
   - Bi-weekly report

   **Premium - $8,000/month**
   - 5 platforms (all major)
   - 30 posts/month (daily)
   - 8 Reels/TikToks/month
   - Premium graphics
   - Full community management
   - Weekly strategy call
   - Weekly report
   - Ad management (ad spend separate)

2. **Create Contract Template**
   - 3-month minimum
   - Payment due net-7
   - Ad spend billed separately
   - 2 revision rounds per post
   - Content approval required 48 hours before posting

3. **Build Proposal Template**
   - Use Canva
   - Include: Social audit, competitor analysis, content strategy, pricing, timeline

### Step 5: Set Up Operations (Days 7-8)
**Time: 4 hours**

1. **Client Onboarding Checklist**
   - Brand assets (logo, colors, fonts)
   - Access to social accounts (or create new)
   - Brand voice guidelines
   - Product/service info
   - Competitor accounts to watch
   - Past top-performing posts
   - Content do's and don'ts

2. **Content Approval Workflow**
   - Create content in Canva
   - Share via Google Drive folder
   - Client reviews & comments (48-hour window)
   - Revise based on feedback
   - Schedule approved posts in Buffer

3. **Reporting Template**
   - Google Sheets or Data Studio
   - Track per platform: Followers, Reach, Engagement, Top Posts, Growth
   - Monthly summary with insights & recommendations

4. **Create SOPs**
   - How to create a post (step-by-step)
   - Hashtag research process
   - Community management guidelines
   - Crisis management (negative comments)
   - Monthly reporting process

### Step 6: Find Clients (Days 9-21)
**Time: 2-3 hours/day**

1. **Target Local Businesses**
   - Restaurants, salons, gyms, real estate agents
   - They need social media but don't have time
   - Search Google Maps, Instagram location tags

2. **Outreach Message Template**
   ```
   Hi [Name],
   
   I noticed [Business] hasn't posted on Instagram in [X weeks]. I help local businesses like yours grow their social presence without the hassle.
   
   We handle everything: content creation, posting, engagement, and reporting. Our clients see 2-3x engagement growth in the first 90 days.
   
   Would you be open to a free social media audit? I'll show you 3 quick wins you can implement this week.
   
   Best,
   [Your Name]
   ```

3. **Alternative Channels**
   - Facebook Groups: Local business groups
   - Instagram: Engage with local business posts, then DM
   - LinkedIn: Search "Small Business Owner" + your city
   - Networking events (local chamber of commerce)
   - Partner with web design agencies (they need social partners)

## 5️⃣ Troubleshooting Guide

### Problem: Client Wants Posts Approved Instantly
**Solutions:**
- Set clear expectations in contract: "48-hour approval window"
- Explain: "Quality content takes time. Rushed = mistakes"
- Offer rush service: "$50 extra for 24-hour turnaround"
- Batch content: Send 2 weeks at a time for approval

### Problem: Client Doesn't Like Any Content
**Solutions:**
- Go back to onboarding: Did you capture their voice?
- Ask for specific examples of posts they love
- Create mood board before creating content
- Start with safer, generic content, then iterate
- Consider if this client is a good fit

### Problem: Engagement Is Low
**Solutions:**
- Review content: Is it too promotional? (80/20 rule: 80% value, 20% promo)
- Post at optimal times (use platform insights)
- Engage with others first (comment on 10 posts before posting)
- Use relevant hashtags (10-15 for Instagram, 3-5 for LinkedIn)
- Try different content types (Reels get more reach)

### Problem: Client Wants to Be in Every Post
**Solutions:**
- Educate: "People follow for value, not constant selling"
- Show examples: Their best posts probably aren't product shots
- Compromise: 1-2 product posts/week max
- Suggest user-generated content instead

### Problem: You're Overwhelmed With Multiple Clients
**Solutions:**
- Hire account manager ($800-$1,200/month)
- Use templates for everything
- Batch content creation (all clients' content on same day)
- Raise prices (fewer clients, same revenue)
- Fire difficult clients

### Problem: Negative Comments on Client Posts
**Solutions:**
- Have crisis SOP ready
- Don't delete (unless spam/hate)
- Respond professionally: "We'd love to make this right. Please DM us."
- Take conversation offline
- Report to client immediately
