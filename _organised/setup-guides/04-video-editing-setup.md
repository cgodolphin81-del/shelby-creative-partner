## 2️⃣ Step-by-Step Setup (Click-by-Click)

### Step 1: Learn Video Editing Basics (Days 1-3)
**Time: 8-10 hours**

1. **Choose Your Editing Software**
   - **DaVinci Resolve** (Recommended for beginners)
     - Download from [blackmagicdesign.com](https://blackmagicdesign.com/products/davinciresolve)
     - Free version is incredibly powerful
     - Watch: "DaVinci Resolve Beginner Tutorial" (2 hours on YouTube)
   
   - **Adobe Premiere Pro** (Industry standard)
     - Subscribe via Adobe Creative Cloud ($23/month)
     - Download and install
     - Watch: "Premiere Pro in 30 Minutes" (YouTube)

2. **Complete Basic Training**
   - YouTube: "Video Editing for Beginners" (4-5 hours)
   - Practice with free stock footage (Pexels, Pixabay)
   - Learn: Cutting, transitions, color correction, audio, text overlays

3. **Create Portfolio Pieces**
   - Edit 3-5 sample videos (use free footage)
   - Show different styles: Talking head, montage, tutorial, vlog
   - Upload to YouTube (unlisted) for portfolio

### Step 2: Set Up Your Agency (Day 4)
**Time: 2 hours**

1. **Create Google Workspace**
   - Go to [workspace.google.com](https://workspace.google.com)
   - Sign up with your business name
   - Create email: `hello@youragency.com`

2. **Set Up Frame.io (Client Review)**
   - Go to [frame.io](https://frame.io)
   - Sign up for free plan (2 projects)
   - This allows clients to comment directly on videos
   - Upgrade to paid ($39/month) as you grow

3. **Create YouTube Channel**
   - Go to YouTube → Create Channel
   - Name: Your Agency Name
   - Upload portfolio videos (unlisted or public)
   - Create channel art in Canva

### Step 3: Hire Your Team (Days 5-7)
**Time: 5-7 hours**

1. **Post Job: Video Editor**
   - Platform: Upwork
   - Title: "YouTube Video Editor - Long Form Content"
   - Description:
     ```
     Looking for experienced YouTube video editor for content creators.
     
     Requirements:
     - 2+ years editing YouTube videos
     - Proficient in Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve
     - Can edit talking head, tutorials, vlogs
     - Understands pacing, retention, storytelling
     - Portfolio required (YouTube links)
     
     Budget: $600-$1,200/month (8-16 videos/month)
     Long-term opportunity
     ```

2. **Post Job: Thumbnail Designer**
   - Search for "YouTube thumbnail designer"
   - Must have portfolio of high-CTR thumbnails
   - Budget: $200-$400/month (20-40 thumbnails)

3. **Post Job: Shorts/Reels Editor**
   - Search for "CapCut editor" or "Shorts editor"
   - Must understand vertical video, trends, hooks
   - Budget: $200-$400/month (8-16 shorts)

4. **Screen & Hire**
   - Review portfolios carefully (look for retention-focused editing)
   - Paid test: Edit 1 video from raw footage ($50)
   - Hire best candidate for each role

### Step 4: Create Service Packages (Day 8)
**Time: 3 hours**

1. **Define Your Offerings**

   **Starter - $2,000/month**
   - 8 long-form videos/month (2/week)
   - Basic editing (cuts, transitions, music)
   - 8 thumbnails
   - 48-hour turnaround
   - 2 revision rounds

   **Growth - $4,000/month**
   - 12 long-form videos/month (3/week)
   - Advanced editing (graphics, B-roll, color)
   - 12 thumbnails
   - 4 Shorts/Reels
   - 24-hour turnaround
   - 3 revision rounds
   - Weekly strategy call

   **Premium - $6,000/month**
   - 16 long-form videos/month (4/week)
   - Premium editing (motion graphics, sound design)
   - 16 thumbnails
   - 8 Shorts/Reels
   - 24-hour turnaround
   - Unlimited revisions
   - Dedicated editor
   - Daily communication

2. **Create Contract Template**
   - 3-month minimum
   - Payment due net-7
   - Raw footage provided by client
   - 48-hour review window
   - Additional revisions: $50/hour

3. **Build Proposal Template**
   - Use Canva
   - Include: Channel audit, editing style examples, pricing, timeline

### Step 5: Set Up Workflow (Days 9-10)
**Time: 4 hours**

1. **File Transfer System**
   - Create Google Drive folder structure:
     ```
     /Client Name
       /01_Raw_Footage
       /02_Work_in_Progress
       /03_For_Review
       /04_Final_Videos
       /05_Thumbnails
     ```
   - Share folder with client (view-only for Raw, edit for others)

2. **Editing Workflow**
   - Client uploads raw footage to Drive
   - Editor downloads, edits
   - Uploads draft to Frame.io
   - Client comments/approves
   - Editor makes revisions
   - Final export uploaded to Drive
   - Client publishes

3. **Thumbnail Workflow**
   - Editor provides key frame or concept
   - Designer creates 2-3 options
   - Client chooses
   - Final delivered with video

4. **Create SOPs**
   - Video editing checklist
   - Thumbnail design guidelines
   - File naming conventions
   - Export settings (1080p, 4K, codec, bitrate)

### Step 6: Find Clients (Days 11-21)
**Time: 2-3 hours/day**

1. **Target YouTubers**
   - Search YouTube for channels with 10K-500K subscribers
   - They have revenue but often overwhelmed with editing
   - Look for inconsistent upload schedules (they need help)

2. **Outreach Message Template**
   ```
   Hi [Name],
   
   Love your content on [topic]! I noticed you're posting [X videos/month]—I help creators like you scale to [2X] without burning out.
   
   We handle everything: editing, thumbnails, shorts. Our clients typically save 15-20 hours/week and increase upload consistency.
   
   Would you be open to a free trial edit? Send me raw footage for one video, and I'll show you what we can do.
   
   Best,
   [Your Name]
   [Your YouTube Channel]
   ```

3. **Alternative Channels**
   - YouTube comments (helpful comments, then DM)
   - Twitter/X (creator community is active)
   - Facebook Groups: "YouTubers", "Content Creators"
   - Discord servers for creators
   - Partner with video equipment companies (affiliate referrals)

## 5️⃣ Troubleshooting Guide

### Problem: Editor Is Too Slow
**Solutions:**
- Set clear deadlines in contract
- Use Frame.io for faster feedback loops
- Have backup editor on bench
- Pay per video (incentivizes speed) vs. hourly
- If chronic: Replace editor

### Problem: Client Wants Endless Revisions
**Solutions:**
- Contract: "3 revision rounds included"
- Charge for additional: "$50/hour after 3 rounds"
- Get detailed feedback upfront (use Frame.io comments)
- Educate: "More revisions = slower turnaround"

### Problem: Video Quality Is Inconsistent
**Solutions:**
- Create detailed style guide for each client
- Provide reference videos (editing style, pacing)
- Weekly quality check calls with editor
- Have senior editor review before sending to client

### Problem: Client's Raw Footage Is Terrible
**Solutions:**
- Set expectations: "We can't fix bad audio or shaky footage"
- Provide client with recording guidelines
- Suggest equipment upgrades (mic, lighting)
- Charge extra for heavy cleanup

### Problem: YouTube Algorithm Changes Affect Views
**Solutions:**
- Educate client: "We control quality, not algorithm"
- Focus on retention metrics (watch time, CTR)
- A/B test thumbnails
- Stay updated on trends (join creator communities)

### Problem: You're the Bottleneck
**Solutions:**
- Hire project manager ($800-$1,200/month)
- Create detailed SOPs so anyone can manage
- Use Asana/Trello for task tracking
- Limit clients to what your team can handle
