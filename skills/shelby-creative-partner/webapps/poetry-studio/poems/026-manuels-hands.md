# Poem 026: "Manuel's Hands"

**Inspired by:** Episode 022 — Manuel, the Quechua caretaker  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

Manuel is sixty.
His hands are lined with stone dust.
His face is lined with sun.
His heart is lined with memory.

He comes to Machu Picchu every week.
To clean. To pray. To remember.

"My family never left," he says.
"We never forgot.
The city was not lost.
It was sleeping."

He touches the Temple of the Sun
like a grandfather touches a grandchild.
Gently. Reverently.
Knowing he will not live forever,
but the stone will.

"You are here, are you not?"
he says to me.
"The Incas are gone.
But you are here.
They built for you.
They built for me.
They built for eternity."

I ask him what he wants people to understand.
He smiles.
"That we are still here.
That the Incas did not disappear.
That we are their children.
That this is not a ruin.
This is our home."

He disappears into the mist.
I stand in the temple, listening.
The stones are speaking.
I am finally learning how to hear.
