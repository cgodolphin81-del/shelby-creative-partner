# Poem 019: "What the Mountain Taught Me"

**Inspired by:** Episode 019 — Mount Kailash kora  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

An Australian trekker asked:
"Why are you doing this?"

I said:
"To see what the mountain teaches me."

"And what has it taught you so far?"

I thought about the thin air.
The burning lungs.
The old woman who laughed
when I asked if it gets easier.

I thought about the pass:
5,630 meters of pure refusal,
the mountain saying:
*You will go at my pace,
not yours.*

I thought about the descent:
knees shaking,
spirit singing,
the knowledge that I was small
and the world was vast
and that was okay.

I told the trekker:
"That I am small.
That the world is vast.
That there are things more important
than my comfort,
my schedule,
my ego."

They nodded.
They understood.

That night I dreamed
of walking forever,
of becoming the path,
of footsteps worn into stone,
of breath becoming wind
on the summit.

The mountain taught me
that surrender is not defeat.
That circling is not aimless.
That some things remain
untouched,
unclimbed,
eternal.

And that is enough.
More than enough.
It is everything.
