# Poem 020: "Dragon's Blood Tree"

**Inspired by:** Episode 020 — Socotra Island  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

The tree does not know
it is unique.

It simply grows:
umbrella canopy
reaching toward the Socotri sun,
thick trunk gnarled
by centuries of cyclones,
red sap bleeding
when the bark is cut.

It does not know
that it grows nowhere else.
Not in Madagascar.
Not in Africa.
Not in the botanical gardens
of Europe.

Just here.
On this island
that looks like it slipped
through a crack in reality.

Ahmed cuts the bark
with reverence,
collects the resin tears,
hands them to me:
warm, fragrant,
the color of fresh blood.

"Medicine. Dye. Varnish. Magic."

I hold the hardened tears
in my palm,
feel the weight of isolation,
of evolution taking a left turn
when the rest of the world
went right.

The tree stands sentinel.
It has stood for five hundred years.
Maybe a thousand.
It will stand long after I am gone.

It does not know it is rare.
It does not know it is precious.
It does not know I am crying.

It simply grows.
It simply bleeds.
It simply remains.

And I carry its tears home,
these small red stones,
reminders that some things
belong to one place only,
and that is why they matter.
