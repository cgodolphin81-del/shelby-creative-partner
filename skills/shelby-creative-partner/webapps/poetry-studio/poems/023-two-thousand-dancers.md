# Poem 023: "Two Thousand Dancers"

**Inspired by:** Episode 21 — The apsaras of Angkor Wat  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

Two thousand dancers.
No two alike.

Different hairstyles.
Different jewelry.
Different expressions.
Different poses.

Each one unique.
Each one perfect.
Each one carved by hands
that would never sign their work.

I knelt beside a lintel,
tracing the carved figure with my eyes,
trying to understand
how a human hand
could capture
the exact curve of a shoulder,
the exact tilt of a head,
the exact smile that says
*I know something you do not.*

The carver did not know
his name would be forgotten.
He did not know
eight hundred years later
I would kneel before his work.
He did not know
his hands would become part of my story.

He simply created.
He believed that his labor mattered.
He believed that beauty is worship.
He believed that art is prayer.

He was right.

I am kneeling here,
honoring his skill,
honoring his devotion,
honoring the knowledge
that he created for the gods,
not for fame.

The apsara dances on.
Her stone feet do not tire.
Her stone smile does not fade.
Her stone dance
outlasts the empire that built her.

I am temporary.
She is eternal.
And yet,
in this moment,
we are equal.
She needs me to see her.
I need her to remember.

Two thousand dancers.
Two thousand prayers.
Two thousand reasons
to believe that what we create
can outlast us.
