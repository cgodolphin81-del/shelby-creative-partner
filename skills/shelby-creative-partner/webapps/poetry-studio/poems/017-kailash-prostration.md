# Poem 017: "Prostration"

**Inspired by:** Episode 019 — Mount Kailash pilgrimage  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

The Tibetan woman
does not walk the kora.
She becomes it.

Forehead to stone.
Hands extended in supplication.
Body measuring earth
in increments of devotion.

Fifty-two kilometers
of this sacred geometry:
rise, step, fall.
rise, step, fall.

Three weeks to circle
what I walked in two.
She will arrive at the same place
having traveled infinitely further.

The mountain does not care
about efficiency.
The mountain cares about
the weight of your soul
against the ground.

I passed her on the descent,
still in the valley,
still becoming the path.

Her eyes met mine.
No envy. No judgment.
Just the quiet certainty
of someone who knows
the destination is not the point.

I wanted to bow.
I wanted to apologize
for my walking shoes,
my two-day timeline,
my tourist heart.

She smiled instead.
"The mountain does not change,"
she said through the wind.
"We change."

Then she fell forward again,
forehead to stone,
measuring her way
toward whatever waits
at the center of devotion.

I walked on,
lighter somehow,
as if her prostrations
had lifted something
I didn't know I was carrying.
