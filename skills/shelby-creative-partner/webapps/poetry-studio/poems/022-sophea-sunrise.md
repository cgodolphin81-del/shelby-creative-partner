# Poem 022: "Sophea's Sunrise"

**Inspired by:** Episode 21 — Sophea, the tuk-tuk driver  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

Sophea has brought tourists to Angkor
every day for ten years.
He knows the sunrise times.
He knows which temples are crowded.
He knows which ones are quiet.

He has seen ten thousand sunrises
over the lotus towers.
And he says he never tires of them.

"Why not?" I asked.
"You must see it constantly."

He was quiet for a moment.
"Because it reminds me that my ancestors
built something that outlasted them.
That we are part of something larger.
That Cambodia is more than war,
more than poverty,
more than the Khmer Rouge.

We are the people who built Angkor."

I thought about my own ancestors,
whatever they built,
whatever they carved into stone.
Did they know I would stand here?
Did they know I would see what they built?

Probably not.
And that's the point.

Sophea does not build temples.
He drives a tuk-tuk.
He brings tourists.
He takes them to the outer temples,
the ones the world does not visit.
The ones where you can still feel
the jungle taking back the stone.

He is a keeper.
Like the woman who walked the kora
body-length by body-length.
Like the monks who maintain Angkor Wat.
Like the carvers who spent thirty years
chiseling apsaras into stone.

Keepers of the story.
Keepers of the memory.
Keepers of the knowledge
that we are temporary,
but what we create
can be eternal.

Sophea has brought me to Angkor
many times.
Every day, the sunrise is different.
Every day, the temple is the same.
Every day, I learn something new.

The temple does not change.
We change.
That is the point.
