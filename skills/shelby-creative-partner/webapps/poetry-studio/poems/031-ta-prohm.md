# Poem 031: "Ta Prohm"

**Inspired by:** Episode 021 — The jungle reclaiming Ta Prohm  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

At Ta Prohm,
the jungle did not destroy the temple.
It embraced it.

Silk-cotton trees grew from the foundations,
their roots snaking through doorways,
wrapping around pillars,
lifting stone blocks like toys.

One tree has grown so large
that its roots form a complete archway
over the main entrance.
Another has split a gallery wall in two,
holding the pieces apart
like a cosmic wedge.

The West calls it destruction.
The Khmer call it collaboration.

You cannot remove the tree
without destroying the temple.
You cannot save the temple
without killing the tree.

So they do nothing.
They let them be.
Nature and human creation, together.

This is the Cambodian way.

I walked through Ta Prohm
in something approaching awe.
This was not restoration.
This was collaboration.

The trees had not destroyed the temple.
They had become part of it,
their roots holding together
what centuries of decay had weakened.

I thought about the West's obsession
with returning things to their "original" state.
At Ta Prohm,
there is no original state.

There is only what exists now:
temple and tree,
stone and root,
human and nature,
inseparable.

The jungle did not win.
The temple did not win.
They became something new together.

And that is the lesson:
sometimes the most beautiful thing
is not preservation,
not destruction,
but the courage to let go
and become something
neither could be alone.
