# Poem 025: "Four Hundred Years"

**Inspired by:** Episode 022 — Machu Picchu waiting  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

The city was never lost.
The Quechua people always knew.
Their grandparents knew.
Their grandparents' grandparents knew.

But the world forgot.

For four hundred years,
the vines crept up the stone walls.
For four hundred years,
the moss softened the temple edges.
For four hundred years,
the orchids bloomed in palace cracks.

The city waited.

Not impatiently.
Not angrily.
Just... waited.

Like a seed in winter.
Like a prayer in the dark.
Like a song no one is singing
but everyone remembers.

In 1911, Hiram Bingham came.
He called it "The Lost City."
The name stuck.
But it was never lost.

It was waiting.

For the right eyes.
For the right hearts.
For the moment when the world
would need to remember
that empires fall,
but the stones remain.

I stood in the Temple of the Sun
and understood:
I am not the first to stand here.
I will not be the last.

The city waited four hundred years.
It will wait four hundred more.
For whoever needs to remember
that we are small,
that the mountains are old,
and that is okay.
