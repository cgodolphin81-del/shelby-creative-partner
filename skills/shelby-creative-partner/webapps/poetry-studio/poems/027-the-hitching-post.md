# Poem 027: "The Hitching Post"

**Inspired by:** Episode 022 — Intihuatana Stone  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

The Incas believed
that the sun could drift away.
That winter was the sun
straying too far from earth.
That stone could anchor light.

So they carved the Intihuatana.
The Hitching Post of the Sun.
A rough block of granite,
two meters tall,
shaped by human hands,
still bearing the marks
of the mountain.

I placed my hand on the stone.
A French woman said it was alive.
I felt nothing.
Then I felt everything.

The weight of centuries.
The prayers of thousands.
The hands that had touched it before—
Inca priests, Quechua villagers,
pilgrims, tourists,
all of them leaving something,
all of them taking something.

The stone is a vessel.
A container.
A witness.

It has seen empires rise.
It has seen empires fall.
It has seen four hundred years of silence.
It has seen the world discover it.

And it has remained.

I removed my hand.
The French woman was gone.
The stone remained.

That is the lesson of the hitching post:
we are temporary.
The stone is eternal.
And that is okay.
