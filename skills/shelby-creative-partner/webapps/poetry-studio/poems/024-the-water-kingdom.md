# Poem 024: "The Water Kingdom"

**Inspired by:** Episode 21 — Angkor's hydraulic empire  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

The Khmer built an empire on water.
Not gold. Not oil. Not power.
Water.

They engineered a system of reservoirs,
canals, moats,
that controlled the monsoon,
that stored the wet season,
that released the dry season.
Rice harvest after rice harvest.
Empire flourishing.

Then the water betrayed them.
Too much. Too little.
Clogged canals. Broken dikes.
The empire could not survive
without its hydraulic heart.

They left.
The capital moved to Phnom Penh.
Angkor was abandoned.
The jungle moved in.
The temples became a secret.

And I wonder:
what is our water?
What is the infrastructure we depend on
that we barely understand?
Our power grids?
Our water systems?
Our supply chains?

What happens if they fail?
What happens if the water betrays us?

The Khmer knew.
They built an empire on water,
and when the water betrayed them,
they left.

Not with a fight.
Not with a last stand.
Just... gone.

The temples remain.
The reservoirs are dry.
The canals are overgrown.
But the lesson remains:
no empire is permanent.
no infrastructure is eternal.
no civilization is safe.

Except one:
the temple.
The art.
The beauty.
The prayer made permanent.

The water kingdom fell.
The stone kingdom remains.

I sit on the edge of the moat,
watching the sunset paint the temple gold,
and I understand:
we are temporary.
The world is vast.
And that is okay.
