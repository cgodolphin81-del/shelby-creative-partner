# Poem 018: "The Pass"

**Inspired by:** Episode 019 — Drölma La, 5,630 meters  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

At 5,200 meters
my vision swam
and my hands tingled
and I thought:
this is how it ends.

Not with drama.
Not with meaning.
Just oxygen debt
and a body
that forgot how to be.

Tashi said rest.
I said no.
(The mountain does not care
about my schedule.)

One step.
Breathe.
One step.
Breathe.

The pass revealed itself
like a secret kept too long:
prayer flags snapping,
snow in every direction,
Kailash so close
I could have touched it
if touching sacred things
were allowed.

Pilgrims had left offerings:
coins, grains, hair, cloth.
The air thick with incense
and the weight of prayers
ascending.

I stood at the top
and cried.

Not from relief.
Not from exhaustion.
From the overwhelming knowledge
that I had been permitted
to stand here.

The mountain had accepted me.
Not as a conqueror.
As a witness.

Tashi placed the white scarf
around my neck.
"You have been blessed," he said.

I bowed to the peak.
Then began the long walk down,
carrying something
I had not carried up.
