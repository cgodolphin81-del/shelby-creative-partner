# Poem 028: "Sunrise Over Angkor"

**Inspired by:** Episode 021 — Angkor Wat sunrise  
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Form:** Free verse

---

The sky was still dark
when Sophea picked me up.
Siem Reap slept:
closed markets, shuttered bars,
a monk in saffron robes
walking silently toward his temple.

We arrived before the crowds.
Perhaps fifty others had made
the same pilgrimage:
Japanese tourists with tripods,
French backpackers with thermoses,
Indian pilgrims already chanting.

We stood together in the predawn hush.
Waiting.

It rose at 5:47 AM.

The first light hit the central tower,
and for a moment,
the entire temple seemed to glow from within.
The stone turned gold.
The reflection in the moat shimmered.

Someone gasped.
Someone else began to cry.

I understood:
This was not just a building.
This was a prayer made permanent.
A king's offering to the gods,
carved in stone,
endured beyond the empire that created it,
beyond the religion that inspired it,
beyond the civilization that built it.

Angkor Wat was no longer Hindu.
It was no longer Buddhist.
It was something older than both:
a testament to the human need
to create something
that outlasts us.

The sun climbed.
The crowds arrived.
The temple remained.

And I carried the gold light with me,
a memory of the moment
when stone became prayer,
when dawn became devotion,
when I understood
that what we build
can outlast
what we are.
