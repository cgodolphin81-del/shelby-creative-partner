# Song 019: "The Mountain That Remains"

**Inspired by:** Episode 019 — Mount Kailash, Tibet  
**Genre:** Folk/Ambient  
**Tempo:** 68 BPM (slow, meditative)  
**Key:** D minor (solemn, spiritual)  
**Duration:** ~4:30

---

## Structure

**Intro** (0:00-0:45)
- Tibetan singing bowl (D note, sustained)
- Distant wind sounds
- Sparse piano notes (D, F, A — letting ring)

**Verse 1** (0:45-1:30)
```
Six thousand meters above the world
Where the air grows thin and the prayers unfurl
Four faiths converge on a single peak
Where the sacred and the silent speak

No boot has touched that summit snow
No rope has climbed where the cold winds blow
The mountain stands, untouched, eternal
A doorway to the celestial
```

**Chorus** (1:30-2:00)
```
Kailash, Kailash
Center of the world
Kailash, Kailash
Where the flags are curled

Walk the kora, shed your name
Nothing here is quite the same
The mountain does not change — you do
Fifty-two kilometers through
```

**Verse 2** (2:00-2:45)
```
Met a woman, eighty years
Seventy koras, maybe more, she swears
"The mountain doesn't change," she said
"We change instead. We change instead."

Drölma La at 5,630
Every breath a negotiation
One step, breathe, one step more
The mountain waits as it did before
```

**Chorus** (2:45-3:15)
```
Kailash, Kailash
Center of the world
Kailash, Kailash
Where the flags are curled

Walk the kora, shed your name
Nothing here is quite the same
The mountain does not change — you do
Fifty-two kilometers through
```

**Bridge** (3:15-3:45)
- Instrumental: Tibetan horn (dungchen), low and resonant
- Prayer wheels spinning (recorded field sound)
- Mantra chant underneath (Om Mani Padme Hum)

**Outro** (3:45-4:30)
```
I came to conquer, left to kneel
The mountain taught me what is real
Not to climb, but to circle round
Where the sacred and the silent...

...remain.
```
- Final singing bowl strike
- Fade to wind silence

---

## Production Notes

**Instruments:**
- Tibetan singing bowls (D, A)
- Dungchen (Tibetan long horn)
- Acoustic guitar (fingerpicking, reverb-heavy)
- Piano (sparse, high register)
- Field recordings: wind, prayer flags, distant chants

**Vocal Style:**
- Intimate, close-mic'd
- Slight reverb to suggest mountain space
- Chorus layers for spiritual feel

**Mood:**
- Reverent
- Meditative
- Humbling
- Transformative
