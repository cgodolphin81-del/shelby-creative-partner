# Song 021: "The Faces of Bayon"

**Inspired by:** Episode 021 — Angkor Wat, Cambodia  
**Genre:** Folk/Ambient with Khmer influences  
**Tempo:** 72 BPM (meditative, slow)  
**Key:** G minor (mysterious, ancient)  
**Duration:** ~4:15

---

## Structure

**Intro** (0:00-0:45)
- Traditional Khmer roneat (xylophone) — slow, resonant notes
- Temple bell (deep, sustained)
- Distant jungle sounds (birds, wind)

**Verse 1** (0:45-1:30)
```
Five towers rise from the morning mist
Ancient stones where the lotus exists
King Suryavarman built it for Vishnu's throne
Now the Buddha sits where the gods have flown

Thirty years of hands carving the sand
Thirty years of faith held in stone's command
The temple doesn't care who rules the land
It just stands, it stands, it stands
```

**Chorus** (1:30-2:00)
```
Oh Angkor, Angkor
Where the jungle meets the stone
Oh Angkor, Angkor
In the heart of the Khmer home

Two hundred faces, watching all
From the Bayon's towers, standing tall
We come and go, but they remain
The faces of the ancient Khmer rain
```

**Verse 2** (2:00-2:45)
```
Ta Prohm's trees have wrapped their arms
Around the corridors, deep in charms
Silk-cotton roots have split the wall
Nature and human, one and all

The apsaras dance on every wall
Two thousand dancers, none alike at all
Each one unique, each one divine
Carved by hands that crossed the line
```

**Chorus** (2:45-3:15)
```
Oh Angkor, Angkor
Where the jungle meets the stone
Oh Angkor, Angkor
In the heart of the Khmer home

Two hundred faces, watching all
From the Bayon's towers, standing tall
We come and go, but they remain
The faces of the ancient Khmer rain
```

**Bridge** (3:15-3:45)
- Khmer flute solo (slow, melancholic)
- Temple bells in background
- Whispered Khmer prayer (Om Tat Sat)

**Outro** (3:45-4:15)
```
The sun sets gold on the moat so still
The temple dreams of a distant hill
Sophea says, "You have learned well"
That we are small, and the world is hell...

...and holy.
```
- Final roneat note
- Temple bell fade
- Jungle night sounds

## Production Notes

**Instruments:**
- Roneat (Khmer xylophone)
- Khmer flute (sralai)
- Temple bells (deep bronze)
- Acoustic guitar (fingerpicking)
- Field recordings: jungle, temple wind, birds

**Vocal Style:**
- Intimate, storytelling delivery
- Slight reverb to suggest temple space
- Khmer language phrases woven in

**Mood:**
- Reverent
- Mysterious
- Timeless
- Humbling
