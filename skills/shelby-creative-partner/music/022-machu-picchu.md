# Song 022: "The Hitching Post of the Sun"

**Inspired by:** Episode 022 — Machu Picchu, Peru  
**Genre:** Folk/Ambient with Andean influences  
**Tempo:** 70 BPM (meditative, mountain pace)  
**Key:** A minor (solemn, ancient)  
**Duration:** ~4:30

---

## Structure

**Intro** (0:00-0:45)
- Andean panpipes (zampoña) — slow, haunting melody
- Charango (small Andean guitar) — arpeggiated
- Mountain wind sounds (field recording)

**Verse 1** (0:45-1:30)
```
Four hundred years the city waited
In the clouds where the condors fly
Hiram Bingham came searching
Found a kingdom touch the sky

Not lost, just sleeping
Not gone, just still
The stones remember everything
The mountains always will
```

**Chorus** (1:30-2:00)
```
Oh Machu Picchu, city in the clouds
Oh Machu Picchu, wrapped in morning shrouds
The Intihuatana anchors the sun
The Incas are gone but their work lives on

Hitching post of the sun
Keep the winter from coming
Hitching post of the sun
Keep the old songs humming
```

**Verse 2** (2:00-2:45)
```
Manuel cleans the Temple of the Sun
His family never left this place
The terraces still grow potatoes
The stones still hold their grace

A French woman touched the hitching stone
Said it holds the prayers of all
The Quechua woman planted seeds
Said the Incas didn't fall
```

**Chorus** (2:45-3:15)
```
Oh Machu Picchu, city in the clouds
Oh Machu Picchu, wrapped in morning shrouds
The Intihuatana anchors the sun
The Incas are gone but their work lives on

Hitching post of the sun
Keep the winter from coming
Hitching post of the sun
Keep the old songs humming
```

**Bridge** (3:15-3:45)
- Andean flute solo (quena, melancholic)
- Charango underneath
- Distant condor call (field recording)

**Outro** (3:45-4:30)
```
I climbed Huayna Picchu at midday
Saw the world from the Inca view
Now I carry the mountain within me
And the stones carry me too

We are small. The mountains are old.
Empires fall. The stones remain.
We are small. The mountains are old.
And that is enough. That is enough.
```
- Final panpipes note
- Wind fade

## Production Notes

**Instruments:**
- Zampoña (Andean panpipes)
- Quena (Andean flute)
- Charango (small Andean guitar)

**Vocal Style:**
- Intimate, storytelling delivery
- Slight reverb to suggest mountain space
- Quechua language phrases woven in

**Mood:**
- Reverent
- Ancient
- Humbling
- Eternal
