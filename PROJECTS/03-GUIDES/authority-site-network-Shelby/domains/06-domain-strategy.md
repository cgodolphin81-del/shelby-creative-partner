# Domain Strategy 2026

**Exact Match Domains vs. Branded Domains, Aged Domains vs. New**

---

## 🎯 Domain Types Explained

### Exact Match Domains (EMDs)

**Definition:** Domain name that exactly matches a target keyword

**Examples:**
- `bestrunning shoes.com`
- `carinsurance.com`
- `weightlossguide.com`

**Historical Context:**
- **2010-2012:** EMDs had massive ranking advantage
- **2012:** Google EMD Update reduced automatic boost
- **2026:** Minimal direct SEO benefit, but still useful in specific scenarios

---

### Partial Match Domains (PMDs)

**Definition:** Domain contains target keyword plus other words

**Examples:**
- `running shoesguide.com`
- `affordablecarinsurance.com`
- `weightlossjourney2026.com`

**2026 Status:**
- Slightly better than pure EMDs (more brandable)
- Still limited SEO benefit
- Can work for local SEO and niche sites

---

### Branded Domains

**Definition:** Unique, memorable name representing your brand

**Examples:**
- `Nike.com`
- `Runner'sWorld.com`
- `FitBit.com`

**2026 Status:**
- ✅ **RECOMMENDED** for most authority sites
- Builds long-term brand equity
- Google rewards brand signals
- AI search platforms recognize brands as entities

---

### Aged Domains

**Definition:** Previously registered domains with existing history

**Examples:**
- Domains expired after 5-10 years of use
- Domains with existing backlink profiles
- Domains with previous content and authority

**2026 Status:**
- Can provide SEO boost IF clean history
- Risky if previous owner spammed
- Requires thorough due diligence

---

### New Domains

**Definition:** Freshly registered domains with no history

**2026 Status:**
- ✅ Safe and predictable
- No baggage from previous owners
- Takes 6-12 months to build authority
- Recommended for most builders

## 📊 EMDs in 2026: Do They Still Work?

### Google's Official Stance

> "Keywords in domain names have minimal impact on rankings. We focus on content quality, user experience, and overall authority."

**Translation:** EMDs alone won't rank your site.

---

### Sector-by-Sector Analysis (2026 Data)

| Sector | EMD Effectiveness | Recommendation |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Finance/Insurance** | ❌ Very Low (0% of top 10) | Avoid EMDs |
| **Health/Medical** | ❌ Very Low (5% of top 10) | Avoid EMDs |
| **Legal** | ❌ Low (8% of top 10) | Avoid EMDs |
| **E-commerce/Retail** | ✅ Moderate (25% of top 10) | PMDs work well |
| **Travel** | ✅ High (40% of top 10) | EMDs still effective |
| **Local Services** | ✅ High (35% of top 10) | Geo-EMDs work |
| **Technology** | ⚠️ Low (12% of top 10) | Branded preferred |
| **Education** | ⚠️ Low (10% of top 10) | Branded preferred |

**Key Insight:** EMDs work best in **transactional, non-YMYL niches** (travel, retail, local services). They fail in **high-trust sectors** (finance, health, legal).

---

### When EMDs Still Make Sense (2026)

#### ✅ Good Use Cases:

1. **Local SEO**
   - `plumbermelbourne.com.au`
   - `denverroofer.com`
   - Instantly communicates location + service
   - Works well for Map Pack rankings

2. **Niche Affiliate Sites**
   - `bestcoffeemakers2026.com`
   - `yogamatsforbeginners.com`
   - Clear topical focus
   - Works for low-competition niches

3. **Landing Pages/Microsites**
   - Campaign-specific domains
   - Redirect to main branded site
   - Targeted keyword focus

4. **Aged EMDs with Authority**
   - Existing backlink profile
   - Clean history
   - Value comes from links, not the EMD itself

#### ❌ Bad Use Cases:

1. **YMYL Niches (Your Money Your Life)**
   - Finance, health, legal, insurance
   - Google prioritizes brand entities
   - EMDs treated with suspicion

2. **Long-Term Brand Building**
   - Limits expansion possibilities
   - Hard to pivot to new topics
   - Less memorable than brands

3. **Competitive Industries**
   - Technology, SaaS, B2B
   - Established brands dominate
   - EMDs appear spammy

---

### EMD Pros & Cons (2026)

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| ✅ Clear topical signal | ❌ Limits brand flexibility |
| ✅ Natural keyword-rich anchor text | ❌ Can appear spammy |
| ✅ Good for local SEO | ❌ No inherent ranking boost |
| ✅ May improve CTR for specific queries | ❌ Hard to expand beyond niche |
| ✅ Can work for microsites | ❌ AI platforms don't weight domains |
| ✅ Useful for aged domains with links | ❌ Expensive to acquire premium EMDs |

## 🏆 Branded Domains: The 2026 Standard

### Why Brands Win in 2026

1. **Google Rewards Brand Signals**
   - Branded search volume is a ranking factor
   - Users searching for your brand = trust signal
   - Brand mentions across web build authority

2. **AI Search Platforms Favor Brands**
   - ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini recognize entities
   - Brands are cited more often in AI answers
   - Easier to become a "trusted source"

3. **User Trust & CTR**
   - Users click branded results more often
   - Higher CTR = better rankings
   - Memorable names drive direct traffic

4. **Flexibility**
   - Can expand into new niches
   - Not locked into one keyword
   - Easier to sell/exit

---

### How to Create a Strong Brand Name

**Characteristics:**
- [ ] **Memorable** - Easy to recall after hearing once
- [ ] **Pronounceable** - Can say it over the phone
- [ ] **Spellable** - Obvious spelling (no confusion)
- [ ] **Short** - 2-3 syllables, <15 characters
- [ ] **Unique** - Not similar to existing brands
- [ ] **Available** - .com domain + social handles
- [ ] **Scalable** - Doesn't limit future growth

**Name Types:**

| Type | Examples | Pros | Cons |
|------|----------|------|------|
| **Invented** | Google, Spotify, Zoom | Unique, trademarkable | No inherent meaning |
| **Compound** | Facebook, YouTube, PayPal | Memorable, descriptive | Can be long |
| **Metaphor** | Amazon, Nike, Apple | Evocative, scalable | May not indicate niche |
| **Acronym** | IBM, BMW, KFC | Short, professional | Hard to build meaning |
| **Real Word** | Slack, Square, Buffer | Familiar, easy | May be taken |

**Tools:**
- NameMesh.com
- LeanDomainSearch.com
- Namemesh.com
- BrandBucket.com (premium names)

## 🔍 Aged Domains: Opportunity or Trap?

### Potential Benefits

**If Clean:**
- ✅ Existing backlink profile (instant authority)
- ✅ Domain age signal (minor factor)
- ✅ Possible existing traffic
- ✅ Indexed pages (faster ranking)
- ✅ Established trust with Google

**Typical Boost:**
- 3-6 months faster to rank
- 20-40% more initial traffic
- Higher starting Domain Rating (DR 10-30 vs. DR 0)

---

### Risks & Red Flags

**🚨 DO NOT BUY if:**

1. **Spam History**
   - Previous site was penalized
   - Used for PBN or link schemes
   - Toxic backlink profile

2. **Irrelevant History**
   - Was a gambling/casino site
   - Was adult content
   - Was pharmaceutical spam
   - Hard to overcome niche association

3. **Manual Penalties**
   - Check Google Search Console (if possible)
   - Search `site:domain.com` (should show pages)
   - If no results, may be deindexed

4. **Toxic Backlinks**
   - Links from spam sites
   - Link farms
   - Unrelated anchor text (viagra, casino, etc.)
   - High percentage of low-quality links

---

### Due Diligence Checklist

**Before Buying an Aged Domain:**

#### Step 1: Check History
- [ ] **Wayback Machine** (archive.org) - See previous content
- [ ] **Google Search** - `site:domain.com` and `domain.com` in quotes
- [ ] **Brand Search** - Search for brand name + "scam", "review"
- [ ] **Trademark Search** - USPTO.gov (avoid trademark issues)

#### Step 2: Analyze Backlinks
- [ ] **Ahrefs/Semrush** - Check backlink profile
- [ ] **Toxic Links** - >20% toxic = avoid
- [ ] **Anchor Text** - Should be branded/natural, not spammy
- [ ] **Referring Domains** - Quality over quantity
- [ ] **Link Velocity** - Sudden spikes = manipulation

#### Step 3: Check Indexation
- [ ] **Google Search** - `site:domain.com` shows pages?
- [ ] **Google Cache** - `cache:domain.com`
- [ ] **Search Console** - If accessible, check for penalties

#### Step 4: Verify Metrics
- [ ] **Domain Rating (DR)** - Ahrefs metric (0-100)
- [ ] **Trust Flow (TF)** - Majestic metric
- [ ] **Citation Flow (CF)** - Majestic metric
- [ ] **Organic Traffic** - SimilarWeb, Semrush estimates
- [ ] **Spam Score** - Moz metric (<5% is good)

#### Step 5: Check for Penalties
- [ ] **Manual Actions** - Search Console (if accessible)
- [ ] **Algorithmic** - Traffic history (sudden drops?)
- [ ] **Deindexing** - No results for `site:domain.com`

## 🎯 Domain Strategy by Site Type

### Type 1: Affiliate Money Site

**Recommendation:** Branded domain (new or aged)

**Why:**
- Long-term asset
- Can expand to multiple niches
- Easier to sell
- Builds brand equity

**Examples:**
- `FitnessGearLab.com` (branded)
- NOT `besttreadmills2026.com` (EMD)

**Exception:** Microsites for specific campaigns can use EMDs.

---

### Type 2: Local Service Site

**Recommendation:** Geo-EMD or Branded + Location

**Why:**
- Local SEO benefit
- Clear service + location signal
- Works for Map Pack

**Examples:**
- `DenverPlumbingPros.com` (PMD)
- `PlumberDenver.com` (EMD)
- `MileHighPlumbing.com` (branded + location)

---

### Type 3: Content/Autority Site

**Recommendation:** Branded domain

**Why:**
- Topical authority across multiple subtopics
- Easier to expand content
- Better for E-E-A-T signals
- AI platforms recognize brands

**Examples:**
- `Runner'sWorld.com` (branded)
- NOT `bestrunningtips.com` (EMD)

---

### Type 4: E-commerce Site

**Recommendation:** Branded or PMD

**Why:**
- Brand building critical for e-commerce
- PMDs can work for product categories
- Avoid pure EMDs (appear spammy)

**Examples:**
- `Zappos.com` (branded)
- `LaptopDeal s.com` (PMD - acceptable)
- NOT `laptops.com` (too generic, expensive)

---

### Type 5: SaaS/Software Site

**Recommendation:** Branded domain (.com or .io/.ai for tech)

**Why:**
- Brand is everything in SaaS
- .io/.ai acceptable for tech
- Avoid keyword domains

**Examples:**
- `Slack.com` (branded)
- `Notion.so` (branded + tech TLD)
- NOT `projectmanagementsoftware.com` (EMD)

## 🌐 TLD (Top-Level Domain) Guide 2026

### TLD Hierarchy

| TLD | Trust Level | SEO Impact | Best For |
|-----|-------------|------------|----------|
| **.com** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Neutral (but users trust it) | Everything |
| **.org** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Neutral | Non-profits, organizations |
| **.edu** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Positive (restricted) | Educational institutions |
| **.gov** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Positive (restricted) | Government |
| **.net** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Neutral | Tech, networks |
| **.io** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Neutral | Tech startups, SaaS |
| **.ai** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Neutral | AI companies |
| **.co** | ⭐⭐⭐ | Neutral | Alternative to .com |
| **Country TLDs** (.uk, .de, .ca) | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Positive for local SEO | Country-specific sites |
| **.info, .biz** | ⭐⭐ | Neutral (lower trust) | Avoid if possible |
| **.xyz, .top, .club** | ⭐ | Negative (spammy) | Avoid |

### Key Takeaways:

1. **.com is king** - Always prefer .com if available
2. **Country TLDs for local** - `.co.uk` for UK, `.com.au` for Australia
3. **Tech TLDs acceptable** - `.io`, `.ai` fine for tech companies
4. **Avoid spammy TLDs** - `.xyz`, `.top`, `.info` have low trust
5. **Google treats most TLDs equally** - But users don't!

## ✅ Domain Selection Checklist

**Before Registering:**

- [ ] **Brandable** - Memorable, pronounceable, spellable
- [ ] **.com available** - Or appropriate country TLD
- [ ] **No trademark issues** - USPTO search
- [ ] **Social handles available** - Twitter, Instagram, Facebook
- [ ] **No negative history** - Wayback Machine check
- [ ] **Clean backlink profile** - If aged domain
- [ ] **No penalties** - Google search, Search Console
- [ ] **Appropriate for niche** - Branded for most, EMD for local
- [ ] **Scalable** - Can expand beyond initial niche
- [ ] **Within budget** - Don't overspend on domain

## 🎯 Final Recommendations

### For Authority Site Networks (2026):

1. **Primary Money Sites:** Branded .com domains (new or clean aged DR 15-25)
2. **Satellite/Test Sites:** Cheap new domains or low-cost aged domains
3. **Local Sites:** Geo-EMDs or branded + location
4. **Avoid:** Pure EMDs for YMYL niches, spammy TLDs, domains with penalty history

### Domain Acquisition Priority:

1. **First:** Secure branded .com for main money site
2. **Second:** Consider aged domain if budget allows and clean
3. **Third:** Register social media handles
4. **Fourth:** Protect brand with common misspellings (.net, country TLDs)

### Time Investment:

- **New domain research:** 2-4 hours
- **Aged domain due diligence:** 4-8 hours per domain
- **Worth it?** Yes, for primary money sites. No, for test/satellite sites.
