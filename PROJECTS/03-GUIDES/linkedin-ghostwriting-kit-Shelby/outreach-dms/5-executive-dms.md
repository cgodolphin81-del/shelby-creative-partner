# 5 Outreach DMs to Executives

---

## DM 1: The Warm Connection (After They Engage With Your Content)

**Subject:** (No subject - LinkedIn DM)

Hey [First Name],

I noticed you commented on my post about [topic they commented on] — really appreciated your perspective on [specific point they made].

I've been following your work at [Company] for a while. The way you [specific achievement or initiative they've led] is exactly the kind of leadership more people should be talking about.

I help executives like you amplify their voice on LinkedIn without adding hours to your week. If that's ever something you've thought about, I'd love to buy you a virtual coffee and hear what you're working on.

Either way, keep doing great work.

Best,
[Your Name]

**Why it works:** Leads with genuine appreciation, shows you've done research, soft ask with no pressure.

## Follow-Up Sequences

### If They Don't Respond (3-Touch Sequence)

**Follow-Up 1 (3 days later):**
Hey [First Name], just bumping this up in case it got buried. No pressure at all — if now's not the right time, totally get it.

**Follow-Up 2 (5 days later):**
[First Name], I'll stop reaching out after this, but I genuinely think you'd be great at this. One last offer: I'll write your first post for free. If you like it, we can talk about working together. If not, you keep it. Fair?

**Follow-Up 3 (7 days later - Breakup):**
[First Name], I appreciate your time. I'll stop reaching out now, but my door's always open if you ever want to explore this. In the meantime, I'll be cheering for you from the sidelines. Take care.

## Best Practices

1. **Personalize every DM** — Generic = deleted
2. **Keep it under 150 words** — Busy executives scan, don't read
3. **One clear CTA** — Don't ask multiple questions
4. **Mobile-friendly formatting** — Short paragraphs, plenty of white space
5. **Follow up 2-3 times** — Most responses come on follow-up #2 or #3
6. **Track your metrics** — Aim for 20-30% response rate on warm outreach
