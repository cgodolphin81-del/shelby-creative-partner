# 10 Sample LinkedIn Posts for Ghostwriting Portfolio

---

## Post 1: The Vulnerable Leadership Post

**Hook:** I almost quit last Tuesday.

**Body:**
Not because the work was hard. Not because the team was struggling.

Because I realized I'd been leading from fear instead of vision.

I was making decisions based on what wouldn't go wrong instead of what could go right.

That day, I called my team together and admitted it. "I've been playing not to lose," I said. "Let's start playing to win."

The energy shifted instantly.

People don't follow perfect leaders. They follow honest ones.

**CTA:** What's one fear you're letting drive your decisions?

**Hashtags:** #Leadership #Vulnerability #ExecutivePresence

---

## Post 2: The Contrarian Take

**Hook:** Stop trying to be "authentic" on LinkedIn.

**Body:**
Here's what nobody tells you:

Authenticity without strategy is just oversharing.

I've worked with CEOs who posted daily "real talk" updates. Raw. Unfiltered. 100% them.

Result? Crickets.

The leaders who actually move the needle? They're strategic about their authenticity.

They share vulnerabilities that serve a purpose.
They tell stories that illustrate a point.
They show personality while staying on brand.

Being real matters. Being relevant matters more.

**CTA:** Agree or am I completely wrong?

**Hashtags:** #PersonalBranding #ContentStrategy #LinkedIn

## Post 3: The Lesson Learned

**Hook:** The worst hire I ever made taught me the best lesson.

**Body:**
She was brilliant. Top of her class. Incredible pedigree.

I hired her in 30 minutes.

Biggest mistake of my career.

Skills? Perfect. Culture fit? Non-existent. She tore through the team like a hurricane.

Here's what I learned:

Hire for values. Train for skills.

You can teach someone to use your systems. You can't teach them to care about your mission.

Now I spend 80% of interviews on culture. 20% on capability.

My retention rate? Up 340%.

**CTA:** What's your non-negotiable in hiring?

**Hashtags:** #Hiring #Leadership #TeamBuilding

## Post 4: The Industry Insight

**Hook:** Everyone's talking about AI replacing jobs.

**Body:**
They're asking the wrong question.

AI won't replace you.

Someone using AI will.

I've spent the last 6 months embedding AI into every part of my business. Not to cut headcount. To amplify what my team already does.

Result?

Same team. 3x output. Zero layoffs.

The companies winning right now aren't the ones replacing humans with machines.

They're the ones giving humans superpowers.

**CTA:** How are you using AI to amplify your team?

**Hashtags:** #AI #FutureOfWork #Innovation

## Post 5: The Behind-the-Scenes

**Hook:** My morning routine is not what you think.

**Body:**
No 5am wakeups. No ice baths. No meditation app subscriptions.

Here's my actual routine:

6:30am - Wake up (yes, I hit snooze)
7:00am - Coffee while reading industry news
8:00am - First meeting of the day
12:00pm - Walk outside (non-negotiable)
2:00pm - Deep work block
6:00pm - Family time (phone away)

The secret isn't the routine. It's the consistency.

I've done this exact schedule for 3 years.

Boring? Maybe.
Effective? Absolutely.

Stop optimizing your morning. Start executing your day.

**CTA:** What's one habit you've kept for years?

**Hashtags:** #Productivity #Routine #ExecutiveLife

## Post 6: The Client Win

**Hook:** "This changed everything." - That's what she said after 90 days.

**Body:**
Client came to me burned out. 70-hour weeks. Team falling apart. Couldn't see a way out.

We didn't add resources. We didn't hire more people.

We subtracted.

I made her cut:
✗ 12 recurring meetings
✗ 3 underperforming projects
✗ 47% of her email responses

She thought I was crazy.

90 days later:
✓ Revenue up 28%
✓ Team retention at 100%
✓ She's home by 6pm

Sometimes the best strategy is subtraction.

**CTA:** What could you remove to improve your business?

**Hashtags:** #BusinessStrategy #Leadership #Growth

## Post 7: The Hot Take

**Hook:** Unpopular opinion: Your network is overrated.

**Body:**
Everyone says "your network is your net worth."

I call BS.

I've seen well-connected people fail spectacularly.
I've seen unknowns build empires.

Here's what actually matters:

Your reputation > Your connections
Your output > Your outreach
Your value > Your visibility

Don't get me wrong—relationships matter.

But a weak product with great connections is still a weak product.

Build something worth talking about first.
The network will follow.

**CTA:** What do you think—network or product?

**Hashtags:** #Networking #Business #Entrepreneurship

## Post 8: The Story Post

**Hook:** Ten years ago, I was rejected by 47 investors.

**Body:**
Every single "no" felt personal.

"You're too young."
"Wrong market."
"Not scalable."

Number 48 said yes.

That meeting changed everything. Not because of the money (it was modest). But because someone finally believed.

That company? Exited for 8 figures last year.

Here's what I learned:

Rejection isn't about your worth. It's about fit.

The right person will see what you see. You just have to keep showing up until you find them.

**CTA:** What's a "no" that led to a better "yes"?

**Hashtags:** #Entrepreneurship #Resilience #Funding

## Post 9: The Quick Tip

**Hook:** The one email subject line that gets 90% open rates:

**Body:**
"Quick question about [specific thing about them]"

Why it works:

✓ It's personal (not generic)
✓ It's specific (not vague)
✓ It's low-pressure ("quick question")
✓ It's about them (not you)

I've used this to reach:
- Fortune 500 CEOs
- Industry leaders
- Potential partners

Response rate? Through the roof.

The best outreach doesn't feel like outreach.

**CTA:** What's your best cold email tip?

**Hashtags:** #Sales #Outreach #BusinessDevelopment

## Post 10: The Values Post

**Hook:** I fired my biggest client last month.

**Body:**
They paid well. Easy work. Great relationship.

But they asked me to compromise on something that mattered.

I said no. They pushed. I walked.

My team was worried. "Are we making the right call?"

Here's what I told them:

Revenue recovers. Reputation doesn't.

Every decision you make tells people who you are. Make sure you like the story.

**CTA:** Have you ever walked away from good money?

**Hashtags:** #Values #BusinessEthics #Leadership

---

## Usage Notes:

- Mix these post types throughout your content calendar
- Adjust tone to match client's voice (more/less formal, more/less personal)
- Add specific metrics and details from client's actual experience
- Post consistently: 3-5x per week for best results
- Engage with comments within 2 hours for algorithm boost
