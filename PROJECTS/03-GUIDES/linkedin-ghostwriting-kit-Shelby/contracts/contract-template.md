# GHOSTWRITING SERVICES AGREEMENT

**This Agreement** is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date")

**BETWEEN:**

**[Your Company Name]**, [Your Address], [Your Email] ("Provider")

**AND:**

**[Client Name]**, [Client Address], [Client Email] ("Client")

---

## 1. SERVICES

Provider agrees to deliver the following ghostwriting services ("Services"):

### 1.1 Content Creation
- Creation of [NUMBER] LinkedIn posts per month
- Content aligned with Client's voice, goals, and brand
- Unlimited revisions until Client approves each post

### 1.2 Strategy
- Initial 90-minute discovery session
- Content strategy and pillar development
- Monthly performance review and optimization

### 1.3 Engagement Management (if applicable)
- Monitoring and responding to comments on Client's posts
- Engagement with target audience posts
- DM management (as authorized by Client)

### 1.4 Reporting
- Monthly analytics report including:
  - Follower growth
  - Post performance metrics
  - Engagement rates
  - Inbound opportunities generated

---

## 2. TERM

**2.1 Initial Term:** This Agreement begins on [START DATE] and continues for an initial period of [3/6/12] months ("Initial Term").

**2.2 Renewal:** After the Initial Term, this Agreement will automatically renew on a month-to-month basis unless either party provides 30 days' written notice of termination.

**2.3 Trial Period:** The first 30 days constitute a trial period. If Client is not satisfied for any reason, they may terminate with 7 days' notice and receive a pro-rated refund for unused services.

---

## 3. COMPENSATION

**3.1 Monthly Fee:** Client agrees to pay $[AMOUNT] USD per month ("Monthly Fee").

**3.2 Payment Schedule:** Payment is due on the 1st of each month, in advance.

**3.3 Payment Method:** Payment shall be made via [credit card/wire transfer/ACH] to Provider's designated account.

**3.4 Late Payment:** Payments not received within 7 days of due date will incur a 5% late fee. Services may be paused if payment is more than 14 days overdue.

**3.5 Price Increases:** Provider may increase the Monthly Fee with 30 days' written notice. Client may terminate without penalty if they do not accept the new rate.

**3.6 Additional Services:** Services beyond the scope defined in Section 1 will be billed at $[RATE] per hour or as otherwise agreed in writing.

---

## 4. CLIENT RESPONSIBILITIES

Client agrees to:

**4.1** Provide timely feedback on content (within [48/72] hours of submission)

**4.2** Participate in scheduled strategy calls (minimum [frequency])

**4.3** Provide accurate information about their business, industry, and goals

**4.4** Notify Provider of any compliance, legal, or PR concerns

**4.5** Grant Provider necessary access to LinkedIn account (or approve posts in a timely manner)

**4.6** Be honest and transparent in all communications with Provider

---

## 5. CONTENT APPROVAL

**5.1 Submission:** Provider will submit content to Client [X days] before scheduled posting dates.

**5.2 Review Period:** Client has [48/72] hours to review and request revisions.

**5.3 Revisions:** Provider will make unlimited revisions until Client approves each post.

**5.4 Final Approval:** Client has final approval on all content. Provider will not post anything Client has not explicitly approved.

**5.5 Deemed Approval:** If Client does not respond within [5] business days, content will be considered approved.

---

## 6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**6.1 Work Made for Hire:** All content created by Provider under this Agreement shall be considered "work made for hire" and shall be the sole property of Client upon full payment.

**6.2 Assignment:** To the extent any content does not qualify as "work made for hire," Provider hereby assigns all rights, title, and interest in such content to Client.

**6.3 Provider's Rights:** Provider retains the right to:
- Use anonymized examples of content in their portfolio
- Reference the working relationship in marketing materials (unless Client opts out in Section 9)
- Use general learnings and insights gained from the engagement

**6.4 Client's Warranty:** Client warrants that all information provided to Provider is accurate and that Client has the right to use any materials they provide to Provider.

---

## 7. CONFIDENTIALITY

**7.1 Definition:** "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either party, including but not limited to business strategies, financial information, customer data, and personal information.

**7.2 Obligations:** Each party agrees to:
- Hold Confidential Information in strict confidence
- Use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement
- Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties without prior written consent

**7.3 Exceptions:** Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is or becomes publicly known through no fault of the receiving party
- Was known to the receiving party prior to disclosure
- Is independently developed by the receiving party
- Is required to be disclosed by law or court order

**7.4 Duration:** Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of [2] years.

---

## 8. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

**8.1 Provider Warrants:**
- Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner
- Content will be original and not infringe on any third-party rights
- Provider has the expertise and capacity to perform the Services

**8.2 Client Warrants:**
- They have the authority to enter into this Agreement
- All information provided is accurate and complete
- They will not request content that is false, misleading, or defamatory

**8.3 Disclaimer:** EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY STATED IN THIS AGREEMENT, PROVIDER MAKES NO WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, OR NON-INFRINGEMENT.

**8.4 No Guarantee:** Provider does not guarantee specific results (follower counts, engagement rates, business outcomes). Social media performance depends on many factors beyond Provider's control.

---

## 9. PUBLICITY

**9.1 Client Consent:** Client [DOES / DOES NOT] consent to Provider referencing this engagement in Provider's marketing materials.

**9.2 If Yes:** Provider may:
- List Client's name and logo on Provider's website
- Use specific post examples (with Client approval)
- Include Client testimonial (separate approval required)

**9.3 If No:** Provider will keep the engagement confidential, except as required by law.

---

## 10. TERMINATION

**10.1 Termination for Convenience:** Either party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days' written notice after the Initial Term.

**10.2 Termination for Cause:** Either party may terminate immediately if the other party:
- Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 10 days of notice
- Becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy
- Engages in conduct that brings the other party into disrepute

**10.3 Effect of Termination:**
- Client will pay Provider for all Services performed through termination date
- Provider will deliver all completed content through termination date
- Provider will transfer all account access and materials to Client
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination

**10.4 Refunds:** If Client terminates during the Initial Term (except during trial period), no refund will be provided for the current month. If Provider terminates without cause, Client will receive a pro-rated refund.

---

## 11. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR

**11.1 Status:** Provider is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, or partner of Client.

**11.2 Taxes:** Provider is responsible for all taxes, insurance, and benefits related to their work.

**11.3 No Authority:** Provider has no authority to bind Client to any agreement or obligation.

---

## 12. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

**12.1 Cap:** Provider's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by Client in the [3] months preceding the claim.

**12.2 Consequential Damages:** IN NO EVENT SHALL PROVIDER BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO LOST PROFITS, LOST DATA, OR BUSINESS INTERRUPTION.

**12.3 Indemnification:** Client agrees to indemnify and hold Provider harmless from any claims arising from:
- Client's use of the content
- Information provided by Client that is false or misleading
- Client's business practices or products

---

## 13. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**13.1 Good Faith:** Parties agree to attempt to resolve any dispute through good faith negotiation before pursuing other remedies.

**13.2 Mediation:** If negotiation fails, parties agree to participate in mediation before pursuing litigation.

**13.3 Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [STATE/COUNTRY].

**13.4 Venue:** Any legal action shall be brought in the courts of [CITY, STATE/COUNTRY].

**13.5 Attorney's Fees:** The prevailing party in any legal action shall be entitled to reasonable attorney's fees and costs.

---

## 14. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**14.1 Entire Agreement:** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.

**14.2 Amendments:** Any amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

**14.3 Assignment:** Client may not assign this Agreement without Provider's prior written consent. Provider may assign this Agreement to a successor business.

**14.4 Severability:** If any provision is found to be unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**14.5 Waiver:** Failure to enforce any provision shall not constitute a waiver of that provision or any other provision.

**14.6 Force Majeure:** Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures due to circumstances beyond their reasonable control.

**14.7 Notices:** All notices shall be in writing and sent to the addresses listed above (or as updated in writing).

**14.8 Counterparts:** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including electronic signatures, each of which shall be considered an original.

## NOTES FOR CUSTOMIZATION

1. **Jurisdiction:** Update governing law and venue to your location
2. **Payment Terms:** Adjust to your billing cycle and methods
3. **Trial Period:** Decide if you want to offer one and for how long
4. **IP Rights:** Some ghostwriters retain portfolio rights by default; adjust to your preference
5. **Liability Cap:** Consult a lawyer about appropriate limits in your jurisdiction
6. **Non-Compete:** Consider adding if you want to work with only one client per industry
7. **Kill Fee:** Consider adding a termination fee if client ends early

**Have a lawyer review this before using.** Laws vary by jurisdiction, and this template may not cover all scenarios.
