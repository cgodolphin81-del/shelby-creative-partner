# Sales Scripts & Materials

## Overview

This document contains proven scripts, templates, and frameworks for converting prospects into clients.

## Sales Process Overview

```
Lead → Discovery Call → Proposal → Follow-up → Close → Onboarding
  ↓          ↓              ↓           ↓          ↓         ↓
 100%      40%            50%         80%       50%      95%
```

**Target Conversion:** 10% overall (Lead to Close)

## Discovery Call Script

### Pre-Call Prep (15 min)

**Research:**
- [ ] Company website reviewed
- [ ] LinkedIn profiles checked
- [ ] Recent news/googled
- [ ] Industry pain points identified
- [ ] Questions prepared

**Technical Setup:**
- [ ] Zoom/Meet link ready
- [ ] Calendar open
- [ ] Note-taking system ready
- [ ] CRM updated

---

### Call Opening (5 min)

**You:** "Hey [Name], thanks for making the time! Before we dive in, I want to make sure this is valuable for you. What prompted you to reach out about AI automation?"

**Listen actively.** Let them explain their situation.

**You:** "Got it. So just to set expectations—this call is for me to understand your business and see if we can help. If there's a fit, I'll show you what's possible. If not, I'll be honest and maybe point you in the right direction. Sound good?"

**Why this works:** Sets honest expectations, builds trust, reduces pressure.

---

### Discovery Questions (15-20 min)

**Current State:**
- "Walk me through how you currently handle [relevant process]."
- "What tools are you using right now?"
- "How many people are involved in this process?"
- "How much time does this take weekly?"

**Pain Points:**
- "What's the most frustrating part of this process?"
- "Where do things typically go wrong or get delayed?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would you change?"
- "What happens when this doesn't work well?"

**Impact:**
- "How does this affect your team's productivity?"
- "What's the cost of this problem—time, money, stress?"
- "How does this impact your customers?"
- "What would solving this mean for your business?"

**Decision-Making:**
- "Who else is involved in decisions like this?"
- "What's your timeline for getting this solved?"
- "Have you looked into automation solutions before?"
- "What would need to be true for this to be a no-brainer?"

**Budget (Tactfully):**
- "What kind of investment were you expecting for a solution like this?"
- "Do you have budget allocated for this, or would we need to build the business case?"

---

### Solution Overview (10 min)

**You:** "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm thinking..."

**Present 2-3 options:**

**Option 1 (Conservative):**
" We could start with [smaller solution]. This would [benefit], typically costs [$X], and takes [timeline]."

**Option 2 (Recommended):**
" Or we could do [recommended solution]. This would [bigger benefit], typically costs [$Y], and takes [timeline]. This is what I'd recommend because [reason]."

**Option 3 (Comprehensive):**
" If you want to go all-in, we could [comprehensive solution]. This would [maximum benefit], typically costs [$Z], and takes [timeline]."

**Then:** "Which of these feels like the right fit for where you are right now?"

---

### Handling Objections

**"It's too expensive."**

**Response 1 (Value Reframe):**
"I get that. Can I ask—compared to what? Because if this saves you [X hours/dollars] per month, it actually pays for itself in [timeframe]. What's the cost of NOT solving this?"

**Response 2 (Payment Options):**
"I hear you. We do have payment plans for projects over [$X]. We could also start smaller with [smaller option] and scale up. What would work better for your cash flow?"

**Response 3 (ROI Focus):**
"Totally fair. Let me ask—what would this solution need to deliver to make it worth the investment? If we could guarantee [specific outcome], would that change the equation?"

---

**"I need to think about it."**

**Response 1 (Understand Concerns):**
"Of course. Usually when someone says that, there's something specific they're unsure about. Is it the investment, the timeline, or whether we can actually deliver what we're promising?"

**Response 2 (Create Urgency):**
"Absolutely, this is an important decision. Just so you know, we have [X] projects starting next month, so if you want to begin by [date], we'd need to move forward by [date]. But no pressure—what specifically do you need to think through?"

**Response 3 (Next Steps):**
"Sure thing. What would be most helpful for your decision? More case studies? A reference call with a similar client? A more detailed ROI analysis?"

---

**"We need to get other quotes."**

**Response:**
"That makes complete sense—you should feel confident in your choice. Out of curiosity, what are you most interested in comparing? Is it price, timeline, approach, or something else?"

**Then:** "I'm happy to provide whatever info you need. One thing I'd mention—we're not the cheapest option, and that's intentional. We focus on [differentiators]. If lowest price is the priority, we might not be the best fit. But if [value] matters most, I'm confident we're your best option."

---

**"Now's not a good time."**

**Response 1 (Understand Timing):**
"I understand. What would need to change for it to be a good time? Is it budget, bandwidth, or something else?"

**Response 2 (Cost of Waiting):**
"Got it. Just so you're aware, every month you wait, you're [losing X hours/dollars]. Sometimes it makes sense to move fast on this. But I respect your timeline—when do you think would be better?"

**Response 3 (Stay in Touch):**
"No problem at all. Can I check back in [specific time]? And in the meantime, I'll send over [resource] that might be helpful."

---

**"We tried AI before and it didn't work."**

**Response:**
"I appreciate you sharing that. Can you tell me more about what you tried and what didn't work? [Listen] Yeah, that's actually pretty common. The issue is usually [reason]. Here's how we approach it differently..."

**Then:** "Would it be helpful if I showed you a similar situation where we [success story]?"

---

### Closing the Call (5 min)

**If Ready to Move Forward:**

**You:** "Awesome. Here's what happens next: I'll send over a proposal by [date] with everything we discussed. It'll include [details]. Once you review and sign, we'll schedule the kickoff and get started. Does that work?"

**Then:** "Any questions before I put this together?"

**If They Need Time:**

**You:** "No problem at all. I'll send over the proposal so you have something concrete to review. What's the best way to follow up—email or a quick call?"

**Then:** "When do you think you'll have a decision? [Get specific date]"

**Then:** "Perfect. I'll send the proposal by [date] and follow up on [date]. Anything else you need from me?"

## Cold Outreach Scripts

### LinkedIn Connection Request

```
Hey [Name]—I help [industry] companies automate [specific process] 
and save 20+ hours/week. Would love to connect and share what's 
working for others in your space.

[Your Name]
```

### LinkedIn Follow-Up (After Connection)

```
Thanks for connecting, [Name]!

Quick question—how is your team currently handling [relevant process]? 
We've been helping companies like [similar company] automate this 
and save [specific result].

Worth a quick chat to see if there's a fit?
```

### Cold Email #1 (Value-First)

```
Subject: Quick question about [process] at [Company]

Hey [Name],

I was looking at [Company] and noticed [specific observation].

We help [industry] companies automate [process]—typically saving 
20-40 hours/week and reducing errors by 80%+.

Recent results:
- [Client 1]: Saved $50K/year in labor costs
- [Client 2]: Reduced processing time from 3 days to 3 hours
- [Client 3]: Eliminated 95% of manual data entry

Worth a 15-minute chat to see if there's a fit? No pitch, 
just exploring if this could help [Company].

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Title]
[Calendly Link]
```

### Cold Email #2 (Follow-Up, 3-4 Days Later)

```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hey [Name],

Just floating this to the top of your inbox.

I know you're busy—if AI automation isn't a priority right now, 
no worries at all.

But if [pain point] is something you'd like to solve, happy to 
show you what's possible in a quick 15-minute call.

Here's my calendar: [Link]

Either way, keep crushing it!

[Your Name]
```

### Cold Email #3 (Break-Up, 7 Days Later)

```
Subject: Should I close your file?

Hey [Name],

I've reached out a couple times about helping [Company] automate 
[process].

Haven't heard back, so I'm assuming:
- This isn't a priority right now, OR
- You're all set with your current setup

Either way, I won't bug you again. But if you ever want to explore 
automation in the future, here's a [resource/case study] that 
might be helpful: [Link]

Best of luck with everything!

[Your Name]
```

## Referral Request Scripts

### After Successful Project

**You:** "Hey [Name], I'm so glad we could help with [result]! We're looking to work with more companies like yours. Do you know anyone else in your network who might be struggling with [similar problem]?"

**If they hesitate:**

**You:** "No pressure at all! Even an introduction would be huge. We're specifically looking for [ideal client profile]."

```

Hey [Name],
```

## Follow-Up Sequences

### Proposal Follow-Up Sequence

**Day 1:** Send proposal (see email above)

**Day 3:** Check-in email
```
Subject: Quick check-in

Hey [Name],

Just making sure you got the proposal I sent over. Any initial 
thoughts or questions?

Happy to hop on a quick call to walk through anything!

[Your Name]
```

**Day 7:** Value-add follow-up
```
Subject: Thought of you

Hey [Name],

Came across this [article/case study] and thought of our 
conversation: [Link]

Similar situation to what you're facing. They achieved [result] 
after implementing [solution].

Still happy to answer any questions about the proposal!

[Your Name]
```

**Day 14:** Direct ask
```
Subject: Where should we go from here?

Hey [Name],

I know you're busy, so I'll be direct.

Are you still interested in moving forward with [project]?

If yes: Great! Let me know what you need from me.
If no: No worries at all—just let me know so I can close the file.
If "not yet": Totally understand. When should I check back in?

Either way, appreciate your honesty!

[Your Name]
```

**Day 30:** Break-up
```
Subject: Closing the loop

Hey [Name],

Haven't heard back, so I'm assuming this isn't the right time 
for [project].

I'll close out your file on my end. But if things change and 
you want to revisit automation down the road, you know where 
to find me!

Wishing you and [Company] all the best.

[Your Name]
```
