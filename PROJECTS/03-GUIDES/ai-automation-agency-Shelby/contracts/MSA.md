# MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

**This Master Services Agreement** ("Agreement") is entered into as of [Date] ("Effective Date") by and between:

**[Your Agency Name]**, a [State] [Entity Type] with principal place of business at [Address] ("Provider")

**AND**

**[Client Company Name]**, a [State] [Entity Type] with principal place of business at [Address] ("Client")

(Collectively, the "Parties" and individually, a "Party")

---

## 1. SERVICES

### 1.1 Scope of Services
Provider agrees to perform certain AI automation, software development, and consulting services as described in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW") executed by the Parties. Each SOW shall be incorporated into this Agreement by reference.

### 1.2 Statement of Work
Each SOW shall include:
- Description of services to be performed
- Deliverables and specifications
- Timeline and milestones
- Fees and payment schedule
- Any additional terms specific to the engagement

### 1.3 Changes to Services
Any changes to the scope of services must be made in writing via a change order signed by both Parties. Provider reserves the right to adjust fees and timelines based on scope changes.

---

## 2. COMPENSATION

### 2.1 Fees
Client agrees to pay Provider the fees specified in each SOW. Unless otherwise stated, fees are quoted in US Dollars.

### 2.2 Payment Terms
- **Invoicing:** Provider shall invoice Client according to the payment schedule in each SOW
- **Due Date:** Invoices are due net 15 days from invoice date
- **Method:** Payment via bank transfer, check, or credit card (3% processing fee applies)

### 2.3 Late Payment
- Interest accrues on overdue amounts at 1.5% per month (18% APR) or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less
- Provider may suspend work if payment is more than 30 days overdue
- Client is responsible for reasonable collection costs, including attorney fees

### 2.4 Expenses
Client shall reimburse Provider for pre-approved, reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with the services. Expenses shall be documented with receipts.

---

## 3. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 3.1 Term
This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated by either Party as provided herein. Individual SOWs may have specific terms.

### 3.2 Termination for Convenience
Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW for any reason upon 30 days' written notice to the other Party.

### 3.3 Termination for Cause
Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:
- Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 15 days of notice
- Becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy
- Ceases business operations

### 3.4 Effect of Termination
Upon termination:
- Client shall pay Provider for all services performed and expenses incurred through the termination date
- Provider shall deliver all completed work product
- Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party's confidential information
- Sections 5 (Intellectual Property), 6 (Confidentiality), 8 (Limitation of Liability), 9 (Indemnification), and 10 (General) shall survive termination

---

## 4. PERFORMANCE

### 4.1 Standard of Care
Provider shall perform services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.

### 4.2 Client Responsibilities
Client shall:
- Provide timely access to necessary systems, data, and personnel
- Designate a project manager as the primary point of contact
- Provide timely feedback and approvals
- Obtain necessary third-party permissions and licenses

### 4.3 Delays
Provider is not liable for delays caused by Client, including but not limited to:
- Delayed feedback or approvals
- Failure to provide access or information
- Scope changes
- Force majeure events

### 4.4 Acceptance
Client has 10 business days to review and accept or reject each deliverable. Rejections must be in writing with specific reasons. Failure to respond within 10 days constitutes acceptance.

---

## 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 5.1 Work Product
Upon full payment of all fees, Client shall own all right, title, and interest in and to the custom deliverables specifically created for Client under each SOW ("Work Product").

### 5.2 Provider IP
Provider retains all rights to:
- Pre-existing intellectual property
- Tools, methodologies, and frameworks used in performing services
- General knowledge, skills, and expertise gained
- Right to use anonymized learnings and insights

### 5.3 Third-Party Components
Work Product may include third-party components (open source software, APIs, etc.) subject to their own license terms. Provider shall disclose such components in advance.

### 5.4 License to Provider IP
Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, non-transferable, perpetual license to use any Provider IP incorporated into the Work Product solely as part of the Work Product.

### 5.5 Portfolio Rights
Provider may reference Client and the Project in its portfolio, marketing materials, and case studies unless Client requests confidentiality in writing.

---

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 6.1 Definition
"Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential.

### 6.2 Obligations
Each Party agrees to:
- Use Confidential Information solely for purposes of this Agreement
- Protect Confidential Information with reasonable care
- Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties except to employees/contractors with a need to know
- Return or destroy Confidential Information upon termination

### 6.3 Exclusions
Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is or becomes publicly known through no fault of the receiving Party
- Was known to the receiving Party before disclosure
- Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
- Is required to be disclosed by law (with notice to disclosing Party)

### 6.4 Duration
Confidentiality obligations survive for 3 years following termination of this Agreement.

---

## 7. DATA AND SECURITY

### 7.1 Data Protection
Provider shall implement reasonable security measures to protect Client data. However, Provider does not guarantee absolute security.

### 7.2 Data Ownership
Client retains all rights to its data. Provider processes data solely on Client's behalf.

### 7.3 AI-Specific Considerations
- Client acknowledges that AI systems may produce outputs that require human review
- Client is responsible for validating AI-generated content before use
- Provider does not guarantee AI outputs will be error-free

### 7.4 Compliance
Each Party is responsible for compliance with applicable data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.) as it relates to their own data processing activities.

---

## 8. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 8.1 Cap on Liability
EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF SECTION 6 (CONFIDENTIALITY), INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, OR CLIENT'S PAYMENT OBLIGATIONS, NEITHER PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE IN THE 12 MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

### 8.2 Exclusion of Consequential Damages
NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

### 8.3 AI-Specific Disclaimer
PROVIDER DOES NOT GUARANTEE THAT AI SYSTEMS WILL BE ERROR-FREE, UNBIASED, OR SUITABLE FOR ANY PARTICULAR PURPOSE. CLIENT IS RESPONSIBLE FOR REVIEWING AND VALIDATING AI OUTPUTS BEFORE USE, ESPECIALLY IN HIGH-RISK APPLICATIONS.

---

## 9. INDEMNIFICATION

### 9.1 By Provider
Provider shall indemnify and hold harmless Client from third-party claims alleging that the Work Product infringes intellectual property rights, except to the extent caused by:
- Client's data or instructions
- Third-party components disclosed in advance
- Client's modification of Work Product

### 9.2 By Client
Client shall indemnify and hold harmless Provider from third-party claims arising from:
- Client's use of Work Product in violation of this Agreement
- Client's data or instructions
- Client's negligence or willful misconduct

### 9.3 Procedure
The indemnified Party shall:
- Promptly notify the indemnifying Party of any claim
- Cooperate in the defense
- Allow the indemnifying Party to control the defense and settlement

---

## 10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 10.1 Independent Contractor
Provider is an independent contractor, not an employee. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, or employment relationship.

### 10.2 Subcontracting
Provider may engage subcontractors to perform portions of the services, provided Provider remains responsible for performance.

### 10.3 Assignment
Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other Party's prior written consent, except in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets.

### 10.4 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of [State], without regard to conflict of law principles.

### 10.5 Dispute Resolution
Any disputes shall be resolved through:
1. Good faith negotiation (30 days)
2. If unsuccessful, binding arbitration in [City, State] under AAA rules
3. Each Party bears its own attorney fees, unless otherwise awarded by arbitrator

### 10.6 Entire Agreement
This Agreement, including all SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.

### 10.7 Amendments
This Agreement may only be amended in writing signed by both Parties.

### 10.8 Severability
If any provision is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in effect.

### 10.9 Waiver
Failure to enforce any provision is not a waiver of that provision or any other provision.

### 10.10 Notices
All notices shall be in writing and sent to the addresses above via email (with confirmation) or certified mail.

### 10.11 Force Majeure
Neither Party is liable for delays or failures due to circumstances beyond reasonable control (natural disasters, war, pandemics, etc.).

### 10.12 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including electronic signatures, each of which constitutes an original.

## 11. AI-SPECIFIC TERMS

### 11.1 AI System Limitations
Client acknowledges that:
- AI systems may produce inaccurate, biased, or unexpected outputs
- Human review is recommended for critical applications
- AI models may change over time as providers update their systems

### 11.2 Training Data
If Provider uses Client data to train or fine-tune AI models:
- Such use shall be specified in the applicable SOW
- Client grants Provider limited license for this purpose
- Provider shall implement appropriate data protection measures

### 11.3 Third-Party AI Services
Services may rely on third-party AI providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.). Client acknowledges:
- These services have their own terms and privacy policies
- Provider is not responsible for third-party service outages or changes
- Data sent to third parties is subject to their policies

### 11.4 Regulatory Compliance
Client is responsible for ensuring its use of AI systems complies with applicable laws and regulations in its industry and jurisdiction.
