# Client Communication Guide

## Best Practices for Client Interactions Throughout the Project Lifecycle

---

## Communication Principles

### 1. Over-Communicate
It's better to share too much than too little. Clients appreciate being kept in the loop.

### 2. No Surprises
Bad news should never be a surprise. Communicate issues early, along with a plan to resolve them.

### 3. Be Proactive
Don't wait for clients to ask for updates. Reach out regularly with progress and insights.

### 4. Listen More Than You Talk
Understand before being understood. Ask questions, clarify, and confirm understanding.

### 5. Match Their Style
Adapt your communication style to the client. Some want detailed reports; others want bullet points.

### 6. Document Everything
Important decisions, changes, and approvals should always be documented in writing.

### 7. Respond Promptly
Aim for 4-hour response time during business hours. Even if you don't have an answer, acknowledge receipt.

## Communication by Project Phase

### Phase 1: Pre-Sale / Discovery

**Goal:** Build trust, understand needs, qualify fit

**Key Communications:**
- Initial outreach (email/LinkedIn)
- Discovery call
- Follow-up emails
- Proposal presentation
- Contract negotiation

**Best Practices:**
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen for pain points and goals
- Share relevant case studies
- Be honest about fit (even if not a match)
- Set clear expectations for next steps

**Common Scenarios:**

*Client asks about price early:*
> "Great question. Our projects typically range from $15K to $100K+ depending on scope. Before I can give you an accurate quote, I need to understand your specific needs. Can we schedule a discovery call to discuss what you're looking to automate?"

*Client wants to skip discovery:*
> "I understand you're eager to get started. The discovery phase is crucial because it ensures we build the right solution. Skipping it would be like building a house without blueprints - we might build something, but it probably won't be what you need. The investment in discovery pays for itself in avoided rework."

### Phase 2: Kickoff

**Goal:** Align on goals, roles, and process

**Key Communications:**
- Kickoff meeting
- Welcome email
- Project charter review
- Communication plan agreement

**Kickoff Meeting Agenda (60-90 min):**
1. Introductions (10 min)
2. Project overview and goals (15 min)
3. Success criteria review (10 min)
4. Timeline and milestones (10 min)
5. Roles and responsibilities (10 min)
6. Communication plan (10 min)
7. Q&A (15-20 min)

**Kickoff Deck Outline:**
- Welcome & introductions
- Project overview
- Goals & success criteria
- Timeline & milestones
- Team & roles
- Communication plan
- Next steps
- Q&A

### Phase 3: Execution

**Goal:** Maintain confidence through consistent communication

**Key Communications:**
- Weekly status reports
- Regular check-in calls
- Sprint demos
- Ad-hoc updates (wins, blockers, decisions needed)

**Weekly Check-in Call Agenda (30 min):**
1. Wins from last week (5 min)
2. Progress review (10 min)
3. Blockers/decisions needed (10 min)
4. Plan for next week (5 min)

**Best Practices:**
- Send agenda 24 hours before
- Start on time, end on time
- Take notes and share after
- Follow up on action items
- Celebrate wins

**Status Report Best Practices:**
- Send same day/time each week
- Keep it scannable (bullets, bold text)
- Lead with wins
- Be transparent about challenges
- Clearly state asks/decisions needed
- Include next week's priorities

### Phase 4: Challenges & Issues

**Goal:** Maintain trust through transparency and problem-solving

**When to Escalate:**
- Timeline at risk (> 1 week delay)
- Budget at risk (> 10% over)
- Scope disagreement
- Quality concerns
- Relationship issues

**Bad News Framework:**
```
1. State the issue clearly
2. Explain the impact
3. Present options/solutions
4. Make a recommendation
5. Ask for decision/input
```

**Example:**
> "I need to share a challenge we've encountered. [Issue]. This impacts [timeline/budget/scope] by [specific impact]. We see three options: [Option 1], [Option 2], [Option 3]. We recommend [Option X] because [reasons]. What are your thoughts?"

**Don't:**
- Hide bad news
- Blame others (including the client)
- Present problems without solutions
- Wait until it's a crisis

**Do:**
- Communicate early
- Own the issue
- Present options
- Have a recommendation
- Follow through on resolution

### Phase 5: Testing & UAT

**Goal:** Ensure client confidence in the solution

**Key Communications:**
- UAT kickoff
- Test plan review
- Regular UAT status updates
- Issue resolution updates
- UAT sign-off request

**UAT Kickoff Email:**
```
Subject: Ready for UAT! [Project Name]

Hi [Client Name],

Great news - we're ready for User Acceptance Testing!

WHAT IS UAT?
------------

------------
- Start: [Date]
- End: [Date]
- Sign-off Due: [Date]

HOW TO TEST
-----------
1. Review test cases (attached)
2. Execute tests in [environment]
3. Log any issues in [tracker]
4. Daily check-ins at [time]

SUPPORT
-------
We're here to help! Reach out anytime:
- Slack: [channel]
- Email: [email]
```

**Handling UAT Feedback:**
- Acknowledge all feedback promptly
- Clarify if something is a bug vs. enhancement
- Prioritize issues with client
- Communicate resolution timeline
- Update and retest

## Difficult Conversations

### Scope Creep Conversation
```
"I'm excited about this new idea, and I can see how it would 
add value. Since it's outside our original scope, let me put 
together a change request with the impact on timeline and 
budget. That way we can make an informed decision together. 
Does that work?"
```

### Timeline Delay Conversation
```
"I need to share an update on our timeline. We've encountered 
[issue], which will impact our delivery by [timeframe]. Here's 
what we're doing to address it: [actions]. We're committed to 
minimizing this impact and will [mitigation]. I wanted to make 
you aware immediately and discuss how you'd like to proceed."
```

### Budget Overrun Conversation
```
"I need to discuss our budget. Due to [reason], we're projecting 
to exceed the original budget by [amount/percentage]. Here are 
the options: [Option 1: reduce scope], [Option 2: increase 
budget], [Option 3: phase the work]. We recommend [option] 
because [reasons]. Let's discuss what makes the most sense 
for you."
```

### Underperforming Stakeholder
```
"I've noticed we haven't been able to get [deliverable/feedback] 
by the agreed dates, which is impacting [downstream impact]. 
Is there something blocking you? How can we adjust the process 
to make this easier? Our goal is to make this smooth for 
everyone."
```

### Dissatisfied Client
```
"I hear that you're frustrated with [issue], and I want to 
understand more about what's not meeting your expectations. 
Can you help me understand what success would look like from 
your perspective? We're committed to making this right, and 
I'd like to work with you on a plan to get there."
```

## Communication Channels

### Email
**Best for:**
- Formal communications
- Documentation and approvals
- Status reports
- Detailed explanations

**Response Time:** 24 hours max

---

### Slack / Chat
**Best for:**
- Quick questions
- Informal updates
- Day-to-day collaboration
- Building rapport

**Response Time:** 4 hours during business hours

---

### Video Calls
**Best for:**
- Kickoff and milestone meetings
- Difficult conversations
- Complex discussions
- Relationship building

**Best Practices:**
- Always use video when possible
- Send agenda in advance
- Record (with permission)
- Share notes afterward

---

### Phone
**Best for:**
- Urgent matters
- Sensitive conversations
- When email/chat isn't working

**Best Practices:**
- Schedule when possible
- Follow up with email summary
- Be present (no multitasking)

---

## Communication Don'ts

❌ Don't go silent (even if there's no news)
❌ Don't blame the client (even if it's their fault)
❌ Don't over-promise and under-deliver
❌ Don't use jargon without explaining
❌ Don't have important conversations only via chat
❌ Don't CC everyone on every email
❌ Don't respond emotionally to difficult messages
❌ Don't make the client chase you for updates

---

## Communication Dos

✅ Do respond promptly (even if just to acknowledge)
✅ Do over-communicate progress
✅ Do document important decisions
✅ Do match the client's communication style
✅ Do escalate issues early
✅ Do celebrate wins together
✅ Do ask for feedback regularly
✅ Do follow up on commitments

---

*Great communication is the foundation of great client relationships. Invest in it.*
