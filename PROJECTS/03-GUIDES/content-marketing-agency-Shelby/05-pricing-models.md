# Pricing Models

Complete pricing strategies, rate cards, and positioning for content marketing services.

## Pricing Philosophy

Our pricing is built on three principles:

1. **Value-Based** - Price based on client outcomes, not just hours
2. **Transparent** - Clear what's included, no hidden fees
3. **Scalable** - Options for startups through enterprise

## Model 4: Performance-Based Pricing

**Best For**: Confident partnerships, aligned incentives, specific outcomes

**Note:** Performance-based pricing is typically combined with a reduced base fee.

### Structure Options

**Option 1: Base + Bonus**
- Base fee: 70% of standard rate
- Bonus: 30% based on achieving KPIs

**Option 2: Tiered Performance**
- Base fee: 50% of standard rate
- Tier 1 (100% of goal): +25%
- Tier 2 (125% of goal): +50%
- Tier 3 (150% of goal): +75%

**Option 3: Revenue Share**
- Reduced base fee
- Percentage of attributed revenue (1-5%)
- Requires robust tracking setup

### Measurable KPIs

| KPI | Measurement | Typical Goal |
|-----|-------------|--------------|
| Organic Traffic | Google Analytics | +30-50% in 6 months |
| Lead Generation | Form submissions | +25-40% in 6 months |
| Keyword Rankings | Ahrefs/SEMrush | 10+ keywords in top 10 |
| Social Engagement | Platform analytics | +50% engagement rate |
| Email Subscribers | Email platform | +20% list growth |
| Content Downloads | Gated assets | 100+ downloads/month |

### Performance-Based Terms

- Clear KPI definition required upfront
- Baseline measurement period (30 days)
- Monthly tracking and reporting
- Bonus paid quarterly
- Minimum 6-month engagement
- Attribution model agreed upon

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring

Always present three options:
1. **Basic** - Minimum viable solution
2. **Recommended** - Best value (what you want them to choose)
3. **Premium** - Everything included (makes recommended look reasonable)

### Framing

**Bad:** "This costs $10,000"  
**Good:** "This investment of $10,000 typically generates $50,000+ in pipeline"

### Scarcity

- Limited availability (if true)
- Pricing increases (if planned)
- Seasonal promotions (time-limited)

### Social Proof

- "Most popular" badge on recommended tier
- Client logos near pricing
- Case study results

## Price Increase Strategy

**When to Raise Prices:**
- Consistently at capacity
- Winning 70%+ of proposals
- Added significant value/services
- Market rates have increased
- Annual review (typical)

**How Much:**
- Annual increase: 5-10%
- Existing clients: Grandfather or 5% increase
- New clients: Full new pricing

**Communication:**
- 60 days notice for existing clients
- Clear rationale (added value, market rates)
- Option to lock in current rate with longer commitment
