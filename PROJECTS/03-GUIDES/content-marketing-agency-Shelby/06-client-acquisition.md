# Client Acquisition

Proven strategies for finding, winning, and onboarding content marketing clients.

---

## Overview

Client acquisition is a numbers game with a strategy layer. Our approach:

```
PROSPECTING → OUTREACH → DISCOVERY → PROPOSAL → CLOSE → ONBOARD
     ↓           ↓           ↓          ↓         ↓        ↓
   Find       Connect      Qualify     Present   Sign    Start
```

**Target**: 3-5 new clients per quarter  
**Close Rate**: 25-40% of qualified opportunities  
**Sales Cycle**: 2-8 weeks (varies by deal size)

---

## Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP: B2B SaaS ($10M-$100M ARR)

| Attribute | Criteria |
|-----------|----------|
| Company Size | 50-500 employees |
| Revenue | $10M-$100M ARR |
| Funding | Series B+ or profitable |
| Marketing Team | 3-10 people |
| Content Maturity | Some content, inconsistent or not strategic |
| Pain Points | Need more leads, building authority, scaling content |
| Budget | $10,000-$50,000/month |
| Decision Maker | VP Marketing, CMO, or Founder |

### Secondary ICP: Professional Services

| Attribute | Criteria |
|-----------|----------|
| Firm Type | Consulting, legal, financial, agency |
| Size | 20-200 employees |
| Revenue | $5M-$50M |
| Marketing Team | 1-5 people (often outsourced) |
| Pain Points | Need thought leadership, lead generation |
| Budget | $5,000-$25,000/month |
| Decision Maker | Managing Partner, CMO, or CEO |

### Tertiary ICP: E-commerce/DTC Brands

| Attribute | Criteria |
|-----------|----------|
| Revenue | $10M-$100M |
| Products | Physical products, subscription |
| Marketing Team | 5-20 people |
| Pain Points | Content for SEO, social, email |
| Budget | $8,000-$30,000/month |
| Decision Maker | Head of Marketing, CMO |

### Red Flags (Avoid These Clients)

- [ ] Expecting viral content overnight
- [ ] No clear budget or approval process
- [ ] Want to pay performance-only (no base)
- [ ] Micromanagement tendencies
- [ ] History of burning through agencies
- [ ] Unreasonable timelines
- [ ] Decision by committee (10+ stakeholders)
- [ ] Won't sign a contract

## Prospecting Strategies

### 1. Content-Driven Inbound

**Strategy**: Create content that attracts your ICP.

**Tactics:**
- Blog posts targeting ICP pain points
- LinkedIn thought leadership
- Podcast appearances
- Guest posts on industry publications
- Original research and reports

**Example Content Topics:**
- "Why Most B2B Content Strategies Fail in Year 2"
- "The Content Marketing Budget Guide for Series B Startups"
- "How We Generated 500 MQLs from Content in 90 Days"

**Expected Results:**
- 3-6 months to see traction
- 5-10 inbound leads per month (after momentum)
- Higher close rates (they're already sold on your expertise)

### 2. LinkedIn Outreach

**Strategy**: Direct, personalized outreach to decision-makers.

**Profile Optimization:**
- Headline: "Helping B2B SaaS companies scale content marketing | [Specific Result]"
- About section: Clear value prop, social proof, CTA
- Featured: Case studies, testimonials, lead magnet
- Activity: Post 3-5x/week, comment on ICP posts daily

**Connection Request Template:**
```
Hi [Name], I've been following [Company]'s growth and 
impressed by [specific observation]. I help companies 
like yours scale content marketing to drive pipeline. 
Would love to connect and share ideas.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Follow-Up Sequence:**

| Day | Message |
|-----|---------|
| 0 | Connection request |
| 2 | Thanks for connecting + value add (article, insight) |
| 5 | Specific observation about their content + suggestion |
| 10 | Case study relevant to their situation |
| 15 | Direct ask: "Would you be open to a 20-min chat?" |

**Volume Targets:**
- 20-30 connection requests per week
- 40-50% acceptance rate
- 10-15% response rate to follow-ups
- 2-4 discovery calls booked per week

### 3. Content Audits (Lead Magnet)

**Strategy**: Offer free mini content audits to start conversations.

**Audit Offer:**
```
Free Content Marketing Audit

Get a 15-page report analyzing:
✓ Your top 10 content opportunities
✓ Competitive gaps you can exploit
✓ Quick wins for immediate impact
✓ 90-day roadmap recommendation

Value: $2,500 | Yours free | No obligation
```

**Process:**
1. Prospect fills out form (URL, goals, challenges)
2. Spend 2-3 hours analyzing their content
3. Create 10-15 slide deck or Google Doc
4. Schedule 30-min walkthrough call
5. Present findings + recommendations
6. Natural transition to "how we can help"

**Close Rate:** 30-40% of audit recipients become clients

**Audit Template Structure:**
```
1. Executive Summary
2. Current State Analysis
   - Content inventory
   - Performance overview
   - SEO assessment
3. Competitive Landscape
   - What competitors are doing
   - White space opportunities
4. Quick Wins (30 days)
   - Easy improvements
   - Expected impact
5. Strategic Recommendations (90 days)
   - Content pillars
   - Priority topics
   - Resource requirements
6. Investment Options
   - How we can help
   - Pricing ranges
```

### 4. Partner Referrals

**Strategy**: Build relationships with complementary service providers.

**Ideal Partners:**
- Web design/development agencies
- SEO agencies (that don't do content)
- Paid media agencies
- Marketing consultants
- Venture capital firms (portfolio companies)
- Business coaches for founders

**Partnership Approach:**
```
Hi [Name],

I've been following [Partner Company] and love the work 
you're doing with [specific observation].

I run a content marketing agency focused on B2B SaaS. 
We often work with companies that need [partner's service] 
and vice versa.

Would you be open to a quick intro call? I'd love to 
explore how we might refer business to each other.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Referral Agreement:**
- 10-15% referral fee for first 6 months of client revenue
- Or reciprocal referrals (no fee)
- Clear handoff process
- Regular check-ins

**Target:** 5-10 active referral partners  
**Expected:** 1-2 referrals per partner per year

### 5. Speaking & Events

**Strategy**: Position as expert through speaking engagements.

**Opportunities:**
- Industry podcasts (guest appearances)
- Webinars (host or guest)
- Conference speaking
- Local business events
- Virtual summits

**Pitch Template:**
```
Subject: Speaking Proposal: [Topic] for [Event]

Hi [Name],

I'm reaching out about a potential speaking opportunity 
for [Event].

Topic: "[Compelling Title]"

Key Takeaways:
- Takeaway 1
- Takeaway 2
- Takeaway 3

This session would be valuable for your audience because 
[reason].

I've spoken at [events] and helped companies like 
[examples] achieve [results].

Would this be a fit for your event? Happy to provide 
more details.

Best,
[Your name]
[Credentials]
```

**Content Repurposing:**
- Recording → YouTube video
- Transcript → Blog post
- Key points → Social media thread
- Slides → SlideShare/LinkedIn

### 6. Cold Email

**Strategy**: Targeted, personalized email outreach.

**List Building:**
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Apollo.io
- ZoomInfo
- Manual research

**Email Structure:**
```
Subject: [Specific, relevant, curiosity]

Hi [Name],

[Personalized observation about them/company]

[Problem they likely have - show you understand]

[How you've helped similar companies]

[Soft CTA - low commitment]

[Sign-off]
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: Content at [Company]

Hi Sarah,

Noticed [Company] just raised Series B - congrats! 
That's an exciting (and challenging) inflection point.

One thing I see companies struggle with at this stage: 
content that worked at $5M ARR doesn't scale to $20M. 
The bar for thought leadership gets much higher.

We recently helped [Similar Company] 3x their content 
output while improving quality - resulting in 247% more 
organic traffic and 180 qualified demos in 6 months.

Would you be open to a quick 20-min chat? I'd love to 
share what's working for companies at your stage.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Metrics:**
- Open rate: 40-60% (with good personalization)
- Reply rate: 5-10%
- Meeting booked: 2-5%
- Close rate: 25-40% of meetings

**Volume:** 50-100 emails per week for consistent pipeline

### 7. Case Study Marketing

**Strategy**: Use client success stories to attract similar companies.

**Case Study Distribution:**
- Website featured section
- LinkedIn posts (tag client)
- Email to prospects in same industry
- Sales deck inclusion
- Proposal attachments
- Paid social (targeted to ICP)
- Guest posts featuring the story

**Example Pitch:**
```
Subject: How [Similar Company] 3X'd Content ROI

Hi [Name],

Thought you might find this interesting given [their situation].

[Similar Company] was facing [challenge similar to prospect's].

Here's what we did: [2-3 sentences]

Results: [Specific, quantified outcomes]

Full case study: [Link]

If you're facing similar challenges, happy to share 
what we learned.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Discovery Call Framework

**Goal**: Qualify the prospect and understand if you can help.

### Call Structure (30 minutes)

| Time | Section | Purpose |
|------|---------|---------|
| 0-5 min | Rapport & Context | Build connection, set agenda |
| 5-15 min | Discovery Questions | Understand their situation |
| 15-20 min | Capabilities Overview | Briefly explain how you help |
| 20-25 min | Fit Assessment | Mutual qualification |
| 25-30 min | Next Steps | Clear path forward |

### Discovery Questions

**Current State:**
- "Tell me about your current content marketing efforts."
- "What's working well? What's not?"
- "How is content currently resourced (team, budget, tools)?"

**Goals:**
- "What are your top 3 marketing goals this year?"
- "How does content fit into those goals?"
- "What would success look like in 6 months?"

**Challenges:**
- "What's your biggest content marketing challenge right now?"
- "What have you tried to solve this?"
- "What's preventing you from achieving your goals?"

**Decision Process:**
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What's your timeline for getting started?"
- "What does your budget range look like?"

**Past Experience:**
- "Have you worked with agencies before?"
- "What did you love? What frustrated you?"
- "What would make this engagement successful?"

### Qualification Criteria (BANT+)

| Criteria | Questions | Green Flags |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Budget | "What's your budget range?" | $5K+/mo for retainers |
| Authority | "Who makes the final decision?" | Decision-maker on call |
| Need | "What problem are you solving?" | Clear, urgent pain point |
| Timeline | "When do you want to start?" | Within 30-60 days |
| Fit | "Why us? Why now?" | Researched you, specific interest |

### Red Flags During Discovery

- [ ] "We need this yesterday" (unrealistic timeline)
- [ ] "Our budget is flexible" (usually means no budget)
- [ ] Can't articulate their goals
- [ ] "We've burned through 3 agencies this year"
- [ ] Decision maker not involved
- [ ] Expecting guarantees ("Can you guarantee #1 rankings?")

## Proposal Process

**Goal**: Present a clear, compelling solution that makes saying "yes" easy.

### Proposal Structure

```
1. Executive Summary (1 page)
   - Their situation
   - Proposed solution
   - Expected outcomes

2. Current State Assessment (1-2 pages)
   - What we learned in discovery
   - Key challenges identified
   - Opportunities

3. Recommended Approach (2-3 pages)
   - Strategy overview
   - Services included
   - Process and timeline

4. Investment (1 page)
   - Pricing options (3 tiers)
   - Payment terms
   - What's included

5. Case Studies (1-2 pages)
   - 2-3 relevant success stories
   - Quantified results

6. About Us (1 page)
   - Team credentials
   - Why choose us
   - Differentiators

7. Next Steps (0.5 page)
   - Timeline
   - What happens after signing
   - Call to action

8. Appendix (as needed)
   - Detailed scope
   - Sample deliverables
   - References
```

### Proposal Best Practices

**Do:**
- Personalize heavily (use their name, company, specifics)
- Lead with their problems, not your services
- Include 3 pricing options (anchoring)
- Make the "recommended" option obvious
- Include clear next steps
- Send as PDF (not editable)
- Follow up within 24 hours

**Don't:**
- Use generic templates without customization
- Lead with your company history
- Only show one price option
- Make them guess what happens next
- Send and ghost

### Pricing Presentation

Always present 3 options:

| Option | Price | Psychology |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Basic | $8,000/mo | Makes recommended look reasonable |
| Recommended | $15,000/mo | What you want them to choose (highlight) |
| Premium | $25,000/mo | Makes recommended look like value |

**Visual Cues:**
- "Most Popular" badge on recommended
- Checkmarks showing value
- Clear differentiation

### Follow-Up Sequence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 0 | Send proposal + personal email |
| 1 | Call to confirm receipt, answer questions |
| 3 | Email: "Any thoughts?" + offer to walk through |
| 7 | Email: Share relevant case study |
| 14 | Call: "Still interested? What's blocking?" |
| 21 | Email: "Should I close your file?" (loss aversion) |
| 30 | Final attempt or mark as lost |

## Closing & Onboarding

### Closing the Deal

**Signs They're Ready:**
- Asking about start date
- Discussing specific deliverables
- Involving other stakeholders
- Asking about payment terms
- "This looks great" language

**Closing Questions:**
- "Does this solution address your main challenges?"
- "What would need to happen for you to move forward?"
- "Is there anything preventing us from getting started?"
- "Would you like me to send over the agreement?"

**Objection Handling:**

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "It's too expensive" | "What budget were you expecting? Let's see what we can adjust." |
| "We need to think about it" | "Of course. What specifically do you need to think through?" |
| "We're talking to other agencies" | "That makes sense. What criteria are you using to decide?" |
| "Can you guarantee results?" | "We can't guarantee, but here's what we've achieved for similar clients..." |

### Onboarding Process

**Week 1: Kickoff**
- [ ] Contract signed
- [ ] Invoice paid
- [ ] Kickoff call scheduled
- [ ] Access requests sent (CMS, analytics, etc.)
- [ ] Stakeholder interviews scheduled

**Week 2: Discovery**
- [ ] Stakeholder interviews complete
- [ ] Content audit initiated
- [ ] Analytics review
- [ ] Competitive research started

**Week 3: Strategy**
- [ ] Strategy presentation
- [ ] Editorial calendar approved
- [ ] First briefs created
- [ ] Workflow established

**Week 4: Production**
- [ ] First content in progress
- [ ] Distribution plan activated
- [ ] Reporting dashboard set up
- [ ] Regular cadence established

### Onboarding Checklist

```
□ Contract signed
□ Invoice paid
□ Welcome email sent
□ Kickoff call scheduled
□ Access collected (all platforms)
□ Stakeholder interviews scheduled
□ Brand guidelines received
□ Content audit complete
□ Strategy presentation delivered
□ First month calendar approved
□ Communication channels established
□ Reporting dashboard created
```
