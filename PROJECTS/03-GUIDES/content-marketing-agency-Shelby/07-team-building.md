# Team Building

Hiring guide, role descriptions, and team structure for scaling a content marketing agency.

---

## Overview

Building the right team is the difference between a lifestyle business and a scalable agency.

**Phases of Team Growth:**

```
PHASE 1: Solo/Founder (0-3 clients)
         ↓
PHASE 2: Core Team (4-10 clients)
         ↓
PHASE 3: Department Structure (11-25 clients)
         ↓
PHASE 4: Scaled Agency (25+ clients)
```

---

## Phase 1: Solo/Founder (0-3 clients, $0-30K/mo)

**Team:** Just you (the founder)

**Your Roles:**
- Sales & business development
- Strategy
- Content creation (writing, video, etc.)
- Client management
- Operations & finance

**Focus:**
- Prove the model
- Build case studies
- Establish processes
- Generate $15K-30K/mo revenue

**When to Hire First:**
- Consistently at capacity (turning down work)
- Revenue covers salary + 20% buffer
- You're doing low-value work that could be delegated
- Client satisfaction at risk due to bandwidth

## Phase 2: Core Team (4-10 clients, $30K-100K/mo)

**Team Structure:**

```
                    FOUNDER
                  (Strategy, Sales)
                       |
        ┌──────────────┼──────────────┐
        ↓              ↓              ↓
   Content         Account        Operations
   Manager         Manager        (You or VA)
        |
   ┌────┴────┐
   ↓         ↓
Writer    Editor
(Freelance) (Freelance)
```

### Key Hires

#### 1. Content Manager / Strategist

**When:** $40K-50K/mo revenue, 5-7 clients  
**Type:** Full-time or part-time  
**Salary:** $60,000-90,000/year

**Responsibilities:**
- Editorial calendar management
- Brief creation
- Freelancer coordination
- Quality review
- Client communication (day-to-day)
- Process documentation

**Profile:**
- 3-5 years content marketing experience
- Agency experience preferred
- Strong writing and editing skills
- Project management skills
- Comfortable with tools (Asana, Google Suite, CMS)

**Interview Questions:**
- "Walk me through how you'd manage 20 blog posts per month across 5 clients."
- "How do you handle a freelancer who misses a deadline?"
- "Show me a content strategy you've developed."
- "How do you prioritize when everything is 'urgent'?"

#### 2. Account Manager

**When:** 6+ clients, multiple stakeholders per client  
**Type:** Full-time  
**Salary:** $55,000-80,000/year + bonus

**Responsibilities:**
- Primary client contact
- Quarterly business reviews
- Upsell/cross-sell identification
- Contract renewals
- Client satisfaction
- Internal coordination

**Profile:**
- 3-5 years account management (agency preferred)
- Strong communication skills
- Comfortable with numbers and reporting
- Problem-solver mindset
- Can say "no" gracefully

#### 3. Freelance Writers (2-5)

**When:** Consistent content volume needs  
**Type:** Contract/Freelance  
**Rate:** $0.15-0.50/word or $500-2,000/piece

**Responsibilities:**
- Write content per brief
- Meet deadlines
- Incorporate feedback
- Research topics thoroughly

**Profile:**
- 2+ years professional writing
- Portfolio in relevant industries
- Reliable and communicative
- Takes feedback well

**Where to Find:**
- ProBlogger job board
- LinkedIn
- Twitter/X
- Content marketing communities
- Referrals from other writers

**Vetting Process:**
1. Review portfolio
2. Paid test assignment ($100-200)
3. Evaluate: quality, deadline, communication
4. Start with 1-2 pieces, scale up if good

#### 4. Freelance Editor

**When:** 3+ writers producing content  
**Type:** Contract/Freelance  
**Rate:** $50-100/hour or $100-300/piece

**Responsibilities:**
- Developmental editing
- Line editing
- Copy editing
- Fact-checking
- Brand voice consistency

**Profile:**
- 5+ years editing experience
- Agency or publication background
- Strong grammar and style knowledge
- Detail-oriented

## Hiring Process

### Job Description Template

```markdown
# [Role Title]

## About Us
[2-3 sentences about agency, mission, clients]

## The Role
[Overview of what they'll be doing]

## Responsibilities
- Responsibility 1
- Responsibility 2
- Responsibility 3
...

## Requirements
- Requirement 1 (must-have)
- Requirement 2 (must-have)
- Requirement 3 (nice-to-have)
...

## What We Offer
- Competitive salary: $X-Y
- Health/dental/vision
- 401k matching
- Unlimited PTO
- Remote-first culture
- Professional development budget
- [Other perks]

## How to Apply
[Instructions - include a task to filter non-serious applicants]
```

### Interview Process

**For Full-Time Roles:**

| Stage | Format | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|--------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Phone Screen | 30 min | Basic fit, salary expectations |
| 2 | Skills Assessment | Take-home | Evaluate actual abilities |
| 3 | Video Interview | 60 min | Deep dive on experience |
| 4 | Team Interview | 45 min | Culture fit, team dynamics |
| 5 | Final Call | 30 min | Close, answer questions |

**For Freelance Roles:**

| Stage | Format | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Portfolio Review | Assess quality |
| 2 | Paid Test | Evaluate skills, reliability |
| 3 | Trial Period | 2-4 weeks, small projects |
| 4 | Ongoing | Scale up if successful |

### Skills Assessments

**Writer Assessment:**
```
Task: Write a 1,000-word blog post
Brief: [Provide detailed brief]
Topic: [Relevant to your ICP]
Deadline: 3-5 days
Compensation: $150-250

Evaluate:
- Quality of writing
- Adherence to brief
- Research depth
- SEO understanding
- Deadline adherence
- Communication
```

**Editor Assessment:**
```
Task: Edit a 1,500-word article
Provide: Draft with intentional errors
Deadline: 2 days
Compensation: $100-150

Evaluate:
- Errors caught
- Clarity improvements
- Tone consistency
- Feedback quality
- Turnaround time
```

**Account Manager Assessment:**
```
Task: Handle a difficult client scenario
Provide: Written scenario with unhappy client
Deadline: 2 days

Evaluate:
- Problem-solving approach
- Communication style
- Escalation judgment
- Commercial awareness
```

### Reference Checks

**Questions to Ask:**
- "What were their primary responsibilities?"
- "What were their biggest strengths?"
- "What areas did they need development in?"
- "How did they handle pressure/deadlines?"
- "Would you rehire them? Why or why not?"
- "How would you describe their communication style?"

## Scaling Considerations

### When to Hire

**Good Reasons:**
- Consistently at capacity (4+ weeks)
- Turning down work
- Quality suffering due to bandwidth
- Revenue supports salary + 20%
- Clear role definition

**Bad Reasons:**
- Hope that they'll generate their own work
- Founder burnout (fix systems first)
- One big client (need 3+ months runway)
- Vague role expectations

### Contractor vs. Employee

| Factor | Contractor | Employee |
|--------|------------|----------|
| Cost | Lower (no benefits) | Higher (benefits, taxes) |
| Flexibility | High | Lower |
| Control | Lower | Higher |
| Loyalty | Lower | Higher |
| Best For | Specialized, variable work | Core, ongoing work |

**Guideline:** Start with contractors, convert to employee when:
- Work is consistent (20+ hours/week)
- Role is core to operations
- You want more control/availability
- Contractor wants stability

### Offshoring Considerations

**Roles Suitable for Offshoring:**
- Content writing (with strong editing)
- Video editing
- Graphic design
- SEO research
- Data entry/operations

**Roles to Keep Local:**
- Strategy
- Account management
- Sales
- Leadership

**Countries to Consider:**
- Philippines: English proficiency, cost-effective
- Latin America: Time zone alignment, good English
- Eastern Europe: Strong technical skills

**Management Tips:**
- Over-communicate expectations
- Invest in onboarding
- Regular video calls
- Visit in person if possible
- Pay above local market (retention)
