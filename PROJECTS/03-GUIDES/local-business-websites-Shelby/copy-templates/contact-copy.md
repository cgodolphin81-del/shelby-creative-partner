## Contact Page Best Practices

### Essential Elements
- ✅ Phone number (clickable on mobile)
- ✅ Email address
- ✅ Physical address (if applicable)
- ✅ Business hours
- ✅ Contact form
- ✅ Map/directions
- ✅ Response time expectations
- ✅ What happens next

### Conversion Optimization
- ✅ Place phone number in header (visible on all pages)
- ✅ Use action-oriented button text
- ✅ Minimize form fields (only ask what's necessary)
- ✅ Explain what happens after submission
- ✅ Include trust elements near forms
- ✅ Mobile-optimized (tap-to-call)
- ✅ Multiple contact options

### Trust Elements
- ✅ Response time promises
- ✅ Privacy assurances
- ✅ No-pressure language
- ✅ Guarantee mentions
- ✅ License/insurance info
- ✅ Real photos (not stock)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Hiding contact information
- ❌ Generic form submissions with no follow-up plan
- ❌ No phone number (some people prefer to call)
- ❌ Complicated forms with too many fields
- ❌ No confirmation of receipt
- ❌ Slow response times
- ❌ Outdated information

---

## Form Field Best Practices

### Required Fields (Keep Minimal)
- Name
- Email OR Phone (at least one)
- Service/Subject
- Message/Details

### Optional Fields (Use Judiciously)
- Phone (if email is required)
- Budget range
- Timeline
- How they heard about you
- Address (for home services)

### Form Tips
- Use clear labels (not placeholder text as labels)
- Include helpful hints for complex fields
- Show character counts for text areas
- Validate in real-time
- Thank you page with next steps
- Auto-responder email confirmation

---

*The goal of your contact page is to remove friction and make it as easy as possible for prospects to reach out. Test different approaches and track which contact methods your customers prefer.*
