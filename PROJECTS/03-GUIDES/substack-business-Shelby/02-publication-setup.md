# 02 — Publication Setup: Branding, About Page, Welcome Email, Landing Page

Your publication's first impression determines whether visitors subscribe or bounce. This guide covers everything you need to set up a professional, conversion-optimized Substack.

---

## 🎨 Section 1: Branding

### Publication Name

**Formula:** [Topic] + [Unique Angle] OR [Your Name] + [Topic]

**Examples:**
- `The Algorithm` (general tech)
- `Money Stuff` (finance + personality)
- `Stratechery` (strategy + tech)
- `The Profile` (your name + implied depth)
- `Lenny's Newsletter` (name + format)

**Naming Checklist:**
- [ ] Easy to spell and remember
- [ ] Available as .com (optional but nice)
- [ ] Not already a famous Substack
- [ ] Works as a Twitter handle
- [ ] Sounds credible in your niche

**Avoid:**
- Clever puns that don't translate
- Names that limit future expansion
- Anything hard to spell after hearing it once

---

### Tagline

**Formula:** [Who it's for] + [What they get]

**Examples:**
- "Business insights for the curious"
- "Investment research for individual investors"
- "AI tools that actually work"
- "The future of work, written weekly"

**Tagline Tips:**
- Keep it under 10 words
- Lead with benefit, not feature
- Avoid jargon

---

### Visual Identity

#### Logo/Icon
- **Size:** 500x500px minimum (displays as 128x128)
- **Format:** PNG with transparency
- **Style:** Simple, recognizable at small sizes
- **Tools:** Canva, Figma, or hire on Fiverr ($20-50)

**Options:**
1. **Text-based:** Publication name in nice font
2. **Symbol:** Simple icon representing your niche
3. **Letter:** First letter, stylized
4. **Your face:** Works for personal brands

#### Color Scheme
- **Primary:** One main color (used for buttons, links)
- **Secondary:** One accent color (optional)
- **Neutral:** Black, white, grays for text/backgrounds

**Psychology:**
- Blue = Trust, professional (finance, tech)
- Green = Growth, money (investing, business)
- Red = Urgency, passion (politics, fitness)
- Purple = Creativity, premium (design, culture)
- Orange = Energy, friendly (lifestyle, food)

#### Fonts
Substack limits font choices, but your graphics should use:
- **Headlines:** Bold, clean sans-serif (Montserrat, Poppins)
- **Body:** Readable serif or sans-serif (Merriweather, Open Sans)

## 📝 Section 2: About Page

Your About page is your sales page. Most visitors will read this before subscribing.

### About Page Template

```markdown
# Welcome to [Publication Name]

**[One-sentence value proposition]**

Hi, I'm [Your Name]. I [credibility statement].

For the past [X years], I've been [relevant experience]. I've [achievement 1], 
[achievement 2], and [achievement 3].

## What You'll Get

Every [frequency], I send out emails covering:

• **[Topic 1]:** [Specific benefit]
• **[Topic 2]:** [Specific benefit]
• **[Topic 3]:** [Specific benefit]

## Who This Is For

This newsletter is for you if:
- You're a [target audience]
- You want to [desired outcome]
- You believe [shared belief]

## Who This Is NOT For

This newsletter is NOT for you if:
- You're looking for [thing you don't offer]
- You prefer [alternative approach]
- You're not serious about [topic]

## Free vs. Paid

**Free subscribers get:**
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]
- [Benefit 3]

**Paid subscribers get:**
- [Exclusive benefit 1]
- [Exclusive benefit 2]
- [Exclusive benefit 3]
- [Community access, if applicable]

## Join [X] Paid Subscribers

[Social proof: subscriber count, notable readers, press mentions]

[Subscribe button]

## About Me

[2-3 paragraphs on your background, why you're qualified, personal touch]

[Photo of you — builds trust]

## Questions?

Reply to any email or reach me at [email/Twitter].

— [Your Name]
```

### About Page Best Practices

**Do:**
- Lead with the reader's benefit, not your story
- Use specific numbers and achievements
- Include a photo (trust signal)
- Make free vs. paid crystal clear
- Show subscriber count (social proof)
- Write like you speak (conversational)

**Don't:**
- Make it all about you
- Use vague claims ("expert," "guru")
- Hide the paywall details
- Write a novel (keep it scannable)
- Sound corporate or stiff

## 📧 Section 3: Welcome Email

Your welcome email has the highest open rate you'll ever get (60-80%). Use it wisely.

### Welcome Email Template

```
Subject: Welcome to [Publication Name]! (+ what to expect)

Hi [Name],

Welcome! I'm [Your Name], and I write [Publication Name].

**Here's what you can expect:**

📬 **Frequency:** [e.g., Every Tuesday and Friday]
📝 **Content:** [Brief description of what you cover]
💎 **Why it matters:** [The transformation/outcome]

**My most popular posts (start here):**

1. [Post Title] — [One-line description]
   → [Link]

2. [Post Title] — [One-line description]
   → [Link]

3. [Post Title] — [One-line description]
   → [Link]

**Want to go deeper?**

Paid subscribers get [key benefit 1], [key benefit 2], and [key benefit 3].

[Upgrade button]

**Quick favor:**

Reply to this email and tell me:
1. What brought you here?
2. What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?

I read every reply and often write posts based on your questions.

Thanks for subscribing!

— [Your Name]

P.S. Add [your email] to your contacts so these emails don't land in spam.
```

### Welcome Email Best Practices

**Timing:** Send immediately upon subscription (Substack does this automatically)

**Goals:**
1. Set expectations (frequency, content)
2. Drive engagement (reply, read archives)
3. Convert to paid (soft pitch)
4. Build relationship (personal tone)

**Subject Line Tips:**
- Include publication name (recognition)
- Use "Welcome" (clear purpose)
- Add intrigue ("what to expect")
- A/B test if you have volume

**Conversion Tactics:**
- Link to 2-3 best free posts (show value)
- Mention paid benefits (plant seed)
- Ask for reply (engagement signal)
- P.S. about contacts (deliverability)

## 🎯 Section 4: Landing Page Optimization

Your Substack homepage is your landing page. Optimize every element.

### Homepage Elements (In Order)

```
1. Publication Name & Tagline
2. Subscribe Form (above the fold!)
3. Recent Posts (social proof of activity)
4. About Section (your About page content)
5. Testimonials/Social Proof (if available)
```

### Conversion Optimization Checklist

#### Above the Fold
- [ ] Clear value proposition in tagline
- [ ] Email field visible without scrolling
- [ ] Subscribe button uses action verb ("Subscribe" not "Submit")
- [ ] Free vs. Paid options clearly shown
- [ ] Mobile-optimized (test on phone!)

#### Trust Signals
- [ ] Subscriber count displayed
- [ ] Your photo visible
- [ ] Recent post dates (shows activity)
- [ ] Professional design (not default theme)
- [ ] No typos or errors

#### Friction Reducers
- [ ] No required fields beyond email
- [ ] Clear privacy statement ("No spam, unsubscribe anytime")
- [ ] Multiple payment options (card, PayPal if available)
- [ ] Annual discount prominently shown

---

### Landing Page Copy Templates

#### Headline Options
- "[Publication Name]: [Benefit] for [Audience]"
- "The [Niche] Newsletter for [Specific Audience]"
- "[Number] [Audience] Get [Benefit] Every [Frequency]"

#### Subheadline Options
- "Join [X] subscribers who [outcome]"
- "Free [frequency]. Upgrade for [premium benefit]."
- "[Credibility]. Now sharing [what you offer]."

#### Call-to-Action Buttons
- "Subscribe Free"
- "Join Free"
- "Get [Benefit]"
- "Start Reading"

**Avoid:** "Submit," "Sign Up" (generic)

## 🧪 Section 5: A/B Testing Framework

Once you have traffic, test these elements:

### Test Priority Order

1. **Headline/Tagline** — Biggest impact
2. **About Page** — Second biggest
3. **Welcome Email** — Conversion impact
4. **Paywall Placement** — Revenue impact
5. **Pricing** — Sensitivity testing

### How to Test on Substack

Substack doesn't have built-in A/B testing, but you can:

1. **Manual Tests:** Change element, track for 2 weeks
2. **Traffic Sources:** Send different versions to different audiences
3. **Notes Posts:** Test headlines via Substack Notes
4. **External Landing Page:** Use Carrd/ConvertKit, link to Substack

### Metrics to Track

| Element | Metric to Watch |
|---------|-----------------|
| Headline | Visit → Subscribe rate |
| About Page | Time on page, subscribe rate |
| Welcome Email | Reply rate, click rate |
| Paywall | Free → Paid conversion |
| Pricing | Paid conversion, churn |

## 🛠️ Section 6: Technical Setup Checklist

### Before Launch

- [ ] Publication name secured
- [ ] Custom domain connected (optional but recommended)
- [ ] Logo uploaded (500x500px)
- [ ] Tagline written
- [ ] About page published
- [ ] Welcome email scheduled
- [ ] 3-5 posts published (archives)
- [ ] Social links added
- [ ] Payment account connected (Stripe)
- [ ] Test subscription (free and paid)
- [ ] Mobile display checked
- [ ] Email delivery test (send to yourself)

### Custom Domain Setup

**Why:** Looks more professional, you own it, portable

**Steps:**
1. Buy domain ($12-15/year)
2. Substack → Settings → Domain
3. Add CNAME record at your registrar
4. Wait 24-48 hours for propagation
5. Enable SSL (automatic)

**Recommended:** `[publicationname].com` or `[yourname].com`
