# 05 — Paid Tier Strategy: Paywall Content, Pricing ($5-50/mo), Annual Discounts

Your paid tier is your business model. This guide helps you price, position, and optimize for maximum conversions and revenue.

---

## 💰 Section 1: Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Psychology

**Key principles:**

1. **Anchoring:** First price seen sets expectations
2. **Decoy effect:** Three tiers make middle look best
3. **Charm pricing:** $7 vs. $10 feels significantly cheaper
4. **Annual framing:** "$60/year" vs. "$5/month" (higher commitment)
5. **Discount framing:** "Save 20%" vs. "Pay $X"

---

### Price Point Analysis

| Price | Best For | Conversion | Revenue/Sub |
|-------|----------|------------|-------------|
| **$5/mo** | General interest, culture, politics | 8-15% | $60/yr |
| **$7/mo** | Business, career, self-improvement | 6-12% | $84/yr |
| **$10/mo** | Specialized business, investing | 5-10% | $120/yr |
| **$15/mo** | B2B, niche expertise | 4-8% | $180/yr |
| **$20/mo** | Premium B2B, research | 3-6% | $240/yr |
| **$25-50/mo** | Enterprise, proprietary data | 2-5% | $300-600/yr |

---

### Recommended Starting Prices

**By Niche:**

| Niche | Starting Price | Rationale |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| AI/Automation | $8-12/mo | High ROI for readers |
| Investing | $15-25/mo | One tip pays for subscription |
| B2B SaaS | $15-30/mo | Business budget, not personal |
| Real Estate | $12-20/mo | One deal = years of value |
| Career | $8-15/mo | Promotion = 100x subscription |
| Health/Fitness | $8-12/mo | Health is priceless |
| Creator Economy | $10-15/mo | Readers want to make money |
| Parenting | $5-8/mo | Personal budget, emotional value |
| Food/Recipes | $5-8/mo | Daily utility |
| Politics/Culture | $5-10/mo | Perspective value |
| Local News | $5-8/mo | Community value |
| Specialized B2B | $20-50/mo | Competitive advantage |

---

### The Pricing Ladder

**Start lower, increase over time:**

```
Launch → $5-7/mo (build base)
1K subs → $7-10/mo (prove value)
5K subs → $10-15/mo (add benefits)
10K+ subs → $15-25/mo (premium positioning)
```

**Why this works:**
- Early adopters get founding member pricing
- Price increases signal growing value
- Grandfather existing subs (they become evangelists)
- New subs pay more as value increases

## 📦 Section 2: Tier Structure

### Single Tier (Recommended for Most)

**Structure:**
- Free: Basic content
- Paid: Everything else

**Pros:**
- Simple decision for readers
- Easy to manage
- Clear value proposition

**Cons:**
- No upsell path
- One-size-fits-all

**Best for:** Most newsletters under 10K subs

---

### Two Tiers

**Structure:**
- Free: 50% of content
- Paid ($8-15/mo): All content + community
- Premium ($25-50/mo): All content + community + 1:1 access

**Pros:**
- Higher revenue per subscriber
- Caters to different commitment levels
- Premium tier = high-touch, low volume

**Cons:**
- More complex to manage
- Need to deliver on premium promises

**Best for:** Established newsletters (5K+ subs), coaching-heavy niches

---

### Three Tiers

**Structure:**
- Free: Teaser content
- Paid ($10-20/mo): Full content
- Premium ($50-100/mo): Content + community + calls + resources

**Pros:**
- Maximum revenue optimization
- Multiple entry points
- Premium = high margin

**Cons:**
- Complex to communicate
- High delivery burden
- Can confuse readers

**Best for:** B2B, specialized expertise, established brands

---

### Recommended Tier Structure by Stage

| Stage | Structure | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| **0-1K subs** | Single tier | Simplicity, prove value |
| **1K-10K subs** | Single or two tiers | Test premium appetite |
| **10K+ subs** | Two or three tiers | Revenue optimization |

## 🎁 Section 3: Annual Discounts

### The Annual Discount Formula

**Standard discount:** 15-20% off monthly × 12

**Examples:**

| Monthly | Annual (Full) | Annual (17% off) | Savings |
|---------|---------------|------------------|---------|
| $5 | $60 | $50 | $10 |
| $7 | $84 | $70 | $14 |
| $10 | $120 | $100 | $20 |
| $15 | $180 | $150 | $30 |
| $20 | $240 | $200 | $40 |
| $25 | $300 | $250 | $50 |

**Psychological pricing:**
- $50/year feels better than $49.99
- Round numbers = premium feel
- "Save $X" is more compelling than percentages

---

### Annual Conversion Tactics

#### 1. **Default to Annual**
- Show annual price first
- Monthly as secondary option
- Most people choose default

#### 2. **Highlight Savings**
- "Save $20/year" (specific number)
- "Get 2 months free" (time framing)
- Badge: "Most Popular"

#### 3. **Payment Plan Option**
- Annual price, but split into 4 payments
- Reduces friction
- Still captures annual commitment

#### 4. **Limited-Time Annual Promo**
- "Switch to annual by [date], get 25% off"
- Creates urgency
- Converts existing monthly subs

---

### Annual Upgrade Email Template

```
Subject: Want to save $X on [Publication Name]?

Hi [Name],

Quick note: If you're loving [Publication Name], you can save 
$[X] by switching to annual.

Here's the math:
- Monthly: $[X]/mo = $[Y]/year
- Annual: $[Z]/year (save $[X]!)

That's [2] months free, just for committing upfront.

[Switch to Annual Button]

You can always switch back to monthly if needed.

Thanks for being a subscriber!

— [Your Name]
```

## 🎯 Section 4: What to Put Behind Paywall

### The Value Ladder

**Free content should be:**
- Genuinely valuable
- Representative of your quality
- Enough to build trust
- A taste of what paid offers

**Paid content should be:**
- Significantly deeper
- More actionable
- Exclusive (can't get elsewhere)
- Worth 10x the price

---

### Paywall Content Matrix

| Content Type | Free Version | Paid Version |
|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Analysis** | Surface-level insight | Deep dive with data |
| **How-to** | Basic steps | Complete system + templates |
| **Case Study** | Overview | Full breakdown with numbers |
| **Roundup** | Links only | Links + commentary + takeaways |
| **Q&A** | General questions | Specific, detailed answers |
| **Tools** | List of tools | Reviews, comparisons, discounts |
| **Community** | Public comments | Private discussions |
| **Access** | Email only | Direct access to you |

---

### High-Value Paid Content Ideas

#### 1. **Templates & Frameworks**
- Downloadable resources
- Fill-in-the-blank systems
- Checklists and worksheets

**Example:** "The Complete Content Calendar Template (Notion + Google Sheets)"

---

#### 2. **Original Research**
- Surveys you conduct
- Data analysis
- Industry reports

**Example:** "2026 State of AI in Business: Survey of 500 Founders"

---

#### 3. **Case Studies with Numbers**
- Real results
- Specific metrics
- Step-by-step breakdown

**Example:** "How I Grew from 0 to 10K Subs: Full Revenue Breakdown"

---

#### 4. **Direct Access**
- Monthly office hours
- Q&A threads
- DM access

**Example:** "Paid subscribers can reply to any email—I answer personally"

---

#### 5. **Community**
- Private chat (Slack, Discord, Circle)
- Subscriber-only comments
- Networking opportunities

**Example:** "Join 800+ founders in our private Slack community"

---

#### 6. **Early Access**
- Read posts 24-48 hours early
- Input on upcoming content
- Beta access to products

**Example:** "Paid subscribers read every post 2 days before free"

---

#### 7. **Audio/Video**
- Podcast episodes
- Video tutorials
- Recorded workshops

**Example:** "Monthly video deep-dive for paid subscribers"

---

#### 8. **Curated Resources**
- Tool database
- Resource library
- Deal/discount access

**Example:** "The Vault: 100+ tools with exclusive discounts"

---

### Paywall Placement Strategies

#### Strategy 1: Post-by-Post (Flexible)
- Choose which posts are paid
- Mix of free and paid
- Most control

**Best for:** Most newsletters

---

#### Strategy 2: Percentage-Based (Simple)
- X% of posts are paid
- Consistent ratio
- Easy to communicate

**Best for:** Newsletters with consistent output

---

#### Strategy 3: Content-Type Based (Clear)
- Certain types always paid
- E.g., all deep dives are paid
- Predictable for readers

**Best for:** Newsletters with distinct content types

---

#### Strategy 4: Freemium (Growth-Focused)
- Almost everything free
- Paid = community + access
- Maximizes audience

**Best for:** Building audience first, monetize later

## 🧪 Section 5: Pricing Experiments

### When to Test Price

**Good times to test:**
- After major value add (new benefit)
- When conversion rate is stable
- At milestone subscriber counts (1K, 5K, 10K)
- New year (people expect changes)

**Bad times to test:**
- During launch
- After negative feedback
- Without adding value
- More than once per quarter

---

### How to Test Price

#### Test 1: New Subscriber Pricing
- Change price for new subs only
- Existing subs grandfathered
- Cleanest test

**Measure:** Conversion rate change, revenue impact

---

#### Test 2: Annual Discount Test
- Test different discount levels (15% vs. 20% vs. 25%)
- Measure annual conversion rate
- No monthly price change

**Measure:** Annual uptake, total revenue

---

#### Test 3: Tier Addition
- Add premium tier
- See who upgrades
- Measure premium conversion

**Measure:** Premium uptake, churn impact

---

#### Test 4: Price Increase (Existing Subs)
- Grandfather option (keep old price)
- Or universal increase
- Communicate value added

**Measure:** Churn rate, revenue impact

---

## 📊 Section 6: Conversion Optimization

### Paywall Conversion Benchmarks

| Metric | Good | Great | Exceptional |
|--------|------|-------|-------------|
| **Free → Paid** | 5% | 8-12% | 15%+ |
| **Annual Uptake** | 20% | 30-40% | 50%+ |
| **Monthly Churn** | 5% | 3% | <2% |
| **Annual Churn** | 20% | 15% | <10% |

---

### Conversion Optimization Tactics

#### 1. **Upgrade CTAs in Every Post**
- Beginning: Soft mention
- Middle: Value reminder
- End: Direct CTA with button

**Example:**
> "Enjoying this? Paid subscribers get [benefit] every week. [Upgrade now]"

---

#### 2. **Paid-Only Post Teasers**
- Send subject line to all
- Free subs see "This post is for paid subscribers"
- They know what they're missing

---

#### 3. **Limited-Time Offers**
- Founding member pricing
- New year promo
- Milestone celebration

**Example:**
> "To celebrate 5K subscribers, I'm offering 50% off annual for the next 48 hours."

---

#### 4. **Social Proof**
- "Join 847 paid subscribers"
- Testimonials from paid subs
- Notable readers (if any)

---

#### 5. **Guarantee**
- 30-day money-back
- "Cancel anytime"
- Risk reversal

---

## 💳 Section 7: Payment & Billing

### Stripe Setup (Substack's Processor)

**What Substack handles:**
- Payment processing
- Subscription management
- Invoicing
- Failed payment retries
- Refunds (you approve)

**Your responsibilities:**
- Set pricing
- Approve refund requests
- Handle billing questions
- Monitor failed payments

---

### Failed Payment Management

**Substack's automatic retry:**
- Day 1: Initial charge fails
- Day 3: First retry
- Day 7: Second retry
- Day 14: Subscription paused
- Day 30: Subscription cancelled

**Your action:**
- Email subscribers with failed payments
- Offer to help resolve
- Provide alternative payment method

---

### Refund Policy

**Recommended approach:**
- 30-day window for refunds
- Case-by-case after 30 days
- No questions asked (builds goodwill)
- Track refund reasons (improve product)

**Refund email template:**

```
Subject: Refund processed

Hi [Name],

I've processed your refund for [Publication Name]. You should 
see the credit within 5-10 business days.

I'm sorry it didn't work out. If you're open to sharing, I'd 
love to know what didn't meet your expectations. Your feedback 
helps me improve.

You're welcome to resubscribe anytime.

Best,
[Your Name]
```
