# 03 — Content Strategy: Posting Frequency, Post Types, Series Planning, Voice

Content is your product. This guide helps you build a sustainable content engine that attracts, engages, and converts readers.

---

## 📅 Section 1: Posting Frequency

### The Frequency Sweet Spot

| Frequency | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-----------|------|------|----------|
| **Daily** | Fast growth, habit-forming | Burnout risk, quality pressure | News, markets, serious writers |
| **2x/week** | Strong momentum, manageable | Requires discipline | Most niches |
| **Weekly** | Sustainable, quality focus | Slower growth | Beginners, busy creators |
| **Bi-weekly** | Very sustainable | Easy to forget | Side projects |
| **Monthly** | Low pressure | Hard to build habit | Not recommended |

### Recommendation by Stage

**Launch (0-500 subs):**
- **Minimum:** 1x/week
- **Ideal:** 2x/week
- **Goal:** Build archive, prove consistency

**Growth (500-5K subs):**
- **Minimum:** 1x/week
- **Ideal:** 2-3x/week
- **Goal:** Maintain momentum, test formats

**Scale (5K+ subs):**
- **Minimum:** 1x/week
- **Ideal:** 2x/week + Notes daily
- **Goal:** Quality over quantity, team support

### The Consistency Rule

**Better to publish weekly for a year than daily for a month.**

Readers build habits around your schedule. Missing emails breaks trust.

**Commitment Framework:**
1. Pick a schedule you can maintain for 6 months
2. Publish on the same day(s) and time(s)
3. Build a 2-week content buffer before launching
4. Have 3-5 draft ideas ready at all times

### Best Send Times

| Day | Best Times (EST) | Notes |
|-----|------------------|-------|
| Tuesday | 6-8 AM, 12 PM | Highest open rates |
| Wednesday | 6-8 AM, 12 PM | Strong mid-week |
| Thursday | 6-8 AM | Good for B2B |
| Friday | 6-8 AM | Lower engagement |
| Weekend | 8-10 AM | Lifestyle niches only |

**Avoid:** Monday mornings (inbox overload), Friday afternoons (checked out)

## 📝 Section 2: Post Types

### Core Post Types

#### 1. **The Deep Dive** (2000-4000 words)
**Purpose:** Establish expertise, drive paid conversions
**Frequency:** 1-2x/month
**Structure:**
- Hook (problem or surprising claim)
- Context/background
- Main argument (3-5 sections)
- Examples/case studies
- Actionable takeaway
- Call-to-action (paid upgrade)

**Example Topics:**
- "The Complete Guide to [Topic]"
- "Why Everyone Is Wrong About [X]"
- "How I [Achieved Result] in [Timeframe]"

---

#### 2. **The Quick Hit** (300-800 words)
**Purpose:** Maintain engagement, share timely insights
**Frequency:** 1-2x/week
**Structure:**
- One clear idea
- Brief context
- Insight or takeaway
- Optional: question for readers

**Example Topics:**
- "One Tool That Changed My Workflow"
- "3 Things I Learned This Week"
- "Quick Take: [News/Event]"

---

#### 3. **The Case Study** (1500-3000 words)
**Purpose:** Show proof, build credibility
**Frequency:** 1-2x/month
**Structure:**
- The situation/problem
- The approach taken
- The results (specific numbers)
- Lessons learned
- How readers can apply it

**Example Topics:**
- "How [Company] Grew from X to Y"
- "I Tried [Strategy] for 30 Days. Here's What Happened"
- "Breakdown: [Successful Example]"

---

#### 4. **The Roundup** (1000-2000 words)
**Purpose:** Curate value, easy to produce
**Frequency:** Weekly or bi-weekly
**Structure:**
- Brief intro
- 5-10 items with commentary
- Closing thought

**Example Topics:**
- "5 Articles Worth Your Time This Week"
- "The Best [Niche] Content from [Month]"
- "Links I'm Loving: [Date]"

---

#### 5. **The Q&A** (1000-2500 words)
**Purpose:** Build community, address reader needs
**Frequency:** Monthly
**Structure:**
- Intro (how questions were collected)
- 5-10 questions with detailed answers
- Closing (how to submit next round)

**Example Topics:**
- "Answering Your Questions About [Topic]"
- "Reader Mail: [Month]"
- "Ask Me Anything: [Specific Theme]"

---

#### 6. **The Personal Story** (1000-2500 words)
**Purpose:** Build connection, differentiate
**Frequency:** 1-2x/month
**Structure:**
- The situation
- The challenge
- The turning point
- The lesson
- How it applies to readers

**Example Topics:**
- "The Mistake That Cost Me $X"
- "Why I Quit [Thing]"
- "What [Experience] Taught Me About [Topic]"

---

#### 7. **The List Post** (800-2000 words)
**Purpose:** Easy to consume, highly shareable
**Frequency:** 1-2x/month
**Structure:**
- Intro (why this list matters)
- Numbered items with explanations
- Closing summary

**Example Topics:**
- "10 Tools Every [Audience] Needs"
- "7 Books That Changed My Thinking"
- "5 Habits of Successful [Role]"

---

#### 8. **The Prediction/Trend** (1000-2000 words)
**Purpose:** Position as thought leader
**Frequency:** Quarterly or annually
**Structure:**
- Context (current state)
- Prediction/trend #1 with reasoning
- Prediction/trend #2 with reasoning
- (Repeat 3-5x)
- Closing (what to watch)

**Example Topics:**
- "[Year] Predictions: What's Coming in [Industry]"
- "The Trend Nobody's Talking About"
- "Why [Thing] Is About to Explode"

---

### Content Mix Formula

**For 2 posts/week:**
- Week 1: Deep Dive + Quick Hit
- Week 2: Case Study + Quick Hit
- Week 3: Deep Dive + Roundup
- Week 4: Personal Story + Q&A

**For 1 post/week:**
- Week 1: Deep Dive
- Week 2: Case Study
- Week 3: Quick Hit or Roundup
- Week 4: Personal Story or Q&A

## 📚 Section 3: Series Planning

Series keep readers coming back and make content creation easier.

### How to Create a Series

1. **Pick a theme** that requires multiple posts
2. **Plan 3-6 episodes** before starting
3. **Name the series** (makes it recognizable)
4. **Publish on consistent schedule**
5. **Link episodes together** (previous/next)

### Series Ideas by Niche

| Niche | Series Idea | Episodes |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| AI/Business | "AI Tool of the Week" | Ongoing |
| Investing | "Stock Deep Dive" | 2x/month |
| SaaS | "SaaS Teardown" | Monthly |
| Career | "Career Playbook" | 6-part |
| Health | "30-Day Challenge" | Daily/weekly |
| Creator | "Monetization Breakdown" | 2x/month |
| Real Estate | "Deal Analysis" | Monthly |
| Parenting | "Age-by-Age Guide" | 12-part |

### Series Structure Template

```
SERIES: [Series Name]
EPISODE: [X of Y]

[Intro: Reference previous episode, set up this one]

[Main content]

[Summary/Takeaway]

[Tease next episode]

---
Missed an episode? Catch up:
→ [Episode 1 Link]
→ [Episode 2 Link]
→ [Episode 3 Link]
```

### Evergreen Series Ideas

These can run indefinitely:

- **"Beginner's Guide to [Topic]"** — Part 1, 2, 3...
- **"[Industry] News Roundup"** — Weekly/Monthly
- **"Reader Questions"** — Monthly
- **"Tool/Resource Reviews"** — Ongoing
- **"Interview Series"** — With experts in your field

## 🎭 Section 4: Voice Development

Your voice is your competitive advantage. AI can write content; only you can write like you.

### Voice Dimensions

Rate yourself 1-10 on each:

| Dimension | 1 | 10 |
|-----------|---|----|
| **Formal ↔ Casual** | Academic paper | Text to friend |
| **Serious ↔ Playful** | News anchor | Comedian |
| **Confident ↔ Humble** | Expert | Learner |
| **Direct ↔ Nuanced** | Blunt | Diplomatic |
| **Personal ↔ Impersonal** | "I" statements | Passive voice |

### Find Your Voice Exercise

**Step 1:** Write 3 emails on the same topic:
- One as formal as possible
- One as casual as possible
- One as you naturally write

**Step 2:** Ask 3 friends: "Which sounds most like me?"

**Step 3:** Double down on what they identify.

### Voice Consistency Checklist

Before publishing, ask:

- [ ] Does this sound like me?
- [ ] Would I say this out loud?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the topic?
- [ ] Am I being authentic or performing?

### Voice Examples

**Formal + Confident + Direct:**
> "The data is clear. Companies that invest in AI infrastructure outperform competitors by 34%. Here's how to build yours."

**Casual + Playful + Personal:**
> "Look, I messed this up big time. Cost me $12K and three weeks of my life. Let me save you the trouble."

**Balanced + Warm + Helpful:**
> "If you're feeling overwhelmed by AI tools, you're not alone. Here's what's actually worth your time (and what isn't)."

## 🧠 Section 5: Idea Generation System

### The Idea Capture System

**Tools:**
- Notes app on phone
- Voice memos
- Physical notebook
- Notion/Airtable database

**Capture triggers:**
- Reader questions
- Personal experiences
- Industry news
- Conversations
- Books/podcasts
- Mistakes/lessons

### The Content Calendar

**Minimum viable calendar:**
- 2 weeks scheduled
- 4 weeks outlined
- 8 weeks idea backlog

**Template:**

| Date | Type | Topic | Status |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| 3/25 | Deep Dive | AI automation guide | Published |
| 3/28 | Quick Hit | Tool of the week | Scheduled |
| 4/1 | Case Study | Client results | Writing |
| 4/4 | Q&A | Reader questions | Outlined |
| 4/8 | Deep Dive | [Topic] | Idea |

### Idea Generation Prompts

**When stuck, answer these:**

1. What question do readers ask me most?
2. What did I learn this week?
3. What's a common misconception in my field?
4. What tool/resource changed my workflow?
5. What's my contrarian take on [trend]?
6. What mistake do beginners always make?
7. What's working for me right now?
8. What would I tell my past self?
9. What's the next step after [previous post]?
10. What's coming in the next 30-90 days?

## ✂️ Section 6: Repurposing Framework

One piece of content → Multiple formats

### The Content Pyramid

```
           1 Deep Dive (2000+ words)
                  ↓
        2-3 Social posts (Twitter/LinkedIn)
                  ↓
           1 Substack Notes post
                  ↓
        1 Podcast episode (record yourself reading)
                  ↓
           5-10 Quote graphics
```

### Repurposing Checklist

After publishing a post:

- [ ] Share on Twitter/X (2-3 tweets)
- [ ] Share on LinkedIn (1 post)
- [ ] Post excerpt on Substack Notes
- [ ] Add to relevant communities (Reddit, FB groups)
- [ ] Quote tweet yourself with key insight
- [ ] Save best quotes for future graphics
- [ ] Consider recording audio version

| Metric | What It Tells You | Good Benchmark |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Open Rate** | Subject line + sender trust | 40%+ |
