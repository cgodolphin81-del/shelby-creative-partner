# Shelby VR Art Gallery - Creative Brief & Visual Design Guide

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Audience:** 3D Artists, Designers, Creative Team  

---

## CREATIVE VISION

### Core Concept

**"The Infinite Gallery exists between dream and reality—a space that could only exist in VR, where architecture defies physics, art comes alive, and every visit reveals something new."**

### Design Pillars

1. **Impossible Architecture** - Spaces that couldn't exist physically
2. **Living Light** - Lighting that responds, breathes, and enhances
3. **Seamless Journey** - Navigation that feels natural, not gamified
4. **Art as Experience** - Artworks aren't displayed, they're encountered
5. **Human Scale** - Despite grandeur, spaces feel intimate and welcoming

### Emotional Goals

| Emotion | Design Approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Wonder** | Unexpected spaces, scale shifts, hidden discoveries |
| **Calm** | Soft lighting, gentle transitions, quiet zones |
| **Curiosity** | Visual hints, partially hidden spaces, exploration rewards |
| **Connection** | Warm social spaces, eye-level interactions, shared moments |
| **Inspiration** | Behind-the-scenes visibility, creation spaces, artist presence |

## ARCHITECTURAL STYLE

### Overall Aesthetic

**"Contemporary Ethereal"** - Clean modern lines softened by organic elements, warm materials balanced with technological accents.

**Influences:**
- Tadao Ando (concrete, light, shadow)
- Zaha Hadid (fluid forms, impossible geometry)
- Olafur Eliasson (light as material, sensory experience)
- James Turrell (light and space manipulation)

### Material Palette

**Primary Materials:**

| Material | Usage | Color/Finish |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| Polished Concrete | Floors, structural walls | Warm gray, matte |
| White Oak | Accent walls, furniture | Natural, light stain |
| Brushed Bronze | Railings, frames, details | Warm metallic |
| Frosted Glass | Partitions, skylights | Translucent, soft diffusion |
| Living Greenery | Planters, vertical gardens | Various greens |

**Lighting as Material:**
- Warm white (2700K-3000K) for social spaces
- Neutral white (4000K) for gallery viewing
- Cool white (5000K+) for generative/digital art
- RGB accent lighting for special exhibitions

### The Helix Structure

**Exterior Concept:**
A spiraling tower that seems to float, with glass walls revealing glimpses of the galleries within. At night, the building glows from within, each level a different warm tone.

**Interior Flow:**
Visitors ascend via a central spiral ramp or glass elevator. Each level opens to a central atrium space, creating visual connection between floors.

                    │   LEVEL 7   │  ← The Apex (VIP)

## LEVEL-BY-LEVEL DESIGN

### LEVEL 1: The Atrium

**Mood:** Welcoming, grand, social

**Key Features:**
- 30m high ceiling with skylight simulation
- Curved reception desk (white oak + bronze)
- Central information hologram (subtle, not distracting)
- Comfortable seating areas (modern lounge furniture)
- Café corner (virtual coffee, social gathering)
- Living wall (vertical garden, 8m high)

**Lighting:**
- Natural skylight (dynamic, follows real time)
- Warm pendant lights over seating
- Subtle uplighting on living wall

**Color Palette:**
```
Primary: Warm White (#F5F5F0)
Secondary: Warm Gray (#C9C7C2)
Accent: Brushed Bronze (#B5946F)
Natural: Oak (#D4C5B0)
Green: Living Wall (#4A7C59)
```

**Reference Images:**
- The Broad Museum lobby (Los Angeles)
- Ace Hotel lobby (various locations)
- 1 Hotel lobbies (biophilic design)

### LEVEL 2: Main Gallery

**Mood:** Contemplative, focused, timeless

**Key Features:**
- 8 gallery rooms, each with distinct character
- Classic museum proportions (4.5m ceilings)
- Track lighting system (adjustable per artwork)
- Seating nooks between rooms
- Quiet zones for extended viewing

**Room Variations:**

| Room | Character | Wall Color | Lighting |
|------|-----------|------------|----------|
| Room 1 | Classic | Warm White (2700K) | Traditional track |
| Room 2 | Modern | Cool Gray (4000K) | Recessed spots |
| Room 3 | Intimate | Warm Beige (3000K) | Wall wash |
| Room 4 | Dramatic | Dark Gray (accent) | Focused spots |
| Room 5 | Bright | Pure White (5000K) | Natural simulation |
| Room 6 | Serene | Soft Blue tint (3500K) | Indirect lighting |
| Room 7 | Bold | Deep Green (accent) | Dramatic spots |
| Room 8 | Flexible | Variable | Programmable |

**Flooring:** Polished concrete with subtle aggregate

**Reference:** Gagosian Gallery, David Zwirner, Pace Gallery

### LEVEL 3: Generative Wing

**Mood:** Dynamic, futuristic, immersive

**Key Features:**
- Open 40m x 40m space (no walls)
- 12 large-format dynamic displays (4m x 3m each)
- "Creation Theater" - central circular stage
- Interactive floor (subtle reactive lighting)
- Ceiling-mounted projection capabilities

**Design Elements:**
- Darker environment (artworks are light sources)
- Minimalist black frames for displays
- Tech-forward materials (brushed aluminum, dark glass)
- LED strip accents (programmable RGB)

**Lighting:**
- Very low ambient (artworks provide most light)
- Programmable accent lighting
- Reactive to generative art (optional sync)

**Color Palette:**
```
Primary: Charcoal (#2B2B2B)
Secondary: Black (#1A1A1A)
Accent: RGB programmable
Metallic: Brushed Aluminum (#8C8C8C)
```

**Reference:** teamLab Borderless, ARTECHOUSE, OnXRT

---

### LEVEL 4: The Infinite Walk

**Mood:** Meditative, endless, surprising

**Key Features:**
- Procedurally generated corridor (never the same twice)
- Floating photograph frames (various sizes)
- Subtle floor lighting (guides forward movement)
- Ambient soundscape (nature, abstract, or silence)
- "Moments" - small alcoves with single featured photographs

**Corridor Design:**
- Width: 4m (intimate, not cramped)
- Ceiling: 3.5m (human scale)
- Wall texture: Smooth concrete with subtle variation
- Frame style: Minimalist black, appears to float

**Lighting:**
- Warm spotlighting on each photograph
- Soft floor glow (subtle navigation)
- Occasional "dawn/dusk" transitions

**Procedural Elements:**
- Corridor length varies (200m - 1000m effective)
- Photograph selection rotates
- Occasional architectural surprises (window views, alcoves)
- Sound zones change as you walk

**Reference:** James Turrell's skyspaces, Hiroshi Sugimoto's photography exhibitions

---

### LEVEL 5: Rotating Exhibitions

**Mood:** Fresh, curated, thematic

**Key Features:**
- 4 exhibition halls (configurable)
- Modular wall system (reconfigurable per exhibition)
- Thematic lighting presets
- Exhibition branding integration
- Catalog/merchandise corner per exhibition

**Modular System:**
- Wall panels: 3m wide x 4m tall
- Configurations: Grid, salon-style, linear, open
- Colors: White, gray, or exhibition-specific
- Quick-change capability (for turnover)

**Exhibition Branding:**
- Entrance signage (digital, changeable)
- Color scheme integration (subtle, not overwhelming)
- Thematic audio (optional, per exhibition)

**Reference:** New Museum (rotating shows), ICA London, The Shed (NYC)

### LEVEL 6: Artist Studios

## AVATAR DESIGN

### Style Direction

**"Approachable Contemporary"** - Avatars should feel like elevated versions of real people, not cartoonish or uncanny.

### Avatar Tiers

**Tier 1: Preset Avatars (50+ options)**
- Diverse representation (age, ethnicity, body type)
- Contemporary fashion (not trendy, timeless)
- Neutral expressions (friendly, approachable)
- Optimized for performance (<10K tris each)

**Tier 2: Customizable Avatars**
- Mix-and-match features
- Clothing options (casual, business, artistic)
- Accessories (glasses, jewelry, hats)
- Skin tones (inclusive range)
- Hair styles (diverse, realistic)

**Tier 3: AI-Generated Photorealistic**
- Upload selfie → AI generates 3D avatar
- Stylization options (realistic to slightly stylized)
- Expression capture (for facial animation)
- Premium feature (Collector tier+)

### Avatar Accessories

**Free:**
- Basic clothing variations
- Simple accessories

**Earnable:**
- Exhibition attendance badges
- Achievement rewards
- Community event items

**Purchasable:**
- Artist-designed clothing
- Limited edition accessories
- Exhibition commemorative items

**Reference:** VRChat (diversity), Meta Avatars (accessibility), Ready Player Me (customization)

## UI/UX DESIGN

### Design Principles

1. **Minimal Intrusion** - UI appears when needed, disappears when not
2. **VR-First** - Designed for VR interaction, not flat screen adaptation
3. **Accessible** - Readable, understandable, usable by all
4. **Consistent** - Same patterns throughout the experience
5. **Delightful** - Subtle animations, satisfying interactions

### UI Style

**Typography:**
- Primary: Inter (clean, readable, variable weight)
- Display: Playfair Display (for titles, exhibition names)
- Monospace: JetBrains Mono (for technical info)

**Color System:**
```
Primary: Gallery White (#F8F8F6)
Secondary: Concrete Gray (#8B8B86)
Accent: Bronze (#B5946F)
Success: Sage Green (#7D9D7B)
Warning: Warm Amber (#D4A574)
Error: Muted Red (#C97B7B)
Info: Soft Blue (#7B9DC9)

Dark Mode:
Background: Charcoal (#1E1E1E)
Text: Off-White (#E8E8E6)
```

**Iconography:**
- Line icons (2px stroke)
- Consistent visual weight
- Recognizable metaphors
- Accessible sizes (minimum 48px in VR)

### Key UI Elements

**Navigation Compass:**
- Minimal ring at periphery of vision
- Shows current level, direction to key areas
- Subtle glow when active
- Toggle on/off

**Artwork Placard:**
- Appears on gaze (0.5s) or approach
- Title, artist, year, medium
- "More info" button for extended description
- Audio narration button
- Purchase/collect button (if applicable)

**Tour Interface:**
- Progress indicator (subtle, peripheral)
- Next stop preview
- Leave tour option (always accessible)
- Group member indicators (for group tours)

**Social Indicators:**
- Name floating above avatars (toggleable)
- Speaking indicator (subtle ring when talking)
- Status icons (available, busy, in-tour)

**Reference:** Meta Quest Home UI, SteamVR Dashboard, Museum app interfaces

## AUDIO DESIGN

### Sonic Identity

**"Warm Minimalism"** - Audio should enhance, not overwhelm. Silence is a valid choice.

### Ambient Soundscapes

**Level 1 (Atrium):**
- Subtle background murmur (distant conversations)
- Soft café sounds (occasional cup clink, espresso machine)
- Gentle ambient music (piano, strings, minimal)
- Volume: 30-40%

**Level 2 (Main Gallery):**
- Near silence
- Very subtle room tone
- Footsteps (soft, on concrete)
- Volume: 10-20%

**Level 3 (Generative Wing):**
- Electronic ambient (generative, evolving)
- Syncs with artwork (optional)
- Subtle bass presence
- Volume: 40-50%

**Level 4 (Infinite Walk):**
- Nature sounds (birds, wind, water) OR
- Abstract soundscape (Brian Eno-style) OR
- Silence (user choice)
- Volume: 20-30%

**Level 5 (Exhibitions):**
- Exhibition-specific (curated per show)
- Often minimal or silence
- Volume: Variable

**Level 6 (Studios):**
- Creative sounds (occasional tool sounds, music)
- Varies by artist
- Volume: 30-40%

**Level 7 (Apex):**
- Sophisticated lounge music (jazz, downtempo)
- Cocktail ambiance (subtle)
- Volume: 30-40%

### Audio Narration

**Voice Talent:**
- Warm, engaging, not overly dramatic
- Multiple voice options (gender, accent)
- Professional narration quality
- Pacing: Moderate (not rushed)

**Narration Structure:**
```
0:00-0:10  - Title, artist, year
0:10-0:40  - Context, meaning, technique
0:40-1:10  - Artist's intent, interesting details
1:10-1:30  - Closing thought, what to notice
```

**Spatial Audio:**
- Narration follows user (not positional)
- Artwork ambient sounds are positional
- Voice chat is positional (proximity-based)

### Interactive Audio

**UI Sounds:**
- Subtle clicks (not gamey)
- Confirmation tones (pleasant, not intrusive)
- Error sounds (gentle, not alarming)

**Movement:**
- Footsteps (surface-dependent)
- Elevator/ramp ambience
- Teleport whoosh (optional, can disable)

**Reference:** Luxury hotel soundscapes, museum audio guides, Brian Eno ambient works

## LIGHTING DESIGN

### Philosophy

**"Light as Architecture"** - Lighting isn't just illumination; it's a design element that shapes space, mood, and experience.

### Lighting Layers

**Layer 1: Ambient**
- Base illumination for safety and navigation
- Warm, even, non-directional
- Dimmable based on time/activity

**Layer 2: Task**
- Artwork viewing illumination
- High CRI (95+) for color accuracy
- Adjustable per artwork

**Layer 3: Accent**
- Architectural features
- Wayfinding
- Mood enhancement

**Layer 4: Dynamic**
- Time-of-day simulation
- Event programming
- Artwork-responsive (generative wing)

### Lighting Specifications

**Gallery Viewing:**
```
Illuminance: 150-200 lux at artwork surface
Color Temperature: 3000K-4000K (artwork dependent)
CRI: 95+
Beam Angle: 24°-40° (adjustable)
Glare Control: UGR < 16
```

**Social Spaces:**
```
Illuminance: 100-150 lux
Color Temperature: 2700K-3000K
CRI: 90+
Quality: Warm, inviting, flattering
```

**Generative Wing:**
```
Illuminance: 50-100 lux (artworks are light sources)
Color Temperature: Variable (RGB)
Special: Programmable, artwork-sync capable
```

### Time-of-Day Simulation

**Morning (6am-12pm):**
- Cool, bright (5000K)
- Higher illuminance
- Crisp shadows

**Afternoon (12pm-6pm):**
- Neutral (4000K)
- Balanced lighting
- Soft shadows

**Evening (6pm-10pm):**
- Warm (3000K)
- Lower illuminance
- Intimate atmosphere

**Night (10pm-6am):**
- Very warm (2700K)
- Minimal ambient
- Accent-focused

## ACCESSIBILITY DESIGN

### Visual Accessibility

**For Low Vision:**
- High contrast mode (toggle)
- Large text option (1.5x, 2x)
- Audio descriptions for all artworks
- Haptic feedback on interactions
- Guide rail option (visible path)

**For Color Blindness:**
- Colorblind-friendly UI modes (Deuteranopia, Protanopia, Tritanopia)
- Never use color alone to convey information
- Patterns/icons as secondary indicators

### Mobility Accessibility

**Seated Experience:**
- All areas accessible while seated
- Seated height presets (0.8m, 1.0m, 1.2m)
- Reachability considerations (UI within arm's reach)
- Teleport navigation (no smooth locomotion required)

**Limited Movement:**
- Gaze-based interaction option
- Voice command support (future)
- Single-controller mode
- Dwell-time activation (gaze + hold)

### Auditory Accessibility

**For Hearing Impairment:**
- All audio content captioned
- Visual indicators for audio cues
- Sign language tour option (future, video overlay)
- Haptic feedback alternatives

### Cognitive Accessibility

**Simplified Mode:**
- Reduced visual complexity
- Clear, direct navigation
- Fewer simultaneous stimuli
- Extended time for interactions

**Sensory Considerations:**
- Reduced motion mode
- No unexpected jumps/scares
- Quiet zones available
- Strobe/flashing warnings

## BRAND EXPRESSION

### Logo Usage

**Primary Logo:**
- Clean, minimal wordmark
- "The Infinite Gallery"
- Bronze or white depending on background

**Icon:**
- Abstract infinity symbol + gallery column
- Used for app icons, favicons, social

**Placement:**
- Entrance (Atrium, subtle)
- Loading screens
- Tickets/receipts
- Social media

### Tone of Voice

**In-World Text:**
- Warm but professional
- Not overly casual, not stiff
- Inclusive language
- Clear and concise

**Examples:**
```
✓ "Welcome to The Infinite Gallery"
✗ "Hey there! Welcome!!!"

✓ "This artwork is part of the Emerging Dreams series"
✗ "This piece is super cool and part of our awesome collection"

✓ "Would you like to join the guided tour?"
✗ "OMG you HAVE to take this tour!"
```

### Photography Style

**For Marketing:**
- Wide shots showing impossible architecture
- Close-ups of art + frame details
- Visitors (avatars) in context
- Golden hour lighting (warm, inviting)

**Avoid:**
- Overcrowded scenes
- Poorly lit artwork
- Glitchy/low-quality renders
- Generic stock photography
