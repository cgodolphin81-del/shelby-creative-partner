# PRICING STRATEGY MASTER GUIDE

## The Pricing Psychology Framework

### Price Anchoring Principles

**The Decoy Effect:**
```
BASIC:    $2,500/month   (limited features)
PRO:      $7,500/month   (best value) ← TARGET
ELITE:    $25,000/month  (everything + VIP) ← Anchor
```

Most will choose PRO because it feels "reasonable" compared to ELITE.

**The Rule of Three:**
People naturally compare three options. Always present three tiers.

**Price Ending Psychology:**
- $7,000 feels more premium than $6,997
- $6,997 feels like a "deal" 
- $7,500 feels confident and round
- Use $97 endings for entry-level, round numbers for premium

# VALUE-BASED PRICING FRAMEWORK

## The Value Calculation Formula

```
STEP 1: Quantify the Problem

What is this problem costing them?
• Lost revenue: $_______ / year
• Wasted time: $_______ / year (hours × hourly rate)
• Emotional cost: $_______ (stress, relationships, health)
• Opportunity cost: $_______ (missed opportunities)

TOTAL COST OF INACTION: $_______ / year

-------------------------------------------

STEP 2: Quantify the Solution

What is the solution worth?
• Revenue increase: $_______ / year
• Time savings: $_______ / year
• Emotional value: $_______ (peace of mind, confidence)
• Opportunity gain: $_______ (new opportunities unlocked)

TOTAL VALUE OF SOLUTION: $_______ / year

-------------------------------------------

STEP 3: Price as Percentage of Value

Industry standard: 10-30% of first-year value

If solution is worth $100,000 in year 1:
• 10% = $10,000 (conservative)
• 20% = $20,000 (standard)
• 30% = $30,000 (premium/confident)

YOUR PRICE: $_______
```

---

## Value-Based Pricing Script

```
YOU: "Let me ask you something. You mentioned this problem 
is costing you about $50,000 per year in lost revenue. Is 
that right?"

CLIENT: "Yes, about that."

YOU: "And if we could solve this in the next 90 days, what 
would that be worth to you?"

CLIENT: [They'll say something like $100K-$200K]

YOU: "Okay, so solving this is worth at least $150,000 to 
you in the next year. 

My program is $15,000. That's 10% of the value you'll get.

Put another way: if this program pays for itself 10x in 
the first year, does the price feel different?"

CLIENT: [Usually yes]

YOU: "The real question isn't whether you can afford the 
investment. It's whether you can afford NOT to solve this."
```

# PAYMENT PLAN STRUCTURES

## Standard Payment Plans

### 3-Month Plan (Most Common)
```
Total Price: $12,000
Pay in Full: $12,000 (save $1,000)
Payment Plan: 3 payments of $4,333

Benefit: Makes high-ticket accessible
Risk: Slightly higher default rate
```

### 6-Month Plan (For $20K+ Programs)
```
Total Price: $24,000
Pay in Full: $24,000 (save $2,000)
Payment Plan: 6 payments of $4,333

Benefit: Very accessible, higher close rate
Risk: Longer exposure, more touchpoints
```

### 12-Month Plan (For $50K+ Programs)
```
Total Price: $60,000
Pay in Full: $60,000 (save $5,000)
Payment Plan: 12 payments of $5,417

Benefit: Makes elite programs accessible
Risk: Highest default risk, requires contract
```

---

## Payment Plan Best Practices

### DO:
✓ Run credit check for 6+ month plans (for $25K+)
✓ Require auto-pay (Stripe, PayPal)
✓ Include late fee clause ($50-100 after 5 days)
✓ State consequences of non-payment (pause services)
✓ Get signed agreement before starting

### DON'T:
✗ Accept manual payments (checks, Venmo) for plans
✗ Continue services without payment
✗ Feel bad about enforcing terms
✗ Skip the contract

---

## Payment Plan Script

```
YOU: "The investment is $15,000. Most clients pay in full, 
but we do offer a payment plan if that works better.

Pay in full: $15,000 (save $1,500)
Payment plan: 3 monthly payments of $5,500

Both options get you the exact same program and support. 
The pay-in-full option just saves you $1,500.

Which works better for you?"
```

# PRICE INCREASE STRATEGY

## When to Raise Prices

### Raise prices when:
✓ You're at 80%+ capacity consistently
✓ You have a waiting list
✓ You've delivered 10+ successful transformations
✓ Your close rate is 50%+ on discovery calls
✓ You feel underpriced (trust this feeling)

### How often:
- Entry-level coaches: Every 3-6 months
- Established coaches: Every 6-12 months
- Premium coaches: Every 12-18 months

---

## How to Raise Prices

### For New Clients:
```
Simple: Just update your website and materials.
No announcement needed. New clients pay new prices.
```

### For Existing Clients:

[Program Name].

# DISCOUNT STRATEGY

## When Discounts Are Okay

### ✅ Appropriate Times:
- Early bird for new program launches (10-20% off, limited time)
- Beta testing with first 3-5 clients (30-50% off, in exchange for testimonials)
- Payment in full incentive (5-10% off for skipping payment plan)
- Special circumstances (hardship, case-by-case)

### ❌ Never Discount:
- Because they asked (devalues your work)
- At the end of a sales call (looks desperate)
- Regularly (trains clients to wait for deals)
- Publicly on social media (attracts bargain hunters)

---

## Discount Scripts

### Early Bird Discount:
```
"We're launching the new cohort on [date]. For the next 
48 hours, I'm offering an early bird rate of $X,XXX 
(regular price $Y,YYY).

After [date], the price goes back to regular. Want to 
lock in the early bird rate?"
```

### Pay-in-Full Discount:
```
"The program is $12,000, or 3 payments of $4,333.

If you pay in full, I can do $11,000—saves you $1,000.

Which works better for you?"
```

### Beta Pricing:
```
"I'm launching a new program and looking for 3 beta 
clients to go through it at a reduced rate in exchange 
for honest feedback and a testimonial.

The regular price will be $X,XXX, but for beta clients, 
it's $Y,YYY.

Interested?"
```
