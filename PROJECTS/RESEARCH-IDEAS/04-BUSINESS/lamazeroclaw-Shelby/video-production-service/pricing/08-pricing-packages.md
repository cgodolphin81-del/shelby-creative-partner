## Pricing Philosophy

### Value-Based Pricing

**Don't sell hours. Sell outcomes.**

```
❌ WRONG: "We charge $150/hour for editing"
✅ RIGHT: "This video will generate $50K in sales"
```

**Price Based On:**
- Client's potential ROI
- Video's business impact
- Your expertise and results
- Market rates in your area
- Complexity and timeline

### Pricing Psychology

**Anchoring:**
- Show Premium package first
- Makes Professional look reasonable
- Basic becomes the "budget" option

**Decoy Effect:**
- Three tiers work better than two
- Middle option is most popular
- Price gaps should be meaningful

**Specific Numbers:**
- $4,850 feels more calculated than $5,000
- But round numbers work for premium packages
- Test what converts better for your market

### When to Adjust Pricing

**Increase Prices When:**
- Consistently booked 2+ months out
- Getting pushback on price (too low)
- Adding new capabilities
- Have strong portfolio/results
- Inflation/cost increases

**Decrease/Discount When:**
- Building portfolio (early career)
- Non-profit/charity work
- Long-term contract commitment
- Referral from ideal client
- Off-season slowdown

## Basic Package ($2K-5K)

### 📦 What's Included

**Pre-Production:**
- 1-hour discovery call
- Basic script development (up to 2 pages)
- Simple storyboard (6-8 frames)
- Location scouting (local, 1 location)
- Talent coordination (1 person)

**Production:**
- Half-day shoot (up to 4 hours)
- 1 camera operator
- Basic lighting setup
- Lav microphone audio
- Client provides location

**Post-Production:**
- Professional editing
- Basic color correction
- Audio mixing
- Simple motion graphics (intro/outro)
- Background music (licensed)
- 2 revision rounds

**Deliverables:**
- 1 final video (up to 2 minutes)
- Web-optimized file (MP4, H.264)
- 1 social media cut (30 seconds)
- Basic thumbnail

**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks

## Payment Terms

### Standard Terms

**Deposit:** 50% due upon signing
- Secures production dates
- Covers pre-production costs
- Non-refundable after shoot begins

**Second Payment:** 25% due at rough cut approval
- Before fine-tuning begins
- Ensures continued work

**Final Payment:** 25% due before final delivery
- All files released after payment
- Watermarked preview available

### Alternative Payment Structures

**For Large Projects ($25K+):**
```
30% - Deposit
25% - After production wrap
25% - After rough cut
20% - Before final delivery
```

**For Retainer Clients:**
```
Monthly flat fee
Pre-negotiated rates
Priority scheduling
Dedicated team
```

**For Startups (Equity Deals):**
- Reduced cash rate + equity
- Requires legal agreement
- Only for high-potential companies
- Clear vesting schedule

### Late Payment Policy

- 5% late fee after 15 days
- 10% late fee after 30 days
- Work stops on overdue accounts
- Final files held until paid
- Legal action for 60+ days overdue

### Cancellation Policy

**Client Cancels:**
- Before pre-production: Deposit refunded (minus $500 admin fee)
- After pre-production: Deposit non-refundable
- After shoot begins: 75% of total due
- After rough cut: 100% of total due

**Production Company Cancels:**
- Full refund of all payments
- Assistance finding replacement
- Reputation protection priority

## Handling Objections

### "It's Too Expensive"

**Response Options:**

1. **Value Reframe:**
```
"I understand budget is a concern. Let's talk about 
what this video will generate for you. If this video 
helps you close just 2 additional clients at $10K each, 
it's already paid for itself 5x over."
```

2. **Package Adjustment:**
```
"We can adjust the scope to fit your budget. What if 
we reduced the shoot to one location and simplified 
the graphics? That would bring it down to $X."
```

3. **Payment Plan:**
```
"We can split payments over 3-6 months to ease cash 
flow. The total stays the same, but it's more manageable."
```

4. **ROI Focus:**
```
"What would it cost you NOT to have this video? 
Lost leads? Confused customers? Your competitors 
winning deals? Sometimes the expensive option is 
not investing."
```

---

### "I Can Get It Cheaper Elsewhere"

**Response:**
```
"You absolutely can. There are people who will do 
this for $500. The question is: will it achieve 
your business goals?

Our videos are an investment, not an expense. We 
focus on results—videos that convert viewers into 
customers. What's more valuable: saving $3,000 
today, or generating $30,000 in revenue this year?

Happy to provide references from clients who've 
seen real ROI from their videos."
```

---

### "We Need to Think About It"

**Response:**
```
"Of course, this is an important decision. What 
specifically do you need to think through?

[Listen to concern]

I want to be transparent—our calendar fills up 
about 6 weeks in advance. If you're looking at 
a [specific date] delivery, we'd need to get 
started by [date].

What would help you feel confident moving forward?"
```

---

### "Can You Give Us a Discount?"

**Response:**
```
"Our pricing is based on the value we deliver and 
the costs to produce quality work. That said, there 
are a few ways we can work with your budget:

1. Adjust the scope (fewer locations, shorter video)
2. Extended timeline (we can offer better rates)
3. Package deal (multiple videos at once)
4. Payment plan (spread cost over time)

What works best for your situation?"
```

**If They Push:**
```
"I can offer 5% off for upfront payment in full, 
or 10% off if you commit to 3 videos. Beyond that, 
we'd be cutting corners that would hurt the final 
product—and that doesn't serve either of us."
```

---

### "We'll Do It In-House"

**Response:**
```
"That's a valid option! Many companies start there. 
The trade-offs to consider:

• Time: Your team's time has value—what else could 
  they be working on?
• Quality: Professional results require professional 
  equipment and expertise
• Consistency: We deliver on deadline, every time
• Results: Our videos average X% conversion rate

If you'd like, we can do a hybrid approach—your 
team handles [X], we handle the heavy lifting on 
[Y]. That could reduce costs while maintaining quality."
```

## Price Positioning

### How to Present Pricing

**DO:**
- Present with confidence
- Lead with value, not price
- Show packages side-by-side
- Explain what's included
- Share success stories
- Offer payment options

**DON'T:**
- Apologize for pricing
- Lead with the cheapest option
- Hide fees or surprises
- Compete on price alone
- Undervalue your work
- Accept unreasonable demands
