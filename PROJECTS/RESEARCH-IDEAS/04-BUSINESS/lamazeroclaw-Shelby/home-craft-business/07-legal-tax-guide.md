## Business Structure

### Sole Proprietorship

**What it is:** You AND the business are the same legal entity

**Pros:**
- ✅ Easiest to set up (often no filing required)
- ✅ Least expensive
- ✅ Simple taxes (report on personal return)
- ✅ Complete control

**Cons:**
- ❌ No liability protection (personal assets at risk)
- ❌ Harder to get business credit
- ❌ Self-employment tax on all income

**Best for:** Hobbyists, very small businesses, testing ideas

**How to start:**
- Often no filing required
- May need DBA ("Doing Business As") if using business name
- Get EIN (free from IRS)

### LLC (Limited Liability Company)

**What it is:** Separate legal entity from you

**Pros:**
- ✅ Liability protection (personal assets protected)
- ✅ Tax flexibility (can choose how to be taxed)
- ✅ More professional appearance
- ✅ Easier to get business bank account/credit

**Cons:**
- ❌ Filing fees ($50-500 depending on state)
- ❌ Annual reports/fees in most states
- ❌ More paperwork

**Best for:** Serious businesses, anything with liability risk

**How to start:**
1. Choose business name
2. File Articles of Organization with state
3. Pay filing fee
4. Get EIN from IRS
5. Create operating agreement
6. Open business bank account

**Cost:** $50-500 initial + $0-800/year (varies by state)

### S-Corp

**What it is:** Tax election for LLCs or corporations

**Pros:**
- ✅ Potential tax savings at higher incomes
- ✅ Liability protection
- ✅ Can reduce self-employment tax

**Cons:**
- ❌ More complex
- ❌ Payroll requirements
- ❌ More expensive to maintain

**Best for:** Businesses making $60,000+ net profit

**Note:** This is a TAX election, not a business structure. You form an LLC, then elect S-Corp taxation.

### Corporation (C-Corp)

**What it is:** Separate legal entity, can sell stock

**Pros:**
- ✅ Strongest liability protection
- ✅ Can raise money through investors
- ✅ Perpetual existence

**Cons:**
- ❌ Double taxation (corporate + dividend)
- ❌ Most expensive to maintain
- ❌ Most complex

**Best for:** Businesses planning to raise investment, go public

**For most craft businesses:** Start with Sole Prop or LLC. Upgrade later if needed.

## Business Registration

### Federal Level (US)

**EIN (Employer Identification Number):**
- Free from IRS
- Like a Social Security Number for your business
- Required for: LLCs, corporations, businesses with employees
- Recommended for: All businesses (use instead of SSN)

**How to get:** IRS.gov (free, takes 10 minutes)

**Trademark (optional but recommended):**
- Protects your business name/logo
- File with USPTO
- Cost: $250-350 per class
- Consider if you're serious about your brand

### State Level

**Business Registration:**
- LLCs and corporations must file with state
- Sole props may need to file DBA
- Cost: $50-500 depending on state

**Sales Tax Permit:**
- Required if selling taxable goods
- Register with state tax agency
- Collect and remit sales tax
- Cost: Usually free to register

**State Taxes:**
- Income tax (in most states)
- Franchise tax (some states)
- Annual report fees (most states for LLCs)

### Local Level

**City/County Business License:**
- Required in most cities
- Cost: $50-200/year typically
- Check with your city clerk

**Home Occupation Permit:**
- Required if operating from home
- Some cities restrict home businesses
- May have limits on signage, traffic, employees

**Zoning:**
- Check if your area allows home businesses
- Most residential areas allow low-impact home businesses
- Restrictions on: signage, customer visits, employees, noise

### Checklist

- [ ] Choose business structure
- [ ] Register business name (DBA if needed)
- [ ] Get EIN from IRS
- [ ] Register for state taxes
- [ ] Get sales tax permit
- [ ] Get city business license
- [ ] Check home occupation requirements
- [ ] Open business bank account

## Licenses & Permits

### General Business License

**Required:** Most cities and counties

**What it does:** Registers your business locally

**Cost:** $50-200/year

**How to get:** City hall or county clerk

### Sales Tax Permit / Seller's Permit

**Required:** If selling taxable goods (most physical products)

**What it does:** Allows you to collect sales tax

**Cost:** Free to register (but you must collect and remit)

**How to get:** State tax agency website

**Important:**
- Collect tax from customers in your state
- Some states require collecting for other states (economic nexus)
- File returns monthly, quarterly, or annually
- Keep records of all sales

### Home Occupation Permit

**Required:** If operating from home (in many cities)

**What it does:** Allows you to run business from residence

**Cost:** $0-100

**Restrictions may include:**
- No signage
- No customer visits
- No employees (other than household members)
- Limited square footage
- Limited storage

### Special Permits by Product Type

**Food/Cosmetics:**
- Cottage food laws (varies by state)
- Kitchen inspection may be required
- Labeling requirements
- FDA registration for some products

**Candles/Soaps:**
- Generally no special permits
- Follow safety guidelines
- Proper labeling (ingredients, warnings)

**Jewelry:**
- Precious metal dealers license (some states, if dealing in gold/silver)
- Generally no special permits

**Children's Products:**
- CPSIA compliance (Consumer Product Safety Improvement Act)
- Testing requirements
- Tracking labels
- More stringent for items for children under 12

**Check with:**
- State health department (for food/cosmetics)
- Consumer Product Safety Commission (for children's items)
- State agriculture department (for some products)

## Taxes

### Federal Taxes (US)

**Income Tax:**
- Report business income on personal return (sole prop/LLC)
- Schedule C (Form 1040)
- Pay tax on net profit

**Self-Employment Tax:**
- 15.3% of net profit
- Covers Social Security and Medicare
- Paid in addition to income tax
- Can deduct half of SE tax

**Estimated Taxes:**
- Pay quarterly if you expect to owe $1,000+
- Due: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Form 1040-ES
- Avoids underpayment penalty

### State Taxes

**Income Tax:**
- Most states have income tax
- File state return
- Rates vary by state

**Sales Tax:**
- Collect from customers
- Remit to state
- Frequency depends on volume
- Keep detailed records

**Franchise Tax:**
- Some states charge annual fee for LLCs/corps
- Varies by state

### Local Taxes

**Business tax:** Some cities tax businesses
**Property tax:** On business equipment (sometimes)

### Tax Deductions for Craft Businesses

**Common deductions:**

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Materials** | All supplies used to make products |
| **Packaging** | Boxes, tape, tissue, labels |
| **Shipping** | Postage, shipping software |
| **Platform fees** | Etsy fees, Shopify, etc. |
| **Home office** | Portion of rent, utilities, internet |
| **Equipment** | Tools, machinery, computer |
| **Vehicle** | Mileage for business trips |
| **Marketing** | Ads, business cards, website |
| **Professional services** | Lawyer, accountant, bookkeeper |
| **Insurance** | Business insurance premiums |
| **Education** | Courses, books, conferences |
| **Bank fees** | Business account fees |
| **Phone/internet** | Business portion |

**Home Office Deduction:**
- Must be exclusive and regular use
- Simplified method: $5/sq ft (max 300 sq ft = $1,500)
- Regular method: Percentage of actual expenses
- Can't deduct more than business income

**Record keeping:**
- Keep receipts for all business expenses
- Use accounting software or spreadsheet
- Separate business and personal accounts
- Track mileage for business trips

### Quarterly Estimated Taxes

**Who needs to pay:**
- Expect to owe $1,000+ in federal tax
- No withholding (no W-2 job)

**How to calculate:**
1. Estimate annual income
2. Estimate expenses
3. Calculate net profit
4. Calculate tax (income + SE tax)
5. Divide by 4

**Safe harbor:**
- Pay 100% of last year's tax (110% if AGI over $150K)
- Avoids underpayment penalty

**Payment options:**
- Online at IRS.gov
- By mail with voucher
- Phone

## Insurance

### Why You Need Insurance

- Protects personal assets
- Covers accidents and injuries
- Required by some platforms/markets
- Professional appearance
- Peace of mind

### Types of Insurance

**General Liability Insurance:**
- **Covers:** Third-party bodily injury, property damage
- **Example:** Customer slips at craft fair, product damages customer's property
- **Cost:** $300-800/year
- **Recommended for:** All businesses

**Product Liability Insurance:**
- **Covers:** Injury or damage caused by your product
- **Example:** Candle causes fire, jewelry causes allergic reaction
- **Cost:** Often included in general liability
- **Recommended for:** All product businesses

**Homeowners/Renters Insurance:**
- **Covers:** Personal property, liability at home
- **Limitation:** May NOT cover business equipment or liability
- **Action:** Call your insurer, add business rider if needed
- **Cost:** $50-150/year rider

**Business Personal Property:**
- **Covers:** Business equipment, inventory, supplies
- **Example:** Fire destroys your supplies
- **Cost:** $200-500/year
- **Recommended for:** Businesses with significant inventory/equipment

**Business Interruption:**
- **Covers:** Lost income if you can't operate
- **Example:** Fire forces you to close temporarily
- **Cost:** Varies
- **Recommended for:** Full-time businesses

**Workers' Compensation:**
- **Required:** If you have employees
- **Covers:** Employee injuries
- **Cost:** Varies by state and risk
- **Not needed:** If you're solo

### Insurance Providers for Craft Businesses

**Specialized in handmade/small business:**
- Craft Industry Alliance (members get discounts)
- Hiscox
- Next Insurance
- Thimble (on-demand, pay-per-event)
- Philadelphia Insurance Companies

**For craft fairs:**
- Thimble (single event coverage)
- Hiscox (event coverage)
- Cost: $10-50 per event

### How Much Coverage?

**Minimum recommended:**
- General Liability: $1 million per occurrence
- Product Liability: $1 million per occurrence

**Craft fair requirement:**
- Often $1-2 million required
- Some fairs require certificate of insurance

## Intellectual Property

### Trademark

**What it protects:** Business name, logo, brand names

**Why register:**
- Legal presumption of ownership
- Can use ® symbol
- Can sue in federal court
- Deters others from using similar marks

**Process:**
1. Search existing trademarks (USPTO.gov)
2. File application ($250-350 per class)
3. Wait 6-12 months for approval
4. Maintain registration (file between years 5-6, renew every 10 years)

**When to trademark:**
- You're serious about your brand
- You've invested in branding
- Someone else is using similar name
- You plan to scale

### Copyright

**What it protects:** Original creative works (art, writing, photos, designs)

**Automatic:** Copyright exists when you create the work

**Registration benefits:**
- Can sue for infringement
- Statutory damages available
- Public record

**Process:**
- File with Copyright Office
- Cost: $45-65
- Online at copyright.gov

**For crafters:**
- Your original designs are automatically copyrighted
- Registration strengthens protection
- Photos of your products are copyrighted

### Patent

**What it protects:** Inventions, processes, functional designs

**Types:**
- Utility patent (how something works)
- Design patent (how something looks)

**For crafters:** Rarely needed unless you've invented something truly unique

**Cost:** $5,000-15,000+ with attorney

**Process:** Complex, requires attorney

### Don't Infringe on Others

**Avoid:**
- Using trademarked names (Disney, NFL, etc.)
- Copying others' designs
- Using copyrighted images without permission
- Making fan art for sale (technically infringement)

**Safe:**
- Your original designs
- Generic terms and shapes
- Public domain works
- Properly licensed assets

## Product Liability

### What Is Product Liability?

Legal responsibility if your product causes:
- Injury
- Property damage
- Illness

### Reduce Your Risk

**1. Use safe materials:**
- Non-toxic, tested materials
- Supplier documentation
- Avoid known allergens or warn clearly

**2. Follow safety guidelines:**
- Candle safety (wick size, container)
- Cosmetic guidelines (preservatives, pH)
- Children's product safety (if applicable)

**3. Test your products:**
- Burn testing (candles)
- Stability testing (cosmetics)
- Durability testing (jewelry, etc.)

**4. Label properly:**
- Ingredients
- Warnings
- Instructions for safe use
- Age recommendations

**5. Keep records:**
- Materials used
- Batch numbers
- Testing results
- Customer complaints

### Warning Labels

**Examples:**

**Candles:**
```
⚠️ BURN SAFELY
- Never leave burning candle unattended
- Keep away from children and pets
- Trim wick to 1/4" before each use
- Burn on heat-resistant surface
- Discontinue use when 1/2" of wax remains
```

**Jewelry:**
```
⚠️ CHOKING HAZARD - Small parts
Not for children under 3 years
Contains: [materials]
Remove before sleeping, bathing, or exercising
```

**Cosmetics:**
```
For external use only
Avoid contact with eyes
Discontinue if irritation occurs
Keep out of reach of children
Ingredients: [list]
```

## Terms & Policies

### Terms of Service

**What it covers:**
- How customers can use your website/products
- Limitations of liability
- Dispute resolution
- Governing law

**For Etsy sellers:** Etsy's Terms apply, but you can add your own policies

**For website:** Need your own Terms of Service

### Privacy Policy

**Required if:**
- You collect any personal information
- You have a website (even just email collection)
- You're in certain jurisdictions (California, EU)

**What it covers:**
- What data you collect
- How you use it
- How you protect it
- Customer rights

**GDPR (EU):**
- Applies if you sell to EU customers
- Requires explicit consent
- Right to be forgotten
- Data portability

**CCPA (California):**
- Similar to GDPR
- California residents have additional rights

### Return/Refund Policy

**Etsy requirement:** You must have one

**Include:**
- Timeframe for returns
- Condition requirements
- Who pays return shipping
- Refund method and timing
- Exceptions (custom items, etc.)

**Sample:**
```
Returns & Exchanges

I want you to love your purchase! I accept returns 
within 14 days of delivery in original condition.

To return: Message me within 14 days. I'll provide 
return instructions.

Return shipping: Buyer pays (unless item is defective)
Refund: Issued within 3 days of receiving return

Custom/personalized items: Final sale (unless damaged)
Sale items: Final sale
```

### Shipping Policy

**Include:**
- Processing time
- Shipping methods
- Delivery estimates
- Shipping costs
- International shipping (if applicable)
- What happens if package is lost/damaged

**Sample:**
```
Shipping Policy

Processing time: 1-3 business days
Shipping: USPS Priority Mail (2-3 days)
Free shipping on orders over $50

International: Available to select countries (10-21 days)
Lost/damaged packages: Contact me within 7 days of 
expected delivery. I'll work with you to resolve!
```

## Record Keeping

### What to Track

**Income:**
- All sales (date, amount, platform, item)
- Refunds issued
- 1099-K forms (from platforms)

**Expenses:**
- All business purchases
- Receipts (digital or physical)
- Mileage (date, purpose, miles)
- Home office (square footage, expenses)

**Inventory:**
- Materials on hand
- Finished goods
- Cost of goods sold (COGS)

**Taxes:**
- Sales tax collected
- Sales tax remitted
- Estimated tax payments
- Deductions taken

### How Long to Keep Records

**IRS requirements:**
- Tax returns: 7 years (3 years minimum, 6 if underreported, 7 if loss from bad debt)
- Employment records: 4 years
- Asset records: Until asset is disposed of + 3 years

**Best practice:** Keep everything 7 years minimum

### Tools

**Spreadsheets:**
- Free (Google Sheets, Excel)
- Good for starting out
- Manual entry

**Accounting Software:**

| Software | Cost | Best For |
|----------|------|----------|
| Wave | Free | Very small businesses |
| QuickBooks Self-Employed | $15/month | Sole props, simple |
| QuickBooks Online | $25-80/month | Growing businesses |
| Xero | $25-60/month | Similar to QuickBooks |
| FreshBooks | $15-50/month | Service-based, simple |

**Receipt tracking:**
- Expensify
- Receipt Bank
- Just take photos and organize in Google Drive

### Bank Accounts

**Separate business account:**
- Required for LLCs/corps
- Highly recommended for sole props
- Makes taxes much easier
- Protects personal finances

**Business credit card:**
- Separate business expenses
- Build business credit
- Rewards on business purchases
- Don't mix personal and business!

## When to Hire Professionals

### Business Attorney

**When to hire:**
- Forming LLC or corporation
- Trademark registration
- Contract review
- Someone infringes on your IP
- You're being sued
- Complex business structure

**Cost:** $200-500/hour, or flat fee for specific services

### CPA / Tax Professional

**When to hire:**
- First year in business (get set up right)
- Complex tax situation
- Making $50,000+ net profit
- Considering S-Corp election
- Audit notice
- Behind on taxes

**Cost:** $200-500/hour, or $500-2000 for tax return

### Insurance Agent

**When to hire:**
- First business insurance policy
- Expanding product line
- Adding employees
- Selling at craft fairs (need event coverage)

**Cost:** Often commission-based (no direct cost to you)

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Mixing personal and business finances
**Fix:** Open separate business accounts

### ❌ Not tracking expenses
**Fix:** Use accounting software or spreadsheet from day 1

### ❌ Not collecting sales tax
**Fix:** Register and start collecting immediately

### ❌ Operating without insurance
**Fix:** Get liability insurance (it's affordable!)

### ❌ Copying others' designs
**Fix:** Create original work, get inspired but don't copy

### ❌ Not having policies
**Fix:** Write clear policies and post them

### ❌ Ignoring legal requirements
**Fix:** Research your local requirements, ask professionals
