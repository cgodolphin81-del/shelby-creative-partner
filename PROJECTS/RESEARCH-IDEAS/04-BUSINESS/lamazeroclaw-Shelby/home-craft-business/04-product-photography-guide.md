## Why Photos Matter

**On Etsy, your photos ARE your product.**

- Buyers can't touch, smell, or try your items
- First impression happens in search results (tiny thumbnail!)
- Good photos = higher click-through = more sales
- Good photos justify higher prices
- Bad photos = no sales, even with great products

**Etsy's algorithm favors:**
- Clear, well-lit photos
- Multiple photos per listing
- Photos that get clicks and favorites

## Lighting Setup

### Natural Light (Best for Beginners)

**Setup:**
1. Find a large window with indirect light
2. Avoid direct sunlight (creates harsh shadows)
3. Place a white foam board opposite the window to bounce light
4. Shoot during the day (10am-3pm is ideal)

```
        WINDOW
          ↓
    ┌─────────────┐
    │   Product   │ ← White foam board (reflector)
    └─────────────┘
          ↑
      Camera
```

**Tips:**
- North-facing windows give consistent, soft light
- Use a sheer curtain to diffuse harsh light
- Turn off overhead lights (creates color cast)
- Cloudy days = perfect diffused light

### Artificial Light

**When you need it:**
- Shooting at night
- Inconsistent natural light
- High-volume shooting

**Budget Setup:**
- 2 LED panels or clamp lights: $40-60
- White foam board reflector: $5
- Total: ~$50

```
    Light 1 (45°)     Light 2 (45°)
         \               /
          \             /
           ↓           ↓
    ┌─────────────────────┐
    │      Product        │
    └─────────────────────┘
              ↑
           Camera
```

**Tips:**
- Use daylight bulbs (5000-5500K color temperature)
- Position lights at 45° angles to product
- Diffuse with white fabric or parchment paper
- Avoid yellow/orange household bulbs

### Lightbox (Photo Tent)

**Pros:**
- Consistent results
- No shadows
- Compact setup
- Great for small items (jewelry, candles)

**Cons:**
- Can look flat without proper styling
- Size limitations

**Setup:**
1. Place lightbox near window or use included lights
2. Choose backdrop (white, gray, or colored)
3. Position product in center
4. Shoot through the opening

## Backgrounds

### White Background (Etsy Standard)

**Best for:** First photo, clean professional look

**How to achieve:**
- White foam board ($5)
- White poster board ($3)
- White seamless paper ($20-40)
- White fabric (ironed smooth)
- Lightbox with white backdrop

**Tips:**
- Create a "sweep" (curve from vertical to horizontal)
- Eliminates horizon line
- Makes editing easier

### Colored Backgrounds

**Best for:** Lifestyle shots, brand consistency

**Options:**
- Colored paper ($5-10)
- Fabric ($10-20)
- Painted wood boards (DIY)
- Marble contact paper ($15)
- Textured wallpaper samples (free!)

**Color psychology:**
- White: Clean, minimal, premium
- Black: Luxury, dramatic
- Gray: Neutral, modern
- Pastels: Soft, feminine
- Earth tones: Natural, organic
- Bold colors: Fun, energetic

### Textured Backgrounds

**Best for:** Adding depth and interest

**Ideas:**
- Wood grain (cutting boards, planks)
- Marble or stone
- Fabric (linen, velvet, burlap)
- Concrete (planters, tiles)
- Plants and greenery
- Props related to your product

**Tips:**
- Keep textures subtle (don't distract from product)
- Ensure product is still the focus
- Match texture to brand aesthetic

## Styling & Composition

### Rule of Thirds

Divide your frame into 9 equal sections:

```
┌─────┬─────┬─────┐
│  •  │     │  •  │
├─────┼─────┼─────┤
│     │  •  │     │
├─────┼─────┼─────┤
│  •  │     │  •  │
└─────┴─────┴─────┘
```

Place your product at intersection points for visual interest.

### Flat Lay

**Best for:** Jewelry, prints, small items, collections

**Setup:**
1. Set up camera directly above (use tripod with horizontal arm)
2. Arrange items on background
3. Create visual flow (diagonal, circular, or grid)
4. Leave some negative space

**Flat lay tips:**
- Arrange in odd numbers (3, 5, 7)
- Vary sizes and textures
- Include related props (not distracting)
- Keep consistent spacing

### Angled Shots

**Best for:** Most products, shows dimension

**Angles to try:**
- 45° angle (most common, shows top and front)
- Eye level (for tall items)
- Slightly above (for bowls, containers)

### Close-Ups / Details

**Best for:** Showing quality, texture, craftsmanship

**What to capture:**
- Texture of materials
- Stitching or construction details
- Clasps, findings, hardware
- Signatures or maker's marks
- Unique features

### Lifestyle Shots

**Best for:** Helping buyers envision using the product

**Examples:**
- Candle lit on a cozy shelf
- Jewelry being worn
- Print framed on a wall
- Tote bag with everyday items
- Soap in a bathroom setting

**Tips:**
- Keep product as the hero
- Use props that tell a story
- Show scale (in hand, next to common objects)
- Match your target customer's aesthetic

## Shot List

### Minimum 5 Photos Per Listing

| Photo # | Purpose | Description |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Main thumbnail | Clean product shot, white/neutral background |
| 2 | Angle variation | Different angle from photo 1 |
| 3 | Detail | Close-up of texture, quality, unique feature |
| 4 | In use / Lifestyle | Product being used or styled |
| 5 | Size reference | In hand, with ruler, or next to common object |

### Recommended 10 Photos

| Photo # | Purpose | Description |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Main thumbnail | Clean product shot |
| 2 | Angle 2 | Side or different angle |
| 3 | Angle 3 | Top-down or back view |
| 4 | Detail 1 | Close-up of key feature |
| 5 | Detail 2 | Another detail (texture, clasp, etc.) |
| 6 | Lifestyle 1 | Styled in setting |
| 7 | Lifestyle 2 | Different lifestyle context |
| 8 | Size comparison | In hand or with reference |
| 9 | Packaging | How it arrives (gift-ready!) |
| 10 | Video | 15-second auto-play video |

### Variation Photos

If you offer options:
- Show all color variations in one photo
- Show all size options
- Label clearly (text overlay or organized layout)

### Video (Photo Slot 10)

**Etsy allows 15-second videos that auto-play**

**What to film:**
- Product rotating 360°
- Product in use (candle being lit, jewelry being worn)
- Close-up of details
- Unboxing experience

**Tips:**
- Keep it steady (use tripod)
- Good lighting is essential
- No sound needed (auto-play is silent)
- Show the product, not your face

## Editing

### Free Editing Apps

| App | Platform | Best For |
|-----|----------|----------|
| Snapseed | iOS/Android | Full-featured, free |
| VSCO | iOS/Android | Filters, presets |
| Lightroom Mobile | iOS/Android | Professional editing (free tier) |
| Canva | Web/iOS/Android | Adding text, graphics |
| Photoshop Express | iOS/Android | Quick edits |

### What to Edit

**Always:**
- ✅ Brightness (make it pop)
- ✅ Contrast (add depth)
- ✅ White balance (accurate colors)
- ✅ Crop (remove distractions)
- ✅ Sharpen (slightly)

**Sometimes:**
- ⚠️ Background cleanup (remove dust, marks)
- ⚠️ Color correction (match actual product)
- ⚠️ Shadows (reduce if too harsh)

**Never:**
- ❌ Over-saturate (misleading colors)
- ❌ Heavy filters (looks unprofessional)
- ❌ Misrepresent the product
- ❌ Add watermarks (against Etsy TOS)

### Editing Workflow

1. **Crop and straighten**
   - Remove distractions
   - Ensure horizontal/vertical lines are straight

2. **Adjust exposure**
   - Brighten if needed
   - Don't blow out highlights

3. **Fix white balance**
   - Whites should look white, not yellow or blue
   - Match actual product color

4. **Enhance contrast**
   - Add depth
   - Don't overdo it

5. **Sharpen**
   - Subtle sharpening for clarity
   - Don't over-sharpen

6. **Export**
   - High resolution (Etsy needs 2000px minimum)
   - JPG format
   - sRGB color space

### Batch Editing

If you have many photos:
- Edit one photo completely
- Copy settings/preset
- Apply to similar photos
- Fine-tune individually

**Lightroom presets** or **VSCO filters** can speed this up.

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Blurry Photos
**Fix:** Use tripod, ensure good light, tap to focus

### ❌ Dark/Underexposed
**Fix:** More light (natural or artificial), increase exposure in editing

### ❌ Yellow/Orange Color Cast
**Fix:** Turn off overhead lights, use daylight bulbs, fix white balance in editing

### ❌ Cluttered Background
**Fix:** Use clean backgrounds, remove distractions

### ❌ No Size Reference
**Fix:** Include hand, ruler, or common object for scale

### ❌ Only One Photo
**Fix:** Take at least 5, ideally 10 photos

### ❌ Inconsistent Style
**Fix:** Use same background, lighting, and editing across all listings

### ❌ Over-Edited
**Fix:** Keep it natural, colors should match actual product

### ❌ Tiny Product in Frame
**Fix:** Get closer, crop tighter, product should fill 60-80% of frame

### ❌ Glare and Reflections
**Fix:** Adjust angle, use diffused light, polarizing filter (for shiny items)

## Photography Checklist

### Before Shooting

- [ ] Clean your lens
- [ ] Charge battery / check phone storage
- [ ] Set up lighting
- [ ] Prepare backgrounds
- [ ] Clean products (remove dust, fingerprints)
- [ ] Gather props and styling items
- [ ] Set up tripod

### During Shooting

- [ ] Take multiple shots of each angle
- [ ] Check focus (zoom in to verify)
- [ ] Check lighting (no harsh shadows)
- [ ] Capture all required shots (see shot list)
- [ ] Shoot in good resolution
- [ ] Keep camera steady

### After Shooting

- [ ] Review all photos
- [ ] Select best shots (delete rejects)
- [ ] Edit consistently
- [ ] Export at correct size
- [ ] Back up originals
- [ ] Organize by product/listing

## Phone Photography Tips

### Camera Settings

**iPhone:**
- Use native Camera app (not Instagram)
- Tap to focus and expose
- Lock exposure (tap and hold)
- Use Portrait mode for depth (optional)
- Shoot in HEIC or JPG (not Live Photo for listings)

**Android:**
- Use Pro mode if available
- Set ISO low (100-400) for less noise
- Adjust shutter speed for lighting
- Use grid lines for composition

### Tips for Better Phone Photos

1. **Clean your lens** (microfiber cloth)
2. **Use good light** (natural is best)
3. **Get close** (don't rely on digital zoom)
4. **Use timer or remote** (avoids shake)
5. **Shoot in burst mode** (select best shot)
6. **Edit on phone** (apps are powerful now)

## Quick Setup Guide (15 Minutes)

**For beginners who need to start NOW:**

1. **Find a window** with good natural light (3 min)
2. **Set up white foam board** as background (2 min)
3. **Place product** on board, 2-3 feet from window (1 min)
4. **Add reflector** (another foam board) on opposite side (2 min)
5. **Mount phone on tripod** or prop against books (2 min)
6. **Take photos** from multiple angles (5 min)
7. **Edit in Snapseed** (brightness, contrast, crop) (5 min)

**Total: 20 minutes for decent product photos!**

## When to Hire a Professional

**Consider it when:**
- You're making consistent sales and ready to scale
- Your photos aren't converting
- You have a complex product line
- You need lifestyle/brand photography
- Budget allows ($300-1000 for a session)

**What to ask for:**
- 10-20 edited images per product
- White background + lifestyle shots
- Commercial usage rights
- Consistent editing style
