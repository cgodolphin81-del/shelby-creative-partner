## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Bali faces a critical water crisis: water tables have dropped over 50 meters in some areas, 60% of water catchments are drying up, and plastic pollution chokes rivers and coastlines. However, the island has a vibrant ecosystem of 20+ environmental organizations actively working on water conservation, river cleanup, coral restoration, and community education. This research identifies key organizations, passionate leaders, current projects, and concrete partnership opportunities for Shelby's platform to amplify impact.

**Key Finding:** The most impactful entry points are **Sungai Watch** (river plastic interception), **Bali Water Protection/IDEP Foundation** (freshwater recharge wells), and **Bye Bye Plastic Bags** (youth movement with global reach). These organizations have proven track records, strong leadership, and are open to partnerships.

## 5. INTRODUCTION STRATEGY

### PHASED APPROACH

#### **Phase 1: Warm Outreach (Weeks 1-2)**
**Target:** Tier 1 organizations (Sungai Watch, BWP/IDEP, BBPB)

**Approach:**
1. **Social Media Engagement:** Follow, comment, share their content for 1-2 weeks before direct outreach
2. **Personalized Email:** Reference specific work, express genuine interest, propose low-commitment intro call
3. **Leverage Connections:** Mention any mutual contacts (e.g., Bali Hotels Association for Sungai Watch)

**Email Template:**
```
Subject: Partnership Opportunity - Amplifying [Organization]'s Impact

Dear [Name],

I've been following [Organization]'s work on [specific project/campaign] and am deeply inspired by [specific achievement]. 

I'm working with Shelby, a platform focused on [brief description], and we're looking to partner with organizations making real impact in Bali's water conservation space.

We'd love to explore how we could:
- Amplify your story to our audience
- Connect you with potential funding partners
- [Specific offer relevant to their work]

Would you be open to a 20-minute call next week to explore possibilities?

Warmly,
Charmaine
```

#### **Phase 2: Relationship Building (Weeks 3-6)**
**Activities:**
- Schedule virtual coffee chats with 3-5 key organizations
- Visit Bali if possible (meet in person at their facilities)
- Attend public events (beach cleanups, coral planting days)
- Introduce organizations to each other (facilitate peer learning)

#### **Phase 3: Pilot Projects (Weeks 7-12)**
**Examples:**
- Feature one organization in a Shelby content piece
- Facilitate one funding introduction
- Co-host one educational webinar

**Measure Success:**
- Engagement metrics on content
- Introductions made → meetings booked
- Feedback from partners

#### **Phase 4: Scale Partnerships (Months 4-6)**
- Formalize partnership agreements
- Develop recurring content series
- Create donor/volunteer pipelines
- Explore co-branded initiatives

### KEY PRINCIPLES

1. **Lead with Value:** Always offer something concrete before asking for anything
2. **Respect Their Time:** Keep initial asks small (20-min call, not "let's change the world")
3. **Be Specific:** Reference their actual work, not generic "environmental conservation"
4. **Long-Term Mindset:** This is relationship building, not transactional
5. **Cultural Sensitivity:** Understand Balinese concepts like "Tri Hita Karana" (harmony between people, nature, divine)
