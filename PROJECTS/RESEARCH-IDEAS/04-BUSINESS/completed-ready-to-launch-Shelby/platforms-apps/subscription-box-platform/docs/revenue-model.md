# Revenue Model

## Overview

BoxFlow operates on a hybrid SaaS + transaction fee model, aligning our success with customer success. This model provides predictable recurring revenue while capturing value as our customers grow.

---

## Revenue Streams

### 1. SaaS Subscription Fees (Base Revenue)

**Recurring monthly/annual fees based on plan tier:**

| Plan | Monthly | Annual | Target % of Revenue |
|------|---------|--------|---------------------|
| Starter | $99 | $990 | 15% |
| Growth | $299 | $2,990 | 30% |
| Professional | $599 | $5,990 | 25% |
| Enterprise | $999+ | $9,990+ | 20% |

**Characteristics:**
- Predictable recurring revenue
- High gross margin (85%+)
- Low churn when customers are successful
- Upsell opportunities as customers grow

---

### 2. GMV Transaction Fees (Variable Revenue)

**Percentage of Gross Merchandise Value processed:**

| Plan | GMV Fee | Target % of Revenue |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| Starter | 2.0% | 10% |
| Growth | 1.5% | 25% |
| Professional | 1.0% | 25% |
| Enterprise | 0.5% | 15% |

**GMV Definition:**
- Total value of subscription boxes shipped
- Excludes taxes and shipping fees
- Calculated monthly
- Billed in arrears

**Characteristics:**
- Scales with customer success
- Aligns incentives (we win when they win)
- Variable but predictable based on customer growth
- Lower rates for higher tiers (volume discount)

## Unit Economics

### Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

**LTV Calculation:**

```
LTV = (ARPC × Gross Margin %) / Churn Rate

Where:
- ARPC = Average Revenue Per Customer per month
- Gross Margin = 80% (blended)
- Churn Rate = 3% monthly (36% annual)
```

**LTV by Plan:**

| Plan | ARPC | Gross Margin | Monthly Churn | LTV |
|------|------|--------------|---------------|-----|
| Starter | $279 | 80% | 5% | $4,464 |
| Growth | $899 | 80% | 3% | $23,973 |
| Professional | $2,299 | 80% | 2% | $91,960 |
| Enterprise | $5,950 | 80% | 1% | $476,000 |

**Blended LTV: $18,500**

### CAC Payback Period

```
Payback Period = CAC / (ARPC × Gross Margin %)

Payback Period = $475 / ($1,106 × 80%)
Payback Period = $475 / $885

```

**Target:** < 12 months

*Note: This is conservative; actual payback improves as customers grow GMV*

## Pricing Strategy

### Value-Based Pricing Philosophy

BoxFlow pricing is based on value delivered, not cost-plus:

**Value Drivers:**
1. **Revenue Enablement:** Customers can launch and scale faster
2. **Cost Savings:** Reduced ops overhead (60% reduction)
3. **Risk Reduction:** Enterprise-grade reliability
4. **Growth Acceleration:** Built-in marketing and retention tools

**Pricing Power:**
- Switching costs are high (operational integration)
- Value increases with scale (network effects)
- Differentiated features (fulfillment integration)
- Strong brand and reputation

---

### Price Optimization

**Annual Price Increases:**
- 5-10% annual increase for existing customers
- grandfathering for long-term contracts
- Competitive analysis quarterly

**Discount Strategy:**
- Annual prepay: 17% discount (2 months free)
- Startup program: 50% off for 6 months (pre-Series A)
- Non-profit: 20% discount
- Volume discounts: Custom for Enterprise

## Revenue Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Customer Churn

**Impact:** Loss of recurring revenue
**Mitigation:**
- Customer success program
- Proactive churn detection
- Win-back campaigns
- Annual contracts with discounts

---

### Risk 2: GMV Concentration

**Impact:** Top 10 customers = 40% of GMV fees
**Mitigation:**
- Diversify customer base
- Focus on mid-market growth
- Reduce dependency on any single customer
- Contract minimums for Enterprise

---

### Risk 3: Payment Processor Dependency

**Impact:** Stripe/PayPal relationship changes
**Mitigation:**
- Multiple payment gateway integrations
- Direct merchant account options
- Backup processors ready
- Regular compliance audits

---

### Risk 4: Economic Downturn

**Impact:** Subscription box industry contraction
**Mitigation:**
- Diversify across verticals
- Focus on essential categories (food, pet)
- Flexible pricing for struggling customers
- Counter-cyclical marketing
