## 2️⃣ Transaction Fees (30%)

### Fee Structure

| Transaction Type | Free Tier | Starter | Pro |
|------------------|-----------|---------|-----|
| Digital Product Sales | 5% + Stripe fees | 0% + Stripe fees | 0% + Stripe fees |
| Tips/Donations | 5% + Stripe fees | 0% + Stripe fees | 0% + Stripe fees |
| Recurring Subscriptions | 5% + Stripe fees | 0% + Stripe fees | 0% + Stripe fees |
| Affiliate Commissions | 0% (pass-through) | 0% | 0% |

### Why 5% on Free Tier?
- Aligns incentives: We only earn when creators earn
- Lower barrier than competitors (Stan Store: 0% but $29/mo minimum)
- Creators can stay free until they make sales
- Natural upgrade trigger: First $20 in sales = 5 months of Starter

### Projected Transaction Volume

| Y1 Q3 | 5,000 | $200 | $1M | $50K |

**Note:** As creators upgrade to paid tiers, transaction fee revenue decreases but subscription revenue increases. This is intentional and profitable.

## 🎯 Pricing Psychology

### Why These Price Points?

#### $5/month (Starter)
- Less than a coffee per week
- Psychological "no-brainer" threshold
- Competes with "one transaction fee" mentality
- Easy expense approval for side hustlers

#### $15/month (Pro)
- 3x Starter but 10x value perception
- Still under "business expense" threshold ($20)
- Positions as professional tool, not hobby
- Room for future price increase to $19

#### 5% Transaction Fee
- Lower than Gumroad (10%)
- Lower than Ko-fi (5% but no subscription option)
- Zero on paid tiers = clear upgrade incentive
- Only charged on successful transactions

### Price Anchoring Strategy
1. Show Pro ($15) first → makes Starter ($5) seem cheap
2. Show annual savings (20%) → encourages commitment
3. Show "most popular" badge on Starter → social proof
4. Enterprise "Contact Us" → makes $15 seem accessible
