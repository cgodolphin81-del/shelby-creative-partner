**Body:**
In the highlands of Ethiopia, there are 11 churches that should not exist.

Carved from a single mass of volcanic rock in the 12th century. Not built up, but down. Each one a monolith—connected to the earth at its base.

King Lalibela had a vision: when pilgrimage to Jerusalem became impossible, he would bring the Holy Land to Ethiopia.

24 years of construction. Possibly longer. The faithful say angels worked at night.

800 years later, Lalibela is still an active pilgrimage site. Priests wake before dawn. Candles are lit. Ancient chants echo through stone that has heard them for eight centuries.

Here's what struck me:

These churches were not built for survival. Not for shelter. Not for profit.

They were built for something that would outlast their creators.

In a world obsessed with quarterly results and instant gratification, Lalibela asks us a different question:

*What are you building that will outlast you?*

Not every legacy needs to be stone. But every legacy needs intention.
