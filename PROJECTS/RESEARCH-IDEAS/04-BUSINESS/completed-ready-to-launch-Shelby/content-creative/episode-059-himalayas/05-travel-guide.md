## ✈️ GETTING TO NEPAL

### Flights to Kathmandu
- **Airport:** Tribhuvan International Airport (KTM)
- **Major Hubs:** Delhi, Dubai, Doha, Istanbul, Bangkok, Singapore
- **Airlines:** Qatar Airways, Emirates, Turkish Airlines, Thai Airways, Air India
- **Flight Time:** 14-20 hours from US/Europe (with connections)
- **Cost:** $800-1,500 USD round trip (varies by season/origin)

### Visa on Arrival
- **Available:** For most nationalities at Kathmandu airport
- **Cost:**
  - 15 days: $30 USD
  - 30 days: $50 USD (recommended)
  - 90 days: $125 USD
- **Requirements:**
  - Passport (6+ months validity)
  - Passport photo
  - Cash (USD, EUR, GBP accepted)
  - Completed arrival card (on plane or at airport)
- **Process:** Fill form → Pay fee → Get visa sticker → Immigration

### Alternative: E-Visa
- **Website:** www.nepalimmigration.gov.np
- **Apply:** Up to 15 days before travel
- **Same fees** as visa on arrival
- **Faster:** Skip visa line at airport

## 🏔️ EBC TREK OVERVIEW

### Standard 12-Day Itinerary

| **Day** | **Route** | **Altitude** | **Hours** |
|---------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| 1 | Lukla → Phakding | 2,610m | 3-4 hrs |
| 2 | Phakding → Namche Bazaar | 3,440m | 5-6 hrs |
| 3 | Namche (acclimatization) | 3,440m | 3-4 hrs hike |
| 4 | Namche → Tengboche | 3,860m | 5-6 hrs |
| 5 | Tengboche → Dingboche | 4,410m | 5-6 hrs |
| 6 | Dingboche (acclimatization) | 4,410m | 3-4 hrs hike |
| 7 | Dingboche → Lobuche | 4,940m | 5-6 hrs |
| 8 | Lobuche → Gorak Shep → EBC → Gorak Shep | 5,170m/5,364m | 7-8 hrs |
| 9 | Gorak Shep → Kala Patthar → Pheriche | 5,545m/4,240m | 7-8 hrs |
| 10 | Pheriche → Namche Bazaar | 3,440m | 6-7 hrs |
| 11 | Namche → Lukla | 2,860m | 6-7 hrs |
| 12 | Lukla → Kathmandu | 1,400m | Flight |

### Why This Itinerary Works
- **Proper acclimatization:** Two rest days built in
- **Climb high, sleep low:** Key altitude principle
- **Reasonable daily stages:** Not too aggressive
- **Buffer days:** Weather delays possible

### Alternative: 14-Day Itinerary
- Add extra acclimatization day at Namche
- Add extra acclimatization day at Dingboche
- Better for older trekkers or altitude concerns
- More expensive (extra days = extra cost)

## 📜 PERMITS REQUIRED

### 1. Sagarmatha National Park Entry Permit
- **Cost:** NPR 3,000 (~$30 USD) per person
- **Where to Get:**
  - Kathmandu: Nepal Tourism Board, Monastery Road
  - Monjo: Checkpoint on trail (before Namche)
- **Documents Needed:**
  - Passport
  - 2 passport photos
  - Completed application form
- **Validity:** Single entry, duration of trek
- **Checkpoint:** Monjo (mandatory stop, permits checked)

### 2. TIMS Card (Trekkers' Information Management System)
- **Cost:**
  - Independent trekkers: NPR 2,000 (~$20 USD)
  - Group/agency: NPR 1,000 (~$10 USD) per person
- **Where to Get:**
  - Kathmandu: Tourist Service Center, Bhrikuti Mandap
  - Pokhara: TIMS counter (if starting from Pokhara)
- **Documents Needed:**
  - Passport
  - 2 passport photos
  - Travel insurance details
  - Emergency contact info
- **Purpose:** Safety tracking, emergency response

### 3. Local Area Permit (If Applicable)
- **Gokyo Lakes:** Additional permit if doing Gokyo variation
- **Three Passes:** Additional permit for high pass trek
- **Cost:** ~$30 USD each
- **Not needed** for standard EBC trek

### Permit Tips
- **Get permits in Kathmandu** before flying to Lukla
- **Make copies** of all permits (keep separate from originals)
- **Digital copies** on phone as backup
- **Checkpoints:** Monjo (Sagarmatha), various points (TIMS)
- **Fines:** Possible if caught without permits

- ✅ Longest days

- ❌ Higher prices

- Lukla: 10-20°C (50-68°F)
- Namche: 5-15°C (41-59°F)
- EBC: -10 to 5°C (14-41°F)

**Recommendation:** **Late March to early May** is ideal

## ⚠️ ALTITUDE SICKNESS

### Understanding Altitude Sickness

**Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS)**
- Mild to moderate illness from rapid ascent
- Affects 25-75% of trekkers above 3,000m
- Preventable with proper acclimatization

**HAPE (High Altitude Pulmonary Edema)**
- Fluid in lungs
- LIFE THREATENING
- Requires immediate descent

**HACE (High Altitude Cerebral Edema)**
- Fluid in brain
- LIFE THREATENING
- Requires immediate descent

---

### AMS Symptoms (Mild-Moderate)
- ✅ Headache (most common)
- ✅ Nausea, loss of appetite
- ✅ Dizziness, lightheadedness
- ✅ Fatigue, weakness
- ✅ Difficulty sleeping
- ✅ Shortness of breath

### Severe Symptoms (DESCEND IMMEDIATELY!)
- 🚨 Severe headache (not relieved by medication)
- 🚨 Vomiting
- 🚨 Confusion, disorientation
- 🚨 Loss of coordination (ataxia)
- 🚨 Coughing up pink, frothy sputum (HAPE)
- 🚨 Inability to walk straight (HACE)
- 🚨 Blue lips/fingernails (cyanosis)

---

### Prevention

**Ascend Slowly**
- "Climb high, sleep low" principle
- Never increase sleeping altitude by more than 500m per day above 3,000m
- Take acclimatization days (built into itinerary)

**Hydration**
- Drink 3-4 liters per day
- Clear urine = well hydrated
- Dark urine = drink more

**Nutrition**
- Eat well, even if not hungry
- Carbohydrates are key
- Avoid heavy, fatty foods

**Avoid Alcohol**
- Especially above Namche Bazaar
- Dehydrates and worsens symptoms
- Wait until back in Kathmandu

**Medication**
- **Diamox (Acetazolamide):** Consult doctor before trek
  - Helps acclimatization
  - Take 1-2 days before ascent
  - Side effects: tingling, frequent urination
- **Ibuprofen:** For headaches
- **Dexamethasone:** Emergency use only (prescription)

---

### Treatment

**Mild AMS:**
- Rest at same altitude
- Hydrate well
- Pain relievers for headache
- Do NOT ascend further
- Wait for symptoms to resolve (24-48 hours)

**Moderate AMS:**
- Descend 500-1,000m immediately
- Rest
- Hydrate
- Do not continue ascending until fully recovered

**Severe AMS / HAPE / HACE:**
- **IMMEDIATE DESCENT** - do not wait
- Oxygen if available
- Gamow bag (portable hyperbaric chamber) if available
- Medical care as soon as possible
- **No one has ever died from descending**

---

### Golden Rules of Altitude
1. If you feel sick, it's probably altitude
2. Don't ascend if you have symptoms
3. If symptoms worsen, descend immediately
4. Pride kills - listen to your body
5. Your guide's decision is final on safety

## 🏥 HEALTH & SAFETY

### Pre-Trek Health Check
- [ ] Doctor visit for clearance
- [ ] Discuss altitude medication (Diamox)
- [ ] Dental work (complete BEFORE trek - dental issues worsen at altitude)
- [ ] Fitness training (3+ months cardio, hiking with pack)
- [ ] Vaccinations (see below)

### Recommended Vaccinations
- [ ] Hepatitis A
- [ ] Hepatitis B
- [ ] Typhoid
- [ ] Tetanus (booster if needed)
- [ ] Rabies (optional, but recommended for remote areas)
- [ ] Japanese Encephalitis (optional, for longer stays)

**Consult:** Travel medicine clinic 6-8 weeks before departure

---

### Common Health Issues

**Altitude Sickness:** See detailed section above

**GI Issues:**
- Traveler's diarrhea common
- Bring: Imodium, Ciprofloxacin/Azithromycin
- Drink purified water only
- Avoid raw vegetables at lower elevations

**Respiratory:**
- Cold, cough common (dry air, cold)
- Bring: Cough drops, throat lozenges
- Stay warm, cover mouth in cold

**Blisters:**
- Very common on long treks
- Bring: Moleskin, blister tape, Compeed
- Break in boots BEFORE trek
- Change socks daily

**Dehydration:**
- Drink 3-4 liters daily
- Electrolyte packets help
- Watch urine color

**Sunburn:**
- High UV at altitude
- SPF 50+ on all exposed skin
- Reapply frequently
- Sunglasses essential (snow blindness risk)

## 🍲 FOOD & WATER ON TREK

### Tea House Food

**Staples:**
- **Dal Bhat:** Rice, lentils, vegetables, curry (unlimited refills!) - **BEST VALUE**
- **Momos:** Tibetan dumplings (vegetable, buffalo, chicken)
- **Thukpa:** Noodle soup
- **Fried Rice/Noodles:** Common, filling
- **Pizza:** Surprisingly common (basic, doughy)
- **Pancakes/Oatmeal:** Breakfast options
- **Apple Pie:** Famous in Namche bakeries

**Drinks:**
- **Tea:** Black, milk, ginger, lemon, herbal
- **Coffee:** Instant or brewed (varies)
- **Hot Lemon/Honey:** Soothing at altitude
- **Hot Chocolate:** Comfort drink
- **Water:** See below

**Dietary Restrictions:**
- Vegetarian: Widely available
- Vegan: Possible, but limited
- Gluten-free: Difficult (lots of noodles, bread)
- Allergies: Communicate clearly, but cross-contamination risk

---

### Water Safety

**Options:**
1. **Bottled Water:**
   - Available at all tea houses
   - Expensive: $3-5 per liter at EBC
   - Environmental issue (plastic waste)
   - **Not recommended**

2. **Boiled Water:**
   - Available at tea houses
   - Cost: $2-5 per liter
   - Safe, but expensive over 12 days
   - **Good backup option**

3. **Water Purification (RECOMMENDED):**
   - **Tablets:** Aquatabs, Potable Aqua ($10-15)
   - **Filter:** Sawyer, Katadyn ($50-100)
   - **UV:** SteriPen ($100+)
   - **Cost:** Pennies per liter
   - **Environmental:** No plastic waste
   - **Best option for most trekkers**

**Daily Water Needs:**
- 3-4 liters per day at altitude
- Start hydrating before you feel thirsty
- Carry 2 bottles (drink from one, refill)

## 🏠 ACCOMMODATION

### Tea Houses

**What to Expect:**
- **Rooms:** Small, twin beds, thin mattress
- **Bedding:** Blankets provided, bring sleeping bag
- **Heating:** None in rooms (dining room heated)
- **Toilet:** Shared, often squat, cold water
- **Light:** Single bulb (if electricity working)
- **WiFi:** Available (paid, $3-5/hour, slow)
- **Charging:** Available (paid, $3-5/device)
- **Hot Shower:** Available (paid, $5-8, bucket or gas)

**Cost:**
- Room: $5-10 per night (often FREE if you eat meals there)
- This is the standard trekking accommodation

**Tips:**
- Bring ear plugs (thin walls)
- Bring headlamp (lighting unreliable)
- Keep valuables with you
- Tip for good service
- Eat at your tea house (room often free)

---

### Luxury Options (Lower Elevations)

**Namche Bazaar:**
- Hotel Everest View (highest altitude hotel)
- More comfortable rooms
- Better heating
- Higher cost ($50-100/night)

**Not Available:**
- Above Namche, options are basic tea houses only

- ✅ Hire local guides and porters
- ✅ Stay in local tea houses

**Minimize Impact:**
- ✅ Stay on established trails
- ✅ Don't pick plants or disturb wildlife
- ✅ Use toilets where available
- ✅ Conserve resources (water, electricity)

## 💡 PRO TIPS

### Before You Go
1. **Train:** Cardio, hiking with pack, stairs
2. **Break in boots:** Wear for 50+ miles before trek
3. **Get travel insurance:** Read altitude coverage fine print
4. **Book flights early:** Lukla flights fill up in peak season
5. **Bring cash:** ATMs unreliable, USD for emergencies
6. **Get permits in Kathmandu:** Don't wait until Lukla
7. **Pack light:** You'll carry your day pack every day
8. **Download offline maps:** No cell service on trek

### On the Trek
1. **Walk slowly:** "Bistaari" - pace yourself
2. **Hydrate:** 3-4 liters daily
3. **Eat well:** Even if not hungry
4. **Listen to your body:** Altitude symptoms = stop
5. **Tip your crew:** Guides 10-15%, porters 10%
6. **Respect culture:** Ask before photographing
7. **Charge devices:** Do it when you can (electricity unreliable)
8. **Enjoy dal bhat:** Unlimited refills, best value

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. ❌ Going too fast (altitude sickness)
2. ❌ Not hydrating enough
3. ❌ Skipping acclimatization days
4. ❌ Wearing cotton (hypothermia risk)
5. ❌ Not breaking in boots (blisters)
6. ❌ Skipping travel insurance
7. ❌ Bringing too much stuff (weight)
8. ❌ Ignoring altitude symptoms

## 📞 EMERGENCY INFORMATION

### Helicopter Evacuation
- **Available:** Yes, weather permitting
- **Cost:** $5,000-15,000 USD
- **Insurance:** Must cover helicopter evacuation
- **Process:** Guide contacts insurance, insurance authorizes
- **Weather:** Can be delayed by days in bad weather

### Emergency Descent
- **Fastest treatment** for altitude sickness
- **Don't wait** if symptoms severe
- **Guide will arrange** porter support if needed
- **No shame:** Better to descend and recover

### Communication
- **Cell service:** Available at lower elevations (Ncell, NTC)
- **WiFi:** Available at tea houses (paid, slow)
- **Satellite phone:** Guides often carry
- **Emergency beacon:** Consider for independent trekkers
