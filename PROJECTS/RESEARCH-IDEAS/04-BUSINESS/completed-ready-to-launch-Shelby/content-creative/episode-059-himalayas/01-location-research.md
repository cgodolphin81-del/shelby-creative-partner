## Himalayas, Nepal - Everest Base Camp Trek

## 🏔️ OVERVIEW

**Region:** Khumbu Region, Sagarmatha National Park, Nepal
**Primary Destination:** Everest Base Camp (5,364m / 17,598ft)
**Gateway City:** Kathmandu (1,400m / 4,600ft)
**Trek Starting Point:** Lukla (2,860m / 9,383ft)
**Typical Duration:** 12-14 days round trip from Lukla
**Total Distance:** ~130km (80 miles) round trip
**Difficulty:** Challenging (altitude, not technical climbing)

## 🛬 KATHMANDU - THE GATEWAY

### Arrival Experience
- **Tribhuvan International Airport (KTM):** Nepal's only international airport
- **Visa on Arrival:** Available for most nationalities ($30 for 15 days, $50 for 30 days, $125 for 90 days)
- **Thamel:** Tourist district, trekking gear shops, restaurants, hotels
- **First impressions:** Chaos, colors, horns, incense, street food, motorbikes, temples

### Key Kathmandu Sites
- **Durbar Square:** UNESCO World Heritage, ancient palace complex, temples
- **Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple):** Buddhist stupa, panoramic city views, monkeys
- **Pashupatinath Temple:** Sacred Hindu temple, cremation ghats on Bagmati River
- **Boudhanath Stupa:** One of world's largest stupas, Tibetan Buddhist center
- **Patan:** Ancient city, metalwork, Buddhist monasteries

### Pre-Trek Preparation in Kathmandu
- **Gear shopping:** Thamel has everything (North Face, Mountain Hardware, local brands)
- **Permit offices:** Nepal Tourism Board, TIMS counter
- **Money:** ATMs available, USD cash for emergencies, Nepalese Rupees for trek
- **Final briefing:** Meet guides, check gear, weigh bags (porter limits)
- **Rest day:** Essential after long flights, adjust to time zone

### Kathmandu Audio Notes
- Temple bells ringing
- Motorbike horns and traffic chaos
- Street vendors calling
- Prayer wheels spinning
- Incense smoke smell
- Mix of Nepali, English, Hindi, Tibetan

## ✈️ LUKLA - THE MOUNTAIN AIRPORT

### Tenzing-Hillary Airport (LUA)
- **Elevation:** 2,860m (9,383ft)
- **Runway:** 527m (1,729ft) - one of world's most dangerous
- **Slope:** 12% gradient (uphill for landing, downhill for takeoff)
- **No go-around:** One chance to land
- **Weather dependent:** Frequent delays and cancellations

### The Flight Experience
- **Duration:** 30-35 minutes from Kathmandu
- **Aircraft:** Small turboprops (DHC-6 Twin Otter, Dornier)
- **Views:** Himalayan panorama on clear days
- **Weight limit:** 10kg checked + 5kg carry-on (strict)
- **Cost:** ~$180-220 USD each way

### Lukla Village
- **Main street:** Single paved path lined with lodges and gear shops
- **Atmosphere:** Trekker hub, nervous energy, last civilization before mountains
- **Acclimatization:** Spend first night here to begin adjusting
- **Mani stones:** Buddhist prayer stones at village entrance

### Audio Notes
- Small plane engines
- Wind howling through valley
- Porters shouting
- Lodge bells
- Prayer flags snapping in wind

## 🥾 EVEREST BASE CAMP TREK ROUTE

### Standard 12-Day Itinerary

**Day 1:** Lukla (2,860m) → Phakding (2,610m) - 3-4 hours
- Gentle descent, warm-up day
- Cross suspension bridges
- Overnight in tea house

**Day 2:** Phakding → Namche Bazaar (3,440m) - 5-6 hours
- Steep climb, first real altitude
- Enter Sagarmatha National Park
- First views of Everest (weather permitting)
- **Critical acclimatization point**

**Day 3:** Namche Bazaar - Acclimatization Day
- Hike to Everest View Hotel (3,880m)
- Return to Namche to sleep
- Explore village, visit museum
- **Rest is essential**

**Day 4:** Namche → Tengboche (3,860m) - 5-6 hours
- Descend to Dudh Kosi River
- Climb to Tengboche
- **Tengboche Monastery** - largest in Khumbu
- Views: Everest, Lhotse, Nuptse, Ama Dablam

**Day 5:** Tengboche → Dingboche (4,410m) - 5-6 hours
- Descend through rhododendron forest
- Cross Imja Khola
- Enter alpine zone
- Trees disappear, landscape opens

**Day 6:** Dingboche - Acclimatization Day
- Hike to Nagarjun Hill (5,100m)
- Panoramic views: Lhotse, Makalu, Cho Oyu
- Return to Dingboche to sleep
- **Critical for altitude adjustment**

**Day 7:** Dingboche → Lobuche (4,940m) - 5-6 hours
- Steep climb, thin air
- Pass memorials to fallen climbers
- Khumbu Glacier comes into view
- Cold, windy, exposed

**Day 8:** Lobuche → Gorak Shep (5,170m) → EBC (5,364m) → Gorak Shep - 7-8 hours
- **Longest, hardest day**
- Gorak Shep: last settlement before EBC
- Lunch, then continue to Base Camp
- **Everest Base Camp reached!**
- Return to Gorak Shep to sleep
- **Not a summit - turnaround point**

**Day 9:** Gorak Shep → Kala Patthar (5,545m) → Pheriche (4,240m) - 7-8 hours
- **Early morning climb to Kala Patthar**
- Best Everest views (closer than EBC)
- Sunrise over Everest
- Long descent to lower altitude
- Sleep in Pheriche

**Day 10:** Pheriche → Namche Bazaar - 6-7 hours
- Long descent
- Retrace steps
- Celebrate in Namche

**Day 11:** Namche → Lukla - 6-7 hours
- Final day of trekking
- Farewell dinner with crew
- Last night in mountains

**Day 12:** Lukla → Kathmandu
- Fly back to civilization
- Hot shower, real food, soft bed

### Alternative: 14-Day Itinerary
- Adds extra acclimatization days
- More conservative altitude gain
- Better for older trekkers or those concerned about altitude

## 🏘️ KEY VILLAGES & STOPS

### Namche Bazaar (3,440m)
- **Capital of Khumbu**, Sherpa cultural center
- **Amphitheater-shaped** village built into mountainside
- **Saturday market:** Local traders, Tibetan goods, yak products
- **Everest View Hotel:** Highest altitude hotel, panoramic views
- **Sherpa Museum:** Culture, history, mountaineering
- **Bakeries:** Famous apple pie, cinnamon rolls (surprising luxury)
- **Internet:** WiFi available (paid, slow)
- **ATM:** Available (unreliable, bring cash)

### Tengboche (3,860m)
- **Tengboche Monastery:** Spiritual heart of Khumbu
- **Founded:** 1916, rebuilt after 1989 fire
- **Daily ceremonies:** 6 AM and 3 PM (visitors welcome)
- **Mani Rimdu Festival:** October/November (masked dances)
- **Views:** Everest, Lhotse, Nuptse, Ama Dablam (panoramic)
- **Accommodation:** Basic tea houses, monastery guest rooms

### Dingboche (4,410m)
- **Highest permanent settlement** in Khumbu
- **Barley fields:** Terraced, highest agriculture in region
- **Acclimatization hub:** Many trekkers rest here
- **Views:** Lhotse, Makalu, Cho Oyu, Ama Dablam
- **Nagarjun Hill:** Best viewpoint (5,100m)
- **Cold:** Significantly colder than lower villages

### Lobuche (4,940m)
- **High altitude stop**, basic accommodations
- **Memorials:** Climbers who died on Everest
- **Glacier views:** Khumbu Glacier visible
- **Cold and windy:** Prepare for harsh conditions
- **Limited menu:** Dal bhat, noodles, basic fare

### Gorak Shep (5,170m)
- **Last settlement** before Base Camp
- **Seasonal:** Only open during trekking seasons
- **Basic:** Very limited facilities, frozen toilets
- **Staging point:** For EBC and Kala Patthar
- **Cold:** Temperatures well below freezing at night

## 🏔️ THE PEAKS

### Mount Everest (Sagarmatha / Chomolungma) - 8,849m
- **World's highest peak**
- **Nepali name:** Sagarmatha (Goddess of the Sky)
- **Tibetan name:** Chomolungma (Mother Goddess of the World)
- **First ascent:** 1953, Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay Sherpa
- **Death zone:** Above 8,000m (no human can survive long-term)
- **Climbing season:** April-May (pre-monsoon)
- **From EBC:** You see the summit, but it's farther than expected
- **From Kala Patthar:** Best close-up view

### Lhotse - 8,516m
- **4th highest peak in world**
- **Connected to Everest** via South Col
- **Name means:** "South Peak" in Tibetan
- **First ascent:** 1956
- **Visible from:** Namche, Tengboche, Dingboche, EBC

### Nuptse - 7,861m
- **"West Peak"** in Sherpa
- **Forms western ridge** of Everest-Lhotse massif
- **Dominates view** from Base Camp
- **First ascent:** 1961
- **Impressive:** Looks massive from EBC (closer than Everest)

### Ama Dablam - 6,812m
- **"Mother's Necklace"** in Sherpa
- **Most beautiful peak** in Khumbu (many say)
- **Distinctive:** Ice hanging like a necklace
- **Climbing:** Technical, popular with experienced climbers
- **Visible from:** Tengboche, Dingboche
- **Photogenic:** Most photographed peak in Nepal

### Other Notable Peaks
- **Makalu (8,485m):** 5th highest, visible from Dingboche
- **Cho Oyu (8,188m):** 6th highest, on Tibet border
- **Pumori (7,161m):** "Mountain Daughter," visible from Kala Patthar
- **Thamserku (6,623m):** Visible from Namche

## 🧭 SHERPA CULTURE & HISTORY

### Who Are the Sherpas?
- **Ethnic group** from Tibet, settled in Khumbu ~500 years ago
- **Name origin:** "Sharwa" = people of the east (Tibetan)
- **Language:** Sherpa (Tibetan dialect), many speak Nepali and English
- **Religion:** Tibetan Buddhism (Nyingma and Kagyu schools)
- **Population:** ~150,000 in Nepal, 20,000+ in Khumbu region

### Sherpa and Mountaineering
- **Historical role:** Porters and guides for Western expeditions
- **Tenzing Norgay:** First ascent of Everest with Hillary (1953)
- **Modern role:** High-altitude guides, route fixers, icefall doctors
- **Risk:** Sherpas face highest mortality rates on Everest
- **Economics:** Mountaineering is primary income for many families
- **Respect:** Earned through skill, experience, humility

### Sherpa Traditions
- **Hospitality:** Guests are honored, fed well
- **Community:** Collective work (nawong), mutual support
- **Animism:** Pre-Buddhist beliefs in mountain deities
- **Miyolangsangma:** Buddhist goddess, resides on Everest summit
- **Respect for mountains:** Sacred, not to be conquered but visited

### Buddhism in Khumbu
- **Monasteries:** Tengboche, Pangboche, Thame, Chiwong
- **Prayer flags:** Five colors, mantras printed, spread blessings on wind
- **Mani stones:** Stones carved with prayers, stacked along trails
- **Stupas:** Buddhist monuments, walk clockwise
- **Prayer wheels:** Spin to send prayers outward
- **Lama:** Buddhist priests, lead ceremonies

### Cultural Etiquette
- **Walk clockwise** around stupas and mani stones
- **Don't touch** offerings or religious objects
- **Ask permission** before photographing people or inside monasteries
- **Dress modestly** in villages and monasteries
- **Use right hand** for giving/receiving (left is considered unclean)
- **Don't point feet** at people or sacred objects
- **Remove shoes** before entering homes and monasteries

## 📜 PERMITS & REGULATIONS

### Required Permits

**1. Sagarmatha National Park Entry Permit**
- **Cost:** NPR 3,000 (~$30 USD) for foreigners
- **Where:** Kathmandu (Monastery Road) or Monjo (checkpoint)
- **Documents:** Passport, passport photos, application form
- **Validity:** Single entry, duration of trek
- **Checkpoint:** Monjo (must show before continuing to Namche)

**2. TIMS Card (Trekkers' Information Management System)**
- **Cost:** NPR 2,000 (~$20 USD) for independent trekkers
- **Group rate:** NPR 1,000 (~$10 USD) with guide/agency
- **Where:** Kathmandu (Tourist Service Center) or Pokhara
- **Purpose:** Safety tracking, emergency response
- **Documents:** Passport, photos, insurance info

**3. Local Area Permit (if applicable)**
- **Gokyo Lakes:** Additional permit if doing Gokyo variation
- **Three Passes:** Additional permit for high pass trek

### Regulations
- **No single-use plastics:** Banned in Sagarmatha National Park (2020)
- **Waste carry-out:** Pack out all trash
- **Guide requirement:** Not mandatory for EBC, but recommended
- ** Porter welfare:** Maximum load 30kg, proper gear required
- **Helicopter rescue:** Available, expensive ($5,000-15,000 USD)
- **Travel insurance:** Required (must cover high-altitude evacuation)

### Permit Checkpoints
- **Lukla:** TIMS check (sometimes)
- **Monjo:** Sagarmatha National Park entry (mandatory)
- **Namche:** Permit verification

## 🏕️ TEA HOUSES & ACCOMMODATION

### What Are Tea Houses?
- **Family-run lodges** along trekking routes
- **Basic rooms:** Twin beds, thin mattress, shared bathroom
- **Common dining room:** Heated by yak dung stove (evening)
- **Menu:** Extensive (dal bhat, noodles, pizza, apple pie)
- **WiFi:** Available (paid, ~$3-5 per hour, slow)
- **Charging:** Available (paid, ~$3-5 per device)
- **Hot shower:** Available (paid, ~$5-8, bucket or gas)

### Typical Room
- **Size:** Small, just enough for beds
- **Beds:** Twin, thin mattress, bring sleeping bag
- **Blankets:** Provided but bring your own bag
- **Heating:** None in rooms (dining room only)
- **Toilet:** Shared, often squat, cold water
- **Light:** Single bulb (if electricity working)

### Food on Trek
- **Dal Bhat:** Rice, lentils, vegetables, curry (unlimited refills)
- **Momos:** Tibetan dumplings (vegetable or meat)
- **Thukpa:** Noodle soup
- **Fried rice/noodles:** Common, filling
- **Pizza:** Surprisingly common (basic, doughy)
- **Apple pie:** Famous in Namche
- **Coffee/tea:** Available everywhere
- **Water:** Bottled (expensive, environmental issue) or purified

### Water Safety
- **Bottled water:** Available, expensive ($3-5 at EBC)
- **Purification:** Tablets, filter, UV (SteriPen)
- **Boiled water:** Available at tea houses ($2-5 per liter)
- **Recommendation:** Bring purification, reduce plastic

### Costs (Approximate)
- **Room:** $5-10 per night (often free if you eat there)
- **Dal Bhat:** $6-10
- **Other meals:** $5-8
- **Hot shower:** $5-8
- **WiFi:** $3-5 per hour
- **Charging:** $3-5 per device
- **Water:** $2-5 per liter (increases with altitude)

## 🌤️ BEST TIMES TO TREK

### Pre-Monsoon (Spring): March-May
**Pros:**
- Stable weather, clear skies
- Warm temperatures
- Rhododendrons blooming (April-May)
- Best visibility
- Most popular season

**Cons:**
- Crowded trails and tea houses
- Higher prices
- Book in advance
- Warmer at lower elevations

**Temperature Range:**
- Lukla: 10-20°C (50-68°F)
- Namche: 5-15°C (41-59°F)
- EBC: -10 to 5°C (14-41°F)

### Post-Monsoon (Autumn): September-November
**Pros:**
- Clear skies after monsoon
- Stable weather
- Good visibility
- Festival season (Dashain, Tihar)
- Second most popular

**Cons:**
- Crowded (especially October)
- Colder than spring
- Shorter days
- Book in advance

**Temperature Range:**
- Lukla: 5-15°C (41-59°F)
- Namche: 0-10°C (32-50°F)
- EBC: -15 to 0°C (5-32°F)

### Winter: December-February
**Pros:**
- Few trekkers (quiet trails)
- Clear skies (when not snowing)
- Lower prices
- Authentic experience

**Cons:**
- Very cold (-20°C at EBC possible)
- Some tea houses closed
- Short days
- Lukla flights often delayed
- High passes may be closed

**Temperature Range:**
- Lukla: 0-10°C (32-50°F)
- Namche: -5 to 5°C (23-41°F)
- EBC: -25 to -5°C (-13 to 23°F)

### Monsoon: June-August
**Pros:**
- Few trekkers
- Lush green landscapes
- Lowest prices

**Cons:**
- Heavy rain, clouds
- Leeches on lower trails
- Poor visibility
- Lukla flights frequently cancelled
- Many tea houses closed
- **NOT RECOMMENDED for EBC**

### Recommended
**Best:** Late March-May, Late September-November
**Good:** December-February (if prepared for cold)
**Avoid:** June-August (monsoon)

## ⚠️ ALTITUDE SICKNESS

### What Is Altitude Sickness?
- **Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS):** Mild to moderate illness from rapid ascent
- **HAPE:** High Altitude Pulmonary Edema (fluid in lungs) - LIFE THREATENING
- **HACE:** High Altitude Cerebral Edema (fluid in brain) - LIFE THREATENING

### AMS Symptoms (Mild-Moderate)
- Headache (most common)
- Nausea, loss of appetite
- Dizziness, lightheadedness
- Fatigue, weakness
- Difficulty sleeping
- Shortness of breath

### Severe Symptoms (Descend Immediately!)
- Severe headache (not relieved by medication)
- Vomiting
- Confusion, disorientation
- Loss of coordination (ataxia)
- Coughing up pink, frothy sputum (HAPE)
- Inability to walk straight (HACE)
- Blue lips/fingernails (cyanosis)

### Prevention
- **Ascend slowly:** "Climb high, sleep low"
- **Acclimatization days:** Rest days at Namche and Dingboche
- **Hydration:** 3-4 liters per day
- **Avoid alcohol:** Especially above Namche
- **Eat well:** Carbohydrates, even if not hungry
- **Listen to your body:** Don't push through symptoms
- **Medication:** Diamox (acetazolamide) - consult doctor

### Treatment
- **Mild AMS:** Rest, hydrate, pain relievers, don't ascend further
- **Moderate AMS:** Descend 500-1000m, rest
- **Severe AMS/HAPE/HACE:** **IMMEDIATE DESCENT**, oxygen, medical care
- **Gamow bag:** Portable hyperbaric chamber (available at some tea houses)

### Golden Rules
1. If you feel sick, it's probably altitude
2. Don't ascend if you have symptoms
3. If symptoms worsen, descend immediately
4. No one has ever died from descending
5. Pride kills - listen to your body

### Evacuation
- **Helicopter rescue:** Available, expensive
- **Insurance:** Must cover high-altitude evacuation (up to 6,000m)
- **Emergency contacts:** Keep guide/agency numbers
- **Travel insurance:** Essential, read fine print

## 🎒 ESSENTIAL GEAR

### Clothing (Layering System)

**Base Layer:**
- Moisture-wicking tops and bottoms (merino or synthetic)
- 2-3 sets
- No cotton (retains moisture)

**Mid Layer:**
- Fleece jacket or down vest
- Insulated pants for high altitude

**Outer Layer:**
- Down jacket (-20°C rating)
- Waterproof/windproof shell jacket
- Waterproof/windproof shell pants

**Extremities:**
- Warm hat (covers ears)
- Sun hat or cap
- Buff or neck gaiter
- Gloves (liner + insulated waterproof)
- Socks (merino wool, 4-5 pairs)
- Trekking boots (broken in, waterproof)
- Camp shoes or sandals

### Gear

**Backpack:**
- 50-70L main pack (or duffel for porters)
- 25-35L day pack
- Rain cover

**Sleeping:**
- Sleeping bag (-20°C rating)
- Sleeping bag liner (adds warmth, hygiene)

**Trekking:**
- Trekking poles (essential for descent)
- Headlamp + extra batteries
- Water bottles or hydration system (2-3L capacity)
- Water purification (tablets, filter, or UV)

**Personal:**
- Sunglasses (UV400, category 3-4)
- Sunscreen (SPF 50+)
- Lip balm with SPF
- Personal first aid kit
- Medications ( Diamox, pain relievers, anti-diarrheal, etc.)
- Hand sanitizer
- Wet wipes (showers are limited)
- Toilet paper (bring your own)
- Quick-dry towel

**Documents:**
- Passport + copies
- Permit copies
- Insurance documents
- Emergency contacts
- Cash (USD and NPR)

**Electronics:**
- Camera + extra batteries (cold drains batteries)

- Power bank

- Headlamp + batteries

- Down jacket ($2-4/day)

## 🏥 HEALTH & SAFETY

### Pre-Trek Health
- **Doctor visit:** Get clearance, discuss altitude medication
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A/B, Typhoid, Tetanus (routine)
- **Dental work:** Complete before trek (dental issues worsen at altitude)
- **Fitness:** Cardio training, hiking with pack, stair climbing
- **Medications:** Bring prescriptions + copies

### Common Health Issues
- **Altitude sickness:** See above
- **GI issues:** Traveler's diarrhea, bring Imodium, antibiotics
- **Respiratory:** Cold, cough (dry air, cold)
- **Blisters:** Common, bring moleskin, tape
- **Dehydration:** Drink 3-4L daily
- **Sunburn:** High UV at altitude, use sunscreen

### First Aid Kit
- Pain relievers (ibuprofen, acetaminophen)
- Altitude medication (Diamox)
- Anti-diarrheal (Imodium)
- Antibiotics (Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin)
- Antihistamines
- Blister treatment (moleskin, tape)
- Bandages, gauze, tape
- Antiseptic wipes
- Personal medications
- Electrolyte packets

### Insurance Requirements
- **Must cover:** High-altitude trekking (up to 6,000m)
- **Must cover:** Helicopter evacuation
- **Must cover:** Medical expenses in Nepal
- **Recommended:** Trip cancellation, gear loss
- **Providers:** World Nomads, Allianz, Global Rescue
- **Cost:** $100-300 depending on coverage

### Emergency Contacts
- **Tourist Police:** 1144
- **Ambulance:** 102
- **Hospital (Kathmandu):** CIWEC Clinic (travel medicine), Grande Hospital
- **Embassy:** Know your country's embassy contact
- **Guide/Agency:** Keep local contact numbers
- **Insurance:** Emergency assistance number

## 🌍 SUSTAINABLE TREKKING

### Environmental Challenges
- **Waste:** Accumulating trash on trails
- **Deforestation:** Trees cut for firewood
- **Water:** Plastic bottle pollution
- **Sewage:** Inadequate waste treatment
- **Carbon footprint:** Flights, tourism

### How to Trek Responsibly

**Reduce Plastic:**
- Bring water purification (don't buy bottled)
- Bring reusable water bottles
- Pack out all trash
- Say no to plastic bags

**Respect Local Culture:**
- Dress modestly
- Ask before photographing
- Support local businesses
- Learn basic Nepali phrases
- Respect religious sites

**Support Local Economy:**
- Hire local guides and porters
- Stay in local tea houses
- Buy local goods
- Tip fairly (guides 10-15%, porters 10%)

**Minimize Impact:**
- Stay on trails
- Don't pick plants or disturb wildlife
- Use toilets where available
- Conserve resources (water, electricity)
- Take shorter showers

**Wildlife:**
- Don't feed animals
- Keep distance
- Don't disturb nesting birds
- Respect protected areas

### Organizations
- **Sagarmatha Next:** Environmental initiative in Khumbu
- **Khumbu Climbing Center:** Training for Sherpas
- **Porter Welfare:** Various NGOs support porter rights
