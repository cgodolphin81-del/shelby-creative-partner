## Getting There

### By Air ✈️

**Nearest Airports:**

**1. Nyaung-U Airport (NYU)**
- **Distance to Bagan:** 5 km (3 miles)
- **Flight time from Yangon:** 1 hour 20 minutes
- **Flight time from Mandalay:** 30 minutes
- **Airlines:** Myanmar National Airlines, Air KBZ, Mann Yadanarpon
- **Frequency:** 2-4 flights daily from Yangon; multiple from Mandalay
- **Cost:** $80-150 USD one-way (Yangon); $50-80 USD (Mandalay)
- **Airport to hotel:** 10-15 minutes by taxi ($5-10 USD)

**2. Heho Airport (HEH)**
- **Distance to Bagan:** 200 km (124 miles)
- **Use case:** If combining with Inle Lake visit
- **Ground transport:** 4-5 hours by car/bus

**International Connections:**
- **Via Bangkok:** Bangkok Airways, Thai Airways (BKK → Yangon/Mandalay)
- **Via Singapore:** Singapore Airlines, Scoot (SIN → Yangon)
- **Via Kuala Lumpur:** Malaysia Airlines, AirAsia (KUL → Yangon)
- **Via Doha:** Qatar Airways (DOH → Yangon)

### By Bus 🚌

**From Mandalay:**
- **Distance:** 195 km (121 miles)
- **Duration:** 4-5 hours
- **Road condition:** Good highway
- **Bus companies:** JJ Express, Bagan Min Thar, Shwe Mandalar
- **Cost:** $8-15 USD (standard); $15-25 USD (VIP)
- **Departures:** Multiple daily (7 AM - 2 PM)
- **Arrival:** Nyaung-U bus station

**From Yangon:**
- **Distance:** 600 km (373 miles)
- **Duration:** 9-10 hours (overnight recommended)
- **Bus companies:** JJ Express, Shwe Mandalar, Bagan Min Thar
- **Cost:** $20-35 USD (VIP sleeper)
- **Departures:** Evening departures (7-9 PM)
- **Arrival:** Early morning in Nyaung-U

**From Inle Lake (Nyaung Shwe):**
- **Distance:** 250 km (155 miles)
- **Duration:** 6-7 hours
- **Cost:** $15-25 USD
- **Departures:** Morning only (7-8 AM)

### By Train 🚂

**Myanmar Railways:**
- **From Mandalay:** Daily train, 8-9 hours, scenic but slow
- **From Yangon:** Overnight train, 14-16 hours
- **Cost:** $5-20 USD (depending on class)
- **Experience:** Authentic but not recommended for comfort
- **Station:** Bagan Station (near Nyaung-U)
- **Note:** Trains often delayed; not time-sensitive travel

### By River Cruise 🚢

**Irrawaddy River Cruises:**
- **Route:** Mandalay → Bagan → Yangon (or reverse)
- **Duration:** 3-7 days depending on itinerary
- **Operators:** Sanctuary Ananda, Road to Mandalay, Pandaw
- **Cost:** $1,500-5,000+ USD per person (luxury)
- **Experience:** Scenic, leisurely, all-inclusive
- **Best for:** Luxury travelers, photographers, slow travel
- **Season:** November - March (high water season)

## Getting Around

### E-Bike (Electric Scooter) ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Cost:** $8-15 USD per day
- **Range:** 40-60 km on single charge
- **Speed:** 25-35 km/h
- **Availability:** Most hotels rent; shops in Nyaung-U
- **Pros:** Flexible, fun, can reach remote temples
- **Cons:** Battery anxiety, hot midday
- **Tip:** Charge overnight; start early to avoid heat

### Bicycle
- **Cost:** $3-5 USD per day
- **Best for:** Fit travelers, short distances
- **Pros:** Cheap, eco-friendly, good exercise
- **Cons:** Very hot midday, limited range
- **Tip:** Use for sunrise/sunset; rest midday

### Private Car with Driver
- **Cost:** $40-70 USD per day
- **Capacity:** 2-4 passengers
- **Pros:** Air conditioning, driver knows best spots
- **Cons:** Less flexible, more expensive
- **Best for:** Families, hot season, luxury travelers

### Horse Cart
- **Cost:** $20-35 USD per half-day
- **Experience:** Traditional, romantic
- **Pros:** Charming, good for photos
- **Cons:** Slow, animal welfare concerns
- **Tip:** Negotiate price; ensure water for horse

### Taxi
- **Cost:** $15-25 USD for half-day temple circuit
- **Availability:** Nyaung-U town; hotels can arrange
- **Pros:** Convenient, air-conditioned
- **Cons:** Fixed routes, less flexible than e-bike

## When to Visit

### Best Time: November - February 🌟

**Weather:** 20-32°C (68-90°F)
- Cool, dry, pleasant
- Clear skies, perfect for photography
- Peak tourist season
- Ballooning season (October-March)

**Months:**
- **November:** End of rainy season, lush green, balloons resume
- **December-January:** Coolest months, peak season, higher prices
- **February:** Still excellent, warming slightly, fewer crowds

### Shoulder Season: March & October

**March:**
- **Weather:** 25-38°C (77-100°F)
- Getting hot, but manageable
- Fewer tourists
- Lower prices
- Ballooning still operating (early March)

**October:**
- **Weather:** 24-34°C (75-93°F)
- End of monsoon, landscape green
- Ballooning resumes mid-October
- Some rain possible
- Good value

### Wet Season: May - September ☔

**Weather:** 25-35°C (77-95°F)
- Frequent rain, especially July-August
- High humidity
- Lush green landscapes
- Dramatic skies
- Few tourists, lowest prices
- Some temples may have restricted access
- Ballooning NOT operating

**Pros:**
- Lowest prices (up to 50% off)
- Few crowds
- Green, photogenic landscape
- Authentic local experience

**Cons:**
- Daily rain (usually afternoon)
- Humidity
- Some roads muddy
- Limited ballooning

### Avoid: April - Early May ☀️

**Weather:** 30-42°C (86-108°F)
- Extremely hot
- Uncomfortable for temple exploration
- Many tourists avoid
- Deep discounts available
- If visiting: start at 5 AM, rest midday, resume late afternoon

## How Long to Stay

### Minimum: 3 Days
- **Day 1:** Arrival, sunset at Shwesandaw, orientation
- **Day 2:** Major temples (Ananda, Thatbyinnyu, Dhammayangyi), sunrise
- **Day 3:** Sunrise balloon flight (optional), departure

### Recommended: 4-5 Days
- **Day 1:** Arrival, sunset temple, explore Nyaung-U
- **Day 2:** Sunrise at Shwesandaw, major temples (Ananda, Shwezigon, Thatbyinnyu)
- **Day 3:** Sunrise balloon flight, afternoon rest, sunset at Pyathadar
- **Day 4:** Explore lesser-known temples, lacquerware workshop, local villages
- **Day 5:** Sunrise at quiet temple, departure

### Ideal: 7 Days
- Relaxed pace
- Time for multiple sunrise/sunset experiences
- Day trip to Mount Popa (volcano, shrine)
- Deeper cultural immersion
- Photography-focused itinerary
- River cruise arrival/departure

## What to See & Do

### Must-Visit Temples

1. **Ananda Temple** - Most beautiful, four Buddhas
2. **Shwesandaw Pagoda** - Best sunrise/sunset views
3. **Thatbyinnyu Temple** - Tallest, panoramic views
4. **Dhammayangyi Temple** - Largest, impressive brickwork
5. **Shwezigon Pagoda** - Most sacred, golden stupa
6. **Sulamani Temple** - "Crowning Jewel," ornate
7. **Htilominlo Temple** - Detailed carvings
8. **Mingalazedi Pagoda** - Last major temple, elegant

### Experiences

**Sunrise Temple Visit**
- **When:** 5:30 AM arrival
- **Where:** Shwesandaw (crowded) or Paya Thone Zu (quieter)
- **Cost:** Included in ticket
- **Tip:** Bring flashlight for climb

**Sunset Temple Visit**
- **When:** 5:00 PM arrival
- **Where:** Pyathadar, Shwesandaw, riverbank
- **Cost:** Included in ticket
- **Tip:** Arrive early for good spots

**Hot Air Balloon Flight**
- **When:** October-March, sunrise
- **Operators:** Balloon Adventures, Oriental Ballooning
- **Cost:** $350-450 USD
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes flight
- **Tip:** Book months in advance

**Lacquerware Workshop Visit**
- **Where:** Nyaung-U, Myinkaba village
- **Cost:** Free (purchase optional)
- **Experience:** Watch traditional crafting process
- **Tip:** Quality varies; bargain politely

**Mount Popa Day Trip**
- **Distance:** 50 km (31 miles) from Bagan
- **Transport:** $40-60 USD car/driver
- **Experience:** Volcano, nat shrine, panoramic views
- **Duration:** Half-day or full-day
- **Tip:** Combine with local village visit

**Irrawaddy River Sunset Cruise**
- **Cost:** $15-30 USD per person
- **Duration:** 1-2 hours
- **Includes:** Drinks, temple views from water
- **Tip:** Book through hotel

**Local Market Visit**
- **Where:** Nyaung-U Market
- **When:** Morning (best)
- **Experience:** Local life, produce, snacks
- **Tip:** Try mohinga (fish noodle soup)

## Culture & Etiquette

### Dress Code

**Temple Visits:**
- **Shoulders:** Must be covered (no tank tops)
- **Knees:** Must be covered (shorts must be knee-length)
- **Footwear:** Remove shoes and socks before entering temples
- **Recommended:** Lightweight long pants, t-shirts, maxi skirts
- **Carry:** Sarong or scarf for quick cover-up

**General:**
- Myanmar is conservative
- Revealing clothing draws unwanted attention
- Light, breathable fabrics best for heat

### Temple Etiquette

- **Remove shoes:** Before entering any temple compound
- **Socks:** Can be worn if feet sensitive to hot ground
- **Photography:** Generally allowed; ask for monks/people
- **Touching:** Do not touch Buddha statues or sacred objects
- **Sitting:** Never point feet at Buddha statues
- **Circumambulation:** Walk clockwise around stupas
- **Voice:** Keep quiet, respectful
- **Donations:** Welcome at most temples (small bills)

### Interactions

- **Greetings:** "Mingalabar" (hello/blessings)
- **Monks:** Show respect; women should not touch monks
- **Head:** Do not touch anyone's head (sacred)
- **Feet:** Do not point feet at people or Buddha images
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing people
- **Bargaining:** Expected at markets; polite, friendly

### Tipping

- **Restaurants:** 5-10% if service charge not included
- **Guides:** $5-10 USD per day
- **Drivers:** $3-5 USD per day
- **Hotel staff:** $1-2 USD for porters
- **Not expected:** Street food, taxis (round up)

## Practical Information

### Visas

**Tourist Visa:**
- **Required:** Yes, for most nationalities
- **Type:** eVisa (online)
- **Cost:** $50 USD
- **Validity:** 90 days from issue; 28 days stay
- **Processing:** 3-5 business days
- **Apply:** https://evisa.moip.gov.mm
- **Requirements:** Passport photo, passport scan, travel details

**Visa on Arrival:**
- **Available:** Limited nationalities
- **Recommendation:** Apply for eVisa in advance

### Currency

**Myanmar Kyat (MMK)**
- **Exchange:** USD, EUR, SGD accepted
- **ATMs:** Available in Nyaung-U (reliable)
- **Credit cards:** Major hotels, some restaurants
- **Cash:** Still king; carry small bills
- **USD notes:** Must be crisp, new, no marks/tears

**Budget Tip:** Bring USD in small denominations ($1, $5, $10) for easier exchange

### Connectivity

**SIM Cards:**
- **Providers:** Telenor, Ooredoo, Mytel
- **Cost:** $5-15 for SIM + data package
- **Availability:** Airport, Nyaung-U shops
- **Coverage:** Good in Bagan area
- **Registration:** Passport required

**WiFi:**
- **Hotels:** Most have WiFi (quality varies)
- **Cafés:** Many offer free WiFi
- **Speed:** Moderate; fine for email, social media

### Health & Safety

**Vaccinations (Recommended):**
- Hepatitis A & B
- Typhoid
- Tetanus
- Japanese Encephalitis (if extended stay)
- **Malaria:** Low risk in Bagan; prophylaxis usually not needed
- **Consult:** Travel doctor 6-8 weeks before trip

**Water & Food:**
- **Water:** Bottled only (sealed)
- **Ice:** Generally safe in tourist establishments
- **Food:** Cooked food safe; be cautious with street food
- **Fruit:** Peel it yourself
- **Hand sanitizer:** Carry always

**Safety:**
- **Crime:** Low; petty theft rare
- **Scams:** Minor (overcharging, gem scams)
- **Political situation:** Monitor current advisories
- **Protests:** Avoid any demonstrations
- **Emergency:** Tourist police in Nyaung-U

## Current Situation (2025)

### Political Context

Myanmar has experienced political instability since February 2021. Travelers should:

- **Check advisories:** Government travel warnings before booking
- **Monitor news:** Situation can change
- **Register:** With your embassy if traveling
- **Avoid:** Protests, political gatherings, military facilities
- **Respect:** Local sentiment; avoid political discussions
- **Bagan:** Generally stable, less affected than urban centers

### Tourism Status

- **Visas:** Tourist eVisas available
- **Flights:** Limited international connections (via Bangkok, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur)
- **Hotels:** Many operating; some at reduced capacity
- **Guides:** Local guides available, eager for work
- **Prices:** Lower than pre-2021 due to reduced tourism
- **Experience:** Fewer crowds, authentic interactions

### Ethical Considerations

**Supporting Local:**
- Hire local guides
- Stay at locally-owned guesthouses
- Eat at local restaurants
- Buy from local artisans (lacquerware, textiles)

**Temple Preservation:**
- Do not climb restricted temples
- Respect barriers and signage
- Do not touch frescoes or carvings
- Contribute to legitimate conservation efforts

**Cultural Respect:**
- Dress modestly
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Be mindful of religious practices
- Learn basic Burmese phrases
