# The Egyptian Museum - Research

## Overview
- **Official Name:** Museum of Egyptian Antiquities (Tahrir Square)
- **Location:** Tahrir Square, Downtown Cairo
- **Opened:** 1902
- **Collection:** Over 120,000 artifacts
- **Note:** Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) near Giza opening 2024-2025 (partial collections moving)

## The Tutankhamun Collection

### The Golden Mask
- **Weight:** 10.23 kg (22.6 lbs) of solid gold
- **Height:** 54 cm (21 inches)
- **Inlays:** Lapis lazuli, turquoise, carnelian, quartz
- **Discovery:** Howard Carter, 1925
- **Significance:** Most iconic artifact of ancient Egypt

### The Treasures
- **Golden Coffins:** Three nested coffins (innermost is solid gold)
- **Throne:** Golden throne with intimate scene of Tut and Ankhesenamun
- **Canopic Shrine:** Four alabaster canopic jars with gold lids
- **Jewelry:** Pectorals, bracelets, anklets, rings
- **Weapons:** Ceremonial daggers (one with iron from meteorite)
- **Chariots:** Six dismantled chariots
- **Statues:** Ka statues, guardian figures
- **Beds:** Ceremonial beds with animal heads
- **Shrines:** Four nested gilded wooden shrines
- **Total Items:** 5,398 objects from KV62

## Other Highlights

### Old Kingdom
- **Narmer Palette:** Unification of Egypt (c. 3100 BCE)
- **Khafre Statue:** Seated pharaoh with Horus falcon
- **Scribe Statue:** Painted limestone, incredibly lifelike
- **Rahotep and Nofret:** Painted statues with crystal eyes

### Middle Kingdom
- **Meketre Models:** Wooden models of daily life (boats, workshops, gardens)
- **Senusret III:** Powerful royal portraits showing aging pharaoh
- **Hatshepsut:** Female pharaoh's statues and sphinxes

### New Kingdom
- **Akhenaten:** Revolutionary pharaoh's distinctive art style
- **Nefertiti:** Bust is in Berlin, but other artifacts here
- **Ramesses II:** Multiple statues, including colossal pieces
- **Tutankhamun:** The complete collection (until GEM opens)

### Royal Mummies
- **Location:** Separate room (requires extra ticket)
- **Pharaohs Include:**
  - Hatshepsut
  - Thutmose III
  - Seti I (best preserved)
  - Ramesses II (the Great)
  - Ramesses III
  - And 10+ others
- **Discovery:** Deir el-Bahri cache (1881) and KV35 cache (1898)
- **Note:** Some mummies moved to National Museum of Egyptian Civilization

## Museum Layout
- **Ground Floor:** Chronological (Old Kingdom → New Kingdom → Late Period)
- **First Floor:** Thematic (Tutankhamun, Mummies, Jewelry, Papyri, Coins)
- **Garden:** Sarcophagi and large sculptures

## Modern Experience
- **Tickets:** Main museum + extra for Royal Mummies room
- **Time Needed:** Minimum 3-4 hours, full day ideal
- **Guides:** Highly recommended (context is crucial)
- **Photography:** Main halls allowed (no flash), Tut room extra fee, Mummies room NO photography
- **Best Time:** Opening time (9 AM) or late afternoon
- **Facilities:** Café, gift shop, restrooms

## The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)
- **Location:** Near Giza Plateau (2 km from pyramids)
- **Size:** Largest archaeological museum in the world
- **Status:** Soft opening 2023, full opening expected 2024-2025
- **Tut Collection:** Will house complete Tutankhamun collection
- **Architecture:** Stunning modern design with pyramid views
- **Note:** Tahrir museum may remain open with reduced collection

## Practical Tips
- Hire a licensed guide (artifact context is invaluable)
- Start early (museum opens 9 AM, gets crowded by 11 AM)
- Bring water (limited inside)
- Wear comfortable shoes (extensive walking)
- Photography pass worth it for personal memories
- Allow full day if possible
- Combine with nearby Cairo Tower, Khan el-Khalili

## Cultural Significance
- Houses the world's most important collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts
- Many items found nowhere else on Earth
- Tells the complete story of 5,000 years of Egyptian civilization
- Continues to reveal new discoveries through ongoing research
- Bridge between ancient world and modern understanding
