## EPISODE 023 DETAILS (For Internal Planning)

**Working Title:** "The Thin Air" / "The Roof of the World"

**Location:** La Rinconada, Peru (or similar high-altitude settlement)
- Elevation: 5,100 meters (16,700 feet)
- Highest permanent human settlement on Earth
- Oxygen: ~50% of sea level
- Population: ~30,000-50,000 people

**Themes:**
- Human endurance at physical limits
- What drives people to live where they shouldn't survive
- Mining, gold, economic desperation
- The body's adaptation to extreme altitude
- Community at the edge of habitability

**Visual Palette:**
- White (snow, ice)
- Grey (rock, mining)
- Blue (thin sky, cold)
- Gold (the reason they're there)

**Connection to Episode 022:**
- Both about edges (geographic vs. physiological)
- Both about human ambition and survival
- Easter Island: What we leave behind
- La Rinconada: What we're willing to endure
- Isolation (Pacific vs. Mountain)
- Community persistence against odds
