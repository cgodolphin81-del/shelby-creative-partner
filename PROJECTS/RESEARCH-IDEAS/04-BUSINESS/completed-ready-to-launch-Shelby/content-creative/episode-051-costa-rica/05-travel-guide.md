## ✈️ Getting There

### International Airports

**Juan Santamaría International Airport (SJO)**
- Location: Alajuela, 20 minutes from San José
- Best for: Central Valley, Pacific Coast, Arenal, Monteverde
- Airlines: United, American, Delta, JetBlue, Southwest, Air Canada, Avianca, Copa
- Facilities: Currency exchange, ATMs, car rentals, restaurants, duty-free

**Daniel Oduber Quirós International Airport (LIR)**
- Location: Liberia, Guanacaste
- Best for: Pacific beaches (Tamarindo, Conchal, Flamingo, Samara)
- Airlines: United, American, Delta, JetBlue, Southwest, Air Canada
- Smaller, easier to navigate than SJO

### Flight Costs (Round-Trip, 2025-2026)

| From | Low Season | High Season |
|------|------------|-------------|
| US East Coast | $300-500 | $500-800 |

**Booking Tips:**
- Book 2-3 months in advance for best prices
- Tuesday/Wednesday flights often cheaper
- Consider flying into one airport, out of another (open-jaw)
- Set price alerts on Google Flights, Skyscanner

## 📅 When to Visit

### Seasons

**High Season (December - April)**
- Weather: Dry, sunny, 75-85°F (24-29°C)
- Crowds: Highest
- Prices: Peak rates
- Best for: Beaches, hiking, wildlife viewing
- Note: Christmas/New Year and Easter are most expensive weeks

**Green Season / Shoulder (May - July, November)**
- Weather: Morning sun, afternoon showers, lush greenery
- Crowds: Moderate
- Prices: 20-40% lower than high season
- Best for: Budget travelers, photographers, birdwatching
- Note: Rain usually brief, predictable (afternoon)

**Low Season (August - October)**
- Weather: Rainiest period, especially Pacific side
- Crowds: Lowest
- Prices: Best deals
- Best for: Caribbean coast (drier), budget travelers, solitude
- Note: Some lodges close, roads may be challenging

### Wildlife Timing

| Wildlife | Best Time | Location |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| Sea Turtles (Green) | July-October | Tortuguero |
| Sea Turtles (Olive Ridley) | Year-round | Ostional |

| Resplendent Quetzal | March-July | Monteverde, Savegre |
| Scarlet Macaws | Year-round | Osa, Manuel Antonio |

## 🦋 Wildlife Viewing Guide

### Best Locations by Species

| Species | Best Location(s) | Tips |
|---------|-----------------|------|
| **Sloths** | Manuel Antonio, La Fortuna, Cahuita | Hire a guide with scope; they spot what you'll miss |
| **Howler Monkeys** | Throughout country | Listen at dawn; follow the sound |
| **White-Faced Capuchins** | Manuel Antonio, Palo Verde | Often visible from trails |
| **Spider Monkeys** | Corcovado, Manuel Antonio | Endangered, less common |
| **Toucans** | Monteverde, Arenal, Osa | Look for fruiting trees |
| **Quetzals** | Monteverde, Savegre (Mar-Jul) | Hire specialist bird guide |
| **Scarlet Macaws** | Osa, Manuel Antonio, Carara | Dawn/dusk best |
| **Jaguars** | Corcovado (rare!) | Very elusive; manage expectations |
| **Sea Turtles** | Tortuguero (Jul-Oct), Ostional | Book nesting tour in advance |
| **Humpback Whales** | Osa Peninsula (Dec-Apr, Aug-Oct) | Boat tour required |
| **Red-Eyed Tree Frogs** | Night walks everywhere | Guides with flashlights essential |

### Wildlife Viewing Ethics

✅ **DO:**
- Keep respectful distance (use zoom lenses)
- Hire local guides (they spot more, support community)
- Stay on marked trails
- Speak quietly
- Follow park rules

❌ **DON'T:**
- Touch or feed wildlife
- Use flash photography on animals
- Make loud noises or sudden movements
- Leave trash
- Buy souvenirs made from wildlife products

## 🍽️ Food & Drink

### Must-Try Dishes

**Gallo Pinto**
- Rice and beans, typically breakfast
- Served with eggs, plantains, sour cream, cheese
- National dish

**Casado**
- Lunch plate: rice, beans, protein (fish/chicken/beef), salad, fried plantains
- "Casado" means "married" — ingredients married together
- $6-12 at sodas

**Ceviche**
- Raw fish "cooked" in lime juice
- Fresh, citrusy, often with cilantro and onion
- Great beach food

**Tamales**
- Corn dough filled with meat, wrapped in banana leaves
- Traditional for Christmas, available year-round

**Chifrijo**
- Beans, rice, fried pork, pico de gallo, avocado
- Bar snack, often with tortilla chips

### Where to Eat

**Sodas**
- Small, local, family-run restaurants
- Cheapest, most authentic option
- Look for places filled with locals
- Cash preferred

**Mercados (Markets)**
- Food courts with multiple vendors
- Great variety, local atmosphere
- San José: Mercado Central

**Restaurants**
- Tourist areas have international options
- Prices 2-3x sodas
- Quality varies

### Dietary Restrictions

- Vegetarian/Vegan: Increasingly common, especially in tourist areas
- Gluten-Free: Limited but available; communicate clearly
- Allergies: Staff generally accommodating; learn key Spanish phrases

### Water Safety

- Tap water safe in most of country (Central Valley, major tourist areas)
- Use bottled/purified water in remote areas, Caribbean coast
- Many hotels provide filtered water
- Bring reusable bottle with filter (reduces plastic)

## 🚗 Getting Around

### Transportation Options

**Public Buses**
- Cheapest option
- Extensive network
- Slow, can be crowded
- Book popular routes in advance
- Companies: Grayline, Tracopa, Andamios del Sur

**Shared Shuttles**
- Tourist-focused, door-to-door
- $30-60 per person for common routes
- Comfortable, reliable
- Book through hotels or online

**Private Transfers**
- Most convenient, priciest
- $60-150 per vehicle depending on distance
- Good for families, groups, tight schedules

**Car Rental**
- Freedom and flexibility
- 4x4 recommended (many unpaved roads)
- Mandatory insurance (~$20-30/day additional)
- Drive on right, speed limits in km/h
- Watch for pedestrians, animals, potholes

**Domestic Flights**
- Sansa Airlines, Aerobell
- SJO to Puerto Jiménez (Osa), Liberia, Tambor, Tortuguero
- $100-200 per person
- Saves time, scenic

**Boats**
- Required for Tortuguero
- Water taxis to some beaches
- Ferry to Paquera (Guanacaste shortcut)

## 🛡️ Safety & Health

### Safety

Costa Rica is one of Latin America's safest countries, but use common sense:

✅ **Safe Practices:**
- Use hotel safes for valuables
- Don't leave belongings unattended on beaches
- Avoid displaying expensive jewelry/electronics
- Use authorized taxis (red with yellow triangle) or Uber
- Don't hike alone in remote areas
- Swim only at designated beaches (rip currents common)

⚠️ **Watch For:**
- Petty theft (especially in San José, bus stations)
- Car break-ins (don't leave anything visible)
- Rip currents (ask locals about safe swimming spots)
- Wildlife (respect distance, don't feed)

### Health

**Vaccinations:**
- Routine vaccines up to date
- Hepatitis A & B recommended
- Typhoid recommended for some areas
- Yellow fever: Required only if arriving from endemic country
- Malaria/Zika: Low risk; consult travel doctor

**Travel Insurance:**
- **Strongly recommended**
- Medical evacuation can cost $50,000+
- Verify coverage for adventure activities
- Recommended providers: World Nomads, SafetyWing, Allianz

**Medical Care:**
- Quality care in San José (public and private hospitals)
- Tourist areas have clinics
- Pharmacies widely available
- Bring prescription medications in original containers

**Sun & Insects:**
- Sunscreen essential (equatorial sun is strong)
- Insect repellent (DEET or picaridin)
- Light, long clothing for evening (mosquitoes)
- Some areas have no-see-ums (tiny biting flies)

## 💡 Practical Tips

### Money

- USD widely accepted (get change in colones)
- Bring small bills ($1, $5, $10, $20); $50+ often rejected
- ATMs widely available (look for Banco Nacional, BAC)
- Credit cards accepted at most hotels, restaurants, tour operators
- Notify bank before travel
- Some places add 3-5% fee for credit card use

### Language

- Spanish is official language
- English common in tourist areas, hotels, tours
- Learning basic Spanish phrases appreciated:
  - *Buenos días* — Good morning
  - *Por favor* — Please
  - *Gracias* — Thank you
  - *¿Cuánto cuesta?* — How much?
  - *La cuenta, por favor* — The check, please
  - *¿Dónde está...?* — Where is...?
  - *Pura Vida* — Pure life (greeting, thanks, all-purpose)

### Tipping

- Restaurants: 10% service charge often included; additional 5-10% for excellent service
- Guides: $10-20 per person per day
- Drivers: $5-10 per person per day
- Hotel housekeeping: $1-5 per day
- Not mandatory but appreciated

### Connectivity

- WiFi common in hotels, restaurants, cafes
- Quality varies in remote areas
- SIM cards available at airport (Kolbi, Claro, Movistar)
- eSIM options: Airalo, Holafly
- US plans: Many include Costa Rica (verify with carrier)

### Cultural Etiquette

- Ticos (Costa Ricans) are warm, polite, relationship-focused
- Greetings important — say *buenos días/tardes* when entering shops
- "Tico time" — schedules flexible; patience appreciated
- Dress modestly when not at beach
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Environmental consciousness highly valued — don't litter

### Sustainability

Costa Rica leads in eco-tourism. Help protect it:

- Choose certified sustainable lodges (CST certification)
- Don't touch or feed wildlife
- Stay on marked trails
- Use reef-safe sunscreen
- Minimize plastic (bring reusable bottle, bag)
- Support local businesses
- Respect local customs and communities
