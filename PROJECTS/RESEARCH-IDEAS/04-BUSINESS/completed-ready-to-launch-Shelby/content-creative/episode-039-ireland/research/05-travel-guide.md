## 🛫 GETTING THERE

### Flights
- **Dublin Airport (DUB):** Main international gateway
  - Direct flights from: New York (7hrs), Boston (6.5hrs), Chicago (8hrs), Los Angeles (10.5hrs)
  - European hubs: London (1.5hrs), Amsterdam (2hrs), Paris (2hrs), Frankfurt (2.5hrs)
  - Airlines: Aer Lingus, Ryanair, Delta, United, American, British Airways
  
- **Cork Airport (ORK):** Smaller, closer to Ring of Kerry
  - Limited international flights (mainly UK/Europe)
  
- **Shannon Airport (SNN):** Closest to Cliffs of Moher (1.5 hours)
  - Direct flights from: New York, Boston, Chicago (seasonal)
  - Good option for west coast exploration

### Entry Requirements
- **US/UK/Canadian citizens:** No visa required for stays up to 90 days
- **Passport:** Must be valid for duration of stay
- **Currency:** Euro (€) - Republic of Ireland (Northern Ireland uses GBP)
- **Customs:** Duty-free allowances apply for alcohol, tobacco, gifts

## 🚗 GETTING AROUND

### Car Rental (RECOMMENDED for Ring of Kerry/Cliffs)
**Companies:**
- Hertz, Avis, Budget, Enterprise (international)
- Europcar, Sixt (European)
- Local: Dan Dooley, Irish Car Rental

**Costs:**
- Economy: €40-60/day
- Compact: €50-80/day
- Automatic transmission: +€15-20/day (most are manual)
- Insurance: €20-40/day (highly recommended)
- Fuel: €1.70-1.90/liter (~€7-8/gallon)

**Important Notes:**
- **Drive on LEFT side** (opposite of US/Europe)
- **Manual transmission** is standard (request automatic if needed)
- **Narrow roads** in countryside (pull over for oncoming buses)
- **Sheep/cattle** on rural roads (slow down!)
- **Age requirement:** 25+ (under 25 surcharge: €30-50/day)
- **Credit card** required for deposit (€1000-2000 hold)

### Public Transportation
**Train (Irish Rail - Iarnród Éireann):**
- Dublin → Killarney: 3.5 hours, €30-50 (advance booking cheaper)
- Dublin → Galway: 2.5 hours, €25-40
- Dublin → Cork: 2.5 hours, €25-40
- Book at: www.irishrail.ie

**Bus (Bus Éireann + Private Tours):**
- Dublin → Cliffs of Moher day tours: €40-60 (includes transport + guide)
- Killarney → Ring of Kerry tours: €35-50 (full day)
- City to city: €15-30

**Best For:** Solo travelers, non-drivers, budget-conscious

### Organized Tours
**Pros:** No driving stress, expert guides, all logistics handled
**Cons:** Less flexibility, fixed schedules, group pace

**Recommended Tour Companies:**
- Paddywagon Tours (budget-friendly, young crowd)
- Irish Day Tours (small groups)
- Wild Rover Tours (adventure-focused)

## 🏨 ACCOMMODATION

### Budget (€40-80/night)
- **Hostels:** Dublin, Killarney, Galway (€25-40/dorm, €60-80/private)
- **Budget B&Bs:** Family-run, breakfast included
- **Recommendations:** 
  - Jacobs Inn (Dublin) - modern hostel
  - Killarney International Hostel
  - Galway City Hostel

### Mid-Range (€80-150/night)
- **B&Bs (Bed & Breakfast):** Traditional Irish experience
  - Breakfast included (full Irish breakfast!)
  - Family-run, local knowledge
  - Book via: booking.com, Airbnb, direct
  
- **Guesthouses:** Similar to B&Bs, more rooms
- **Recommendations:**
  - The Killarney Park Hotel (€120-180)
  - Cliffs of Moher Hotel (Doolin, €100-150)
  - Dublin City B&Bs (various, €90-140)

### Luxury (€150-400+/night)
- **Castle Hotels:** Stay in actual castles!
  - Dromoland Castle (€400-800/night)
  - Ashford Castle (€500-1000/night)
  - Ballyfin (€600-1200/night)
  
- **Boutique Hotels:**
  - The Conrad (Dublin, €200-400)
  - The Europe Hotel (Killarney, €180-350)
  - Ballyfin (Laois, €800+)

### Booking Tips
- **Book ahead** for summer (June-August) and festivals
- **B&Bs** often cheaper booking direct (call or email)
- **Breakfast included** = save €15-20/day
- **Rural areas** = limited options, plan ahead

## 🍺 FOOD & DRINK

### Traditional Irish Food to Try
- **Full Irish Breakfast:** Eggs, bacon, sausage, black/white pudding, beans, toast (€12-18)
- **Fish and Chips:** Fresh Atlantic cod, thick chips (€12-18)
- **Irish Stew:** Lamb, potatoes, carrots, onions (€15-22)
- **Seafood Chowder:** Creamy, with fresh fish and shellfish (€12-18)
- **Boxty:** Potato pancake, various fillings (€10-15)
- **Coddle:** Dublin dish, sausage, bacon, potatoes (€12-16)
- **Soda Bread:** Fresh-baked, with butter (often complimentary)

### Pub Culture
- **Ordering:** Go to the bar (not table service in traditional pubs)
- **Tipping:** Not expected for drinks, 10% for food
- **Pint of Guinness:** €5-7 (takes 119.5 seconds to pour properly!)
- **Other Drinks:**
  - Irish Coffee: €6-8 (invented at Foynes, 1943)
  - Jameson Whiskey: €5-8 (single measure)
  - Cider (Magners/Bulmers): €5-7
  - Local Craft Beers: €6-8

### Recommended Pubs
**Dublin:**
- The Brazen Head (est. 1198, oldest pub in Ireland)
- Temple Bar (touristy but iconic)
- The Long Hall (Victorian interior)
- Kehoe's (traditional, no food)

**Killarney:**
- O'Connor's Traditional Pub
- The Killarney Inn
- Courtney's Bar

**Doolin (Cliffs area):**
- McDermott's Pub (live trad music nightly)
- Gus O'Connor's Pub
- Doolin Inn

## 🌦️ WHEN TO VISIT

### Best Time: May-June, September
- **Weather:** Mild (15-20°C / 59-68°F), less rain than winter
- **Crowds:** Moderate (avoid July-August peak)
- **Daylight:** Long days (sunset 9:30pm in June)
- **Prices:** Mid-range (not peak summer prices)
- **Nature:** Wildflowers (May-June), heather blooms (August-September)

### Peak Season: July-August
- **Pros:** Warmest weather, longest days, all attractions open
- **Cons:** Crowded, expensive, book everything months ahead
- **Tip:** Avoid cruise ship days in Killarney/Cork (check schedules)

### Shoulder Season: April, October
- **Pros:** Fewer crowds, lower prices, spring flowers or autumn colors
- **Cons:** Unpredictable weather, some seasonal closures
- **Note:** Good for photographers (dramatic skies)

### Winter: November-March
- **Pros:** Very few tourists, lowest prices, cozy pub atmosphere
- **Cons:** Short days (sunset 4:30pm in December), some closures, cold/rainy
- **Special:** Christmas markets (Dublin, Galway), St. Patrick's Day (March 17)

## 📱 PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money
- **Currency:** Euro (€)
- **Credit Cards:** Widely accepted (Visa, Mastercard)
- **ATMs:** Widely available (called "cash machines")
- **Tipping:** 10-15% restaurants, not expected in pubs
- **Budget:** Bring €200-300 cash for small towns, markets

### Connectivity
- **SIM Cards:** Buy at airport or in Dublin (Three, Vodafone, Eir)
- **Cost:** €20-40 for 10-20GB data (2 weeks)
- **WiFi:** Widely available in hotels, pubs, cafes
- **US Phones:** Most work (check with carrier)

### Packing List
- **Waterproof jacket** (essential, even in summer)
- **Layers** (weather changes quickly)
- **Comfortable walking shoes** (waterproof if possible)
- **Umbrella** (compact, wind-resistant)
- **Adapter:** Type G (UK/Ireland 3-pin)
- **Camera** (you'll want it!)
- **Daypack** (for hikes, day trips)

### Safety
- **Very safe country** (low crime rate)
- **Pickpockets:** Rare, but watch bags in crowded Dublin
- **Driving:** Narrow roads, drive defensively
- **Cliffs:** Stay on marked paths (no barriers in many areas)
- **Emergency:** 112 or 999 (works like 911)

### Cultural Etiquette
- **Pubs:** Order at bar, not table (traditional pubs)
- **Conversation:** Friendly, witty, self-deprecating humor
- **Politics:** Avoid Northern Ireland unless local brings it up
- **Religion:** Historically Catholic, now secularizing
- **"Craic":** Pronounced "crack" = fun, good times, gossip

## 🎵 CULTURAL EXPERIENCES

### Traditional Music Sessions
- **What:** Live trad music in pubs (fiddle, whistle, pipes, bodhrán)
- **When:** Nightly in most towns (7-11pm)
- **Cost:** Free (buy a drink)
- **Etiquette:** Listen during songs, applaud after sets, join in if you play

**Best Pubs for Trad Music:**
- McDermott's (Doolin)
- The Front Bar (Killarney)
- Tigh Neachtain (Galway)
- The Cobblestone (Dublin - authentic, not touristy)

### Festivals
- **St. Patrick's Day** (March 17, nationwide)
- **Galway Arts Festival** (July)
- **Doolin Folk Festival** (August)
- **Killarney Christmas Festival** (December)

### Sports
- **Gaelic Football:** Attend a match (Croke Park, Dublin)
- **Hurling:** Ancient Irish sport (fast-paced, exciting)
- **Horse Racing:** Punchestown, Cheltenham Festival

## 🚨 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Underestimating drive times** (Irish roads are slow, scenic)
2. **Not booking ahead** (summer = fully booked)
3. **Only visiting Dublin** (the real Ireland is in the countryside)
4. **Expecting Mediterranean weather** (it rains, pack accordingly)
5. **Skipping the pubs** (pub culture is essential Ireland)
6. **Driving Ring of Kerry counter-clockwise** (tours go clockwise, you'll meet them head-on)
7. **Not trying traditional food** (beyond just potatoes!)
8. **Rushing** (Ireland rewards slow travel)
