## Versailles & Paris, France

## GETTING THERE

### From North America
**Flights to Paris:**
- **Charles de Gaulle (CDG):** Main international airport, 25km northeast of Paris
- **Orly (ORY):** Secondary airport, 13km south of Paris
- **Flight time:** 7-8 hours from East Coast, 10-11 hours from West Coast
- **Airlines:** Air France, Delta, United, American, French Bee (budget)

### From Within Europe
- **Eurostar:** London to Paris (2h 15m), arrives at Gare du Nord
- **High-speed trains:** From Amsterdam, Brussels, Barcelona, Frankfurt
- **Budget airlines:** EasyJet, Ryanair, Transavia (often to Beauvais, 85km from Paris)

### Airport to City Center

**From CDG:**
- **RER B Train:** €11.80, 35-40 minutes to central Paris (Châtelet, Saint-Michel)
- **Roissybus:** €16, 60 minutes to Opéra
- **Taxi/Uber:** €50-70, 45-60 minutes depending on traffic
- **Private transfer:** €60-100, meet-and-greet service

**From ORLY:**
- **Orlybus:** €9.50, 30 minutes to Denfert-Rochereau
- **Tram T7 + Metro:** €2.15, 45 minutes
- **Taxi/Uber:** €35-50, 30-45 minutes
- **Orlyval + RER B:** €13.45, 35 minutes

## GETTING AROUND PARIS

### Metro
- **Single ticket (t+):** €2.15
- **Carnet (10 tickets):** €17.35 (save 19%)
- **Day pass (Mobilis zones 1-2):** €8.95
- **Weekly pass (Navigo Découverte):** €30.75 + €5 for card (zones 1-5, Mon-Sun only)
- **Hours:** 5:30 AM - 1:15 AM (2:15 AM Fri/Sat)
- **Coverage:** All major attractions in zones 1-2

**Tip:** Buy Navigo Découverte if staying Monday-Sunday week. Otherwise, carnets offer best value.

### RER (Regional Train)
- **To Versailles:** RER C from central Paris, €4.45 each way, 35-40 minutes
- **To Disneyland:** RER A, €7.60 each way, 35 minutes
- **To CDG Airport:** RER B, €11.80, 35-40 minutes

### Bus
- **Single ticket:** €2.15 (same as metro, interchangeable)
- **Good for:** Sightseeing, areas not well-served by metro
- **Tourist buses:** Hop-on-hop-off, €35-45/day

### Walking
- **Best way to see Paris:** Many attractions within walking distance
- **Average walk:** 20-30 minutes between major sites in central Paris
- **Comfortable shoes essential:** Cobblestones, metro stairs

### Bikes & Scooters
- **Vélib' bike share:** €3/day or €15/month, first 30 min free
- **E-scooters:** Lime, Bird, Tier (€1 unlock + €0.25/min)
- **Caution:** Traffic can be aggressive, bike lanes improving

### Taxis & Ride-Share
- **Uber/Bolt/Heetch:** Available throughout Paris
- **Taxi stands:** Throughout city, can hail on street
- **Surge pricing:** Common during rush hour, rain, strikes

## VERSAILLES SPECIFIC

### Getting There
- **RER C:** From central Paris (Gare d'Austerlitz, Saint-Michel, Musée d'Orsay), €4.45 each way, 35-40 min
- **Train from Montparnasse:** SNCF to Versailles Chantiers, €5-10, 20 min
- **Guided tour:** €60-100 including transport and entry
- **Taxi/Uber:** €60-80 each way, 45-60 min

### Tickets
- **Palace only:** €21
- **Passport (Palace + Gardens + Trianons):** €32
- **2-day Passport:** €45
- **Free:** Under 18, EU under 26, first Sunday Nov-Mar
- **Book online:** Essential in peak season, skip ticket lines

### Hours (2025)
- **Palace:** Tue-Sun 9:00 AM - 6:30 PM (closed Mon)
- **Gardens:** Daily 8:00 AM - 8:30 PM (summer), shorter in winter
- **Trianons:** Tue-Sun 12:00 PM - 6:30 PM (closed Mon)

### Best Times to Visit
- **Arrive:** At opening (9:00 AM) or after 2:30 PM
- **Days:** Weekdays (Tue-Fri), avoid weekends
- **Season:** April-May, September-October (pleasant weather, fewer crowds)
- **Avoid:** Summer weekends, French holidays, fountain show days (more expensive, crowded)

### Fountain Shows
- **When:** Select weekends April-October, Tue/Sat in summer
- **Cost:** Additional €9.50 on top of palace ticket
- **Worth it:** Yes, spectacular, but adds to cost and crowds

### How Much Time
- **Minimum:** 4-5 hours (palace + gardens)
- **Ideal:** Full day (palace, gardens, Trianons, Marie Antoinette's estate)
- **With kids:** Half day maximum, they'll get bored

## FOOD & DINING

### French Meal Times
- **Breakfast (Petit Déjeuner):** 7-10 AM
- **Lunch (Déjeuner):** 12-2 PM (kitchens close between 2-7 PM)
- **Dinner (Dîner):** 7:30-10 PM

### Budget Eats (€5-15)
- **Bakeries:** Croissant €1.20, baguette sandwich €5-7
- **Crêperies:** Sweet crêpe €4-6, savory galette €8-12
- **Kebabs:** €6-8 (late night staple)
- **Supermarkets:** Monoprix, Carrefour, Franprix
- **Street food:** Marais falafel (€8-10)

### Mid-Range (€15-40)
- **Bistros:** Plat du jour €15-20, 2 courses €25-35
- **Brasseries:** Classic French fare, €20-40 per person
- **Wine bars:** Small plates, natural wine, €25-40

### Fine Dining (€50-200+)
- **Michelin-starred:** 3-course €80-200+
- **Tasting menus:** €100-300+
- **Reservations:** Essential, book weeks/months ahead

### Must-Try Foods
- Croissants & pain au chocolat
- Baguette (fresh, same day)
- Macarons (Ladurée, Pierre Hermé)
- Crêpes and galettes
- Coq au vin
- Boeuf bourguignon
- Ratatouille
- Crème brûlée
- Tarte Tatin

- **Café de Flore:** Sartre's haunt, same as above

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### Seasons

**Spring (April-June):**
- **Weather:** 10-20°C (50-68°F), mild, occasional rain
- **Crowds:** Moderate, increasing toward summer
- **Pros:** Blooming gardens, pleasant temps, outdoor cafés
- **Cons:** Unpredictable weather, Easter crowds
- **Best for:** First-time visitors, photographers, garden lovers

**Summer (July-August):**
- **Weather:** 15-25°C (59-77°F), can hit 35°C+ (95°F+)
- **Crowds:** Peak season, longest lines
- **Pros:** Long days (sunset 10 PM), outdoor events, festivals
- **Cons:** Hot, crowded, expensive, some locals away (shops close)
- **Best for:** Families (school holidays), festival-goers

**Fall (September-October):**
- **Weather:** 10-20°C (50-68°F), crisp, beautiful foliage
- **Crowds:** Decreasing after September
- **Pros:** Pleasant weather, harvest season, fashion week
- **Cons:** Rain increases in October, days shorten
- **Best for:** Foodies, culture lovers, photographers

**Winter (November-March):**
- **Weather:** 3-10°C (37-50°F), cold, can be grey
- **Crowds:** Lowest (except Christmas/New Year's)
- **Pros:** Fewer tourists, lower prices, Christmas markets, museum free days
- **Cons:** Cold, short days, some attractions closed/reduced hours
- **Best for:** Budget travelers, museum lovers, romance seekers

### Days of Week
- **Museums:** Many closed Monday OR Tuesday (Louvre closed Tue, Orsay closed Mon)
- **Versailles:** Closed Monday, busiest weekends
- **Shops:** Many close Sunday (except Marais, tourist areas)
- **Restaurants:** Many close Sunday night and/or Monday

### Events to Consider
- **Paris Fashion Week:** Feb/Mar, Sep/Oct (crowded, expensive)
- **Bastille Day:** July 14 (parade, fireworks, closures)
- **Nuit Blanche:** First Saturday in October (all-night arts festival)
- **Christmas Markets:** Late Nov-Dec (Champs-Élysées, La Défense)
- **Sales (Soldes):** January and June (major shopping discounts)

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Language
- **Learn basics:** Bonjour, merci, s'il vous plaît, pardon, au revoir
- **Always greet:** Say "Bonjour" before any interaction
- **English:** Widely spoken in tourist areas, less so in neighborhoods
- **Apps:** Google Translate, DeepL for menus/signs

### Money
- **Currency:** Euro (€)
- **Cards:** Widely accepted, contactless common
- **Cash:** Still needed for small purchases, markets, tips
- **ATMs:** Use bank ATMs (BNP, Société Générale), avoid Euronet
- **Tipping:** Service included, leave €1-2 at cafés, 5-10% at restaurants for exceptional service

### Safety
- **Pickpockets:** Major issue in tourist areas (Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Metro)
- **Scams:** Petition scammers, bracelet scammers, ring scam
- **Safe areas:** Most of Paris is safe, use normal city precautions
- **Avoid:** Empty metro cars late night, certain suburbs (banlieues)
- **Emergency:** 112 (EU-wide), 17 (police), 15 (medical)

### Connectivity
- **SIM cards:** Orange, SFR, Bouygues, Free (€20-40 for tourist SIM)
- **eSIM:** Airalo, Holafly (data-only, convenient)
- **WiFi:** Cafés, hotels, some public spaces
- **Free WiFi:** Many museums, some parks

### Power
- **Outlets:** Type C/E (220V, 50Hz)
- **Adapters:** US visitors need plug adapter
- **Voltage:** Most electronics dual-voltage, check labels

### Etiquette
- **Greetings:** Always say bonjour when entering shops/cafés
- **Dress:** Parisians dress well, smart casual for most restaurants
- **Volume:** Speak quietly in public, especially on Metro
- **Photos:** Ask before photographing people, no flash in museums
- **Dining:** Keep hands on table (not lap), say bon appetit

### Accessibility
- **Metro:** Limited accessibility, many stations have stairs
- **Museums:** Most are accessible, check ahead
- **Taxis:** Can request accessible vehicles
- **Versailles:** Wheelchair accessible, loan chairs available
