357 mirrors. 1 king. Infinite reflections. ✨

The Hall of Mirrors at Versailles wasn't built for beauty—it was built as a power move. Louis XIV wanted to prove France could outshine Venice at their own game. So he lured Venetian glassmakers away (some say they were assassinated in retaliation) and created the most opulent room in Europe.

Today, it's still breathtaking. Still golden. Still reflecting light the way the Sun King intended.

Some things outlive their creators. Some beauty is worth the cost.

### Post 2: Eiffel Tower Sunset
**Image:** Eiffel Tower at golden hour

**Caption:**
They called it a monstrosity. Said it should be torn down after 20 years. 🗼

The artists of Paris signed a petition against the Eiffel Tower. 300 writers and architects called it "useless and monstrous." Gustave Eiffel built it anyway for the 1889 World's Fair.

Now? It's the most recognizable structure on Earth. 7 million visitors a year. The symbol of Paris itself.

Sometimes the things people hate the most become the things we can't imagine living without.

Progress always costs something. Usually it costs the past.

### Post 4: Mona Lisa
**Image:** Mona Lisa viewing experience (crowd + painting)

**Caption:**
She's smaller than you expect. Everyone says that. 🖼️

The Mona Lisa. 77cm x 53cm. A woman who may or may not have existed, painted by a man who carried her with him until he died.

Leonardo worked on her for 16 years. Never delivered her to the patron. Maybe he wasn't satisfied. Maybe he loved her too much to let go. Maybe he knew.

Now she hangs behind bulletproof glass, surrounded by crowds, watched by millions who will all die too.

Art is the lie that tells the truth.
