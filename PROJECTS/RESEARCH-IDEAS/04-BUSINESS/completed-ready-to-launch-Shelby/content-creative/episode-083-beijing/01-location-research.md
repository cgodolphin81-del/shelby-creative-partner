## THE FORBIDDEN CITY (紫禁城 - Zǐjìnchéng)

### Overview
- **Location:** Dongcheng District, central Beijing
- **Built:** 1406-1420 (Ming Dynasty)
- **Size:** 180 acres (72 hectares), 980 surviving buildings
- **UNESCO World Heritage:** 1987
- **Visitors:** 17+ million annually (pre-pandemic)
- **Emperors:** Home to 24 emperors (Ming and Qing dynasties)

### Key Areas

#### 1. The Outer Court (Front Court)
- **Meridian Gate (Wumen):** Main southern entrance, "Five Phoenix Gate"
- **Hall of Supreme Harmony (Taihedian):** Largest hall, coronations and imperial weddings
- **Hall of Central Harmony (Zhonghedian):** Emperor's preparation room
- **Hall of Preserving Harmony (Baohedian):** Imperial examinations and banquets

#### 2. The Inner Court
- **Palace of Heavenly Purity (Qianqinggong):** Emperor's residence
- **Hall of Union (Jiaotaidian):** Imperial seals stored here
- **Palace of Earthly Tranquility (Kunninggong):** Empress's residence
- **Six Eastern Palaces:** Concubines' quarters
- **Six Western Palaces:** Additional concubine residences
- **Imperial Garden:** 12,000 sq meters, ancient cypress trees, rockeries

#### 3. Notable Features
- **Nine Dragon Screen:** Glazed tile screen wall with nine dragons
- **Clock and Watch Gallery:** Imperial timepiece collection
- **Treasure Gallery:** Crown jewels, jade, gold artifacts
- **Palace Museum:** Over 1.8 million artifacts

### Historical Significance
- Symbol of imperial power for 500 years
- Forbidden to commoners until 1925
- Largest palace complex in the world
- Represents Confucian hierarchy and cosmic order
- North-south axis alignment with Beijing's central line

### Visiting Information
- **Hours:** 8:30 AM - 5:00 PM (Apr-Oct), 8:30 AM - 4:30 PM (Nov-Mar)
- **Closed:** Mondays (except holidays)
- **Ticket:** ¥60 (peak), ¥40 (off-peak)
- **Best Time:** Early morning (opens at 8:30) or late afternoon
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours minimum

## THE GREAT WALL (长城 - Chángchéng)

### Overview
- **Total Length:** 21,196 km (all sections combined)
- **Built:** 7th century BC - 17th century AD
- **UNESCO World Heritage:** 1987
- **Most Famous Quote:** "He who does not reach the Great Wall is not a true man"

### Beijing-Area Sections

#### 1. Badaling (八达岭)
- **Distance:** 70 km northwest of Beijing
- **Restoration:** Most restored, tourist-friendly
- **Features:** Cable car, wheelchair accessible, crowded
- **Best For:** First-time visitors, families, accessibility
- **Hours:** 6:30 AM - 7:00 PM (summer), 7:00 AM - 6:00 PM (winter)
- **Ticket:** ¥40 (off-peak), ¥45 (peak)

#### 2. Mutianyu (慕田峪)
- **Distance:** 73 km north of Beijing
- **Restoration:** Well-restored, less crowded than Badaling
- **Features:** Cable car, toboggan slide down, scenic
- **Best For:** Photography, moderate hiking, families
- **Hours:** 7:30 AM - 6:00 PM (summer), 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM (winter)
- **Ticket:** ¥45

#### 3. Jinshanling (金山岭)
- **Distance:** 125 km northeast of Beijing
- **Restoration:** Partially restored, partially wild
- **Features:** Original architecture, fewer tourists, challenging hike
- **Best For:** Serious hikers, photographers, sunrise/sunset
- **Hours:** 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM (summer), 7:00 AM - 5:00 PM (winter)
- **Ticket:** ¥65

#### 4. Jiankou (箭扣)
- **Distance:** 80 km north of Beijing
- **Restoration:** Unrestored, wild and dangerous
- **Features:** Dramatic cliffs, original state, risky
- **Best For:** Adventurous hikers, photographers (dangerous!)
- **Warning:** Several fatalities, not recommended for casual visitors

#### 5. Simatai (司马台)
- **Distance:** 120 km northeast of Beijing
- **Restoration:** Partially restored
- **Features:** Steep, original features, night tours available
- **Best For:** Experienced hikers, night visits
- **Hours:** 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM (day), 6:00 PM - 10:00 PM (night)
- **Ticket:** ¥40

### Construction Facts
- **Materials:** Stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, sand
- **Workers:** Millions (soldiers, peasants, prisoners)
- **Deaths:** Estimated hundreds of thousands to millions
- **Purpose:** Defense against northern nomadic tribes
- **Watchtowers:** ~25,000 along entire wall
- **Highest Point:** 1,534 meters above sea level

## TEMPLE OF HEAVEN (天坛 - Tiāntán)

### Overview
- **Location:** Dongcheng District, southern Beijing
- **Built:** 1420 (Ming Dynasty), expanded in Qing Dynasty
- **Size:** 267 hectares (larger than Forbidden City)
- **UNESCO World Heritage:** 1998
- **Purpose:** Imperial ceremonies for good harvests

### Key Structures

#### 1. Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (祈年殿)
- **Height:** 38 meters
- **Design:** Triple-gabled circular hall, no nails used
- **Symbolism:** Blue tiles represent heaven
- **Construction:** Entirely wooden, intricate bracket system

#### 2. Imperial Vault of Heaven (皇穹宇)
- **Design:** Single-eaved circular building
- **Feature:** Echo Wall - whispers travel 130 meters
- **Purpose:** Storage for memorial tablets

#### 3. Circular Mound Altar (圜丘坛)
- **Design:** Three-tiered marble platform
- **Purpose:** Winter solstice sacrifice ceremonies
- **Acoustics:** Center stone amplifies sound dramatically

#### 4. Echo Wall (回音壁)
- **Length:** 193.26 meters circumference
- **Feature:** Whisper gallery effect
- **Physics:** Sound travels along curved wall surface

### Historical Significance
- Emperors prayed for good harvests annually
- Winter solstice: Most important ceremony
- Architecture represents cosmic beliefs (round = heaven, square = earth)
- Last imperial sacrifice: 1914 (Yuan Shikai)
- Opened to public: 1918

### Visiting Information
- **Hours:** 6:00 AM - 10:00 PM (park), 8:00 AM - 5:30 PM (buildings)
- **Ticket:** ¥15 (park only), ¥35 (with buildings)
- **Best Time:** Early morning for locals practicing tai chi
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Nearby:** Tiantan East Metro Station (Line 5)

## SUMMER PALACE (颐和园 - Yíhéyuán)

### Overview
- **Location:** Haidian District, northwest Beijing
- **Built:** 1750 (Qing Dynasty), rebuilt 1886-1895
- **Size:** 290 hectares (75% water)
- **UNESCO World Heritage:** 1998
- **Known As:** "Royal Garden Museum"

### Key Areas

#### 1. Kunming Lake (昆明湖)
- **Size:** 220 hectares
- **Features:** West Causeway, Seventeen-Arch Bridge, Nanhu Island
- **Activities:** Boat rentals, walking paths
- **Winter:** Ice skating when frozen

#### 2. Longevity Hill (万寿山)
- **Height:** 60 meters
- **Structures:** Tower of Buddhist Incense, Hall of Dispelling Clouds
- **Views:** Panoramic views of lake and palace

#### 3. Tower of Buddhist Incense (佛香阁)
- **Height:** 41 meters
- **Design:** Octagonal, four-story pavilion
- **Symbol:** Most iconic Summer Palace structure

#### 4. Long Corridor (长廊)
- **Length:** 728 meters
- **Features:** 14,000+ painted scenes (mythology, history, nature)
- **UNESCO:** Longest corridor in Chinese gardens

#### 5. Marble Boat (石舫)
- **Design:** Stone boat with wooden superstructure
- **Symbolism:** Qing Dynasty stability (unsinkable)
- **History:** Burned 1860, rebuilt 1893

#### 6. Suzhou Street (苏州街)
- **Design:** Recreation of Jiangnan water town
- **Features:** Shops, bridges, canals
- **History:** Built for Emperor Qianlong's mother

### Historical Significance
- **Original:** 1750, Emperor Qianlong for mother's birthday
- **Destruction:** 1860, Anglo-French forces (Second Opium War)
- **Rebuilding:** 1886-1895, Empress Dowager Cixi (used navy funds)
- **Cixi's Residence:** Lived here during summer months
- **Cultural Revolution:** Protected by Zhou Enlai

### Visiting Information
- **Hours:** 6:30 AM - 6:00 PM (Apr-Oct), 7:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Nov-Mar)
- **Ticket:** ¥30 (peak), ¥20 (off-peak), ¥60 (all-inclusive)
- **Best Time:** Spring (flowers) or autumn (foliage)
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours minimum
- **Metro:** Line 4 to Beigongmen Station

## IMPERIAL HISTORY OF CHINA

### Dynastic Timeline (Relevant to Beijing)

#### Ming Dynasty (1368-1644)
- **Founder:** Zhu Yuanzhang (Emperor Hongwu)
- **Capital Move:** 1421, Nanjing → Beijing (Emperor Yongle)
- **Forbidden City:** Built 1406-1420
- **Great Wall:** Major reconstruction and expansion
- **Notable Emperors:**
  - Yongle (1402-1424): Built Forbidden City, sponsored Zheng He's voyages
  - Wanli (1572-1620): Longest reign, tomb excavated
- **Fall:** 1644, peasant rebellion (Li Zicheng), Manchu invasion

#### Qing Dynasty (1644-1912)
- **Founder:** Emperor Shunzhi (first Qing emperor in Beijing)
- **Ethnicity:** Manchu (not Han Chinese)
- **Notable Emperors:**
  - Kangxi (1661-1722): Longest reign, territorial expansion
  - Qianlong (1735-1796): Cultural peak, built Summer Palace
  - Cixi (1861-1908): Empress Dowager, de facto ruler
- **Fall:** 1912, Xinhai Revolution, Republic of China
- **Last Emperor:** Puyi (abdicated age 6, died 1967)

### Key Imperial Concepts

#### Mandate of Heaven (天命)
- Divine right to rule
- Lost through natural disasters, rebellion, corruption
- Justified dynastic changes

#### Confucian Hierarchy
- Emperor at center of cosmic order
- North-south axis = imperial power line
- Forbidden City aligned with Polaris (North Star)

#### Imperial Succession
- Not always eldest son
- Often chosen by emperor or power struggles
- Qing: Secret succession system (name in box)

### Imperial Daily Life

#### Emperor's Schedule
- **3:00-5:00 AM:** Wake, dress, prayers
- **5:00-7:00 AM:** Morning audiences with ministers
- **7:00-9:00 AM:** Breakfast (main meal)
- **9:00 AM-3:00 PM:** Governance, studies, rituals
- **3:00-5:00 PM:** Leisure, arts, concubines
- **5:00-7:00 PM:** Light supper
- **7:00-9:00 PM:** Evening activities
- **9:00 PM:** Sleep

#### Imperial Cuisine
- **Meals:** 2 main, 2 light per day
- **Dishes:** 100+ per meal (emperor tasted everything)
- **Safety:** Silver utensils (detect poison), food tasters
- **Ingredients:** Rare delicacies from across empire

#### Concubine System
- **Hierarchy:** Empress, imperial consorts, concubines (multiple ranks)
- **Selection:** Beautiful young women from across empire
- **Life:** Strict rules, competition for emperor's favor
- **Children:** Succession depended on male heirs

### The Last Emperor: Puyi

#### Early Life (1906-1924)
- **Born:** 1906, became emperor 1908 (age 2)
- **Abdication:** 1912 (age 6), Republic of China
- **Forbidden City:** Allowed to live there until 1924
- **Education:** British tutor Reginald Johnston

#### Later Life (1924-1967)
- **Manchukuo:** Puppet emperor of Japanese-controlled Manchuria (1932-1945)
- **Arrest:** 1945 by Soviets, imprisoned 10 years
- **Rehabilitation:** 1959, became ordinary citizen
- **Career:** Gardener, writer, political advisor
- **Death:** 1967 (kidney cancer, Cultural Revolution)
- **Autobiography:** "From Emperor to Citizen"

## CULTURAL CONTEXT

### Beijing's Layout

#### Central Axis
- **Length:** 8.8 km north-south line
- **Significance:** Cosmic alignment, imperial power
- **Structures:** Yongding Gate → Forbidden City → Jingshan → Bell/Drum Towers
- **UNESCO:** Proposed World Heritage Site

#### Feng Shui Principles
- **North-South:** Emperor faces south (yang direction)
- **Mountains:** North (protection from evil winds)
- **Water:** South (flow of qi/energy)
- **Colors:** Yellow (emperor), red (happiness), blue (heaven)

### Traditional Beliefs

#### Numerology
- **Nine:** Imperial number (nine dragons, nine gates)
- **Five:** Five elements (wood, fire, earth, metal, water)
- **Dragon:** Emperor symbol, five claws = imperial

#### Colors
- **Yellow/Gold:** Emperor, earth, center
- **Red:** Happiness, good fortune, walls
- **Blue:** Heaven, Temple of Heaven tiles
- **Green:** Growth, roof tiles (lesser buildings)

## PRACTICAL INFORMATION

### Getting to Beijing

#### By Air
- **Beijing Capital International (PEK):** 30 km northeast, major international hub
- **Beijing Daxing (PKX):** 46 km south, newer, growing international routes
- **Airport Express:** Metro connection to city center (¥25, 30-40 min)

#### By Train
- **Beijing Railway Station:** Central, traditional trains
- **Beijing West:** High-speed trains to south/west
- **Beijing South:** High-speed trains (Shanghai 4.5 hrs, ¥550)
- **Beijing North:** Trains to Inner Mongolia, Great Wall area

#### By Metro
- **Lines:** 27 lines, 700+ km (world's largest metro system)
- **Cost:** ¥3-10 depending on distance
- **Apps:** Beijing Metro app, Alipay, WeChat Pay
- **English:** All signs in English and Chinese

### Best Times to Visit

#### Seasons
- **Spring (Apr-May):** Mild, flowers, moderate crowds
- **Summer (Jun-Aug):** Hot (35°C+), humid, peak tourist season
- **Autumn (Sep-Oct):** Best weather, clear skies, foliage
- **Winter (Nov-Mar):** Cold (-10°C possible), fewer tourists, cheaper

#### Avoid
- **Chinese New Year:** Late Jan/early Feb (crowded, closures)
- **National Day:** Oct 1-7 (massive domestic tourism)
- **Summer holidays:** July-August (families, heat)

### Visa Requirements

#### Tourist Visa (L Visa)
- **Required:** Most nationalities (check specific country)
- **Validity:** 30-90 days typical
- **Processing:** 4-7 business days
- **Cost:** Varies by nationality ($30-140 USD)
- **Documents:** Passport, application, photo, itinerary, hotel booking

#### Visa-Free Transit
- **72/144 hours:** Available for many nationalities
- **Requirements:** Onward ticket, specific entry/exit cities
- **Coverage:** Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, etc.

### Costs (2024 estimates)

#### Accommodation (per night)
- **Budget:** ¥150-300 (hostels, budget hotels)
- **Mid-range:** ¥400-800 (3-4 star)
- **Luxury:** ¥1,000-3,000+ (5 star, Palace Hotel)

#### Food (per meal)
- **Street food:** ¥10-30
- **Local restaurant:** ¥30-80
- **Mid-range:** ¥80-200
- **Fine dining:** ¥300-1,000+

#### Transportation
- **Metro:** ¥3-10 per trip
- **Taxi/Didi:** ¥15-50 typical trips
- **Airport Express:** ¥25
- **Day pass:** ¥20 (unlimited metro)

#### Attractions
- **Forbidden City:** ¥60
- **Great Wall:** ¥40-65
- **Temple of Heaven:** ¥35 (combo)
- **Summer Palace:** ¥60 (combo)
- **Beijing Opera:** ¥200-800

### Language Tips

#### Basic Mandarin
- **Hello:** Nǐ hǎo (你好)
- **Thank you:** Xièxie (谢谢)
- **How much?:** Duōshǎo qián? (多少钱？)
- **Toilet:** Xǐshǒujiān (洗手间)
- **Delicious:** Hǎochī (好吃)
- **I don't understand:** Wǒ tīng bù dǒng (我听不懂)

#### Apps
- **Pleco:** Best Chinese dictionary

- **Didi:** Chinese Uber
- **Maps.me:** Offline maps

### Cultural Etiquette

#### Do's
- Remove shoes when entering homes
- Accept tea with both hands
- Try everything offered (polite)
- Use both hands to give/receive items
- Respect elders and hierarchy

#### Don'ts
- Stick chopsticks upright in rice (funeral ritual)
- Point with one finger (use open hand)
- Give clocks as gifts (sounds like "death")
- Discuss sensitive political topics
- Tip (not customary in China)

## FOOD & CUISINE

### Beijing Specialties

#### Peking Duck (北京烤鸭)
- **History:** Imperial dish, 600+ years
- **Preparation:** Air-dried, roasted in closed oven
- **Serving:** Sliced tableside, pancakes, hoisin, cucumber, scallions
- **Famous Restaurants:** Quanjude, Da Dong, Siji Minfu
- **Cost:** ¥200-500 per duck

#### Zhajiangmian (炸酱面)
- **Description:** Noodles with fermented soybean paste
- **Toppings:** Cucumber, radish, soybeans, pork
- **Style:** Northern Chinese comfort food
- **Cost:** ¥15-30

#### Jianbing (煎饼)
- **Description:** Savory crepe, breakfast staple
- **Fillings:** Egg, scallions, cilantro, crispy wonton, sauces
- **Street Food:** ¥8-15
- **Best:** Morning street vendors

#### Hot Pot (火锅)
- **Style:** Communal cooking in simmering broth
- **Beijing Style:** Copper pot, charcoal, mutton focus
- **Ingredients:** Thin-sliced meat, vegetables, tofu, noodles
- **Cost:** ¥80-200 per person

#### Dumplings (饺子)
- **Varieties:** Pork/cabbage, lamb, shrimp, vegetarian
- **Cooking:** Boiled, pan-fried, steamed
- **Occasion:** Chinese New Year tradition
- **Cost:** ¥20-50 per plate

### Food Areas

#### Wangfujing Snack Street
- **Location:** Dongcheng District
- **Famous For:** Street food, tourist-friendly
- **Specialties:** Scorpions, starfish (novelty), traditional snacks
- **Note:** Touristy but iconic

#### Ghost Street (Gui Jie)
- **Location:** Dongzhimen Inner Street
- **Famous For:** Night dining, spicy crayfish
- **Hours:** Evening to late night
- **Vibe:** Red lanterns, bustling

#### Niujie (Muslim Quarter)
- **Location:** Xicheng District
- **Famous For:** Halal food, beef, lamb
- **Specialties:** Beef pancakes, lamb skewers, yogurt
- **Cultural:** Historic Muslim community

## MODERN BEIJING

### Contemporary Context

#### Population & Scale
- **Population:** 21+ million (city proper)
- **Area:** 16,410 sq km
- **GDP:** $600+ billion (2023)
- **Global City:** Alpha+ (GaWC ranking)

#### Development
- **2008 Olympics:** Massive infrastructure investment
- **Skyline:** CBD with China Zun (528m tallest)
- **Transport:** World's largest metro system
- **Technology:** Major tech hub (Zhongguancun "Silicon Valley of Beijing")

#### Challenges
- **Air Quality:** Improved significantly since 2013
- **Traffic:** Severe congestion, license plate restrictions
- **Housing:** Extremely expensive in central areas
- **Preservation:** Balancing modernization with heritage

### Arts & Culture

#### 798 Art District
- **Location:** Dashanzi, northeast Beijing
- **History:** Former electronics factory, 1950s
- **Current:** Galleries, studios, cafes, shops
- **Vibe:** Industrial chic, contemporary art

#### National Centre for Performing Arts
- **Nickname:** "The Egg"
- **Design:** Paul Andreu, titanium and glass dome
- **Programs:** Opera, ballet, concerts, theater
- **Location:** Near Tiananmen Square

#### Beijing Opera (Peking Opera)
- **History:** 200+ years
- **Elements:** Singing, dialogue, acting, acrobatics, martial arts
- **Costumes:** Elaborate, symbolic colors
- **Venues:** Liyuan Theatre, Chang'an Grand Theatre
