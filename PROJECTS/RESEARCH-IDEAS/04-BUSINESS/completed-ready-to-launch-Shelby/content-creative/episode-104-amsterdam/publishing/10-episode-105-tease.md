## KEY THEMES (Episode 105 Preview)

### 1. The Archipelago
- 14 islands that make up Stockholm
- 24,000+ islands in the greater archipelago
- Water as connector, not divider
- Island-hopping culture
- Summer houses (stugor) on remote islands

### 2. Viking History
- Viking Age origins (8th-11th centuries)
- Gamla Stan (Old Town) medieval streets
- Vasa Museum (17th-century warship)
- Runestones scattered through city
- Norse mythology in modern culture

### 3. Design & Innovation
- IKEA (founded in Älmhult, but Stockholm is design capital)
- Spotify (born in Stockholm)
- Minimalist Scandinavian aesthetic
- Form follows function
- Design as democracy (beautiful things for everyone)

### 4. Light & Darkness
- Extreme seasonal light variation
- Midnight sun (June: 18+ hours of daylight)
- Polar night (December: 6 hours of daylight)
- How light shapes Swedish psyche
- Fika culture (coffee breaks as light in darkness)

### 5. Swedish Values
- Lagom: Not too little, not too much. Just right.
- Jantelagen: Don't think you're better than anyone
- Consensus-driven society
- Work-life balance (6-hour workday experiments)
- Gender equality leadership

### 6. Modern Stockholm
- Tech startup capital of Europe (per capita)
- Sustainable city goals (fossil fuel-free by 2040)
- Public transportation excellence
- Affordable (for Scandinavia) vs. expensive
- Immigration and integration challenges

## STOCKHOLM RESEARCH STARTER

### Key Locations to Research
1. **Gamla Stan** - Old Town, medieval heart
2. **Vasa Museum** - 17th-century warship
3. **Skansen** - Open-air museum, Swedish history
4. **Stockholm City Hall** - Nobel Prize banquet hall
5. **Fotografiska** - Photography museum
6. **ABBA Museum** - Pop culture icon
7. **Royal Palace** - Official residence
8. **Södermalm** - Trendy island, local vibe
9. **Östermalm** - Upscale, architecture
10. **Archipelago islands** - Vaxholm, Grinda, Sandhamn

### Key Themes to Explore
- Viking Age (800-1050 CE)
- Hanseatic League trading post
- Swedish Empire (17th century)
- Industrialization and neutrality
- Modern welfare state
- Tech startup boom
- Immigration and integration
- Environmental leadership

### Swedish Concepts to Understand
- **Lagom:** Not too little, not too much. Just right.
- **Fika:** Coffee break as social institution
- **Jantelagen:** Law of Jante (don't think you're better)
- **Allemansrätten:** Right of public access (nature)
- **Mysig:** Cozy (Swedish version of gezelligheid)
