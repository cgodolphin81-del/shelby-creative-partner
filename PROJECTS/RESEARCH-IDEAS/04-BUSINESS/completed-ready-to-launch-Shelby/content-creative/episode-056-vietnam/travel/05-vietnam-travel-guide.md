- **Da Nang International Airport (DAD)** - Da Nang (Central Vietnam, 30min from Hoi An)

**For This Episode:**
- **Fly into Hanoi (HAN)** - Start your journey in the north
- **Fly out of Da Nang (DAD)** - End in Hoi An, depart from nearby Da Nang

**Major Airlines:**
- Vietnam Airlines (national carrier, full service)
- VietJet Air (budget, reliable)
- Bamboo Airways (mid-range, good service)
- International: Singapore Airlines, Cathay Pacific, Thai Airways, ANA, Korean Air

**Flight Times (from major cities):**
- Los Angeles: 15-18 hours (1-2 stops)
- New York: 18-22 hours (1-2 stops)
- London: 13-16 hours (1 stop)
- Sydney: 9-11 hours (direct or 1 stop)
- Singapore: 3.5 hours (direct)
- Bangkok: 2 hours (direct)
- Tokyo: 5 hours (direct)

### Visa Requirements

**E-Visa (Recommended):**
- Available to citizens of 80+ countries
- 30-day single entry
- Apply online at: evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn
- Processing: 3-5 business days
- Cost: $25 USD
- Print approval letter, present on arrival

**Visa on Arrival:**
- Requires pre-approval letter from visa agency
- More expensive ($35-50 + service fee)
- Good for last-minute travel
- Apply through: vietnamvisa.govt.vn or agencies

**Visa Exemptions:**
- UK, France, Germany, Italy, Spain: 15 days (check current rules)
- ASEAN countries: 14-30 days depending on nationality
- South Korea, Japan, Denmark, Finland, Norway, Sweden: 15 days

**Important:**
- Passport must be valid 6+ months beyond entry date
- Have passport photos ready (for visa on arrival)
- Keep visa approval letter with passport at all times

## GETTING AROUND

### Hanoi to Ha Long Bay

**Option 1: Shuttle Bus (Most Common)**
- Duration: 3.5-4 hours
- Cost: $10-15 USD
- Pick up from Old Quarter hotels
- Drop off at Ha Long cruise port
- Book through cruise company or hotel
- Comfortable limousine vans available ($20-25)

**Option 2: Private Car**
- Duration: 3 hours
- Cost: $70-100 USD
- Door-to-door service
- Flexible departure time
- Book through hotel or cruise company

**Option 3: Public Bus**
- Duration: 4-5 hours
- Cost: $5-8 USD
- From My Dinh or Gia Lam bus stations
- Less comfortable, not recommended for tourists

**Option 4: Seaplane (Luxury)**
- Duration: 45 minutes
- Cost: $350-500 USD round trip
- Hai Au Aviation
- Spectacular aerial views
- Book in advance

### Hanoi to Hoi An

**Option 1: Flight (Recommended)**
- Duration: 1h 15min flight + 30min drive
- Cost: $50-100 USD
- Airlines: Vietnam Airlines, VietJet, Bamboo
- Fly Hanoi (HAN) to Da Nang (DAD)
- Taxi from Da Nang airport to Hoi An: $15-20

**Option 2: Train**
- Duration: 15-17 hours
- Cost: $30-80 USD (depending on class)
- Reunification Express
- Sleeper berths available (recommended for overnight)
- Scenic coastal route
- Book at: dsvn.vn or 12go.asia

**Option 3: Overnight Bus**
- Duration: 14-16 hours
- Cost: $20-35 USD
- Sleeper buses available
- Less comfortable than train
- Not recommended unless budget travel

**Option 4: Private Car with Driver**
- Duration: 12-14 hours (with stops)
- Cost: $200-300 USD
- Stop at Hue, Hai Van Pass
- Good for groups or families

### Ha Long Bay to Hoi An

**Best Route:**
1. Return to Hanoi from Ha Long Bay (shuttle bus, 4 hours)
2. Fly Hanoi to Da Nang (1h 15min)
3. Taxi to Hoi An (30min)

**Total Travel Time:** 6-7 hours  
**Total Cost:** $80-120 USD

**French Quarter**

**On the Water (Recommended)**
- Overnight cruise is the best experience

### 10-Day Vietnam Highlights

**Days 1-3: Hanoi**
- Day 1: Arrive Hanoi, Old Quarter exploration, street food tour

**Days 6-9: Hoi An**
- Day 6: Ancient Town exploration, Japanese Bridge, Assembly Halls, lantern making

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### North Vietnam (Hanoi, Ha Long Bay)

**October - April (BEST)**
- Dry season
- Cool, pleasant temperatures (18-25°C / 65-77°F)
- Clear skies, good visibility
- Peak tourist season
- Higher prices

**December - February**
- Coolest months (can be 10-15°C / 50-60°F)
- Possible drizzle
- Pack layers, light jacket
- Tet (Lunar New Year) - late January/early February
  - Many businesses close 3-7 days
  - Book well in advance
  - Higher prices

**May - September**
- Hot and humid (30-35°C / 86-95°F)
- Rainy season (afternoon showers)
- Fewer tourists
- Lower prices
- Typhoon risk (July-September)
- Some Ha Long cruises may cancel

### Central Vietnam (Hoi An, Hue, Da Nang)

**February - May (BEST)**
- Dry, warm weather (25-30°C / 77-86°F)
- Ideal beach weather
- Comfortable for sightseeing

**June - August**
- Very hot (35-40°C / 95-104°F)
- Dry
- Beach weather but hot for touring

**September - January**
- Rainy season
- October-November: Peak rain, flooding possible in Hoi An
- Some businesses may close during floods
- Lower prices
- Lush, green landscapes

### Year-Round Strategy

**Best Overall:** October-April
- Good weather in both North and Central
- Follow good weather: Start in North (Oct-Apr), move to Central (Feb-May)

**Avoid:** September-November in Central Vietnam
- High rain and flooding risk in Hoi An
- Can disrupt travel plans

**Shoulder Season:** May, September
- Good weather
- Fewer crowds
- Better prices
- Some rain possible

- Ca Phe Trung (egg coffee) - Dessert/drink

**Hoi An Specialties:**
- Cao Lau (noodles with pork) - Hoi An signature dish
- Banh Bao Banh Vac (White Rose dumplings) - Local delicacy
- Com Ga (chicken rice) - Simple, delicious

**National Favorites:**
- Goi Cuon (fresh spring rolls)
- Pho Ga (chicken pho)
- Bun Bo Hue (spicy beef noodle soup from Hue)
- Banh Xeo (crispy pancake)
- Che (sweet dessert soup)

### Where to Eat

**Street Food (Safe & Delicious)**
- Look for busy stalls with high turnover
- Eat where locals eat
- Plastic stools = authentic experience
- Average cost: $1-3 per dish

**Recommended Hanoi Street Food Areas:**
- Old Quarter (Hang Buom, Hang Giay streets)
- Dong Xuan Market area
- Train Street (check access)

**Recommended Hoi An:**
- Central Market (morning)
- Tran Hung Dao Street
- Nguyen Thai Hoc Street

**Restaurants (Mid-Range)**
- Air conditioning, English menus
- Cost: $5-15 per person
- Good for groups, families

**Fine Dining**
- Available in Hanoi and Hoi An
- Modern Vietnamese cuisine
- Cost: $30-80 per person
- Reservations recommended

### Food Safety Tips

**Generally Safe:**
- Street food with high turnover
- Cooked-to-order dishes
- Peeled fruits
- Bottled water

**Be Cautious:**
- Raw vegetables (wash thoroughly)
- Ice in non-tourist areas
- Pre-cut fruit from street vendors
- Tap water (never drink)

**What to Bring:**
- Hand sanitizer
- Imodium or similar
- Rehydration salts
- Probiotics (preventive)

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Greetings

- Handshake common (especially with men)
- Slight bow shows respect
- Address elders first
- Use both hands when giving/receiving items
- "Xin chào" (sin chow) = Hello

### Temples & Pagodas

- Remove shoes before entering
- Cover shoulders and knees
- Speak quietly
- Ask before photographing people praying
- Don't point feet at Buddha statues

### Dining Etiquette

- Wait to be seated
- Chopsticks and spoon (no fork typically)
- Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice (resembles incense for dead)
- Try everything offered (polite)
- Leave a little food (shows you're full)
- "Chúc ngon miệng" = Enjoy your meal

### General Etiquette

- Remove shoes when entering homes
- Don't touch people's heads (sacred)
- Don't point with single finger (use whole hand)
- Public displays of affection: minimal
- Face-saving important (don't cause embarrassment)
- Dress modestly, especially at religious sites

### Photography

- Ask before photographing people
- Street photographers very active in Hanoi
- Some temples restrict photography
- Military sites: no photography
- Drones: require permit (check regulations)

## PRACTICAL INFORMATION

### Currency

**Vietnamese Dong (VND)**
- Symbol: ₫
- Exchange rate: ~24,000 VND = $1 USD (2024)
- Cash-based society
- ATMs widely available ($2-5 fee per withdrawal)
- Credit cards accepted in hotels, upscale restaurants

**Money Tips:**
- Many zeros on bills—learn to count quickly
- $1 = 24,000, $10 = 240,000, $100 = 2,400,000
- Keep small bills for street vendors
- Hotels can exchange currency
- Notify bank before travel

### Connectivity

**SIM Cards:**
- Available at airports, phone shops
- Viettel, Vinaphone, Mobifone (major carriers)
- Cost: $5-10 for 10-20GB data
- Easy to set up
- Good coverage in cities and tourist areas

**WiFi:**
- Available in most hotels, cafes, restaurants
- Generally reliable in cities
- Slower in rural areas

**Useful Apps:**
- Grab (ride-hailing, like Uber)
- Google Maps (works well)
- Google Translate (Vietnamese offline pack)
- Xe (currency converter)
- 12go.asia (transport booking)

### Health & Safety

**Vaccinations (Recommended):**
- Routine vaccines up to date
- Hepatitis A & B
- Typhoid
- Japanese encephalitis (if rural, long-term)
- Malaria prophylaxis (rural areas only, not needed for this itinerary)
- Consult travel doctor 6-8 weeks before travel

**Common Health Issues:**
- Traveler's diarrhea (pack Imodium)
- Mosquito-borne diseases (use repellent)
- Sunburn (tropical sun is strong)
- Heat exhaustion (stay hydrated)

**Safety:**
- Very safe for tourists
- Violent crime rare
- Petty theft (bag snatching, pickpockets) - be aware in crowded areas
- Scams (taxi overcharging, gem scams) - use common sense
- Traffic accidents (biggest risk - be careful crossing streets)

**Emergency Numbers:**
- Police: 113
- Fire: 114
- Ambulance: 115
- Tourist Police: Available in major cities

## SHOPPING

### Hanoi

**Old Quarter:**
- Hang Gai (Silk Street): Silk products, scarves, ao dai
- Hang Ma: Paper products, decorations
- Dong Xuan Market: Everything (bargain hard)
- Night Market (Fri-Sun): Souvenirs, clothes, street food

**What to Buy:**
- Silk products (scarves, ties, fabric)
- Lacquerware
- Embroidery
- Coffee (Vietnamese robusta)
- Art and paintings

### Hoi An

**Tailoring (Famous)**
- 400+ tailor shops
- Suits, dresses, ao dai, shirts
- 24-48 hour turnaround
- Multiple fittings recommended
- Quality varies—research before choosing
- Reputable shops: Yaly Couture, Be Be Tailor, A Dong Silk

**Lanterns:**
- Beautiful silk lanterns
- Various shapes and colors
- Easy to pack (collapsible)
- $5-20 depending on size

**What to Buy:**
- Custom-tailored clothing
- Silk lanterns
- Lacquerware
- Art and paintings
- Leather goods
- Spices and coffee

**Bargaining:**
- Expected in markets, not in shops with price tags
- Start at 50-60% of asking price
- Be friendly, smile
- Walk away if price not right (often they'll call you back)
- Know when to stop (few dollars difference not worth it)

## RESPONSIBLE TOURISM

### How to Travel Responsibly

**Support Local:**
- Eat at local restaurants
- Stay in locally-owned hotels
- Buy from local artisans
- Use local guides

**Respect Culture:**
- Dress modestly at temples
- Ask before photographing people
- Learn basic Vietnamese phrases
- Respect local customs

**Environmental:**
- Avoid single-use plastics
- Use reusable water bottle (with filter)
- Don't litter (especially in Ha Long Bay)
- Choose eco-friendly cruises
- Don't touch or step on coral

**Wildlife:**
- Don't buy products from endangered species
- Avoid attractions with animal cruelty
- Don't feed wild animals
- Keep distance from monkeys

**Give Back:**
- Support local charities if you wish to donate
- Volunteer through reputable organizations
- Don't give money to begging children (encourages exploitation)

## FINAL TIPS

**Before You Go:**
- Book Ha Long Bay cruise in advance (especially Oct-Apr)
- Download offline Google Maps
- Get travel insurance
- Notify bank of travel
- Download Grab app
- Learn basic Vietnamese phrases

**Upon Arrival:**
- Get SIM card at airport
- Exchange some money (or withdraw from ATM)
- Confirm Ha Long Bay pickup with cruise company
- Get hotel business card (for taxi returns)

**During Your Trip:**
- Drink bottled or filtered water only
- Cross streets slowly and steadily (don't run)
- Bargain with a smile
- Try everything (food-wise!)
- Wake up early for best photos (no crowds)
- Be patient—things move at Vietnamese time

**Remember:**
Vietnam is safe, welcoming, and incredibly rewarding. The Vietnamese people are proud of their country and happy to share it with visitors. Be respectful, be curious, be open—and you'll have the trip of a lifetime.
