## TYPOGRAPHY

### Primary Typeface: Series Consistency

**Display Font: Playfair Display** (or similar serif)
```
Weights: Regular (400), Italic (400), Bold (700)
Style: Elegant, literary, timeless
Usage: Titles, pull quotes, episode numbers
```

**Why:**
- Maintains series consistency with Episodes 001-005
- Literary feel matches poetic essay style
- Elegant without being pretentious
- Excellent readability at large sizes

**Example:**
```
EPISODE 006
Ice & Silence
A Walk Through Antarctica
```

---

### Body Font: Modern Sans-Serif

**Body Font: Inter** (or similar geometric sans)
```
Weights: Light (300), Regular (400), Medium (500)
Style: Clean, modern, highly readable
Usage: Body text, captions, descriptions
```

**Why:**
- Excellent screen readability
- Neutral, doesn't compete with imagery
- Modern feel balances classic serif
- Free, open-source (Google Fonts)

**Example:**
```
I arrived at the edge of the world. Not metaphorically. 
The map literally ends here. Below 60 degrees south, 
the meridians converge, the distances shrink...
```

### Typography for Antarctica (vs. Zhangjiajie)

| Element | Zhangjiajie (Ep 005) | Antarctica (Ep 006) |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Letter Spacing** | Normal | +0.5 (more spacious, feels colder) |
| **Font Weight** | Regular/Bold | Light/Regular (lighter, more delicate) |
| **Color Contrast** | Dark on light | White on dark (reversed, feels colder) |
| **Line Height** | 1.5 | 1.6 (more breathing room) |
| **Text Density** | Moderate | Sparse (more white space) |

**Rationale:** Antarctica typography should feel colder, more sparse, more delicate—matching the landscape.

## COMPOSITION & LAYOUT

### Guiding Principles

**1. Vastness Over Detail**
- Emphasize negative space (60-80% of frame)
- Small subjects against large backgrounds
- Horizontal orientation (endlessness, not verticality)

**2. Minimalism**
- Remove unnecessary elements
- Simple, clean compositions
- One focal point maximum

**3. Cold Color Temperature**
- Cool white balance (6500K+)
- Enhance blue tones
- Reduce warmth (except in human/wildlife moments)

**4. High Contrast (When Appropriate)**
- Harsh shadows in bright sun
- Deep blacks, bright whites
- Except: overcast scenes (low contrast, moody)

---

### Composition Templates

**Template 1: The Vast Horizon**
```
Layout: Rule of thirds (horizon on bottom third)
Sky: 66% of frame (overcast, gradient)
Ice: 33% of frame (foreground)
Subject: Tiny silhouette on horizon line
Mood: Isolation, scale, insignificance
Aspect Ratio: 21:9 (cinematic) or 16:9
```

**Template 2: The Iceberg Portrait**
```
Layout: Centered subject
Iceberg: 60% of frame, centered
Water: 30% of frame (reflection)
Sky: 10% of frame (minimal)
Mood: Majesty, stillness, time
Aspect Ratio: 4:5 (portrait) or 1:1 (square)
```

**Template 3: The Wildlife Moment**
```
Layout: Eye-level with subject
Penguin/Seal: 40% of frame, rule of thirds
Background: Blurred colony or ice
Human element: Optional, distant, respectful
Mood: Intimacy, life, behavior
Aspect Ratio: 16:9 or 4:5
```

**Template 4: The Human Scale**
```
Layout: Wide shot, human tiny
Landscape: 90% of frame
Human (Shelby): 5% of frame, silhouette
Sky: 5% of frame
Mood: Exploration, solitude, journey
Aspect Ratio: 16:9 or 21:9
```

**Template 5: The Detail Study**
```
Layout: Macro, fill frame
Subject: Ice texture, penguin feather, etc.
Background: None (abstract)
Focus: Sharp, detailed
Mood: Intimacy, texture, pattern
Aspect Ratio: 1:1 (square)
```

---

### Framing Guidelines

**Landscape Shots:**
- Horizon line: Bottom third (emphasize sky) OR top third (emphasize ice)
- Never center horizon (unless reflection symmetry intended)
- Include foreground interest (ice chunk, footprint, etc.)
- Leading lines: Iceberg edges, glacier flows, shorelines

**Wildlife Shots:**
- Eye level: Get down to subject's level
- Eye focus: Sharp focus on nearest eye
- Behavior: Capture action, not just portraits
- Distance: Respectful, never disturbing

**Human Shots:**
- Silhouette: Preferred (anonymous, universal)
- Scale: Show human smallness against landscape
- Back to camera: Invites viewer into scene
- Parka color: Orange (#ED8936) for contrast

## VISUAL ELEMENTS

### Ice Formations

**Characteristics to Capture:**
- Layered compression (annual deposits)
- Blue interior (when fractured)
- Surface texture (wind-carved sastrugi)
- Fracture patterns (lightning-like cracks)
- Melt pools (bright blue on white)

**Lighting:**
- Side lighting: Emphasizes texture
- Back lighting: Glowing translucency
- Overcast: Even, soft, moody

**Avoid:**
- Overexposed snow (gray instead of white)
- Harsh midday sun (unless intentional)
- Tourist boats in frame (unless for scale)

---

### Wildlife

**Penguins:**
- Focus: Gentoo, Adélie (most photogenic)
- Behavior: Feeding chicks, waddling, swimming
- Context: Colony shots + individual portraits
- Ethics: Never disturb, maintain distance

**Seals:**
- Focus: Weddell (friendly), Leopard (dramatic)
- Behavior: Hauled out, swimming, hunting
- Context: Ice edge, water, beaches
- Ethics: Extra distance for mothers with pups

**Whales:**
- Focus: Humpback (breaching), Minke (common)
- Behavior: Surfacing, spouting, tail flukes
- Context: Open water, boat for scale
- Reality: Unpredictable, never guaranteed

---

### Human Element

**Shelby (Protagonist):**
- Appearance: Silhouette, gender-neutral, anonymous
- Clothing: Bright orange parka (#ED8936)
- Pose: Contemplative, observing, small
- Placement: Rule of thirds, never centered (unless intentional)

**Other Humans:**
- Researchers: Distant, contextual
- Fellow travelers: Group shots, Zodiacs
- Focus: Always on landscape, not people

**Equipment:**
- Zodiacs: Orange, for scale and color
- Ships: Distant, contextual
- Research stations: Red buildings, tiny against landscape

## LIGHTING & ATMOSPHERE

### Natural Light Scenarios

**Midnight Sun (2 AM Sunset)**
```
Time: 10 PM - 4 AM (December)
Color Temperature: 3000-4000K (warm)
Colors: Pink, orange, gold on ice
Mood: Surreal, magical, dreamlike
Exposure: -1 stop (protect highlights)
White Balance: 5500K (preserve warmth)
```

**Overcast Day**
```
Time: Any time
Color Temperature: 6500-7500K (cool)
Colors: Blue, gray, white (muted)
Mood: Moody, contemplative, cold
Exposure: +1 stop (brighten scene)
White Balance: 6500K (enhance cool tones)
```

**Clear Day (Harsh Sun)**
```
Time: 10 AM - 4 PM
Color Temperature: 5500K (neutral)
Colors: High contrast, deep shadows
Mood: Dramatic, powerful, intense
Exposure: 0 (meter for mid-tones)
White Balance: 5500K (natural)
```

**Aurora (Night)**
```
Time: Night (winter only, not tourist season)
Color Temperature: Variable (green/purple)
Colors: Green (#68D391), purple, black sky
Mood: Magical, rare, spiritual
Exposure: Long exposure (10-30 seconds)
White Balance: 3500K (preserve aurora colors)
```

### Atmospheric Conditions

**Whiteout**
```
Visibility: <50 meters
Contrast: Near zero
Colors: White, light gray only
Mood: Disorienting, ethereal, abstract
Use: Sparingly, for specific emotional effect
```

**Blowing Snow**
```
Visibility: Variable
Texture: Horizontal streaks
Mood: Harsh, challenging, extreme
Use: Action shots, Drake Passage crossing
```

**Mist/Fog**
```
Visibility: 100-500 meters
Effect: Layers, depth, mystery
Mood: Mysterious, soft, dreamlike
Use: Iceberg scenes, arrival moments
```

**Crystal Clear**
```
Visibility: 50+ kilometers
Effect: Sharp detail, deep contrast
Mood: Clarity, revelation, awe
Use: Panorama shots, mountain views
```

## ACCESSIBILITY

### Color Contrast
- **Text on background:** Minimum 4.5:1 ratio (WCAG AA)
- **Large text:** Minimum 3:1 ratio
- **Avoid:** Light gray on white, blue on black

### Alt Text
- **Describe:** Subject, action, context, mood
- **Example:** "Gentoo penguin waddling across rocky Antarctic beach, pink guano visible, glacier in background"
- **Avoid:** "Image of penguin" (too vague)

### Captions
- **All videos:** Closed captions required
- **Style:** White text, black outline, readable font
- **Timing:** Sync with audio, not too fast
