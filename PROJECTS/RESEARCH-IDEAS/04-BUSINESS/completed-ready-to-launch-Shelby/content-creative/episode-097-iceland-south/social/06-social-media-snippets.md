### Post 4: Northern Lights
**Image:** Aurora over glacier lagoon
**Caption:**
```
The sky is dancing.

The northern lights aren't static. They flow. They ripple. They send ribbons of green and purple across the Arctic night.

You're witnessing a collision between the sun and the Earth. Solar particles, traveling millions of kilometers, colliding with our atmosphere, guided by magnetic fields to the poles.

Physics made visible. But it feels like magic.

The Icelanders called it the Bifröst—the burning bridge connecting Earth to the realm of the gods. Scientists call it aurora borealis. Either way, it's the same awe.

Best viewing: September-April, 10 PM - 3 AM, KP index 3+, dark skies away from cities.

2025-2026 is a solar maximum year. The lights are stronger, brighter, more frequent. If you're going to Iceland for aurora, this is your year.
```
