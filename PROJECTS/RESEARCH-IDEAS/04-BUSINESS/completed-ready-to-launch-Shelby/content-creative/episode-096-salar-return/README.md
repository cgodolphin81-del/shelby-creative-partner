## Episode Summary

### The Narrative Arc

**Opening:** Arrival at the edge of the world, the thin air, the whiteness

**Section 1 - The Mirror:** Wet season transformation, sky and earth merge, reflection as truth

**Section 2 - The Hexagons:** Dry season geometry, natural order from chaos

**Section 3 - The Island:** Incahuasi's ancient cacti, witnesses to time

**Section 4 - The Flamingos:** Life at altitude, Laguna Colorada, adaptation and beauty

**Section 5 - Return:** Coming back changed, the mirror within, carrying the lesson

**Closing:** Departure, memory, infinity as home

- **Reflection:** Literal (mirror) and metaphorical (self-examination)

- **Balance:** Tourism vs. preservation, progress vs. sacred
- **Perspective:** Up/down dissolve, boundaries are illusions

## Key Facts (For Show Notes & Promotion)

### Geography
- **Size:** 10,582 km² (4,086 sq mi) - world's largest salt flat
- **Altitude:** 3,650m (11,975 ft) above sea level
- **Location:** Southwest Bolivia, near Uyuni town
- **Formation:** Remnant of prehistoric Lake Minchin

### Seasons
- **Wet (Nov-Apr):** Mirror effect, Jan-Mar peak
- **Dry (May-Oct):** Hexagonal patterns, island access
- **Shoulder:** Potential for both

### Wildlife
- **Flamingos:** 3 species (Chilean, Andean, James's)
- **Laguna Colorada:** Red algae, flamingo food source
- **Incahuasi Island:** Giant cacti (1,000+ years old)
- **Other:** Viscachas, vicuñas, Andean fox

### Lithium
- **Reserves:** 21-23 million tons
- **Global Share:** ~22% of world's known lithium
- **Uses:** EV batteries, electronics, renewable energy
- **Concerns:** Environmental impact, water usage, arsenic

### Travel
- **Best Time:** Jan-Mar (mirror), May-Oct (access)
- **Tour Cost:** $150-350 USD (3-day from Uyuni)
- **Altitude:** Acclimatize 2-3 days in La Paz/Sucre
- **Getting There:** Flight or bus from La Paz (8-10 hrs)
