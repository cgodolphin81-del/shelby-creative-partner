## Visa & Permits

### Tourist Visa
- **Type:** Yemeni tourist visa (Socotra-specific)
- **Cost:** $150–200 USD
- **Validity:** 30 days
- **Processing Time:** 7–10 days
- **Application:** MUST be arranged through registered Socotra tour company
- **Requirements:**
  - Passport valid 6+ months beyond return date
  - Passport copy (color scan)
  - ID photo (digital)
  - Completed application form (provided by tour operator)

**Process:**
1. Book tour with registered operator
2. Submit passport copy and photo
3. Operator processes visa application
4. Receive printed visa copy before departure
5. Original visa handed over at Socotra airport arrival

### Important Visa Notes

⚠️ **Israel Restriction:** Israeli passport holders are denied entry. Evidence of Israel travel in passport (stamps, visas) will result in denial.

⚠️ **US ESTA Warning:** A Yemeni visa stamp in your passport may complicate future US ESTA (Visa Waiver Program) eligibility. You may need to apply for a full US visa instead. Some travelers request visa on separate paper—ask your tour operator.

### Permits
- All necessary permits handled by tour operator
- Included in tour package
- No independent application required
- Drone permit: $100 USD (if applicable, arrange in advance)

## When to Visit

### High Season (RECOMMENDED)
**October – May**

**Peak Months:** November, February, March

**Weather:**
- Temperature: 20°C–28°C (68°F–82°F)
- Conditions: Warm, sunny, dry
- Rainfall: Minimal
- Sea: Calm, good for swimming

**Pros:**
- Best weather conditions
- All flights operating
- Optimal for hiking and photography
- Marine life visible

**Cons:**
- Higher prices
- Need to book 2–4 months ahead
- More tourists (relatively speaking)

---

### Low Season (NOT RECOMMENDED)
**June – September**

**Weather:**
- Temperature: 30°C–40°C+ (86°F–104°F+)
- Conditions: Hot, humid, monsoon influence
- Rainfall: Possible
- Sea: Rough, strong winds

**Pros:**
- Fewer visitors
- Lower prices (sometimes)

**Cons:**
- Flights limited or suspended
- Extreme heat
- Some areas inaccessible
- Monsoon conditions

## Accommodation

### Hotels (Hadibo Only)
Limited options. Manage expectations—this is not a luxury destination.

- **Hadibo Hotel:** Basic rooms, private bathroom, AC
- **Socotra Resort:** Slightly upgraded, restaurant on-site
- **Cost:** $50–100 USD per night
- **Booking:** Through tour operator

### Camping (Most Tours)
The authentic Socotra experience. Most tours include 5–6 nights camping.

**What's Provided:**
- Quality tents (Qeshua or similar)
- Sleeping mats
- Shared toilet facilities (basic)
- Camp meals prepared by support staff

**What to Bring:**
- Sleeping bag (lightweight)
- Headlamp/flashlight
- Personal toiletries
- Quick-dry towel

**Experience:**
- Sleeping under the stars
- Dragon blood trees surrounding camp
- Campfire gatherings
- Basic but functional facilities

Consult your travel doctor 6–8 weeks before departure.

**Routine:**
- MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella)
- Tdap (Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis)
- Polio
- Influenza

**Travel-Specific:**
- Hepatitis A
- Hepatitis B

**Required:**

⚠️ **Very limited on Socotra**

## Safety & Security

### Current Situation (2025–2026)

**Good News:**
- ✅ Socotra is isolated from mainland Yemen conflict
- ✅ Relative peace and stability
- ✅ Locals are friendly and welcoming to tourists
- ✅ Tourist access generally open
- ✅ UAE and Saudi presence provides infrastructure support

**Considerations:**
- ⚠️ Fragmented political environment (Southern Transitional Council influence)
- ⚠️ Presence of armed groups (generally non-confrontational to tourists)
- ⚠️ Occasional regional tensions can affect flight schedules
- ⚠️ Limited medical facilities
- ⚠️ Underdeveloped infrastructure

### Travel Advisories
Many foreign ministries maintain "avoid all travel to Yemen" advisories that technically include Socotra. However:
- Socotra is geographically and politically distinct from mainland Yemen
- Many travelers visit safely with proper preparation
- Check your government's current advisory before booking
- Some insurers cover Socotra despite official advisories

### On the Ground Safety

**General Precautions:**
- Travel with reputable tour operator
- Follow guide instructions
- Stay on marked trails
- Respect local customs and dress codes
- Avoid political discussions
- Keep valuables secure
- Stay hydrated and protected from sun

**Emergency Contacts:**
- Tour operator emergency line (provided before departure)
- Local guide (with you at all times)
- Hotel front desk (Hadibo)
- Embassy contacts (if applicable)

## Cultural Etiquette

### Dress Code
- **Women:** Modest clothing recommended (covered shoulders, knees). Swimwear acceptable at beaches/camps away from villages.
- **Men:** Shorts and t-shirts acceptable. Avoid sleeveless shirts in villages.
- **General:** Respectful, modest dress shows cultural sensitivity.

### Photography
- ✅ Landscapes, wildlife, nature: Always okay
- ✅ Your travel group: Always okay
- ⚠️ Local people: Ask permission first
- ❌ Government/military installations: Prohibited
- ❌ Sensitive locations: Follow guide's advice

### Social Customs
- Greetings are important (Arabic: "As-salamu alaykum" = Peace be upon you)
- Use right hand for eating and greeting
- Respect prayer times (shops may close briefly)
- Alcohol is not available on Socotra
- Ramadan: If visiting during Ramadan, be respectful of fasting

### Environmental Responsibility
Socotra's ecosystem is fragile. Practice Leave No Trace:

- 🚮 Pack out ALL trash (nothing left behind)
- 🥾 Stay on marked trails
- 🌿 Do not pick plants or disturb wildlife
- 📸 Observe from distance, no feeding animals
- 🧴 Use reef-safe sunscreen
- 💧 Conserve water (limited resource)
- 🔥 No open fires outside designated areas

## Photography Tips

### Best Locations
- **Dixam Plateau:** Dragon blood trees at sunrise/sunset
- **Homhil Plateau:** Natural pools, panoramic views
- **Arher Beach:** Dunes meeting ocean
- **Firhmin Forest:** Densest dragon blood tree forest
- **Zahek Sand Dunes:** Golden hour desert scenes
- **Wadi Dirhur:** Canyon landscapes

### Best Light
- **Golden Hour:** Sunrise (5:30–7:00 AM), Sunset (5:30–7:00 PM)
- **Blue Hour:** 30 minutes before sunrise/after sunset
- **Midday:** Harsh light, seek shade or shoot details

### Gear Recommendations
- **Camera:** DSLR or mirrorless with good dynamic range
- **Lenses:** Wide-angle (16–35mm), telephoto (70–200mm)
- **Drone:** Allowed with $100 permit (check current regulations)
- **Filters:** Polarizing filter, ND filters for long exposures
- **Protection:** Dust covers, silica gel packs

## Common Questions

**Q: Is Socotra safe for solo female travelers?**  
A: Yes, with a reputable tour operator. Many solo female travelers visit safely. Dress modestly, follow guide instructions, and use common sense.

**Q: Can I visit independently without a tour?**  
A: Technically possible but NOT recommended. A local guide is mandatory for most areas. Infrastructure is limited. Book at minimum a guide/driver.

**Q: What about vegetarian/vegan diets?**  
A: Communicate dietary requirements when booking. Most tour operators can accommodate with advance notice. Options may be limited but manageable.

**Q: Is the water safe to drink?**  
A: No. Drink only bottled water provided by your tour. Bring a reusable bottle to reduce plastic waste.

**Q: Can I use my credit card?**  
A: No. Bring USD cash. ATMs are unreliable.

**Q: What about language?**  
A: Arabic is official. Many guides speak English. Learning basic Arabic greetings is appreciated.

**Q: How physically demanding is the trip?**  
A: Moderate. Daily hiking 2–5 km over uneven terrain. Not suitable for those with significant mobility issues. Discuss with operator if concerned.

**Q: What if flights are cancelled?**  
A: Build buffer days into your schedule if possible. Tour operators assist with rebooking. Travel insurance covers delays.
