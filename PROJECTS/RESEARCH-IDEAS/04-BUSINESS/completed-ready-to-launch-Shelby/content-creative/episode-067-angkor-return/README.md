## 📍 Episode Overview

Episode 067 returns to Angkor Wat, Cambodia—where ancient stone meets embracing jungle, where 200 faces watch from temple towers, and where a lost civilization teaches profound lessons about impermanence, beauty, and what it means to be a "temporary custodian" of something greater than ourselves.

**Core Themes:**
- Return and homecoming
- Impermanence and transformation
- Nature reclaiming civilization
- Compassionate leadership (Bayon's faces)
- Eternal beauty (apsara carvings)
- The walk that circles back

## 📊 Research Highlights

### Key Facts
- **Angkor Wat Built:** 1113-1150 AD (King Suryavarman II)
- **Ta Prohm Built:** Late 12th century (King Jayavarman VII)
- **Bayon Faces:** 200+ faces on 54 towers
- **Apsara Carvings:** 1,500+ at Angkor Wat alone
- **Angkor Pass Prices (2025-2026):** 1-day $37, 3-day $62, 7-day $72
- **Best Time to Visit:** November-March (dry season)
- **Sunrise Time:** Gates open 5:00 AM, arrive by 4:30 AM

### Cultural Notes
- Dress code: Shoulders and knees must be covered
- Sacred site: Speak quietly, respect religious practices
- Photography: Ask permission before photographing monks
- Tipping: 10-15% appreciated in restaurants
- Currency: Dual system (USD + Cambodian Riel)
