## GETTING THERE

### Flights to Bali
**Airport**: Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS), Denpasar
**Major Hubs with Direct/Connecting Flights**:
- Singapore (SIN) - 2.5 hours, most frequent connections
- Kuala Lumpur (KUL) - 3 hours, budget options available
- Jakarta (CGK) - 1.5 hours, domestic connection
- Bangkok (BKK) - 4 hours
- Sydney (SYD) - 6 hours direct
- Melbourne (MEL) - 6.5 hours direct
- Perth (PER) - 3.5 hours direct (closest Australian city)
- Tokyo (NRT/HND) - 7 hours
- Seoul (ICN) - 7.5 hours
- Hong Kong (HKG) - 5.5 hours
- Doha (DOH) - 9 hours (Middle East connection)
- Dubai (DXB) - 10 hours (Middle East connection)

**Best Booking Strategy**:
- Book 2-3 months in advance for best prices
- Use Skyscanner or Google Flights for comparison
- Consider multi-city: fly into DPS, out of another Indonesian city
- Budget airlines: AirAsia, Jetstar, Lion Air (check baggage fees)

### Visa Requirements (2026)
**Visa on Arrival (VoA)**: Available for 90+ countries
- **Cost**: IDR 500,000 (~$32 USD)
- **Duration**: 30 days, extendable once for another 30 days
- **Process**: Pay at airport counter before immigration, or apply online (e-VoA)
- **Countries**: USA, UK, Canada, Australia, most EU, Singapore, Japan, South Korea

**Visa-Free Entry**: 10 ASEAN countries (Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand, etc.)
- **Duration**: 30 days, non-extendable

**B211A Visit Visa** (for longer stays):
- **Duration**: 60 days, extendable up to 6 months
- **Cost**: ~IDR 2,000,000 ($130 USD) + agent fees
- **Process**: Apply through visa agent before travel
- **Best for**: Digital nomads, extended stays

### Airport to Ubud
**Distance**: 35-40km, 1-1.5 hours depending on traffic

**Options**:

1. **Private Driver** (RECOMMENDED)
   - **Cost**: IDR 300,000-400,000 ($20-26 USD)
   - **Pros**: Meet & greet, AC, help with bags, reliable
   - **Booking**: Hotel can arrange, or use Klook/GetYourGuide
   - **Companies**: Bali Airport Transfer, Day Trip Driver

2. **Taxi**
   - **Cost**: IDR 350,000-450,000 ($23-29 USD)
   - **Pros**: Available on arrival, no booking needed
   - **Cons**: Touts can be aggressive, negotiate price first
   - **Official**: Blue Bird Taxi (most reliable)

3. **Grab/Gojek** (Ride-sharing apps)
   - **Cost**: IDR 250,000-350,000 ($16-23 USD)
   - **Pros**: Cheaper, app-based pricing
   - **Cons**: Pickup point at airport (need to walk), surge pricing
   - **Note**: Download apps before arrival, need local SIM or roaming

4. **Hotel Shuttle**
   - **Cost**: Free-IDI 500,000 (varies by hotel)
   - **Pros**: Convenient, reliable
   - **Cons**: Need to book in advance, may wait for other guests

**My Recommendation**: Pre-book private driver for first arrival. Stress-free, reasonable price, someone waiting for you.

## GETTING AROUND BALI

### Scooter/Motorcycle Rental
**Cost**: IDR 70,000-100,000/day ($4.50-6.50 USD)
**Weekly**: IDR 400,000-600,000 ($26-39 USD)

**Requirements**:
- International Driving Permit (IDP) with motorcycle endorsement
- Passport copy (some places ask, some don't)
- Helmet (always provided, ALWAYS wear it)
- Confidence in chaotic traffic

**Pros**: 
- Ultimate freedom, can go anywhere
- Cheap, fun, authentic Bali experience
- Easy parking

**Cons**:
- Dangerous if inexperienced (accidents are common)
- Police checkpoints target tourists (bring license!)
- Hot, rainy season is miserable
- Not suitable for long distances (>50km)

**Safety Tips**:
- Always wear helmet (police fine: IDR 500,000)
- Drive defensively—locals drive differently
- Avoid riding at night (poor visibility, animals on road)
- Don't ride in heavy rain (slippery, zero visibility)
- Take photos of scooter before rental (document existing damage)

### Private Driver Hire
**Cost**: IDR 600,000-800,000/day ($39-52 USD) for 8-10 hours
**Includes**: Driver, car, fuel, parking

**Best For**:
- Temple hopping (multiple locations in one day)
- Long distances (Ubud to East Bali, North Bali)
- Groups of 2-4 people (cost-effective split)
- First-time visitors who want stress-free transport

**How to Book**:
- Ask hotel for recommendation (most reliable)
- WhatsApp drivers directly (many have cards)
- Klook/GetYourGuide (slightly more expensive, but vetted)
- TripAdvisor forums (find highly-rated drivers)

**Typical Day Trip**: Ubud → Tegalalang → Kintamani → Besakih → Tirta Empul → Back to Ubud

### Grab/Gojek (Ride-Sharing)
**Cost**: Varies by distance, usually IDR 50,000-150,000 per trip
**Availability**: Good in Ubud, Denpasar, tourist areas
**Limitations**: Some temple areas restricted, can't pick up at certain locations

**Pros**: App-based pricing, cheap, convenient
**Cons**: Restricted areas, drivers may cancel, need data

**Tip**: Gojek also offers food delivery and massage-at-home services!

### Bicycle Rental
**Cost**: IDR 50,000-100,000/day ($3-6.50 USD)
**Best For**: Exploring Ubud center, short distances, flat areas

**Pros**: Eco-friendly, good exercise, fun
**Cons**: Very hilly, hot and humid, not safe for main roads

**Recommendation**: Good for Campuhan Ridge Walk area, not for temple touring

### Walking
**Ubud Center**: Very walkable (restaurants, shops, palace, Monkey Forest)
**Temple Areas**: Not walkable from Ubud (need transport)
**Rice Terraces**: Walkable from some Ubud hotels (20-30 min)

## WHERE TO STAY

### Ubud Areas

#### 1. Central Ubud (Jalan Raya Ubud)
**Best For**: First-time visitors, walkability, restaurants, nightlife
**Pros**: Everything within walking distance, vibrant, lots of options
**Cons**: Noisy, busy, less "authentic" feel

**Price Range**: 
- Budget: IDR 300,000-600,000/night ($20-39 USD)
- Mid: IDR 600,000-1,500,000/night ($39-97 USD)
- Luxury: IDR 1,500,000+/night ($97+ USD)

**Recommended Hotels**:
- Budget: Puri Garden Hostel, Ubud Backpackers
- Mid: The Kayon Jungle Resort, Komaneka at Monkey Forest
- Luxury: Four Seasons Sayan, Mandapa Ritz-Carlton Reserve

#### 2. Penestanan (West Ubud)
**Best For**: Quiet, artistic vibe, Campuhan Ridge access
**Pros**: Peaceful, rice field views, close to nature, cheaper
**Cons**: Need scooter/taxi for restaurants, hilly

**Price Range**: Slightly cheaper than central Ubud

#### 3. Tegallalang (North Ubud)
**Best For**: Rice terrace views, nature lovers, photographers
**Pros**: Waking up to rice terrace views, peaceful, authentic
**Cons**: Far from Ubud center (15-20 min drive), limited dining

**Price Range**: 
- Mid: IDR 800,000-2,000,000/night ($52-130 USD)
- Luxury: Hanging Gardens of Bali, Tebesaya Cottage

#### 4. Sayan (Southwest Ubud)
**Best For**: Luxury seekers, jungle views, wellness retreats
**Pros**: Stunning valley views, high-end resorts, peaceful
**Cons**: Expensive, need transport for everything

**Price Range**: Mostly luxury (IDI 2,000,000+/night, $130+ USD)

#### 5. Monkey Forest Area (South Ubud)
**Best For**: Convenience, monkey watching, central location
**Pros**: Walking distance to everything, fun monkey encounters
**Cons**: Monkeys can be aggressive, busy tourist area

## BEST TIME TO VISIT

### Dry Season (April - October)
**Best For**: Walking, hiking, photography, beach days
**Weather**: Sunny, low humidity, minimal rain
**Temperatures**: 26-32°C (79-90°F) daytime, cooler at night in highlands
**Crowds**: High in July-August, moderate in shoulder months

**Recommended**: April-May, September-October (sweet spot: good weather, fewer crowds)

### Wet Season (November - March)
**Best For**: Lush landscapes, fewer crowds, lower prices, surfing
**Weather**: Afternoon/evening storms, mornings often clear
**Temperatures**: 24-30°C (75-86°F), more humid
**Crowds**: Low except December 20-January 5 (holiday rush)

**Note**: Rice terraces are MOST green during wet season (stunning for photography)

### Monthly Breakdown

**January**: Wet, humid, fewer crowds (except New Year), good for spa/wellness
**February**: Wet season continues, cheapest month, lush greenery
**March**: Transition month, occasional rain, good deals
**April**: Start of dry season, excellent choice, Easter crowds
**May**: Perfect weather, fewer crowds, highly recommended
**June**: Dry, sunny, start of peak season
**July**: Peak season, crowded, expensive, book ahead
**August**: Peak season (European/Australian holidays), very crowded
**September**: Still dry, crowds thinning, excellent choice
**October**: End of dry season, great weather, fewer crowds, highly recommended
**November**: Start of wet season, good deals, occasional rain
**December**: Holiday rush (expensive, crowded), then quiet after Christmas

### Religious Holidays & Festivals

**Nyepi (Day of Silence)** - March/April (Balinese New Year)
- Entire island shuts down for 24 hours
- No flights, no traffic, no lights, no activities
- Tourists must stay within hotel grounds
- Unique cultural experience, but plan around it
- **2026 Date**: March 19, 2026

**Galungan & Kuningan** - Every 210 days (Balinese calendar)
- Temples decorated, ceremonies throughout island
- Great cultural experience
- **Next Dates**: Check Balinese calendar

**Full Moon (Purnama)** - Monthly
- Auspicious day for temple ceremonies
- Tirta Empul especially busy
- Good day to witness culture, expect crowds

## CULTURAL ETIQUETTE

### Temple Visits
**Dress Code**:
- Sarong required (covers legs below knees)
- Sash around waist (provided with entry or bring your own)
- Shoulders covered (no tank tops)
- Menstruating women cannot enter temples (Balinese Hindu belief)

**Behavior**:
- Speak softly, respectful tone
- Don't climb on sacred structures
- Ask before photographing ceremonies
- Don't point feet at shrines or people
- Walk clockwise around temples

**Offerings (Canang Sari)**:
- Don't step on them (walk around)
- Don't touch or move them
- They're placed on ground, doorsteps, shrines daily

### General Etiquette
**Greetings**:
- "Selamat pagi" (good morning), "Selamat siang" (good afternoon)
- Slight bow with hands in prayer position (sembah) is appreciated
- Handshakes common, but wait for woman to extend hand first

**Head & Hands**:
- Don't touch anyone's head (sacred, where soul resides)
- Use right hand for giving/receiving (left hand considered unclean)
- Don't point with index finger (use thumb or whole hand)

**Public Behavior**:
- Public displays of affection: Keep minimal (holding hands OK, kissing not appropriate)
- Dress modestly outside beach areas (cover shoulders and knees)
- Raise voice/anger: Considered shameful, stay calm

**Photography**:
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Especially important during ceremonies
- Some temples prohibit photography inside

**Bargaining**:
- Expected at markets, not in shops/restaurants
- Be friendly, smile, negotiate respectfully
- Start at 50% of asking price, meet in middle
- Don't bargain if you're not going to buy

## HEALTH & SAFETY

### Vaccinations (Recommended)
- Routine vaccines (MMR, tetanus, etc.) up to date
- Hepatitis A & B
- Typhoid
- Japanese encephalitis (for extended rural stays)
- Rabies (if planning animal encounters)

**Consult**: Travel clinic 4-6 weeks before departure

### Bali Belly (Traveler's Diarrhea)
**Prevention**:
- Drink bottled water ONLY (not tap water)
- Avoid ice in drinks (unless sure it's filtered)
- Eat at busy places (high turnover = fresh food)
- Peel fruits, avoid raw vegetables
- Wash hands frequently

**Treatment**:
- Stay hydrated (electrolyte packets helpful)
- BRAT diet (bananas, rice, applesauce, toast)
- Imodium for symptoms
- See doctor if severe/persistent (many English-speaking clinics)

### Mosquito Protection
**Dengue & Malaria Risk**: Present (low in Ubud, higher in rural areas)
**Prevention**:
- DEET repellent (30-50%)
- Long sleeves/pants at dawn/dusk
- Mosquito nets if accommodation doesn't have AC/screens
- Antimalarial meds: Consult doctor (usually not needed for Ubud)

### Sun Protection
- UV index extreme (11+) year-round
- SPF 50+ sunscreen, reapply every 2 hours
- Hat, sunglasses, seek shade 11am-3pm
- Stay hydrated (coconut water excellent for electrolytes)

### Emergency Numbers

### Hospitals & Clinics (Ubud Area)

## CONNECTIVITY

### SIM Cards
**Options**:
1. **Airport SIM** (Telkomsel/Turist SIM)
   - Cost: IDR 350,000-500,000 ($23-32) for 30 days
   - Data: 25-50GB depending on package
   - Pros: Convenient, reliable (Telkomsel best coverage)
   - Cons: More expensive than local shops

2. **Local Shop SIM** (in Ubud)
   - Cost: IDR 100,000-200,000 ($6.50-13) for 30 days
   - Data: 20-40GB
   - Pros: Cheaper, support local businesses
   - Cons: Need passport, registration takes time

3. **eSIM** (Airalo, Nomad, etc.)
   - Cost: $10-25 USD for 10-20GB
   - Pros: No physical SIM, activate before arrival
   - Cons: More expensive per GB, need eSIM-compatible phone

**Recommendation**: Telkomsel Tourist SIM at airport for convenience, or local shop in Ubud for value.

### WiFi
- Most hotels, cafes, restaurants have free WiFi
- Quality varies (good in tourist areas, spotty in rural)
- Don't rely on it for important calls/meetings
- Have backup data plan

## MONEY MATTERS

### Currency
- **Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)**
- **Bills**: 1,000, 2,000, 5,000, 10,000, 20,000, 50,000, 100,000
- **Coins**: 500, 1,000, 2,000 (rarely used)

### Exchange Rates (Approximate 2026)
- 1 USD = 15,500-16,500 IDR
- 1 EUR = 16,500-17,500 IDR
- 1 AUD = 10,000-11,000 IDR
- 1 GBP = 19,000-20,000 IDR

**Check**: XE.com or Google for current rates

### Where to Exchange
- **Avoid**: Airport (terrible rates), street money changers (scams common)
- **Use**: Authorized money changers (look for official signage), banks
- **Best**: ATMs (good rates, but check fees)

### ATMs
- Widely available in Ubud, tourist areas
- International cards accepted (Visa, Mastercard)
- Withdrawal limit: Usually IDR 2,000,000-5,000,000 per transaction
- Fees: Varies by bank, expect 20,000-50,000 IDR + your bank's foreign fee
- **Tip**: Withdraw larger amounts less frequently (fewer fees)

### Credit Cards
- Accepted at hotels, restaurants, larger shops
- Not accepted at warungs, markets, small vendors
- Visa/Mastercard widely accepted, Amex less common
- Notify bank before travel (avoid fraud blocks)
- Some places add 2-3% surcharge for credit cards

### Cash is King
- Always carry cash (small denominations)
- Markets, warungs, drivers, tips all cash
- Keep separate stash for emergency
- Don't flash large amounts in public

## FINAL TIPS FOR EPISODE 060 PILGRIMS

### If You're Walking in Shelby's Footsteps

**Campuhan Ridge Walk**:
- Go at sunrise (6-7am) for the experience Shelby described
- Bring water, wear good shoes
- Allow 1-2 hours round trip

**Tirta Empul Purification**:
- Bring change of clothes (or rent sarong there)
- Follow the flow: enter water, move from spout to spout
- Pray at each spout, or simply experience the moment
- Respect the ritual—this is sacred, not a photo op

**Uluwatu Kecak Dance**:
- Book tickets in advance (Klook or hotel)
- Arrive by 5pm for good seats
- Watch sunset during performance
- Monkeys aggressive—secure all belongings

**Tegalalang Rice Terrace**:
- Visit early morning (before 9am) or late afternoon
- Support local farmers (buy coffee, fruit from warungs)
- Don't walk on rice plants
- Swing photos: negotiate price first

### Respectful Tourism
- We're guests on this island
- Support local businesses (warungs, local drivers)
- Don't haggle too hard (few dollars mean more to them)
- Take only photos, leave only footprints
- Learn a few Indonesian words (terima kasih = thank you)
