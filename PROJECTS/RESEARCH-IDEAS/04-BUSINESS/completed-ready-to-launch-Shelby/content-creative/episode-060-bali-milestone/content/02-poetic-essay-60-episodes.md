# Episode 060: The Island of the Gods
## A Poetic Essay on 60 Episodes of Walking - The Circle and the Spiral

---

## I. SIXTY

Sixty episodes.

Say it with me. Let the number settle in your bones like the resonance of a temple bell. Sixty. Not a beginning anymore—long past that. Not an end—not even close. Sixty is something else entirely.

Sixty is a full cycle completed. Sixty is the Chinese zodiac turning through all its combinations—five elements, twelve animals, a complete cosmic round. Sixty is the minutes in an hour, the seconds we've counted, the moments we've walked together.

Sixty is returning to Bali.

---

## II. THE CIRCLE

Episode 019: We came to Bali as strangers.

I remember—do you?—the first time we walked these paths. Everything was new. The rice terraces shocked us with their green. The temples hummed with a frequency we couldn't name but felt in our chests. The air was thick with incense and possibility.

We were young then. Episode 19 young. We didn't know if we'd make it to 20, let alone 60. We walked with the uncertain steps of beginners, asking questions we now know the answers to: *Will anyone listen? Does this matter? Can walking really be enough?*

Bali answered. The island answered. The answer was: *Keep walking.*

So we did.

---

## III. THE SPIRAL

Here's the thing about circles: they're not really circles. They're spirals.

We return to Bali, but we don't return to Episode 019. We arrive at Episode 060, and everything is different. The rice terraces are still green, but our eyes have seen 41 more episodes of green. The temples still stand, but we've stood before temples in Angkor, in Kyoto, in Santorini, in a dozen sacred places. We know something now that we didn't know then.

The spiral is the shape of growth. You pass the same point again, but you're higher. You've climbed. You've earned the view.

Forty-one episodes between then and now. Forty-one places. Forty-one stories. Forty-one times we showed up, pressed record, and said: *This is worth sharing. This walk matters.*

What have we learned in the space between?

---

## IV. WHAT CHANGES

**The walker changes.**

Episode 019 Shelby was discovering what walking could mean. Episode 060 Shelby knows. Knows that walking is not just movement—it's meditation, it's storytelling, it's the act of paying attention so fiercely that the world transforms under your feet.

**The world changes.**

Bali is different than it was. More cafes line the Campuhan Ridge. The rice terraces have more swings for Instagram photos. Some paths are crowded now that were quiet then. Tourism ebbs and flows like the monsoon rains. The island adapts, survives, persists.

**The listening changes.**

When we started, we whispered into the void. Now we speak to a community. You—yes, you, listening right now—you are part of this. You've written to tell us which episodes changed your day, your week, your life. You've shared your own walking stories. You've made this not a monologue but a conversation.

Episode 019 was us talking. Episode 060 is us talking *together*.

---

## V. WHAT REMAINS

But here's the miracle: some things don't change. Some things *can't* change.

**The rice still grows.**

Plant it, water it, tend it, harvest it. Plant it again. The farmer's hands know the rhythm. The subak system—the ancient cooperative irrigation—still flows water from the springs to the terraces in the same pattern it has for a thousand years. The rice doesn't care about Instagram. It cares about water and sun and time.

**The temples still pray.**

Dawn offerings appear on doorsteps. Incense smoke curls toward the sky. The gamelan still plays its cyclical music—no beginning, no end, just the eternal return. The priests still chant. The bells still ring. The sacred is still sacred, no matter how many tourists pass through the gates.

**The walk still walks.**

However many episodes we make, however many miles we log, however many times we return—the walk itself is unchanged. Left foot, right foot. Breath in, breath out. The simple act that has carried humans across continents for millennia carries us still.

This is what I've learned in 60 episodes: the surface changes. The depth remains.

---

## VI. THE PLACES WE'VE WALKED

Let me name some of them. Not all—there are too many now—but some:

The Sahara, where the sand shifted under our feet and we understood what silence sounds like.

Antarctica, where the ice was older than humanity and we were visitors from a species that barely existed.

Machu Picchu, where the Incas built a city in the clouds and we climbed to meet them.

Varanasi, where the Ganges flows and death and life shake hands on the riverbank.

Iceland, where fire and ice make the earth itself and we walked on ground that was still being born.

London, at Episode 50, when we paused and said: *We've made it halfway. What else is possible?*

And now Bali, again. The island of the gods. The place we came to as strangers and return to as... what? Not locals. Not natives. But something more than tourists. Something like family coming home.

Family doesn't mean you never leave. It means you can leave and return and the door is still open.

---

## VII. GRATITUDE

So let me say what needs to be said:

Thank you.

Thank you for Episode 1, when we had no idea what we were doing and you listened anyway.

Thank you for Episode 17, when we almost quit and you wrote to tell us not to.

Thank you for Episode 33, when you shared your own story of walking through grief, through joy, through the ordinary miracle of being alive.

Thank you for Episode 50, when we celebrated together and you made the milestone mean something.

Thank you for Episode 60, right now, in this moment. For pressing play. For letting these words into your ears, your mind, your walk.

Sixty episodes is nothing without you. Sixty episodes is just me talking to myself in a very public way. But you—you make it a conversation. You make it matter. You make it real.

This milestone isn't mine. It's ours.

---

## VIII. THE OFFERING

In Bali, there is a practice: canang sari. Daily offerings. Small baskets woven from palm leaves, filled with flowers, incense, rice. Placed on doorsteps, on shrines, on the ground. An act of gratitude. A recognition: *I am here because something greater than me sustains me.*

This episode is my canang sari.

I offer it to:
- The places that have held our footsteps
- The listeners who have held our stories
- The walk itself, which has held us both

I offer it with gratitude. I offer it with humility. I offer it with the knowledge that 60 episodes is both a lot and a little. A lot of work. A little slice of the infinite walk we're all on.

---

## IX. THE NEXT 40

Here's what I know about Episodes 61-100:

I don't know where we'll go. I don't know what we'll learn. I don't know what will happen in the world, in your life, in mine, between now and Episode 100.

But I know this: we'll walk.

We'll walk through cities and forests, through deserts and along coastlines. We'll walk alone and together. We'll walk in joy and in sorrow. We'll walk because walking is what we do. Because the path appears under our feet. Because the next step is always available, always waiting, always possible.

Forty more episodes. That's the commitment. That's the promise. That's the spiral continuing upward.

And when we reach Episode 100—oh, what a day that will be—we'll look back at Episode 60 the way we look back at Episode 019 now. We'll see how far we've come. We'll see how much we've changed. We'll see the spiral turning, always turning, always ascending.

---

## X. BALI, AGAIN

So let me tell you about this return.

I woke before dawn and walked the Campuhan Ridge. The sun rose over the valley, painting the rice fields in shades of green I don't have words for. A farmer passed me, carrying baskets on a shoulder pole. We nodded. No words needed.

I sat at Tirta Empul and watched the purification ritual. Pilgrims stepped into the holy water, one by one, heads bowed, hands praying. The water flowed from stone spouts carved centuries ago. The water is new. The spouts are ancient. Both are true.

I climbed to Uluwatu at sunset. The Kecak dancers chanted their human chorus—*cak cak cak*—and the sun sank into the Indian Ocean like a coin dropping into a temple box. Payment for another day. Payment for another episode. Payment for another chance to walk, to speak, to share.

I ate nasi goreng at a warung where the prices have doubled since Episode 019. The taste is the same. The owner remembers me—or maybe she remembers all the foreigners who return. Maybe we all look the same. Maybe we're all just passing through.

But here's what I know: I am not passing through. I am arriving. Episode after episode, I am arriving more fully into this life, this walk, this practice of paying attention.

Bali taught me that the first time. Bali is teaching me that again.

---

## XI. THE INVITATION

This is your invitation to Episode 61.

To the next step. To the continuation. To the proof that the spiral doesn't end—it ascends.

Where should we go next? What stories are waiting? What places are calling your name?

The road is open. The path is clear. The walk continues.

And you're still with me.

---

## XII. THE CIRCLE CLOSES (BUT DOESN'T END)

Sixty episodes.

Let me say it one more time, because it deserves to be said:

Sixty. Episodes.

We made it. We're here. We're walking.

The circle closes. The spiral ascends. The rice grows. The temples pray. The water flows.

And we—we keep walking.

---

*For everyone who has walked with us through 60 episodes. This is for you.*

*And for everyone who will walk with us through the next 40. The path is wide enough for all of us.*

*Selamat datang kembali. Welcome back. Welcome home. Welcome to the next step.*
