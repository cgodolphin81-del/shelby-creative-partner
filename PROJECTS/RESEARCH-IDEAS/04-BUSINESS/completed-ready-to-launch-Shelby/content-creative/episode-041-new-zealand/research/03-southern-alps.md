# The Southern Alps / Kā Tiritiri o te Moana

## Overview

The Southern Alps are the mountainous backbone of New Zealand's South Island, a dramatic range that rises from the Tasman Sea to the Pacific Ocean. This young, rapidly rising mountain chain contains New Zealand's highest peaks, largest glaciers, and some of the most spectacular alpine scenery on Earth.

## Māori Names & Significance

### Kā Tiritiri o te Moana
- **Meaning:** "The place of the great reflection of the sea" or "The mountains that stand like a barrier"
- **Significance:** Traditional Māori name for the Southern Alps
- **Ngāi Tahu:** The local iwi with mana whenua over the region

### Aoraki / Mount Cook
- **Height:** 3,724 meters (12,218 feet) — New Zealand's highest peak
- **Māori Legend:** Aoraki was a young boy who, with his brothers, climbed onto their canoe (the South Island) and was turned to stone
- **Dual Name:** Officially known as Aoraki / Mount Cook since 1998
- **Sacred Status:** Considered tapu (sacred) by Ngāi Tahu

### Other Māori Peak Names
- **Aoraki:** Mount Cook (3,724m)
- **Mount Tasman:** 3,497m (no traditional Māori name recorded)
- **Mount Sefton:** 3,151m
- **Mount Aspiring:** 3,033m — Tititea in Māori

## Geography & Formation

### Range Statistics
- **Length:** 450 kilometers (280 miles)
- **Width:** 10-60 kilometers (6-37 miles)
- **Highest Peak:** Aoraki / Mount Cook, 3,724m (12,218 ft)
- **Peaks Over 3,000m:** 19 mountains
- **Peaks Over 2,000m:** Over 350 mountains

### Geological Formation
- **Age:** Young mountains, still rising
- **Formation:** Collision of Pacific and Australian tectonic plates
- **Uplift Rate:** 10mm per year (among the fastest rising mountains globally)
- **Erosion:** Rapid erosion due to high rainfall and glaciation
- **Rock Type:** Primarily greywacke (hard sedimentary rock)

### The Alpine Fault
- **Type:** Major transform fault
- **Location:** Runs along the western edge of the Southern Alps
- **Movement:** Pacific Plate moving west relative to Australian Plate
- **Earthquake Risk:** Major earthquakes occur approximately every 300 years
- **Last Major Event:** 1717 AD (magnitude ~8.0)

## Glaciers

### Overview
The Southern Alps contain over 3,000 glaciers, though most are retreating due to climate change.

### Major Glaciers

**Franz Josef Glacier / Kā Roimata o Hine Hukatere**
- **Length:** 12 kilometers (7.5 miles)
- **Terminus:** 300 meters above sea level (unusually low for latitude)
- **Name Meaning:** "The tears of Hine Hukatere" (legend of a woman who loved climbing)
- **Access:** Guided walks and heli-hikes available
- **Status:** Retreating but still accessible

**Fox Glacier / Te Moeka o Tuawe**
- **Length:** 13 kilometers (8 miles)
- **Name Meaning:** "The bed of Tuawe" (legendary lover of Hine Hukatere)
- **Features:** Ice falls, seracs, crevasses
- **Access:** Guided hikes, helicopter tours
- **Status:** Retreating, access varies

**Tasman Glacier**
- **Length:** 27 kilometers (17 miles) — longest glacier in New Zealand
- **Location:** Eastern side of Southern Alps, below Aoraki/Mt Cook
- **Features:** Tasman Lake (glacial lake formed by retreat)
- **Access:** Boat tours on Tasman Lake, helicopter tours
- **Status:** Rapidly retreating, lake expanding

**Hooker Glacier**
- **Location:** Below Aoraki/Mount Cook
- **Features:** Terminal lake, icebergs
- **Access:** Hooker Valley Track (day walk)
- **Status:** Retreating

### Glacier Retreat
- **Cause:** Climate change, rising temperatures
- **Rate:** Many glaciers retreating 10-30 meters per year
- **Impact:** Tourism access changing, water resources affected
- **Monitoring:** Regular surveys by NIWA (National Institute of Water and Atmospheric Research)

## Climate & Weather

### General Characteristics
- **West Coast:** Extremely wet (up to 15,000mm annual rainfall)
- **East Coast:** Drier, more continental climate (600-1,000mm)
- **Rain Shadow:** Eastern side sheltered from prevailing westerlies
- **Temperature:** Varies dramatically with altitude
- **Snow:** Year-round snow above 2,500m

### Weather Patterns
- **Prevailing Winds:** Westerlies from Tasman Sea
- **Orographic Rainfall:** Moist air forced up mountains, drops rain on west side
- **Nor'wester:** Warm, dry wind on eastern side
- **Southerly Change:** Cold fronts from Antarctic, dramatic temperature drops
- **Four Seasons in a Day:** Rapid weather changes common

### Seasonal Conditions
- **Summer (Dec-Feb):** Warmest, most stable, best for hiking
- **Autumn (Mar-May):** Clear air, stable weather, fewer crowds
- **Winter (Jun-Aug):** Snow, skiing, some passes closed
- **Spring (Sep-Nov):** Variable, snow melting, waterfalls at peak

## National Parks

### Aoraki / Mount Cook National Park
- **Area:** 707 square kilometers
- **Established:** 1953
- **Features:** New Zealand's highest peaks, largest glaciers
- **UNESCO:** Part of Te Wāhipounamu World Heritage Site
- **Activities:** Hiking, mountaineering, stargazing, scenic flights

### Westland Tai Poutini National Park
- **Area:** 1,175 square kilometers
- **Established:** 1960
- **Features:** Franz Josef and Fox glaciers, rainforest
- **UNESCO:** Part of Te Wāhipounamu World Heritage Site
- **Activities:** Glacier hikes, rainforest walks, helicopter tours

### Mount Aspiring National Park
- **Area:** 3,555 square kilometers
- **Established:** 1964
- **Features:** Mount Aspiring, Matukituki Valley, Routeburn Track
- **UNESCO:** Part of Te Wāhipounamu World Heritage Site
- **Activities:** Multi-day hikes, mountaineering, remote wilderness

### Fiordland National Park
- **Area:** 12,607 square kilometers (largest in NZ)
- **Established:** 1952
- **Features:** Fiords, waterfalls, ancient rainforest
- **UNESCO:** Part of Te Wāhipounamu World Heritage Site
- **Activities:** Milford Track, Doubtful Sound, kayaking

## Great Walks

The Southern Alps contain several of New Zealand's nine Great Walks:

### Milford Track
- **Duration:** 4 days
- **Distance:** 53.5 kilometers (33 miles)
- **Features:** Waterfalls, ancient forest, Mackinnon Pass
- **Reputation:** "The finest walk in the world"
- **Booking:** Essential, books out months in advance

### Routeburn Track
- **Duration:** 3-4 days
- **Distance:** 32 kilometers (20 miles)
- **Features:** Alpine passes, lakes, mountain views
- **Access:** From both Queenstown and Te Anau sides
- **Season:** November-April (hut season)

### Kepler Track
- **Duration:** 3-4 days
- **Distance:** 60 kilometers (37 miles)
- **Location:** Near Te Anau
- **Features:** Lakes, mountains, alpine meadows
- **Unique:** Only Great Walk that forms a loop

### Rakiura Track (Stewart Island)
- **Note:** Not in Southern Alps, but part of Great Walks system

## Wildlife

### Birds
- **Kea:** World's only alpine parrot, intelligent, curious
- **Rock Wren:** Rare, lives above treeline
- **Kepler:** Not applicable (that's a walk)
- **Falcons:** New Zealand falcon, endangered
- **Morepork:** Native owl, heard at night
- **Tūī, Bellbird:** Common in forested areas

### Mammals
- **No Native Land Mammals:** Except bats and marine mammals
- **Introduced Species:** Deer, chamois, tahr (popular for hunting)
- **Marine Mammals:** Seals, dolphins on coasts
- **Conservation:** Predator-free islands, intensive pest control

### Plants
- **Beech Forest:** Dominant forest type (red, silver, mountain beech)
- **Alpine Flora:** Over 500 alpine plant species
- **Hebe:** Diverse genus, many alpine species
- **Mount Cook Lily:** Large buttercup, iconic alpine flower
- **Moss & Lichen:** Abundant in wet western forests

## Mountaineering History

### First Ascents
- **Aoraki/Mt Cook:** 1894, Tom Fyfe, George Graham, Jack Clarke
- **Mount Tasman:** 1895, Douglas, von Lendenfeld, Clarke
- **Many Peaks:** First ascents in late 1800s and early 1900s

### Sir Edmund Hillary
- **Connection:** Trained in Southern Alps before Everest
- **Local Hero:** New Zealand's most famous mountaineer
- **Legacy:** Hillary Museum at Aoraki/Mt Cook village
- **First Everest Ascent:** 1953, with Tenzing Norgay

### Modern Mountaineering
- **Difficulty:** Range from beginner to extreme
- **Guides:** Professional guiding companies operate
- **Equipment:** Full alpine gear required for serious climbs
- **Safety:** Weather changes rapidly, accidents occur

## Tourism & Access

### Main Gateways
- **Aoraki/Mt Cook Village:** Main base for eastern side
- **Franz Josef:** West coast glacier town
- **Fox Glacier:** West coast glacier town
- **Wanaka:** Eastern side, gateway to Mount Aspiring
- **Queenstown:** Adventure capital, southern access

### Scenic Flights
- **Availability:** From multiple towns
- **Options:** Fixed-wing, helicopter
- **Features:** Glacier landings, mountain views
- **Price:** $200-600 NZD depending on duration

### Hiking & Tramping
- **Day Walks:** Numerous options for all levels
- **Multi-Day:** Great Walks require booking
- **Independent Tramping:** Backcountry huts available
- **Safety:** Register intentions, check weather, carry proper gear

### Stargazing
- **Aoraki Mackenzie International Dark Sky Reserve:** World's largest
- **Quality:** Exceptional stargazing, minimal light pollution
- **Activities:** Guided star tours, observatory visits
- **Best Time:** Clear winter nights

## Conservation & Challenges

### Te Wāhipounamu
- **UNESCO Status:** World Heritage Site since 1990
- **Area:** 26,000 square kilometers
- **Significance:** Best modern representation of Gondwana flora and fauna
- **Protection:** Strict conservation management

### Threats
- **Climate Change:** Glaciers retreating, ecosystems changing
- **Invasive Species:** Deer, possums, stoats, rats
- **Tourism Pressure:** Managing visitor numbers and impact
- **Infrastructure:** Roads, tracks, facilities in fragile environment

### Conservation Efforts
- **Predator Control:** Intensive trapping and poisoning programs
- **Native Restoration:** Replanting, species translocation
- **Sustainable Tourism:** Guidelines for operators
- **Research:** Ongoing monitoring of glaciers, wildlife, ecosystems

## Best Times to Visit

### For Hiking
- **Best:** December-March (summer, stable weather)
- **Shoulder:** November, April (fewer crowds, variable weather)
- **Winter:** May-October (snow, some tracks closed)

### For Scenic Flights
- **Best:** Year-round (weather dependent)
- **Clearest:** Winter months (less cloud)
- **Warmest:** Summer months

### For Skiing
- **Season:** June-October
- **Best Snow:** July-August
- **Heli-Skiing:** Available in remote areas

### For Photography
- **Best Light:** Sunrise and sunset
- **Clearest Air:** Winter months
- **Most Dramatic:** Stormy weather (if safe)

## Interesting Facts

1. The Southern Alps are still rising at 10mm per year
2. Despite the name, there's no "Northern Alps" in New Zealand
3. The west coast receives up to 15,000mm of rain annually — among the wettest places on Earth
4. Aoraki/Mount Cook was 3,764m before a 1991 rockfall reduced its height
5. Franz Josef Glacier retreats and advances independently of other glaciers
6. The Kea is the world's only alpine parrot and is highly intelligent
7. The Tasman Glacier has lost over 5km in length since 1990
8. Mount Aspiring National Park is larger than some countries
9. The Alpine Fault is capable of producing magnitude 8+ earthquakes
10. Fiordland receives more rainfall than almost anywhere else on Earth
