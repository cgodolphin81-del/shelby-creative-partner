## GETTING THERE

### From La Paz (Most Common Route)

#### By Flight ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Duration**: ~1 hour
- **Airlines**: Boliviana de Aviación (BoA), Amaszonas
- **Cost**: $100-200 USD roundtrip
- **Frequency**: 1-2 flights daily
- **Airport**: El Alto International (LPZ) → Uyuni Airport (UYU)
- **Note**: Flights can be delayed/cancelled due to weather—build in buffer days

#### By Overnight Bus
- **Duration**: 8-10 hours
- **Cost**: $15-25 USD
- **Departure**: Terminal de Buses, La Paz
- **Companies**: Trans Omar, La Paz Tours, others
- **Comfort**: Reclining seats, some with semi-cama (half-bed)
- **Departure Time**: Usually 9-10 PM, arrive 6-7 AM
- **Tip**: Bring warm layers, neck pillow, snacks

#### By Private Transfer
- **Duration**: 6-7 hours
- **Cost**: $200-400 USD depending on vehicle
- **Flexibility**: Stop for photos, breakfast, bathroom breaks
- **Best For**: Groups, those prone to motion sickness
- **Booking**: Arrange through tour operators or hotels

### From Other Cities

#### From Oruro
- **Bus**: 3-4 hours, $10-15 USD
- **Train**: Scenic route, 7 hours, $25-50 USD (check schedule)
- **Note**: Oruro is on the main La Paz-Uyuni route

#### From Potosí
- **Bus**: 3-4 hours, $10-15 USD
- **Combined Tours**: Many 3-day tours start/end in Potosí

#### From Tupiza
- **Tour Route**: Common starting point for 3-4 day salt flat tours
- **Bus**: 2-3 hours to Uyuni

### International Access
- **Fly into**: Viru Viru International (Santa Cruz) or El Alto (La Paz)
- **Visa**: Check requirements for your nationality
  - US citizens: Visa on arrival (~$160 USD)
  - EU/UK/Canada/Australia: Usually visa-free for 90 days
- **Vaccinations**: Yellow fever recommended for some regions

## BEST TIME TO VISIT

### For the Mirror Effect 🌊
**When**: December - April (Rainy Season)
**Peak**: January - March

**Pros:**
- Perfect mirror reflections
- Surreal "walking on clouds" experience
- Dramatic sky reflections
- Unique photography opportunities

**Cons:**
- Some areas may be inaccessible due to flooding
- Tours may be rerouted
- Rain can disrupt plans
- Isla Incahuasi may be unreachable
- Colder nights

**Ideal Conditions:**
- Light rain the day before
- Clear, calm day of visit
- No wind (critical for mirror effect)
- Shallow water depth (2-10 cm)

### For Salt Patterns & Island Access 🏜️
**When**: May - November (Dry Season)
**Peak**: June - October

**Pros:**
- Hexagonal salt crystal patterns visible
- Full access to Isla Incahuasi and other islands
- Clear skies, intense sunlight
- Stable weather
- Better road conditions

**Cons:**
- No mirror effect
- Intense sun reflection (eye protection essential)
- Cold nights (can drop below freezing)
- Very bright during midday

**Ideal For:**
- Perspective photography
- Island exploration
- Multi-day tours to lagoons
- Clear sky photography

### Shoulder Seasons 🌅
**When**: April-May, November-December

**Pros:**
- Possible to experience both conditions
- Fewer tourists
- Lower prices
- Unpredictable = adventure!

**Cons:**
- Luck-dependent
- Conditions can change daily
- May not get full mirror or full dry experience

**Recommendation**: If mirror effect is your priority, aim for February. If you want guaranteed access and clear skies, aim for August.

## ALTITUDE & HEALTH

### Altitude Sickness (Soroche)

**Salar de Uyuni Elevation**: 3,656m (11,995 ft)
**La Paz Elevation**: 3,650m (11,975 ft)

#### Symptoms
- Headache
- Nausea
- Fatigue
- Shortness of breath
- Dizziness
- Loss of appetite
- Difficulty sleeping

#### Prevention
1. **Acclimatize First**: Spend 2-3 days in La Paz before going to Uyuni
2. **Stay Hydrated**: Drink 3-4 liters of water daily
3. **Avoid Alcohol**: Especially first 48 hours
4. **Eat Light**: Carbohydrate-rich meals
5. **Coca Tea**: Local remedy, widely available
6. **Medication**: Diamox (acetazolamide) - consult doctor
7. **Move Slowly**: No running, take it easy first day
8. **Sleep Low**: If possible, sleep at lower elevation

#### What to Do If Affected
- Rest and hydrate
- Descend if symptoms worsen
- Seek medical attention if severe
- Oxygen is available in Uyuni clinics

### Who Should Be Cautious
- Heart or lung conditions
- Pregnant women
- Elderly travelers
- Those with previous severe altitude sickness
- **Consult your doctor before traveling**

## WHAT TO PACK

### Essential Gear

#### Clothing
- **Layers**: Temperature swings are extreme
  - Base layer (moisture-wicking)
  - Insulating layer (fleece or down)
  - Outer shell (wind/waterproof)
- **Warm Jacket**: Even in summer, nights are cold
- **Hat**: Warm beanie for cold nights
- **Gloves**: For early morning/evening
- **Comfortable Walking Shoes**: Closed-toe recommended
- **Sandals**: For salt hotel showers

#### Sun Protection (CRITICAL)
- **Sunscreen**: SPF 50+, high UV at altitude
- **Lip Balm**: With SPF, salt air is drying
- **Sunglasses**: Polarized, UV protection (Category 3-4)
- **Hat**: Wide-brimmed for sun protection
- **Long Sleeves**: Despite heat, protect skin

#### Photography
- **Camera**: With extra batteries (cold drains batteries)
- **Polarizing Filter**: Enhances reflections
- **Tripod**: For sunrise/sunset/night shots
- **Memory Cards**: Extra capacity
- **Lens Cloth**: Salt spray can coat lenses
- **Waterproof Bag**: Protect gear from water/dust

#### Health & Safety
- **Altitude Medication**: Diamox (if prescribed)
- **Pain Relievers**: For altitude headaches
- **Stomach Medication**: Imodium, antacids
- **Hand Sanitizer**: Limited facilities
- **Personal Medications**: Bring enough + extras
- **First Aid Kit**: Basic supplies
- **Insect Repellent**: For extended tours

#### Miscellaneous
- **Cash**: Bolivianos, small bills (ATMs limited)
- **Passport**: Required for some checkpoints
- **Travel Insurance**: Essential, ensure altitude coverage
- **Power Bank**: For charging devices
- **Headlamp**: For early morning starts
- **Water Bottle**: Reusable, stay hydrated
- **Snacks**: Energy bars, nuts
- **Toilet Paper**: Carry your own
- **Wet Wipes**: For cleaning

### What NOT to Bring
- Expensive jewelry (unnecessary)
- Too much luggage (space limited in jeeps)
- Single-use plastics (be environmentally conscious)
- Drones (restricted in some areas)

## TOUR OPTIONS

### Day Tours (Most Popular)

**Typical Itinerary:**
- 7:00 AM: Hotel pickup in Uyuni
- 8:00 AM: Arrive at salt flat
- 8:30-11:30: Explore salt flat, photos, mirror effect
- 12:00 PM: Lunch (often at salt hotel or picnic)
- 1:00-3:00 PM: Visit Isla Incahuasi (dry season)
- 4:00 PM: Train cemetery
- 5:00 PM: Return to Uyuni

**What's Included:**
- Transport (4WD jeep)
- Driver/guide
- Lunch
- Entrance fees

**Group Size**: 4-6 people per jeep

### 3-Day Extended Tours

**Typical Itinerary:**

**Day 1**: Uyuni → Salt Flat → Isla Incahuasi → Colchani (salt village)
- Overnight in Colchani or salt hotel

**Day 2**: Salt Flat → Ojo de Agua (hot springs) → Laguna Colorada
- See flamingos, volcanic landscapes
- Overnight near lagoons

**Day 3**: Laguna Colorada → Desert → Rock formations → Uyuni or Tupiza
- Return to Uyuni or continue to Tupiza

**Highlights:**
- Multiple lagoons with flamingos
- Hot springs
- Desert landscapes
- Rock formations
- Wildlife viewing

**Accommodation**: Basic hostels or refugios (can be cold!)

### Private Tours

**Benefits:**
- Flexible timing (chase the light!)
- Personalized photo stops
- Choose your own pace
- Privacy for special moments
- Custom itinerary

**Cost**: 2-3x group tour prices

**Best For:**
- Photographers
- Couples
- Special occasions
- Those with specific requirements

## PHOTOGRAPHY TIPS

### For Mirror Effect
- **Timing**: Early morning or late afternoon (best light)
- **Conditions**: Calm days only (wind ruins reflections)
- **Angle**: Low angle maximizes mirror effect
- **Filter**: Polarizing filter enhances reflections
- **Focus**: Manual focus often works better
- **Exposure**: Slightly underexpose for richer colors

### For Salt Patterns
- **Time**: Midday sun creates strong shadows in cracks
- **Angle**: Low angle emphasizes texture
- **Composition**: Use leading lines of polygons
- **Details**: Get close for abstract patterns

### For Perspective Photos
- **Technique**: Forced perspective (objects appear tiny/huge)
- **Distance**: Photographer far from subject
- **Height**: Camera low to ground
- **Examples**: "Holding" the horizon, "crushing" friends

### For Sunrise/Sunset
- **Arrive Early**: 30-45 minutes before sunrise
- **Stay Late**: Blue hour after sunset is magical
- **Dress Warm**: Very cold at these times
- **Tripod**: Essential for low light
- **Bracket**: Take multiple exposures

### Night Photography
- **Milky Way**: Visible May-September
- **Location**: Away from any light pollution
- **Settings**: Wide aperture, high ISO, long exposure
- **Focus**: Manual focus on bright star
- **Safety**: Don't wander far in darkness

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money
- **Currency**: Boliviano (BOB)
- **Exchange**: Better rates in La Paz than Uyuni
- **ATMs**: Limited in Uyuni, bring cash
- **Cards**: Some hotels accept, don't rely on it
- **Budget**: Bring extra for emergencies

### Language
- **Spanish**: Essential for basic communication
- **Quechua/Aymara**: Indigenous languages
- **English**: Limited outside tourist areas
- **Learn**: Basic Spanish phrases helpful

### Connectivity
- **WiFi**: Available in most hotels (speed varies)
- **Mobile**: Entel has best coverage
- **SIM Cards**: Available in La Paz, Uyuni
- **Expect**: Limited connectivity on salt flat

### Safety
- **Crime**: Low, but watch belongings
- **Solo Travel**: Generally safe
- **Women Travelers**: Common, take normal precautions
- **Emergency**: Tourist police in Uyuni
- **Insurance**: Essential, ensure altitude coverage

### Environmental Responsibility
- **Leave No Trace**: Pack out all trash
- **Stay on Paths**: Protect salt surface
- **Wildlife**: Observe flamingos from distance
- **Water**: Bring reusable bottle
- **Support Local**: Choose local tour operators

### Cultural Respect
- **Photography**: Ask before photographing locals
- **Tipping**: 10% standard in restaurants
- **Guides**: $5-10 USD/day tip appreciated
- **Bargaining**: Acceptable in markets, not restaurants

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Not Acclimatizing**: Spend time in La Paz first
2. **Underestimating Sun**: UV is intense—protect yourself
3. **Not Bringing Cash**: ATMs unreliable
4. **Booking Last Minute**: Peak season sells out
5. **Ignoring Weather**: Check forecast, be flexible
6. **Overpacking**: Limited space in jeeps
7. **Skipping Insurance**: Altitude emergencies happen
8. **Expecting Luxury**: Facilities are basic
9. **Rushing**: Allow time for the experience
10. **Forgetting the Moment**: Put the camera down sometimes
