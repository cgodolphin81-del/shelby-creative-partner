## ✈️ GETTING TO STOCKHOLM

### By Air

#### Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN)
- **Distance:** 37 km north of city center
- **Airlines:** SAS, Norwegian, Ryanair, major international carriers
- **Transfer Options:**
  - **Arlanda Express:** Train to Central Station, 20 minutes, ~$30 USD
    - Runs every 10-15 minutes
    - Fastest option, tickets online or at station
  - **Flygbussarna:** Airport coach, 45 minutes, ~$15 USD
    - Multiple stops in city
    - Budget-friendly, frequent departures
  - **Taxi:** 40-50 minutes, ~$50-70 USD
    - Fixed price available (ask before getting in)
    - Good for groups or late arrivals
  - **Public Bus + Train:** Commuter train (pendeltåg), 50 minutes, ~$5 USD
    - Most budget option
    - Requires SL travel card

#### Stockholm Bromma Airport (BMA)
- **Distance:** 8 km west of city center
- **Airlines:** Primarily domestic and European flights
- **Transfer Options:**
  - **Bus:** Line 110 or 152 to city, 25 minutes, ~$5 USD
  - **Taxi:** 20 minutes, ~$30-40 USD

#### Stockholm Skavsta (NYO)
- **Distance:** 100 km south (used by Ryanair, Wizz Air)
- **Transfer:** Flygbussarna coach, 80 minutes, ~$20 USD
- **Note:** Budget airlines use this airport—factor in travel time

### By Train
- **From Copenhagen:** SJ high-speed train, 5 hours, ~$60-100 USD
  - Scenic route across Öresund Bridge
  - Book in advance for best prices
- **From Gothenburg:** 3 hours, ~$40-70 USD
- **From Oslo:** 6 hours, ~$50-90 USD
- **Central Station:** Stockholm C, heart of city, metro access

### By Ferry
- **From Helsinki, Finland:** Viking Line, Silja Line, overnight cruises
  - 16-17 hours, cabins available
  - Experience: Party船上, duty-free shopping, arrival in morning
  - Prices: $50-200+ depending on cabin
- **From Tallinn, Estonia:** 16 hours overnight
- **Terminals:** Värtahamnen or Stadsgården, bus/metro to center

### By Bus
- **FlixBus:** From Berlin (12h), Copenhagen (8h), Oslo (7h)
- **Budget option:** $30-80 depending on origin
- **Terminal:** Cityterminalen (next to Central Station)

## ☕ FIKA CULTURE

### What is Fika?
Fika is the Swedish coffee break ritual—more than just coffee, it's a social institution. It happens 2-3 times daily and is considered essential, not optional.

### Fika Etiquette
1. **Sit Down:** Fika is not grab-and-go
2. **Take Your Time:** 30-60 minutes is normal
3. **Order Coffee + Pastry:** Minimum expectation
4. **Socialize:** Talk, relax, people-watch
5. **No Rush:** Cafés won't hurry you out

### Essential Fika Pastries
- **Kanelbullar:** Cinnamon buns (the classic)
- **Kardemummabullar:** Cardamom buns (slightly more sophisticated)
- **Chokladbollar:** Chocolate balls (oats, cocoa, coconut, no baking required)
- **Prinsesstårta:** Princess cake (green marzipan, special occasions)
- **Bullar:** General term for sweet buns

### Best Fika Spots

#### Gamla Stan
- **Vete-Katten:** Classic bakery since 1928, red leather booths, traditional
- **Chokladkoppen:** Hot chocolate specialty, cozy, tourist-friendly
- **Café Pascal:** Modern, cardamom buns, local favorite

#### Södermalm
- **Café Saturnus:** Vegan fika, creative pastries, hip atmosphere
- **Fabrique:** Artisan breads, multiple locations, always fresh
- **Drop Coffee:** Specialty coffee, minimalist, serious about beans

#### Östermalm
- **Sturekatten:** Traditional, upscale, white tablecloths
- **Östermalms Saluhall:** Food hall, multiple vendors, historic

#### General Tips
- **Peak Times:** 10-11am and 2-4pm (avoid if you want seats)
- **Payment:** Card only at most places (Swedes rarely use cash)
- **Tipping:** Not expected, but rounding up is appreciated

## 🗓️ BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### Peak Season: June-August
**Pros:**
- Warm weather (20-25°C / 68-77°F)
- Long days (18+ hours daylight in June)
- All attractions open, extended hours
- Archipelago fully accessible
- Outdoor dining, festivals, events
- Midnight sun experience

**Cons:**
- Crowds (especially Gamla Stan, Vasa Museum)
- Highest prices (accommodation up 50-100%)
- Book months in advance
- Some locals away on summer vacation

**Best For:** First-time visitors, archipelago exploration, outdoor activities

### Shoulder Season: May & September
**Pros:**
- Fewer crowds
- Mild weather (12-18°C / 54-64°F)
- Good light for photography
- Lower prices than summer
- Most attractions still open

**Cons:**
- Some seasonal closures begin (late September)
- Cooler, pack layers
- Archipelago less lively

**Best For:** Budget travelers, photographers, city exploration

### Winter: November-March
**Pros:**
- Christmas markets (late November-December)
- Cozy atmosphere (mysig—Swedish hygge)
- Lowest prices (except Christmas/New Year)
- Ice skating, winter activities
- Northern Lights possible (rare, but possible)
- Nobel Week (December 8-14)

**Cons:**
- Cold (-5 to 5°C / 23-41°F)
- Short days (6 hours daylight in December)
- Some attractions reduced hours
- Archipelago limited access

**Best For:** Christmas markets, budget travelers, cozy culture, Nobel enthusiasts

### Special Events

#### Nobel Week (December 8-14)
- Nobel Prize ceremonies and banquets
- Public lectures by laureates
- Nobel Museum special exhibits
- **Book Early:** Hotels fill up, prices surge

#### Midsummer (Late June)
- Traditional celebration (Friday-Saturday)
- Maypole dancing, flower crowns
- Herring, aquavit, strawberries
- **Note:** Many businesses close, city empties as people go to countryside

#### Stockholm Culture Festival (August)
- Free concerts, performances, workshops
- City-wide celebration
- Great atmosphere

#### Royal Marathon (Early June)
- 42km through city streets
- Spectator-friendly
- City buzzes with energy

## 🚇 GETTING AROUND

### Public Transport (SL)
Stockholm has excellent public transport: metro (Tunnelbana), buses, trams, commuter trains, and some ferries.

**Metro (Tunnelbana):**
- 3 lines: Red, Green, Blue
- Clean, safe, efficient
- Art stations: Solna Centrum, Stadion, T-Centralen (must-see)
- Runs 5am-1am (24 hours on weekends)

**Buses:**
- Extensive network, reaches everywhere
- Night buses on weekends
- Real-time info in SL app

**Trams:**
- Line 7 to Djurgården (museums)
- Nockebybanan to western suburbs
- Scenic, historic

**Commuter Trains (Pendeltåg):**
- To Arlanda Airport, suburbs
- Faster than metro for longer distances

**Ferries:**
- Djurgårdsfärjan: Slussen to Djurgården (tourist route, not SL)
- Waxholmsbolaget: Archipelago ferries (SL pass valid on some)

### Walking
- Stockholm is very walkable
- Gamla Stan best explored on foot
- Comfortable shoes essential (cobblestones!)
- Distances: Central to Gamla Stan (15 min), to Södermalm (20 min)

### Biking
- **City Bikes:** Stockholm City Bikes (April-October)
  - Day pass: 65 SEK, 3-day: 165 SEK
  - 100+ stations around city
  - First 30 minutes free, then small fee
- **Bring Your Own:** Bike-friendly city, dedicated lanes
- **Safety:** Helmets recommended, follow traffic rules

### Taxis & Rideshare
- **Taxis:** Expensive but convenient
  - Use official companies: Taxi Stockholm, Taxi Kurir
  - Agree on price before getting in (fixed price available)
  - Apps: Taxi Stockholm, Bolt
- **Uber:** Available, often cheaper than taxis
- **Bolt:** Budget rideshare option

## 📱 PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money
- **Cashless Society:** Swedes rarely use cash
- **Cards:** Visa/Mastercard accepted everywhere
- **Contactless:** Tap-to-pay standard
- **ATMs:** Available, but you may not need cash
- **Currency:** SEK (Swedish Krona), not Euro!

### Language
- **Swedish:** Official language
- **English:** Widely spoken, nearly universal
- **Signage:** Often bilingual (Swedish/English)
- **Learning Swedish:** Not necessary for tourists, but appreciated

### Safety
- **Very Safe:** Stockholm is one of the safest capitals in Europe
- **Pickpockets:** Rare, but watch bags in crowded areas
- **Emergency:** 112 (police, fire, medical)
- **Health Care:** EU citizens use EHIC; others need travel insurance

### Connectivity
- **WiFi:** Widely available (cafés, hotels, public spaces)
- **SIM Cards:** Telia, Tele2, Telenor, 3 (available at airport)
- **EU Roaming:** EU residents can use home plan
- **Apps to Download:**
  - SL (public transport)
  - Stockholm Pass
  - The Fork (restaurant reservations)
  - Visit Stockholm (official tourism)

### Etiquette
- **Punctuality:** Arrive on time (early is rude)
- **Queuing:** Swedes love orderly lines
- **Tipping:** Not expected, but rounding up appreciated
- **Shoes:** Remove when entering homes
- **Personal Space:** Respect it, don't stand too close
- **Volume:** Speak quietly in public (loud = rude)

### Sustainability
- **Recycling:** Extensive system, follow local rules
- **Tap Water:** Excellent quality, drink freely
- **Public Transport:** Excellent, use it
- **Allemansrätten:** Right of Public Access—enjoy nature responsibly
  - "Do not disturb, do not destroy"
  - Pick berries, mushrooms, flowers
  - Camp overnight (not near homes)

## 🎒 PACKING LIST

### Year-Round Essentials
- Comfortable walking shoes (cobblestones!)
- Layers (weather changes quickly)
- Rain jacket (Stockholm gets rain)
- Power adapter (Type C/F, 230V)
- Reusable water bottle (tap water is excellent)

### Summer (June-August)
- Light clothing, but bring sweater
- Sunglasses (long days)
- Sunscreen
- Swimwear (archipelago swimming)
- Light scarf (evenings)

### Winter (November-March)
- Warm coat (down or wool)
- Thermal layers
- Hat, gloves, scarf (essential)
- Waterproof boots
- Lip balm, moisturizer (dry cold)
- Light therapy lamp (if sensitive to darkness)

### Shoulder Season (May, September)
- All-weather jacket
- Layers (warm days, cool nights)
- Umbrella
- Comfortable walking shoes
