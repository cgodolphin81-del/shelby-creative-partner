## Getting There

### International Flights

**Primary Gateway: Cairns Airport (CNS)**
- Direct flights from: Singapore, Tokyo, Seoul, Kuala Lumpur
- Connecting flights from: Brisbane, Sydney, Melbourne
- Airport to city: 15 minutes by taxi/shuttle ($25-35 AUD)

**Alternative: Brisbane (BNE)**
- More international flight options
- Domestic connection to Cairns: 2.5 hours flight
- Or take the scenic train/bus route (longer but beautiful)

### Domestic Travel

**From Brisbane to Cairns:**
- **Flight:** 2.5 hours, $150-300 AUD
- **Train (Spirit of Queensland):** 25 hours, $150-250 AUD (scenic)
- **Bus:** 24+ hours, $100-180 AUD (budget option)

**From Sydney to Cairns:**
- **Flight:** 3 hours, $200-400 AUD

**From Melbourne to Cairns:**
- **Flight:** 3.5 hours, $250-450 AUD

### Airport Transfers (Cairns)

- **Taxi:** $25-35 AUD to city center
- **Shuttle bus:** $15-20 AUD per person
- **Rideshare (Uber):** $20-30 AUD
- **Rental car:** Available at airport ($50-100 AUD/day)

## When to Visit

### Dry Season ⭐ (May - November) - BEST TIME

**Weather:**
- Temperature: 22-26°C (72-79°F)
- Humidity: Lower, comfortable
- Rainfall: Minimal
- Water visibility: 30-50+ meters

**Pros:**
- Best diving and snorkeling conditions
- Stable weather
- Minimal jellyfish risk
- Whale watching (June-September)

**Cons:**
- Peak tourist season
- Higher prices
- Need to book in advance

### Wet Season (December - April)

**Weather:**
- Temperature: 26-31°C (79-88°F)
- Humidity: High
- Rainfall: Afternoon thunderstorms common
- Water visibility: 10-25 meters (reduced by runoff)

**Pros:**
- Lower prices (20-40% off)
- Fewer crowds
- Lush rainforest
- Turtle nesting season

**Cons:**
- Stinger season (November-May)
- Cyclone risk (January-March)
- Reduced visibility
- Some tours may be cancelled

## Diving & Snorkeling

### Certification Requirements

**Snorkeling:**
- No certification required
- Basic swimming ability recommended
- Most tours provide instruction and equipment

**Introductory Diving (Discover Scuba):**
- No certification required
- Minimum age: 10-12 years (varies by operator)
- Includes brief training and supervised dive
- Cost: +$129-180 AUD on top of tour price

**Certified Diving:**
- Must show certification card (PADI, SSI, NAUI, etc.)
- Minimum age: 10 years (Junior Open Water)
- Bring logbook for advanced dives
- Cost: +$95-160 AUD on top of tour price

### Getting Certified

**PADI Open Water Course:**

**Cairns Options:**
- **3-day course:** $550-650 AUD (includes eLearning)
- **4-day course:** $500-600 AUD (includes eLearning)
- **5-day resort course:** $600-700 AUD (pool + liveaboard)

**What's Included:**
- All equipment
- Pool sessions
- Knowledge development
- 4 open water dives
- Certification card

**Recommended Operators:**
- Pro Dive Cairns
- Divers Den
- Cairns Dive Academy
- Quicksilver Cruises (Port Douglas)

### Best Dive Sites by Experience Level

**Beginner:**
- Flynn Reef (Cairns) - calm, shallow, turtles common
- Milln Reef (Cairns) - 10-20m depth, clear water
- The Low Isles (Port Douglas) - sheltered lagoon
- Green Island - easy shore diving

**Intermediate:**
- Hastings Reef - diverse marine life
- Norman Reef - coral formations
- Agincourt Reef (Port Douglas) - multiple sites
- Moore Reef - coral gardens

**Advanced:**
- Cod Hole (Ribbon Reefs) - potato cod, requires liveaboard
- S.S. Yongala Wreck (Townsville) - world-class wreck dive
- Ribbon Reefs - remote, pristine
- Osprey Reef (Coral Sea) - shark diving, liveaboard only

### Liveaboard Diving

**What is a Liveaboard?**
Multi-day diving expedition where you sleep on the boat, accessing remote reef sites unreachable by day trips.

**Popular Routes:**

**Ribbon Reefs (3-7 days):**
- Includes Cod Hole
- Price: $1,200-2,500 AUD
- Operators: Spirit of Freedom, Reef Encounter

**Coral Sea Expedition (7 days):**
- Osprey Reef, Bougainville Reef
- Shark diving
- Price: $2,500-4,000 AUD
- Operators: Mike Ball Dive Expeditions

**Southern Great Barrier Reef (3-4 days):**
- Less crowded
- Price: $900-1,500 AUD
- Operators: Various from Gladstone

**What's Included:**
- All meals
- 3-5 dives per day
- Equipment (tanks, weights)
- Accommodation (shared cabin)
- Nitrox (on some boats)

**What to Bring:**
- Certification card and logbook
- Reef-safe sunscreen
- Seasickness medication
- Warm layer (nights can be cool)
- Camera gear

## Health & Safety

### Marine Stingers

**Season:** November to May (peak December-February)

**Types:**
- **Box Jellyfish:** Large, highly venomous
- **Irukandji:** Tiny, extremely venomous

**Protection:**
- Wear stinger suit (provided by most operators)
- Swim at patrolled beaches
- Follow local warnings
- Vinegar is the first aid treatment (carry small bottle)

**Symptoms (seek immediate help):**
- Severe pain at sting site
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain
- Nausea/vomiting

### Sun Protection

**UV Index:** Extreme (11+) in summer

**Recommendations:**
- Reef-safe sunscreen only (mandatory on reef)
- SPF 50+
- Reapply every 2 hours
- Wear rash guard
- Seek shade midday

### Diving Safety

**General Rules:**
- Never dive alone
- Check equipment before each dive
- Monitor air supply
- Never hold your breath
- Ascend slowly (max 9m/30ft per minute)
- Safety stop at 5m for 3 minutes
- Don't fly within 18-24 hours after diving

**Health Considerations:**
- No diving with cold/congestion
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid alcohol before diving
- Declare medical conditions on dive forms

### Wildlife

**Generally Safe:**
- Most fish, corals, invertebrates
- Sea turtles (observe, don't touch)
- Reef sharks (not aggressive, give space)

**Use Caution:**
- Stonefish (venomous, don't touch)
- Blue-ringed octopus (tiny, highly venomous)
- Cone shells (don't handle)
- Sea snakes (venomous but docile)
- Crocodiles (northern inshore areas only)

**Do Not Touch:**
- Any coral (fragile, can cause injury)
- Fire coral (causes burning sensation)
- Unknown marine life

## Eco-Tourism & Responsible Travel

### Choose Responsible Operators

**Look For:**
- Eco-certification (Ecotourism Australia)
- Marine Park High-Standard Tourism Operator
- Green Travel Leadership accreditation
- Small group sizes
- Marine biologist guides
- Citizen science programs

**Recommended Eco-Operators:**
- Silverswift (Cairns)
- Passions of Paradise (Cairns)
- Down Under Cruise & Dive (Cairns)
- Wavedancer (Port Douglas)
- Lady Elliot Island Eco-Resort

### Reef-Safe Sunscreen

**Required Ingredients:**
- Zinc oxide or titanium dioxide (non-nano)
- Biodegradable formula

**Avoid:**
- Oxybenzone
- Octinoxate
- Octocrylene
- Parabens

**Recommended Brands:**
- Sun Bum Reef Friendly
- Blue Lizard Australian Sunscreen
- ThinkSport
- All Good Sunscreen

### How to Minimize Your Impact

**On the Reef:**
- Don't touch or stand on coral
- Maintain good buoyancy control
- Don't feed fish
- Don't chase marine life
- Take only photos, leave only bubbles
- Use reef-safe sunscreen
- Don't collect shells or coral

**General:**
- Reduce plastic use (bring reusable bottle)
- Choose sustainable seafood
- Support local businesses
- Respect Traditional Owner culture
- Offset your carbon footprint
- Donate to reef conservation

### Conservation Organizations

**Support These Groups:**
- Great Barrier Reef Foundation (barrierreef.org)
- Australian Marine Conservation Society
- Reef Restoration and Adaptation Program
- Citizens of the Great Barrier Reef
- Traditional Owner Sea Country groups

**Citizen Science:**
- Eye on the Reef (report sightings)
- Coral Watch (monitor coral health)
- Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Authority apps

## Cultural Respect

### Acknowledging Traditional Owners

The Great Barrier Reef is the Sea Country of 70+ Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Traditional Owner groups.

**When Visiting:**
- Acknowledge you are on Traditional Land
- Respect cultural sites (don't touch rock art)
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Listen to Indigenous guides
- Support Indigenous-owned tourism businesses

**Indigenous Tourism Experiences:**
- Kuku Yalanji cultural tours (Daintree)
- Djunbunti Indigenous Tours
- Walkabout Cultural Adventures
- Mossman Gorge Dreamtime Walks

### Tipping

**Australia:** Tipping is not expected but appreciated for exceptional service

**Guidelines:**
- Tour guides: $10-20 AUD per person for full-day tours
- Dive instructors: $10-20 AUD per day
- Restaurant: 10% for exceptional service (not expected)
- Hotels: Not expected

## Quick Tips

### Money-Saving Tips
1. Book reef trips in advance online (often 10-15% discount)
2. Travel in shoulder season (April-May, October-November)
3. Look for combo deals (reef + rainforest)
4. Stay in accommodations with kitchen facilities
5. Bring your own snorkel gear (saves rental fees)
6. Group discounts available (10+ people)
7. Student/senior discounts on many tours

### Best Photo Spots
1. Heart Reef (scenic flight only)
2. Underwater coral gardens (any outer reef trip)
3. Turtle encounters (Fitzroy Island, Green Island)
4. Manta rays (Lady Elliot Island, winter)
5. Aerial views from helicopter tours

### Don't Miss
1. Outer reef diving/snorkeling (not just islands)
2. Great Barrier Reef Marine Park visitor centers
3. Cairns Esplanade Lagoon (free swimming)
4. Local seafood (barramundi, prawns)
5. Daintree Rainforest day trip (combine with reef)
