## The Tease (Long Form)

### Blog Post / Social Thread

**Title:** After the Yucatan, We Go Higher

**Text:**
Episode 073 walks through the Yucatan Peninsula—the northern reaches of Maya territory. The land of Chichen Itza, cenotes, and coastal Tulum. The places where the jungle embraces the ruins, where the Caribbean meets ancient stone.

But the heart of the Maya world beats further south.

In the Guatemalan highlands, the Maya never left. They didn't become ruins. They didn't become archaeological sites. They became grandmothers weaving in mountain villages. They became farmers planting corn on volcanic slopes. They became priests keeping the old calendar, the old ways, the old prayers.

Lake Atitlán, surrounded by three volcanoes, is said to be the deepest lake in Central America. The Tz'utujil and Kaqchikel Maya fish its waters, farm its shores, speak their ancestors' languages.

Chichicastenango's market has operated for over 400 years. On Thursdays and Sundays, the town fills with color: textiles, ceramics, copal incense, offerings for the mountain spirits.

And Tikal—the great city that the Yucatan ruins whisper about. Tikal, where the pyramids rise above the jungle canopy, where jaguars still prowl the plazas, where the Maya kings spoke to gods we're still trying to understand.

Episode 073 is the Children of the Corn. Episode 074 is where the corn comes home.

The Highlands of the Heart. Guatemala. The circle continues.

Coming soon to Infinite Walk.
