## 🗓️ PLANNING YOUR TRIP

### How Far in Advance to Book

**Peak Season (May–September):**
- Machu Picchu tickets: 3–4 months ahead
- Huayna Picchu permits: 4–6 months ahead
- Inca Trail: 6–10 months ahead (sells out in hours!)
- Trains: 2–3 months ahead
- Hotels: 2–3 months ahead

**Shoulder Season (April, October–November):**
- Machu Picchu tickets: 6–8 weeks ahead
- Huayna Picchu: 2–3 months ahead
- Inca Trail: 3–4 months ahead
- Trains: 1–2 months ahead
- Hotels: 1–2 months ahead

**Rainy Season (December–March):**
- Machu Picchu tickets: 4–6 weeks ahead
- Huayna Picchu: 1–2 months ahead
- Inca Trail: CLOSED in February
- Trains: 3–4 weeks ahead
- Hotels: 3–4 weeks ahead

### How Many Days You Need

**Minimum (Rushed):** 1 day
- Early train from Cusco
- 4 hours at Machu Picchu
- Return train same day
- Exhausting but doable

**Recommended:** 2–3 days
- Day 1: Travel to Aguas Calientes, explore town
- Day 2: Machu Picchu sunrise, full exploration
- Day 3: Huayna Picchu hike, return to Cusco

**Ideal:** 4–5 days
- Includes acclimatization in Cusco/Sacred Valley
- Relaxed pace
- Time for Sacred Valley sites
- Buffer for weather delays

**Where to Buy:**

- Authorized tour operators (verify legitimacy)

**Ticket Types:**

**Discounts:**

- Name on ticket MUST match passport exactly

- Date changes NOT allowed (must buy new ticket)
- Arrive 30 minutes before entry time
- Late arrival may result in denied entry

### Huayna Picchu Permits

**What It Is:** The iconic peak behind Machu Picchu
**Elevation:** 2,720m (8,920 ft)
**Difficulty:** Moderate to challenging
**Duration:** 1.5–2 hours round trip
**Daily Limit:** 400 visitors (two time slots)

**Time Slots:**
- Group 1: 7:00–8:00 AM entry
- Group 2: 10:00–11:00 AM entry

**Cost:** Additional $25 on top of entrance ticket
**Booking:** 3–4 months ahead (sells out fast!)
**Restrictions:** Not suitable for young children or those with fear of heights

### Machu Picchu Mountain (Montaña)

**What It Is:** Alternative hike, less steep than Huayna Picchu
**Elevation:** 3,082m (10,111 ft)
**Difficulty:** Moderate
**Duration:** 2–3 hours round trip
**Daily Limit:** 800 visitors

**Cost:** Additional $25 on top of entrance ticket
**Booking:** 2–3 months ahead
**Better For:** Those wanting views without steep climbs

### Inca Trail Permits

**What It Is:** 4-day trek to Machu Picchu via original Inca path
**Distance:** 42 km (26 miles)
**Difficulty:** Moderate to challenging
**Highest Point:** Dead Woman's Pass, 4,215m (13,828 ft)
**Daily Limit:** 500 permits (200 trekkers, 300 staff)

**Cost:** $600–1,500 (includes guide, porters, food, equipment, permits)
**Booking:** 6–10 months ahead for peak season
**Requirements:** Must go with licensed tour operator (no independent hiking)
**Closed:** All of February for maintenance

**Alternative Treks:**
- Salkantay Trek (5 days) — No permit needed
- Lares Trek (4 days) — Cultural immersion
- Inca Jungle (4 days) — Biking + hiking
- Choquequirao (9 days) — Remote sister city

## 🚂 GETTING THERE

### Route 1: Train + Bus (Most Popular)

**Step 1: Fly to Lima, Peru**
- International flights from most major cities
- Allow 1–2 days in Lima for acclimatization

**Step 2: Lima to Cusco**
- Flight: 1.5 hours, $120–250 round-trip
- Bus: 20+ hours, $25–70 (not recommended)

**Step 3: Cusco to Ollantaytambo**
- Private transfer: 1.5 hours, $60–80
- Shared van: 1.5 hours, $11/person
- Train from Cusco (Poroy station): Limited service

**Step 4: Ollantaytambo to Aguas Calientes**
- PeruRail or Inca Rail
- Expedition: $140–190 round-trip
- Vistadome: $180–220 round-trip
- Hiram Bingham (luxury): $950+ one-way
- Duration: 1.5–2 hours

**Step 5: Aguas Calientes to Machu Picchu**
- Bus: 25 minutes, $12 one-way / $24 round-trip
- Hike: 1.5–2 hours, steep, free
- First bus: 5:30 AM (for sunrise)

### Route 2: Inca Trail Trek

**Day 1:** Cusco → Km 82 → Wayllabamba (12 km)
**Day 2:** Wayllabamba → Dead Woman's Pass → Pacaymayo (12 km)
**Day 3:** Pacaymayo → Wiñay Wayna (10 km)
**Day 4:** Wiñay Wayna → Sun Gate → Machu Picchu (6 km)

**What's Included:**
- Licensed guide
- Porters (carry camping gear + personal belongings)
- All meals
- Camping equipment
- Entrance fees
- Bus down from Machu Picchu

**What to Bring:**
- Personal items (max 7 kg given to porters)
- Sleeping bag (can rent)
- Hiking boots (broken in!)
- Rain gear
- Warm layers
- Headlamp
- Passport (required at checkpoints)

### Route 3: Budget (Hydroelectric)

**For:** Backpackers, budget travelers
**Cost:** ~$25 total

**Steps:**
1. Train to Hydroelectric station ($15–20)
2. Walk 2–3 hours along tracks to Aguas Calientes (free)
3. Bus or hike up to Machu Picchu

**Pros:** Cheapest option
**Cons:** Long, less scenic, safety concerns

## 🏥 HEALTH & SAFETY

### Altitude Sickness

**Cusco Elevation:** 3,400m (11,150 ft)
**Machu Picchu:** 2,453m (8,048 ft) — lower than Cusco!

**Prevention:**
- Arrive in Cusco 2–3 days before trekking
- Stay hydrated (3–4 liters water/day)
- Avoid alcohol first 48 hours
- Eat light meals
- Drink coca tea
- Consider acetazolamide (Diamox) — consult doctor

**Symptoms:**
- Headache
- Nausea
- Fatigue
- Shortness of breath
- Dizziness
- Loss of appetite

**If Symptoms Occur:**
- Rest
- Hydrate
- Descend if severe
- Seek medical attention if worsening

### Vaccinations (Consult Doctor)

**Recommended:**
- Hepatitis A
- Typhoid
- Routine vaccines (MMR, tetanus, etc.)

**Consider:**
- Hepatitis B
- Rabies (if extended rural travel)
- Yellow fever (certificate may be required)

### Water & Food Safety

**Water:**
- Bottled water widely available
- Avoid tap water
- Use water purification tablets for trekking
- Bring reusable bottle (fill from safe sources)

**Food:**
- Eat at reputable restaurants
- Avoid street food if sensitive stomach
- Peel fruits yourself
- Avoid raw vegetables in budget establishments
- "Cook it, boil it, peel it, or forget it"

## 📱 PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money

**Currency:** Peruvian Sol (PEN)
**Exchange Rate:** ~3.7 soles = $1 USD (varies)

**Tips:**
- Bring USD cash for exchange
- ATMs widely available in Cusco and Aguas Calientes
- Credit cards accepted at hotels, restaurants, train stations
- Bring small bills for tips
- Budget $10–20/day for tips (guides, porters, drivers)

### Connectivity

**WiFi:**
- Available at most hotels
- Cafes in Cusco and Aguas Calientes
- Not available at Machu Picchu

**Cell Service:**
- Good in Cusco and towns
- Spotty on Inca Trail
- None at Machu Picchu summit

**SIM Cards:**
- Claro, Movistar, Entel available
- Tourist SIMs at airport
- Consider eSIM if phone supports

### Language

**Official:** Spanish
**Indigenous:** Quechua (widely spoken in Sacred Valley)

**Basic Phrases:**
- Hola — Hello
- Gracias — Thank you
- Por favor — Please
- ¿Cuánto cuesta? — How much?
- No hablo español — I don't speak Spanish
- ¿Habla inglés? — Do you speak English?

### Etiquette

**At Machu Picchu:**
- Stay on designated paths
- Do not touch structures
- Keep voices down
- No tripods or selfie sticks
- No drones without permit
- Pack out all trash
- Respect other visitors

**Tipping:**
- Guides: $10–20/day per person
- Porters: $15–20/day per porter (group tip)
- Drivers: $5–10/day
- Restaurant: 10% if service not included

## 🌤️ WEATHER & TIMING

### Best Time to Visit

**Dry Season (April–October):**
- ✅ Clear skies, best visibility
- ✅ Ideal for photography
- ✅ All trails open
- ❌ Peak crowds
- ❌ Higher prices
- ❌ Need to book far ahead

**Rainy Season (November–March):**
- ✅ Fewer tourists
- ✅ Lush green landscapes
- ✅ Lower prices
- ❌ Afternoon showers common
- ❌ Clouds may obscure views
- ❌ Inca Trail closed in February

### Daily Timing

**Morning Shift (6 AM–12 PM):**
- ✅ Best light for photos
- ✅ Cooler temperatures
- ✅ Mist often clears by mid-morning
- ❌ Most crowded
- ❌ Need to arrive early

**Afternoon Shift (12 PM–5:30 PM):**
- ✅ Fewer crowds
- ✅ Softer light
- ✅ More relaxed pace
- ❌ Afternoon clouds possible
- ❌ Less time to explore

**Sunrise:**
- ✅ Magical atmosphere
- ✅ Fewest people
- ✅ Best photography
- ❌ Requires 5:30 AM bus or staying at Sanctuary Lodge
- ❌ Cold and dark
