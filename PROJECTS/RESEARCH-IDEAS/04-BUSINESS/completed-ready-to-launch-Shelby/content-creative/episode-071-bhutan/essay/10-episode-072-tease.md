## 📚 Research Notes (Episode 072)

### The Maya Calendar

**Long Count:**
- 13 b'ak'tun = ~5,125 years
- December 21, 2012 = completion of 13th b'ak'tun
- NOT the end of the world
- Beginning of new cycle (like odometer rolling over)

**Other Cycles:**
- Tzolk'in: 260-day sacred calendar
- Haab': 365-day solar calendar
- Calendar Round: 52-year cycle

### Corn in Maya Culture

**Popol Vuh (Creation Story):**
- First humans: made from mud (failed)
- Second humans: made from wood (failed)
- Third humans: made from corn (succeeded)
- Corn = sacred substance of humanity

**Modern Practice:**
- Corn planted with ceremony
- Multiple varieties (not monoculture)
- Nixtamalization (traditional preparation)
- Corn in every meal

### Modern Maya

**Population:**
- ~7 million Maya people today
- Guatemala: ~40% of population
- Mexico: Yucatán, Chiapas
- Belize, Honduras, El Salvador

**Languages:**
- 30+ Maya languages still spoken
- K'iche', Q'eqchi', Kaqchikel, Yucatec
- Many bilingual (Maya + Spanish)

**Challenges:**
- Poverty, discrimination
- Land rights
- Cultural preservation
- Climate change impact

## 📝 Notes for Writers

### Cultural Sensitivity

**DO:**
- Research extensively before writing
- Use "Maya" (not "Mayan" for people)
- Center living Maya, not just ancient
- Acknowledge colonial impact
- Respect sacred knowledge (some things not for public sharing)

**DON'T:**
- Perpetuate 2012 doomsday myths
- Treat Maya as extinct civilization
- Appropriate sacred symbols
- Exoticize or romanticize poverty
- Speak for Maya people (amplify instead)

### Sources to Consult

**Academic:**
- "Popol Vuh" (creation story)
- "The Maya" by Coe & Coe
- "Breaking the Maya Code" by Coe
- Academic papers on Maya calendar

**Contemporary Maya Voices:**
- Rigoberta Menchú (Nobel laureate)
- Maya organizations (online)
- Modern Maya writers, activists
- Documentary films by Maya filmmakers

**Travel Resources:**
- Guatemala tourism (official)
- Responsible travel guides
