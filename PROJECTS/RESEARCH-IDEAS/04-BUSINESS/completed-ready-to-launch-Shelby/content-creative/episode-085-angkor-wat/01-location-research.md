## ANGKOR WAT (អង្គរវត្ត - "Temple City")

### Overview
- **Location:** Angkor Archaeological Park, 6 km north of Siem Reap, Cambodia
- **Built:** Early 12th century (1113-1150 AD)
- **Commissioned by:** King Suryavarman II
- **Original Dedication:** Hindu god Vishnu
- **Later Transformation:** Buddhist temple (14th century)
- **Size:** 500 acres (200 hectares), largest religious monument in the world
- **UNESCO World Heritage:** 1992
- **Featured on:** Cambodian national flag (only building on a national flag)
- **Visitors:** 2.6+ million annually (pre-pandemic)

### Architectural Significance
- **Style:** Classical Khmer architecture (Angkor Wat style)
- **Design:** Represents Mount Meru, home of the gods in Hindu cosmology
- **Five Towers:** Central quincunx symbolizing Mount Meru's five peaks
- **Moat:** 190 meters wide, 5 km circumference, represents mythical oceans
- **Orientation:** Faces west (unusual, associated with death/funerary temples)
- **Bas-Reliefs:** 1,200 square meters of carved galleries
- **Height:** Central tower rises 65 meters (213 feet)

### Key Areas

#### 1. Western Entrance & Causeway
- **Main Gateway:** Three ruined towers, laterite construction
- **Causeway:** 200 meters long, 12 meters wide, naga balustrades
- **Moat Bridge:** Current floating bridge (renovation access)

#### 2. Outer Gallery (Bas-Reliefs)
- **West Gallery:** Battle of Kurukshetra (Mahabharata)
- **South Gallery:** Army of Suryavarman II, Judgment by Yama
- **East Gallery:** Churning of the Ocean of Milk (famous scene)
- **North Gallery:** Battle between Devas and Asuras
- **Corner Towers:** Four libraries, sacred ponds

#### 3. Inner Galleries
- **Second Level:** "Ballet Dancers" - 1,700+ Apsara carvings
- **Third Level:** Upper terrace, central sanctuary
- **Steep Stairs:** "Angel's Staircase" - 40-degree incline

#### 4. Central Sanctuary
- **Four Faces:** Buddha images (later addition)
- **Central Tower:** 65 meters, highest point
- **View:** 360° panorama of temple complex and jungle

### Historical Timeline
- **1113-1150:** Construction under Suryavarman II
- **1177:** Sacked by Cham invaders
- **1181-1219:** Jayavarman VII restores and converts to Buddhism
- **1431:** Abandoned after Ayutthaya invasion
- **1860:** "Rediscovered" by French naturalist Henri Mouhot
- **1992:** UNESCO World Heritage designation
- **2016:** 2.6 million visitors

## ANGKOR THOM (អង្គរធំ - "Great City")

### Overview
- **Built:** Late 12th century (1181-1219)
- **Commissioned by:** King Jayavarman VII
- **Size:** 9 square kilometers (walled city)
- **Walls:** 8 meters high, 3 km per side, laterite
- **Moat:** 100 meters wide
- **Gates:** Five monumental gopuras (east has two)
- **Last Capital:** Final great capital of Khmer Empire

### Key Structures Within Angkor Thom

#### 1. Bayon Temple (Center)
- **Faces:** 216 gigantic stone faces on 54 towers (173 remain)
- **Height:** Central tower 43 meters
- **Style:** Baroque Khmer (most expressive)
- **Galleries:** Outer (historical scenes), Inner (mythological)
- **Theories:** Faces represent Avalokiteshvara or Jayavarman VII

#### 2. Baphuon
- **Era:** 11th century (pre-dates Angkor Thom)
- **Restoration:** Completed 2011 (40-year project)
- **Features:** Massive reclining Buddha (15th century)

#### 3. Phimeanakas
- **Era:** 10th-11th century
- **Purpose:** Royal palace temple
- **Legend:** Nine-headed naga king, king must sleep with serpent woman nightly

#### 4. Terrace of the Elephants
- **Length:** 300 meters
- **Carvings:** Elephants, Garuda, lions
- **Purpose:** Royal viewing platform for ceremonies

#### 5. Terrace of the Leper King
- **Name:** Either Yasovarman I (leper) or Yama (god of death)
- **Carvings:** Nagas, demons, deities (double walls)
- **Hidden Gallery:** Discovered 1990s

#### 6. South Gate
- **Height:** 23 meters
- **Faces:** Four-faced towers
- **Causeway:** 54 stone figures (Devas left, Asuras right)
- **Naga Bridge:** 100-meter balustrade

## BAYON TEMPLE (បាយ័ន)

### Overview
- **Location:** Center of Angkor Thom
- **Built:** Late 12th/early 13th century
- **Commissioned by:** King Jayavarman VII
- **Original Name:** Jayagiri ("Victory Mountain")
- **Religion:** Mahayana Buddhist (later Hindu, then Theravada Buddhist)
- **Style:** Baroque Khmer architecture
- **Faces:** Originally 52-59 towers, 180-236 faces; now 37 towers, 173 faces

### Architectural Features
- **Layout:** Two galleried enclosures, upper terrace
- **Orientation:** East-facing
- **Central Tower:** 43 meters, originally housed Buddha statue
- **Galleries:** 
  - Outer: Historical scenes (Khmer army, naval battles, daily life, markets)
  - Inner: Mythological scenes (Hindu gods, Buddhist ceremonies)
- **Face Towers:** Each tower has 4 faces (cardinal directions)

### The Faces - Theories
1. **Avalokiteshvara:** Bodhisattva of compassion (most accepted)
2. **Jayavarman VII:** King as god-king (devaraja)
3. **Brahma:** Four-faced Hindu deity
4. **Combination:** King + Bodhisattva synthesis
5. **Guardians:** Protectors watching over the kingdom

### Bas-Relief Highlights
- **Naval Battle:** Khmer vs. Cham forces on Tonle Sap
- **Market Scenes:** Daily life, cooking, trading
- **Royal Procession:** Jayavarman VII on elephant
- **Construction:** Building temples, palaces
- **Churning of Ocean:** Hindu creation myth
- **Hospital Scenes:** 102 hospitals mentioned in inscriptions

## TA PROHM (តាព្រហ្ម - "Ancestor of Brahma")

### Overview
- **Original Name:** Rajavihara ("Royal Monastery")
- **Built:** 1186 AD
- **Commissioned by:** King Jayavarman VII
- **Dedicated to:** King's mother (Buddhist)
- **Type:** Mahayana Buddhist monastery and university
- **Size:** 100+ hectares
- **Population:** 12,500+ residents (priests, dancers, workers)
- **Support:** 800,000+ people from 3,000+ villages
- **Treasure:** Gold, pearls, silks stored here
- **Current State:** Deliberately left jungle-entangled

### The Trees
- **Species:** Silk-cotton trees (Ceiba pentandra), Strangler figs (Ficus)
- **Age:** 400-500+ years old
- **Growth:** Roots wrap around, through, and lift stone blocks
- **Famous Tree:** "Tomb Raider Tree" in central sanctuary
- **Conservation:** Trees preserved with ruins (unusual approach)

### Key Features
- **Western Entrance:** Main causeway over moat
- **Central Sanctuary:** Tomb Raider tree location
- **Hall of Dancers:** Apsara carvings
- **Framed Doorways:** Tree roots framing ancient doors
- **Eastern Entrance:** Less crowded alternative
- **Hidden Chambers:** Collapsed galleries being cleared

### Historical Inscriptions
- **Stele:** Details temple wealth and personnel
- **Hospitals:** 102 hospitals across Khmer Empire
- **Rice Stores:** 13,506 tons of rice annually
- **Jewels:** Gold, pearls, precious stones
- **Workers:** 18 high priests, 615 dancers, 12,640 workers

## SUNRISE LOCATIONS

### 1. Angkor Wat Reflection Pools
- **Classic Spot:** Left (north) pool - most popular, symmetrical reflection
- **Alternative:** Right (south) pool - less crowded, palm tree framing
- **Arrival:** 5:00 AM (gates open), best spots by 5:15 AM
- **Photography:** Tripod essential, long exposure
- **Crowds:** Thousands at peak times

### 2. Southern Library (Angkor Wat)
- **Elevation:** Raised platform
- **Advantage:** Dry seating, shelter from rain
- **View:** Elevated perspective over temple
- **Crowds:** Less than reflection pools

### 3. Phnom Bakheng
- **Type:** Hilltop temple (pyramid)
- **Height:** 67 meters above plain
- **View:** Panoramic jungle vista, distant Angkor Wat
- **Limit:** 300 visitors max for sunrise
- **Arrival:** 4:30 AM recommended
- **Note:** More popular for sunset

### 4. Pre Rup
- **Type:** Pyramid temple
- **Era:** 10th century
- **Crowds:** Fewer than Angkor Wat
- **View:** 360° sunrise over jungle
- **Best:** Dry season (November-April)

### 5. Sras Srang
- **Type:** Royal bathing pool (baray)
- **Feature:** Water reflections (seasonal)
- **Crowds:** Peaceful, few tourists
- **Best:** Sunrise with interesting sky colors
- **Note:** No temple silhouette

## CULTURAL CONTEXT

### Khmer Empire
- **Peak:** 9th-15th centuries
- **Territory:** Modern Cambodia, Thailand, Laos, Vietnam
- **Population:** Estimated 1 million at Angkor (largest pre-industrial city)
- **Decline:** 1431 (Ayutthaya invasion), gradual abandonment

### Religion
- **Original:** Hindu (Vishnu, Shiva)
- **Transition:** Mahayana Buddhism (Jayavarman VII)
- **Current:** Theravada Buddhism (since 14th century)
- **Syncretism:** Hindu-Buddhist blend visible throughout

### Architecture Symbolism
- **Mount Meru:** Central towers represent cosmic mountain
- **Moats:** Mythical oceans surrounding the world
- **Galleries:** Cosmic serpent (naga) bridges
- **Orientation:** East (birth, life), West (death, funerary)
- **Numbers:** 5 towers = 5 peaks of Meru, 108 = sacred number

### Modern Cambodia
- **Population:** 17 million (2024)
- **Religion:** 97% Theravada Buddhist
- **Economy:** Tourism (pre-pandemic 6.6 million visitors), agriculture, textiles
- **Currency:** Cambodian Riel (KHR), USD widely accepted
- **Language:** Khmer (official), English common in tourism

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### What to Bring
- **Clothing:** Modest dress (shoulders, knees covered)
- **Footwear:** Comfortable walking shoes (lots of stairs)
- **Sun Protection:** Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
- **Hydration:** Water (2-3 liters per day)
- **Camera:** Extra batteries, memory cards, tripod
- **Cash:** USD for tickets, tips, vendors
- **Light:** Headlamp/flashlight for sunrise

### Etiquette
- **Dress Code:** Shoulders and knees must be covered
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing monks/people
- **Touch:** Do not touch carvings or bas-reliefs
- **Voices:** Keep quiet in sacred areas
- **Feet:** Never point feet at Buddha images
- **Heads:** Do not touch anyone's head (sacred)
- **Monks:** Women should not touch monks or hand objects directly

### Health & Safety
- **Heat:** Rest during midday (11 AM - 2 PM)
- **Hydration:** Drink water constantly
- **Stairs:** Very steep, use handrails
- **Monkeys:** Do not feed, keep belongings secure
- **Scams:** Verify prices before services
- **Landmines:** Stay on marked paths (outer areas)

### Transportation
- **Tuk-Tuk:** $15-25/day (Small Circuit), $25-35/day (Grand Circuit)
- **Car:** $35-50/day (air-conditioned)
- **Bicycle:** $5-10/day (for fit, experienced cyclists)
- **Motorcycle:** $10-15/day (license required)
- **Grab:** App available in Siem Reap

## KEY DATES & FACTS

### Timeline
- **802:** Jayavarman II declares independence, founds Khmer Empire
- **889:** Yasovarman I moves capital to Angkor
- **1113-1150:** Suryavarman II builds Angkor Wat
- **1177:** Cham invasion, Angkor sacked
- **1181-1219:** Jayavarman VII reigns, builds Angkor Thom, Bayon, Ta Prohm
- **1431:** Ayutthaya invasion, Angkor abandoned
- **1860:** Henri Mouhot "rediscovers" Angkor
- **1907:** French begin restoration
- **1975-1979:** Khmer Rouge, damage to some temples
- **1992:** UNESCO World Heritage
- **2001:** Tomb Raider filmed at Ta Prohm
- **2016:** 2.6 million visitors (peak)
- **2020-2022:** Pandemic closures
- **2024:** Tourism recovering

### By the Numbers
- **Angkor Wat:** 500 acres, 65m central tower, 1,700+ Apsaras
- **Bayon:** 54 towers, 216 faces (173 remain), 43m central tower
- **Ta Prohm:** 100+ hectares, 12,500+ residents historically
- **Angkor Thom:** 9 sq km, 8m walls, 100m moat
- **Complex:** 1,000+ temples identified, 200+ significant

### Names & Meanings
- **Angkor:** "City" (from Sanskrit nagara)
- **Wat:** "Temple" (Khmer)
- **Angkor Wat:** "Temple City"
- **Angkor Thom:** "Great City"
- **Bayon:** Possibly "Vejayant" (Indra's palace) or "beautiful"
- **Ta Prohm:** "Ancestor of Brahma"
- **Rajavihara:** "Royal Monastery" (Ta Prohm's original name)
