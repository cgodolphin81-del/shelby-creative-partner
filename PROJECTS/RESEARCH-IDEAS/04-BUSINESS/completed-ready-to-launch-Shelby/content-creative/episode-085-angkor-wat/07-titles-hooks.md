## HOOK OPENINGS (First 30 Seconds)

### Hook 1: Poetic (USED IN EPISODE)
"Before the sun remembers its own name... before the rooster crows to the sleeping village... before the monk's bowl fills with rice... there is darkness. And there is stone."

**Why it works:** Sets meditative tone, creates anticipation, poetic rhythm

---

### Hook 2: Direct & Powerful
"Five AM. Hundreds of people standing in complete silence. Cameras pointed east. Everyone waiting for the same moment. This... is Angkor Wat. And this is why we travel."

**Why it works:** Immediate immersion, sensory details, universal theme

---

### Hook 3: Historical Weight
"Eight hundred years ago, someone carved a face into stone. That person is dust. Their name is forgotten. But the face... the face remains. And it's still smiling."

**Why it works:** Mortality contrast, mystery, emotional connection

---

### Hook 4: Question-Based
"What survives an empire? What outlives a genocide? What remains when everything else is gone? In Cambodia, the answer is written in stone."

**Why it works:** Provocative questions, sets up journey, historical gravity

---

### Hook 5: Sensory Immersion
"The air is thick. Humidity. Frangipani. Darkness so complete you can taste it. And ahead, rising from the night... five towers. Angkor Wat."

**Why it works:** Multi-sensory, builds atmosphere, visual payoff

---

### Hook 6: Personal & Intimate
"I didn't expect to cry. I've been to temples before. I've seen sunrises. But nothing... nothing prepared me for this moment."

**Why it works:** Vulnerability, relatability, curiosity gap

---

### Hook 7: Bold Statement
"The largest religious monument on Earth. Built before the pyramids were old. Still standing. Still sacred. Still dreaming. This is Angkor Wat."

**Why it works:** Superlatives, historical context, active verbs

---

### Hook 8: The Contrast
"Tourists come for the photos. They leave with something else. Something they can't explain. Something that stays with them long after the tan fades."

**Why it works:** Subverts expectations, promises transformation
