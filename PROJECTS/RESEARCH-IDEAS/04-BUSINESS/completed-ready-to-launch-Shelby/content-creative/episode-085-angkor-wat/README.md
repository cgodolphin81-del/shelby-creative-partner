## 📍 The Journey

Before the sun remembers its own name... before the rooster crows to the sleeping village... before the monk's bowl fills with rice... there is darkness. And there is stone.

Eight hundred years of darkness. Eight hundred years of stone dreaming.

Episode 085 takes you to Angkor Wat, Cambodia—the largest religious monument on Earth. Through sunrise at the reflection pool, the mysterious faces of Bayon Temple, and the jungle-reclaimed ruins of Ta Prohm, this episode explores a simple question:

**What endures?**

What survives empires? What outlasts genocide? What remains when everything else is gone?

From the Khmer builders whose names are lost but whose work remains, to the stone faces that have watched for eight centuries, to the trees that embrace ancient temples—this is a meditation on legacy, resilience, and what truly lasts.

## 📊 Key Statistics

### Angkor Wat
- **Built:** 1113-1150 AD (early 12th century)
- **Size:** 500 acres (largest religious monument)
- **Central Tower:** 65 meters (213 feet)
- **Apsara Carvings:** 1,700+ unique figures
- **Visitors:** 2.6+ million annually (pre-pandemic)

### Bayon Temple
- **Built:** Late 12th/early 13th century
- **Faces:** 216 original (173 remain)
- **Towers:** 54 original (37 remain)
- **Height:** 43 meters central tower

### Ta Prohm
- **Built:** 1186 AD
- **Original Name:** Rajavihara ("Royal Monastery")
- **Historical Population:** 12,500+ residents
- **Support:** 800,000+ villagers from 3,000+ villages

## 📍 Location Details

### Getting There
- **Airport:** Siem Reap-Angkor International Airport (SAI)
- **Distance:** 50 km from city center (45-60 minutes)
- **Visa:** $30 USD on arrival or e-visa online

### Angkor Pass (2026)
- **1-Day:** $37 USD
- **3-Day:** $62 USD (valid 10 days)
- **7-Day:** $72 USD (valid 1 month)

### Best Time to Visit
- **Dry Season:** November-April (clear skies, cooler)
- **Wet Season:** May-October (lush green, fewer crowds)
- **Sunrise:** Arrive by 5:00 AM (gates open)
