# Doge's Palace (Palazzo Ducale)

## Historical Significance
- **Seat of Power:** Home to the Doge (Venice's elected ruler) for 1,000 years
- **Republic of Venice:** Center of one of history's most powerful maritime republics
- **Peak Power:** 13th-15th centuries, controlled Mediterranean trade routes
- **Decline:** Napoleon conquered Venice in 1797, ended the Republic

## Architecture
- **Style:** Venetian Gothic (unique to Venice)
- **Facade:** Diamond-patterned pink and white Istrian stone
- **Design:** Appears to float (light upper structure on heavy colonnade)
- **Symbolism:** Open arcades represented transparency of Venetian justice (ironic given the secret tribunals)

## Must-See Rooms

### 1. Scala d'Oro (Golden Staircase)
- Built 1555-1559 by Sansovino
- Stucco work by Alessandro Vittoria
- Led to Doge's private apartments

### 2. Chamber of the Great Council
- **Size:** 53m x 25m (largest room in Europe when built)
- **Capacity:** Held up to 2,000 noblemen
- **Ceiling:** Elaborate wood carvings and paintings
- **Wall Art:** Tintoretto's "Paradise" (largest oil painting in the world, 22m x 7m)

### 3. Scrutinio Room
- Where Doges were elected (complex voting system)
- Later: Public voting and celebrations
- Artworks depicting conquests of Venice

### 4. Doge's Apartments
- Private quarters of the Doge
- Richly decorated with period furniture
- Views over the lagoon

### 5. Chamber of the Council of Ten
- Secret tribunal room
- Intricate ceiling by Veronese
- Where state secrets were decided

### 6. Prisons & Bridge of Sighs
- **Name:** Coined by Lord Byron (1812)
- **Bridge:** Enclosed limestone bridge (1600)
- **Purpose:** Connected palace to New Prison
- **Legend:** Prisoners sighed at final view of Venice
- **Reality:** Most were already condemned, knew their fate

  - Hidden archives

## Casanova's Escape
- **Date:** October 31, 1755 (Halloween!)
- **Prison:** "I Piombi" (the Leads) - under the roof
- **Method:** Escaped through roof, helped by fellow prisoner
- **Story:** Wrote detailed account, some question accuracy

## Practical Information
- **Hours:** 8:30 AM - 7:00 PM (varies by season)
- **Cost:** €25-30 standard, includes Museum of St. Mark's
- **Skip the line:** Book online, arrive early
- **Audio guide:** Included, highly recommended
- **Photography:** Allowed (no flash)
- **Accessibility:** Limited (historic building)

## Best Times to Visit
- **Early:** 8:30 AM opening (beat tour groups)
- **Late:** Last entry 1 hour before close
- **Avoid:** Midday, cruise ship days (check schedule)
- **Season:** November-February (fewer crowds, atmospheric)

## Interesting Facts
- Doge was elected for life but could be deposed
- Complex election system designed to prevent corruption
- "Bucintoro" was the Doge's ceremonial galley
- Annual "Marriage of the Sea" ceremony
- Palace connected to basilica for Doge's private worship
