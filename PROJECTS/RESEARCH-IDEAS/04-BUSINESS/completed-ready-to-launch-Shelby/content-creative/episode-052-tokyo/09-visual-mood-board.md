## 🖼️ IMAGE COMPOSITION GUIDELINES

### Rule of Thirds
- Place horizon on upper or lower third (not center)
- Position key subjects at intersection points
- Use for landscapes, cityscapes, portraits

### Leading Lines
- Train tracks leading to Fuji
- Temple paths drawing eye inward
- Neon signs creating vertical lines
- Crossing patterns (diagonal movement)

### Framing
- Torii gate framing temple
- Window framing city view
- Cherry branches framing skyline
- Doorway framing ritual

### Depth
- Foreground: Cherry blossoms or lantern
- Midground: Temple or street scene
- Background: Skyline or Fuji
- Creates three-dimensional feel

### Scale
- Tiny human against massive Fuji
- Single person in crowded crossing
- Small temple in urban sprawl
- Emphasizes contrast and emotion

## 🎬 VIDEO/ANIMATION STYLE

### Motion Graphics

**Text Animation:**
- Fade in/out (gentle, not abrupt)
- Slight scale up on appearance
- Japanese characters can animate stroke-by-stroke
- Use cherry petal fall as transition

**Transitions:**
- Petal wipe (petals fall across screen)
- Light leak (neon glow transition)
- Blur zoom (entering new scene)
- Incense smoke dissolve

**Lower Thirds:**
- Minimal, clean
- Temple Red or Neon Blue accent line
- White or light gray text
- Subtle animation (slide in, not pop)

### Color Grading

**Day Scenes:**
- Slightly desaturated
- Warm highlights (golden hour feel)
- Cool shadows (blue undertones)
- Soft contrast

**Night Scenes:**
- High contrast
- Saturated neon (blue, purple, pink)
- Deep blacks (crushed slightly)
- Glow effect on light sources

**Temple/Spiritual:**
- Warm, golden tones
- Soft highlights
- Lower contrast (dreamy)
- Slight haze/glow

**Urban/Tech:**
- Cool tones (blue, cyan)
- High saturation on neon
- Sharp contrast
- Clean, crisp

## 🎨 TYPOGRAPHY

### Primary Font (Headings)

**Font:** Montserrat Bold (or similar geometric sans-serif)
- Modern, clean, readable
- Works in all caps for impact
- Good weight variety

**Usage:**
- Episode titles
- Social media headers
- Quote card main text
- Video titles

**Sizing:**
- Instagram: 60-80px for main text
- YouTube: 100-120px (thumbnail)

---

**Font:** Open Sans Regular (or similar humanist sans-serif)
- Highly readable

**Usage:**
- Episode descriptions
- Social media captions
- Body text in graphics
- Subtitles in video

**Sizing:**
- Instagram: 30-40px

**Font:** Noto Sans JP (or similar Japanese-compatible font)

- Clean, modern

- Japanese words (Tokyo, Sakura, etc.)
- Cultural context
- Decorative elements

## 🎯 VISUAL DO'S AND DON'TS

### DO ✅

- Use the color palette consistently
- Include both traditional and modern elements
- Show human scale against vast city/mountain
- Capture different times of day
- Include seasonal variety (focus on spring)
- Maintain brand consistency across platforms
- Use high-quality, high-resolution images
- Test readability at small sizes
- Include negative space for text
- Respect cultural elements (accurate, not stereotypical)

### DON'T ❌

- Use cliché geisha/samurai imagery (unless contextually accurate)
- Over-saturate to the point of unreality
- Use low-resolution or pixelated images
- Ignore cultural sensitivity
- Mix too many fonts (stick to 2-3)
- Crowd compositions (leave breathing room)
- Use colors that clash with palette
- Forget mobile optimization (most viewers on phone)
- Use copyrighted images without permission
- Stereotype or exoticize Japanese culture
