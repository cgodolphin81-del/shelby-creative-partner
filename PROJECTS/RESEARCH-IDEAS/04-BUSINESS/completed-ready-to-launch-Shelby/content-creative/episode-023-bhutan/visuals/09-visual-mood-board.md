## VISUAL STYLES

### Landscape Photography Style
**Reference:** National Geographic, Steve McCurry
**Characteristics:**
- Wide dynamic range
- Rich, saturated colors
- Dramatic lighting (golden hour)
- Human element for scale
- Environmental storytelling

**Application:**
- Himalayan peak shots
- Tiger's Nest exteriors
- Valley panoramas
- Prayer flag landscapes

---

### Temple Interior Style
**Reference:** Sacred space photography, ambient light
**Characteristics:**
- Warm color temperature (2700-3200K)
- Soft, directional lighting
- Deep shadows for depth
- Focus on details (statues, murals, butter lamps)
- Reverent, quiet mood

**Application:**
- Temple interiors
- Monk meditation scenes
- Ritual moments
- Sacred artifact close-ups

---

### Portrait Style
**Reference:** Humanist photography, environmental portraits
**Characteristics:**
- Natural light when possible
- Context included (not just faces)
- Authentic expressions
- Cultural respect (no exploitation)
- Story in every frame

**Application:**
- Local Bhutanese people
- Monks in daily life
- Artisans at work
- Festival participants

---

### AI Art Style (for Generated Imagery)
**Reference:** Photorealistic Midjourney v6, cinematic
**Characteristics:**
- High detail, 8K resolution
- Cinematic composition (rule of thirds, leading lines)
- Consistent color grading (see palette above)
- Atmospheric depth (mist, light rays)
- Emotional resonance

**Application:**
- Tiger's Nest variations
- Conceptual shots (GNH visualization)
- Shelby meditation scenes
- Abstract/spiritual imagery

**Prompt Guidelines:**
- Always include aspect ratio (--ar 16:9 for video)
- Specify lighting (golden hour, dawn, dusk)
- Include mood keywords (serene, majestic, peaceful)
- Use consistent seed for related images

## COMPOSITION GUIDELINES

### Rule of Thirds
- Place Tiger's Nest at upper-right intersection
- Horizon on lower third for mountain emphasis
- Prayer flags as leading lines

### Leading Lines
- Mountain ridges drawing eye to peaks
- Prayer flag strings creating diagonals
- Temple architecture lines
- Hiking paths as journey metaphor

### Framing
- Temple doorways framing monks
- Prayer flags framing landscapes
- Tree branches as natural frames
- Cave openings (Tiger's Nest interior)

### Scale
- Human figures for mountain scale
- Monastery on cliff for impossibility
- Prayer flags for vastness
- Close-ups for intimacy

### Depth
- Foreground: Prayer flags, flowers
- Midground: Monasteries, villages
- Background: Himalayan peaks
- Atmospheric perspective (layers fading to blue)

## LIGHTING GUIDELINES

### Golden Hour (Primary)
**Time:** Sunrise/sunset
**Color Temp:** 3200-4000K
**Quality:** Warm, soft, directional
**Usage:** Hero shots, emotional peaks, Tiger's Nest

### Blue Hour (Secondary)
**Time:** Just before sunrise/after sunset
**Color Temp:** 8000-10000K
**Quality:** Cool, even, ethereal
**Usage:** Transitions, contemplative moments, monastery lights

### Midday (Use Sparingly)
**Time:** 11 AM - 2 PM
**Color Temp:** 5500K
**Quality:** Harsh, high contrast
**Usage:** Avoid for landscapes, okay for interiors

### Interior Lighting
**Primary:** Butter lamps (warm, flickering)
**Secondary:** Window light (directional, dramatic)
**Accent:** Incense smoke (atmospheric, visible rays)

## DO's AND DON'Ts

### DO ✅
- Use warm, golden light for emotional moments
- Include human scale in vast landscapes
- Feature authentic cultural elements
- Maintain consistent color grading
- Leave negative space for breathing room
- Show reverence in sacred spaces
- Use prayer flag colors purposefully
- Feature mountain majesty

### DON'T ❌
- Over-saturate (keep natural)
- Use harsh midday light for landscapes
- Exploit or exoticize local people
- Inconsistent color temperature
- Cluttered compositions
- Disrespectful photography in temples
- Cliché "exotic East" tropes
- Ignore cultural context
