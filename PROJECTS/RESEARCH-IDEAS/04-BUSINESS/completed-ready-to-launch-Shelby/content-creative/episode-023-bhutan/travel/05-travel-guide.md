## GETTING THERE

### By Air

**Paro International Airport (PBH)**
- Only international airport in Bhutan
- One of the most challenging airports in the world (mountain approach)
- Only 8 pilots certified to land here

**Airlines:**
- **Drukair** (Royal Bhutan Airlines) - www.drukair.com
  - Flights from: Bangkok, Delhi, Kolkata, Singapore, Kathmandu, Dhaka
- **Bhutan Airlines** - www.bhutanairlines.bt
  - Flights from: Bangkok, Delhi, Kathmandu, Singapore, Mumbai

**Typical Routes:**
- Bangkok → Paro (3.5 hours)
- Delhi → Paro (2.5 hours)
- Singapore → Paro (via Bangkok, 6+ hours)
- Kathmandu → Paro (1 hour)

**Pro Tip:** Book morning flights for best mountain views during approach. The flight over the Himalayas is spectacular on clear days.

### By Land

**From India:**
- Entry via Phuentsholing (border town)
- Requires Indian visa and Bhutan visa clearance
- 6-hour drive from Phuentsholing to Thimphu
- Only for Indian, Bangladeshi, and Maldivian passport holders (different fee structure)

**Note:** Most international tourists must fly into Paro.

## VISA REQUIREMENTS

### Who Needs a Visa?
- All nationalities EXCEPT: India, Bangladesh, Maldives

### Visa Process

**Step 1: Book Through Licensed Tour Operator**
- Independent travel not permitted for most tourists
- Must book through Bhutan Tourism Council licensed operator
- Operator handles visa application

**Step 2: Pay Sustainable Development Fee (SDF)**
- **Adults:** $100 USD per day (reduced from $200 in 2023)
- **Children 6-12:** $50 USD per day
- **Children under 6:** Free
- **Indian tourists:** 1,200 INR per day (~$15 USD)

**Step 3: Provide Documents**
- Passport copy (valid 6+ months)
- Passport photo
- Travel itinerary
- Flight details

**Step 4: Visa Clearance**
- Processed in 3-5 working days
- Visa clearance letter sent via email
- Print and present on arrival

**Step 5: Visa Stamp**
- Actual visa stamped in passport on arrival at Paro
- Fee: $40 USD (usually included in tour package)

### Tour Packages

**Minimum Daily Package (through operators):**
- Budget: $200-250 USD/day (includes SDF, accommodation, meals, guide, transport)
- Mid-range: $300-400 USD/day
- Luxury: $500-1,000+ USD/day

**What's Typically Included:**
- Sustainable Development Fee
- 3-star accommodation (upgrade available)
- All meals
- Licensed guide
- Private transport
- Camping equipment (for treks)
- All internal taxes

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### Spring (March - May) ⭐ RECOMMENDED
**Weather:** Mild, 15-25°C (59-77°F)
**Highlights:**
- Rhododendrons in bloom
- Clear mountain views
- Paro Tshechu festival (usually March/April)
- Ideal trekking conditions

**Crowds:** Moderate
**Prices:** Standard

### Summer (June - August)
**Weather:** Warm, rainy, 20-30°C (68-86°F)
**Highlights:**
- Lush green landscapes
- Fewer tourists
- Lower prices
- Good for cultural visits (not trekking)

**Crowds:** Low
**Prices:** Lower
**Note:** Monsoon season, trekking not recommended

### Autumn (September - November) ⭐ BEST
**Weather:** Clear, crisp, 10-25°C (50-77°F)
**Highlights:**
- Best mountain visibility
- Thimphu Tshechu festival (September/October)
- Ideal for trekking and hiking
- Harvest season

**Crowds:** High (peak season)
**Prices:** Standard to high
**Note:** Book 3-6 months in advance

### Winter (December - February)
**Weather:** Cold, 0-15°C (32-59°F), snow at altitude
**Highlights:**
- Fewest tourists
- Clear skies (when not cloudy)
- Unique winter landscapes
- Lower prices

**Crowds:** Low
**Prices:** Lower
**Note:** Some high passes may be closed, very cold at night

## MADESTINATIONS

### Paro Valley
**Must-See:**
- Tiger's Nest Monastery (Taktsang)
- Kyichu Lhakhang (7th century temple)
- National Museum
- Traditional farmhouses
- Weekend market

**Must-See:**
- Buddha Dordenma (giant Buddha statue)
- Tashichho Dzong (royal seat)
- Memorial Chorten
- Weekend Market
- Textile Museum
- Folk Heritage Museum

**Time Needed:** 2 days

### Punakha
**Must-See:**
- Punakha Dzong (most beautiful dzong)
- Chimi Lhakhang (Fertility Temple)
- Suspension bridge
- Rice terraces

**Time Needed:** 2 days

### Dochula Pass
**Must-See:**
- 108 Memorial Stupas
- Himalayan panoramic views
- Druk Wangyal Festival (December)

**Time Needed:** Half day stop

### Phobjikha Valley
**Must-See:**
- Black-necked cranes (November-February)
- Gangtey Monastery
- Nature trails
- Pristine wetland

**Time Needed:** 1-2 days

### Bumthang (Cultural Heartland)
**Must-See:**
- Jakar Dzong
- Kurjey Lhakhang
- Jambay Lhakhang
- Tamzhing Monastery
- Traditional breweries

**Time Needed:** 3-4 days

### Haa Valley
**Must-See:**
- Remote, pristine valley
- Traditional nomadic culture
- Haa Summer Festival
- Less touristy

**Time Needed:** 2 days

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Dress Code
- **Temples/Monasteries:** Shoulders and knees must be covered
- Remove hats and sunglasses inside temples
- Remove shoes before entering temple buildings
- Dress modestly throughout the country

### Behavior
- **Photography:** Always ask permission before photographing people
- **Temples:** No photography inside main temple halls
- **Respect:** Walk clockwise around stupas and temples
- **Feet:** Never point feet at Buddha images or people
- **Head:** Don't touch anyone's head (considered sacred)
- **Public Displays:** Minimal physical contact in public

### Tipping
- **Guides:** $10-15 USD per day
- **Drivers:** $8-10 USD per day
- **Hotel Staff:** $2-5 USD per day
- **Restaurants:** Not expected (service included)

### Do's
- ✅ Greet with "Kuzu zangpo" (Hello)
- ✅ Accept tea/offers with both hands
- ✅ Walk clockwise around religious structures
- ✅ Ask before entering private spaces
- ✅ Respect local customs and traditions

### Don'ts
- ❌ Don't wear revealing clothing
- ❌ Don't touch religious artifacts without permission
- ❌ Don't point with one finger (use open hand)
- ❌ Don't discuss politics critically
- ❌ Don't litter (Bhutan is pristine—keep it that way)

## HEALTH & SAFETY

### Vaccinations (Recommended)
- Routine vaccines (MMR, tetanus, etc.)
- Hepatitis A
- Typhoid
- Japanese Encephalitis (for extended stays)
- Rabies (if trekking/remote areas)

### Altitude
- Paro: 2,280m (7,480 ft)
- Thimphu: 2,320m (7,610 ft)
- Dochula Pass: 3,100m (10,170 ft)
- **Risk:** Moderate altitude sickness possible
- **Prevention:** Ascend gradually, stay hydrated, rest first day

### Medical Facilities
- Thimphu: JDWNRH (main hospital), private clinics
- Paro: Basic hospital
- Rural areas: Very limited facilities
- **Recommendation:** Travel insurance with medical evacuation

### Water & Food
- **Water:** Bottled water recommended (avoid tap water)
- **Food:** Generally safe, well-cooked
- **Street Food:** Limited, exercise caution
- **Dietary Restrictions:** Can be accommodated (inform tour operator)

### Safety
- **Crime:** Very low (one of safest countries in world)
- **Political Stability:** Stable constitutional monarchy
- **Natural Disasters:** Occasional earthquakes, landslides in monsoon
- **Solo Travel:** Safe, but must be with guide

## PRACTICAL INFORMATION

### Connectivity
- **WiFi:** Available in hotels, cafes, restaurants
- **SIM Cards:** Bhutan Telecom or Tashi Cell (available at airport)
- **Coverage:** Good in towns, limited in remote areas
- **Cost:** ~$10-20 for tourist SIM with data

### Electricity
- **Voltage:** 230V, 50Hz
- **Plugs:** Type D, F, G (universal adapter recommended)
- **Reliability:** Generally good in towns

### Language
- **Official:** Dzongkha
- **Tourism:** English widely spoken
- **Other:** Nepali, Hindi in some areas
- **Tip:** Learn a few Dzongkha phrases (locals appreciate it)

### Basic Dzongkha Phrases
- Hello: Kuzu zangpo
- Thank you: Kadrin chey la
- Yes: Lag
- No: Ma lag
- How much?: Katsa?
- Good: Yag
- Beautiful: Jingdu

### Packing List

**Essentials:**
- Passport + visa clearance
- Cash (USD for exchange, credit cards limited)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Layers (temperature varies greatly)
- Rain jacket (even in dry season)
- Sun protection (high altitude = strong UV)
- Medications (limited availability)
- Power adapter

**For Trekking:**
- Warm layers (fleece, down jacket)
- Hiking boots (broken in)
- Sleeping bag (if camping)
- Day pack
- Water bottle/purification
- Trekking poles (optional)

**Cultural Respect:**
- Modest clothing for temples
- Scarf/shawl for covering shoulders
- Easy-to-remove shoes

### What to Buy (Souvenirs)
- **Textiles:** Handwoven kira/gho fabric, scarves
- **Paper:** Handmade paper products
- **Woodwork:** Bowls, cups, decorative items
- **Thangkas:** Buddhist paintings (expensive, authentic ones)
- **Incense:** Traditional Bhutanese incense
- **Tea:** Suja (butter tea), green tea
- **Alcohol:** Ara (local spirit), whiskey

**Avoid:**
- Animal products (ivory, fur, etc.)
- Antiques (export restrictions)
- Religious artifacts without proper documentation

## TREKKING IN BHUTAN

### Popular Treks

**Jomolhari Trek (7-9 days)**
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to challenging
- **Max Altitude:** 4,900m
- **Highlights:** Jomolhari base camp, Lingshi Dzong, traditional villages
- **Best Time:** April-May, September-October

**Druk Path Trek (5-6 days)**
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Max Altitude:** 4,520m
- **Highlights:** Paro to Thimphu, mountain lakes, rhododendron forests
- **Best Time:** April-May, September-October

**Snowman Trek (25-30 days)**
- **Difficulty:** Very challenging
- **Max Altitude:** 5,320m
- **Highlights:** Most remote regions, 11 high passes, ultimate Bhutan trek
- **Best Time:** September-October
- **Note:** One of world's most difficult treks

**Bumthang Cultural Trek (3-4 days)**
- **Difficulty:** Easy to moderate
- **Max Altitude:** 3,800m
- **Highlights:** Cultural sites, villages, monasteries
- **Best Time:** Year-round

### Trekking Requirements
- Must be booked through licensed operator
- Guide required
- Camping equipment provided
- Pack horses/yaks carry gear
- Good fitness level required

## FESTIVALS (TCHECHUS)

### Major Festivals

**Paro Tshechu (March/April)**
- One of largest festivals
- Mask dances, thongkol unveiling
- Huge crowds (book early)

**Thimphu Tshechu (September/October)**
- Capital's main festival
- 3 days of mask dances
- Large local and tourist attendance

**Punakha Drubchen (February/March)**
- Historical reenactment
- Unique festival with battle scenes
- Less crowded

**Jambay Lhakhang Drup (October/November)**
- Bumthang
- Famous for naked dance (early morning)
- Fire dance at night

**Note:** Festival dates follow lunar calendar, vary each year

## SUSTAINABLE TOURISM

### How to Travel Responsibly

**Environmental:**
- ✅ Use refillable water bottles
- ✅ Don't litter (pack out all trash)
- ✅ Respect wildlife (don't feed animals)
- ✅ Stay on marked trails
- ❌ No single-use plastics (banned in Bhutan)

**Cultural:**
- ✅ Learn about customs before visiting
- ✅ Dress modestly at religious sites
- ✅ Ask before photographing people
- ✅ Support local artisans
- ❌ Don't touch religious artifacts

**Economic:**
- ✅ Buy local products
- ✅ Tip guides and drivers fairly
- ✅ Choose locally-owned accommodations
- ❌ Don't bargain aggressively

### Why Bhutan's Model Works
- High-value, low-volume tourism
- SDF funds free healthcare and education
- Limits environmental impact
- Preserves culture and traditions
- Benefits local communities

## FINAL TIPS

1. **Book Early:** Especially for peak season and festivals
2. **Cash is King:** Limited ATMs, credit cards not widely accepted
3. **Pack Layers:** Weather changes quickly in mountains
4. **Respect Culture:** Bhutanese value traditions deeply
5. **Slow Down:** This isn't a rush destination—savor it
6. **Talk to Locals:** Bhutanese are friendly and welcoming
7. **Try the Food:** Ema datshi (chili cheese) is national dish
8. **Hire a Good Guide:** Makes all the difference
9. **Bring Patience:** Things move at Bhutanese pace
10. **Leave No Trace:** Keep this paradise pristine
