## Episode 032: The Machu Picchu | Peru (Andes Mountains)

---

## The Incan Citadel

**Name:** Machu Picchu (Quechua: "Old Peak" or "Old Mountain")
**Location:** Cusco Region, Urubamba Province, Machupicchu District, Peru
**Coordinates:** 13°09′48″S 72°32′44″W
**Elevation:** 2,430 meters (7,970 feet) above sea level
**Built:** Approximately 1450 CE
**Abandoned:** Circa 1572 (during Spanish Conquest)
**Rediscovered:** July 24, 1911 by Hiram Bingham

### Historical Significance
- Royal estate for Inca emperor Pachacuti (1438-1472)
- Religious sanctuary dedicated to Inti (Sun God)
- Astronomical observatory aligned with solstices
- Part of the Qhapaq Ñan (Inca Road System) - UNESCO World Heritage
- One of the New Seven Wonders of the World (2007)

### Architecture
- 200+ structures across 32.5 square kilometers
- Dry-stone construction (no mortar)
- Three main sectors: Urban, Agricultural, Residential
- Intihuatana Stone (hitching post of the sun)
- Temple of the Sun (Torreón)
- Temple of the Three Windows
- Room of the Condor
- Principal Temple

## Huayna Picchu

**Name:** Huayna Picchu (Quechua: "Young Peak")
**Elevation:** 2,720 meters (8,920 feet)
**Height above Machu Picchu:** ~290 meters

### The Climb
- Steep, narrow stone steps carved into the mountain
- Duration: 45 minutes to 1.5 hours ascent
- Limited permits: 400 visitors per day (two entry groups)
- Not recommended for those with fear of heights
- "Stairs of Death" - near-vertical sections with sheer drops

### From the Summit
- 360-degree panoramic views of Machu Picchu
- View of the Urubamba River winding below
- Temple of the Moon (additional hike from summit)
- Cloud forest canopy views
- Condors often spotted soaring on thermals

## Sacred Valley (Valle Sagrado)

**Location:** Between Pisac and Ollantaytambo
**Elevation:** ~2,800 meters (9,200 feet)
**River:** Urubamba (Vilcanota)

### Key Sites
- **Pisac:** Incan ruins, terraced hillsides, Sunday market
- **Ollantaytambo:** Living Inca town, fortress ruins, train departure point
- **Chinchero:** Colonial church on Inca foundations, textile markets
- **Moray:** Circular agricultural terraces (Inca experimental station)
- **Maras:** Salt pans (salineras) - 3,000+ salt pools

### Agricultural Importance
- Breadbasket of the Inca Empire
- Lower elevation = warmer climate than Cusco
- Maize, quinoa, potatoes, coca grown here
- Sophisticated irrigation systems still in use

## Cloud Forest (Selva Alta)

**Elevation Range:** 1,000-2,500 meters
**Classification:** Yungas ecosystem (warm, humid montane forest)

### Flora
- Orchids (300+ species in the region)
- Bromeliads
- Tree ferns
- Cedar and mahogany trees
- Coca plants
- Coffee (grown on lower slopes)

### Fauna
- **Spectacled Bear** (Andean bear) - rare, elusive
- **Cock-of-the-Rock** (Peru's national bird) - brilliant orange
- **Andean Condor** - wingspan up to 3 meters
- **Jaguar** - very rare, mostly nocturnal
- **Puma** - occasionally spotted
- **Deer** (Andean deer)
- **Capybara** (near rivers)
- **Toucans** and **Parrots**
- **Butterflies** (hundreds of species)

### Climate
- Mist and clouds common (hence "cloud forest")
- High humidity (80-90%)
- Temperature range: 15-25°C (59-77°F)
- Rainy season: November-March
- Dry season: April-October

## Geographic Context

### The Andes
- Longest continental mountain range (7,000 km)
- Highest peak: Aconcagua (6,961m) in Argentina
- Formed by subduction of Nazca Plate under South American Plate
- Multiple climatic zones by elevation

### Urubamba River
- Sacred to the Incas (willka-mayu = sacred river)
- Flows through Sacred Valley
- Makes dramatic U-turn around Machu Picchu
- Part of Amazon River watershed

### Access Routes
1. **Train:** Cusco/Ollantaytambo → Aguas Calientes (Machu Picchu Pueblo)
2. **Inca Trail:** 4-day trek (permits required, 500/day limit)
3. **Bus:** Aguas Calientes → Machu Picchu entrance (30 min)
4. **Hike:** Aguas Calientes → Machu Picchu (2 hours, steep)

## Cultural Context

### Quechua Language
- Official language of the Inca Empire
- Still spoken by 8-10 million people
- Recognized as official language of Peru (1975)
- "Machu Picchu" = Old Mountain
- "Huayna Picchu" = Young Mountain

### Inca Cosmology
- **Hanan Pacha:** Upper world (gods, sun, moon, stars)
- **Kay Pacha:** This world (humans, animals, nature)
- **Uku Pacha:** Underworld (dead, unborn, ancestors)
- **Pachamama:** Earth Mother (still revered today)

### Modern Significance
- Symbol of Peruvian identity
- Tourism: 1.5+ million visitors annually (pre-pandemic)
- Conservation challenges (erosion, overcrowding)
- UNESCO World Heritage Site (1983)
- Protected by Peruvian government
