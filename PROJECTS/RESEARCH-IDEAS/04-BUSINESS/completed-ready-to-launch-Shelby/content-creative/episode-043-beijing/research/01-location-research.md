# Episode 043: The Forbidden City
## Location Research - Beijing, China

## Overview

Beijing, the capital of China, is a city where ancient imperial grandeur meets modern superpower ambition. At its heart lies the Forbidden City, the largest palace complex in the world, surrounded by layers of history: the Great Wall to the north, the Temple of Heaven to the south, ancient hutongs weaving through the old city, and Tiananmen Square—one of the world's largest public squares—at its front gate.

**City Population:** ~21.9 million (2024)
**Metro Area:** ~24.5 million
**Founded:** As imperial capital in 1267 (Yuan Dynasty)
**UNESCO World Heritage Sites:** 7 (Forbidden City, Great Wall, Temple of Heaven, Summer Palace, Ming Tombs, Peking Man Site, Grand Canal)

**Best Time to Visit:** 
- **Spring (April-May):** Mild weather, blooming flowers, moderate crowds
- **Autumn (September-October):** Clear skies, comfortable temperatures, fall colors
- **Avoid:** Chinese New Year (late Jan/early Feb), National Day Golden Week (Oct 1-7), summer peak (July-August hot and crowded)

## THE FORBIDDEN CITY (故宫 / Gùgōng)

### The Imperial Heart

**History:**
- Built: 1406-1420 during the Ming Dynasty (Emperor Yongle)
- Served as imperial palace for 24 emperors (Ming and Qing dynasties)
- Total reign: Nearly 500 years (1420-1912)
- Last emperor: Puyi, who abdicated in 1912 but lived in the Inner Court until 1924
- Became Palace Museum in 1925
- UNESCO World Heritage Site: 1987

**Scale & Architecture:**
- **Area:** 72 hectares (180 acres / 0.72 km²)
- **Buildings:** 980+ structures
- **Rooms:** Traditionally said to have 9,999 rooms (legend says the Emperor of Heaven had 10,000 rooms; the earthly emperor had one less)
- **Walls:** 10 meters (33 feet) high, 8 meters (26 feet) thick at base
- **Moat:** 52 meters (170 feet) wide, 6 meters (20 feet) deep
- **Layout:** Strictly symmetrical along north-south axis
- **Colors:** Imperial yellow roof tiles, vermilion red walls, white marble bases

**Two Main Sections:**

### Outer Court (Front Section)
The ceremonial heart where emperors exercised supreme power.

**Key Halls:**
1. **Meridian Gate (Wumen / 午门):** Main southern entrance, where imperial edicts were announced and military campaigns launched
2. **Hall of Supreme Harmony (Taihedian / 太和殿):** Largest hall, site of coronations, imperial weddings, and major ceremonies
3. **Hall of Central Harmony (Zhonghedian / 中和殿):** Emperor's preparation room before ceremonies
4. **Hall of Preserving Harmony (Baohedian / 保和殿):** Imperial examinations and banquets

### Inner Court (Rear Section)
The emperor's private residence and family quarters.

**Key Areas:**
1. **Palace of Heavenly Purity (Qianqinggong / 乾清宫):** Emperor's residence (Ming and early Qing)
2. **Hall of Union (Jiaotaidian / 交泰殿):** Stored imperial seals
3. **Palace of Earthly Tranquility (Kunninggong / 坤宁宫):** Empress's residence
4. **Six Eastern Palaces & Six Western Palaces:** Concubines' quarters
5. **Imperial Garden:** Landscaped garden at northern end

**Visitor Experience:**
- **Entrance:** Meridian Gate (south)
- **Exit:** Gate of Divine Might (north) or East/West gates
- **Time Needed:** 3-5 hours minimum for thorough visit
- **Audio Guide:** Highly recommended (available in multiple languages)
- **Crowds:** Extremely busy; arrive early (opens 8:30 AM)
- **Tickets:** Must book in advance online (especially peak season)
- **Cost:** ~60 RMB (peak season April-October), ~40 RMB (off-season)

**Photography Tips:**
- Best light: Early morning (first 2 hours after opening)
- Golden hour: Late afternoon (but closes by 5 PM typically)
- Iconic shots: Meridian Gate from outside, Hall of Supreme Harmony courtyard, imperial roof details, red walls with yellow tiles
- Avoid: Tripods require special permission

**Interesting Facts:**
- No trees in the main ceremonial courtyards (security—no hiding places, and trees would obscure the view of imperial majesty)
- All roof figurines are mythical creatures protecting against fire and evil
- The number 9 (and multiples) appears everywhere—considered the supreme yang number
- Floors were made of "golden bricks" (actually clay, but so fine they rang like metal when struck)

## TIANANMEN SQUARE (天安门广场)

### The People's Square

**History:**
- Original gate built: 1417 (Ming Dynasty)
- Reconstructed: 1651 (Qing Dynasty)
- Expanded to current size: 1950s under Mao Zedong
- Site of major historical events: founding of PRC (1949), Cultural Revolution rallies, Tiananmen Square protests (1989)

**Scale:**
- **Area:** 440,000 square meters (109 acres / 0.44 km²)
- **Capacity:** Up to 1 million people for major events
- **Dimensions:** 880 meters north-south, 500 meters east-west
- World's largest public square by some measures

**Key Landmarks:**

1. **Tiananmen Gate (Gate of Heavenly Peace):** Northern end, entrance to Forbidden City
   - Portrait of Mao Zedong hangs above the central archway
   - Inscription: "Long Live the People's Republic of China"

2. **Monument to the People's Heroes:** Center of square
   - 38 meters (125 feet) tall obelisk
   - Honors revolutionary martyrs

3. **Great Hall of the People:** Western side
   - Meeting place of National People's Congress
   - 171,800 square meters, seats 10,000 in main auditorium

4. **National Museum of China:** Eastern side
   - World's most-visited museum (2023)
   - Chinese history and art exhibitions

5. **Mao Zedong Mausoleum:** Southern end
   - Contains embalmed body of Mao Zedong
   - Open for viewing (no photography, strict security)

**Visitor Experience:**
- **Security:** Extensive airport-style screening; bring passport
- **Cost:** Free (but requires advance reservation online)
- **Time Needed:** 1-2 hours for square itself
- **Best Time:** Early morning for flag-raising ceremony (sunrise)
- **Flag Raising:** Daily at sunrise by PLA honor guard (major spectacle)
- **Flag Lowering:** At sunset

**Photography:**
- Tiananmen Gate with Mao portrait
- Monument to People's Heroes
- Wide shots showing scale of square
- Night illumination (square lit up after dark)

**Important Notes:**
- Cannot enter Forbidden City directly from Tiananmen Square (must go around)
- Drones strictly prohibited
- Political sensitivity: be respectful, avoid protests or demonstrations

## THE GREAT WALL (Sections Near Beijing)

### The Dragon's Backbone

While the Great Wall stretches thousands of kilometers, several accessible sections are within day-trip distance from Beijing. For Episode 043, focus on the sections most relevant to a Beijing-centered narrative.

**Three Main Options:**

### 1. BADALING (八达岭) - The Famous Section
**Distance from Beijing:** 70 km (43 miles) north, 1.5 hours

**Characteristics:**
- Most visited section (70% of all Great Wall tourists)
- Best restored and most accessible
- Widest section (5-7 meters wide, can fit 10 soldiers abreast)
- Cable car and pulley systems available
- Wheelchair accessible in parts
- Very crowded, especially weekends and holidays

**Best For:** First-time visitors, families, those with mobility issues, iconic photographs

**Cost:** 
- Entry: 40 RMB (off-season), 45 RMB (peak)
- Cable car: 140 RMB round-trip
- Bus from Beijing: 12-30 RMB

---

### 2. MUTIANYU (慕田峪) - The Balanced Choice
**Distance from Beijing:** 73 km (45 miles) northeast, 1.5-2 hours

**Characteristics:**
- Second most popular, but less crowded than Badaling
- Well-restored but more authentic feel
- 23 watchtowers over 5.4 km stretch
- Surrounded by forest (90%+ tree coverage)
- Cable car up, toboggan slide down (unique!)
- Beautiful in autumn (foliage)

**Best For:** Most visitors, photographers, those wanting balance of access and authenticity

**Cost:**
- Entry: 45 RMB
- Cable car: 120 RMB round-trip or 100 RMB one-way
- Toboggan: 100 RMB
- Shuttle bus from parking: 15 RMB

---

### 3. JINSHANLING (金山岭) - The Hiker's Choice
**Distance from Beijing:** 140 km (87 miles) northeast, 2.5-3 hours

**Characteristics:**
- Partially restored, partially wild
- Steep, challenging sections
- Fewer crowds (especially on weekdays)
- Original Ming Dynasty bricks visible
- Some sections crumbling, overgrown
- Best for serious hiking and photography
- Can hike to Simatai section (5-6 km)

**Best For:** Adventurous travelers, photographers, hikers, those seeking authenticity

**Cost:**
- Entry: 65 RMB
- Cable car: 70 RMB one-way, 120 RMB round-trip
- Night tours available (rare for Great Wall)

---

### 4. JIANkou (箭扣) - The Wild Section
**Distance from Beijing:** 80 km (50 miles) north, 2-2.5 hours

**Characteristics:**
- Completely unrestored, dangerous
- Steep cliffs, crumbling walls
- No safety features
- Popular with extreme hikers and photographers
- Several deaths have occurred
- Not recommended for casual visitors

**Best For:** Experienced hikers, adventure seekers, dramatic photography (from safe distances)

**Cost:** Free (but access sometimes restricted)

---

**Recommendation for Episode 043:**
Focus on **Mutianyu** as the primary Great Wall section—it offers the best balance of visual drama, accessibility, and authenticity. Mention Badaling as the famous alternative and Jinshanling for adventurous viewers.

## TEMPLE OF HEAVEN (天坛 / Tiāntán)

### Where Emperors Prayed for Good Harvests

**History:**
- Built: 1420 (Ming Dynasty, same era as Forbidden City)
- Purpose: Annual ceremonies where emperors prayed for good harvests
- Last imperial ceremony: 1911
- UNESCO World Heritage Site: 1998
- Park opened to public: 1918

**Architecture & Symbolism:**
- **Area:** 273 hectares (675 acres) — larger than Forbidden City
- **Design:** Based on ancient Chinese cosmology
  - Northern end: Circular buildings (heaven is round)
  - Southern end: Square buildings (earth is square)
  - Symbolizes "round heaven, square earth"
- **Colors:** Deep blue roof tiles (color of heaven), not imperial yellow

**Key Structures:**

### 1. Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests (祈年殿 / Qínián Diàn)
- **The Icon:** Triple-eaved circular hall, 38 meters (125 feet) tall
- **Construction:** Entirely wood, no nails used
- **Pillars:** 28 massive wooden pillars
  - Inner 4: Represent the four seasons
  - Middle 12: Represent the 12 months
  - Outer 12: Represent the 12 two-hour periods of the day
- **Base:** Triple white marble terrace
- **Roof:** Deep blue tiles with golden finial

### 2. Imperial Vault of Heaven (皇穹宇 / Huángqióng Yǔ)
- Single-eaved circular hall
- Stores spirit tablets of ancestors
- Famous Echo Wall: whisper at one end, heard clearly at other end (193 meters / 633 feet away)

### 3. Circular Mound Altar (圜丘坛 / Yuánqiū Tán)
- Open-air, triple-tiered marble altar
- Emperor performed winter solstice sacrifices here
- Acoustic design: sounds amplify at the center

**Visitor Experience:**
- **Entrance:** Multiple gates; South Gate or East Gate recommended
- **Tickets:** 
  - Park entry only: 15 RMB (off-season), 20 RMB (peak)
  - Full access (including halls): 30-35 RMB
- **Time Needed:** 2-3 hours
- **Best Time:** Early morning (6-8 AM) to see locals doing tai chi, dancing, calligraphy with water
- **Crowds:** Less crowded than Forbidden City

**Cultural Experience:**
- Morning exercise: Locals practice tai chi, fan dancing, sword dancing
- Music: Traditional instruments, group singing
- Calligraphy: Elderly practitioners write with giant water brushes on pavement
- Chess: Chinese chess (xiangqi) games everywhere
- This is a living park, not just a tourist site

**Photography:**
- Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests from south (classic view)
- Reflection in water features
- Blue roof details against sky
- Morning activities (ask permission)
- Red pillars and blue tiles create striking contrast

## HUTONGS (胡同)

### The Old Beijing Neighborhoods

**What Are Hutongs?**
- Narrow alleyways and lanes that form traditional Beijing neighborhoods
- Built during Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties
- Siheyuan (courtyard houses) line the hutongs
- Once numbered in thousands; now rapidly disappearing

**History:**
- **Yuan Dynasty (1271-1368):** Hutong system established
- **Ming/Qing:** Expanded and refined
- **1949:** ~7,000 hutongs in Beijing
- **Today:** ~1,000 remain (many preserved, some gentrified)
- **Threats:** Urban development, modernization, tourism

**Architecture:**
- **Siheyuan (四合院):** Traditional courtyard houses
  - Single-story buildings around central courtyard
  - Main house faces south (best light, feng shui)
  - Side houses for family members
  - Gate often ornate, with guardian lions
  - High walls for privacy
- **Hutong Width:** 
  - Wide hutongs: 9+ meters
  - Standard: 6-9 meters
  - Narrow: 3-6 meters
  - "Skinny" hutongs: <3 meters

**Life in the Hutongs:**
- Close-knit communities
- Elders playing chess, chatting
- Children playing in alleys
- Bicycles and electric scooters parked outside
- Street vendors (declining)
- Shared public toilets (many homes still lack private bathrooms)
- Laundry hanging outside
- Bird cages (traditional hobby)

**Key Hutong Areas to Visit:**

### 1. NANLUOGUXIANG (南锣鼓巷)
- **Location:** Near Shichahai, central Beijing
- **Character:** Most commercialized, touristy
- **Features:** Boutique shops, cafes, bars, street food
- **Best For:** First-time visitors, shopping, dining
- **Crowds:** Very busy, especially weekends

### 2. YANDAIXIEJIE (烟袋斜街) - Tobacco Pipe Street
- **Location:** Near Shichahai
- **Character:** Historic street with modern shops
- **Features:** Traditional architecture, souvenir shops, restaurants
- **Length:** ~230 meters
- **Best For:** Short visit, photos, souvenirs

### 3. WUDAoyING HUTONG (五道营胡同)
- **Location:** Near Lama Temple
- **Character:** Hip, artsy, gentrified
- **Features:** Cafes, boutiques, galleries, international restaurants
- **Vibe:** Beijing's answer to Brooklyn
- **Best For:** Younger crowd, coffee, brunch, unique shopping

### 4. SHICHAHAI AREA (什刹海)
- **Location:** Around three lakes (Qianhai, Houhai, Xihai)
- **Character:** Mix of residential and commercial
- **Features:** Lakeside walks, bars, restaurants, Prince Gong's Mansion
- **Best For:** Evening strolls, lake views, hutong tours

### 5. DONGJIAOMINXIANG (东交民巷)
- **Location:** Near Tiananmen
- **Character:** Historic foreign legation quarter
- **Features:** Colonial-era buildings, embassies, churches
- **Best For:** Architecture, history, quieter walks

**Hutong Experiences:**

### Rickshaw Tours
- **Cost:** 100-300 RMB per hour (negotiate)
- **Duration:** 1-2 hours typical
- **Includes:** Ride through hutongs, stop at local home, tea
- **Tip:** Agree on price and route beforehand

### Walking Tours
- **Free:** Self-guided exploration
- **Guided:** 200-500 RMB for 2-3 hours
- **Best Areas:** Shichahai, Nanluoguxiang side streets

### Local Home Visits
- Some tour operators arrange visits to local families
- Experience siheyuan life
- Tea, conversation, learn about traditions
- **Cost:** 150-300 RMB per person

**Photography:**
- Early morning or late afternoon light
- Door details (ornate gates, door knockers)
- Daily life scenes (respectfully)
- Roof lines and grey brick textures
- Red lanterns against grey walls
- Contrast between old and new

**Preservation Efforts:**
- Some hutongs designated as "protected historical areas"
- Gentrification is double-edged: preserves buildings but displaces residents
- Tension between modernization and preservation
- Government programs to improve infrastructure while maintaining character

## BEIJING LAYOUT & NAVIGATION

### City Structure

**Concentric Ring Roads:**
- **2nd Ring Road:** Encircles old city (within which Forbidden City sits)
- **3rd Ring Road:** Expands further out
- **4th-6th Ring Roads:** Suburban and exurban areas
- Most tourist sites within 2nd-4th Ring Roads

**Cardinal Directions:**
- Beijing is laid out on strict north-south axis
- Forbidden City is the geometric center of imperial Beijing
- Tiananmen Square is south of Forbidden City
- Most major sites aligned along central axis

**Districts Relevant to Tourists:**
- **Dongcheng (东城):** Forbidden City, Wangfujing, Lama Temple
- **Xicheng (西城):** Shichahai, hutongs, Financial Street
- **Chaoyang (朝阳):** CBD, 798 Art District, embassies
- **Haidian (海淀):** Summer Palace, universities (Peking, Tsinghua)

### Transportation

**Metro:**
- Extensive network covering all major areas
- Signs in Chinese and English
- Cost: 3-7 RMB depending on distance
- Avoid rush hours (7:30-9:00 AM, 5:30-7:00 PM)
- Download Beijing Metro app or use maps.me

**Bus:**
- Cheap (2 RMB typically)
- Extensive network
- Can be crowded and confusing for non-Chinese speakers
- Use apps for routing

**Taxi/Didi (Chinese Uber):**
- Readily available
- Relatively inexpensive
- Have destination written in Chinese
- Didi app works for foreigners (English interface)

**Bike Sharing:**
- Mobike, Ofo (check availability)
- Cheap and convenient for short distances
- Dedicated bike lanes in many areas

**Walking:**
- Many sites clustered in central Beijing
- Forbidden City area is pedestrian-friendly
- Hutongs best explored on foot

## PRACTICAL INFORMATION

### Visas
- **Tourist Visa (L Visa):** Required for most nationalities
- **Duration:** Typically 30-90 days, single or multiple entry
- **Processing:** 4-10 business days typically
- **Cost:** Varies by nationality (~140-200 USD for US citizens)
- **Apply:** Chinese embassy/consulate or visa service
- **144-Hour Visa-Free Transit:** Available for many nationalities if transiting through Beijing to third country

### Language
- **Official:** Mandarin Chinese (Putonghua)
- **English:** Limited outside tourist areas
- **Helpful Phrases:**
  - Hello: Nǐ hǎo (你好)
  - Thank you: Xièxie (谢谢)
  - How much?: Duōshǎo qián? (多少钱)
  - I don't understand: Wǒ tīng bù dǒng (我听不懂)
  - Toilet: Xǐshǒujiān (洗手间)

### Currency
- **Currency:** Chinese Yuan (CNY / RMB)
- **Cash vs. Digital:** Cash still useful, but WeChat Pay and Alipay dominate
- **ATMs:** Widely available, international cards accepted at major banks
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted at hotels, upscale restaurants, chains

### Connectivity
- **SIM Cards:** Available at airport and phone stores (passport required)
- **WiFi:** Available at hotels, cafes, many restaurants
- **Great Firewall:** Google, Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Twitter blocked
- **VPN:** Many travelers use VPNs (legality is gray area)

### Health & Safety
- **Water:** Do not drink tap water; bottled water recommended
- **Air Quality:** Can be poor; check AQI, bring masks if sensitive
- **Safety:** Very safe for tourists; violent crime rare
- **Scams:** Watch for tea ceremony scams, fake art deals, overly friendly strangers
- **Emergency:** 110 (police), 120 (ambulance)

### Cultural Etiquette
- **Respect:** Show respect at historical and political sites
- **Photos:** Ask permission before photographing people
- **Temples:** Dress modestly, speak quietly
- **Tipping:** Not customary (sometimes refused)
- **Queue:** Lines can be chaotic; be prepared
- **Negotiation:** Expected at markets, not at fixed-price stores

## SEASONAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Spring (March-May)
- **Temperature:** 10-22°C (50-72°F)
- **Pros:** Pleasant weather, flowers blooming, moderate crowds
- **Cons:** Can be windy, occasional sandstorms from Gobi
- **Best For:** Overall visiting, photography

### Summer (June-August)
- **Temperature:** 25-35°C (77-95°F), can exceed 40°C
- **Pros:** Long days, all sites open, green landscapes
- **Cons:** Very hot, humid, crowded (summer vacation), occasional heavy rain
- **Best For:** Early morning visits, indoor sites

### Autumn (September-November)
- **Temperature:** 10-22°C (50-72°F)
- **Pros:** Clear skies, comfortable, fall colors (especially Great Wall)
- **Cons:** Crowded during National Day (Oct 1-7)
- **Best For:** Overall best time, hiking Great Wall

### Winter (December-February)
- **Temperature:** -10 to 5°C (14-41°F), can be colder
- **Pros:** Few crowds, snow on Forbidden City and Great Wall is stunning, lower prices
- **Cons:** Very cold, some outdoor areas less enjoyable, shorter days
- **Best For:** Photography (snow scenes), avoiding crowds

## KEY DATES & FESTIVALS

### Chinese New Year (Late January/Early February)
- **Impact:** Massive crowds, many businesses close, transport packed
- **Avoid:** Unless specifically seeking festival experience

### Qingming Festival (Early April)
- Tomb-Sweeping Day
- **Impact:** Moderate crowds at tourist sites

### Labor Day (May 1-5)
- **Impact:** Very crowded, avoid if possible

### National Day Golden Week (October 1-7)
- **Impact:** EXTREMELY crowded, highest prices, longest lines
- **Avoid:** Unless you enjoy crowds

### Mid-Autumn Festival (September/October)
- Moon viewing, mooncakes
- **Impact:** Moderate crowds

## RECOMMENDED ITINERARY (Beijing Focus)

### 4-Day Core Itinerary

**Day 1: Imperial Beijing**
- Morning: Tiananmen Square (flag raising if early)
- Late Morning through Afternoon: Forbidden City (3-5 hours)
- Evening: Wangfujing Street (shopping, food)

**Day 2: Great Wall Day Trip**
- Full Day: Mutianyu Great Wall (leave early, return late afternoon)
- Evening: Rest or local neighborhood exploration

**Day 3: Temple & Hutongs**
- Morning: Temple of Heaven (watch morning activities)
- Late Morning: Lama Temple (Yonghegong)
- Afternoon: Hutong exploration (Shichahai area)
- Evening: Hutong dinner or Houhai bars

**Day 4: Deep Dive**
- Option A: Summer Palace (large, needs half-day)
- Option B: More hutong exploration, local neighborhoods
- Option C: 798 Art District (contemporary art)
- Evening: Peking Duck dinner

### Extended Additions
- **Day Trip:** Ming Tombs (near Great Wall)
- **Day Trip:** Chengde Mountain Resort (further, but impressive)
- **Extra Days:** More hutong time, local experiences, food tours
