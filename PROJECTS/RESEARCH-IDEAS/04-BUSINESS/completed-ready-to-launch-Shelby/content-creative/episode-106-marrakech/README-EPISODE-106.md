## Episode Summary

### The Story

Episode 106 explores Marrakech, Morocco—the Red City (Al Hamra). Founded in 1070 by the Almoravid dynasty, Marrakech is a living tapestry woven from Berber, Arab, Andalusian, and French threads.

This episode walks through:
- The medina's 2,400 alleyways (where getting lost is the point)
- Jardin Majorelle's electric blue sanctuary (YSL's spiritual home)
- Riad courtyards (oases of calm within the chaos)
- Atlas Mountain villages (Berber hospitality, terraced fields)
- Jemaa el-Fnaa at dusk (the square transforms, the city awakens)

Marrakech is not a city you visit. It's a city that *visits you*—lingering in memory long after departure, in the scent of cumin, the glow of red stone at sunset, the echo of the muezzin's call.

### Key Themes

- **Contrast:** Ancient/modern, chaos/calm, red/blue, desert/mountain
- **Sensory Immersion:** Spice scents, call to prayer, vibrant colors, textures
- **Cultural Respect:** Authentic representation, avoiding stereotypes
- **Timelessness:** A city that has welcomed travelers for nearly 1,000 years
- **Hospitality:** The riad experience, Moroccan "Moroccan whiskey" (mint tea)
