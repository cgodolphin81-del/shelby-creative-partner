## When to Visit

### Seasonal Guide

#### 🌸 Spring (April–May) — RECOMMENDED
- **Temperature:** 15–24°C (59–75°F)
- **Conditions:** Misty mornings, blooming rhododendrons
- **Crowds:** Moderate
- **Pros:** Magical mist, comfortable temps, fewer tourists than summer
- **Cons:** Occasional rain, pack raincoat
- **Photography:** Excellent — mist adds ethereal quality

#### ☀️ Summer (June–August) — AVOID IF POSSIBLE
- **Temperature:** 20–30°C (68–86°F)
- **Conditions:** Hot, humid, afternoon thunderstorms
- **Crowds:** PEAK (school holidays)
- **Pros:** All facilities open, long days
- **Cons:** Insanely crowded, hot, expensive
- **Photography:** Challenging — haze, crowds in shots

#### 🍂 Autumn (September–October) — RECOMMENDED
- **Temperature:** 15–25°C (59–77°F)
- **Conditions:** Crisp air, golden foliage, clear skies
- **Crowds:** Moderate to high (avoid Golden Week Oct 1–7)
- **Pros:** Best visibility, comfortable, beautiful colors
- **Cons:** Popular season, book ahead
- **Photography:** Ideal — sharp skies, warm light

#### ❄️ Winter (December–February) — FOR ADVENTURERS
- **Temperature:** 5–15°C (41–59°F)
- **Conditions:** Cold, possible snow/ice, some closures
- **Crowds:** Low
- **Pros:** Minimal tourists, dramatic atmosphere, lower prices
- **Cons:** Some cable cars/routes may close, cold
- **Photography:** Unique — snow-capped pillars, moody

### 🎯 Best Months Ranked
1. **Late September** — Perfect balance of weather and crowds
2. **April** — Misty, magical, spring blooms
3. **October** (after Golden Week) — Clear skies, autumn colors
4. **May** — Warm, green, moderate crowds
5. **Late November** — Secret month, first frost, empty trails

### 📅 Days to Avoid
- **Golden Week:** October 1–7 (extreme crowds)
- **Chinese New Year:** Late January/early February (varies)
- **Summer holidays:** Mid-July through August
- **Weekends:** Always busier than weekdays

## Getting There

### ✈️ By Air

**Zhangjiajie Hehua International Airport (DYG)**

**Direct flights from:**
- Beijing — 2.5 hours, multiple daily
- Shanghai — 2.5 hours, multiple daily
- Guangzhou — 2 hours, daily
- Chengdu — 1.5 hours, daily
- Xi'an — 2 hours, daily

**International travelers:**
Most will connect through Beijing, Shanghai, or Guangzhou. Major airlines serving these hubs:
- Air China
- China Eastern
- China Southern
- United, Delta, American (via partners)

**Airport to Wulingyuan:**
- **Taxi:** 40 minutes, ~¥150
- **Airport bus:** 1 hour, ~¥30, runs hourly
- **Hotel shuttle:** Many hotels offer pickup (arrange in advance)

### 🚄 By Train

**High-Speed Rail from Changsha**
- **Duration:** 3.5–4 hours
- **Cost:** ~¥200 (second class), ~¥320 (first class)
- **Frequency:** Multiple daily departures
- **Station:** Zhangjiajie West Station

**Why train over plane?**
- More reliable (fewer delays)
- Scenic journey
- City center to city center
- Often cheaper when you factor in airport transfers

**From Zhangjiajie West Station to Wulingyuan:**
- **Bus:** 40 minutes, ~¥15
- **Taxi:** 35 minutes, ~¥100

### 🚌 By Bus

**From Changsha:**
- **Duration:** 4–5 hours
- **Cost:** ~¥100
- **Departure:** Changsha West Bus Station
- **Note:** Less comfortable than train, but budget option

## Tickets & Passes

### Park Entrance

**Zhangjiajie National Forest Park**
- **Adult:** ¥225–248 (varies by season)
- **Validity:** 4 consecutive days
- **Includes:** Zhangjiajie NF Park, Suoxi Valley, Yangjiajie, Tianzi Mountain
- **Children:** Half price for 1.3–1.5m height, free under 1.3m
- **Seniors:** Discounts for 60+ (bring passport)

**How to Buy:**
1. **Online (Recommended):** Trip.com, official park website
   - Accepts foreign credit cards
   - Skip ticket lines
   - Book 1 week ahead (peak), 1–2 days (off-season)
2. **On-site:** Ticket windows at park entrances
   - Bring passport
   - May have queues during peak season

**Important:**
- Ticket is **non-transferable** (photo taken at entry)
- Valid for **consecutive days** (not flexible)
- **2025 Update:** New 3-Day Flex Pass available online

### Additional Attractions

|------------|-----------|-------|
| **Bailong Elevator** | 72 | One-way, round-trip ¥144 |
| **Tianmen Mountain Cableway** | 70–90 | Per ride, seasonal |
| **Tianmen Mountain Entry** | 258–328 | Includes cable car |
| **Grand Canyon + Glass Bridge** | 178 | Combo ticket required |
| **Grand Canyon Activities (7-choose-5)** | 298 | Zipline, slides, VR, boat |
| **Yellow Dragon Cave** | 140 | Karst cave system |
| **Baofeng Lake** | 96 | Boat ride included |

### Money-Saving Tips
1. **Buy combo tickets** where available
2. **Skip some cable cars** if you're fit (hiking is free)
3. **Bring snacks** — park food is expensive
4. **Student discounts** — bring ISIC card
5. **Off-season travel** — some attractions 20–30% cheaper

## Where to Stay

### Wulingyuan District ⭐ RECOMMENDED

**Why stay here:**
- Walking distance to park entrances
- Local atmosphere
- Budget-friendly options
- Restaurants geared toward tourists

**Price Range:**
- **Hostels:** ¥80–180/night
- **Mid-range:** ¥250–500/night
- **Luxury:** ¥600–1,200+/night

**Recommended Hotels:**

**Luxury:**

- **Crowne Plaza Zhangjiajie Wulingyuan** — Reliable chain, good location

**Mid-Range:**
- **Xiangruige Boutique Hotel** — Character, good reviews

**Budget:**
- **Youth Hostels** — Several options, ¥80–120/night

### Zhangjiajie City Center

**Why stay here:**
- More dining options
- Nightlife
- Better transport connections
- Closer to Tianmen Mountain

**Downsides:**
- 40 minutes to Forest Park each way
- Less atmospheric

**Recommended:**
- **Best Western Plus** — Reliable, central
- **Various local hotels** — ¥200–400/night

### Mountain Villages

**For cultural immersion:**
- **Tujia homestays** near Huangshi Village
- **Home-cooked meals** included
- **Local insights** on hidden trails
- **Price:** ¥150–300/night including meals

**How to book:** Ask at visitor center, or through local tour operators

## Suggested Itineraries

### 3-Day Classic (Sweet Spot) ⭐

**Day 1: Yuanjiajie + Tianzi Mountain**
- Morning: Enter park early (7 AM)
- Take Bailong Elevator to Yuanjiajie
- Explore Avatar Hallelujah Mountain, First Bridge
- Afternoon: Cable car to Tianzi Mountain
- Sunset at Tianzi overlook
- Stay: Wulingyuan

**Day 2: Yangjiajie + Golden Whip Stream**
- Morning: Yangjiajie cable car (quieter than Yuanjiajie)
- Explore Peak Wall formation
- Afternoon: Golden Whip Stream walk (5.7 km, flat)
- Evening: Relax in Wulingyuan
- Stay: Wulingyuan

**Day 3: Tianmen Mountain**
- Morning: Bus to Tianmen Mountain
- Cable car ascent (world's longest)
- Glass Skywalk, Tianmen Cave
- Afternoon: Return to city
- Evening: Depart or extend stay

### 4-Day Comprehensive

**Days 1–2:** As above (Yuanjiajie, Tianzi, Yangjiajie, Golden Whip)

**Day 3: Grand Canyon + Glass Bridge**
- Morning: Travel to Grand Canyon (40 min from Wulingyuan)
- Walk Glass Bridge
- Optional: 7-choose-5 activities (zipline, slides)
- Afternoon: Return, rest
- Stay: Wulingyuan

**Day 4: Tianmen Mountain + Departure**
- Morning: Tianmen Mountain
- Afternoon: Depart or explore city

### 5–6 Day Extended

**Days 1–4:** As comprehensive itinerary above

**Day 5: Fenghuang Ancient Town**
- Bus: 3–4 hours
- Explore ancient architecture, Tuojiang River
- Stay overnight in riverside inn

**Day 6: Return + Yellow Dragon Cave**
- Morning: Return to Zhangjiajie
- Afternoon: Yellow Dragon Cave (karst formations)
- Evening: Depart

## Photography Guide

### Best Locations

| Location | Best Time | Subject |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Yuanjiajie** | Sunrise | Avatar pillars, mist |
| **Tianzi Mountain** | Sunrise/sunset | Panorama, sea of clouds |
| **Glass Bridge** | Morning | Bridge + canyon, reactions |
| **Golden Whip Stream** | Late afternoon | Reflections, peaceful scenes |
| **Bailong Elevator** | Midday | Interior shots, cliff views |
| **Huangshizhai** | Any time | 360° overview |

### Camera Settings (General)

**Landscape:**
- **Aperture:** f/8–f/11 for sharpness
- **ISO:** 100–400 (lowest possible)
- **Shutter:** 1/125 or faster (handheld)
- **Focus:** Hyperfocal or 1/3 into scene

**Mist/Fog:**
- **Exposure compensation:** +0.7 to +1.3 (mist fools meters)
- **White balance:** Slightly cool for mood

**Sunrise/Sunset:**
- **Bracket exposures** for HDR
- **Tripod essential** for low light
- **Golden hour:** First/last hour of daylight

### Gear Recommendations

**Essential:**
- Wide-angle lens (16–35mm)
- Telephoto lens (70–200mm) for distant pillars
- Sturdy tripod (for sunrise/sunset)
- Polarizing filter (reduces haze)
- Rain cover for camera

**Optional:**
- Drone (check regulations — often restricted in parks)
- GoPro for Glass Bridge
- Extra batteries (cold drains them faster)

### Photography Tips

1. **Arrive early** — best light and fewest crowds
2. **Shoot in RAW** — more flexibility in editing
3. **Use mist creatively** — layers add depth
4. **Include human element** — scale against pillars
5. **Visit same spot multiple times** — light changes dramatically
6. **Overcast days are gold** — soft, even lighting
7. **Clean your glass** — especially on Glass Bridge

## Practical Tips

### Avoiding Crowds

1. **Enter at 7 AM** (gate opening) — be among the first
2. **Start at Yangjiajie** — reverse route, quieter
3. **Visit Golden Whip Stream late afternoon** — tour groups leave by 4 PM
4. **Take Shenatang Bay path** — hidden trail in Yuanjiajie
5. **Avoid weekends** and Chinese holidays
6. **Stay late** — best light and fewest people

### Safety

- **Trails:** Generally safe, but watch footing when wet
- **Glass Bridge:** No running, no sharp objects, follow staff instructions
- **Wildlife:** Monkeys may approach — don't feed, keep food secured
- **Altitude:** Up to 1,300m — take it easy if sensitive
- **Weather:** Check forecast, trails may close in severe weather

### Cultural Etiquette

- **Greetings:** A simple nod or smile is fine
- **Photos:** Ask before photographing locals
- **Temples:** Dress modestly, speak quietly
- **Tipping:** Not expected in China
- **Bargaining:** Acceptable at markets, not in stores

### Connectivity

- **WiFi:** Available at most hotels and restaurants
- **SIM Card:** Buy at airport or city (China Mobile, China Unicom)
- **VPN:** Many Western apps blocked (Google, Facebook, Instagram) — install VPN before arrival
- **Emergency:** 110 (police), 120 (ambulance)

### Health

- **Water:** Drink bottled or boiled water
- **Food:** Street food generally safe, watch for cleanliness
- **Medications:** Bring basics (pain relievers, stomach meds)
- **Travel Insurance:** Recommended for all travelers
