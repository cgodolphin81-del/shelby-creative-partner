# CV Template Design Specifications

**For:** Canva Template Creation
**Requirement:** ATS-Friendly (Applicant Tracking System compatible)
**Total Templates:** 15

---

## ATS-Friendly Design Rules (CRITICAL)

✅ **DO:**
- Use standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills, etc.)
- Use common fonts (Arial, Calibri, Helvetica, Times New Roman, Georgia)
- Maintain clear hierarchy with font sizes (Name: 24-32pt, Headings: 14-16pt, Body: 10-12pt)
- Use standard bullet points (• or -)
- Keep single-column layout (ATS struggles with multi-column)
- Use standard margins (0.5" - 1" on all sides)
- Save as PDF and DOCX formats
- Use black text on white background (or very light gray)
- Include keywords naturally throughout

❌ **DON'T:**
- No graphics, icons, or images (except subtle lines)
- No tables or text boxes (ATS can't read them)
- No headers/footers (information gets lost)
- No columns or complex layouts
- No unusual fonts or script fonts
- No photos (unless specific industry requires it)
- No infographics or charts
- No bright colors (subtle accent colors only)

## Notes for Charmaine

1. **Create in Canva:** Use Canva's resume templates as starting point but ensure they meet ATS requirements above
2. **Two sizes:** Each template needs A4 (210 x 297mm) and US Letter (8.5" x 11") versions
3. **Export formats:** PDF for final use, DOCX for editable version customers can modify
4. **Color usage:** Keep accent colors minimal - only for headings and subtle lines
5. **Test ATS compatibility:** Copy content from PDF and paste into plain text editor - should maintain structure
6. **Naming convention:** `CV_Template##_Name_Size_Format` (e.g., `CV_Template01_ClassicProfessional_A4_PDF`)
