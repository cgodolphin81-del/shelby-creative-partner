## Content Strategy Overview

### The Authority Site Content Model

**Goal:** Build comprehensive topical authority through systematic content creation.

**Key Principles:**
- **Quality over quantity** (but you need both)
- **Human oversight essential** (AI alone won't rank)
- **Consistency beats intensity** (sustainable pace)
- **Update existing content** (not just create new)

### Content Volume Benchmarks

| Site Goal | Articles Needed | Timeframe | Weekly Output |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Starter Site** | 25-50 | 3-6 months | 2-3/week |
| **Competitive Site** | 100-200 | 6-12 months | 4-6/week |
| **Authority Site** | 200-500 | 12-24 months | 5-10/week |
| **Dominant Site** | 500-1000+ | 24+ months | 10+/week |

### The 80/20 Content Rule

**80% of your traffic will come from 20% of your content.**

Focus on:
- High-value topics (search volume + commercial intent)
- Evergreen content (stays relevant for years)
- Comprehensive coverage (better than competitors)

Don't obsess over:
- Trending topics (short lifespan)
- Ultra-niche long-tail (low impact)
- Perfection (publish and improve)

### Step-by-Step AI Content Workflow

#### Step 1: Keyword & Topic Research (15 min/article)

**Tools:** Ahrefs, SEMrush, or free alternatives

**Process:**
1. Identify target keyword (primary)
2. Find 5-10 related keywords (secondary)
3. Analyze top 10 ranking pages
4. Note content gaps and opportunities
5. Determine search intent (informational, commercial, transactional)

**Output:** Content brief with:
- Target keyword
- Secondary keywords
- Word count target
- Competitor analysis
- Unique angle

---

#### Step 2: AI Outline Generation (5 min/article)

**Prompt Template:**
```
Create a detailed outline for an article about [TOPIC].

Target keyword: [KEYWORD]
Search intent: [INTENT]
Target audience: [AUDIENCE]

Requirements:
- Include H2 and H3 headings
- Cover all aspects a reader would want to know
- Include a FAQ section
- Structure for readability (short sections, bullet points)
- Target word count: [WORD COUNT]

Competitor topics to cover:
- [TOPIC 1]
- [TOPIC 2]
- [TOPIC 3]
```

**Review:** Ensure outline covers all important subtopics and has logical flow.

---

#### Step 3: AI First Draft (10-15 min/article)

**Prompt Template:**
```
Write a comprehensive article based on this outline:

[PASTE OUTLINE]

Requirements:
- Write in a [CONVERSATIONAL/PROFESSIONAL] tone
- Target reading level: 8th-10th grade
- Include specific examples and data where relevant
- Use short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Include bullet points and numbered lists
- Add transition sentences between sections
- Target word count: [WORD COUNT]

Important:
- Do not make up statistics or claims
- Mark any claims that need fact-checking with [VERIFY]
- Write naturally, avoid repetitive phrases
```

**Output:** 2,000-4,000 word first draft

---

#### Step 4: Human Review & Enhancement (30-60 min/article)

**Checklist:**

**Accuracy:**
- [ ] Fact-check all statistics and claims
- [ ] Verify product details and pricing
- [ ] Update any time-sensitive information
- [ ] Remove [VERIFY] markers with accurate info

**E-E-A-T:**
- [ ] Add personal experience where relevant
- [ ] Include expert quotes or citations
- [ ] Add author bio with credentials
- [ ] Link to authoritative sources

**Readability:**
- [ ] Improve awkward phrasing
- [ ] Vary sentence structure
- [ ] Add transition sentences
- [ ] Ensure logical flow

**SEO:**
- [ ] Include target keyword in title, H1, first 100 words
- [ ] Add secondary keywords naturally
- [ ] Optimize meta title and description
- [ ] Add internal links to related content
- [ ] Add external links to authoritative sources

**Engagement:**
- [ ] Add compelling introduction
- [ ] Include relevant images/screenshots
- [ ] Add call-to-action
- [ ] Include FAQ section if appropriate

---

#### Step 5: Final Optimization (10 min/article)

**On-Page SEO:**
- Title tag (60 characters max)
- Meta description (155 characters max)
- URL slug (short, keyword-rich)
- Image alt text
- Internal links (3-10 per article)
- External links (2-5 per article)

**Technical:**
- Add schema markup
- Optimize images (compress, WebP format)
- Ensure mobile responsiveness
- Check page speed

### Recommended Approach by Stage

**Months 1-3 (Foundation):**
- **Goal:** 25-50 articles
- **Approach:** Founder writes with AI assistance
- **Focus:** Quality over speed, establish voice
- **Time commitment:** 10-20 hours/week

**Months 4-6 (Growth):**
- **Goal:** 50-100 articles total
- **Approach:** Add 1-2 freelance writers
- **Focus:** Scale while maintaining quality
- **Time commitment:** 5-10 hours/week (management)

**Months 7-12 (Scale):**
- **Goal:** 100-250 articles total
- **Approach:** AI + editor workflow
- **Focus:** Systematic production, topic cluster completion
- **Time commitment:** 5 hours/week (oversight)

**Months 13-24 (Authority):**
- **Goal:** 250-500+ articles total
- **Approach:** Mix of in-house, freelancers, AI
- **Focus:** Maintain quality at scale, update existing content
- **Time commitment:** 5-10 hours/week (strategy)

## Quality Control Systems

### The Content Quality Framework

**Four Pillars of Quality:**

1. **Accuracy** - Factually correct, up-to-date
2. **Completeness** - Covers topic thoroughly
3. **Clarity** - Easy to understand, well-organized
4. **Actionability** - Readers can apply what they learn

### Quality Checklist (Per Article)

```markdown
## Pre-Publish Checklist

### Content Quality
- [ ] Article answers the search intent
- [ ] All facts and statistics verified
- [ ] No grammatical or spelling errors
- [ ] Readable at 8th-10th grade level
- [ ] Includes original insights or experience

### SEO
- [ ] Target keyword in title, H1, first paragraph
- [ ] Secondary keywords used naturally
- [ ] Meta title and description optimized
- [ ] Internal links added (3-10)
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources (2-5)
- [ ] Image alt text completed

### User Experience
- [ ] Compelling introduction
- [ ] Clear headings and subheadings
- [ ] Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- [ ] Bullet points and lists where appropriate
- [ ] Relevant images or screenshots
- [ ] Table of contents (for long articles)
- [ ] FAQ section (if applicable)

### Monetization
- [ ] Affiliate links added (if relevant)
- [ ] Disclosure present (if affiliate links)
- [ ] Call-to-action included
- [ ] Email signup opportunity (if appropriate)

### Technical
- [ ] URL slug optimized
- [ ] Schema markup added
- [ ] Images compressed and WebP format
- [ ] Page speed acceptable
- [ ] Mobile responsive
```

- Native or fluent English

## Content Updates & Maintenance

### Why Updates Matter

Google's **Freshness Algorithm** favors:
- Recently updated content
- Content that stays current
- Sites that maintain their content

**Impact:**
- Updated articles can regain rankings
- Freshness signal for competitive keywords
- Better user experience (accurate info)

### Update Schedule

| Content Age | Update Frequency | Priority |
|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| **0-6 months** | As needed | Low |
| **6-12 months** | Review quarterly | Medium |
| **12-24 months** | Review every 3-4 months | High |
| **24+ months** | Review every 2-3 months | High |

### What to Update

**Every Update Should Include:**
- [ ] Check all statistics and data
- [ ] Update year references (2024 → 2025)
- [ ] Verify affiliate links still work
- [ ] Check for new competitors/products
- [ ] Add new information or developments
- [ ] Refresh images/screenshots
- [ ] Update "last updated" date

**Optional Enhancements:**
- Add new sections based on search queries
- Improve internal linking
- Add video or visual content
- Expand word count if competitors have more

### Content Audit Process

**Quarterly Audit Checklist:**

1. **Traffic Analysis**
   - Identify top 20% of articles (by traffic)
   - Identify bottom 20% (underperformers)
   - Note any significant drops or gains

2. **Ranking Analysis**
   - Check current positions for target keywords
   - Identify articles on page 2 (opportunity for improvement)
   - Note any lost rankings

3. **Content Quality Review**
   - Spot-check 10-20 articles for accuracy
   - Verify affiliate links and recommendations
   - Check for outdated information

4. **Action Items**
   - Update top performers (protect rankings)
   - Improve page 2 articles (push to page 1)
   - Consider consolidating or deleting thin content
   - Plan new content based on gaps

## Scaling Content Production

### When to Hire Help

**Signs You're Ready to Scale:**
- Consistently hitting your personal capacity
- Revenue can support hiring
- Systems and processes documented
- Quality standards clearly defined

**First Hires (in order):**
1. **Editor** - Maintain quality as you scale
2. **Writer(s)** - Increase output
3. **VA** - Handle administrative tasks (uploading, formatting)
4. **SEO Specialist** - Optimize strategy and execution

### Outsourcing Options

| Option | Cost | Quality Control | Scalability |
|--------|------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Freelance Writers** | $50-300/article | Medium | Medium |
| **Content Agencies** | $100-500/article | Variable | High |
| **AI + Editor** | $20-50/article | High | High |
| **In-House Team** | $40-80K/year | High | Low-Medium |

### Building a Content Team

**Writer Onboarding:**
1. Provide style guide
2. Share example articles (best performers)
3. Explain target audience
4. Review SEO requirements
5. Start with paid test assignment
6. Provide feedback on first 3-5 articles
7. Regular check-ins and quality reviews

**Editor Onboarding:**
1. Explain quality standards
2. Provide editing checklist
3. Review E-E-A-T requirements
4. Share voice and tone guidelines
5. Start with supervised editing
6. Calibrate on first 5-10 articles

### Quality at Scale

**Systems to Maintain Quality:**

1. **Standardized Briefs**
   - Every article starts with same brief template
   - Clear requirements and expectations
   - Examples of good vs. bad

2. **Editorial Guidelines**
   - Documented style guide
   - Voice and tone examples
   - Grammar and formatting rules

3. **Quality Checkpoints**
   - Editor review before publishing
   - Random spot-checks by founder
   - Reader feedback monitoring

4. **Performance Tracking**
   - Track rankings by writer
   - Monitor engagement metrics
   - Identify top performers
