## Phase 2: Kickoff Call (Days 4-10)

### Kickoff Call Agenda (90 minutes)

**1. Introductions (10 min)**
- Meet the team
- Roles & responsibilities
- Communication preferences

**2. Business Deep Dive (20 min)**
- Review questionnaire responses
- Clarify business model
- Understand product details
- Discuss customer insights

**3. Goals & Expectations (15 min)**
- Review stated goals
- Set realistic expectations
- Discuss potential challenges
- Align on success metrics

**4. Current State Review (15 min)**
- Website audit (if exists)
- Ad account review (if applicable)
- Email setup review
- Analytics check

**5. Strategy Overview (20 min)**
- Initial recommendations
- Proposed approach
- Timeline & milestones
- Quick wins identification

**6. Next Steps & Q&A (10 min)**
- Action items
- Timeline confirmation
- Address any questions
- Schedule next check-in

### Kickoff Call Prep Checklist

**Internal Team:**
- [ ] Review completed questionnaire
- [ ] Audit website (if exists)
- [ ] Review any existing ad accounts
- [ ] Prepare initial recommendations
- [ ] Set up shared project folder
- [ ] Prepare agenda document

**Client:**
- [ ] Complete onboarding questionnaire
- [ ] Grant necessary access
- [ ] Prepare product samples (if applicable)
- [ ] Gather brand assets
- [ ] Identify key stakeholders for call
- [ ] Prepare questions

## Client Communication Guidelines

### Communication Channels

**Slack (Day-to-Day):**
- Quick questions
- Updates & approvals
- Casual check-ins
- Response time: Within 4 business hours

**Email (Formal):**
- Proposals & contracts
- Official reports
- Strategic recommendations
- Response time: Within 24 hours

**Calls (Strategy & Reviews):**
- Weekly check-ins (30-60 min)
- Monthly reviews (60-90 min)
- Quarterly strategy (90 min)
- As-needed escalation

### Escalation Path

```
Level 1: Account Manager
         (day-to-day, routine questions)
              ↓
Level 2: Strategy Lead
         (strategic decisions, issues)
              ↓
Level 3: Agency Owner
         (major issues, contract matters)
```

### Response Time SLA

| Issue Type | Response Time | Resolution Time |
|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Critical** (site down, ads paused) | 1 hour | 4 hours |
| **Urgent** (campaign issue) | 4 hours | 24 hours |
| **Standard** (questions, requests) | 24 hours | 3-5 days |
| **Strategic** (new initiatives) | 48 hours | 1-2 weeks |

## Red Flags During Onboarding

### Watch Out For:

**Access Delays:**
- Client takes >1 week to grant access
- Multiple follow-ups needed
- Wrong access levels granted

**Communication Issues:**
- Slow to respond to questions
- Unclear decision-making
- Too many stakeholders

**Unrealistic Expectations:**
- Expecting immediate results
- Unwilling to invest in ad spend
- Wanting guarantees

**Scope Creep:**
- Requests outside agreed scope
- "While you're at it..." requests
- Unclear on what's included

### How to Address:

**For Access Delays:**
```
"Hey [Name], just checking in on the access to [Platform]. 
We need this to stay on schedule for [milestone]. Is there 
anything blocking this that we can help with?"
```

**For Scope Creep:**
```
"Happy to help with [request]! Just to note, this falls 
outside our agreed scope. We can either:
1. Add it as a separate project ($X)
2. Include it in next month's priorities
3. I can show you how to do it yourself

What works best for you?"
```

**For Unrealistic Expectations:**
```
"I want to make sure we're aligned on expectations. Based 
on [data/experience], here's what's realistic:
- Month 1: [X]
- Month 2: [Y]
- Month 3: [Z]

We'll push for more, but I want to be transparent about 
what's achievable."
```
