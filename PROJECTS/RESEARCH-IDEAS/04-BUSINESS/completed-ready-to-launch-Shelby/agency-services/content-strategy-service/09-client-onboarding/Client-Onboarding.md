## 3. Stakeholder Interview Guide

### 3.1 Executive Interview (CEO/Founder)

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes

**Questions:**
```
1. What's the company vision for the next 3-5 years?

2. What role does marketing play in achieving that vision?

3. What does success look like for content marketing?

4. What concerns do you have about content marketing?

5. How do you want the brand to be perceived?

6. What makes this company unique?

7. What's one thing competitors can't replicate?

8. How involved do you want to be in content?

9. What's your biggest fear about this project?

10. What would make this engagement a home run?
```

### 3.2 Marketing Manager Interview

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes

**Questions:**
```
1. What's your current marketing strategy?

2. Where does content fit in that strategy?

3. What's working well right now?

4. What's frustrating about current efforts?

5. What resources do you have for content?

6. What does your content workflow look like today?

7. What tools are you using?

8. How do you measure success?

9. What support do you need from us?

10. What would make your job easier?
```

### 3.3 Sales Manager Interview

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes

**Questions:**
```
1. What questions do prospects ask most?

2. What objections do you hear repeatedly?

3. What content would help you close more deals?

4. What's the typical buyer journey?

5. Who are you competing against most?

6. Why do customers choose you?

7. Why do you lose deals?

8. What content do prospects engage with?

9. How can content make your job easier?

10. What do you wish marketing understood about sales?
```

### 3.4 Customer Success Interview

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes

**Questions:**
```
1. What questions do customers have after buying?

2. What causes confusion or frustration?

3. What makes customers successful?

4. What do your best customers have in common?

5. What content would help customers succeed?

6. What are common reasons for churn?

7. How can content improve retention?

8. What do customers love most about the product?

9. What features are underutilized?

10. How can content reduce support tickets?
```

## 4. Goal Setting Framework

### 4.1 Goal Categories

| Category | Examples | Measurement |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Awareness** | Traffic, reach, impressions | Google Analytics, Social analytics |
| **Engagement** | Time on page, shares, comments | Analytics, Social platforms |
| **Lead Generation** | Email signups, downloads, demos | CRM, Marketing automation |
| **Sales** | Opportunities, revenue | CRM, Revenue attribution |
| **Retention** | Customer engagement, renewal | Customer analytics |
| **Authority** | Backlinks, mentions, speaking | SEO tools, Media monitoring |

### 4.2 SMART Goal Template

```markdown
## Goal: [Name]

### Specific
What exactly do we want to accomplish?


### Measurable
How will we measure success?
- Baseline: 
- Target: 
- Metric: 

### Achievable
Is this realistic given resources and timeline?
- Resources available: 
- Potential obstacles: 
- Mitigation: 

### Relevant
How does this align with business goals?


### Time-Bound
What's the deadline?
- Start Date: 
- Target Date: 
- Check-in Dates: 
```

### 4.3 Goal Hierarchy

```
BUSINESS GOALS (Annual)
    │
    ├──→ MARKETING GOALS (Quarterly)
    │       │
    │       └──→ CONTENT GOALS (Monthly)
    │               │
    │               └──→ TACTICS (Weekly)
    │
    └──→ SALES GOALS (Quarterly)
```

**Example:**
```
BUSINESS: $5M revenue (Annual)
    │
    ├──→ MARKETING: 500 MQLs (Q1)
    │       │
    │       └──→ CONTENT: 100 MQLs from blog (Q1)
    │               │
    │               └──→ TACTICS: 12 blog posts, 4 lead magnets
    │
    └──→ SALES: 50 new customers (Q1)
```
