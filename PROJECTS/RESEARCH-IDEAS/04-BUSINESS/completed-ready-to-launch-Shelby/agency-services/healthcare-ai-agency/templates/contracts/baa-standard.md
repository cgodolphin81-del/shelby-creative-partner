# BUSINESS ASSOCIATE AGREEMENT

**This Business Associate Agreement** ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date"), by and between:

**Covered Entity:** [COVERED ENTITY NAME], with principal place of business at [ADDRESS] ("Covered Entity")

## RECITALS

**WHEREAS,** Covered Entity is a "covered entity" as defined in the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA"), 42 U.S.C. § 1320d, and the regulations promulgated thereunder;

**WHEREAS,** Business Associate performs certain functions, activities, or services for or on behalf of Covered Entity that involve the use or disclosure of "protected health information" ("PHI") as defined in HIPAA;

**WHEREAS,** the Parties desire to ensure that Business Associate will appropriately safeguard PHI in accordance with HIPAA requirements;

**NOW, THEREFORE,** in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 Defined Terms
Capitalized terms used but not otherwise defined in this Agreement shall have the same meaning as such terms in HIPAA, the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 160 and Subparts A and E of Part 164), the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 160 and Subparts A and C of Part 164), and the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 160 and Subparts A and D of Part 164) (collectively, "HIPAA Rules").

### 1.2 Key Definitions
- **"Breach"** means the unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted by the HIPAA Privacy Rule which compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.
- **"Electronic PHI" or "ePHI"** means PHI that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted electronically.
- **"HIPAA"** means the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, Pub. L. No. 104-191, and all regulations promulgated thereunder.
- **"PHI"** means protected health information as defined in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.
- **"Secretary"** means the Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services or his or her designee.
- **"Subcontractor"** means a person or entity to whom Business Associate delegates a function, activity, or service, other than in the capacity of a member of the workforce of Business Associate.

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

### 2.1 Permitted Uses and Disclosures
Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this Agreement or as Required by Law. Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only:

(a) To perform the functions, activities, or services specified in the underlying agreement between the Parties ("Underlying Agreement");

(b) For the proper management and administration of Business Associate, provided that disclosures are Required by Law or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the person to whom the PHI is disclosed that it will remain confidential and be used or further disclosed only as Required by Law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed;

(c) To provide Data Aggregation services to Covered Entity as permitted by 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e)(2)(i)(B);

(d) For the following specific purposes: [LIST ANY ADDITIONAL PERMITTED USES];

(e) As necessary for Business Associate to engage legal counsel, auditors, or other advisors to ensure compliance with HIPAA or this Agreement, provided such parties are bound by confidentiality obligations.

### 2.2 Safeguards
Business Associate shall use reasonable safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of PHI other than as provided for by this Agreement. Such safeguards shall include, but not be limited to:

(a) **Administrative Safeguards:**
   - Designation of a privacy and security officer
   - Workforce training on HIPAA requirements
   - Implementation of policies and procedures
   - Regular risk assessments
   - Incident response procedures

(b) **Physical Safeguards:**
   - Facility access controls
   - Workstation security
   - Device and media controls
   - Environmental controls

(c) **Technical Safeguards:**
   - Access controls (unique user IDs, MFA)
   - Audit controls and activity monitoring
   - Integrity controls (checksums, version control)
   - Transmission security (encryption in transit and at rest)

### 2.3 Minimum Necessary
Business Associate shall request, use, and disclose only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose of the use, disclosure, or request, in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.502(b) and § 164.514(d).

### 2.4 Reporting of Uses and Disclosures
Business Associate shall document and disclose to Covered Entity information about uses and disclosures of PHI as requested by Covered Entity, to the extent such information is in the possession of Business Associate.

### 2.5 Compliance with HIPAA Security Rule
With respect to ePHI, Business Associate shall:

(a) Comply with the applicable requirements of the HIPAA Security Rule (45 C.F.R. Part 160 and Subparts A and C of Part 164);

(b) Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI;

(c) Ensure that any agent, including a subcontractor, to whom it provides ePHI agrees to implement reasonable and appropriate safeguards;

(d) Report to Covered Entity any security incident of which it becomes aware.

### 2.6 Subcontractors
Business Associate shall:

(a) Ensure that any Subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of Business Associate agrees in writing to comply with the applicable requirements of this Agreement;

(b) Remain liable for any breach of this Agreement by a Subcontractor;

(c) Provide Covered Entity with a list of Subcontractors upon request;

(d) Obtain Covered Entity's written approval before engaging any new Subcontractor that will have access to PHI.

### 2.7 Access to PHI
Business Associate shall make PHI available to Covered Entity (or as directed by Covered Entity to an individual) in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.524, within ten (10) business days of request, in the format requested if readily producible.

### 2.8 Amendment of PHI
Business Associate shall make PHI available for amendment and incorporate any amendments to PHI as directed by Covered Entity in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.526, within ten (10) business days of request.

### 2.9 Accounting of Disclosures
Business Associate shall document and disclose to Covered Entity information required for an accounting of disclosures as set forth in 45 C.F.R. § 164.528, within ten (10) business days of request.

### 2.10 Access to Books and Records
Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI received from Covered Entity, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, available to the Secretary of HHS or Covered Entity's designated auditor for purposes of determining compliance with HIPAA and this Agreement, upon reasonable notice and during normal business hours.

### 2.11 Compliance with Laws
Business Associate represents and warrants that it shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations relating to the privacy and security of PHI, including but not limited to HIPAA, HITECH, and applicable state privacy laws.

Covered Entity shall not request or require Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would not be permissible under the HIPAA Privacy Rule if done by Covered Entity.

### 3.2 Notice of Changes
Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any changes in law or in Covered Entity's practices or operations with respect to uses and disclosures of PHI that may affect Business Associate's permitted or required uses and disclosures.

### 3.3 Notice of Privacy Practices

### 3.4 Authorization
Covered Entity shall obtain any necessary authorizations from individuals for uses or disclosures of PHI by Business Associate that require individual authorization under the HIPAA Privacy Rule.

### 3.5 Breach Notification
Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any Breach of Unsecured PHI of which Covered Entity becomes aware.

## 4. BREACH NOTIFICATION

### 4.1 Discovery of Breach
Upon discovery of a Breach of Unsecured PHI, Business Associate shall:

(a) **Notify Covered Entity:** Notify Covered Entity without unreasonable delay and in no case later than five (5) business days after discovery of the Breach;

(b) **Content of Notice:** The notice shall include, to the extent possible:
   - Identification of each individual whose PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, or disclosed during the Breach;
   - Identification of each element of PHI that has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, or disclosed;
   - Date of the Breach and date of discovery;
   - Names and contact information of individuals affected (if available);
   - Brief description of what happened;
   - Steps Business Associate is taking to investigate, mitigate, and prevent future breaches;
   - Contact information for questions;

(c) **Cooperation:** Cooperate with Covered Entity in the investigation of the Breach;

(d) **Mitigation:** Mitigate, to the extent practicable, any harmful effect of the Breach.

### 4.2 Breach Risk Assessment
Business Associate shall perform and document a risk assessment to determine whether a Breach requires notification, using the four-factor test:

(a) Nature and extent of PHI involved;
(b) Unauthorized person who used or received PHI;
(c) Whether PHI was actually acquired or viewed;
(d) Extent to which risk has been mitigated.

### 4.3 Notification Costs
Business Associate shall be responsible for all costs associated with breach notification resulting from Business Associate's breach of this Agreement or negligence, including:

- Notification to individuals
- Notification to HHS
- Notification to media (if required)
- Credit monitoring services (if offered)
- Call center support
- Legal and public relations support

## 5. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 5.1 Term
This Agreement shall be effective as of the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until all PHI received from Covered Entity, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, is destroyed or returned to Covered Entity, or until terminated as provided in this Section.

### 5.2 Termination for Cause
Covered Entity may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Business Associate:

(a) Materially breaches any provision of this Agreement;
(b) Fails to cure a breach within thirty (30) days of notice;
(c) Files for bankruptcy or becomes insolvent;
(d) Engages in a pattern of violations of HIPAA or this Agreement.

### 5.3 Effect of Termination
Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason:

(a) **Return or Destruction:** Business Associate shall return or destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity. This provision shall apply to PHI that is in the possession of Subcontractors. Business Associate shall retain no copies of the PHI.

(b) **Timing:** Return or destruction shall be completed within thirty (30) days of termination.

(c) **Documentation:** Business Associate shall provide written certification of return or destruction.

(d) **Exception:** If return or destruction is not feasible, Business Associate shall extend the protections of this Agreement to the PHI and limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make the return or destruction infeasible.

### 5.4 Survival
The rights and obligations of Business Associate under Section 5.3 (Effect of Termination) and Section 6 (General Provisions) shall survive termination of this Agreement.

## 7. SPECIFIC PROVISIONS FOR AI SERVICES

### 7.1 AI Model Training
Business Associate shall not use PHI received from Covered Entity to train artificial intelligence or machine learning models except:
(a) As expressly authorized in writing by Covered Entity;
(b) With PHI that has been de-identified in accordance with 45 C.F.R. § 164.514(b);
(c) As part of providing services to Covered Entity under this Agreement.

### 7.2 Algorithm Transparency
Upon request, Business Associate shall provide Covered Entity with:
(a) Description of AI/ML algorithms used in processing PHI
(b) Information about model training data sources
(c) Performance metrics and validation results
(d) Known limitations and biases

### 7.3 Human Oversight
Business Associate shall maintain appropriate human oversight of AI systems processing PHI, including:
(a) Regular review of AI outputs
(b) Escalation procedures for uncertain results
(c) Ability to override AI decisions
(d) Documentation of human review

### 7.4 Model Updates
Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity before deploying significant updates to AI models that process Covered Entity's PHI, including:
(a) Changes to model architecture
(b) Changes to training data
(c) Changes that affect output or performance
(d) Validation results for updated models
