## 🏢 Organizational Structure

### Agency Growth Stages

#### Stage 1: Solo Founder (0-5 clients)
```
Founder
├── Community Management
├── Moderation (contractors)
├── Sales
└── Operations
```

**Team Size:** 1-3 (including contractors)  
**Revenue:** $10K-30K/mo

---

#### Stage 2: Small Team (5-15 clients)
```
Founder/CEO
├── Head of Community
│   ├── Community Manager 1
│   └── Community Manager 2
├── Head of Moderation

│   └── Moderators (4-8)
```

## 🎯 Hiring Guides

### Community Manager Hiring

#### Job Description Template

```
JOB TITLE: Community Manager

LOCATION: Remote

COMPENSATION: $50,000-70,000 + bonus + benefits

ABOUT US:
We're a fast-growing Discord community management 
agency helping SaaS companies, creators, and Web3 
projects build thriving communities. We manage 20+ 
communities with over 500,000 combined members.

THE ROLE:
As a Community Manager, you'll be the face of our 
agency to our clients. You'll manage 8-12 community 
accounts, driving engagement, growth, and satisfaction.

RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Manage day-to-day community operations for clients
- Develop and execute engagement strategies
- Coordinate and host community events
- Create monthly performance reports
- Communicate with clients weekly
- Identify growth opportunities
- Escalate issues appropriately

REQUIREMENTS:
- 2+ years community management experience
- Expert-level Discord knowledge
- Excellent written communication
- Experience with community tools (bots, analytics)
- Self-starter, comfortable working remotely
- Available for occasional evening/weekend events

BONUS POINTS:
- Experience in SaaS, Web3, or gaming
- Event hosting experience
- Basic design skills (Canva)
- Data analysis experience

PERKS:
- Fully remote
- Flexible hours
- Health insurance
- 401(k) matching
- Professional development budget
- Work with exciting brands

TO APPLY:
Send resume and a brief note about your favorite 
community (and why) to careers@company.com
```

---

#### Interview Questions

**Screening Call (15 min):**
1. Tell me about your background in community management
2. What's your experience with Discord specifically?
3. What are you looking for in your next role?
4. Salary expectations?

**First Interview (45 min):**
1. Walk me through how you'd handle a toxic community member
2. Describe a successful community initiative you led
3. How do you measure community health?
4. What's your approach to handling multiple clients?
5. Scenario: A client wants something that goes against best practices. How do you handle it?

**Second Interview (60 min):**
1. Present a community audit exercise (give them a real server to review)
2. Role-play a client check-in call
3. Meet the team (culture fit)
4. Q&A about the role

**Practical Assessment:**
```
TASK: Community Audit (take-home, 2-3 hours)

We're giving you access to a sample Discord server. 
Please provide:

1. 3 strengths you observed
2. 3 areas for improvement
3. A 30-day engagement plan
4. One creative event idea

Submit as a Google Doc or slide deck.
```

## 📋 SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures)

### Daily Operations

#### Morning Handoff (Shift Start)
```
1. Review handoff notes from previous shift
2. Check #mod-logs for overnight activity
3. Review #reports for pending items
4. Check client Slack/Discord for urgent messages
5. Review shift schedule for any changes
6. Log in to all assigned client servers
7. Post "Good morning" in team channel
```

#### Client Check-In (Daily)
```
1. Review each client server (5-10 min each)
2. Check engagement metrics
3. Review any escalations
4. Post updates in client channel
5. Respond to any client messages
6. Log activities in CRM
```

#### End of Shift Handoff
```
1. Update shift log with:
   - Key incidents
   - Escalations pending
   - Member issues to watch
   - Any client requests
2. Post summary in team channel
3. Ensure all tickets have next steps
4. Tag relevant team members for follow-ups
```

## 🌍 Remote Work Best Practices

### Communication

**Synchronous:**
- Daily standup (15 min, team)
- Weekly 1:1s (30 min, manager)
- Client calls (as needed)
- Team meetings (weekly, 60 min)

**Asynchronous:**
- Slack for quick questions
- Email for formal communication
- Loom for video updates
- Notion/Docs for documentation

**Response Time Expectations:**
- Slack: Within 2 hours (business hours)
- Email: Within 24 hours
- Client urgent: Within 30 minutes
- Internal urgent: Within 1 hour
