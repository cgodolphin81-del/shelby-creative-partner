# Course Topic Validator
## How to Pick Topics People Actually Pay For

---

## The Core Principle

**People don't buy information. They buy transformation.**

Your course topic must solve a specific problem for a specific person who has already shown they'll spend money to solve it.

---

## The 5-Point Validation Framework

### 1. 💰 PROOF OF PAYMENT
*Has anyone paid to solve this problem before?*

**Validation Signals:**
- [ ] Existing courses on Udemy/Coursera with 1,000+ students
- [ ] Books on Amazon with 100+ reviews in this category
- [ ] Active Facebook groups with 5,000+ members
- [ ] Subreddits with 10,000+ subscribers asking questions
- [ ] People hiring coaches/consultants for this problem
- [ ] Software/tools exist specifically for this niche

**Red Flags:**
- ❌ "This is so new, no one has done it"
- ❌ Only free content exists
- ❌ People say "I'd pay" but haven't

**Action:** Search Udemy, Amazon, and Google Ads. If ads exist, money exists.

---

### 2. 🎯 SPECIFIC AUDIENCE
*Can you name exactly who this is for?*

**Good Examples:**
- "Freelance designers who want to raise rates from $50 to $150/hour"
- "New managers at tech companies struggling with difficult conversations"
- "Women over 40 returning to the workforce after a career break"

**Bad Examples:**
- "Anyone who wants to learn business"
- "People interested in health"
- "Entrepreneurs" (too broad)

**Validation Test:** Can you describe your ideal student in 3 sentences including their job, income, and specific frustration?

---

### 3. 🔥 URGENT PAIN
*Is this a "must solve" or "nice to solve"?*

**Must-Solve Indicators:**
- Problem costs them money NOW
- Problem affects their job/career
- Problem causes daily stress/anxiety
- Problem blocks a specific goal with deadline
- Problem affects their identity/status

**Nice-to-Solve Indicators:**
- "Someday I should learn this"
- "Would be cool to know"
- No immediate consequence for inaction

**The Pain Scale:** Rate 1-10. If average is below 7, pivot.

---

### 4. 📈 MARKET SIZE
*Are there enough buyers to make this worthwhile?*

**Quick Math:**
```
Target Revenue ÷ Course Price = Students Needed

Example:
$100,000 ÷ $500 = 200 students
$100,000 ÷ $2,000 = 50 students
```

**Market Indicators:**
- [ ] Google Trends shows stable or growing interest
- [ ] Industry reports show market growth
- [ ] Job postings in this skill are increasing
- [ ] Conference events exist for this topic
- [ ] Industry publications/newsletters exist

**Minimum Viable Market:** You need access to at least 10,000 potential students to realistically enroll 100-500.

---

### 5. ✨ YOUR UNIQUE ANGLE
*Why should they buy from YOU specifically?*

**Credibility Markers:**
- Personal results (you achieved what you're teaching)
- Client results (you've helped others achieve it)
- Unique methodology (your framework/system)
- Unique perspective (your background/story)
- Speed advantage (you can get them results faster)

**Positioning Formula:**
```
"I help [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE] achieve [SPECIFIC RESULT] 
in [TIMEFRAME] without [COMMON OBSTACLE]"

Example:
"I help freelance designers raise their rates from 
$50 to $150/hour in 90 days without losing clients"
```

## Pre-Sale Validation (The Ultimate Test)

Before creating ANY content, test demand:

### The $100 Test
1. Create a simple landing page (Carrd, Gumroad, ConvertKit)
2. Describe the transformation (not the curriculum)
3. Set price at 50% of intended launch price
4. Drive 100 targeted visitors (ads, posts, outreach)
5. **Goal:** 3-5 pre-sales before building

### The Beta Test
1. Recruit 10-20 beta students at 70% discount
2. Deliver live via Zoom (no pre-recorded content)
3. Record everything
4. Use feedback to refine before full launch

### The Waitlist Test
1. Create landing page with email capture
2. Drive traffic for 2 weeks
3. **Goal:** 100+ emails = validated interest
4. Survey waitlist about specific needs

## Topic Ideas That Always Sell

### Career & Business
- Getting promoted / raising rates
- Career transitions (especially into tech)
- Starting specific business types (agency, consulting, e-commerce)
- Management/leadership skills
- Sales and negotiation

### Health & Wellness
- Weight loss for specific demographics
- Stress/anxiety management
- Sleep optimization
- Fitness for busy professionals
- Nutrition for specific conditions

### Relationships
- Dating for specific demographics
- Marriage counseling/repair
- Parenting specific challenges
- Social skills/confidence

### Money
- Getting out of debt
- Investing for beginners
- Real estate (specific strategies)
- Side income streams
- Tax optimization

### Skills & Hobbies
- Language learning (specific goals)
- Musical instruments (adult beginners)
- Creative skills with income potential
- Technical skills (coding, design, marketing)

---

## Red Flags: Topics to Avoid

❌ **Oversaturated without differentiation**
- "Make money online"
- "Lose weight"
- "Learn guitar"

❌ **No proven buyers**
- Highly theoretical/academic topics
- Topics only students would buy (no budget)
- "Passion projects" without market

❌ **Against platform terms**
- Get-rich-quick schemes
- Medical advice (without credentials)
- Financial advice (without credentials)
- Dating advice that's manipulative

❌ **Trend-dependent**
- Platform-specific hacks (algorithm changes)
- Crypto trading (extremely volatile)
- News-dependent topics

## Your Validation Action Plan

### Week 1: Market Research
- [ ] Identify 3 potential topics
- [ ] Score each using the 5-point framework
- [ ] Research competitors (courses, books, coaches)
- [ ] Join 3 Facebook groups, read 50 posts each
- [ ] Search Reddit for pain point language

### Week 2: Audience Discovery
- [ ] Interview 5-10 potential students
- [ ] Ask: "What's your biggest frustration with X?"
- [ ] Ask: "What have you tried? What worked/didn't?"
- [ ] Ask: "What would a solution be worth to you?"
- [ ] Document exact language they use

### Week 3: Pre-Sale Test
- [ ] Create simple landing page
- [ ] Write sales copy using their language
- [ ] Set up payment (Stripe, Gumroad, PayPal)
- [ ] Drive 100 visitors (organic + small ad spend)
- [ ] Measure conversion rate

### Week 4: Decision
- [ ] Review validation data
- [ ] Choose topic or pivot
- [ ] If validated: outline curriculum
- [ ] If not validated: return to Week 1

## Validation Interview Script

**Opening:** "I'm researching [topic] and would love 15 minutes to understand your experience. I'm not selling anything."

**Questions:**
1. "Tell me about your current situation with [topic]"
2. "What's the biggest frustration you're facing?"
3. "How long has this been a problem?"
4. "What have you tried to solve it?"
5. "What was the result?"
6. "If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?"
7. "What would solving this be worth to you financially?"
8. "Why haven't you solved this yet?"

**Listen for:**
- Emotional language (frustrated, stuck, overwhelmed)
- Money mentions (cost them, lost revenue, expensive mistake)
- Time mentions (wasting time, been months/years)
- Failed solutions (tried X, Y, Z, nothing worked)

---

## The "Money Question"

The single best validation question:

**"Have you ever paid anyone to help you with this?"**

- If YES: They're a proven buyer. Ask who, how much, results.
- If NO but "I would": They're not a buyer yet. Dig deeper.
- If NO and "It's not that important": Not urgent enough. Pivot.

---

## Final Checklist Before You Build

- [ ] Proof of payment exists (competitors making money)
- [ ] Specific audience identified (can describe in detail)
- [ ] Pain is urgent (7+/10 on pain scale)
- [ ] Market is large enough (10K+ potential students)
- [ ] You have unique angle/credibility
- [ ] You've interviewed 5+ potential students
- [ ] You have pre-sales OR waitlist of 100+
- [ ] You can articulate transformation in one sentence

**If all boxes checked: BUILD IT.**
