# Recording Setup Guide
## Equipment, Software & Editing Workflow for All Budgets

## The Truth About Quality

**Good enough beats perfect.** Your first course won't be your last. Start recording, improve as you go.

That said, bad audio will kill your course. Viewers forgive mediocre video. They don't forgive bad audio.

**Priority Order:**
1. 🎤 Audio (MOST IMPORTANT)
2. 💡 Lighting
3. 📹 Video
4. 🎨 Background

## Part 2: Recording Space Setup

### The Ideal Space

**What you need:**
- Quiet (no traffic, neighbors, HVAC noise)
- Controllable lighting (windows you can cover)
- Enough depth (you 3-5 feet from background)
- Consistent (same setup every time)

### Room Treatment (Budget-Friendly)

**Problem:** Echo and reverb make you sound amateur.

**Solutions:**

| Solution | Cost | Effectiveness |
|----------|------|---------------|
| Record in closet (clothes absorb sound) | $0 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| Hang blankets on walls | $20-40 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| Foam panels (Amazon) | $50-100 | ⭐⭐⭐ |
| Professional acoustic panels | $200-500 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| Move mattress against wall | $0 (if you have one) | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |

**DIY Acoustic Panel:**
1. Buy rigid fiberglass insulation (Owens Corning 703)
2. Wrap in breathable fabric (burlap, speaker cloth)
3. Mount on walls at reflection points
4. Cost: ~$30 per panel vs. $100+ for pre-made

### Background Setup

**Good Backgrounds:**
- Clean, organized bookshelf
- Plants (real or fake)
- Neutral wall with subtle decor
- Intentional backdrop (paper, fabric)
- Your actual workspace (if tidy)

**Bad Backgrounds:**
- Cluttered rooms
- Personal photos/documents (privacy)
- Busy patterns (distracting)
- Windows behind you (silhouette effect)
- Unmade beds, laundry, etc.

**Pro Tip:** Depth of field is your friend. Position yourself 3-5 feet from your background and use a wider aperture to blur it slightly.

## Part 3: Lighting Setup

### The 3-Point Lighting System

```
        KEY LIGHT
           ●
            \
             \
              \
    SUBJECT ---●--- FILL LIGHT
              /
             /
            /
        BACK LIGHT
           ●
```

**Key Light:** Main light source, brightest, 45° to subject
**Fill Light:** Softer, opposite side, fills shadows
**Back Light:** Behind subject, separates from background

### Budget Lighting Setup ($50-100)

**Option 1: Ring Light**
- Neewer 18" Ring Light: $60-80
- Position: Directly in front, slightly above eye level
- Pros: Even light, catchlight in eyes
- Cons: Can look flat, ring reflection in glasses

**Option 2: Softbox Kit**
- Neewer 2-pack softbox kit: $80-100
- Position: One as key (45°), one as fill (opposite)
- Pros: Softer, more natural light
- Cons: Takes more space

### Pro Lighting Setup ($300-800)

**Key Light:** Aputure 120d II + Light Dome ($400)
**Fill Light:** Aputure MC RGB ($150)
**Back Light:** Generic LED panel ($50)

**Positioning:**
- Key: 45° to subject, slightly above eye level
- Fill: Opposite side, lower intensity (50% of key)
- Back: Behind and above, pointing at subject's head/shoulders

### Natural Light Setup (Free!)

**If you have a window:**
1. Face the window (don't have it behind you)
2. Use sheer curtains to diffuse harsh light
3. Record during consistent light times (morning/afternoon)
4. Use a reflector (white foam board) to fill shadows

**Avoid:**
- Midday sun (too harsh)
- Changing light conditions (clouds moving)
- Mixed color temperatures (window + indoor lights)

## Part 5: Recording Workflow

### Pre-Recording Checklist

- [ ] Room is quiet (turn off AC, fans, notify household)
- [ ] Phone on Do Not Disturb
- [ ] Computer notifications disabled
- [ ] Microphone positioned correctly (6-8 inches from mouth)
- [ ] Camera at eye level (not looking up or down)
- [ ] Lighting tested (no harsh shadows)
- [ ] Background is tidy
- [ ] Water nearby (stay hydrated)
- [ ] Script/outline ready
- [ ] Test recording done (check audio/video levels)

### Recording Best Practices

**Before You Start:**
1. Do a 30-second test recording
2. Check audio levels (aim for -12 to -6 dB, never hitting 0)
3. Check video framing (headroom, eye level)
4. Check focus (autofocus can hunt during recording)

**During Recording:**
1. Speak clearly and slightly slower than normal
2. Pause between thoughts (easier to edit)
3. If you mess up, pause, then restart that sentence
4. Keep energy up (slightly higher than conversation)
5. Look at the lens, not yourself on screen

**After Recording:**
1. Save file immediately with clear name
2. Back up to second location
3. Note any pickups needed
4. Log the recording (date, module, lesson, file name)

### File Naming Convention

```
COURSENAME_MODULE#_LESSON#_TAKE#_DATE.mp4

Example:
EMAILMASTERCLASS_M03_L02_TAKE1_20250115.mp4
```

**Why this matters:** You'll record hundreds of files. Organization prevents chaos.

## Part 6: Editing Workflow

### The Editing Process

**Phase 1: Assembly (1-2 hours per hour of footage)**
1. Import all footage
2. Create sequence/timeline
3. Place clips in order
4. Remove obvious mistakes and long pauses

**Phase 2: Rough Cut (2-3 hours per hour of footage)**
1. Tighten pacing (remove dead air)
2. Add B-roll or screen shares where needed
3. Insert graphics/titles
4. Check audio levels

**Phase 3: Fine Cut (1-2 hours per hour of footage)**
1. Color correction
2. Audio cleanup (noise reduction, EQ, compression)
3. Add music (if using)
4. Add intro/outro

**Phase 4: Export & Review (30 min per video)**
1. Export in platform-appropriate format
2. Watch entire video
3. Note any fixes needed
4. Re-export if necessary

| **DaVinci Resolve** | Free (Studio: $300) | Mac/PC | Intermediate-Pro |

**Recommendation:** Start with DaVinci Resolve (free). It's professional-grade and has a learning curve, but countless tutorials exist.

### Audio Editing Basics

**Essential Audio Processing Chain:**

1. **Noise Reduction** (remove background hum/hiss)
   - Capture noise profile from silent section
   - Apply to entire track

2. **EQ** (shape the tone)
   - High-pass filter at 80-100 Hz (remove rumble)
   - Slight boost at 3-5 kHz (clarity)
   - Cut at 200-400 Hz if muddy

3. **Compression** (even out volume)
   - Ratio: 3:1 to 4:1
   - Threshold: -18 to -12 dB
   - Attack: 5-10 ms
   - Release: 50-100 ms

4. **Limiter** (prevent clipping)
   - Ceiling: -1 dB
   - Threshold: -6 to -3 dB

**Descript Alternative:** Edit audio by editing text. Revolutionary for spoken content. Automatically removes filler words, pauses, and mistakes.

### Graphics & Overlays

**What to Add:**
- Lower thirds (your name/title)
- Module/lesson titles
- Key points as text overlays
- Callouts for important info
- Progress indicators

**Tools:**
- Canva (free templates)
- Adobe After Effects (pro motion graphics)
- Built-in title tools in your editor

**Keep It Simple:** Don't over-design. Clean, readable, consistent.

## Part 7: Export Settings

### For Course Platforms

Most platforms (Teachable, Kajabi, Thinkific) handle uploads well. Use these settings:

**Video:**
- Resolution: 1920x1080 (1080p) or 3840x2160 (4K)
- Frame Rate: 30 fps (or match source)
- Codec: H.264
- Bitrate: 10-20 Mbps (1080p), 40-60 Mbps (4K)

**Audio:**
- Codec: AAC
- Bitrate: 192 kbps minimum, 320 kbps preferred
- Sample Rate: 48 kHz

**Container:** MP4

### For YouTube (if using as host)

Same as above, but YouTube recommends:
- Upload in highest quality available
- They'll compress, so give them the best source

### File Size Management

**Problem:** 4K files are HUGE.

**Solutions:**
1. Record in 4K, export in 1080p (future-proof source, web-friendly delivery)
2. Use Handbrake (free) to compress after editing
3. Store originals on external drive, upload compressed versions

## Part 8: Common Problems & Fixes

### Audio Issues

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Background noise | HVAC, traffic, neighbors | Record at quiet times, treat room |
| Echo/reverb | Hard surfaces, empty room | Add soft materials, record in closet |
| Plosives (popping P's) | Mic too close, no pop filter | Use pop filter, angle mic slightly |
| Inconsistent volume | Moving around, varying speech | Use compression, stay consistent distance |
| Hum/buzz | Electrical interference | Use balanced cables, check ground loops |

### Video Issues

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Too dark | Not enough light | Add more lights, face window |
| Too bright/harsh | Direct light, midday sun | Diffuse light, use softboxes |
| Out of focus | Autofocus hunting | Use manual focus, lock it |
| Shaky | Handheld, unstable tripod | Use tripod, stabilize |
| Wrong color | Mixed light sources | Match color temps, white balance |

### Software Issues

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| OBS not capturing audio | Check audio mixer, set correct device |
| Video stuttering | Close other apps, lower recording resolution |
| File too large | Lower bitrate, use H.265 codec |
| Sync issues | Record clap at start, sync in post |

## Part 9: Outsourcing Options

### When to Outsource

**Do it yourself when:**
- Starting out, limited budget
- You want full creative control
- You enjoy the process
- Building your personal brand

**Outsource when:**
- You value time over money
- Scaling to multiple courses
- Technical learning curve is too steep
- You want polished, professional results

### What to Outsource

| Task | Typical Cost | Where to Find |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Video Editing | $50-150/hour or $200-500/video | Upwork, Fiverr, VideoEditors.com |
| Audio Editing | $30-75/hour | Fiverr, SoundBetter, Upwork |
| Thumbnail Design | $10-50 each | Fiverr, Canva templates |
| Course Upload/Setup | $500-2,000/project | Upwork, course platform experts |
| Full Production | $5,000-20,000+ | Production companies, agencies |

### Managing Editors

**Provide:**
1. Raw footage (organized, labeled)
2. Edit notes (timestamps for cuts, additions)
3. Brand guidelines (fonts, colors, style)
4. Example videos (what you like)
5. Clear deadline

**Review Process:**
1. First cut review (note all changes)
2. Revision round 1
3. Final review
4. Approval and payment

## Quick Start: Your First Recording Session

### Today (2 hours)
1. Set up in quietest room available
2. Position phone/laptop camera at eye level
3. Face a window or set up lamp as key light
4. Use headphones with built-in mic or USB mic
5. Record a 5-minute test video

### This Week (5 hours)
1. Watch your test, note improvements
2. Adjust lighting, audio, framing
3. Record your first full lesson (10-15 min)
4. Edit in free software (DaVinci Resolve, iMovie)
5. Export and upload to test platform

### This Month (20 hours)
1. Record 3-5 lessons
2. Establish your workflow
3. Get feedback from 2-3 people
4. Refine based on feedback
5. Continue recording remaining content
