## Sponsorship Pricing Models

### Agency Commission Structure

#### Standard Commission

**Rate**: 25% of sponsorship value

**Applies To**:
- Most brand partnerships
- One-off campaigns
- Short-term contracts (1-3 months)

**Example**:
- Sponsorship deal: $10,000
- Agency commission: $2,500
- Streamer receives: $7,500

---

#### Volume Discount Commission

**Rate**: 20% of sponsorship value

**Applies To**:
- Monthly retainers (4+ months)
- Quarterly programs
- Annual partnerships
- Streamers in Elite package

**Example**:
- Sponsorship deal: $25,000
- Agency commission: $5,000
- Streamer receives: $20,000

---

#### Performance-Based Commission

**Structure**: Lower base + performance bonus

**Example**:
- Base commission: 15%
- Performance bonus: 5% if KPIs met
- Total potential: 20%

**KPIs for Bonus**:
- Minimum impression targets
- Engagement rate thresholds
- Conversion targets (if applicable)
- Brand satisfaction score (8/10+)

## Pricing Strategy Principles

### Value-Based Pricing

**When to Use Value-Based Pricing**:
- Streamer has unique audience demographics
- High engagement rates (above category average)
- Proven track record of conversions
- Exclusive category rights available
- Multi-platform reach

**Value-Based Pricing Formula**:
```
Base Rate + (Value Premium × Multiplier)

Example:
Base rate (100 avg viewers × $3): $300
Value premium (200% above average engagement): $600
Multiplier (premium positioning): 1.5x
Total: $300 + ($600 × 1.5) = $1,200
```

---

### Tiered Pricing Strategy

**Psychological Pricing**:
- Use odd numbers ($4,997 vs $5,000)
- Anchor with premium option
- Make middle tier most attractive
- Clearly differentiate tiers

**Example**:

| Package | Price | Value Perception |
|---------|-------|------------------|
| Starter | $2,000 | Entry-level, basic |
| Growth | $5,000 | **Best Value** (highlighted) |
| Elite | $10,000 | Premium, comprehensive |

**Why This Works**:
- Starter makes Growth look reasonable
- Elite makes Growth look like smart choice
- Growth has best margin and fit

---

### Discount Strategy

**When to Offer Discounts**:
- Annual prepayment (10-15% discount)
- Long-term commitment (3+ months, 5-10%)
- Bundle multiple services (10%)
- Non-profit/charity work (20% - for case studies)
- First-time client (10% first month only)

**When NOT to Discount**:
- Premium tier Elite package
- High-demand streamers
- Urgent/short-notice work
- Services with high fixed costs
- When client can afford full price

## Payment Terms

### Standard Terms

**Streamers**:
- Payment due: 1st of each month
- Accepted methods: Bank transfer, ACH, wire
- Late fee: 5% after 7 days, 10% after 14 days
- Auto-renewal: Monthly rolling (30-day cancellation notice)

**Brands**:
- Payment terms: Net-30 (standard)
- Early payment discount: 2% if Net-10
- Deposit required: 50% upfront for new clients
- Late fee: 1.5% per month on overdue amounts

---

### Sponsorship Payment Flow

**Standard Flow**:
1. Brand pays agency (Net-30)
2. Agency pays streamer (Net-15 after brand payment)
3. Streamer receives net amount minus commission

**Alternative Flow (Preferred)**:
1. Brand pays 50% deposit upfront
2. Brand pays 50% on campaign completion
3. Agency pays streamer 50% of their share upfront
4. Agency pays remaining 50% within 15 days of full payment

**Why This Matters**:
- Improves cash flow
- Reduces payment risk
- Streamer gets paid faster
- Agency doesn't carry receivables

## Pricing Adjustments & Reviews

### Annual Price Review

**When to Increase Prices**:
- Inflation adjustments (CPI + 1-2%)
- Increased demand (waitlist growing)
- Enhanced service offerings
- Improved results/metrics
- Market rate adjustments

**Recommended Increase**:
- Existing clients: 5% annually
- New clients: 10-15% increase
- New service tiers: Premium pricing

---

### Custom Pricing Scenarios

#### Below-Market Pricing
**When**: Strategic accounts, case study potential
**Limit**: Never below 80% of standard rate
**Duration**: First 1-2 months only

#### Above-Market Pricing
**When**: Premium streamer with unique value
**Justification**: Documented performance, exclusive rights
**Approach**: Value-based pricing with ROI case

#### Bundle Pricing
**When**: Multiple services or long-term commitment
**Discount**: 10-15% off à la carte total
**Structure**: Clearly show savings

## Pricing Objection Handling

### "Your prices are too high"

**Response Framework**:
1. Acknowledge concern
2. Ask about budget/expectations
3. Reiterate value proposition
4. Offer payment terms adjustment
5. Propose scaled-down scope if needed

**Example**:
```
"I understand budget is a consideration. Can you share 
what budget range you were working with? Our pricing 
reflects [X] years of experience, [Y] successful campaigns, 
and [Z] results delivered. We could also structure this 
as [alternative approach] at a lower initial investment."
```

---

### "Can you match [Competitor]'s price?"

**Response Framework**:
1. Ask about competitor's offering
2. Highlight differentiators
3. Explain why pricing reflects value
4. Offer trial or pilot if appropriate

**Example**:
```
"I'd be curious to hear what they're including. Our 
pricing reflects [specific differentiators]. That said, 
if you're serious about moving forward, I can offer 
[a pilot campaign at reduced rate] to demonstrate value."
```

---

### "We need to see more case studies first"

**Response Framework**:
1. Acknowledge valid concern
2. Offer pilot at reduced rate
3. Share relevant (even if indirect) examples
4. Propose performance-based elements

**Example**:
```
"Totally understand. Let me propose a pilot approach: 
we run a 4-week test campaign at [reduced rate] with 
clear KPIs. If we hit targets, we scale. If not, no 
hard feelings. This way you get proof of concept 
without full commitment."
```
