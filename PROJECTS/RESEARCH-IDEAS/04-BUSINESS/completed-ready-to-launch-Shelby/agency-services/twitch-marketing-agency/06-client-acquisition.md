# Client Acquisition

## Target Market Strategy

This document outlines how to identify, approach, and close brands as clients for your Twitch marketing agency. We cover four primary target segments with specific strategies for each.

---

## Target Segment 1: Gaming Brands

### Category Overview

**Gaming-endemic brands** are the easiest entry point for Twitch partnerships. They already understand the platform, have dedicated budgets, and see clear ROI.

### Brand Sub-Categories

#### Game Publishers
**Examples**: Riot Games, Blizzard, EA, Ubisoft, Activision, Epic Games

**What They Want**:
- Game launches and updates
- Player acquisition and retention
- Community engagement
- Esports team partnerships
- In-game item promotion

**Budget Range**: $50K-500K+ per campaign
**Decision Makers**: Influencer Marketing Manager, Community Manager, Brand Manager
**Sales Cycle**: 4-12 weeks

**Approach Strategy**:
1. Research upcoming game launches (6+ months ahead)
2. Identify influencer marketing contacts on LinkedIn
3. Pitch streamer roster for launch campaigns
4. Offer comprehensive activation packages
5. Propose long-term ambassador relationships

**Pitch Angle**:
```
"Our streamers generated 2.3M hours watched during 
[Similar Game] launch. We can deliver comparable 
results for [New Game] with authentic integration 
and measurable player acquisition."
```

---

#### Gaming Hardware
**Examples**: Razer, Logitech, NVIDIA, AMD, Corsair, SteelSeries

**What They Want**:
- Product placement during streams
- Hardware reviews and demonstrations
- Affiliate sales through unique codes
- Brand ambassador relationships
- Co-marketing opportunities

**Budget Range**: $25K-200K per campaign
**Decision Makers**: Marketing Director, Influencer Partnerships Manager
**Sales Cycle**: 3-8 weeks

**Approach Strategy**:
1. Identify streamers already using their products
2. Document organic mentions and usage
3. Pitch formal partnership amplifying existing advocacy
4. Propose product seeding + paid integration
5. Offer exclusive early access opportunities

**Pitch Angle**:
```
"[Streamer] has been using [Product] organically for 
6 months with 47 on-stream mentions. A formal 
partnership would amplify this authentic advocacy 
with dedicated segments and affiliate tracking."
```

---

#### Gaming Peripherals & Accessories
**Examples**: HyperX, Secretlab, Scuf Gaming, Elgato

**What They Want**:
- Product visibility during streams
- Authentic endorsements
- Discount code performance
- User-generated content
- Community engagement

**Budget Range**: $10K-100K per campaign
**Decision Makers**: Brand Manager, Partnerships Lead
**Sales Cycle**: 2-6 weeks

**Approach Strategy**:
1. Start with product seeding (free gear)
2. Track organic usage and mentions
3. Convert to paid partnership with performance data
4. Scale to multi-streamer campaigns
5. Propose exclusive co-branded products

---

#### Energy Drinks & Gaming Nutrition
**Examples**: GFuel, Red Bull, Monster, Ghost Energy

**What They Want**:
- Brand visibility during long streams
- Authentic consumption (not forced)
- Flavor promotion and launches
- Event and tournament activation
- Lifestyle association

**Budget Range**: $50K-300K per campaign
**Decision Makers**: Marketing Director, Sponsorship Manager
**Sales Cycle**: 4-10 weeks

**Approach Strategy**:
1. Position streamers as lifestyle ambassadors
2. Emphasize long-stream endurance angle
3. Propose flavor launch partnerships
4. Include IRL and event components
5. Offer exclusive discount codes

---

### Gaming Brand Acquisition Tactics

#### 1. Event-Based Outreach

**Major Gaming Events**:
- E3 (June)
- Gamescom (August)
- TwitchCon (October)
- The Game Awards (December)
- PAX events (year-round)

**Strategy**:
- Research attending brands 8 weeks before event
- Identify marketing decision-makers
- Send personalized outreach 4 weeks before
- Propose meeting at event
- Follow up within 48 hours post-event

---

#### 2. Launch Calendar Prospecting

**Game Launch Tracking**:
- Maintain calendar of major releases
- Identify marketing windows (typically 4 weeks pre + 4 weeks post)
- Pitch 8-12 weeks before launch
- Offer launch week exclusivity
- Propose sustained campaign (8-12 weeks)

**Tools**:
- Steam release calendar
- IGN release schedule
- Game publisher investor relations pages
- Industry newsletters (GamesIndustry.biz, Polygon)

---

#### 3. Competitive Intelligence

**Approach**:
- Monitor which streamers competitors sponsor
- Identify gaps in their roster
- Pitch similar streamers with better value
- Highlight unique differentiators

**Example Pitch**:
```
"We noticed [Competitor Agency] is running a successful 
campaign with [Similar Streamer]. Our roster includes 
[Your Streamer] who has 40% higher engagement rates 
in the same category at a more efficient CPM."
```

```

monthly. We can showcase [Product] performance in 
real-world gaming scenarios with authentic creator 
endorsements."
```

---

### Tech Brand Acquisition Tactics

#### 1. Problem-Solution Framing

**Approach**: Frame Twitch as solution to their marketing challenge

**Example**:
```
Challenge: "We need to reach Gen Z males interested in technology"

Solution: "Our streamer roster reaches 2M+ monthly viewers 
in this exact demographic with 3x the engagement of 
traditional digital advertising."
```

---

#### 2. Case Study Leverage

**Strategy**: Use gaming brand success to open tech conversations

**Pitch Flow**:
1. Share gaming brand case study
2. Highlight transferable audience
3. Propose similar approach for tech product
4. Offer pilot campaign at reduced rate
5. Scale based on results

---

#### 3. Industry Event Targeting

**Tech Events with Gaming Presence**:
- CES (January)
- Computex (May/June)
- PAX (multiple dates)
- TwitchCon (October)

**Strategy**:
- Attend with specific target list
- Schedule meetings in advance
- Bring streamer portfolio
- Follow up within 48 hours

### CPG Brand Acquisition Tactics

#### 1. Demographic Alignment

**Key Data Points to Lead With**:
- 65% of Twitch users are 18-34 (prime CPG demographic)
- 73% male, 27% female (varies by category)
- High disposable income (gaming hobby investment)
- Early adopter tendencies
- Brand loyalty when authentically engaged

**Pitch Framework**:
```
"If your target customer is males 18-34 with 
disposable income and strong brand loyalty, 
Twitch should be core to your media mix. 
Our streamers deliver this audience with 
authentic integration and measurable results."
```

---

#### 2. Traditional Media Comparison

**Strategy**: Compare Twitch to traditional channels CPG brands understand

**Comparison Points**:
| Channel | CPM | Engagement | Targeting | Measurability |
|---------|-----|------------|-----------|---------------|
| TV | $20-35 | Low | Broad | Limited |
| Digital Display | $5-15 | Low-Med | Medium | Good |
| Social Media | $10-25 | Medium | Good | Good |
| **Twitch** | **$8-20** | **Very High** | **Excellent** | **Excellent** |

---

#### 3. Pilot Campaign Approach

**Strategy**: Reduce risk with small pilot

**Pilot Structure**:
- 4-week campaign
- 3-5 streamers
- $25K-50K investment
- Clear KPIs defined upfront
- Scale decision at week 3

**Success Criteria**:
- Brand lift survey results
- Discount code redemptions
- Social media engagement
- Sentiment analysis
- Cost per acquisition

**What They Want**:

- User-generated content

**Budget Range**: $25K-150K per campaign
**Decision Makers**: Founder, Marketing Director
**Sales Cycle**: 2-6 weeks

---

#### Health & Wellness
**Examples**: Athletic Greens, Huel, Ritual, supplements

**What They Want**:
- Health messaging
- Routine integration
- Subscription conversion
- Long-term partnerships

**Budget Range**: $50K-200K per campaign
**Decision Makers**: Growth Marketing, Partnerships
**Sales Cycle**: 4-8 weeks

---

#### Finance & Apps
**Examples**: CashApp, Robinhood, Coinbase, Chime

**What They Want**:
- App downloads
- Account creation
- Active user conversion
- Compliance adherence

**Budget Range**: $100K-500K+ per campaign
**Decision Makers**: Growth Marketing, Performance Marketing
**Sales Cycle**: 6-12 weeks

---

### DTC Brand Acquisition Tactics

#### 1. Founder-to-Founder Outreach

**Advantage**: DTC brands often have accessible founders

**Approach**:
- Find founder on LinkedIn or Twitter
- Send personalized message about their brand
- Share relevant case study
- Propose brief call to explore fit
- Move quickly (DTC decisions are fast)

---

#### 2. Performance-Based Proposals

**DTC Preference**: They love performance marketing

**Structure**:
- Lower base fee
- Strong performance incentives
- Clear attribution model
- Weekly optimization calls
- Scale what works

**Example**:
```
Base Fee: $10,000
CPA Bonus: $25 per qualified signup
Target: 400 signups = $10,000 bonus
Total Potential: $20,000
```

---

#### 3. Rapid Testing Framework

**DTC brands want to test fast and scale fast**

**2-Week Sprint Structure**:
- Week 1: 3-5 streamers, varied approaches
- Week 2: Double down on winners, cut losers
- Day 14: Scale decision

**Benefits**:
- Fast learning
- Limited risk
- Data-driven decisions
- Quick scale opportunities

## Outreach & Sales Process

### The 7-Touch Sales Sequence

**Touch 1**: Personalized email introduction
**Touch 2**: Follow-up email (Day 3) with case study
**Touch 3**: LinkedIn connection + message (Day 5)
**Touch 4**: Phone call (Day 7)
**Touch 5**: Email with specific campaign idea (Day 10)
**Touch 6**: Voicemail + email (Day 14)
**Touch 7**: Breakup email (Day 21)

### Email Templates

#### Initial Outreach Template

```
Subject: Partnership opportunity: [Their Brand] × [Your Agency]

Hi [Name],

I've been following [Brand]'s marketing lately—really 
impressed with [specific campaign or initiative].

I run [Agency], and we specialize in Twitch partnerships 
for brands like yours. Our streamers generated [X] million 
hours watched last quarter with an average engagement rate 
of [X]%—significantly above platform averages.

Given [Brand]'s focus on [their goal], I think there's a 
strong fit. I'd love to share a few specific campaign ideas 
that could drive real results.

Are you open to a 15-minute call next week? I'm available 
[2-3 specific time options].

Best,
[Your Name]
[Agency] | [Website]
[Phone]
```

---

#### Follow-Up Template (Day 3)

```
Subject: Re: Partnership opportunity

Hi [Name],

Following up on my note about Twitch partnerships for [Brand].

I've attached a case study from our work with [Similar Brand]—
they saw [specific result] over an 8-week campaign.

The approach we used could work well for [Brand] given 
[your observation about their brand].

Still open to a brief call if you're exploring Twitch.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

---

#### Breakup Email Template (Day 21)

```
Subject: Closing the file

Hi [Name],

I've reached out a few times about Twitch partnerships 
for [Brand] without hearing back.

I'll close the file on my end—no need to respond.

If Twitch becomes a priority down the road, feel free 
to reach out. Happy to help when the timing is right.

Best of luck with [current initiative you mentioned],

[Your Name]
```

## Client Retention Strategy

### Onboarding Process

**Week 1**:
- Kickoff call with all stakeholders
- Brand guidelines and asset collection
- Streamer matching and approval
- Campaign timeline confirmation
- Tracking setup (codes, links, pixels)

**Week 2**:
- Creative development and approval
- Streamer briefing and training
- Technical testing
- Pre-campaign checklist completion

**Week 3**:
- Campaign launch
- Daily monitoring and support
- Mid-week check-in call

**Week 4+**:
- Weekly performance reports
- Optimization recommendations
- Renewal discussion (Week 6 for monthly campaigns)

### Retention Tactics

**During Campaign**:
- Proactive communication (don't wait for issues)
- Share positive feedback and wins
- Identify optimization opportunities
- Suggest upsell opportunities

**Post-Campaign**:
- Comprehensive performance report
- Case study development (with permission)
- Testimonial request
- Renewal proposal with learnings incorporated
- Stay-in-touch cadence (monthly check-ins)

### Renewal Rates to Target

| Campaign Type | Target Renewal Rate |
|---------------|---------------------|
| One-off campaign | 30-40% |
| Monthly retainer | 60-70% |
| Quarterly program | 70-80% |
| Annual partnership | 80-90% |
