# Cold Call Scripts
## Phone Scripts for Lead Gen Client Acquisition

---

## 📞 Call Best Practices

**Before You Call:**
- ✅ Research the business (website, services, reviews)
- ✅ Find the owner's name (call and ask if needed)
- ✅ Know their service area and specialties
- ✅ Prepare your pitch (but sound natural)
- ✅ Have your calendar ready to book
- ✅ Smile while talking (they can hear it!)

**Mindset:**
- You're calling to HELP them, not sell them
- You have something valuable (leads)
- Rejection is normal - it's a numbers game
- Every "no" gets you closer to "yes"
- Confidence comes from preparation

**Timing:**
- **Best**: Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-12pm or 2pm-4pm
- **Okay**: Monday 12pm-4pm, Friday 10am-12pm
- **Avoid**: Monday morning, Friday afternoon, lunch (12-1pm)

## 🎯 Call Opening (Gatekeeper)

### When Receptionist Answers

**Receptionist**: "[Business Name], how can I help you?"

**You**: "Hi! This is [Your Name] with [Your Company]. I'm trying to reach [Owner Name] - is he/she available?"

**If they ask what it's about:**

**You**: "I have a business opportunity I'd like to discuss with [Owner Name] directly. Is he/she around?"

**If they push for details:**

**You**: "It's about generating more local customers for [Business Name]. It'll only take 2 minutes of [Owner Name]'s time. Is he/she available?"

### When Owner Answers

**Owner**: "[Business Name], this is [Name]."

**You**: "Hi [Owner Name], this is [Your Name]. I know I'm calling out of the blue - do you have 30 seconds to hear why I'm calling, and then you can decide if we keep talking?"

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges the interruption
- Asks permission (respectful)
- Sets time expectation (low commitment)
- Gives them control

## 🎯 The Pitch (30-Second Version)

### Version 1: Direct Approach

```
"Great, I'll be quick.

I run a local website that connects [City] residents with 
[service] companies. We're ranking #1 for '[keyword]' and 
generating about [X] leads per month.

Right now, I'm looking for one partner company in [City] to 
receive these leads exclusively.

I came across [Business Name] because of your [reviews/reputation/
years in business], and I think you'd be a great fit.

Are you currently accepting new [service] jobs?"
```

### Version 2: Problem/Solution

```
"Here's the thing: when someone in [City] searches for 
'[service]' on Google, they usually call the first company 
they see.

Right now, that's your competitors - [Competitor 1], 
[Competitor 2], etc.

I built a website that ranks above them, and it's getting 
all those calls. Last month we had [X] people looking for 
[service] in [City].

Instead of sending them to your competitors, I'd rather 
partner with a quality company like yours.

Would you be interested in seeing how many leads we're 
talking about?"
```

### Version 3: Case Study Approach

```
"I'll be direct. I help [service] companies get more local 
customers without spending on ads.

We just launched a site in [Nearby City] for a [service] 
company. After 90 days, they're getting 40+ leads/month, 
all from Google.

I'm looking to replicate that in [City], and [Business Name] 
came up as one of the top-rated companies in the area.

Would you be open to seeing the numbers and discussing if 
this could work for you?"
```

## 🎯 Handling Common Responses

### "I'm interested, tell me more."

**You**: "Awesome! Here's how it works:

We own and operate local websites that rank in Google for 
[service] searches. When someone searches and contacts us 
through the site, we forward that lead to our partner company.

Right now in [City], we're generating about [X] leads/month. 
I'd send all of them exclusively to you.

You'd pay a flat monthly fee of $[X,XXX], or we can do 
pay-per-lead at $[X]/lead. No long-term contract - either 
of us can walk away with 30 days notice.

Does that sound like something worth exploring?"

### "How much does it cost?"

**You**: "Great question. We have two options:

Option 1: Flat rental at $[X,XXX]/month. You get ALL leads, 
exclusive to you.

Option 2: Pay-per-lead at $[X]/lead. You only pay for actual 
qualified leads.

Most clients start with the flat rate because it's predictable, 
and the leads usually pay for themselves within the first week.

Which sounds better for your business?"

### "I need to think about it."

**You**: "Totally understand. What specifically do you want to 
think about? Is it the price, the commitment, or something else?"

**Listen to their concern, then address it:**

If price: "I get it. What if we started with a 30-day trial 
at a reduced rate? That way you can see the leads before 
committing."

If commitment: "No problem - there's no long-term contract. 
It's month-to-month with 30 days notice. You're not locked in."

If leads: "Fair concern. Let me show you the actual lead 
data from last month. I can text or email you the dashboard 
right now."

### "We already have enough business."

**You**: "That's actually great to hear - it means you're 
doing well! A lot of our best partners were in the same spot.

The thing is, business can be unpredictable. Having a 
consistent lead source gives you a safety net for slow 
periods, and lets you be more selective about which jobs 
you take.

Plus, if you ever want to grow, you'll need a steady stream 
of new customers. Would it make sense to at least see what 
kind of volume we're talking about?"

### "We've been burned by lead gen before."

**You**: "I hear that a lot, and I'm sorry you had a bad 
experience. Can I ask what happened?

[Listen to their story]

Yeah, that's unfortunately common. Here's how we're different:

1. We OWN the website - we're not reselling someone else's leads
2. You get EXCLUSIVE leads - we don't sell to your competitors
3. You can see the dashboard in real-time - total transparency
4. No long-term contract - if you're not happy, you can leave

Would you be willing to try it for 30 days and see the 
difference?"

### "Send me some information."

**You**: "Absolutely, I can do that. Let me ask you this though 
- is there a specific reason you want information, or are you 
just being polite?

[If they're genuinely interested]

Happy to send info. But honestly, a 10-minute call would be 
more valuable. I can show you the actual leads, answer your 
questions in real-time, and you can decide if it's worth it.

When's a good time this week? I have [Day] at [Time] or 
[Day] at [Time] open."

### "What's your website?"

**You**: "It's [WebsiteURL].com. Feel free to check it out.

[Pause]

What do you think? Pretty professional, right? We invest in 
making sure it ranks well and converts visitors into leads.

I can show you the backend dashboard too - you'll see exactly 
how many calls and form submissions we get. Want me to send 
you a demo login?"

### "I'm the wrong person to talk to."

**You**: "No problem! Who would be the best person to discuss 
this with? Is there a partner, marketing manager, or someone 
else who handles new business opportunities?"

[Get the right person's name and contact info]

"Perfect. Should I mention that you suggested I reach out? 
And what's the best way to reach [Name] - email or phone?"

## 🎯 Closing the Call

### When They're Interested

**You**: "Awesome! Here's what I suggest:

Let's do a quick 15-minute call later this week. I'll show you:
1. The live lead dashboard
2. What keywords we rank for
3. Exactly how the partnership would work
4. Answer any questions you have

I have [Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time]. Which works better 
for you?"

**[Book the call]**

"Perfect. I'll send you a calendar invite with a Zoom link. 
The number to reach me is [Your Number] if you need to reschedule.

Looking forward to it!"

### When They Need to Discuss with Partner

**You**: "Totally makes sense. This is a business decision, 
not just yours to make.

Here's what I suggest: Let me send you a quick overview email 
with the key points. You can review it with [Partner], and 
then we can hop on a 10-minute call together to answer any 
questions.

What's the best email for you?"

**[Get email, send follow-up]**

"I'll send that over today. When's a good time to follow up? 
Would [Day] work for a quick call with both of you?"

### When They Say No

**You**: "No problem at all, I appreciate you taking the time 
to talk.

Can I ask - is it just not a good time right now, or is this 
not something you'd ever be interested in?

[If not a good time]

Totally get it. Can I follow up in [3 months]? Business needs 
change, and this could be a good fit down the road.

[If never interested]

Understood. Thanks for being direct. If you happen to know 
another [service] company in [City] who might be interested, 
I'd appreciate the introduction.

Either way, I wish you the best with [Business Name]!"

## 🎯 Voicemail Scripts

### Voicemail #1 (First Attempt)

```
"Hi [Owner Name], this is [Your Name] with [Your Company]. 
I'm calling because I have a business opportunity that could 
help [Business Name] get more local customers.

I was going to send an email, but I think a quick conversation 
would be more valuable. 

Give me a call back at [Your Number] when you have 2 minutes. 
Again, that's [Your Number]. Thanks!"
```

### Voicemail #2 (Follow-Up)

```
"Hey [Owner Name], [Your Name] again. Just following up on 
my voicemail from [Day].

I'm working with a few [service] companies in [City] to help 
them get more leads from Google, and I think [Business Name] 
would be a great fit.

If you're interested, call me at [Your Number]. If not, no 
worries - I won't keep bothering you. Thanks!"
```

### Voicemail #3 (Breakup)

```
"Hi [Owner Name], this is [Your Name]. This is my last 
voicemail - I promise!

I had an idea that could help [Business Name] get more 
customers, but I haven't been able to reach you.

If you change your mind, my number is [Your Number]. Either 
way, I wish you the best. Take care!"
```

## 🎯 Call Flow Cheat Sheet

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. OPENING                                              │
│    → Gatekeeper: "Is [Owner] available?"                │
│    → Owner: "Do you have 30 seconds?"                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                           ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 2. PITCH (30 seconds)                                   │
│    → What you do                                        │
│    → Social proof (leads/results)                       │
│    → Why them (compliment/research)                     │
│    → Ask: "Are you accepting new jobs?"                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                           ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 3. HANDLE OBJECTIONS                                    │
│    → Listen fully                                       │
│    → Acknowledge concern                                │
│    → Address with specific answer                       │
│    → Confirm resolution                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                           ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 4. CLOSE                                                │
│    → If interested: Book call                           │
│    → If unsure: Send info + follow-up                   │
│    → If no: Ask for referral + exit gracefully          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Stand up while calling** - More energy in your voice
2. **Smile while talking** - They can hear it
3. **Speak slower than normal** - Sounds more confident
4. **Pause after asking questions** - Let them answer
5. **Take notes during the call** - Reference them later
6. **Send follow-up immediately** - Strike while iron is hot
7. **Call from a local number** - Higher answer rate
8. **Practice your pitch** - Until it sounds natural
9. **Record yourself** - Hear how you sound to prospects
10. **Celebrate small wins** - Every conversation is progress
