# Pricing Models

## Comprehensive Pricing Strategies & Calculators

---

## Pricing Philosophy

**Value-Based, Not Cost-Plus:** Price based on client ROI, not your costs.

**Transparent but Flexible:** Clear structure with room for customization.

**Aligned Incentives:** Client success = your success.

## 4. Hybrid Model (Recommended)

### Overview
Combines project fee for setup with ongoing usage/retainer for operations.

### Structure

```
Phase 1: Implementation (One-Time)
- Discovery & Design
- Development
- Integration
- Training
- Deployment

Phase 2: Operations (Ongoing)
- Usage fees OR retainer
- Support
- Maintenance
- Optimization
```

### Example Hybrid Deal

```
Implementation Fee: $100,000 (one-time)
Monthly Retainer: $15,000 (includes 50K minutes)
Overage: $0.15/minute
Contract Term: 24 months

Total Contract Value:
Implementation: $100,000
Monthly (24 months): $360,000
                    -----------
Total:              $460,000
```

### Benefits

**For Client:**
- Lower upfront cost than pure project
- Predictable ongoing costs
- Aligned incentives (you want them to succeed)
- Easier budget approval

**For You:**
- Immediate cash flow from implementation
- Recurring revenue from operations
- Longer client relationships
- Higher lifetime value

## 5. Performance-Based Pricing

### Overview
Tie fees to measurable outcomes. Use as differentiator or in combination with other models.

### Structures

#### Gain-Share Model
```
Base Fee: 50-70% of standard pricing
Performance Bonus: 20-30% of verified savings/revenue

Example:
- Standard monthly fee: $20,000
- Base fee: $12,000
- Verified monthly savings: $50,000
- Performance bonus (25%): $12,500
- Total: $24,500 (client still saves $25,500)
```

#### Outcome-Based Model
```
Fee tied to specific KPIs:

- Cost per resolution: $X per successful resolution
- Revenue generated: X% of attributed revenue
- Appointments booked: $X per kept appointment
- Collections: X% of collected amount

Example (Collections):
- Standard fee: $20,000/month
- Performance fee: 15% of collections
- Monthly collections: $200,000
- Performance fee: $30,000
- Total: $30,000 (client nets $170,000)
```

#### Risk-Reversal Model
```
"Pay only if we deliver" structure:

- No upfront implementation fee
- Higher usage/retainer fees
- Minimum performance guarantees
- Exit clause if targets not met

Example:
- Implementation: $0 (normally $100K)
- Monthly fee: $25,000 (normally $15,000)
- Minimum savings guarantee: $50,000/month
- If savings < $50K: Fee reduced proportionally
```

### When to Use Performance Pricing

**Good For:**
- Collections (direct revenue impact)
- Sales/lead gen (direct revenue impact)
- Cost reduction (clear baseline)
- Confident in your solution
- Building case studies

**Avoid When:**
- Hard to measure outcomes
- Client data/infrastructure is poor
- Long sales cycles
- Uncertain about performance
- Client has unrealistic expectations

## 7. Pricing Negotiation Guide

### Discount Guidelines

| Situation | Max Discount | Conditions |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| Annual prepay | 15-20% | Full year upfront |
| Multi-year contract | 10-15% | 2-3 year commitment |
| High volume commitment | 10-20% | Minimum volume guarantee |
| Case study rights | 5-10% | Public reference allowed |
| Fast close | 5% | Sign within 2 weeks |
| Bundle multiple projects | 10-15% | 2+ projects committed |
| **Maximum combined** | **25-30%** | Never exceed |

### Negotiation Tactics

**Client Says:** "Your pricing is too high"

**Response:**
```
"I understand budget is a concern. Let's look at the ROI:

Your current cost per call: $5.00
Our cost per call: $1.50
Monthly call volume: 10,000
Monthly savings: $35,000
Annual savings: $420,000

Our fee: $180,000/year
Net benefit: $240,000/year

The investment pays for itself in 5 months. 
Would a phased approach help with cash flow?"
```

**Client Says:** "Competitor X is cheaper"

**Response:**
```
"I appreciate you shopping around. May I ask what's included 
in their quote? Often the lower price means:

- Less customization
- Fewer integrations
- Limited support
- Lower accuracy
- Hidden fees

Our pricing includes [list your differentiators]. 
What matters most to you: lowest price or best outcomes?"
```

**Client Says:** "We need to start small"

**Response:**
```
"That makes sense. Let's start with a pilot:

Pilot Scope: [limited use case]
Pilot Duration: 60 days
Pilot Investment: $25,000

If we achieve [success criteria], we expand to full deployment.
If not, you've only invested $25K in learning.

Does that reduce your risk?"
```

### Value Articulation Framework

```
1. Current State Cost
   - Labor costs
   - Technology costs
   - Opportunity costs
   - Error/rework costs

2. Future State Benefits
   - Cost reduction
   - Revenue increase
   - Productivity gains
   - Customer satisfaction

3. Investment Required
   - Implementation
   - Ongoing operations

4. ROI Calculation
   - Payback period
   - Year 1 return
   - 3-year NPV

5. Risk Mitigation
   - Pilot option
   - Performance guarantees
   - Exit clauses
```

## 8. Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring

**Always present the Enterprise package first.** It sets a high anchor, making Professional seem reasonable.

```
❌ "We have a $50K package..."
✅ "Our Enterprise solution is $250K, but most clients 
    start with our Professional package at $100K..."
```

### Framing

**Frame as investment, not cost.**

```
❌ "The cost is $100,000"
✅ "The investment is $100,000, with a 4-month payback 
    and 200% first-year ROI"
```

### Options

**Always present 3 options.** People tend to choose the middle.

```
Option 1: $50K (basic)
Option 2: $100K (recommended) ← Most choose this
Option 3: $200K (premium)
```

### Urgency

**Create legitimate urgency.**

```
- "We have 2 implementation slots left this quarter"
- "Current pricing valid until [date]"
- "Volume discount requires commitment by [date]"
```
