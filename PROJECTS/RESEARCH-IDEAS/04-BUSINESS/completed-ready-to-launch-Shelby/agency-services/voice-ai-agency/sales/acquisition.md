# Client Acquisition Playbook

## Go-to-Market Strategy for Voice AI Agency

---

## Executive Summary

**Target:** Contact centers, enterprises, SMBs in healthcare, finance, retail, real estate

**Goal:** 10 clients Year 1, 50 clients Year 2, 150+ clients Year 3

**Revenue:** $500K Year 1, $3M Year 2, $10M Year 3

**Strategy:** Multi-channel outbound + inbound + partnerships

---

## 1. Target Market Segmentation

### Primary Targets (Tier 1)

| Segment | Characteristics | Deal Size | Sales Cycle |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|-------------|
| Contact Centers | 100+ agents, high call volume | $150K-500K | 3-6 months |
| Healthcare Systems | 5+ locations, patient services | $100K-300K | 4-8 months |
| Financial Services | Banks, credit unions, insurance | $150K-400K | 6-12 months |
| Enterprise Retail | E-commerce, customer service | $100K-300K | 3-6 months |

### Secondary Targets (Tier 2)

| Segment | Characteristics | Deal Size | Sales Cycle |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|-------------|
| SMB Healthcare | Single practice, 5-20 staff | $30K-80K | 1-3 months |
| Real Estate Teams | 10+ agents, high lead volume | $30K-100K | 1-2 months |
| Professional Services | Law firms, accounting, consulting | $50K-150K | 2-4 months |
| Local Enterprise | Regional businesses, 100-500 employees | $75K-200K | 2-5 months |

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Company:**
- 100+ employees (or 10+ for SMB focus)
- $10M+ annual revenue
- High call volume (1,000+ calls/month)
- Customer service or sales focus
- Technology-adopting (uses modern CRM, etc.)

**Stakeholders:**
- VP/Director of Customer Service
- VP/Director of Sales
- CTO/CIO
- COO
- (Healthcare) Practice Manager
- (Real Estate) Team Leader/Broker

**Pain Points:**
- High call center costs
- Long wait times
- Missed calls/leads
- Inconsistent customer experience
- Agent turnover
- After-hours coverage gaps

**Buying Signals:**
- Recently raised funding
- Hiring for customer service roles
- Implemented new CRM/phone system
- Expanding to new locations
- Negative customer reviews mentioning wait times
- Competitor announced AI initiative

## 2. Lead Generation Strategies

### Outbound Prospecting

#### Cold Email Campaigns

**Volume:** 500-1,000 emails/week
**Target Response Rate:** 3-5%
**Target Meeting Rate:** 1-2%

**Email Template 1: Problem-Aware**

```
Subject: Quick question about your call center

Hi [Name],

Noticed [Company] handles [volume] calls monthly. Curious - 
what % of calls are you currently able to answer within 
30 seconds?

We've helped companies like [Similar Company] automate 70% 
of routine calls while improving CSAT scores by 20 points.

Worth a 15-minute chat to see if there's a fit?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Email Template 2: Value-First**

```
Subject: Idea for [Company]'s customer service

Hi [Name],

I analyzed [Company]'s customer service setup and noticed 
[specific observation - long wait times, limited hours, etc.].

We recently helped [Similar Company] reduce wait times by 
80% and save $30K/month in call center costs using voice AI.

Created a quick 2-minute video showing how this could work 
for [Company]: [Loom link]

Open to a brief call next week?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Email Template 3: Social Proof**

```
Subject: [Competitor/Similar Company] just did this

Hi [Name],

[Competitor/Similar Company] recently deployed voice AI to 
handle their customer service calls. Results after 90 days:

- 72% of calls automated
- $45K/month cost savings
- CSAT increased from 3.8 to 4.5

I'd love to show you how this could work for [Company]. 
15 minutes next week?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

#### Cold Calling

**Volume:** 50-100 calls/day
**Target Connection Rate:** 15-25%
**Target Meeting Rate:** 5-10%

**Opening Script:**

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. 

I know I'm calling you out of the blue - do you have 30 seconds 
to tell me if this is worth continuing, or should I let you go?"

[Wait for response - most say "go ahead" or "what's this about?"]

"Appreciate it. We work with [their industry] companies to 
automate routine customer service calls with AI. 

Quick question - what's your biggest headache with customer 
service right now? Is it wait times, after-hours coverage, 
or something else?"

[Listen and qualify]

"That's exactly what we help with. Would it make sense to 
schedule a 15-minute call to show you how it works?"
```

**Common Objections:**

```
"We're happy with our current setup"
→ "That's great to hear. Most of our clients were too before 
   they saw the ROI. What if I could show you a way to cut 
   costs by 50% while improving customer satisfaction? 
   Worth 15 minutes to see?"

"We don't have budget for this"
→ "I understand. Most clients fund this from existing call 
   center budget - the savings pay for it in 3-4 months. 
   Would it be helpful to see the ROI calculation?"

"We've tried AI before, didn't work"
→ "I hear that a lot. Early AI was pretty bad. The technology 
   has improved dramatically in the last 18 months. 
   What if I showed you a live demo with your actual use cases?"

"Send me information"
→ "Happy to. To make sure I send relevant info, can you tell 
   me - what's the one thing you'd most want to improve about 
   your current customer service?"
```

## 3. Sales Process

### 6-Stage Sales Funnel

```
Stage 1: Prospecting → Stage 2: Discovery → Stage 3: Demo
    1000 leads          200 qualified         80 demos
         ↓                    ↓                    ↓
Stage 6: Close ← Stage 5: Negotiation ← Stage 4: Proposal
    20 deals            40 proposals        60 proposals
```

### Stage 1: Prospecting

**Goal:** Generate qualified leads

**Activities:**
- Cold email (500-1,000/week)
- Cold calls (50-100/day)
- LinkedIn outreach (100/week)
- Inbound lead follow-up (<5 min response)
- Partner referrals

**Qualification Criteria (BANT):**
- Budget: $50K+ available or can be freed up
- Authority: Decision maker or strong influencer
- Need: Clear pain point (wait times, costs, missed calls)
- Timeline: Looking to solve in next 6 months

**Tools:**
- CRM: HubSpot, Salesforce, or Pipedrive
- Prospecting: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo, ZoomInfo
- Email: Outreach, Salesloft
- Calling: Aircall, RingCentral

### Stage 2: Discovery Call (30 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand pain points, quantify impact, qualify

**Agenda:**
1. Introductions (2 min)
2. Their current situation (10 min)
3. Pain points and impact (10 min)
4. Solution overview (5 min)
5. Next steps (3 min)

**Key Questions:**

```
Current State:
- "Walk me through your current call center setup"
- "How many calls do you handle monthly?"
- "What's your average wait time?"
- "What % of calls are you missing?"
- "What systems are you using? (CRM, phone, helpdesk)"

Pain Points:
- "What's your biggest frustration with customer service?"
- "What happens when you miss a call?"
- "How do you handle after-hours calls?"
- "What's your customer satisfaction score?"
- "What's your agent turnover rate?"

Impact:
- "What does a missed call cost you?"
- "How much do you spend on customer service monthly?"
- "What would improving CSAT by 20 points mean for your business?"
- "How much time do agents spend on routine questions?"

Decision Process:
- "Have you explored AI solutions before?"
- "What would need to be true for this to be a priority?"
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What's your ideal timeline?"
- "What does your budget process look like?"
```

**Discovery Call Checklist:**
- [ ] Pain points identified and quantified
- [ ] Current costs/situation understood
- [ ] Decision makers identified
- [ ] Timeline understood
- [ ] Budget range confirmed
- [ ] Next steps scheduled

### Stage 3: Demo (45-60 minutes)

**Goal:** Show solution relevance, build confidence

**Demo Structure:**

1. **Recap (5 min)**
   - Confirm understanding of their situation
   - Confirm goals for the demo

2. **Platform Overview (10 min)**
   - Show dashboard
   - Explain architecture (briefly)
   - Highlight key features relevant to them

3. **Live Demo (25 min)**
   - Call the bot live (most powerful)
   - Show their specific use cases
   - Demonstrate integrations
   - Show analytics

4. **ROI Discussion (10 min)**
   - Present custom ROI calculation
   - Compare to current costs
   - Show payback period

5. **Next Steps (5 min)**
   - Confirm interest
   - Discuss proposal process
   - Schedule follow-up

**Demo Best Practices:**
- Always do a live call (not slides)
- Use their branding/voice if possible
- Show their specific use cases
- Have a backup plan (recorded demo)
- Include technical stakeholder

### Stage 4: Proposal (3-5 days)

**Goal:** Present customized solution and pricing

**Proposal Structure:**

1. **Executive Summary (1 page)**
   - Their situation
   - Recommended solution
   - Expected outcomes
   - Investment

2. **Current State Analysis (2-3 pages)**
   - Pain points documented
   - Current costs
   - Opportunities identified

3. **Recommended Solution (5-10 pages)**
   - Solution overview
   - Use cases
   - Technical architecture
   - Integration plan
   - Timeline

4. **Investment & ROI (2-3 pages)**
   - Pricing
   - ROI calculation
   - Payback period
   - 3-year projection

5. **Case Studies (2-3 pages)**
   - Similar client examples
   - Results achieved
   - Testimonials

6. **Next Steps (1 page)**
   - Implementation timeline
   - Decision process
   - Contract terms

**Proposal Tips:**
- Customize heavily (use their logo, their data)
- Lead with value, not features
- Include ROI calculator
- Make it skimmable (executives scan)
- Send as PDF + schedule review call

### Stage 5: Negotiation (1-4 weeks)

**Goal:** Reach agreement on terms

**Common Negotiation Points:**

| Issue | Client Wants | Your Position | Compromise |
|-------|--------------|---------------|------------|
| Price | 20-30% discount | List price | 10-15% for annual |
| Payment terms | Net 60-90 | Net 15 | Net 30 |
| Contract length | 12 months | 24-36 months | 18-24 months |
| SLA | 99.99% uptime | 99.9% | 99.9% with credits |
| Customization | Unlimited | Scoped | 10 hrs/month included |
| Termination | 30 days | 90 days | 60 days |

**Negotiation Tactics:**

```
"If we can agree on [X], we can do [Y]"
→ Trade concessions, don't give them away

"Let me check with my team"
→ Buy time, don't commit on the spot

"Your competitor is offering lower pricing"
→ "What's included in their quote? Let's compare apples to apples"

"We need to think about it"
→ "Of course. What specific concerns do you have? 
   I may be able to address them now"
```

**Closing Signals:**
- Asking about implementation timeline
- Discussing contract terms
- Involving legal/procurement
- Asking for references
- "What happens if..." questions

### Stage 6: Close

**Goal:** Signed contract, kickoff scheduled

**Closing Checklist:**
- [ ] Contract signed
- [ ] First payment received
- [ ] Kickoff meeting scheduled
- [ ] Stakeholder introductions made
- [ ] Access/credentials requested
- [ ] Success metrics confirmed
- [ ] Internal handoff complete
