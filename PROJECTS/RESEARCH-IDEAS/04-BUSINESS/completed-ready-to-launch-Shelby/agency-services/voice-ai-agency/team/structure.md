# Team Building Guide

---

## Overview

Building the right team is critical to scaling a voice AI agency. This guide covers:
- Organizational structure by stage
- Key roles and responsibilities
- Hiring profiles and interview questions
- Compensation benchmarks
- Team development

## 1. Organizational Structure by Stage

### Stage 1: Founder-Led (0-10 clients, $0-500K revenue)

```
┌─────────────────┐
│    Founder      │
│  (Sales + PM)   │
└────────┬────────┘
         │
    ┌────┴────┐
    │         │
┌───┴───┐ ┌───┴───┐

│ Lead  │ │ VA    │
```

**Focus:** Prove model, close first clients, deliver well

**Roles:**
- **Founder:** Sales, client relationships, product vision
- **Tech Lead:** All development, architecture, deployments
- **Ops VA:** Admin, scheduling, basic customer support

**Monthly Burn:** $25K - $50K

## 3. Hiring Process

### Standard Hiring Process

```
Application → Screen → Interview → Assessment → Reference → Offer
   (1 day)    (1 day)   (3-5 days)   (3-5 days)   (2-3 days)  (1 day)
   
Total: 10-15 days average
```

### Interview Structure

#### Phone Screen (30 minutes)

**Goals:**
- Verify basic qualifications
- Assess communication
- Gauge interest
- Explain role and company

**Questions:**
- "Walk me through your background"
- "What interests you about this role?"
- "What are you looking for in your next position?"
- "What do you know about our company?"
- "What are your salary expectations?"

#### Technical/Functional Interview (60 minutes)

**For Engineers:**
- Coding exercise (live or take-home)
- System design discussion
- Past project deep-dive
- Problem-solving scenarios

**For Sales:**
- Role-play sales call
- Pipeline discussion
- Past performance review
- Sales philosophy

**For CS:**
- Scenario-based questions
- Customer handling examples
- Process thinking
- Escalation management

#### Culture Interview (45 minutes)

**Goals:**
- Values alignment
- Team fit
- Working style
- Growth mindset

**Questions:**
- "Describe your ideal work environment"
- "Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned"
- "What motivates you?"
- "How do you handle conflict?"
- "Where do you want to be in 3 years?"

#### Final Interview with Founder/Executive (30 minutes)

**Goals:**
- Final assessment
- Sell the opportunity
- Answer candidate questions
- Decision

### Assessment Examples

#### Engineer Take-Home Assessment

```
Build a simple voice bot that:
1. Greets the caller
2. Asks for their name
3. Collects their reason for calling
4. Routes based on intent (support, sales, other)
5. Sends a summary via webhook

Time limit: 4 hours
Tech: Python or Node.js
Submit: Code + brief documentation
```

#### Sales Role-Play

```
Scenario: You're selling voice AI to a contact center VP.

I'll play the VP. I'm skeptical about AI, worried about 
job displacement, and have tried a solution before that failed.

Your goal: Get me to agree to a follow-up demo.

Time: 15 minutes
```

#### Conversation Design Exercise

```
Design a conversation flow for appointment scheduling.

Requirements:
- Collect: name, phone, preferred date/time, reason
- Handle: rescheduling, cancellation
- Tone: Friendly, professional, efficient

Deliver: Flow diagram + sample dialogues
Time: 3 hours
```

### Reference Check Questions

**For Managers:**
- "What were their biggest strengths?"
- "What areas did they need development in?"
- "How did they handle pressure?"
- "Would you rehire them?"
- "How would you describe their work style?"

**For Peers:**
- "What was it like working with them?"
- "How did they contribute to team success?"
- "How did they handle disagreements?"
- "What did you learn from them?"

## 5. Team Development

### Onboarding Program

**Week 1: Orientation**
- Company overview
- Team introductions
- Tools and systems setup
- Product training
- Initial assignments

**Week 2-3: Role-Specific Training**
- Deep-dive on responsibilities
- Shadowing team members
- First projects
- Regular check-ins

**Week 4: Integration**
- Full responsibility
- First independent work
- 30-day review

**90-Day Plan:**
- Clear goals for first 90 days
- Weekly 1:1s with manager
- 30/60/90 day check-ins
- Probation completion review

### Performance Management

**Review Cycle:**
- Quarterly check-ins (informal)
- Annual review (formal)
- Continuous feedback

**Review Components:**
- Goal achievement
- Competency assessment
- Values alignment
- Development areas
- Compensation discussion

**Goal Setting (OKRs):**
- Company-level OKRs (quarterly)
- Team-level OKRs (quarterly)
- Individual goals (aligned to team)

### Career Development

**Growth Paths:**
- Individual Contributor track
- Management track
- Parallel progression (equal comp, different focus)

**Development Opportunities:**
- Conference attendance
- Training programs
- Mentorship
- Stretch assignments
- Internal mobility

### Culture Building

**Values:**
- Customer obsession
- Ownership
- Bias for action
- Continuous learning
- Transparency

**Rituals:**
- All-hands (weekly or biweekly)
- Team standups (daily or weekly)
- 1:1s (weekly)
- Retrospectives (after projects)
- Celebrations (wins, milestones)

## 7. Remote vs. Office

### Recommendation: Hybrid

**Why:**
- Access to broader talent pool
- Cost savings on office space
- Flexibility for team
- In-person collaboration when needed

**Structure:**
- Core hours for collaboration (e.g., 10am-3pm local)
- Quarterly team offsites
- Optional office space (co-working or small office)
- Home office stipend

**Tools for Remote:**
- Slack (communication)
- Zoom (video)
- Linear/Jira (project management)
- Notion (documentation)
- Loom (async video)
- GitHub (code)
