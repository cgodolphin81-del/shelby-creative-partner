# 🎯 Client Acquisition

Complete guide to finding and closing Snapchat marketing clients.

---

## 1. Ideal Client Profile

### Primary Targets

**DTC E-commerce Brands**
- Revenue: $1M-50M/year
- Products: Fashion, beauty, food, lifestyle
- Target demographic: 16-35 years old
- Marketing budget: $10K-100K/month
- Pain point: Struggling to reach Gen Z on traditional platforms

**Mobile Apps**
- Category: Gaming, social, lifestyle, productivity
- Downloads: 10K-1M+
- Monetization: Freemium, subscriptions, IAP
- Marketing budget: $20K-200K/month
- Pain point: High user acquisition costs on Meta/Google

**Entertainment & Media**
- Type: Streaming, podcasts, events, publications
- Audience: Gen Z/Millennial focus
- Content: Video, audio, live experiences
- Marketing budget: $15K-150K/month
- Pain point: Declining organic reach, need paid distribution

**Fashion & Beauty Brands**
- Type: Apparel, accessories, cosmetics, skincare
- Price point: $20-200 average order value
- Distribution: DTC + retail
- Marketing budget: $10K-100K/month
- Pain point: Need visual platform for product showcase

**Food & Beverage**
- Type: CPG, restaurants, delivery, subscription
- Target: Urban, young consumers
- Marketing budget: $5K-50K/month
- Pain point: Local + national reach needed

### Client Qualification Criteria

**Green Flags ✅:**
- Already spending on social ads (Meta, TikTok, etc.)
- Have creative resources or budget for production
- Decision-maker is accessible
- Understand performance marketing
- Budget aligns with services ($5K+/month minimum)
- Gen Z is meaningful part of target audience
- Open to testing new channels

**Red Flags ❌:**
- Expecting overnight success
- No budget for ad spend (only agency fees)
- Decision requires 10-person committee
- Want guarantees on results
- Previous agency relationships ended badly (they were the problem)
- B2B enterprise (wrong platform fit)
- Budget under $3K/month total

## 3. Sales Process

### Discovery Call Framework (30 minutes)

**Minutes 0-5: Rapport & Context**
- Welcome and thanks
- Confirm agenda
- Ask about their background
- Build connection

**Minutes 5-15: Discovery Questions**

**Current State:**
- "What's your current marketing mix?"
- "Which channels are performing best?"
- "What's your monthly ad spend?"
- "Have you tried Snapchat before?"

**Goals:**
- "What are your growth targets for this year?"
- "What does success look like in 90 days?"
- "What's the biggest marketing challenge right now?"

**Audience:**
- "Who's your ideal customer?"
- "What percentage is Gen Z?"
- "Where does your audience spend time online?"

**Budget:**
- "What budget have you allocated for new channels?"
- "How do you typically evaluate marketing investments?"

**Minutes 15-25: Presentation**
- Share relevant case studies
- Explain your approach
- Outline potential strategy for them
- Show expertise and credibility

**Minutes 25-30: Next Steps**
- "Does this sound like it could work for you?"
- "What would you need to move forward?"
- Schedule proposal review call
- Thank them for time

### Discovery Call Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
✅ Listen more than you talk (80/20 rule)
✅ Ask open-ended questions
✅ Take notes
✅ Validate their challenges
✅ Share relevant examples
✅ Be honest about fit

**Don't:**
❌ Pitch immediately
❌ Make guarantees
❌ Badmouth competitors
❌ Talk over them
❌ Skip discovery and jump to solution
❌ Pressure for immediate decision

### Proposal Structure

**1. Executive Summary (1 page)**
- Their goals
- Your solution
- Expected outcomes
- Investment summary

**2. Situation Analysis (1-2 pages)**
- Current state assessment
- Market opportunity
- Competitive landscape
- SWOT analysis

**3. Recommended Strategy (2-3 pages)**
- Campaign objectives
- Target audiences
- Creative approach
- Channel mix
- Timeline

**4. Service Details (1-2 pages)**
- Specific deliverables
- Service inclusions
- Team structure
- Communication plan

**5. Investment (1 page)**
- Fee breakdown
- Ad spend recommendation
- Payment terms
- Contract length

**6. Case Studies (1-2 pages)**
- 2-3 relevant examples
- Metrics and results
- Client testimonials

**7. Next Steps (1 page)**
- Timeline to launch
- What you need from them
- Contract details
- Contact information

### Proposal Follow-Up

**Day 1:** Send proposal + calendar link
**Day 2:** "Did you get a chance to review?"
**Day 4:** Share additional case study
**Day 7:** "Any questions I can answer?"
**Day 10:** Phone call
**Day 14:** Final follow-up / breakup

## 4. Closing Strategies

### Objection Handling

**"It's too expensive"**
```
"I understand budget is a consideration. Let me ask — 
compared to what you're currently spending on [Meta/TikTok], 
how does this compare on a cost-per-acquisition basis?

Our clients typically see 30-50% lower CPAs on Snapchat, 
which means you're actually getting more for your investment.

What if we started with a smaller test budget to prove 
the channel before scaling?"
```

**"We've tried Snapchat before and it didn't work"**
```
"That's actually pretty common — and usually it comes down 
to creative or targeting not being optimized for the platform.

Snapchat requires a totally different approach than Meta or 
TikTok. The creative that works there often falls flat on Snap.

Can you share what you tried before? I'd love to diagnose 
what might have gone wrong and show you how we'd approach 
it differently."
```

**"I need to think about it"**
```
"Absolutely — this is an important decision. What specifically 
do you need to think through?

Is it the budget, the timeline, or something about the strategy?

[Listen to their concern and address it directly]

Would it help if I [specific accommodation]?"
```

**"We're happy with our current agency"**
```
"That's great — it sounds like you have a good partnership.

I'm not asking you to replace them. Many of our clients work 
with us specifically for Snapchat while their main agency 
handles other channels.

Would you be open to a test campaign? If we can't beat their 
results, you've lost nothing. If we do, you've got a powerful 
new channel in your mix."
```

**"Send me more information"**
```
"Happy to! To make sure I send the most relevant info, 
what specifically would be most helpful?

- More case studies in your industry?
- Detailed breakdown of our approach?
- Pricing information?
- Something else?

[Send what they ask for within 24 hours with personal note]"
```

### Closing Techniques

**1. Assumptive Close**
```
"Great — I'll get the contract over to you today. 
What's the best email for that?"
```

**2. Urgency Close**
```
"We have capacity for one more client this month. 
If we can get started by [date], we can have your 
campaigns live before [relevant event/season]."
```

**3. Risk Reversal**
```
"We're confident this will work for you. That's why 
we offer a 30-day satisfaction guarantee — if you're 
not happy with our work in the first month, we'll 
refund your agency fees."
```

**4. Social Proof Close**
```
"[Similar Brand] was hesitant at first too. They decided 
to run a 90-day test and just renewed for year two after 
seeing 4x ROAS. I can connect you with their CMO if 
you'd like to hear directly from them."
```

**5. Summary Close**
```
"Let me make sure I understand — you're looking to 
[goal 1], [goal 2], and [goal 3]. Our plan addresses 
all three by [solution summary]. The investment is 
[amount], which is [comparison to current spend or 
expected ROI]. Does this make sense for [Company]?"
```

## 5. Pricing & Packaging

### Service Packages

**Starter Package - $3,000/month**
- Ad management (up to $5K spend)
- 2 ad creatives/month
- Basic reporting
- Email support
- Minimum 3-month contract

**Growth Package - $7,500/month**
- Ad management (up to $20K spend)
- 4 ad creatives/month
- Story ads (2 campaigns)
- Organic content (5 Snaps/week)
- Weekly calls
- Priority support
- Minimum 3-month contract

**Enterprise Package - $15,000/month**
- Full ad management (up to $50K spend)
- 8 ad creatives/month
- 1 AR Lens/quarter
- Influencer campaign (1/month)
- Daily organic content
- Dedicated team
- 24/7 support
- Minimum 6-month contract

### Performance-Based Options

**Base + Performance:**
- Lower base fee ($2K-5K/month)
- Percentage of ad spend (10-15%)
- Performance bonus (5-10% of revenue above target)

**Pure Performance:**
- No monthly fee
- 20-30% of revenue generated
- Requires strong tracking
- Higher risk/reward

**Hybrid:**
- Base fee covers costs
- Performance bonus for hitting targets
- Aligns incentives

### Contract Terms

**Standard Terms:**
- 3-month minimum (test period)
- Month-to-month after
- 30-day cancellation notice
- Payment due Net 15
- Ad spend billed separately (direct to platform or prepaid)

**Enterprise Terms:**
- 6-12 month contract
- Quarterly reviews
- 60-day cancellation notice
- Payment due Net 30
- Volume discounts available

## 7. Retention & Growth

### Keeping Clients Happy

**Communication:**
- Weekly performance reports
- Bi-weekly strategy calls
- Monthly business reviews
- Quarterly strategy sessions
- Responsive to questions (24-hour response time)

**Performance:**
- Hit agreed KPIs
- Proactive optimization
- Test new strategies
- Share insights and learnings
- Bring ideas, not just execution

**Relationship:**
- Understand their business deeply
- Celebrate their wins
- Be honest about challenges
- Act as a partner, not vendor
- Make their job easier

### Upselling Opportunities

**After 90 Days (proven results):**
- Increase ad spend budget
- Add AR lens campaigns
- Influencer marketing
- Organic content management
- Additional creative production

**After 6 Months (strong partnership):**
- Multi-platform expansion
- Full-funnel strategy
- Brand partnership programs
- Event activations
- Long-term ambassador programs

**After 1 Year (trusted advisor):**
- Annual planning partnership
- Product launch strategy
- Market expansion
- Team training
- White-label services

### Churn Prevention

**Warning Signs:**
- Decreased engagement in calls
- Slow payment
- Communication delays
- Questioning value repeatedly
- Competitor outreach

**Intervention Strategies:**
- Schedule emergency strategy call
- Revisit goals and alignment
- Share additional case studies
- Offer additional value (free audit, bonus creative)
- Escalate to senior leadership
- Consider pricing adjustment (if appropriate)
