# 💰 Pricing Models

Complete guide to pricing your Snapchat marketing agency services.

---

## 1. Pricing Philosophy

### Value-Based Pricing

**The Mindset Shift:**
- Don't sell hours → sell outcomes
- Don't compete on price → compete on value
- Don't be a commodity → be a specialist
- Don't discount → add value

**Pricing Formula:**
```
Price = (Value Delivered - Client's Current State) × Confidence Factor

Example:
- Client currently makes $100K/month from social
- Your service could add $50K/month (based on case studies)
- Annual value = $600K
- Your fee = $90K-180K/year (15-30% of value created)
```

### Positioning Impact on Pricing

| Positioning | Price Range | Competition |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Generalist Agency | $2K-5K/month | High |
| Social Media Agency | $3K-8K/month | Medium-High |
| Multi-Platform Specialist | $5K-12K/month | Medium |
| **Snapchat-Only Specialist** | **$7K-20K/month** | **Low** |
| Snapchat + AR Innovation | $10K-30K/month | Very Low |

**Key Insight:** Specialization commands premium pricing. Being "the Snapchat agency" lets you charge 2-3x more than generalists.

## 2. Pricing Models

### Model 1: Monthly Retainer

**Structure:** Fixed monthly fee for defined services

**Pros:**
- Predictable revenue
- Easy to sell
- Clear scope
- Builds long-term relationships

**Cons:**
- Scope creep risk
- Revenue capped
- Can become commoditized

**Best For:**
- Ongoing ad management
- Content creation
- Account management
- Most common model

**Example Packages:**

```
STARTER - $3,000/month
- Ad management (up to $5K spend)
- 2 ad creatives/month
- Weekly reporting
- Email support
- 3-month minimum

GROWTH - $7,500/month
- Ad management (up to $20K spend)
- 4 ad creatives/month
- Story ads (2 campaigns)
- Organic content (5 Snaps/week)
- Weekly calls
- Priority support
- 3-month minimum

ENTERPRISE - $15,000/month
- Full ad management (up to $50K spend)
- 8 ad creatives/month
- 1 AR Lens/quarter
- Influencer campaign (1/month)
- Daily organic content
- Dedicated team
- 24/7 support
- 6-month minimum
```

---

### Model 2: Percentage of Ad Spend

**Structure:** Fee = X% of monthly ad spend

**Typical Rates:**
- 10-15% for spend under $50K/month
- 8-12% for spend $50K-200K/month
- 5-10% for spend $200K+/month
- Minimum fee: $3K-5K/month

**Pros:**
- Scales with client success
- Aligns incentives
- Simple to understand
- Revenue grows with client

**Cons:**
- Revenue fluctuates
- Clients may limit spend
- Doesn't account for creative/strategy work
- Can create tension around budget increases

**Best For:**
- Large spend clients ($50K+/month)
- Performance-focused relationships
- Clients who understand media buying value

**Example:**
```
Client ad spend: $100,000/month
Agency fee: 12% = $12,000/month

Total client investment: $112,000/month
```

---

### Model 3: Performance-Based

**Structure:** Fee tied to specific results

**Common Structures:**

**A. Revenue Share:**
- 10-30% of revenue generated
- Requires robust tracking
- High risk/reward

**B. CPA (Cost Per Acquisition):**
- $X per purchase/sign-up/lead
- Client sets target CPA
- You optimize to hit it

**C. ROAS Bonus:**
- Base fee + bonus for hitting ROAS targets
- Example: $5K base + $5K bonus if ROAS > 4:1

**D. Hybrid:**
- Lower base retainer
- Performance bonus on top
- Example: $5K/month + 10% of revenue above target

**Pros:**
- Ultimate alignment with client
- Can charge premium for results
- Easy sell ("we only win if you win")
- Potential for very high earnings

**Cons:**
- High risk (external factors affect results)
- Requires ironclad tracking
- Can create disputes over attribution
- Cash flow uncertainty

**Best For:**
- E-commerce with clear attribution
- Confident in your abilities
- Established track record
- Clients comfortable with performance model

**Example:**
```
Base Fee: $5,000/month
Performance: 15% of revenue above $50K/month

Month 1 Results:
- Revenue from Snapchat: $80,000
- Above target: $30,000
- Performance fee: $4,500
- Total fee: $9,500
```

### Model 4: Project-Based

**Structure:** Fixed fee for specific project

**Common Projects:**

| Project | Price Range | Timeline |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Snapchat Audit | $2,500-5,000 | 1-2 weeks |
| AR Lens Creation | $3,000-25,000 | 2-6 weeks |
| Creative Package (5 videos) | $3,000-8,000 | 2-3 weeks |
| Influencer Campaign | $5,000-30,000 | 4-8 weeks |
| Account Setup + Training | $3,000-7,000 | 1-2 weeks |
| Strategy Consulting | $250-500/hour | Varies |

**Pros:**
- Clear scope and timeline
- Good for one-off needs
- Easier to sell initially
- Can lead to retainer

**Cons:**
- Not recurring revenue
- Feast/famine cycle
- Limited upside
- Client may not see ongoing value

**Best For:**
- Initial engagement
- Specific deliverables
- Clients not ready for retainer
- Supplementing retainer revenue

---

### Model 5: Hourly/Consulting

**Structure:** Charge per hour of work

**Typical Rates:**
- Junior: $75-150/hour
- Senior: $150-300/hour
- Principal/Strategy: $300-500/hour
- Specialized (AR): $200-400/hour

**Pros:**
- Simple to understand
- Fair for undefined scope
- Good for consulting
- Easy to calculate

**Cons:**
- Punishes efficiency
- Revenue capped by time
- Clients watch the clock
- Not scalable

**Best For:**
- Consulting calls
- Strategy sessions
- Training/workshops
- Overflow work
- Not recommended as primary model

## 3. Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring

**Technique:** Present higher price first to make actual price seem reasonable

**Example:**
```
"We typically work with brands at $15K-25K/month for full-service 
Snapchat marketing including AR campaigns and influencer management.

For your needs, I think our Growth package at $7,500/month would 
be the perfect starting point."
```

### Tiered Pricing

**3-Tier Structure:**
- **Good:** Basic package (anchor low)
- **Better:** Target package (what you want to sell)
- **Best:** Premium package (makes target seem reasonable)

**Why It Works:**
- Gives choice (psychological control)
- Most choose middle option
- Premium makes middle seem reasonable

**Example:**
```
Starter:     $3,000/month  ← Makes Growth seem reasonable
Growth:      $7,500/month  ← Target (most will choose this)
Enterprise: $15,000/month  ← Makes Growth seem like a deal
```

### Framing

**Bad Frame:**
```
"Our fee is $7,500 per month."
```

**Good Frame:**
```
"For $7,500/month, you're getting a dedicated team that will 
manage your entire Snapchat presence, create professional ad 
creative, and optimize toward your ROAS targets. Based on our 
client averages, you can expect 3-5x return on that investment 
within the first 90 days."
```

**Key:** Always tie price to value and outcomes.

### Decoy Pricing

**Technique:** Add option that makes target option more attractive

**Example:**
```
Basic:    $3,000/month (limited features)
Pro:      $7,500/month (best value ← target)
Premium:  $8,500/month (only slightly more than Pro, but much more than Basic)
```

The Premium makes Pro look like a steal.

## 5. Pricing Negotiation

### When to Negotiate

**Good Reasons:**
- Long-term contract (6-12 months)
- Multiple services bundled
- High ad spend (economies of scale)
- Referral from existing client
- Case study opportunity (brand new to your niche)

**Bad Reasons:**
- They asked for discount
- They're price-shopping
- They don't see value
- You're desperate for the client
- They're a bad fit

### Negotiation Tactics

**1. Trade, Don't Give**
```
Client: "Can you do $5K instead of $7.5K?"

You: "I can do $6K if we extend to a 6-month contract 
instead of 3 months. That gives us time to really optimize 
and prove the channel. Does that work?"
```

**2. Remove Scope, Not Price**
```
Client: "This is more than we budgeted."

You: "I understand. We can adjust the scope to fit your 
budget. We could start with just ad management at $5K/month 
and add creative production once we're seeing results. 
How does that sound?"
```

**3. Payment Terms Instead of Price**
```
Client: "Can you discount for upfront payment?"

You: "I can do 10% off if you pay quarterly upfront. 
That's $20,250 instead of $22,500 for the quarter. 
Interested?"
```

**4. Value Reinforcement**
```
Client: "Your competitor charges 30% less."

You: "I appreciate you sharing that. Can I ask what's 
included in their package? We've found that brands who 
go with the lowest price often end up spending more in 
the long run due to wasted ad spend and missed opportunities.

Our clients average 4x ROAS within 90 days. At that 
performance level, our fee pays for itself many times over.

What matters more to you — the monthly fee or the results?"
```

### Walk-Away Criteria

**Know When to Say No:**
- Budget under $3K/month (not sustainable)
- They don't value your expertise
- Red flags in communication
- Unrealistic expectations
- Bad fit for your services
- They're disrespectful of your time
- Payment history issues (check references)

**Polite Decline:**
```
"Thank you for considering us. After reviewing your needs 
and budget, I don't think we're the right fit. I'd 
recommend [alternative solution] for what you're looking 
to accomplish.

I wish you the best with your Snapchat marketing!"
```

## 6. Price Increases

### When to Raise Prices

**Good Times:**
- After 6-12 months with client
- When you've proven results
- When adding new services
- When demand exceeds capacity
- Annually (inflation, increased expertise)

**How Much:**
- Existing clients: 10-20% annually
- New clients: Market rate (no grandfathering)
- After major results: 25-50%
- New service offerings: Premium pricing

### How to Communicate Increases

**Email Template:**
```
Subject: Update to Our Services & Pricing

Hi [Client Name],

I wanted to give you a heads-up about an upcoming change 
to our pricing.

Since we started working together [X months/years] ago, 
we've [list achievements: improved ROAS, scaled spend, 
added services, etc.]. We're proud of what we've 
accomplished together.

To continue delivering the results you expect and invest 
in our team and tools, we're adjusting our pricing 
effective [date - give 60 days notice].

Your new monthly investment will be $[new amount], 
reflecting [reason: expanded services, increased scope, 
market adjustment, etc.].

I'm confident this investment will continue driving 
strong returns for [Company]. Let's hop on a call to 
discuss if you have any questions.

Thank you for being a valued client!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Handling Pushback

**If They Push Back:**
```
"I understand this is an increase. Let me share the value 
you've received:

- ROAS improved from X to Y
- Revenue from Snapchat grew from $A to $B
- We've added [new services]
- Your account gets [specific benefits]

At these results, you're getting [X]x return on our fees. 
We're confident we'll continue delivering that value.

That said, I value our partnership. What would make this 
work for you?"
```

**Options:**
- Extend contract length (lock in lower rate)
- Adjust scope to match budget
- Phase in increase over 2-3 months
- Stand firm (if value is clear)

## 7. Payment Terms

### Standard Terms

**Invoicing:**
- Invoice sent: 1st of month (for that month)
- Payment due: Net 15 (15 days from invoice)
- Late fee: 1.5% per month (18% APR)
- Payment methods: ACH, wire, credit card (+3%)

**Ad Spend:**
- Option 1: Client pays Snapchat directly
- Option 2: Agency pays, client reimburses (prepaid)
- Option 3: Agency marks up ad spend (not recommended)

### Contract Protection

**Payment Clause:**
```
"Client agrees to pay all invoices within 15 days of 
receipt. Late payments will incur a 1.5% monthly fee. 
Agency reserves the right to pause work on accounts with 
invoices over 30 days past due."
```

**Termination Clause:**
```
"Either party may terminate this agreement with 30 days 
written notice. Client remains responsible for all fees 
through the termination date. Ad accounts and assets 
will be transferred upon full payment of all outstanding 
invoices."
```

### Collections Process

**Day 1:** Invoice sent
**Day 15:** Payment due
**Day 16:** Friendly reminder email
**Day 25:** Second reminder + phone call
**Day 30:** Formal notice + work pause warning
**Day 35:** Pause work
**Day 45:** Final notice
**Day 60:** Collections/termination
