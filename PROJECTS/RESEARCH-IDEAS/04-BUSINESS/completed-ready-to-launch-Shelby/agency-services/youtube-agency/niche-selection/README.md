## 🏆 Top 5 Agency Niches (Recommended)

### 1. 💼 Business/Entrepreneurship
**Why it wins:**
- High RPM ($10-$35)
- Endless content angles
- Strong sponsor potential
- B2B monetization paths
- Evergreen content

**Sub-niches:**
- Startup advice
- Side hustles
- E-commerce
- Agency building
- Freelancing
- Passive income

**Client profile:** Coaches, consultants, agency owners, course creators

**Avg client budget:** $5K-$15K/month

**Competition level:** Medium (quality bar is low)

## 🎯 Niche Selection Framework

### Step 1: Profitability Score
Rate each niche 1-10 on:

| Factor | Weight | Score |
|--------|--------|-------|
| RPM potential | 25% | /10 |
| Sponsor availability | 20% | /10 |
| Affiliate potential | 15% | /10 |
| Product potential | 15% | /10 |
| Content longevity | 15% | /10 |
| Client budget availability | 10% | /10 |

**Formula:** `(RPM×0.25 + Sponsor×0.20 + Affiliate×0.15 + Product×0.15 + Longevity×0.15 + Budget×0.10) = Total`

**Target score:** 7.5+

---

### Step 2: Competition Analysis

**Use these tools:**
- YouTube search (manual)
- VidIQ (free/paid)
- TubeBuddy (free/paid)
- SocialBlade (free)

**What to look for:**

| Signal | Green Flag | Red Flag |
|--------|------------|----------|
| Top channels | 5-500K subs | All 1M+ subs |
| Upload frequency | Inconsistent | Daily (saturated) |
| Thumbnail quality | Mixed | All professional |
| Video quality | Variable | All high production |
| Engagement | High comments/shares | Low engagement |
| Content gaps | Missing topics | Everything covered |

**Sweet spot:** Top channels have 50K-500K subs with room to grow

---

### Step 3: Client Availability

**Where to find clients in each niche:**

| Niche | Where to find them | Budget signals |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| Business | LinkedIn, Twitter, podcasts | Already running ads, have team |
| SaaS | Product Hunt, Twitter, IndieHackers | Funding raised, hiring |
| Marketing | Agency Facebook groups, Twitter | Case studies, client results |
| Finance | LinkedIn, conferences, podcasts | AUM disclosed, team size |
| Education | Course platforms, email lists | Existing students, revenue shares |

**Qualifying questions:**
1. "What's your current monthly revenue?"
2. "Are you currently investing in content/marketing?"
3. "What's your customer lifetime value?"
4. "Do you have a product/service to monetize viewers?"

**Minimum viable client:**
- $20K+/month revenue
- Already spending $2K+/month on marketing
- Has a clear monetization path
- Understands long-term investment

## 📊 Niche Validation Checklist

Before committing to a niche, confirm:

- [ ] RPM is $5+ (check SocialBlade estimates)
- [ ] At least 10 active sponsors in the niche
- [ ] Affiliate programs available ($50+ commissions)
- [ ] Top 10 channels aren't all 1M+ subs
- [ ] You can identify 50+ video ideas immediately
- [ ] At least 3 potential clients identified
- [ ] Clients have budget ($3K+/month)
- [ ] Content can be evergreen (not just trending)
- [ ] You have expertise or access to expertise
- [ ] Compliance/legal issues are manageable

**Score 9-10:** Green light, proceed
**Score 7-8:** Proceed with caution, validate further
**Score <7:** Consider different niche

## 🚫 Niches to Avoid (For Agencies)

### Low RPM + High Competition
- Gaming (RPM $2-$8, oversaturated)
- Entertainment commentary (RPM $2-$10, personality-dependent)
- General vlogs (RPM $2-$8, hard to scale)
- Pranks/challenges (RPM $2-$6, brand safety issues)

### Compliance Nightmares
- Medical advice (licensing required, liability)
- Legal advice (bar association rules)
- Get-rich-quick schemes (platform strikes, bans)
- Adult content (demonetization)

### Hard to Monetize
- Kids content (COPPA restrictions)
- Music only (copyright issues)
- Compilation channels (reused content strikes)
- Faceless low-effort (demonetization risk)

## 🎯 Niche Positioning Strategies

### 1. The Specialist
**Approach:** Go deep on one sub-niche
**Example:** "SaaS marketing for B2B startups"
**Pros:** Less competition, higher authority
**Cons:** Smaller total audience

### 2. The Generalist
**Approach:** Cover broad topic with unique angle
**Example:** "Business growth for creators"
**Pros:** Larger audience, more content ideas
**Cons:** More competition

### 3. The Contrarian
**Approach:** Challenge common beliefs
**Example:** "Why most startups fail (and how to not)"
**Pros:** High engagement, memorable
**Cons:** Can alienate some viewers

### 4. The Case Study Channel
**Approach:** Document real experiments
**Example:** "Building a $10K/month business in public"
**Pros:** Highly engaging, builds trust
**Cons:** Requires real business to document

### 5. The Interview Show
**Approach:** Feature experts/guests
**Example:** "Conversations with 7-figure founders"
**Pros:** Leverages guest audiences, easier content
**Cons:** Scheduling, guest quality variance
