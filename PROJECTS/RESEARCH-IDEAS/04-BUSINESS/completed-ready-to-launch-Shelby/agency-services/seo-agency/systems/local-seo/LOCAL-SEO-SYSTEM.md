## Phase 1: Google Business Profile Optimization

### GBP Setup Checklist

**Basic Information:**
- [ ] Business name (exact, no keywords stuffed)
- [ ] Address (consistent with website)
- [ ] Phone number (local number preferred)
- [ ] Website URL (homepage or location page)
- [ ] Business hours (accurate, including holidays)
- [ ] Category (primary + secondary)
- [ ] Service areas (if service-area business)

---

### Category Optimization

**Primary Category:**
- Choose most specific category available
- This is the #1 ranking factor for GBP
- Don't stuff keywords in business name

**Secondary Categories:**
- Add all relevant categories (up to 10)
- Be specific, not generic
- Example: "Italian Restaurant" not just "Restaurant"

---

### Business Description

**Best Practices:**
- 750 characters max
- Include primary keywords naturally
- Highlight unique value proposition
- Include services offered
- Include service areas
- No promotional language or links

**Template:**
```
[Business Name] is a [primary category] serving [city/area] 
since [year]. We specialize in [core services] and are known 
for [unique differentiator].

Our team of [credibility indicator] is committed to 
[value proposition]. We serve customers in [service areas].

Visit us at [location] or call [phone] to [CTA].
```

---

### Photos & Videos

**Photo Requirements:**
- [ ] Logo (square, 720x720 minimum)
- [ ] Cover photo (landscape, 1080x608)
- [ ] Interior photos (5-10)
- [ ] Exterior photos (3-5)
- [ ] Team photos (3-5)
- [ ] Product/service photos (10-20)
- [ ] Before/after photos (if applicable)

**Best Practices:**
- High-quality, well-lit photos
- Show real business (no stock photos)
- Update monthly with new photos
- Add geo-tags when possible
- Videos: 30 seconds max, under 100MB

**Impact:** Businesses with photos get 42% more requests for directions

---

### Products & Services

**Products Section:**
- Add individual products with photos
- Include descriptions and prices
- Organize by category
- Update regularly

**Services Section:**
- List all services offered
- Include descriptions
- Add prices if possible
- Organize logically

---

### GBP Posts

**Post Types:**
1. **What's New** - General updates
2. **Events** - Upcoming events
3. **Offers** - Special promotions
4. **Products** - Product highlights

**Posting Schedule:**
- Minimum: 1x/week
- Optimal: 3-4x/week
- Include call-to-action
- Add high-quality photos
- Use relevant keywords naturally

**Post Template:**
```
[Attention-grabbing headline]

[2-3 sentences about the update/offer/event]

[Clear CTA: Call now, Book online, Learn more, etc.]

[High-quality photo]
```

---

### Q&A Section

**Strategy:**
1. Add common questions yourself (as business owner)
2. Provide detailed, helpful answers
3. Monitor and respond to new questions quickly
4. Upvote helpful customer questions

**Common Questions to Add:**
- "What are your hours?"
- "Do you offer [specific service]?"
- "Is parking available?"
- "Do you accept insurance?"
- "What's your price range?"

---

### Attributes

**Select All That Apply:**
- Accessibility features
- Payment options
- Amenities
- Crowd information
- Planning requirements
- Service options

**Impact:** Helps with filtering and relevance

**Types:**

- **Unstructured:** News articles, blogs, social media

### Citation Audit Process

**Step 1: Find Existing Citations**
- Use tools: BrightLocal, Whitespark, Moz Local
- Manual Google searches: "[Business Name] [City]"
- Check major directories individually

**Step 2: Check NAP Consistency**
- Name: Exact match everywhere
- Address: Same format everywhere
- Phone: Same format everywhere
- Website: Same URL everywhere

**Step 3: Fix Inconsistencies**
- Claim unclaimed listings
- Update incorrect information
- Remove duplicate listings
- Delete low-quality/spam citations

---

### Citation Building Schedule

**Month 1:**
- Audit existing citations
- Fix major inconsistencies
- Submit to data aggregators
- Claim top 10 directories

**Month 2:**
- Build 20-30 industry citations
- Build 10-20 local citations
- Monitor for duplicates

**Month 3+:**
- Ongoing citation monitoring
- Add new citations as discovered
- Quarterly audits

## Phase 3: Review Management

### Review Strategy

**Goals:**
- 50+ reviews (minimum for credibility)
- 4.5+ average rating
- Recent reviews (within 30 days)
- Respond to all reviews

---

### Getting More Reviews

#### In-Person Requests

**Best Practices:**
- Ask at peak satisfaction moments
- Make it easy (QR code, direct link)
- Train staff to ask
- Don't incentivize (against Google's guidelines)

**Script:**
```
"We're so glad you're happy with our service! 
Would you mind taking 30 seconds to leave us a 
quick review on Google? It really helps us grow."
```

---

#### Email Requests

**Timing:**
- Service businesses: 1-2 hours after service
- E-commerce: 3-5 days after delivery
- B2B: 1 week after project completion

**Email Template:**
```
Subject: How did we do, [Name]?

Hi [Name],

Thanks for choosing [Business Name]! We hope you're 
happy with [service/product].

Would you mind taking 30 seconds to leave us a review 
on Google? It helps us continue to serve customers 
like you.

[Review Link Button]

Thank you for your support!

[Your Name]
[Business Name]
```

---

#### SMS Requests

**Template:**
```
Hi [Name]! Thanks for visiting [Business]. Would you 
mind leaving us a quick review? It takes 30 seconds: 
[Short Link]

Thanks! -[Business Name]
```

**Response Rate:** 3-5x higher than email

---

#### Review Request Cards

**For Physical Locations:**
- Leave cards at tables/counters
- Include QR code
- Simple instructions
- Train staff to mention

---

### Responding to Reviews

#### Positive Reviews

**Template:**
```
Hi [Name],

Thank you so much for the kind words! We're thrilled 
that you [specific thing they mentioned]. 

Our team works hard to [value proposition], and it's 
great to hear that showed through.

We look forward to serving you again soon!

[Your Name]
[Title]
[Business Name]
```

**Best Practices:**
- Respond within 48 hours
- Mention something specific
- Keep it personal
- Include keywords naturally

---

#### Negative Reviews

**Template:**
```
Hi [Name],

Thank you for your feedback, and I'm sorry to hear 
about your experience. This doesn't reflect our 
typical standards.

I'd like to make this right. Please contact me 
directly at [phone/email] so we can discuss this 
further.

[Your Name]
[Title]
[Business Name]
```

**Best Practices:**
- Respond within 24 hours
- Stay calm and professional
- Don't argue publicly
- Take it offline
- Show you care about fixing it

## Phase 4: Local Content Strategy

### Location Pages

**For Multi-Location Businesses:**

**Each Location Page Should Include:**
- Unique content (no duplication)
- NAP (name, address, phone)
- Google Maps embed
- Location-specific photos
- Services offered at that location
- Staff introductions
- Local testimonials
- Local landmarks/neighborhoods
- Driving directions
- Hours of operation
- Local phone number
- Schema markup

**URL Structure:**
```
domain.com/locations/city-state/
domain.com/locations/new-york-ny/
domain.com/locations/los-angeles-ca/
```

---

### Local Content Topics

#### Neighborhood Guides
- "Best [Service] in [Neighborhood]"
- "[Neighborhood] Business Spotlight"
- "Things to Do in [Neighborhood]"

#### Local Events
- Event coverage
- Sponsorship announcements
- Community involvement

#### Local News
- Industry news with local angle
- Business milestones
- New hires/expansions

#### Local FAQs
- "How to [solve problem] in [City]"
- "[City] regulations for [industry]"
- "Best time to [activity] in [City]"

---

### Local Keyword Targeting
