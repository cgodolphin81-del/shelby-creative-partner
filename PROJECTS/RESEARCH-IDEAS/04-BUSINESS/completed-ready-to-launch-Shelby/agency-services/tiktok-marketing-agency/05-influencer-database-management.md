## 1. Building Your Influencer Database

### Database Structure

**Essential Fields:**
```
- Influencer Name/Handle
- Follower Count
- Engagement Rate
- Niche/Category
- Location/Country
- Audience Demographics
- Average Views
- Content Style
- Past Brand Collabs
- Rate Card
- Contact Info
- Status (Prospect, Contacted, Active, Past)
- Notes/Tags
- Performance History
```

**Spreadsheet Template:**
| Handle | Followers | ER% | Niche | Location | Avg Views | Rates | Contact | Status | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-----|-------|----------|-----------|-------|---------|--------|-------|
| @user1 | 150K | 8.2% | Fitness | US | 45K | $2K/video | email@.com | Active | Great engagement |

### Database Categories

**By Follower Count:**
- **Nano (1K-10K)**: $50-500/post, highest engagement
- **Micro (10K-100K)**: $500-5K/post, balanced reach/engagement
- **Mid-Tier (100K-500K)**: $5K-20K/post, solid reach
- **Macro (500K-1M)**: $20K-50K/post, broad reach
- **Mega (1M+)**: $50K+/post, celebrity level

**By Content Type:**
- Educational/Tutorials
- Entertainment/Humor
- Lifestyle/Vlog
- Product Reviews
- Challenges/Trends
- Behind-the-Scenes

**By Audience Demographics:**
- Age groups (18-24, 25-34, 35-44, etc.)
- Gender split
- Geographic concentration
- Income level indicators

### Database Growth Strategy

**Month 1-2: Build Foundation**
- Add 50-100 nano/micro influencers
- Focus on your niche
- Document basic info
- Identify 10-15 dream partners

**Month 3-6: Expand & Qualify**
- Add 100-200 more influencers
- Deep-dive on top 50
- Track performance history
- Build relationships with top tier

**Month 6+: Optimize**
- Maintain 300-500 influencer database
- Regular updates on metrics
- Performance tracking
- Relationship nurturing

## 3. Vetting & Qualification Framework

### Red Flags to Avoid

**Follower Quality:**
- ❌ Sudden follower spikes (bought followers)
- ❌ Low engagement relative to followers (<1%)
- ❌ Generic comments ("nice!", "great post")
- ❌ High follower count but low views
- ❌ Suspicious follower growth patterns

**Content Quality:**
- ❌ Inconsistent posting
- ❌ Poor video quality (unless intentional)
- ❌ Plagiarized content
- ❌ Controversial/offensive past content
- ❌ Overly promotional (no organic content)

**Audience Authenticity:**
- ❌ Audience in wrong demographics
- ❌ High percentage of followers from suspicious locations
- ❌ Mismatched audience interests
- ❌ Bot-like engagement patterns

**Professionalism:**
- ❌ Poor communication
- ❌ Unresponsive to emails
- ❌ Unrealistic expectations
- ❌ Negative reviews from past brands
- ❌ Contract disputes history

### Vetting Checklist

**Pre-Outreach Screening:**
```
[ ] Follower count: [_____]
[ ] Engagement rate: [_____]% (target: 3%+)
[ ] Average views: [_____]
[ ] Posting frequency: [_____]/week
[ ] Content quality: [Good/Excellent]
[ ] Audience demographics match: [Yes/No]
[ ] Past brand collabs: [Number]
[ ] Content style aligns: [Yes/No]
[ ] No red flags: [Yes/No]
[ ] Contact info available: [Yes/No]
```

**Deep-Dive Analysis:**
```
[ ] 3-month engagement trend: [Stable/Growing/Declining]
[ ] Comment quality: [Real/Spammy]
[ ] Audience growth pattern: [Organic/Suspicious]
[ ] Content consistency: [High/Medium/Low]
[ ] Brand safety: [Clear/Questionable]
[ ] Exclusivity conflicts: [None/Potential/Confirmed]
[ ] Rate within budget: [Yes/No]
[ ] Timeline flexibility: [Yes/No]
```

### Engagement Rate Benchmarks

**By Follower Count:**
- **Nano (1K-10K)**: 8-15% (highest)
- **Micro (10K-100K)**: 5-10%
- **Mid-Tier (100K-500K)**: 3-7%
- **Macro (500K-1M)**: 2-5%
- **Mega (1M+)**: 1-3%

**Formula:**
```
Engagement Rate = (Likes + Comments + Shares) ÷ Followers × 100
```

**View-to-Follower Ratio:**
- Excellent: 30%+ (45K views on 150K followers)
- Good: 20-30%
- Average: 10-20%
- Poor: <10%

### Audience Demographics Check

**Use TikTok Analytics (if accessible):**
- Age distribution
- Gender split
- Top locations
- Active times

**Third-Party Tools:**
- **Modash**: Audience insights
- **HypeAuditor**: Audience quality
- **Exolyt**: Demographic data

**What to Look For:**
- Age matches target customer
- Geographic concentration in target markets
- Gender split aligns with product
- Active during your posting times

### Past Performance Review

**Check Previous Brand Collabs:**
- How did the content perform?
- Did it drive engagement?
- Any visible results (sales, signups)?
- Did they disclose properly (#ad, #sponsored)?

**Content Quality Assessment:**
- Consistency in quality
- Creative skills
- Editing ability
- On-camera presence
- Authenticity

**Professionalism Indicators:**
- On-time delivery
- Communication responsiveness
- Flexibility with revisions
- Contract adherence
- Relationship with brands

## 5. Negotiation Strategies

### Pricing Models

**Per Post/Video:**
- Most common for one-off campaigns
- Nano: $50-500
- Micro: $500-5,000
- Mid-Tier: $5,000-20,000
- Macro: $20,000-50,000+

**Per Month (Retainer):**
- Better for ongoing partnerships
- 20-30% discount vs. per-post
- Example: $10K/month for 4 posts + stories
- Includes priority scheduling

**% of Ad Spend:**
- For influencer-managed ad campaigns
- Typically 10-20% of ad spend
- Requires trust and transparency

**Performance-Based:**
- Base fee + commission on sales
- Commission: 5-20% of attributed revenue
- Requires tracking links/codes
- Higher risk, higher reward

**Product Compensation:**
- Common for nano/micro influencers
- Free products + small fee
- Full product line + commission
- Good for testing partnerships

### Rate Card Negotiation

**When Influencer Sends Rate Card:**
- Review against your budget
- Compare to market rates
- Negotiate based on:
  - Volume (more posts = lower per-post rate)
  - Usage rights (longer license = higher cost)
  - Exclusivity (category exclusivity = premium)
  - Timeline (rush jobs = premium)

**Negotiation Scripts:**

**If Too Expensive:**
```
"Thanks for sending over your rate card! We're really excited about the possibility of working together. 

Our budget for this campaign is [amount], which is a bit below your rates. Would you be flexible on this, especially if we're looking at [X posts] or [longer usage rights]?

We love your content and think this would be a great partnership - hoping we can make the numbers work!"
```

**If They Won't Budge:**
```
"I understand your rates reflect your value and quality. 

Could we explore a few options:
1. Adjusting deliverables (fewer posts, shorter video)
2. Product compensation + reduced fee
3. Performance-based component

Or we could keep you in mind for future campaigns when our budget allows. Would any of those options work?"
```

**For Long-Term Partnerships:**
```
"For a 6-month partnership with [X posts/month], we're looking at [total budget]. This breaks down to [per-post rate], which includes [usage rights, exclusivity, etc.].

This represents a [X%] discount from your standard rates, but provides guaranteed work over 6 months. Thoughts?"
```

### Usage Rights Negotiation

**Standard Usage (Included):**
- 30-day campaign use
- Their platforms only
- Non-exclusive

**Extended Usage (Additional Cost):**
- **3-6 months**: +25-50% of fee
- **1 year**: +50-100% of fee
- **Perpetual**: +100-200% of fee

**Platform Expansion:**
- TikTok only → All social: +20%
- Social only → Paid ads: +50%
- Paid ads → TV/OOH: +100%+

**Exclusivity:**
- Category exclusivity (30 days): +25%
- Category exclusivity (90 days): +50%
- Category exclusivity (1 year): +100%

### Payment Terms

**Standard Terms:**
- 50% upfront, 50% on delivery
- Or 100% upon completion
- Net 15 or Net 30 for agencies

**For New Partners:**
- 100% upfront (builds trust)
- Or 50/50 split

**For Long-Term:**
- Net 30 after invoice
- Monthly retainer, first of month

**Payment Methods:**
- Bank transfer
- PayPal
- Wise (international)
- Platform escrow (some marketplaces)


### Disclosure Requirements

**FTC Guidelines (US):**
- Clear and conspicuous disclosure
- #ad, #sponsored, #partner at beginning
- Cannot hide in hashtag wall
- Must be visible without clicking "more"

**TikTok's Requirements:**
- Use TikTok's paid partnership label
- Disclose in caption
- Both required for compliance

**Best Practices:**
- #ad or #sponsored in first line
- "Paid partnership with [Brand]" label
- Verbal disclosure in video ("This video is sponsored by...")
- Clear, unambiguous language

### Intellectual Property

**What Brand Typically Owns:**
- License to use content for agreed period/platforms
- Not ownership of content (influencer retains)

**What Influencer Typically Keeps:**
- Ownership of content
- Right to showcase in portfolio
- Right to repost after campaign ends

**Work-for-Hire (Rare):**
- Brand owns content outright
- Influencer gets higher fee
- Influencer cannot use in portfolio
- Usually only for macro/mega influencers

### ROI Calculation

**Simple ROI:**
```
ROI = (Revenue - Investment) ÷ Investment × 100

Example:
Revenue: $50,000
Influencer Fee: $10,000
ROI = ($50K - $10K) ÷ $10K × 100 = 400%
```

**ROAS:**
```
ROAS = Revenue ÷ Ad Spend

Example:
Revenue: $50,000
Fee: $10,000
ROAS = $50K ÷ $10K = 5x
```

**Break-Even Analysis:**
```
Break-Even ROAS = 1 ÷ Profit Margin

Example:
50% profit margin
Break-Even ROAS = 1 ÷ 0.50 = 2x
Anything above 2x is profitable
```

## 9. Relationship Management

### Building Long-Term Partnerships

**Signs of Good Fit:**
- On-time delivery
- Quality content
- Good communication
- Engaged audience response
- Professionalism
- Creative collaboration

**Retention Strategies:**
- Pay on time (or early)
- Treat as partner, not vendor
- Provide creative freedom
- Share results and feedback
- Offer renewal bonuses
- Invite to exclusive events
- Feature on brand channels

### Ambassador Programs

**Structure:**
- 3-12 month contracts
- Guaranteed monthly fee
- X posts per month
- Exclusive category rights
- Performance bonuses
- Early access to products
- Free product allowance

**Tier System:**
- **Tier 1 (Top)**: 10-20 posts/year, $5K-20K/month, exclusivity
- **Tier 2 (Active)**: 4-8 posts/year, $2K-5K/month, some exclusivity
- **Tier 3 (Occasional)**: 2-4 posts/year, project-based, no exclusivity

### Relationship Maintenance

**Regular Check-Ins:**
- Monthly: Active partners
- Quarterly: Past partners
- Annually: All database contacts

**Value-Add Communication:**
- Share campaign results
- Send exclusive updates
- Invite to product launches
- Provide early access
- Send branded swag
- Personal notes on milestones

**Community Building:**
- Creator Slack/Discord
- Monthly creator calls
- Annual creator summit
- Peer networking opportunities

### Handling Underperformance

**If Content Underperforms:**
- Analyze why (audience, timing, creative?)
- Discuss openly but constructively
- Offer support for future content
- Consider one-time vs. ongoing relationship

**If Influencer Is Unresponsive:**
- Send 2-3 follow-ups
- Try alternative contact methods
- Set clear deadline
- Move on if no response

**If Quality Is Poor:**
- Provide specific feedback
- Request revisions (if in contract)
- Document issues
- Consider non-renewal

### Termination Clauses

**For Cause:**
- Breach of contract
- Poor quality (beyond revisions)
- Unprofessional behavior
- Controversial content that harms brand

**Without Cause:**
- 30-day notice
- Pay for work completed
- Return unused products
- Usage rights for existing content

## Budget Allocation Guide

### By Campaign Type

**Brand Awareness:**
- 60%: Macro/Mega influencers
- 30%: Mid-tier influencers
- 10%: Nano/Micro influencers

**Consideration/Engagement:**
- 20%: Macro/Mega
- 50%: Mid-tier
- 30%: Nano/Micro

**Conversion/Sales:**
- 10%: Macro/Mega
- 30%: Mid-tier
- 60%: Nano/Micro

### By Follower Tier (Annual Budget: $100K)

**Conservative:**
