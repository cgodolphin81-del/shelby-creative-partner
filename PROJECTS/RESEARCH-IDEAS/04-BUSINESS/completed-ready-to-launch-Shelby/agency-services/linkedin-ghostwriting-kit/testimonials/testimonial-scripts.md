# TESTIMONIAL SCRIPTS

---

## HOW TO USE THESE SCRIPTS

**When to request testimonials:**
- After 30 days (early win)
- After 90 days (major milestone)
- After a specific breakthrough (viral post, big deal closed, etc.)
- When ending engagement (on good terms)

**How to ask:**
1. Make the ask specific and easy
2. Provide a draft they can edit (use these scripts)
3. Offer multiple formats (written, video, LinkedIn recommendation)
4. Make it about THEIR story, not your service

**Best practices:**
- Ask when they're happiest (after a win)
- Make it low-friction (offer to write it for them)
- Be specific about what you need
- Always get permission to use publicly

## TIPS FOR GETTING BETTER TESTIMONIALS

1. **Ask at the peak** — Right after a win, not during a stressful period
2. **Make it specific** — Vague testimonials are forgettable. Guide them to specifics.
3. **Offer to write it** — Most people want to help but don't have time. Do the work for them.
4. **Give options** — Written, video, LinkedIn recommendation. Different strokes for different folks.
5. **Show examples** — People mirror what you show them.
6. **Follow up (gently)** — Most testimonials come from follow-up #2 or #3.
7. **Say thank you** — Send a small gift or handwritten note after they provide one.
8. **Keep them updated** — Tell them how their testimonial helped you (they'll feel good about it).

## TESTIMONIAL FORMATS TO COLLECT

- **Written quote** (for website, one-pager)
- **Video testimonial** (1-2 minutes, for landing pages)
- **LinkedIn recommendation** (for your profile)
- **Case study interview** (30-minute recorded call)
- **Tweet/LinkedIn post** (public endorsement)
- **Email testimonial** (for sales conversations)

**Pro Tip:** Create a "Testimonial Bank" folder with all testimonials organized by:
- Client name
- Date
- Type (written/video/etc.)
- Key results mentioned
- Permission level (public/private/anonymous)
