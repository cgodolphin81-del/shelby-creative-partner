## 2. Revenue Share Models

### 2.1 Performance-Based Pricing

For clients who prefer lower upfront costs with performance incentives.

---

#### **HYBRID MODEL** - Lower Base + Revenue Share

**Structure:**
- Base Fee: 50% of standard package rate
- Revenue Share: 30% of influencer revenue
- Minimum Guarantee: 25% of standard package rate

**Example (Growth Package):**
```
Standard Package: $12,000/month
Hybrid Structure:
- Base Fee: $6,000/month
- Revenue Share: 30% of all revenue
- Minimum: $3,000/month (if revenue is very low)

Scenario A - $20,000 revenue:
$6,000 base + $6,000 (30% of $20K) = $12,000 total

Scenario B - $50,000 revenue:
$6,000 base + $15,000 (30% of $50K) = $21,000 total

Scenario C - $5,000 revenue:
$6,000 base + $1,500 (30% of $5K) = $7,500
But minimum applies: $9,000 total
```

**Best For:**
- Confident in monetization potential
- Clients with limited upfront budget
- High-growth potential influencers

**Contract Terms:**
- 12-month minimum (revenue share requires time)
- Monthly revenue reporting required
- Audit rights for agency
- Revenue definition clearly specified

---

#### **PURE REVENUE SHARE** - No Base Fee

**Structure:**
- Base Fee: $0
- Revenue Share: 40-50% of influencer revenue
- Setup Fee: $5,000-15,000 (one-time)

**Example:**
```
Setup Fee: $10,000 (one-time)
Revenue Share: 45% of all revenue
No monthly minimum

Month 1 - $5,000 revenue:
Agency: $2,250
Client: $2,750

Month 6 - $50,000 revenue:
Agency: $22,500
Client: $27,500
```

**Best For:**
- Agency taking significant risk
- Exceptional growth potential
- Strategic partnerships

**Contract Terms:**
- 24-month minimum
- Detailed revenue tracking
- Monthly settlements
- Exit clauses and buyout options

---

### 2.2 Revenue Definition

**What Counts as Revenue:**

✅ **Included:**
- Brand partnership payments
- Platform monetization (Creator Fund, ad revenue)
- Subscription revenue (OnlyFans, Patreon, etc.)
- Affiliate commissions
- Merchandise sales (net after costs)
- Tips and donations
- Licensing fees
- Appearance fees

❌ **Excluded:**
- Client's initial investment
- Reimbursed expenses
- Taxes collected
- Platform fees (before revenue share calculation)

**Revenue Share Calculation:**
```
Gross Revenue
- Platform Fees (OnlyFans 20%, etc.)
- Payment Processing (Stripe 2.9%, etc.)
- Direct Costs (merch production, shipping)
= Net Revenue

Net Revenue × Revenue Share % = Agency Fee
```

## 5. Merchandise Pricing

### 5.1 Agency Fees

**Merchandise Management:**

| Service | Fee Structure |
|---------|---------------|
| **Design Only** | $500-2,000 per design |
| **Full Management** | 20% of net revenue |
| **Fulfillment Only** | 10% of net revenue |
| **Consulting** | $150-300/hour |

**Net Revenue Calculation:**
```
Retail Price
- Product Cost
- Shipping Cost
- Platform Fees
- Payment Processing
= Net Revenue

Net Revenue × Agency % = Management Fee
```

**Example:**
```
T-Shirt sold for $35
- Product cost: $12
- Shipping: $5
- Platform fee: $2
- Payment processing: $1
= Net: $15

Agency fee (20%): $3
Client profit: $12
```

### 6.2 Contract Variations

**Exclusive vs. Non-Exclusive:**

| Term | Exclusive | Non-Exclusive |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **Definition** | Client works only with this agency | Client can work with multiple agencies |
| **Pricing** | 10-20% discount | Standard pricing |
| **Commitment** | Longer (12-24 months) | Shorter (3-6 months) |
| **Termination** | Stricter (60-90 days) | Easier (30 days) |

**Recommendation:** Start non-exclusive, offer discount for exclusive commitment.

---

### 6.3 Exit Clauses

**Buyout Options:**

```
- Option to buy out future revenue share

POST-TERMINATION:
- Agency retains commission on deals closed during term
- Revenue share continues for [X] months on existing subscribers
- Content usage rights: [negotiated terms]
```

## 7. Pricing Strategy Tips

### 7.1 Positioning

**Premium Positioning:**
- Higher prices signal quality
- Attract serious clients
- Better profit margins
- More resources per client

**Value Positioning:**
- Competitive pricing
- Higher volume
- Market penetration
- Build portfolio

**Recommendation:** Start premium, prove value, maintain pricing.

---

### 7.2 Negotiation Guidelines

**Discount Authority:**

| Team Member | Max Discount |
|-------------|--------------|
| Account Manager | 5% |
| Sales Director | 10% |
| VP/Partner | 20% |
| CEO | 25%+ |

**Discount Guidelines:**
- Never discount without getting something in return
- Trade discounts for: longer contracts, upfront payment, case study rights, exclusivity
- Document all discounts and reasoning
- Review discount patterns quarterly

**Common Negotiation Trades:**
```
Client wants: 20% discount
Agency counters: 10% discount + 18-month contract (vs 12)

Client wants: 20% discount
Agency counters: 10% discount + 50% upfront (vs monthly)

Client wants: 20% discount
Agency counters: 5% discount + case study + testimonial
```

---

### 7.3 Price Increases

**When to Raise Prices:**
- Consistently booked solid
- Adding significant value
- Market rates have increased
- Inflation/cost increases
- Annual review (standard practice)

**How to Implement:**
- 30-60 days notice
- Grandfather existing clients (optional)
- Communicate value added
- Offer annual lock-in at current rate

**Sample Communication:**
```
Subject: 2024 Pricing Update

Hi [Client],

I'm writing to share our 2024 pricing updates. After [X] years 
of serving you and significant investments in [improvements], 
we're adjusting our rates effective [date].

Your [package] will increase from $[X] to $[Y] per month.

As a valued client, you have the option to:
1. Lock in current rates with annual prepayment
2. Upgrade to [new package] at promotional pricing
3. Continue at new rates with enhanced [benefits]

We're committed to delivering exceptional value and look 
forward to continuing our partnership.

Best,
[Name]
```
