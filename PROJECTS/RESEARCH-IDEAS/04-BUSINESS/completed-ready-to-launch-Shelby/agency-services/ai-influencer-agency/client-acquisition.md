# Client Acquisition Strategy
## Brand & Creator Acquisition Playbook

---

## Overview

This document provides comprehensive strategies for acquiring two types of clients:
1. **Brands** - Companies wanting to partner with AI influencers
2. **Creators/Investors** - Individuals wanting to launch AI influencers

---

### 2.2 Cold Email Strategy

**Email Volume Goals:**
- 100-200 emails/week per sales rep
- 20-30% open rate target
- 3-5% response rate target
- 1-2% conversion to call

**Email Sequence (5 Touches):**

```
DAY 1: Initial Outreach
Subject: [Personalized - see templates below]

DAY 3: Follow-up #1
Subject: Re: [Original subject]
- Brief, add value

DAY 7: Follow-up #2
Subject: Quick question
- Single question, easy to answer

DAY 14: Follow-up #3
Subject: [Case study relevant to them]
- Share relevant success story

DAY 21: Break-up Email
Subject: Should I close your file?
- Final attempt, often gets response
```

### 3.4 Sales Call Framework

**30-Minute Discovery Call Structure:**

```
MINUTES 1-5: Rapport & Agenda
- Build connection
- Confirm agenda
- Set expectations

MINUTES 5-15: Discovery
- Their goals and motivation
- Current situation
- Budget and timeline
- Concerns and objections

MINUTES 15-25: Presentation
- How we solve their problem
- Relevant case studies
- Investment and ROI
- Next steps

MINUTES 25-30: Close
- Address final questions
- Present offer
- Ask for commitment
- Schedule onboarding
```

**Key Questions to Ask:**
1. "What got you interested in AI influencers?"
2. "What are your financial goals with this?"
3. "What's your timeline for launch?"
4. "What's your investment budget?"
5. "What concerns do you have about moving forward?"

**Common Objections & Responses:**

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "It's too expensive" | "What would you say is an acceptable ROI timeline? Most clients recoup in 6-9 months." |
| "I'm not sure AI influencers work" | "Fair concern. Let me share 3 case studies with real numbers..." |
| "I need to think about it" | "Of course. What specific concerns do you want to think through?" |
| "Can't I do this myself?" | "You could. Our clients value the time savings and expertise. What's your time worth?" |

## 4. Sales Team Structure

### 4.1 Team Roles

**Early Stage (0-10 clients):**
- Founder handles all sales
- 1 Account Manager for onboarding
- Contractor for lead gen (optional)

**Growth Stage (10-50 clients):**
- 1 Sales Director (manage + sell)
- 2-3 Account Executives (closers)
- 1-2 SDRs (lead gen)
- 2-3 Account Managers (onboarding/success)

**Scale Stage (50+ clients):**
- VP of Sales
- Sales Managers (per team)
- 5-10 Account Executives
- 3-5 SDRs
- 5-10 Account Managers
- Sales Operations

---

### 4.2 Compensation Structure

**SDR (Sales Development Rep):**
- Base: $45,000-60,000
- Commission: $50-100 per qualified meeting
- OTE (On-Target Earnings): $60,000-80,000

**Account Executive:**
- Base: $60,000-80,000
- Commission: 10-15% of first-year contract
- OTE: $100,000-200,000

**Sales Director:**
- Base: $100,000-140,000
- Commission: 5% of team revenue + overrides
- OTE: $150,000-300,000

**Account Manager:**
- Base: $50,000-70,000
- Bonus: 5-10% based on retention/upsells
- OTE: $60,000-90,000

- [ ] Launch cold email (50/day)
- [ ] Start LinkedIn outreach
- [ ] Publish 4 blog posts

- [ ] Create lead magnet

### Month 2: Optimization

**Week 5-6:**
- [ ] Analyze email performance, iterate
- [ ] Scale LinkedIn outreach (100/day)
- [ ] Launch webinar series
- [ ] Optimize paid ads
- [ ] Close first 3-5 clients

**Week 7-8:**
- [ ] Double down on winning channels
- [ ] Create case studies from early wins

**Week 9-10:**
- [ ] Scale email to 200/day
