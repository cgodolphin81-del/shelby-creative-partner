# Client Acquisition Strategy

**How to find, attract, and close DTC brands doing $50K-500K/month**

---

## Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

### Primary Target

| Criteria | Specification |
|----------|---------------|
| **Revenue** | $50K - $500K/month |
| **Model** | DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) |
| **Platform** | Shopify (preferred), WooCommerce |
| **AOV** | $40+ |
| **Margin** | 60%+ gross margin |
| **Ad Spend** | $10K-100K/month currently |
| **Team** | 5-50 employees |
| **Stage** | Post-product-market fit, ready to scale |
| **Funding** | Bootstrapped or Series A |

### Secondary Target

| Criteria | Specification |
|----------|---------------|
| **Revenue** | $30K - $50K/month |
| **Growth** | 20%+ month-over-month |
| **Potential** | Clear path to $100K+/month |
| **Founder** | Growth-minded, investment-ready |

### Red Flags (Avoid)

- Revenue < $30K/month (not ready)
- Revenue > $1M/month (enterprise needs)
- Margin < 50% (can't support ad spend)
- Single product (limited scaling)
- Founder is bottleneck (won't delegate)
- Previously burned by 3+ agencies (trust issues)
- Looking for "magic bullet" (unrealistic)
- Won't share data/access (partnership blockers)

#### Outreach Sequences

**Email Sequence (5 emails over 14 days)**

```

Not sure if this is relevant for you, but if you're looking to 
scale past your current revenue, I'd love to share what worked.

Worth a quick call?

Best,
[Your Name]

Day 3 - Value Add
Subject: [Competitor] is doing this...

Hi [First Name],

Quick observation - I noticed [Competitor] is running [specific ad 
tactic] that's probably driving solid results for them.

This is the kind of thing we test for our e-com partners. If you're 
ever curious about what's working in your category, happy to share.

No pitch - just thought it was interesting.

[Your Name]

Day 7 - Social Proof
Subject: [Result] in 90 days

[First Name],

Just wrapped up a project with [Brand] - similar to you, they were 
stuck at around [$X]/month.

In 90 days, we helped them:
- Scale to [$Y]/month

The playbook is repeatable. If scaling is a priority this quarter,

[Your Name]

[Link to free resource/case study]

All the best,
[Your Name]

Day 14 - Final Value
Subject: One more thing

[First Name],

Last email from me. Before I go, I wanted to share this:

[Specific, actionable tip relevant to their business]

We've seen this drive [X]% improvement for similar brands.

If you ever want to chat growth, you know where to find me.

Cheers,
[Your Name]
```

- Offer value (resource, introduction, insight)

## Sales Process

### Stage 1: Discovery Call (30 minutes)

**Agenda:**
1. Introductions (5 min)
2. Their story & business (10 min)
3. Current challenges (10 min)
4. Next steps (5 min)

**Key Questions:**
- "Tell me about [Brand] - how did you get started?"
- "What's working well right now?"
- "What's your biggest growth challenge?"
- "What have you tried to solve this?"
- "What would success look like in 90 days?"
- "What's your current marketing budget?"
- "Have you worked with agencies before?"

**Qualification Criteria:**
- [ ] Revenue $50K+/month
- [ ] Decision maker on call
- [ ] Budget available ($10K+/month)
- [ ] Pain point is urgent
- [ ] Good cultural fit

### Stage 2: Audit (5-7 days)

See AUDIT-PROCESS.md for full methodology

### Stage 3: Proposal Call (60 minutes)

**Agenda:**
1. Present audit findings (30 min)
2. Present recommendations (15 min)
3. Present proposal (10 min)
4. Handle objections (5 min)

### Stage 4: Close

**Closing Techniques:**

| Technique | When to Use | Script |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Assumptive** | Strong signals | "Great, let's get the contract sent over. What's the best email?" |
| **Urgency** | Limited capacity | "We have one spot left this month. If you want to start in [month], we need to move this week." |
| **Summary** | Logical buyer | "Let me summarize: you need X, we'll deliver Y, the investment is Z. Does that align with what you're looking for?" |
| **Question** | Hesitant buyer | "What would need to be true for you to move forward today?" |

## Client Retention

### Retention Strategies

| Strategy | Implementation |
|----------|---------------|
| **Over-communicate** | Weekly updates, proactive alerts |
| **Deliver wins early** | Quick wins in first 30 days |
| **Be strategic** | Think beyond tactics |
| **Show ROI** | Clear attribution, regular reporting |
| **Build relationships** | Know the humans, not just the business |
| **Surprise & delight** | Occasional bonuses, insights, intros |
| **Quarterly reviews** | Strategic planning sessions |
| **Annual partnerships** | Reward loyalty with value |

### Churn Prevention

**Warning Signs:**
- Slower response times
- Missing meetings
- Questioning value
- Budget concerns
- Leadership changes

**Intervention:**
- Schedule honest conversation
- Revisit goals & expectations
- Adjust strategy if needed
- Consider pricing adjustment
- Know when to part ways
