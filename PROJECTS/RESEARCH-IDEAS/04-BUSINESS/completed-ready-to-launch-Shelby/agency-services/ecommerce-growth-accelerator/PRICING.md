# Pricing Strategy

**Performance-based + retainer models for e-commerce growth services**

---

## Pricing Philosophy

We price for **partnership, not vendor relationships**.

Our goal: Align incentives so we only win when clients win.

### Core Principles
1. **Value-based, not hours-based** - Price on outcomes, not time
2. **Skin in the game** - We share risk/reward
3. **Transparent** - No hidden fees or surprises
4. **Scalable** - Pricing grows with client success
5. **Fair** - Sustainable for both parties

---

## Pricing Models

### Model 1: Retainer + Performance (Recommended)

**Structure:**
- Base retainer: Covers fixed costs, strategy, account management
- Performance fee: % of ad spend OR % of revenue lift

**Why This Works:**
- Predictable revenue for agency
- Aligned incentives
- Scales with client success
- Easy to understand

#### Tier Structure

| Tier | Revenue Range | Base Retainer | Performance Fee | Total Range |
|------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Launch | $50K-100K/mo | $5K-8K/mo | 10% of ad spend | $8K-15K/mo |
| Growth | $100K-300K/mo | $10K-15K/mo | 8% of ad spend | $15K-35K/mo |
| Scale | $300K-500K+/mo | $15K-25K/mo | 6% of ad spend | $25K-60K/mo |

**Example Calculation:**
```
Client: $200K/month revenue
Ad Spend: $60K/month
Tier: Growth

Base Retainer: $12,000
Performance (8% of $60K): $4,800
Total Monthly: $16,800
```

### Model 2: Pure Performance

**Structure:**
- No base retainer (or minimal $2K-5K)
- 15-25% of revenue attributed to our efforts

**When to Use:**
- High-confidence opportunities
- Strong track record with similar brands
- Client is risk-averse
- You have capital to float costs

**Pros:**
- Easy sell (low risk for client)
- Uncapped upside
- Ultimate alignment

**Cons:**
- Revenue volatility
- Attribution disputes
- Cash flow challenges
- Requires more capital

**Example:**
```
Client: $150K/month baseline
We drive to: $250K/month
Attributed lift: $100K
Our fee (20%): $20K/month
```

### Model 3: Retainer Only

**Structure:**
- Fixed monthly fee
- No performance component

**When to Use:**
- Consulting-only engagements
- Project-based work
- Clients with internal execution teams
- Specific scope (e.g., email only)

**Pricing:**
| Service | Monthly Retainer |
|---------|-----------------|
| Paid Media Only | $5K-15K |
| Email/SMS Only | $3K-8K |
| CRO Only | $5K-10K |
| Full-Service | $15K-40K |

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring Strategy

Present three options:
1. **Basic** - Minimum viable ($8K-12K)
2. **Recommended** - What you want them to buy ($15K-25K)
3. **Premium** - Everything ($30K-50K)

Most will choose middle option.

### Framing Techniques

**Don't say:** "It costs $15K/month"  
**Do say:** "The investment is $15K/month"

**Don't say:** "Our fee is 10%"  
**Do say:** "We partner with you at 10% of ad spend"

**Don't say:** "That's extra"  
**Do say:** "That's available as an add-on"

### ROI Framing

Always tie pricing to expected returns:

```
Investment: $15,000/month
Expected ROAS: 3.0x
Ad Spend: $50,000
Attributed Revenue: $150,000
Net Profit (at 20% margin): $30,000
ROI: 100%+ (doubles investment)
```

## Negotiation Guidelines

### When to Discount

| Situation | Discount | Conditions |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Annual prepay | 10-15% | Full year upfront |
| Multi-brand | 10% | 2+ brands/locations |
| Referral | 5% | Referred by existing client |
| Case study rights | 5-10% | Public testimonial + data |
| Launch partner | 20% | First client in new vertical |

### When NOT to Discount

- First conversation (establishes value)
- Client is price-shopping (race to bottom)
- No commitment to growth
- Unrealistic expectations
- Red flags in discovery

### Negotiation Scripts

**"Can you do $10K instead of $15K?"**

Response: "I understand budget is a consideration. At $10K, we'd need to reduce scope. Here's what we could do at that level... [present stripped package]. My recommendation is starting at $15K because [value reasons]. What's driving the budget concern?"

**"Other agencies charge less."**

Response: "That's possible. We're not the cheapest option, and that's by design. We focus on [differentiation: results, service, expertise]. What matters most to you: lowest cost or best outcomes?"

**"We need to see results before committing."**

Response: "I appreciate that. Here's what I propose: 90-day trial at [standard rate] with a clear success metric. If we hit [X], we continue. If not, we part ways amicably. Fair?"

## Pricing Review & Adjustment

### When to Raise Prices

1. **Consistently booked** - 80%+ capacity
2. **Delivering exceptional results** - Case studies to prove it
3. **Inflation/cost increases** - Annual adjustment
4. **Added services** - More value = higher price
5. **Market positioning** - Moving upmarket

### How to Raise Prices

**For Existing Clients:**
- 90 days notice minimum
- Explain value delivered
- Offer grandfather option (6-12 months)
- Present as "updated pricing" not "price increase"

**Script:**
> "As we've grown together over the past [X months], we've expanded our services and capabilities. Our pricing has remained the same since we started, but we're updating our rates to reflect the value we're delivering. Your new rate will be [X] effective [date]. We value our partnership and are committed to continuing to deliver exceptional results."

**For New Clients:**
- Simply quote new pricing
- No explanation needed
- Confidence signals value

## Pricing FAQ

**Q: Should we offer discounts for startups?**  
A: Generally no. Discounting devalues your service. Instead, offer reduced scope at lower price point.

**Q: How do we handle ad spend?**  
A: Client pays platforms directly. Never front ad spend unless you have significant capital and ironclad contracts.

**Q: What if client wants to pay per result?**  
A: Define "result" clearly. Revenue lift? ROAS? New customers? Make it measurable and attributable.

**Q: Should pricing be on the website?**  
A: Depends on positioning. Premium agencies often don't show pricing. Volume agencies do. Test both.

**Q: How often should we review pricing?**  
A: Quarterly for new clients. Annually for existing clients.
