## Communication Norms

### Slack Etiquette

**Client Channel:** #client-[brand-name]

**Response Times:**
- Urgent (site down): 30 minutes
- High (campaign issue): 2 hours
- Normal (questions): 24 hours
- Low (general): 48 hours

**What to Post:**
- Quick questions
- Approvals needed
- Wins to celebrate
- Heads-ups

**What NOT to Post:**
- Large files (use email)
- Sensitive data
- Lengthy discussions (schedule call)

### Email

**Use For:**
- Formal approvals
- Weekly reports
- Strategic documents
- Sensitive information

**Response Time:** 24 hours (business days)

### Meetings

**Standing Calls:**
- Weekly check-in: 30 min (Wednesday)
- Monthly business review: 60 min (last week)
- Quarterly planning: 90 min (quarterly)

**Ad-hoc Calls:**
- Schedule via Calendly
- 24-hour notice preferred
- Agenda required

## Escalation Path

### Issue Resolution

**Level 1:** Account Manager  
*Most issues resolved here*

**Level 2:** Department Head  
*Strategic issues, escalations*

**Level 3:** Founder/CEO  
*Major concerns, relationship issues*

### How to Escalate

Client should:
1. Start with Account Manager (Slack/email)
2. If unresolved in 48 hours, request call with manager
3. If still unresolved, ask for leadership involvement

## Red Flags to Watch

### Client-Side

- [ ] Slow responses (>48 hours)
- [ ] Budget concerns raised early
- [ ] Multiple stakeholders disagreeing
- [ ] Unrealistic expectations
- [ ] Access delays

### Agency-Side

- [ ] Missing deadlines
- [ ] Quality issues
- [ ] Communication gaps
- [ ] Team capacity problems

### Action Plan

If red flags appear:
1. Document the issue
2. Discuss internally
3. Address with client promptly
4. Create action plan
5. Follow up
