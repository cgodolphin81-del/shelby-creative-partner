# Discovery Process

## Client Discovery Methodology

The discovery phase is where deals are won or lost. This systematic approach ensures we understand client needs, qualify opportunities, and position solutions effectively.

---

## Phase 1: Initial Qualification (30-45 minutes)

### Objective
Determine if the prospect is a good fit before investing significant time in discovery.

### Qualification Criteria (BANT Framework)

**Budget:**
- Do they have budget allocated for automation/AI?
- What's their typical technology spend?
- Have they invested in similar initiatives before?

**Authority:**
- Is this person the decision-maker?
- Who else needs to be involved?
- What's their approval process?

**Need:**
- What specific pain points are they experiencing?
- How are they currently solving these problems?
- What happens if they don't solve this?

**Timeline:**
- When do they need this implemented?
- Are there any external deadlines?
- What's driving the urgency?

### Qualification Call Script

```
Opening (5 min)
---------------
"Thanks for taking the time today. I want to make sure we're a good fit 
before we dive deep. Can you share a bit about what prompted you to 
reach out about AI automation?"

Discovery (20 min)
------------------
Current State:
- "Walk me through how this process works today."
- "Who's involved in this workflow?"
- "What tools are you currently using?"

Pain Points:
- "What's the most frustrating part of this process?"
- "How much time does this take your team weekly?"
- "What errors or issues come up regularly?"

Impact:
- "If this were solved, what would that enable?"
- "How would you measure success?"
- "What's the cost of not solving this?"

Budget & Timeline:
- "Have you set aside budget for this initiative?"
- "When are you hoping to have this implemented?"
- "What does your decision process look like?"

Closing (10 min)
----------------
"Based on what you've shared, I think we could help with [specific solution]. 
The next step would be a deeper discovery session where we map out your 
workflows in detail and provide a formal proposal. Does that make sense?"
```

### Qualification Scorecard

Rate each criterion 1-5:

| Criterion | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Budget clarity | /5 | |
| Decision authority | /5 | |
| Pain severity | /5 | |
| Timeline urgency | /5 | |
| **Total** | /20 | **15+ = Qualified** |

## Phase 2: Deep Discovery (2-3 hours, may be split)

### Objective
Understand current workflows in detail, identify automation opportunities, and quantify potential ROI.

### Preparation
- Send pre-discovery questionnaire (see templates)
- Request access to relevant systems (read-only)
- Review their website, LinkedIn, any public information
- Prepare Miro/Mural board for workflow mapping

### Discovery Session Agenda

**Session 1: Current State Mapping (60-90 min)**

1. **Organizational Context (15 min)**
   - Company structure and key departments
   - Decision-making processes
   - Technology stack overview
   - Current automation efforts

2. **Process Deep-Dive (60 min)**
   - Map 2-3 priority workflows end-to-end
   - Identify all touchpoints and handoffs
   - Document tools and systems used
   - Capture pain points at each step
   - Note exceptions and edge cases

3. **Data & Integration Review (30 min)**
   - Data sources and formats
   - API availability for key systems
   - Data quality issues
   - Security and compliance requirements

**Session 2: Future State & Requirements (60-90 min)**

1. **Ideal State Vision (30 min)**
   - "If this were perfect, what would it look like?"
   - Success metrics and KPIs
   - Non-negotiable requirements
   - Nice-to-have features

2. **Technical Requirements (30 min)**
   - Integration requirements
   - Performance expectations
   - Security and compliance needs
   - Scalability considerations

3. **Change Management (30 min)**
   - Who will be affected by changes?
   - Training needs
   - Adoption concerns
   - Internal champions

### Workflow Mapping Template

For each workflow, document:

```
WORKFLOW: [Name]
TRIGGER: [What starts this process?]
FREQUENCY: [How often does this occur?]
VOLUME: [How many instances per day/week/month?]

STEPS:
1. [Step description]
   - Owner: [Role]
   - Tools: [Software used]
   - Time: [Duration]
   - Pain points: [Issues]

2. [Step description]
   ...

OUTPUTS: [What is produced?]
DEPENDENCIES: [What systems/people does this depend on?]
EXCEPTIONS: [What can go wrong?]
METRICS: [How is success measured?]
```

## Phase 3: ROI Calculation

### Objective
Quantify the business value of proposed automation to justify investment.

### ROI Formula

```
ROI = (Annual Benefits - Annual Costs) / Annual Costs × 100

Payback Period = Initial Investment / Monthly Benefits
```

### Benefit Categories

**1. Labor Cost Savings**
```
Hours saved per week × Weeks per year × Hourly rate = Annual savings
```

Example:
- 10 hours/week saved × 50 weeks × $35/hour = $17,500/year

**2. Error Reduction**
```
Current error rate × Cost per error × Annual volume = Current cost
New error rate × Cost per error × Annual volume = New cost
Difference = Annual savings
```

Example:
- 5% error rate × $100/error × 10,000 transactions = $50,000 current cost
- 1% error rate × $100/error × 10,000 transactions = $10,000 new cost
- Savings = $40,000/year

**3. Speed/Throughput Improvement**
```
Additional capacity × Revenue per unit = Additional revenue
```

Example:
- 20% faster processing = 100 additional orders/month
- 100 × $50 average order = $5,000/month = $60,000/year

**4. Opportunity Cost (Revenue Enabled)**
```
Time freed × Revenue-generating activities = Additional revenue
```

Example:
- Sales team saves 5 hours/week on admin
- 5 hours × $200/hour revenue generation = $1,000/week = $50,000/year

**5. Customer Satisfaction/Retention**
```
Improved response time → Higher retention → Lifetime value preserved
```

Example:
- 10% improvement in response time
- 2% reduction in churn
- 2% × 1,000 customers × $5,000 LTV = $100,000/year

```
---------------

-----------------

```

## Phase 4: Proposal Development

### Objective
Create a compelling, customized proposal that addresses client needs and justifies investment.

### Proposal Structure

1. **Executive Summary (1 page)**
   - Client challenges (in their words)
   - Proposed solution overview
   - Expected outcomes
   - Investment summary

2. **Current State Analysis (2-3 pages)**
   - Workflow documentation
   - Pain points identified
   - Root cause analysis
   - Impact assessment

3. **Proposed Solution (3-5 pages)**
   - Solution architecture
   - Workflow diagrams (before/after)
   - Technology stack
   - Implementation approach

4. **Implementation Plan (2-3 pages)**
   - Phases and milestones
   - Timeline with key dates
   - Resource requirements
   - Risk mitigation

5. **Investment & ROI (2-3 pages)**
   - Pricing breakdown
   - Payment schedule
   - ROI analysis
   - Comparison to status quo

6. **About Us (1-2 pages)**
   - Company overview
   - Relevant case studies
   - Team credentials
   - Differentiators

7. **Terms & Next Steps (1 page)**
   - Terms and conditions
   - Acceptance process
   - Timeline to start
   - Contact information

### Proposal Best Practices

✅ **Do:**
- Use client's language and terminology
- Include specific metrics and numbers
- Show before/after workflow diagrams
- Provide clear timeline with milestones
- Include relevant case studies
- Make the next step obvious

❌ **Don't:**
- Use generic, templated language
- Focus only on features, not outcomes
- Hide pricing or make it confusing
- Over-promise on timelines
- Skip the ROI analysis
- Make them guess what to do next

## Phase 5: Proposal Presentation

### Objective
Walk the client through the proposal, address concerns, and close the deal.

### Presentation Agenda (60 min)

1. **Recap Discovery (10 min)**
   - Confirm understanding of their challenges
   - Validate pain points and priorities
   - Ensure alignment before presenting solution

2. **Present Solution (25 min)**
   - Walk through proposed approach
   - Show workflow diagrams
   - Explain technology choices
   - Highlight key benefits

3. **Review Investment & ROI (15 min)**
   - Present pricing clearly
   - Walk through ROI calculation
   - Compare to cost of inaction
   - Address budget concerns

4. **Q&A and Objections (10 min)**
   - Address any concerns
   - Clarify scope and timeline
   - Discuss terms if needed

### Common Objections & Responses

**"It's too expensive."**
→ "I understand budget is a concern. Let's look at the ROI again. 
   You're investing $X to save/make $Y, which pays for itself in Z months. 
   What part of the investment is concerning?"

**"We need to think about it."**
→ "Of course. What specific aspects do you want to consider? 
   Is there anything I can clarify that would help with that decision?"

**"We want to start smaller."**
→ "That makes sense. Here's what we could do in Phase 1 for $X, 
   and here's how it sets up Phase 2. Would a phased approach work better?"

**"We need to get other stakeholders involved."**
→ "Absolutely. Who else needs to be part of this decision? 
   Would it help to schedule a session with them directly?"

### Closing Questions

- "Does this solution address the challenges we discussed?"
- "Is there anything that would prevent you from moving forward?"
- "What would need to be true for this to be an easy yes?"
- "If we can address [concern], are you ready to move forward?"

```

===========================

-------------------
Company Name:
Industry:
Number of Employees:
Annual Revenue (range):

Current Challenges
------------------
1. What are the top 3 operational challenges you're facing?

2. What processes take up the most time for your team?

3. Where do you see the most errors or rework?

Technology Stack
----------------
4. What CRM do you use?

5. What project management tools?

6. What communication tools (email, Slack, etc.)?

7. Any other key business software?

Automation History
------------------
8. Have you implemented automation before? If yes, what worked/didn't work?

9. What's your team's comfort level with new technology?

---------------

Additional Context
------------------
16. Anything else we should know before our discovery session?
```
