## Cash Flow Management

### Payment Schedule Impact

**Typical Project Cash Flow:**
```
Week 1:   +$10,000 (50% deposit on $20K project)
Week 3-4: -$7,000  (Contractor payments)
Week 6:   -$3,000  (More contractor payments)
Week 8:   +$10,000 (Final payment)

Net: +$10,000 profit over 8 weeks
```

### Cash Flow Best Practices

**Do:**
- ✅ Require 50% deposit before starting work
- ✅ Pay contractors net 15 or net 30 (after you get paid)
- ✅ Maintain 3-month operating reserve
- ✅ Invoice immediately upon milestones
- ✅ Follow up on overdue invoices within 3 days

**Don't:**
- ❌ Start work without deposit
- ❌ Pay contractors before client pays you
- ❌ Let invoices go >30 days overdue
- ❌ Mix personal and business finances
- ❌ Forget to set aside taxes

### Operating Reserve Calculation

```
Monthly Expenses: $X
Target Reserve: 3 months
Required Reserve: $X × 3 = $Y

Example:
Monthly Expenses: $10,000
Target Reserve: 3 months
Required Reserve: $30,000
```

## Tax Planning

### Tax Considerations

**Business Structure:**
- **LLC (Sole Proprietorship):** Simple, pass-through taxation
- **LLC (S-Corp Election):** Potential tax savings at $100K+ profit
- **C-Corp:** Rarely needed for service businesses

**Estimated Taxes:**
- Pay quarterly if expecting >$1K tax liability
- Set aside 25-30% of profit for taxes
- Work with CPA to optimize

**Deductible Expenses:**
- Home office (if applicable)
- Software and tools
- Marketing and advertising
- Professional services (legal, accounting)
- Contractor payments
- Business travel
- Education and training
- Health insurance (if self-employed)

## Funding & Investment

### Bootstrap (Recommended)

**Why bootstrap:**
- Maintain full ownership
- Focus on profitability from day 1
- No investor pressure
- Keep decision-making control

**How to bootstrap:**
- Start with personal savings
- Reinvest profits
- Keep expenses lean
- Grow organically

### When to Consider Funding

**Consider if:**
- Want to scale faster than cash flow allows
- Need capital for product development
- Want to acquire competitors
- Planning to exit within 5-7 years

**Options:**
- **SBA Loan:** $50K-5M, 5-10 year terms, 6-10% interest
- **Line of Credit:** $10K-500K, flexible, 8-15% interest
- **Angel Investor:** $50K-500K, 10-25% equity
- **VC:** $1M+, 20-40% equity (rarely appropriate for agencies)

## Exit Strategy

### Valuation Multiples

**Agency Valuation:**
- **SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings):** 2-3x
- **EBITDA:** 3-5x
- **Revenue:** 0.5-1.5x

**Example:**
```
Annual Revenue: $1M
EBITDA: $300K (30% margin)

Valuation Range:
  SDE Method: $600K - $900K
  EBITDA Method: $900K - $1.5M
  Revenue Method: $500K - $1.5M

Most Likely: $800K - $1.2M
```

### Exit Options

| Option | Timeline | Proceeds | Notes |
|--------|----------|----------|-------|
| Sell to Competitor | 6-12 months | 2-4x EBITDA | Most common |
| Sell to Employee | 1-3 years | Structured deal | Good for legacy |
| Merge with Larger Firm | 6-12 months | Cash + earnout | Growth opportunity |
| Close Business | Immediate | Assets only | Last resort |

### Building Saleable Value

**To maximize valuation:**
- ✅ Diversified client base (no client >20% of revenue)
- ✅ Recurring revenue (retainers 30%+ of revenue)
- ✅ Documented processes (not dependent on founder)
- ✅ Strong management team (can run without you)
- ✅ Clean financials (3+ years of profitable history)
- ✅ Growth trajectory (increasing revenue and profit)
