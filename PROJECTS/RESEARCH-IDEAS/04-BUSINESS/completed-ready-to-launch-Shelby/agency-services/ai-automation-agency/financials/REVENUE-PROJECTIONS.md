# Revenue Projections

## 3-Year Financial Model for AI Automation Agency

---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines a 3-year revenue projection model for an AI automation agency, based on three growth scenarios:

- **Conservative:** 10 clients by Year 3
- **Base Case:** 50 clients by Year 3
- **Aggressive:** 200 clients by Year 3

All projections assume a mix of project revenue and recurring retainer revenue.

---

## Key Assumptions

### Pricing Assumptions

| Service | Average Price | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| Starter Project | $10,000 | 1-2 workflows, basic chatbot |
| Growth Project | $30,000 | 3-5 workflows, AI integration |
| Enterprise Project | $75,000 | Comprehensive automation |
| Bronze Retainer | $2,000/month | 10 hours, maintenance |
| Silver Retainer | $5,000/month | 25 hours, enhancements |
| Gold Retainer | $10,000/month | 60 hours, dedicated support |
| Platinum Retainer | $20,000/month | 120 hours, enterprise |

| Client Type | % of Base | Avg Project | Avg Retainer |

**Weighted Average:**
- Project: $25,750
- Retainer: $4,250/month ($51,000/year)

## Key Metrics to Track

### Monthly Metrics

| Metric | Target | Formula |
|--------|--------|---------|
| MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) | Growing 10-20%/month | Sum of all retainer revenue |
| New Projects Closed | 2-8/month | Count of new project contracts |
| Average Deal Size | $25K+ | Total project revenue / # projects |
| Sales Cycle Length | < 45 days | Average days from lead to close |
| Lead-to-Close Rate | 3-5% | Closed deals / Total leads |
| Client Churn Rate | < 5%/year | Clients lost / Total clients |
| Net Revenue Retention | 120%+ | (Ending MRR - Churn + Expansion) / Starting MRR |

### Quarterly Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Revenue vs. Plan | Within 10% |
| Gross Margin | 60%+ |
| EBITDA Margin | 40%+ |
| Client Satisfaction (CSAT) | 4.5/5+ |
| Employee Satisfaction | 4.0/5+ |
| Cash Runway | 6+ months |

## Cash Flow Considerations

### Payment Terms Impact

**Standard Terms:**
- 50% upfront, 50% on delivery (small projects)
- 40/40/20 (medium projects)
- 30/30/30/10 (large projects)

**Cash Flow Timing:**
- Projects: 50-70% cash collected in first 30 days
- Retainers: Collected monthly in advance
- Expenses: Paid monthly (salaries, tools, etc.)

**Working Capital Needs:**
- Year 1: $100K-200K (to cover payroll before project collections)
- Year 2: $300K-500K (scaled operations)
- Year 3: Self-funding from operations

## Funding Requirements

### Bootstrap Path (Recommended)
- Year 1: Founder investment + revenue
- Year 2: Revenue-funded growth
- Year 3: Profitable, self-sustaining

**Initial Capital Needed:** $100K-200K
- 6 months operating expenses
- Buffer for slow start
- Marketing budget

---

### Raised Capital Path (Optional)
- Seed Round: $500K-1M (Year 1)
- Series A: $3M-5M (Year 2)
- Use for: Aggressive hiring, marketing, product development

**Trade-offs:**
- Faster growth
- Less control
- Pressure for exits
- Dilution

---

## Exit Opportunities

### Potential Exit Paths

**1. Acquisition by Larger Agency**
- Typical multiple: 3-5x revenue
- Year 3 valuation (base case): $45M-75M
- Likely acquirers: Digital agencies, consulting firms, tech companies

**2. Acquisition by Tech Company**
- Typical multiple: 5-10x revenue
- Year 3 valuation (base case): $75M-150M
- Likely acquirers: Automation platforms (Zapier, Make, n8n), AI companies

**3. Private Equity Roll-Up**
- Typical multiple: 4-6x EBITDA
- Year 3 valuation (base case): $35M-50M
- Strategy: Combine with similar agencies

**4. Continue as Cash Cow**
- Take profits as distributions
- No exit required
- Sustainable long-term business

---

## Risk Factors

### Internal Risks
- **Key person dependency:** Founder is primary salesperson
- **Delivery bottlenecks:** Can't hire good engineers fast enough
- **Quality issues:** Growing too fast, quality suffers
- **Cash flow:** Client payments delayed, payroll due

**Mitigation:**
- Document processes early
- Build management layer
- Maintain cash reserves
- Diversify client base

---

### External Risks
- **Economic downturn:** Clients cut discretionary spending
- **Competition:** More agencies enter the space
- **Technology changes:** AI/automation landscape shifts
- **Platform risk:** Key tools (Zapier, OpenAI) change pricing/terms

**Mitigation:**
- Focus on ROI-positive projects
- Build defensible IP and relationships
- Stay current with technology
- Diversify tool stack
