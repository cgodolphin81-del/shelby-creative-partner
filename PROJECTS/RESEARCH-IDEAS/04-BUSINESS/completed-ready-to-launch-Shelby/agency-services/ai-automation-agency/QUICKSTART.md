# AI Automation Agency - Quick Start Guide

## 🚀 Launch Your Agency in 7 Days

This guide walks you through the essential steps to launch your AI automation agency using this playbook.

---

## Day 1: Foundation

### ✅ Define Your Positioning

**Fill in the blanks:**
```
I help [TARGET MARKET] solve [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT] 
through AI automation, typically saving them [QUANTIFIED RESULT].
```

**Example:**
```
I help e-commerce companies solve customer service overload 
through AI automation, typically saving them 40+ hours/week 
and $60K/year.
```

### ✅ Set Up Your Business Infrastructure

**Legal:**
- [ ] Register business entity (LLC recommended)
- [ ] Get EIN/tax ID
- [ ] Open business bank account
- [ ] Get business insurance (E&O, general liability)

**Tools (Essential):**
- [ ] CRM: HubSpot (free) or Pipedrive ($50/mo)
- [ ] Project Management: ClickUp ($30/mo) or Asana ($30/mo)
- [ ] Communication: Slack (free)
- [ ] Scheduling: Calendly ($15/mo)
- [ ] Email: Google Workspace ($6/mo)
- [ ] Website: Carrd ($19/yr) or Webflow ($20/mo)
- [ ] Contracts: PandaDoc ($50/mo) or DocuSign

**Budget: ~$200-300/mo to start**

### ✅ Customize Your Templates

**Update these files with your info:**
- [ ] `/contracts/MSA.md` - Add your business details
- [ ] `/templates/PROPOSAL.md` - Add your branding
- [ ] `/pricing/PRICING.md` - Adjust rates for your market

## Day 2: Service Offerings

### ✅ Choose Your Initial Services

**Don't offer everything at once. Start with 2-3:**

**Recommended Starter Package:**
1. **Workflow Automation** ($2K-5K) - Quick wins, easy to deliver
2. **Customer Service Agent** ($5K-15K) - High demand, clear ROI
3. **Retainer Support** ($1K-2.5K/mo) - Recurring revenue

**Why these three:**
- Different price points (entry, mid, recurring)
- Can be delivered with existing tools (Make, OpenAI)
- Clear, measurable ROI
- 2-3 week delivery time

### ✅ Define Your Delivery Process

**For each service, document:**
- [ ] What's included
- [ ] Timeline
- [ ] What you need from client
- [ ] Your contractor (if needed)
- [ ] Quality checklist

**Use `/fulfillment/FULFILLMENT.md` as reference**

### ✅ Find Your First Contractors

**You need:**
- 1 Automation Specialist (Make/Zapier expert)
- 1 AI Engineer (for custom agents)

**Where to find:**
- Upwork (search "Make.com expert" or "AI developer")
- Toptal (higher quality, higher rates)
- Twitter/X (search "Make.com" or "AI automation")
- Discord communities (Automation Champions, AI communities)

**Vet them:**
- [ ] Review portfolio
- [ ] 30-min video call
- [ ] Small paid test project ($500)
- [ ] Check references

## Day 3: Sales Materials

### ✅ Create Your One-Page Website

**Essential sections:**
```
1. Headline: "I help [X] achieve [Y] through AI automation"
2. Problem: Describe their pain point
3. Solution: How you solve it
4. Results: 2-3 case studies or examples
5. Services: What you offer
6. About: Your background
7. Contact: Calendly link + email
```

**Tools:** Carrd (simplest), Webflow (more custom), Framer (modern)

### ✅ Prepare Your Sales Deck

**10-slide pitch deck:**
1. Problem they face
2. Cost of the problem
3. Your solution
4. How it works
5. Results/case studies
6. Your process
7. Pricing
8. About you
9. Next steps
10. Contact

**Format:** PDF or Google Slides (send after discovery call)

### ✅ Set Up Your CRM Pipeline

**Stages:**
```
New Lead → Contacted → Discovery Scheduled → 
Discovery Complete → Proposal Sent → Negotiation → 
Closed Won / Closed Lost
```

**Fields to track:**
- Company name
- Contact info
- Lead source
- Pain point
- Budget range
- Timeline
- Last contact date
- Next action

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Pricing Too Low
**Problem:** Attracts bad clients, unsustainable
**Solution:** Price based on value, not hours. Minimum $5K projects.

### ❌ Overpromising
**Problem:** Can't deliver, damages reputation
**Solution:** Underpromise, overdeliver. Add 20% buffer to timelines.

### ❌ No Contract
**Problem:** Scope creep, payment issues
**Solution:** Always use MSA + SOW. No exceptions.

### ❌ Doing Everything Yourself
**Problem:** Burnout, limited scale
**Solution:** Hire contractors early. Focus on sales and PM.

### ❌ Chasing Bad Fits
**Problem:** Difficult clients, low margins
**Solution:** Qualify hard. Walk away from red flags.

### ❌ Ignoring Cash Flow
**Problem:** Can't pay contractors, stress
**Solution:** Require deposits. Follow up on invoices. Maintain 3-month reserve.

## Red Flags in Clients

**Walk away if they:**
- ❌ Don't have budget (can't afford your minimum)
- ❌ Want everything yesterday (unrealistic timeline)
- ❌ Have been burned by automation before (and blame everyone else)
- ❌ Can't articulate their problem (not ready)
- ❌ Want to skip the contract (not professional)
- ❌ Haggle on price aggressively (will be difficult)
- ❌ Have decision by committee (will take forever)

**Green flags:**
- ✅ Clear pain point
- ✅ Budget aligned with your pricing
- ✅ Decision-maker in the room
- ✅ Timeline is reasonable
- ✅ Professional communication
- ✅ Values expertise over lowest price

## Scaling Beyond $100K/mo

### When to Hire

| Revenue | Hire |
|---------|------|
| $50K/mo | Virtual Assistant ($2K/mo) |
| $75K/mo | Project Manager ($5K/mo) |
| $100K/mo | Sales Person ($4K + commission) |
| $150K/mo | Technical Lead ($8K/mo) |

### Productization

**Package your services:**
- Fixed scope
- Fixed price
- Fixed timeline
- Repeatable delivery

**Example packages:**
- "Starter Automation" - $7,500, 2 weeks
- "Growth Package" - $25,000, 4 weeks
- "Enterprise AI" - $50,000, 8 weeks

### Build Systems

**Document everything:**
- Sales process
- Onboarding process
- Delivery process
- QA process
- Offboarding process

**Use:** Notion, ClickUp, or internal wiki
