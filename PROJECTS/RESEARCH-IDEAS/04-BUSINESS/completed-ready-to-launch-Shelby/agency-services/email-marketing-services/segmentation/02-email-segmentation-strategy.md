# Email Segmentation Strategy
## Advanced Segmentation Tactics for Maximum ROI

---

## Executive Summary

Effective segmentation is the difference between generic blasting and personalized communication that drives revenue. This guide provides advanced segmentation strategies that go beyond basic demographics to behavioral, predictive, and lifecycle-based targeting.

**Key Principle:** The right message to the right person at the right time = Maximum ROI

## 1. FOUNDATIONAL SEGMENTS (Must-Have)

### 1.1 New Subscribers
**Definition:** Subscribed within last 7 days, no purchase
**Purpose:** Welcome series, brand introduction
**Criteria:**
- `Subscribed to list` = Any list
- `Date subscribed` = Last 7 days
- `Placed order` = 0 times

**Use Cases:**
- Welcome flow enrollment
- First-purchase incentive
- Brand story education

---

### 1.2 First-Time Buyers
**Definition:** Completed first purchase
**Purpose:** Post-purchase nurture, second purchase conversion
**Criteria:**
- `Placed order` = 1 time
- `Order date` = Any time

**Use Cases:**
- Thank you flow
- Review request
- Second purchase incentive (30 days)

---

### 1.3 Repeat Customers
**Definition:** 2+ purchases
**Purpose:** Loyalty building, VIP progression
**Criteria:**
- `Placed order` = 2+ times

**Use Cases:**
- Loyalty program invitation
- VIP tier progression
- Exclusive offers

---

### 1.4 VIP Customers
**Definition:** Top 10% by lifetime value
**Purpose:** Retention, exclusivity, advocacy
**Criteria:**
- `Lifetime spend` = Top 10 percentile
OR
- `Placed order` = 5+ times
AND
- `Lifetime spend` = $500+

**Use Cases:**
- Early access
- Exclusive products
- Personal outreach

---

### 1.5 At-Risk Customers
**Definition:** Previously active, now dormant
**Purpose:** Winback campaigns
**Criteria:**
- `Placed order` = 1+ times
- `Last order date` = 60-180 days ago

**Use Cases:**
- Winback flow
- "We miss you" campaigns
- Special incentives

---

### 1.6 Lost Customers
**Definition:** Previously purchased, highly dormant
**Purpose:** Breakup or final winback
**Criteria:**
- `Placed order` = 1+ times
- `Last order date` = 180+ days ago

**Use Cases:**
- Final winback attempt
- Sunset flow
- Reactivation survey

---

## 2. BEHAVIORAL SEGMENTS (High-Impact)

### 2.1 Cart Abandoners
**Definition:** Added to cart, didn't purchase
**Purpose:** Cart recovery
**Criteria:**
- `Added to cart` = Last 30 days
- `Placed order` = 0 times (since cart addition)

**Sub-segments:**
- High-value cart ($100+)
- Low-value cart (<$50)
- First-time abandoner
- Serial abandoner (3+ times)

**Use Cases:**
- Abandoned cart flow
- Exit-intent popup
- Retargeting ads

---

### 2.2 Browse Abandoners
**Definition:** Viewed products, didn't add to cart
**Purpose:** Re-engagement, product interest
**Criteria:**
- `Viewed product` = Last 7 days
- `Added to cart` = 0 times (since view)

**Sub-segments:**
- Category browsers (specific product categories)
- Price-sensitive (viewed sale items)
- New product browsers

**Use Cases:**
- Browse abandonment flow
- Related product recommendations
- Educational content

---

### 2.3 Email Engagers
**Definition:** Actively opens/clicks emails
**Purpose:** Reward engagement, test new offers
**Criteria:**
- `Opened email` = 3+ times (last 30 days)
OR
- `Clicked email` = 1+ times (last 30 days)

**Sub-segments:**
- Super engagers (opens 80%+ of emails)
- Click-only engagers
- Recent engagers (last 7 days)

**Use Cases:**
- Early access offers
- Beta testing new products
- Referral program invites

---

### 2.4 Email Non-Engagers
**Definition:** Subscribed but inactive
**Purpose:** Re-engagement or list cleaning
**Criteria:**
- `Opened email` = 0 times (last 90 days)
AND
- `Clicked email` = 0 times (last 90 days)

**Sub-segments:**
- Recently inactive (30-90 days)
- Long-term inactive (90+ days)
- Never engaged (since subscription)

**Use Cases:**
- Re-engagement campaign
- Preference center prompt
- Sunset flow

---

### 2.5 Product Researchers
**Definition:** High page views, multiple product views
**Purpose:** Conversion nudging, social proof
**Criteria:**
- `Viewed product` = 5+ times (last 7 days)
OR
- `Sessions` = 3+ (last 7 days)
AND
- `Placed order` = 0 times

**Use Cases:**
- Social proof emails (reviews)
- Comparison guides
- Limited-time offers

---

### 2.6 Deal Seekers
**Definition:** Only purchases during sales
**Purpose:** Strategic discounting
**Criteria:**
- `Placed order` = During sale periods only
- `Discount code used` = 80%+ of orders

**Use Cases:**
- Sale announcements (priority)
- Flash sale early access
- Clearance notifications

## 3. PREDICTIVE SEGMENTS (Advanced)

### 3.1 Likely to Purchase (Next 7 Days)
**Definition:** High purchase probability based on behavior
**Purpose:** Timely offers, conversion optimization
**Criteria (Predictive Model):**
- Recent site visits (3+ in 7 days)
- Product views (5+ unique products)
- Email engagement (opened last 3 emails)
- Historical purchase pattern match

**Use Cases:**
- Personalized product recommendations
- Time-sensitive offers
- Cart incentives

---

### 3.2 Churn Risk (Next 30 Days)
**Definition:** High probability of not returning
**Purpose:** Proactive retention
**Criteria (Predictive Model):**
- Purchase frequency declining
- Email engagement dropping
- Time since last purchase > average cycle
- Support ticket history

**Use Cases:**
- Proactive outreach
- Satisfaction surveys
- Retention offers

---

### 3.3 High LTV Potential
**Definition:** Likely to become VIP based on early behavior
**Purpose:** Accelerated nurture, VIP fast-track
**Criteria (Predictive Model):**
- First purchase AOV above average
- Quick repeat purchase (<30 days)
- High email engagement
- Premium product interest

**Use Cases:**
- VIP preview access
- Personalized recommendations
- Loyalty program acceleration

---

### 3.4 Best Customer Lookalikes
**Definition:** New customers matching VIP profiles
**Purpose:** Accelerated relationship building
**Criteria:**
- Demographics match VIP segment
- Early behavior matches VIP patterns
- Product preferences align with top categories

**Use Cases:**
- VIP-like treatment from start
- Curated product recommendations
- Exclusive content access

## 4. LIFECYCLE SEGMENTS (Journey-Based)

### 4.1 Awareness Stage
**Definition:** New subscribers, no purchase
**Timeframe:** Days 0-14
**Purpose:** Education, trust building
**Content Strategy:**
- Brand story
- Social proof
- Educational content
- Low-pressure CTAs

---

### 4.2 Consideration Stage
**Definition:** Engaged but haven't purchased
**Timeframe:** Days 14-45
**Purpose:** Overcome objections, drive first purchase
**Content Strategy:**
- Product comparisons
- Reviews and testimonials
- FAQ content
- First-purchase incentives

---

### 4.3 Conversion Stage
**Definition:** First-time purchasers
**Timeframe:** First purchase
**Purpose:** Delight, set expectations
**Content Strategy:**
- Order confirmation
- Shipping updates
- Welcome to customer club
- Usage tips

---

### 4.4 Retention Stage
**Definition:** Repeat purchasers
**Timeframe:** 2-5 purchases
**Purpose:** Build loyalty, increase frequency
**Content Strategy:**
- Loyalty program
- New product announcements
- Personalized recommendations
- Exclusive offers

---

### 4.5 Advocacy Stage
**Definition:** VIP customers, brand advocates
**Timeframe:** 5+ purchases or $500+ LTV
**Purpose:** Amplify advocacy, maximize LTV
**Content Strategy:**
- Referral program
- User-generated content requests
- Exclusive access
- Personal relationships

---

### 4.6 Decline Stage
**Definition:** Decreasing engagement/purchases
**Timeframe:** 60-180 days since last purchase
**Purpose:** Re-engagement
**Content Strategy:**
- "We miss you" messages
- Surveys to understand why
- Winback offers
- Preference updates

---

### 4.7 Lost Stage
**Definition:** Dormant customers
**Timeframe:** 180+ days since last purchase
**Purpose:** Reactivation or sunset
**Content Strategy:**
- Final winback attempt
- Breakup email
- Unsubscribe option
- Alternative channels (social)

---

## 5. ENGAGEMENT SEGMENTS (Activity-Based)

### 5.1 Super Fans
**Definition:** Highest engagement across all channels
**Criteria:**
- Email open rate: 80%+
- Email click rate: 40%+
- Social media: Follows + engages
- Purchase frequency: Above average

**Treatment:**
- First access to everything
- Personal thank you messages
- Surprise gifts
- Beta testing opportunities

---

### 5.2 Active Engagers
**Definition:** Regularly opens and clicks
**Criteria:**
- Email open rate: 40-80%
- Email click rate: 15-40%
- Recent purchase: Last 60 days

**Treatment:**
- Regular promotional emails
- New product announcements
- Loyalty rewards

---

### 5.3 Passive Subscribers
**Definition:** Opens occasionally, rarely clicks
**Criteria:**
- Email open rate: 15-40%
- Email click rate: 5-15%
- Purchase: 1+ times

**Treatment:**
- Reduced email frequency
- High-value content only
- Re-engagement campaigns

---

### 5.4 Inactive Subscribers
**Definition:** Rarely or never engages
**Criteria:**
- Email open rate: 0-15%
- Email click rate: 0-5%
- No purchase: 90+ days

**Treatment:**
- Re-engagement series
- Preference center prompts
- Sunset flow consideration

## 6. VALUE-BASED SEGMENTS (RFM Model)

### RFM Scoring System

**Recency (R):** Days since last purchase
- R5: 0-30 days (5 points)
- R4: 31-60 days (4 points)
- R3: 61-90 days (3 points)
- R2: 91-180 days (2 points)
- R1: 181+ days (1 point)

**Frequency (F):** Number of purchases (lifetime)
- F5: 10+ purchases (5 points)
- F4: 5-9 purchases (4 points)
- F3: 3-4 purchases (3 points)
- F2: 2 purchases (2 points)
- F1: 1 purchase (1 point)

**Monetary (M):** Lifetime spend
- M5: $1000+ (5 points)
- M4: $500-999 (4 points)
- M3: $250-499 (3 points)
- M2: $100-249 (2 points)
- M1: <$100 (1 point)

---

### RFM Segment Combinations

| Segment | RFM Score | Description | Strategy |
|---------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Champions | R5 F5 M5 | Best customers | Reward, exclusive access, referral asks |
| Loyal | R4-5 F4-5 M3-5 | Regular buyers | Loyalty program, upsell, new products |
| Potential Loyalists | R5 F2-3 M2-4 | Recent multi-buyers | Membership offers, volume discounts |
| New Customers | R5 F1 M1-3 | First purchase | Welcome series, second purchase incentive |
| Promising | R4-5 F1 M1 | Recent first-timers | Education, build trust |
| Needs Attention | R2-3 F3-5 M3-5 | Previously good, now dormant | Re-engagement, surveys |
| About to Sleep | R2 F2-3 M2-3 | Declining activity | Winback offers |
| At Risk | R1 F3-5 M3-5 | Was valuable, now gone | Strong winback, personal outreach |
| Can't Lose | R1 F5 M5 | Was champion, now lost | VIP winback, phone call |
| Hibernating | R1 F1-2 M1-2 | One-time, long ago | Low-cost reactivation or sunset |

## 7. PRODUCT-BASED SEGMENTS (Interest-Driven)

### 7.1 Category Enthusiasts
**Definition:** Purchases concentrated in specific categories
**Criteria:**
- 60%+ of purchases in single category
OR
- 5+ views in category, 2+ purchases

**Examples:**
- Skincare lovers
- Home decor enthusiasts
- Fitness gear buyers

**Use Cases:**
- Category-specific new arrivals
- Related product recommendations
- Category expert content

---

### 7.2 Full-Price Buyers
**Definition:** Rarely or never uses discounts
**Criteria:**
- `Discount code used` = 0-10% of orders
- Purchases full-price items

**Use Cases:**
- Premium product launches
- Exclusive collections
- No discount needed (protect margin)

---

### 7.3 Sale Shoppers
**Definition:** Only purchases during promotions
**Criteria:**
- `Discount code used` = 80%+ of orders
- Purchases primarily during sale periods

**Use Cases:**
- Sale announcements
- Flash sale early access
- Clearance notifications

---

### 7.4 Product-Specific Segments
**Definition:** Purchased specific products
**Criteria:**
- `Purchased product X` = Yes

**Use Cases:**
- Complementary product recommendations
- Replenishment reminders
- Product-specific tips/content

---

### 7.5 High AOV Customers
**Definition:** Above-average order value
**Criteria:**
- `Average order value` = Top 20 percentile

**Use Cases:**
- Premium product recommendations
- Bundle offers
- Free shipping threshold optimization

## 8. GEOGRAPHIC SEGMENTS (Location-Based)

### 8.1 By Country
**Purpose:** Localization, shipping, compliance
**Segments:**
- Domestic (primary market)
- International (by region)
- High-shipping-cost regions

**Use Cases:**
- Localized content
- Shipping promotions
- Currency display
- Compliance (GDPR, etc.)

---

### 8.2 By Climate/Season
**Purpose:** Seasonal relevance
**Segments:**
- Northern hemisphere (winter/summer opposite)
- Tropical climates
- Cold climates
- Warm climates

**Use Cases:**
- Seasonal product recommendations
- Weather-triggered campaigns
- Appropriate timing

---

### 8.3 By City/Region
**Purpose:** Local events, store visits
**Segments:**
- Major metropolitan areas
- Near physical store locations
- Event host cities

**Use Cases:**
- Store event invitations
- Local pickup promotions
- Regional events

---

### 8.4 Time Zone Segments
**Purpose:** Optimal send times
**Segments:**
- EST, CST, MST, PST (US)
- GMT, CET, etc. (International)

**Use Cases:**
- Send time optimization
- Real-time event emails
- Flash sale timing

## 10. DYNAMIC SEGMENTATION (Real-Time)

### 10.1 Real-Time Behavioral Triggers
**Examples:**
- Viewed product 3+ times in 1 hour → Send browse abandonment
- Added to cart, left site → Cart abandonment flow
- Reached VIP threshold → VIP welcome flow
- Birthday today → Birthday email

**Implementation:**
- Use Klaviyo real-time events
- Set up webhooks for instant triggers
- Configure flow filters for precision

---

### 10.2 Predictive Send Time
**Definition:** Send when each user most likely to engage
**Implementation:**
- Use Klaviyo's predictive send time
- Or calculate based on historical open times
- Segment by typical engagement window

---

### 10.3 Inventory-Based Segments
**Examples:**
- Viewed product now low stock → Urgency email
- Waitlisted product now available → Back in stock
- Price drop on viewed items → Price alert

---

## 11. SEGMENT COMBINATIONS (Power Strategies)

### 11.1 VIP + Engaged + High AOV
**Purpose:** Maximum value customers
**Treatment:**
- Personal account manager
- Exclusive product previews
- Highest tier rewards

---

### 11.2 At-Risk + High LTV
**Purpose:** Prevent valuable customer loss
**Treatment:**
- Personal outreach (phone/email)
- Significant winback offer
- Satisfaction survey

---

### 11.3 New Subscriber + High Intent
**Purpose:** Accelerate first purchase
**Criteria:**
- Subscribed <7 days
- 3+ page views
- Viewed pricing page

**Treatment:**
- Immediate welcome + offer
- Chat prompt
- Expedited nurture

---

### 11.4 Repeat Customer + Single Category
**Purpose:** Cross-category expansion
**Treatment:**
- Educational content about other categories
- Cross-category bundle offers
- Social proof from similar customers

---

### 11.5 Cart Abandoner + Price Sensitive
**Purpose:** Recover with right incentive
**Treatment:**
- Tiered offers (start low, increase)
- Payment plan options
- Price match guarantee

## 12. TESTING & OPTIMIZATION

### 12.1 Segment Performance Metrics
Track for each segment:
- Open rate
- Click rate
- Conversion rate
- Revenue per recipient
- Unsubscribe rate
- Spam complaint rate

### 12.2 A/B Testing by Segment
Test variables:
- Subject lines
- Offer amounts
- Send times
- Content length
- CTA placement
- Personalization level

### 12.3 Segment Refinement
**Monthly Review:**
- Are segments still accurate?
- Are there new behavioral patterns?
- Which segments underperforming?
- Should segments be split or combined?

### 12.4 List Hygiene
**Quarterly Actions:**
- Remove hard bounces immediately
- Sunset flow for 180+ day inactive
- Re-engagement campaigns
- Preference center updates

## KLAVIYO SETUP GUIDE

### Creating Segments in Klaviyo

1. **Navigate to Segments**
   - Click "Segments & Lists" in left sidebar
   - Click "Create Segment/List"

2. **Define Conditions**
   - Use "Properties about someone" for profile data
   - Use "What someone has done" for behaviors
   - Use "Predictive Analytics" for AI segments

3. **Set Timeframes**
   - Use "over all time" for lifetime behaviors
   - Use "in the last X days" for recent behaviors

4. **Save and Name**
   - Use clear, descriptive names
   - Add descriptions for team clarity

### Segment Update Frequency
- Real-time: Behavioral triggers
- Daily: Engagement segments
- Weekly: RFM scores
- Monthly: Lifecycle stages
