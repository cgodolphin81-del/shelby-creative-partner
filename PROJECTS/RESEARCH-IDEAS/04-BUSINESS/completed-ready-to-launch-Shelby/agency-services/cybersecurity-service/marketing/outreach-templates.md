## Email Templates

### Template 1: Cold Outreach - Security Audit

**Subject:** Quick question about [Company]'s security posture

```

etc.]. Congratulations on the growth!

• Preparing for SOC 2 compliance to close enterprise deals
• Scaling security practices with rapid team growth
• Managing third-party and vendor risk

We recently helped [Similar Company] achieve SOC 2 Type II in 12 weeks, 
which unlocked $2M+ in enterprise pipeline.

Would you be open to a brief 15-minute call next week to discuss your 
security priorities for [current year]?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Title]
[Company]
[Phone]
[Calendar Link]
```

### Qualification Call Script

```
OPENING (2 min):
"Thanks for taking the time today. The goal of this call is to learn about 
your security priorities and see if we might be a good fit to help. 

Does that work for you?"

DISCOVERY (10 min):
"Can you tell me about your current security program?"
"What are your biggest security concerns right now?"
"Have you done any security assessments or audits before?"
"What compliance requirements do you have?"
"What's your timeline for addressing security?"

BUDGET/AUTHORITY (3 min):
"Have you budgeted for security initiatives this year?"
"Who else is involved in decisions like this?"

NEXT STEPS (5 min):
"Based on what you've shared, I think we could help with [specific areas].

Here's what I'd recommend: [brief proposal].

Would you be open to [next step: technical call, proposal, assessment]?"

CLOSING:
"Great. Let me send over a calendar invite for [date/time]. I'll also 
include [materials] in that email.

Anything else I can answer for you today?"
```

### Objection Handling

**"We're already working with someone"**
```
"That's great - it shows you take security seriously. Out of curiosity, 
what services are they providing? 

[Listen]

Many of our clients came to us while maintaining their existing providers 
for [specific area where we differentiate]. 

Would it make sense to at least have a conversation about [our specialty]?"
```

---

**"We don't have budget"**
```
"I understand. Security budgets can be tight. 

When do you typically plan your budget for next year?

[Listen]

In the meantime, would it be valuable to have a conversation now so you 
have options when budget is available? We can also discuss phased 
approaches that might fit current constraints."
```

---

**"Send me information"**
```
"Absolutely - I'd be happy to send information. 

To make sure it's relevant, can you tell me a bit about your current 
security priorities?

[Listen]

Great. I'll send over [specific resources] and follow up next week. 

Would [Day] at [Time] work for a brief follow-up call?"
```

---

**"We're too busy right now"**
```
"I completely understand - everyone's stretched thin these days. 

When would be a better time to reconnect?

[Listen]

In the meantime, I'll send over a resource that might be helpful: 
[relevant content]. 

I'll touch base in [timeframe] if that works for you."
```

## Best Practices

### Email Best Practices
- Keep subject lines under 50 characters
- Personalize with company/role specifics
- Focus on their challenges, not your features
- Include clear call-to-action
- Mobile-friendly formatting
- A/B test subject lines and content
- Follow up 3-5 times (most responses come after 3rd touch)

### LinkedIn Best Practices
- Optimize your profile before outreach
- Personalize every connection request
- Engage with prospect content before messaging
- Don't sell in first message
- Use video messages for higher response
- Share valuable content regularly

### Call Best Practices
- Research before calling
- Have clear objective for each call
- Listen more than you talk
- Ask open-ended questions
- Handle objections with empathy
- Always have next step planned

### General Tips
- Track everything in CRM
- Follow up promptly (within 24 hours)
- Provide value in every interaction
- Build relationships, not just pipeline
- Ask for referrals from happy clients
- Continuously test and optimize
