## EMAIL 3: VALUE-FIRST (For Professional Network)

**Subject:** 5 resume mistakes I saw as a hiring manager

```
Hi [First Name],

I was a hiring manager for 5+ years. During that time, I reviewed over
10,000 resumes.

I learned a lot. But mostly, I learned that great candidates get
overlooked for preventable reasons.

Here are the 5 biggest mistakes I saw:

1. WALLS OF TEXT
   Recruiters scan—they don't read. Use bullet points. Keep it scannable.

2. VAGUE RESPONSIBILITIES
   "Managed team" tells me nothing. "Led 8-person team, increased output
   34%" tells me everything.

3. ATS-UNFRIENDLY FORMATTING
   Tables, text boxes, graphics, and creative layouts often break automated
   screening systems. Keep it clean.

4. ONE-SIZE-FITS-ALL APPROACH
   Tailor your resume to each job. Include keywords from the description.
   It matters.

5. HIDING YOUR BEST WORK
   Your top achievements should be in the top third of page one. That's
   prime real estate. Don't waste it.

BONUS: Save as PDF for humans. Apply with .docx for ATS systems.
```

## 📧 EMAIL BEST PRACTICES

### Subject Line Tips
- Keep it under 50 characters (mobile preview)
- Create curiosity without clickbait
- Personalize when possible ("Quick question, [Name]")
- Avoid spam triggers (FREE, $$$$, ACT NOW)

### Content Tips
- Lead with value, not the ask
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences max)
- Use bold for key points (scannable)
- Include one clear call-to-action
- Sign with your real name (not just the shop)

### Timing
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Best times:** 9-11 AM or 2-4 PM (recipient's timezone)
- **Avoid:** Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, weekends

### List Segmentation (If You Have It)
- **Close friends/family:** Use Email 2 (soft ask)
- **Professional contacts:** Use Email 3 (value-first)
- **General network:** Use Email 1 (launch announcement)
- **Non-openers:** Use Email 4 (follow-up)
- **After success stories:** Use Email 5 (social proof)

### Compliance
- Include your physical address (required by CAN-SPAM)
- Include an unsubscribe option
- Don't buy email lists (only email people who know you)
- Honor unsubscribe requests immediately

## 📊 TRACKING & FOLLOW-UP

### What to Track
- Open rate (aim for 25%+)
- Click-through rate (aim for 3%+)
- Reply rate (any replies are good—means people care)
- Conversions (use Etsy stats + discount code tracking)

### Follow-Up Strategy
- **Day 1:** Send initial email
- **Day 3-4:** Send follow-up to non-openers (if your platform allows)
- **Day 7:** Share success story email
- **Ongoing:** Reply personally to anyone who responds

### Thank-You Protocol
When someone replies, purchases, or shares:
- Respond within 24 hours
- Be genuinely grateful (not robotic)
- Offer continued support
- Consider a handwritten thank-you note for big supporters

## 🎯 SUBJECT LINE A/B TESTS

If you're sending to a large list, test these variations:

**Version A:** "I launched something (and I think it might help you)"
**Version B:** "Quick news + a gift for you"
**Version C:** "[Name], I could use your support"

**Version E:** "Something I've been working on..."

Send to 10% of your list each, then send the winner to the remaining 50%.
