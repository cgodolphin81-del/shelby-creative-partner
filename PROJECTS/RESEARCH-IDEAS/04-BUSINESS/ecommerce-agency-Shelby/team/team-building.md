## Role Descriptions

### Account Manager

**Responsibilities**:
- Primary client contact
- Weekly/monthly reporting
- Strategy calls facilitation
- Cross-sell/upsell identification
- Client satisfaction (NPS)
- Internal coordination

**Skills Required**:
- Excellent communication
- Project management
- E-commerce knowledge
- Data analysis basics
- Empathy and patience

**Experience**: 2-5 years in account management, agency experience preferred

**Salary**: $60K-90K base + 5-10% bonus

**KPIs**:
- Client retention rate (90%+)
- NPS score (50+)
- Upsell revenue
- Response time (< 4 hours)

## Hiring Process

### Step 1: Define the Role

**Role Scorecard Template**:

```
ROLE: Paid Media Manager

MISSION:
Manage and optimize paid media campaigns for 8-12 e-commerce clients, 
delivering 3.0+ ROAS and 20%+ QoQ revenue growth.

OUTCOMES (First 90 Days):
- Take over 5 client accounts with minimal performance disruption
- Identify and implement 3+ optimization opportunities per account
- Establish testing roadmap for each client
- Achieve 3.0+ average ROAS across portfolio

OUTCOMES (Ongoing):
- Manage $500K+/month in ad spend
- Maintain 3.0+ average ROAS
- Drive 20%+ QoQ revenue growth
- Launch 4+ tests/month per client

COMPETENCIES:
- Meta Ads Manager (expert)
- Google Ads (advanced)
- Analytics and data analysis (advanced)
- Creative strategy (intermediate)
- Client communication (intermediate)

VALUES FIT:
- Owner mentality
- Data-driven decisions
- Continuous learning
- Client-first mindset
```

### Step 2: Source Candidates

**Sourcing Channels**:

| Channel | Quality | Cost | Speed | Best For |
|---------|---------|------|-------|----------|
| Employee referrals | High | Low ($ referral bonus) | Fast | All roles |
| LinkedIn | High | Medium (Recruiter license) | Medium | Senior roles |
| Indeed/Glassdoor | Medium | Medium (posting fees) | Fast | Mid-level roles |
| specialized job boards | High | Low-Medium | Medium | Specialized roles |
| Agencies | High | High (15-25% of salary) | Fast | Executive roles |
| Freelance → FT | High | Low (trial period) | Slow | All roles |

**Recommended Job Boards**:
- General: LinkedIn, Indeed
- Marketing: MarketerHire, GrowthHackers, Online Geniuses
- Design: Dribbble, Behance
- Copywriting: ProBlogger, Copywriting Jobs
- Remote: We Work Remotely, Remote OK, Working Nomads

### Step 3: Screen & Interview

**Screening Process**:

```
Application → Resume Review → Phone Screen → Assessment → 
Interview 1 (Skills) → Interview 2 (Culture) → Reference Check → Offer
```

**Phone Screen (30 minutes)**:
- Background and experience overview
- Motivation for leaving current role
- Salary expectations
- Availability
- Red flag check

**Skills Assessment**:

*Paid Media Manager Example*:
```
PRACTICAL ASSESSMENT (2-3 hours)

Scenario: You've inherited a Meta Ads account for a DTC skincare brand.

Current State:
- Monthly spend: $50K
- ROAS: 2.1
- CPA: $65
- Primary campaign: CBO, 5 ad sets, 15 ads

Task:
1. Audit the account (we'll provide sample data)
2. Identify 3-5 optimization opportunities
3. Create a 30-day action plan
4. Present your recommendations (10-minute Loom video)

Deliverables:
- Written audit (Google Doc)
- 30-day plan (Google Sheet)
- Video presentation (Loom)
```

*Email Marketing Manager Example*:
```
PRACTICAL ASSESSMENT (2-3 hours)

Scenario: A DTC fashion brand wants to improve their email performance.

Current State:
- List size: 100K subscribers
- Email revenue: 15% of total (target: 30%)
- Welcome flow: 3 emails (open rate: 35%)
- Campaigns: 2/week (open rate: 18%)

Task:
1. Audit their current email program (we'll provide examples)
2. Design a new welcome flow (5 emails, write subject lines + outlines)
3. Create a 4-week campaign calendar
4. Present your strategy (10-minute Loom video)

Deliverables:
- Written audit (Google Doc)
- Welcome flow outlines (Google Doc)
- Campaign calendar (Google Sheet)
- Video presentation (Loom)
```

**Interview 1: Skills (60 minutes)**:
- Deep dive into assessment
- Scenario questions
- Technical knowledge check
- Problem-solving exercises

**Sample Questions**:
```
Paid Media:
- "Walk me through how you'd structure a Meta Ads account for a new DTC brand."
- "A client's ROAS dropped from 4.0 to 2.5 overnight. How do you diagnose?"
- "What's your framework for creative testing?"
- "How do you think about iOS14+ attribution challenges?"

Email Marketing:
- "How do you approach segmentation for a brand with 500K subscribers?"
- "What flows are absolutely essential for any e-commerce brand?"
- "How do you balance promotional vs. value emails?"
- "What's your process for improving deliverability?"

CRO:
- "Walk me through your CRO audit process."
- "How do you prioritize which tests to run?"
- "A test shows 90% confidence after 3 days. What do you do?"
- "How do you think about mobile vs. desktop optimization?"
```

**Interview 2: Culture (45 minutes)**:
- Values alignment
- Work style preferences
- Team dynamics
- Growth mindset
- Conducted by founder or department head

**Sample Questions**:
```
- "Tell me about a time you failed. What did you learn?"
- "Describe your ideal work environment."
- "How do you handle conflicting priorities?"
- "What are you looking for in your next role?"
- "Where do you want to be in 3 years?"
- "What type of manager brings out your best work?"
```

### Step 4: Reference Checks

**Reference Questions**:
```
1. How do you know the candidate and for how long?
2. What were their primary responsibilities?
3. What are their biggest strengths?
4. What's one area they could improve?
5. How do they handle feedback?
6. How do they perform under pressure?
7. Would you rehire them? Why or why not?
8. On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate their overall performance?
9. What type of role/environment would they thrive in?
10. Anything else I should know?
```

### Step 5: Offer & Close

**Offer Components**:
- Base salary
- Bonus structure (individual + company)
- Equity (optional, for senior roles)
- Benefits (health, dental, vision, 401k)
- PTO (unlimited or fixed)
- Remote work policy
- Professional development budget
- Start date

**Offer Script**:
```
"Hi [Candidate],

We're thrilled to offer you the [Role] position at [Company]!

We were impressed by your [specific strengths] and believe you'll 
be instrumental in helping us [company goal].

Here are the details:
- Base salary: $[X]
- Bonus: [X]% based on [metrics]
- Equity: [X] options (if applicable)
- Benefits: [summary]
- Start date: [date]

We'd love to have you on the team. Can we schedule a call tomorrow 
to discuss and answer any questions?

Excited to potentially work together!

[Your name]"
```

## Onboarding Process

### Week 1: Orientation

**Day 1**:
- Welcome call with manager
- IT setup (laptop, accounts, tools)
- HR onboarding (benefits, policies)
- Team introductions
- Review role scorecard

**Day 2-3**:
- Tool training (internal systems)
- Shadow team members
- Review documentation
- Meet key stakeholders

**Day 4-5**:
- Begin hands-on work (low stakes)
- Daily check-ins with manager
- Week 1 reflection call

### Week 2-4: Ramp-Up

**Goals**:
- Complete core training modules
- Take on initial responsibilities
- Build relationships with team
- Understand client portfolio

**Check-ins**:
- Daily standup with team
- 2x/week 1:1 with manager
- End of week 2 and 4 reviews

### Month 2-3: Independence

**Goals**:
- Own core responsibilities
- Contribute to strategy
- Identify improvement opportunities
- Begin contributing to team knowledge

**Check-ins**:
- Weekly 1:1 with manager
- 30/60/90 day reviews
- First performance check-in at day 90

### Bonus Structures

**Individual Bonus** (5-15% of base):
- Based on individual KPIs
- Paid quarterly or annually
- Example: 10% bonus if 100% of KPIs met

**Company Bonus** (5-10% of base):
- Based on company revenue/profit targets
- Paid annually
- Example: 5% bonus if company hits $5M revenue

**Commission** (for sales roles):
- 5-15% of new business closed
- Accelerators for exceeding quota
- Example: 10% on first $500K, 15% above

### Equity

**When to Offer**:
- Senior hires (Director+)
- Early employees (first 10)
- Critical roles hard to fill

**Typical Ranges**:
- Early employee (first 10): 0.5-2.0%
- Director level: 0.25-0.75%
- VP level: 0.5-1.5%
- C-level: 2-5%

**Vesting**: 4 years, 1-year cliff

### Benefits

**Standard Benefits**:
- Health insurance (medical, dental, vision)
- 401(k) with match (optional)
- PTO (unlimited or 15-25 days)
- Sick leave
- Parental leave
- Remote work flexibility
- Home office stipend ($500-1,000)

**Nice-to-Have**:
- Professional development budget ($1K-3K/year)
- Conference attendance
- Gym membership
- Mental health support
- Team retreats (1-2x/year)
- Company equity pool

## Culture & Retention

### Culture Principles

**Define Your Values** (examples):
1. **Client First**: We win when our clients win
2. **Owner Mentality**: Think and act like an owner
3. **Data-Driven**: Decisions backed by data, not opinions
4. **Continuous Learning**: Always improving, always curious
5. **Radical Candor**: Care personally, challenge directly
6. **Bias for Action**: Bias toward action over perfection
7. **Team Over Ego**: Collaborate, don't compete internally

### Retention Strategies

**1. Growth Opportunities**:
- Clear career paths
- Regular promotions
- Skill development budget
- Mentorship programs

**2. Recognition**:
- Public recognition in meetings
- Employee of the month
- Spot bonuses for exceptional work
- Anniversary celebrations

**3. Work-Life Balance**:
- Flexible hours
- Remote work options
- No weekend expectations
- Encourage PTO usage

**4. Competitive Compensation**:
- Annual salary reviews
- Market-rate adjustments
- Performance bonuses
- Equity for tenured employees

**5. Meaningful Work**:
- Connect work to impact
- Share client success stories
- Involve team in strategy
- Autonomy in execution

### Exit Interviews

**When someone leaves, learn why**:
```
1. What prompted you to start looking for a new role?
2. What did you enjoy most about working here?
3. What did you enjoy least?
4. Did you feel supported in your role?
5. Did you see a future for yourself here?
6. How was your relationship with your manager?
7. What could we do to improve retention?
8. Would you consider returning in the future?
9. Would you recommend us as a place to work?
10. Anything else you'd like to share?
```

## Summary

```
Team Building Formula:

Right People + Right Roles + Right Systems + Right Culture = Scalable Team

Hiring Priorities (by stage):
Phase 1 ($0-50K MRR): Founder does everything
Phase 2 ($50K-150K MRR): Account Manager, Paid Media, Email
Phase 3 ($150K-500K MRR): COO, Department Heads, Specialists
Phase 4 ($500K+ MRR): Executive Team, Full Departments

Key Principles:
1. Hire slow, fire fast
2. Values fit > Skills (skills can be taught)
3. Invest in onboarding (first 90 days are critical)
4. Pay market rate (cheap hires are expensive)
5. Create growth paths (retain your best people)
6. Document everything (systems scale, chaos doesn't)
```
