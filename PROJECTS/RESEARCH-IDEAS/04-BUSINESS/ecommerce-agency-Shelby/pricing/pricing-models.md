# Pricing Models

## Project-Based, Retainer, and Performance-Based Pricing

---

## Pricing Philosophy

### Our Approach

**Value-Based Pricing**: We price based on the value we deliver, not the hours we work. A $50,000 project that generates $500,000 in incremental revenue is a bargain.

**Transparency**: Clear pricing, no hidden fees, clear scope definitions.

**Flexibility**: Different models for different client needs and stages.

**Partnership**: We succeed when our clients succeed. Performance-based options align incentives.

---

## Pricing Model Overview

| Model | Best For | Pros | Cons | Our Usage |
|-------|----------|------|------|-----------|
| **Project-Based** | One-time builds, specific initiatives | Predictable cost, clear scope | No ongoing optimization, client manages post-launch | 30% of revenue |
| **Retainer** | Ongoing growth, full-service | Predictable revenue, deep partnership | Requires consistent delivery, scope creep risk | 50% of revenue |
| **Performance-Based** | Confident in results, aligned incentives | Unlimited upside, strong alignment | Revenue volatility, longer payment cycles | 15% of revenue |
| **Hybrid** | Most clients | Best of both worlds | More complex to structure | 5% of revenue |

### Project Pricing Formula

```
Project Price = (Estimated Hours × Hourly Rate) × Complexity Multiplier

Where:
- Hourly Rate: $150-250/hour (based on role)
- Complexity Multiplier: 1.0 (standard) to 2.0 (high complexity)

Example:
Shopify Professional Build
- Design: 40 hours × $150 = $6,000
- Development: 60 hours × $175 = $10,500
- Project Management: 20 hours × $125 = $2,500
- Subtotal: $19,000
- Complexity Multiplier: 1.2 (some custom work)
- Total: $22,800 → Rounded to $20,000-25,000
```

### Retainer Pricing Formula

```
Retainer Price = (Team Hours × Hourly Rate) + Overhead + Margin

Example: Growth Partner Retainer

- Total: $19,500 → Rounded to $10,000-15,000 (volume discount)
```

## Performance-Based Pricing

### When to Use
- Confident in ability to drive results
- Client wants risk-sharing
- Established brands with clear baseline
- When we have significant leverage

### Performance Models

#### 1. Revenue Share Model

**Structure**:
- Base retainer: 50-75% of standard rate
- Performance fee: 10-20% of incremental revenue

**Example**:
```
Standard retainer: $20,000/month
Performance structure:
- Base: $10,000/month
- Performance: 15% of revenue above baseline

Baseline: $500,000/month (3-month average before engagement)

Month 1 Results:
- Revenue: $600,000
- Incremental: $100,000
- Performance fee: $100,000 × 15% = $15,000
- Total: $10,000 + $15,000 = $25,000

Effective rate: 4.2% of revenue
```

**Pros**: Unlimited upside, strong alignment
**Cons**: Revenue volatility, attribution complexity

#### 2. ROAS-Based Model

**Structure**:
- Base retainer: Covers costs
- Performance bonus: Based on ROAS tiers

**Example**:
```
Base retainer: $10,000/month
Ad spend: $100,000/month

Performance tiers:
- ROAS < 2.0: No bonus
- ROAS 2.0-3.0: 5% of ad spend ($5,000)
- ROAS 3.0-4.0: 10% of ad spend ($10,000)
- ROAS 4.0-5.0: 15% of ad spend ($15,000)
- ROAS 5.0+: 20% of ad spend ($20,000)

Month 1 Results:
- ROAS: 4.2
- Bonus: 15% × $100,000 = $15,000
- Total: $10,000 + $15,000 = $25,000

Effective rate: 15% of ad spend
```

**Pros**: Clear metrics, rewards efficiency
**Cons**: May discourage testing, ROAS not everything

#### 3. Profit Share Model

**Structure**:
- Base retainer: Minimal (covers costs)
- Performance fee: 20-30% of incremental profit

**Example**:
```
Base retainer: $5,000/month
Baseline profit: $100,000/month (3-month average)
Profit share: 25% of incremental profit

Month 1 Results:
- Profit: $180,000
- Incremental: $80,000
- Performance fee: $80,000 × 25% = $20,000
- Total: $5,000 + $20,000 = $25,000
```

**Pros**: Best alignment, focuses on profitability
**Cons**: Requires profit transparency, complex tracking

#### 4. CPA/CAC Model

**Structure**:
- Pay per acquisition
- Fixed rate per customer

**Example**:
```
Target CAC: $50
Performance fee: $40 per new customer

Month 1 Results:
- New customers: 2,000
- Performance fee: 2,000 × $40 = $80,000
- Base retainer: $10,000
- Total: $90,000

Effective CAC for client: $45 (includes our fee in ad spend)
```

**Pros**: Simple, clear value
**Cons**: May encourage low-quality acquisitions

### Performance Pricing Considerations

**Attribution**:
- Use multi-touch attribution (not last-click)
- Agree on attribution window (7-day click, 1-day view standard)
- Consider blended metrics (not just platform-reported)
- Use tracking tools (Triple Whale, Northbeam) for accuracy

**Baseline Setting**:
- Use 3-month average before engagement
- Account for seasonality
- Consider year-over-year growth trends
- Document baseline in contract

**Payment Terms**:
- Base retainer: Paid monthly in advance
- Performance fee: Paid monthly in arrears (after month closes)
- Reconciliation: 15 days after month-end
- Audit rights: Client can request attribution review

**Risk Mitigation**:
- Minimum base retainer (covers costs)
- Maximum performance cap (optional)
- Force majeure clauses
- Exit clauses for both parties

## Pricing Psychology

### Best Practices

**1. Anchor High**
- Present premium option first
- Makes other options seem reasonable
- Sets value expectation

**2. Three Tiers**
- Good, Better, Best
- Most choose middle option
- Clear differentiation

**3. Value Framing**
- Frame as investment, not cost
- Show ROI projections
- Compare to in-house hiring

**4. Scarcity**
- Limited spots available
- Price increases on [date]
- Current pricing for early adopters

**5. Risk Reversal**
- 30-day satisfaction guarantee
- Performance guarantees (where appropriate)
- Easy cancellation (after minimum term)

### Pricing Presentation

**Do**:
- Present in person (video call)
- Show value before price
- Use specific numbers (not ranges)
- Provide written proposal within 24 hours
- Follow up within 48 hours

**Don't**:
- Lead with price
- Apologize for pricing
- Negotiate immediately
- Undervalue your services
- Compete on price alone

## Pricing FAQ

**Q: Do you offer discounts for startups?**
A: We offer a Startup Package ($15,000/month) for pre-Series B companies, includes reduced scope with path to full service as you grow.

**Q: Can we start with a project and move to retainer?**
A: Yes! Many clients start with a project (audit, setup) and transition to retainer once they see results.

**Q: What if we're not happy with results?**
A: We have a 30-day satisfaction guarantee on retainers. If you're not happy in the first 30 days, we'll work to fix it or release you from the contract.

**Q: Do you work with performance-only pricing?**
A: We offer performance-based pricing for qualified clients (typically $1M+ revenue, clear baseline, strong product-market fit).

**Q: Can we adjust scope mid-retainer?**
A: Yes, we review scope monthly and can adjust based on priorities. Significant changes may affect pricing.

**Q: What's not included in retainers?**
A: Major projects (redesigns, migrations), custom development, and production costs (UGC, video) are scoped separately.

**Q: Do you offer payment plans?**
A: For projects $25K+, we offer 3-6 month payment plans with approved credit.
