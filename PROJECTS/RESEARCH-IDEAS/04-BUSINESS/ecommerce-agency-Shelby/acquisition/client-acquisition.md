# Client Acquisition

## Targeting DTC Brands, E-Commerce Businesses, and Outreach Strategies

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP: Growth-Stage DTC Brands

```
Company Profile:
- Revenue: $1M - $50M annually
- Growth rate: 20-100% YoY
- Funding: Bootstrapped or Series A/B
- Team size: 10-100 employees
- E-commerce %: 50%+ of revenue

Decision Maker:
- Title: Founder, CEO, CMO, VP Marketing
- Pain points: Plateaued growth, inefficient spend, lack of expertise
- Goals: Scale to next revenue tier, improve profitability
- Budget: $10K-100K/month for marketing

Tech Stack:
- Platform: Shopify or Shopify Plus
- Email: Klaviyo (preferred) or similar
- Analytics: GA4, possibly Triple Whale/Northbeam
- Ads: Meta, Google, possibly TikTok

Marketing Maturity:
- Stage 2-3 (out of 5)
- Some in-house capability
- Recognize need for expertise
- Ready to invest in growth
```

### Secondary ICP: Established E-Commerce Brands

```
Company Profile:
- Revenue: $10M - $200M annually
- Growth rate: 10-50% YoY
- Team size: 50-500 employees
- Multiple sales channels (DTC + Amazon + Wholesale)

Decision Maker:
- Title: CMO, VP E-commerce, Director of Growth
- Pain points: Channel optimization, attribution, scaling efficiently
- Goals: Improve ROAS, expand channels, increase LTV
- Budget: $50K-200K/month for marketing

Key Needs:
- Multi-channel expertise
- Advanced attribution
- International expansion
- Team augmentation
```

### Tertiary ICP: Emerging DTC Brands

```
Company Profile:
- Revenue: $500K - $5M annually
- Growth rate: 50-200% YoY
- Team size: 2-20 employees
- Product-market fit achieved

Decision Maker:
- Title: Founder/CEO (hands-on)
- Pain points: Need expertise, limited budget, wearing many hats
- Goals: Reach $10M, build foundation for scale
- Budget: $5K-25K/month for marketing

Key Needs:
- Foundation building
- Cost-effective solutions
- Education and guidance
- Flexible engagement models
```

#### Cold Email Best Practices

**Do**:
- Personalize first 2 lines (specific observation)
- Keep under 150 words
- Include 1 clear CTA
- Follow up 3-5 times
- Use plain text (no HTML)
- Send from personal domain (not generic)

**Don't**:
- Use generic templates
- Write novels
- Make vague claims
- Give up after 1 email
- Use spammy language ("guaranteed", "no risk")
- Send from no-reply addresses

#### Cold Email Metrics

| Metric | Target | Industry Avg |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Open rate | 40-60% | 20-30% |
| Reply rate | 5-15% | 1-5% |
| Meeting booked | 2-5% | 0.5-2% |
| Unsubscribe | < 1% | 1-3% |

## Sales Process

### Stage 1: Discovery Call (30 minutes)

**Agenda**:
1. Introductions (2 min)
2. Their business overview (10 min)
3. Current challenges and goals (10 min)
4. Qualification (5 min)
5. Next steps (3 min)

**Qualification Questions**:
```
Business:
- What's your current monthly revenue?
- What's your growth goal for the next 12 months?
- What's your biggest growth challenge right now?

Marketing:
- What channels are you currently using?
- What's working well? What's not?
- What's your current marketing budget?

Decision:
- Who else is involved in this decision?
- What's your timeline for getting started?
- Have you worked with an agency before? What was that like?
```

**Qualification Criteria (BANT)**:
- **Budget**: $10K+/month available
- **Authority**: Decision maker or strong influencer
- **Need**: Clear pain point we can solve
- **Timeline**: Ready to start within 90 days

### Stage 2: Proposal & Presentation (45-60 minutes)

**Proposal Structure**:
1. Executive Summary
2. Current State Analysis
3. Recommended Solution
4. Scope of Work
5. Investment
6. Timeline
7. Case Studies
8. Next Steps

**Presentation Flow**:
1. Recap of their challenges (5 min)
2. Our approach (10 min)
3. Specific recommendations (15 min)
4. Investment and ROI (10 min)
5. Case studies (10 min)
6. Q&A (10 min)
7. Close and next steps (5 min)

### Stage 3: Closing

**Objection Handling**:

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "It's too expensive" | "I understand. Can I ask what budget you had in mind? Let's see if we can adjust scope to fit." |
| "We need to think about it" | "Of course. What specifically do you need to think through? I want to make sure I've addressed all your concerns." |
| "We're talking to other agencies" | "That's smart. What factors are most important in your decision? I want to make sure you have all the info you need." |
| "We tried an agency before and it didn't work" | "I'm sorry to hear that. Can you share what didn't work? I want to make sure we address those concerns upfront." |
| "We need to hire in-house first" | "That's a valid approach. Have you calculated the total cost of hiring (salary, benefits, tools, training)? An agency can often be more cost-effective while you're scaling." |

**Closing Techniques**:
- Assumptive close: "When would you like to get started?"
- Summary close: "Based on everything we've discussed, here's what makes sense..."
- Urgency close: "We have 2 spots available for [month]. Would you like to secure one?"

### Stage 4: Onboarding

**Onboarding Checklist**:
- [ ] Contract signed
- [ ] First payment received
- [ ] Kickoff call scheduled
- [ ] Access to all platforms (Shopify, GA, Ads, Email, etc.)
- [ ] Brand guidelines received
- [ ] Stakeholder introductions
- [ ] Success metrics defined
- [ ] Communication cadence established

## Summary

```
Client Acquisition Formula:

Leads = (Content × Distribution) + (Outreach × Volume) + (Partnerships × Leverage)

Conversion = (Qualification × Fit) + (Process × Trust) + (Follow-up × Persistence)

Targets:
- 100 MQLs/month
- 30 SQLs/month
- 20 Discovery calls/month
- 10 Proposals/month
- 3-5 Closes/month
- $50K-150K new MRR/month

Key Principles:
1. Focus on ICP (don't chase bad fits)
2. Provide value first (educate, don't pitch)
3. Follow up persistently (most deals close on 5th+ touch)
4. Track everything (optimize what works)
5. Build relationships (not transactions)
```
