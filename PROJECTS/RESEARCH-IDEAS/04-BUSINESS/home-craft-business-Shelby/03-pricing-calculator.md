## Why Pricing Matters

**Underpricing hurts everyone:**
- You burn out working for less than minimum wage
- You can't reinvest in your business
- You devalue your entire craft category
- Customers don't trust "too cheap" handmade items

**Right pricing enables:**
- Sustainable income
- Business growth
- Quality materials
- Time for creativity
- Emergency funds and taxes

---

## The Pricing Formula

### Basic Formula

```
MATERIALS + LABOR + OVERHEAD + FEES + PROFIT = WHOLESALE PRICE
WHOLESALE PRICE × 2 = RETAIL PRICE
```

### Detailed Formula

```
1. MATERIALS (all supplies used)
2. LABOR (your time × hourly rate)
3. OVERHEAD (business expenses ÷ items made)
4. FEES (platform, payment processing, shipping)
5. PROFIT (what's left for you to grow/save)
```

## Calculate Your Costs

### 1. Materials

**Track EVERYTHING:**

| Item | Cost | Amount Used | Cost Per Item |
|------|------|-------------|---------------|
| Soy wax | $50/10lbs | 0.5 lbs | $2.50 |
| Fragrance oil | $15/4oz | 0.5 oz | $1.88 |
| Wick | $10/100 | 1 | $0.10 |
| Jar | $2/each | 1 | $2.00 |
| Label | $0.50/each | 1 | $0.50 |
| Packaging | $1/box | 1 | $1.00 |
| **TOTAL** | | | **$7.98** |

**Pro Tips:**
- Weigh/measure everything
- Include "hidden" materials (glue, thread, tape)
- Track packaging costs
- Update prices when material costs change

### 2. Labor

**What's your time worth?**

| Skill Level | Hourly Rate |
|-------------|-------------|
| Beginner | $10-15/hr |
| Intermediate | $15-25/hr |
| Advanced | $25-40/hr |
| Expert/Premium | $40-75+/hr |

**Calculate labor time:**
- Making time
- Prep time (measuring, setup)
- Cleanup time
- Packaging time
- Admin time (listing, customer service) ÷ items

**Example:**
```
Making: 20 minutes
Cleanup: 5 minutes
Packaging: 5 minutes
Admin: 5 minutes (averaged)
Total: 35 minutes = 0.58 hours

Labor cost: 0.58 hrs × $20/hr = $11.60
```

### 3. Overhead

**Monthly business expenses:**

| Expense | Monthly Cost |
|---------|--------------|
| Etsy fees | $50 |
| Website | $15 |
| Insurance | $30 |
| Utilities (portion) | $40 |
| Rent/studio space | $200 |
| Equipment depreciation | $50 |
| Marketing/ads | $100 |
| Subscriptions (software) | $30 |
| **TOTAL** | **$515** |

**Allocate to each item:**
```
Monthly overhead ÷ items made per month = overhead per item

$515 ÷ 100 items = $5.15 per item
```

**Simplified approach** (if starting out):
- Add 10-20% of materials as overhead
- Or use flat $2-5 per item

### 4. Fees

**Etsy fees (example):**

| Fee | Calculation | Amount |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Listing | Flat | $0.20 |
| Transaction | 6.5% of (price + shipping) | Varies |
| Payment processing | 3% + $0.25 | Varies |
| Offsite ads | 15% (only if applicable) | Varies |

**Estimate fees as 10-15% of sale price**

### 5. Profit

**Profit is NOT optional.** It funds:
- Business growth
- Equipment upgrades
- Emergency fund
- Retirement savings
- Taxes

**Target profit margins:**
- Minimum: 20%
- Good: 30-40%
- Excellent: 50%+

### Template 2: Spreadsheet Formula

```
A1: Product Name
A2: Materials Cost          | B2: [enter amount]
A3: Labor Hours             | B3: [enter hours]
A4: Hourly Rate             | B4: [enter rate]
A5: Labor Cost              | B5: =B3*B4
A6: Overhead                | B6: =B2*0.15 (or enter flat)
A7: Base Cost               | B7: =B2+B5+B6
A8: Fee Percentage          | B8: 0.12 (12%)
A9: Profit Percentage       | B9: 0.35 (35%)
A10: Final Price            | B10: =B7/(1-B8-B9)
A11: Fee Amount             | B11: =B10*B8
A12: Profit Amount          | B12: =B10*B9
```

## Real Examples

### Example 1: Soy Candle (8oz)

```
MATERIALS
─────────
Soy wax (0.5 lbs @ $5/lb):        $2.50
Fragrance oil (0.5 oz):           $1.88
Wick:                             $0.10
Jar:                              $2.00
Label:                            $0.50
Box + filler:                     $1.50
───────────────────────────────────────
Total Materials:                  $8.48

LABOR
─────
Making (25 min):                  $8.33
Packaging (5 min):                $1.67
(@ $20/hr)
───────────────────────────────────────
Total Labor:                      $10.00

OVERHEAD
────────
(15% of materials):               $1.27

BASE COST:                        $19.75

FEES (12% of final price):        ~$3.50
PROFIT (35% of final price):      ~$10.25

RETAIL PRICE:                     $29.00
```

**Check:** $19.75 + $3.50 + $10.25 = $33.50... let's recalculate properly:

```
Formula: Price = Base Cost / (1 - fee% - profit%)
Price = $19.75 / (1 - 0.12 - 0.35)
Price = $19.75 / 0.53
Price = $37.26 → Round to $37.00 or $39.00
```

## Common Pricing Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake 1: Charging Only for Materials

```
WRONG: "Materials cost $5, so I'll charge $10"
RIGHT: Materials ($5) + Labor ($15) + Overhead ($3) + Fees ($2) + Profit ($10) = $35
```

### ❌ Mistake 2: Comparing to Mass-Produced Items

```
WRONG: "Target sells this for $15, so I should too"
RIGHT: "My item is handmade with quality materials. Mass-produced ≠ handmade."
```

### ❌ Mistake 3: Not Paying Yourself

```
WRONG: "I'll just keep whatever is left"
RIGHT: "My labor has a set hourly rate, paid BEFORE profit"
```

### ❌ Mistake 4: Discounting Too Much

```
WRONG: Constant 50% off sales
RIGHT: Occasional 10-15% sales, or bundle discounts
```

### ❌ Mistake 5: Ignoring Fees

```
WRONG: "I sold it for $30, I made $30!"
RIGHT: "I sold it for $30, after fees I have $26, after costs I have $12 profit"
```

## Price Adjustment Strategy

### When to Raise Prices

✅ Material costs increased
✅ You're consistently selling out
✅ Your skills have improved
✅ You're adding premium features
✅ Your waitlist is growing
✅ It's been 6+ months since last increase

### How to Raise Prices

**Gradual approach:**
- Increase by 10-15% at a time
- Update listings as they renew
- Announce to email list first (give 2-week notice)

**Communication:**
```
📢 Price Update Notice

Dear customers,

Due to rising material costs and increased demand, 
I'll be adjusting prices on [date]. 

This ensures I can continue creating the quality 
products you love while running a sustainable business.

Thank you for supporting small business! 💕
```

### When to Lower Prices

⚠️ Rarely do this. Instead:
- Improve photos
- Better SEO
- Run a limited sale
- Bundle items
- Offer free shipping

**Only lower if:**
- Consistently no sales after 3+ months
- Major quality issues you've fixed
- Clearing inventory for new line

## Wholesale Pricing

Selling to boutiques? Use this formula:

```
RETAIL PRICE ÷ 2 = WHOLESALE PRICE

Wholesale price must still cover:
- Materials
- Labor (may need to reduce time per item for bulk)
- Overhead
- Fees (lower than retail typically)
- SOME profit (can be lower margin for volume)
```

**Example:**
```
Retail: $40
Wholesale: $20

At wholesale:
- Materials: $8
- Labor (bulk efficiency): $6
- Overhead: $2
- Fees (2% payment processing): $0.40
- Cost: $16.40
- Profit: $3.60 per item

Sell 50 units:
- Revenue: $1,000
- Profit: $180

vs. Retail:
- Sell 25 units at $40 = $1,000 revenue
- Higher profit per item, more work
```

**Wholesale tips:**
- Minimum order quantities (MOQ): 10-50 units
- Payment terms: 50% deposit, net 30
- Don't discount more than 50% off retail
- Have a wholesale line sheet ready

## Pricing Psychology

### Charm Pricing
- $27 vs. $30 (feels significantly cheaper)
- $99 vs. $100 (psychological barrier)

### Premium Pricing
- $100 vs. $99 (signals quality)
- Round numbers feel more premium

### Anchoring
- Show a "regular price" next to sale price
- Offer 3 tiers (good, better, best)

### Bundling
- "Buy 3, get 10% off"
- Increases average order value
