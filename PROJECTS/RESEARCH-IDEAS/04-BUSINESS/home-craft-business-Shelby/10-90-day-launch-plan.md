## How to Use This Plan

### This Plan Is For:

✅ Aspiring craft entrepreneurs
✅ Hobbyists ready to sell
✅ People with a product idea
✅ Those who learn best with structure

### Time Commitment:

- **Phase 1:** 5-10 hours/week
- **Phase 2:** 10-15 hours/week
- **Phase 3:** 15-20 hours/week

### Before You Start:

**You should have:**
- A craft skill (or willingness to learn)
- Some startup capital ($200-1000 depending on craft)
- 3-6 months of runway (don't quit your day job yet!)
- Commitment to follow through

**You don't need:**
- A huge following
- Perfect products
- All the answers
- A business degree

### Flexibility:

This is a GUIDE, not a rigid rulebook.

**Adjust if:**
- You need more time on certain tasks
- Life happens (illness, family, etc.)
- You discover a better approach
- Your craft requires different timing

**The goal:** Launch in 90 days, not perfection.

## Troubleshooting

### "I Didn't Get Any Sales"

**Normal!** Most shops take 2-8 weeks for first sale.

**Actions:**
- Review your photos (are they as good as top sellers?)
- Review your SEO (are you using the right keywords?)
- Review your pricing (competitive but profitable?)
- Increase your marketing (more content, more engagement)
- Consider a promotion
- Be patient and consistent

### "I Got a Sale, Now What?"

**Celebrate!** Then:
- Fulfill quickly and beautifully
- Include a thank-you note
- Ship promptly
- Follow up for a review
- Learn from the experience

### "I'm Overwhelmed"

**Normal!** Running a business is hard.

**Actions:**
- Pick ONE thing to focus on this week
- Lower your standards (done is better than perfect)
- Ask for help (friends, family, hire help)
- Take a break if needed
- Remember your why

### "I'm Not Making Money"

**Common in early months.**

**Actions:**
- Review your pricing (are you actually profitable?)
- Track all expenses
- Look at your time investment
- Consider raising prices
- Focus on bestsellers
- Cut underperforming products

### "I Want to Quit"

**Also normal!** Most entrepreneurs feel this.

**Ask yourself:**
- Is it the business or just a hard season?
- What would make it better?
- Can I adjust my approach?
- Do I need a break?
- What did I learn?

**It's okay to:**
- Pivot your products
- Adjust your goals
- Take a break
- Close the business (if it's truly not working)

**But don't quit on a bad day.** Give it time.
