## Philosophy & Principles

### Our Approach

Community management is not just about responding to messages—it's about building relationships, fostering trust, and creating a space where people feel heard and valued.

### Core Principles

**1. Be Human**
- Respond as a real person, not a bot
- Use conversational language
- Show empathy and understanding
- It's okay to be imperfect

**2. Be Helpful**
- Every interaction is an opportunity to add value
- Answer questions thoroughly
- Go above and when possible
- Point to resources when helpful

**3. Be Consistent**
- Maintain brand voice across all interactions
- Follow established protocols
- Document unusual situations
- Keep the team informed

**4. Be Proactive**
- Don't just wait for messages—engage
- Start conversations
- Celebrate community members
- Anticipate needs

**5. Be Professional**
- Stay calm under pressure
- Never argue publicly
- Know when to take things offline
- Protect the brand's reputation

## Comment Response Guidelines

### Positive Comments

**Goal:** Amplify positivity and build relationship

**Examples:**

| Comment | Response |
|---------|----------|
| "Love this!" | "Thank you so much! We're thrilled you enjoyed it. 💙" |
| "This is exactly what I needed!" | "That's what we love to hear! So glad we could help. 🙌" |
| "Great content as always!" | "You're the best! Thanks for always supporting us. ❤️" |
| "Tagging a friend who needs this" | "Love that you're sharing! Thanks for spreading the value. 👏" |

**Best Practices:**
- Always acknowledge and thank
- Use emojis appropriately for brand voice
- Personalize when possible
- Like/heart the comment

### Questions

**Goal:** Provide helpful, accurate information

**Response Framework:**

1. **Acknowledge** the question
2. **Answer** clearly and concisely
3. **Offer** additional help if needed
4. **Direct** to resources when appropriate

**Examples:**

| Comment | Response |
|---------|----------|
| "How much does this cost?" | "Great question! Our pricing starts at $X. The best way to get a custom quote is to DM us or visit [link]. Happy to help! 😊" |
| "Do you ship internationally?" | "Yes, we do! We ship to [X] countries. You can check shipping rates at checkout. Anything specific you're looking to order? 🌍" |
| "When is this launching?" | "Coming [date/timeframe]! Make sure you're following us so you don't miss the announcement. 👀" |

**Best Practices:**
- Answer publicly when the info is generally useful
- Take pricing/account-specific questions to DM
- Be honest if you don't know—commit to finding out
- Never make promises you can't keep

### Neutral/Informational Comments

**Goal:** Acknowledge and engage

**Examples:**

| Comment | Response |
|---------|----------|
| "Interesting perspective" | "Thanks for reading! What's your take on it? 👇" |
| "I've been thinking about this lately" | "Great minds! We'd love to hear your thoughts. 💭" |
| "Saving this for later" | "Smart move! Let us know if you have any questions when you dive in. 📌" |

## Direct Message Handling

### DM Categories

**1. Customer Service**
- Product questions
- Order issues
- Technical support
- Billing inquiries

**2. Sales Inquiries**
- Pricing requests
- Service information
- Partnership opportunities
- Speaking/podcast requests

**3. General Engagement**
- Compliments
- Casual conversation
- Networking
- Community building

**4. Sensitive Issues**
- Complaints
- Crisis situations
- Legal matters
- PR concerns

### DM Response Framework

```
1. GREET
   - Use their name if available
   - Warm, friendly opening

2. ACKNOWLEDGE
   - Show you understand their message
   - Validate their concern/question

3. ADDRESS
   - Provide answer or solution
   - Be specific and actionable

4. ACTION
   - Next steps (if any)
   - Timeline expectations

5. CLOSE
   - Offer additional help
   - Warm sign-off
```

### DM Examples

**Customer Service:**
```
Hi [Name]! 👋 Thanks for reaching out.

I understand you're having trouble with [issue]. Let me help you with that.

[Provide solution or steps]

If this doesn't resolve it, please let me know and I'll escalate this to our tech team right away.

Anything else I can help with?

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Sales Inquiry:**
```
Hey [Name]! Great to hear from you.

Thanks for your interest in [service/product]. I'd love to tell you more!

[Brief overview]

The best next step would be [call/demo/consultation]. Here's a link to book: [link]

Any specific questions before we chat?

Talk soon,
[Your name]
```

## Negative Comment Protocol

### Stay Calm & Assess

**Before responding, ask:**

1. Is this a legitimate complaint or a troll?
2. Is this public or private?
3. Does this require escalation?
4. What's the desired outcome?

### Response Strategy by Type

**Type 1: Legitimate Complaint**

**Goal:** Resolve publicly, show you care

**Framework:**
1. Acknowledge the issue
2. Apologize sincerely (no "if" or "but")
3. Explain what happened (briefly)
4. State what you're doing to fix it
5. Offer to take it offline if needed

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name], thank you for bringing this to our attention.

You're absolutely right—this shouldn't have happened, and I sincerely apologize for [specific issue].

Here's what went wrong: [brief, honest explanation]

Here's what we're doing: [action being taken]

I'd like to make this right. Can you DM us your [info] so we can [solution]?

Again, I'm sorry for the frustration. We're committed to doing better.

- [Name]
```

**Type 2: Misunderstanding**

**Goal:** Clarify without being defensive

**Framework:**
1. Acknowledge their perspective
2. Clarify gently with facts
3. Offer to discuss further
4. Keep tone friendly

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name], I totally understand the confusion!

Just to clarify: [factual correction, friendly tone]

Happy to chat more about this if you'd like—feel free to DM us!

Thanks for the feedback. 👍
```

**Type 3: Troll/Spam**

**Goal:** Minimize engagement, protect community

**Actions:**
- Don't feed the trolls
- Delete spam immediately
- Block repeat offenders
- Report if abusive

**When to engage:**
- Only if there's a genuine question hidden in the noise
- Keep it brief and factual
- Don't argue

**Type 4: Competitor Baiting**

**Goal:** Stay professional, don't take the bait

**Response:**
```
Thanks for your perspective! We're focused on [our value/mission] and serving our customers well. Appreciate you stopping by. 👋
```

### When to Take It Offline

**Move to DM/Email when:**
- Personal account information is needed
- Issue requires detailed troubleshooting
- Conversation is becoming heated
- Multiple back-and-forths with no resolution

**Transition phrase:**
```
"I'd like to give this the attention it deserves. Can you DM us [info] so we can look into this personally?"
```

## Crisis Management

### What Constitutes a Crisis?

**Level 1: Minor Issue**
- Single negative comment
- Routine complaint
- Misunderstanding

**Level 2: Escalating Issue**
- Multiple complaints about same issue
- Negative press mention
- Influencer criticism
- Viral negative post

**Level 3: Full Crisis**
- Widespread negative coverage
- Boycott calls
- Legal threats
- Safety concerns
- Executive misconduct
- Data breach

### Crisis Response Protocol

**IMMEDIATE (First 30 Minutes):**

1. **STOP** all scheduled posts
2. **ASSESS** the situation
3. **ALERT** the crisis team
4. **DOCUMENT** everything (screenshots)
5. **MONITOR** sentiment and spread

**HOURS 1-4:**

1. **CONVENE** crisis team call
2. **DETERMINE** official position
3. **DRAFT** holding statement
4. **APPROVE** through chain of command
5. **PUBLISH** initial response

**HOURS 4-24:**

1. **IMPLEMENT** action plan
2. **COMMUNICATE** updates regularly
3. **ENGAGE** with concerned parties
4. **MONITOR** and adjust approach
5. **DOCUMENT** all actions

### Crisis Communication Principles

**DO:**
- Respond quickly
- Be transparent
- Show empathy
- Take responsibility
- Communicate actions being taken
- Update regularly
- Stay consistent across channels

**DON'T:**
- Go silent
- Delete legitimate criticism
- Argue publicly
- Make promises you can't keep
- Speculate or guess
- Blame others
- Use defensive language

### Crisis Statement Template

```
[Headline: Clear and direct]

We are aware of [situation].

[What happened - factual, brief]

This is not acceptable to us because [values/principles].

Here's what we're doing:
→ [Action 1]
→ [Action 2]
→ [Action 3]

We are committed to [value/principle] and will share updates as we have them.

For questions: [contact info]

Signed,
[Name/Title]
```

## Tone & Voice Guidelines

### Brand Voice Attributes

[Customize based on client brand from onboarding]

**Example:**
- **Warm** - Friendly and approachable
- **Knowledgeable** - Confident but not arrogant
- **Helpful** - Always looking to add value
- **Authentic** - Real and transparent

### Tone Adjustments by Situation

| Situation | Tone | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Celebration | Enthusiastic | "This is AMAZING! 🎉" |
| Complaint | Empathetic | "I completely understand your frustration." |
| Question | Helpful | "Great question! Here's what you need to know..." |
| Crisis | Serious | "We take this very seriously." |
| Casual engagement | Friendly | "Love this! Thanks for sharing. 😊" |

### Language Guidelines

**Use:**
- Contractions (we're, you'll, don't)
- Active voice
- Simple, clear language
- Inclusive terminology
- Brand-approved emojis

**Avoid:**
- Jargon and buzzwords
- Overly formal language
- Slang that dates quickly
- Controversial topics (unless brand-aligned)
- Excessive emojis (2-3 max per response)

## Do's and Don'ts

### ✅ DO

- Respond within established timeframes
- Use the person's name when possible
- Personalize responses (no copy-paste when avoidable)
- Show empathy and understanding
- Admit mistakes openly
- Follow up when you say you will
- Escalate when unsure
- Document unusual interactions
- Stay calm and professional
- Protect user privacy
- Report bugs and issues internally
- Celebrate community wins

### ❌ DON'T

- Argue or get defensive
- Delete legitimate criticism
- Make promises you can't keep
- Share confidential information
- Engage with trolls
- Use all caps (seems like shouting)
- Overuse emojis or seem insincere
- Ghost after promising follow-up
- Discuss other customers publicly
- Share personal opinions on sensitive topics
- Respond when emotional—take a break
- Ignore red flags
