## 1. Ideal Client Profile

### Best Client Characteristics

**Industry Fit:**
- ✅ E-commerce brands ($1M-50M annual revenue)
- ✅ SaaS companies (Series A-B funded)
- ✅ Mobile apps (growth stage)
- ✅ Fashion & beauty brands
- ✅ Food & beverage (national/regional)
- ✅ Health & wellness companies
- ❌ Local businesses (low budget)
- ❌ Bootstrapped startups (cash-constrained)
- ❌ Enterprise (sales cycles too long)

**Budget Requirements:**
- Minimum marketing budget: $20K/month
- TikTok allocation: $5K-20K/month
- Total agency retainer capacity: $5K-15K/month

**Mindset Indicators:**
- Already spending on TikTok or similar platforms
- Understand influencer marketing value
- Data-driven decision making
- Quick decision makers (no 6-month sales cycles)
- Willing to invest in testing

### Client Personas

**Persona 1: E-commerce Founder**
- **Name**: "E-commerce Emma"
- **Age**: 28-45
- **Revenue**: $500K-10M/year
- **Pain Points**: Rising ad costs, need new channels, inconsistent ROAS
- **Goals**: Scale profitably, build brand, diversify traffic
- **Decision Time**: 2-4 weeks
- **Budget**: $5K-15K/month

**Persona 2: SaaS Growth Lead**
- **Name**: "SaaS Steve"
- **Age**: 30-50
- **Company Size**: 50-500 employees
- **Funding**: Series A-B ($10M-50M raised)
- **Pain Points**: High CAC, need quality leads, standing out in crowded market
- **Goals**: Reduce CAC, increase MRR, brand awareness
- **Decision Time**: 4-8 weeks
- **Budget**: $7K-20K/month

**Persona 3: App Marketing Manager**
- **Name**: "App Amanda"
- **Age**: 26-40
- **Company**: Mobile app company
- **Pain Points**: Expensive user acquisition, low organic discovery
- **Goals**: Cost-effective installs, engaged users, viral growth
- **Decision Time**: 3-6 weeks
- **Budget**: $5K-12K/month

### Where Your Clients Hang Out

**Online Communities:**
- **E-commerce**: Reddit r/ecommerce, r/Shopify, Facebook groups
- **SaaS**: SaaS Club, Indie Hackers, LinkedIn groups

**Events & Conferences:**
- **E-commerce**: Shopify Reclaim, eCommerce Fuel

**Online Platforms:**

### Lead Qualification Framework

**Budget:**
- [ ] Has marketing budget of $20K+/month
- [ ] Can afford $5K+ agency retainer
- [ ] Payment terms acceptable (Net 15-30)

**Authority:**
- [ ] Talking to decision maker
- [ ] Can approve budget
- [ ] No additional stakeholders needed

**Need:**
- [ ] Has clear TikTok/marketing goals
- [ ] Experiencing pain we solve
- [ ] Ready to invest in solution

**Timeline:**

## 2. Lead Generation Channels

### Channel 1: LinkedIn Outreach

**Strategy:**
- Build personal brand as TikTok expert
- Direct outreach to decision makers
- Content marketing + inbound

**Tactics:**

**Profile Optimization:**
- Headline: "Helping [niche] scale on TikTok | $XM in ad spend managed"

- Featured: Case studies, testimonials, lead magnet

- Connection requests: 20-30/day
- Follow-up messages: 10-15/day

## 4. Sales Process

### Discovery Call Structure (30 min)

**Minutes 0-5: Build Rapport**
- Welcome and thank you
- Brief intro (who you are)
- Icebreaker question
- Set agenda

**Minutes 5-15: Discovery Questions**
- "What's your current marketing mix?"
- "What are your biggest challenges right now?"
- "What's your goal with TikTok specifically?"
- "What's your budget range?"
- "What's your timeline?"
- "Who else is involved in the decision?"

**Minutes 15-25: Present Solution**
- Based on their answers, present relevant services
- Show how you solve their specific problems
- Share relevant case study
- Explain process

**Minutes 25-30: Next Steps**
- "Based on what you've shared, I think..."
- "Here's what I'd recommend..."
- "Next step would be [proposal/presentation]"
- "Does that make sense?"
- "When should I send the proposal?"

### Discovery Call Script

**Opening:**
"Thanks so much for making time today, [Name]. I know you're busy, so I'll keep this focused.

Quick intro - I'm [Name], founder of [Agency]. We help [niche] scale on TikTok. Recently helped [similar company] achieve [result].

Today, I'd love to:
1. Understand your goals and challenges
2. See if there's a fit
3. If there is, share how we could help

Does that work for you?"

**Discovery Questions:**
"Before we dive in, I have a few questions:

1. What's your current approach to social media marketing?
2. What are your biggest marketing challenges right now?
3. Why TikTok specifically? What are you hoping to achieve?
4. What's your current budget for marketing/TikTok?
5. What's your timeline for getting started?
6. Who else is involved in making this decision?

[Listen carefully and take notes]

Based on what you're saying, it sounds like [summarize their situation]. Is that accurate?"

**Presenting Solution:**
"Based on what you've shared, here's what I'd recommend:

[Present specific package/services]

This would include:
- [Deliverable 1]
- [Deliverable 2]
- [Deliverable 3]

Here's how it would work:
[Explain process]

We recently worked with [similar company] who had similar goals. Here's what we achieved:
[Share case study]

Does this align with what you're looking for?"

**Closing:**
"Next step would be for me to put together a formal proposal with:
- Specific deliverables
- Investment
- Timeline

I can send that over by [timeframe]. From there, you can review and we can schedule a follow-up to answer any questions.

How does that sound?

And what's the best email to send that to?"

### Handling Objections

**Objection 1: "It's too expensive"**

Response:
"I understand budget is a consideration. Can I ask - compared to what?

[Listen]

Here's how I think about it: We're investing $[amount], and based on [similar company] results, you should see $[return]. That's a [X]x return.

If we can deliver similar results for you, does that make sense as an investment?"

**Objection 2: "We need to think about it"**

Response:
"Totally understand - this is a decision you want to get right.

Help me understand - what specifically are you wanting to think through? Is it:
- The investment?
- The timing?
- Whether it will work for your business?

[Address their concern]"

**Objection 3: "We're happy with our current agency"**

Response:
"That's great to hear! What are they handling for you right now?

[Listen]

Interesting. We actually specialize specifically in TikTok, so we bring deep expertise in this one area. Some of our clients work with us alongside their general agency for TikTok specifically.

Would you be open to a competitive bid? No obligation to switch - just want to show you what's possible."

**Objection 4: "We've tried TikTok before, it didn't work"**

Response:
"I appreciate your honesty. What happened specifically?

[Listen]

That's actually really common. Most brands approach TikTok the wrong way - they post polished ads that don't fit the platform.

What we do differently is [explain your approach]. We recently turned around a brand that had the same experience - they went from [bad result] to [good result] in [timeframe].

Would you be open to exploring what a different approach could look like?"

**Objection 5: "Send me info and I'll get back to you"**

Response:
"Happy to send information over. Before I do, can I ask - what specific information would be most helpful for you?

[Listen]

Great. I'll send that over. Also, I'd love to schedule a quick 15-min call next week to walk through it and answer any questions. How's [day/time] looking?"

## 8. Closing Strategies

### Creating Urgency

**Scarcity:**
- "We're currently accepting 2-3 new clients this month"
- "Our content team has capacity for 2 more accounts"
- "Early bird pricing ends [date]"

**Timing:**
- "Q2 is a critical period for TikTok - now is the time to start"
- "Competitors in your space are already gaining momentum"
- "The algorithm favors early, consistent posters"

**Value:**
- "Every week we wait is a week of lost momentum"
- "Early movers in [niche] are capturing significant share"
- "ROI compounds over time - the sooner we start, the sooner you see results"

### Trial Close Techniques

**Assumptive Close:**
"When we get started, would you prefer we focus on [option A] or [option B] first?"

**Summary Close:**
"So to summarize, we'll help you achieve [goals] through [strategies], with an investment of $[amount]. Does that align with what you're looking for?"

**Question Close:**
"What would need to happen for this to be a no-brainer decision?"

**Trial Close:**
"How does this approach align with what you were expecting?"

### Decision-Making Timeline

**Fast Deciders (1-2 weeks):**
- Smaller budgets
- E-commerce founders
- Action-oriented personalities
- Have tried TikTok before

**Medium Deciders (3-4 weeks):**
- Growth-stage companies
- Marketing managers
- Need to align with team

**Slow Deciders (5-8 weeks):**
- Enterprise companies
- Need multiple approvals
- Longer procurement processes

### Follow-Up After Proposal

**Day 1:** Send proposal + calendar link
**Day 3:** Check-in email ("Any questions?")
**Day 7:** Value-add ("Saw this trend and thought of you...")
**Day 14:** Final check-in ("Happy to answer any remaining questions")
**Day 21:** Break-up email ("Should I close your file?")

### Contract Signing

**Tools:**
- **HelloSign**: Simple e-signatures
- **DocuSign**: Enterprise-grade
- **PandaDoc**: Proposals + contracts

**Process:**
1. Send contract via e-sign platform
2. Follow up if not signed in 48 hours
3. Confirm receipt once signed
4. Send invoice for deposit
5. Schedule kickoff
