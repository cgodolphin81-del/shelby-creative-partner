## 1. UX Researcher

**Understands users, generates insights, informs design decisions.**

### Responsibilities
- Plan and conduct user research (interviews, surveys, usability tests)
- Analyze qualitative and quantitative data
- Create personas, journey maps, research reports
- Present findings to clients and internal team
- Advise on research methodology
- Build research repository

### Skills Required
- **Hard skills:**
  - Qualitative research (interviews, ethnography)
  - Quantitative research (surveys, analytics)
  - Usability testing (moderated and unmoderated)
  - Data analysis and synthesis
  - Research tools (UserTesting, Maze, Qualtrics, Dovetail)

- **Soft skills:**
  - Curiosity and empathy
  - Communication (written and verbal)
  - Storytelling with data
  - Stakeholder management
  - Critical thinking

### Experience Level
- **Mid-level:** 3-5 years research experience
- **Senior:** 5-8 years, agency or product experience
- **Lead:** 8+ years, team management experience

### Interview Questions
1. "Walk me through a research project from brief to impact."
2. "How do you decide which research method to use?"
3. "Tell me about a time research changed a product decision."
4. "How do you handle stakeholders who disagree with findings?"
5. "Show me a research report you're proud of."

### Portfolio Review
**Look for:**
- Clear research questions
- Appropriate methodology
- Rigorous analysis
- Actionable recommendations
- Evidence of impact

**Red flags:**
- Only one method (interviews only, etc.)
- No connection to business outcomes
- Findings without recommendations
- Can't explain methodological choices

### Salary & Compensation
| Level | Base Salary | Equity | Total Comp |
|-------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Mid | $90K-$120K | 0-0.5% | $90K-$120K |
| Senior | $120K-$150K | 0.5-1% | $120K-$150K |
| Lead | $150K-$180K | 1-2% | $150K-$180K |

### When to Hire
- Consistently selling research as standalone service
- Projects suffering from lack of user insights
- Can bill researcher 70%+ of time
- Revenue: $500K+

## 2. UI Designer

**Creates beautiful, functional interfaces that drive results.**

### Responsibilities
- Design high-fidelity mockups and prototypes
- Create and maintain design systems
- Collaborate with UX researchers and designers
- Prepare developer handoff materials
- Participate in design reviews
- Stay current with design trends and tools

### Skills Required
- **Hard skills:**
  - Figma (expert level)
  - Visual design (layout, typography, color)
  - Prototyping (Figma, Protopie)
  - Design systems
  - Responsive design
  - Basic HTML/CSS understanding

- **Soft skills:**
  - Attention to detail
  - Receptiveness to feedback
  - Time management
  - Collaboration
  - Problem-solving

### Experience Level
- **Junior:** 1-2 years, strong portfolio
- **Mid-level:** 3-5 years, agency or product experience
- **Senior:** 5-8 years, end-to-end project experience

### Interview Questions
1. "Walk me through your design process on a recent project."
2. "How do you approach designing for accessibility?"
3. "Tell me about a design system you've built or contributed to."
4. "How do you handle feedback you disagree with?"
5. "Show me a project where you had to balance business and user needs."

### Portfolio Review
**Look for:**
- Strong visual hierarchy
- Consistent design language
- Attention to detail (spacing, alignment, states)
- Range of projects (mobile, web, different industries)
- Process documentation (not just finals)

**Red flags:**
- Only Dribbble shots (no context)
- Inconsistent quality
- Can't explain design decisions
- No mobile or responsive work
- Accessibility not considered

### Salary & Compensation
| Level | Base Salary | Equity | Total Comp |
|-------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Junior | $70K-$90K | 0-0.25% | $70K-$90K |
| Mid | $90K-$120K | 0.25-0.5% | $90K-$120K |
| Senior | $120K-$140K | 0.5-1% | $120K-$140K |

### When to Hire
- First design hire (founder is strategist/sales)
- Overwhelmed with production work
- Turning down work due to capacity
- Revenue: $200K+

## 3. Senior Designer

**End-to-end design leadership on complex projects.**

### Responsibilities
- Lead design on major projects
- Mentor junior and mid-level designers
- Client communication and presentations
- Design strategy and problem framing
- Quality assurance on team output
- Contribute to new business efforts

### Skills Required
- **Hard skills:**
  - All UI Designer skills (advanced)
  - UX research and strategy
  - Information architecture
  - Workshop facilitation
  - Design systems architecture
  - Presentation and storytelling

- **Soft skills:**
  - Leadership and mentorship
  - Client management
  - Strategic thinking
  - Conflict resolution
  - Autonomy and ownership

### Experience Level
- **Senior:** 5-8 years full-stack design experience
- **Staff:** 8-12 years, recognized expertise
- **Principal:** 12+ years, industry reputation

### Interview Questions
1. "Describe the most complex project you've led. What made it complex?"
2. "How do you approach a project with ambiguous requirements?"
3. "Tell me about a time you had to push back on a client."
4. "How do you mentor less experienced designers?"
5. "Walk me through how you'd approach [hypothetical project]."

### Portfolio Review
**Look for:**
- End-to-end project ownership
- Strategic thinking (not just execution)
- Complex problem-solving
- Evidence of business impact
- Leadership and collaboration

**Red flags:**
- Only execution work (no strategy)
- Can't articulate business context
- No evidence of mentorship
- All work looks the same (no adaptation)

### Salary & Compensation
| Level | Base Salary | Equity | Total Comp |
|-------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Senior | $120K-$150K | 0.5-1.5% | $120K-$150K |
| Staff | $150K-$180K | 1-2% | $150K-$180K |
| Principal | $180K-$220K | 2-3% | $180K-$220K |

### When to Hire
- Need to lead projects without founder involvement
- Junior designers need mentorship
- Selling larger, more complex projects
- Revenue: $500K+

## 4. Design Director

**Strategic leadership, quality oversight, team development.**

### Responsibilities
- Set design vision and strategy
- Manage and grow design team
- Ensure quality across all projects
- Client relationships (key accounts)
- Hiring and team development
- Contribute to agency strategy
- New business participation

### Skills Required
- **Hard skills:**
  - 10+ years design experience
  - Team management (5+ people)
  - Strategic planning
  - Budget and resource management
  - Client relationship management
  - Business acumen

- **Soft skills:**
  - Leadership and inspiration
  - Difficult conversations
  - Strategic thinking
  - Emotional intelligence
  - Executive presence

### Experience Level
- **Director:** 10-15 years, 5+ years management
- **VP:** 15+ years, 8+ years management
- **Chief Design Officer:** 15+ years, executive experience

### Interview Questions
1. "How do you build and maintain a strong design culture?"
2. "Tell me about a time you had to let someone go."
3. "How do you balance quality with business constraints?"
4. "Describe your approach to growing junior talent."
5. "How do you measure design team success?"

### Portfolio Review
**Look for:**
- Leadership impact (not just individual work)
- Team growth and development
- Strategic contributions
- Business outcomes
- Industry recognition

**Red flags:**
- Can't discuss team development
- Only talks about their own work
- No evidence of strategic thinking
- Toxic management style indicators

### Salary & Compensation
| Level | Base Salary | Equity | Total Comp |
|-------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Director | $150K-$200K | 2-5% | $150K-$200K |
| VP | $200K-$280K | 3-7% | $200K-$280K |
| CDO | $280K-$400K+ | 5-10% | $280K-$400K+ |

### When to Hire
- Team of 8-10+ designers
- Founder can't manage team and grow business
- Quality inconsistency across projects
- Revenue: $1.5M+

## 5. Design Ops

**Makes the design team more efficient and effective.**

### Responsibilities
- Manage design tools and infrastructure
- Optimize design processes and workflows
- Maintain design system infrastructure
- Onboard new designers
- Manage design budget and vendors
- Measure and report on team performance
- Enable collaboration (design ↔ dev ↔ PM)

### Skills Required
- **Hard skills:**
  - Design tools expertise (Figma, Zeroheight, etc.)
  - Process design and documentation
  - Data analysis and reporting
  - Project management
  - Basic technical understanding

- **Soft skills:**
  - Systems thinking
  - Communication across functions
  - Problem-solving
  - Detail-oriented
  - Servant leadership

### Experience Level
- **Mid-level:** 3-5 years in design ops, PM, or related
- **Senior:** 5-8 years, agency or product experience
- **Head of Design Ops:** 8+ years, team leadership

### Interview Questions
1. "How would you audit and improve our design workflow?"
2. "What metrics would you track for design team health?"
3. "Tell me about a process you improved in a previous role."
4. "How do you balance standardization with flexibility?"
5. "What's your experience with design system management?"

### Salary & Compensation
| Level | Base Salary | Equity | Total Comp |
|-------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Mid | $100K-$130K | 0.5-1% | $100K-$130K |
| Senior | $130K-$160K | 1-2% | $130K-$160K |
| Head | $160K-$200K | 2-3% | $160K-$200K |

### When to Hire
- Team of 10-15+ designers
- Process chaos slowing down delivery
- Design system needs dedicated ownership
- Tool sprawl and inefficiency
- Revenue: $2M+

- **Mid-level:** 3-5 years PM experience (agency preferred)

## 📋 Hiring Process

### Stage 1: Sourcing (1-2 weeks)
**Channels:**
- LinkedIn (post jobs, reach out directly)
- Design communities (Dribbble, Behance, ADPList)
- Referrals (employee bonuses: $2K-$5K)
- Job boards (Krop, Working Not Working, AngelList)
- Universities (design programs)

**Outbound template:**
```
Subject: Design opportunity at [Agency]

Hi [Name],

I came across your work on [platform] - really impressed
with [specific project/aspect].

I'm building a design agency focused on [specialty], and
your background in [area] caught my attention.

We're looking for a [role] to [impact]. Would you be open
to a quick chat to learn more?

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Stage 2: Screening Call (30 min)
**Agenda:**
- Their background and interest (10 min)
- Role overview and agency intro (10 min)
- Logistics (salary, location, start date) (5 min)
- Their questions (5 min)

**Evaluate:**
- Communication skills
- Cultural fit indicators
- Interest level
- Salary alignment
- Red flags

### Stage 3: Portfolio Review (45-60 min)
**Format:**
- Candidate presents 2-3 projects (20 min)
- Deep dive questions (20 min)
- Their questions (10 min)

**Evaluate:**
- Quality of work
- Thought process
- Role in projects
- Impact and outcomes
- Range of skills

### Stage 4: Practical Exercise (Take-home or Live)
**Options:**
- **Take-home:** Design challenge (4-8 hours max, paid)
- **Live workshop:** Collaborative problem-solving (2 hours)
- **Pair design:** Work with team member on real problem (2-3 hours)

**Evaluate:**
- Problem-solving approach
- Quality of output
- Collaboration style
- Time management
- Receptiveness to feedback

### Stage 5: Team Interviews (2-3 sessions)
**Interviewers:**
- Peer designers (cultural fit, collaboration)
- Design leadership (skills, growth potential)
- Cross-functional (PM, dev - working style)

### Stage 6: Final Interview + Offer (Founder/Director)
**Agenda:**
- Final culture conversation
- Career goals alignment
- Offer discussion
- Reference check authorization

**Reference checks:**
- 2-3 professional references
- Ask about strengths, growth areas, collaboration
- Verify dates and role

## 🎓 Onboarding Program

### Week 1: Orientation
- Company overview and culture
- Tools setup (Figma, Slack, project management)
- Meet the team (1:1s)
- Review current projects
- First small task

### Week 2-3: Ramp-up
- Shadow senior team members
- Contribute to ongoing project
- Training on agency processes
- Design system deep-dive
- First client interaction

### Week 4: First Project
- Own a small project or piece of larger project
- Regular check-ins with manager
- 30-day review

### Month 2-3: Full Contribution
- Full project ownership
- Client-facing responsibilities
- 60 and 90-day reviews
- Goal setting for year

## 💡 Hiring Best Practices

### Do
✅ Write clear, specific job descriptions
✅ Pay for interview work (take-home challenges)
✅ Move quickly (good candidates have options)
✅ Check references thoroughly
✅ Invest in onboarding
✅ Create growth paths
✅ Regular feedback and 1:1s

### Don't
❌ Hire for cheap (hire for value)
❌ Skip portfolio review
❌ Ignore culture fit (but don't hire clones)
❌ Overpromise on role or growth
❌ Rush the process (but don't drag it out)
❌ Hire without capacity to onboard
❌ Tolerate toxic behavior (no matter how talented)
