# Client Acquisition

Strategies for acquiring and converting high-value design clients across startups, enterprises, and agency partners.

---

## 🎯 Target Client Segments

| Segment | Characteristics | Budget Range | Decision Speed | Lifetime Value |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Startups** | Fast-moving, resource-constrained | $10K-$100K | 1-4 weeks | $50K-$300K |
| **Enterprises** | Slow, process-heavy, large budgets | $100K-$500K+ | 2-6 months | $250K-$2M+ |
| **Agencies** | White-label, overflow work | $20K-$150K | 1-4 weeks | $100K-$500K |

**Recommended focus:** Start with startups (faster sales), build case studies, move upmarket to enterprises.

---

## 1. Startup Acquisition

**Fast sales cycles, growth-focused, referral-rich.**

### Ideal Startup Profile
- **Stage:** Seed to Series B
- **Funding:** $2M+ raised
- **Team:** 10-100 employees
- **Pain point:** Need design to raise next round or scale
- **Decision maker:** Founder, Head of Product, VP Design

### Acquisition Channels

#### Warm Introductions (Highest Conversion)
**Sources:**
- Portfolio company networks (VCs, accelerators)
- Past client referrals
- Investor relationships
- Founder communities

**Strategy:**
1. Build relationships with 5-10 VCs/accelerators
2. Offer free office hours/workshops for their portfolio
3. Become their "go-to" design partner
4. Get introduced to portfolio companies

**Conversion rate:** 30-50%

#### Content Marketing
**Platforms:**
- **LinkedIn:** Design insights, case studies, before/after
- **Twitter/X:** Design threads, hot takes, building in public
- **Medium/Substack:** Deep dives on design process
- **YouTube:** Design critiques, process videos

**Content calendar:**
- 3-5 LinkedIn posts/week
- 1-2 Twitter threads/week
- 1 long-form article/month
- 1 case study/quarter

**Conversion rate:** 2-5% (but compounds over time)

#### Speaking & Events
**Opportunities:**
- Startup conferences (TechCrunch Disrupt, Web Summit)
- Design conferences (Config, UXDX, Interaction)
- Local meetups (Product Tank, Design Thinking)
- Podcasts (design, startup, product shows)

**Strategy:**
1. Start small (local meetups, small podcasts)
2. Build speaking reel
3. Pitch larger conferences
4. Leverage talks for lead generation

**Conversion rate:** 5-15% of attendees

#### Outbound (Cold Outreach)
**When to use:** Building pipeline, specific target accounts

**Cold email template:**
```
Subject: Quick design question about [Company]

Hi [Name],

I was looking at [specific feature/page] on [product] and noticed
[specific observation - not generic praise].

We recently helped [similar company] [specific outcome - metric
if possible]. I had a few ideas that might help [Company] with
[specific goal].

Worth a 15-minute chat this week?

Best,
[Your name]
[Link to relevant case study]
```

**Best practices:**
- Personalize heavily (no templates visible)
- Reference specific observations
- Include social proof
- Clear, low-commitment CTA
- Follow up 3-5 times

**Conversion rate:** 1-3%

#### Job Boards (Backdoor Entry)
**Strategy:**
- Monitor startup job boards (AngelList, Wellfound)
- Look for "Head of Design" or "Product Designer" roles
- Reach out: "Saw you're hiring for design. While you search,
  we could help with [specific project]."

**Conversion rate:** 5-10%

### Startup Sales Process

```
Week 1: Initial contact → Discovery call
Week 2: Proposal sent → Follow-up call
Week 3: Negotiation → Contract signed
Week 4: Kickoff

Total: 3-4 weeks average
```

### Pricing for Startups
- **Entry project:** $15K-$30K (get foot in door)
- **Core offering:** $50K-$75K (sweet spot)
- **Retainer:** $5K-$15K/month (ongoing relationship)

### Red Flags
❌ Pre-revenue, pre-funding (can't pay)
❌ Founder wants to "pick your brain" (free work)
❌ "We'll pay you in equity" (always no)
❌ Unclear decision maker
❌ Rushing without budget

## 2. Enterprise Acquisition

**Long sales cycles, large budgets, relationship-driven.**

### Ideal Enterprise Profile
- **Size:** 500+ employees
- **Revenue:** $50M+
- **Industry:** Tech, Finance, Healthcare, Retail
- **Pain point:** Legacy systems, design debt, scaling design
- **Decision maker:** VP Design, CPO, CTO, sometimes Procurement

### Acquisition Channels

#### Relationships & Networks (Primary)
**Build over time:**
- Former colleagues now at enterprises
- Conference connections
- LinkedIn relationship building
- Advisory roles

**Strategy:**
1. Identify target companies (20-30 accounts)
2. Map decision makers
3. Warm introduction or LinkedIn connection
4. Nurture relationship (6-18 months typical)
5. Opportunity arises → you're top of mind

**Conversion rate:** 20-40% (but long timeline)

#### Case Studies & Social Proof
**Enterprises need proof:**
- Similar company logos
- Quantified results
- Security/compliance credentials
- Team credentials

**Create:**
- 3-5 detailed case studies
- Video testimonials
- Reference calls available
- Security documentation (SOC 2, etc.)

#### RFPs (Request for Proposals)
**Where to find:**
- Procurement portals
- Industry-specific RFP sites
- Network referrals

**RFP response process:**
1. Qualify (can you win? worth the effort?)
2. Assemble team
3. Write proposal (2-4 weeks)
4. Present to selection committee
5. Negotiate contract

**Win rate:** 10-25% (but large deals)

#### Partnerships
**Types:**
- **Development agencies:** They build, you design
- **Consulting firms:** Strategy + design partnership
- **Technology vendors:** Design partner for their platform
- **VCs:** Portfolio company introductions

**Partnership pitch:**
"We specialize in [niche]. When you have clients who need
[capability], we'd love to be your go-to partner. Here's how
we've helped similar companies..."

#### Speaking & Thought Leadership
**Enterprise venues:**
- Industry conferences (not just design conferences)
- Executive roundtables
- Webinar series
- Research reports

**Goal:** Become known as expert in their industry, not just "designer"

### Enterprise Sales Process

```
Month 1-2: Initial contact → Discovery workshops
Month 3-4: Proposal → Stakeholder presentations
Month 5-6: Procurement → Legal review → Contract
Month 7: Kickoff

Total: 4-6 months average (can be 12+ months)
```

### Enterprise Pricing
- **Minimum engagement:** $100K+
- **Typical project:** $200K-$500K
- **Enterprise retainer:** $25K-$50K/month
- **Multi-year deals:** $500K-$2M+

### Enterprise Buying Committee
| Role | Concern | How to Address |
|------|---------|----------------|
| VP Design | Quality, team capability | Show work, team bios, process |
| CPO | Business impact | ROI, metrics, case studies |
| CTO | Technical feasibility | Developer handoff, tech fluency |
| Procurement | Price, terms | Competitive, flexible terms |
| Legal | Risk, IP, liability | Standard contracts, insurance |

### Red Flags
❌ "We need this done in 2 weeks" (unrealistic)
❌ "We have 10 agencies bidding" (price shop)
❌ Procurement-only contact (no champion)
❌ Budget not approved
❌ Constant stakeholder changes

## 3. Agency Partnerships

**White-label work, overflow capacity, steady pipeline.**

### Ideal Agency Profile
- **Type:** Development shop, marketing agency, consulting firm
- **Size:** 20-200 employees
- **Gap:** In-house design capacity or expertise
- **Decision maker:** Agency Owner, Creative Director, VP Services

### Acquisition Channels

#### Direct Outreach
**Research:**
- Identify agencies without strong design practice
- Look for agencies winning work you could support
- Find agencies in complementary spaces (dev, marketing, strategy)

**Outreach message:**
```
Subject: Design partnership opportunity

Hi [Name],

I've been following [Agency]'s work in [area] - impressive
stuff, especially [specific project].

I run [Your Agency], a design studio specializing in
[specialty]. We partner with agencies like yours to provide
white-label design support when you need extra capacity or
specialized expertise.

We've helped agencies like [similar agency] [specific outcome].

Worth exploring if there's a fit? Happy to hop on a quick call.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Conversion rate:** 5-15%

#### Agency Networks
**Join:**
- Local agency associations
- Online communities (Agency Analytics, r/agency)
- Mastermind groups
- Slack communities

**Participate:**
- Answer questions
- Share insights
- Offer help before asking for work
- Build reputation

#### Referrals from Other Agencies
**Strategy:**
- Build relationships with non-competing agencies
- Geographic (different city)
- Specialty (different focus)
- Size (different capacity)

**When they're at capacity → they refer to you**

### Agency Partnership Models

#### White-Label (Most Common)
- You do the work, they client-face
- They markup your services (typically 20-40%)
- You stay behind the scenes
- They manage client relationship

**Pros:** Steady work, no sales needed
**Cons:** Lower margins, less credit

#### Co-Branded
- Both agencies credited
- Joint proposals
- Shared client relationship
- Revenue split (typically 50/50 or 60/40)

**Pros:** Higher margins, shared risk
**Cons:** More complex, relationship management

#### Referral Fee
- You refer client to them (or vice versa)
- Referral fee: 10-20% of first project
- No ongoing involvement

**Pros:** Passive income, simple
**Cons:** One-time, no control

### Agency Pricing
- **White-label rate:** Your standard rate (they markup)
- **Volume discount:** 10-15% off for committed capacity
- **Retainer:** Dedicated capacity at preferential rate

### Agency Sales Process

```
Week 1: Initial contact → Capability call
Week 2: Case studies shared → Reference calls
Week 3: Trial project discussed → Agreement
Week 4: First project kickoff

Total: 2-4 weeks average
```

### Red Flags
❌ Agency wants your direct client contact (bypass)
❌ Unrealistic timelines (their sales problem)
❌ Won't sign proper agreement
❌ Payment terms >60 days
❌ History of agency-hopping (check references)

## 📊 Pipeline Management

### CRM Setup
**Tools:**
- HubSpot (free tier available)
- Pipedrive (sales-focused)
- Notion (flexible, DIY)
- Airtable (customizable)

### Pipeline Stages

```
1. Lead Identified
   ↓
2. Initial Contact Made
   ↓
3. Discovery Call Completed
   ↓
4. Proposal Sent
   ↓
5. Negotiation
   ↓
6. Contract Sent
   ↓
7. Closed Won/Lost
```

### Pipeline Metrics to Track
| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Lead velocity | New leads/month | 20-50 |
| Conversion rate | Won/Total opportunities | 20-30% |
| Sales cycle | Avg days from lead to close | 30-90 days |
| Pipeline coverage | Pipeline value / Quota | 3-5x |
| Win rate by source | Won by channel / Total by channel | Track by channel |

### Weekly Pipeline Review
```markdown

### New Leads This Week
- [Lead 1]: [Source], [Segment], [Next Step]

- [Opportunity]: [Stage change], [Next Step]

- [Opportunity]: [Days stalled], [Re-engagement plan]

### Closing This Month
- [Opportunity]: [Value], [Confidence], [Blockers]

### Actions for Next Week
- [Action 1]
- [Action 2]
- [Action 3]
```

## 🎣 Lead Generation Tactics

### High-ROI Activities (Do These First)

#### 1. Referrals (Best ROI)
**Ask every happy client:**
"Do you know any other founders/teams who might benefit from
similar work? I'd love an introduction."

**Incentivize:**
- Referral fee (10% of first project)
- Credit toward future work
- Gift/experience

#### 2. Content Marketing (Compounds)
**Weekly commitment:**
- 3 LinkedIn posts
- 1 Twitter thread
- Engage with 10 target accounts

**Monthly:**
- 1 case study
- 1 long-form article

#### 3. Strategic Partnerships
**Goal:** 5-10 active partnerships
- 1-2 VC/accelerator relationships
- 2-3 agency partnerships
- 2-3 complementary service providers

### Medium-ROI Activities

#### 4. Speaking & Events
- 1-2 talks per quarter
- Attend 1-2 conferences per quarter
- Host 1 workshop per quarter

#### 5. Outbound
- 20-50 personalized emails/week
- 5-10 follow-up sequences active
- Track response rates, iterate

### Low-ROI Activities (Minimize)

#### 6. Job Boards
- Time-consuming
- Price-sensitive clients
- Use selectively

#### 7. Cold Calls
- Low conversion
- High effort
- Not scalable

## 📝 Proposal & Pitch Templates

### Discovery Call Agenda (30 min)
```
1. Their story (10 min)
   - Company background
   - Current challenge
   - What's prompted this now?

2. Problem exploration (10 min)
   - Impact of current state
   - What have they tried?
   - What does success look like?

3. Your approach (5 min)
   - How you'd tackle it
   - Relevant experience
   - High-level process

4. Next steps (5 min)
   - Proposal timeline
   - What you need from them
   - Questions
```

### Proposal Structure
```markdown
# Proposal: [Project Name]

## Executive Summary
[2-3 paragraphs on the opportunity]

## Current State
[Their problem, impact, cost of inaction]

## Proposed Approach
[Your methodology, phases, timeline]

## Investment
[Pricing, payment terms]

## Case Studies
[2-3 relevant examples]

## Team
[Who will work on this]

## Timeline
[Key milestones]

## Terms
[Standard terms, validity period]

## Next Steps
[What happens if they say yes]
```

### Follow-Up Sequence
```
Day 0: Proposal sent
Day 3: "Did you have a chance to review?"
Day 7: "Happy to answer any questions"
Day 14: "Checking in - still interested?"
Day 21: "Should I close your file?" (pattern interrupt)
```
