# Pricing Models

Comprehensive pricing strategies for UX/UI design services, from project-based to retainer models.

---

## 💰 Pricing Strategy Overview

| Model | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|-------|----------|------|------|
| **Project-Based** | Defined scope, one-off work | Clear deliverables, predictable | Scope creep risk, feast/famine |
| **Retainer** | Ongoing work, long-term clients | Predictable revenue, deep relationships | Capacity planning, client dependency |
| **Hourly** | Uncertain scope, support work | Fair for changes, flexible | Ceiling on earnings, tracking burden |
| **Value-Based** | High-impact projects | Captures value, premium pricing | Hard to quantify, negotiation heavy |
| **Productized** | Repeatable services | Scalable, easy to sell | Less customization, commoditization |

**Recommended:** Hybrid approach - Project for core work, Retainer for ongoing support

---

## 1. Project-Based Pricing

**Fixed price for defined scope.**

### Pricing Tiers

#### Small Projects ($10K - $30K)
**Typical scope:**
- Landing page design
- Mobile app (5-10 screens)
- Brand refresh
- Usability audit

**Timeline:** 3-6 weeks

**Example breakdown:**
```
UX Research:          $8,000
UI Design:           $15,000
Prototyping:          $5,000
Handoff:              $2,000
─────────────────────────────
Total:               $30,000
```

#### Medium Projects ($30K - $75K)
**Typical scope:**
- Web application (15-30 screens)
- Mobile app (full MVP)
- Design system (foundation)
- Rebrand with implementation

**Timeline:** 6-10 weeks

**Example breakdown:**
```
Discovery:           $10,000
UX Research:         $15,000
UI Design:           $35,000
Design System:       $10,000
Prototyping:          $5,000
─────────────────────────────
Total:               $75,000
```

#### Large Projects ($75K - $200K)
**Typical scope:**
- Enterprise platform redesign
- Complete design system
- Multi-product ecosystem
- Full brand + digital transformation

**Timeline:** 12-20 weeks

**Example breakdown:**
```
Discovery & Strategy:    $25,000
UX Research (comprehensive): $30,000
Design System (enterprise): $60,000
UI Design (50+ screens):    $50,000
Prototyping & Testing:      $20,000
Handoff & Support:          $15,000
─────────────────────────────
Total:                   $200,000
```

### Payment Terms

#### Standard Structure
```
50% upfront (project start)
25% at midpoint (design approval)
25% at completion (final delivery)
```

#### Milestone-Based (Large Projects)
```
25% - Project kickoff
25% - Research complete
25% - Design approved
15% - Prototype approved
10% - Final handoff
```

#### Net Terms
- **Startups:** Due on receipt or Net 7
- **SMBs:** Net 15
- **Enterprises:** Net 30-45 (factor into pricing)

### Change Order Process
**Scope changes beyond agreed deliverables:**
1. Document requested change
2. Estimate additional hours/cost
3. Present change order to client
4. Obtain written approval
5. Adjust timeline and invoice

**Change order rate:** Same as project rate or +10% for rush

## 2. Retainer Pricing

**Monthly recurring revenue for ongoing work.**

### Retainer Tiers

#### Starter Retainer ($5K/month)
**Includes:**
- 20 hours of design time
- Weekly check-in call
- Email/Slack support
- Monthly reporting

**Best for:**
- Early-stage startups
- Ongoing optimization work
- Design support for small team

**Capacity:** 1-2 clients per designer

#### Growth Retainer ($12K/month)
**Includes:**
- 50 hours of design time
- Dedicated designer
- Bi-weekly strategy sessions
- Priority support
- Monthly reporting + quarterly review

**Best for:**
- Series A/B startups
- Growing product teams
- Regular feature releases

**Capacity:** 1 client per designer

#### Enterprise Retainer ($25K-$30K/month)
**Includes:**
- 100+ hours of design time
- Dedicated team (2-3 designers)
- Weekly strategy sessions
- Design system maintenance
- Unlimited support
- Comprehensive reporting

**Best for:**
- Enterprise product teams
- Multiple product lines
- Design system governance

**Capacity:** 1 team per 2-3 clients

### Retainer Rules

#### Hour Rollover
- **Option A:** No rollover (use it or lose it)
- **Option B:** 50% rollover (max 1 month)
- **Option C:** Full rollover (quarterly cap)

**Recommended:** Option B - balances flexibility with capacity planning

#### Overage Rates
- Hours beyond retainer: **1.25x base hourly rate**
- Rush work (<48 hours): **1.5x base hourly rate**
- Weekend/holiday: **2x base hourly rate**

#### Term Length
- **Minimum:** 3 months (allows for ramp-up)
- **Standard:** 6-12 months
- **Enterprise:** 12-24 months

#### Termination
- **30-day notice** standard
- **60-day notice** for enterprise
- Prepaid months non-refundable

### Retainer Pricing Calculator

```
Base hourly rate: $150-250/hour (depending on seniority)

Starter ($5K/mo):
  20 hours × $250 = $5,000
  Effective rate: $250/hour

Growth ($12K/mo):
  50 hours × $240 = $12,000
  Effective rate: $240/hour (volume discount)

Enterprise ($25K/mo):
  100 hours × $250 = $25,000
  Effective rate: $250/hour (team premium)
```

## 3. Hourly Pricing

**Time-and-materials for uncertain scope.**

### Hourly Rate Structure

| Role | Rate Range | Use Case |
|------|------------|----------|
| Junior Designer | $75-100/hr | Production work, simple tasks |
| Mid-Level Designer | $125-175/hr | Standard design work |
| Senior Designer | $175-250/hr | Complex design, strategy |
| Design Director | $250-350/hr | Strategy, leadership, enterprise |
| UX Researcher | $150-225/hr | Research planning & execution |

### When to Use Hourly
- Scope is unclear or likely to change
- Ongoing support/maintenance
- Quick turnaround requests
- Consulting/advisory work
- Overflow from retainer

### Time Tracking
**Tools:**
- Toggl Track
- Harvest
- Clockify
- Figma plugin (Time Tracker)

**Best practices:**
- Track in 15-minute increments
- Log daily (don't batch)
- Include detailed descriptions
- Share weekly reports with client

### Weekly Hourly Report Template
```markdown
# Weekly Time Report
**Client:** [Name]
**Week:** [Date range]
**Total Hours:** XX.X

## Breakdown by Task
| Date | Task | Hours | Notes |
|------|------|-------|-------|
| Mon | Homepage redesign | 4.5 | Completed hero section |
| Mon | Team sync | 0.5 | Weekly check-in |
| Tue | User flow diagrams | 3.0 | Checkout flow v2 |
...

## Summary by Category
- Design: XX hours
- Research: XX hours
- Meetings: XX hours
- PM: XX hours

## Next Week Forecast
[Expected focus areas]
```

## 4. Value-Based Pricing

**Price based on business impact, not hours.**

### When to Use
- High-impact projects (conversion optimization, revenue features)
- Projects with clear ROI
- Enterprise transformations
- When you have proven track record

### Pricing Framework

```
Value Price = (Client Value × Your % Capture) - Risk Adjustment

Example:
- Project expected to generate: $2M additional revenue
- Your value capture: 5-10%
- Risk adjustment: -10% (for execution risk)

Value Price = ($2M × 7.5%) × 0.9 = $135,000
```

### Value Capture Guidelines
| Project Type | Typical Capture |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Conversion optimization | 10-20% of incremental revenue |
| New product launch | 5-10% of Year 1 revenue |
| Cost reduction | 10-15% of savings |
| Brand transformation | 3-5% of brand value increase |

### Communicating Value
**Do:**
- Quantify expected impact
- Share case studies with metrics
- Tie design decisions to business outcomes
- Present ROI analysis

**Don't:**
- Lead with hours or deliverables
- Undersell your impact
- Accept vague success criteria

### Value-Based Proposal Structure
```markdown
# Proposal: [Project Name]

## Current State
[Problem and cost of inaction]

## Opportunity
[Quantified opportunity]

## Approach
[How you'll deliver value]

## Investment
$XXX,XXX (based on value, not hours)

## Expected ROI
- [Metric 1]: X% improvement
- [Metric 2]: $X additional revenue
- [Metric 3]: X months payback period

## Case Studies
[Relevant examples with metrics]

## Terms
[Payment schedule, timeline, etc.]
```

## 5. Productized Services

**Packaged offerings with fixed scope and price.**

### Benefits
- Easy to sell (clear offering)
- Scalable delivery
- Predictable margins
- Marketing-friendly

### Example Productized Offerings

#### UX Audit - $8,000
**Fixed scope:**
- Heuristic evaluation (Nielsen's 10 heuristics)
- Analytics review (1 hour)
- 5 user tests (unmoderated)
- Findings report (15-20 pages)
- 1-hour presentation

**Timeline:** 1 week

**Delivery:** Remote, async

#### Landing Page Design - $12,000
**Fixed scope:**
- 1 landing page design
- 3 concepts, 2 revision rounds
- Mobile responsive
- Handoff specs
- 2-week timeline

**Timeline:** 2 weeks

#### Design System Starter - $25,000
**Fixed scope:**
- Color palette (20+ colors)
- Typography system
- Grid & spacing
- 20 core components
- Figma library
- Documentation

**Timeline:** 4 weeks

### Productized Service Rules
1. **Strict scope** - No customization
2. **Fixed timeline** - Start dates, not deadlines
3. **Prepaid** - 100% upfront or 50/50
4. **No calls** - Async communication only (or limited)
5. **Template-driven** - Reusable frameworks

## 📊 Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring
**Present high option first:**
```
Enterprise: $200,000
Professional: $75,000 ← Target
Starter: $30,000
```

### Decoy Pricing
**Make target option more attractive:**
```
Basic: $25,000 (limited features)
Professional: $75,000 ← Target (best value)
Premium: $85,000 (marginally more)
```

### Charm Pricing
**Use .000 for premium, .900 for value:**
- Premium services: $75,000 (clean number = quality)
- Entry services: $7,900 (charm pricing = value)

### Framing
**Daily rate framing:**
- "$75,000 project" vs. "$1,500/day for 50 days"
- Larger number first, then break down

**Value framing:**
- "Investment" not "Cost"
- "Generate $500K" not "Spend $50K"

## 💳 Payment Terms & Policies

### Invoice Terms
| Client Type | Terms | Notes |
|-------------|-------|-------|
| Startup (Seed) | Due on receipt | Cash flow critical |
| Startup (Series A+) | Net 7-15 | Better cash position |
| SMB | Net 15 | Standard |
| Enterprise | Net 30-45 | Factor into pricing |
| Agency Partners | Net 15-30 | Depends on relationship |

### Late Payment Policy
```
1. Invoice due date: Payment expected
2. 7 days late: Friendly reminder email
3. 15 days late: Formal notice + late fee (1.5%/month)
4. 30 days late: Work暂停, escalation call
5. 45 days late: Collection process begins
```

### Late Fee
- **1.5% per month** on outstanding balance (18% APR)
- Disclose in contract
- Enforce consistently

### Refund Policy
- **Before work begins:** Full refund, minus 10% admin fee
- **After kickoff:** No refund, credit toward future work
- **Retainers:** Prepaid months non-refundable
- **Discretion:** Agency reserves right to partial refunds for exceptional circumstances

## 🎯 Pricing by Client Segment

### Startups (Seed to Series B)
**Budget:** $10K-$100K
**Preferred model:** Project-based or small retainer
**Decision speed:** Fast (1-2 weeks)
**Key buyer:** Founder, Head of Product
**Selling points:** Speed, ROI, fundraising support

### Enterprises (500+ employees)
**Budget:** $100K-$500K+
**Preferred model:** Large project or enterprise retainer
**Decision speed:** Slow (2-6 months)
**Key buyer:** VP Design, CPO, Procurement
**Selling points:** Scale, security, compliance, case studies

### Digital Agencies (White-label)
**Budget:** $20K-$150K
**Preferred model:** Project-based or capacity retainer
**Decision speed:** Medium (2-4 weeks)
**Key buyer:** Agency Owner, Creative Director
**Selling points:** White-label, reliability, overflow capacity

## 📈 Rate Increases

### When to Raise Rates
- Consistently booked 3+ months out
- New case studies/metrics
- Expanded capabilities
- Market rate changes
- Annual adjustment (inflation)

### How Much to Raise
- **Existing clients:** 5-10% annually
- **New clients:** 10-20% based on market
- **Retainer renewals:** 5-15% depending on value delivered

### Communicating Rate Increases
**For retainers (60-day notice):**
```
Hi [Client],

As we approach our [X]-month anniversary, I wanted to share
an update on our engagement.

Over the past [timeframe], we've [list key achievements].
We're excited about [future plans].

To reflect the expanded value and market rates, our monthly
retainer will adjust from $X to $Y effective [date, 60 days out].

This reflects [reason: expanded scope, market rates, etc.].
We're committed to [value proposition].

Happy to discuss. Let's schedule time to review.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## 💡 Pricing Best Practices

### Do
✅ Research market rates before pricing
✅ Price based on value, not just hours
✅ Build in buffer for revisions
✅ Get payment terms in writing
✅ Require deposit before starting work
✅ Track profitability by project
✅ Raise rates annually

### Don't
❌ Compete on price (compete on value)
❌ Discount without getting something in return
❌ Start work without signed contract
❌ Be vague about what's included
❌ Accept unlimited revisions
❌ Work with clients who don't respect pricing
❌ Undervalue your expertise
