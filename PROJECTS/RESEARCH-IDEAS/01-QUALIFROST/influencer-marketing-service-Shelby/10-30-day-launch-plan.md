## Overview

### What This Plan Achieves

In 30 days, you will:
✅ Define your influencer marketing strategy
✅ Build your influencer database
✅ Set up tracking and measurement
✅ Launch your first campaign
✅ Gather initial results and learnings
✅ Create a plan for scaling

### Who This Is For

- **New agencies** launching influencer services
- **Brands** starting influencer marketing for the first time
- **Marketers** tasked with building an influencer program
- **Consultants** adding influencer marketing to offerings

### Time Commitment

- **Week 1:** 15-20 hours
- **Week 2:** 15-20 hours
- **Week 3:** 10-15 hours
- **Week 4:** 10-15 hours
- **Total:** 50-70 hours over 30 days

### Budget Expectations

**Minimum Viable Campaign:**
- Influencer compensation: $2,000-5,000
- Tools/software: $0-200 (free trials available)
- Product/gifting: $500-1,000
- **Total: $2,500-6,000**

**Recommended Launch Budget:**
- Influencer compensation: $5,000-10,000
- Tools/software: $200-500
- Product/gifting: $1,000-2,000
- **Total: $6,200-12,500**

## Week 1: Foundation & Setup

### Day 1-2: Define Your Strategy

#### Morning Session (Day 1)

**Goal Setting Exercise:**

Answer these questions:

1. **Why influencer marketing?**
   - What problem are we trying to solve?
   - Why now?
   - What have we tried before?

2. **What does success look like?**
   - Primary goal: [Awareness / Sales / UGC / Leads / Other]
   - Specific targets: [e.g., "$50K revenue, 4:1 ROAS"]
   - Timeline: [When do we need results?]

3. **Who is our target audience?**
   - Demographics: [Age, gender, location, income]
   - Psychographics: [Interests, values, lifestyle]
   - Where do they spend time online?

4. **What's our budget?**
   - Total budget: $[X]
   - Influencer compensation: $[X]
   - Product/gifting: $[X]
   - Tools/software: $[X]
   - Contingency (10%): $[X]

#### Afternoon Session (Day 1)

**Campaign Type Selection:**

Choose your primary campaign type:

☐ **Product Launch** - New product introduction

### Day 3-4: Set Up Tracking & Measurement

#### Day 3: Technical Setup

**UTM Parameter Structure:**

Create your tracking link format:

```
Base URL: https://yourwebsite.com/[landing-page]

UTM Parameters:
utm_source=instagram (or tiktok, youtube, etc.)
utm_medium=influencer
utm_campaign=[campaign_name]
utm_content=@[influencer_handle]

Example:
https://yourwebsite.com/product?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=influencer&utm_campaign=spring_launch&utm_content=@janesmith
```

**Tools to Set Up:**

☐ **Google Analytics** - Website traffic and conversions
☐ **Bitly** or **Rebrandly** - Link shortening and tracking
☐ **Discount Code System** - In your e-commerce platform
☐ **Spreadsheet** - Master tracking sheet
☐ **Pixel** - Facebook/TikTok pixel installed

**Step-by-Step:**

1. Create UTM builder spreadsheet (or use Google's Campaign URL Builder)
2. Set up Bitly/Rebrandly account
3. Create discount code format (e.g., JANE20 for @janesmith)
4. Set up conversion tracking in Google Analytics
5. Test all tracking links

## Week 2: Influencer Discovery & Outreach

### Day 6-7: Build Your Influencer List

#### Day 6: Discovery

**Target Numbers:**
- Find 50-100 potential influencers
- Aim for 20-30 to outreach to
- Expect 5-10 to respond positively
- Plan to activate 3-5 for first campaign

**Discovery Methods:**

**Manual (Free):**
1. Hashtag search: #[your niche], #[your industry]
2. Competitor tagged posts
3. Location-based search (if local)
4. Comment section mining on popular posts
5. Google search: "[niche] + influencer"

**Tools (Paid but faster):**
- Modash ($49/mo) - Good for micro-influencers
- Heepsy ($49/mo) - Affordable discovery
- AspireIQ ($99+/mo) - Full platform
- Grin ($100+/mo) - E-commerce focused

**Vetting Criteria:**

For each influencer, check:

☐ Follower count in target range
☐ Engagement rate > 3% (for micro)
☐ Audience demographics match your target
☐ Content quality is high
☐ Posting frequency is consistent (4+ times/week)
☐ No red flags (fake followers, controversial content)
☐ Brand alignment (values, aesthetic, tone)

**Create Your Database:**

Set up a spreadsheet with:
- Name
- Handle
- Platform
- Followers
- Engagement rate
- Niche
- Contact info
- Status (prospect, contacted, negotiating, etc.)
- Notes

#### Day 7: Prioritize & Research

**Scoring System:**

Rate each influencer 1-5 on:
- Audience fit (1-5)
- Engagement quality (1-5)
- Content quality (1-5)
- Brand alignment (1-5)
- Professionalism (1-5)

**Total Score: /25**

**Prioritization:**
- 20-25: Hot lead (outreach first)
- 15-19: Warm lead (outreach second)
- 10-14: Cold lead (outreach if needed)
- <10: Pass

**Deep Research on Top 20:**

For your top 20 influencers:
- Review their last 10 posts
- Check their brand partnership history
- Read comments on their posts
- Check their story highlights
- Look for contact info (email in bio, Linktree, etc.)
- Note their typical rates (if visible)

**Deliverable by End of Day 7:**
- Database of 50-100 influencers
- Top 20 prioritized and researched
- Contact info gathered


**DM Template (for Instagram/TikTok):**


#### Day 9-10: Send Outreach

**Daily Goals:**
- Day 9: Send 15-20 outreach messages
- Day 10: Send 15-20 more outreach messages

**Best Practices:**

**Timing:**
- Send Tuesday-Thursday (higher response rates)
- Send 10am-2pm in their timezone
- Avoid Mondays (inbox overload) and Fridays (checking out)

**Follow-Up Schedule:**
- Initial outreach: Day 1
- Follow-up #1: Day 4 (if no response)
- Follow-up #2: Day 8 (if no response)
- Follow-up #3: Day 14 (final attempt)

**Follow-Up Template:**

```
Let me know if you're interested or if now's not the right time.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Track Everything:**

Update your database with:
- Date of outreach
- Method (email, DM, etc.)
- Response (yes, no, maybe, no response)
- Next action
- Notes from conversation

**Expected Response Rates:**
- Cold email: 20-30% response rate
- DM: 30-40% response rate

### Day 11-12: Negotiation & Selection

#### Day 11: Review Responses

**Categorize Responses:**

**Yes/Interested:**
- Schedule calls with top candidates
- Send media kit requests
- Begin rate negotiations

**Maybe/Questions:**
- Answer their questions promptly
- Provide additional info
- Gauge genuine interest level

**No/Not Interested:**
- Thank them politely
- Ask if you can keep them in database for future
- Move on

**No Response:**
- Send follow-up #1
- If still no response after follow-up #2, move on

**Rate Negotiation:**

**Research:**
- Check their media kit (if provided)
- Research typical rates for their tier
- Consider their engagement rate (higher ER = higher value)
- Factor in usage rights (more rights = higher rate)

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Be respectful of their rates (they know their value)
- Offer long-term partnership for better rates
- Bundle multiple posts for discount
- Be clear about what's included (revisions, usage, etc.)
- Don't lowball - you get what you pay for

**Typical Rate Ranges:**

| Followers | Post | Story | Reel/TikTok |
|-----------|------|-------|-------------|
| 5K-10K | $75-200 | $50-100 | $150-400 |
| 10K-25K | $200-500 | $100-250 | $400-800 |
| 25K-50K | $500-1,500 | $250-500 | $800-2,000 |
| 50K-100K | $1,500-5,000 | $500-1,500 | $2,000-6,000 |

### Day 18-19: Content Review Prep

#### Day 18: Set Up Approval Process

**Decide Your Approach:**

**Option 1: Pre-Approval Required**
- Influencer sends draft before posting
- You review and approve (or request changes)
- Pros: Quality control, compliance
- Cons: Slower, more back-and-forth

**Option 2: Post-Approval (Review After)**
- Influencer posts without pre-approval
- You review after and provide feedback for future
- Pros: Faster, more authentic
- Cons: Less control, may need to request edits

**Option 3: Hybrid**
- Pre-approval for first post
- Trust influencer for subsequent posts
- Pros: Balance of control and efficiency
- Cons: Requires good judgment on who to trust

**Recommendation for First Campaign:**
Use Option 1 (pre-approval) to ensure quality and compliance.

**Set Up Review System:**

1. Create shared folder (Google Drive, Dropbox)
2. Influencers upload drafts there
3. You review and comment
4. Track approval status

**Review Checklist:**

When reviewing content:
- ✓ Key messages included
- ✓ FTC disclosure clear and conspicuous
- ✓ Brand guidelines followed
- ✓ Product shown accurately
- ✓ No factual errors
- ✓ Quality is high
- ✓ Tags and hashtags correct
- ✓ Tracking link/code included

**Turnaround Time:**

Commit to:
- Review within 24-48 hours
- Clear feedback (approve or specific changes needed)
- Maximum 2 rounds of revisions (specify in contract)

## Week 4: Launch & Optimization

### Day 20-22: Campaign Launch

#### Day 20: First Posts Go Live

**Launch Day Checklist:**

Morning:
☐ Confirm first influencer posts on schedule
☐ Review posts for compliance
☐ Like and comment from brand account
☐ Share to brand stories
☐ Monitor initial engagement

Afternoon:
☐ Check tracking links are working
☐ Monitor discount code usage
☐ Respond to comments and DMs
☐ Screenshot posts for records
☐ Check analytics dashboard

Evening:
☐ Review day 1 performance
☐ Note any issues or learnings
☐ Prepare for day 2 posts

**Engagement Best Practices:**

**From Brand Account:**
- Like the post within 1 hour
- Leave genuine comment (not just "🔥")
- Share to your stories (tag influencer)
- Respond to comments on the post
- Answer DMs about the post

**From Influencer:**
- Should respond to comments for first 2-4 hours
- Should answer DMs about the product
- Should engage with commenters

**Monitor For:**

⚠️ Negative comments or complaints
⚠️ Questions you should answer
⚠️ Technical issues (links not working, etc.)
⚠️ FTC compliance issues
⚠️ Brand safety concerns

#### Day 21-22: Continue Launch

**Daily Routine:**

Each day of the launch:

**Morning (9-10am):**
- Check overnight posts
- Review and approve any drafts
- Respond to comments/DMs
- Check analytics from previous day

**Midday (12-2pm):**
- Share top-performing posts to brand stories
- Engage with comments
- Monitor discount code usage
- Check in with influencers

**Afternoon (4-5pm):**
- Review day's performance
- Update tracking sheet
- Note any issues
- Prepare for next day's posts

**Optimization Opportunities:**

Based on early performance:

**If engagement is low:**
- Have influencer engage more with comments
- Share post to brand stories
- Consider boosting post with paid ads

**If clicks are low:**
- Have influencer add link to stories
- Create more urgency in CTA
- Check that link is working properly

**If conversions are low:**
- Check landing page experience
- Verify discount code is working
- Consider stronger offer

**If content is performing well:**
- Ask influencer to post additional stories
- Consider extending partnership
- Request additional content types

**Deliverable by End of Day 22:**
- All posts published
- Initial performance data collected
- Issues identified and addressed

**Long-Tail Metrics:**

At 30 days post-campaign, measure:
- Total revenue (including delayed conversions)
- Customer retention rate
- Customer LTV
- Brand search volume lift
- Social follower growth
- Email list growth

```
30-Day ROAS = Total 30-Day Revenue ÷ Total Campaign Cost
```

### Scaling Framework

**If ROAS > 4:1 (Excellent):**
- 3x budget next campaign
- Add more influencers
- Expand to new platforms
- Test macro-influencers
- Build ambassador program

**If ROAS 2-4:1 (Good):**
- 2x budget next campaign
- Focus on top-performing influencers
- Optimize content strategy
- Improve landing page experience

**If ROAS 1-2:1 (Break-even):**
- Keep budget same
- Make significant optimizations
- Test different influencer profiles
- Adjust offer/messaging

**If ROAS < 1:1 (Loss):**
- Pause and diagnose
- Don't throw good money after bad
- Make fundamental changes
- Consider different strategy

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### ❌ Pitfall 1: Not Setting Clear Goals

**Problem:** Vague goals like "increase awareness" without specifics.

**Solution:** Set SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).

**Example:** "Generate $50,000 in revenue from influencer-driven sales within 30 days of campaign launch."

---

### ❌ Pitfall 2: Choosing Influencers by Follower Count Alone

**Problem:** Picking influencers with most followers, ignoring engagement and fit.

**Solution:** Prioritize engagement rate, audience quality, and brand alignment over follower count.

**Rule of Thumb:** Engagement rate > 3% is good for micro-influencers.

---

### ❌ Pitfall 3: Over-Controlling Creative

**Problem:** Giving influencers word-for-word scripts.

**Solution:** Provide key messages and guidelines, but trust their expertise.

**Balance:** Clear on what to say, flexible on how to say it.

---

### ❌ Pitfall 4: Not Tracking Properly

**Problem:** No UTMs, no discount codes, can't measure ROI.

**Solution:** Set up tracking before campaign launches. Test all links.

**Minimum:** Unique discount code per influencer + UTM parameters.

---

### ❌ Pitfall 5: Ignoring FTC Requirements

**Problem:** Influencers don't disclose partnerships properly.

**Solution:** Include FTC requirements in briefs. Review posts before they go live.

**Requirement:** Clear, conspicuous disclosure (#ad, #sponsored) at beginning of post.

---

### ❌ Pitfall 6: One-and-Done Mindset

**Problem:** Treating influencers as transactional, not building relationships.

**Solution:** Plan for ongoing partnerships. Invest in relationships.

**Benefit:** Better rates, better content, more authentic partnerships.

---

### ❌ Pitfall 7: Not Budgeting Enough

**Problem:** Expecting macro results with micro budgets.

**Solution:** Budget realistically. Start small but plan to scale.

**Minimum:** $2,500-5,000 for meaningful first campaign.

---

### ❌ Pitfall 8: Giving Up Too Soon

**Problem:** First campaign doesn't meet expectations, abandon influencer marketing.

**Solution:** Treat first campaign as learning. Optimize and iterate.

**Reality:** Most brands see significant improvement by campaign 3-4.
