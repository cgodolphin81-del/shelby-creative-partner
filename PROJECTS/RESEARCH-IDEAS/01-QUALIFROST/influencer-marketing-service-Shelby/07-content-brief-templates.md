## Why Content Briefs Matter

### The Problem Without Briefs:
❌ Influencer misses key messages
❌ Wrong product features highlighted
❌ FTC disclosure forgotten
❌ Brand guidelines ignored
❌ Multiple revision rounds needed
❌ Campaign goals not met

### The Solution With Briefs:
✅ Clear expectations from the start
✅ All key messages included
✅ Proper disclosures
✅ On-brand content
✅ Faster approvals
✅ Better results

### The Goldilocks Rule:

**Too Little Guidance:**
- "Just post about our product!"
- Results: Generic content, missed opportunities

**Too Much Control:**
- 10-page document with script word-for-word
- Results: Stiff, inauthentic content

**Just Right:**
- Clear objectives + key messages + creative freedom
- Results: Authentic content that drives results

## Brief Components

### Essential Elements (Include in Every Brief)

1. **Campaign Overview**
   - Campaign name
   - Brand background (1-2 sentences)
   - Campaign goal
   - Timeline

2. **Deliverables**
   - What content to create
   - Number of posts
   - Platforms
   - Posting dates

3. **Key Messages**
   - 3-5 main points to communicate
   - Product benefits (not just features)
   - Unique selling propositions

4. **Brand Guidelines**
   - Tone of voice
   - Visual style
   - Words to use/avoid
   - Competitor mentions (yes/no)

5. **Disclosure Requirements**
   - FTC compliance
   - Platform-specific disclosure tools
   - Hashtag requirements

6. **Tracking**
   - Discount code
   - UTM links
   - How to track performance

7. **Approval Process**
   - Do posts need approval?
   - Turnaround time
   - Revision policy

8. **Compensation**
   - Payment amount
   - Payment terms
   - Product details (if applicable)

### Optional Elements (Add as Needed)

- Creative examples/inspiration
- Mood board
- Script outlines (for video)
- Shot list
- Talking points
- FAQ about product
- Customer testimonials
- Press coverage
- Competitive differentiation
- Call-to-action specifics
- Comment engagement expectations
- Usage rights details
- Exclusivity requirements

## Best Practices

### ✅ Writing Effective Briefs

**1. Be Clear, Not Controlling**
- Provide guidance, not scripts
- Trust the influencer's expertise
- Allow creative freedom within guardrails

**2. Keep It Scannable**
- Use headers and sections
- Bullet points over paragraphs
- Bold key requirements
- Include a TL;DR summary

**3. Provide Context**
- Why this campaign matters
- Who the audience is
- What success looks like
- How this fits into broader strategy

**4. Set Clear Expectations**
- Exact deliverables
- Specific deadlines
- Approval process
- Revision policy

**5. Make It Easy**
- Provide all assets in one folder
- Include tracking links ready to use
- Pre-write disclosure language
- Offer examples and inspiration

### 📋 Brief Checklist

Before sending, ensure your brief includes:

- [ ] Campaign overview and goals
- [ ] Clear deliverables (what, when, where)
- [ ] Key messages (3-5 points max)
- [ ] Brand guidelines (tone, visual, do's/don'ts)
- [ ] FTC disclosure requirements
- [ ] Tracking links and codes
- [ ] Approval process (if any)
- [ ] Compensation details
- [ ] Contact information
- [ ] Timeline/deadlines

### 🎯 Customization Tips

**For Nano-Influencers (1K-10K):**
- Simpler briefs (1-2 pages)
- More guidance (they're often newer)
- Product-focused (they value the product)
- Flexible on timing

**For Micro-Influencers (10K-100K):**
- Standard briefs (2-4 pages)
- Collaborative tone
- Balance of guidance and freedom
- Professional but friendly

**For Macro-Influencers (100K+):**
- Detailed briefs (4+ pages)
- They may have their own templates
- Expect to work with their manager
- More negotiation on terms

## Do's and Don'ts

### ✅ DO:

- Send briefs at least 1 week before content is due
- Include visual examples and inspiration
- Provide all necessary assets upfront
- Be responsive to questions
- Allow creative freedom
- Include FTC disclosure requirements
- Set realistic deadlines
- Follow up with a call for complex campaigns
- Update briefs based on learnings
- Thank influencers for their work

### ❌ DON'T:

- Don't send briefs last-minute
- Don't write word-for-word scripts (unless agreed)
- Don't overwhelm with 20+ key messages
- Don't ignore influencer's creative input
- Don't forget disclosure requirements
- Don't change requirements after approval
- Don't ghost after campaign ends
- Don't expect free revisions beyond agreed amount
- Don't use briefs as legal contracts (use actual contracts)
- Don't copy-paste the same brief to every influencer

## Brief Delivery Tips

### Format Options:

**PDF:**
- ✅ Professional, polished
- ✅ Preserves formatting
- ❌ Hard to update
- ❌ Can't easily copy/paste

**Google Doc:**
- ✅ Easy to update
- ✅ Can comment and collaborate
- ✅ Easy to copy/paste
- ❌ Less formal

**Email:**
- ✅ Quick and easy
- ✅ Everything in one place
- ❌ Can get lost in threads
- ❌ Hard to reference later

**Project Management Tool:**
- ✅ Organized (Asana, Trello, Notion)
- ✅ Trackable
- ✅ Can include checklists
- ❌ Requires influencer to use tool

**Recommended:** Send as PDF + Google Doc link (for reference + collaboration)

### Timing:

- **Initial Brief:** 2-3 weeks before posting
- **Reminder:** 1 week before deadline
- **Final Check:** 2-3 days before posting
- **Follow-Up:** After posting (thank you + analytics request)
