## Pricing Models

### 1. Monthly Retainer (Recommended)

**Structure:**
- Fixed monthly fee
- Defined scope of work
- 6-12 month minimum
- Auto-renewing

**Pros:**
- Predictable revenue
- Easier to staff
- Client commitment
- Compound results

**Cons:**
- Scope creep risk
- Need to deliver consistently
- Churn if results don't materialize

**Best For:** Most SEO engagements

---

### 2. Project-Based

**Structure:**
- One-time fee
- Defined deliverables
- Fixed timeline

**Examples:**
- Technical SEO Audit: $5,000-$25,000
- Site Migration: $10,000-$50,000
- Content Strategy: $5,000-$15,000
- Link Building Campaign: $10,000-$50,000

**Pros:**
- Clear scope
- Good for specific needs
- Lower commitment for clients

**Cons:**
- One-time revenue
- Feast/famine cycle
- Harder to scale

**Best For:** Audits, migrations, specific projects

---

### 3. Performance-Based

**Structure:**
- Base fee + performance bonus
- Bonus tied to rankings/traffic/revenue

**Examples:**
- $5,000/mo + $500 per top-10 keyword
- $10,000/mo + 10% of organic revenue growth
- $3,000/mo + $100 per qualified lead

**Pros:**
- Aligned incentives
- Easy sell
- High upside

**Cons:**
- Revenue volatility
- Attribution challenges
- Can discourage long-term thinking

**Best For:** Confident agencies, specific verticals

---

### 4. Hybrid Model (Recommended for Growth)

**Structure:**
- Base retainer + project work + performance bonuses

**Example:**
- Base: $10,000/mo (ongoing SEO)
- Projects: Content creation ($3,000/month average)
- Performance: $50,000 bonus if organic revenue grows 50%

**Pros:**
- Predictable base
- Upside potential
- Flexibility

**Cons:**
- More complex to manage
- Need clear agreements

**Best For:** Established agencies with track record

## Service Packages

#### Starter Package ($3,000-$5,000/mo)

**Best For:** Small businesses, local SEO, startups

**Includes:**
- Technical SEO (basic monitoring)
- 4 blog posts/month (1,500 words)
- 5 link placements/month
- Local SEO (GBP management)
- Monthly reporting
- Email support

**Client Profile:**

- Website: Basic, under 100 pages
- Goals: Local visibility, foundational SEO

**Margins:** 50-60%

## Pricing Psychology

### 1. Anchoring

Show highest package first to make others seem reasonable.

```
Enterprise: $50,000/mo
Professional: $20,000/mo ← Looks reasonable
Growth: $10,000/mo
```

---

### 2. Decoy Pricing

Add a package to make target package more attractive.

```
Basic: $5,000/mo (limited)
Professional: $12,000/mo (best value) ← Target
Enterprise: $25,000/mo (everything)
```

---

### 3. Charm Pricing

$9,500 feels less than $10,000 (even though it's close).

```
$4,900 vs $5,000
$9,900 vs $10,000
$24,500 vs $25,000
```

---

### 4. Tier Differentiation

Make differences clear and meaningful.

| Feature | Starter | Growth | Professional |
|---------|---------|--------|--------------|
| Articles | 4 | 8 | 16 |
| Links | 5 | 10 | 20 |
| Calls | Monthly | Bi-weekly | Weekly |
| Support | Email | Priority | Dedicated |

## Unit Economics

### Client Lifetime Value (LTV)

**Formula:**
```
LTV = Average Monthly Revenue × Gross Margin % × Average Lifespan (months)
```

**Example:**
```
Average Monthly Revenue: $10,000
Gross Margin: 60%
Average Lifespan: 18 months

LTV = $10,000 × 60% × 18 = $108,000
```

---

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Formula:**
```
CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Cost / New Clients Acquired
```

**Example:**
```
Monthly Sales & Marketing: $30,000
New Clients/Month: 3

CAC = $30,000 / 3 = $10,000
```

---

### LTV:CAC Ratio

**Formula:**
```
LTV:CAC = LTV / CAC
```

**Example:**
```
LTV: $108,000
CAC: $10,000

LTV:CAC = 10.8:1
```

**Benchmarks:**
- 1:1 = Unsustainable
- 3:1 = Good
- 5:1+ = Excellent

---

### Payback Period

**Formula:**
```
Payback = CAC / (Monthly Revenue × Gross Margin %)
```

**Example:**
```
CAC: $10,000
Monthly Revenue: $10,000
Gross Margin: 60%

Payback = $10,000 / ($10,000 × 60%) = 1.67 months
```

**Target:** < 6 months

---

### Breakeven Analysis

**Fixed Costs (Monthly):**
- Salaries: $40,000
- Tools: $3,000
- Office: $5,000
- G&A: $7,000
- **Total Fixed:** $55,000

**Variable Costs:**
- 40% of revenue (labor, contractors)

**Breakeven Formula:**
```
Breakeven = Fixed Costs / (1 - Variable Cost %)
Breakeven = $55,000 / (1 - 0.40) = $91,667/mo
```

**Clients Needed (@ $10K avg):** 9-10 clients

## Pricing Increases

### When to Raise Prices

**Triggers:**
- 80%+ capacity for 3+ months
- Consistent results (case studies)
- Increased demand (inbound)
- Team expansion costs
- Annual review (inflation)

**Frequency:**
- New clients: As market allows
- Existing clients: Annually (10-20%)

---

### How to Raise Prices

**For New Clients:**
- Simply update pricing
- No explanation needed
- Market-based

**For Existing Clients:**
```
Subject: Updated Pricing for 2024

Hi [Name],

As we head into our [X] year together, I wanted to 
share some updates to our pricing.

Since we started working together, we've [list wins]. 
We've also expanded our team and capabilities to 
serve you better.

Effective [date], our monthly investment will adjust 
to $[new price], reflecting:
- [Enhancement 1]
- [Enhancement 2]
- [Enhancement 3]

We're committed to delivering even more value in 
the coming year. Let's schedule a call to discuss.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Retention:** 80-90% typically accept

## Churn Management

### Churn Benchmarks

| Agency Stage | Monthly Churn | Annual Churn |
|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| Early | 5-10% | 40-60% |
| Growth | 3-5% | 25-40% |
| Mature | 2-3% | 15-25% |
| Enterprise | 1-2% | 10-15% |

**Target:** <3% monthly, <30% annual

---

### Churn Reasons & Solutions

| Reason | % of Churn | Solution |
|--------|------------|----------|
| No results | 30% | Better expectation setting, quicker wins |
| Budget cuts | 25% | Show ROI, flexible packages |
| Poor communication | 20% | Regular check-ins, proactive updates |
| Agency fit | 15% | Better qualification upfront |
| Other | 10% | N/A |

---

### Churn Reduction Tactics

1. **Onboarding excellence** - Set up for success
2. **Quick wins** - Show value in first 30 days
3. **Regular communication** - No surprises
4. **Proactive problem-solving** - Address issues early
5. **QBRs** - Show strategic value
6. **Relationship building** - Multiple stakeholders
7. **Results reporting** - Clear ROI
8. **Flexible terms** - Payment plans, pauses

### Cash Flow Management

**Challenges:**
- Clients pay net-30
- Team paid bi-weekly
- Growth requires upfront investment

**Solutions:**
- Require payment upfront (first month)
- Auto-billing (credit card)
- Late fees (enforce them)
- Line of credit (backup)
- 6+ months cash reserve

## Best Practices

1. **Price for value, not hours** - Results, not time
2. **Raise prices annually** - Inflation + value
3. **Fire bad clients** - Make room for good ones
4. **Track unit economics** - Know your numbers
5. **Diversify revenue** - Not too concentrated
6. **Build recurring revenue** - Retainers > projects
7. **Show ROI constantly** - Justify investment
8. **Invest in retention** - Cheaper than acquisition
