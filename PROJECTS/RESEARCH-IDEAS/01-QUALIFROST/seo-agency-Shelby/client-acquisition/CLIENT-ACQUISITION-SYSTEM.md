---

### Client Qualification Criteria

**Green Flags (Ideal Clients):**
- ✅ Revenue $1M+ annually
- ✅ Understands SEO is long-term (6+ months)
- ✅ Has budget ($5K+/month minimum)
- ✅ Decision maker is engaged
- ✅ Previous marketing experience (knows what works)
- ✅ Website has foundation (not starting from zero)
- ✅ Competitive market (understands need for SEO)
- ✅ Can provide access (CMS, analytics, etc.)

**Red Flags (Avoid):**
- ❌ Expects #1 rankings in 30 days
- ❌ Budget under $3K/month
- ❌ No clear decision maker
- ❌ Unwilling to invest in content/links
- ❌ Previous bad agency experience (trust issues)
- ❌ Website is fundamentally broken (needs rebuild first)
- ❌ Unresponsive/ghosting during sales process
- ❌ Wants "guaranteed" rankings

## Lead Generation Strategies

### 1. Cold Outreach

#### Cold Email

**Volume:** 100-500 emails/week
**Response Rate:** 5-15%
**Close Rate:** 1-3%

**Email Structure:**
```
Subject: [Personalized, specific]

Hi [Name],

[Personalized observation about their business]

[Specific problem/opportunity identified]

[Credibility/proof]

[Soft CTA]

[Signature]
```

```
Subject: Quick SEO observation for [Company]

I was searching for [keyword] and noticed [Company] 
ranks on page 2, just behind [Competitor].

to position 3 for similar terms, resulting in [result].

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call to discuss 
what I'm seeing? No pitch, just insights.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Lead Qualification

### Qualification Framework: BANT

| Factor | Questions to Ask |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Budget** | "What budget have you allocated for SEO?" |
| **Authority** | "Who else is involved in this decision?" |
| **Need** | "What specific problems are you trying to solve?" |
| **Timeline** | "When are you looking to get started?" |

---

### Qualification Call Script

**Introduction (5 min):**
```
"Thanks for making time, [Name]. To make sure this 
is a good fit, I'd love to learn more about [Company] 
and what you're looking to achieve with SEO.

Can you tell me a bit about the business and what 
brought you to look into SEO right now?"
```

**Discovery (15 min):**
```
Questions to ask:
- What are your main business goals this year?
- What's working/not working with current marketing?
- Have you done SEO before? What was the experience?
- What does success look like in 6-12 months?
- What budget have you allocated?
- Who else is involved in this decision?
- What's your timeline for getting started?
```

**Positioning (10 min):**
```
"Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm seeing...

[Share 2-3 specific observations]

Here's how we typically help companies like yours...

[Share relevant case study]

Does this sound like it could be a good fit?"
```

**Next Steps (5 min):**
```
"If this makes sense, here's what I'd recommend...

[Outline next steps: audit, proposal, etc.]

Does that work for you?"
```

## The Audit Pitch

### Free Mini-Audit (Lead Magnet)

**What to Include:**
- 3-5 specific issues found
- 2-3 quick wins
- 1-2 competitive insights
- Rough opportunity estimate

**What NOT to Include:**
- Full audit (save for paid)
- Complete solution (save for proposal)
- Pricing (discuss on call)

**Goal:** Show expertise, create desire for full audit

---

### Paid Audit ($2,500-$5,000)

**Pitch:**
```
"Before we recommend any work, we do a comprehensive 
audit to understand exactly what's happening with 
your site and where the opportunities are.

The audit includes [list deliverables] and takes 
[timeframe]. The investment is $[price].

If we move forward with ongoing work, we'll credit 
the audit fee toward your first month.

This ensures we're making recommendations based on 
data, not guesses."
```

**Benefits:**
- Qualifies serious clients
- Generates revenue even if they don't continue
- Gives you ammo for proposal
- Shows value before ongoing commitment

## Proposal System

### Proposal Structure

**1. Executive Summary (1 page)**
- Client's goals
- Your understanding of their situation
- High-level approach
- Expected outcomes

**2. Current State Analysis (3-5 pages)**
- SEO audit summary
- Key issues identified
- Competitive landscape
- Opportunity assessment

**3. Recommended Strategy (5-10 pages)**
- Technical SEO plan
- Content strategy
- Link building approach
- Local SEO (if applicable)
- Timeline/phases

**4. Investment (1-2 pages)**
- Package options (2-3 tiers)
- What's included
- Pricing
- Terms

**5. About Us (2-3 pages)**
- Company overview
- Team bios
- Relevant case studies
- Testimonials

**6. Next Steps (1 page)**
- Timeline
- Onboarding process
- What we need from them
- Agreement/signature

---

### Pricing Presentation

**Present 3 Options:**

| Package | Investment | Best For |
|---------|------------|----------|
| Essential | $5,000/mo | Foundational SEO |
| Growth | $10,000/mo | Aggressive growth |
| Dominance | $20,000/mo | Market leadership |

**Psychology:**
- Middle option is usually chosen
- Highest option makes middle seem reasonable
- All options should be profitable

## Closing Strategies

### Common Objections & Responses

**"It's too expensive"**
```
"I understand. Can I ask what you're comparing it to?

[Listen]

SEO is an investment, not a cost. Based on the 
opportunity we identified, even a 20% increase in 
organic traffic would generate $[X] in additional 
revenue.

The question isn't whether you can afford to invest 
in SEO—it's whether you can afford not to when 
your competitors are."
```

---

**"We've been burned by SEO agencies before"**
```
"I completely understand, and that's actually pretty 
common. Can you share what happened?

[Listen, empathize]

Here's how we're different:

[Specific differentiators: transparency, reporting, 
communication, guarantees]

We're so confident in our approach that we [risk 
reversal: month-to-month after initial term, 
performance guarantees, etc.]."
```

---

**"We need to think about it"**
```
"Of course, this is an important decision. What 
specifically do you need to think through?

[Listen - identify real objection]

Is there any information I can provide that would help?

[Address concern]

What would need to happen for you to feel confident 
moving forward?"
```

---

**"We want to wait until [next quarter/year]"**
```
"I understand timing is important. The challenge with 
SEO is that it compounds over time.

Every month you wait is a month of traffic and revenue 
your competitors are capturing.

If we started today, in 6 months you'd be seeing 
[specific outcome]. If you start in [future date], 
you're pushing that back.

That said, I'd rather you start when you're ready 
than rush into it. Can we check in [specific date]?"
```

---

### Closing Techniques

**1. Assumptive Close**
```
"Great! I'll send over the agreement. Do you prefer 
we start on the 1st or 15th?"
```

**2. Summary Close**
```
"So to recap: you're looking to [goals], we'll help 
by [strategy], and the investment is [price]. 

Does that sound right? Great, let's get started."
```

**3. Urgency Close (Use Sparingly)**
```
"We have capacity for one more client this month. 
If we can get started by [date], we can include 
[bonus]. After that, our next availability is 
[next month]."
```

## Client Retention

### Communication Cadence

| Touchpoint | Frequency | Format |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| Performance Report | Monthly | Email + PDF |
| Strategy Call | Monthly | Video call |
| Quick Check-in | Bi-weekly | Email/Slack |
| QBR (Quarterly Business Review) | Quarterly | Video call + deck |
| Executive Summary | Quarterly | Email |

---

### Monthly Report Structure

**1. Executive Summary**
- Key wins
- Key metrics
- Focus for next month

**2. Performance Overview**
- Organic traffic
- Rankings (top keywords)
- Conversions
- Revenue (if tracked)

**3. Work Completed**
- Technical fixes
- Content published
- Links earned
- Other activities

**4. Next Month's Plan**
- Priorities
- Expected outcomes
- Any asks from client

**5. Appendix**
- Detailed rankings
- Full traffic data
- Backlink report

---

### Retention Best Practices

1. **Under-promise, over-deliver** - Set realistic expectations
2. **Communicate proactively** - Don't wait for them to ask
3. **Show value constantly** - Remind them what you're doing
4. **Be responsive** - 24-hour response time maximum
5. **Bring new ideas** - Don't go on autopilot
6. **Celebrate wins** - Highlight successes
7. **Address issues quickly** - Don't hide problems
8. **Build relationships** - Not just transactional

## Best Practices

1. **Niche down** - Specialize in an industry
2. **Build case studies** - Proof sells better than promises
3. **Be consultative** - Advisor, not vendor
4. **Qualify hard** - Bad clients cost more than they're worth
5. **Follow up** - Most sales happen after 5+ touches
6. **Track everything** - Data drives improvement
7. **Ask for referrals** - Happy clients are your best source
8. **Never stop prospecting** - Pipeline should always be full
