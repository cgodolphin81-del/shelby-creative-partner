## 1. PRICING PHILOSOPHY

### Value-Based Pricing Model

**Traditional (Hourly):**
```
Hours × Rate = Price
Problem: Penalizes efficiency
```

**Value-Based:**
```
Client Value Delivered = Price
Benefit: Aligns with client success
```

### Pricing Factors

| Factor | Impact on Price |
|--------|-----------------|
| List Size | Larger = Higher |
| Revenue Impact | Higher = Higher |
| Complexity | More = Higher |
| Industry | Competitive = Higher |
| Timeline | Rush = Higher |
| Agency Expertise | Premium = Higher |

### Pricing Psychology

**DO:**
- ✅ Anchor high, then present options
- ✅ Show ROI projections
- ✅ Package services (not à la carte only)
- ✅ Include clear deliverables
- ✅ Offer payment plans for large projects

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Lead with price (lead with value)
- ❌ Undersell your expertise
- ❌ Compete on price alone
- ❌ Hide fees or charges
- ❌ Discount without reason

## 6. PERFORMANCE-BASED PRICING

### Revenue Share Model

**Structure:**
- Lower base retainer
- Percentage of email-attributed revenue

**Typical Terms:**
```
Base Retainer: $2,000 - $5,000/month
Revenue Share: 5-15% of email revenue
OR
Base Retainer: $3,000 - $7,000/month
Revenue Share: 3-8% of INCREMENTAL email revenue
```

**Example:**
```
Month 1:
Email Revenue: $50,000
Base: $4,000
Revenue Share (10%): $5,000
Total: $9,000

Month 2:
Email Revenue: $75,000
Base: $4,000
Revenue Share (10%): $7,500
Total: $11,500
```

**Pros:**
- Aligned incentives
- Unlimited upside
- Client sees you as partner

**Cons:**
- Revenue volatility
- Attribution disputes
- Requires trust

---

### Performance Bonus Model

**Structure:**
- Standard retainer
- Bonus for hitting targets

**Typical Terms:**
```
Base Retainer: $5,000/month
Targets:
- Revenue target: $100K/month
- Bonus: 5% of revenue above target

Example:
Revenue: $150,000
Above target: $50,000
Bonus: $2,500
Total: $7,500
```

**Bonus Triggers:**
- Revenue milestones
- List growth targets
- Engagement improvements
- ROI targets

---

### Hybrid Model (Recommended)

**Structure:**
- Fair base retainer
- Modest revenue share
- Bonus for exceptional performance

**Example:**
```
Base Retainer: $4,000/month
Revenue Share: 5% of email revenue
Performance Bonus: $2,000 if ROI > 4000%

Typical Month:
Base: $4,000
Revenue Share (5% of $80K): $4,000
Total: $8,000

Great Month:
Base: $4,000
Revenue Share (5% of $120K): $6,000
Bonus: $2,000
Total: $12,000
```

## 8. CONTRACT TERMS

### Standard Terms

**Contract Length:**
- Minimum: 3 months (probationary)
- Standard: 6-12 months
- Preferred: 12 months (better pricing)

**Payment Terms:**
- Setup fee: 50% upfront, 50% on completion
- Monthly: Due on 1st of month
- Late fee: 1.5% per month on overdue balances

**Termination:**
- During probation: 30 days notice
- After probation: 60 days notice
- Immediate termination for cause

---

### Scope of Work

**Clearly Define:**
- Number of campaigns per month
- Number of revisions included
- Response time expectations
- What's included vs. extra
- Approval process
- Communication channels

**Example:**
```
INCLUDED:
- 6 campaigns per month
- 2 rounds of revisions per campaign
- 24-hour response time (business days)
- Monthly strategy call
- Monthly performance report

NOT INCLUDED (Available À La Carte):
- Additional campaigns beyond 6
- Rush delivery (<48 hours)
- Weekend support
- Custom development
- Additional templates
```

---

### Pricing Escalation

**Annual Increase:**
- Standard: 5-10% annual increase
- Justification: Increased expertise, inflation, added value
- Notice: 60 days before increase

**List Size Tiers:**
```
Tier 1: 0-10K contacts - Base price
Tier 2: 10K-50K contacts - Base + 25%
Tier 3: 50K-200K contacts - Base + 50%
Tier 4: 200K+ contacts - Base + 100%
```

**Scope Creep Protection:**
- Change order process for out-of-scope work
- Hourly rate for additional work: $150-300/hour
- Written approval required before work begins

## 10. PROPOSAL TEMPLATES

### Proposal Structure

```
1. Executive Summary
   - Understanding of their business
   - Key opportunities identified
   - Proposed solution overview

2. Current State Analysis
   - What's working
   - What's not working
   - Gaps identified

3. Proposed Solution
   - Scope of work
   - Deliverables
   - Timeline

4. Investment
   - Setup fees
   - Monthly retainer
   - Payment terms

5. Expected Results
   - Revenue projections
   - ROI calculation
   - Timeline to results

6. About Us
   - Agency credentials
   - Case studies
   - Testimonials

7. Terms & Conditions
   - Contract length
   - Termination
   - Scope boundaries

8. Next Steps
   - How to accept
   - Onboarding process
   - Start date
```

**Better:**

### Objection Handling

**"It's too expensive"**
```
"I understand budget is a concern. Let's look at the ROI:
- Your current email revenue: $X/month
- Our clients average 40% increase
- Additional revenue: $Y/month
- Our fee: $Z/month
- Net gain: $[Y-Z]/month

The program pays for itself in [X] weeks. 
Can we explore a phased approach to start?"
```

**"We can do this in-house"**
```
"Absolutely, you could. Consider:
- Hiring: $60K-100K/year for specialist
- Training & learning curve: 6-12 months
- Our expertise: Immediate
- Our results: Proven track record
- Total cost: Similar, but we deliver faster

Would a 90-day trial make sense to prove value?"
```

**"Let me think about it"**
```
"Of course, this is an important decision. 
What specific concerns do you have?

[Address concerns]

Based on our conversation, I'm confident we can 
deliver $X in additional revenue within 90 days. 
What would you need to see to move forward?"
```
