## The Challenge

### Brand Background

Vitality Labs makes science-backed supplements for active adults 30-55. Their flagship products include a daily multivitamin, omega-3 fish oil, and probiotic complex. Founded by a former pharma researcher, they emphasize transparency, third-party testing, and clinical dosages.

### The Problem

- **Plateaued at $100K-130K/month** for 8 months
- **High churn** - 45% of customers never reordered
- **Subscription program failing** - 8% attach rate, 15% monthly churn
- **Over-reliant on Meta** - 85% of paid traffic from one channel
- **No Google strategy** despite high-intent search demand
- **AOV declining** from $95 to $78
- **Previous agency burned them** - promised 5x ROAS, delivered 2x

### Goals

1. Scale to $400K+/month within 9 months
2. Diversify traffic (no channel >50%)
3. Increase subscription attach to 25%+
4. Improve LTV to $300+
5. Build defensible moat (brand, community, content)

## Our Approach

### Unique Aspect: Performance-Based Pricing

**The Deal:**
- Base retainer: $12K/month (covers costs)
- Performance fee: 8% of revenue ABOVE $120K baseline
- Example: At $350K revenue, fee = $12K + 8%($230K) = $30.4K

**Why This Worked:**
- Aligned incentives (we only win if they win)
- Showed confidence in our abilities
- Reduced their risk
- Built trust quickly

### Phase 1: Deep Dive (Weeks 1-3)

**Audit Findings:**

**Subscription Issues:**
- No onboarding sequence for subscribers
- No engagement between shipments
- Difficult to skip/cancel (created resentment)
- No subscriber-exclusive perks
- Replenishment reminders came too late

**Paid Media:**
- All budget on Meta (risky)
- No YouTube strategy (perfect for education)
- Google Shopping feed poorly optimized
- No influencer/affiliate program
- Creative was product-focused, not benefit-focused

**Content Gap:**
- No educational content
- Blog was empty
- No video content
- Missing trust signals (testing results, founder story)

**Retention:**
- No post-purchase education
- No community building
- No loyalty program
- Reorder reminders at 45 days (too late for 30-day supply)

### Phase 2: Strategy (Weeks 3-4)

**Core Pillars:**

1. **Subscription 2.0**
   - Complete Skio implementation
   - Subscriber onboarding flow
   - Engagement campaigns between shipments
   - Easy skip/swap (reduces cancellations)
   - Subscriber-exclusive products

2. **Channel Diversification**
   - Maintain Meta (40% of spend)
   - Add Google (30%: Search + Shopping + YouTube)
   - Add TikTok (20%: prospecting)
   - Add Pinterest (10%: evergreen traffic)

3. **Content & Education**
   - Weekly blog posts (SEO)
   - YouTube channel (supplement education)
   - Email education series
   - Founder story/mission content

4. **Community & Loyalty**
   - Private Facebook group
   - Loyalty program with tiers
   - User-generated content program
   - Ambassador program

## What Made the Difference

### 1. Subscription Rebuild

**The Insight:** Subscribers weren't churning because of the product—they were churning because of the experience.

**What We Changed:**
- Onboarding sequence (5 emails over 30 days)
- "How to use" guides for each product
- Mid-cycle engagement (recipes, tips, community)
- Easy self-service portal (skip, swap, cancel)
- Subscriber-only products and early access
- Loyalty points for subscribers (2x points)

**Results:**
- Churn dropped from 15% to 6%
- LTV increased from $220 to $580
- MRR grew from $9.6K to $144K
- Subscribers became brand advocates

### 2. YouTube Education Strategy

**The Insight:** Supplement buyers want education before purchase. YouTube is the second-largest search engine.

**What We Built:**
- Weekly videos (supplement education, not sales)
- "Ask the Founder" series
- Ingredient deep-dives
- Customer testimonials
- Comparison videos (us vs. competitors)

**Results:**
- 50K subscribers in 9 months
- 500K+ monthly views
- 15% of traffic from YouTube
- 4.2x ROAS on YouTube ads
- Organic search rankings improved

### 3. Community Building

**The Insight:** Supplements are a journey, not a transaction. Community drives accountability and retention.

**What We Built:**
- Private Facebook group (Vitality Insiders)
- Weekly challenges
- Expert AMAs (nutritionists, trainers)
- User transformation stories
- VIP events (virtual and IRL)

**Results:**
- 8,500 members in 9 months
- Members have 2.3x higher LTV
- 40% of members are subscribers
- UGC content library (500+ posts)
- Organic referrals (12% of new customers)

## Key Takeaways

### For Supplement Brands:

1. **Education sells** - Customers need to understand WHY before they buy. Content is your competitive advantage.

2. **Subscriptions require work** - It's not "set and forget." Onboarding, engagement, and perks are essential.

3. **Community compounds** - A strong community reduces CAC, increases LTV, and creates defensible moat.

4. **Diversification protects** - Don't rely on one channel. Build a portfolio of profitable acquisition sources.

### Performance Model Learnings:

**Pros:**
- Easier to close (lower risk for client)
- Unlimited upside
- Ultimate alignment
- Builds trust fast

**Cons:**
- Revenue volatility
- Attribution complexity
- Need capital to float costs
- Requires confidence

**Our Take:** Performance pricing works when you're confident in your abilities and have case studies to prove it. Start with retainer + performance, move to pure performance for ideal clients.
