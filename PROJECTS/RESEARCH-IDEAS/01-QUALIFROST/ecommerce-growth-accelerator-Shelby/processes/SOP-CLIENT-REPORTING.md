# SOP: Client Reporting

**Standard Operating Procedure**

---

## Overview

**SOP Name:** Client Reporting Process  
**Department:** Account Management  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** March 2026  
**Owner:** Account Management Team

---

## Purpose

Ensure consistent, timely, and insightful reporting that demonstrates value, drives action, and strengthens client relationships.

---

## Scope

**Who This Applies To:** Account Managers, Media Buyers, Email Specialists  
**When to Use:** Weekly and monthly for all active clients  
**Frequency:** Weekly (every Monday), Monthly (first week)

---

## Weekly Report Process

### Timeline

| Day | Task | Owner |
|-----|------|-------|
| **Friday PM** | Pull data, create draft | AM / Specialists |
| **Monday AM** | Review & finalize | AM |
| **Monday 12pm** | Send to client | AM |
| **Monday 3pm** | Internal review call | Team |
| **Wednesday** | Client check-in call | AM + Client |

### Data Collection

**Automated (90% of data):**
- [ ] Triple Whale / Northbeam dashboard
- [ ] Klaviyo analytics
- [ ] Platform native analytics (Meta, Google, etc.)

**Manual (10% of data):**
- [ ] Qualitative insights
- [ ] Test results summary
- [ ] Upcoming initiatives
- [ ] Blockers/asks

### Report Structure

1. **Executive Summary** (top of email)
   - Revenue, spend, ROAS
   - Key wins (3 bullet points)
   - Key challenges (2 bullet points)

2. **Paid Media Performance**
   - Channel breakdown table
   - Top campaigns
   - Creative winners
   - Tests running

3. **Email/SMS Performance**
   - Revenue, send volume
   - Flow performance
   - Campaign results
   - List growth

4. **Website & CRO**
   - Traffic, conversion rate
   - A/B test results
   - Insights

5. **Next Week's Plan**
   - Priorities
   - Tests launching
   - Budget changes
   - Approvals needed

6. **Blockers & Asks**
   - What we need from client
   - Deadlines

### Quality Checklist

Before sending:
- [ ] All data accurate (spot check vs. platforms)
- [ ] WoW and MTD comparisons included
- [ ] Insights, not just data
- [ ] Clear asks/approvals highlighted
- [ ] No typos or formatting issues
- [ ] Charts/screenshots included
- [ ] Client name correct throughout

```
[Your Name]
```

## Monthly Report Process

### Timeline

| Day | Task | Owner |
|-----|------|-------|
| **Month End** | Data collection begins | AM |
| **Day 1-2** | Report creation | AM |
| **Day 3** | Internal review | Team |
| **Day 4** | Send to client | AM |
| **Day 5-7** | Monthly business review call | AM + Client |

### Monthly Report Additions

Beyond weekly report, include:

1. **Month Overview**
   - Total revenue vs. goal
   - Total spend vs. budget
   - Overall ROAS
   - Month-over-month trends

2. **Channel Deep-Dive**
   - Full funnel analysis
   - Attribution breakdown
   - Channel contribution

3. **Initiative Progress**
   - 30-60-90 day plan progress
   - Completed projects
   - Upcoming milestones

4. **Financial Summary**
   - Revenue: $[X]
   - Ad Spend: $[X]
   - Agency Fees: $[X]
   - Gross Profit: $[X]
   - ROI: [X]%

5. **Strategic Recommendations**
   - What to start
   - What to stop
   - What to continue
   - Investment recommendations

6. **Next Month Plan**
   - Goals
   - Key initiatives
   - Budget allocation
   - Resource needs

## Business Review Meetings

### Monthly Business Review (MBR)

**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Attendees:** Client leadership, Agency team

**Agenda:**
1. Performance review (15 min)
2. Initiative progress (15 min)
3. Insights & learnings (15 min)
4. Next month planning (15 min)

**Preparation:**
- [ ] Deck created (10-15 slides)
- [ ] Data validated
- [ ] Recommendations prepared
- [ ] Sent to client 24h in advance

### Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

**Duration:** 90 minutes  
**Attendees:** Client leadership, Agency leadership

**Agenda:**
1. Quarter performance (20 min)
2. Strategic review (20 min)
3. Market/competitive insights (15 min)
4. Next quarter plan (25 min)
5. Partnership health (10 min)

**Deliverables:**
- Comprehensive deck (20-30 slides)
- Case study draft (if results warrant)
- Renewal discussion (if applicable)

## Reporting Best Practices

### Do's:
✅ Lead with insights, not just data  
✅ Compare to previous periods (WoW, MoM)  
✅ Highlight wins AND challenges  
✅ Make clear recommendations  
✅ Use visuals (charts, graphs)  
✅ Keep it scannable (bullet points, bold text)  
✅ Send on time, every time  
✅ Follow up on action items  

### Don'ts:
❌ Data dumps without analysis  
❌ Hiding poor performance  
❌ Blaming external factors  
❌ Vague recommendations  
❌ Walls of text  
❌ Typos and errors  
❌ Missing deadlines  
❌ Forgetting follow-ups

## Client Communication

### When to Send Alerts

**Immediate (within 1 hour):**
- Site down / tracking broken
- Campaign paused by platform
- Major performance drop (>50%)
- Account/ads disapproved

**Same Day:**
- Significant win (2x+ ROAS day)
- Budget concerns
- Creative fatigue detected

**Weekly:**
- Regular performance updates
- Test results
- Optimization changes

### Escalation Triggers

Escalate to leadership if:
- Client expresses dissatisfaction
- Performance below target for 3+ weeks
- Budget/payment issues
- Strategic pivot needed
- Renewal at risk

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Data discrepancy | Cross-check with platform native data |
| Missing data | Check tracking, API connections |
| Client not reading | Summarize key points in email body |
| Too many questions | Add more context/insights |
| Report fatigue | Simplify, focus on key metrics |
| Negative reaction to results | Schedule call, explain context, present plan |

---

## Quality Standards

**Success Criteria:**
- Reports sent on time (100%)
- Data accuracy (>99%)
- Client satisfaction (quarterly survey)
- Action items completed (>90%)

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
1. Sending without proofreading
2. Wrong date range
3. Incorrect client/brand name
4. Missing key metrics
5. No insights or recommendations
6. Not following up on asks
