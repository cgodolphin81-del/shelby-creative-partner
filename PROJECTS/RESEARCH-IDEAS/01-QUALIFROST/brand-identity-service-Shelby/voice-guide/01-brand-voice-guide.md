# Brand Voice Guide

## Introduction

This guide defines how [Company Name] communicates across all channels. Our voice is as important as our visual identity—it's how people experience our brand.

---

## Our Brand Voice

### The 4 Pillars of Our Voice

Our voice is built on four core pillars. Every piece of content should reflect these traits.

---

### 1. [TRAIT 1: e.g., APPROACHABLE]

**What it means:** [We're friendly and easy to talk to. We don't hide behind jargon or corporate speak.]

**In Practice:**
- Use contractions (we're, you'll, don't)
- Write like you speak
- Address the reader as "you"
- Keep sentences clear and direct

**Example:**
> ✅ "We're here to help you succeed."
> ❌ "Our organization is committed to facilitating your success."

---

### 2. [TRAIT 2: e.g., KNOWLEDGEABLE]

**What it means:** [We know our stuff, but we don't show off. We make complex things simple.]

**In Practice:**
- Explain, don't impress
- Use analogies and examples
- Define technical terms when needed
- Back up claims with evidence

**Example:**
> ✅ "Think of it like a filing cabinet for your data—organized and easy to find."
> ❌ "Our proprietary algorithm leverages machine learning paradigms."

---

### 3. [TRAIT 3: e.g., OPTIMISTIC]

**What it means:** [We see possibilities, not problems. We're energized by what could be.]

**In Practice:**
- Focus on solutions
- Use positive language
- Inspire action
- Celebrate wins (big and small)

**Example:**
> ✅ "Let's build something amazing together."
> ❌ "This might work if we're lucky."

---

### 4. [TRAIT 4: e.g., AUTHENTIC]

**What it means:** [We're real. No pretense, no fake enthusiasm, just honest communication.]

**In Practice:**
- Admit what you don't know
- Own mistakes quickly
- Avoid hyperbole (best, amazing, revolutionary)
- Be transparent about limitations

**Example:**
> ✅ "This feature is still in beta, and here's what we're working on..."
> ❌ "This is the most incredible feature ever created!"

## Tone Variations

Our **voice** is consistent. Our **tone** adapts to the situation.

### Marketing & Advertising

**Tone:** Energetic, inspiring, benefit-focused

**Characteristics:**
- Lead with the value
- Create excitement
- Clear call-to-action
- Emphasize transformation

**Example:**
> "Ready to transform the way you work? Join 10,000+ teams who've already made the switch. Start your free trial today."

---

### Customer Support

**Tone:** Empathetic, patient, solution-oriented

**Characteristics:**
- Acknowledge the issue
- Show understanding
- Provide clear steps
- Follow up

**Example:**
> "I understand how frustrating this must be. Let's get this sorted out for you. Here's what we'll do..."

---

### Technical Documentation

**Tone:** Clear, precise, instructional

**Characteristics:**
- Step-by-step format
- No fluff
- Screenshots and examples
- Troubleshooting section

**Example:**
> "To reset your password:
> 1. Go to Settings > Account
> 2. Click 'Reset Password'
> 3. Check your email for the reset link
> 4. Enter your new password"

---

### Social Media

**Tone:** Conversational, engaging, human

**Characteristics:**
- Use emojis (sparingly)
- Ask questions
- Respond to comments
- Share behind-the-scenes

**Example:**
> "Monday motivation: What's one thing you're excited to tackle this week? Drop it in the comments 👇"

---

### Crisis Communication

**Tone:** Serious, transparent, accountable

**Characteristics:**
- Lead with empathy
- Take responsibility
- Explain what happened
- Share the fix
- Prevent recurrence

**Example:**
> "We messed up. Here's what happened, here's what we're doing to fix it, and here's how we'll make sure it doesn't happen again."

## Writing Guidelines

### Grammar & Style

**Use Active Voice**
> ✅ "We built this feature for you."
> ❌ "This feature was built for you."

**Write in Present Tense**
> ✅ "Our platform helps teams collaborate."
> ❌ "Our platform helped teams collaborate."

**Use Contractions**
> ✅ "We're excited to share..."
> ❌ "We are excited to share..."

**Address the Reader as "You"**
> ✅ "You can customize your dashboard."
> ❌ "Users can customize their dashboard."

---

### Word Choice

**Do Use:**
- Simple, everyday words
- Action verbs
- Specific details
- Inclusive language

**Don't Use:**
- Jargon and buzzwords
- Corporate speak
- Vague qualifiers (very, really, quite)
- Superlatives (best, greatest, revolutionary)

---

### Jargon Translator

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| Leverage | Use |
| Synergy | Working together |
| Best-in-class | High-quality |
| Cutting-edge | Modern |
| Robust | Strong, reliable |
| Seamless | Smooth, easy |
| Empower | Enable, help |
| Optimize | Improve |
| Utilize | Use |
| Facilitate | Help, enable |

---

### Punctuation & Formatting

**Oxford Comma:** Use it
> ✅ "We work with designers, developers, and marketers."
> ❌ "We work with designers, developers and marketers."

**Em Dash:** Use for emphasis (with spaces or without, be consistent)
> ✅ "This is important—really important."
> ✅ "This is important — really important."

**Ellipsis:** Use sparingly, for trailing thoughts
> ✅ "But that's just the beginning..."

**Exclamation Points:** Use sparingly (1 per message max)
> ✅ "Great news!"
> ❌ "Great news!!!"

**Capitalization:**
- Headlines: Sentence case
- Product names: As branded
- Features: Sentence case

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Website

**Homepage:**
- Lead with value proposition
- Use benefit-driven headlines
- Include clear CTAs
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences)

**Product Pages:**
- Feature → Benefit structure
- Use bullet points for specs
- Include social proof
- Add FAQ section

**About Page:**
- Tell your story
- Show your team
- Share your values
- Be authentic

**Blog:**
- Write for your audience, not yourself
- Use clear headlines
- Include examples and images
- End with a CTA

---

### Email

**Subject Lines:**
- Keep under 50 characters
- Be specific, not clever
- Avoid spam words (free, guarantee, act now)
- Test variations

**Email Body:**
- Get to the point quickly
- Use short paragraphs
- Include one clear CTA
- Mobile-friendly formatting

**Signatures:**
```
[Name]
[Title]

[Company]
[Phone] | [Website]
```

---

### Social Media

**General Rules:**
- Match the platform's vibe
- Engage, don't just broadcast
- Use visuals (images, video)
- Respond to comments

**Platform Nuances:**

**LinkedIn:** Professional, industry insights, company news

**Twitter/X:** Conversational, timely, concise (280 characters)

**Instagram:** Visual-first, behind-the-scenes, lifestyle

**Facebook:** Community-focused, longer posts, events

**TikTok:** Authentic, entertaining, trending formats

---

### Presentations

**Slide Design:**
- One idea per slide
- Minimal text (6x6 rule: 6 words per line, 6 lines max)
- Use brand colors and fonts
- Include speaker notes

**Delivery:**
- Speak conversationally
- Tell stories
- Engage the audience
- Leave time for Q&A

## Do's and Don'ts

### ✅ DO

- Write like a human, not a corporation
- Put the customer first (use "you" more than "we")
- Be clear over clever
- Own mistakes quickly and transparently
- Use examples and specifics
- Read your writing out loud
- Edit ruthlessly

### ❌ DON'T

- Use jargon or buzzwords
- Make promises you can't keep
- Sound like every other company
- Use ALL CAPS (it's shouting)
- Overuse exclamation points!!!
- Write long, dense paragraphs
- Forget to proofread

## Voice in Action: Examples

### Before & After

**Before (Corporate):**
> "Our organization is dedicated to leveraging cutting-edge technology to facilitate optimal outcomes for our valued clientele."

**After (On-Brand):**
> "We use smart technology to help you get better results."

---

**Before (Vague):**
> "We offer comprehensive solutions for businesses of all sizes."

**After (Specific):**
> "From 5-person startups to 5,000-person enterprises, we've got you covered."

---

**Before (Jargon):**
> "Our platform synergizes cross-functional workflows to maximize productivity."

**After (Clear):**
> "Our platform helps teams work together better and get more done."

## Voice Checklist

Before publishing any content, ask:

- [ ] Does this sound like us?
- [ ] Is it clear and easy to understand?
- [ ] Would a real person say this?
- [ ] Does it respect the reader's intelligence?
- [ ] Is it free of jargon and buzzwords?
- [ ] Does it match the tone for this channel?
- [ ] Have I proofread it?
