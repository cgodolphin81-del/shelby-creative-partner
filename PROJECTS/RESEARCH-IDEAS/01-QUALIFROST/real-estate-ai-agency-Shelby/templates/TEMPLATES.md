## Sales Call Script

```
=============================================================================
                    DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT
                    Duration: 30-45 minutes
=============================================================================

OPENING (2-3 minutes)
---------------------
"Thanks for making time today, {First Name}. I'm excited to learn more 
about {Brokerage Name} and see if we might be a good fit.

Before we dive in, let me make sure I understand - you're looking to 
{improve lead conversion / reduce admin time / etc.}, is that right?

Great. I'll ask some questions about your current setup, then share a 
bit about how we help similar brokerages. Sound good?"

CURRENT STATE (10-15 minutes)
-----------------------------
Lead Generation:
"Tell me about your current lead sources. Where are most of your leads 
coming from?"
→ Follow-up: "How many leads per month would you say that is?"
→ Follow-up: "And what's your current process for following up?"
→ Follow-up: "What's your lead-to-appointment conversion rate?"

Technology Stack:
"What CRM are you currently using?"
→ Follow-up: "How long have you been using it?"
→ Follow-up: "What do you love about it? What frustrates you?"

Team Structure:
"How many agents are on your team?"
→ Follow-up: "Are they all full-time?"
→ Follow-up: "How do you currently assign leads?"

Pain Points:
"What's the biggest challenge you're facing with lead management right 
now?"
→ Follow-up: "How is that impacting your business?"
→ Follow-up: "What have you tried to solve this?"

Goals:
"If we were having this conversation 12 months from now, what would have 
to happen for you to say this was a home run?"

BUDGET & AUTHORITY (5 minutes)
------------------------------
"Have you worked with AI or automation tools before?"
→ Follow-up: "What was that experience like?"

"What kind of investment have you made in technology like this in the 
past?"

"Besides yourself, who else would be involved in evaluating a solution 
like this?"

" What's your decision-making process typically look like?"

TIMELINE:
"When are you hoping to have something in place?"

TRANSITION (2 minutes)
----------------------
"This is really helpful, thank you. Based on what you've shared, I think 
we could potentially help with {specific pain points they mentioned}.

Let me share briefly how we've helped similar brokerages, then I can 
show you the platform and we can talk about next steps. Fair enough?"

VALUE PROPOSITION (5 minutes)
-----------------------------
"We work with brokerages like {theirs} to implement AI-powered lead 
management and valuation tools. Here's what that typically looks like:

[Share 1-2 relevant case studies]

The common thread is: instant lead response, intelligent prioritization, 
and automation of routine tasks. That's what frees up agents to focus on 
what they do best - closing deals.

Does this resonate with what you're looking for?"

NEXT STEPS (3-5 minutes)
------------------------
"I think there could be a good fit here. Here's what I'd suggest:

1. I'll send over a customized proposal based on what we discussed
2. We schedule a demo for you and {other stakeholders}
3. If it looks good, we can discuss timeline and get started

Does that work for you?

What does your calendar look like for a demo? I have {Option 1} or 
{Option 2}."

OBJECTION HANDLING
------------------
"Send me info."
→ "Happy to. But honestly, our stuff is pretty visual - hard to get the 
   impact over email. What if we do a quick 30-minute demo instead? I 
   can show you exactly how it would work for your team."

"We're happy with our current tools."
→ "That's great - most of our clients were too before they switched. 
   What I'd suggest is a quick comparison - 30 minutes, no pressure. If 
   what you have is working better, I'll be the first to tell you to 
   stick with it. Fair enough?"

"It's too expensive."
→ "I understand budget is a consideration. Can I ask - compared to what? 
   The cost of the system, or the cost of not solving this problem? 
   Because {specific cost of inaction based on their pain points}."

"I need to think about it."
→ "Of course. What specifically do you need to think through? Is it 
   about fit, timing, or something else?" [Listen - this tells you the 
   real objection]

=============================================================================
```
