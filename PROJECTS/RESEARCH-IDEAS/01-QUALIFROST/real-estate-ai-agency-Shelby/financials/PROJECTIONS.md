### Lifetime Value (LTV)

**Formula:** `LTV = (Avg MRR × Gross Margin %) ÷ Churn Rate`

| Segment | Avg MRR | Gross Margin | Monthly Churn | Lifetime (months) | LTV |
|---------|---------|--------------|---------------|-------------------|-----|
| SMB | $1,500 | 75% | 4% | 25 | $28,125 |
| Mid-Market | $3,500 | 80% | 2.5% | 40 | $112,000 |
| Enterprise | $8,000 | 85% | 1.5% | 67 | $453,333 |

**Blended LTV (Year 3):** $85,000

---

### LTV:CAC Ratio

| Year | LTV | CAC | Ratio | Assessment |
|------|-----|-----|-------|------------|
| Year 1 | $45,000 | $12,000 | 3.75:1 | Good (early stage) |
| Year 2 | $60,000 | $10,000 | 6:1 | Very Good |
| Year 3 | $85,000 | $8,500 | 10:1 | Excellent |
| Year 4 | $95,000 | $7,500 | 12.7:1 | Excellent |
| Year 5 | $110,000 | $7,000 | 15.7:1 | Outstanding |

**Rule of Thumb:**
- <3:1 = Problem (CAC too high or retention too low)
- 3-5:1 = Good
- 5-10:1 = Very Good
- 10:1+ = Excellent (or under-investing in growth)

## 🎓 Financial Management Best Practices

### Cash Management
- Maintain 12-18 months runway
- Review cash flow weekly
- Negotiate favorable payment terms (annual prepay discounts)
- Monitor DSO (Days Sales Outstanding) - target <45 days

### Pricing Discipline
- Annual price increases (CPI + 3-5%)
- Don't over-discount (protect LTV:CAC)
- Tier pricing to capture value across segments
- Performance pricing for high-confidence deals

### Unit Economics
- Track CAC payback by channel
- Monitor cohort retention
- Optimize for NRR (expansion revenue)
- Fire unprofitable customers (if support costs exceed revenue)
