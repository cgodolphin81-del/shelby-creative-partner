# Client Acquisition Guide

## How to Find and Close Pinterest Marketing Clients

This comprehensive guide covers everything from identifying ideal clients to closing high-ticket deals.

## Ideal Client Profiles

### 1. E-commerce Brands

**Characteristics:**
- Physical products (home decor, fashion, beauty, food, etc.)
- Online store (Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.)
- $10K-100K+ monthly revenue
- Already investing in marketing
- Visual products that photograph well

**Pain Points:**
- Struggling with Facebook/Instagram ad costs
- Low organic reach on social media
- Need consistent traffic source
- Want to diversify marketing channels
- Seasonal sales fluctuations

**Why Pinterest:**
- Users have high purchase intent
- Pins have long lifespan
- Lower CPC than Facebook/Instagram
- Perfect for visual products
- Drives qualified traffic

**Where to Find Them:**
- Shopify store directories
- Instagram shops
- Etsy sellers scaling up
- Facebook ad library (see who's advertising)
- Product Hunt launches
- Local business directories

## Where to Find Clients

### Online Strategies

**1. LinkedIn Outreach**

**Search Strategy:**
- Search: "E-commerce owner" + [niche]
- Search: "Founder" + "Shopify"
- Search: "Marketing Director" + [industry]
- Filter by company size (10-50 employees ideal)

**Connection Request Template:**
```
Hi [Name], I noticed [Company] has beautiful products 
perfect for Pinterest. I help e-commerce brands like 
yours drive 30-50% of traffic from Pinterest with lower 
CPCs than Facebook. Would love to share some ideas if 
you're open to it. No pitch, just insights. Cheers!
```

**Follow-Up Message (after connection):**
```
Thanks for connecting, [Name]! I took a look at [Company]'s 
Pinterest and see some huge opportunities. A few quick ideas:

1. Your products would perform great with [specific pin style]
2. [Competitor] is getting 100K+ monthly views with similar products
3. Pinterest users are actively searching for [their product category]

I specialize in Pinterest marketing for e-commerce brands. 
If you're curious about what's possible, I'd be happy to do 
a free Pinterest audit and share a custom strategy. 

Either way, keep up the great work with [specific compliment]!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## The Sales Process

### Step 1: Discovery Call (15-30 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their business, goals, and challenges

**Questions to Ask:**
1. Tell me about your business and target customer
2. What's your current marketing strategy?
3. Have you used Pinterest before? What was your experience?
4. What are your main goals for the next 6-12 months?
5. What's your biggest marketing challenge right now?
6. What's your monthly marketing budget?
7. What would success look like for you on Pinterest?
8. What's your timeline for getting started?

**Listen For:**
- Pain points you can solve
- Budget alignment with your pricing
- Decision-making authority
- Timeline urgency
- Cultural fit

---

### Step 2: Pinterest Audit (Free Value)

**What to Include:**
1. Current Pinterest account analysis (or opportunity if no account)
2. Competitor analysis (2-3 competitors)
3. Keyword opportunities
4. Content gaps
5. Quick wins (3-5 immediate actions)
6. 90-day strategy overview
7. Projected results (traffic, followers, sales)

**Audit Format:**
- 10-15 slide deck (Google Slides or Canva)
- 15-20 minute video walkthrough (Loom)
- Or live presentation on follow-up call

**Audit Template Structure:**
```
Slide 1: Introduction & Overview
Slide 2: Current Pinterest Performance (or Opportunity)
Slide 3: Competitor Analysis
Slide 4: Keyword Research Summary
Slide 5: Content Gap Analysis
Slide 6-8: Quick Wins (3 slides)
Slide 9: 90-Day Strategy
Slide 10: Projected Results
Slide 11: Investment & Next Steps
Slide 12: Q&A / Thank You
```

---

### Step 3: Proposal & Pricing

**Send Within 24 Hours of Audit Call**

**Proposal Structure:**
1. Executive Summary
2. Current Situation Analysis
3. Proposed Strategy
4. Scope of Work (detailed)
5. Timeline & Milestones
6. Investment (pricing)
7. Case Studies / Testimonials
8. Terms & Conditions
9. Next Steps / Call to Action

**Pricing Presentation:**
- Present 2-3 package options
- Anchor with highest package first
- Show ROI projection
- Include payment terms
- Add urgency (limited spots, seasonal timing)

---

### Step 4: Closing

**Common Objections & Responses:**

**"It's too expensive"**
```
I understand budget is a consideration. Let's look at the ROI:
If we drive 20K additional visitors/month and convert at 2%, 
that's 400 new customers. At your average order value of $75, 
that's $30K in monthly revenue. Our fee is [X], so you're 
looking at [Y] return on investment.

Does that math make sense? If budget is the only concern, 
we could start with a smaller package and scale up.
```

**"I need to think about it"**
```
Of course! This is an important decision. What specifically do 
you need to think through? Is there something I haven't addressed 
or a concern I can help with?

[Listen to their concern]

I'd be happy to [address concern]. Also, I want to mention that 
I have [X] other clients starting next month, so I only have 
[X] spots available. If you're ready to move forward, I can 
reserve your spot with a 50% deposit.

What questions can I answer to help you decide?
```

**"We've tried Pinterest before and it didn't work"**
```
I hear that often. Can you tell me more about what you tried and 
what results you saw?

[Listen - usually they posted inconsistently, didn't optimize for 
SEO, or gave up too soon]

That makes sense. The Pinterest landscape has changed a lot, and 
most brands make these common mistakes: [list mistakes]. 

Here's how we do it differently: [explain your unique approach]. 
My clients typically see [specific results] within [timeframe]. 

Would you be open to a 90-day trial to see the difference?
```

**"Can you guarantee results?"**
```
I can't guarantee specific numbers because Pinterest, like any 
marketing channel, has variables. But I can guarantee:

✅ Daily pinning and optimization
✅ SEO best practices on every pin
✅ Transparent reporting every week
✅ Strategy adjustments based on data
✅ My full expertise and effort

Based on my work with [X] clients, here's what they typically 
see: [share case study results].

I'm confident we can get you similar results, and I'm 
committed to your success. Let's start with a 90-day 
commitment and review progress monthly.
```

## Outreach Metrics & Goals

### Weekly Activity Goals

**Solo Agency Owner:**
- 20-30 LinkedIn connection requests
- 15-20 Instagram DMs
- 10-15 cold emails
- 5-10 Facebook group interactions
- 2-3 partnership conversations
- 1-2 networking events (monthly average)

**Expected Conversion Rates:**
- Connection/Acceptance Rate: 30-50%
- Response Rate: 10-20%
- Discovery Call Booking: 3-5%
- Close Rate: 20-30% of calls

**Math Example:**
```
100 outreach attempts/week
→ 40 connections (40%)
→ 8 responses (20% of connections)
→ 4 discovery calls (50% of responses)
→ 1 new client (25% close rate)

At $3,000/month average = $3,000/week = $12,000/month
```

### Monthly Goals

**Month 1-3 (Building):**
- 2-3 clients
- $3,000-9,000 MRR
- Refine pitch and process
- Build case studies

**Month 4-6 (Growth):**
- 5-8 clients
- $10,000-25,000 MRR
- Hire first team member (VA or designer)
- Systematize processes

**Month 7-12 (Scale):**
- 10-15 clients
- $30,000-75,000 MRR
- Build full team
- Consider raising prices

## Follow-Up System

### The Fortune Is in the Follow-Up

**Most sales happen after 5-12 contacts, but most people give up after 2!**

### Follow-Up Sequence

**Day 0:** Initial outreach
**Day 2:** Follow-up #1 (gentle reminder)
**Day 5:** Follow-up #2 (add value - article, tip, insight)
**Day 10:** Follow-up #3 (case study or social proof)
**Day 15:** Follow-up #4 (urgency - limited spots)
**Day 25:** Follow-up #5 (breakup email)
**Day 45:** Re-engagement (new case study or offer)
**Day 90:** Final check-in

### Follow-Up Email Templates

**Follow-Up #1 (Day 2):**
```
Subject: Re: Quick Pinterest idea for [Company]

Hi [Name],

Just wanted to make sure you saw my email about Pinterest 
opportunities for [Company]. 

I'd love to share a few insights specific to your brand. Do 
you have 15 minutes this week for a quick chat?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Follow-Up #2 (Day 5) - Add Value:**
```
Subject: Pinterest tip for [Company]

Hi [Name],

I came across this article about [relevant Pinterest trend] 
and thought of [Company]: [link]

This is exactly what I was mentioning - Pinterest users are 
actively searching for [their product category] right now.

Still happy to do a free audit if you're curious about what's 
possible. Let me know!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Follow-Up #3 (Day 10) - Social Proof:**
```
Subject: Quick case study

Hi [Name],

I just helped [Similar Company] achieve [specific result] on 
Pinterest in 90 days. Here's what we did: [brief summary]

I think [Company] could see similar results. Still open to 
chatting if you're interested!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Follow-Up #4 (Day 15) - Urgency:**
```
Subject: Last spot for [Month]

Hi [Name],

I'm finalizing my client roster for [next month] and have one 
spot left.

If you're still curious about Pinterest for [Company], now 
would be a great time to start. I'd love to help you get set 
up before [upcoming season/holiday].

Interested in a quick call this week?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Follow-Up #5 (Day 25) - Breakup:**
```

I've reached out a few times about Pinterest opportunities for 
[Company], but haven't heard back.

I'm assuming now's not the right time, which is totally fine! 
I'll close your file so I don't keep bothering you.

If Pinterest becomes a priority down the road, feel free to 
reach out. I'd love to help when the timing is better.

Wishing you all the best!

[Your Name]
```

**Re-engagement (Day 45):**
```

Pinterest just launched [new feature] that's perfect for
```
