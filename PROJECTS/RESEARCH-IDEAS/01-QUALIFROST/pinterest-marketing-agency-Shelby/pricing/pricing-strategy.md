# Pricing Strategy Guide

## How to Price Your Pinterest Marketing Services for Maximum Profit

Pricing is one of the most critical decisions for your agency. This guide covers pricing models, packages, and strategies to maximize revenue while delivering value.

---

## Pricing Philosophy

### Value-Based Pricing > Hourly Pricing

**Why Not Hourly:**
- Punishes efficiency (faster work = less pay)
- Clients focus on time, not results
- Limits income potential
- Hard to scale

**Value-Based Advantages:**
- Clients focus on ROI, not hours
- Rewards expertise and efficiency
- Scalable income
- Aligns with client success

**Example:**
```
Hourly: 20 hours × $75/hour = $1,500
Value: Client makes $30,000 from Pinterest = $5,000 fee

Same work, 3.3x more revenue.
```

### Price for Transformation, Not Tasks

Clients don't buy "pin design" or "scheduling" - they buy:
- Increased traffic
- More sales
- Brand visibility
- Time freedom
- Peace of mind

**Frame Your Pricing Around:**
- Revenue increase they'll achieve
- Time they'll save
- Stress they'll avoid
- Opportunities they'll gain

## Pricing Models

### Model 1: Monthly Retainer (Recommended)

**Structure:** Fixed monthly fee for ongoing services

**Pros:**
- Predictable, recurring revenue
- Long-term client relationships
- Easier to deliver results over time
- Better work-life balance

**Cons:**
- Client commitment required
- Need to deliver consistent value
- Can feel "stale" if not careful

**Best For:**
- Most Pinterest marketing services
- Ongoing management
- Content creation and scheduling
- Ads management

**Typical Range:** $1,500-10,000/month

---

### Model 2: Project-Based

**Structure:** One-time fee for specific project

**Examples:**
- Pinterest account setup: $1,500-3,000
- Board optimization: $750-1,500
- Pin design package (20 pins): $1,000-2,000
- SEO audit: $500-1,000
- Training workshop: $2,500-5,000

**Pros:**
- Clear scope and deliverables
- Good for clients not ready for retainer
- Quick cash infusion
- Lower commitment for clients

**Cons:**
- One-time revenue (not recurring)
- Constant need for new clients
- Limited relationship building
- Income unpredictability

**Best For:**
- Entry-level clients
- Specific, bounded projects
- Consulting and training
- Audit services

---

### Model 3: Performance-Based

**Structure:** Base fee + percentage of results

**Examples:**
- Base $2,000 + 10% of Pinterest-driven revenue
- Base $1,500 + $50 per 1,000 visitors
- Base $3,000 + 5% of ad spend savings
- Pure performance: 15-20% of revenue generated

**Pros:**
- Aligned incentives (you win when they win)
- Unlimited earning potential
- Easy sell to skeptical clients
- Builds trust

**Cons:**
- Revenue attribution can be tricky
- Longer time to see results
- Risk if Pinterest doesn't perform
- Requires robust tracking

**Best For:**
- Confident in your abilities
- E-commerce with clear tracking
- Clients with established Pinterest presence
- Performance marketers

---

### Model 4: Hybrid Model (Best of All Worlds)

**Structure:** Base retainer + performance bonuses + project fees

**Example:**
```
Base Retainer: $3,000/month
Includes: Pin design, scheduling, optimization

Performance Bonus: 
- $500/month if traffic exceeds 20K visitors
- $500/month if ROAS exceeds 4:1
- $1,000 bonus if follower goal exceeded

Project Fees:
- Video pin creation: $500/video
- Seasonal campaign: $750/campaign
- Additional strategy calls: $150/30 min
```

**Pros:**
- Predictable base revenue
- Upside potential
- Flexible for clients
- Rewards exceptional results

**Cons:**
- More complex to track and explain
- Need clear metrics and tracking
- Can create misaligned expectations

**Best For:**
- Established agencies
- Clients focused on ROI
- Long-term partnerships
- Full-service management

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring Strategy

**Present packages in this order:**
1. Enterprise ($15,000/month) - Anchor
2. Scale ($8,500/month) - Target
3. Growth ($4,000/month) - Alternative
4. Starter ($2,000/month) - Entry

**Why It Works:**
- Enterprise makes Scale look reasonable
- Scale becomes the "smart choice"
- Growth is the "budget option"
- Starter is for "just testing"

Most clients will choose Scale or Growth (your sweet spot).

---

### Price Ending Psychology

**Avoid:** $1,500, $3,000, $5,000 (too round, feels arbitrary)

**Use:** $1,500, $2,997, $4,997, $7,997

**Why:**
- Specific numbers feel calculated and justified
- Ending in 7 is proven to convert better
- Avoids "round number resistance"

---

### Payment Terms

**Standard Terms:**
- Monthly retainer: Due on 1st of month
- Projects: 50% deposit, 50% on completion
- Net-7 or Net-15 for established clients

**Discounts for Prepayment:**
- Quarterly (3 months): 10% discount
- Semi-annual (6 months): 15% discount
- Annual (12 months): 20% discount

**Example:**
```
Growth Package: $4,000/month
Monthly: $4,000 × 12 = $48,000/year
Quarterly: $3,600 × 12 = $43,200/year (save $4,800)
Annual: $3,200 × 12 = $38,400/year (save $9,600)
```

**Benefits:**
- Improves cash flow
- Reduces churn
- Commits clients longer
- Rewards commitment

---

### Price Increases

**When to Raise Prices:**
- Consistently booked (3+ months waitlist)
- Delivering exceptional results
- Added significant value/services
- Market rates have increased
- Inflation/cost increases
- 12+ months since last increase

**How Much:**
- Annual increase: 5-10%
- Major value add: 15-25%
- Repositioning: 30-50%

**How to Communicate:**
```
Subject: Update to Our Pricing

Hi [Client Name],

I'm writing to let you know about an upcoming update to our 
pricing structure, effective [date - give 60 days notice].

Since we started working together [X months/years] ago, we've:
- [Achievement 1]
- [Achievement 2]
- [Achievement 3]

We've also expanded our services to include [new services] 
and invested in [tools/training/team] to serve you better.

To continue delivering the exceptional results you expect, 
our [Package Name] will be adjusting from $[X] to $[Y] 
per month, effective [date].

Your current rate is locked in until [date] as a thank you 
for being a valued client.

I'm confident you'll agree the value you're receiving far 
exceeds this investment. If you have any questions or concerns, 
I'd love to hop on a call to discuss.

Thank you for your continued trust and partnership!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Grandfather Existing Clients:**
- Give 60-90 days notice
- Offer to lock in current rate for 6-12 more months
- Or offer upgrade to new services at current price

## Common Pricing Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake 1: Pricing Too Low

**Why It Happens:**
- Imposter syndrome
- Fear of rejection
- Underestimating value
- Comparing to freelancers

**The Problem:**
- Attracts difficult clients
- Leaves money on table
- Hard to raise prices later
- Burnout from overwork

**Solution:**
- Calculate your value delivered
- Research market rates
- Start higher than comfortable
- Remember: price signals quality

---

### ❌ Mistake 2: No Package Tiers

**Why It Happens:**
- Simplicity
- Don't want to confuse clients
- One-size-fits-all approach

**The Problem:**
- Leaves money on table
- Doesn't serve different budgets
- No upsell path
- Clients self-select out

**Solution:**
- Offer 3-4 tiers
- Make middle tier most attractive
- Clear differentiation
- Easy upgrade path

---

### ❌ Mistake 3: Hourly Pricing

**Why It Happens:**
- Easy to calculate
- Feels fair
- Common freelancer model

**The Problem:**
- Punishes efficiency
- Limits income
- Wrong incentives
- Clients watch the clock

**Solution:**
- Price by value/outcome
- Package your services
- Focus on ROI
- Track time internally only

---

### ❌ Mistake 4: Not Raising Prices

**Why It Happens:**
- Fear of losing clients
- Comfortable with current income
- Don't feel "ready"
- Clients might complain

**The Problem:**
- Income stagnation
- Inflation erosion
- Attracts wrong clients
- Resentment builds

**Solution:**
- Raise prices every 6-12 months
- Grandfather existing clients
- Communicate value clearly
- Fire low-paying clients gradually

---

### ❌ Mistake 5: Discounting Too Easily

**Why It Happens:**
- Want to close the deal
- Feel bad about price
- Client asks for discount
- Desperate for work

**The Problem:**
- Devalues your service
- Attracts price shoppers
- Reduces profit margins
- Sets bad precedent

**Solution:**
- Stand firm on pricing
- Offer payment plans instead
- Remove services to lower price
- Emphasize value and ROI

**If Client Asks for Discount:**
```
"I understand budget is a consideration. Rather than 
discounting, let's look at adjusting the scope to fit 
your budget. We could [remove X service] or [start with 
a smaller package] and scale up once you see results.

What matters most to you: [priority 1] or [priority 2]?
```

## Quick Pricing Calculator

**Calculate Your Minimum Viable Price:**

```
Monthly Expenses (business + personal): $_____
Desired Profit/Savings: $_____
Taxes (25-30% of revenue): $_____
Client Capacity (per month): _____ clients

Minimum Price Per Client = 
(Expenses + Profit + Taxes) ÷ Clients

Example:
Expenses: $5,000
Profit Goal: $5,000
Taxes: $3,333 (30% of $10K revenue)
Clients: 5

($5,000 + $5,000 + $3,333) ÷ 5 = $2,667/client minimum

Round up to: $3,000/month per client
```

**Calculate Your Ideal Price:**

```
Value Delivered to Client: $_____
Your Percentage of Value (10-20%): _____%
Ideal Price = Value × Percentage

Example:
Client makes $50,000/month from Pinterest
Your fee at 10% = $5,000/month

This is VALUE-BASED PRICING
```
