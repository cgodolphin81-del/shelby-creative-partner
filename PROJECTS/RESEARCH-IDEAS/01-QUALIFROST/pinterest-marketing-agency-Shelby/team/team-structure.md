# Team Structure & Hiring Guide

## Building Your Pinterest Marketing Agency Team

This guide covers when to hire, what roles to fill, how to find talent, and how to structure your team for maximum efficiency and profitability.

---

## When to Hire

### Signs You're Ready

✅ **Consistently at capacity** - Working 50+ hours/week
✅ **Turning down clients** - More leads than you can handle
✅ **Consistent revenue** - 3+ months of stable income
✅ **Systems documented** - Processes can be taught
✅ **Cash reserves** - 3 months of operating expenses saved
✅ **Confident in sales** - Can consistently close new clients

### Hiring Triggers by Revenue

| Monthly Revenue | Team Size | First Hires |
|----------------|-----------|-------------|
| $5K-10K | Solo | Virtual Assistant (part-time) |
| $10K-20K | 2-3 people | Designer, Account Manager |
| $20K-40K | 4-6 people | Ads Specialist, Full-time VA |
| $40K-75K | 7-10 people | Operations Manager, Second Designer |
| $75K-150K | 10-20 people | Department Heads, Sales Team |
| $150K+ | 20+ people | Executive Team, Specialized Roles |

## Key Roles & Responsibilities

### 1. Virtual Assistant (VA)

**When to Hire:** Month 2-4, or when spending 10+ hours/week on admin

**Responsibilities:**
- Email management and responses
- Scheduling and calendar management
- Data entry and organization
- Basic Pinterest tasks (scheduling pins, board organization)
- Client onboarding coordination
- Social media posting (non-strategic)
- Research tasks
- Travel booking

**Skills Required:**
- Excellent English communication
- Organized and detail-oriented
- Tech-savvy (Google Suite, Canva basics)
- Self-motivated and proactive
- Time management skills

**Time Commitment:** 10-40 hours/week

**Compensation:**
- **Philippines/Eastern Europe:** $5-10/hour
- **Latin America:** $8-15/hour
- **US/Canada:** $18-30/hour

**Where to Find:**
- Upwork
- OnlineJobs.ph (Philippines)
- Virtual Assistant Facebook groups
- Belay Solutions
- Time Etc

**Interview Questions:**
1. What's your experience with Pinterest or social media management?
2. Describe a time you had to manage multiple priorities. How did you handle it?
3. What tools are you proficient in? (Google Suite, Canva, etc.)
4. What are your working hours and availability?
5. Give an example of when you went above and beyond for a client/employer.

- **Full-time (International):** $18,000-35,000/year

## Hiring Process

### Step 1: Define the Role

**Create a Detailed Job Description:**
- Role title and summary
- Key responsibilities (8-12 bullet points)
- Required skills and qualifications
- Nice-to-have skills
- Compensation range
- Working hours and location
- Benefits (if any)
- Company culture and values
- Application instructions

**Example Job Post:**

```
POSITION: Pinterest Designer (Part-Time, Remote)

ABOUT US:
We're a growing Pinterest marketing agency helping e-commerce 
brands drive traffic and sales through Pinterest. We work with 
10+ amazing clients in home decor, fashion, and lifestyle niches.

ROLE:
We're looking for a talented Pinterest Designer to create 
scroll-stopping pin designs for our clients. You'll work with 
our strategy team to bring content to life with beautiful, 
on-brand designs.

RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Design 20-30 custom pins per week in Canva
- Create multiple variations for A/B testing
- Maintain brand consistency across all designs
- Design Idea Pin graphics and simple videos
- Update and improve pin templates
- Collaborate with strategy team on creative direction
- Meet deadlines consistently

REQUIREMENTS:
- 2+ years of Canva design experience
- Portfolio showing Pinterest or social media designs
- Understanding of Pinterest best practices (2:3 ratio, etc.)
- Eye for clean, modern design
- Ability to take feedback and iterate
- Excellent time management
- Reliable internet and computer

NICE TO HAVE:
- Experience with Pinterest marketing
- Video editing skills (CapCut, Premiere Rush)
- Experience in e-commerce or lifestyle niches

COMPENSATION:
- $25-40/hour depending on experience
- 20-30 hours/week to start
- Flexible schedule
- Potential for full-time

TO APPLY:
Send your resume, portfolio, and answer these questions:
1. What's your favorite Pinterest pin design you've created?
2. How do you approach designing for a new brand?
3. What's your typical turnaround time for 10 pins?

Applications without portfolio will not be considered.
```

### Step 3: Screen Applicants

**Resume Review Criteria:**
- Relevant experience (2+ years preferred)
- Portfolio quality
- Career progression
- Stability (not job-hopping)
- Communication skills (no typos!)

**Initial Screening Call (15-20 minutes):**
- Confirm basic qualifications
- Assess communication skills
- Discuss availability and expectations
- Explain role and company
- Gauge enthusiasm and fit
- Answer their questions

**Red Flags:**
- Late to call or no-show
- Poor communication
- Vague about experience
- Only interested in money
- No questions about the role
- Bad-mouthing previous employers

---

### Step 4: Skills Assessment

**For Designers:**
- Paid test project (3-5 pin designs)
- Provide brand guidelines and content
- 48-72 hour turnaround
- Evaluate: quality, creativity, adherence to brief, speed

**For Account Managers:**
- Case study analysis (give them an account to audit)
- Strategy presentation (30 minutes)
- Mock client call (role-play)
- Evaluate: strategic thinking, communication, Pinterest knowledge

**For Ads Specialists:**
- Campaign audit (review existing campaigns)
- Strategy document (how they'd structure campaigns)
- Past results (ask for case studies)
- Evaluate: expertise, analytical skills, results-driven

**For VAs:**
- Test tasks (scheduling, research, email drafting)
- Attention to detail test
- Time management scenario
- Evaluate: accuracy, initiative, communication

**Always Pay for Test Work:**
- Shows you value their time
- Attracts serious candidates
- Filters out tire-kickers
- Builds goodwill

---

### Step 5: Final Interview

**Panel Interview (45-60 minutes):**
- You (founder)
- Relevant team member (if applicable)
- Deep dive into experience
- Cultural fit assessment
- Scenario-based questions
- Discuss compensation and start date

**Questions to Ask:**
1. Tell me about a time you [relevant scenario].
2. What's your greatest strength? Weakness?
3. How do you handle feedback or criticism?
4. Describe your ideal work environment.
5. Where do you see yourself in 2 years?
6. Why do you want to work here specifically?
7. What questions do you have for us?

**Reference Checks:**
- Contact 2-3 previous employers/clients
- Ask about: work quality, reliability, communication, strengths, areas for improvement
- "Would you rehire this person?" (most important question)

---

### Step 6: Make an Offer

**Offer Letter Should Include:**
- Position title
- Start date
- Compensation (salary/hourly, payment schedule)
- Benefits (if any)
- Working hours and location
- Reporting structure
- At-will employment statement (US)
- Contingencies (background check, etc.)
- Deadline to accept offer

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Know your max before the conversation
- Be flexible on non-salary items (hours, benefits, equipment)
- Explain the full value package (flexibility, growth, culture)
- Don't lowball - good talent knows their worth
- Get verbal agreement, then send written offer

## Onboarding New Hires

### Week 1: Orientation & Training

**Day 1-2: Company Orientation**
- Welcome call with founder
- Company mission, values, culture
- Team introductions
- Tool access setup (email, Slack, project management)
- Review employee handbook

**Day 3-5: Role-Specific Training**
- Shadow team members
- Review processes and SOPs
- Tool training (Canva, Tailwind, etc.)
- First small assignments
- Daily check-ins

### Week 2-3: Guided Practice

- Work on real client accounts (with supervision)
- Daily feedback sessions
- Gradual increase in responsibility
- Weekly 1:1 with manager
- Complete training modules

### Week 4: Independence

- Handle own workload
- Weekly check-in (instead of daily)
- First monthly goals set
- 30-day review meeting

### 30-60-90 Day Plan

**30 Days:**
- Complete all training
- Handle basic tasks independently
- Understand company processes
- Build relationships with team

**60 Days:**
- Full productivity in core responsibilities
- Take on more complex tasks
- Provide input on improvements
- 60-day performance review

**90 Days:**
- Fully integrated team member
- Consistent quality and output
- Identify growth opportunities
- 90-day review and goal setting

### Meeting Cadence

**Daily Standup (15 minutes):**
- What did you do yesterday?
- What will you do today?
- Any blockers or challenges?
- Keep it quick and focused

**Weekly Team Meeting (60 minutes):**
- Company updates and wins
- Client highlights
- Team challenges and solutions
- Upcoming priorities
- Team bonding

**Monthly All-Hands (90 minutes):**
- Business performance review
- Strategic updates
- Team recognitions
- Q&A with leadership
- Culture building

**Quarterly Planning (Half-day):**
- Review past quarter
- Set next quarter goals
- Strategic planning
- Team building activity

---

### Performance Management

**Regular 1:1s:**
- Weekly or bi-weekly
- 30-45 minutes
- Discuss: wins, challenges, goals, feedback
- Not just status updates - development focused

**Performance Reviews:**
- Quarterly or semi-annually
- Review goals and achievements
- Discuss growth and development
- Compensation adjustments
- Career path planning

**KPIs by Role:**

**Designer:**
- Pins created per week
- Revision rate (<20%)
- On-time delivery (95%+)
- Client satisfaction scores

**Account Manager:**
- Client retention rate (90%+)
- Client satisfaction scores
- Results delivered (traffic, ROAS)
- Response time (<24 hours)

**Ads Specialist:**
- ROAS achieved vs. goals
- Ad spend managed
- Test velocity (tests per month)
- Client retention

**VA:**
- Task completion rate
- Accuracy rate
- Response time
- Initiative score

## When to Use Contractors vs. Employees

### Contractors (1099 in US)

**Best For:**
- Specialized skills (design, ads, copywriting)
- Project-based work
- Flexible hours needed
- Testing a role before hiring full-time
- Cost savings (no benefits, payroll taxes)

**Pros:**
- Lower cost (no benefits, payroll taxes)
- Flexibility (hire/fire as needed)
- Less administrative burden
- Access to global talent

**Cons:**
- Less control over schedule
- May have other clients
- Less loyalty/commitment
- Can't require specific hours

**Legal Note:** Ensure compliance with contractor classification laws (ABC test in some states)

---

### Employees (W-2 in US)

**Best For:**
- Core team members
- Long-term roles
- Need control over schedule
- Client-facing roles
- Roles requiring deep company knowledge

**Pros:**
- More control and commitment
- Exclusive focus on your business
- Better cultural fit
- Easier to train and develop

**Cons:**
- Higher cost (benefits, payroll taxes = +20-30%)
- More administrative work
- Harder to terminate if needed
- Legal requirements (labor laws, etc.)

## Building Company Culture (Remote)

### Challenges of Remote Culture
- Lack of face-to-face interaction
- Time zone differences
- Communication barriers
- Feeling isolated
- Harder to build relationships

### Solutions

**Regular Video Interaction:**
- Camera-on meetings when possible
- Virtual coffee chats (random pairing)
- Video messages (Loom) for updates

**Team Bonding Activities:**
- Virtual game nights
- Online escape rooms
- Trivia competitions
- Show-and-tell sessions
- Book club or learning group

**Communication Norms:**
- Response time expectations
- Slack etiquette guidelines
- Meeting-free days
- Respect for time zones
- Async-first culture

**Recognition:**
- Public shout-outs in Slack
- Monthly awards
- Performance bonuses
- Anniversary celebrations
- Client win celebrations

**In-Person Gatherings:**
- Annual team retreat
- Quarterly meetups (regional)
- Conference attendance together
- Local coworking days

## Scaling Your Team

### Phase 1: Solo (Months 1-6)
**You do:** Strategy, sales, design, account management, admin
**Support:** Freelance designer, part-time VA
**Goal:** Get to $15K-20K/month

### Phase 2: Small Team (Months 6-18)
**You do:** Strategy, sales, key client relationships
**Hire:** Account Manager, Designer, VA (all full-time)
**Goal:** Get to $40K-60K/month

### Phase 3: Department Structure (Months 18-36)
**You do:** Vision, key relationships, strategy
**Hire:** Operations Manager, Ads Specialist, Sales Manager, 2nd Designer
**Goal:** Get to $100K-150K/month

### Phase 4: Scaled Agency (Year 3+)
**You do:** CEO role (vision, culture, major decisions)
**Hire:** Department heads, specialized roles, expanded team
**Goal:** Get to $250K-500K+/month

## Key Takeaways

1. **Hire slowly, fire quickly** - Take time to find right fit, but act fast if not working
2. **Document before delegating** - Create SOPs before hiring
3. **Invest in training** - Good onboarding pays dividends
4. **Pay fairly** - Underpaying leads to high turnover
5. **Culture matters** - Even remote teams need connection
6. **Start with contractors** - Test roles before full-time hires
7. **Promote from within** - Reward loyalty and growth
8. **Measure performance** - Clear KPIs and regular feedback

---

**Your team is your greatest asset. Invest in finding, developing, and retaining great people, and they'll help you build the agency of your dreams.**

*Remember: A small, high-performing team beats a large, mediocre one every time. Quality over quantity.*
