## Fulfillment Options Overview

### Option 1: Self-Fulfillment

**What It Is:** You store, pack, and ship products yourself

**Best For:**
- Starting out (<50 orders/month)
- Custom/handmade products
- Tight quality control needs
- Limited budget

**Pros:**
- Complete control over quality
- No minimum order requirements
- Lower cost at very low volume
- Personal touch (handwritten notes)

**Cons:**
- Time-intensive
- Hard to scale
- Limited shipping discounts
- Storage space needed
- You handle returns

**Cost Breakdown (per order):**
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Packaging materials | $0.50-2.00 |
| Shipping label | $5-15 |
| Labor (your time) | $3-5 |
| **Total** | **$8.50-22.00** |

---

### Option 2: Dropshipping

**What It Is:** Supplier ships directly to customer

**Best For:**
- Testing new products
- No upfront inventory investment
- Wide product variety
- Location independence

**Pros:**
- No inventory risk
- Low startup cost
- Easy to test products
- Scalable product range

**Cons:**
- Lower margins (20-30% vs 50-70%)
- Less quality control
- Longer shipping times
- Supplier errors affect you
- Harder to brand experience

**Popular Dropshipping Suppliers:**
| Supplier | Best For | Shipping Time |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| **CJ Dropshipping** | General products | 7-15 days |
| **Spocket** | US/EU suppliers | 2-7 days |
| **AliExpress** | Lowest cost | 15-45 days |
| **SaleHoo** | Vetted suppliers | Varies |
| **Zendrop** | Fast shipping | 7-12 days |

---

### Option 3: Third-Party Logistics (3PL)

**What It Is:** Professional fulfillment company stores and ships for you

**Best For:**
- 50+ orders/month
- Scaling businesses
- Wanting to focus on marketing
- Multiple sales channels

**Pros:**
- Scalable infrastructure
- Faster shipping (2-5 days)
- Better shipping rates
- Professional handling
- Multi-channel fulfillment
- Time freed for growth

**Cons:**
- Monthly fees
- Minimum order requirements
- Less hands-on control
- Integration setup required
- Returns still need management

**Cost Breakdown (per order):**
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Receiving (per unit) | $0.50-1.50 |
| Storage (per month) | $0.50-2.00/sq ft |
| Pick & pack | $2.50-5.00 |
| Packaging | $0.50-2.00 |
| Shipping | $5-12 |
| **Total** | **$11-22.50** |

### 3PL Onboarding Process

**Timeline: 2-4 weeks**

**Week 1: Setup**
- Sign contract
- Set up account
- Integrate e-commerce platform
- Configure shipping rules
- Set up packaging preferences

**Week 2: Inventory Send**
- Receive shipping labels from 3PL
- Pack and label inventory
- Ship inventory to 3PL
- Provide inventory manifest

**Week 3: Receiving**
- 3PL receives and counts inventory
- Quality check
- Inventory entered into system
- Photos of any damage

**Week 4: Testing**
- Place test orders
- Verify fulfillment accuracy
- Check shipping times
- Confirm tracking uploads
- Go live!

### Free Shipping Strategy

**Why Offer Free Shipping:**
- 73% of shoppers say free shipping influences purchase
- Increases average order value
- Reduces cart abandonment
- Competitive necessity

**How to Offer Profitably:**

**Option 1: Minimum Order Threshold**
```
Free shipping on orders $75+
Average order value: $55
Result: Customers add $20+ to qualify
```

**Option 2: Build Into Product Price**
```
Product price: $50 + $8 shipping = $58
New price: $58 with "free shipping"
Psychology: Customers prefer free shipping
```

**Option 3: Membership/Subscription**
```
"$99/year for unlimited free shipping"
Locks in repeat purchases
Works for consumables/frequent buyers
```

**Option 4: Promotional**
```
"Free shipping this weekend only!"
Creates urgency
Use for slow periods
```

### Shipping Rate Calculator

**Formula:**
```
Shipping Rate = Carrier Cost + Packaging + Handling

Example:
Carrier cost: $7.50
Packaging: $1.00
Handling: $1.50
Total cost: $10.00
Charge customer: $9.99 (absorb $0.01)
```

**Best Practice:** Charge 80-90% of actual cost, absorb rest

## Returns Management

### Returns Statistics

| Statistic | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Average return rate (e-commerce) | 20-30% |
| Return rate (clothing) | 30-40% |
| Return rate (electronics) | 10-15% |
| Customers who return and repurchase | 92% |
| Cost of processing return | $10-20 |

### Returns Policy Best Practices

**What to Include:**

**Time Window:**
- Standard: 30 days
- Generous: 60-90 days
- Holiday extended: Until Jan 31

**Condition Requirements:**
- Unused/unworn
- Original packaging
- Tags attached
- All accessories included

**Refund Method:**
- Original payment method (best)
- Store credit (faster, keeps money in business)
- Exchange (no refund processed)

**Who Pays Shipping:**
- Free returns (best customer experience)
- Customer pays (saves money, may reduce conversions)
- Free for defects, customer pays otherwise

**Processing Time:**
- State clearly (5-10 business days)
- Send confirmation when received
- Notify when refund issued

### Sample Returns Policy

```
RETURNS & EXCHANGES

We want you to love your purchase! If you're not 
completely satisfied, here's how to return it:

TIMEFRAME: 30 days from delivery date

CONDITION: Items must be unused, unworn, and in 
original packaging with tags attached.

HOW TO RETURN:
1. Log into your account and click "Returns"
2. Select the item(s) you want to return
3. Print your prepaid return label
4. Pack securely and drop off at any [Carrier] location

REFUNDS:
- Processed within 5-7 business days of receipt
- Refunded to original payment method
- Original shipping not refundable (unless defective)

EXCHANGES:
- Free exchanges for different size/color
- New item ships immediately upon return scan

FREE RETURNS:
- We provide prepaid return labels
- Deduct $6.99 from refund if you prefer

NON-RETURNABLE:
- Final sale items
- Personalized/custom items
- Intimate apparel (for hygiene)

QUESTIONS? Email returns@brand.com
```

### Returns Process Flow

```
1. Customer requests return (website/email)
        ↓
2. Auto-approve or manual review
        ↓
3. Send return label (email)
        ↓
4. Customer ships item back
        ↓
5. 3PL/warehouse receives and inspects
        ↓
6. Approve or reject (with photo evidence)
        ↓
7. Process refund or exchange
        ↓
8. Notify customer
        ↓
9. Restock or dispose
```

### Reducing Returns

**Common Reasons & Solutions:**

| Reason | Solution |
|--------|----------|
| **Wrong size** | Better size guide, fit quiz, customer reviews with size info |
| **Not as described** | Better photos, detailed descriptions, video |
| **Changed mind** | Clear policies, excellent product pages |
| **Defective** | Quality control, better suppliers |
| **Damaged in shipping** | Better packaging, insurance |

**Prevention Tactics:**
- Detailed size charts with measurements
- Customer reviews with photos
- Video demonstrations
- Live chat for questions
- Clear product descriptions
- Quality control before shipping

## International Shipping

### Should You Ship Internationally?

**Pros:**
- Larger market (95% of world is outside US)
- Higher average order values
- Less competition in some markets
- Diversified revenue streams

**Cons:**
- Complex customs/duties
- Higher shipping costs
- Longer delivery times
- More customer service issues
- Returns are complicated

**Recommendation:** Start domestic, expand internationally after 100+ orders/month

### International Shipping Options

**Option 1: Direct Shipping (USPS, UPS, FedEx)**

**Pros:**
- Simple setup
- No inventory abroad
- Full control

**Cons:**
- Expensive ($30-80 per package)
- Long delivery (10-21 days)
- Customer pays duties
- Complicated returns

**Best For:** Testing international demand

**Option 2: International 3PL**

**Pros:**
- Faster shipping (2-5 days local)
- Lower shipping costs
- Better customer experience
- Local returns

**Cons:**
- Inventory in multiple locations
- More complex management
- Higher minimum commitments

**Best For:** Established brands with international demand

**Option 3: Marketplaces (Amazon Global, eBay)**

**Pros:**
- Built-in international infrastructure
- Handles customs/duties
- Large existing audience

**Cons:**
- Lower margins
- Less brand control
- Platform fees

**Best For:** Supplemental revenue

### Duties & Taxes

**Key Concepts:**

**HS Code:** Harmonized System code for product classification
- Determines duty rate
- Required for customs forms
- Find at usitc.gov/tariff-affairs

**De Minimis Value:** Threshold below which no duty is charged
- Varies by country
- Important for pricing strategy

**DDP vs DDU:**
- **DDP (Delivered Duty Paid):** You pay duties upfront
- **DDU (Delivered Duty Unpaid):** Customer pays on delivery

**Recommendation:** Use DDP for better customer experience

### Customs Documentation

**Required for Every International Shipment:**

1. **Commercial Invoice**
   - Shipper and recipient details
   - Detailed product description
   - HS codes
   - Value of goods
   - Reason for export (sale, gift, sample)

2. **Packing List**
   - Contents of each package
   - Weights and dimensions

3. **Customs Declaration**
   - CN22 (small packages)
   - CN23 (larger packages)

**Tips:**
- Be specific in descriptions (not just "gift")
- Accurate values (don't undervalue)
- Include HS codes
- Keep copies of all documentation

### International Returns

**Challenges:**
- High shipping costs
- Customs on returned goods
- Long processing times
- Customer friction

**Solutions:**

**Option 1: Local Return Address**
- Use 3PL or virtual address
- Customer ships locally
- Consolidate and ship back monthly

**Option 2: Refund Without Return**
- For low-value items
- Customer keeps or donates
- Build into cost model

**Option 3: Store Credit Only**
- No international refunds
- Offer generous store credit
- Customer can use on future orders
