# Upsell Script & Expansion Path
## When and How to Offer Additional Services

**Purpose:** Systematically identify and present expansion opportunities that serve clients better while increasing account value.

---

## PHILOSOPHY

**Good Upsell =** "I noticed you need X. We can help with that."
**Bad Upsell =** "Please buy more stuff from us."

**Rules:**
1. Only offer what genuinely helps the client
2. Time it right (after wins, not during problems)
3. Make it easy to say yes (clear value, low friction)
4. Never pressure—always give an out

---

## TIMING: WHEN TO PITCH

### ✅ Green Light Moments

| Trigger | Why It Works | Best Offer |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| Client celebrates a win | They see value, trust is high | Related service expansion |
| They mention a pain point | You're solving, not selling | Solution to stated problem |
| After strong monthly results | ROI is proven | Tier upgrade or add-on |
| They ask "Can you also...?" | They're already sold | Whatever they asked for |
| 90+ days in, happy client | Past probation, established trust | Strategic expansion |
| Referral given | They're advocates | VIP/enterprise tier |

### ❌ Red Light Moments

| Trigger | Why It's Bad | What to Do Instead |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| After a complaint | Seems opportunistic | Fix the problem first |
| During contract renewal stress | Feels like a holdup | Separate conversations |
| First 30 days | Too early, no trust yet | Deliver value first |
| When they're busy/stressed | Bad timing | Wait for better moment |
| After a bad month | ROI unclear | Turn results around first |

## PITCH SCRIPTS BY SCENARIO

### SCRIPT 1: TIER UPGRADE (After Strong Results)

**When:** Client just had a breakthrough month (viral post, big lead, etc.)

**Approach:** Email or strategy call

```
Subject: Quick idea to build on this momentum

Hi [Client Name],

Huge congrats on [specific win]! That post about [topic] really 
resonated—25K impressions and counting.

I've been thinking about how to capitalize on this momentum. Right 
now you're on the Starter plan (2 posts/week). Given how well your 
content is performing, I'd recommend considering the Pro tier 
(3 posts/week).

Here's why:
• Your audience is clearly hungry for more from you
• 3x/week would keep you top-of-mind without overwhelming your network
• We could test more content types (carousels, video scripts)
• Based on your current engagement, the extra post could drive 
  an additional [X] inbound leads per month

Investment: Additional $1,500/month
Expected ROI: [X] additional leads @ [avg value] = $[X] potential revenue

No pressure at all—I wanted to float the idea while the momentum 
is hot. Want to discuss on our call next week?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Key Elements:**
- ✅ Lead with their win (not your ask)
- ✅ Specific, data-backed reasoning
- ✅ Clear investment and ROI
- ✅ Easy out ("no pressure")

### SCRIPT 2: PROFILE OPTIMIZATION (Natural Add-On)

**When:** Client's profile isn't optimized, or they mention profile views

**Approach:** Mention during strategy call

```
"I noticed something while reviewing your analytics. Your posts are 
getting great traction, but I'm seeing a lot of profile views that 
aren't converting to followers.

Your profile is the landing page for all this traffic. Right now, 
it's [honestly describe: 'not fully optimized' / 'could be stronger'].

We offer a Profile Optimization service where we:
• Rewrite your headline for maximum impact
• Craft an 'About' section that converts visitors
• Optimize your featured section with your best content
• Add strategic keywords for search visibility
• Review your profile photo and banner

It's a one-time $500 investment. Clients typically see a 30-50% 
increase in profile-to-follower conversion after the refresh.

Want me to send over some before/after examples?"
```

**Close:**
```
"Great! I'll send the details. We can schedule this for next week 
once you approve. Any specific messaging you want to emphasize?"
```

### SCRIPT 3: ENGAGEMENT MANAGEMENT (Time-Saver Pitch)

**When:** Client is busy, not responding to comments, mentions being overwhelmed

**Approach:** Empathetic check-in

```
Subject: Saving you time on LinkedIn

Hi [Client Name],

I know you're juggling a lot right now. I noticed there are some 
great conversations happening in the comments on your posts that 
are going unanswered.

Here's the thing: responding to comments within 2 hours can 2-3x 
the reach of your posts. But I also get that you don't have time 
to be on LinkedIn all day.

We offer an Engagement Management add-on where we:
• Monitor all comments on your posts
• Draft thoughtful responses in your voice (you approve)
• Engage with comments from other accounts in your niche
• Identify and flag high-value conversations for your attention

It's $750/month and typically saves clients 3-5 hours per week 
while boosting their post performance.

Interested in testing this for a month?"
```

**If They Hesitate:**
```
"Totally get it. How about we test it for 2 weeks at a prorated 
rate ($375)? If you don't feel like it's worth it, no hard feelings 
and we'll cancel. Fair?"
```

## PRICING PSYCHOLOGY

### Good Better Best Framing

When presenting tier upgrades:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  STARTER              PRO ← Most Popular    ENTERPRISE      │
│  $1,500/mo           $3,000/mo              $5,000/mo       │
│                                                              │
│  • 2 posts/week      • 3 posts/week         • 5 posts/week  │
│  • Basic analytics   • Full dashboard       • Everything    │
│  • Email support     • Strategy calls       • in Pro +      │
│                      • Profile opt.         • Engagement    │
│                                             • Team training │
│                                             • Priority supp.│
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Why It Works:**
- Middle option feels like the "smart choice"
- Enterprise makes Pro seem reasonable
- Clear value progression

---

### Anchoring Technique

When discussing add-ons:

```
"Profile optimization is $500 one-time. For context, a typical 
marketing agency charges $2,000-5,000 for the same service. We 
include it as a value-add because we know it improves your results."
```

**Why It Works:**
- Sets expectation of higher value
- Makes your price feel like a deal
- Shows you're giving them a break

---

### Trial/Test Frame

For hesitant clients:

```
"I get that this is a new investment. How about we do a 30-day 
trial? You pay the normal rate, we implement, and after 30 days 
we review the results together. If you don't feel it was worth it, 
we can pause with no hard feelings. Fair?"
```

**Why It Works:**
- Reduces perceived risk
- Shows confidence in your service
- Gives them an out (they probably won't use it)

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### Objection 1: "It's Too Expensive"

**Response:**
```
"I hear you. Let me ask—when you say expensive, is it about the 
total investment, or are you unsure about the ROI?"

[If ROI concern:]
"Totally fair. Let's look at the numbers. If this generates 
[conservative estimate] in new business, would that justify 
the investment?"

[If budget concern:]
"I get it. Here are a couple options:
• We could start with a smaller scope and scale up
• We could do a 30-day trial to prove value first
• We could revisit in [X months] when budget opens up

What feels most comfortable?"
```

---

### Objection 2: "I Need to Think About It"

**Response:**
```
"Of course! This is a real investment and worth considering.

To help you think it through:
• What specific questions do you still have?
• Is there anything blocking you from moving forward?
• What would need to be true for this to be a 'hell yes'?"

[Listen carefully—this reveals the real objection]

"Would it help if I [sent more info / connected you with a 
current client / created a custom ROI projection]?"
```

---

### Objection 3: "Let Me Talk to My [Partner/Team/Board]"

**Response:**
```
"Absolutely—this affects more than just you. What would be most 
helpful for that conversation?

I can:
• Create a one-pager summarizing the value prop
• Join a call to answer questions
• Provide case studies from similar companies
• Build a custom ROI model for your situation

What would make that conversation easier?"
```

**Follow-up:**
```
Send them armed with materials. Then:
"Thanks for discussing with your team. How did it go? Any 
questions I can help with?"
```

---

### Objection 4: "We're Happy with Current Results"

**Response:**
```
"That's awesome to hear! I'm glad it's working well.

The reason I'm bringing this up is that [specific opportunity]. 
Since things are going great, it's actually the perfect time to 
test expansion—while you have momentum and budget confidence.

But if now's not the right time, I totally respect that. Let's 
revisit in a quarter?"
```

---

### Objection 5: "Can You Discount It?"

**Response:**
```
"I appreciate you asking. Here's the thing—we've priced this to 
reflect the value it delivers, and I want to make sure we can 
give you the full experience without cutting corners.

That said, here's what I can do:
• Bundle discount if you commit to 3+ months
• Include [smaller add-on] at no extra cost
• Payment plan to spread the investment

Would any of those help?"
```

**Never:** Immediately discount. It devalues your service.

## DO's AND DON'Ts

### ✅ DO:

- Lead with client value, not your revenue
- Time pitches after wins
- Use data to support recommendations
- Give easy outs (no pressure)
- Follow up once, then let it go
- Celebrate when they say yes
- Honor when they say no

### ❌ DON'T:

- Pitch during problem conversations
- Pressure or guilt-trip
- Over-promise results
- Pitch too early (first 30 days)
- Take "no" personally
- Pitch the same thing repeatedly
- Forget to follow through on what you promise

## FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

### If They Say "Let Me Think":

**Day 1:** Send additional info they requested
**Day 7:** Gentle check-in
> "Hey! Just circling back on [offer]. Any thoughts? Happy to 
> answer questions or hop on a quick call."
**Day 14:** Final touch
> "No pressure at all, but wanted to check one more time before 
> I close the loop. Is this something you'd like to move forward 
> with, or should we revisit next quarter?"

### If They Say Yes:

**Immediately:**
- Send thank you note
- Outline next steps and timeline
- Schedule kickoff (if applicable)
- Update internal systems

**Day 3:**
- Confirm everything is on track
- Answer any new questions

**Week 2:**
- Check in on early experience
- Address any friction

**Day 30:**
- Review results
- Confirm they're happy
- Discuss continuation or expansion
