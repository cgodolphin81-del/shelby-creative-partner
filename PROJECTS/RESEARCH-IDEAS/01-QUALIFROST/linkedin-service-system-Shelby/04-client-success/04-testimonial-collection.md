# Testimonial & Case Study Collection System
## How to Get Powerful Social Proof

**Purpose:** Systematically collect testimonials and case studies from happy clients to fuel marketing and sales.

---

## WHEN TO ASK

### Optimal Timing:

| Trigger | Why It Works | Ask Method |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| After a big win (viral post, major lead) | Emotions are high, value is clear | Immediate DM or call |
| 90-day mark (if results are strong) | Probation period over, proven ROI | Email + call |
| After they give unsolicited praise | They're already thinking it | "Can I quote you on that?" |
| Contract renewal (when they re-up) | They've chosen to stay | Include in renewal process |
| Quarterly Business Review (strong quarter) | Fresh results in mind | End of QBR call |
| When they refer someone | They're advocating | "Would you also be willing to..." |

### Never Ask When:
- ❌ They're unhappy or frustrated
- ❌ Results are mediocre or declining
- ❌ Right after a complaint (even if resolved)
- ❌ During contract negotiation stress

---

## THE ASK: SCRIPTS BY CHANNEL

### Script 1: Post-Win DM (LinkedIn or Slack)

**When:** They just celebrated a result

```
[Client]: "That post you wrote got me 3 inbound leads! Incredible."

[You]: "That's AMAZING! 🎉 So glad it's translating to real business.

Quick question—would you be open to me quoting you on that? Something 
like:

'This post generated 3 qualified inbound leads in 48 hours.'

No pressure at all, but social proof like that helps us show other 
business owners what's possible. And obviously I'd run anything by 
you before using it.

Let me know! Either way, let's keep this momentum going."
```

**Why It Works:**
- Tied to their excitement
- Specific, concrete result
- Low friction (just a quote, not a full case study)
- You'll show them before publishing

---

### Script 2: 90-Day Email Request

**When:** Client hit 3-month mark with good results

```
Subject: Quick favor + celebrating 90 days!

Hi [Client Name],

Can't believe it's been 3 months! When we started, your LinkedIn was 
[describe starting point]. Now you've got:

• [X,XXX] new followers
• [XX,XXX] total impressions
• [XX] inbound leads
• [Specific win, e.g., "That post about leadership went viral with 25K views"]

I'm incredibly proud of what we've built together.

I have a favor to ask: Would you be open to sharing a brief testimonial 
about your experience working with us?

It would help us connect with more founders and executives who need 
what you've gotten from this partnership.

Here's what I'm thinking:
• 2-3 sentences about your results
• 1 sentence about what it's like to work with us
• Your name, title, and company (or we can keep it anonymous)

I can also draft something for you to edit—whatever's easiest.

No pressure at all. But if you're willing, it would mean a lot.

Either way, thank you for being an incredible client. Here's to 
many more months of wins!

Best,
[Your Name]

P.S. If you'd rather hop on a quick call and I'll record it (then 
transcribe), I'm happy to do that too!
```

**Follow-Up (if no response in 5 days):**
```
Subject: Re: Quick favor + celebrating 90 days!

Hi [Client Name],

Just bumping this up in case it got buried! No worries if now's 
not the right time—we can revisit anytime.

Also, I sent over next month's content calendar for your review. 
Let me know if you have any tweaks!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Script 3: End-of-Call Ask (Strategy Call)

**When:** They express satisfaction during your monthly call

```
[During call, after they say something positive]

You: "I'm so glad to hear that. Actually, would you mind if I 
quoted you on that?

What you just said—'[repeat their exact words]'—that's exactly 
what we aim for. With your permission, I'd love to use that in 
our materials.

Client: [Usually says yes]

You: "Amazing, thank you! I'll send you a quick email after this 
call to confirm the exact wording. And of course you can review 
before we use it anywhere.

Also—would you be open to a quick 10-minute recorded chat sometime 
in the next couple weeks? We're putting together a few client 
stories, and your journey would be perfect. Totally optional, but 
I think it could inspire other leaders in your position."
```

**Why It Works:**
- They're already in a positive headspace
- You're asking permission, not ambushing
- Recorded testimonial is higher value (video/audio)
- You framed it as helping others (appeals to their generosity)

### Script 4: The Case Study Pitch

**When:** Client has exceptional results (6+ months, strong metrics)

```
Subject: Idea: Turn your LinkedIn success into a case study

Hi [Client Name],

I was reviewing your results from the past 6 months, and honestly, 
they're remarkable:

• Followers: [X,XXX] → [X,XXX] ([+XXX%])
• Avg Engagement: [X.X%] → [X.X%]
• Inbound Leads: [XX] qualified opportunities
• [Specific big win, e.g., "Landed speaking gig at XYZ Conference"]

This is exactly the kind of transformation other executives dream of.

I'd love to feature your story as a case study. Here's what that 
would involve:

1. A 30-minute recorded call where I interview you about:
   - Where you were before working with us
   - What results you've seen

3. We'll share it on our site, social, and in sales conversations

Why do this?
• Inspires other leaders facing similar challenges
• Puts your expertise on display (great for your own brand)
• Helps us reach more people who need this service

Of course, you'd have full approval rights. And if you'd prefer 
to stay anonymous, we can do that too.

Interested? Happy to hop on a call to discuss.

Either way, thank you for being such an incredible partner in 
this work.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## TESTIMONIAL FORMATS

### Format 1: Written Quote (Easiest)

**Length:** 2-4 sentences
**Use:** Website, sales deck, social proof

**Template to Send Client:**
```
Here's a draft based on what you shared—edit freely!

"[Specific result]. Working with [Your Company] has been 
[adjective]. They [what you do well]. I'd recommend them to 
[type of person]."

— [Your Name], [Title] at [Company]
```

**Examples:**
```
"In 90 days, my LinkedIn went from a ghost town to generating 
3-5 inbound leads per month. The team nailed my voice from day 
one. Best investment I've made in my personal brand."
— Sarah Chen, CEO at TechFlow

"I was skeptical about ghostwriting, but the results speak for 
themselves. 50K followers in 6 months and multiple speaking 
opportunities. They're strategic partners, not just writers."
— Marcus Rodriguez, Partner at VentureFirst
```

---

### Format 2: Video Testimonial (High Value)

**Length:** 2-5 minutes
**Use:** Website homepage, sales pages, ads

**How to Collect:**

**Option A: Zoom Call (You Interview Them)**
```
Questions to Ask:
1. "What was LinkedIn like for you before working with us?"
2. "What results have you seen since we started?"
3. "What surprised you about the experience?"
4. "What would you say to someone considering this service?"
5. "Who is this service perfect for?"

Tips:
- Keep it conversational, not scripted
- Ask follow-up questions ("Can you give me an example?")
- Let them talk—don't interrupt
- Record with their permission
```

**Option B: Self-Recorded (Lower Friction)**
```
Send them a Loom-style prompt:

"Hey! If you're open to recording a quick video testimonial, 
here are some prompts (answer any that resonate):

• What was your LinkedIn situation before working with us?
• What results have you seen?
• What's it been like to work with our team?
• Who would you recommend this to?

No need to script it—just speak from the heart. 1-3 minutes 
is perfect. You can record on your phone or use Loom.

Send it over and I'll handle the rest!"
```

### Format 3: LinkedIn Post (Social Proof + Tag)

**When:** Client is active on LinkedIn and happy

**Ask Script:**
```
"Hey! Would you be open to sharing your experience in a LinkedIn 
post? It would mean the world to us—and it's great for your 
brand too (shows you invest in quality partnerships).

I can draft something for you to edit, or you can write from 
scratch. Either way, tag us and we'll amplify it!"
```

**Draft Template:**
```
I'll be honest—I was skeptical about LinkedIn ghostwriting.

But 6 months in, the results speak for themselves:

→ 40K new followers
→ 500K+ impressions
→ Multiple inbound leads that turned into [specific outcome]

The team at [Your Company] didn't just write posts. They learned 
my voice, my story, and my mission. Every piece of content feels 
authentically me.

If you're an executive or founder sitting on the sidelines of 
LinkedIn, I get it. But this has been a game-changer for me.

Grateful for the partnership. 🙏

[Tag Your Company]
```

### Format 4: Case Study (Most Comprehensive)

**Length:** 1-3 pages or blog post
**Use:** Website, sales enablement, content marketing

**Structure:**

```
TITLE: [Result] in [Timeframe]: How [Client] Used LinkedIn to [Achieve Outcome]

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (2-3 sentences)
- Who the client is
- What they achieved
- Key metric

THE CHALLENGE (1-2 paragraphs)
- Where they were before
- What was holding them back
- Why they sought help

THE SOLUTION (2-3 paragraphs)
- What service they chose
- How the engagement worked
- What made it different

THE RESULTS (1-2 paragraphs + visuals)
- Specific metrics (followers, engagement, leads)
- Qualitative wins (speaking gigs, partnerships, etc.)
- Before/after comparison

CLIENT QUOTE (1-2 sentences)
- Their testimonial in their words

KEY TAKEAWAYS (3-5 bullets)
- What other readers can learn
- Actionable insights

CALL TO ACTION
- How to work with you
- Link to schedule a call
```

**Example:**
```
TITLE: From 2K to 50K Followers in 6 Months: How a VC Partner 
Built a LinkedIn Empire

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
Marcus Rodriguez, Partner at VentureFirst, went from posting 
occasionally to 50K+ followers and 500K monthly impressions. 
His LinkedIn presence now drives 30% of his deal flow.

THE CHALLENGE:
Marcus knew LinkedIn was important for deal flow and LP 
relationships, but he didn't have time to write. His profile 
was stale, his posts were sporadic, and he was invisible 
compared to peers...

[Continue with full story]
```

## INCENTIVES (Optional but Effective)

### What to Offer:

| Incentive | When to Use | Cost to You |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| 10% off next month | For written testimonial | Low |
| Free profile refresh | For video testimonial | Medium (your time) |
| Bonus content piece | For case study | Medium |
| Donation to their charity | For any testimonial | Variable |
| Featured on your platforms | For active promoters | Free |
| Referral commission | For testimonials that convert | Performance-based |

### Script with Incentive:
```
"As a thank-you, I'd like to offer you [incentive]. It's a small 
way to show our appreciation for your time and for being such a 
great partner.

No strings attached—even if you decline the testimonial, the 
[incentive] is yours. You've earned it."
```

## ORGANIZATION SYSTEM

### Where to Store Testimonials:

**Notion/Airtable Database:**
```
Fields:
- Client Name
- Company
- Testimonial Text
- Format (written, video, case study)
- Date Received
- Usage Rights (where can you use it)
- Results Mentioned
- Tags (industry, service type, outcome)
- Status (draft, approved, published)
```

**Folder Structure:**
```
/testimonials
  /written
  /video
  /case-studies
  /linkedin-posts
  /approved
  /pending-review
```

## USAGE RIGHTS & PERMISSIONS

### Always Clarify:

```
"Just to confirm—where are you comfortable with us using this?

□ Our website
□ Sales deck
□ Social media
□ Paid ads
□ Speaking presentations
□ Anywhere (you trust us)

And would you prefer we use:
□ Your full name and company
□ Name only (no company)
□ Anonymous ('Partner at Fortune 500 company')
```

### Get It in Writing:

**Simple Email Confirmation:**
```
Thanks again for the testimonial! Just confirming what we discussed:

You've given us permission to use your testimonial on:
- Our website
- Our sales materials
- Our social media

We'll use your name and title as: "[Name], [Title] at [Company]"

You can revoke this permission at any time by emailing me.

Sound right?

Best,
[Your Name]
```
