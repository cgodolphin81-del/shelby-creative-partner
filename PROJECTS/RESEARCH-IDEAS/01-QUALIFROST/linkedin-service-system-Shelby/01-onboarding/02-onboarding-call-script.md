# 60-Minute Onboarding Call Script
## LinkedIn Ghostwriting Premium Service

**Pre-Call Prep:**
- [ ] Client has completed Voice Capture Questionnaire
- [ ] Review their LinkedIn profile, recent posts, engagement patterns
- [ ] Note any obvious voice mismatches or opportunities
- [ ] Have their questionnaire responses open

---

## MINUTE 0-5: WARM-UP & CONTEXT SETTING

**Opening:**
> "Thanks for making time today. This call is about making sure I can sound exactly like you—so when people read your posts, they think 'that's so [Name],' not 'this feels ghostwritten.'
>
> By the end of this call, I'll have everything I need to capture your voice. We'll also clarify your goals, your audience, and what success looks like for you.
>
> Sound good? Any questions before we dive in?"

---

## MINUTE 5-20: DEEP DIVE INTO QUESTIONNAIRE RESPONSES

**Purpose:** Clarify and expand on their written answers. Don't just accept surface-level responses—dig for specifics.

### Tone & Personality Probes:

> "You described your style as [their 3 words]. Can you give me an example of a time when someone specifically commented on that? What did they say?"

> "You mentioned you often say [their phrase]. In what context do you usually say it? Is it in tough conversations, celebrations, teaching moments?"

> "When you said you get frustrated when [their frustration]—what's a recent example of that happening? Walk me through it."

### Story Mining:

> "You mentioned [failure/milestone from Q6-7]. I want to get the details—what was the moment you realized things weren't working? What did you do the next day?"

> "For the belief you changed [Q8]—what was the specific moment or experience that changed your mind? Was it a conversation, a failure, a book?"

> "Tell me more about [influential person from Q10]. What's something they said that you still quote or think about?"

### Opinion Sharpening:

> "You said [industry thing] is overrated [Q11]. What should people be doing instead? What would you tell your younger self?"

> "For the underrated thing [Q12]—why do you think people ignore it? What makes it so valuable?"

> "You mentioned you're willing to be misunderstood about [Q19]. Have you ever pushed back publicly on this? What happened?"

**Note-Taking:** Capture exact phrases, metaphors, and emotional language they use. These are voice gold.

---

## MINUTE 20-30: GOALS & AUDIENCE CLARITY

### Primary Goals (Pick 1-2 Focus Areas):

> "LinkedIn can do a lot of things. What's the #1 outcome you want in the next 90 days?"
>
> - [ ] Build personal brand / thought leadership
> - [ ] Generate inbound leads for [service/product]
> - [ ] Attract job opportunities / board positions
> - [ ] Recruit talent to join company
> - [ ] Drive traffic to [podcast/newsletter/product]
> - [ ] Build network in [specific industry/role]
> - [ ] Other: _______________

### Target Audience:

> "Who specifically are you trying to reach? Be as specific as possible."
>
> **Probe for:**
> - Job titles / roles
> - Industry / company size
> - Geographic location
> - Their pain points / challenges
> - Where they currently spend time online

> "What do you want them to think, feel, or do after reading your posts?"

### Content Boundaries:

> "Are there any topics you absolutely do NOT want to post about?"
>
> - [ ] Politics
> - [ ] Religion
> - [ ] Personal family details
> - [ ] Specific competitors
> - [ ] Pricing / revenue numbers
> - [ ] Company internals
> - [ ] Other: _______________

> "Are there topics you WANT to be known for? What should people associate with your name?"

---

## MINUTE 30-40: LOGISTICS & WORKFLOW

### Posting Frequency:

> "How many posts per week feels sustainable and valuable?"
>
> - [ ] 2x/week (maintenance mode)
> - [ ] 3x/week (steady growth)
> - [ ] 4x/week (aggressive growth)
> - [ ] 5x/week (maximum visibility)

**Recommendation:** Start with 3x/week for first month, adjust based on engagement and bandwidth.

### Approval Process:

> "Here's how our workflow will work:
> 1. I draft posts based on our strategy
> 2. You review in [Notion/Trello] with 24-48 hours to provide feedback
> 3. I revise if needed
> 4. You approve and I schedule
>
> Does that work for you? Who else needs to review before posting (if anyone)?"

### Response Expectations:

> "When people comment on your posts, how do you want to handle engagement?"
>
> - [ ] I'll respond to all comments myself
> - [ ] I'll respond to important ones, you draft replies for the rest
> - [ ] You draft all replies, I approve/post
> - [ ] Other: _______________

**Best Practice:** Responding to comments within 2 hours boosts algorithmic reach significantly.

---

## MINUTE 40-50: CONTENT PILLARS & THEMES

**Present 3-4 Content Pillars based on questionnaire + call:**

> "Based on what you've shared, I recommend we focus on these themes:
>
> **Pillar 1: [e.g., Leadership Lessons]**
> - Stories from your career
> - Mistakes and learnings
> - Management philosophy
>
> **Pillar 2: [e.g., Industry Insights]**
> - Trends you're seeing
> - Contrarian takes
> - Predictions
>
> **Pillar 3: [e.g., Tactical Advice]**
> - How-to content
> - Frameworks you use
> - Tools and resources
>
> **Pillar 4: [e.g., Personal/Behind-the-Scenes]**
> - Day-in-the-life
> - Values in action
> - Humanizing moments
>
> How does this feel? What would you add or remove?"

**Get their buy-in on the pillars before moving forward.**

---

## MINUTE 50-55: SUCCESS METRICS & CHECK-INS

### Define Success:

> "Three months from now, what would make you say 'this was absolutely worth it'?"
>
> Capture specific metrics:
> - Follower count: ______ → ______
> - Average post impressions: ______ → ______
> - Inbound messages/leads per month: ______ → ______
> - Speaking invites / media requests: ______ → ______
> - Other: _______________

### Check-In Cadence:

> "I recommend we have a 30-minute check-in call every month to:
> - Review what's working/not working
> - Adjust content pillars if needed
> - Discuss any new ideas or priorities
> - Plan for any upcoming launches or events
>
> Does [Day/Time] work for a recurring monthly call?"

---

## MINUTE 55-60: NEXT STEPS & CLOSING

### Immediate Next Steps:

> "Here's what happens next:
>
> 1. **Within 24 hours:** I'll send you a summary of everything we discussed plus your content pillars
> 2. **Within 48 hours:** You'll get your first 3 post drafts for review
> 3. **You review and provide feedback** within 24-48 hours
> 4. **I revise and schedule** approved posts
> 5. **We launch** and start tracking results
>
> Any concerns about this timeline?"

### Final Questions:

> "Before we wrap—what questions do you have for me? Anything I haven't covered that's important to you?"

### Closing:

> "I'm excited to work together. You have a unique perspective and I'm confident we'll create content that resonates. I'll be in touch within 24 hours with the summary.
>
> Thanks for your time and honesty today—that's what makes this work."

---

## POST-CALL: Ghostwriter Action Items

- [ ] Send call summary email within 24 hours (template in `05-templates/`)
- [ ] Create client folder in Notion/Trello with content calendar
- [ ] Draft first 3 posts based on voice + pillars
- [ ] Set up analytics tracking sheet for this client
- [ ] Schedule recurring monthly check-in call
- [ ] Add client to CRM with key details and start date

---

## RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

During the call, note any of these warning signs:

| Red Flag | What It Means | Mitigation |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Vague on goals | May not see value in LinkedIn | Re-clarify ROI in summary email |
| Wants to post daily immediately | Unrealistic expectations | Set boundaries, recommend 3x/week start |
| Micromanages word choice | Trust issues | Establish approval process clearly |
| No time for review calls | Low engagement risk | Emphasize importance of feedback loop |
| Expects viral overnight | Misaligned expectations | Show realistic growth curves |
