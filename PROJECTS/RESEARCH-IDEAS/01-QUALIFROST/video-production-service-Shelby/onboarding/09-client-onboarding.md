```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    CLIENT JOURNEY MAP                            │

│                                                                  │

│                                                                  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Onboarding Goals

1. **Set Expectations** – Clear timeline, deliverables, process
2. **Build Trust** – Professional, organized, responsive
3. **Gather Information** – Everything needed for success
4. **Prevent Problems** – Address concerns before they arise
5. **Create Advocates** – Turn clients into promoters

## Discovery Phase

### Initial Inquiry Response (Within 24 Hours)

**Email Template:**

```
Subject: Re: Video Project Inquiry – [Company Name]

Hi [Name],

Thanks for reaching out about your video project! I'd love to 
learn more about what you're looking to create.

To make sure I understand your needs, could you share:

1. What type of video are you looking for? (explainer, testimonial, 
   product demo, etc.)
2. What's your primary goal for this video?
3. Do you have a target timeline or deadline?
4. What's your approximate budget range?

Once I have a better sense of your project, I'll send over some 
next steps and we can schedule a discovery call.

Looking forward to hearing more!

Best,
[Your Name]
[Company]
[Contact Info]
```

---

### Discovery Call Agenda (30-45 minutes)

**Preparation:**
- [ ] Research the company
- [ ] Review their website/social media
- [ ] Prepare relevant portfolio examples
- [ ] Set up Zoom/recording (with permission)

**Call Structure:**

```
0:00-5:00   → Introductions & rapport
5:00-15:00  → Their story & business
15:00-25:00 → Project specifics
25:00-35:00 → Budget & timeline
35:00-45:00 → Next steps & Q&A
```

**Key Questions:**

**Business Context:**
- Tell me about your company
- What do you do/sell?
- Who is your ideal customer?
- What makes you different from competitors?

**Project Goals:**
- Why do you need this video now?
- What problem are you trying to solve?
- What does success look like?
- Where will this video be used?

**Audience:**
- Who are we trying to reach?
- What do they care about?
- What action should they take after watching?

**Practical:**
- What's your ideal timeline?
- Do you have a budget range in mind?
- Who will be the decision-maker?
- Have you worked with video producers before?

**Red Flags to Watch For:**
- Unrealistic timeline ("Can you do it by Friday?")
- Budget mismatch (wants Premium, has Basic budget)
- Too many decision-makers ("Our committee needs to approve")
- Vague goals ("We just need a video")
- Bad history with previous vendors

## Script Development

### Script Writing Process

**Timeline:** 5-10 business days

```
Day 1-2   → Research & outline
Day 3-4   → First draft
Day 5     → Internal review
Day 6-7   → Revisions
Day 8     → Client delivery
Day 9-10  → Client feedback & revisions
```

### Script Template

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                         VIDEO SCRIPT                            │
│  Project: [Name]                                                │
│  Version: [1.0]                                                 │
│  Date: [Date]                                                   │
│  Length: [Target duration]                                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

SCENE 1
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TIME: 0:00-0:15
VISUAL: [Description of what we see]
AUDIO: [Voiceover/dialogue]
NOTES: [Any special instructions]

SCENE 2
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TIME: 0:15-0:30
VISUAL: [Description of what we see]
AUDIO: [Voiceover/dialogue]
NOTES: [Any special instructions]

[Continue for all scenes...]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL ESTIMATED LENGTH: [X:XX]
WORD COUNT: [XXX words]
```

### Script Writing Tips

**Structure:**
- Hook in first 5 seconds
- Problem/setup (15-25%)
- Solution/main content (50-60%)
- Proof/social proof (10-15%)
- Call-to-action (5-10%)

**Writing Style:**
- Conversational, not formal
- Short sentences
- Active voice
- Specific over vague
- Show, don't tell

**Timing Guide:**
- 130-150 words = 1 minute (average speaking pace)
- Always read aloud to check flow
- Time each section

### Handling Script Revisions

**Best Practices:**

1. **Set Clear Expectations**
   - Number of rounds included
   - Turnaround time for feedback
   - What happens after max rounds

2. **Consolidate Feedback**
   - One document with all comments
   - One point of contact
   - No "surprise" feedback from new stakeholders

3. **Push Back When Needed**
   ```
   "I understand you'd like to add [X]. My concern is that 
   will extend the video to [length], which may impact 
   retention on [platform]. 
  
   Alternative suggestion: [compromise option]
   
   What do you think?"
   ```

4. **Get Formal Approval**
   - Email confirmation
   - Signed approval form
   - Clear "this is final" statement

## Pre-Production Checklist

### 2 Weeks Before Shoot

```
□ Script finalized and approved
□ Storyboard completed
□ Shot list created
□ Location(s) secured and permitted
□ Talent cast and contracted
□ Crew hired and briefed
□ Equipment reserved
□ Call sheets distributed
□ Wardrobe selected
□ Props acquired
□ Catering arranged (if full-day shoot)
□ Backup plans in place (weather, illness, etc.)
□ Client confirmed all details
```

### 1 Week Before Shoot

```
□ Location scout completed (if not done)
□ Final shot list with client
□ Talent confirmed (call time, wardrobe, etc.)
□ Crew confirmed
□ Equipment tested
□ Weather check (for outdoor shoots)
□ Parking/load-in confirmed
□ Client reminder sent
□ Emergency contacts exchanged
□ Backup equipment identified
```

### Day Before Shoot

```
□ Equipment packed and charged
□ Memory cards formatted
□ Call sheet re-sent
□ Weather check (again)
□ Route planned (account for traffic)
□ Gear check complete
□ Client confirmation received
□ Rest and prepare!
```

## Production Phase

### Shoot Day Timeline

**Half-Day Shoot (4 hours):**

```
8:00 AM  → Crew call (load-in, setup)
9:00 AM  → Talent call (HMU, wardrobe)
10:00 AM → First shot
12:00 PM → Lunch break
1:00 PM  → Resume shooting
2:00 PM  → B-roll pickup
3:00 PM  → Wrap
4:00 PM  → Load-out complete
```

**Full-Day Shoot (8 hours):**

```
7:00 AM  → Crew call
8:00 AM  → Talent call
9:00 AM  → First shot
12:00 PM → Lunch
1:00 PM  → Resume
4:00 PM  → Location change (if applicable)
5:00 PM  → Resume at new location
6:00 PM  → B-roll pickup
7:00 PM  → Wrap
8:00 PM  → Load-out complete
```

### Client On-Set Guidelines

**Send This Before Shoot Day:**

```
CLIENT ON-SET GUIDELINES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

We love having clients on set! To ensure a smooth shoot:

✅ DO:
• Arrive at the call time specified
• Designate one point person for decisions
• Provide feedback during playback, not during takes
• Trust the crew to do their jobs
• Enjoy the process!

❌ DON'T:
• Bring extra people (space is limited)
• Give direction to talent during takes
• Make changes to the script on set
• Expect to see a finished product (it's raw footage)
• Be surprised if things take time (quality takes time)

DECISION MAKING:
We'll need quick decisions on:
• Performance takes (which one is best)
• Wardrobe choices
• Location adjustments
• Any script changes

Please have the decision-maker available throughout the shoot.

QUESTIONS?
Call/text [Name] at [Number]
```

### Managing Revision Creep

**Signs of Revision Creep:**
- New stakeholders providing feedback late
- "While we're at it..." requests
- Subjective preferences vs. objective issues
- Endless tweaking of approved elements

**How to Address:**

```
"Thanks for the detailed feedback! I want to make sure we 
stay on track for the [date] delivery.

Of the 15 items listed:
• 8 are included in our revision round
• 4 are new requests (outside scope)
• 3 were already addressed in v[X.X]

Let's prioritize the 8 included items for this round. 
For the additional requests, I can send a separate quote.

Does that work for you?"
```
