# Post-Engagement Support

## Client Success, Retention, and Ongoing Support Models

---

## Overview

The engagement doesn't end when the final report is delivered. Post-engagement support is critical for client success, retention, and long-term relationship building. This document outlines our approach to ongoing client support.

### Post-Engagement Goals

1. **Ensure Remediation Success:** Help clients implement recommendations
2. **Maintain Relationship:** Stay connected for future opportunities
3. **Generate Recurring Revenue:** Convert to retainer or managed services
4. **Build Advocacy:** Create references and referral sources
5. **Continuous Improvement:** Learn from each engagement

---

## Support Models

### Model 1: Remediation Support

**Duration:** 30-90 days post-engagement
**Price:** Included or $5,000 - $15,000

**Included Services:**
- Remediation guidance calls (weekly/bi-weekly)
- Architecture review for fixes
- Configuration validation
- Retesting of critical/high findings
- Email support for questions

**Deliverables:**
- Remediation tracking spreadsheet
- Weekly status updates
- Retest reports
- Final remediation summary

**Success Metrics:**
- % of findings remediated
- Time to remediate
- Client satisfaction

---

### Model 2: Compliance Maintenance

**Duration:** Ongoing (monthly/quarterly)
**Price:** $5,000 - $20,000/month

**Included Services:**
- Continuous control monitoring
- Policy updates and reviews
- Employee training coordination

**Deliverables:**
- Monthly compliance dashboard

**Success Metrics:**

**Success Metrics:**

- Client satisfaction

### Ongoing Engagement (Monthly/Quarterly)

**Monthly Activities:**
- Service delivery
- Status reporting
- Issue resolution
- Relationship check-ins

**Quarterly Activities:**
- Quarterly Business Review (QBR)
- Success metrics review

1. Previous quarter review (15 min)
2. Metrics and KPIs (15 min)
3. Achievements and wins (10 min)

6. Strategic discussion (15 min)

**QBR Deck Template:**
- Executive summary
- Service delivery summary
- Key metrics and trends
- Major accomplishments
- Issues and resolutions
- Upcoming initiatives
- Strategic recommendations
- Investment options

## Expansion Strategy

### Identify Expansion Opportunities

**Signals for Expansion:**
- Client expresses new challenges
- Business growth (funding, hiring, M&A)
- New compliance requirements
- Security incident
- Technology changes
- Positive feedback and satisfaction

**Expansion Paths:**
| Current Service | Expansion Opportunity |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Security Audit | Penetration testing, compliance |
| Penetration Testing | Continuous testing, MSSP |
| Compliance (SOC 2) | ISO 27001, HIPAA, ongoing |

| One-time project | Retainer, MSSP, vCISO |

1. **Identify Opportunity**

2. **Qualify**

   - Budget availability

4. **Present**

   - Contract negotiation

## Renewal Process

### Timeline

| Time Before Expiry | Activity |
|--------------------|----------|
| 120 days | Internal renewal planning |
| 90 days | Initial renewal conversation |
| 60 days | Proposal sent |
| 45 days | Follow-up and negotiation |
| 30 days | Contract finalization |
| 15 days | Signed contract |
| 0 days | Renewal effective |

### Renewal Conversation Script

```
OPENING:
"Thanks for taking the time today. As we approach the end of our current 
engagement, I wanted to discuss continuing our partnership.

We've achieved [list key accomplishments] together over the past [timeframe].

I'd love to hear your perspective on how things have gone and discuss 
options for continuing."

DISCOVERY:
"What's worked well in our engagement?"
"What could we improve?"
"What are your security priorities for next year?"
"How do you see our relationship evolving?"

PRESENTATION:
"Based on our conversation, here are the options I recommend:
[Option 1: Continue current services]
[Option 2: Expand to include X]
[Option 3: Transition to retainer/MSSP]

Here's the investment and value for each."

CLOSING:
"Which option makes the most sense for [Company]?"
"What do you need from me to move forward?"
"What's your decision timeline?"
```

### Renewal Pricing

**Standard Renewal:**
- Same services: 5% increase
- Expanded services: Current pricing + 5%
- Multi-year: Lock current rates, 10% discount

**Negotiation Guidelines:**
- Maximum discount: 15%
- Requires: Multi-year or expanded scope
- Approval needed: >10% discount

## Offboarding (When Clients Leave)

### Exit Interview

**Objectives:**
- Understand reasons for leaving
- Gather feedback
- Leave door open
- Maintain relationship

**Questions:**
- "What led to the decision to not renew?"
- "What could we have done better?"
- "What worked well in our engagement?"
- "Would you consider working with us again?"
- "Would you be comfortable providing a reference?"

### Exit Process

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Final deliverables provided
- [ ] All access revoked
- [ ] Data returned or destroyed
- [ ] Final invoice sent
- [ ] Knowledge transfer completed
- [ ] Exit interview conducted
- [ ] CRM updated
- [ ] Team notified
- [ ] Alumni program invitation sent

### Alumni Program

**Purpose:** Maintain relationships with past clients

**Activities:**
- Quarterly newsletter
- Invitation to events/webinars
- Annual check-in call
- Special offers for return
- Referral requests

**Benefits:**
- Potential return client
- Referral source
- Reference provider
- Industry intelligence

## Escalation Management

### Escalation Levels

| Level | Trigger | Response | Owner |
|-------|---------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Minor issue | 24 hours | Account Manager |
| 2 | Service failure | 4 hours | Director |
| 3 | Relationship at risk | 1 hour | VP |
| 4 | Critical/Churn risk | Immediate | C-level |

### Escalation Process

1. **Issue Identified**
   - Client complaint
   - Service failure
   - Health score decline

2. **Assessment**
   - Impact evaluation
   - Root cause analysis
   - Stakeholder identification

3. **Response Plan**
   - Immediate actions
   - Communication plan
   - Resolution timeline

4. **Execution**
   - Implement fixes
   - Regular updates
   - Stakeholder management

5. **Follow-up**
   - Resolution confirmation
   - Lessons learned
   - Process improvement

### Escalation Communication Template

```
Subject: [URGENT] Addressing your concerns - [Company]

Hi [Client Name],

I wanted to reach out personally regarding [issue]. I understand this 
has been [frustrating/concerning], and I want to assure you that we're 
treating this with the highest priority.

Here's what we're doing:
1. [Immediate action]
2. [Investigation/fix in progress]
3. [Timeline for resolution]

I'm personally overseeing this and will provide updates [frequency].

Please don't hesitate to reach out to me directly at [phone] if you need 
anything in the meantime.

Best regards,
[Name]
[Title]
[Direct Contact Info]
```

## Best Practices

### Do's
- ✓ Respond promptly to all inquiries
- ✓ Proactively communicate issues
- ✓ Regular business reviews
- ✓ Document everything
- ✓ Build personal relationships
- ✓ Understand client's business
- ✓ Celebrate wins together
- ✓ Ask for feedback regularly

### Don'ts
- ✗ Disappear after project completion
- ✗ Only reach out when selling
- ✗ Make promises you can't keep
- ✗ Ignore warning signs
- ✗ Let issues escalate without action
- ✗ Assume satisfaction without asking
- ✗ Treat all clients the same
- ✗ Forget to say thank you
