# Revenue Projections - 3-Year Financial Model

## Executive Summary

This document presents a comprehensive 3-year financial model for a virtual events agency, including revenue projections, expense forecasts, profitability analysis, and key financial metrics.

---

## Business Model Overview

### Revenue Streams

| Stream | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Event Production Services | $480,000 | $1,200,000 | $2,400,000 |
| Monthly Retainers | $240,000 | $600,000 | $1,500,000 |
| **Total Revenue** | **$720,000** | **$1,800,000** | **$3,900,000** |

### Key Assumptions

**Year 1:**
- 2-3 events per month (average)
- 2-3 retainer clients
- Average event value: $25,000
- Average retainer: $10,000/month
- Team size: 5-8 people

**Year 2:**
- 4-5 events per month (average)
- 5-7 retainer clients
- Average event value: $35,000
- Average retainer: $15,000/month
- Team size: 12-18 people

**Year 3:**
- 7-10 events per month (average)
- 10-15 retainer clients
- Average event value: $40,000
- Average retainer: $20,000/month
- Team size: 25-35 people

## Conclusion

### Summary

This financial model demonstrates a viable path to building a **$5M+ virtual events agency** within 3 years with:

- **Strong profitability** (20-25% net margins)
- **Recurring revenue base** (40% from retainers by Year 3)
- **Scalable business model** (growing revenue per employee)
- **Multiple exit options** (3-5x revenue valuations)

### Critical Success Factors

1. **Client Retention** - Achieve 75%+ retention to build stable base
2. **Retainer Growth** - Focus on monthly recurring revenue
3. **Operational Efficiency** - Maintain 50%+ gross margins
4. **Team Building** - Hire A-players, invest in culture
5. **Cash Management** - 50% deposits, manage receivables

### Next Steps

1. Secure $400-500K in starting capital
2. Hire core team (CEO, Producer, TD, Sales)
3. Land first 3-5 anchor clients
4. Build case studies and testimonials
5. Scale marketing and sales in Year 2
