# Client Acquisition - Target Markets & Strategies

## Overview

This document outlines target client segments, acquisition strategies, and sales processes for growing a virtual events agency. Success requires focused targeting, clear value propositions, and systematic outreach.

---

## Target Client Segments

### Segment 1: Corporate Enterprise

**Company Profile:**
- Size: 1,000+ employees
- Industries: Technology, Finance, Healthcare, Manufacturing, Retail
- Budget: $50,000 - $500,000+ per event
- Decision Makers: VP/Director of Events, Internal Communications, HR, Marketing

**Event Needs:**
- All-hands meetings and town halls
- Sales kickoffs and training
- Product launches
- Leadership communications
- Employee engagement events
- Customer conferences

**Pain Points:**
- Inconsistent event quality
- Technical difficulties damaging credibility
- Low employee engagement
- Lack of production expertise internally
- Time constraints on internal teams

**Value Proposition:**
"Professional-grade virtual events that engage your employees and protect your brand—without burdening your internal team."

**Acquisition Strategies:**
1. **LinkedIn Outreach** - Target event leaders, internal communications
2. **Referral Partnerships** - AV companies, corporate meeting planners
3. **Content Marketing** - Articles on employee engagement, internal comms
4. **Speaking Engagements** - HR conferences, internal comms summits
5. **Case Studies** - Showcase similar enterprise clients

**Sales Cycle:** 4-12 weeks
**Average Deal Size:** $50,000 - $200,000

## Client Acquisition Funnel

### Stage 1: Awareness

**Goal:** Make target clients aware of our services

**Tactics:**
- Content marketing (blog, guides, case studies)
- Social media (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Speaking at industry events
- PR and media coverage
- SEO and paid search
- Webinars and workshops

**Metrics:**
- Website visitors
- Content downloads
- Social media followers
- Event attendees
- Media mentions

**Target:** 1,000+ qualified prospects in pipeline awareness stage

---

### Stage 2: Interest

**Goal:** Generate interest and capture leads

**Tactics:**
- Lead magnets (guides, templates, assessments)
- Webinar series on virtual event best practices
- Free consultations/discovery calls
- Email nurture campaigns
- Retargeting ads
- LinkedIn outreach

**Metrics:**
- Lead conversion rate
- Email open/click rates
- Consultation bookings
- Marketing qualified leads (MQLs)

**Target:** 200+ MQLs per quarter

---

### Stage 3: Consideration

**Goal:** Move prospects to active evaluation

**Tactics:**
- Discovery calls and needs assessment
- Custom proposals and demos
- Case studies and testimonials
- Platform demonstrations
- Reference calls
- Pilot projects (smaller events)

**Metrics:**
- Sales qualified leads (SQLs)
- Proposal acceptance rate
- Demo-to-proposal rate
- Average sales cycle length

**Target:** 50+ SQLs per quarter, 30% proposal rate

---

### Stage 4: Decision

**Goal:** Close the deal

**Tactics:**
- Proposal refinement
- Negotiation and contracting
- Executive alignment meetings
- Final presentations
- Contract execution
- Deposit collection

**Metrics:**
- Close rate
- Average deal size
- Sales cycle length
- Revenue per quarter

**Target:** 15-20 deals per quarter, $500K+ quarterly revenue

---

### Stage 5: Retention & Expansion

**Goal:** Retain clients and grow account value

**Tactics:**
- Exceptional event delivery
- Post-event reviews and optimization
- Quarterly business reviews
- Upsell additional services
- Referral requests
- Case study development

**Metrics:**
- Client retention rate
- Net revenue retention
- Referral rate
- Case studies produced

**Target:** 80%+ retention, 120%+ net revenue retention

## Sales Process

### Step 1: Discovery Call (30-45 minutes)

**Agenda:**
1. Introductions and background (5 min)
2. Event overview and goals (10 min)
3. Challenges and pain points (10 min)
4. Budget and timeline (5 min)
5. Next steps (5 min)

**Key Questions:**
- What type of event are you planning?
- What are your primary goals for this event?
- Who is your target audience?
- What's worked well (or not) in past events?
- What's your budget range?
- What's your timeline?
- Who else is involved in the decision?

**Outcome:**
- Qualify the opportunity
- Understand needs and pain points
- Set expectations for proposal
- Schedule follow-up

---

### Step 2: Needs Assessment (if needed)

**For Complex Events:**
- Additional stakeholder interviews
- Technical requirements gathering
- Platform evaluation
- Detailed scope definition

**Deliverable:**
- Requirements document
- Preliminary recommendations
- Budget range confirmation

---

### Step 3: Proposal Development (3-5 days)

**Proposal Components:**
1. Executive summary
2. Understanding of needs
3. Recommended approach
4. Scope of work
5. Timeline
6. Investment (pricing)
7. Case studies/testimonials
8. Terms and conditions
9. Next steps

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Present 2-3 options (good, better, best)
- Clear value articulation
- ROI framing where possible

---

### Step 4: Proposal Presentation (30-60 minutes)

**Agenda:**
1. Review of their needs (5 min)
2. Our recommended approach (15 min)
3. Proposal walkthrough (20 min)
4. Q&A (15 min)
5. Next steps (5 min)

**Best Practices:**
- Present to all decision makers
- Focus on value and outcomes
- Address objections proactively
- Create urgency (limited availability, etc.)
- Ask for the business

---

### Step 5: Negotiation & Closing

**Common Objections & Responses:**

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | "I understand budget is a concern. Let's discuss what's most important and see if we can adjust scope. Also, consider the ROI of [specific outcome]." |
| "We can do this internally" | "Absolutely, many teams start that way. What we bring is [expertise, time savings, quality, risk mitigation]. What would it cost to pull your team away from their core work?" |
| "We need to think about it" | "Of course. What specific questions or concerns do you have? I want to make sure you have everything you need to decide." |
| "We're talking to other vendors" | "That's smart—you should evaluate options. What criteria are most important in your decision? I'm confident we stand out on [differentiators]." |
| "Can you discount?" | "We price based on value delivered. That said, for [multi-event commitment/annual contract/etc.], we can offer [specific discount]." |

**Closing Techniques:**
- Assumptive close: "When should we schedule the kickoff?"
- Urgency: "We have limited availability in [month]. Should I hold that for you?"
- Trial close: "Does this approach make sense for your team?"
- Summary close: Review all agreed points, then ask for commitment

---

### Step 6: Onboarding

**After Contract Signed:**
1. Send welcome email with next steps
2. Collect deposit
3. Schedule kickoff meeting
4. Set up project management workspace
5. Introduce team
6. Begin discovery and planning

**Goal:** Start strong, build confidence, set tone for partnership
