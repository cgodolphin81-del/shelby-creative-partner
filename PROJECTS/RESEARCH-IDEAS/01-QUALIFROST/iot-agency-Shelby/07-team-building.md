# Team Building Guide

## Overview

Building the right team is critical to IoT agency success. This guide covers roles, hiring, compensation, and team structure for different growth stages.

---

- Key customer relationships
- Investor relations (if applicable)

- Final decision authority

**Qualifications:**
- 10+ years industry experience
- Previous leadership experience
- Strong network in target markets
- Business acumen

## Hiring Strategy

### Hiring Priorities by Stage

#### Stage 1: Foundation (0-5 employees)
1. **Founder(s)** - Sales + Technical expertise
2. **Senior Engineer** - Can do everything technical
3. **Field Technician** - Installation and support
4. **Project Manager** - Keep things organized
5. **Sales Rep** - Generate pipeline

#### Stage 2: Growth (5-20 employees)
1. **VP Sales** - Scale sales function
2. **Additional Engineers** - Handle increased workload
3. **Project Managers** - Manage multiple projects
4. **Marketing Manager** - Generate inbound leads
5. **Customer Success** - Retain and expand accounts

#### Stage 3: Scale (20-50 employees)
1. **Specialized Roles** - Architects, specialists
2. **Middle Management** - Directors, team leads
3. **Support Team** - Handle support volume
4. **Operations** - Finance, HR, admin
5. **Enterprise Sales** - Target large deals

### Interview Process

#### Standard Process (Individual Contributors)
1. **Phone Screen** (30 min) - Recruiter/Hiring Manager
2. **Technical Assessment** - Skills evaluation
3. **Technical Interview** (60 min) - Team members
4. **Behavioral Interview** (60 min) - Hiring Manager
5. **Final Interview** (30 min) - Leadership (if needed)
6. **Reference Checks** - 2-3 references
7. **Offer**

#### Executive Process
1. **Initial Conversation** (30 min) - CEO/Founder
2. **Deep Dive** (60-90 min) - Leadership team
3. **Case Study/Presentation** - Strategic thinking
4. **Team Interviews** - Direct reports, peers
5. **Final Interview** - CEO/Board
6. **Reference Checks** - 3-5 references
7. **Offer + Negotiation**

## Compensation & Benefits

### Salary Benchmarking

**Sources:**
- Glassdoor
- Payscale
- LinkedIn Salary
- Industry surveys
- Recruiter data

**Review Frequency:** Annual
**Adjustment Timing:** Q1 (post-performance reviews)

---

### Benefits Package

#### Standard Benefits
- **Health Insurance:** Medical, dental, vision (company pays 70-80%)
- **Retirement:** 401(k) with 3-4% match
- **PTO:** 15-20 days + holidays
- **Sick Leave:** 10 days
- **Life Insurance:** 1x salary
- **Disability:** Short and long-term

#### Additional Perks
- **Flexible Work:** Hybrid remote options
- **Professional Development:** $2K-5K/year per employee
- **Certifications:** Paid + bonus for completion
- **Equipment:** Laptop, phone, home office stipend
- **Team Events:** Quarterly offsites, team dinners
- **Stock Options:** For key employees (Years 2+)

#### Commission Structures

**Sales Commission:**
```
Base Commission: 8-12% of gross profit
Accelerators:
- 100-125% of quota: 1.25x commission
- 125-150% of quota: 1.5x commission
- 150%+ of quota: 2x commission

SPIFFs (Sales Performance Incentive Fund):
- New logo bonus: $1K-5K
- Product-specific bonus: Additional 2-5%
- Quarterly accelerator: Extra 2-5%
```

**Project Bonus:**
```
Project Manager Bonus:
- On-time delivery: 5% of bonus pool
- On-budget delivery: 5% of bonus pool
- Client satisfaction >4.5: 5% of bonus pool
- Margin target achieved: 5% of bonus pool

Engineering Bonus:
- Project completion: 2-5% of project margin
- Quality metrics: Additional 2-3%
```

## Training & Development

### Onboarding Program

#### Week 1: Orientation
- Company overview and culture
- Team introductions
- Systems and tools training
- Product/service training
- Safety training (field roles)

#### Week 2-3: Role-Specific Training
- Shadowing experienced team members
- Hands-on practice
- Process and procedure training
- Initial assignments

#### Week 4: Integration
- First independent work
- 30-day check-in
- Goal setting
- Mentor assignment

#### 90-Day Plan
- Clear milestones
- Regular check-ins (weekly → bi-weekly)
- 90-day review
- Full integration

## Performance Management

### Review Cycle

**Annual Reviews:**
- Self-assessment
- Manager assessment
- 360 feedback (for leaders)
- Goal setting for next year
- Compensation discussion

**Quarterly Check-ins:**
- Progress on goals
- Feedback exchange
- Course correction
- Support needed

### Performance Metrics

**Individual KPIs by Role:**

| Role | Key Metrics |
|------|-------------|
| Sales | Revenue, pipeline, close rate, activity |
| Engineering | Project completion, quality, customer satisfaction |
| PM | On-time, on-budget, margin, satisfaction |
| Support | Response time, resolution time, CSAT |
| Field | Install quality, efficiency, customer feedback |

### Performance Improvement

**PIP Process:**
1. Document performance gaps
2. Create improvement plan (30-60-90 days)
3. Weekly check-ins
4. Support and resources provided
5. Final evaluation
6. Decision: improve or terminate

## Contractor vs. Employee

### When to Use Contractors

**Good for:**
- Specialized expertise (short-term)
- Overflow work
- Geographic coverage
- Trial before hire
- Project-based work

**Not Good for:**
- Core competencies
- Long-term needs
- Customer-facing (primary)
- Confidential work

### Contractor Management

- Clear SOW and expectations
- Regular check-ins
- Quality reviews
- Knowledge transfer plan
- Compliance (1099 vs. W-2)
