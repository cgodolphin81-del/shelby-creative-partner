# 30-Day PR Launch Plan

## Overview

This 30-day plan provides a structured approach to launching a PR program, building momentum, and securing early wins. Designed for new clients or companies launching a dedicated PR function.

---

## Pre-Launch Checklist (Before Day 1)

- [ ] Contract signed and onboarding complete
- [ ] Message house approved
- [ ] Spokespeople identified and available
- [ ] Media list built (minimum 100 contacts)
- [ ] Press kit complete and accessible
- [ ] Press release drafted (if applicable)
- [ ] Approval workflow established
- [ ] Tracking systems set up (coverage tracker, analytics)

---

## Week 1: Foundation & Setup (Days 1-7)

### Day 1: Internal Alignment

**Morning:**
- [ ] Team kickoff meeting (internal)
- [ ] Review client goals and objectives
- [ ] Assign roles and responsibilities
- [ ] Set up project management board (Asana, Trello, etc.)
- [ ] Create shared folders (Google Drive, Dropbox)

**Afternoon:**
- [ ] Review all onboarding materials
- [ ] Audit existing media assets
- [ ] Identify gaps in press kit
- [ ] Begin media list expansion

**Deliverables:**
- Project plan with milestones
- Contact list for both teams
- Shared workspace established

---

### Day 2: Media List Finalization

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Verify all media contacts (email, beat, outlet)
- [ ] Segment list by tier and beat
- [ ] Add 25-50 new contacts to reach 150 total
- [ ] Research journalist pitch preferences
- [ ] Document personalization angles for top 20 journalists

**Media List Goals:**
- Tier 1 (National): 30 contacts
- Tier 2 (Trade/Regional): 70 contacts
- Tier 3 (Blogs/Local): 50 contacts
- **Total: 150 verified contacts**

**Deliverables:**
- Finalized media list in CRM/spreadsheet
- Segmentation tags applied
- Personalization notes added

---

### Day 3: Message Refinement

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Review message house with fresh eyes
- [ ] Test messages with internal team
- [ ] Refine soundbites for memorability
- [ ] Create message variations for different audiences
- [ ] Develop Q&A document for common questions

**Message Testing:**
Ask team members:
1. "What's the main takeaway?"
2. "What would you remember from this?"
3. "What questions do you have?"

**Deliverables:**
- Refined message house (v2.0)
- Audience-specific message variations
- Initial Q&A document

---

### Day 4: Press Release Finalization

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Complete press release draft (if applicable)
- [ ] Internal review and edits
- [ ] Client review and feedback
- [ ] Incorporate feedback
- [ ] Final approval
- [ ] Prepare distribution list

**Press Release Checklist:**
- [ ] Compelling headline (under 100 characters)
- [ ] Strong lead paragraph (who, what, when, where, why)
- [ ] Supporting quotes (CEO + customer/partner if possible)
- [ ] Key facts and statistics
- [ ] Clear call-to-action
- [ ] Company boilerplate
- [ ] Media contact information
- [ ] ### end mark

**Deliverables:**
- Approved press release
- Distribution list
- Wire service booking (if using)

---

### Day 5: Pitch Development

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Develop 3 pitch email variations
- [ ] Create personalization templates
- [ ] Write subject lines (test 5 variations)
- [ ] Prepare follow-up email templates
- [ ] Set up email tracking (if using tool)

**Pitch Email Structure:**
```
Subject: [Personalized, under 50 characters]

Hi [First Name],

[Hook: Reference their work or explain relevance]

[Context: What's happening, why now]

[Proof: Data, traction, credibility]

[Ask: What you're requesting]

[Offer: Exclusive, interview, assets]

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Deliverables:**
- 3 pitch email variations
- Follow-up templates (2 versions)
- Subject line options
- Email tracking configured

---

### Day 6-7: Preparation & Buffer

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Catch up on any incomplete items
- [ ] Prepare interview briefing templates
- [ ] Set up media monitoring alerts
- [ ] Test all technology (email, tracking, etc.)
- [ ] Weekend: No outreach (journalists don't work weekends)

**Deliverables:**
- All systems tested and ready
- Monitoring alerts active
- Team briefed and ready

## Week 2: Launch & Initial Outreach (Days 8-14)

### Day 8: Soft Launch (Exclusives)

**Strategy:** Offer exclusives to 3-5 top-tier journalists before broader outreach

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Identify 3-5 exclusive targets
- [ ] Send personalized exclusive pitch emails
- [ ] Offer 24-48 hour embargo
- [ ] Be available for immediate follow-up calls
- [ ] Track responses

**Exclusive Pitch Approach:**
```
Subject: Exclusive: [Compelling news hook] for [Publication]

Hi [Name],

I'm reaching out with an exclusive opportunity for [Publication] before 
we announce broadly tomorrow.

[2-3 sentences on why this matters to THEIR audience]

We'd love to offer you first look, including:
• CEO interview
• Early data/access
• Customer references

Interested in discussing?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Success Metrics:**
- 1-2 exclusives secured
- Relationships initiated with top-tier journalists

---

### Day 9: Press Release Distribution

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Distribute press release via wire service (if using)
- [ ] Send embargoed releases to exclusive journalists
- [ ] Post to company newsroom
- [ ] Share on company social channels
- [ ] Notify internal team
- [ ] Monitor for pickup

**Distribution Timing:**
- Wire service: 8:00 AM ET
- Direct pitches: 9:00-10:00 AM ET
- Social: 10:00 AM ET

**Deliverables:**
- Press release distributed
- Social posts scheduled
- Internal announcement sent

---

### Day 10-11: Broad Pitch Campaign (Wave 1)

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Send pitch emails to Tier 1 journalists (30 contacts)
- [ ] Personalize each email (reference recent work)
- [ ] Track opens and responses
- [ ] Begin follow-up on exclusives
- [ ] Log all responses in tracker

**Daily Goals:**
- Day 10: Pitch 30 Tier 1 contacts
- Day 11: Pitch 40 Tier 2 contacts

**Response Handling:**
- Positive response: Schedule interview within 48 hours
- Interested but busy: Follow up in 1 week
- Not interested: Log and move on
- No response: Follow up in 3 days

---

### Day 12: Follow-Up (First Wave)

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Follow up with all non-responders from Days 10-11
- [ ] Send second follow-up to exclusive pitches if no response
- [ ] Schedule confirmed interviews
- [ ] Prepare interview briefs for spokespeople
- [ ] Continue monitoring coverage

**Follow-Up Email Template:**
```
Subject: Re: [Original subject]

Hi [Name],

Just floating this to the top of your inbox in case it got buried.

Quick recap: [One sentence on the news].

Still happy to arrange [interview/demo/exclusive] if interested.

No pressure if not a fit—just let me know and I won't follow up again.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Interview Day Checklist:**
- [ ] Brief spokesperson (30 min before)
- [ ] Test technology (15 min before)
- [ ] Join call 5 minutes early
- [ ] Take notes during interview
- [ ] Send thank-you within 24 hours
- [ ] Log interview details
- [ ] Monitor for publication

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: Low Response Rates

**Solutions:**
- Improve subject lines (A/B test)
- Increase personalization
- Refine news angle
- Try different journalist segments
- Follow up more persistently (but respectfully)

### Challenge: No Tier 1 Placements

**Solutions:**
- Focus on exclusives
- Develop stronger data/trends angle
- Leverage customer stories
- Try contributed articles instead of news
- Build relationships before pitching

### Challenge: Spokesperson Availability

**Solutions:**
- Block interview times on calendar
- Identify backup spokespersons
- Offer written Q&A as alternative
- Record evergreen content in advance
- Set clear expectations with client

### Challenge: Negative or Off-Target Coverage

**Solutions:**
- Provide clearer briefing materials
- Offer pre-interview with comms team
- Correct inaccuracies politely and quickly
- Learn from misfires for next time
- Consider if story should be pitched differently

## Post-30-Day: Sustaining Momentum

### Month 2-3 Focus Areas

1. **Deepen Relationships:** Move from transactional to relational with key journalists
2. **Expand Angles:** Develop 2-3 new story angles beyond launch
3. **Thought Leadership:** Pitch bylined articles and speaking opportunities
4. **Optimize:** Double down on what's working, cut what's not
5. **Measure:** Connect PR to business outcomes (traffic, leads, pipeline)

- Maintain 20%+ Tier 1 mix

- Drive measurable website/conversion impact
