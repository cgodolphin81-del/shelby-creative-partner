# Media List Building Guide

## Overview

A quality media list is the foundation of successful PR. This guide covers how to find, verify, and maintain journalist contacts that actually respond.

---

## Section 1: Finding Journalists

### 1.1 Target Identification Framework

**Before you search, define:**
- Industry/vertical (tech, healthcare, finance, consumer, etc.)
- Geography (local, regional, national, international)
- Outlet type (trade publications, mainstream media, blogs, podcasts)
- Audience size/reach requirements
- Beat specificity (AI, fintech, sustainability, etc.)

### 1.2 Primary Research Methods

#### Manual Research (Highest Quality)
1. **Google News Search**
   - Search: `"[your industry]" + "writer" OR "reporter" OR "journalist"`
   - Search: `site:forbes.com "[topic]"` to find authors covering your space
   - Search recent articles and note bylines

2. **Outlet Websites**
   - Visit "Masthead" or "Team" pages
   - Look for "Contact Us" with beat assignments
   - Check author profile pages for contact info

3. **LinkedIn**
   - Search: `Journalist AND [beat] AND [publication]`
   - Filter by current company
   - Note: Many list email in contact info

4. **Twitter/X**
   - Search: `"[publication]" journalist`
   - Look for bios with "DMs open for tips"
   - Many list email or Substack links

## Section 2: Verification Process

### 2.1 Contact Verification Checklist

**✅ Must Verify:**
- [ ] Currently employed at listed outlet (check recent bylines)
- [ ] Still covers relevant beat (beats change!)
- [ ] Email format correct (test with email verifier)
- [ ] Not moved to different role/publication
- [ ] Accepts pitches (some explicitly say "no pitches")

**⚠️ Red Flags:**
- No bylines in 6+ months (may have left)
- LinkedIn shows different current employer
- Email bounces on verification
- Bio says "not accepting pitches"
- Only accepts submissions via form

### 2.2 Verification Tools

| Tool | Purpose | Cost |
|------|---------|------|
| Hunter.io Email Verifier | Email validation | Free tier available |
| NeverBounce | Bulk email verification | $0.008/email |
| ZeroBounce | Email verification + spam trap detection | $0.008/email |
| Muck Rack | Journalist verification | $$$ |
| LinkedIn Sales Navigator | Employment verification | $$ |

### 2.3 Verification Workflow

```
1. Find contact → 2. Check recent bylines (last 90 days)
                 → 3. Verify email format
                 → 4. Cross-reference LinkedIn
                 → 5. Check Twitter for activity
                 → 6. Add to list with verification date
                 → 7. Set 90-day re-verification reminder
```

### 3.3 Segmentation Strategy

**Segment by:**
1. **Tier Level**
   - Tier 1: Top-tier national (NYT, WSJ, Forbes, etc.)
   - Tier 2: Industry trade publications
   - Tier 3: Regional/local, blogs, podcasts

2. **Beat Specificity**
   - Primary beat (exact match)
   - Secondary beat (adjacent coverage)
   - General assignment

3. **Relationship Status**
   - Cold (never contacted)
   - Warm (1-2 interactions)
   - Hot (responded positively)
   - VIP (regular contact, covers you)

## Section 4: Maintenance & Hygiene

### 4.1 Update Schedule

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Verify all contacts | Every 90 days |
| Add new journalists | Weekly |
| Remove bounced emails | Immediately |
| Update beat changes | As discovered |
| Review response rates | Monthly |

### 4.2 Churn Management

**Journalists move frequently. Track:**
- Who left their publication (note new outlet)
- Who changed beats
- Who left journalism entirely
- New journalists joining target outlets

**Set up Google Alerts:**
- `"[Journalist Name]" + "joins"`
- `"[Publication]" + "new reporter"`
- `"[Publication]" + "masthead"`

### 4.3 List Hygiene Best Practices

1. **Never buy static lists** — they decay 30% annually
2. **Always verify before major campaigns**
3. **Track bounces immediately** — remove after 2 hard bounces
4. **Respect opt-outs** — maintain suppression list
5. **Document everything** — notes on interactions matter

## Section 5: Outreach Best Practices

### 5.1 Before You Pitch

- [ ] Read 3+ recent articles by this journalist
- [ ] Understand their angle/style
- [ ] Verify they cover your topic
- [ ] Check if they have pitch preferences
- [ ] Ensure your story is newsworthy (not promotional)

### 5.2 Personalization Requirements

**Minimum personalization:**
- Reference specific recent article
- Explain why it's relevant to THEIR beat
- Show you understand their audience
- Keep it under 200 words

**Never:**
- Mass BCC emails
- Obvious copy/paste with wrong name
- Attach press releases without asking
- Follow up more than twice
- Pitch on Monday morning or Friday afternoon
