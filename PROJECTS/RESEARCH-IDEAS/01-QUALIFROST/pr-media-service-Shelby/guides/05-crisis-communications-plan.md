# Crisis Communications Plan

## Overview

This plan prepares [Company Name] to respond quickly, effectively, and consistently to crises that could damage reputation, operations, or stakeholder trust.

---

## Section 1: Crisis Classification

### Crisis Levels

| Level | Description | Response Time | Decision Makers |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Level 1: Minor** | Limited impact, easily contained | 4 hours | Communications Lead |
| **Level 2: Moderate** | Media attention, customer concern | 2 hours | Comms + Legal + CEO |
| **Level 3: Major** | Significant reputation/financial risk | 30 minutes | Full Crisis Team |
| **Level 4: Severe** | Existential threat, regulatory action | 15 minutes | Board + Executive Team |

### Crisis Types

#### Category A: Product/Service Issues
- Product malfunction or defect
- Service outage affecting customers
- Data breach or security incident
- Safety concerns

#### Category B: People Issues
- Executive misconduct
- Employee lawsuit or complaint
- Workplace safety incident
- Harassment/discrimination allegations

#### Category C: Business Issues
- Financial irregularities
- Bankruptcy/funding crisis
- Major customer loss
- Regulatory investigation

#### Category D: External Issues
- Natural disaster affecting operations
- Supply chain disruption
- Partner/vendor scandal
- Negative viral social media

## Section 3: Response Protocols

### Phase 1: Immediate Response (First 2 Hours)

**Hour 0-1: Assess & Activate**
- [ ] Confirm facts of the situation
- [ ] Determine crisis level (1-4)
- [ ] Activate crisis team (call/Slack)
- [ ] Open crisis communication channel
- [ ] Assign note-taker/documenter
- [ ] Begin incident log

**Hour 1-2: Initial Response**
- [ ] Draft holding statement (see templates)
- [ ] Legal review of statement
- [ ] CEO approves statement
- [ ] Post to company website/newsroom
- [ ] Prepare internal employee communication
- [ ] Monitor social media and news
- [ ] Document all inquiries

### Phase 2: Active Management (Hours 2-48)

**Ongoing Actions:**
- [ ] Hold crisis team check-ins (every 2-4 hours)
- [ ] Update holding statement as facts emerge
- [ ] Respond to media inquiries
- [ ] Monitor sentiment and coverage
- [ ] Coordinate customer communications
- [ ] Prepare Q&A document
- [ ] Brief board of directors
- [ ] Document all decisions and communications

**Media Response:**
- [ ] Designate single spokesperson
- [ ] Route all inquiries through communications
- [ ] Log all media contacts
- [ ] Respond within 2 hours during business hours
- [ ] Prepare for interviews if warranted

### Phase 3: Resolution & Recovery (Day 3+)

**Actions:**
- [ ] Develop long-term communication plan
- [ ] Implement remediation measures
- [ ] Communicate progress to stakeholders
- [ ] Plan post-crisis reputation repair
- [ ] Conduct after-action review
- [ ] Update crisis plan based on learnings
- [ ] Resume normal communications cadence

**A:** [Timeline - only share confirmed facts]

**A:** [Scope - be specific if known, general if not]

**A:** [Actions being taken - show proactivity]

**A:** [Preventive measures - concrete steps]

**A:** [Accountability - avoid blaming individuals prematurely]

## Section 7: Social Media Response Guidelines

### Response Matrix

| Situation | Response Approach | Timing |
|-----------|------------------|--------|
| **Misinformation** | Correct with facts, link to official statement | Within 1 hour |
| **Customer Complaint** | Acknowledge, move to DM, resolve | Within 2 hours |
| **Viral Negative Content** | Pause scheduled posts, monitor, respond per crisis plan | Immediate |
| **Positive Support** | Amplify, thank supporters | Ongoing |
| **Troll/Spam** | No engagement, document, report if necessary | N/A |

### Social Media Holding Statement

```
We are aware of [situation] and are looking into it. We will share more 
information as soon as we have it. Thank you for your patience.

[Link to full statement on website]
```

### Social Media Blackout Guidelines

**When to pause scheduled content:**
- Level 3 or 4 crisis
- Any crisis involving customer harm
- Executive misconduct
- Legal/regulatory issues

**Pause duration:** Until crisis team approves resumption

**Exceptions:**
- Critical service announcements
- Previously committed customer communications
- Legal/regulatory required posts

### Reputation Recovery Strategies

**Short-term (Weeks 1-4):**
- Continue transparent communication
- Deliver on all commitments made during crisis
- Engage with concerned stakeholders directly
- Monitor sentiment closely

**Medium-term (Months 1-3):**
- Launch positive initiatives aligned with values
- Secure third-party endorsements
- Publish progress updates
- Rebuild media relationships

**Long-term (Months 3-12):**
- Integrate lessons into company culture
- Update policies and procedures
- Measure reputation recovery
- Share learnings (if appropriate)
