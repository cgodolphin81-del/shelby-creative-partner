# Sustainability Frameworks Comparison Guide

## Overview

This guide compares the major sustainability and climate frameworks to help clients select the right standards for their needs.

---

## Framework Comparison Matrix

| Framework | Primary Focus | Mandatory/Voluntary | Best For | Reporting Frequency | Assurance Required |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| GHG Protocol | Carbon accounting | Voluntary | All organizations | Annual | Recommended |
| SBTi | Target setting | Voluntary (commitment) | Science-based targets | Annual progress | Initial validation |
| TCFD | Climate risk | Mandatory (many jurisdictions) | Risk disclosure | Annual | Recommended |
| GRI | ESG reporting | Voluntary/Mandatory (varies) | Sustainability reporting | Annual | Recommended |
| B-Corp | Holistic impact | Voluntary (certification) | Stakeholder governance | Every 3 years | Required (audit) |
| CSRD/ESRS | ESG disclosure | Mandatory (EU) | EU companies/large non-EU | Annual | Required (limited → reasonable) |
| ISSB/IFRS S1/S2 | Sustainability disclosure | Mandatory (adopting jurisdictions) | Investor-focused reporting | Annual | Required |
| CDP | Environmental disclosure | Voluntary (investor-driven) | Supply chain, investors | Annual | No |
| SASB | Industry-specific ESG | Voluntary/Mandatory (varies) | Investor communications | Annual | Recommended |

## 1. GHG Protocol

### Overview
The Gold Standard for corporate greenhouse gas accounting.

### Key Features
- **Scope Definition**: Clear Scope 1, 2, 3 boundaries
- **Accounting Principles**: Relevance, completeness, consistency, accuracy, transparency
- **Standards Suite**:
  - Corporate Standard (organizational inventory)
  - Scope 2 Guidance (market vs. location-based)
  - Scope 3 Standard (value chain emissions)
  - Product Standard (lifecycle assessment)
  - Project Protocol (offset projects)

### Requirements
- Organizational boundary setting (equity share, financial control, operational control)
- Base year establishment and recalculation policy
- Emissions quantification using appropriate factors
- Consolidation approach documentation
- Quality assurance procedures

### Strengths
- ✅ Most widely adopted standard globally
- ✅ Clear, detailed methodology
- ✅ Compatible with other frameworks
- ✅ Free guidance and tools
- ✅ Regular updates and sector guidance

### Limitations
- ❌ Doesn't set targets (use with SBTi)
- ❌ Doesn't address climate risk (use with TCFD)
- ❌ Scope 3 can be complex and data-intensive
- ❌ Some sectors need additional guidance

### Best Paired With
- SBTi (for targets)
- TCFD (for risk disclosure)
- CDP (for disclosure platform)

### Ideal Clients
- Any organization starting carbon accounting
- Companies preparing for mandatory reporting
- Supply chain leaders needing Scope 3 methodology

## 2. Science Based Targets Initiative (SBTi)

### Overview
Validates corporate emissions reduction targets aligned with climate science.

### Key Features
- **Target Types**:
  - Near-term targets (5-10 years, 1.5°C aligned)
  - Net-zero targets (by 2050, 90-95% reduction + neutralization)
  - Long-term targets (post-2030 for some sectors)
- **Scope Coverage**: Scope 1, 2, and 3 (if >40% of total)
- **Sector Pathways**: Industry-specific decarbonization pathways
- **FLAG Guidance**: Forest, Land, and Agriculture sector guidance

### Requirements
- Commitment letter to SBTi
- Target development (1.5°C aligned)
- Validation submission and fee
- Annual progress reporting
- Net-zero Standard compliance for net-zero claims

### Target Criteria (1.5°C Aligned)
| Scope | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| Scope 1+2 | 4.2% annual reduction (absolute) or intensity |
| Scope 3 | 2.5% annual reduction if >40% of total |
| Renewable Energy | 100% by 2030 for near-term (RE100 alignment) |
| Net-Zero | 90-95% reduction by 2050, neutralize residual |

### Strengths
- ✅ Science-based credibility
- ✅ Clear target criteria
- ✅ Growing investor and customer recognition
- ✅ Sector-specific pathways
- ✅ Net-zero Standard provides long-term clarity

### Limitations
- ❌ Validation fees can be high for SMEs
- ❌ Process can take 6-12 months
- ❌ Scope 3 targets can be challenging
- ❌ Limited flexibility for unique circumstances
- ❌ Net-zero Standard is demanding

### Best Paired With
- GHG Protocol (for accounting)
- TCFD (for transition planning)
- CDP (for disclosure)

### Ideal Clients
- Companies with significant emissions
- Organizations seeking credibility
- Supply chain leaders (customer requirements)
- Pre-IPO companies (investor expectations)

## 3. Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD)

### Overview
Framework for disclosing climate-related financial risks and opportunities.

### Key Features
- **Four Pillars**:
  1. Governance (board oversight, management role)
  2. Strategy (risks, opportunities, scenarios, impact)
  3. Risk Management (identification, assessment, integration)
  4. Metrics & Targets (emissions, targets, performance)
- **Scenario Analysis**: 1.5°C, 2°C, 3°C+ scenarios
- **Financial Impact**: Quantification where possible

### Requirements (Mandatory in Many Jurisdictions)
- UK (listed companies, large private)
- EU (CSRD incorporates TCFD)
- Switzerland (large companies)
- New Zealand (financial institutions)
- Japan (listed companies)
- Brazil (listed companies)
- Singapore (listed companies)
- Canada (federally regulated entities)
- US (SEC Climate Rule - pending)

### Strengths
- ✅ Focuses on financial materiality
- ✅ Widely recognized by investors
- ✅ Flexible implementation
- ✅ Compatible with other frameworks
- ✅ Becoming mandatory globally

### Limitations
- ❌ Can be qualitative without strong data
- ❌ Scenario analysis is complex
- ❌ Financial quantification is challenging
- ❌ Less prescriptive than some frameworks

### Best Paired With
- GHG Protocol (for metrics)
- SBTi (for targets and transition plan)
- ISSB (for investor reporting)

### Ideal Clients
- Public companies
- Financial institutions
- Companies with climate-exposed assets
- Organizations in mandatory jurisdictions

## 4. Global Reporting Initiative (GRI)

### Overview
Most widely used sustainability reporting standard globally.

### Key Features
- **Modular Structure**:
  - Universal Standards (GRI 1, 2, 3)
  - Sector Standards (industry-specific)
  - Topic Standards (30+ topics)
- **Double Materiality**: Impact and financial materiality
- **Topic Standards Include**:
  - GRI 305: Emissions
  - GRI 302: Energy
  - GRI 303: Water
  - GRI 401-419: Social topics
  - GRI 201-206: Economic topics

### Requirements
- Materiality assessment process
- Stakeholder engagement
- Boundary setting
- Topic-specific disclosures
- Index and content index

### Strengths
- ✅ Most comprehensive ESG framework
- ✅ Global recognition and adoption
- ✅ Free standards
- ✅ Compatible with other frameworks
- ✅ Strong stakeholder focus
- ✅ Detailed topic guidance

### Limitations
- ❌ Can be overwhelming for first-time reporters
- ❌ Less investor-focused than SASB/ISSB
- ❌ Extensive disclosure requirements
- ❌ Quality varies without assurance

### Best Paired With
- SASB (for investor materiality)
- TCFD (for climate-specific)
- UN SDGs (for alignment)

### Ideal Clients
- Large corporations
- Companies with diverse stakeholders
- Organizations seeking comprehensive reporting
- CSRD reporters (GRI-ESRS aligned)

## 5. B-Corp Certification

### Overview
Holistic certification measuring overall social and environmental performance.

### Key Features
- **B Impact Assessment (BIA)**: 200+ questions across 5 areas
  - Governance (mission, ethics, transparency)
  - Workers (compensation, benefits, development)
  - Community (diversity, engagement, giving)
  - Environment (emissions, water, waste, supply chain)
  - Customers (stewardship, data privacy)
- **Certification Requirements**:
  - Minimum 80 points on BIA
  - Legal requirement (stakeholder governance)
  - Transparency (public BIA profile)
  - Recertification every 3 years

### Scoring Breakdown (Typical)
| Area | Points Available | Typical Certified Score |
|------|-----------------|------------------------|
| Governance | ~35 | 20-25 |
| Workers | ~55 | 35-45 |
| Community | ~45 | 25-35 |
| Environment | ~45 | 30-40 |
| Customers | ~20 | 10-15 |
| **Total** | **~200** | **80+ to certify** |

### Strengths
- ✅ Holistic impact measurement
- ✅ Strong brand and consumer recognition
- ✅ Community of certified businesses
- ✅ Legal accountability
- ✅ Continuous improvement model

### Limitations
- ❌ Certification fees ($2K-50K+ based on revenue)
- ❌ Time-intensive process (6-12 months)
- ❌ Not all industries score well
- ❌ Recertification burden
- ❌ Less focused on climate specifically

### Best Paired With
- SBTi (for climate targets)
- GRI (for reporting)
- 1% for the Planet (for giving)

### Ideal Clients
- Mission-driven companies
- SMEs seeking differentiation
- Consumer-facing brands
- Companies valuing stakeholder governance

## 6. CSRD / ESRS (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive)

### Overview
EU mandatory sustainability reporting for ~50,000 companies.

### Key Features
- **Scope**:
  - All EU listed companies
  - Large EU companies (2 of 3: 250+ employees, €20M+ assets, €40M+ revenue)
  - Non-EU companies with €150M+ EU revenue
- **ESRS Standards**:
  - ESRS 1-2: General requirements
  - ESRS E1-5: Environmental (climate, pollution, water, biodiversity, circular)
  - ESRS S1-4: Social (workforce, value chain, communities, consumers)
  - ESRS G1: Governance
- **Double Materiality**: Impact + financial materiality
- **Digital Tagging**: XBRL/electronic format
- **Assurance**: Limited (2024) → Reasonable (2028)

### Timeline
| Year | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 2024 | Report on 2025 data (existing NFRD companies) |
| 2025 | Report on 2026 data (large companies) |
| 2026 | Report on 2027 data (listed SMEs, small non-EU) |
| 2028 | Reasonable assurance required |

### Strengths
- ✅ Comprehensive and detailed
- ✅ Mandatory (level playing field)
- ✅ Double materiality approach
- ✅ Value chain coverage
- ✅ Digital, machine-readable

### Limitations
- ❌ Extremely detailed (1,000+ data points)
- ❌ Complex implementation
- ❌ High compliance costs
- ❌ Assurance requirements add cost
- ❌ Extraterritorial reach creates complexity

### Best Paired With
- GRI (aligned standards)
- TCFD (climate disclosure)
- GHG Protocol (emissions)

### Ideal Clients
- EU companies in scope
- Non-EU companies with significant EU presence
- Supply chain partners to EU companies

## 7. ISSB / IFRS S1 & S2

### Overview
Global baseline for investor-focused sustainability disclosure.

### Key Features
- **IFRS S1**: General sustainability disclosure
  - Governance, strategy, risk management, metrics/targets
  - Materiality definition (investor-focused)
  - Connectivity with financial statements
- **IFRS S2**: Climate-specific (incorporates TCFD)
  - Cross-industry metrics
  - Industry-specific metrics (SASB-based)
  - Scenario analysis requirements

### Requirements
- Materiality assessment (investor lens)
- Integration with financial reporting
- Scenario analysis for climate
- Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions (GHG Protocol)
- Transition plan disclosure
- Industry-specific metrics

### Strengths
- ✅ Investor-focused (financial materiality)
- ✅ Global baseline (jurisdictional adoption)
- ✅ Builds on TCFD and SASB
- ✅ Connected to financial statements
- ✅ Supported by major investors

### Limitations
- ❌ Less comprehensive on social topics
- ❌ Jurisdictional adoption varies
- ❌ Still evolving (interpretation guidance)
- ❌ Less stakeholder-focused than GRI

### Best Paired With
- TCFD (already incorporated)
- SASB (industry metrics)
- GRI (for broader stakeholder reporting)

### Ideal Clients
- Public companies
- Companies seeking investor credibility
- Jurisdictions adopting ISSB
- Financial institutions

## 8. CDP (Carbon Disclosure Project)

### Overview
Global environmental disclosure platform used by investors and supply chain programs.

### Key Features
- **Questionnaires**:
  - Climate Change (most common)
  - Water Security
  - Forests
- **Scoring**: A (Leadership) to D- (Disclosure)
- **Integration**: Linked to TCFD, SBTi, GHG Protocol
- **Supply Chain Program**: Customer-driven supplier disclosure

### Requirements
- Annual disclosure via CDP platform
- Climate questionnaire (~100 questions)
- Emissions data (Scope 1, 2, 3)
- Targets and actions
- Governance and risk disclosure

### Strengths
- ✅ Single platform for multiple stakeholders
- ✅ Investor and customer recognition
- ✅ Benchmarking against peers
- ✅ Free for companies to disclose
- ✅ Drives supplier engagement

### Limitations
- ❌ Can be questionnaire-heavy
- ❌ Score doesn't always reflect performance
- ❌ Platform can be complex
- ❌ Primarily environmental (less social/governance)

### Best Paired With
- TCFD (aligned disclosure)
- SBTi (targets recognition)
- GHG Protocol (emissions data)

### Ideal Clients
- Companies with investor pressure
- Suppliers to CDP-member customers
- Organizations seeking benchmarking
- Public companies

## Framework Selection Guide

### By Company Type

| Company Type | Priority Frameworks |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Startup/SME | GHG Protocol, B-Corp (optional) |
| Growth Company | GHG Protocol, SBTi, CDP |
| Public Company | GHG Protocol, SBTi, TCFD, ISSB, CDP |
| EU Large Company | CSRD/ESRS, GHG Protocol, SBTi, TCFD |
| Multinational | All relevant frameworks by jurisdiction |
| Financial Institution | TCFD, ISSB, CDP, PCAF (financed emissions) |
| Manufacturing | GHG Protocol, SBTi, ISO 14001, CDP |
| Consumer Goods | B-Corp, GRI, SBTi, supply chain standards |

### By Driver

| Driver | Frameworks |
|--------|------------|
| Regulatory Compliance | CSRD, SEC Rule, TCFD (mandatory jurisdictions) |
| Investor Pressure | ISSB, TCFD, CDP, SBTi |
| Customer Requirements | CDP Supply Chain, SBTi, carbon accounting |
| Brand/Consumer | B-Corp, GRI, Climate Neutral |
| Risk Management | TCFD, climate scenario analysis |
| Operational Efficiency | GHG Protocol, ISO 14001 |

## Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-6)
1. GHG Protocol carbon accounting (Scope 1, 2)
2. Materiality assessment
3. Governance structure

### Phase 2: Target Setting (Months 6-12)
1. SBTi commitment and target development
2. Scope 3 assessment
3. Reduction strategy

### Phase 3: Disclosure (Months 12-18)
1. Sustainability report (GRI or CSRD)
2. CDP disclosure
3. TCFD/ISSB alignment

### Phase 4: Optimization (Months 18+)
1. Certification (B-Corp, etc.)
2. Supply chain engagement
3. Continuous improvement
