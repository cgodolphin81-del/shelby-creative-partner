# Sustainability Assessment Process

## Overview

End-to-end methodology for conducting sustainability assessments, from baseline audit through reduction roadmap development.

---

## Phase 1: Baseline Audit

### Objective
Establish current state sustainability performance across environmental, social, and governance dimensions.

### Timeline: 8-12 Weeks

---

### Week 1-2: Project Initiation

#### 1.1 Kickoff & Stakeholder Alignment
**Activities:**
- Project kickoff meeting with leadership team
- Identify key stakeholders and working group
- Define project scope and boundaries
- Establish communication protocols
- Review existing sustainability efforts

**Deliverables:**
- Project charter
- Stakeholder map
- Communication plan
- Data request list

**Key Questions:**
- What are the primary drivers for this assessment?
- What previous sustainability work exists?
- Who are the decision-makers and influencers?
- What are the timeline and budget constraints?

#### 1.2 Boundary Setting
**Organizational Boundary:**
- Determine consolidation approach (operational, financial, equity share)
- List all entities, facilities, and operations
- Identify joint ventures and partnerships
- Define reporting boundary for each framework

**Operational Boundary:**
- Scope 1: Direct emissions sources
- Scope 2: Purchased energy
- Scope 3: Value chain categories (screening level)

**Deliverables:**
- Boundary documentation
- Organizational chart with in-scope entities
- Facility list with locations and operations

---

### Week 3-6: Data Collection

#### 2.1 Environmental Data

**Energy Consumption:**
- Electricity (kWh by meter/location)
- Natural gas (therms/m³)
- Heating oil, propane, coal
- District heating/cooling
- Renewable energy (on-site generation, RECs)
- Energy bills (12-24 months)

**Fleet & Mobile Combustion:**
- Vehicle list (type, fuel, mileage)
- Fuel consumption records
- Company-owned vs. employee-owned
- Air travel (flight hours or spend)
- Rail, bus, rental car data
- Shipping and logistics data

**Refrigerants & F-Gases:**
- HVAC equipment inventory
- Refrigerant types and quantities
- Leak records and top-ups
- Fire suppression systems

**Water:**
- Water consumption by meter
- Wastewater volumes
- Water quality data (if available)

**Waste:**
- Waste audits or hauler records
- Waste types and quantities
- Recycling and composting rates
- Hazardous waste

**Materials:**
- Raw material purchases
- Packaging materials
- Product composition data
- Supplier spend data (for Scope 3)

#### 2.2 Social Data

**Workforce:**
- Employee headcount by location, gender, ethnicity
- Turnover rates
- Compensation data (aggregate)
- Benefits coverage
- Training hours
- Health and safety incidents (TRIR, LTIR)
- Employee survey results

**Supply Chain:**
- Supplier list by spend category
- Existing supplier assessments
- Audit reports
- Certifications held by suppliers

**Community:**
- Charitable contributions
- Volunteer hours
- Community investments
- Local hiring data

#### 2.3 Governance Data

**Board & Leadership:**
- Board composition (diversity, independence)
- Committee structure
- Meeting frequency and attendance
- Executive compensation structure

**Policies:**
- Code of conduct
- Environmental policies
- Human rights policies
- Anti-corruption policies
- Whistleblower procedures

**Risk Management:**
- Enterprise risk register
- Climate risk assessment (if exists)
- Compliance tracking
- Audit findings

#### 2.4 Data Collection Methods

| Method | Best For | Quality | Effort |
|--------|----------|---------|--------|
| Utility bills | Scope 1, 2 | High | Low |
| Meter data | Energy monitoring | High | Medium |
| Expense data | Scope 3 screening | Medium | Low |
| Supplier surveys | Scope 3 primary | High | High |
| Spend-based factors | Scope 3 estimation | Low-Medium | Low |
| Activity data | Precise accounting | High | Medium-High |
| Estimates | Gap filling | Low | Low |

**Data Quality Scoring:**
- **A**: Primary data, verified, complete
- **B**: Primary data, unverified, mostly complete
- **C**: Secondary data, reasonable estimates
- **D**: Rough estimates, significant gaps

**Deliverables:**
- Data collection tracker
- Data quality assessment
- Gap analysis report

### Week 7-8: Analysis & Calculation

#### 3.1 Emissions Calculations

**Scope 1 (Direct Emissions):**
```
Emissions = Activity Data × Emission Factor
```

**Sources:**
- Stationary combustion (boilers, furnaces)
- Mobile combustion (company vehicles)
- Fugitive emissions (refrigerants)
- Process emissions (manufacturing)

**Scope 2 (Indirect - Energy):**
- Location-based method (grid average)
- Market-based method (contractual instruments)
- Both must be reported per GHG Protocol

**Scope 3 (Value Chain):**
- Category screening (all 15 categories)
- Prioritization (>40% threshold for SBTi)
- Calculation approach per category:
  - Purchased goods: Spend-based or supplier-specific
  - Capital goods: Spend-based
  - Fuel & energy: Well-to-tank factors
  - Upstream transport: Distance × mode × factor
  - Waste: Waste type × treatment method
  - Business travel: Distance × mode
  - Employee commuting: Survey or estimates
  - Downstream: Use phase, end-of-life

**Emission Factor Sources:**
- EPA eGRID (US electricity)
- IEA (international electricity)
- DEFRA (UK government factors)
- Ecoinvent (lifecycle database)
- EXIOBASE (multi-region input-output)
- Supplier-specific data (highest quality)

#### 3.2 Uncertainty Analysis

**Approach:**
- Quantify uncertainty for each scope
- Identify high-uncertainty areas
- Prioritize data improvement

**Typical Uncertainty Ranges:**
| Scope | Typical Uncertainty |
|-------|---------------------|
| Scope 1 | ±5-10% |
| Scope 2 (location) | ±5-15% |
| Scope 2 (market) | ±5-10% |
| Scope 3 (spend-based) | ±30-50% |
| Scope 3 (primary data) | ±10-20% |

#### 3.3 Benchmarking

**Internal:**
- Year-over-year trends (if historical data)
- Facility-level comparison
- Business unit comparison

**External:**
- Industry averages (CDP, sector reports)
- Peer comparison (if data available)
- Intensity metrics (per revenue, per employee, per unit)

**Deliverables:**
- Emissions inventory report
- Uncertainty analysis
- Benchmarking analysis
- Data quality summary

---

### Week 9-10: Baseline Report

#### 4.1 Report Structure

**Executive Summary:**
- Total emissions (tCO2e) by scope
- Key findings
- Priority opportunities
- Recommended next steps

**Methodology:**
- Boundaries and scope
- Standards applied
- Calculation methods
- Emission factors used
- Data quality assessment

**Results:**
- Scope 1 breakdown by source
- Scope 2 breakdown (location and market)
- Scope 3 breakdown by category
- Historical trends (if available)
- Intensity metrics

**Analysis:**
- Hotspot identification
- Comparison to benchmarks
- Data gaps and limitations
- Improvement opportunities

**Appendices:**
- Data tables
- Emission factor sources
- Calculation spreadsheets
- Facility list

#### 4.2 Quality Assurance

**Internal Review:**
- Calculation verification
- Data completeness check
- Consistency review
- Error checking

**External Review (Optional):**
- Third-party verification
- Limited assurance
- Stakeholder review

**Deliverables:**
- Baseline Assessment Report
- Executive presentation
- Raw data files
- Calculation workbook

## Phase 2: Scope 1, 2, 3 Deep Dive

### Objective
Detailed analysis of emissions sources with reduction opportunity identification.

### Timeline: 6-10 Weeks (after baseline)

---

### Scope 1 Analysis

#### Stationary Combustion
**Data Required:**
- Fuel consumption by type and facility
- Equipment efficiency ratings
- Operating hours
- Maintenance records

**Reduction Opportunities:**
- Fuel switching (natural gas → biogas/renewable)
- Efficiency improvements (equipment upgrades)
- Electrification (heat pumps, electric boilers)
- Operational optimization (scheduling, controls)

#### Mobile Combustion
**Data Required:**
- Vehicle inventory (type, age, fuel)
- Mileage by vehicle
- Fuel consumption
- Driver behavior data (if available)

**Reduction Opportunities:**
- Fleet electrification
- Right-sizing vehicles
- Route optimization
- Driver training
- Alternative fuels (biofuels, hydrogen)
- Modal shift (air → rail, road → rail)

#### Fugitive Emissions
**Data Required:**
- Refrigerant inventory
- Leak rates and top-up records
- Equipment age and type
- GWP of refrigerants

**Reduction Opportunities:**
- Leak detection and repair (LDAR)
- Equipment upgrades (low-GWP refrigerants)
- Proper end-of-life handling
- Alternative technologies

---

### Scope 2 Analysis

#### Energy Procurement
**Data Required:**
- Electricity consumption by meter
- Contract details (term, pricing, attributes)
- Renewable energy certificates
- Power purchase agreements

**Reduction Opportunities:**
- Energy efficiency (lighting, HVAC, equipment)
- Renewable energy procurement (PPAs, RECs)
- On-site generation (solar, wind)
- Demand response programs
- Energy storage

#### Location-Based vs. Market-Based
**Analysis:**
- Compare location-based and market-based emissions
- Identify renewable energy gaps
- Develop procurement strategy

**Renewable Energy Hierarchy:**
1. On-site generation (highest quality)
2. Virtual/physical PPAs
3. Utility green tariffs
4. Unbundled RECs (lowest quality)

---

### Scope 3 Analysis

#### Category Prioritization
**Screening Criteria:**
- Magnitude (% of total emissions)
- Influence (ability to affect change)
- Risk (exposure to climate/regulatory risk)
- Stakeholder interest

**SBTi Requirement:**
- Scope 3 must be addressed if >40% of total
- At least 67% of Scope 3 must be covered by targets

#### Deep Dive by Priority Category

**Category 1: Purchased Goods & Services**
- Spend analysis by category
- Supplier engagement strategy
- Product redesign opportunities
- Material substitution
- Circular economy approaches

**Category 4: Upstream Transportation**
- Freight spend by mode
- Distance and routing analysis
- Mode shift opportunities
- Logistics optimization
- Supplier location strategy

**Category 6: Business Travel**
- Travel spend by type
- Video conferencing alternatives
- Travel policy updates
- Rail vs. air analysis
- Carbon budget approach

**Category 7: Employee Commuting**
- Commuting survey
- Remote work policies
- Public transit incentives
- EV charging infrastructure
- Shuttle programs

**Category 11: Use of Sold Products**
- Product energy consumption
- Efficiency improvement opportunities
- Customer engagement
- Product design changes

**Category 12: End-of-Life**
- Product take-back programs
- Recycling rates
- Design for disassembly
- Circular business models

**Deliverables:**
- Scope 3 Deep Dive Report
- Category-specific action plans
- Supplier engagement framework

## Phase 3: Reduction Roadmap

### Objective
Develop actionable, science-aligned decarbonization pathway.

### Timeline: 8-12 Weeks

---

### Step 1: Opportunity Identification

#### Brainstorming Sessions
**Participants:**
- Sustainability team
- Operations/facilities
- Procurement
- Finance
- Product/engineering
- HR (for commuting, culture)

**Methods:**
- Abatement curve development
- Technology scan
- Peer benchmarking
- Expert interviews

**Output:**
- Long list of reduction opportunities (50-100+)

---

### Step 2: Opportunity Assessment

#### Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Description | Weight |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Emissions Impact | tCO2e reduction potential | 25% |
| Cost | NPV, payback period | 20% |
| Feasibility | Technical and operational | 20% |
| Timeline | Speed of implementation | 15% |
| Co-benefits | Cost savings, brand, etc. | 10% |
| Risk | Implementation and performance risk | 10% |

#### Abatement Cost Curve

**Development:**
- Estimate abatement potential (tCO2e/year)
- Calculate cost per tCO2e abated
- Sort by cost (negative to positive)
- Create cumulative chart

**Cost Categories:**
- **Negative cost**: Saves money (efficiency)
- **Low cost**: <€50/tCO2e
- **Medium cost**: €50-200/tCO2e
- **High cost**: >€200/tCO2e

**Example Opportunities:**
| Opportunity | Abatement (tCO2e/yr) | Cost (€/tCO2e) | Payback |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|---------|
| LED lighting | 500 | -150 | 1 year |
| HVAC optimization | 800 | -50 | 2 years |
| Solar PV | 1,200 | 25 | 7 years |
| Fleet electrification | 2,000 | 100 | 5 years |
| Sustainable aviation fuel | 300 | 400 | N/A |
| Carbon removal | 100 | 600+ | N/A |

---

### Step 3: Target Setting

#### Science-Based Targets (SBTi)

**Near-Term Target (5-10 years):**
- Scope 1+2: 4.2% annual reduction (1.5°C aligned)
- Scope 3: 2.5% annual reduction (if >40% of total)
- Base year: Most recent year with reliable data
- Target year: 2030 or earlier

**Long-Term/Net-Zero Target (by 2050):**
- 90-95% reduction across value chain
- Neutralize residual emissions
- Permanent carbon removal for residuals

#### Target Types

**Absolute Targets:**
- Reduce total emissions by X% by year Y
- Best for: Most organizations

**Intensity Targets:**
- Reduce emissions per unit (revenue, production)
- Best for: High-growth companies
- Must demonstrate absolute reduction trajectory

**Renewable Energy Target:**
- 100% renewable electricity by 2030
- RE100 alignment

---

### Step 4: Roadmap Development

#### Roadmap Structure

**By Time Horizon:**

| Horizon | Focus | Examples |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Short-term (1-2 yrs) | Quick wins, foundations | Efficiency, policy, data systems |
| Medium-term (3-5 yrs) | Major investments | Renewables, electrification, product |
| Long-term (5-10+ yrs) | Transformation | New business models, carbon removal |

**By Scope:**

| Scope | Short-term | Medium-term | Long-term |
|-------|------------|-------------|-----------|
| Scope 1 | Efficiency, fuel switch | Electrification | Alternative fuels |
| Scope 2 | Efficiency, RECs | PPAs, on-site | 100% renewable |
| Scope 3 | Engagement, policy | Supplier programs, design | Value chain transformation |

#### Implementation Plan

**For Each Initiative:**
- Description and scope
- Emissions impact (tCO2e)
- Cost and ROI
- Timeline and milestones
- Owner and stakeholders
- Dependencies
- Risks and mitigations
- Success metrics

**Governance:**
- Steering committee
- Working groups by theme
- Progress reporting cadence
- Escalation process

**Investment Plan:**
- Capital requirements by year
- Operating cost impact
- Funding sources
- ROI analysis
