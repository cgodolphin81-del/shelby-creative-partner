# Pricing Models - Sustainability Consulting

## Overview

Comprehensive pricing framework for sustainability consulting services, from project-based engagements to long-term retainers.

---

## Pricing Philosophy

### Core Principles

1. **Value-Based**: Price reflects client value created, not just hours worked
2. **Transparent**: Clear scope, deliverables, and assumptions
3. **Flexible**: Multiple engagement models to fit client needs
4. **Sustainable**: Margins support quality delivery and team development
5. **Scalable**: Pricing grows with client maturity and scope

### Target Margins

| Service Type | Target Gross Margin | Target Net Margin |
|--------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| Project-based | 50-60% | 25-35% |
| Retainer | 60-70% | 30-40% |
| Advisory/Expert | 70-80% | 40-50% |
| Software/Tools | 80-90% | 50-60% |

## Hybrid Models

### Project + Retainer
**Structure:**
- Initial project to establish baseline/strategy
- Retainer for ongoing management

**Example:**
- Net-zero strategy project: $100,000 (one-time)
- Implementation retainer: $15,000/month (12 months)
- **Total Year 1:** $280,000
- **Year 2+:** $180,000/year

**Benefits:**
- Clear starting point
- Sustainable ongoing support
- Knowledge transfer
- Budget predictability

---

### Phased Project
**Structure:**
- Large project broken into phases
- Each phase priced separately
- Option to pause between phases

**Example:**
- Phase 1 (Baseline): $75,000
- Phase 2 (Strategy): $75,000
- Phase 3 (Implementation): $100,000
- **Total:** $250,000 (over 12-18 months)

**Benefits:**
- Cash flow management
- Flexibility to adjust
- Early wins build momentum

---

### Performance-Based Component
**Structure:**
- Base fee + success bonus
- Bonus tied to specific outcomes

**Example:**
- SBTi validation project
- Base fee: $80,000
- Bonus: $20,000 upon SBTi validation
- **Total potential:** $100,000

**Use Cases:**
- Certification projects
- Target validation
- Rating improvements
- Cost savings initiatives

**Caution:** Ensure outcomes are within control

## Payment Terms

### Standard Terms

**Invoicing:**
- Project: Milestone-based or monthly
- Retainer: Monthly in advance
- T&M: Monthly in arrears

**Payment Terms:**
- Standard: Net 30
- Large clients: Net 45-60 (negotiate)
- Small clients: Net 15 or upfront

**Late Payment:**
- 1.5% per month (18% APR)
- Work pause after 60 days
- Collection after 90 days

### Deposits & Retainers

**Project Deposits:**
- <50K: 50% upfront
- 50-100K: 40% upfront
- 100-200K: 30% upfront
- 200K+: 25-30% upfront

**Retainer Prepayment:**
- Always prepaid monthly or quarterly
- Unused hours: Roll over (max 1 month) or forfeit
- Overage: Billed at standard rates

### Expense Policy

**Billable Expenses:**
- Travel (pre-approved)
- Third-party services (software, verification)
- Printing/production (reports)
- Venue/catering (workshops)

**Non-Billable:**
- Routine communications
- Standard software subscriptions
- Internal meetings

**Markup:**
- Third-party services: 10-15% management fee
- Travel: At cost (no markup)

## Discounting Guidelines

### When to Discount

**Appropriate:**
- Multi-year contracts (10-15%)
- Multiple projects (10-20%)
- Non-profit/government (15-20%)
- Strategic reference clients (10-15%)
- Payment upfront (5-10%)

**Not Appropriate:**
- Budget constraints alone
- Competitive pressure alone
- Standard single projects
- Retainer conversions

### Approval Authority

| Discount | Approval Required |
|----------|-------------------|
| 0-10% | Engagement Manager |
| 10-20% | Director |
| 20-30% | Partner |
| 30%+ | Partner + Finance |

## Pricing Psychology & Negotiation

### Anchoring

**Strategy:** Present premium option first
**Example:** Show Enterprise package before Growth package
**Effect:** Makes mid-tier seem reasonable

### Framing

**Value Frame:** "Investment of $100K to achieve $500K in savings"
**Risk Frame:** "$100K vs. $2M regulatory fine"
**Competitive Frame:** "Industry leaders are investing X"

### Trade-offs

**Never discount without getting something:**
- Longer contract term
- Larger scope
- Faster payment
- Case study rights
- Reference calls
- Expanded access

### Handling Objections

**"Too expensive":**
- Reframe as investment
- Show ROI
- Offer phased approach
- Compare to cost of inaction

**"We can do it cheaper":**
- Acknowledge
- Differentiate on quality/outcomes
- Offer scaled-down scope
- Share case studies

**"Need to think about it":**
- Understand concerns
- Provide additional info
- Set follow-up date
- Create urgency (timeline, pricing)
