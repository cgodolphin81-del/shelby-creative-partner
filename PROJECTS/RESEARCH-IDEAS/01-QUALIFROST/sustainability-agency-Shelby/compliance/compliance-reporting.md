## Major Regulations by Jurisdiction

### European Union

#### CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive)

**Scope:**
- All EU listed companies
- Large EU companies (meet 2 of 3: 250+ employees, €20M+ assets, €40M+ revenue)
- Non-EU companies with €150M+ net EU revenue
- **Estimated companies in scope:** ~50,000

**Timeline:**
| Reporting Year | First Report Due | Companies |
|----------------|------------------|-----------|
| 2024 | 2025 | Existing NFRD companies |
| 2025 | 2026 | Large companies (new) |
| 2026 | 2027 | Listed SMEs, small non-EU |
| 2028 | 2029 | Reasonable assurance required |

**Requirements:**
- Double materiality assessment
- ESRS standards compliance (1000+ data points)
- Digital tagging (XBRL)
- Value chain coverage
- Limited assurance (2024-2027)
- Reasonable assurance (2028+)

**ESRS Standards:**
- ESRS 1: General requirements
- ESRS 2: General disclosures
- ESRS E1: Climate change
- ESRS E2: Pollution
- ESRS E3: Water and marine resources
- ESRS E4: Biodiversity and ecosystems
- ESRS E5: Resource use and circular economy
- ESRS S1: Own workforce
- ESRS S2: Workers in value chain
- ESRS S3: Affected communities
- ESRS S4: Consumers and end-users
- ESRS G1: Business conduct

**Penalties:**
- Member state determined
- Typically: €10K-€500K+
- Reputation damage
- Potential delisting (extreme)

---

#### EU Taxonomy

**Purpose:** Classification system for environmentally sustainable activities

**Six Environmental Objectives:**
1. Climate change mitigation
2. Climate change adaptation
3. Sustainable use of water and marine resources
4. Transition to circular economy
5. Pollution prevention and control
6. Protection of biodiversity and ecosystems

**Requirements for Companies:**
- Disclose % of revenue from Taxonomy-aligned activities
- Disclose % of CapEx related to aligned activities
- Disclose % of OpEx related to aligned activities
- Part of CSRD reporting

**Technical Screening Criteria:**
- Substantial contribution to one objective
- Do no significant harm (DNSH) to others
- Minimum safeguards (human rights, anti-corruption)

---

#### SFDR (Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation)

**Scope:** Financial market participants and advisors

**Key Requirements:**
- Entity-level disclosures
- Product-level disclosures
- Principal adverse impacts (PAI)
- Sustainability risk integration

**Article 6:** No sustainability focus (basic disclosure)
**Article 8:** Promotes environmental/social characteristics ("light green")
**Article 9:** Sustainable investment objective ("dark green")

**Impact on Corporates:**
- Investor due diligence
- Data requests from financial institutions
- Alignment expectations

---

#### CBAM (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism)

**Purpose:** Carbon price on imports to prevent carbon leakage

**Scope (2026):**
- Cement
- Iron and steel
- Aluminium
- Fertilizers
- Electricity
- Hydrogen

**Requirements:**
- Quarterly CBAM reports
- Embedded emissions calculation
- CBAM certificates purchase
- Verification by accredited verifier

**Timeline:**
- 2023-2025: Transitional (reporting only)
- 2026+: Full implementation (financial obligation)

**Impact:** Importers must calculate and report embedded emissions

### United States

#### SEC Climate Rule (Finalized 2024)

**Scope:**
- All SEC registrants (public companies)
- Tiered by materiality

**Requirements:**

**All Companies:**
- Climate-related risks (financial statement impact)
- Governance (board oversight, management role)
- Targets and goals (if set)

**Large Accelerated & Accelerated Filers:**
- Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions (if material)
- Attestation (limited → reasonable over time)

**Timeline:**
| Fiscal Year | Requirements |
|-------------|--------------|
| 2025 | Risk & governance (FY2024 reports) |
| 2026 | + Scope 1/2 if material |
| 2027 | + Scope 1/2 attestation (limited) |
| 2029 | + Scope 1/2 attestation (reasonable) |

**Status:** Legal challenges ongoing; implementation may be delayed

---

#### California Climate Rules

**SB 253: Climate Corporate Data Accountability Act**

**Scope:**
- US companies doing business in California
- $1B+ annual revenue
- Public and private companies

**Requirements:**
- Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions disclosure
- Annual reporting
- Third-party assurance
- Public disclosure on company website

**Timeline:**
- 2026: Scope 1 and 2 (FY2025)
- 2027: Scope 3 (FY2026)
- Assurance: Limited (2026), Reasonable (2030)

**Penalties:**
- Up to $500K/year for Scope 1/2 non-compliance
- Up to $500K/year for Scope 3 non-compliance

---

**SB 254: Climate-Related Financial Risk Act**

**Scope:**
- US companies doing business in California
- $500M+ annual revenue
- Public and private companies

**Requirements:**
- Biennial climate-related financial risk report
- TCFD-aligned disclosure
- Public disclosure on company website

**Timeline:**
- 2026: First report (and every 2 years thereafter)

**Penalties:**
- Up to $50K/year for non-compliance

### United Kingdom

#### UK SECR (Streamlined Energy and Carbon Reporting)

**Scope:**
- UK incorporated companies meeting 2 of 3:
  - 250+ employees
  - £36M+ turnover
  - £18M+ balance sheet
- LLPs meeting same criteria
- Quoted companies (separate requirements)

**Requirements:**
- UK energy use (kWh)
- Scope 1 and 2 emissions
- At least one intensity metric
- Methodology
- Prior year comparison
- Energy efficiency actions

**Reporting:**
- Annual report (Directors' Report)
- Public (Companies House)

**Timeline:** Annual (financial year)

**Penalties:**
- Director fines
- Reputation damage
- Potential criminal offense (knowing false statement)

---

#### UK TCFD Mandate

**Scope:**
- Premium listed commercial companies
- Large private companies (500+ employees)
- LLPs (500+ employees)
- Pension schemes (asset threshold)

**Requirements:**
- TCFD-aligned disclosure
- Comply or explain
- Annual reporting

**Timeline:** Phased from 2021-2025

## Assurance & Verification

### Assurance Levels

#### Limited Assurance

**Definition:**
- Negative assurance ("nothing came to our attention")
- Lower level of scrutiny
- Analytical procedures + inquiry
- Lower cost

**Typical For:**
- First-time reporters
- CSRD 2024-2027
- SEC Rule (initial)
- Voluntary reports

**Process:**
- Planning and risk assessment
- Understanding of systems
- Analytical procedures
- Inquiry and discussion
- Limited testing

**Output:**
- Limited assurance conclusion
- Key findings
- Recommendations

---

#### Reasonable Assurance

**Definition:**
- Positive assurance ("in our opinion...")
- Higher level of scrutiny
- Detailed testing
- Higher cost (2-3x limited)

**Typical For:**
- Mature reporters
- CSRD 2028+
- SEC Rule (after phase-in)
- Financial statement integration

**Process:**
- All limited assurance procedures, plus:
- Detailed testing of controls
- Substantive testing
- Evidence verification
- Site visits (sample)
- Third-party confirmations

**Output:**
- Reasonable assurance opinion
- Materiality assessment
- Detailed findings

---

## Audit Preparation

### Types of Audits

#### Internal Audit

**Purpose:** Self-assessment of sustainability program

**Scope:**
- Data accuracy
- Control effectiveness
- Compliance status
- Risk assessment

**Frequency:** Annual or biennial

**Conducted By:** Internal audit team or external consultant

---

#### External Audit (Assurance)

**Purpose:** Independent verification

**Scope:** Defined by assurance engagement

**Frequency:** Annual (typically aligned with reporting)

**Conducted By:** Third-party assurance provider

---

#### Regulatory Audit

**Purpose:** Compliance verification

**Scope:** Regulatory requirements

**Frequency:** As determined by regulator

**Conducted By:** Regulatory body or delegated auditor

---

#### Supply Chain Audit

**Purpose:** Supplier compliance verification

**Scope:** Supplier operations and data

**Frequency:** Risk-based (1-3 years)

**Conducted By:** Internal team, third-party, or collaborative

---

### Audit Response Process

#### During Audit

**Kickoff:**
- Understand scope and timeline
- Assign roles
- Establish communication
- Provide document request list

**Fieldwork:**
- Respond to information requests (within agreed timeline)
- Facilitate interviews
- Provide site access (if needed)
- Track requests and responses

**Findings:**
- Review draft findings
- Provide response/corrective action
- Negotiate wording (if needed)
- Agree on timeline

---

#### Post-Audit

**Report:**
- Review draft report
- Management response
- Final report issuance

**Corrective Actions:**
- Action plan development
- Implementation
- Tracking and monitoring
- Follow-up audit (if required)

**Lessons Learned:**
- Internal debrief
- Process improvements
- Control enhancements
- Training updates

## Best Practices

### Compliance Excellence

1. **Start Early:** Don't wait for deadline
2. **Invest in Systems:** Data management is critical
3. **Build Controls:** Prevent errors, don't just detect
4. **Train Thoroughly:** Everyone plays a role
5. **Document Everything:** If it's not documented, it didn't happen
6. **Engage Experts:** Know when to get help
7. **Be Transparent:** Disclose limitations honestly
8. **Continuous Improvement:** Learn from each cycle

---

### Common Pitfalls

❌ **Last-minute rush:** Leads to errors and stress
❌ **Siloed approach:** Sustainability is cross-functional
❌ **Poor data quality:** Garbage in, garbage out
❌ **Inadequate documentation:** Can't prove compliance
❌ **Ignoring Scope 3:** Often majority of emissions
❌ **Overclaiming:** Greenwashing risk
❌ **Static program:** Regulations evolve
❌ **No assurance:** Reduced credibility
