# Client Acquisition Strategy

## Overview

Go-to-market strategy for acquiring sustainability consulting clients across corporate, SMB, startup, and government segments.

---

## Target Market Segmentation

### Primary Segments

#### 1. Large Corporates ($1B+ revenue)

**Characteristics:**
- Dedicated sustainability team (3-10 people)
- Public sustainability commitments (net-zero, SBTi)
- Regulatory exposure (CSRD, SEC, etc.)
- Complex value chains
- Investor pressure

**Decision Makers:**
- Chief Sustainability Officer
- VP/Director of Sustainability
- CFO (for ROI-focused projects)
- General Counsel (for compliance)
- Board Sustainability Committee

**Buying Triggers:**
- New regulatory requirements
- Investor/customer pressure
- Leadership change
- Reputation crisis
- M&A activity
- Annual reporting cycle

**Sales Cycle:** 6-12 months
**Deal Size:** $200K - $1M+
**Competition:** Big 4, specialized boutiques, in-sourcing

---

#### 2. Mid-Market Companies ($50M - $1B revenue)

**Characteristics:**
- 1-3 person sustainability team (or single champion)
- Growing stakeholder expectations
- Supply chain pressure from larger customers
- Resource constraints
- Seeking expertise and bandwidth

**Decision Makers:**
- CEO/Founder (smaller end)
- COO or CFO
- Head of Operations
- Sustainability Manager (if exists)
- Board members

**Buying Triggers:**
- Customer RFP requirements
- Investor due diligence
- Competitor actions
- Cost savings opportunities
- Brand/reputation goals
- Talent attraction

**Sales Cycle:** 3-6 months
**Deal Size:** $50K - $300K
**Competition:** Regional firms, independent consultants

---

#### 3. Startups & Growth Companies (<$50M revenue)

**Characteristics:**
- No dedicated sustainability staff
- Founder-led values
- Investor expectations (VC pressure)
- Building from scratch
- Budget conscious
- Speed important

**Decision Makers:**
- CEO/Founder
- CFO/Head of Finance
- Head of Operations
- Investor relations

**Buying Triggers:**
- Fundraising (investor DD)
- Customer requirements (enterprise sales)
- B-Corp certification interest
- Hiring (employer brand)
- Product development (sustainability features)

**Sales Cycle:** 1-3 months
**Deal Size:** $20K - $100K
**Competition:** DIY tools, low-cost consultants

---

#### 4. Government & Public Sector

**Characteristics:**
- Mandated sustainability goals
- Public accountability
- Long budget cycles
- Procurement processes
- Grant funding availability

**Decision Makers:**
- Sustainability Director
- Department heads
- Procurement officers
- Elected officials (major initiatives)

**Buying Triggers:**
- New mandates
- Budget cycles
- Grant opportunities
- Public pressure
- Infrastructure projects

**Sales Cycle:** 6-18 months
**Deal Size:** $50K - $500K
**Competition:** Large consultancies, local firms

---

#### 5. Private Equity & Portfolio Companies

**Characteristics:**
- Multiple portfolio companies
- Value creation focus
- Exit preparation
- Standardized approaches
- Fast timelines

**Decision Makers:**
- PE Operating Partners
- Portfolio Company CEOs
- PE ESG/Value Creation teams

**Buying Triggers:**
- Acquisition (due diligence)
- 100-day plans
- Exit preparation
- Portfolio-wide initiatives
- LP reporting requirements

**Sales Cycle:** 2-4 months
**Deal Size:** $100K - $500K (portfolio-wide)
**Competition:** PE-focused consultancies

## Sales Process

### Stage 1: Discovery (Week 1-2)

**Activities:**
- Initial call (30 min)
- Needs assessment
- Stakeholder mapping
- Budget/timeline discussion
- Qualification (BANT: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)

**Qualification Criteria:**
- ✅ Clear sustainability driver
- ✅ Decision-maker engaged
- ✅ Budget identified (or path to budget)
- ✅ Timeline realistic
- ✅ Good cultural fit

**Disqualification Signals:**
- ❌ No clear business case
- ❌ No decision-maker access
- ❌ Unrealistic expectations
- ❌ Budget mismatch
- ❌ Shopping for cheapest option

**Deliverable:** Discovery summary email

---

### Stage 2: Proposal (Week 2-4)

**Activities:**
- Proposal development
- Internal review
- Client presentation
- Q&A and refinement

**Proposal Components:**
- Executive summary
- Understanding of needs
- Proposed approach
- Team and qualifications
- Investment
- Terms and conditions

**Presentation:**
- 60-minute walkthrough
- Key stakeholders present
- Interactive Q&A
- Clear next steps

**Deliverable:** Formal proposal + presentation

---

### Stage 3: Negotiation (Week 4-6)

**Activities:**
- Address concerns
- Scope refinement
- Pricing discussion
- Contract negotiation
- Legal review

**Common Objections:**
- Price: Show ROI, offer phased approach
- Timeline: Discuss priorities, resource options
- Scope: Clarify must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
- Competition: Differentiate on expertise/outcomes

**Negotiation Guidelines:**
- Trade, don't concede
- Protect margin
- Get something for every give
- Know walk-away point

**Deliverable:** Signed contract

---

### Stage 4: Onboarding (Week 6-8)

**Activities:**
- Kickoff meeting
- Stakeholder introductions
- Data request initiation
- Project plan finalization
- Communication protocols

**Onboarding Package:**
- Welcome email
- Project charter
- Contact list
- Timeline
- Data request list
- Communication plan

**Deliverable:** Project kickoff complete
