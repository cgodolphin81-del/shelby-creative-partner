# Carbon Offset Programs Guide

## Overview

Comprehensive guide to carbon offset procurement, management, and quality assessment for organizations pursuing net-zero pathways.

---

## Offset Hierarchy

### The Mitigation Hierarchy

```
                    AVOID
                      ↓
                   REDUCE
                      ↓
                 SUBSTITUTE
                      ↓
                  OFFSET
                      ↓
              NEUTRALIZE
```

**Key Principle:** Offsets are for residual emissions only, after maximum reduction efforts.

### Offset vs. Inset vs. Beyond Value Chain

| Approach | Definition | Claims |
|----------|------------|--------|
| **Offset** | Credits from projects outside value chain | Carbon neutral claims (transitioning) |
| **Inset** | Projects within value chain (suppliers, land use) | Scope 3 reduction claims |
| **BVCM** | Beyond Value Chain Mitigation (SBTi term) | Contribution claims, not neutralization |

## Offset Types

### 1. Avoidance/Reduction Projects

Projects that prevent emissions that would have otherwise occurred.

#### Renewable Energy
- **Types**: Wind, solar, hydro, geothermal
- **Methodologies**: ACM0002, AMS-I.D.
- **Co-benefits**: Energy access, air quality, jobs
- **Price Range**: $3-15/tCO2e
- **Quality Considerations**: Additionality (would it have happened anyway?), grid displacement factors

#### Energy Efficiency
- **Types**: Efficient cookstoves, building efficiency, industrial efficiency
- **Methodologies**: AMS-II series, ACM0005
- **Co-benefits**: Health (cookstoves), cost savings, comfort
- **Price Range**: $5-20/tCO2e
- **Quality Considerations**: Baseline establishment, persistence

#### Methane Capture
- **Types**: Landfill gas, agricultural methane, coal mine methane
- **Methodologies**: ACM0001, ACM0014
- **Co-benefits**: Air quality, odor reduction, energy generation
- **Price Range**: $8-25/tCO2e
- **Quality Considerations**: Leakage, monitoring

#### Forestry (Avoided Deforestation - REDD+)
- **Types**: Avoided deforestation, avoided degradation
- **Methodologies**: VM0009, VM0015, CCB Standards
- **Co-benefits**: Biodiversity, indigenous rights, water
- **Price Range**: $10-30/tCO2e
- **Quality Considerations**: Leakage, permanence, baseline accuracy

---

### 2. Removal Projects

Projects that physically remove CO2 from the atmosphere.

#### Nature-Based Removals

**Afforestation/Reforestation**
- **Types**: Tree planting, forest restoration, agroforestry
- **Methodologies**: AR-AM0014, VM0003, Verra VM0047
- **Co-benefits**: Biodiversity, soil health, water, rural livelihoods
- **Price Range**: $15-50/tCO2e
- **Quality Considerations**: 
  - Permanence (fire, disease, harvesting risk)
  - Leakage (displacement of activities)
  - Additionality (would trees have been planted anyway?)
  - Measurement accuracy (allometric equations)

**Soil Carbon**
- **Types**: Regenerative agriculture, biochar, grassland restoration
- **Methodologies**: VM0042, ACR Soil Carbon
- **Co-benefits**: Soil health, water retention, biodiversity, farmer income
- **Price Range**: $20-60/tCO2e
- **Quality Considerations**: 
  - Permanence (reversible with tillage)
  - Measurement (soil sampling uncertainty)
  - Saturation limits

**Blue Carbon**
- **Types**: Mangrove restoration, seagrass, salt marsh
- **Methodologies**: VM0033, Plan Vivo
- **Co-benefits**: Coastal protection, fisheries, biodiversity
- **Price Range**: $25-70/tCO2e
- **Quality Considerations**: Permanence (sea level rise), measurement

#### Technology-Based Removals

**Direct Air Capture (DAC)**
- **Types**: Chemical absorption, adsorption
- **Companies**: Climeworks, Carbon Engineering, Heirloom
- **Storage**: Geological sequestration, utilization
- **Price Range**: $600-1,000/tCO2e (current), targeting $100-300
- **Quality Considerations**: 
  - Energy source (must be renewable for net removal)
  - Storage permanence (geological = permanent)
  - Measurement (high accuracy)
  - Additionality (clear)

**Bioenergy with Carbon Capture and Storage (BECCS)**
- **Types**: Biomass power + CCS, biofuel + CCS
- **Status**: Limited commercial deployment
- **Price Range**: $150-400/tCO2e (projected)
- **Quality Considerations**: 
  - Biomass sustainability
  - Land use impacts
  - Storage permanence

**Enhanced Weathering**
- **Types**: Olivine spreading, concrete carbonation
- **Status**: Early stage
- **Price Range**: $100-300/tCO2e (projected)
- **Quality Considerations**: Measurement, ecological impacts

**Carbon Mineralization**
- **Types**: In-situ (Basalt), ex-situ (concrete)
- **Companies**: Carbfix, Carbon Cure
- **Price Range**: $100-300/tCO2e
- **Quality Considerations**: Permanence (excellent), measurement

## Quality Assessment Framework

### Core Criteria (ICVCM Assessment)

#### 1. Additionality
**Definition:** The reduction/removal would not have occurred without the carbon finance.

**Assessment Questions:**
- Would the project have happened anyway? (regulatory, economic, common practice)
- Is there a clear barrier that carbon finance overcomes?
- Is the investment analysis credible?

**Red Flags:**
- Project already required by law
- Project already economically viable
- Common practice in the region/sector
- Vague or missing additionality analysis

#### 2. Permanence
**Definition:** The carbon stays out of the atmosphere for the claimed duration.

**Assessment Questions:**
- What is the risk of reversal? (fire, disease, policy, market)
- Is there a buffer pool contribution?
- What is the monitoring period?
- Is there insurance or guarantee?

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Buffer pools (typically 10-30% of credits)
- Long-term monitoring commitments
- Legal protections (conservation easements)
- Diversification across projects

#### 3. Accurate Quantification
**Definition:** Emissions reductions/removals are measured accurately and conservatively.

**Assessment Questions:**
- Is the methodology approved and current?
- Are emission factors appropriate?
- Is uncertainty addressed?
- Is there third-party verification?

**Red Flags:**
- Outdated methodologies
- Overestimated baselines
- High uncertainty without conservative adjustments
- Weak verification

#### 4. No Double Counting
**Definition:** The credit is only counted once toward climate targets.

**Assessment Questions:**
- Is the credit uniquely numbered and tracked?
- Is there corresponding adjustment (for international transfers)?
- Is the retirement properly recorded?
- Are claims aligned with credit ownership?

**Mechanisms:**
- Registry tracking (serial numbers)
- Retirement certificates
- Corresponding adjustments (Article 6)

#### 5. Sustainable Development Benefits
**Definition:** Project contributes to local sustainable development.

**Assessment Questions:**
- Are SDG contributions identified and measured?
- Is there community engagement?
- Are there safeguards for indigenous peoples?
- Are benefits equitably shared?

**Certifications:**
- CCB Standards (Climate, Community, Biodiversity)
- SD VISta (Sustainable Development Verified Impact Standard)
- Fair Trade, Organic (for certain project types)

#### 6. Governance & Transparency
**Definition:** Project has strong governance and transparent operations.

**Assessment Questions:**
- Is project documentation publicly available?
- Is there stakeholder consultation?
- Is there a grievance mechanism?
- Is the project developer reputable?

## Offset Standards & Registries

### Major Standards

| Standard | Focus | Strengths | Considerations |
|----------|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Verra (VCS)** | Broadest portfolio | Largest volume, diverse projects | Quality varies by project |
| **Gold Standard** | SDG focus | Strong co-benefits, rigorous | Smaller volume, higher cost |
| **American Carbon Registry (ACR)** | US focus | Rigorous, US projects | Limited geographic scope |
| **Climate Action Reserve (CAR)** | US protocols | High quality, US focus | Limited international |
| **Plan Vivo** | Community projects | Smallholders, strong community | Smaller scale |
| **Puro.earth** | Carbon removal | Removal-focused, durable | Limited to removal |

### Registry Functions

**What Registries Do:**
- Approve methodologies
- Validate and verify projects
- Issue credits (with unique serial numbers)
- Track ownership transfers
- Record retirements
- Maintain public registry

**Key Registries:**
- Verra Registry
- Gold Standard Registry
- ACR Registry
- CAR Registry

## Offset Procurement Strategy

### Portfolio Approach

**Diversification Principles:**
- Mix of project types (nature-based + technology)
- Mix of geographies (risk diversification)
- Mix of vintages (avoid concentration)
- Mix of prices (budget management)

**Example Portfolio:**
| Category | Allocation | Rationale |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| Nature-based removals | 40% | Co-benefits, cost-effective |
| Technology removals | 20% | Durability, innovation support |
| Avoidance (renewable) | 20% | Cost-effective, SDGs |
| Avoidance (forestry) | 15% | Co-benefits, transition |
| Emerging tech | 5% | Innovation, future optionality |

### Sourcing Channels

#### Direct Purchase
**Pros:**
- Direct relationship with developer
- Potential for customization
- Better pricing for large volumes
- Storytelling opportunities

**Cons:**
- Higher transaction costs
- Due diligence burden
- Limited deal flow for small buyers

**Best For:** Large buyers ($500K+ annual), strategic partnerships

#### Brokers & Aggregators
**Examples:** South Pole, 3Degrees, Climate Impact Partners, NativeEnergy

**Pros:**
- Curated portfolios
- Due diligence support
- Flexible volumes
- Managed services

**Cons:**
- Markup on credits
- Less direct connection
- Varies by broker quality

**Best For:** Most corporate buyers, especially SMBs

#### Exchanges & Marketplaces
**Examples:** Xpansiv CBL, AirCarbon Exchange, Carbonplace

**Pros:**
- Transparent pricing
- Standardized contracts
- Liquidity
- Easy execution

**Cons:**
- Limited project information
- Less customization
- Spot market volatility

**Best For:** Standardized purchases, hedging

#### Forward Purchases
**Definition:** Purchase credits before they are issued (pre-finance)

**Pros:**
- Lower prices
- Support project development
- Secure future supply
- Additionality signal

**Cons:**
- Delivery risk
- Capital commitment
- Project may not succeed

**Best For:** Strategic buyers, removal projects

## Claims & Communications

### Evolving Standards

#### Historical: Carbon Neutral
**Traditional Claim:** "We are carbon neutral"
**Basis:** Offset all emissions
**Current Status:** Being phased out, criticized as greenwashing

#### Current Best Practice: Net-Zero (SBTi)
**Claim Framework:**
1. Reduce emissions 90-95% (science-based)
2. Neutralize residual emissions (permanent removal)
3. No use of avoidance offsets for net-zero claim

**Timeline:**
- 2030: Near-term SBT (50%+ reduction)
- 2050: Net-zero (90-95% reduction + neutralization)

#### Contribution Claims
**Claim:** "We are contributing to climate action beyond our value chain"
**Basis:** Finance mitigation beyond SBT requirements
**Use Case:** While working toward SBT, or beyond net-zero

### Claim Guidelines

#### Do's
✅ Be specific about what is claimed
✅ Disclose emissions and offset quantities
✅ Describe offset projects and quality
✅ Emphasize reduction efforts first
✅ Use "carbon neutral" only for specific products/events (with clear scope)
✅ Align with SBTi for net-zero claims

#### Don'ts
❌ Claim "carbon neutral" for entire organization without deep cuts
❌ Use low-quality offsets for claims
❌ Imply offsets equal no emissions
❌ Double-count credits
❌ Make claims without verification
❌ Use offsets to avoid reduction

### Disclosure Requirements

**Minimum Disclosure:**
- Total emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3)
- Emissions covered by offset claim
- Quantity of offsets used
- Project types and locations
- Standards and registries
- Vintage years
- Retirement documentation

**Enhanced Disclosure:**
- Quality assessment methodology
- Portfolio breakdown
- Co-benefits achieved
- Investment amounts
- Future offset strategy

## Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

| Pitfall | Risk | Mitigation |
|---------|------|------------|
| Low-quality offsets | Reputation damage, greenwashing | Use quality frameworks, third-party ratings |
| Over-reliance on offsets | Delayed decarbonization | Set SBT, prioritize reduction |
| Single project concentration | Delivery risk, reversal risk | Diversify portfolio |
| Poor claims | Regulatory risk, backlash | Follow guidance, legal review |
| Missing documentation | Verification failure | Maintain comprehensive records |
| Ignoring co-benefits | Missed value | Assess and communicate SDG impacts |
| No forward strategy | Supply risk, price risk | Long-term procurement plan |
