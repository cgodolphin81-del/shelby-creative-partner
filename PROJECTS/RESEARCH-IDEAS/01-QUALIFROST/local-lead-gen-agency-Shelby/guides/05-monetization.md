# 05 - Monetization Strategies
## Rental Model, Pay-Per-Lead & Hybrid Approaches

Once your site is ranking and generating leads, it's time to monetize. This guide covers all monetization models with pricing strategies and contract templates.

---

## 💰 Monetization Models Overview

| Model | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|-------|------|------|----------|
| **Flat Rental** | Predictable income, passive | Capped upside | Stable niches |
| **Pay-Per-Lead** | Unlimited upside, fair | More tracking needed | High-value niches |
| **Hybrid** | Best of both worlds | More complex | Most situations |
| **Revenue Share** | Aligned incentives | Requires trust | Long-term partners |
| **Site Sale** | Large lump sum | Lose recurring income | Exit strategy |

---

## 🏠 Model 1: Flat Monthly Rental

### How It Works

Business pays fixed monthly fee for exclusive rights to all leads from your site.

### Pricing Guidelines

**By Niche Tier:**

| Tier | Niches | Monthly Rental |
|------|--------|----------------|
| **Tier 1** | Personal Injury, Water Damage, Roofing | $2,000-$5,000 |
| **Tier 2** | HVAC, Plumbing, Dental Implants | $1,200-$3,000 |
| **Tier 3** | Tree Service, Electrician, Garage Door | $800-$2,000 |
| **Tier 4** | Cleaning, Pool Service, Landscaping | $500-$1,500 |

**By City Size:**

| City Population | Rental Multiplier |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| <50,000 | 0.5x (base rate) |
| 50,000-200,000 | 1.0x (base rate) |
| 200,000-500,000 | 1.5x (base rate) |
| 500,000-1M | 2.0x (base rate) |
| 1M+ | 3.0x+ (base rate) |

### Pricing Formula

```
Base Rate × City Multiplier × Niche Multiplier = Monthly Rental

Example:
$1,000 (base) × 1.5 (Denver) × 1.2 (HVAC) = $1,800/month
```

### Rental Agreement Terms

**Standard Terms:**

| Term | Typical | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Contract Length** | 6-12 months | Start with 6, renew |
| **Payment Terms** | Monthly, upfront | Auto-pay preferred |
| **Lead Exclusivity** | Yes | They get ALL leads |
| **Phone Number** | Transfers to them | Or you answer & forward |
| **Termination** | 30 days notice | Either party |
| **Price Increases** | 10-15%/year | Or at renewal |

### What's Included

**Standard Package:**
- ✅ Exclusive leads from website
- ✅ Call forwarding to their number
- ✅ Form lead forwarding (email/SMS)
- ✅ Basic monthly report
- ✅ Site maintenance & hosting

**Premium Package (+$200-500/mo):**
- ✅ Everything in Standard
- ✅ Weekly reports
- ✅ Priority support
- ✅ Additional landing pages
- ✅ Review management
- ✅ GBP management

### Pros & Cons

**✅ Pros:**
- Predictable, recurring revenue
- Passive income (once set up)
- Easy to understand for clients
- Less tracking required
- Can rent multiple sites to same client

**❌ Cons:**
- Capped income (can't earn more if leads increase)
- Client may leave if leads slow down
- Need to find replacement if they cancel
- May leave money on the table in high-volume niches

### When to Use Flat Rental

✅ **Good For:**
- Stable, consistent lead volume
- Clients who prefer predictable costs
- Lower-value niches (<$100/lead value)
- Long-term relationships
- Passive income focus

❌ **Not Ideal For:**
- New sites (unproven lead volume)
- High-value niches (leaving money on table)
- Seasonal businesses
- Clients who want to pay for performance

## 💵 Model 2: Pay-Per-Lead (PPL)

### How It Works

Business pays only for actual leads generated. You set a price per lead based on niche value.

### Pricing by Niche

| Niche | Price Per Lead | Avg. Monthly Leads | Potential Revenue |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Personal Injury | $200-500 | 10-30 | $2,000-$15,000 |
| Water Damage | $150-400 | 15-40 | $2,250-$16,000 |
| Roofing | $100-300 | 20-50 | $2,000-$15,000 |
| HVAC | $75-200 | 30-60 | $2,250-$12,000 |
| Plumbing | $50-150 | 40-80 | $2,000-$12,000 |
| Tree Service | $75-200 | 20-40 | $1,500-$8,000 |
| Garage Door | $50-125 | 30-60 | $1,500-$7,500 |
| Electrician | $60-150 | 30-60 | $1,800-$9,000 |
| Dental Implants | $100-300 | 10-25 | $1,000-$7,500 |
| Pest Control | $40-100 | 40-80 | $1,600-$8,000 |

### Lead Qualification

**What Counts as a Lead?**

✅ **Qualified Leads (Billable):**
- Phone call over 60 seconds
- Form submission with valid info
- Request for quote/estimate
- Service area match
- During business hours (or after-hours if they offer)

❌ **Not Leads (Non-Billable):**
- Calls under 30 seconds
- Spam/test submissions
- Wrong number
- Outside service area
- Competitors calling
- Job seekers

### Lead Validation Process

```
Lead Comes In → Auto-Qualify → Manual Review (if needed) → Billable? → Invoice
     ↓
Call > 60 sec?
Form Complete?
Valid Contact Info?
In Service Area?
```

### Pricing Tiers

**Option 1: Flat Rate Per Lead**
```
All qualified leads: $X each
Simple, easy to understand
```

**Option 2: Tiered Pricing**
```
1-20 leads/month: $X each
21-40 leads/month: $X - 10% each
41+ leads/month: $X - 20% each
Volume discount encourages more leads
```

**Option 3: Lead Type Pricing**
```
Phone calls: $X
Form submissions: $Y (usually less than calls)
Emergency/After-hours: $X + 50%
High-value services: Premium pricing
```

### Payment Terms

**Standard PPL Terms:**

| Term | Typical | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Payment Schedule** | Weekly or Bi-weekly | Faster = better cash flow |
| **Minimum Commitment** | $500-1,000/mo | Guarantees baseline |
| **Lead Cap** | Optional | Max leads/month they pay for |
| **Exclusivity** | Usually yes | They get first right of refusal |
| **Payment Method** | Auto-pay (card/ACH) | Avoid chasing payments |

### Tracking Requirements

**Essential for PPL:**

1. **Call Recording** - Verify lead quality
2. **Call Duration Tracking** - Filter out spam
3. **Form Validation** - Check contact info
4. **Lead Dashboard** - Real-time visibility
5. **Dispute Process** - Handle disagreements

**Tools Needed:**
- CallRail (call tracking + recording)
- CRM (lead management)
- Spreadsheet or dashboard (lead log)
- Automated invoicing

### Pros & Cons

**✅ Pros:**
- Unlimited earning potential
- Fair for both parties (pay for performance)
- Easier to sell (no risk for client)
- Can charge premium for high-quality leads
- Aligns incentives (you want more leads)

**❌ Cons:**
- More tracking/admin required
- Income fluctuates month-to-month
- Potential disputes over lead quality
- Need to prove lead validity
- More hands-on management

### When to Use Pay-Per-Lead

✅ **Good For:**
- High-value niches ($100+/lead)
- New sites (prove value first)
- Clients hesitant about flat rental
- Seasonal businesses
- Performance-focused clients

❌ **Not Ideal For:**
- Low-value niches (too much admin)
- Clients who want predictable costs
- When you want passive income
- If you can't track leads properly

## 🔄 Model 3: Hybrid Model (Recommended)

### How It Works

Combines flat rental + pay-per-lead. Base fee covers baseline, PPL for overflow.

### Structure Example

```
Base Rental: $1,000/month
Includes: First 20 leads
Overage: $50/lead after 20

Scenario A (15 leads): Client pays $1,000
Scenario B (35 leads): Client pays $1,000 + (15 × $50) = $1,750
```

### Benefits

**For You:**
- Guaranteed baseline income
- Upside potential for high performance
- Easier to sell than pure PPL
- Retains client during slow months

**For Client:**
- Predictable base cost
- Only pay extra when they get extra value
- Lower risk than pure rental
- Scales with their capacity

### Hybrid Pricing Examples

**Example 1: Plumbing Site (Medium City)**
```
Base: $1,200/month
Includes: 25 leads
Overage: $45/lead
Average Month: 35 leads = $1,650 total
```

**Example 2: Roofing Site (Large City)**
```
Base: $2,000/month
Includes: 15 leads
Overage: $125/lead
Average Month: 25 leads = $3,250 total
```

**Example 3: HVAC Site (Suburban)**
```
Base: $1,500/month
Includes: 30 leads
Overage: $50/lead
Average Month: 45 leads = $2,250 total
```

### Hybrid Contract Terms

```
HYBRID LEAD GENERATION AGREEMENT

Base Fee: $X,XXX/month (due 1st of each month)
Included Leads: XX qualified leads/month
Overage Rate: $XX/lead for leads beyond included amount

Payment Terms:
- Base fee: Monthly, auto-pay
- Overage: Invoiced weekly/bi-weekly
- Payment due: Net 7 days

Lead Definition:
- Phone calls over 60 seconds
- Form submissions with valid contact info
- Within service area
- During business hours (unless after-hours service offered)

Exclusions:
- Spam/test submissions
- Wrong numbers
- Competitors
- Job seekers
- Outside service area

Term: 12 months, auto-renew
Termination: 30 days written notice
```

## 📊 Model 4: Revenue Share

### How It Works

You get a percentage of revenue generated from your leads.

### Typical Structures

| Model | Your Share | Requirements |
|-------|------------|--------------|
| **Per Job** | 10-20% | Track closed deals |
| **Monthly Revenue** | 5-15% | Access to their books |
| **Profit Share** | 20-30% | Full transparency needed |

### When Revenue Share Works

✅ **Good For:**
- Very high-value niches (personal injury, roofing)
- Long-term, trusted partnerships
- When you can track closed deals
- Clients with high close rates

❌ **Challenges:**
- Requires trust and transparency
- Need access to their CRM/sales data
- Harder to verify actual revenue
- Payment delayed until they get paid
- Potential for disputes

### Revenue Share Example

```
Personal Injury Site
- 20 leads/month
- 5 become clients (25% close rate)
- Avg. case value: $10,000
- Monthly revenue: $50,000
- Your share (10%): $5,000/month

vs. Flat Rental: $3,000/month
vs. PPL ($300/lead): $6,000/month
```

## 💸 Model 5: Site Sale (Exit Strategy)

### When to Sell

- Site is mature (2+ years old)
- Consistent revenue history
- You want lump sum vs. recurring
- Building portfolio to sell
- Retirement/exit plan

### Valuation Multiples

| Site Age | Monthly Multiple | Example ($2K/mo site) |
|----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| <1 year | 15-20x | $30,000-$40,000 |
| 1-2 years | 20-30x | $40,000-$60,000 |
| 2-3 years | 25-35x | $50,000-$70,000 |
| 3+ years | 30-40x | $60,000-$80,000 |

### Where to Sell

| Platform | Fee | Best For |
|----------|-----|----------|
| **Empire Flippers** | 15% | Established sites ($50K+) |
| **Flippa** | 5-10% | All sizes, auction format |
| **FE International** | 10-15% | Premium sites ($100K+) |
| **Quiet Light** | 10-15% | Established businesses |
| **Direct Sale** | 0% | Selling to client/competitor |

### Sale Preparation

**6 Months Before Sale:**
- ✅ Document all processes
- ✅ Clean up financials
- ✅ Ensure contracts are transferable
- ✅ Build standard operating procedures
- ✅ Diversify traffic sources
- ✅ Grow revenue (increases multiple)

**Sale Package Includes:**
- Financial statements (12-24 months)
- Traffic analytics
- Lead reports
- Contract details
- Operations manual
- Transition plan

## 📋 Pricing Strategy Framework

### Step 1: Calculate Lead Value

```
Client's Avg. Job Value × Close Rate = Lead Value

Example (Plumbing):
$800 (avg. job) × 25% (close rate) = $200 lead value

Example (Roofing):
$12,000 (avg. job) × 10% (close rate) = $1,200 lead value
```

### Step 2: Determine Your Price

**Rule of Thumb:** Charge 25-50% of lead value

```
Lead Value × 25-50% = Your Price Per Lead

Plumbing: $200 × 40% = $80/lead (charge $50-100)
Roofing: $1,200 × 30% = $360/lead (charge $200-400)
```

**For Rental:**
```
Expected Monthly Leads × Price Per Lead = Monthly Rental

30 leads × $75 = $2,250/month rental
```

### Step 3: Market Validation

**Research Competitors:**
- What are other lead gen companies charging?
- What are clients currently paying for leads?
- What's the local market rate?

**Ask Potential Clients:**
- "What do you currently pay per lead?"
- "What's a new customer worth to you?"
- "What would you pay for 30 qualified leads/month?"

### Step 4: Adjust for Factors

| Factor | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| High competition niche | +10-20% |
| Exclusive rights | +20-30% |
| Premium lead quality | +15-25% |
| Long-term contract | -10% (discount) |
| Multiple sites (volume) | -10-15% |
| New/unproven site | -20-30% (initial discount) |

## 📄 Contract Essentials

### Key Contract Clauses

**1. Lead Definition**
```
"Qualified Lead" means:
(a) Phone call lasting more than 60 seconds, OR
(b) Form submission with valid name, phone, and service request
(c) Caller/requester is within the Service Area
(d) Request is for services provided by Client
(e) Excludes: spam, competitors, job seekers, wrong numbers
```

**2. Payment Terms**
```
Client agrees to pay:
- Base Fee: $X,XXX due on the 1st of each month
- Overage Fees: $XX per qualified lead beyond [X] leads/month
- Payment Method: Automatic credit card/ACH charge
- Late Fees: 1.5% per month on overdue balances
```

**3. Exclusivity**
```
Provider grants Client exclusive rights to all leads generated from 
the Property during the Term. Provider will not sell, transfer, or 
forward leads to any other party in the Service Area.
```

**4. Term & Termination**
```
Initial Term: 12 months from Start Date
Auto-Renewal: Month-to-month after Initial Term
Termination: Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice
Early Termination Fee: 50% of remaining contract value (if applicable)
```

**5. Performance Guarantee (Optional)**
```
Provider guarantees minimum of [X] qualified leads per month.
If leads fall below minimum for 2 consecutive months, Client may:
(a) Terminate without penalty, OR
(b) Receive credit for shortfall at pro-rated lead price
```

**6. Ownership**
```
Provider retains all ownership rights to the Property, including 
website domain, content, phone numbers, and Google Business Profile.
Client receives license to use leads only.
```

## 💡 Monetization Best Practices

### Do's

✅ **Start with hybrid model** - Balances risk/reward
✅ **Get paid upfront** - Monthly, auto-pay
✅ **Define leads clearly** - Avoid disputes
✅ **Track everything** - Prove your value
✅ **Raise prices annually** - 10-15% at renewal
✅ **Offer volume discounts** - Incentivize multiple sites
✅ **Build relationships** - Retention > acquisition
✅ **Deliver reports** - Show your value monthly

### Don'ts

❌ **Don't underprice** - You're providing valuable leads
❌ **Don't accept net-30** - Get paid upfront
❌ **Don't be vague** - Clear contracts prevent issues
❌ **Don't overpromise** - Under-promise, over-deliver
❌ **Don't ignore slow months** - Communicate proactively
❌ **Don't work without contract** - Always have written agreement
❌ **Don't commingle leads** - Keep leads separate per client
❌ **Don't forget to raise prices** - Inflation + increased value
