## Creative Testing Framework

### The Creative Testing Pyramid

```
         /\
        /  \
       /    \      1-2 Winning Creatives
      /------\     (Scale these)
     /        \
    /          \    5-10 Testing Creatives
   /------------\   (Iterate on angles)
  /              \
 /                \   20+ Raw Variations
/------------------\  (Test everything)
```

### Creative Angles to Test

**Angle 1: Problem/Solution**
- Show the problem dramatically
- Introduce product as solution
- Show transformation

**Angle 2: Product Demo**
- Show product in action
- Highlight key features
- Prove it works

**Angle 3: Social Proof**
- Customer testimonials
- Before/after photos
- Review highlights

**Angle 4: Founder Story**
- Why you started the company
- Personal connection to problem
- Mission-driven

**Angle 5: Lifestyle/Aspirational**
- Show desired outcome
- Emotional connection
- Identity-based ("people like you...")

**Angle 6: Comparison**
- Your product vs. competitors
- Your product vs. old way
- Before vs. after using product

**Angle 7: Objection Handling**
- Address common concerns
- Show guarantee
- Prove quality/durability

**Angle 8: Urgency/Scarcity**
- Limited time offer
- Low stock warning
- Seasonal relevance

### Creative Formats to Test

**1. Single Image**
- Clean product shot
- Lifestyle image
- User-generated content (UGC)
- Before/after split
- Text overlay image

**2. Carousel (2-10 cards)**
- Product features (one per card)
- Step-by-step process
- Multiple products
- Story sequence
- Before → After progression

**3. Video (6-60 seconds)**
- Product demo
- Customer testimonial
- Unboxing
- How-to/tutorial
- Founder message
- UGC-style review

**4. Collection Ads**
- Cover image/video
- 4 product images below
- Great for catalogs

**5. Reels/Stories (9:16 vertical)**
- Full-screen vertical video
- Casual, authentic feel
- Trending audio (for organic)

### Video Creative Templates

**Template 1: UGC Review (15-30 seconds)**
```
0-3s:   Hook - "I finally found something that actually works for [problem]"
3-10s:  Show product, explain what it is
10-20s: Demonstrate use/results
20-25s: Show transformation/outcome
25-30s: CTA - "Link in bio" or "Try it risk-free"
```

**Template 2: Problem/Solution (30 seconds)**
```
0-3s:   Show problem dramatically (relatable pain point)
3-8s:   Agitate - "If you're like me, you've tried everything..."
8-15s:  Introduce product as solution
15-25s: Show how it works + results
25-30s: CTA with offer/guarantee
```

**Template 3: Product Demo (30-45 seconds)**
```
0-3s:   Hook - "Watch how this works"
3-10s:  Show product from multiple angles
10-25s: Demonstrate key features in action
25-35s: Show results/outcome
35-45s: Social proof + CTA
```

**Template 4: Before/After (15-30 seconds)**
```
0-3s:   Show "before" state (problem)
3-8s:   Introduce product
8-15s:  Show usage process
15-25s: Reveal "after" result
25-30s: CTA
```

### Creative Production Checklist

**For Each Creative:**
- [ ] Hook in first 3 seconds
- [ ] Clear value proposition
- [ ] Show product (don't just tell)
- [ ] Include social proof if possible
- [ ] Strong CTA
- [ ] Optimized for sound off (captions)
- [ ] Mobile-first (vertical or square)
- [ ] Brand visible but not overwhelming

**Technical Specs:**
- [ ] 1080x1080 (square) or 1080x1920 (vertical)
- [ ] MP4 format
- [ ] Under 100MB
- [ ] Captions included
- [ ] Clear audio (if using sound)

## Audience Targeting Strategies

### Audience Types

**1. Broad (No Targeting)**
- Age: 18-65+
- Gender: All
- Location: Your shipping countries
- No interests or behaviors

*Best for: Scaling, when pixel has 1000+ purchases*

**2. Interest-Based**
- Target specific interests related to your product
- Layer 2-3 interests together
- Keep audience size 1M-10M

*Best for: Testing new products, cold audiences*

**3. Lookalike Audiences (LAL)**
- Based on: Purchasers, ATC, Initiate Checkout, Email List
- Percentages: 1% (most similar), 2-5%, 5-10% (broader)
- Create from best customers (high LTV)

*Best for: Finding new customers similar to your best*

**4. Custom Audiences (Retargeting)**
- Website visitors (30, 60, 90 days)
- Add to cart (14, 30 days)
- Initiate checkout (14, 30 days)
- View content (7, 14 days)
- Email list
- Engagers (social media)

*Best for: Converting warm traffic*

### Interest Targeting Ideas

**By Product Category:**

| Category | Interest Examples |
|----------|-------------------|
| Fitness | Gymshark, CrossFit, Yoga, Personal training |
| Beauty | Sephora, Ulta, Skincare, Makeup tutorials |
| Home | Home decor, Interior design, IKEA, Wayfair |
| Pets | Chewy, Petco, Dog training, Cat lovers |
| Tech | Gadgets, Apple, Android, Tech reviewers |
| Fashion | Zara, H&M, Fashion bloggers, Style |
| Health | Wellness, Supplements, Organic food, Yoga |
| Parents | Baby products, Parenting, Toys, Family |

### Layering Strategy

**Narrow Your Audience:**
```
Interest 1: Fitness enthusiasts (15M)
AND MUST ALSO MATCH:
Interest 2: Gymshark (2M)
AND MUST ALSO MATCH:
Interest 3: Personal training (1M)

Result: ~500K-1M highly targeted audience
```

**Expand Your Audience:**
```
Interest 1 OR Interest 2 OR Interest 3
(Fitness OR Gymshark OR Personal training)

Result: ~20M broader audience
```

### Audience Testing Strategy

**Phase 1: Interest Testing (Week 1-2)**
- 5-10 different interest audiences
- $20-50/day per audience
- Kill losers after $50 spent with no purchases
- Scale winners

**Phase 2: Creative Testing (Week 3-4)**
- Take winning audiences
- Test 5-10 creatives per audience
- Find best creative/audience combos

**Phase 3: Lookalike Testing (Month 2+)**
- Create LALs from purchasers
- Test 1%, 2%, 5% LALs
- Compare to interest audiences

**Phase 4: Broad Scaling (Month 3+)**
- Remove targeting entirely
- Let algorithm find buyers
- Requires 1000+ purchases in pixel

## Campaign Structure Templates

### Campaign Structure Options

**Option 1: Simple Structure (Beginner)**
```
Campaign: Purchase (CBO - Campaign Budget Optimization)
├── Ad Set 1: Broad (no targeting)
├── Ad Set 2: Interest Stack 1
├── Ad Set 3: Interest Stack 2
├── Ad Set 4: Lookalike 1% Purchasers
└── Ad Set 5: Retargeting (30-day visitors)
    └── 3-5 Ads per Ad Set
```

**Option 2: Creative Testing Structure**
```
Campaign: Creative Testing (ABO - Ad Set Budget Optimization)
├── Ad Set 1: Audience A + Creative 1
├── Ad Set 2: Audience A + Creative 2
├── Ad Set 3: Audience B + Creative 1
├── Ad Set 4: Audience B + Creative 2
└── Each ad set: $20-50/day
```

**Option 3: Full Funnel Structure**
```
Campaign 1: Cold Traffic (Purchase)
├── Ad Set: Broad
├── Ad Set: Interests
└── Ad Set: Lookalikes

Campaign 2: Retargeting (Purchase)
├── Ad Set: 30-day visitors
├── Ad Set: Add to cart (14 days)
└── Ad Set: Initiate checkout (14 days)

Campaign 3: Email List (Purchase)
└── Ad Set: Custom audience (email upload)
```

### Budget Allocation

**Starting Budget ($100-200/day):**
- 70% Cold traffic ($70-140/day)
- 20% Retargeting ($20-40/day)
- 10% Email list ($10-20/day)

**Scaling Budget ($500-1000/day):**
- 60% Cold traffic
- 25% Retargeting
- 15% Lookalikes

### Campaign Settings

**Objective:** Sales (Conversions)
**Conversion Event:** Purchase
**Buying Type:** Auction
**Campaign Budget:** 
- CBO for scaling (let FB allocate)
- ABO for testing (control per ad set)

**Ad Set Settings:**
- **Conversion Location:** Website
- **Pixel:** Your Meta Pixel
- **Event:** Purchase
- **Attribution:** 7-day click, 1-day view (default)
- **Bid Strategy:** Lowest cost (no bid cap initially)

**Audience:**
- **Location:** Countries you ship to
- **Age:** 18-65+ (or your demo)
- **Gender:** All (or your demo)
- **Detailed Targeting:** Interests/LALs
- **Placements:** Advantage+ (automatic)

**Optimization:**
- **Optimize for:** Conversions
- **Conversion Event:** Purchase
- **Bid Cap:** None (start with lowest cost)

## Scaling Methodologies

### When to Scale

**Green Lights (Scale When):**
- ✅ ROAS above target for 3+ consecutive days
- ✅ CPA below target for 5+ purchases
- ✅ Consistent daily spend without issues
- ✅ Frequency under 3.0 (not fatigued)
- ✅ CTR above 1% (creative still working)

**Red Lights (Don't Scale Yet):**
- ❌ Less than 3 days of data
- ❌ Fewer than 10-20 purchases
- ❌ Inconsistent results
- ❌ High frequency (>4.0)
- ❌ Rising CPA

### Scaling Methods

**Method 1: Vertical Scaling (Increase Budget)**
```
Current: $100/day → 3 days stable → $150/day
$150/day → 3 days stable → $200/day
$200/day → 3 days stable → $300/day
...
```

**Rules:**
- Increase by 20-50% at a time
- Wait 3 days between increases
- Don't scale more than once per week per ad set
- Monitor CPA closely

**Method 2: Horizontal Scaling (Duplicate Winners)**
```
Winning Ad Set → Duplicate 3-5 times
Each duplicate: Same budget as original
Let them compete and find new audiences
```

**When to Use:**
- When vertical scaling causes CPA to rise
- When you've hit audience saturation
- When you want to maintain stable CPA

**Method 3: Broad Scaling (Remove Targeting)**
```
Take winning creative
Put it in broad ad set (no targeting)
Let algorithm find buyers
Budget: 50-100% of total spend
```

**Requirements:**
- 1000+ purchases in pixel
- Strong creative (proven winner)
- Patience (2-3 weeks to optimize)

**Method 4: Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO)**
```
Create CBO campaign
Add multiple ad sets (broad + LALs + interests)
Set campaign-level budget
Facebook allocates to best performers
```

**Best Practices:**
- Minimum 3-5 ad sets
- Similar audiences (don't mix cold + retargeting)
- Let it run 5-7 days before judging

### Scaling Checklist

Before Scaling:
- [ ] At least 7 days of stable data
- [ ] ROAS above target (3.0+ ideally)
- [ ] CPA profitable
- [ ] Frequency under 3.0
- [ ] CTR stable or improving
- [ ] No major creative fatigue

During Scaling:
- [ ] Monitor daily
- [ ] Check CPA trend
- [ ] Watch frequency
- [ ] Pause if performance drops
- [ ] Be ready to scale back

After Scaling:
- [ ] Evaluate after 3-5 days
- [ ] Compare to pre-scale metrics
- [ ] Decide: continue, pause, or adjust
- [ ] Document learnings

## ROAS Optimization Tactics

### Understanding ROAS

**ROAS Formula:**
```
ROAS = Revenue from Ads / Ad Spend

Example:
$10,000 revenue / $2,500 ad spend = 4.0 ROAS
```

**Break-Even ROAS:**
```
Break-Even ROAS = 1 / Profit Margin

Example (70% margin):
1 / 0.70 = 1.43 break-even ROAS

Example (50% margin):
1 / 0.50 = 2.0 break-even ROAS
```

**Target ROAS:**
```
Aim for 2-3x break-even for healthy profit

70% margin → 1.43 break-even → Target 3.0-4.0 ROAS
50% margin → 2.0 break-even → Target 4.0-6.0 ROAS
```

### ROAS Optimization Levers

**Lever 1: Increase AOV (Average Order Value)**
- Product bundles
- Upsells (pre and post-purchase)
- Free shipping threshold (2-3x current AOV)
- Volume discounts (Buy 2, Save 10%)
- Premium product options

**Lever 2: Increase Conversion Rate**
- Better landing pages
- More trust signals
- Clearer value proposition
- Faster site speed
- Better mobile experience
- More payment options

**Lever 3: Decrease CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)**
- Better creatives (higher CTR = lower CPC)
- Better targeting (more relevant audiences)
- Optimize ad delivery (let algorithm work)
- Scale winning ads (better delivery)
- Test new audiences

**Lever 4: Increase LTV (Lifetime Value)**
- Email marketing flows
- Retention campaigns
- Subscription model
- Loyalty program
- Repeat purchase incentives

### ROAS Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low ROAS (<2.0)**

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| High CPC | Poor creative, wrong audience | Test new creatives, refine targeting |
| Low CTR (<1%) | Weak hook, wrong audience | New creative angles, better targeting |
| Low CVR (<2%) | Landing page issues | Optimize product page, check load speed |
| High CPA | All of the above | Full funnel audit |

**Problem: ROAS Dropping Over Time**

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Frequency rising (>4) | Creative fatigue | Launch new creatives |
| CTR declining | Audience tired of ad | Refresh creative, new angles |
| CPA rising | Competition, fatigue | Scale horizontally, new audiences |
| All metrics declining | Market saturation | New products, new markets |

### Optimization Checklist

**Daily:**
- [ ] Check spend vs. budget
- [ ] Review ROAS by campaign
- [ ] Identify underperformers (ROAS <1.5)
- [ ] Pause clear losers

**Weekly:**
- [ ] Full account review
- [ ] Launch 2-3 new creatives
- [ ] Scale winners (20-50% budget increase)
- [ ] Kill underperformers (7+ days, ROAS <2.0)
- [ ] Review frequency and CTR trends

**Monthly:**
- [ ] Creative refresh (50%+ new creatives)
- [ ] Audience expansion
- [ ] Test new campaign structures
- [ ] Review attribution windows
- [ ] Analyze LTV by cohort
