## 1. Securities Law Analysis

### The Howey Test (United States)

The SEC uses the Howey Test to determine if something is a security:

1. **Investment of Money** - Are people investing capital?
2. **In a Common Enterprise** - Is there a shared business venture?
3. **With Expectation of Profits** - Do investors expect returns?
4. **Derived from Efforts of Others** - Are profits from others' work?

**If ALL four are met → Likely a Security**

### Token Classification

| Token Type | Security Risk | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Utility Token | Low-Medium | Must have actual utility |
| Governance Token | Medium | Depends on structure |
| Payment Token | Low | If primarily for transactions |
| NFT (Art) | Low | If truly unique art |
| NFT (Fractional) | High | Treated like security |
| Staking Token | Medium-High | Profit expectation |
| Meme Token | High | Speculative nature |

### Avoiding Security Classification

**Strategies:**

✅ **Do:**
- Launch with functional product
- Emphasize utility over investment
- Decentralize governance quickly
- Avoid profit promises
- No marketing about price appreciation
- Broad, fair distribution

❌ **Don't:**
- Promise returns or profits
- Market as investment opportunity
- Centralized control indefinitely
- Pre-sell to investors only
- Guarantee buybacks
- Emphasize speculation

### SAFT Framework (For Private Sales)

**Simple Agreement for Future Tokens:**

- For accredited investors only
- Tokens delivered at network launch
- Clear utility described
- Proper disclosures included
- Legal counsel required

**Cost:** $15K-$50K (legal drafting)

## 3. AML/KYC Compliance

### When AML/KYC Applies

**Required For:**
- Centralized exchanges
- Custodial services
- Fiat on/off ramps
- Some token sales (jurisdiction-dependent)
- High-value transactions (>$10K)

**Generally NOT Required For:**
- Pure DeFi protocols
- Non-custodial wallets
- NFT minting (typically)
- Token swaps (DEX)

## 4. Tax Considerations

### Token Tax Treatment (US)

**Token Sales:**
- Generally taxable as income
- Fair market value at receipt
- Form 1099 reporting may apply

**Token Appreciation:**
- Capital gains when sold
- Short-term (<1 year): Ordinary income rates
- Long-term (>1 year): Preferential rates

**Staking Rewards:**
- Taxable as income when received
- Basis = fair market value at receipt
- Additional gain/loss on sale

### NFT Tax Treatment

**Creator:**
- Income tax on sales
- Royalties = ordinary income

**Collector:**
- Capital gains on sale
- Collectibles rate (28%) may apply
- Wash sale rules don't apply (currently)

### International Tax

**Key Considerations:**
- VAT/GST on digital goods (EU, UK, Australia)
- Withholding taxes on cross-border payments
- Transfer pricing for international entities
- Permanent establishment risks

### Tax Planning Strategies

✅ **Do:**
- Consult tax advisor early
- Document everything
- Consider entity jurisdiction
- Plan token distribution timing
- Understand investor tax implications

❌ **Don't:**
- Ignore tax obligations
- Assume crypto = tax-free
- Mix personal and project finances
- Forget about international obligations

## 6. International Regulations

### United States

**Key Regulators:**
- SEC (securities)
- CFTC (commodities)
- FinCEN (AML)
- OFAC (sanctions)
- IRS (tax)

**Key Requirements:**
- Securities registration or exemption
- AML program (if applicable)
- Sanctions compliance
- Tax reporting

**Enforcement Trends:**
- Increased scrutiny of token sales
- Staking services under review
- DeFi protocols in scope
- NFT fractionalization = security

### European Union

**MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation):**
- Effective: 2024-2025
- Comprehensive framework
- Licensing requirements
- Consumer protections
- Stablecoin regulations

**Key Requirements:**
- White paper for token offerings
- Licensed service providers
- Capital requirements
- AML compliance
- Consumer disclosures

### United Kingdom

**FCA Regulations:**
- Financial promotion rules
- Registration for crypto businesses
- AML compliance
- Consumer protections

**Key Requirements:**
- Approved financial promotions
- FCA registration (certain activities)
- Clear risk warnings

### Singapore

**MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore):**
- Payment Services Act
- Licensing for exchanges
- AML compliance
- Token classification

**Key Requirements:**
- License for certain activities
- AML/CFT compliance
- Capital requirements

### UAE (Dubai/ADGM)

**VARA (Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority):**
- Comprehensive licensing
- Activity-specific permits
- Consumer protections
- AML compliance

**Key Requirements:**
- VARA license for most activities
- Local presence required
- Compliance officer
- Capital requirements

## 7. Compliance Best Practices

### Ongoing Compliance

**Monthly:**
- Monitor regulatory developments
- Review community communications
- Check OFAC/sanctions lists
- Update risk assessments

**Quarterly:**
- Compliance review meeting
- Policy updates if needed
- Team training refresh
- Audit preparation

**Annually:**
- Full compliance audit
- Policy comprehensive review
- Legal counsel review
- Regulatory filings (if required)

### Communication Guidelines

**Safe Statements:**
- "This token provides access to..."
- "Holders can participate in..."
- "The protocol enables..."
- "Utility includes..."

**Avoid Statements:**
- "This will increase in value..."
- "Invest now for returns..."
- "Guaranteed profits..."
- "Early investors will..."

### Red Flags for Enforcement

🚩 **High Risk:**
- Promises of returns
- Emphasis on speculation
- Centralized control with no plan to decentralize
- Targeting US investors without compliance
- History of regulatory issues
- Anonymous team

🟢 **Lower Risk:**
- Clear utility
- Functional product at launch
- Decentralized governance
- Proper legal structure
- Transparent team
- Compliance-first approach

## Finding Legal Counsel

### What to Look For

**Experience:**
- 5+ years in crypto/blockchain
- Previous token sale experience
- Regulatory defense experience
- International expertise

**Services:**
- Entity formation
- Securities analysis
- Document drafting
- Ongoing counsel
- Regulatory strategy

### Questions to Ask

1. How many token projects have you advised?
2. What's your view on current regulatory environment?
3. Have you dealt with SEC inquiries?
4. What jurisdictions do you recommend for our project?
5. What's your fee structure?
6. Can you provide references?

### Finding Counsel

- Word of mouth (other founders)
- Crypto law firms (Cooley, Perkins Coie, etc.)
- Crypto-specific firms (DLA Piper, K&L Gates)
- Boutique crypto firms
- Legal directories (Chambers, Legal 500)
