# Client Acquisition

**Strategies for Landing Web3 Agency Clients**

---

## Overview

This guide covers proven strategies for acquiring high-value Web3 agency clients, from outreach to close.

**Target Client Value:** $50K - $500K per project
**Sales Cycle:** 2-8 weeks (typical)
**Close Rate Goal:** 20-30% of qualified leads

---

## 1. Ideal Client Profile

### Client Tiers

**Tier 1: Enterprise/Brand ($250K-$500K+)**
- Established companies entering Web3
- Marketing budgets: $1M+
- Decision makers: CMO, CTO, Innovation Lead
- Timeline: 3-6 months
- Examples: Nike, Adidas, Starbucks, Reddit

**Tier 2: Funded Startups ($100K-$250K)**
- VC-backed crypto startups
- Raise: $5M-$50M
- Decision makers: Founder, CEO
- Timeline: 1-3 months
- Examples: New L1/L2, DeFi protocols, NFT platforms

**Tier 3: Growth Projects ($50K-$100K)**
- Community-driven projects
- Some funding/revenue
- Decision makers: Founder, Community Lead
- Timeline: 2-6 weeks
- Examples: NFT collections, meme tokens, DeFi projects

### Client Qualification Criteria

**Green Flags ✅:**
- Clear budget ($50K+)
- Realistic timeline
- Decent existing presence
- Responsive communication
- Previous Web3 experience (or committed to learning)
- Aligned values

**Red Flags 🚩:**
- No budget clarity
- "We'll pay in tokens" (100%)
- Unrealistic expectations
- Slow/unresponsive
- History of failed projects
- Ethical concerns

### Qualification Questions

**Budget:**
- "What budget range have you allocated for this project?"
- "Is this budget approved and available?"
- "How do you typically structure payments?"

**Timeline:**
- "What's your target launch date?"
- "What milestones are you working toward?"
- "Are there any hard deadlines?"

**Decision Process:**
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What does your approval process look like?"
- "When do you need to make a decision by?"

**Project Scope:**
- "What's the primary goal of this project?"
- "What does success look like?"
- "What have you tried so far?"

## 3. Outreach Templates

### Twitter DM Template

```
Hey [Name]! Been following [Project] and love what you're 
building with [specific detail]. 

I run a Web3 agency that's helped projects like [similar 
project] launch successfully ([result - e.g., "$2M raise", 
"10K sellout"]).

Not sure if you're looking for support, but happy to share 
some insights from our work if useful. Either way, keep 
crushing it! 🚀
```

### LinkedIn Connection Request

```
Hi [Name],

I've been following [Company]'s work in [area] and am 
impressed by [specific achievement]. 

I specialize in helping Web3 projects with [your specialty] 
and thought there might be synergies to explore.

Would love to connect and learn more about what you're 
building.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Cold Email Template

```
Subject: Quick question about [Project Name]

Hi [Name],

I came across [Project] while researching [relevant topic] 
and was impressed by [specific observation].

I run [Agency], a Web3 agency that's helped projects like 
[Client 1] and [Client 2] [specific results].

Given what you're building with [Project], I noticed 
[opportunity/observation]. We recently helped a similar 
project [result].

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call to discuss? 
No pressure at all - happy to share insights either way.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Title]
[Agency]
[Website]
[Calendar Link]
```

### Follow-Up Sequence

**Email 1:** Initial outreach (above)
**Email 2:** 3 days later - add value (article, insight)
**Email 3:** 5 days later - case study
**Email 4:** 7 days later - final check-in

```
Email 2 Example:
Subject: Thought you might find this useful

Hi [Name],

Following up on my note below. Came across this 
[article/report] about [relevant topic] and thought of 
[Project].

[1-2 sentence summary of value]

Still happy to chat if useful.

Best,
[Name]
```

## 4. Sales Process

### Stage 1: Discovery Call (30 min)

**Agenda:**
1. Introductions (5 min)
2. Their story & project (10 min)
3. Challenges & goals (10 min)
4. Next steps (5 min)

**Key Questions:**
- "Tell me about the project and vision"
- "What's the biggest challenge right now?"
- "What does success look like?"
- "What's your timeline?"
- "What budget range are you working with?"
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"

**Goals:**
- Understand their needs
- Qualify budget/timeline
- Build rapport
- Schedule proposal meeting

### Stage 2: Proposal Meeting (45-60 min)

**Preparation:**
- Custom proposal deck
- Scope of work
- Timeline
- Investment
- Case studies

**Agenda:**
1. Recap their goals (5 min)
2. Present solution (20 min)
3. Investment & terms (10 min)
4. Q&A (15 min)
5. Next steps (10 min)

**Goals:**
- Show you understand their needs
- Present clear solution
- Address concerns
- Move toward close

### Stage 3: Closing (1-2 weeks)

**Activities:**
- Answer follow-up questions
- Adjust proposal if needed
- Contract negotiation
- Legal review
- Signature & deposit

**Closing Techniques:**
- "Does this solution address your main concerns?"
- "What would need to happen for you to move forward?"
- "Is there anything preventing you from starting?"
- "Would it help to [address specific concern]?"

## 6. Pricing Strategy

### Value-Based Pricing

**Don't price on hours. Price on value.**

```
Client's Expected Raise: $5M
Your Fee: $150K (3% of raise)
Value: Clear - you're helping them raise $5M
```

```
Client's NFT Project:
Expected Revenue: $2M (10K × 0.05 ETH × $4K)
Your Fee: $100K (5% of expected revenue)
Value: Clear - you're enabling $2M launch
```

### Pricing Psychology

**Anchoring:**
- Show premium option first
- Makes standard option seem reasonable

**Decoy:**
- Three options, middle is target
- Makes target look like best value

**Payment Terms:**
```
Standard Terms:
- 30% upfront
- 40% at milestone (50% complete)
- 30% at delivery

Alternative:
- 50% upfront
- 50% at delivery

Enterprise:
- 25% upfront
- 25% at 33%
- 25% at 66%
- 25% at completion
```

### Token/Equity Component

**When to Accept:**
- High-conviction project
- Strong team
- Clear path to value
- Can afford to take risk

**Typical Structures:**
```
Option 1: Fee + Tokens
- 70% cash
- 30% tokens (at current valuation)
- 1 year vesting

Option 2: Reduced Fee + Tokens
- 50% of normal fee
- 5-10% token supply
- 2 year vesting

Option 3: Advisory Tokens
- Minimal cash ($10K-$50K)
- 1-3% advisory tokens
- 2 year vesting, 1 year cliff
```

## 11. Common Objections

### "It's too expensive"

**Response:**
```
"I understand budget is a consideration. Can I ask what 
you were expecting to invest?

[Listen]

Given that we're helping you [achieve X result worth $Y], 
the investment actually represents [Z%] of the expected 
value.

That said, we can explore phasing the project or adjusting 
scope to better fit your budget."
```

### "We can do this in-house"

**Response:**
```
"That's a valid approach. Many teams consider that. 

Out of curiosity, do you have [specific expertise] on the 
team already? [Wait for answer]

The reason clients work with us is [specific value - speed, 
expertise, network]. We've seen teams save 3-6 months by 
partnering vs. building in-house.

What's the opportunity cost of delaying launch by X months?"
```

### "We need to think about it"

**Response:**
```
"Absolutely - this is an important decision. 

To help with your thinking, is there anything specific 
you'd like more clarity on? Any concerns I can address?

Also, what's your ideal timeline for getting started?"
```

### "We want to pay in tokens"

**Response:**
```
"We do consider token arrangements for the right projects. 

Typically we do a hybrid: [50-70%] cash + [30-50%] tokens 
with vesting. This aligns us with your success while 
covering our costs.

What's your token allocation strategy for advisors/partners?"
```
