# Team Building Guide

## How to Build and Scale Your TikTok Agency Team

From solo operator to full-service agency. Hiring, managing, and scaling your team.

---

## 👥 TEAM STRUCTURE OVERVIEW

### Phase 1: Solo (0-$10K/month)
**You do everything:**
- Sales
- Strategy
- Content creation
- Editing
- Posting
- Client communication
- Analytics

**Goal:** Get to $10K/month, then hire

---

### Phase 2: First Hire ($10K-30K/month)
**Hire: Video Editor**
- Frees up 10-15 hours/week
- Allows you to focus on sales and strategy
- Cost: $1,500-3,000/month (part-time or per video)

**You still do:**
- Sales
- Strategy
- Client communication
- Quality control

---

### Phase 3: Small Team ($30K-70K/month)
**Team of 3-5:**
- You (CEO/Creative Director)
- 1-2 Video Editors
- 1 Content Strategist/Scriptwriter
- 1 Account Manager (or VA)

**Costs:** $8,000-20,000/month
**Capacity:** 15-25 clients

---

### Phase 4: Scaled Agency ($70K-150K/month)
**Team of 8-15:**
- You (CEO)
- COO/Operations Manager
- 3-5 Video Editors
- 2 Content Strategists
- 2 Account Managers
- 1 Ads Specialist
- 1-2 Junior staff/VAs

**Costs:** $30,000-60,000/month
**Capacity:** 40-60 clients

---

### Phase 5: Full Agency ($150K+/month)
**Department Structure:**
- **Leadership:** CEO, COO, CFO
- **Sales:** Sales Director, 2-3 AEs, SDR
- **Operations:** Operations Manager, Project Managers
- **Creative:** Creative Director, 5-10 Editors, 3-5 Scriptwriters
- **Strategy:** Strategy Director, 3-5 Strategists
- **Account Management:** 4-6 Account Managers
- **Ads:** Ads Director, 2-3 Specialists
- **Support:** 2-3 VAs, HR, Admin

**Costs:** $80,000-150,000+/month
**Capacity:** 75-150+ clients

## 🎬 KEY ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES

### 1. Video Editor

**Responsibilities:**
- Edit 10-20 videos per week
- Add captions, effects, transitions
- Source trending sounds
- Maintain brand consistency
- Meet deadlines
- Revise based on feedback

**Skills Needed:**
- Proficient in CapCut, Premiere Pro, or Final Cut
- Understanding of TikTok trends
- Fast turnaround (24-48 hours)
- Attention to detail
- Creative eye

**Where to Hire:**
- Upwork
- Fiverr Pro
- OnlineJobs.ph (Philippines)
- TikTok creator Discords
- Referrals from other editors

**Compensation:**
- Entry: $15-25/hour or $50-100/video
- Experienced: $25-50/hour or $100-200/video
- Full-time: $3,000-6,000/month

**Trial Task:**
Give candidates raw footage and ask them to edit a 30-second TikTok. Evaluate:
- Pacing and retention
- Caption quality
- Trend awareness
- Turnaround time
- Communication

---

- Upwork

**Trial Task:**

## 🔍 HIRING PROCESS

### Step 1: Write a Clear Job Description

**Include:**
- Role title and summary
- Key responsibilities
- Required skills
- Nice-to-have skills
- Compensation range
- Location/timezone requirements
- How to apply

**Example Job Post:**

```
🎬 TikTok Video Editor Needed (Remote)

We're a fast-growing TikTok agency looking for a 
talented video editor to join our team.

📋 Responsibilities:
- Edit 15-20 TikToks per week
- Add captions, effects, and trending elements
- Maintain quick turnaround (24-48 hours)
- Collaborate with strategy team

✅ Requirements:
- 2+ years editing short-form video
- Proficient in CapCut or Premiere Pro
- Understanding of TikTok trends
- Portfolio of TikTok edits
- Reliable internet and equipment

💰 Compensation: $3,000-5,000/month 
   (based on experience and volume)

📍 Location: Remote (any timezone)

To apply: Send your portfolio and edit 1 sample 
video from this raw footage: [link]

Applications without a sample will not be considered.
```

---

### Step 2: Source Candidates

**Where to Post:**
- Upwork (great for editors, writers)
- LinkedIn (for strategic roles)
- Twitter/X (creator community)
- Facebook groups (agency owner groups)
- Discord servers (creator economies)
- Referrals (best source)

**Outbound Recruiting:**
- Find editors on TikTok (check credits)
- DM promising candidates
- Check competitor agencies (poach carefully)
- Post in niche communities

---

### Step 3: Screen Applications

**Look For:**
- Relevant portfolio
- Clear communication
- Understanding of TikTok
- Professionalism
- Enthusiasm for the role

**Red Flags:**
- Generic applications
- No portfolio
- Poor communication
- Unrealistic expectations
- Negative about past employers

**Screening Questions:**
1. What's your favorite TikTok trend right now?
2. How do you approach retention in edits?
3. What's your typical turnaround time?
4. Describe a challenging project and how you handled it.
5. What are your salary expectations?

---

### Step 4: Trial Project (CRITICAL)

**Never hire without a paid trial.**

**For Editors:**
- Provide raw footage
- Give clear direction
- Set deadline (24-48 hours)
- Pay them for the work ($50-100)
- Evaluate quality, speed, communication

**For Strategists:**
- Give them a real client account (anonymized)
- Ask for audit and content plan
- Evaluate strategic thinking
- Pay them for the work ($100-200)

**For Account Managers:**
- Role-play client scenarios
- Write sample reports
- Handle objections
- Evaluate communication style

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Quality of work
- Speed/efficiency
- Communication
- Receptiveness to feedback
- Cultural fit

---

### Step 5: Onboarding

**Week 1:**
- Welcome call with CEO
- Tool access (Slack, Asana, Google Drive)
- Training on processes
- Meet the team
- Review style guides

**Week 2:**
- Shadow team members
- Handle small tasks
- Daily check-ins
- Feedback sessions

**Week 3-4:**
- Take on real work
- Weekly 1:1 with manager
- Performance review at 30 days

**30-60-90 Day Plan:**
- 30 days: Fully ramped up
- 60 days: Working independently
- 90 days: Full productivity

## 💰 COMPENSATION STRUCTURES

### Salary vs. Contractor

**Contractors (1099):**
- Pros: Flexibility, no benefits, scale up/down
- Cons: Less loyalty, can work for competitors, less control
- Best for: Editors, writers, VAs

**Employees (W-2):**
- Pros: Loyalty, exclusivity, more control, culture
- Cons: Benefits, payroll taxes, harder to terminate
- Best for: Account managers, strategists, leadership

### Performance Bonuses

**For Editors:**
- Volume bonus: +$500/month for 25+ videos/week
- Quality bonus: +$200/month for 4.8+ avg client rating
- Retention bonus: +$300/month for 6+ month tenure

**For Account Managers:**
- Retention bonus: 5% of retained client value
- Upsell commission: 10% of upsell value
- Satisfaction bonus: $500/month for 9+ avg NPS

**For Strategists:**
- Performance bonus: Based on client growth metrics
- Innovation bonus: $250 for implemented new strategies

## 📈 SCALING THE TEAM

### When to Hire

**Hire an Editor When:**
- You're spending 15+ hours/week editing
- You have 5+ clients
- Revenue is $10K+/month
- You're turning down work due to capacity

**Hire a Strategist When:**
- You're spending 10+ hours/week on strategy
- You have 10+ clients
- Revenue is $20K+/month
- Strategy quality is slipping

**Hire an Account Manager When:**
- You're spending 10+ hours/week on client calls
- You have 10+ clients
- Revenue is $30K+/month
- Client communication is slipping

**Hire a Sales Person When:**
- You're turning down inbound leads
- You have capacity for more clients
- Revenue is $50K+/month
- You want to focus on operations

### Scaling Principles

**1. Hire Ahead of Need**
- Hire when you're 80% at capacity
- Takes 30-60 days to ramp up
- Better to have slight bench than turn work

**2. Document Everything**
- Create SOPs before hiring
- Record Loom videos of processes
- Build training materials
- Makes onboarding faster

**3. Hire for Culture Fit**
- Skills can be taught
- Attitude is harder to change
- Look for hungry, humble, smart

**4. Invest in Training**
- Weekly team training
- Share wins and learnings
- Send to conferences
- Better team = better results

**5. Create Career Paths**
- Junior → Senior → Lead → Manager
- Clear promotion criteria
- Retention increases with growth

## ⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES

### Hiring Mistakes
- ❌ Hiring too cheap (you get what you pay for)
- ❌ Skipping trial projects
- ❌ Hiring friends/family without vetting
- ❌ Not documenting processes
- ❌ Micromanaging instead of training

### Management Mistakes
- ❌ Not giving clear expectations
- ❌ Infrequent feedback
- ❌ No career growth path
- ❌ Underpaying top performers
- ❌ Tolerating toxic behavior

### Scaling Mistakes
- ❌ Scaling before product-market fit
- ❌ Hiring too fast
- ❌ Losing culture during growth
- ❌ Not investing in systems
- ❌ Founder bottlenecking decisions
