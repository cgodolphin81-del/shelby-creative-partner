# Client Acquisition System

## How to Land High-Ticket TikTok Agency Clients ($2K-10K/month)

A complete system for finding, pitching, and closing TikTok agency clients.

---

## 🎯 IDEAL CLIENT PROFILE

### Who to Target

**Best Clients:**
- E-commerce brands ($500K+ revenue)
- Coaches and consultants (already making $10K+/month)
- SaaS companies (Series A+)
- Real estate agents (top producers)
- Personal brands (authors, speakers, experts)
- Local businesses with multiple locations
- Apps and digital products
- Health/wellness brands

**Avoid:**
- Businesses with no marketing budget
- Owners who don't understand social media value
- Companies expecting overnight results
- Businesses in declining industries
- Clients who've burned through 5+ agencies

### Client Qualification Checklist

Before taking on a client, verify:

- [ ] Monthly revenue: $50K+ (for e-commerce) or $20K+ (for services)
- [ ] Marketing budget: $5K-20K/month available
- [ ] Decision maker is engaged (not delegating entirely)
- [ ] They understand it takes 3-6 months to see results
- [ ] They have product-market fit (not figuring out their offer)
- [ ] They're responsive and provide feedback timely
- [ ] They trust experts (not micromanagers)
- [ ] They've had success with marketing before

**Red Flags:**
- ❌ "We need results in 30 days"
- ❌ "Our budget is tight but..."
- ❌ Owner unavailable for calls
- ❌ Constant strategy changes
- ❌ Unwilling to invest in ads
- ❌ No clear target audience
- ❌ Expecting organic-only to work quickly

## 🔍 PROSPECTING STRATEGIES

### Strategy 1: The TikTok Hunt

**Find businesses already on TikTok but doing it poorly:**

1. Search niche hashtags (#ecommerce, #coaching, #saas)
2. Find brand accounts with:
   - 1K-50K followers (they care but struggling)
   - Inconsistent posting
   - Low engagement (<2%)
   - Poor video quality
3. Note their pain points
4. Reach out with specific feedback

**Script:**
```
Hey [Name],

I was checking out [Brand]'s TikTok and love what you're 
doing with [specific thing]. 

I noticed a few opportunities that could 3-5x your 
engagement:
- [Specific observation 1]
- [Specific observation 2]
- [Specific observation 3]

I run a TikTok agency that specializes in [niche]. We 
recently helped [similar brand] go from [X] to [Y] in 
[timeframe].

Would you be open to a quick 15-min call? I'll share 
a few more specific ideas for [Brand] - no pitch, 
just value.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## 📞 THE SALES CALL

### Call Structure (30-45 minutes)

**Part 1: Rapport Building (5 min)**
- Small talk, build connection
- Confirm agenda
- Set expectations

**Part 2: Discovery (15 min)**
Ask questions like:
- "What's your current TikTok presence?"
- "What goals are you trying to hit?"
- "What's worked/not worked in the past?"
- "What's your ideal customer?"
- "What's your marketing budget?"
- "What does success look like in 90 days?"

**Part 3: Presentation (10 min)**
- Share your framework
- Show relevant case studies
- Explain your process
- Present pricing/packages

**Part 4: Handle Objections (10 min)**
- Address concerns
- Provide clarity
- Reinforce value

**Part 5: Close (5 min)**
- Ask for the sale
- Next steps
- Get commitment

---

### Common Objections & Responses

**"It's too expensive"**
```
I totally get that. Can I ask - compared to what?

If you're comparing this to doing it yourself or 
hiring a cheap freelancer, I get it. But we're not 
just posting videos - we're building a revenue 
channel.

Our clients typically see [ROI]. At that rate, 
the investment pays for itself in [timeframe].

What's your target ROI on marketing spend?
```

**"We need to think about it"**
```
Of course! What specifically do you need to think 
through?

Is it the investment? The timeline? Whether we're 
the right fit?

I ask because I want to make sure I address any 
concerns before you make a decision.
```

**"We've tried agencies before and they didn't deliver"**
```
I hear that a lot, and honestly, I don't blame you 
for being cautious. The agency space is full of 
people who overpromise and underdeliver.

Can I ask what specifically didn't work with the 
last agency?

[Listen carefully]

Here's how we're different: [specific differentiation]

Plus, we [guarantee/risk reversal if you offer one].

Would it help if we started with a smaller pilot 
project to prove ourselves first?
```

**"We want to try it ourselves first"**
```
That makes total sense. Let me ask - what's the 
cost of figuring it out on your own?

Your time has value. Most brands we work with 
spent 6+ months trying DIY before coming to us, 
and they wish they'd started sooner.

We've already made all the mistakes and figured 
out what works. You'd be paying for that knowledge 
and saving 6-12 months of trial and error.

But I respect wanting to try it yourself. How about 
this - let's stay in touch. If you hit a wall or 
want to accelerate, reach out. Here's my card.
```

**"Send me more information"**
```
Happy to! What specific information would be most 
helpful?

- More case studies in your industry?
- Detailed breakdown of our process?
- References from current clients?
- Something else?

I want to make sure I send you what's actually 
useful for your decision.
```

## 💰 PRICING STRATEGY

### Tier 1: Starter ($2,000-4,000/month)
**For:** Small businesses, solopreneurs
**Includes:**
- 15-20 videos/month
- Basic strategy
- Community management
- Monthly reporting

### Tier 2: Growth ($4,000-7,000/month)
**For:** Established businesses, funded startups
**Includes:**
- 20-30 videos/month
- Advanced strategy
- Trend integration
- Bi-weekly calls
- Ads management (optional)
- Detailed analytics

### Tier 3: Enterprise ($7,000-10,000+/month)
**For:** Brands, larger companies
**Includes:**
- 30+ videos/month
- Full-service management
- Dedicated team
- Weekly calls
- Ads management included
- Influencer coordination
- Custom reporting dashboard
- Priority support

### Pricing Psychology Tips

**1. Always offer 3 tiers**
- Makes middle option look reasonable
- Anchors high price
- Gives options

**2. Present high to low**
- Start with Enterprise
- Then Growth
- Then Starter
- Makes Starter seem affordable

**3. Show ROI, not cost**
- "Investment of $5K/month"
- "Expected return: $20-50K/month"
- Frame as investment, not expense

**4. Annual discount**
- Offer 10-20% off for annual prepay
- Improves cash flow
- Commits client longer

**5. Performance bonuses**
- Base fee + performance incentives
- Aligns interests
- Upside for you

## 🎯 CLOSING RATE OPTIMIZATION

### Improve Your Close Rate:

**1. Qualify Harder**
- Don't pitch everyone
- Only pitch qualified prospects
- Better to walk away than take bad clients

**2. Show, Don't Tell**
- Case studies over claims
- Specific numbers over vague promises
- Video testimonials over text

**3. Create Urgency (Ethically)**
- "We have capacity for 2 more clients this month"
- "Pricing increases next quarter"
- "This strategy works best when implemented now"

**4. Risk Reversal**
- 30-day satisfaction guarantee
- Performance-based pricing option
- Month-to-month after initial term

**5. Follow Up**
- 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups
- Most agents give up after 2
- Follow up for 30 days minimum

### Follow-Up Sequence:

**Day 1:** Send proposal
**Day 3:** "Did you get a chance to review?"
**Day 7:** Share relevant case study
**Day 14:** "Any questions I can answer?"
**Day 21:** "Closing out this month's capacity..."
**Day 30:** "Final follow-up - should I close your file?"
