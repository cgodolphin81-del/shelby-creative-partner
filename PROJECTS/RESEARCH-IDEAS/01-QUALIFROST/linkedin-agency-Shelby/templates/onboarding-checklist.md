# Client Onboarding Checklist

## 🎯 Onboarding Timeline: 7 Days

**Goal:** Get client from signed contract to full execution in 7 days.

---

## Day 1: Welcome & Access

### ☐ Send Welcome Email

**Template:**
```
Subject: Welcome to [Your Company]! Next Steps 🚀

Hi [Client Name],

Welcome aboard! We're excited to partner with you on your LinkedIn growth.

Here's what happens next:

**This Week:**
1. Complete the onboarding questionnaire (link below)
2. Schedule your discovery call (Calendly link)
3. Grant LinkedIn access (instructions below)

**Next Week:**
1. Profile optimization begins
2. Voice capture call
3. First content batch delivered

**Onboarding Questionnaire:** [Typeform/Google Form Link]
**Discovery Call:** [Calendly Link]

**LinkedIn Access:**
We need access to post on your behalf. Here are your options:

Option A: Add us as a Page Admin (if Company Page)
Option B: Share login credentials (secure password manager)
Option C: Use a scheduling tool (Buffer, Hootsuite)

Let us know which option works best for you.

**What to Expect:**
- Response time: Within 24 hours (business days)
- Primary contact: [Account Manager Name, Email]
- Strategy calls: [Frequency, e.g., Bi-weekly on Tuesdays]

We're thrilled to get started!

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Company]
```

---

### ☐ Send Onboarding Questionnaire

**Key Questions:**

**Business Background:**
1. What does your company do? (1-2 sentences)
2. Who is your ideal customer?
3. What's your unique value proposition?
4. What are your top 3 business goals this quarter?

**LinkedIn History:**
5. How long have you had a LinkedIn profile?
6. What's your current posting frequency?
7. What's worked well so far?
8. What hasn't worked?
9. Have you worked with an agency/contractor before? What was that like?

**Goals & Expectations:**
10. What does success look like in 90 days?
11. What does success look like in 6 months?
12. What metrics matter most to you?
13. What's your biggest fear/concern about this project?

**Voice & Style:**
14. How would you describe your communication style?
15. Any words/phrases you overuse or avoid?
16. Who are 3 people whose LinkedIn content you admire?
17. What topics should we avoid?

**Logistics:**
18. Who else should be involved in content approval?
19. What's your preferred communication method?
20. What time zone are you in?
21. Any upcoming events, launches, or news we should know about?

---

### ☐ Grant Tool Access

**Access Needed:**
- [ ] LinkedIn profile (login or scheduling tool)
- [ ] LinkedIn Company Page (if applicable)
- [ ] LinkedIn Analytics
- [ ] Google Analytics (for tracking website traffic)
- [ ] CRM (optional, for lead tracking)
- [ ] Slack (for ongoing communication)

**Security Best Practices:**
- Use password manager (1Password, LastPass)
- Enable 2FA on all accounts
- Create dedicated user accounts where possible
- Document all access in secure location

---

### ☐ Schedule Discovery Call

**Send Calendly link with these options:**
- 90-minute block (voice capture + strategy)
- Buffer time before/after (no back-to-back)
- Time zone clearly displayed
- Reminder emails enabled (24hr, 1hr)

**Calendar Invite Should Include:**
- Zoom/Google Meet link
- Agenda
- Pre-work (if any)
- Your phone number (backup)

## Day 2-3: Discovery & Strategy

### ☐ Conduct Discovery Call (90 minutes)

**Agenda:**

**Part 1: Background & Story (30 min)**
- Origin story: How did you get here?
- Biggest challenges overcome
- proudest achievements
- What drives you?

**Part 2: Business & Goals (30 min)**
- Current business situation
- Target customer profile
- Competitive landscape
- 90-day and 6-month goals
- How LinkedIn fits into broader strategy

**Part 3: Voice & Content (30 min)**
- Communication style preferences
- Topics you're comfortable with
- Topics to avoid
- Content you've created that you loved
- Content creators you admire
- Review of their past posts (what worked, what didn't)

**Record the Call:**
- Get permission first
- Use Otter.ai or similar for transcription
- Key quotes will be gold for voice profile

## Day 6: Review & Approval

### ☐ Send Content for Review

**Email Template:**
```
Subject: Your First Month of Content - Review Needed 📝

Hi [Client Name],

Exciting milestone—your first batch of content is ready!

Attached is a Google Doc with 20 posts (4 weeks of content), organized by week.

**Please Review:**
1. Does the voice sound like you?
2. Any facts/details that need correction?
3. Anything that doesn't feel right?
4. Any topics you'd like added/removed?

**How to Provide Feedback:**
- Add comments directly in the Google Doc (easiest)
- Or reply via email
- Or we can hop on a quick call

**Deadline:** [Date, 48 hours from send]

Once approved, we'll begin scheduling and you'll see your first post go live on [Date].

Questions? Just reply to this email.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

---

### ☐ Review Client Feedback

**Common Feedback Types:**

**"This doesn't sound like me"**
- Ask for specific examples
- Reference voice profile
- Offer quick call to discuss
- Revise and resend

**"Can we make this more specific?"**
- Great! Ask for the specific story/data
- Incorporate and resend that post
- Note for future batches

**"I love these, no changes"**
- Perfect. Get written approval.
- Move to scheduling.

**"Can we add more about [topic]?"**
- Note for future batches
- Adjust pillar percentages if needed

---

## Day 7: Launch & Handoff

### ☐ Schedule Content

**Options:**

**LinkedIn Native Scheduler (Free)**
- Schedule up to 3 months out
- Free and native
- No collaboration features

**Buffer/Hootsuite (Paid)**
- Scheduling + analytics
- Team collaboration
- More expensive

**Manual Posting**
- Post in real-time
- Maximum engagement
- Time-intensive

**Best Practice:** Schedule 2 weeks at a time, leave room for timely posts

## 🚨 Common Onboarding Issues

### Issue 1: Client Slow to Provide Access

**Solution:**
- Send reminder at 24 hours
- Offer to hop on quick call to walk through
- Provide video tutorial
- Escalate if no response after 3 days

---

### Issue 2: Client Wants Major Revisions

**Solution:**
- Understand the root concern
- Reference voice profile and discovery call
- Offer call to discuss (faster than email back-and-forth)
- Set expectations: 2 rounds included, additional = scope change

---

### Issue 3: Client Can't Attend Discovery Call

**Solution:**
- Offer multiple time options
- Record async video with questions
- Have them fill out detailed questionnaire
- Schedule within 5 days max (momentum matters)

---

### Issue 4: Client Expects Immediate Results

**Solution:**
- Set expectations upfront (3-6 months for real traction)
- Show leading indicators (engagement rate, not just followers)
- Share case studies with realistic timelines
- Weekly communication on progress

---

### Issue 5: Scope Creep During Onboarding

**Solution:**
- Reference signed proposal
- "Happy to add that—let me send over a change order"
- Be friendly but firm
- Document everything
