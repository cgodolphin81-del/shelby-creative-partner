# Analytics System

## 📊 The LinkedIn Analytics Framework

**Goal:** Track what matters, report clearly, optimize continuously.

---

## 📈 Key Metrics to Track

### Awareness Metrics (Top of Funnel)

| Metric | What It Measures | Good Benchmark |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| Impressions | How many times posts were seen | 5,000-20,000/month |
| Reach | Unique viewers | 60-80% of impressions |
| Profile Views | People who visited profile | 200-500/month |
| Search Appearances | Found via search | 500-2,000/month |
| Follower Growth | New followers | 500-2,000/month |

---

### Engagement Metrics (Middle of Funnel)

| Metric | What It Measures | Good Benchmark |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| Reactions | Likes, celebrates, etc. | 200-500/month |
| Comments | Conversations started | 50-150/month |
| Shares | Content reshared | 20-50/month |
| Saves | Bookmarked for later | 30-100/month |
| Engagement Rate | (Reactions+Comments+Shares)/Impressions | 2-5% |
| CTR (Link Clicks) | Clicks on links in comments | 1-3% of impressions |

---

### Conversion Metrics (Bottom of Funnel)

| Metric | What It Measures | Good Benchmark |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| Connection Requests | New connections | 100-300/month |
| Inbound DMs | Messages received | 20-50/month |
| Lead Inquiries | Qualified interest | 10-30/month |
| Discovery Calls | Booked meetings | 5-15/month |
| Opportunities | Pipeline created | 3-10/month |
| Closed Deals | Revenue generated | 2-5/month |

- **Best for:** Attribution tracking

## 🔍 Attribution Tracking

### The Challenge:
LinkedIn doesn't directly track revenue. You need to connect the dots.

### Solution: Multi-Touch Attribution

**Track These Touchpoints:**
1. First LinkedIn interaction (follow, like, comment)
2. Content engagement (post views, clicks)
3. Connection request
4. DM conversation
5. Call booking
6. Proposal sent
7. Deal closed

---

### UTM Parameters for Links:

**Format:**
```
?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=[campaign]&utm_content=[post-date]
```

**Example:**
```
https://yourclient.com/lead-magnet?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=q1-growth&utm_content=032524
```

**Track in Google Analytics:**
- Sessions from LinkedIn
- Conversions (form fills, downloads)
- Revenue (if e-commerce or tracked)

---

### CRM Integration:

**Add Fields to CRM:**
- Lead source: "LinkedIn"
- Content engaged with: [Post/topic]
- Connection date: [Date]
- First touch: [Date]
- Touchpoints: [Number]

## 📊 ROI Calculation

### Formula:

```
ROI = (Revenue - Investment) / Investment × 100

Example:
Revenue from LinkedIn: $100,000
Investment (agency fee): $15,000
ROI = ($100,000 - $15,000) / $15,000 × 100 = 567%
```

---

### Pipeline Attribution:

**Conservative Approach:**
Only count deals where LinkedIn was the PRIMARY source.

**Generous Approach:**
Count any deal where LinkedIn was A touchpoint.

**Recommended:**
Weighted attribution:
- LinkedIn as only source: 100%
- LinkedIn as primary: 70%
- LinkedIn as secondary: 30%
- LinkedIn as minor: 10%

## 🎯 Benchmarking

### Industry Benchmarks:

**B2B SaaS:**
- Engagement rate: 2-4%
- Impressions/post: 2,000-5,000
- Follower growth: 5-10%/month
- Lead rate: 1-3% of engaged users

**Consulting:**
- Engagement rate: 3-5%
- Impressions/post: 3,000-7,000
- Follower growth: 8-15%/month
- Lead rate: 2-5% of engaged users

**Financial Services:**
- Engagement rate: 2-3%
- Impressions/post: 1,500-4,000
- Follower growth: 3-7%/month
- Lead rate: 1-2% of engaged users

**Tech Executives:**
- Engagement rate: 4-7%

## ✅ Analytics Best Practices

### Do's:
✓ Track consistently (same time each week)
✓ Focus on trends, not single posts
✓ Tie to business outcomes (leads, revenue)
✓ Share both wins and learnings
✓ Use visuals (charts > tables)
✓ Benchmark against goals, not just past performance
✓ Attribute conservatively (under-promise)

### Don'ts:
✗ Cherry-pick only good metrics
✗ Report vanity metrics without context
✗ Ignore negative trends
✗ Over-complicate reports (keep simple)
✗ Forget to include insights/recommendations
✗ Promise specific results (algorithm changes)

## 📊 Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

### Structure (60-90 min call):

**Part 1: Performance Review (30 min)**
- Quarter overview
- Goals vs. actuals
- Top wins
- Challenges faced

**Part 2: Insights & Learnings (20 min)**
- What worked best
- What didn't work
- Industry trends observed
- Competitive landscape

**Part 3: Next Quarter Plan (30 min)**
- Goals for next quarter
- Strategy adjustments
- New initiatives
- Investment discussion (upsell if appropriate)

**Part 4: Q&A (10 min)**
- Address concerns
- Clarify expectations
- Action items
