# Niche Selection Strategy

## 🎯 Why Niche Down?

**Generalist agencies:**
- Compete on price
- Generic messaging
- Harder to stand out
- Lower close rates (~5-10%)

**Specialist agencies:**
- Command premium pricing
- Targeted messaging
- Clear differentiation
- Higher close rates (~20-40%)

---

## 💰 Top-Paying B2B Niches

### Tier 1: Highest Paying ($8K-15K/mo retainers)

#### 1. SaaS Companies (B2B Software)
**Why they pay:**
- High customer LTV ($10K-500K+)
- Long sales cycles need nurturing
- Founder/CEO personal brand = company brand
- Competitive talent market

**Ideal clients:**
- Series A-C funded ($10M-100M raised)
- 50-500 employees
- $5M-50M ARR
- Selling to enterprise/mid-market

**Decision makers:**
- CEO/Founder
- VP Marketing
- VP Demand Generation
- Head of Content

**Budget reality:** $10K-20K/mo common for quality

**Your angle:**
- "We help SaaS founders build authority that drives demo requests"
- Case studies: Demo bookings, pipeline influenced
- Speak their language: MRR, CAC, LTV, pipeline

---

#### 2. Management Consulting Firms
**Why they pay:**
- Partners need personal brands to win deals
- Deals worth $100K-1M+
- Relationship-based business
- Talent attraction critical

**Ideal clients:**
- Boutiques (10-100 consultants)
- Specialized firms (digital transformation, cybersecurity, etc.)
- $10M-100M revenue
- Partner-led sales

**Decision makers:**
- Managing Partner
- Senior Partners
- Marketing Director

**Budget reality:** $8K-15K/mo per partner, or $20K-50K/mo firm-wide

**Your angle:**
- "We help consulting partners become thought leaders who attract inbound deals"
- Case studies: Speaking gigs, inbound leads, partner visibility
- Focus on expertise demonstration

---

#### 3. Financial Services (VC, PE, Investment Banking)
**Why they pay:**
- Deal flow depends on reputation
- LP relationships critical
- Talent wars for top analysts
- High margins

**Ideal clients:**
- VC firms ($100M+ AUM)
- PE firms
- Investment banks (boutique)
- Family offices

**Decision makers:**
- Managing Partner
- General Partner
- Head of Investor Relations

**Budget reality:** $10K-25K/mo (they have money)

**Your angle:**
- "We build partner brands that attract deal flow and LP commitment"
- Case studies: Deal introductions, LP meetings, talent inbound
- Discretion and professionalism paramount

**Note:** Compliance review required for all content

---

#### 4. Tech Executives (FAANG, Big Tech)
**Why they pay:**
- Career mobility
- Speaking opportunities
- Board positions
- Personal brand = career insurance

**Ideal clients:**
- VP+ at tech companies
- Engineering leaders
- Product leaders
- Aspiring C-suite

**Decision makers:** The executives themselves (personal spend or company)

**Budget reality:** $5K-15K/mo (often personal expense)

**Your angle:**
- "We position you for your next role before you need it"
- Case studies: Speaking gigs, board seats, job offers
- Privacy and reputation management

---

### Tier 2: Strong Paying ($5K-10K/mo retainers)

#### 5. Digital Marketing Agencies
**Why they pay:**
- They understand the value
- Need to practice what they preach
- Client acquisition challenge
- Often too busy for own marketing

**Ideal clients:**
- 10-50 employees
- $2M-20M revenue
- B2B focus
- Service-based

**Decision makers:**
- CEO/Founder
- Head of Growth

**Budget reality:** $5K-10K/mo

**Your angle:**
- "We handle your LinkedIn so you can handle client work"
- Case studies: Inbound leads, partnership opportunities
- They get it—easier sell

---

#### 6. Coaches & Consultants (High-Ticket)
**Why they pay:**
- Personal brand IS the business
- Course/cohort launches
- $5K-50K programs need trust
- Speaking = lead gen

**Ideal clients:**
- Business coaches
- Executive coaches
- Specialty consultants
- Already making $500K-5M/yr

**Decision makers:** The coach/consultant

**Budget reality:** $5K-10K/mo

**Your angle:**
- "We build the audience that buys your programs"
- Case studies: Launch revenue, waitlist growth, speaking invites
- ROI directly tied to their revenue

---

#### 7. Professional Services (Law, Accounting, Advisory)
**Why they pay:**
- High-value clients ($10K-100K+ engagements)
- Referral-based traditionally, shifting online
- Partner recruitment
- Firm prestige

**Ideal clients:**
- Law firms (50+ attorneys)
- CPA firms
- Advisory firms
- $10M-100M revenue

**Decision makers:**
- Managing Partner
- Marketing Partner
- Practice Group Leaders

**Budget reality:** $5K-12K/mo

**Your angle:**
- "We help partners attract clients without violating ethics rules"
- Case studies: Inbound inquiries, speaking, lateral hire interest
- Compliance-aware content

---

#### 8. E-commerce Brands (B2B/B2C Hybrid)
**Why they pay:**
- Founder brand drives DTC sales
- Retail partnership opportunities
- Investor relations
- Talent acquisition

**Ideal clients:**
- $10M-100M revenue
- DTC or hybrid
- Raised funding
- Growth stage

**Decision makers:**
- CEO/Founder
- CMO

**Budget reality:** $5K-10K/mo

**Your angle:**
- "We turn your founder into a brand asset"
- Case studies: PR, partnerships, retail deals
- Revenue attribution possible

---

**Budget reality:** $5K-10K/mo

#### 11. Manufacturing & Industrial (B2B)

- Succession planning
- Talent shortage

**Budget reality:** $3K-7K/mo

#### 12. Nonprofit & Education (Leadership)
**Why they pay:**
- Fundraising
- Board recruitment
- Speaking opportunities

**Budget reality:** $3K-6K/mo (lower budgets)

## 🎯 Recommended Starting Niches

### Best for Beginners:
1. **Coaches & Consultants** - Easy to understand, direct ROI
2. **Marketing Agencies** - They get it, easier sell
3. **SaaS (early stage)** - Growing, understand digital

### Best for Premium Positioning:
1. **VC/PE Firms** - Highest budgets, prestige
2. **Management Consulting** - High value, recurring need
3. **SaaS (Series B+)** - Big budgets, measurable ROI

### Best for Scale:
1. **SaaS** - Large market, standardized needs
2. **Professional Services** - Many firms, similar needs
3. **Tech Executives** - Individual clients, less complex

## 📊 Niche Selection Framework

### Score each niche 1-10:

| Factor | Weight | Niche A | Niche B | Niche C |
|--------|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Budget availability | 25% | | | |
| Market size | 20% | | | |
| Your expertise/interest | 20% | | | |
| Competition level | 15% | | | |
| Sales cycle length | 10% | | | |
| Results measurability | 10% | | | |
| **Total** | **100%** | | | |

### Questions to ask:
1. Can I speak their language authentically?
2. Do I have case studies (or can I get them)?
3. Are they reachable on LinkedIn?
4. Do they already invest in marketing?
5. Is there a clear ROI story?
