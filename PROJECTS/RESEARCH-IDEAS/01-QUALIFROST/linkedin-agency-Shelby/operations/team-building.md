# Team Building

## 👥 When to Hire

### Solo Operator (You Do Everything)
**Revenue:** $0-30K/mo
**Team:** Just you
**Focus:** Prove the model, get results, build systems

---

### First Hire: Writer/Contractor
**Revenue:** $20K-40K/mo
**Trigger:** You're at capacity (8-10 clients)
**Role:** Content creation only
**Cost:** $3K-6K/mo (contractor)

---

### Second Hire: Account Manager
**Revenue:** $40K-70K/mo
**Trigger:** Client communication taking 20+ hours/week
**Role:** Client comms, reporting, coordination
**Cost:** $4K-7K/mo

---

### Third Hire: Second Writer
**Revenue:** $60K-100K/mo
**Trigger:** More clients than you can write for
**Role:** Content creation
**Cost:** $3K-6K/mo

---

### Fourth Hire: Designer
**Revenue:** $80K-150K/mo
**Trigger:** Carousels/visuals bottleneck
**Role:** Carousel design, visual content
**Cost:** $4K-8K/mo

---

### Fifth Hire: Sales/BD
**Revenue:** $150K+/mo
**Trigger:** You're too busy delivering to sell
**Role:** Outreach, calls, closing
**Cost:** $5K-10K/mo + commission

---

## 📝 Role Descriptions

### LinkedIn Ghostwriter

**Responsibilities:**
- Write 15-25 posts/week across 3-5 clients
- Match client voice (use voice profiles)
- Research industry trends
- Incorporate client feedback
- Meet deadlines consistently
- Participate in strategy calls (as needed)

**Requirements:**
- 2+ years copywriting/content experience
- Portfolio of LinkedIn or social content
- Excellent English (native or near-native)
- Fast writer (can produce quality quickly)
- Receptive to feedback
- Self-starter, minimal hand-holding

**Nice to Have:**
- B2B experience
- SaaS/tech background
- Understanding of LinkedIn algorithm
- Personal LinkedIn presence (1K+ followers)

**Compensation:**
- Entry: $3K-4K/mo (10-15 posts/week)
- Mid: $4K-6K/mo (15-25 posts/week)
- Senior: $6K-8K/mo (25-35 posts/week + strategy)

**Test:** Give them a voice profile and 3 post ideas. Have them write 3 posts. Pay for the test.

---

### Account Manager

**Responsibilities:**
- Primary client contact (weekly check-ins)
- Coordinate content approval workflow
- Manage content calendar
- Create monthly reports
- Handle client questions/concerns
- Coordinate with writers/designers
- Identify upsell opportunities
- Onboard new clients

**Requirements:**
- 2+ years account management experience
- Excellent communication skills
- Organized, detail-oriented
- Comfortable with tools (Google Suite, Slack, Asana)
- Problem-solver, proactive
- Client-service mindset

**Nice to Have:**
- Agency experience
- LinkedIn knowledge
- Project management certification
- Experience with reporting/analytics

**Compensation:**
- Entry: $4K-5K/mo (5-8 clients)
- Mid: $5K-7K/mo (8-12 clients)
- Senior: $7K-9K/mo (12-18 clients + team lead)

**Test:** Give them a difficult client scenario. Ask how they'd handle it. Look for empathy + problem-solving.

---

### LinkedIn Strategist

**Responsibilities:**
- Client strategy calls (weekly/bi-weekly)
- Content planning & pillar development
- Voice capture & profile audits
- Performance analysis & optimization
- Train writers on client voice
- Quality control on content
- Client relationship management

**Requirements:**
- 3+ years LinkedIn marketing experience
- Proven track record (case studies)
- Strong personal LinkedIn presence (5K+ followers ideal)
- Deep understanding of algorithm
- Excellent communication
- Strategic thinker

**Nice to Have:**
- Agency experience
- Ghostwriting background
- Speaking/training experience
- Multiple niches expertise

**Compensation:**
- Mid: $6K-8K/mo
- Senior: $8K-12K/mo
- Lead: $10K-15K/mo + equity

**Test:** Audit their LinkedIn. Ask for a sample strategy doc. Have them do a mock strategy call.

---

### Graphic Designer (Carousel Specialist)

**Responsibilities:**
- Design LinkedIn carousels (10-30/week)
- Create visual content (banners, post graphics)
- Maintain brand consistency across clients
- Iterate based on performance data
- Manage design file organization
- Quick turnaround (24-48 hours)

**Requirements:**
- 2+ years graphic design experience
- Portfolio with carousel/social designs
- Proficient in Figma, Canva, or Adobe
- Fast turnaround without quality loss
- Receptive to feedback
- Understanding of LinkedIn best practices

**Nice to Have:**
- B2B design experience
- Motion graphics skills
- Personal brand/collateral design
- Understanding of conversion design

**Compensation:**
- Entry: $3K-4K/mo (10-15 carousels/week)
- Mid: $4K-6K/mo (15-25 carousels/week)
- Senior: $6K-8K/mo (25-35 carousels/week + creative direction)

**Test:** Give them a carousel brief. 48-hour turnaround. Evaluate design + speed.

---

### Sales/Business Development

**Responsibilities:**
- Outbound outreach (LinkedIn, email)
- Discovery calls (10-20/week)
- Proposal creation
- Closing deals
- Pipeline management
- Follow-up sequences
- Handoff to account management

**Requirements:**
- 3+ years B2B sales experience
- Proven track record (quota attainment)
- Excellent communication
- Comfortable with rejection
- Self-motivated, hunter mindset
- CRM proficiency

**Nice to Have:**
- Agency sales experience
- LinkedIn knowledge
- Marketing/services selling experience
- Existing network

**Compensation:**
- Base: $4K-6K/mo
- Commission: 10-20% of first 3 months
- OPE (On-Target Earnings): $8K-15K/mo

**Test:** Role-play a discovery call. Give them leads to outreach. Evaluate in 1 week.

---

### Virtual Assistant

**Responsibilities:**
- Inbox management
- Scheduling & calendar management
- Data entry & research
- Basic content scheduling
- Report compilation
- Administrative tasks
- Tool management

**Requirements:**
- 1+ years VA experience
- Excellent English
- Highly organized
- Tech-savvy (Google Suite, tools)
- Proactive, anticipates needs
- Reliable, consistent

**Nice to Have:**
- Agency experience
- LinkedIn familiarity
- Basic design skills
- Time zone alignment

**Compensation:**
- Part-time (20 hrs/week): $800-1,500/mo
- Full-time (40 hrs/week): $2K-3K/mo

**Test:** Give them a multi-step task with deadline. Evaluate completion + communication.

## 📋 Hiring Process

### Step 1: Job Description

**Include:**
- Role title & summary
- Responsibilities (bullet points)
- Requirements (must-haves)
- Nice-to-haves
- Compensation range
- How to apply
- Your company story (1-2 paragraphs)

**Example Opening:**
```
We're a fast-growing LinkedIn growth agency working with B2B SaaS founders and executives. We're profitable, fully remote, and growing 20% month-over-month.

We're looking for an experienced LinkedIn Ghostwriter who can capture executive voices and create content that drives real business results.

If you love writing, understand LinkedIn, and want to work with ambitious founders, keep reading.
```

---

### Step 2: Screening

**Application Questions:**
1. "Why do you want this role?"
2. "What's your experience with LinkedIn?"
3. "Share 2-3 writing samples (links)"
4. "What's your availability?"
5. "What are your compensation expectations?"
6. "Tell us about a time you matched someone else's voice."
7. "What's your process for researching a new topic?"

**Red Flags:**
- Generic answers (clearly copy-pasted)
- No LinkedIn presence
- Can't provide samples
- Unrealistic compensation expectations
- Slow to respond

**Green Flags:**
- Personalized application
- Active LinkedIn profile
- Relevant portfolio
- Thoughtful answers
- Prompt communication

---

### Step 3: Skills Assessment

**For Writers:**
```
Assignment: Write 3 LinkedIn posts

Client: SaaS Founder, Series B, $20M ARR
Voice: Direct, data-driven, occasional humor
Pillars: Fundraising, hiring, product, leadership

Topics:
1. Lesson from a recent hiring mistake
2. Hot take on SaaS metrics
3. Story about nearly running out of cash

Deadline: 48 hours
Compensation: $150 for the assignment
```

**For Designers:**
```
Assignment: Design 1 LinkedIn carousel

Topic: "5 SaaS Metrics Every Founder Should Track"
Brand colors: Blue (#0066FF), White, Black
Style: Clean, professional, modern
Slides: 8-10

Deadline: 48 hours
Compensation: $100 for the assignment
```

**For Account Managers:**
```
Assignment: Client scenario response

Scenario: Client is unhappy with last week's posts. They say "these don't sound like me" and are threatening to cancel.

Task: Write the email you'd send and outline your action plan.

Deadline: 24 hours
Compensation: $50 for the assignment
```

**Always pay for test work.** It's ethical and attracts better candidates.

---

### Step 4: Interview

**Structure (45-60 min):**

**0-10 min: Rapport & Background**
- How they found the role
- Career story
- Why this role/company

**10-30 min: Role-Specific Questions**

*For Writers:*
- "Walk me through your writing process."
- "How do you capture someone's voice?"
- "What's your favorite post you've written? Why?"
- "How do you handle feedback/revisions?"
- "What industries have you written for?"

*For Account Managers:*
- "Describe your ideal client relationship."
- "How do you handle difficult clients?"
- "What's your system for staying organized?"
- "Tell me about a time you saved an at-risk client."
- "How do you identify upsell opportunities?"

*For Sales:*
- "Walk me through your sales process."
- "What's your close rate?"
- "How do you handle objections?"
- "Tell me about your biggest deal."
- "How do you prospect?"

**30-40 min: Scenario/Role-Play**
- Give them a realistic scenario
- Watch how they think through it
- Look for problem-solving + communication

**40-50 min: Their Questions**
- What they ask tells you a lot
- Good candidates have thoughtful questions
- Red flag: No questions

**50-60 min: Next Steps & Close**
- Timeline for decision
- What happens next
- Thank them for their time

---

### Step 5: Reference Check

**Ask:**
1. "Can you confirm their role and dates of employment?"
2. "What were their biggest strengths?"
3. "What areas did they need to improve?"
4. "How did they handle feedback?"
5. "Would you rehire them? Why or why not?"
6. "Anything else I should know?"

**Red Flags:**
- Can't reach references
- Hesitant or vague answers
- Only provides personal references (no professional)

---

### Step 6: Offer & Onboarding

**Offer Letter Includes:**
- Role title
- Start date
- Compensation
- Benefits (if any)
- Reporting structure
- At-will statement (if applicable)
- Contingencies (background check, etc.)

**Onboarding Checklist:**
- [ ] Contract signed
- [ ] Equipment/software access
- [ ] Tool accounts created
- [ ] Training materials provided
- [ ] Intro calls with team
- [ ] First assignments given
- [ ] 30/60/90 day goals set
- [ ] Weekly check-ins scheduled

## 💰 Compensation Structures

### Full-Time Employees:
- Base salary (market rate)
- Benefits (health, PTO, etc.)
- Bonus (performance-based, 10-20%)
- Equity (optional, 0.1-1% for key hires)

### Contractors:
- Per project or per month
- No benefits
- Flexible hours
- 1099 (US) or equivalent
- Can work with other clients

### Commission (Sales):
- Base: 40-60% of OTE
- Commission: 40-60% of OTE
- Accelerators: Higher % after quota
- SPIFFs: Bonuses for specific wins

## 🚨 Hiring Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Hiring too early** - Prove the model first
2. **Hiring cheap** - You get what you pay for
3. **No trial period** - Always test before committing
4. **Skipping references** - Past behavior predicts future
5. **Hiring clones** - Diversity of thought helps
6. **Not firing fast** - Bad hires cost more than you think
7. **No onboarding** - Set them up for success
8. **Unclear expectations** - Document everything
9. **Micromanaging** - Hire good people, trust them
10. **Not paying market rate** - Top talent costs money
