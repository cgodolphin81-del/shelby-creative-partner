# Ghostwriting Framework

## 🎯 The Ghostwriting System

**Goal:** Create authentic content at scale that sounds like the client, not you.

**Challenge:** Most ghostwriting fails because it sounds generic.

**Solution:** Systematic voice capture + proven frameworks + efficient workflow.

---

## 🎙️ Voice Capture Process

### Step 1: The Discovery Call (60-90 min)

**Questions to Ask:**

#### Background & Story
1. "Tell me your origin story. How did you get to where you are?"
2. "What's the biggest challenge you've overcome?"
3. "What's a failure that taught you something valuable?"
4. "What are you most proud of professionally?"
5. "What's something people misunderstand about you?"

#### Beliefs & Opinions
6. "What's an unpopular opinion you have about your industry?"
7. "What advice do you disagree with that's commonly given?"
8. "What's the biggest mistake you see others making?"
9. "What do you believe about [industry] that most don't?"
10. "What's changed in your industry in the last 5 years?"

#### Communication Style
11. "How would you describe your communication style?"
12. "Do you prefer direct or diplomatic?"
13. "Are you more data-driven or story-driven?"
14. "What's your humor level? (None / Dry / Playful)"
15. "Any words or phrases you overuse?"

#### Audience & Goals
16. "Who are you trying to reach on LinkedIn?"
17. "What action do you want readers to take?"
18. "What does success look like in 6 months?"
19. "What topics should we avoid?"
20. "Any competitors whose content you admire?"

---

### Step 2: Content Audit

**Review their existing content:**
- Last 10 posts they wrote
- Comments they've made
- Emails they've sent
- Any articles/speeches

**Note:**
- Sentence structure (short vs. long)
- Vocabulary level (simple vs. technical)
- Tone (formal vs. casual)
- Common phrases/expressions
- Emoji usage (none, few, many)
- Hashtag patterns

---

**Create a 1-page reference:**

```
CLIENT VOICE PROFILE: [Name]

- Primary: [Direct/Warm/Authoritative/Friendly]
- Secondary: [Trait]
- Avoid: [What doesn't sound like them]

- Paragraph style: [1-liners/Standard/Long-form]
- Punctuation: [Minimal/Standard/Expressive]

- Level: [Simple/Technical/Mixed]
- Industry jargon: [Yes/No/Limited]
```

## ✍️ Batch Creation System

### The Content Batch Workflow

**Time required:** 3-4 hours for 20 posts

**Step 1: Review & Plan (15 min)**
- Check content calendar
- Review pillar distribution
- Note any timely topics/events
- Gather any client updates/news

**Step 2: Idea Expansion (45 min)**
- Take 20 ideas from content bank
- Expand each to 2-3 bullet points
- Note relevant stories/examples
- Identify hook for each

**Step 3: First Draft (90 min)**
- Write all 20 posts (don't edit yet)
- Stay in flow state
- Aim for 100-250 words each
- Mark posts that need client input

**Step 4: Edit & Polish (45 min)**
- Read each post aloud
- Check voice alignment
- Fix typos, improve hooks
- Add emojis/hashtags
- Ensure variety in formats

**Step 5: QA Check (15 min)**
- Verify pillar distribution
- Check for repetition
- Ensure CTAs included
- Final read-through

### Post Templates (Fill in the Blanks)

#### Template 1: Lesson Post
```
[Hook: Contrarian statement or surprising lesson]

[Context: Situation where you learned this]

[What happened: Brief story]

[The lesson: Clear takeaway]

[Application: How readers can use this]

[CTA: Question or action]
```

#### Template 2: Story Post
```
[Hook: Moment of tension/decision]

[Setup: Context and stakes]

[Conflict: What went wrong/challenge]

[Resolution: How it was resolved]

[Lesson: What you learned]

[CTA: Relatable question]
```

#### Template 3: List Post
```
[Hook: Promise of value]

[Number] [things] that [benefit]:

1. [Item + brief explanation]
2. [Item + brief explanation]
3. [Item + brief explanation]
[Continue for 5-10 items]

[Summary/Key takeaway]

[CTA: Which resonates most?]
```

#### Template 4: Case Study Post
```
[Hook: Specific result]

[Client context: Who, what challenge]

[Approach: What you did]

[Results: Specific numbers]

[Key lesson: What made the difference]

[CTA: Comment or DM for details]
```

#### Template 5: Hot Take Post
```
[Hook: Controversial statement]

[Your position: Clear stance]

[Supporting argument 1]

[Supporting argument 2]

[Supporting argument 3]

[Nuance: Acknowledge complexity]

[CTA: Agree or disagree?]
```

## 🔄 Approval Workflow

### The Client Review Process

**Goal:** Minimize back-and-forth while maintaining quality.

---

### Step 1: Initial Batch Delivery

**Send:**
- 20 posts in Google Doc or Notion
- Organized by week (5 posts/week x 4 weeks)
- Color-coded by pillar
- Notes on any questions for client

**Message:**
```
Hi [Name],

Here's your first batch of 20 posts (4 weeks of content).

I've organized them by week and ensured a good mix of:
- Story posts
- Educational content
- Case studies
- Industry insights

Please review and:
1. Add any personal details/stories I missed
2. Flag anything that doesn't sound like you
3. Approve or suggest edits

I need feedback by [date] to stay on schedule.

You can comment directly in the doc or reply via email.

Thanks!
```

---

### Step 2: Client Feedback

**Common feedback types:**

**"This doesn't sound like me"**
- Ask for specific examples of what does
- Reference their voice profile
- Offer a quick call to discuss

**"Can we make this more specific?"**
- Great! Ask for the specific story/data
- Incorporate and resend that post

**"I love these, no changes"**
- Perfect. Get written approval.
- Move to scheduling.

**"Can we add more about [topic]?"**
- Note for future batches
- Adjust pillar percentages if needed

---

### Step 3: Revisions

**Process:**
1. Make all requested changes
2. Track changes or highlight edits
3. Send back within 24-48 hours
4. Note any posts needing second review

**Revision limits:**
- 2 rounds included in standard package
- Additional rounds = scope creep (address gently)
- Major rewrites = voice profile needs updating

---

### Step 4: Final Approval

**Get explicit approval:**
```
Hi [Name],

All revisions complete! Here's the final batch.

Please reply "APPROVED" and I'll begin scheduling.

Posts will go live starting [date].

Thanks!
```

**Save their "APPROVED" response** (paper trail)

## 🚨 Common Ghostwriting Challenges

### Challenge 1: "It Doesn't Sound Like Me"

**Solution:**
- More discovery time upfront
- Record calls and transcribe (capture natural speech)
- Have them write 2-3 posts as examples
- Iterate quickly on first batch

---

### Challenge 2: Client is Slow to Approve

**Solution:**
- Set clear deadlines upfront
- Build buffer into schedule
- Send reminders (automated if possible)
- Make review easy (comment directly on posts)
- Offer quick call for feedback

---

### Challenge 3: Running Out of Ideas

**Solution:**
- Interview client quarterly (new stories emerge)
- Monitor industry news (timely content)
- Repurpose old content (6-month refresh)
- Ask audience questions (they suggest topics)
- Review competitor content (adapt, don't copy)

---

### Challenge 4: Client Wants to Post More Frequently

**Solution:**
- Explain quality > quantity
- Show data on their best-performing posts
- Suggest 5x/week sweet spot
- If they insist, adjust pricing accordingly
- Don't burn out your writers

---

### Challenge 5: Performance Not Meeting Expectations

**Solution:**
- Set realistic expectations upfront (3-6 months)
- Show leading indicators (engagement rate, not just followers)
- A/B test different formats
- Review voice alignment
- Check posting times
- Increase engagement activity (not just posting)
