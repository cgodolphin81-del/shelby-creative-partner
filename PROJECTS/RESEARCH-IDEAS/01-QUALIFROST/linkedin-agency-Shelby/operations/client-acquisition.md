# Client Acquisition

## 🎯 The Client Acquisition System

```
Outreach → Conversations → Calls → Proposals → Closed Deals
   ↓           ↓              ↓         ↓           ↓
 100/week   30-40%       20-30%    50-70%    30-50%
            response     show-up   close     overall
```

**Math:** 100 outreaches/week → 3-5 clients/month at $5K-10K = $15K-50K/mo new revenue

---

## 📧 Outreach Channels

### Channel 1: LinkedIn DMs (Highest ROI)

**Why it works:**
- They're already on LinkedIn
- You can reference their content
- Warm context (you're both active)
- Low friction to respond

**Daily goal:** 20-30 personalized DMs

---

### Channel 2: Email (Good for Scale)

**Why it works:**
- More formal, taken seriously
- Can include attachments (case studies)
- Easier to automate (carefully)
- Decision makers check email

**Daily goal:** 30-50 personalized emails

---

### Channel 3: Warm Introductions (Highest Close Rate)

**Why it works:**
- Trust transferred from mutual connection
- 5-10x higher response rate
- Shorter sales cycle
- Higher close rates

**Goal:** 5-10 intro requests/week

---

### Channel 4: Content Inbound (Long-term)

**Why it works:**
- They come to you (warmest)
- Already convinced of your expertise
- Shortest sales cycle
- Highest lifetime value

**Goal:** Consistent content (5x/week)

---

### Channel 5: Events/Networking (Relationship Building)

**Why it works:**
- Face-to-face (or virtual face)
- Build real relationships
- Multiple touchpoints
- Trust accelerates

**Goal:** 2-4 events/month

## 📨 Outreach Templates

### Template 1: Content-Based DM (Best Performer)

```
Subject: Loved your post on [topic]

Hi [Name],

Just read your post on [specific topic]—the point you made about [specific insight] really hit home.

We work with [their industry] companies to [result you deliver], and that's exactly the challenge we see most founders struggling with.

Curious—have you found [related question]?

No pitch, just genuinely interested in your take.

Best,
[Your name]

P.S. Your content on [topic] has been some of the best I've seen in [industry]. Keep it up.
```

**Why it works:**
- Specific compliment (not generic)
- Shows you actually read their content
- Asks a thoughtful question
- "No pitch" reduces defensiveness
- P.S. adds genuine appreciation

**Response rate:** 40-60%

## 📞 The Sales Call Framework

### Call Structure (30-45 min):

**Minutes 0-5: Rapport & Context**
- Thank them for their time
- Confirm agenda
- Build rapport (genuine, not fake)

**Minutes 5-15: Discovery**
- Understand their situation
- Identify pain points
- Uncover goals

**Minutes 15-25: Presentation**
- Share relevant case studies
- Explain your approach
- Show how you solve their problem

**Minutes 25-35: Address Concerns**
- Answer questions
- Handle objections
- Clarify scope/pricing

**Minutes 35-45: Close**
- Present proposal
- Ask for the business
- Next steps

---

### Discovery Questions:

#### Current Situation:
1. "What's your current LinkedIn strategy?"
2. "Who's managing it today?"
3. "How much time are you spending on it?"
4. "What's working? What's not?"

#### Pain Points:
5. "What's your biggest frustration with LinkedIn right now?"
6. "What have you tried that hasn't worked?"
7. "What happens if you don't solve this?"
8. "How is this affecting your business?"

#### Goals:
9. "What would success look like in 90 days?"
10. "What about 6 months?"
11. "How would this impact your business?"
12. "Why is this important now?"

#### Budget/Authority:
13. "Have you worked with agencies before?"
14. "What's your budget range for this?"
15. "Who else is involved in this decision?"
16. "What does your decision process look like?"

---

### Common Objections & Responses:

**"It's too expensive."**

Response:
```
I understand budget is a consideration.

Can I ask—compared to what? 

If you're comparing this to hiring in-house, a full-time content marketer is $60K-100K/year plus benefits, and they don't have our expertise.

If you're comparing to the revenue potential—one client at $10K ACV pays for 2-3 months of our service. Most clients see 5-10x ROI.

What's the cost of NOT solving this problem?
```

---

**"We'll do it in-house."**

Response:
```
That's totally valid. A lot of companies start that way.

Can I ask—do you have someone on your team who:
- Knows LinkedIn's algorithm inside-out?
- Can write compelling content daily?
- Has time to engage 2+ hours/day?
- Understands lead gen strategy?

If yes, awesome. If not, you're looking at 6+ months of trial and error while competitors pull ahead.

We've already made those mistakes so you don't have to.

What's your timeline for getting this right?
```

---

**"We need to think about it."**

Response:
```
Of course. This is a big decision.

Can I ask—what specifically do you need to think through?

Is it:
- The investment?
- Whether we're the right fit?
- Timing?
- Something else?

[Listen to their answer]

If it's [their concern], here's what I'd suggest...

[Address specific concern]

Would it help if we [specific solution]?
```

---

**"We've had bad experiences with agencies."**

Response:
```
I'm sorry to hear that. That's unfortunately common in our industry.

Can I ask what went wrong?

[Listen—this is gold. You'll know exactly what to address.]

I totally understand why you'd be hesitant. Here's how we're different:

[Address their specific pain point]

Plus:
- We work month-to-month after 3 months (we have to earn your business)
- You work directly with me/founder (not passed to junior staff)
- Transparent reporting (you see exactly what we're doing)
- We only work with 10-15 clients (we're not a factory)

Would it help to talk to a current client? Happy to make an intro.
```

## 📄 Proposal Template

### Structure:

**1. Executive Summary (1 page)**
- Their situation
- Their goals
- Your solution
- Expected outcomes

**2. Current State Analysis (1-2 pages)**
- Profile audit findings
- Content performance review
- Competitive analysis
- Opportunities identified

**3. Proposed Solution (2-3 pages)**
- Scope of work
- Deliverables
- Timeline
- Process overview

**4. Investment (1 page)**
- Pricing options
- Payment terms
- What's included
- What's extra

**5. Case Studies (2-3 pages)**
- 2-3 relevant client examples
- Specific results
- Testimonials

**6. Next Steps (1 page)**
- How to get started
- Timeline
- Contact info
