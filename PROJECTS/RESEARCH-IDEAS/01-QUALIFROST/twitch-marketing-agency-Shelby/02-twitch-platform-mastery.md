Success on Twitch requires deep platform knowledge. This document covers the mechanics, algorithms, and unwritten rules that drive discovery and growth.

## The Twitch Algorithm: How Discovery Works

### Key Ranking Factors

Twitch's recommendation system prioritizes the following signals:

**1. Viewer Engagement (Weight: 35%)**
- Chat messages per viewer
- Average watch time
- Return viewer rate
- Clip creation rate
- Follows during stream

**2. Stream Quality Signals (Weight: 25%)**
- Consistent streaming schedule
- Stream duration (optimal: 3-6 hours)
- Video quality (720p60 minimum recommended)
- Audio quality (clear, balanced levels)
- Professional overlays and branding

**3. Growth Velocity (Weight: 20%)**
- Follower growth rate
- Viewer growth rate
- Subscriber growth rate
- Comparison to category averages

**4. Category Dynamics (Weight: 15%)**
- Viewer-to-streamer ratio in category
- Category growth trends
- Time of day performance
- Day of week performance

**5. Community Health (Weight: 5%)**
- Moderator activity
- Chat rule enforcement
- Ban/time-out rates
- Community guidelines strikes

### Algorithm Optimization Strategies

#### The "Golden Window" Strategy
- **First 30 minutes** are critical for algorithm pickup
- Aim for 50%+ of your average viewers in first 30 min
- Pre-announce stream start times across all platforms
- Use "Stream Starting Soon" screen with countdown
- Engage immediately with early viewers

#### Category Selection Framework

| Strategy | Best For | Risk Level |
|----------|----------|------------|
| Top Categories (Just Chatting, GTA V, LoL) | Established streamers 500+ avg | High competition |
| Mid-Tier Categories (500-2000 viewers) | Growing streamers 100-500 avg | Moderate |
| Emerging Categories (<500 viewers) | New streamers <100 avg | Low competition, low ceiling |
| Oversaturated Categories | Avoid | Very High |

**Category Selection Formula**:
```
Opportunity Score = (Category Viewers / Category Streamers) × Growth Trend
```

Target categories with:
- Viewer/Streamer ratio > 50
- Positive 30-day growth trend
- Alignment with streamer's strengths

## Category Strategy

### Category Types

**1. Evergreen Categories** (Stable, predictable)
- Just Chatting
- League of Legends
- Minecraft
- Grand Theft Auto V
- Valorant

**Strategy**: Consistency and differentiation. These categories don't go anywhere, but competition is fierce. Success requires unique personality or exceptional skill.

**2. Trending Categories** (High growth, temporary)
- New game releases
- Seasonal events
- Viral games (Among Us, Fall Guys phenomena)

**Strategy**: Ride the wave early. Be in the top 20 streamers when a category explodes. Have exit strategy when category peaks.

**3. Niche Categories** (Small but dedicated)
- Retro games
- Speedrunning
- Creative arts
- Music performance

**Strategy**: Become the category leader. Easier to rank #1, but ceiling is limited. Use as home base while exploring other categories.

### Category Rotation Strategy

**The 80/20 Rule**:
- 80% of streams in primary category (build audience)
- 20% of streams in secondary/experimental categories (growth opportunities)

**Monthly Category Review**:
- Analyze performance by category
- Identify emerging opportunities
- Adjust based on viewer feedback
- Monitor competitor movements

## Raids: The Growth Multiplier

### Raid Mechanics

**What Happens During a Raid**:
1. Streamer initiates raid (minimum 1 viewer required)
2. 10-second countdown with raid notification
3. Viewers transported to target channel
4. Target streamer receives raid notification
5. Raiding community appears in chat

**Algorithm Impact**:
- Raids signal community engagement
- Recipients often raid back (relationship building)
- Creates cross-pollination of audiences
- Boosts both channels' discovery metrics

### Raid Strategy Framework

#### Finding Raid Targets

**Ideal Raid Partners**:
- Similar or slightly larger audience (100-150% of your size)
- Compatible content and community
- Active in same time zone
- Professional and welcoming
- Likely to raid back

**Tools for Finding Targets**:
- TwitchRaid.com
- RaidTimes.com
- Discord raid communities
- Manual category browsing

#### Raid Etiquette

**DO**:
- Announce raid 5-10 minutes in advance
- Explain why you're raiding this channel
- Encourage chat to be respectful
- Stay in raid for first 2-3 minutes
- Follow up with thank you message

**DON'T**:
- Raid without warning
- Raid significantly larger channels (waste of opportunity)
- Raid incompatible content (risk community mismatch)
- Expect immediate raid-back (build relationship first)
- Raid-bait (announce then cancel)

#### Building a Raid Network

**Step 1: Identify 20-30 Potential Partners**
- Same size range
- Compatible content
- Professional presentation
- Active streaming schedule

**Step 2: Initial Outreach**
- Attend their streams as viewer
- Engage genuinely in chat (no self-promotion)
- Build familiarity over 1-2 weeks
- Send thoughtful DM expressing appreciation

**Step 3: Establish Raid Rhythm**
- Propose regular raid exchanges
- Create Discord group for coordination
- Schedule raids during mutual active times
- Track raid relationships in CRM

**Expected Outcome**: 3-5 reliable raid partners within 60 days

## Hosts: Passive Discovery

### Host Mechanics

**How Hosting Works**:
- Streamer goes offline
- Automatically displays another channel
- Viewers can stay and watch hosted channel
- Hosted channel receives notification
- No viewer count transfer (unlike raids)

**Strategic Value**:
- Passive discovery while offline
- Relationship building with other streamers
- Community exposure to compatible content
- Algorithm signal of community engagement

### Host Strategy

**Best Practices**:
1. Host channels slightly larger than yours (150-200%)
2. Host channels with compatible content
3. Rotate hosts (don't always host same channel)
4. Communicate with hosts you regularly feature
5. Use auto-host for consistency when offline

**Host Networks**:
- Create reciprocal hosting agreements
- Join hosting Discords and communities
- Coordinate with streamers in similar time zones
- Track hosting relationships

**Auto-Host Setup**:
```
Settings → Channel → Auto-Host Channels
Add 10-20 trusted partners
Enable "Auto-host channels from my list"
```

## Engagement Mechanics

### Chat Engagement Strategies

**The First 100 Viewers Framework**:

1. **Greet Every Viewer by Name** (0-50 viewers)
   - Welcome message for each new chatter
   - Remember returning viewers
   - Ask questions to encourage participation

2. **Facilitate Community Conversation** (50-100 viewers)
   - Pose questions to the group
   - Reference previous conversations
   - Create inside jokes and community language

3. **Empower Community Leaders** (100+ viewers)
   - Identify and nurture regulars
   - Give moderators meaningful roles
   - Create VIP tier for top supporters
   - Delegate community management

**Chat Engagement Tactics**:

| Tactic | Description | Frequency |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Question of the Day | Single question for chat to discuss | Daily |
| Channel Point Rewards | Custom rewards for engagement | Always on |
| Chat Games | Trivia, predictions, polls | 2-3x per stream |
| Storytelling | Personal stories, behind-the-scenes | Every stream |
| React Content | Respond to clips, memes, chat creations | Weekly |

### Retention Optimization

**Average View Duration Benchmarks**:
- <50 avg viewers: 15-25 minutes
- 50-200 avg viewers: 25-40 minutes
- 200-500 avg viewers: 40-60 minutes
- 500+ avg viewers: 60+ minutes

**Retention Improvement Strategies**:

1. **Hook in First 5 Minutes**
   - Start with energy, not technical setup
   - Preview what's happening today
   - Acknowledge early viewers personally

2. **Create Natural Breakpoints**
   - Bathroom/break every 60-90 minutes
   - Use "starting soon" screens strategically
   - Build anticipation for return

3. **Maintain Energy Consistency**
   - Avoid long silent periods
   - Narrate gameplay/thoughts continuously
   - Music during low-energy moments

4. **End Strong**
   - Don't just disappear
   - Recap highlights
   - Preview next stream
   - Thank specific community members

## Twitch Features Mastery

### Channel Points

**Strategic Implementation**:

**Low-Cost Rewards (100-500 points)**:
- Hydrate reminder
- Stretch break
- Song request
- Emote-only mode (1 min)

**Mid-Cost Rewards (500-2000 points)**:
- Choose next game/mode
- Handicap challenge
- Truth or dare
- Chat decides loadout

**High-Cost Rewards (5000+ points)**:
- 1-on-1 game with streamer
- IRL challenge completion
- Custom emote submission
- Discord call with streamer

**Best Practices**:
- Limit concurrent redemptions
- Set appropriate cooldowns
- Pause during intense moments
- Review and adjust monthly

### Predictions

**Optimal Usage**:
- 2-4 predictions per stream
- Clear, binary outcomes
- 5-15 minute duration
- Meaningful stakes (channel points or small $)

**Prediction Ideas**:
- Win/loss next game
- Over/under on stats
- Multiple choice outcomes
- Community vs. streamer challenges

### Polls

**Strategic Polling**:
- Use for community input (not engagement farming)
- Keep options clear and limited (2-4 options)
- 1-5 minute duration
- Act on results visibly

**Poll Applications**:
- Game/mode selection
- Challenge parameters
- Content direction
- Community preferences

## Technical Optimization

### Stream Settings

**Recommended Encoding Settings**:

| Quality | Bitrate | Resolution | FPS | Use Case |
|---------|---------|------------|-----|----------|
| High | 6000 Kbps | 1080p | 60 | 500+ avg viewers |
| Standard | 4500 Kbps | 720p | 60 | 100-500 avg viewers |
| Basic | 3000 Kbps | 720p | 30 | <100 avg viewers |

**Audio Configuration**:
- Stream audio: -14 to -16 LUFS
- Game audio: -18 to -20 LUFS
- Voice clearly above game audio
- Compressor and limiter on mic chain

### Overlay Best Practices

**Essential Elements**:
- Webcam frame (branded)
- Recent follower/subscriber alerts
- Chat box (for smaller streams)
- Social media handles
- Sponsor logos (when applicable)

**Overlay Principles**:
- Less is more (don't obscure gameplay)
- Consistent branding
- Readable fonts
- Mobile-friendly design

## Platform Rules & Compliance

### Community Guidelines (Critical)

**Zero Tolerance Violations**:
- Hate speech or discrimination
- Sexual content or nudity
- Harassment or bullying
- Self-harm content
- Illegal activities

**Common Pitfalls**:
- DMCA music (use licensed/royalty-free)
- Gambling restrictions (varies by region)
- Age-restricted content labeling
- Political content guidelines

### DMCA Protection

**Safe Music Sources**:
- StreamBeats (Spotify, all platforms)
- Epidemic Sound (subscription)
- Monstercat Gold (subscription)
- Twitch Soundtrack (built-in)
- Pretend to be a Wizard (free)

**DMCA Best Practices**:
- Never play mainstream music
- Mute VODs for music sections
- Keep music library documented
- Respond promptly to takedown notices
