# Sponsorship Management

## Overview

Sponsorship management is the art and science of connecting streamers with brands for mutually beneficial partnerships. This document covers the complete lifecycle: prospecting, negotiation, activation, and reporting.

---

## The Sponsorship Landscape

### Brand Categories on Twitch

**Tier 1: Gaming-Endemic Brands** (Highest fit, easiest sell)
- Game publishers (Riot, Blizzard, EA, Ubisoft)
- Gaming hardware (Razer, Logitech, NVIDIA, AMD)
- Gaming peripherals (SteelSeries, HyperX, Corsair)
- Gaming chairs (Secretlab, Herman Miller)
- Energy drinks (GFuel, Red Bull, Monster)

**Typical Deal Size**: $2K-50K per campaign
**Conversion Rate**: 15-25% of pitches
**Relationship Duration**: 3-12 months average

---

**Tier 2: Adjacent Tech Brands** (Good fit, moderate education needed)
- PC components (Intel, AMD, ASUS, MSI)
- Streaming software (OBS, Streamlabs, Restream)
- Internet/ISP (Xfinity, Verizon, AT&T)
- VPN services (NordVPN, ExpressVPN)
- Cloud gaming (GeForce Now, Xbox Cloud)

**Typical Deal Size**: $3K-30K per campaign
**Conversion Rate**: 10-20% of pitches
**Relationship Duration**: 6-18 months average

---

**Tier 3: Lifestyle & DTC Brands** (Lower fit, higher education, bigger budgets)
- Food delivery (DoorDash, UberEats, GrubHub)
- Finance apps (CashApp, Robinhood, Coinbase)
- Apparel (Nike, Adidas, fashion brands)
- Subscription boxes (BarkBox, FabFitFun)
- Health & wellness (Huel, Athletic Greens)

**Typical Deal Size**: $5K-100K+ per campaign
**Conversion Rate**: 5-15% of pitches
**Relationship Duration**: 3-6 months average (can extend)

---

**Tier 4: Mainstream Brands** (Lowest fit, highest education, largest budgets)
- Automotive (Honda, Toyota, BMW)
- CPG (Coca-Cola, Pepsi, snack brands)
- Retail (Target, Walmart, Amazon)
- Entertainment (Netflix, Disney, movie studios)
- Travel (Airlines, hotels, booking platforms)

**Typical Deal Size**: $25K-500K+ per campaign
**Conversion Rate**: 2-10% of pitches
**Relationship Duration**: 1-3 campaigns typical

## Sponsorship Readiness Assessment

### Streamer Qualification Criteria

**Minimum Requirements for Sponsorship Representation**:

| Metric | Minimum | Preferred |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Average Viewers | 100 | 250+ |
| Stream Frequency | 4x/week | 5-6x/week |
| Content Quality | Good audio/video | Professional production |
| Community Health | Active chat, low toxicity | Engaged, positive community |
| Professionalism | Responsive, reliable | Business-minded, easy to work with |
| Social Presence | Active on 2+ platforms | Strong multi-platform presence |
| Content Consistency | Regular schedule | Consistent branding & messaging |

**Red Flags** (Disqualifiers):
- Recent community guidelines strikes
- Controversial content or statements
- Inconsistent streaming schedule
- Unresponsive to communication
- History of problematic brand behavior
- Toxic community culture

### Media Kit Essentials

Every streamer needs a professional media kit. Include:

**Page 1: Overview**
- Professional headshot
- Channel name and logo
- One-sentence positioning statement
- Key metrics snapshot

**Page 2: Audience Demographics**
- Age breakdown
- Gender breakdown
- Geographic distribution
- Interests and affinities

**Page 3: Performance Metrics**
- Average viewers (30/60/90 day)
- Peak viewers
- Follower count and growth rate
- Subscriber count
- Chat engagement rate
- Clip performance

**Page 4: Content Portfolio**
- Primary games/categories
- Content style description
- Streaming schedule
- Platform presence (all socials)

**Page 5: Partnership Options**
- Available activation types
- Pricing tiers (or "custom packages available")
- Past successful partnerships
- Testimonials (if available)

**Page 6: Contact Information**
- Agency/management contact
- Email, phone
- Social media links
- Response time commitment

**Media Kit Best Practices**:
- Update quarterly with fresh metrics
- Keep design clean and professional
- PDF format, under 5MB
- Include both light and dark versions
- Have a one-page "leave-behind" version

## Prospecting & Outreach

### Finding Brand Opportunities

**Inbound Lead Sources**:
- Brand outreach forms (register streamer on brand websites)
- Influencer platforms (CreatorIQ, Aspire, Grin)
- Agency relationships
- Referrals from other streamers
- Social media inquiries

**Outbound Prospecting Targets**:

**Research Framework**:
1. **Identify brands already advertising on Twitch**
   - Watch for sponsored segments on similar streamers
   - Check Twitch sponsor directory
   - Monitor Twitter/X for brand announcements

2. **Find brands advertising in adjacent spaces**
   - YouTube gaming creators
   - Esports teams and events
   - Gaming podcasts and media
   - Gaming conventions and events

3. **Discover brands with relevant target audiences**
   - Brands targeting 18-34 demographic
   - Brands with gaming-adjacent products
   - Brands looking for authentic influencer partnerships

### Outreach Strategy

#### The Perfect Pitch Email

**Subject Line** (Choose one):
- "Partnership opportunity: [Streamer] × [Brand]"
- "[Brand] + [Streamer]: Authentic gaming audience reach"
- "Sponsored content idea for [Brand] on Twitch"

**Email Structure**:

```
Paragraph 1: Hook
- Who you are (briefly)
- Why you're reaching out to THEM specifically
- One compelling stat or achievement

Paragraph 2: Value Proposition
- What makes this streamer unique
- Audience alignment with brand
- Specific idea for partnership

Paragraph 3: Social Proof
- Past successful partnerships
- Relevant metrics
- Testimonials or case studies

Paragraph 4: Call to Action
- Clear next step (call, meeting, deck review)
- Timeline suggestion
- Easy way to respond

Signature:
- Name, title, agency
- Contact information
- Media kit attachment
```

**Email Template**:

```
Subject: Partnership opportunity: [Streamer Name] × [Brand Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name] with [Agency], and I've been following [Brand]'s 
recent campaigns in the gaming space—particularly loved your 
activation with [Reference Specific Campaign].

I represent [Streamer Name], a [Category] streamer with [X] average 
viewers and a highly engaged community of [demographic]. Their 
audience overlaps perfectly with [Brand]'s target customer, and 
I have a specific activation idea that could drive real results.

[Streamer] recently partnered with [Similar Brand] and delivered 
[X] results (see attached case study). Their authentic integration 
style and loyal community make them an ideal partner for [Brand].

Would you be open to a 15-minute call next week to explore this 
further? I'm available [2-3 specific time options].

Looking forward to connecting,

[Your Name]
[Title] | [Agency]
[Phone] | [Email]
[Media Kit Attached]
```

### Outreach Volume & Expectations

**Realistic Conversion Metrics**:
- 100 outbound emails → 20-30 responses (20-30%)
- 20-30 responses → 5-10 meetings (25-33%)
- 5-10 meetings → 2-4 deals (20-40%)

**Monthly Outreach Targets**:
- **Junior Agent**: 50-75 brands/month
- **Senior Agent**: 30-50 brands/month (higher quality)
- **Agency Total**: 200-500 brands/month

**Follow-Up Cadence**:
- Day 0: Initial email
- Day 3: Follow-up #1 (brief, add value)
- Day 7: Follow-up #2 (case study or social proof)
- Day 14: Follow-up #3 (final attempt)
- Day 30: Re-engage with new angle (if still no response)

## Negotiation Framework

### Pricing Models

#### 1. Flat Fee Per Activation

**Best For**: One-off campaigns, specific deliverables

**Pricing Formula**:
```
Base Rate = (Average Viewers × $2-5) × Platform Multiplier

Platform Multipliers:
- Twitch only: 1.0x
- Twitch + Twitter: 1.3x
- Twitch + Twitter + TikTok: 1.5x
- Full multi-platform: 1.7-2.0x
```

**Example**:
- 250 avg viewers × $3 × 1.5x = $1,125 per activation
- Typical package: 3-4 activations = $3,375-4,500

---

#### 2. Monthly Retainer

**Best For**: Long-term partnerships, ambassador roles

**Pricing Tiers**:

| Tier | Deliverables | Price Range |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| Bronze | 2 streams/month, logo placement | $2K-4K/mo |
| Silver | 4 streams/month, dedicated segment, social posts | $5K-8K/mo |
| Gold | 8 streams/month, ambassador role, full integration | $10K-20K/mo |
| Platinum | Exclusive partnership, custom content, events | $25K+/mo |

---

#### 3. Performance-Based

**Best For**: Direct response campaigns, affiliate partnerships

**Structures**:
- **CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)**: $X per sign-up/purchase
- **Revenue Share**: X% of sales from unique code/link
- **Hybrid**: Lower base fee + performance bonus

**Example Hybrid Deal**:
- Base fee: $2,000
- CPA bonus: $10 per conversion
- Expected conversions: 200
- Total potential: $4,000

---

#### 4. Equity/Revenue Share

**Best For**: Startup brands, long-term partnerships

**Structures**:
- Equity stake in company (0.1-2% typical)
- Revenue share on product line (5-15% typical)
- Combination of cash + equity

**Considerations**:
- Only for brands with genuine growth potential
- Requires legal documentation
- Longer timeline to realization
- Higher risk, higher potential reward

---

### Negotiation Leverage Points

**Streamer Leverage**:
- Unique audience demographics
- High engagement rates
- Authentic integration style
- Multi-platform reach
- Exclusive category rights
- Content ownership/usage rights
- Performance track record

**Brand Leverage**:
- Budget constraints
- Multiple streamer options
- Long-term partnership potential
- Product/value exchange
- Exposure to brand's audience
- Production support
- Cross-promotion opportunities

### Common Negotiation Sticking Points

| Issue | Streamer Preference | Brand Preference | Resolution |
|-------|---------------------|------------------|------------|
| Exclusivity | Narrow category, short term | Broad category, long term | Compromise on category scope, 3-6 month terms |
| Usage Rights | Limited, additional fees | Broad, perpetual | Tiered rights (social only, paid media extra) |
| Approval Process | Minimal, trust-based | Full creative control | Reasonable review period (48-72 hrs) |
| Payment Terms | Net-15 or faster | Net-60 or 90 | Net-30 standard, discount for early payment |
| Deliverables | Flexible, authentic | Specific, guaranteed | Clear minimums with flexibility on execution |
| Morals Clause | Narrow definition | Broad definition | Specific, reasonable conduct standards |

## Contract Essentials

### Key Contract Sections

**1. Parties & Term**
- Legal names of all parties
- Contract start and end dates
- Renewal terms and conditions
- Termination clauses (with and without cause)

**2. Deliverables**
- Specific activation requirements
- Number of streams/posts
- Duration requirements
- Content guidelines
- Approval process and timelines

**3. Compensation**
- Total contract value
- Payment schedule
- Payment terms (Net-30, etc.)
- Bonus/incentive structures
- Expense reimbursement policies

**4. Exclusivity**
- Category exclusivity definition
- Geographic scope
- Duration of exclusivity
- Pre-existing relationship exceptions
- Competitor brand list (if applicable)

**5. Usage Rights**
- Platforms where content can be used
- Duration of usage rights
- Paid media usage (and fees)
- Organic social usage
- Territory restrictions
- Exclusivity of usage

**6. Content Approval**
- Review process
- Turnaround time requirements
- Revision limits
- Deemed approval clauses
- Final approval authority

**7. Disclosure & Compliance**
- FTC disclosure requirements
- Platform-specific rules (Twitch, etc.)
- Brand guideline adherence
- Legal compliance requirements

**8. Termination**
- Termination for cause
- Termination for convenience
- Notice periods
- Payment obligations on termination
- Content removal requirements

**9. Indemnification & Liability**
- Mutual indemnification
- Limitation of liability
- Insurance requirements
- Force majeure clauses

**10. General Provisions**
- Governing law
- Dispute resolution
- Assignment rights
- Entire agreement clause
- Amendment process

### Contract Review Checklist

- [ ] All deliverables clearly defined
- [ ] Payment terms acceptable (Net-30 or better)
- [ ] Exclusivity scope reasonable
- [ ] Usage rights appropriately limited
- [ ] Approval process includes deadlines
- [ ] Termination clauses fair to both parties
- [ ] FTC compliance addressed
- [ ] Morals clause specific and reasonable
- [ ] Insurance requirements achievable
- [ ] Legal review completed

## Campaign Activation

### Pre-Campaign Checklist

**4 Weeks Before**:
- [ ] Contract signed by all parties
- [ ] Creative brief received and reviewed
- [ ] Campaign goals confirmed
- [ ] Key performance indicators defined
- [ ] Tracking mechanisms in place (codes, links)
- [ ] Content calendar aligned

**2 Weeks Before**:
- [ ] Script/talking points drafted
- [ ] Brand approval on content (if required)
- [ ] Technical requirements confirmed
- [ ] Product received and tested (if applicable)
- [ ] Social posts scheduled
- [ ] Community briefed (moderators, VIPs)

**1 Week Before**:
- [ ] Final confirmation with brand
- [ ] Reminder posts on social media
- [ ] Stream overlay updated (if needed)
- [ ] Chat commands configured
- [ ] Team briefed on campaign

**Day Of**:
- [ ] Pre-stream technical check
- [ ] Talking points accessible
- [ ] Tracking links tested
- [ ] Moderators briefed on Q&A
- [ ] Go live and execute!

---

### Activation Types

#### 1. Sponsored Segment (Most Common)

**Format**: 5-15 minute dedicated segment within regular stream

**Best Practices**:
- Natural transition into sponsored content
- Clear disclosure at segment start
- Authentic personal connection to product
- Demonstrate product in action
- Clear call-to-action
- Thank sponsor at segment end

**Example Flow**:
```
"Alright chat, before we jump into the next round, 
I want to talk about today's partner [Brand]. 
I've actually been using [Product] for [timeframe] 
and it's genuinely helped me with [benefit]. 
Here's how it works... [demonstration]. 
If you want to check it out, use code [CODE] 
for [discount]. Thanks to [Brand] for supporting the stream!"
```

---

#### 2. Branded Stream

**Format**: Entire stream sponsored with brand integration

**Best Practices**:
- Brand overlay throughout stream
- Branded intro/outro
- Multiple natural mentions (not constant selling)
- Brand representative appearance (optional)
- Giveaway or community activation
- Strong social media amplification

**When to Use**:
- Product launches
- Major brand campaigns
- High-budget partnerships
- Exclusive partnerships

---

#### 3. Challenge/Event Activation

**Format**: Special event built around brand partnership

**Examples**:
- 24-hour charity stream sponsored by brand
- Tournament with brand prize pool
- IRL challenge with brand integration
- Community event with brand support

**Best Practices**:
- Make it entertaining first, sponsored second
- Clear brand connection to activity
- Community participation element
- Multi-day promotion buildup
- Post-event content and highlights

---

#### 4. Affiliate/Performance Campaign

**Format**: Ongoing promotion with performance tracking

**Best Practices**:
- Unique discount code for tracking
- Dedicated landing page (if possible)
- Regular but not excessive promotion
- Performance updates to community
- Bonus incentives for milestones

---

### During Campaign: Best Practices

**Do's**:
✅ Be authentic—only promote products you genuinely like
✅ Disclose sponsorship clearly (FTC requirement)
✅ Engage with chat questions about the brand
✅ Follow brand guidelines while maintaining your voice
✅ Track and report performance metrics
✅ Thank the brand publicly (when appropriate)

**Don'ts**:
❌ Over-sell or sound like a commercial
❌ Make claims you can't verify
❌ Ignore chat questions or concerns
❌ Forget disclosure requirements
❌ Promise results you can't deliver
❌ Bad-mouth competitors

## Post-Campaign Reporting

### Report Structure

**Executive Summary** (1 page)
- Campaign overview
- Key results
- ROI highlights
- Recommendations

**Performance Metrics** (2-3 pages)
- Reach and impressions
- Engagement metrics
- Click-through rates
- Conversion data
- Revenue generated (if trackable)

**Content Performance** (1-2 pages)
- Stream performance vs. baseline
- Clip performance
- Social media performance
- Top performing moments

**Audience Insights** (1 page)
- Demographic breakdown
- Engagement patterns
- Sentiment analysis
- Feedback highlights

**Recommendations** (1 page)
- What worked well
- Areas for improvement
- Future opportunity ideas
- Optimization suggestions

### Reporting Timeline

- **48 hours post-campaign**: Preliminary metrics
- **7 days post-campaign**: Full report delivery
- **14 days post-campaign**: Follow-up call to review
- **30 days post-campaign**: Long-tail performance update (if applicable)

### Case Study Development

For successful campaigns, develop case studies for future pitches:

**Case Study Template**:
1. Client/Brand overview
2. Challenge/objective
3. Strategy and approach
4. Execution highlights
5. Results (quantitative)
6. Testimonials (qualitative)
7. Key learnings

## Sponsorship Management Best Practices

### Relationship Management

**During Partnership**:
- Regular check-ins (weekly for active campaigns)
- Proactive communication about any issues
- Share positive community feedback
- Identify upsell opportunities
- Request testimonials for successful campaigns

**Post-Partnership**:
- Send thank you note
- Share full performance report
- Request testimonial/case study participation
- Stay in touch (monthly/quarterly check-ins)
- Pitch renewal or new campaigns

### Crisis Management

**If Campaign Underperforms**:
1. Analyze what went wrong (content, timing, product-market fit)
2. Communicate proactively with brand
3. Propose remediation (make-good content, extended terms)
4. Document learnings for future campaigns

**If Streamer Controversy Occurs**:
1. Assess severity and brand impact
2. Communicate immediately with brand partners
3. Follow contract termination clauses if needed
4. Manage public response carefully
5. Rebuild trust with affected brands over time

### Scaling Sponsorship Operations

**When to Hire Additional Support**:
- 20+ active brand relationships
- 50+ outreach emails/week needed
- Campaign activation taking 20+ hours/week
- Missing follow-ups or deadlines

**Roles to Consider**:
- **Sponsorship Coordinator**: Handle logistics, reporting, communication
- **Outreach Specialist**: Focus on prospecting and initial pitches
- **Activation Manager**: Oversee campaign execution
- **Account Manager**: Manage key brand relationships
