# Weekly Report Template

## Purpose
Provide clear visibility into work completed, current priorities, blockers, and upcoming items. Keeps client informed without requiring check-in meetings for routine updates.

## 🔄 Automation Ideas

### Automated Weekly Report Generation

**Tools That Can Help:**
- **Notion**: Database views + templates
- **Asana**: Portfolio reporting
- **Monday.com**: Dashboard widgets
- **ClickUp**: Custom reports
- **Toggl/Clockify**: Time tracking reports
- **Zapier**: Auto-compile data from multiple sources

**Zapier Automation Example:**
```
Trigger: Every Friday at 4 PM
Actions:
1. Get tasks completed this week from Asana
2. Get time entries from Toggl
3. Get calendar events from Google Calendar
4. Compile into Google Doc template
5. Send email draft to VA for review
6. VA reviews, edits, sends to client
```

## Best Practices

### For VAs Writing Reports

**Do:**
- Send consistently on the same day/time (e.g., Friday at 4 PM)
- Keep it scannable with clear headers and bullet points
- Quantify accomplishments when possible
- Flag blockers early, not at the last minute
- Include wins, not just tasks
- Make recommendations, not just observations
- Proofread before sending

**Don't:**
- Write a novel (keep it to 1-2 pages max)
- Include every tiny task (summarize)
- Wait until there's a problem to communicate
- Be vague about blockers or needs
- Forget to celebrate wins
- Send without reviewing for accuracy

### For Clients Reading Reports

**Do:**
- Read within 24 hours of receipt
- Respond to blockers promptly
- Provide feedback on priorities
- Acknowledge wins and good work
- Use as agenda input for weekly check-in
- Save for reference and performance reviews

**Don't:**
- Ignore blockers until they become crises
- Change priorities without discussing impact
- Forget to provide feedback
- Micromanage the details
- Skip reading for weeks at a time
