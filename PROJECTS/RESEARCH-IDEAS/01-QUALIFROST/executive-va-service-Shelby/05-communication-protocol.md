# Communication Protocol

## Purpose
Establish clear expectations and guidelines for all communication between client and VA to ensure efficiency, clarity, and mutual respect.

---

## 📞 Communication Channels

### Primary Channels

| Channel | Use For | Response Time | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------------|-------|
| **Email** | Non-urgent requests, documentation, detailed instructions | Within 4 business hours | Best for complex tasks requiring context |
| **Slack/Teams** | Quick questions, real-time collaboration, urgent matters | Within 1 hour during work hours | Use threads to keep conversations organized |
| **Text/SMS** | True emergencies, time-sensitive updates | Within 30 minutes | Use sparingly; respect boundaries |
| **Video Call** | Weekly check-ins, complex discussions, relationship building | Scheduled | Always with agenda sent in advance |
| **Task Management Tool** | Task requests, status updates, project tracking | Within 2 business hours | Single source of truth for all work |

### Channel Selection Guide

**Use Email When:**
- Requesting a task that requires detailed instructions
- Sharing documents or files
- Needing a paper trail
- Providing feedback on completed work
- Non-urgent matters (can wait 4+ hours)

**Use Slack/Teams When:**
- Asking a quick question (< 5 min to answer)
- Needing real-time collaboration
- Sharing quick updates
- Urgent but not emergency matters
- Casual check-ins

**Use Text/SMS When:**
- True emergency (client or family urgent situation)
- Time-critical update (flight cancelled, meeting running late)
- Client is traveling and email access is limited
- Pre-agreed urgent notifications only

**Use Video Call When:**
- Weekly or bi-weekly check-in
- Complex topic requiring discussion
- Building relationship and rapport
- Onboarding or training sessions
- Sensitive conversations

**Use Task Management Tool When:**
- Requesting any task or project
- Providing task updates
- Tracking progress
- Documenting completion
- Anything that should be tracked long-term

## 📧 Email Etiquette

### Subject Line Standards

**Format:** `[Priority] Topic - Due Date (if applicable)`

**Examples:**
```
[HIGH] Schedule Q2 Board Meeting - Need by Friday
[MEDIUM] Research CRM Options
[LOW] Update Contact Database
[URGENT] Flight Cancelled - Need Rebooking
```

### Email Structure

```
Hi [Name],

[One sentence on purpose/context]

[Main content - use paragraphs and bullet points for readability]

[Clear ask or next steps]

Thanks,
[Name]
```

### Email Best Practices

**Do:**
- Use clear, specific subject lines
- Get to the point in the first sentence
- Use bullet points for multiple items
- Include deadlines when relevant
- Specify if response is needed or for info only
- Keep emails focused (one topic per email when possible)

**Don't:**
- Send one-word emails ("Thanks", "OK")
- Use vague subject lines ("Question", "Quick thing")
- Bury the ask in a long email
- Expect immediate response to non-urgent emails
- Reply-all unless necessary
- Use email for urgent matters (use Slack/text instead)

### Email Signatures

**VA Signature:**
```
[VA Name]
Executive Assistant to [Client Name]
[Email] | [Phone]
[Company Website]
```

**When Drafting for Client:**
```
Best regards,

[Client Name]
[Title]
[Company]
[Phone]
[Email]

[Optional: Company tagline or social links]
```

## 💬 Slack/Teams Etiquette

### Message Structure

**Good:**
```
@Name Quick question - do you have the login for [Service]? 
Need to book travel for next week's trip.
```

**Also Good (with thread):**
```
@Name Update on the board meeting prep:
• Venue confirmed
• Catering ordered
• Awaiting final attendee list

Thread for details →
```

### Slack Best Practices

**Do:**
- Use @mentions thoughtfully (only when action needed)
- Use threads to keep channels clean
- Set status when away or in meetings
- Use emoji reactions for quick acknowledgments (👍, ✅, 👀)
- DM for private matters; channel for team visibility

**Don't:**
- Send multiple short messages in a row (combine into one)
- Use @channel or @here unless truly everyone needs to see
- Expect immediate response (use urgent marker if needed)
- Have long back-and-forth conversations (hop on a call)
- Share sensitive info in public channels

### Status Indicators

| Status | Meaning | Use When |
|--------|---------|----------|
| 🟢 Active | Available | Working normally |
| 🟡 Away | Stepped away briefly | Bathroom, coffee, quick call |
| 🔴 In Meeting | In a meeting | Scheduled meetings, calls |
| ⛔ Focus | Do not disturb | Deep work, heads down |
| 🌙 Offline | Not working | After hours, PTO |

## 📱 Text/SMS Protocol

### When to Text

**Appropriate:**
- True emergencies (health, safety, security)
- Time-critical travel updates (flight cancelled, running late)
- Pre-arranged urgent notifications
- Client specifically requests text for something

**Not Appropriate:**
- Routine task requests (use email or task tool)
- Non-urgent questions (use Slack or email)
- Long conversations (use call or Slack)
- Sharing documents or links (use email)

### Text Format

**Good:**
```
URGENT: Flight AA1234 cancelled. Rebooked on AA5678, departs 3pm instead of 11am. 
New confirmation: ABC123. Details emailed.
```

**Good:**
```
Running 10 min late to 2pm meeting. Please let them know. Thanks!
```

### Text Boundaries

- Text only during agreed hours unless emergency
- Keep texts brief and actionable
- Follow up with email for documentation
- Don't expect immediate response to non-emergency texts
- Use for notification, not conversation

## 📅 Meeting & Call Protocol

### Scheduling Meetings

**VA Scheduling Meetings On Behalf of Client:**
- Always include clear agenda
- Specify expected duration
- Include video link or location
- List all attendees
- Note any pre-work or preparation needed

**Meeting Invite Template:**
```
Subject: [Meeting Topic] - [Date] at [Time]

Hi [Attendees],

Purpose: [One sentence on why we're meeting]

Agenda:
1. [Topic 1] - [Time]
2. [Topic 2] - [Time]
3. [Topic 3] - [Time]

Prep: [Any pre-reading or preparation]

[Video Link] or [Location]

Please let me know if you have any conflicts.

Thanks,
[Name]
```

### Weekly Check-In Structure

**Recommended Cadence:** Weekly, 30 minutes

**Standard Agenda:**
1. **Review** (5 min): What got done last week
2. **Priorities** (10 min): Top 3-5 priorities for this week
3. **Blockers** (5 min): Anything stuck or needing help
4. **Calendar** (5 min): Upcoming week review
5. **Open** (5 min): Questions, feedback, relationship

**Preparation:**
- VA sends brief summary 24 hours before
- Client reviews and adds any topics
- Both come ready to discuss priorities

### Video Call Best Practices

**Before:**
- Test technology 5 minutes early
- Have agenda and notes ready
- Close unnecessary tabs/apps
- Find quiet, well-lit space

**During:**
- Camera on when possible (builds relationship)
- Mute when not speaking (if background noise)
- Stay focused (no multitasking)
- Take notes on action items

**After:**
- Send summary with action items
- Update task tracker
- Schedule any follow-ups needed

## 🎯 Task Communication

### Requesting Work

**Best Practice:** Use task management tool for all requests

**If Requesting via Email/Slack:**
```
TASK REQUEST
━━━━━━━━━━━━
📌 Task: [Clear description]
🎯 Priority: [P1-P4 or Urgent/High/Medium/Low]
📅 Due: [Date and time]
⏱️ Estimated time: [If known]

📋 Details:
[Full description, context, and any special instructions]

📎 Resources:
[Links, attachments, logins needed]

✅ Done Looks Like:
[Specific criteria for completion]
```

### Providing Updates

**VA to Client:**
- Daily summary email (end of day or next morning)
- Slack updates for significant milestones
- Immediate notification of blockers or issues
- Weekly summary report (see Weekly Report Template)

**Client to VA:**
- Feedback on completed work (what worked, what to adjust)
- Priority changes as soon as known
- Context that might affect work (travel, deadlines, etc.)
- Appreciation and recognition (goes a long way!)

### Handling Blockers

**When VA is Blocked:**

1. **Identify the blocker clearly**
   - What specifically is preventing progress?
   - What information/decision/access is needed?

2. **Communicate promptly**
   - Don't sit on a blocker for hours
   - Send clear message: "Blocked on X, need Y to proceed"

3. **Suggest solutions if possible**
   - "I need your approval on the hotel. Options are A ($200) or B ($300). Recommend A."

4. **Document in task tracker**
   - Update task status to "Blocked"
   - Note what's needed and from whom

**When Client is Delayed on Providing Input:**

1. **VA sends gentle reminder** (after agreed timeframe)
2. **Escalates urgency** if deadline at risk
3. **Suggests workaround** if possible
4. **Adjusts timeline** and communicates new expectations

## 🔒 Confidentiality & Privacy

### What Stays Confidential

**Always Confidential:**
- Business strategy and plans
- Financial information
- Personal family matters
- Health information
- Passwords and credentials
- Private communications
- Proprietary information

### Secure Communication Practices

**Do:**
- Use password manager for sharing credentials
- Encrypt sensitive documents
- Use secure channels for sensitive info
- Verify identity before sharing sensitive info
- Lock computer when stepping away

**Don't:**
- Share passwords via email or Slack
- Discuss confidential matters in public spaces
- Leave sensitive documents visible
- Share client info with others without permission
- Use personal accounts for client work

### Data Handling

- All client data stored in agreed-upon systems only
- No local downloads of sensitive information
- Regular password updates
- Two-factor authentication enabled everywhere
- Devices encrypted and password-protected

## 🚨 Escalation Protocol

### When to Escalate Immediately

**VA Should Escalate to Client:**
- Crisis or emergency situation
- Time-sensitive opportunity closing soon
- Legal or compliance concern
- Major travel disruption
- VIP requesting immediate attention
- Decision needed that's outside VA's authority

**Client Should Escalate to VA:**
- Change in priorities that affects current work
- Upcoming deadline that wasn't previously known
- Feedback that requires immediate adjustment
- Concerns about quality or timeliness

### Escalation Method

| Urgency | Method | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Immediate** (within 30 min) | Text + Slack | Emergency, crisis |
| **Urgent** (within 1 hour) | Slack + Email | Time-sensitive decision |
| **Soon** (within 4 hours) | Email or Slack | Priority change |
| **Routine** (next check-in) | Weekly meeting | General feedback |

- Communicate to VA in advance
- Specify what should continue vs. pause
- Designate backup decision-maker if needed
- Set up out-of-office with VA contact info

## 📊 Feedback & Continuous Improvement

### Regular Feedback Cadence

**Weekly:**
- Quick wins and appreciations
- Minor adjustments and preferences

**Monthly:**
- What's working well
- What could be improved
- Process optimization ideas

**Quarterly:**
- Formal review of working relationship
- Goal setting for next quarter
- Compensation and scope discussion (if applicable)

### Giving Feedback

**Constructive Feedback Formula:**
1. **Situation:** "In yesterday's travel booking..."
2. **Behavior:** "...you booked a 6am flight..."
3. **Impact:** "...which doesn't work with my morning routine."
4. **Request:** "Going forward, please keep flights after 8am unless I approve."

**Positive Feedback:**
- Be specific about what worked
- Explain the impact
- Reinforce you want more of it

**Example:** "The meeting brief you prepared for the Acme meeting was perfect. I felt fully prepared and the meeting went smoothly. This is exactly what I need for all external meetings."

### Receiving Feedback

**VA Commitments:**
- Listen without defensiveness
- Ask clarifying questions
- Confirm understanding
- Implement changes promptly
- Follow up to confirm improvement

**Client Commitments:**
- Provide feedback timely (not weeks later)
- Be specific and actionable
- Balance constructive with positive
- Acknowledge improvements

## 🎭 Working Relationship Norms

### Communication Style

**Tone:**
- Professional but warm
- Direct and clear
- Respectful of time
- Appropriate humor when it fits

**Language:**
- Avoid jargon when possible
- Explain acronyms first use
- Be culturally sensitive
- Match client's formality level

### Building Trust

**VA Builds Trust By:**
- Doing what you say you'll do
- Communicating proactively
- Admitting mistakes quickly
- Maintaining confidentiality
- Showing genuine care for client's success

**Client Builds Trust By:**
- Providing clear direction
- Giving timely feedback
- Respecting VA's expertise
- Showing appreciation
- Being available for questions

### Boundaries

**Healthy Boundaries:**
- Clear working hours
- Respect for personal time
- Appropriate topics only
- Professional relationship
- Mutual respect

**Boundary Violations (Address Promptly):**
- Contact outside agreed hours (non-emergency)
- Requests outside scope without discussion
- Disrespectful communication
- Sharing confidential info inappropriately
- Unrealistic expectations without discussion
