## 1. Email Triage & Response

### Purpose
Process incoming emails efficiently, ensuring important messages are addressed promptly while minimizing time spent on low-value emails.

### Frequency
Daily (morning and afternoon review)

### Time Required
30-90 minutes per session, depending on volume

### Tools Needed
- Email client (Gmail, Outlook, etc.)
- Task management system
- Templates for common responses

---

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Initial Scan (5 minutes)
- [ ] Open inbox and sort by date (newest first)
- [ ] Quickly scan sender names and subject lines
- [ ] Identify any urgent senders (VIPs, key clients, family)
- [ ] Flag or star anything that looks time-sensitive

#### Step 2: The Four D's Method
For each email, decide immediately:

**DELETE** (or unsubscribe)
- [ ] Spam and obvious scams
- [ ] Promotional emails from unknown senders
- [ ] Newsletters you don't read
- [ ] Old notifications and alerts

**DELEGATE**
- [ ] Emails that someone else should handle
- [ ] Forward with clear instructions
- [ ] Add to task list with assignee

**DO** (if under 2 minutes)
- [ ] Quick confirmations
- [ ] Simple yes/no responses
- [ ] Scheduling responses
- [ ] Forwarding information

**DEFER** (add to task list)
- [ ] Emails requiring thoughtful responses
- [ ] Complex requests
- [ ] Items needing research
- [ ] Anything taking more than 2 minutes

#### Step 3: Process Deferred Emails
- [ ] Create tasks in your task management system
- [ ] Include deadline and priority
- [ ] Attach or link to original email
- [ ] Add any context or notes

#### Step 4: Draft Responses
For emails requiring client input:
- [ ] Draft response in email or document
- [ ] Mark clearly as "[DRAFT - For Review]"
- [ ] Include any questions for the client
- [ ] Flag for client review

#### Step 5: File and Organize
- [ ] Move processed emails to appropriate folders
- [ ] Apply labels/categories consistently
- [ ] Archive anything not needing future reference
- [ ] Keep inbox at or near zero

---

### Email Folder Structure (Recommended)

```
📧 Inbox (active only)
├── 📁 01_Action Required (needs your attention)
├── 📁 02_Awaiting Response (waiting on others)
├── 📁 03_References (important info to keep)
├── 📁 04_Travel (confirmations, itineraries)
├── 📁 05_Financial (invoices, receipts, statements)
├── 📁 06_Legal (contracts, agreements)
├── 📁 07_Meetings (agendas, notes, follow-ups)
├── 📁 08_Projects (organized by project name)
└── 📁 09_Archive (everything else, searchable)
```

### Response Templates

#### Template: Meeting Request (Accepting)
```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [Name],

[Client Name] is available for a meeting on [Date] at [Time] [Time Zone].

[If virtual:]
We'll send a calendar invite with the video conferencing link shortly.

[If in person:]
The meeting will be at [Location]. Please let us know if you need parking or building access instructions.

Looking forward to connecting.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
Executive Assistant to [Client Name]
```

#### Template: Meeting Request (Declining)
```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [Name],

Thank you for reaching out. Unfortunately, [Client Name]'s schedule is fully committed during the time you've suggested.

[Optional: suggest alternative]
If this is time-sensitive, [Client Name] has limited availability on [Alternative Date/Time]. Would that work for you?

[Optional: delegate]
For matters related to [topic], you may want to reach out to [Colleague Name] at [email].

Best regards,
[Your Name]
Executive Assistant to [Client Name]
```

#### Template: Information Request (Acknowledgment)
```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

Hi [Name],

Thank you for your message. I've received your request for [information] and have passed it along to [Client Name].

You can expect a response within [timeframe]. If this is urgent, please let me know and I'll do my best to expedite.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
Executive Assistant to [Client Name]
```

### Quality Checklist
- [ ] All VIP emails addressed or flagged
- [ ] Inbox at zero (or designated threshold)
- [ ] Tasks created for all deferred items
- [ ] Drafts prepared for client review
- [ ] Emails filed in correct folders
- [ ] Unsubscribed from unnecessary newsletters

### Escalation Criteria
Escalate to client immediately if:
- Email is from VIP and requires immediate response
- Legal or compliance issue identified
- Crisis or emergency situation
- Time-sensitive opportunity (closing soon)

## 2. Meeting Preparation

### Purpose
Ensure the client is fully prepared for every meeting with relevant context, materials, and clear objectives.

### Frequency
For every scheduled meeting

### Time Required
15-45 minutes per meeting, depending on complexity

### Tools Needed
- Calendar access
- Research tools (LinkedIn, company websites, Google)
- Document creation (Google Docs, Notion, etc.)
- Video conferencing platform

---

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Meeting Details Confirmation (24-48 hours before)
- [ ] Confirm date, time, and time zone
- [ ] Verify location or video link
- [ ] Check for any special requirements
- [ ] Send calendar invite if not already sent

#### Step 2: Participant Research
For each attendee (especially external):
- [ ] LinkedIn profile review
- [ ] Role and company background
- [ ] Previous interactions with client (check CRM/notes)
- [ ] Any recent news about them or their company

#### Step 3: Agenda & Objectives
- [ ] Request agenda from meeting organizer if not provided
- [ ] Clarify desired outcomes with client
- [ ] Prepare talking points if needed
- [ ] Identify any pre-work required

#### Step 4: Background Materials
Gather and organize:
- [ ] Company background (one-pager)
- [ ] Previous meeting notes (if applicable)
- [ ] Relevant documents or presentations
- [ ] Data or reports that may be discussed

#### Step 5: Create Meeting Brief
Compile into a single document (see template below)

#### Step 6: Pre-Meeting Reminder (1-2 hours before)
- [ ] Send brief to client
- [ ] Confirm any last-minute changes
- [ ] Test technology if virtual meeting
- [ ] Provide directions or access info if in-person

## 3. Travel Booking

### Purpose
Arrange seamless travel experiences that align with client preferences, budget, and schedule.

### Frequency
As needed (typically 1-4 times per month)

### Time Required
- Domestic: 1-2 hours
- International: 2-4 hours
- Complex multi-city: 4-8 hours

### Tools Needed
- Travel booking platforms (Expedia, Kayak, company travel portal)
- Airline/hotel loyalty accounts
- Calendar
- Itinerary template

---

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Gather Requirements
- [ ] Destination(s)
- [ ] Dates (flexible or fixed?)
- [ ] Purpose of trip (affects timing and location)
- [ ] Budget parameters
- [ ] Preferred airlines/hotels
- [ ] Any special requirements (accessibility, dietary, etc.)

#### Step 2: Flight Research & Booking
- [ ] Search multiple platforms for best options
- [ ] Consider: price, timing, layovers, airline preference
- [ ] Check seat availability (preferred seating)
- [ ] Verify baggage allowance
- [ ] Book flight and save confirmation
- [ ] Add to loyalty program if not already member

#### Step 3: Hotel Booking
- [ ] Research hotels near meeting locations
- [ ] Check reviews and ratings
- [ ] Verify amenities match needs (gym, wifi, breakfast, etc.)
- [ ] Book with flexible cancellation when possible
- [ ] Add special requests (high floor, away from elevator, etc.)

#### Step 4: Ground Transportation
- [ ] Determine needs (rental car, rideshare, private car, public transit)
- [ ] Book in advance for better rates
- [ ] Confirm pickup/dropoff locations and times
- [ ] Save driver/company contact info

#### Step 5: Create Master Itinerary
- [ ] Compile all confirmations
- [ ] Add meeting schedule
- [ ] Include local information (weather, timezone, currency)
- [ ] Add emergency contacts
- [ ] Share with client and relevant parties

#### Step 6: Pre-Trip Preparation (2-3 days before)
- [ ] Send trip briefing to client
- [ ] Check in for flights (when available)
- [ ] Confirm all reservations
- [ ] Verify weather and pack accordingly
- [ ] Check for any travel advisories

#### Step 7: During Trip Support
- [ ] Monitor flight status
- [ ] Be available for changes/issues
- [ ] Handle any rebooking if needed
- [ ] Confirm upcoming reservations

## 4. Expense Tracking & Reimbursement

### Purpose
Accurately track, categorize, and process expenses for timely reimbursement and financial record-keeping.

### Frequency
Weekly or bi-weekly

### Time Required
30-60 minutes per session

### Tools Needed
- Expense tracking software (Expensify, Concur, etc.)
- Receipt capture app
- Spreadsheet or accounting software
- Corporate card statements

---

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Receipt Collection
- [ ] Gather all receipts (physical and digital)
- [ ] Scan/photograph physical receipts immediately
- [ ] Save email receipts to designated folder
- [ ] Organize by date or category

#### Step 2: Receipt Review & Categorization
For each receipt:
- [ ] Verify date and amount
- [ ] Identify business purpose
- [ ] Categorize (meals, travel, office supplies, etc.)
- [ ] Note any personal portions to exclude
- [ ] Flag any missing information

#### Step 3: Data Entry
- [ ] Enter each expense into tracking system
- [ ] Attach receipt image
- [ ] Include: date, vendor, amount, category, business purpose
- [ ] Code to appropriate project/client if applicable

#### Step 4: Reconciliation
- [ ] Compare expenses against credit card statements
- [ ] Verify all charges are accounted for
- [ ] Identify any duplicate entries
- [ ] Flag any discrepancies for review

#### Step 5: Report Generation
- [ ] Generate expense report for period
- [ ] Review for accuracy
- [ ] Add any required notes or explanations
- [ ] Submit for approval

#### Step 6: Follow-Up
- [ ] Track approval status
- [ ] Address any questions or rejections
- [ ] Confirm reimbursement received
- [ ] File completed reports

---

### Expense Categories (Standard)

| Category | Examples | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Meals & Entertainment | Client dinners, team lunches | Note attendees and purpose |
| Travel - Air | Flights, baggage fees | Include confirmation # |
| Travel - Hotel | Lodging, resort fees | Separate personal charges |
| Travel - Ground | Rental car, rideshare, parking, tolls | Include mileage if personal car |
| Office Supplies | Pens, paper, printer supplies | Under $X can be expensed |
| Technology | Software, hardware, subscriptions | May need capitalization |
| Professional Services | Contractors, consultants, legal | Include W-9 if needed |
| Marketing | Ads, events, promotional items | Tie to campaign/project |
| Education | Courses, conferences, books | Business-related only |
| Miscellaneous | Other business expenses | Requires detailed description |

## 5. Calendar Management & Scheduling

### Purpose
Optimize the client's schedule to maximize productivity while maintaining work-life balance and meeting all commitments.

### Frequency
Daily monitoring, ongoing scheduling

### Time Required
30-60 minutes daily

### Tools Needed
- Calendar platform (Google Calendar, Outlook, etc.)
- Scheduling tool (Calendly, Chili Piper, etc.)
- Time zone converter
- Communication channels for coordination

---

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Daily Calendar Review
Each morning:
- [ ] Review today's schedule
- [ ] Confirm all meetings have agendas and links
- [ ] Check for any conflicts or double-bookings
- [ ] Identify any preparation needed
- [ ] Send daily briefing to client

#### Step 2: Incoming Meeting Requests
For each request:
- [ ] Check if request fits scheduling criteria
- [ ] Verify requester's identity and purpose
- [ ] Check available time slots
- [ ] Consider meeting priority and client's goals
- [ ] Accept, decline, or propose alternatives

#### Step 3: Scheduling Best Practices
- [ ] Maintain buffer time between meetings (10-15 min)
- [ ] Protect focus blocks and personal time
- [ ] Group similar meetings when possible
- [ ] Consider energy levels (creative work AM, meetings PM, etc.)
- [ ] Avoid scheduling across lunch unless necessary

#### Step 4: Meeting Coordination
- [ ] Send calendar invites with all details
- [ ] Include video link or location
- [ ] Add agenda and pre-read materials
- [ ] Send reminders 24 hours and 1 hour before
- [ ] Confirm attendance of all required participants

#### Step 5: Calendar Maintenance
Weekly:
- [ ] Review upcoming week for optimization
- [ ] Decline or delegate low-value meetings
- [ ] Ensure work-life balance boundaries
- [ ] Plan for travel time if needed
- [ ] Sync with other calendars if applicable

---

## 6. Research & Brief Creation

### Purpose
Provide comprehensive, actionable research to support decision-making and preparation.

### Frequency
As needed (typically 2-5 requests per week)

### Time Required
1-8 hours depending on depth

### Tools Needed
- Search engines (Google, specialized databases)
- Research tools (LinkedIn, Crunchbase, etc.)
- Document creation (Google Docs, Notion)
- Citation/bookmarking tools

---

#### Step 1: Clarify Research Request
- [ ] Understand the end goal/decision to be made
- [ ] Identify key questions to answer
- [ ] Determine scope and depth needed
- [ ] Confirm deadline
- [ ] Ask about preferred format

#### Step 2: Source Identification
- [ ] Identify primary sources (company websites, official docs)
- [ ] Identify secondary sources (news, analysis, reviews)
- [ ] Consider expert opinions or interviews if needed
- [ ] Plan search strategy

- [ ] Note any gaps in information

- [ ] Organize findings by theme or question
- [ ] Identify patterns and insights
- [ ] Note contradictions or uncertainties

- [ ] Create executive summary

#### Step 5: Brief Creation
- [ ] Structure document logically
- [ ] Write clear, concise content
- [ ] Include citations and links
- [ ] Add visual elements if helpful (charts, tables)
- [ ] Review for accuracy and completeness

#### Step 6: Delivery & Follow-Up
- [ ] Send brief with clear subject line
- [ ] Highlight key findings upfront
- [ ] Offer to answer questions
- [ ] Note any follow-up research that may be needed

## 7. Document Creation & Formatting

### Purpose
Create professional, polished documents that effectively communicate information and reflect well on the client.

### Frequency
As needed (typically 3-10 documents per week)

### Time Required
30 minutes to 3 hours depending on complexity

### Tools Needed
- Word processing (Google Docs, Microsoft Word)
- Presentation software (Google Slides, PowerPoint, Canva)
- Spreadsheet software (Google Sheets, Excel)
- Design tools (Canva, Adobe Express)

---

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Understand Requirements
- [ ] Clarify document purpose and audience
- [ ] Determine required content and sections
- [ ] Identify any templates or brand guidelines
- [ ] Confirm format and delivery method
- [ ] Establish deadline

#### Step 2: Content Gathering
- [ ] Collect all source materials
- [ ] Organize content logically
- [ ] Identify any gaps needing client input
- [ ] Draft outline for approval if complex

#### Step 3: Document Creation
- [ ] Set up document with proper formatting
- [ ] Apply brand guidelines (fonts, colors, logo)
- [ ] Write or paste content into structure
- [ ] Add headings, subheadings, and formatting
- [ ] Insert images, charts, or graphics as needed

#### Step 4: Review & Refinement
- [ ] Check for spelling and grammar errors
- [ ] Verify all facts and figures
- [ ] Ensure consistent formatting throughout
- [ ] Check document flow and readability
- [ ] Test any links or interactive elements

#### Step 5: Client Review
- [ ] Send draft with clear subject line
- [ ] Note any areas needing client input
- [ ] Incorporate feedback
- [ ] Final polish

#### Step 6: Final Delivery
- [ ] Export in required format(s)
- [ ] Name file appropriately
- [ ] Store in designated location
- [ ] Send to client and/or stakeholders

---

### Document Type Guidelines

#### Business Letters
- Use professional letterhead
- Include date, recipient address, salutation
- Keep to one page when possible
- End with professional closing and signature

#### Reports
- Include executive summary
- Use clear headings and subheadings
- Add page numbers and table of contents for longer docs
- Include sources and appendix as needed

#### Presentations
- Follow 10-20-30 rule (10 slides, 20 minutes, 30pt font minimum)
- Use visuals over text where possible
- Maintain consistent design throughout
- Include speaker notes if presenting

#### Spreadsheets
- Use clear column headers
- Apply consistent formatting
- Include data source and date
- Add charts/visuals for key data points

## 10. Event Planning & Coordination

### Purpose
Plan and execute seamless events that achieve objectives and create positive experiences for all attendees.

### Frequency
As needed (typically 1-4 events per quarter)

### Time Required
- Small event (10-20 people): 5-10 hours
- Medium event (20-50 people): 15-30 hours
- Large event (50+ people): 40+ hours

### Tools Needed
- Event management platform (Eventbrite, etc.)
- Project management tool
- Budget tracking spreadsheet
- Communication tools

---

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Event Definition
- [ ] Clarify event purpose and objectives
- [ ] Determine target audience and expected attendance
- [ ] Establish budget parameters
- [ ] Identify key stakeholders
- [ ] Set preliminary date(s)

#### Step 2: Venue & Vendor Sourcing
- [ ] Research venue options based on needs
- [ ] Request proposals and pricing
- [ ] Check availability for target dates
- [ ] Source caterers, AV, decor as needed
- [ ] Review contracts and negotiate terms

#### Step 3: Planning & Coordination
- [ ] Create detailed project plan with deadlines
- [ ] Coordinate with all vendors
- [ ] Develop run-of-show/agenda
- [ ] Plan logistics (parking, signage, etc.)
- [ ] Prepare materials (name tags, handouts, etc.)

#### Step 4: Invitation & Registration
- [ ] Design and send invitations
- [ ] Set up registration system
- [ ] Track RSVPs
- [ ] Send reminders as event approaches
- [ ] Manage waitlist if applicable

- [ ] Arrive early for setup
- [ ] Coordinate vendor arrivals
- [ ] Manage registration/check-in
- [ ] Troubleshoot any issues
- [ ] Ensure agenda runs on time

#### Step 6: Post-Event Follow-Up
- [ ] Send thank you notes to attendees
- [ ] Process vendor payments
- [ ] Gather feedback/surveys
- [ ] Review event against objectives
- [ ] Document lessons learned
