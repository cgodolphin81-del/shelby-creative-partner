# Quick Start Guide

## 🚀 Getting Started with Your Mobile App Dev Agency

This guide helps you implement the mobile app development service package quickly.

---

## Phase 1: Setup (Week 1)

### 1.1 Review Core Documents

**Read in order:**
1. [Service Offerings](./01-Service-Offerings.md) - Define what you'll sell
2. [Pricing Models](./05-Pricing-Models.md) - Set your rates
3. [Tech Stack Guide](./02-Tech-Stack-Guide.md) - Choose your technologies

### 1.2 Customize for Your Agency

**Update these with your info:**
- Company name and branding
- Pricing (adjust based on your market)
- Team structure (based on current size)
- Technology preferences

### 1.3 Set Up Tools

**Essential tools to configure:**
- [ ] Project management (Linear/Jira)
- [ ] Communication (Slack)
- [ ] Design (Figma)
- [ ] Version control (GitHub)
- [ ] CRM (HubSpot or similar)
- [ ] Proposal tool (PandaDoc or similar)

## Phase 2: First Client (Weeks 2-4)

### 2.1 Find Your First Client

**Quick wins:**
- Reach out to your network
- Post on LinkedIn
- Contact local businesses
- Partner with agencies

**Use:** [Client Acquisition](./06-Client-Acquisition.md)

### 2.2 Run Discovery

**Follow the process:**
1. Send [Discovery Questionnaire](./templates/discovery-questionnaire.md)
2. Schedule consultation call
3. Run requirements workshop
4. Create technical spec using [template](./templates/technical-spec-template.md)

**Use:** [Discovery Process](./03-Discovery-Process.md)

### 2.3 Create Proposal

**Use:** [Proposal Template](./templates/proposal-template.md)

**Key sections:**
- Executive summary
- Understanding of requirements
- Proposed solution
- Scope of work
- Timeline
- Investment (present 3 options)
- Terms & conditions

### 2.4 Close the Deal

**Negotiation tips:**
- Present 3 options (anchor high)
- Focus on value, not price
- Be willing to walk away
- Get verbal commitment before sending revised proposal

---

## Phase 3: Deliver (Weeks 5-20)

### 3.1 Kickoff

**First week:**
- Sign contract
- Collect deposit
- Schedule kickoff meeting
- Set up project in PM tool
- Create [Project Charter](./templates/project-charter.md)

**Use:** [Project Management](./08-Project-Management.md)

### 3.2 Development

**Follow the process:**
- 2-week sprints
- Daily standups
- Sprint planning, review, retro
- Weekly client updates

**Use:** [Development Process](./04-Development-Process.md)

### 3.3 Quality Assurance

**Before launch:**
- All user stories complete
- Tests passing (>70% coverage)
- No critical/high bugs
- Performance benchmarks met
- Client UAT sign-off

**Use:** QA checklist in [Development Process](./04-Development-Process.md)

### 3.4 Launch

**Launch checklist:**
- App Store submission (iOS: 24-48hr review)
- Play Store submission (Android: 2-7 day review)
- Monitor crash reports
- Check analytics
- Respond to early reviews

---

## Phase 4: Maintain & Grow (Ongoing)

### 4.1 Post-Launch Support

**Offer maintenance:**
- Use [Maintenance Agreement](./templates/maintenance-agreement.md)
- Choose tier based on client needs
- Set up monitoring and analytics

**Use:** [Post-Launch Support](./09-Post-Launch-Support.md)

### 4.2 Get Referrals

**Ask for referrals:**
- After successful launch
- After positive feedback
- During maintenance check-ins

**Offer:** 10% referral fee or $5K bonus

### 4.3 Build Case Studies

**Document every project:**
- Before/after metrics
- Client testimonial
- Technology used
- Timeline and budget
- Challenges overcome

**Use for:** Website, proposals, sales calls

### 4.4 Scale

**Hiring triggers:**
- $500K revenue: First PM
- $1M revenue: First designer (FT), third developer
- $1.5M revenue: First QA
- $2M revenue: First sales hire

**Use:** [Team Structure](./07-Team-Structure.md)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### ❌ Pricing Mistakes
- Underpricing to win deals
- Not including contingency
- Free scope creep
- No change order process

### ❌ Delivery Mistakes
- Overcommitting on timeline
- Not saying no to scope creep
- Skipping discovery
- Poor communication

### ❌ Business Mistakes
- No pipeline (feast or famine)
- Too dependent on one client
- Not collecting deposits
- No maintenance revenue
