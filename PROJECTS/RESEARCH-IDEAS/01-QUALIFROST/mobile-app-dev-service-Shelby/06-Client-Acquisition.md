## Ideal Client Profiles (ICP)

### ICP 1: Funded Startup

**Profile:**
- Stage: Seed to Series B
- Funding: $500K - $20M raised
- Team: 5-50 employees
- Budget: $50K - $200K
- Timeline: 2-6 months

**Pain Points:**
- Need MVP to validate/grow
- Technical co-founder may be missing
- Pressure from investors to launch
- Limited internal dev capacity

**Where to Find:**
- Crunchbase (recent funding announcements)
- AngelList/Wellfound
- Startup events and demo days
- VC portfolio companies
- Y Combinator, Techstars networks

**Messaging:**
- "Launch in 8 weeks, not 8 months"
- "Investor-ready product"
- "Technical partnership, not just development"
- "We've helped 20+ startups raise their next round"

---

### ICP 2: SMB Digital Transformation

**Profile:**
- Industry: Retail, Healthcare, Professional Services
- Revenue: $5M - $50M
- Employees: 20-200
- Budget: $75K - $150K
- Timeline: 3-6 months

**Pain Points:**
- Losing customers to digital-first competitors
- Manual processes need automation
- Customer engagement declining
- Need mobile presence

**Where to Find:**
- Local business associations
- Industry conferences
- LinkedIn (decision makers)
- Chamber of Commerce events
- Industry publications

**Messaging:**
- "Modernize your customer experience"
- "Compete with digital-native brands"
- "ROI-focused development"
- "Industry expertise in [their vertical]"

## Sales Process

### Stage 1: Initial Contact

**Response Time:** < 2 hours during business hours

**Qualification Questions:**
1. What problem are you solving?
2. Who is your target user?
3. What's your timeline?
4. What's your budget range?
5. Have you worked with developers before?

**Qualification Criteria:**
- ✅ Budget > $25K
- ✅ Timeline realistic (8+ weeks)
- ✅ Decision maker engaged
- ✅ Clear problem definition
- ✅ Responsive communication

**Disqualify If:**
- ❌ Budget < $20K (refer to no-code options)
- ❌ Wants app in < 4 weeks
- ❌ Only shopping on price
- ❌ Unclear on problem/users
- ❌ Unresponsive or difficult

---

### Stage 2: Discovery Call (30-60 min)

**Agenda:**
| Time | Topic |
|------|-------|
| 0-5 min | Introductions, agenda |
| 5-15 min | Their story, problem, vision |
| 15-25 min | Target users, use cases |
| 25-35 min | Technical context, constraints |
| 35-45 min | Budget, timeline expectations |
| 45-55 min | Our approach, relevant experience |
| 55-60 min | Next steps |

**Call Notes Template:**
```
COMPANY: [Name]
CONTACT: [Name, Title]
DATE: [Date]

PROBLEM:
[Summary]

TARGET USERS:
[Description]

MUST-HAVE FEATURES:
- [Feature 1]
- [Feature 2]

BUDGET: [Range]
TIMELINE: [Target]

COMPETITORS:
[List]

TECHNICAL NOTES:
[Any integrations, constraints]

NEXT STEPS:
- [ ] Send proposal by [date]
- [ ] Schedule follow-up [date]
- [ ] [Other action items]
```

---

### Stage 3: Proposal & Demo

**Proposal Timeline:** 2-3 business days after discovery

**Proposal Components:**
1. Executive summary
2. Understanding of requirements
3. Proposed solution
4. Scope of work (detailed)
5. Timeline with milestones
6. Investment
7. Team introductions
8. Case studies
9. Terms & conditions
10. Acceptance signature

**Proposal Presentation:**
- Schedule 45-60 min call
- Walk through proposal
- Address questions/concerns
- Discuss timeline to start
- Send contract after call

---

### Stage 4: Negotiation

**Common Objections & Responses:**

**"Your price is higher than other quotes"**
```
"I understand budget is important. Can I ask what the 
other quotes include? Often we find differences in scope, 
post-launch support, or team expertise. Let's make sure 
we're comparing apples to apples."
```

**"We need to think about it"**
```
"Of course, this is a significant decision. What 
specifically would you like to think through? Is there 
anything I can clarify or provide more information on?"
```

**"Can you do it for less?"**
```
"We can explore options. We could reduce scope for a 
lower price, or look at a phased approach. What's most 
important to you - timeline, features, or budget?"
```

**"We want to start with a smaller project"**
```
"That makes sense. We could start with discovery ($10K) 
or an MVP ($40-50K) to prove the concept before the 
full build. Would that work better?"
```

**Negotiation Guidelines:**
- Never discount without getting something
- Offer scope reduction, not price reduction
- Maintain minimum margin thresholds
- Know your walk-away point
- Get verbal commitment before sending revised proposal

---

### Stage 5: Close & Onboard

**Closing Signals:**
- Asking about start date
- Discussing payment logistics
- Introducing other team members
- "This looks good" comments
- Quick responses to contract questions

**Contract Sending:**
- Use DocuSign or similar
- Send with personalized note
- Follow up in 24 hours if unsigned
- Celebrate when signed!

**Onboarding Kickoff:**
- Welcome email with next steps
- Schedule kickoff meeting
- Set up communication channels
- Share project management access
- Begin discovery process
