## Retention Strategy Overview

### Retention Framework

```
CLIENT RETENTION PYRAMID

        ▲
       / \
      /   \
     / Advocacy \      ← Turn customers into advocates
    /────────────\
   /  Expansion   \    ← Grow account value
  /────────────────\
 /    Retention     \  ← Prevent churn
/────────────────────\
│    Onboarding      │  ← Start strong
└────────────────────┘
```

### Retention Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Gross Retention Rate | (Customers End - New Customers) / Customers Start | >95% |
| Net Retention Rate | (MRR End - Churn MRR) / MRR Start | >110% |
| Logo Churn Rate | Lost Customers / Total Customers | <5% annually |
| Revenue Churn Rate | Lost MRR / Total MRR | <10% annually |
| Customer Lifetime | 1 / Churn Rate | >5 years |
| LTV:CAC Ratio | Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost | >3:1 |

### Retention Levers

| Lever | Impact | Effort | Timeline |
|-------|--------|--------|----------|
| Onboarding Excellence | High | Medium | 0-90 days |
| Proactive Success | High | High | Ongoing |
| QBR Excellence | Medium | Medium | Quarterly |
| Expansion Programs | High | Medium | 6+ months |
| Advocacy Programs | Medium | Low | 12+ months |

# Quarterly Business Review Framework

## Purpose

QBRs are strategic meetings to:
- Review performance and ROI
- Align on goals and priorities
- Identify expansion opportunities
- Strengthen relationships
- Ensure customer success

## QBR Cadence

| Customer Tier | Frequency | Format | Attendees |
|---------------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Enterprise | Quarterly | In-person/Virtual | Executives + Teams |
| Mid-Market | Quarterly | Virtual | Managers + Teams |
| SMB | Semi-Annual | Virtual | Primary Contact |
| At-Risk | Monthly | Virtual | All Stakeholders |

## QBR Structure (60 minutes)

```
0-5 min   │ Welcome & Agenda
5-15 min  │ Business Review (Their Goals)
15-25 min │ Product Usage & Adoption
25-35 min │ ROI & Value Delivered
35-45 min │ Challenges & Roadblocks
45-55 min │ Forward Plan & Opportunities
55-60 min │ Next Steps & Close
```

## Preparation Checklist

**2 Weeks Before:**
- [ ] Schedule meeting with all stakeholders
- [ ] Send calendar invite with agenda
- [ ] Begin data collection

**1 Week Before:**
- [ ] Complete usage analysis
- [ ] Calculate ROI metrics
- [ ] Prepare success stories
- [ ] Identify expansion opportunities
- [ ] Draft presentation

**2 Days Before:**
- [ ] Send pre-read deck
- [ ] Confirm attendance
- [ ] Test technology

**Day Before:**
- [ ] Review account notes
- [ ] Prepare for objections
- [ ] Rehearse presentation
```

### Expansion Framework

**File: `upsell/expansion-framework.md`**

```markdown
# Expansion Framework

## Expansion Types

### 1. Seat Expansion (More Users)

**Triggers:**
- High usage per user
- Waitlist for access
- Team expressing interest
- Company growth

**Approach:**
```
"Based on your usage, you're getting great value. 
I noticed [Team X] is still waiting for access. 
Adding them would [specific benefit]. 
Let me show you the ROI..."
```

**Pricing:** Per-seat or tier-based

---

### 2. Feature Expansion (More Capabilities)

**Triggers:**
- Using adjacent features manually
- Asking about capabilities
- Hitting usage limits
- New use cases identified

**Approach:**
```
"I noticed you're [manual work]. Our [Feature] 
automates this and would save [X hours/$]. 
Customers like [Similar Company] saw [Result]..."
```

**Pricing:** Add-on or tier upgrade

---

### 3. Usage Expansion (More Volume)

**Triggers:**
- Approaching limits
- Business growth
- Seasonal spikes
- New markets

**Approach:**
```
"You're at 85% of your [limit]. Based on your 
growth, you'll hit it in [X weeks]. Let's discuss 
options to avoid [negative consequence]..."
```

**Pricing:** Usage-based or tier upgrade

---

### 4. Geographic Expansion (More Locations)

**Triggers:**
- New office openings
- International expansion
- Multi-location needs
- Compliance requirements

**Approach:**
```
"Congratulations on the [new market] expansion! 
We can support you there with [specific capabilities]. 
Here's how we've helped similar companies..."
```

**Pricing:** Multi-region pricing

---

### 5. Service Expansion (More Support)

**Triggers:**
- Complex implementations
- Strategic initiatives
- Limited internal resources
- High-value accounts

**Approach:**
```
"Given the strategic importance of [initiative], 
our [Service] would ensure [outcome]. This includes 
[specific deliverables]..."
```

**Pricing:** Professional services, premium support

## Expansion Timing

### Best Times to Expand

| Timing | Why | Approach |
|--------|-----|----------|
| After success milestone | Value proven | "Now that you've achieved X..." |
| QBR meetings | Strategic mindset | "Looking at next quarter..." |
| Company funding/growth | Budget available | "Congratulations on..." |
| Before renewal | Leverage | "As we discuss renewal..." |
| New stakeholder | Fresh perspective | "Let me show you what's possible..." |
| Product launch | New capabilities | "We just launched..." |

### Expansion Readiness Score

| Factor | Score 1 | Score 3 | Score 5 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Product Usage | <50% | 50-80% | >80% |
| Satisfaction (NPS) | <30 | 30-50 | >50 |
| Relationship | New | Established | Champion |
| Business Health | Uncertain | Stable | Growing |
| Budget | Unknown | Some | Confirmed |

**Total Score:**
- 5-10: Not ready (nurture)
- 11-18: Possible (qualify)
- 19-25: Ready (pitch)

## Expansion Playbook

### Play 1: The Success Story

**When:** Customer achieves measurable success

**Steps:**
1. Document the win
2. Quantify the value
3. Share broadly in account
4. Connect to additional use cases
5. Propose expansion

**Script:**
```
"Congratulations on [achievement]! The [X% improvement] 
is fantastic. I'm curious—could we apply this same 
approach to [adjacent area]? Teams that do typically 
see [benefit]..."
```

### Play 2: The Gap Analysis

**When:** QBR or strategic review

**Steps:**
1. Review current state
2. Identify gaps vs. goals
3. Map features to gaps
4. Quantify impact of closing gaps
5. Propose solution

**Script:**
```
"Looking at your goals for next quarter, I see a 
potential gap in [area]. Our [solution] addresses 
this by [how]. Based on similar customers, you could 
expect [outcome]..."
```

### Play 3: The Usage Limit

**When:** Approaching limits

**Steps:**
1. Alert before hitting limit
2. Explain consequences
3. Present options
4. Recommend best fit
5. Make transition easy

**Script:**
```
" heads up—you're at 85% of your [limit] and on 
track to hit it in 3 weeks. When that happens, 
[consequence]. Let's get ahead of this. You have 
a few options..."
```

### Play 4: The Strategic Initiative

**When:** Customer announces initiative

**Steps:**
1. Understand the initiative
2. Map to your capabilities
3. Build business case
4. Present to decision makers
5. Offer implementation support

**Script:**
```
"Congratulations on [initiative]—that's exciting! 
We've helped similar companies with this by 
[approach]. I'd love to share some ideas on how 
we could accelerate your success..."
```

## Objection Handling

### Common Objections

**"No Budget"**
```
Response: "I understand budget is tight. Let's look at 
the ROI—if this generates [X] and costs [Y], the 
payback is [Z months]. Could we explore creative 
options like [alternative]?"
```

**"Not a Priority"**
```
Response: "Help me understand your priorities. If 
[this] impacts [their priority], would it move up? 
What would need to change for this to become a 
priority?"
```

**"We're Happy with Current Setup"**
```
Response: "That's great to hear! Many of our best 
customers said the same before they saw [specific 
benefit]. Could I show you what's possible?"
```

**"Need to Think About It"**
```
Response: "Of course. What specific questions do 
you have? What would help you feel confident in 
moving forward?"
```

**"Too Expensive"**
```
Response: "I hear you on price. Compared to what? 
When you factor in [ROI elements], the net benefit 
is [X]. Would it help to look at different options?"
```

## Churn Prevention Playbook

### Play 1: The Save Call

**When:** High-risk signal detected

**Preparation:**
1. Review account history
2. Identify potential issues
3. Prepare recovery plan
4. Escalate internally if needed

**Call Structure:**
```
1. Acknowledge the situation
   "I've noticed [specific signal], and I wanted to 
   reach out personally..."

2. Listen and understand
   "Can you help me understand what's going on?"
   "What's changed from your perspective?"

3. Validate concerns
   "I completely understand why that's concerning..."

4. Present solution
   "Here's what I'd like to propose..."

5. Commit to action
   "I'll personally ensure [specific action]..."

6. Follow up
   "Let's check in on [date] to ensure..."
```

### Play 2: The Executive Alignment

**When:** Strategic misalignment or executive concern

**Steps:**
1. Identify executive sponsor
2. Request executive meeting
3. Prepare business review
4. Listen to concerns
5. Commit to resolution
6. Follow through

**Script for Executive:**
```
"[Executive], I wanted to personally reach out 
regarding [Customer]. We value this partnership 
highly, and I understand there may be some concerns. 
I'd appreciate 30 minutes to hear directly from you 
about how we can better support your goals..."
```

### Play 3: The Value Reinforcement

**When:** Customer not seeing value

**Steps:**
1. Audit usage and outcomes
2. Identify quick wins
3. Create success plan
4. Increase touchpoints
5. Demonstrate value

**Actions:**
- Usage review session
- Additional training
- Success milestone planning
- Regular check-ins
- Value reporting

### Play 4: The Competitive Defense

**When:** Competitor evaluation

**Steps:**
1. Understand competitor appeal
2. Highlight differentiation
3. Address gaps honestly
4. Leverage switching costs
5. Offer competitive terms

**Script:**
```
"I understand you're evaluating [Competitor]. They're 
a solid company. Help me understand what's driving 
the evaluation. Is there something specific we're 
not delivering? I'd like to address it if we can..."
```
