```

```

### Four Risk Categories

| Category | Description | Requirements | Examples |
|----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **PROHIBITED** ❌ | Cannot be placed on EU market | Ban | Social scoring, real-time biometric ID in public, manipulation |
| **HIGH-RISK** ⚠️ | Strict compliance required | Full conformity assessment | Recruitment AI, credit scoring, medical diagnosis, critical infrastructure |
| **LIMITED-RISK** ⚡ | Transparency obligations | Disclose AI use | Chatbots, deepfakes, emotion recognition |
| **MINIMAL-RISK** ✅ | No obligations | Voluntary codes | Spam filters, AI-enabled games, most business tools |

### Key Principle: **Proportionality**

Higher risk = More requirements. Lower risk = Lighter touch.

## APPENDIX: Q&A PREPARATION

### Common Questions & Answers

**Q: "We're a US company with no EU office. Does this apply to us?"**
A: Yes, if your AI systems are used in the EU or affect EU residents. The EU AI Act has extraterritorial reach similar to GDPR.

**Q: "Our AI is just a feature, not a product. Are we affected?"**
A: If the AI feature falls into a high-risk category (e.g., recruitment screening), yes. Risk classification is use-case specific, not product-specific.

**Q: "We use third-party AI APIs. Are we responsible?"**
A: Both provider and deployer have obligations. You need to verify their compliance and meet deployer requirements.

**Q: "Can we self-certify?"**
A: Some high-risk systems allow internal conformity assessment. Others require a notified body. Depends on the specific category.

**Q: "What if we miss the deadline?"**
A: Grace periods are limited. You risk market exclusion, fines, and forced withdrawal. Proactive compliance is strongly recommended.

**Q: "How long is certification valid?"**
A: There's no expiration, but you must maintain ongoing compliance, update documentation, and report incidents. Significant changes require re-assessment.
