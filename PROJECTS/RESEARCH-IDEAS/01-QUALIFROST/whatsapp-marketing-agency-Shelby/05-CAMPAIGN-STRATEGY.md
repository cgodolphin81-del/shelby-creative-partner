# Campaign Strategy - Broadcast Timing, Segmentation & Personalization

## 🎯 Overview

Great campaigns combine the right message, right audience, right time. This guide covers the strategy behind high-converting WhatsApp campaigns.

## ⏰ BROADCAST TIMING

### Optimal Send Times by Industry

| Industry | Best Days | Best Times | Avoid |
|----------|-----------|------------|-------|
| **E-commerce** | Tue-Thu | 11am-2pm, 7-9pm | Monday mornings, weekends |
| **Restaurants** | Wed-Fri | 10am-12pm, 4-6pm | Late night, early morning |
| **Real Estate** | Tue-Sat | 10am-1pm, 6-8pm | Sunday, Monday |
| **Education** | Tue-Thu | 12-2pm, 6-8pm | Exam periods, holidays |
| **Healthcare** | Mon-Wed | 9-11am, 2-4pm | Evenings, weekends |
| **B2B Services** | Tue-Thu | 10am-12pm, 2-4pm | Monday, Friday afternoon |
| **Entertainment** | Thu-Sat | 12-3pm, 7-10pm | Early morning |
| **Travel** | Mon-Wed | 12-3pm, 7-9pm | Friday-Sunday |

### Time Zone Strategy

**For Local Businesses:**
- Send in recipient's local time
- Use geo-segmentation for accuracy
- Respect local holidays and customs

**For National/Global Brands:**
```
STRATEGY 1: Staggered Sends
- Segment by time zone
- Send at optimal time for each zone
- Example: 11am EST, 11am CST, 11am PST

STRATEGY 2: Peak Overlap
- Find time that works for most recipients
- Example: 2pm EST = 11am PST (covers both coasts)

STRATEGY 3: Multiple Sends
- Same campaign, different times
- A/B test which performs better
- Use for large lists (50K+)
```

## 🎯 SEGMENTATION STRATEGIES

### Why Segmentation Matters

**Segmented campaigns outperform blast campaigns:**
- 2x higher open rates
- 3x higher click-through rates
- 5x higher conversion rates
- 50% lower opt-out rates

### Segmentation Dimensions

#### 1. Demographic Segmentation

```
Age Groups:
- 18-24: Trend-focused, price-sensitive
- 25-34: Quality + value, mobile-first
- 35-44: Family-oriented, convenience
- 45-54: Premium, loyalty-focused
- 55+: Trust, simplicity, customer service

Gender:
- Product preferences
- Messaging tone
- Offer types

Location:
- Country/region
- City size
- Climate zone
- Urban vs. rural
```

#### 2. Behavioral Segmentation

```
Purchase Behavior:
- First-time buyers
- Repeat customers
- VIP/high-spenders
- Discount-seekers
- Category preferences

Engagement Level:
- Highly engaged (opens every message)
- Moderately engaged (opens 50%+)
- Low engagement (opens <25%)
- Inactive (no opens in 60+ days)

Browse Behavior:
- Product categories viewed
- Time on site
- Pages per session
- Cart abandoners
- Wishlist users

Customer Journey Stage:
- Awareness (new subscribers)
- Consideration (browsing, comparing)
- Decision (ready to buy)
- Retention (existing customers)
- Advocacy (referring others)
```

#### 3. Psychographic Segmentation

```
Values & Interests:
- Eco-conscious
- Luxury seekers
- Deal hunters
- Early adopters
- Traditionalists

Lifestyle:
- Busy professionals
- Stay-at-home parents
- Students
- Retirees
- Fitness enthusiasts

Personality:
- Impulse buyers
- Research-driven
- Brand loyal
- Variety seekers
```

#### 4. Transactional Segmentation

```
Recency (Last Purchase):
- 0-7 days: Recent buyer
- 8-30 days: Active customer
- 31-90 days: At-risk
- 90+ days: Lapsed

Frequency (Purchase Rate):
- 1x: One-time buyer
- 2-5x: Occasional
- 6-10x: Regular
- 11+: VIP

Monetary (Total Spend):
- $0-50: Low value
- $51-200: Medium value
- $201-500: High value
- $500+: VIP

RFM Score Example:
- R5 F5 M5 = Best customer (recent, frequent, high spend)
- R1 F1 M1 = Lowest priority (old, one-time, low spend)
```

### Advanced Segmentation Techniques

#### 1. Predictive Segmentation

Use AI/ML to predict:
- Churn risk
- Purchase likelihood
- Product preferences
- Optimal message timing
- Lifetime value

```
Example: Churn Risk Score

HIGH RISK (Score 80-100):
- No purchase in 90+ days
- Decreasing engagement
- Visited cancellation page
→ Action: Win-back campaign, special offer

MEDIUM RISK (Score 50-79):
- No purchase in 60-90 days
- Stable engagement
→ Action: Re-engagement, survey

LOW RISK (Score 0-49):
- Recent purchase
- High engagement
→ Action: Loyalty rewards, upsell
```

#### 2. Lookalike Segments

Find new prospects similar to your best customers:

```
Steps:
1. Identify your top 10% customers (by LTV)
2. Analyze common characteristics
3. Find similar profiles in your broader audience
4. Target with acquisition campaigns
5. Measure conversion vs. control group
```

#### 3. Lifecycle Segments

```
NEW SUBSCRIBER (Day 0-7):
Goal: Build relationship
Content: Welcome series, brand story, first offer

ACTIVE PROSPECT (Day 8-30):
Goal: Convert to customer
Content: Educational, social proof, targeted offers

FIRST-TIME BUYER (After 1st purchase):
Goal: Drive 2nd purchase
Content: Onboarding, support, related products

REPEAT CUSTOMER (2+ purchases):
Goal: Increase frequency/value
Content: Loyalty rewards, VIP access, referrals

VIP CUSTOMER (Top 10% by spend):
Goal: Retain and maximize LTV
Content: Exclusive offers, early access, personal touch

LAPSED CUSTOMER (90+ days no purchase):
Goal: Win back
Content: "We miss you", strong offer, survey
```

## ✨ PERSONALIZATION TACTICS

### Personalization Levels

#### Level 1: Basic (Everyone Should Do This)

```
✓ Use first name
✓ Reference location (city/region)
✓ Mention signup source
✓ Acknowledge customer status

Example:
"Hi Sarah! Welcome to the NYC crew. As a new member, 
here's 15% off your first order..."
```

#### Level 2: Intermediate (Top 25% of Marketers)

```
✓ Reference past purchases
✓ Recommend based on browsing
✓ Acknowledge important dates (birthday, anniversary)
✓ Reference engagement history

Example:
"Hi Sarah! Your favorite brand just dropped new arrivals. 
Based on your love for [Brand X], we think you'll love these..."
```

#### Level 3: Advanced (Top 5% of Marketers)

```
✓ Predictive product recommendations
✓ Dynamic pricing based on behavior
✓ Contextual timing (life events)
✓ Cross-channel personalization

Example:
"Hi Sarah! We noticed you've been eyeing the Summer Collection. 
Since you're a VIP member, we've reserved your size and 
applied your exclusive 25% discount. Plus, free shipping 
because you're only $15 from your next reward tier! 🎁"
```

```
First-Party Data (Most Valuable):
```

## 🧪 A/B TESTING FRAMEWORK

### What to Test

**High-Impact Tests:**
1. Send time (morning vs. afternoon vs. evening)
2. Message length (short vs. long)
3. Offer type (% off vs. $ off vs. BOGO)
4. CTA wording ("Shop Now" vs. "Get Yours" vs. "Claim")
5. Emoji usage (none vs. moderate vs. heavy)
6. Personalization level (name only vs. deep personalization)
7. Urgency framing (hours left vs. items left vs. both)

**Medium-Impact Tests:**
1. Subject line style (emoji vs. no emoji)
2. Image vs. text-only
3. Single CTA vs. multiple CTAs
4. Social proof placement (top vs. bottom)
5. Price display (was/now vs. % off)

### Testing Methodology

```
STEP 1: Form Hypothesis
"Shorter messages will have higher CTR because..."

STEP 2: Define Success Metric
Primary: Click-through rate
Secondary: Conversion rate, revenue per send

STEP 3: Determine Sample Size
- Minimum 1,000 recipients per variant
- For statistical significance: use calculator
- Test for at least 24-48 hours

STEP 4: Split Audience Randomly
- 50/50 split for A/B test
- Ensure segments are comparable
- Use same time zone, time of day

STEP 5: Run Test
- Send simultaneously
- Monitor for technical issues
- Don't peek early (wait for full duration)

STEP 6: Analyze Results
- Check statistical significance (95%+ confidence)
- Consider practical significance (is difference meaningful?)
- Look at secondary metrics

STEP 7: Implement Winner
- Roll out to remaining audience
- Document learnings
- Plan next test
```

### Sample Test Results

**Test: Send Time Optimization**

| Variant | Time | Open Rate | CTR | Conversion | Revenue |
|---------|------|-----------|-----|------------|---------|
| A | 9am | 82% | 18% | 4.2% | $1,240 |
| B | 12pm | 88% | 24% | 5.8% | $1,890 |
| C | 7pm | 91% | 28% | 6.4% | $2,340 |

**Winner:** Variant C (7pm) - 89% more revenue than 9am

**Action:** Shift all campaigns to 7pm send time
