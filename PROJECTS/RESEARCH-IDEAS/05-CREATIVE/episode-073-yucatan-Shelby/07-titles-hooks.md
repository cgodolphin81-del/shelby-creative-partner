## Hooks by Platform

### Podcast/Audio Platforms (Spotify, Apple Podcasts, etc.)

**Hook 1 (Mystery):**
"Six thousand cenotes. Six thousand doorways to the underworld. The ancient Maya threw gold, jade, and human sacrifices into these sacred waters. Today, you can swim in the same pools where prayers were offered for three thousand years. What do the cenotes remember? And what can they teach us about life, death, and the cycles that bind us all?"

**Hook 2 (Creation Story):**
"In the beginning, the gods tried to make humans from mud. Failed. Then from wood. They became monkeys. Third time, from white and yellow corn—success. This is the Popol Vuh. This is the Maya truth: we are corn people. Our skin is the color of maize. Our bones are corn stalks. Our blood runs sweet as atole. Welcome to the Yucatan, homeland of the Children of the Corn."

**Hook 3 (Time Philosophy):**
"The Western mind imagines time as an arrow—launched from the past, piercing the present, vanishing into the future. The Maya saw time as a circle. Interlocking wheels. Gears within gears. In 2012, the thirteenth baktun ended. The world panicked: 'The apocalypse!' But the Maya smiled. Of course a cycle ended. Another begins. Time is not a line with an end. Time is a circle. Always returning."

**Hook 4 (Equinox Phenomenon):**
"Stand at Chichen Itza on the equinox. The sun is directly overhead. The pyramid casts a shadow on its northern staircase. Seven triangles of light. Seven segments of a serpent's body. As the sun moves, the shadow crawls downward. The serpent descends from heaven to earth. The Maya built this without computers. Without telescopes. Without metal tools. And it still works. A thousand years later, the serpent still descends. The circle still turns."

**Hook 5 (Jungle Reclamation):**
"The jungle does not mourn the fallen. It reclaims them. Walk through the ruins of Coba, where trees burst from temple roofs like green flames. Roots snake through stone corridors. Vines strangle the old gods' houses, not in anger, but in love. 'Come home,' the jungle whispers. 'Return to earth.' Stone becomes soil. Soil becomes tree. Tree becomes oxygen, breath, life. Nothing is lost. Everything transforms. The circle turns."
