## KEY FACTS (For Show Notes & Marketing)

### Geographic
- **Distance from Chile:** 2,175 miles (3,500 km)
- **Distance from Tahiti:** 2,500 miles
- **Nearest neighbor:** Pitcairn Island, 1,200 miles
- **Status:** Most remote inhabited place on Earth
- **Size:** 64 square miles (166 sq km)

### The Moai
- **Total count:** 1,043 documented statues
- **Creation period:** 1250-1600 AD
- **Average size:** 13 feet (4m) tall, 12.5 tons
- **Largest (unfinished):** 69 feet (21m), 270 tons
- **Material:** Volcanic tuff (soft, easy to carve)
- **Orientation:** Most face inland (protecting the people)

### Key Sites
- **Rano Raraku:** Volcanic quarry, ~400 moai remaining
- **Ahu Tongariki:** 15 moai, largest platform
- **Tahai Complex:** Best sunset viewing, near Hanga Roa
- **Ahu Akivi:** Only inland moai (7 statues)
- **Rano Kau:** Largest volcano, ceremonial village Orongo

### Modern Context
- **Current population:** ~4,000 Rapa Nui people
- **Annual tourism:** ~100,000 visitors
- **UNESCO status:** World Heritage Site (1995)
- **Political status:** Special territory of Chile (annexed 1888)

### Revised History (2024-2026 Research)
- **Old narrative:** Self-inflicted ecocide and collapse
- **New understanding:** Sustainable society, external collapse
- **DNA evidence:** No population bottleneck until 1860s
- **Real collapse:** Peruvian slave raids took 1/3 of population
- **Innovation:** Lithic mulching (rock gardening) maximized yields

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Audio Recording
**Recommended Setup:**
- Quiet space with minimal reverb
- High-quality microphone (e.g., Shure SM7B, Rode NT1)
- Pop filter and shock mount
- Record at 44.1kHz/24-bit minimum

**Voice Direction:**
- Contemplative, reverent tone
- Intimate, as if sharing a secret
- Pauses for impact (marked in script)
- Build emotional intensity through middle
- Return to calm for closing

**Sound Design:**
- Ocean waves (constant, varying intensity)
- Distant seabirds (frigate birds, boobies)
- Wind (subtle, constant)
- Stone carving SFX (brief, in carving section)
- Ambient music: Pacific-inspired, contemplative

### Music Cues (from script)
- **00:00-00:30:** Opening (ambient, mysterious)
- **00:30-03:00:** Journey (building anticipation)
- **03:00-07:00:** Moai (ancient, reverent)
- **07:00-11:00:** Collapse (questioning → hopeful)
- **11:00-15:00:** Legacy (personal, intimate)
- **15:00-18:00:** Silence (sparse, minimal)
- **18:00-20:00:** Closing (return to opening theme)
