# Episode 022: Travel Guide - Easter Island (Rapa Nui)
## Practical Information for Visiting the Edge of the World

---

## GETTING THERE

### Flights
**The Only Route:** Santiago, Chile (SCL) → Hanga Roa, Easter Island (IPC)

**Airline:** LATAM Airlines (exclusive service)
- **Frequency:** Daily flight (one per day in each direction)
- **Departure from Santiago:** 9:10 AM
- **Arrival at Hanga Roa:** 12:30 PM (local time, 2 hours behind Santiago)
- **Return departure:** 2:15 PM from Easter Island
- **Return arrival:** 10:05 PM in Santiago

**Flight Duration:** 5 hours 30 minutes

**Cost (Round-trip from Santiago):**
- Economy: $450-650 USD
- Premium Economy: $800-1,000 USD
- Business: $1,500-2,000+ USD

**From North America:**
- No direct flights from USA/Canada
- Route: Your city → Santiago (SCL) → Easter Island (IPC)
- Flights to Santiago from major US cities: $800-1,500 USD round-trip
- Total travel time: 12-18 hours depending on connections

**Booking Tips:**
- Book 4-6 months in advance (flights sell out, especially Dec-Feb)
- Flights frequently oversubscribed during peak season
- Consider booking as part of Chile + Easter Island package (15-20% savings)
- LATAM website sometimes has better availability than third-party sites

## ENTRY REQUIREMENTS

### Documents Needed
1. **Valid Passport:** Must be valid for duration of stay
2. **Unique Entry Form (FUI):** Complete online at Ingreso Rapa Nui before travel
3. **Round-trip Flight Ticket:** Must show departure from Easter Island
4. **Confirmed Accommodation:** Must be registered with SERNATUR (Chilean National Tourism Service)

### Visa Requirements
- **US Citizens:** No visa required for stays up to 90 days
- **EU Citizens:** No visa required for stays up to 90 days
- **Most Western Countries:** 90-day visa-free entry
- **Check Specific Requirements:** Based on your nationality

### Maximum Stay
- **Standard Tourist Stay:** 30 days
- **Extension Possible:** Up to 90 days with special permit
- **Overstay Fines:** Significant penalties for exceeding visa-free period

## COSTS & BUDGET

### Park Entrance Fee
- **Rapa Nui National Park:** $80 USD (foreign visitors)
- **Validity:** 10 days from first use
- **Coverage:** Most archaeological sites (moai, ahu, quarry)
- **Purchase:** Online in advance or at airport/entry points
- **Required:** Must show pass at major sites (Rano Raraku, Ahu Tongariki)

### Accommodation (Per Night)

**Budget:**
- Hostels/Guesthouses: $60-80 USD
- Basic private rooms: $80-120 USD
- Shared facilities common at this level

**Mid-Range:**
- Hotels: $150-250 USD
- Boutique accommodations: $200-300 USD
- Most common choice for tourists

**Luxury:**
- Explora Rapa Nui: $800-1,200 USD/night (all-inclusive)
- The Taha Tai: $500-800 USD/night
- Premium amenities, guided tours included

### Food & Dining

**Budget:**
- Local eateries (kioscos): $10-15 USD per meal
- Self-catering (supermarkets): $30-40 USD/day
- Limited grocery options, higher prices than mainland Chile

**Mid-Range:**
- Restaurants: $40-60 USD per person daily
- Seafood specialties, Chilean-Polynesian fusion
- Reservations recommended for dinner

**High-End:**
- Fine dining: $80-120 USD per meal
- Explora resort dining (guests only)
- Limited options, book ahead

### Tours & Activities

**Guided Tours:**
- Half-day group tour: $80-120 USD per person
- Full-day tour: $150-200 USD per person
- Private guide (full day): $300-500 USD

**Specific Activities:**
- Snorkeling tour: $100-150 USD
- Horseback riding: $80-120 USD (half-day)
- Scuba diving: $150-200 USD (two tanks)
- Sunset tour (Tahai): $60-80 USD
- Bike rental: $25-40 USD/day
- Scooter rental: $50-80 USD/day

### Transportation on Island

**Rental Vehicles:**
- Small car: $80-120 USD/day
- 4x4 SUV: $120-180 USD/day
- Scooter/Motorcycle: $50-80 USD/day
- Bicycle: $25-40 USD/day

**Taxis:**
- Hanga Roa to Ahu Tongariki: $60-80 USD one-way
- Airport transfers: $30-50 USD
- Negotiate fare before departure

**Important:**
- Limited vehicles on island
- Book rentals in advance
- Roads are mostly paved but some sites require 4x4
- Speed limits strictly enforced (40 km/h max)

## PERMITS & RESTRICTIONS

### Archaeological Site Rules
- **Certified Guide Required:** Must hire local guide or be accompanied by Rapa Nui resident (18+) at most sites
- **Stay on Paths:** Do not walk on or around ahu platforms
- **No Touching:** Do not touch or climb on moai
- **No Drones:** Drone use requires special permit (rarely granted)
- **Photography:** Allowed for personal use; commercial requires permit

### Cultural Respect
- **Sacred Sites:** Some areas restricted for cultural reasons
- **Ask Permission:** Before photographing locals or private property
- **Remove Nothing:** Do not take rocks, artifacts, or natural materials
- **Leave Nothing:** Pack out all trash

### Environmental Protection
- **No Single-Use Plastics:** Chile has banned many single-use plastics
- **Water Conservation:** Island has limited fresh water
- **Wildlife:** Do not disturb seabirds or marine life

## LIMITED TOURISM

### Visitor Caps
- **Annual Visitors:** ~100,000 (pre-pandemic levels)
- **Daily Flights:** Limited by single daily flight (~150-200 passengers)
- **Accommodation Capacity:** ~3,000 beds island-wide
- **Peak Season:** December-February (books out months ahead)

### Restrictions That May Apply
- **Local Emergencies:** Island can close during crises (e.g., pandemic, natural disasters)
- **Site Closures:** Individual sites may close for preservation or cultural reasons
- **Resident Priority:** During emergencies, residents given priority for flights

### Why Limited Tourism Matters
- **Fragile Ecosystem:** Small island, limited resources
- **Cultural Preservation:** Protect Rapa Nui heritage and way of life
- **Infrastructure:** Limited water, waste management, medical facilities
- **Resident Quality of Life:** Prevent overtourism impacts

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Rapa Nui People
- **Population:** ~4,000 (majority Rapa Nui descent)
- **Language:** Rapa Nui (Polynesian) and Spanish
- **Culture:** Blend of Polynesian heritage and Chilean influence
- **Land Ownership:** Restricted to Rapa Nui people and long-term residents

### Cultural Tips
- **Learn Basic Phrases:**
  - "Iorana" = Hello
  - "Maururu" = Thank you
  - "Pehea koe?" = How are you?
- **Respect Sacred Sites:** These are ancestral places, not just tourist attractions
- **Support Local:** Buy from Rapa Nui artisans, eat at local restaurants
- **Listen to Guides:** Local knowledge is invaluable and supports community

### Tipping
- **Not Mandatory:** But appreciated for good service
- **Restaurants:** 10% for excellent service
- **Guides:** $10-20 USD per day for good tours
- **Taxis:** Round up or small tip

## BEST TIME TO VISIT

### Optimal Seasons

**Shoulder Season (RECOMMENDED):**
- **March-May:** Pleasant weather, fewer crowds, good prices
- **October-December:** Spring blooms, warming up, pre-holiday

**Peak Season:**
- **December-February:** Warmest weather, highest prices, most crowded
- **Book 6+ months ahead**
- **Expect higher flight and hotel costs**

**Low Season:**
- **June-August:** Cooler, more rain, better deals
- **Some tours may have reduced schedules**
- **Still viable, pack layers**

### Weather Overview
- **Average Temperature:** 68-80°F (20-27°C) year-round
- **Rainiest Months:** May-June
- **Driest Months:** January-March
- **Water Temperature:** 68-77°F (20-25°C)

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Health & Safety
- **Medical Facilities:** Limited; serious issues require evacuation to Chile
- **Travel Insurance:** Highly recommended (include medical evacuation)
- **Vaccinations:** No special requirements beyond routine
- **Sun Protection:** Equatorial sun is intense (SPF 50+, hat, sunglasses)
- **Mosquitoes:** Present but not a major disease risk

### Money
- **Currency:** Chilean Peso (CLP)
- **ATMs:** Limited (2-3 in Hanga Roa); can run out of cash
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted at most hotels and restaurants
- **Cash Recommended:** For small vendors, tips, markets
- **USD Exchange:** Possible but better rates in Santiago

### Connectivity
- **WiFi:** Available at most hotels (speed varies)
- **Cell Service:** Limited; Chilean carriers have coverage
- **International Roaming:** Check with your carrier
- **Offline Maps:** Download before arrival (Google Maps works partially)

### Packing List
- **Lightweight Clothing:** Warm climate year-round
- **Rain Jacket:** Especially May-August
- **Sturdy Walking Shoes:** For hiking and archaeological sites
- **Swimwear:** For beaches and snorkeling
- **Sun Protection:** Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
- **Insect Repellent:** For evenings
- **Power Adapter:** Chilean Type C/L (220V)
- **Cash:** Chilean pesos for small purchases

## SUGGESTED ITINERARY (5-7 Days)

**Day 1:** Arrive, settle in, sunset at Tahai
**Day 2:** Rano Raraku quarry + Ahu Tongariki (sunrise)
**Day 3:** Anakena Beach + Ahu Akivi (inland moai)
**Day 4:** Rano Kau crater + Orongo ceremonial village
**Day 5:** Snorkeling or diving, Hanga Roa exploration
**Day 6:** Bike/scooter around island, revisit favorite sites
**Day 7:** Departure
