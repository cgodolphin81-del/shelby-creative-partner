## LIGHTING GUIDE

### Golden Hour 1 (Sunrise)

**Time:** Gate opening to ~8am
**Color Temperature:** 2500-3500K (warm)
**Shadow Quality:** Long, sharp, deep blue
**Direction:** Low angle from east

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Dune crests blaze orange-red
- Western faces in deep indigo shadow
- Maximum contrast between light and shadow
- Sand texture highly visible
- Cool shadows, warm highlights

**Mood:** Awakening, revelation, beginning, awe

**Best For:**
- Dune ridge shots (light/shadow divide)
- Silhouettes against brightening sky
- Deadvlei trees (dramatic shadows)
- Wide landscapes with defined dune shapes

**Avoid:**
- Direct sun in frame (too harsh)
- Mid-morning when light flattens

---

### Golden Hour 2 (Sunset)

**Time:** ~4pm to gate closing
**Color Temperature:** 3000-4000K (warm, slightly cooler than sunrise)
**Shadow Quality:** Long, stretching east
**Direction:** Low angle from west

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Dunes glow from within
- Shadows reverse (eastern faces dark)
- Slightly softer than sunrise
- Warm, forgiving light
- Sky may show color gradient

**Mood:** Completion, warmth, reflection, peace

**Best For:**
- West-facing dune slopes
- Silhouettes on ridge lines
- Warm portraits (if including human figure)
- Emotional, contemplative content

**Avoid:**
- Waiting too late (light disappears fast)
- East-facing subjects (in shadow)

---

### Blue Hour

**Time:** 20-30 min before sunrise / after sunset
**Color Temperature:** 8000-12000K (cool)
**Shadow Quality:** Soft, minimal
**Direction:** Ambient, no direct light

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Even, soft lighting
- Deep blue sky
- Dunes become soft shapes
- Minimal texture visible
- Calm, meditative quality

**Mood:** Peace, transition, meditation, mystery

**Best For:**
- Minimalist compositions
- Silhouettes against blue sky
- Calm, contemplative content
- Transition scenes

**Avoid:**
- Expecting detail or texture
- Warm color grading (fight the blue)

---

### Midday (Harsh Light)

**Time:** 11am-3pm
**Color Temperature:** 5500-6500K (neutral/cool)
**Shadow Quality:** Minimal, directly beneath objects
**Direction:** Overhead

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Bleached, almost white sand
- Minimal shadows
- High contrast, harsh
- Heat shimmer visible
- Colors desaturated

**Mood:** Harshness, challenge, survival, emptiness

**Best For:**
- Abstract sand patterns (top-down)
- Conveying heat/harshness
- Canyon shots (Sesriem — shaded)
- "The desert is unforgiving" narrative

**Avoid:**
- Dune landscapes (flat, no definition)
- Portraits (harsh shadows on face)
- Most photography (rest instead)

---

### Night (Starlight)

**Time:** Full darkness (2+ hours after sunset)
**Color Temperature:** Variable (starlight ~4000K, Milky Way varies)
**Shadow Quality:** Minimal, soft
**Direction:** Overhead (stars)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Dunes faintly visible (silvery)
- Brilliant stars, Milky Way
- No light pollution
- Long exposure reveals detail
- Otherworldly quality

**Mood:** Wonder, cosmos, smallness, infinity

**Best For:**
- Astrophotography
- Contemplative, cosmic content
- "Deep time" narrative
- Episode transitions

**Avoid:**
- Expecting color (mostly monochrome)
- Handheld shots (need tripod)

## COMPOSITION GUIDELINES

### Rule of Thirds (Classic)

**Apply To:**
- Horizon placement (lower third for sky emphasis, upper third for land)
- Dune ridge lines (along third lines)
- Figure placement (at intersection points)

**Example:**
- Dune crest along upper third line
- Figure at lower-right intersection
- Sky fills upper two-thirds

---

### Leading Lines

**Natural Lines in Desert:**
- Dune ridges (S-curves, diagonals)
- Sand ripples (repeating patterns)
- Shadows (long lines at sunrise/sunset)
- Tracks in sand (journey narrative)

**Apply To:**
- Guide eye through frame
- Create depth and dimension
- Suggest movement, journey

**Example:**
- Dune ridge leading from bottom-left to top-right
- Figure walking along ridge (following the line)

---

### Negative Space

**Desert Advantage:**
- Vast empty skies
- Empty sand expanses
- Minimal visual clutter

**Apply To:**
- Emphasize smallness/scale
- Create minimalist, zen compositions
- Add text overlays (copy space)

**Example:**
- Tiny figure on dune, 2/3 of frame is sky
- Single tree in vast pan
- Text overlay in sky area

---

### Scale & Perspective

**Show Scale By:**
- Including human figure (Shelby)
- Known objects (camera, backpack)
- Trees (for Deadvlei shots)
- Multiple dune layers (atmospheric perspective)

**Perspective Techniques:**
- **Low angle:** Makes dunes tower, emphasizes scale
- **High angle (drone):** Shows patterns, vastness
- **Eye level:** Immersive, "you are there"
- **Worm's eye:** Abstract, texture-focused

---

### Framing

**Natural Frames:**
- Dune curves (crescent shapes)
- Dead tree branches (Deadvlei)
- Canyon walls (Sesriem)
- Between two dunes (corridor effect)

**Apply To:**
- Focus attention on subject
- Add depth layers
- Create "window" effect

**Example:**
- Dead tree branches framing distant dune
- Shooting through gap between dunes

## TEXTURE & DETAIL

### Sand Textures

**Wind Ripples:**
- **Scale:** 5-20cm wavelength
- **Best Light:** Low angle (sunrise/sunset)
- **Technique:** Get low, shoot across ripples
- **Mood:** Pattern, repetition, zen

**Grain Detail (Macro):**
- **Scale:** Individual grains (mm)
- **Best Light:** Any (even, soft)
- **Technique:** Macro lens, focus stacking
- **Mood:** Abstract, scientific, beauty in small

**Slipface (Avalanche):**
- **Scale:** Meters
- **Best Light:** Side-lit (morning/evening)
- **Technique:** Capture cascading sand
- **Mood:** Movement, change, impermanence

---

### Rock & Canyon Textures

**Sesriem Canyon Walls:**
- **Characteristics:** Layered sediment, erosion patterns
- **Best Light:** Midday (canyon provides shade)
- **Technique:** Wide angle to show depth
- **Mood:** Ancient, geological, cool

**Fossilized Dunes:**
- **Characteristics:** Preserved ancient dune structures
- **Best Light:** Side-lit for texture
- **Technique:** Medium telephoto to compress
- **Mood:** Deep time, preservation

---

### Organic Textures

**Dead Tree Bark:**
- **Characteristics:** Smooth (sand-polished), black, metallic
- **Best Light:** Side-lit or overcast
- **Technique:** Close-up, show texture
- **Mood:** Death, preservation, time

**Welwitschia Leaves:**
- **Characteristics:** Split, weathered, gray-green
- **Best Light:** Overcast or shade
- **Technique:** Show full plant + detail shots
- **Mood:** Ancient, strange, resilient

**Animal Tracks:**
- **Characteristics:** Crisp impressions in sand
- **Best Light:** Low angle (shows depth)
- **Technique:** Get low, shallow depth of field
- **Mood:** Life, presence, story

## BRAND VISUAL IDENTITY

### Color Grading Style

**Overall Look:**
- Warm tones (orange, red, gold)
- Deep shadows (indigo, purple)
- High contrast (but not crushed)
- Slightly desaturated (except orange/red)
- Film-like quality (not digital-sharp)

**Lightroom/Editing Presets:**
- **Temperature:** Slightly warm (unless blue hour)
- **Tint:** Slightly toward magenta (enhances reds)
- **Contrast:** +20 to +40
- **Highlights:** -30 to -50 (recover dune detail)
- **Shadows:** +20 to +40 (lift but keep depth)
- **Vibrance:** +10 to +20
- **Saturation:** -5 to -10 (muted, not oversaturated)
- **Clarity:** +10 to +20 (texture without harshness)
- **Dehaze:** +10 to +20 (desert clarity)

**Orange/Red Specific:**
- Target orange saturation: +20 to +30
- Target red saturation: +10 to +20
- Target orange luminance: -10 to -20 (deepen)
- Keep natural, avoid "Instagram orange"

### Typography (For Text Overlays)

**Primary Font (Titles):**
- **Style:** Clean, modern, sans-serif
- **Examples:** Montserrat, Proxima Nova, Helvetica Neue
- **Weight:** Bold or Semi-Bold
- **Color:** White or pale gold
- **Use:** Episode titles, major text overlays

**Secondary Font (Body):**
- **Style:** Simple, readable, sans-serif or slab
- **Examples:** Lato, Open Sans, Roboto
- **Weight:** Regular or Light
- **Color:** White or off-white
- **Use:** Captions, quotes, smaller text

**Treatment:**
- Slight drop shadow (for readability on bright sand)
- Kerning: Slightly increased (elegant)
- Alignment: Center or left (avoid right-align)
- Size: Large enough for mobile viewing

## QUICK REFERENCE: DO'S & DON'TS

### DO:
✅ Shoot at golden hour (sunrise/sunset)
✅ Include human figure for scale
✅ Use negative space
✅ Show light/shadow contrast
✅ Grade warm (orange/red emphasis)
✅ Keep compositions simple
✅ Capture texture (sand, trees, rock)
✅ Embrace minimalism

### DON'T:
❌ Shoot dunes at midday (flat light)
❌ Oversaturate (keep natural)
❌ Overcrowd frame (less is more)
❌ Ignore the sky (it's 2/3 of many shots)
❌ Forget scale (show how big dunes are)
❌ Rush (desert rewards patience)
❌ Over-edit (maintain authenticity)
