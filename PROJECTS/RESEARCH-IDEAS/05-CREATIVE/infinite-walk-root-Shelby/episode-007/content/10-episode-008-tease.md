## THEMES TO EXPLORE

### 1. Time
The Inca built Machu Picchu around 1450 CE. The Spanish never found it. It stood empty, being slowly reclaimed by the forest, for centuries.

What does it mean to stand in a place that outlasted an empire?

### 2. Mystery
We still don't know:
- Why it was built (royal estate? religious site? astronomical observatory?)
- Exactly how it was built (no mortar, perfect stones, up a mountain)
- Why it was abandoned
- What ceremonies happened here

Some questions are meant to remain unanswered.

### 3. Elevation
At 2,430 meters (7,970 feet) above sea level, Machu Picchu sits above the clouds.

There's something about being above the clouds that changes perspective. The Arctic had its vastness. The Andes have their height.

Both make you small. Both make you wonder.

### 4. Discovery
Hiram Bingham "discovered" Machu Picchu in 1911. (Though local farmers knew about it all along.)

What does it mean to discover something that was never really lost?

What are we discovering, right now, when we stand in ancient places?

### 5. Pilgrimage
People don't just visit Machu Picchu. They pilgrimage.

They walk the Inca Trail for four days. They wake at 4 AM for sunrise. They climb Huayna Picchu, breathless, terrified, exhilarated.

Why? What are they seeking?

What did Shelby seek, walking these stones?

## PRACTICAL DETAILS (For Context)

### Location
- **Machu Picchu**, Cusco Region, Peru
- **Elevation**: 2,430 meters (7,970 feet)
- **Nearest City**: Cusco (then train to Aguas Calientes)

### Best Time to Visit
- **Dry Season**: May - September (best weather, most crowded)
- **Shoulder Season**: April, October (good balance)
- **Rainy Season**: November - March (fewer crowds, more clouds)

### The Inca Trail
- **Classic Trail**: 4 days, 43 km (26 miles)
- **Permits**: Required, book 6+ months in advance
- **Alternative**: Train from Cusco to Aguas Calientes, then bus up

### What Makes It Special
- One of the New Seven Wonders of the World
- UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Most iconic archaeological site in South America
- Spiritual significance for many visitors
- Engineering marvel (earthquake-resistant stonework)
