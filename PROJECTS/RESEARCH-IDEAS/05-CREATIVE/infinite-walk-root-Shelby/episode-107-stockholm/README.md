## 🎯 Episode Summary

**Title:** The Northern Light  
**Theme:** Light and darkness, patience, the philosophy of "enough" (lagom), the sacred pause (fika)

**Core Message:**
> "There is a particular quality to light in the far north—a light that knows darkness intimately. Stockholm understands this. The city does not fight the darkness. It befriends it."

**Key Locations:**
- Gamla Stan (Old Town) - medieval cobblestones, 800 years of history
- Vasa Museum - the ship that waited 333 years to rise again
- Stockholm Archipelago - 30,000 islands of patience
- ABBA Museum & music culture - Sweden's outsized cultural impact
- Swedish design institutions - minimalism, functionality, lagom

**Philosophical Themes:**
1. **Light in Darkness** - Candles at 3 PM. Warmth as resistance.
2. **Lagom** - "Just the right amount." Enough is a destination.
3. **Archipelago Patience** - Beauty exists whether or not anyone sees it.
4. **Fika** - The radical act of stopping. Pause is not wasted time.
5. **Resurrection** - The Vasa teaches: failure is not final.

**Closing Line:**
> "We walk, and we remember: the light always returns. Always."
