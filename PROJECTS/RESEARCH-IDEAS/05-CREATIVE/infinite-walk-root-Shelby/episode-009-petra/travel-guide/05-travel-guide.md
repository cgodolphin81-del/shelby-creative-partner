## 🛂 Before You Go

### Visa Requirements

**Most travelers need a visa to enter Jordan.**

#### Option 1: Jordan Pass (RECOMMENDED)
The Jordan Pass is the best value for most visitors.

**What's Included:**
- Tourist entry visa (waives 40 JD visa fee)
- Entry to Petra (1, 2, or 3 days)
- Entry to 40+ other attractions across Jordan

**Cost (2025-2026):**
| Pass Type | Petra Access | Total Cost | USD Equivalent |
|-----------|--------------|------------|----------------|
| Jordan Wanderer | 1 day | 70 JD | ~$99 |
| Jordan Explorer | 2 days | 75 JD | ~$106 |
| Jordan Expert | 3 days | 80 JD | ~$113 |

**Important:**
- MUST be purchased online BEFORE arriving in Jordan
- Website: jordanpass.jo
- Print or save digital copy on phone
- Valid for 12 months from purchase
- Must stay minimum 3 nights in Jordan to qualify for visa waiver

#### Option 2: Visa on Arrival
- Available at Queen Alia International Airport (Amman) and most border crossings
- Cost: 40 JD (~$57 USD)
- Single entry, valid for 30 days
- Plus separate Petra ticket (see below)

#### Option 3: E-Visa
- Apply online before travel
- Same cost as visa on arrival (40 JD)
- Faster entry at border

### Petra Entrance Fees (Without Jordan Pass)

If you don't purchase the Jordan Pass, buy tickets at the Petra Visitor Centre:

| Pass Type | Cost (JD) | Cost (USD) |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| 1-day pass | 50 JD | ~$70 |
| 2-day pass | 55 JD | ~$78 |
| 3-day pass | 60 JD | ~$85 |
| Arabs (non-Jordanian) | 30 JD | ~$42 |
| Jordanians | 1 JD | ~$1.40 |

**Children under 12:** Free entry (but still need visa if required)

**What You Need:**
- Passport (required for ticket purchase)
- Jordan Pass (if purchased) OR cash/card for tickets
- Printed or digital confirmation

### Petra by Night

A separate ticketed experience.

- **Schedule:** Monday, Wednesday, Thursday only
- **Time:** 8:30 PM - 10:30 PM
- **Cost:** 17 JD (~$24 USD)
- **What's Included:** Walk through candlelit Siq (1,500 candles), view Treasury illuminated, traditional Bedouin music
- **Requirements:** Valid Petra day ticket also needed
- **Tip:** Not photography-friendly (too dark), but magical atmosphere

## ✈️ Getting to Jordan

### Flying In

**Queen Alia International Airport (AMM) — Amman**
- Main international gateway
- Airlines: Royal Jordanian, Ryanair, EasyJet, Emirates, Qatar, Turkish, and more
- From airport to Petra: 3 hours by car

**King Hussein International Airport (AQJ) — Aqaba**
- Smaller, closer to Petra (2 hours)
- Fewer international flights
- Good option if combining Petra with Red Sea diving

### Overland Crossings

**From Israel:**
- Allenby/King Hussein Bridge (near Jericho)
- Yitzhak Rabin/Arava Crossing (near Eilat/Aqaba)
- Check current border status before travel

**From Saudi Arabia:**
- Durra Border Crossing (closed to tourists as of 2024)
- Check current status

**From Syria/Iraq:**
- NOT recommended due to security concerns

## 🚗 Getting to Petra

### From Amman

| Method | Cost | Duration | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|----------|------|------|
| **JETT Bus** | 10 JD | 4-5 hrs | Cheap, reliable, direct | Fixed schedule (6:30 AM departure) |
| **Private Taxi** | ~100 JD (full day) | 3 hrs | Flexible, door-to-door | More expensive, negotiate price |
| **Rental Car** | Variable | 3 hrs | Maximum flexibility | Desert driving, parking fees |
| **Organized Tour** | 100-350 JD | Full day | Guide included, hassle-free | Less flexibility, group pace |

**JETT Bus Schedule:**
- Departure from Amman: 6:30 AM daily (from JETT station, 7th Circle)
- Return from Petra: 5:00 PM daily
- Booking: Book 1-2 days in advance at JETT office or online
- Drop-off: Petra Archaeological Site car park (walking distance to Visitor Centre)

**Private Taxi:**
- Negotiate price BEFORE getting in
- Confirm if price is one-way or round-trip
- Confirm waiting time if doing day trip
- Typical: 90-120 JD for round-trip with waiting time

**Rental Car:**
- Companies: Europcar, Hertz, Budget, local agencies
- Cost: 35-60 JD/day depending on vehicle
- Route: Desert Highway (Route 15) — well-maintained
- Fuel: Fill up in Amman or major towns
- Parking: Free at Petra Visitor Centre

### From Aqaba

| Method | Cost | Duration | Notes |
|--------|------|----------|-------|
| **JETT Bus** | 7-10 JD | 2 hours | Limited schedule, check times |
| **Private Taxi** | 50-70 JD | 2 hours | Negotiate fare |
| **Rental Car** | Variable | 2 hours | Scenic desert drive via Route 15 |
| **Tour** | 80-150 JD | Full day | Often combined with Wadi Rum |

### From Wadi Rum

- **Distance:** 100 km (1.5 hours)
- **Transport:** Taxi or organized tour

- **Common Combination:** Many visitors do Wadi Rum + Petra in same trip

## 🏨 Where to Stay

### Wadi Musa (Petra's Gateway Town)

Stay in Wadi Musa, the town adjacent to Petra's entrance.

#### Budget (15-40 JD/night)
- **Petra Moon Hotel** — Clean, friendly, rooftop views
- **Old Village Hotel** — Traditional architecture, good location
- **Petra Guest House Hotel** — Closest to entrance (owned by Petra Authority)
- **Hostels:** Petra Hostel, Nomads Hostel (dorms from 10 JD)

#### Mid-Range (45-80 JD/night)
- **Mövenpick Resort Petra** — Pool, spa, 2-minute walk to entrance
- **Petra Marriott Hotel** — Modern, reliable chain quality
- **Aden Hotel** — Rooftop restaurant with Treasury views
- **Seven Wonders Hotel** — Good value, central location

#### Luxury (100+ JD/night)
- **Mövenpick Resort Petra** (also in mid-range)
- **Hyatt Regency Petra** — New (2023), luxury amenities
- **Petra Bubble Luxotel** — Unique transparent domes, stargazing

### Booking Tips
- Book in advance for spring/autumn (peak seasons)
- Many hotels offer Petra tour packages
- Ask about early breakfast for 6 AM Petra entry
- Some hotels offer packed lunches for Petra visits
- Lower town = closer to Visitor Centre; Upper town = views but more walking

## ⏰ Planning Your Visit

### Best Time of Year

| Season | Months | Temperature | Crowds | Recommendation |
|--------|--------|-------------|--------|----------------|
| **Spring** | March-May | 15-25°C (59-77°F) | High | ⭐⭐⭐ Ideal |
| **Autumn** | Sept-Nov | 22-28°C (72-82°F) | High | ⭐⭐⭐ Ideal |
| **Winter** | Dec-Feb | 10-16°C (50-61°F) | Low | ⭐⭐ Good (cold evenings) |
| **Summer** | June-Aug | Up to 38°C+ (100°F+) | Medium | ⭐ Avoid if possible |

**Spring (March-May):**
- Pros: Mild temperatures, wildflowers, long days
- Cons: Peak crowds, higher prices
- Tip: Book accommodation 2-3 months ahead

**Autumn (September-November):**
- Pros: Warm but not extreme, clear skies
- Cons: Still crowded, especially October
- Tip: Similar to spring, book ahead

**Winter (December-February):**
- Pros: Few crowds, lower prices, dramatic skies
- Cons: Cold (especially mornings/evenings), possible rain, shorter days
- Tip: Bring warm layers, start later (7-8 AM)

**Summer (June-August):**
- Pros: Very long days, least rain
- Cons: Extremely hot (dangerous for hiking), peak sun
- Tip: If you must go, start at 6 AM, avoid midday, carry extra water

### Best Time of Day

**Golden Hours:**
- **Sunrise to 9 AM:** Best light, fewest crowds, coolest temperatures
- **3 PM to Sunset:** Warm light, thinning crowds, good for photography

**Avoid:**
- **10 AM - 3 PM:** Peak heat, peak crowds, harsh light

**Strategy:**
1. Arrive at Visitor Centre by 5:45 AM (opens 6 AM)
2. Walk the Siq and see Treasury before 8 AM
3. Take midday break (return to hotel, rest, eat)
4. Return 3-4 PM for afternoon exploration
5. Stay until closing for sunset light

### How Many Days?

**1 Day:**
- Possible but rushed
- See: Siq, Treasury, Main Trail, Royal Tombs
- Skip: Monastery hike, High Place of Sacrifice, distant trails
- Pace: Fast, lots of walking (15-20 km)
- Best for: Day-trippers from Amman/Aqaba

**2 Days (RECOMMENDED):**
- Comfortable pace
- Day 1: Siq, Treasury, Main Trail, Royal Tombs, Theater
- Day 2: Monastery hike, High Place of Sacrifice, explore side trails
- Best for: Most visitors

**3 Days:**
- Comprehensive exploration
- Days 1-2: As above
- Day 3: Hidden trails, lesser-known tombs, repeat favorites, photography
- Best for: Photography enthusiasts, hikers, history buffs

## ⚠️ Safety & Practical Tips

### Safety
- **Overall:** Jordan is very safe for tourists
- **Crime:** Low violent crime rate; petty theft rare
- **Political Stability:** Stable monarchy, tourist-friendly
- **Regional Tensions:** Generally unaffected by neighboring conflicts
- **Solo Travelers:** Safe, including women (use common sense)

### Health
- **Water:** Bottled water recommended (tap water not for drinking)
- **Food:** Generally safe; avoid undercooked meat, wash fruit
- **Sun:** Extreme in summer; sunscreen, hat, hydration essential
- **Heat:** Summer temperatures dangerous for hiking; start early
- **Medical:** Clinics in Wadi Musa; hospitals in Amman

### Scams to Avoid
- **"Free" horse rides:** Will demand high tips (20-50 JD)
- **Photo spot fees:** Some locals charge for viewpoint access (negotiate or walk away)
- **Unofficial guides:** Use official guides from Visitor Centre
- **Overpriced water/snacks:** Buy in Wadi Musa before entering
- **Fake "closed" signs:** Some vendors claim sites are closed to redirect you

### Cultural Etiquette
- **Dress:** Modest clothing recommended (cover shoulders and knees)
- **Greetings:** "As-salamu alaykum" (peace be upon you)
- **Right Hand:** Use for eating, greeting, exchanging money
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing people
- **Ramadan:** If visiting during Ramadan, be respectful of fasting hours
- **Tipping:** Expected for guides, drivers, hotel staff (5-10%)

### Re-entry Policy
- Same-day re-entry IS allowed
- Inform staff at checkpoint BEFORE exiting
- Keep your ticket/pass accessible
- Useful for: midday break, forgotten items, lunch in town

## 📸 Photography Tips

### Best Light
- **Treasury:** Morning light (arrive by 7 AM for empty shots)
- **Monastery:** Morning or late afternoon
- **Siq:** Mid-morning when light penetrates the gorge
- **Royal Tombs:** Afternoon light brings out colors
- **Landscapes:** Golden hour (sunrise/sunset)

### Best Viewpoints
- **Al Khubta Trail:** Free Treasury viewpoint (moderate 1-hour hike)
- **Treasury Top:** Official viewpoint (15 JD, easier access)
- **Monastery Summit:** Panoramic desert views
- **High Place of Sacrifice:** 360° views of entire site

### Camera Gear
- **Lens:** Wide-angle (16-35mm) for facades; telephoto (70-200mm) for details
- **Tripod:** Useful for low-light (Petra by Night); may require permit
- **Filters:** Polarizing filter enhances sky and reduces glare
- **Protection:** Dust covers; sandstone dust is fine and pervasive

### Restrictions
- **Inside Treasury:** Not permitted (preservation)
- **Drones:** Not allowed without special permit
- **Commercial Photography:** Requires permit from Petra Authority
- **Tripods:** May be restricted in crowded areas

## 🌍 Responsible Tourism

### How to Visit Responsibly

**Protect the Site:**
- Stay on marked trails
- Do not climb on carved facades
- Do not touch or lean on ancient surfaces
- Pack out all trash
- Use designated toilets

**Support Local:**
- Hire local guides (official, from Visitor Centre)
- Buy from local vendors (negotiate fairly)
- Eat at local restaurants in Wadi Musa
- Stay in locally-owned accommodations when possible

**Respect Culture:**
- Dress modestly
- Ask before photographing people
- Learn basic Arabic greetings
- Be patient and friendly

**Animal Welfare:**
- Horse/donkey/camel rides available
- Consider welfare concerns before booking
- If using, agree on price beforehand
- Report mistreatment to Visitor Centre

**Water Conservation:**
- Jordan is water-scarce
- Use water sparingly at hotels
- Reuse water bottles (refill stations available)
- Short showers
