## CRISIS COMMUNICATION

### Potential Issues & Responses

**Issue:** "This is just guilt-tripping without solutions"
**Response:** "Fair critique. The episode includes specific, actionable steps in the travel guide and conservation resources. Hope is something you *do*. What action resonates most with you?"

**Issue:** "Indigenous voices should be centered, not yours"
**Response:** "Absolutely agree. The episode centers indigenous quotes, perspectives, and leadership. I'm a messenger amplifying their voices. Here are indigenous-led organizations to support: [list]"

**Issue:** "Tourism destroys the Amazon"
**Response:** "Valid concern. Responsible, community-controlled tourism can fund conservation and support indigenous economies. The episode includes guidance on choosing ethical operators. Sometimes the choice is responsible tourism vs. extractive industries."

**Issue:** "Statistics are outdated/inaccurate"
**Response:** "Thank you for catching that. I used [source] from [date]. Can you share the updated data? I'll correct and credit you."

### General Principles

- Acknowledge valid criticism promptly
- Correct errors transparently
- Center indigenous voices when discussing indigenous issues
- Don't be defensive; be curious
- Take conversations to DMs when appropriate
- Block/report bad-faith actors, don't engage
