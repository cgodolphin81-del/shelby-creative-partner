## Getting There

### Step 1: International Flight to Islamabad

**Airport:** Islamabad International Airport (ISB)
**Airlines:** Most major carriers connect through Middle East hubs (Dubai, Doha, Abu Dhabi) or Istanbul
**Visa:** Required for most nationalities
- E-visa available for 175+ countries
- Apply online: nadra.gov.pk
- Processing time: 5-10 business days
- Cost: ~$50-100 USD depending on nationality
- Validity: Typically 3 months, multiple entry

**Tip:** Apply for visa at least 1 month before travel. Print multiple copies.

---

### Step 2: Islamabad to Skardu

**Option A: Domestic Flight (RECOMMENDED)**

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Duration** | 45-60 minutes |
| **Airlines** | PIA, Air Sial, Serene Air |
| **Frequency** | 2-4 flights daily (weather permitting) |
| **Cost** | $100-200 USD round trip |
| **Baggage** | 20-25kg checked, 5-7kg carry-on |

**Important Notes:**
- Flights are HIGHLY weather-dependent
- Cancellations common—build in 2-3 buffer days
- Morning flights more likely to operate
- Book through trek operator when possible (they monitor conditions)
- Seats fill quickly in peak season—book early

**Option B: Road Journey via Karakoram Highway**

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Duration** | 12-16 hours (often split into 2 days) |
| **Distance** | ~600 km |
| **Route** | Islamabad → Chilas → Gilgit → Skardu |
| **Transport** | Private jeep or bus |
| **Cost** | $150-300 USD for private jeep |

**Important Notes:**
- Scenic but exhausting journey
- Road conditions variable (landslides common)
- Stop overnight in Chilas or Gilgit recommended
- Karakoram Highway is engineering marvel—worth seeing
- Border with China visible (Khunjerab Pass, world's highest border crossing)
- Only choose this option if you have extra time and want adventure

---

### Step 3: Skardu to Askole (Trailhead)

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Transport** | High-clearance 4WD jeep |
| **Duration** | 6-8 hours |
| **Distance** | ~130 km |
| **Road** | Rough, unpaved, river crossings |
| **Cost** | Usually included in trek package |

**Important Notes:**
- Road ends at Askole—last village before glacier
- Jeep ride can be rough and dusty
- Bring buff/bandana for dust
- Toilet stops limited—plan accordingly
- Scenic drive through Shigar Valley
- Last chance to buy snacks/supplies in Askole (limited selection, higher prices)

## Permits & Documentation

### Required Permits (2025-2026)

**1. Trekking Permit (Gilgit-Baltistan Adventure)**
- Cost: $150-300 USD
- Issued by: Gilgit-Baltistan Tourism Department
- Validity: Duration of trek
- Arranged by: Licensed trekking operator

**2. Central Karakoram National Park (CKNP) Fee**
- Cost: ~$150-190 USD
- Issued by: CKNP Administration
- Includes: Waste management fee ($150)
- Arranged by: Operator

**3. No Objection Certificate (NOC)**
- Cost: Included in operator fees
- Required for: Foreign trekkers
- Processing time: 2-4 weeks
- Arranged by: Operator

**4. Liaison Officer**
- Cost: Included in package
- Role: Government-appointed officer accompanies group
- Purpose: Ensure compliance with regulations
- Arranged by: Operator

### Documents to Carry

**Essential:**
- Passport (valid 6+ months beyond travel dates)
- Pakistan visa (printed copies)
- Travel insurance documents (printed)
- Permit copies (operator provides)
- Passport photos (4-6 extra)
- Emergency contact information

**Recommended:**
- Scanned copies of all documents (email to yourself)
- Physical copies in waterproof bag
- Operator contact details
- Embassy contact information

**Important:** Independent trekking NOT permitted for foreigners. You MUST book through licensed Pakistani operator.

## Best Times to Visit

### Primary Trekking Season: Mid-June to Late September

**Optimal Window: July to Early August**

| Month | Pros | Cons |
|-------|------|------|
| **Late June** | Fewer trekkers, lower prices | Possible snow at higher camps, colder |
| **July** | Best weather, longest days, warmest | More trekkers, higher prices |
| **Early August** | Stable weather, clear skies | Afternoon clouds common |
| **Late August** | Fewer crowds | Shorter days, colder nights |
| **Early September** | Clear skies, good visibility | Colder, possible early snowfall |
| **Late September** | Very few trekkers | Risk of season-ending storms |

### Weather Patterns

**Temperatures:**
- Islamabad: 25-35°C (77-95°F)
- Skardu: 20-30°C (68-86°F)
- Askole: 15-25°C (59-77°F)
- Urdukas (4,050m): 5-15°C (41-59°F) day, -5 to -10°C (23-14°F) night
- Concordia (4,690m): 0-10°C (32-50°F) day, -10 to -15°C (14-5°F) night
- K2 Base Camp (5,150m): -5 to 5°C (23-41°F) day, -15 to -20°C (5 to -4°F) night

**Precipitation:**
- Karakoram is in rain shadow—less monsoon impact than Himalayas
- Afternoon cloud buildup common
- Sudden snowfall possible at any time above 4,500m
- Storms can last 1-3 days

**Wind:**
- Can be severe at higher camps
- Afternoon winds stronger than morning
- Wind chill significant factor

### When NOT to Go

**October-March:** Winter conditions
- Extreme cold (-30°C possible)
- Many operators closed
- Flights frequently cancelled
- Only for experienced winter expeditions

**April-May:** Pre-monsoon
- Unstable weather
- Snow-covered trails
- Many routes impassable
- Some operators offer spring treks (less common)

## Physical Preparation

### Fitness Requirements

**This trek is STRENUOUS. You need:**

- Excellent cardiovascular endurance
- Strong legs and core
- Ability to hike 6-8 hours daily for 15+ consecutive days
- Comfort with altitude (up to 5,150m)
- Mental resilience for challenging conditions

### Training Timeline: 3-6 Months Minimum

**Months 1-2: Base Building**
- Cardio 4x/week (running, cycling, swimming) - 45-60 min
- Strength training 2x/week (full body)
- Weekend hikes 2-3 hours with light pack

**Months 3-4: Intensity Increase**
- Cardio 5x/week - 60-90 min
- Add hill repeats/stair climbing
- Strength training 3x/week (focus legs/core)
- Weekend hikes 4-6 hours with 10kg pack

**Months 5-6: Peak Training**
- Cardio 5x/week including long sessions
- Back-to-back weekend hikes (Sat + Sun)
- Weekend hikes 6-8 hours with 12-15kg pack
- Practice with trekking poles
- Test all gear on long hikes

**Final 2 Weeks: Taper**
- Reduce intensity
- Focus on rest and recovery
- Light activity only
- Arrive in Pakistan 2-3 days early for rest

### Specific Exercises

**Cardio:**
- Running (trail running ideal)
- Cycling (hill climbing)
- Swimming (full body)
- Stair master/step mill
- Hiking with weighted pack

**Strength:**
- Squats and lunges
- Step-ups (weighted)
- Deadlifts
- Core work (planks, Russian twists)
- Calf raises
- Push-ups and pull-ups

**Balance & Agility:**
- Single-leg stands
- Bosu ball exercises
- Yoga or Pilates
- Trail running on uneven terrain

### Altitude Preparation

**If Possible:**
- Spend time at altitude before trek (Colorado, Alps, etc.)
- Test how your body responds to 3,000m+
- Consider altitude training mask (controversial but some find helpful)

**On Trek:**
- Follow acclimatization days built into itinerary
- "Climb high, sleep low" principle
- Stay hydrated (3-4 liters daily)
- Avoid alcohol
- Report any AMS symptoms immediately

## Health & Safety

### Altitude Sickness (AMS)

**Risk Levels on This Trek:**
- Skardu (2,200m): Low
- Askole (3,000m): Moderate
- Urdukas (4,050m): Moderate-High
- Concordia (4,690m): High
- K2 Base Camp (5,150m): Very High

**Prevention:**
- Ascend gradually (itinerary includes acclimatization days)
- "Climb high, sleep low" principle
- Stay hydrated (3-4 liters daily)
- Avoid alcohol and sedatives
- Eat well (carbohydrates important)
- Consider Diamox (acetazolamide) - consult doctor before trip

**Symptoms (Mild AMS):**
- Headache
- Nausea
- Dizziness
- Fatigue
- Loss of appetite
- Difficulty sleeping

**Action for Mild AMS:**
- Rest at same altitude
- Hydrate
- Take pain reliever for headache
- Do not ascend until symptoms resolve
- Consider Diamox if prescribed

**Symptoms (Severe AMS - EMERGENCY):**
- Severe headache (not relieved by medication)
- Vomiting
- Confusion
- Loss of coordination
- Shortness of breath at rest
- Cough with pink/frothy sputum (pulmonary edema)

**Action for Severe AMS:**
- **DESCEND IMMEDIATELY** (500-1,000m minimum)
- Seek medical attention
- Oxygen if available
- Do not wait for morning
- This is life-threatening

### Other Health Risks

**Glacier Hazards:**
- Crevasses (hidden under snow bridges)
- Rockfall (especially afternoon)
- Icefall (serac collapse)
- Slips and falls on ice/moraine

**Weather Hazards:**
- Sudden storms
- Whiteout conditions
- Hypothermia
- Frostbite (extremities)
- Sunburn (intense UV at altitude)

**Water/Food Safety:**
- Drink only treated/boiled water
- Avoid raw vegetables/fruit you can't peel
- Hand sanitizer before meals
- Most operators provide safe meals

### Rescue & Evacuation

**Helicopter Evacuation:**
- Cost: $5,000-15,000+ USD
- Weather-dependent (not guaranteed)
- Insurance MUST cover high-altitude rescue
- Deposit often required upfront (~$2,000-5,000)

**Ground Evacuation:**
- Skardu hospital: 6-8 days on foot + flight
- Limited medical facilities on trek
- Guides carry basic first aid and satellite phone

**Insurance Requirements:**
**MUST COVER:**
- High-altitude trekking (up to 5,500m minimum)
- Helicopter evacuation
- Emergency medical treatment
- Repatriation
- Trip cancellation/interruption

**Recommended Providers:**
- World Nomads
- Allianz Global Assistance
- Travel Guard
-救援 (Chinese insurance, for Asian travelers)

**Verify Before You Go:**
- Read policy carefully
- Confirm altitude coverage
- Get written confirmation
- Carry policy documents on trek
- Save emergency numbers in phone

## Cultural Etiquette

### Dress Code

**In Cities/Villages:**
- Conservative dress expected
- Cover shoulders and knees
- Loose-fitting clothing preferred
- Women: head scarf optional but respectful

**On Trek:**
- Technical trekking gear acceptable
- Still dress modestly around porters/staff
- Swimwear inappropriate (no swimming anyway)

### Photography

**Ask Permission:**
- Always ask before photographing people
- Especially women and children
- Some may decline (respect this)
- Offer to show/send photos if possible

**Religious Sites:**
- Remove shoes before entering mosques
- Dress conservatively
- Ask before photographing
- Avoid prayer times

### General Etiquette

**Greetings:**
- "Assalamu alaikum" (peace be upon you)
- Handshake (right hand only)
- Men and women: wait for local person to initiate

**Eating:**
- Right hand only (left hand considered unclean)
- Wait for host to invite you to eat
- Finish food on plate (compliment to cook)

**Tipping:**
- Expected and appreciated
- Guide: $15-25/day
- Assistant guide: $10-15/day
- Cook: $10-15/day
- Porter: $8-12/day
- Pool tips among staff

**Respect:**
- Pakistanis are famously hospitable
- Accept tea/food when offered
- Show interest in culture
- Be patient (things move slower)
- Avoid political/religious debates

## Environmental Responsibility

### Leave No Trace

**Principles:**
1. Plan ahead and prepare
2. Travel and camp on durable surfaces
3. Dispose of waste properly
4. Leave what you find
5. Minimize campfire impacts (no fires on this trek)
6. Respect wildlife
7. Be considerate of other visitors

**On This Trek:**
- Pack out ALL trash (including toilet paper)
- Use toilet tents at camps
- Do not disturb wildlife
- Stay on established trails
- Do not remove rocks, plants, or artifacts

### Waste Management

**CKNP Waste Management Fee:** $150 USD (included in most packages)

**Operator Responsibilities:**
- Carry out all group waste
- Provide trash barrels at camps
- Proper disposal in Skardu

**Your Responsibilities:**
- Minimize single-use plastics
- Bring refillable water bottles
- Pack personal trash
- Do not litter (even biodegradable)

### Plastic Reduction

**Bring:**
- Refillable water bottles (2L+ capacity)
- Water purification (tablets or filter)
- Reusable food bags

**Avoid:**
- Single-use plastic bottles
- Plastic-wrapped snacks (buy in bulk)
- Disposable wet wipes (use biodegradable)

## Booking & Operators

### When to Book

**Ideal Timeline:**
- 6-12 months in advance for peak season (July-August)
- 3-6 months for shoulder season (June, September)
- Last-minute possible but limited options, higher prices

### How to Choose an Operator

**Questions to Ask:**
1. How many years have you been operating K2 treks?
2. What is your safety record?
3. What is included/excluded in the price?
4. What is the guide-to-client ratio?
5. What happens in case of emergency/evacuation?
6. Is helicopter deposit included?
7. What is your cancellation policy?
8. Can you provide references from past clients?
9. What permits are included?
10. What gear is provided vs. what do I bring?

**Red Flags:**
- Prices significantly below market rate
- Vague answers about safety
- No written contract
- Poor communication
- No online reviews or presence
- Unwilling to provide references
