# Episode 066: The Phantom City
## Petra Travel Guide (2025-2026)

---

## ESSENTIAL INFORMATION

### Location
- **Country:** Jordan
- **Region:** Ma'an Governorate, southwestern Jordan
- **Nearest Town:** Wadi Musa (adjacent to Petra entrance)
- **Distance from Amman:** ~235 km (3-4 hours drive)
- **Distance from Aqaba:** ~130 km (2 hours drive)
- **Coordinates:** 30.3285° N, 35.4444° E

### What Is Petra?
- Ancient Nabatean city carved into rose-red sandstone
- Founded around 300 BCE
- UNESCO World Heritage Site (1985)
- New Seven Wonder of the World (2007)
- Covers 26 square kilometers (main tourist area: 10 sq km)
- Famous for: The Treasury (Al-Khazneh), The Monastery (Ad-Deir), The Siq

## GETTING THERE

### From Amman (Most Common Route)

**By JETT Bus (Recommended for Budget/Mid-Range)**
- **Departure:** JETT Bus Station, Amman (7th Circle)
- **Schedule:** Daily departures at 6:30 AM
- **Duration:** 4-5 hours
- **Cost:** ~12-15 JD one-way (~$17-21 USD)
- **Return:** Departs Wadi Musa at 3:00 PM (arrive Amman ~7 PM)
- **Booking:** Book 1-2 days in advance, especially in peak season
- **Comfort:** Air-conditioned, comfortable seats, onboard WC

**By Private Taxi/Driver**
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours via Desert Highway
- **Cost:** 70-100 JD one-way (~$100-140 USD)
- **Pros:** Flexible departure time, door-to-door, can stop for photos
- **Cons:** More expensive, negotiate price in advance
- **Booking:** Hotel can arrange, or use ride-hailing apps

**By Rental Car**
- **Cost:** 25-40 JD/day (~$35-56 USD) + fuel (~10-15 JD round trip)
- **Route:** Desert Highway (highway 15) - well-maintained
- **Parking:** Available at Petra Visitor Center (~2 JD)
- **Pros:** Maximum flexibility, can explore en route
- **Cons:** Need international driving permit, navigate yourself
- **Tip:** Fill up gas in Amman or major towns

**By Domestic Flight**
- **Route:** Amman (AMM) to Ma'an Airport
- **Airlines:** Royal Jordanian (limited schedule)
- **Duration:** 45 minutes flight + 30 min drive to Petra
- **Cost:** 80-150 JD one-way (varies significantly)
- **Pros:** Fastest option
- **Cons:** Expensive, limited flights, still need ground transport

### From Aqaba (Red Sea)

**By Private Taxi/Driver**
- **Duration:** 2 hours
- **Cost:** 50-70 JD one-way (~$70-100 USD)
- **Route:** Via Wadi Rum (scenic desert highway)
- **Tip:** Combine with Wadi Rum visit

**By Rental Car**
- **Duration:** 2 hours
- **Cost:** Same as Amman rental + less fuel
- **Route:** Well-maintained highway through Wadi Rum area

**By Public Bus**
- **Availability:** Limited, less reliable than Amman route
- **Cost:** ~5-7 JD
- **Not recommended** for time-sensitive travelers

### From Wadi Rum

**By Private Taxi**
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Cost:** 40-60 JD (~$56-85 USD)
- **Route:** Direct desert highway
- **Tip:** Many travelers do Wadi Rum + Petra combination (4-5 days total)

## ENTRANCE FEES & TICKETS (2025-2026)

### Standard Petra Tickets (Non-Jordanian Visitors)

**For Visitors Staying 1+ Nights in Jordan:**
- **1-Day Pass:** 50 JD (~$70 USD / £53 GBP / €60 EUR)
- **2-Day Pass:** 55 JD (~$78 USD / £58 GBP / €66 EUR)
- **3-Day Pass:** 60 JD (~$85 USD / £63 GBP / €72 EUR)

**For Same-Day Visitors (No Overnight in Jordan):**
- **1-Day Pass:** 90 JD (~$127 USD / £95 GBP / €108 EUR)

**Children:**
- Under 12 years: FREE
- 12+ years: Adult price

**Jordanians & Residents:**
- 1 JD (with valid ID held for 1+ year)

### Jordan Pass (HIGHLY RECOMMENDED)

**What It Includes:**
- Petra entrance (1, 2, or 3 days)
- Visa fee waiver (normally 40 JD / ~$56 USD)
- Entry to 40+ attractions across Jordan

**Pricing:**
- **Jordan Wanderer (1-day Petra):** 70 JD (~$99 USD)
- **Jordan Explorer (2-day Petra):** 75 JD (~$106 USD)
- **Jordan Expert (3-day Petra):** 80 JD (~$113 USD)

**Requirements:**
- Purchase BEFORE arriving in Jordan
- Minimum 3 consecutive nights stay in Jordan
- Valid for 2 weeks after first activation

**Savings:** Saves $40-70 USD overall if visiting Petra + other sites

**Where to Buy:**
- Official website: www.jordanpass.jo
- Avoid third-party sites (they charge markup)

### Petra By Night

**What It Is:**
- Evening experience with Treasury lit by 1,500 candles
- Traditional Bedouin music
- Tea included
- Magical atmosphere, fewer crowds

**Schedule:**
- Monday, Wednesday, Thursday evenings
- Starts: 8:30 PM
- Duration: ~2 hours

**Cost:** 30 JD (~$42 USD) - NOT included in standard tickets

**Where to Buy:**
- Kiosk inside Visitor Centre
- Hotels in Wadi Musa (sometimes includes transport)
- Online (higher price, includes transport)

**Worth It?** Yes, for the atmosphere. Less crowded, mystical experience.

### Where to Buy Tickets

**1. Petra Visitor Centre (Wadi Musa)**
- Opens: 6:00 AM
- Payment: Cash or card (card has 2% fee)
- ATM available outside
- Queues move quickly
- **Bring:** Passport (sometimes requested for pricing verification)

**2. Online (Petra Pass Website)**
- Website: petra.pass.jo (official)
- Same prices + 1-2 JD processing fee
- Valid for 3 months from purchase
- Select expected arrival date/time
- Skip ticket office line

**3. Jordan Pass**
- Must purchase before arrival
- Show QR code at entrance
- No need to visit ticket office

**Important:** If booking tours from Amman/Dead Sea, verify if tickets are INCLUDED. Most tours do NOT include entrance fees.

## WHEN TO VISIT

### Best Seasons

**SPRING (March - May) - HIGHLY RECOMMENDED**
- **Temperatures:** 18-27°C (64-81°F) daytime
- **Weather:** Mild, occasional wildflowers
- **Crowds:** Moderate to high (especially Easter)
- **Pros:** Comfortable hiking, green landscape, good light
- **Cons:** More tourists, higher prices
- **Best for:** Full-day hiking, photography, first-time visitors

**AUTUMN (September - Early November) - HIGHLY RECOMMENDED**
- **Temperatures:** 18-27°C (64-81°F) daytime
- **Weather:** Clear skies, dry trails
- **Crowds:** Moderate (busy in October)
- **Pros:** Perfect light for photography, comfortable temperatures
- **Cons:** Similar to spring for crowds
- **Best for:** Photography, hiking to Monastery, extended exploration

**SHOULDER SEASON (February, late November, December)**
- **Temperatures:** 12-20°C (54-68°F) daytime
- **Weather:** Cooler, chance of rain
- **Crowds:** Lower
- **Pros:** Fewer tourists, lower prices
- **Cons:** Shorter days, possible rain, colder mornings
- **Best for:** Budget travelers, photographers wanting moody light

**WINTER (December - February)**
- **Temperatures:** 5-15°C (41-59°F) daytime, near-freezing at night
- **Weather:** Cold, possible rain/snow, flash flood risk
- **Crowds:** Lowest
- **Pros:** Empty sites, dramatic skies, lowest prices
- **Cons:** Cold, short days (sunset ~5 PM), some trails may close
- **Best for:** Solitude seekers, experienced travelers

**SUMMER (June - August) - NOT RECOMMENDED**
- **Temperatures:** 35-40°C+ (95-104°F+) daytime
- **Weather:** Intense heat, full sun, no shade
- **Crowds:** High (European summer holidays)
- **Pros:** Long days (sunset ~8 PM)
- **Cons:** Dangerous heat, exhausting hikes, crowded
- **Best for:** Heat-tolerant travelers only, must start at 6 AM

### Best Times of Day

**EARLY MORNING (6:00-8:00 AM) - BEST**
- Arrive at opening (6 AM)
- Cool temperatures
- Soft light for photography
- Fewest crowds (especially in Siq)
- Treasury empty for photos
- **Strategy:** Stay in Wadi Musa night before, walk to entrance

**LATE AFTERNOON (3:00-5:00 PM)**
- Temperatures dropping
- Warm golden light
- Day-trippers leaving
- Good for Monastery (in shade)
- **Strategy:** Enter after lunch, head to Monastery first

**MIDDAY (11:00 AM-2:00 PM) - AVOID**
- Hottest temperatures
- Harsh overhead light (bad for photos)
- Peak crowds (cruise tours arrive)
- Treasury courtyard packed
- **Strategy:** Rest at café, explore shaded areas, have lunch

## ACCOMMODATION

### Where to Stay

**Wadi Musa (Recommended)**
- Town adjacent to Petra entrance (5-10 min walk)
- Range: Budget hostels to 5-star hotels
- Restaurants, shops, supermarkets
- Early access to Petra (walk to entrance)
- **Best for:** All travelers, especially photographers wanting sunrise

**Inside Petra (Limited)**
- **Petra Guest House Hotel:** Only hotel near entrance (2-min walk)
- Owned by Petra authorities
- Expensive but unbeatable location
- **Best for:** Sunrise photographers, luxury travelers

**Nearby Alternatives:**
- **Little Petra (Siq al-Barid):** 15 min drive, quieter
- **Wadi Rum:** 1.5 hours, combine visits
- **Aqaba:** 2 hours, Red Sea extension

### Budget Options (Wadi Musa)
- **Hostels:** 10-20 JD/night (~$14-28 USD)
- **Budget Hotels:** 25-40 JD/night (~$35-56 USD)
- **Examples:** Petra Moon Hotel, Seven Wonders Hotel

### Mid-Range Options
- **3-4 Star Hotels:** 50-100 JD/night (~$70-140 USD)
- **Examples:** Petra Palace Hotel, My Story Hotel

### Luxury Options
- **5-Star Hotels:** 150-300+ JD/night (~$210-420+ USD)
- **Examples:** Movenpick Resort Petra, Wadi Musa Marriott

### Booking Tips
- Book in advance for spring/autumn (peak season)
- Hotels can arrange airport/bus station transfers
- Many include breakfast
- Ask about Petra ticket assistance

## HOW LONG TO STAY

### One Day (Rushed but Possible)
**Itinerary:**
- 6:00 AM: Enter Petra (be at gate at opening)
- 6:00-7:30 AM: Walk Siq, Treasury (empty!)
- 7:30-9:30 AM: Street of Facades, Theater
- 9:30-11:00 AM: Royal Tombs
- 11:00 AM-12:00 PM: Lunch break
- 12:00-3:00 PM: Hike to Monastery (800 steps)
- 3:00-4:30 PM: Return, explore missed areas
- 4:30-5:00 PM: Exit (winter) or 6:00 PM (summer)

**Pros:** Doable, covers highlights
**Cons:** Exhausting, no time for relaxation, must be very early
**Best for:** Time-limited travelers, return visitors

### Two Days (RECOMMENDED)
**Day 1:**
- Morning: Siq, Treasury (dawn light)
- Late morning: Street of Facades, Theater
- Afternoon: Royal Tombs, High Place of Sacrifice
- Evening: Rest in Wadi Musa

**Day 2:**
- Morning: Hike to Monastery (cooler)
- Midday: Rest at café, enjoy views
- Afternoon: Return via different route, explore details
- Optional: Petra by Night (if scheduled)

**Pros:** Comfortable pace, see everything, time for photography
**Cons:** More expensive (2-day ticket only 5 JD more!)
**Best for:** Most travelers, photographers, hikers

### Three Days (Ideal for Deep Exploration)
**Day 1:** Main trail (Siq, Treasury, central Petra)
**Day 2:** Monastery + High Place of Sacrifice
**Day 3:** Back routes, Little Petra, hiking trails, relaxation

**Pros:** Comprehensive, include Little Petra, hiking trails, no rush
**Cons:** More expensive, requires 3 nights
**Best for:** Hikers, photographers, history enthusiasts

### Four+ Days
- Include Dana to Petra trek (2-3 days)
- Multiple hikes to viewpoints
- Time for rest days
- Explore lesser-known tombs and trails
- **Best for:** Serious hikers, extended Jordan trips

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### What to Bring
- **Water:** 2-3 liters minimum per person (buy at entrance, cheaper)
- **Food:** Snacks, energy bars (prices inside are higher)
- **Sun Protection:** Hat, sunscreen (SPF 50+), sunglasses
- **Footwear:** Sturdy hiking shoes or trail sneakers (NO flip-flops)
- **Clothing:** Light, breathable layers; long sleeves for sun protection
- **Cash:** Jordanian dinars (small bills for tips, vendors)
- **Camera:** Fully charged, extra memory cards
- **ID/Passport:** For ticket verification
- **Jordan Pass:** Printed or on phone
- **First Aid:** Blister plasters, pain relievers
- **Backpack:** Comfortable daypack (20-30L)

### What NOT to Bring
- Excessive valuables
- Heavy luggage (use hotel storage)
- Glass bottles (use reusable plastic)
- Drones (require special permit)

### Inside Petra

**Food & Drink:**
- **Basin Restaurant:** Main cafeteria, mid-range prices, buffet
- **Monastery Café:** Simple drinks, juices, snacks
- **Treasury Area:** Vendors with water, tea, snacks (higher prices)
- **Prices Inside:** 2-3x Wadi Musa prices
- **Tip:** Bring your own snacks and water

**Restrooms:**
- Visitor Centre (before entrance)
- Basin Restaurant area (midway)
- Monastery (at top, basic)
- Limited elsewhere—use when available

**ATMs:**
- Visitor Centre (outside ticket office)
- Wadi Musa town (multiple)
- Limited inside Petra—withdraw beforehand

**Connectivity:**
- Mobile signal: Spotty (Zain/Orange work best)
- WiFi: Visitor Centre, some cafés
- Download offline maps beforehand

### Animal Rides
- **Available:** Horses, donkeys, camels, carriages
- **Routes:** Siq entrance to Treasury, some other areas
- **Cost:** 5-20 JD (negotiate firmly in advance)
- **Ethics:** Animal welfare concerns—many operators mistreat animals
- **Recommendation:** Walk when possible (Siq is only 20-30 min)
- **If Using:** Agree on price BEFORE mounting, confirm currency (JD not USD)

### Guides
- **Official Guides:** Available at Visitor Centre
- **Cost:** 50-70 JD (half-day), 80-100 JD (full-day)
- **Languages:** English, Arabic, others on request
- **Worth It?** Yes for first visit (historical context)
- **Tip:** Hire official guides only (badges, regulated)
- **Alternative:** Audio guides, guidebooks, self-guided

### Safety
- **General:** Very safe for tourists
- **Crime:** Low (petty theft rare)
- **Health:** Heat exhaustion is main risk
- **Terrain:** Uneven, slippery when wet
- **Flash Floods:** Rare but possible (avoid Siq in heavy rain)
- **Emergency:** Tourist police at Visitor Centre
- **Insurance:** Travel insurance recommended

### Accessibility
- **Siq:** Accessible (paved, flat)
- **Treasury:** Accessible from Siq exit
- **Most Sites:** Require stairs/hiking (not wheelchair accessible)
- **Donkey Rides:** Available for those with mobility issues (negotiate)
- **Best for Limited Mobility:** Siq, Treasury, Street of Facades (partial)

## CULTURAL ETIQUETTE

### Dress Code
- **Modest Dress:** Recommended (shoulders, knees covered)
- **Not Enforced:** But respectful dress appreciated
- **Practical:** Light, breathable fabrics that cover skin (sun protection)
- **Women:** No headscarf required

### Photography
- **Allowed:** Yes, throughout Petra
- **Tripods:** Allowed (no permit needed for personal use)
- **Drones:** Require special permit (apply in Amman)
- **Local People:** Ask permission before photographing Bedouin/vendors
- **Commercial:** Requires permit

### Behavior
- **Respect Site:** Don't climb on fragile structures
- **Trash:** Pack out what you pack in
- **Noise:** Keep voices low (sacred site)
- **Vendors:** Polite but firm if not interested
- **Tipping:** 1-2 JD for small services, 5-10 JD for guides

## HEALTH & SAFETY

### Heat Management
- **Start Early:** 6 AM entrance (beat heat and crowds)
- **Hydrate:** 2-3 liters minimum, more in summer
- **Rest:** Take breaks in shade
- **Recognize Symptoms:** Dizziness, nausea, headache = heat exhaustion
- **Treatment:** Shade, water, rest, cool compress

### Physical Requirements
- **Siq:** Easy (flat, 2 km)
- **Treasury:** Easy (from Siq exit)
- **Monastery:** Moderate-hard (800 steps, 45-90 min climb)
- **High Place of Sacrifice:** Moderate (stairs, 30-45 min)
- **Back Routes:** Hard (longer, uneven terrain)

### Medical Facilities
- **Wadi Musa:** Small clinics, pharmacies
- **Serious Issues:** Aqaba or Amman hospitals
- **Insurance:** Ensure coverage for medical evacuation
- **Medications:** Bring your own (limited availability)

### Water Safety
- **Tap Water:** Not recommended for drinking
- **Bottled Water:** Widely available, safe
- **Refill:** Some hotels/cafés offer filtered water
