Episode 066: The Phantom City - Petra, Jordan

The second time you walk through the Siq, the stone remembers you.

In this 20-minute episode, we return to Petra—the ancient Nabatean city carved into rose-red sandstone over 2,000 years ago. This isn't a typical travel episode. It's an exploration of what return teaches us: about memory, about time, about the things we carve into stone hoping they'll outlast us.

The Nabateans were masters of subtraction. They built by removal, trusting the form already existed within the material. Two millennia later, wind and water continue their work. This isn't decay. It's collaboration.

In This Episode:
- Walking the Siq for the second time
- The 800-step climb to the Monastery (Ad-Deir)
- What the Nabateans understood about time
- Why return is depth, not regression
- The promise of the phantom city

Plus: Complete Petra travel guide (2025-2026) with entrance fees, best times to visit, how to get there, accommodation tips, and budget breakdowns.

The phantom city is not a ghost. It is a promise.

## CRISIS MANAGEMENT

### Potential Issues
- **Technical problems:** Have backup audio file, test before launch
- **Negative feedback:** Respond professionally, learn from criticism
- **Low engagement:** Don't panic, evergreen content grows over time
- **Fact-checking concerns:** Have sources documented, correct if wrong

### Response Plan
- Monitor comments/messages daily for first week
- Respond within 24 hours
- Be gracious, professional, authentic
- Correct errors publicly if found
