# Episode 100: The Century Walk
## Travel Reflection Guide — Lessons from 100 Places

### How to Travel with Intention

*By Shelby*

---

## INTRODUCTION

After 100 episodes, 47 countries, 15,000 kilometers of walking, I've learned something:

Travel is not about the places. It's about the attention you bring to them.

This guide is not a list of destinations. It's a framework for traveling differently. For letting travel change you, not just entertain you.

Use it however serves you. Skip what doesn't resonate. Keep what does.

The walk continues — yours and mine.

---

## PART ONE: BEFORE YOU GO

### 1. Ask Why

Before booking anything, ask yourself:

- Why am I going?
- What am I running toward?
- What am I running from?
- What do I hope this place will teach me?

**From the Danakil:** I went because I needed to understand what "extreme" actually means. Not the Instagram version. The actual, physical, your-body-might-fail-you version.

Your why doesn't need to be profound. "I need rest" is valid. "I need adventure" is valid. "I don't know, I just need to leave" is valid.

But know it. Name it. Write it down.

### 2. Research Deeply, Then Let Go

Research enough to be safe. To be respectful. To understand what you're walking into.

Then let go of the itinerary.

**From Petra:** The best moments happened when I abandoned the plan. When I sat in the Siq for an hour, just watching light move on stone. When I followed a Bedouin guide to a tomb nobody visits.

Research is a map. The territory is what matters.

### 3. Pack Light — Physically and Mentally

**Physical:**
- One bag if possible
- Clothes that layer
- Shoes you've already broken in
- Leave space for what you'll acquire

**Mental:**
- Leave expectations at home
- Leave the checklist mentality
- Leave the need to prove anything

**From the Sahara:** I carried 6 liters of water. That was heavy. But the expectations I carried — to have the perfect photo, to feel transformed, to justify the trip — those were heavier.

### 4. Learn Five Phrases

In the local language:
1. Hello
2. Thank you
3. Please
4. Beautiful
5. I'm sorry / Excuse me

**From Kyoto:** A tea ceremony master taught me that effort is a form of respect. Trying — even badly — to speak someone's language says: *I see you. This is your home. I'm a guest.*

## PART TWO: WHILE YOU'RE THERE

### 5. Arrive Before You Arrive

Don't spend the first day checking off landmarks.

Spend it walking. Without destination. Let your body adjust. Let your mind settle.

**From Venice:** I spent day one getting intentionally lost. No map. No goal. Just canals and stone and the sound of water. By day two, the city felt like a friend.

### 6. Wake Up Early

Not for the photo. For the place.

Most tourist sites are empty at 6 AM. The light is better. But more importantly: you'll see the place before it performs for visitors.

**From Machu Picchu:** Sunrise crowds are real. But I arrived at 5:30 AM, sat on a stone, and watched mist clear. For 20 minutes, before the buses came, it was just me and 500 years of history.

### 7. Talk to Locals — But Don't Extract

Ask questions. Listen. Don't treat people like content.

Good questions:
- "What's your favorite place nearby?"
- "What should I know about being here?"
- "What's changed since you were young?"

Bad questions:
- "Where can I get the best photo?"
- "What's the most Instagrammable spot?"
- Anything that treats their home as your backdrop

**From the Afar Guides:** One guide said, "You ask about the heat. I live in the heat. This is normal." He wasn't rude. He was honest. I was the outsider. He was home.

### 8. Spend Money Consciously

Your money is a vote. Vote for:
- Local businesses
- Local guides
- Local accommodations
- Local food

Avoid:
- International chains (unless necessary)
- Exploitative tourism
- Anything that harms the place or people

**From Bhutan:** They measure Gross National Happiness, not GDP. Tourism is limited, expensive, intentional. It funds schools and hospitals. I paid more — and knew it mattered.

### 9. Embrace Discomfort

Comfort is not the goal. Growth is.

The heat of the Danakil. The cold of Antarctica. The altitude of K2. The silence of the Namib.

**From Patagonia:** I watched a glacier calve — ice the size of buildings falling into water. It was uncomfortable. It was grief. But I needed to witness it. Comfort would have been a hotel. Growth was standing there, watching loss.

### 10. Practice Attention

This is the core skill.

**Exercise: The 10-Minute Sit**
- Find a place to sit
- Set a timer for 10 minutes
- Don't take photos
- Don't write
- Just notice

What do you see? Hear? Smell? Feel?

**From Kyoto:** A tea ceremony is 45 minutes of attention. Every movement deliberate. I learned more in those 45 minutes than in days of rushing.

## PART THREE: THE HARD STUFF

### 11. When Things Go Wrong

They will.

Flights miss. Bags lose. Weather turns. Illness strikes.

**From Svalbard:** -15°C. My camera froze. My fingers went numb. The boat was delayed. I was angry. Then I sat with it. The Arctic doesn't care about my plans. That's not cruelty. It's freedom.

When things go wrong:
- Breathe
- Assess safety
- Adjust plans
- Look for the story

The best stories come from the worst days.

### 12. When You're Lonely

Solo travel is lonely. That's not a bug — it's a feature.

Loneliness is the space where you meet yourself.

**From Antarctica:** No phone signal. No escape. Just me, the crew, and 10 million square kilometers of ice. I wrote in my journal. I watched whales. I sat with my own thoughts. Some were ugly. Some were beautiful. All were mine.

When lonely:
- Write
- Walk
- Call someone (if you can)
- Remember: this is temporary
- Ask: what is this loneliness trying to tell me?

### 13. When You Witness Harm

You will.

Coral bleaching. Glaciers retreating. Trash on beaches. Exploitation.

**From the Great Barrier Reef:** Half of it is dead. Not dying — dead. I swam over white bone-coral and cried. In the water. Nobody saw.

When you witness harm:
- Feel it. Don't numb.
- Learn about it
- Share responsibly (not trauma porn)
- Donate if you can
- Change your own behavior
- Vote. Everywhere.

### 14. When You're the Problem

You will be.

You'll take up space. You'll misunderstand. You'll offend.

**From a temple in Kyoto:** I spoke too loudly. A monk looked at me. Not angry. Disappointed. I apologized. I learned.

When you're the problem:
- Apologize. Simply.
- Listen
- Learn
- Do better
- Don't make it about your guilt

## PART FOUR: COMING HOME

### 15. The Return Is Hard

Nobody asks about the silence. The slowness. The way you saw things.

**From the Sahara:** I came home and the noise was physical. Traffic. Notifications. Expectations. I missed the desert's honesty.

The return is part of the journey. Honor it.

### 16. Integrate, Don't Perform

Don't:
- Post 47 photos in one day
- Talk about your trip constantly
- Compare everything to "when I was in..."

Do:
- Let it settle
- Write privately
- Share when it serves others
- Let the change be quiet

**From Iceland:** I stood between tectonic plates. Two continents, touching. I didn't post it for three days. I let it be mine first.

### 17. Keep Practicing

Travel ends. Practice doesn't.

**Attention:** Keep noticing. Your neighborhood has stories.

**Slowness:** Walk without destination. Sit without phone.

**Gratitude:** Say thank you. To baristas. To bus drivers. To the ground.

**From Episode 100:** The walk changed my body. My mind. My spirit. But the walk didn't end. It just became... how I live.

### 18. Leave It Better

You took. Now give.

- Donate to local conservation
- Support guides you worked with
- Share platforms with local voices
- Reduce your footprint
- Vote for policies that protect places

**From every place:** We tried to leave each location cleaner, quieter, more respected than we found it. Not perfect. Trying.

## PART FIVE: THE DEEPER WORK

### 19. Travel Is Not Salvation

You will not find yourself in Machu Picchu.

You will not fix your life in Bali.

You will not become a different human in Bhutan.

**From Episode 001:** I thought the Danakil would transform me. It did — but not by magic. By showing me what I was made of. By asking: can you handle this?

Travel reveals. It doesn't create.

### 20. Privilege Is Real

That you can travel? That's privilege.

That you can choose to leave? That's privilege.

That you can return home? That's privilege.

**From every guide:** The Afar, the Sherpa, the Bedouin, the monks — they're not characters in your story. They're people. With lives. With struggles. With wisdom you don't have.

Acknowledge privilege. Don't apologize for it. Use it well.

### 21. The Best Travel Is Return Travel

Going back is not failure. It's depth.

**From Zhangjiajie:** First time: I saw Avatar mountains. Second time: I saw what tourism does. Third time: I saw myself in both.

Returns are honest. They show you what you missed. What you changed. What changed you.

### 22. Home Is Also a Place

You don't need a plane ticket to travel.

**From NYC (Episode 099):** Eight million people, each with a story. I watched sunrise from Brooklyn Bridge and understood: this is an ecosystem. As complex as a rainforest.

Walk your neighborhood. Talk to neighbors. Notice what you've stopped seeing.

## PART SIX: PRACTICAL TOOLS

### Pre-Trip Checklist

**Research:**
- [ ] Basic history of place
- [ ] Cultural norms / taboos
- [ ] Safety considerations
- [ ] Local phrases (5 minimum)
- [ ] Current events / political situation

**Packing:**
- [ ] One bag (if possible)
- [ ] Broken-in shoes
- [ ] Layers for weather
- [ ] Medications + copies of prescriptions
- [ ] Power adapter
- [ ] Offline maps downloaded
- [ ] Physical copies of important docs

**Mindset:**
- [ ] Written "why" for this trip
- [ ] Expectations released
- [ ] Openness to change
- [ ] Commitment to respect

### During-Trip Practices

**Daily:**
- [ ] 10-minute sit (no phone)
- [ ] One conversation with local
- [ ] One photo (not 100)
- [ ] Journal entry (3 sentences minimum)

**Weekly:**
- [ ] Review: what am I learning?
- [ ] Adjust: what's not working?
- [ ] Rest: one day with no plans

### Post-Trip Integration

**First Week Home:**
- [ ] Unpack immediately
- [ ] Wash clothes
- [ ] Sort photos (don't post yet)
- [ ] Write: what changed?

**First Month:**
- [ ] Share stories when asked (not unsolicited)
- [ ] Keep one travel practice (morning walk, meditation, etc.)
- [ ] Stay in touch with one person you met

**Ongoing:**
- [ ] Support organizations working in places you visited
- [ ] Reduce your footprint
- [ ] Plan return trips to places that called to you

## CLOSING: THE WALK CONTINUES

After 100 episodes, here's what I know:

Travel is not about the places. It's about the attention you bring.

It's not about collecting stamps. It's about letting the world change you.

It's not about escape. It's about arrival.

**The walk continues.**

Yours. Mine. Always.

One step. Then another.
