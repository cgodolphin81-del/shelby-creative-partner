## Getting There

### Step 1: Fly to Peru
**International Airport:** Jorge Chávez International Airport (LIM) - Lima
**Connecting Flight:** Lima → Cusco (CUZ) or Lima → Juliaca (JUL)
**Airlines:** LATAM, Avianca, Sky Airline, JetSMART
**Flight Time:** 1.5 hours (Lima to Cusco)
**Cost:** $80-200 USD one-way (varies by season)

### Step 2: Cusco to Machu Picchu

**Option A: Train (Most Common)**
- **Departure:** Cusco (Poroy Station) or Ollantaytambo (Sacred Valley)
- **Arrival:** Aguas Calientes (Machu Picchu Pueblo)
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours from Cusco, 1.5 hours from Ollantaytambo
- **Train Companies:**
  - PeruRail (Expedition, Vistadome, Hiram Bingham)
  - Inca Rail (Voyager, 360°, First Class)
- **Cost:** $60-500+ USD per person (round-trip, varies by class)
- **Booking:** Book weeks/months in advance, especially May-September

**Option B: Inca Trail Trek (4 Days)**
- **Starting Point:** Km 82 (Piscacucho)
- **Duration:** 4 days, 3 nights
- **Distance:** 26 miles (42 km)
- **Permits:** Required, limited to 500/day (including porters)
- **Cost:** $600-1,200 USD (includes guide, porters, food, permits)
- **Booking:** 6-12 months in advance (permits sell out)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to challenging
- **Best For:** Adventurers, photographers, bucket-list seekers

**Option C: Alternative Treks**
- **Salkantay Trek:** 5 days, more challenging, stunning scenery
- **Lares Trek:** 4 days, cultural focus, village visits
- **Inca Jungle Trek:** 4 days, includes biking and ziplining
- **Cost:** $400-800 USD

**Option D: Bus + Hike (Budget)**
- Cusco → Santa Maria → Santa Teresa → Hydroelectric → Walk to Aguas Calientes
- **Duration:** 6-8 hours total
- **Cost:** $30-50 USD
- **Best For:** Budget travelers, backpackers
- **Note:** Less comfortable, more time-consuming

### Step 3: Aguas Calientes to Machu Picchu

**Option A: Bus**
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Frequency:** Every 10-15 minutes, starting 5:30 AM
- **Cost:** $12 USD one-way, $24 USD round-trip
- **Tickets:** Purchase at bus station in Aguas Calientes

**Option B: Hike**
- **Duration:** 1.5-2 hours (steep uphill)
- **Distance:** 8 km (5 miles)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Best For:** Budget travelers, hikers, early birds (catch sunrise)
- **Note:** Starts at bridge in Aguas Calientes, follows bus road

## Entry Permits & Tickets

### Machu Picchu Entry Tickets
**Important:** Tickets are LIMITED and TIMED. Book in advance!

**Ticket Types:**
1. **Machu Picchu Only** - $47 USD (adults), $24 USD (students/children)
   - Access to citadel only
   - Two entry times: 6:00 AM - 12:00 PM or 12:00 PM - 5:30 PM

2. **Machu Picchu + Huayna Picchu** - $64 USD
   - Includes citadel + Huayna Picchu climb
   - Limited to 400 people/day
   - Two entry groups: 7-8 AM or 10-11 AM

3. **Machu Picchu + Mountain** - $64 USD
   - Includes citadel + Machu Picchu Mountain (higher, less crowded than Huayna)
   - Limited to 800 people/day

4. **Machu Picchu + Museum** - $57 USD
   - Includes citadel + site museum in Aguas Calientes

**Where to Buy:**
- **Official Website:** www.machupicchu.gob.pe (best prices, direct)
- **In Person:** Ministerio de Cultura office in Cusco or Aguas Calientes
- **Through Tour Operator:** Convenient but adds markup

**Booking Tips:**
- Book 2-3 months in advance for high season (May-September)
- Book 1 month in advance for low season (November-March)
- Huayna Picchu permits sell out fastest—book 4-6 months ahead
- Bring passport for entry (must match ticket name)

### Inca Trail Permits
- **Permits:** 500 per day (200 tourists, 300 staff)
- **Cost:** Included in tour package ($600-1,200 USD)
- **Booking:** 6-12 months in advance (sells out for May-September)
- **Closed:** February (maintenance)
- **Official Booking:** Through licensed tour operators only

## Altitude & Health

### Altitude Information
- **Cusco:** 3,400m (11,152 ft)
- **Sacred Valley:** 2,800m (9,200 ft) - *lower, better for acclimatization*
- **Machu Picchu:** 2,430m (7,970 ft) - *lower than Cusco*
- **Huayna Picchu:** 2,720m (8,920 ft)
- **Inca Trail (highest point):** 4,215m (13,828 ft) - Dead Woman's Pass

### Altitude Sickness (Soroche)
**Symptoms:**
- Headache
- Nausea
- Dizziness
- Shortness of breath
- Fatigue
- Loss of appetite

**Prevention:**
1. **Acclimatize:** Spend 2-3 days in Cusco or Sacred Valley before trekking
2. **Hydrate:** Drink 3-4 liters of water daily
3. **Avoid alcohol:** First 48 hours at altitude
4. **Eat light:** Carbohydrates are easier to digest
5. **Coca tea:** Traditional remedy (legal in Peru)
6. **Medication:** Acetazolamide (Diamox) - consult doctor

**Treatment:**
- Rest
- Descend if symptoms worsen
- Oxygen (available in Cusco clinics)
- Seek medical attention if severe

### Who Should Avoid High Altitude?
- Pregnant women
- People with heart or lung conditions
- Severe anemia
- Recent surgery
- Consult your doctor before traveling

### Vaccinations & Health
**Recommended:**
- Hepatitis A
- Typhoid
- Yellow Fever (if visiting Amazon)
- Routine vaccines (MMR, tetanus, etc.)

**Malaria:**
- Not a risk in Cusco/Machu Picchu
- Risk in Amazon regions only

**Water:**
- Drink bottled or filtered water
- Avoid ice in drinks
- Use water bottle with filter (Grayl, LifeStraw)

## Best Times to Visit

### Dry Season (May - September) ⭐ BEST
**Pros:**
- Minimal rain
- Clear skies, best views
- Ideal for hiking and photography
- Warm days (15-20°C / 59-68°F)

**Cons:**
- Peak tourist season
- Crowded (especially June-August)
- Higher prices
- Permits sell out fastest

**Crowd Level:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)

### Shoulder Season (April, October)
**Pros:**
- Fewer crowds
- Good weather
- Lower prices
- Green landscapes

**Cons:**
- Some rain possible
- Unpredictable weather

**Crowd Level:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Moderate)

### Wet Season (November - March)
**Pros:**
- Fewest tourists
- Lowest prices
- Lush, green scenery
- Good for solitude

**Cons:**
- Frequent rain (especially Jan-Feb)
- Cloudy views
- Inca Trail closed in February
- Mud and slippery trails

**Crowd Level:** ⭐⭐ (Low)

### Special Events
- **Inti Raymi (June 24):** Festival of the Sun in Cusco - amazing but VERY crowded
- **Peru Independence (July 28-29):** National holidays, busy domestically
- **Holy Week (March/April):** Religious processions, cultural experience

### Best for Photography
- **Sunrise:** April-May (clear mornings)
- **Sunset:** September-October (dramatic clouds)
- **Green landscapes:** January-March (lush but rainy)
- **Clear views:** June-August (dry but crowded)

## Important Rules & Regulations

### At Machu Picchu
- **No large backpacks:** Maximum 40x35x20 cm (16x14x8 in)
- **No food:** Eat before entering or in designated areas
- **No tripods:** Professional photography requires permit
- **No touching:** Do not touch the ruins or lean on walls
- **Stay on paths:** Do not climb on structures
- **No drones:** Strictly prohibited
- **Use restrooms before entry:** No bathrooms inside citadel
- **Guides required:** As of 2024, all visitors must have a guide (can be shared group)
- **Time limits:** 4 hours maximum per entry ticket
- **No loud noise:** Respect the sacred nature of the site

### Huayna Picchu Climb
- **Age restriction:** Minimum 12 years old
- **Fitness level:** Moderate to challenging
- **No large bags:** Small daypack only
- **One-way trail:** Different paths up and down
- **Time limit:** 45 minutes at summit
- **Weather dependent:** May close in heavy rain

### Environmental Responsibility
- **Pack it in, pack it out:** No trash cans on trails
- **Stay on marked paths:** Protect vegetation
- **Do not feed wildlife:** Llamas, birds, etc.
- **Respect local culture:** Ask before photographing people
- **Support local businesses:** Buy from Peruvian vendors
