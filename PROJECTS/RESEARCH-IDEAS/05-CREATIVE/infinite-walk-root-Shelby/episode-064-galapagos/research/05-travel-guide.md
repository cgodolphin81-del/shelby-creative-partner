# The Forgotten Archipelago: Galapagos Travel Guide
## Practical Information for Visiting the Galapagos Islands

---

## GETTING THERE

### From International → Ecuador

**Main Gateway:** Quito (UIO) or Guayaquil (GYE), Ecuador

**Flight Options:**
- **From North America:** Direct flights to Quito from Miami, Houston, Atlanta, New York, Los Angeles
- **From Europe:** Connections through Madrid (Iberia), Amsterdam (KLM), Paris (Air France)
- **From Latin America:** Direct from Panama City, Bogotá, Lima, San José

**Recommended:** Arrive in Ecuador at least 1-2 days before your Galapagos flight. Missed connections are common, and Galapagos flights fill up quickly.

---

### From Ecuador → Galapagos

**Airlines:**
- **LATAM Airlines** - Most flights, reliable
- **Avianca** - Good alternative
- **EQUAIR** - New Ecuadorian carrier (budget option)

**Departure Cities:**
- **Quito (UIO)** - 2.5 hours to Galapagos (stops in Guayaquil)
- **Guayaquil (GYE)** - 1.5 hours direct (more convenient)

**Destination Airports:**
- **Baltra (GPS)** - Most common, near Santa Cruz Island
- **San Cristóbal (SCY)** - Eastern islands, fewer flights
- **Isabela (IBB)** - Limited flights, check schedules

**Flight Cost:** $450-650 USD round-trip (varies by season)

**Important:** Flights sell out weeks/months in advance. Book early!

## REQUIRED DOCUMENTS & PERMITS

### 1. Transit Control Card (TCT)
- **Cost:** $20 USD per person
- **Where:** Airport counter in Quito/Guayaquil BEFORE check-in
- **What:** Migration control document (keep it—you need it to leave)
- **Validity:** Single entry

### 2. Galapagos National Park Entry Fee
- **Cost:** 
  - Adults (non-Andean): $100 USD
  - Children under 12: $50 USD
  - Ecuadorian/Andean nationals: Lower rates
- **Where:** Paid at Galapagos airport upon arrival
- **Payment:** Cash only (USD) - ATM unreliable
- **Validity:** Single entry, multiple islands

### 3. Passport
- Must be valid for 6+ months beyond travel dates
- Ecuador does not require visa for most tourists (90 days)

### 4. Travel Insurance
- **Strongly recommended** - Medical evacuation is expensive
- Must cover adventure activities (snorkeling, hiking)

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### HOT/WET SEASON (December - May)

**Temperature:**
- Air: 25-30°C (77-86°F)
- Water: 23-26°C (73-79°F)

**Pros:**
- Warm water for snorkeling (no wetsuit needed)
- Calm seas (less seasickness)
- Land wildlife breeding season
- Sea turtle nesting (December-April)
- Lush green vegetation
- Good for photography (clear skies)

**Cons:**
- More rain (usually short afternoon showers)
- More crowded (peak season: Dec-Jan, Easter)
- Higher prices
- Marine life less active

**Best For:** 
- First-time visitors
- Families with children
- Snorkelers who prefer warm water
- Photographers wanting clear skies

---

### COOL/DRY SEASON (June - November)

**Temperature:**
- Air: 20-25°C (68-77°F)
- Water: 18-22°C (64-72°F)

**Pros:**
- Active marine life (nutrient-rich waters)
- Seabird nesting season
- Fewer tourists (except July-August)
- Lower prices (except summer)
- Better for diving
- Whale sharks (June-November, peak Sept-Oct)

**Cons:**
- Cooler water (wetsuit required for snorkeling)
- Rougher seas (more seasickness)
- Overcast skies common (garúa mist)
- Some species less visible

**Best For:**
- Serious wildlife enthusiasts
- Divers
- Experienced travelers
- Budget-conscious (shoulder months)

---

### MONTH-BY-MONTH BREAKDOWN

**January-February:** Peak season, warm, calm, expensive, crowded
**March-April:** Excellent, sea turtles nesting, Easter busy
**May:** Great shoulder month, good weather, fewer crowds
**June:** Marine life peaks, whale sharks arrive, cooler
**July-August:** Busy (summer holidays), good wildlife, rough seas
**September-October:** Best for diving, whale sharks, fewer tourists
**November:** Shoulder month, variable weather, good deals
**December:** Holiday rush begins, warm, calm

**Sweet Spot:** May or September-October (good weather, fewer crowds, decent prices)

## HOW TO VISIT: OPTIONS & COSTS

### OPTION 1: CRUISE (Most Popular)

**What You Get:**
- Accommodation on boat
- All meals
- Guided excursions (2 per day)
- Transportation between islands
- Snorkeling equipment

**Boat Categories:**

**Tourist Class (Budget)**
- Capacity: 16-20 passengers
- Cabin: Basic, often shared bathrooms
- Cost: $1,200-1,800 USD for 4 days/3 nights
- Good for: Budget travelers, social atmosphere

**First Class (Mid-Range)**
- Capacity: 16 passengers
- Cabin: Private with bathroom
- Cost: $2,000-3,500 USD for 4 days/3 nights
- Good for: Comfort seekers, couples

**Luxury Class**
- Capacity: 12-16 passengers
- Cabin: Spacious, premium amenities
- Cost: $4,000-7,000+ USD for 4 days/3 nights
- Good for: Special occasions, maximum comfort

**Expedition Class (Top Tier)**
- Capacity: 12-100 passengers (larger vessels)
- Features: Naturalist guides, kayaks, panga rides
- Cost: $5,000-10,000+ USD
- Good for: Serious wildlife enthusiasts, photographers

**Duration Options:**
- 4 days/3 nights (minimum, rushed)
- 5 days/4 nights (good balance)
- 8 days/7 nights (comprehensive, recommended)
- 11+ days (complete archipelago)

**Pros:**
- Access to remote islands
- Expert naturalist guides
- All-inclusive convenience
- Wake up in new location daily
- Better wildlife viewing

**Cons:**
- Most expensive option
- Confined space
- Seasickness risk
- Fixed itinerary

---

### OPTION 2: LAND-BASED (Island Hopping)

**What You Get:**
- Stay in hotels on populated islands
- Day tours to nearby sites
- More flexibility
- Support local communities

**Main Islands for Land-Based:**

**Santa Cruz (Puerto Ayora)**
- Population: ~15,000
- Hotels: 100+ options
- Attractions: Darwin Station, Tortoise Highlands, nearby day tours
- Best for: First-time visitors, most amenities

**San Cristóbal (Puerto Baquerizo Moreno)**
- Population: ~7,000
- Hotels: 40+ options
- Attractions: Interpretation Center, nearby beaches, frigatebirds
- Best for: Quieter experience, good diving

**Isabela (Puerto Villamil)**
- Population: ~2,500
- Hotels: 30+ options
- Attractions: Giant tortoises (wild), penguins, less developed
- Best for: Authentic feel, budget-friendly, wildlife

**Floreana** - Very limited accommodation (rustic)
**Santiago** - No tourist accommodation

**Cost Breakdown (per person, 5 days):**
- Hotels: $50-200/night = $250-1,000
- Meals: $30-60/day = $150-300
- Day tours: $150-250 per tour (3-4 tours) = $450-1,000
- Local transport: $50-100
- **Total: $900-2,400 USD**

**Pros:**
- More affordable (generally)
- More flexibility
- Support local economy
- Less seasickness
- More cultural interaction

**Cons:**
- Limited island access (only nearby sites)
- Daily packing/unpacking
- Tour quality varies
- Some sites only accessible by cruise

---

### OPTION 3: COMBINED APPROACH

**Strategy:**
- 3-4 days land-based (Santa Cruz)
- 4-5 days cruise (remote islands)

**Cost:** $2,500-4,500 USD for 7-9 days

**Best For:** Travelers wanting both flexibility and remote access

## ECO-TOURISM & RESPONSIBLE TRAVEL

### Galapagos National Park Rules (STRICTLY ENFORCED)

1. **Stay on marked trails** - No wandering off
2. **Maintain 2-meter (6-foot) distance** from all wildlife
3. **Never touch animals** - No exceptions
4. **No feeding wildlife** - Ever
5. **No flash photography** - Disturbs animals
6. **No drones** - Completely prohibited without special permit
7. **No food on visitor sites** - Eat on boat/hotel only
8. **No souvenirs from nature** - No shells, rocks, plants
9. **Biosecurity checks** - All bags inspected for organic material
10. **Follow guide instructions** - Always

### Biosecurity Measures

**Upon Arrival:**
- All luggage scanned for organic material
- Shoes inspected and sometimes sprayed
- Declare any food, plants, animal products

**Between Islands:**
- Similar checks when changing boats/islands
- Prevents invasive species spread

**What NOT to Bring:**
- Fresh fruits/vegetables
- Seeds, plants, flowers
- Animal products
- Honey, dairy
- Unprocessed wood

### Choosing Responsible Operators

**Look For:**
- Galapagos National Park Directorate certification
- Smart Voyager certification (Rainforest Alliance)
- Local Ecuadorian ownership
- Fair wages for crew and guides
- Sustainability practices (water, waste, energy)

**Ask:**
- Guide certifications (Level III Naturalist Guide is highest)
- Group size (smaller = less impact)
- Waste management practices
- Community support initiatives

**Red Flags:**
- Prices significantly below market (cutting corners)
- Vague answers about sustainability
- No certification documentation
- Large groups (20+ passengers on day tours)

## HEALTH & SAFETY

### Vaccinations

**Required:**
- None (for most travelers)

**Recommended:**
- Routine vaccines (MMR, tetanus, etc.)
- Hepatitis A
- Typhoid
- Yellow fever (if coming from endemic country)

**Consult:** Travel medicine clinic 4-6 weeks before departure

### Health Considerations

**Sun Exposure:**
- Equatorial sun is intense
- UV index regularly 11+ (extreme)
- Sunburn possible in 15 minutes
- Use reef-safe sunscreen, reapply often

**Dehydration:**
- Drink 3-4 liters water daily
- Avoid excessive alcohol
- Electrolyte supplements helpful

**Seasickness:**
- Common on boats, even in calm seas
- Start medication night BEFORE boarding
- Scopolamine patches (prescription) most effective
- Ginger, acupressure bands (mild cases)

**Medical Facilities:**
- Santa Cruz: Small hospital, basic care
- San Cristóbal: Clinic
- Serious issues: Evacuation to mainland
- **Travel insurance with evacuation coverage is essential**

### Safety

**Crime:** Very low in Galapagos
- Petty theft possible in ports
- Use hotel safes
- Don't leave valuables on boats/beaches

**Wildlife:** Generally safe
- Sea lions can be aggressive (give space)
- Sharks not dangerous to humans
- No venomous snakes
- Marine iguanas harmless

**Ocean:**
- Strong currents possible
- Always snorkel with buddy
- Follow guide instructions
- Wear wetsuit for warmth AND jellyfish protection

## COMMUNICATION & MONEY

### Currency

- **US Dollar** is official currency of Ecuador
- Bring small bills ($1, $5, $10, $20)
- $50 and $100 bills sometimes rejected (counterfeit concerns)
- Credit cards accepted in hotels, restaurants, larger shops
- ATMs available in Puerto Ayora, Puerto Baquerizo, Puerto Villamil
- **Bring cash for park fees, tips, small purchases**

### Connectivity

**Cell Service:**
- Claro and Movistar have coverage on populated islands
- Spotty on remote islands, boats
- International roaming expensive
- Consider local SIM card (purchase in Quito/Guayaquil)

**WiFi:**
- Available in most hotels, restaurants
- Speed: Slow to moderate (don't expect to stream)
- Often unavailable on boats
- Embrace the digital detox!

### Time Zone

- **Galapagos Time:** GMT-6 (one hour behind mainland Ecuador)
- Does not observe daylight saving time
