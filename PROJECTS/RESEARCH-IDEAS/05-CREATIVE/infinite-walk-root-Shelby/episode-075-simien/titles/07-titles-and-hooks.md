*Simple. Evocative. Accurate.*

The Simien Mountains are literally called "The Roof of Africa" throughout Ethiopia and travel literature. This title:
- ✅ Immediately identifies the location (Africa)
- ✅ Conveys altitude and grandeur

- ✅ Works across all platforms
- ✅ Matches the poetic essay's central metaphor

## HOOKS BY PLATFORM

### PODCAST INTRO HOOKS (First 30 seconds)

**Hook 1: The Drop**
"There is a place where the ground beneath your feet drops away—two thousand meters, straight down—into valleys so deep you can watch clouds form below your boots. They call it the Roof of Africa..."

**Hook 2: The Gelada**
"I sat on a mountain at 3,100 meters, and a wild baboon touched my boot. It wasn't afraid. It wasn't curious. It was... indifferent. In that moment, I understood something about belonging."

**Hook 3: The Number**
"Five hundred. That's how many Walia ibex exist on this entire planet. All of them. Right here. In the Simien Mountains. And I was watching one from 300 meters below, perched on a cliff that looked vertical."

**Hook 4: The Question**
"What does it feel like to stand at the edge of the world? Not metaphorically. Literally. Where the path ends, there's no railing, and two thousand meters of empty air waits below?"

**Hook 5: The Home**
"My guide looked at the terraced fields clinging to the mountainside and said: 'My grandfather's grandfather farmed this land. His children will too.' No question. No uncertainty. This is not hardship. This is home."

**Hook 6: The Scale**
"The Simien Mountains don't need you. They've existed for millions of years. Will exist for millions more. Your visit is a blip. Your photographs are temporary. Your awe is fleeting. But for a few days, you're allowed to walk among them..."
