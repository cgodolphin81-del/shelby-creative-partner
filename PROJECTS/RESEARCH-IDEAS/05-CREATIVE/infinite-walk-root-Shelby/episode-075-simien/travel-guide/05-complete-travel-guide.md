## GETTING THERE

### International Flights
**Destination:** Addis Ababa Bole International Airport (ADD)
**Airlines:** Ethiopian Airlines (most direct options), Lufthansa, Emirates, Qatar, Turkish Airlines
**Typical Routes:**
- Europe → Addis: 6-8 hours
- North America → Addis: 14-18 hours (usually via Europe)
- Asia → Addis: 6-10 hours

**Visa:** Available on arrival for most nationalities (~$50 USD) or e-visa online before travel

### Domestic Travel to Gondar

**Option 1: Flight (RECOMMENDED)**
- **Route:** Addis Ababa (ADD) → Gondar (GDQ)
- **Airline:** Ethiopian Airlines
- **Frequency:** 4-6 flights daily
- **Duration:** 1 hour 15 minutes
- **Cost:** $80-150 USD one-way (discounted if booked with international ticket)
- **Tip:** Book domestic flights with your international ticket for better rates

**Option 2: Public Bus (BUDGET/ADVENTUROUS)**
- **Departure:** Meskel Square, Addis Ababa
- **Time:** 4-5 AM departure
- **Duration:** 10-12 hours
- **Cost:** ~$15-25 USD
- **Conditions:** Rough roads, crowded, basic amenities
- **Recommendation:** Only for experienced travelers with time flexibility

**Option 3: Private Vehicle from Bahir Dar**
- **If visiting Lake Tana first**
- **Duration:** ~3 hours
- **Cost:** $100-200 USD depending on vehicle

### Gondar to Simien Mountains

**Gondar → Debark (Park Headquarters)**
- **Distance:** 100 km
- **Duration:** 2-2.5 hours
- **Road Condition:** Mixed paved and unpaved
- **Transport:** 4x4 vehicle recommended
- **Cost:** $50-100 USD private hire, or join tour

**Debark → Park Entrance (Buyit Ras)**
- **Distance:** 14 km
- **Duration:** 20-30 minutes
- **Process:** Stop here to finalize permits and meet mandatory guide/scout

**Park Entrance → First Campsite (Sankaber)**
- **Distance:** 20-30 km
- **Duration:** 1-2 hours (slow, rough road)
- **Transport:** 4x4 required

## PERMITS & FEES

### Required Permits

**All visitors MUST have:**
1. Park entrance permit
2. Mandatory park scout/guide
3. Camping permit (if overnighting)

### Fee Structure (Foreign Tourists)

**Entrance Fee:**
- 90 Birr per person per 24 hours (~$1.60 USD at official rate)
- Children under 12: 10 Birr

**Scout Fee (MANDATORY):**
- 150 Birr per day (~$2.70 USD)
- Cannot trek without scout

**Guide Fee (RECOMMENDED):**
- 300-400 Birr per day (~$5.35-7.15 USD)
- More experienced guides cost more

**Camping Fee:**
- 40-60 Birr per tent per 24 hours
- Varies by group size

**Vehicle Fee:**
- Up to 12 seats: 20 Birr
- Over 12 seats: 40 Birr

### Total Daily Base Cost
**Budget trekker:** ~$15-25 USD per day (permits + basic guide)
**Mid-range:** ~$50-100 USD per day (organized tour)
**Luxury:** ~$200-400+ USD per day (lodge + private guide)

### Where to Pay
- **Debark Park Office:** All permits and fees
- **Payment:** Cash (Ethiopian Birr) preferred
- **ATM:** Available in Gondar, NOT in Debark or park
- **Tip:** Withdraw enough Birr in Gondar or Addis before heading to park

## WHEN TO VISIT

### BEST TIME: Dry Season (October - May)

**Peak Season: October - March**
- **Weather:** Clear skies, minimal rain
- **Daytime temps:** 11-18°C (52-64°F)
- **Nighttime temps:** -2 to -10°C (28-14°F)
- **Visibility:** Excellent
- **Wildlife:** Easy to spot
- **Crowds:** Moderate to high
- **Costs:** Higher

**Shoulder Season: April - May**
- **Weather:** Warming up, occasional showers
- **Temps:** Slightly warmer
- **Visibility:** Good
- **Crowds:** Lower
- **Costs:** Moderate
- **Note:** End of dry season, some water sources may be low

### AVOID: Rainy Season (June - September)

**Why Not to Go:**
- Heavy daily rainfall
- Trails become slippery and dangerous
- Some campsites close
- Routes can be blocked by landslides
- Wildlife harder to spot
- Clouds obscure views
- Leeches in wet areas

**If You Must Go:**
- Expect limited access
- Bring serious rain gear
- Flexible itinerary essential
- Some operators won't run trips

## TREKKING ROUTES

### Classic Trek: Sankaber to Ras Dashen (7-8 Days)

**Day 1:** Gondar → Michibiny → Sankaber (3,100m)
- Drive from Gondar
- Short acclimatization hike
- Sleep: Sankaber campsite
- Wildlife: Geladas common

**Day 2:** Sankaber → Gich
- Trek: 4-5 hours
- Pass Jinbar Falls
- Sleep: Gich campsite
- Elevation gain: +300m

**Day 3:** Gich → Imet Gogo (day hike) → Gich
- Day hike to viewpoint (3,926m)
- 5-6 hours round trip
- Sleep: Gich campsite
- Views: Panoramic

**Day 4:** Gich → Chennek
- Trek: 4-5 hours
- Wildlife hotspot
- Sleep: Chennek campsite
- Wildlife: Walia ibex likely

**Day 5:** Chennek → Ambiko
- Trek: 4-5 hours
- Base for Bwahit summit
- Sleep: Ambiko campsite
- Elevation: ~3,800m

**Day 6:** Ambiko → Bwahit Peak (4,430m) → Return
- Summit attempt
- Trek: 6-7 hours
- Sleep: Ambiko or Chennek
- Views: Spectacular

**Day 7:** Chennek → Ras Dashen approach
- Trek: 5-6 hours
- Sleep: High camp (~4,200m)
- Preparation for summit

**Day 8:** Ras Dashen Summit (4,550m) → Descent → Gondar
- Early summit attempt
- Long descent
- Drive back to Gondar
- Total: 8-10 hours

**Difficulty:** Challenging
**Fitness Required:** Good to excellent
**Altitude:** Significant (up to 4,550m)
**Best For:** Experienced trekkers, summit seekers

## WHAT TO PACK

### Clothing (Layering is Essential)

**Base Layers:**
- Moisture-wicking tops (2-3)
- Thermal underwear (top and bottom)
- Hiking socks (4-5 pairs, wool blend)

**Mid Layers:**
- Fleece jacket or pullover
- Light down jacket
- Long-sleeve hiking shirts (2)

**Outer Layers:**
- Waterproof/windproof shell jacket
- Waterproof pants (optional, dry season)
- Warm hat (beanie)
- Sun hat with brim
- Gloves (lightweight)

**Footwear:**
- Well-broken-in hiking boots (ankle support)
- Camp shoes/sandals (for evenings)
- Gaiters (optional, for dust/mud)

### Gear

**Essential:**
- Backpack (40-60L for multi-day, 20-30L for day hikes)
- Sleeping bag (rated to -10°C / 14°F)
- Sleeping pad (if not provided)
- Trekking poles (highly recommended)
- Headlamp + extra batteries
- Water bottles/bladder (3L capacity minimum)
- Water purification (tablets or filter)

**Recommended:**
- Camera + extra batteries (cold drains batteries)
- Binoculars (for wildlife)
- Sunglasses (UV protection essential)
- Sunscreen (SPF 50+, high altitude)
- Lip balm with SPF
- First aid kit (blister care, pain relievers, altitude meds)
- Toiletries (biodegradable soap)
- Quick-dry towel
- Power bank (charging limited)

**Optional:**
- Camera tripod
- Book/entertainment for evenings
- Snacks from home (familiar comfort food)
- Cash for tips (small bills)

### What NOT to Bring
- Cotton clothing (stays wet, cold)
- New, unbroken boots
- Excessive luggage (porters have limits)
- Valuables (keep in Gondar/Addis)
- Plastic bags (Ethiopia has restrictions)

## HEALTH & SAFETY

### Altitude Sickness

**Risk:** Moderate to High (trekking above 3,000m, summit at 4,550m)

**Prevention:**
- Ascend gradually (built into most itineraries)
- Stay hydrated (3-4L water daily)
- Avoid alcohol
- Eat well (carbohydrates)
- Consider acetazolamide (Diamox) - consult doctor

**Symptoms (Mild):**
- Headache
- Nausea
- Dizziness
- Fatigue
- Shortness of breath

**Symptoms (Severe - DESCEND IMMEDIATELY):**
- Confusion
- Difficulty walking (ataxia)
- Severe headache unresponsive to medication
- Coughing up pink/frothy sputum
- Loss of consciousness

**Treatment:**
- Mild: Rest, hydrate, don't ascend further
- Severe: DESCEND immediately, seek medical help

### Medical Preparations

**Vaccinations (Consult Travel Clinic 6-8 Weeks Before):**
- Yellow Fever (required if coming from endemic country)
- Hepatitis A & B
- Typhoid
- Tetanus (booster if needed)
- Meningitis (consider)
- Rabies (consider for remote travel)

**Malaria:**
- Simien Mountains: Low risk (high altitude)
- Addis Ababa: No risk
- Other Ethiopia regions: Variable
- Consult doctor about prophylaxis

**Medical Kit:**
- Blister treatment (moleskin, bandages)
- Pain relievers (ibuprofen, acetaminophen)
- Altitude medication (acetazolamide)
- Anti-diarrheal (loperamide)
- Antibiotics (broad-spectrum, prescription)
- Antihistamines
- Rehydration salts
- Personal medications (bring extra)

### Safety Considerations

**General Safety:**
- Ethiopia is generally safe for tourists
- Simien Mountains: Very safe, well-trafficked by trekkers
- Always trek with guide/scout (mandatory anyway)
- Keep valuables secure
- Register with embassy if desired

**Wildlife:**
- Geladas: Generally not aggressive, give space
- Walia ibex: Harmless, keep distance
- No large predators commonly encountered
- Respect wildlife, don't feed

**Weather:**
- Can change rapidly at altitude
- Afternoon storms possible (even in dry season)
- Hypothermia risk at night (cold temperatures)
- Sun exposure intense (high altitude)

**Emergency Contacts:**
- **Park Office (Debark):** +251-58-771-0021
- **Ethiopian Emergency:** 911
- **Tour Operator:** Keep contact info handy
- **Embassy:** Register before travel

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Amhara Culture

**People:** Predominantly Amhara ethnic group
**Language:** Amharic (official language)
**Religion:** Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity (majority)
**Hospitality:** Known for warmth and generosity

### Do's

✅ **Greet people properly**
- Handshake with slight bow
- "Selam" (hello) goes a long way
- Take time for greetings

✅ **Ask before photographing people**
- Especially in villages
- Offer to show them the photo
- Some may expect small payment

✅ **Dress modestly**
- Especially in villages and churches
- Cover shoulders and knees
- Remove shoes in churches

✅ **Respect religious sites**
- Churches are active places of worship
- Speak quietly
- Follow local customs

✅ **Accept hospitality**
- Coffee ceremonies are important
- Refusing can be rude
- Try local food when offered

✅ **Support local economy**
- Hire local guides
- Buy from local shops
- Tip appropriately

### Don'ts

❌ **Point with finger**
- Use open hand instead
- Pointing considered rude

❌ **Show public affection**
- Conservative culture
- Keep physical contact minimal

❌ **Photograph military/police**
- Strictly prohibited
- Can cause serious problems

❌ **Discuss politics aggressively**
- Sensitive topics
- Listen more than speak

❌ **Waste food**
- Ethiopia faces food insecurity
- Take only what you'll eat

❌ **Give money to begging children**
- Encourages dependency
- Support schools/organizations instead

### Tipping Guidelines

**Guide:** $10-20 USD per day (more for exceptional)
**Scout:** $5-10 USD per day
**Cook:** $5-10 USD per day
**Porter/Muleteer:** $3-5 USD per day
**Lodge Staff:** $1-5 USD as appropriate

**Note:** Tips are often shared among team. Can give to lead guide to distribute.

## PHOTOGRAPHY TIPS

### Best Subjects

**Wildlife:**
- Gelada baboons (abundant, approachable)
- Walia ibex (rare, dramatic locations)
- Lammergeyer vultures (in flight)
- Various bird species (180+ recorded)

**Landscape:**
- Escarpment views (especially sunrise/sunset)
- Ras Dashen summit
- Imet Gogo viewpoint
- Jinbar Falls
- Afro-alpine vegetation

**Culture:**
- Highland villages
- Terraced farming
- Local people (ask permission)
- Churches and religious sites

### Technical Tips

**Camera Gear:**
- DSLR or mirrorless preferred
- Wide angle (16-35mm) for landscapes
- Telephoto (70-200mm or longer) for wildlife
- Tripod for low light/astrophotography
- Extra batteries (cold drains them)
- Large memory cards (limited charging)

**Settings:**
- Landscapes: f/8-f/11, low ISO, tripod for sharpness
- Wildlife: Fast shutter (1/1000+), continuous focus
- Portraits: f/2.8-f/4 for subject isolation
- Sunrise/sunset: Bracket exposures

**Light:**
- Golden hour: Sunrise and sunset (spectacular at altitude)
- Midday: Harsh, seek shade or shoot details
- Blue hour: Excellent for lodge/camp scenes

### Ethics

- **Wildlife:** Maintain distance, don't feed, no flash
- **People:** Always ask permission, respect refusals
- **Culture:** Be sensitive in religious sites
- **Environment:** Leave no trace, pack out all trash
