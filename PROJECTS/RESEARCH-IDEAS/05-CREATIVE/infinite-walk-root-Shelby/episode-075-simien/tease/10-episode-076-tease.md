### Long Tease (60 seconds - Email/Newsletter)
**SHELBY:**
"Episode 075 took you to the edge of the world—the Simien Mountains, the Roof of Africa, where gelada baboons graze like cattle and Walia ibex cling to impossible cliffs.

But Ethiopia's wonders don't end at the escarpment.

Next week, we descend from the highlands to a small town in the Amhara Region. A place that holds one of the greatest architectural miracles of the medieval world.

In the 12th century, King Lalibela ordered something extraordinary: eleven churches, carved from single blocks of volcanic tuff. Not built—*carved*. Workers chiseled downward from the surface, creating structures that are both buildings and caves, both inside and outside, both earth and art.

The most famous is Bet Giyorgis—the Church of Saint George. Cross-shaped. Perfect. Carved from a single massive block of stone, surrounded by a trench that separates it from the earth around it.

Today, these churches are still active. Pilgrims come from across Ethiopia and beyond. They dress in white. They sing in Ge'ez, an ancient language that predates most modern tongues. They touch stone that human hands shaped eight hundred years ago.

UNESCO calls it a World Heritage Site. Pilgrims call it holy. I call it... unforgettable.

Episode 076: 'The New Jerusalem.'

Lalibela, Ethiopia.

Coming next week to The Infinite Walk."
