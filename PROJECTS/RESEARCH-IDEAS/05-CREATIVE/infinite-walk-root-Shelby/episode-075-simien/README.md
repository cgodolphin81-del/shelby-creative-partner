## 📝 Episode Summary

**Title:** The Roof of Africa
**Location:** Simien Mountains National Park, Ethiopia
**Elevation:** Up to 4,550 meters (Ras Dashen)
**UNESCO Status:** World Heritage Site (1978)

**Key Themes:**
- Altitude and perspective
- Human-wildlife coexistence
- Endemic species (gelada baboons, Walia ibex)
- Highland village life
- The meaning of "home" and belonging
- Scale, time, and human smallness

**Featured Wildlife:**
- Gelada baboons (2,500 in park)
- Walia ibex (~500 remaining, endemic)
- Ethiopian wolf (rare)
- Lammergeyer vulture

**Key Locations:**
- Sankaber Campsite (3,100m)
- Gich Campsite
- Chennek Campsite
- Imet Gogo Viewpoint (3,926m)
- Ras Dashen Summit (4,550m)
- Jinbar Falls
- Bwahit Peak (4,430m)

## 📊 Key Statistics

### Research Data Points
- **UNESCO inscription:** 1978 (one of first sites)
- **Park area:** 220 km² (original), 412 km² (expanded)
- **Highest peak:** Ras Dashen, 4,550m (10th in Africa)
- **Escarpment height:** 2,000m drop
- **Gelada population:** ~2,500 in park
- **Walia ibex population:** ~500 (endemic, endangered)
- **Bird species:** 180+ recorded
- **Best trekking season:** October - May
- **Average trek duration:** 4-8 days

### Travel Guide Data
- **International airport:** Addis Ababa (ADD)
- **Domestic airport:** Gondar (GDQ)
- **Flight time:** 1h 15m (Addis to Gondar)
- **Drive to park:** 2.5 hours (Gondar to Debark)
- **Park entrance fee:** 90 Birr/day (~$1.60 USD)
- **Scout fee:** 150 Birr/day (~$2.70 USD)
- **Base daily cost:** $15-25 USD (budget trekker)
- **Tour package:** $400-1,200 USD (7 days)
