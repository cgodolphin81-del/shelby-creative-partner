## 🗼 SHIBUYA CROSSING

**The Heart of Modern Tokyo**

- **World's busiest pedestrian crossing** - 3,000+ people cross during each green light
- **Iconic scramble** - All directions go simultaneously, organized chaos
- **Surrounding landmarks:**
  - Shibuya 109 (shopping mecca)
  - Hachiko Statue (famous loyal dog memorial)
  - Shibuya Sky observation deck (229m high)
  - Center Gai (pedestrian street)

**Best times to visit:**
- Rush hour (7-9 AM, 5-8 PM) for maximum crowd energy
- Late night (10 PM+) for neon-lit atmosphere
- Rain creates reflective surfaces perfect for photography

**Audio notes:** The crossing creates a unique soundscape - traffic lights chirping (Japanese pedestrian signals), distant train announcements, footsteps, muted conversations in multiple languages.

## ⛩️ SENSO-JI TEMPLE

**Tokyo's Oldest Temple (645 AD)**

**Location:** Asakusa district

**Key features:**
- **Kaminarimon Gate** - Thunder Gate with massive red lantern (700kg, 3.9m tall)
- **Nakamise Shopping Street** - 250m approach lined with traditional shops
- **Main Hall (Hondo)** - Rebuilt after WWII, houses Kannon bodhisattva
- **Five-Story Pagoda** - 53 meters tall, each roof level represents an element
- **Incense cauldron** - Visitors waft smoke over themselves for healing

**Cultural significance:**
- Dedicated to Kannon, goddess of mercy
- Survived WWII bombing (mostly)
- Represents Edo-period Tokyo

**Best times:**
- Early morning (6-7 AM) for peaceful atmosphere
- Evening for illuminated grounds
- Avoid weekends if seeking quiet

**Audio notes:** Temple bells, incense burning, traditional shamisen music from street performers, wooden geta sandals on stone.

## 🗻 MT. FUJI DAY TRIP

**Japan's Sacred Mountain (3,776m)**

**Distance from Tokyo:** ~100km southwest

**Best viewing spots:**
1. **Lake Kawaguchi** - Classic reflection views, hot springs
2. **Hakone** - Hot springs, pirate ships on Lake Ashi, ropeway
3. **Chureito Pagoda** - Iconic pagoda + Fuji + cherry blossoms combo
4. **Shinkansen** - See Fuji from bullet train (seat E on Tokyo-Osaka route)

**Day trip options:**
- **Kawaguchiko** (2 hours by bus) - Most accessible, great views
- **Hakone Loop** (2.5 hours) - Hot springs, museums, lake cruise
- **Fuji Five Lakes** - Full day, multiple viewpoints

**Climbing season:** July-September only
- Most climbers start at 5th Station (~2,300m)
- Summit sunrise ("Goraiko") is the goal
- Requires preparation, not casual day trip

**Best viewing months:**
- October-March: Clearest views
- April: Cherry blossoms + Fuji
- Summer: Often cloudy/hidden

**Audio notes:** Wind through mountain passes, temple bells at base, quiet reverence of observers.

## 🌸 CHERRY BLOSSOMS (SAKURA)

**Japan's Obsession**

**Tokyo bloom timing:**
- **Typical:** Late March - Early April
- **Peak:** About 1 week after first bloom
- **Variation:** Year to year depends on winter temps

**Best Tokyo viewing spots:**
1. **Ueno Park** - 1,000+ trees, festival atmosphere, crowded
2. **Chidorigafuchi** - Moat around Imperial Palace, boat rentals
3. **Meguro River** - 800 trees along canal, illuminated at night
4. **Shinjuku Gyoen** - Multiple varieties, spacious, entry fee
5. **Yasukuni Shrine** - Traditional setting, fewer tourists

**Hanami culture:**
- Picnics under blooming trees
- Blue tarps reserved days in advance
- Evening illuminations (yozakura)
- Transience theme (mono no aware) - beauty in impermanence

**Audio notes:** Festival drums, laughter, clinking bottles, wind through petals, traditional folk songs.

## 🤖 TECH CULTURE

**Akihabara - Electric Town**

**What it is:**
- Electronics district since 1950s
- Now: anime, manga, gaming, maid cafes, otaku culture
- Multi-story buildings packed with shops

**Key experiences:**
- **Electronics shops:** Yodobashi Camera, Laox, Sofmap
- **Anime/manga:** Animate, Mandarake (8 floors of collectibles)
- **Arcades:** SEGA GiGO (multi-floor gaming complexes)
- **Maid cafes:** Costumed servers, interactive experience
- **Gachapon halls:** Hundreds of capsule toy machines
- **Retro gaming:** Super Potato (vintage games, consoles)

**Otaku culture:**
- Obsessive fandom (anime, manga, idols, trains, etc.)
- Cosplay common in district
- Figure collecting is serious business
- Maid cafes blend service + performance

**Audio notes:** Electronic game sounds, J-pop from storefronts, capsule toy machines dispensing, excited conversations in Japanese/English.

## 🍜 FOOD SCENE

**Tokyo: World's Most Michelin-Starred City**

**Must-try categories:**

### Sushi
- **Sukiyabashi Jiro** - 3 Michelin stars, legendary (nearly impossible to book)
- **Sushi Saito** - Currently considered #1
- **Mid-range:** Sushi Zanmai (Tsukiji), affordable quality
- **Conveyor belt:** Kura Sushi, Sushiro (fun, cheap)

### Ramen
- **Ichiran** - Famous tonkotsu, solo booths
- **Afuri** - Light yuzu shio ramen
- **Tsuta** - First Michelin-starred ramen
- **Local shops:** Best ramen is often in tiny 8-seat shops

### Traditional
- **Tempura:** Tempura Kondo (vegetable tempura master)
- **Yakitori:** Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane, Shinjuku)
- **Tonkatsu:** Maisen (pork cutlet specialist)
- **Soba:** Traditional buckwheat noodles
- **Unagi:** Grilled eel, summer specialty

### Street Food
- **Takoyaki** - Octopus balls
- **Okonomiyaki** - Savory pancake
- **Taiyaki** - Fish-shaped cake with filling
- **Melon pan** - Sweet bread

### Tsukiji/Toyosu Fish Market
- **Outer market** still open for food
- **Fresh sushi breakfast** is iconic experience
- **Toyosu** replaced inner market (2018), viewing galleries

**Food culture notes:**
- "Itadakimasu" before eating (gratitude)
- Slurping noodles = appreciation, not rude
- Don't tip (included in service)
- Many places cash-only
- Reservations essential for high-end

**Audio notes:** Sizzling grills, chef calls, slurping, kitchen chaos, traditional wooden geta in alleyways.

## 🎌 CULTURAL NOTES FOR VISITORS

**Etiquette:**
- Bow slightly when greeting
- Remove shoes when required (look for slippers)
- No tipping
- Quiet on trains (no phone calls)
- No eating while walking (generally)
- Proper trash sorting (complex system)
- Onsen (hot spring) rules: wash thoroughly first, no tattoos (often), no swimsuits

**Useful phrases:**
- Arigatou gozaimasu (Thank you very much)
- Sumimasen (Excuse me / Sorry)
- Irasshaimase (Welcome - you'll hear this everywhere)
- Oishii (Delicious)
- Kanpai (Cheers)

**Transportation:**
- JR Pass for intercity travel
- Suica/Pasmo cards for local transit
- Trains incredibly punctual (delays announced with apology)
- Last trains ~midnight, taxis expensive after
