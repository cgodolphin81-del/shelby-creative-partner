## VISA REQUIREMENTS

### e-Visa (Recommended for Most Travelers)

**Eligibility:** Citizens of 160+ countries including USA, UK, Canada, Australia, EU nations

**Types:**
- **e-Tourist Visa (30 days):** $25 USD, double entry
- **e-Tourist Visa (1 year):** $40 USD, multiple entry, max 90 days per visit
- **e-Tourist Visa (5 years):** $80 USD, multiple entry, max 90 days per visit

**Application Process:**
1. Apply online at https://indianvisaonline.gov.in/evisa/
2. Upload passport scan (bio page) and recent photo
3. Pay fee online (credit/debit card)
4. Receive e-visa via email within 72 hours (apply minimum 4 days before travel)
5. Print e-visa and carry with passport

**Required Documents:**
- Passport valid 6+ months from arrival date
- Recent passport-size photograph (white background)
- Return/onward ticket
- Proof of sufficient funds

### Regular Tourist Visa

**For:** Longer stays, countries not eligible for e-visa

**Process:** Apply through Indian embassy/consulate or visa processing center (VFS Global)

**Duration:** Up to 10 years (multiple entry), max 180 days per visit

**Cost:** Varies by nationality and duration ($80-200 USD)

### Important Notes
- **Registration:** Required for stays over 180 days (FRRO registration)
- **Protected Areas:** Some border regions require special permits (not applicable to Kerala)
- **Overstaying:** Fines up to $500 USD + possible deportation

## THE BACKWATERS

### What Are the Backwaters?
A network of 900+ km of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and lagoons running parallel to Kerala's coast. Freshwater from the Western Ghats meets saltwater from the Arabian Sea, creating a unique ecosystem.

### Key Backwater Destinations

#### Alleppey (Alappuzha) - "Venice of the East"
**Best For:** First-time visitors, houseboat cruises, snake boat races

**Getting There:**
- From Kochi: 1.5 hours by car (55 km)
- Train: Kochi to Alappuzha (1.5 hours)
- Bus: Regular service from major cities

**Activities:**
- Overnight houseboat cruise (most popular)
- Day cruise on shared boat ($15-30 per person)
- Canoe tour through narrow canals ($10-20)
- Visit Alappuzha Beach and lighthouse
- Nehru Trophy Boat Race (August)

**Recommended Stay:** 1-2 nights

#### Kumarakom
**Best For:** Luxury resorts, bird watching, serene backwaters

**Getting There:**
- From Kochi: 2 hours by car (50 km)
- Nearest rail: Kottayam (16 km), then taxi

**Highlights:**
- Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary (migratory birds, October-March)
- Luxury backwater resorts
- Less crowded than Alleppey
- Vembanad Lake views

**Recommended Stay:** 2-3 nights

#### Kollam (Quilon)
**Best For:** Southern gateway, authentic experience, 8-hour boat journey

**Getting There:**
- From Thiruvananthapuram: 1.5 hours by train
- From Kochi: 3 hours by train/car

**Activities:**
- 8-hour boat journey to Alappuzha (scenic, full day)
- Ashtamudi Lake (8 arms, serene)
- Less touristy, more authentic

**Recommended Stay:** 1-2 nights

#### Kuttanad
**Best For:** Rice bowl region, below sea-level farming, authentic villages

**Getting There:**
- From Alappuzha: 1 hour by boat/car

**Highlights:**
- Unique below sea-level farming
- Traditional village life
- Ancient churches
- Off the beaten path

### Houseboat Experience

#### Types of Houseboats

| Category | Features | Price (per night) |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Standard** | 1 bedroom, basic AC, attached bathroom, meals | $100-180 |
| **Deluxe** | 1-2 bedrooms, AC, spacious deck, better meals | $180-300 |
| **Premium** | 2-3 bedrooms, modern amenities, gourmet meals | $300-500 |
| **Luxury** | 2-4 bedrooms, AC throughout, premium furnishings | $500-800+ |

#### What's Included
- Accommodation in bedrooms (varies by boat size)
- All meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks)
- Traditional Kerala cuisine prepared onboard
- Crew: Captain, cook, helper
- Drinking water
- Basic toiletries

#### What to Expect
- **Check-in:** 12:00 PM (noon)
- **Check-out:** 9:00 AM next day
- **Route:** Cruise 4-6 hours, anchor overnight in quiet cove
- **Speed:** 4-8 knots (very slow, peaceful)
- **AC:** Usually runs only in bedroom (not common areas)
- **Electricity:** Generator runs limited hours (typically 6 PM - 10 PM)

#### Booking Tips
- Book in advance during peak season (December-February)
- Verify boat age and condition (newer = better maintenance)
- Confirm menu in advance (vegetarian/non-vegetarian preferences)
- Check reviews on TripAdvisor, Booking.com
- Negotiate directly with operators for better rates
- Avoid touts at Alleppey jetty (go to registered operators)

#### Responsible Houseboat Tourism
- Choose eco-friendly houseboats (solar power, waste treatment)
- Do not litter—plastic kills the backwater ecosystem
- Respect local villages (ask before photographing people)
- Support local communities (buy from village vendors)

## AYURVEDA IN KERALA

### Understanding Ayurveda

Ayurveda is a 5,000-year-old medical system, not a spa treatment. Authentic Ayurveda requires:
- Consultation with qualified vaidya (doctor)
- Diagnosis of your prakriti (constitution) and imbalances
- Customized treatment plan
- Minimum 7-14 days for meaningful results
- Dietary and lifestyle recommendations

### Major Treatments

| Treatment | Description | Duration | Cost (per session) |
|-----------|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Abhyanga** | Full-body synchronized oil massage | 45-60 min | $20-40 |
| **Shirodhara** | Warm oil poured on forehead | 30-45 min | $25-50 |
| **Pizhichil** | Synchronized oil bath therapy | 60-90 min | $50-80 |
| **Navarakizhi** | Rice bolus massage | 45-60 min | $30-50 |
| **Thalappothichil** | Medicated paste on head | 45 min | $25-40 |
| **Kathakali Massage** | Special massage for flexibility | 60 min | $30-50 |

### Authentic Ayurvedic Centers

#### Kottakkal Arya Vaidya Sala
- **Location:** Kottakkal (near Kozhikode), branches statewide
- **Founded:** 1902, most renowned institution
- **Type:** Hospital and research center
- **Cost:** $50-150/day (treatment + accommodation)
- **Best For:** Serious medical treatment, authenticity

#### Somatheeram Ayurvedic Beach Resort
- **Location:** Kovalam Beach
- **Founded:** 1985, first Ayurvedic beach resort
- **Type:** Resort with medical supervision
- **Cost:** $200-500/day (all-inclusive)
- **Best For:** Wellness vacation, beach + treatment

#### Kalari Kovilakam
- **Location:** Palakkad (heritage palace)
- **Type:** Heritage property, authentic treatments
- **Cost:** $300-600/day
- **Best For:** Luxury, heritage experience

#### Government Ayurveda College Hospital
- **Location:** Thiruvananthapuram
- **Type:** Government hospital, affordable
- **Cost:** $10-30/day (treatment only)
- **Best For:** Budget, authentic medical care

### Monsoon Ayurveda (Karkidakam)
- **When:** July-August (Karkidakam month in Malayalam calendar)
- **Why:** Cool, moist air opens pores; body more receptive
- **Special:** Monsoon packages offered by most centers
- **Discount:** 20-30% off peak season rates
- **Best For:** Serious treatment, rejuvenation

### Booking Tips
- Verify doctor qualifications (BAMS degree minimum)
- Ask about treatment customization (avoid one-size-fits-all)
- Clarify what's included (consultation, treatments, meals, accommodation)
- Read reviews, especially from long-term guests
- Avoid places that promise "miracle cures"
- Authentic Ayurveda is medical, not recreational

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Dress Code

#### Temples
- **Men:** Dhoti or lungi (often provided), bare-chested in sanctum
- **Women:** Saree or salwar kameez, shoulders covered
- **Both:** Remove footwear before entry
- **Note:** Some temples restrict non-Hindu entry (Padmanabhaswamy, Guruvayur, Sabarimala)

#### Churches & Mosques
- **Both:** Modest dress, shoulders and knees covered
- **Women:** Head covering required in some mosques

#### General Public
- **Acceptable:** T-shirts, jeans, shorts (knee-length)
- **Conservative Areas:** Cover shoulders and knees
- **Beaches:** Swimwear OK on beach, cover up when leaving

### Social Etiquette

#### Greetings
- **Traditional:** Namaste (palms together, slight bow)
- **Modern:** Handshake common in urban areas
- **Elders:** Touch feet as sign of respect (traditional)

#### Dining
- **Right Hand Only:** Eat with right hand (left considered unclean)
- **Sadya:** Eat from banana leaf, specific order of dishes
- **Hospitality:** Accepting food/drink is polite (even if just a taste)

#### Photography
- **People:** Always ask permission first
- **Temples:** Often prohibited inside sanctum
- **Funerals/Ceremonies:** Do not photograph without explicit permission

#### Public Behavior
- **PDA:** Minimal public displays of affection (cultural norm)
- **Feet:** Do not point feet at people or religious objects
- **Head:** Do not touch anyone's head (sacred)
- **Shoes:** Remove before entering homes and religious places

### Religious Sensitivities
- Kerala is religiously diverse (Hindu 55%, Muslim 27%, Christian 18%)
- Respect all faiths equally
- Remove footwear at all religious sites
- Follow posted rules and local customs
- During Ramadan, avoid eating/drinking in public during daylight hours in Muslim areas

### Tipping
- **Restaurants:** 10% if service charge not included
- **Hotels:** $1-2/day for housekeeping
- **Drivers/Guides:** 10-15% of daily rate
- **Porters:** ₹50-100 per bag

## GETTING AROUND KERALA

### By Air
- **Cochin International (COK):** Main gateway, international and domestic
- **Thiruvananthapuram (TRV):** International, southern Kerala
- **Calicut (CCJ):** Northern Kerala, Gulf connections
- **Domestic Flights:** Kochi ↔ Trivandrum (1 hour, $50-100)

### By Train
- **Network:** Extensive, connects all major cities
- **Classes:** 
  - AC First Class (1A) - Most expensive, private cabins
  - AC 2-Tier (2A) - Comfortable, recommended
  - AC 3-Tier (3A) - Good value
  - Sleeper (SL) - Budget, no AC
- **Booking:** www.irctc.co.in or at stations
- **Tip:** Book in advance (especially December-February)

### By Bus
- **KSRTC (Government):** Reliable, affordable, air-conditioned options
- **Private Buses:** More frequent, variable quality
- **Types:** Ordinary, Express, Deluxe, AC, Volvo
- **Booking:** At bus stations or online (redbus.in)

### By Taxi/Auto
- **App-Based:** Uber and Ola in Kochi, Trivandrum, Kozhikode
- **Local Taxis:** Negotiate fare or insist on meter
- **Auto-Rickshaws:** Short distances, negotiate first
- **Day Rental:** $40-80/day with driver (8-10 hours)

### By Boat
- **Public Ferries:** Affordable backwater transport
- **Tourist Boats:** Scheduled sightseeing cruises
- **Houseboats:** Overnight experience
- **Private Boats:** Charter for groups

### Recommended Route (10-14 Days)
```
Day 1-3: Kochi (Fort Kochi, Mattancherry, heritage walk)
Day 4-5: Alleppey/Kumarakom (houseboat cruise, backwaters)
Day 6-8: Munnar (tea plantations, Western Ghats)
```

## WHEN TO VISIT

### Peak Season (October - March)
- **Weather:** Pleasant, dry, 23-32°C (73-90°F)
- **Pros:** Best weather, all activities available, festivals
- **Cons:** Highest prices, crowds, book in advance
- **Events:** Onam (August-September), Christmas/New Year, Thrissur Pooram (April-May)

### Monsoon Season (June - September)
- **Weather:** Heavy rain, high humidity, 25-28°C (77-82°F)
- **Pros:** Lowest prices, lush green, Ayurveda treatments, fewer tourists
- **Cons:** Rain disruptions, some activities closed, humidity
- **Best For:** Ayurveda, photography, budget travelers, monsoon experience

### Shoulder Season (April - May, October - November)
- **Weather:** Hot and humid, occasional rain
- **Pros:** Lower prices than peak, manageable crowds
- **Cons:** Hot, some rain, not ideal for all activities

### Festival Calendar

| Festival | When | Where | Highlights |
|----------|------|-------|------------|
| **Onam** | August-September | Statewide | Sadya, snake boats, flower carpets, dances |
| **Thrissur Pooram** | April-May | Thrissur | 15 elephants, fireworks, percussion |
| **Nehru Trophy Boat Race** | August | Alleppey | Snake boat races, crowds, celebration |
| **Cochin Carnival** | December-January | Fort Kochi | Parade, costumes, New Year celebration |
| **Theyyam** | October-May | North Kerala | Ritual dance, performers become deities |
| **Christmas** | December | Statewide | Churches, celebrations, especially in Kochi |

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Health & Safety
- **Water:** Drink bottled or filtered water only
- **Food:** Street food is generally safe; choose busy stalls
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, Typhoid recommended; check with doctor
- **Mosquitoes:** Use repellent, especially during monsoon
- **Medical Care:** Good hospitals in cities; travel insurance recommended
- **Emergency:** 108 (ambulance), 100 (police)

### Money Matters
- **Currency:** Indian Rupee (INR)
- **ATMs:** Widely available in cities and towns
- **Cards:** Accepted in hotels, restaurants, larger shops
- **Cash:** Needed for small vendors, markets, rural areas
- **Exchange:** Airport, banks, authorized exchange counters
- **Tipping:** 10% in restaurants (if service charge not included)

### Connectivity
- **SIM Cards:** Airtel, Jio, Vodafone Idea (buy at airport or stores)
- **Required:** Passport, visa, photo, local address (hotel works)
- **Cost:** $5-10 for tourist SIM with data
- **WiFi:** Available in most hotels, cafes, restaurants
- **Coverage:** Good in cities, variable in rural/backwater areas

### Packing List

#### Essentials
- Light, breathable clothing (cotton/linen)
- Modest attire for temples (shoulders, knees covered)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Sandals/flip-flops
- Rain jacket/umbrella (monsoon season)
- Sunscreen, sunglasses, hat
- Insect repellent
- Basic medications (stomach, pain, allergies)
- Hand sanitizer

#### Electronics
- Power adapter (Type C, D, M)
- Phone, camera, chargers
- Power bank
- Headphones (for travel)

#### Documents
- Passport (6+ months validity)
- Visa (printed e-visa)
- Travel insurance
- Flight tickets
- Hotel bookings
- Emergency contacts

### Responsible Tourism
- **Plastic:** Avoid single-use plastic; carry reusable water bottle
- **Backwaters:** Do not litter; use eco-friendly houseboats
- **Wildlife:** Maintain distance; do not feed animals
- **Communities:** Support local businesses; fair prices
- **Temples:** Respect dress codes and photography rules
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing people
- **Children:** Do not give money/candy directly (encourages begging)
