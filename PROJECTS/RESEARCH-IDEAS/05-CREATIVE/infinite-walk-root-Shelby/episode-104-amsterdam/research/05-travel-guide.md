## GETTING THERE

### By Air
**Amsterdam Airport Schiphol (AMS)**
- One of Europe's major hubs, 15km southwest of city center
- Direct train to Amsterdam Central Station: 15-20 minutes, €5-10
- Trains run 24/7 (less frequent at night)
- Taxi to center: 30 minutes, €45-60
- Uber/Bolt available: €35-50

**Airlines:**
- KLM (flag carrier, extensive network)
- Transavia (budget, European routes)
- EasyJet, Ryanair (budget options)
- Most major international carriers

### By Train
**Amsterdam Central Station** - Heart of the city
- From Paris: Thalys/Eurostar, 3h20m, €35-150
- From London: Eurostar via Brussels, 4h, €50-200
- From Berlin: ICE, 6h30m, €40-120
- From Brussels: Thalys, 1h50m, €25-80

**Tip:** Book train tickets in advance for best prices. NS (Dutch Railways) app is essential.

### By Bus
- FlixBus, Eurolines from major European cities
- Budget option but slower (London: 10-12h, Paris: 6-7h)
- Arrives at Sloterdijk or Duivendrecht stations
- Connect to city center by train (10-15 minutes)

### By Car
- **Not recommended** for city exploration
- Parking expensive: €5-8/hour in center
- Many areas restricted to residents only
- Excellent for day trips to countryside

### By Boat
- Cruise ships dock at Passenger Terminal Amsterdam
- River cruises from Germany/Belgium possible
- Not a primary transportation method

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### Spring (March-May) ⭐ BEST OVERALL
**Weather:** 8-18°C (46-64°F), variable
**Pros:**
- Tulip season (mid-April to early May)
- Fewer crowds than summer
- Long daylight hours
- Parks in bloom
**Cons:**
- Rain possible (pack layers)
- Accommodation prices rise during tulip peak
**Events:**
- King's Day (April 27) - massive orange celebration
- Tulip Festival (April)
- Liberation Day (May 5)

### Summer (June-August)
**Weather:** 15-25°C (59-77°F), warmest months
**Pros:**
- Best weather, longest days (sunset after 10pm in June)
- Outdoor cafés, canal-side dining
- Festivals and events
- Day trips to beaches
**Cons:**
- Peak tourist season (crowded!)
- Highest accommodation prices
- Anne Frank House books out weeks ahead
- Can be uncomfortably busy
**Events:**
- Pride Amsterdam (late July/early August)
- Open Air Cinema
- Grachtenfestival (canal concert festival, August)

### Autumn (September-November) ⭐ GREAT VALUE
**Weather:** 8-18°C (46-64°F), cooling down
**Pros:**
- Fewer tourists
- Lower prices
- Beautiful fall colors
- Cozy café culture
- Museum weather
**Cons:**
- Shorter days
- More rain in November
- Some outdoor activities close
**Events:**
- Amsterdam Dance Event (October) - electronic music festival
- Museum Night (November)

### Winter (December-February)
**Weather:** 0-8°C (32-46°F), can be cold
**Pros:**
- Fewest tourists
- Lowest prices
- Christmas markets and lights
- Cozy gezelligheid at its peak
- Possible ice skating on canals (if frozen)
**Cons:**
- Cold, often gray
- Short days (sunset ~4:30pm in December)
- Some attractions have reduced hours
- Rain/wind common
**Events:**
- Amsterdam Light Festival (November-January)
- Christmas markets (December)
- New Year's Eve fireworks

### **RECOMMENDED:** 
**Late April-Early May** (tulips, good weather, manageable crowds)
**September-October** (fewer crowds, good weather, lower prices)

## CYCLING IN AMSTERDAM

### Why Cycle?
- 48% of all trips in Amsterdam made by bicycle
- Faster than cars in city center
- Authentic Dutch experience
- Cheap and flexible
- Extensive, safe infrastructure

### Bike Rental Options

**Budget (€10-15/day):**
- MacBike (multiple locations, touristy but reliable)
- Star Bikes Rental
- Black Bikes

**Mid-Range (€15-25/day):**
- Mike's Bike Tours (includes tours)
- Amsterdam Bike Rent
- Better bikes, better service

**Premium/E-Bikes (€25-40/day):**
- E-bikes for easier riding
- Cargo bikes for families
- Vintage bikes for style

**OV-Fiets:**
- Only with Dutch NS train subscription
- €4.35/day, pick up at train stations
- Not available to tourists

### Cycling Rules & Tips

**DO:**
- Use bike lanes (marked with red asphalt and bicycle symbols)
- Signal turns with your arm
- Lock both wheels and frame (bikes get stolen!)
- Follow traffic lights (bicycle-specific signals)
- Ride predictably, in direction of traffic
- Use front and rear lights at night (required by law, €35 fine)

**DON'T:**
- Ride on sidewalks (fines up to €140)
- Use your phone while cycling (€140 fine)
- Ride against traffic
- Assume cars see you (defensive cycling)
- Park in no-parking zones (bikes removed)

**Safety:**
- Cyclists have right of way in most situations
- But always watch for opening car doors
- Trams have absolute right of way
- Watch for pedestrians crossing bike lanes
- Rain = slippery cobblestones and tram tracks

**Parking:**
- Use designated bike parking areas
- Multi-story bike parking at Central Station (€1.40/day)
- Never chain to random railings (may be removed)
- Remove lights and accessories (they get stolen)

### Cycling Routes

**Beginner-Friendly:**
- Vondelpark loop (car-free, scenic)
- Canal ring (flat, well-marked)
- Amsterdamse Bos (forest park south of city)

**Day Trips:**
- Zaanse Schans (windmills, 20km north)
- Haarlem (30 minutes by train or 45km cycle)
- Amstelveen (suburban, 15km south)

**Advanced:**
- Full canal ring + outer neighborhoods
- North Amsterdam (free ferry behind Central Station)
- Coastal route to Zandvoort (30km)

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Dutch Directness
- Dutch people are famously direct
- Not rude—just honest and efficient
- Don't take it personally
- They appreciate directness in return

### Greetings
- Handshake for first meetings
- Three kisses on cheek for friends (right-left-right)
- "Hallo" or "Goedemorgen/middag/avond" (Good morning/afternoon/evening)
- "Dank je wel" = Thank you
- "Alstublieft" = Please / Here you go

### Tipping
- Not obligatory (service included)
- Round up or 5-10% for good service
- Cafés: round up or leave small change
- Restaurants: 5-10% if service was good
- Taxis: round up

### Dining
- Dinner typically 6-8pm
- Breakfast/lunch often just bread with toppings
- "Borrelen" = pre-dinner drinks with snacks
- Try: stroopwafels, bitterballen, herring, cheese
- Water is not free in restaurants (ask for "kraanwater" = tap water)

### Shopping
- Shops close early: 6pm weekdays, 5pm Saturday, closed Sunday (often)
- Albert Cuyp Market (daily, best in morning)
- Sunday shopping in tourist areas only
- VAT refund available for non-EU residents (€50+ purchase)

### Language
- Dutch is official language
- 90%+ speak English fluently
- Learning basic Dutch appreciated:
  - Dank je wel = Thank you
  - Alsjeblieft = Please
  - Sorry = Sorry / Excuse me
  - Spreekt u Engels? = Do you speak English?
  - Ik begrijp het niet = I don't understand

### Social Norms
- Punctuality is valued
- Split bills common ("going Dutch")
- Environmental consciousness high (recycle, bike, sustainable)
- Tolerance is core value but don't abuse it
- Cannabis: legal in coffeeshops but public consumption frowned upon

## NEIGHBORHOODS TO EXPLORE

### Centrum (Center)
- Historic heart, canals, Dam Square
- Touristy but essential
- Anne Frank House, Royal Palace
- Stay: Central but busy and expensive

### Jordaan
- Former working-class, now trendy
- Narrow streets, hidden courtyards
- Cafés, boutiques, art galleries
- Stay: Charming, walkable, mid-range to expensive

### De Pijp
- Vibrant, multicultural
- Albert Cuyp Market
- Heineken Experience, Sarphatipark
- Stay: Young, foodie scene, mid-range

### Oud-West (Old West)
- Local vibe, less touristy
- Vondelpark access
- Cafés, vintage shops
- Stay: Residential, good value

### Noord (North)
- Former shipyards, now creative hub
- A'DAM Lookout, NDSM wharf
- Free ferry from Central Station
- Stay: Up-and-coming, budget-friendly, industrial chic

### Oost (East)
- Residential, local
- Oosterpark, Dappermarkt
- Authentic Amsterdam
- Stay: Quiet, good value, less touristy

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money
- Euro (€) currency
- Cards widely accepted (Visa/Mastercard)
- Some places cash-only (markets, small cafés)
- ATM ("pinautomaat") everywhere
- Notify bank of travel

### Connectivity
- Excellent WiFi in cafés, hotels, public spaces
- SIM cards: KPN, Vodafone, T-Mobile
- EU roaming applies for EU visitors
- Download offline maps

### Safety
- Very safe city overall
- Watch for pickpockets (tourist areas, trams)
- Bicycle theft is rampant (lock properly!)
- Emergency: 112 (EU-wide)
- Police non-emergency: 0900-8844

### Health
- Tap water is safe to drink
- Pharmacies ("apotheek") for prescriptions
- GPs for non-emergencies
- EU visitors: bring EHIC card
- Travel insurance recommended

### Accessibility
- Improving but historic city has challenges
- Many buildings have stairs, no elevators
- Trams have low-floor access
- Canal boats vary (some wheelchair accessible)
- Check ahead for specific needs

### Sustainability
- Bring reusable water bottle (tap water excellent)
- Recycle (separate bins everywhere)
- Use public transport or bike
- Support local businesses
- Respect residential areas (noise, behavior)
