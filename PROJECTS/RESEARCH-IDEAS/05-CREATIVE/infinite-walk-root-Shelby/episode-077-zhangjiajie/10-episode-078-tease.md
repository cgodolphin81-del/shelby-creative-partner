**Title:** From the Jade Empire to the Highlands of the Heart

**Body:**

Three hundred and eighty million years.

That's how long it took for Zhangjiajie's pillars to rise from ocean floor to cloud-piercing peaks.

In Episode 077, we walked among those pillars. We stood on glass bridges 300 meters above canyons. We climbed 999 steps to heaven's gate. We learned that we are all visitors here—the mountain remembers.

But the highlands are not done with us yet.

**The Vertical World Calls Again**

There's something about high places.

Maybe it's the thin air that clears the mind.
Maybe it's the perspective that comes with altitude.
Maybe it's the knowledge that we're standing where few have stood, breathing air that's touched fewer lungs.

Whatever it is, the call is real.

And Episode 078 answers that call.

**Ancient Stones, Older Stories**

Zhangjiajie taught us about nature's patience—380 million years of water, ice, and gravity sculpting stone into art.

But what about the places where human hands met stone with intention?

Where pilgrims carved sanctuaries from living rock?
Where monks built monasteries on cliffs that defy engineering?
Where every stone has a story, and every story has a stone?

Episode 078 is about those places.
