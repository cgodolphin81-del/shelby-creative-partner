## GETTING THERE

### Option 1: Car + Ferry (Most Popular)

**Step 1: Drive to Landeyjahöfn Ferry Port**
- **Route**: Reykjavík → Route 1 (Ring Road) → Route 209
- **Distance**: 127 km (79 miles)
- **Drive Time**: 1.5-2 hours
- **Road Conditions**: Paved, well-maintained (summer); winter driving requires caution

**Step 2: Herjólfur Ferry**
- **Operator**: www.herjolfur.is
- **Summer Schedule** (May-September):
  - Departure: Landeyjahöfn
  - Duration: 35-45 minutes
  - Frequency: 7 daily crossings
  - First ferry: ~8:00 AM
  - Last ferry: ~10:00 PM (varies)
- **Winter Schedule** (October-April):
  - Departure: Þorlákshöfn (Landeyjahöfn closes)
  - Duration: 2 hours 45 minutes
  - Frequency: 1-2 daily crossings
  - Weather-dependent (cancellations possible)

**Ferry Costs** (2024-2025):
| Passenger Type | One Way | Round Trip |
|----------------|---------|------------|
| Adult (16+) | 2,400 ISK (~$18 USD) | 4,800 ISK |
| Child (4-15) | 1,200 ISK | 2,400 ISK |
| Senior (67+) | 1,920 ISK | 3,840 ISK |
| Vehicle (car) | 2,800 ISK | 5,600 ISK |
| Bicycle | 800 ISK | 1,600 ISK |

**Booking Tips**:
- **Vehicle reservations**: ESSENTIAL in summer (book weeks/months ahead)
- **Foot passengers**: Can often buy on arrival, but booking recommended
- **Þjóðhátíð Festival** (first weekend August): Book 6+ months ahead
- **Online**: www.herjolfur.is (credit card accepted)
- **Check schedules**: Weather can cause cancellations, especially in winter

### Option 2: Bus + Ferry

**Strætó Bus Route 52** (Seasonal: June-August)
- **Route**: Reykjavík (BSÍ Bus Terminal) → Landeyjahöfn
- **Duration**: ~2 hours
- **Cost**: ~3,500 ISK one way
- **Frequency**: 1-2 daily (connects with ferry)
- **Booking**: www.straeto.is

**Winter Bus Option**:
- Route to Þorlákshöfn (longer journey)
- Fewer departures
- Check current schedules

**Pros**: Budget-friendly, no parking worries
**Cons**: Less flexibility, limited schedule

---

### Option 3: Flight

**Eagle Air** (Scheduled Flights)
- **Route**: Reykjavík Airport (RKV, not Keflavík) → Heimaey (VEY)
- **Duration**: 25-30 minutes
- **Frequency**: Limited (check schedule, often 1-2 daily)
- **Cost**: 15,000-25,000 ISK one way (~$115-190 USD)
- **Booking**: www.eagleair.is

**Pros**: Fast, scenic views, avoids ferry weather issues
**Cons**: Expensive, limited baggage, weather-dependent

---

### Option 4: Organized Day Tour

**From Reykjavík**:
- **Duration**: 12-14 hours total
- **Includes**: Transport, ferry, guide, some meals
- **Cost**: 25,000-40,000 ISK (~$190-305 USD)
- **Operators**: Arctic Adventures, Reykjavík Excursions, others

**Pros**: No planning stress, expert guide, all logistics handled
**Cons**: Less flexibility, fixed itinerary, more expensive

## GETTING AROUND HEIMAEY

### Walking
- **Town**: Compact, everything within 20-30 minutes walk from harbor
- **Eldfell Hike**: 45-90 minutes round trip
- **Stórhöfði**: 3 km from town (45 min walk each way)
- **Cost**: FREE

### Local Bus
- **Service**: Strætó local routes on Heimaey
- **Coverage**: Town, some outer areas
- **Cost**: ~500 ISK per ride
- **Frequency**: Limited (check schedules)

### Bicycle Rental
- **Availability**: Several shops in town
- **Cost**: 3,000-5,000 ISK/day
- **Terrain**: Hilly, but manageable
- **Best For**: Exploring island at own pace

### Car Rental
- **Availability**: Limited (1-2 agencies)
- **Cost**: 8,000-15,000 ISK/day
- **Note**: Bring car on ferry or rent on island
- **Parking**: Free, widely available

### Taxi
- **Availability**: Limited (2-3 taxis)
- **Cost**: 2,000-5,000 ISK per ride (depending on distance)
- **Booking**: Call ahead or ask hotel to arrange

### ATV Tours
- **Operators**: Volcano ATV and others
- **Tours**: Puffin tours, volcano tours, combo tours
- **Cost**: 15,000-25,000 ISK (2-3 hours)
- **Best For**: Adventure, accessing remote areas

## ATTRACTIONS & ACTIVITIES

### Must-See Attractions

#### 1. Eldfell Volcano Hike
- **Trailhead**: Near Eldheimar Museum
- **Distance**: ~2 km round trip
- **Elevation**: 200m gain
- **Duration**: 45-90 minutes
- **Difficulty**: Moderate (steep sections, loose scree)
- **Cost**: FREE
- **Best Time**: Year-round (weather dependent)
- **Tips**: Wear sturdy shoes, bring windproof layer, allow extra time for summit views

#### 2. Eldheimar Museum ("Pompeii of the North")
- **Location**: Gerðisbraut 10
- **Admission**: 
  - Adults: 3,200 ISK (~$24 USD)
  - Seniors/Students: 2,560 ISK
  - Children (under 18): FREE
- **Hours**: Daily 10:00-18:00 (year-round)
- **Duration**: 30-90 minutes
- **Audio Guide**: Included (English, Icelandic, German, French, Norwegian)
- **Best For**: Understanding 1973 eruption, emotional experience
- **Tip**: Visit early in trip for context

#### 3. Stórhöfði Peninsula & Puffin Viewing
- **Location**: Southernmost point of Heimaey
- **Distance from Town**: 3 km (walk, bike, or drive)
- **Admission**: FREE
- **Facilities**: Puffin viewing hut, walking paths, parking
- **Best Time**: May-August, early morning or evening (6 PM-sunset)
- **Duration**: 1-2 hours
- **Tips**: 
  - Bring binoculars
  - Windproof clothing essential (very exposed)
  - Stay on marked paths (respect nesting burrows)
  - Telephoto lens for photography

#### 4. Puffin Watching Boat Tours
- **Operators**: Multiple companies from harbor
- **Duration**: 1-3 hours
- **Cost**: 8,000-15,000 ISK (~$60-115 USD)
- **Includes**: Puffins, other seabirds, Elephant Rock, sea caves
- **Best Time**: May-August
- **Tip**: Book ahead in peak season

#### 5. SEA LIFE Trust Beluga Whale Sanctuary
- **Location**: Klettsvegur 1 (downtown)
- **Admission**: 
  - Adults: 3,560 ISK (~$27 USD)
  - Children: 2,140 ISK
- **Hours**: Varies by season (check website)
- **Duration**: 1-2 hours
- **Best For**: Off-season puffin viewing (rescued birds), whale sanctuary
- **Note**: Not a traditional aquarium—rescue/rehabilitation facility

### Additional Activities

#### 6. Sævík Black Sand Beach
- **Location**: Northern Heimaey
- **Access**: Short walk or drive
- **Cost**: FREE
- **Best For**: Walking, photography, reflection

#### 7. Kirkjuvogur Bay Walk
- **Location**: Harbor area
- **Cost**: FREE
- **Best For**: Evening strolls, harbor views, dining

#### 8. Elephant Rock (Fíllinn)
- **Access**: Boat tours only
- **Cost**: Included in boat tour price
- **Best For**: Photography, natural formations

#### 9. Surtsey Viewing
- **Location**: Visible from eastern Heimaey cliffs
- **Access**: Boat tours circle at 500m distance (no landing)
- **Cost**: FREE from land; included in boat tours
- **Best Time**: Clear days
- **Note**: UNESCO World Heritage, strictly protected

#### 10. Þjóðhátíð Festival (First Weekend August)
- **Location**: Herjólfsdalur valley
- **Admission**: Festival pass required (~10,000-15,000 ISK)
- **Accommodation**: Camping or book months ahead
- **Best For**: Cultural immersion, music, community
- **Warning**: Extremely crowded, book 6+ months ahead

## BEST TIME TO VISIT

### For Puffins
- **Optimal**: Mid-May to mid-August
- **Peak**: June-July (most active, chicks in burrows)
- **Arrival**: Mid-April (early birds)
- **Departure**: Mid-August to early September
- **Avoid**: September-April (puffins at sea)

### For Weather
- **Warmest**: July-August (9-14°C / 48-57°F)
- **Most Stable**: June-August
- **Windiest**: October-March
- **Daylight**:
  - June: Nearly 24 hours (midnight sun)
  - December: 4-5 hours

### For Crowds
- **Busiest**: July-August, Þjóðhátíð (first weekend August)
- **Moderate**: June, September
- **Quietest**: October-May (but limited services, no puffins)

### For Budget
- **Cheapest**: October-April (off-season)
- **Moderate**: May, September (shoulder)
- **Most Expensive**: June-August (peak)

### For Photography
- **Best Light**: June (midnight sun), September (golden hour)
- **Puffins Active**: May-August, early morning/evening
- **Dramatic Weather**: Year-round (Icelandic weather is moody)

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Packing List

**Summer (June-August)**:
- Windproof/waterproof jacket (ESSENTIAL)
- Fleece or warm layer
- Sturdy hiking shoes
- Hat and gloves (yes, even in summer!)
- Sunglasses
- Sunscreen (Arctic sun is strong)
- Binoculars (for puffins)
- Camera with telephoto lens
- Backpack
- Water bottle
- Snacks

**Winter (October-April)**:
- Heavy winter coat
- Multiple warm layers
- Waterproof boots
- Warm hat, gloves, scarf
- Hand/toe warmers
- Headlamp (limited daylight)
- All summer items plus winter gear

### Money & Payments

- **Currency**: Icelandic Króna (ISK)
- **Cards**: Credit/debit accepted everywhere (Visa, Mastercard)
- **Cash**: Rarely needed, but good to have small amounts
- **ATMs**: Available in town (by harbor)
- **Tipping**: Not expected (service included)

### Connectivity

- **WiFi**: Widely available (hotels, cafes, restaurants)
- **Mobile**: Good 4G/LTE coverage
- **Emergency**: 112 (same as mainland Iceland)
- **Non-Emergency**: 448-1000 (Heimaey health center)

### Language

- **Official**: Icelandic
- **Tourism**: English widely spoken (near-universal)
- **Other**: Some German, Danish, Norwegian
- **Tip**: Learn "takk" (thank you)—locals appreciate it

### Health & Safety

- **Medical**: Heimaey Health Center (Heilsugæsla)
- **Serious Emergencies**: Evacuated to mainland (helicopter/fast boat)
- **Travel Insurance**: Recommended
- **Water**: Tap water is safe, excellent quality
- **Volcanic Safety**: Eldfell dormant, stay on marked trails

### Accessibility

- **Town/Harbor**: Wheelchair accessible
- **Eldfell Hike**: NOT accessible (steep, uneven)
- **Stórhöfði**: Partially accessible (some paths, but windy/exposed)
- **Eldheimar Museum**: Wheelchair accessible
- **Ferry**: Accessible (elevators, ramps)
- **Restaurants**: Most accessible (ask ahead)

### Environmental Responsibility

- **Leave No Trace**: Pack out all trash
- **Puffin Ethics**: 
  - Stay on marked paths
  - Keep 2-3m distance from burrows
  - No flash photography near nests
  - Do not attempt to touch wild puffins
- **Surtsey**: Do not attempt to land (strictly forbidden)
- **Respect**: Private property, nesting areas, local customs

## COMMON QUESTIONS

### Q: Can I visit Surtsey?
**A**: No. Landing is strictly forbidden (scientists only). Boat tours circle at 500m distance.

### Q: When is puffin season?
**A**: Mid-April to early September, peak June-July.

### Q: Is Eldfell still active?
**A**: Dormant. Ground still warm in places, but no eruption risk. Safe to hike.

### Q: Do I need a car on Heimaey?
**A**: No. Town is walkable. Bike rental or local bus for longer distances.

### Q: Can I visit in winter?
**A**: Yes, but ferry schedule reduced, weather unpredictable, no puffins.

### Q: Is puffin hunting legal?
**A**: Yes, regulated and sustainable. August season. Tourists cannot hunt.

### Q: How windy is it?
**A**: Very. Windproof clothing essential year-round.

### Q: Can I bake bread in volcanic earth?
**A**: Some locals do. Not recommended for tourists (safety, respect).

### Q: What if ferry is cancelled?
**A**: Stay extra night (book flexible accommodation). Check weather forecasts.

### Q: Is it expensive?
**A**: Yes. Iceland is pricey. Westman Islands more than mainland (import costs).
