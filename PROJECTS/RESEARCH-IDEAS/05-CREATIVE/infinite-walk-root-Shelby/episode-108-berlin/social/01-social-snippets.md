**Image:** East Side Gallery murals or graffiti art

**Caption:**
1.3 kilometers of hope painted on concrete. 🎨

The East Side Gallery is the longest remaining stretch of the Berlin Wall. After the Wall fell in 1989, artists from around the world transformed this barrier into a canvas.

"My God, Help Me to Survive This Deadly Love"—the famous fraternal kiss mural—is now one of the most recognizable images of the Cold War's end.

From death strip to art walk. From division to expression.

This is what rises from destruction.

### Post 5: Museum Island (Feed)
**Image:** Museum Island aerial or Nefertiti bust

**Caption:**
The "Acropolis on the Spree." 🏛️

Museum Island is a UNESCO World Heritage site with five world-class museums built between 1830-1930.

The crown jewel? Nefertiti's bust. 3,370 years old. One of the most copied ancient artworks in history. Egypt wants it back. Germany says no.

The Pergamon Altar. The Ishtar Gate of Babylon. Greek sculptures. 19th-century masterpieces.

€24 day pass. Minimum one day. Ideally 2-3.

This is cultural rebirth. Art that survived bombings, looting, war. Treasures returned and contested.

Berlin wears its memory like skin. You cannot look away.

Episode 108 explores Museum Island and what it means to preserve culture through destruction.
