# Episode 054: Canyon Kingdom Travel Guide
## Practical Information for Grand Canyon & Utah National Parks

---

## GETTING THERE

### To Grand Canyon National Park, Arizona

**By Air**:
- **Phoenix Sky Harbor (PHN)**: 230 miles to South Rim, 3.5-hour drive
  - Best option: Most flights, car rental available
  - Shuttle: Arizona Shuttle from Phoenix ($89 one-way, 4 hours)
- **Las Vegas (LAS)**: 275 miles to South Rim, 4.5-hour drive
  - Good option: Combine with Utah parks road trip
  - Shuttle: Grand Canyon Express from Las Vegas ($99 one-way)
- **Flagstaff (FLG)**: 80 miles to South Rim, 1.5-hour drive
  - Closest airport, limited flights
  - Shuttle: Groome Transportation ($60 one-way, 1.5 hours)
- **Grand Canyon National Park Airport (GCN)**: 7 miles to South Rim
  - Seasonal flights from Phoenix, Las Vegas
  - Expensive, limited schedule, but closest

**By Car**:
- **From Phoenix**: I-17 North to Flagstaff, US-180 West to South Rim
- **From Las Vegas**: US-93 South to I-40 East, AZ-64 North to South Rim
- **From Albuquerque**: I-40 West to Williams, AZ-64 North to South Rim

**By Train**:
- **Amtrak Southwest Chief**: Stops in Flagstaff, Williams
- **Grand Canyon Railway**: Williams to South Rim (historic train, $69+ round trip)
  - Vintage experience, entertainment onboard
  - 2.5 hours each way, departs 9:30 AM, returns 3:30 PM

**By Tour**:
- Multiple day tours from Las Vegas, Phoenix, Flagstaff
- Range from $150-400/day depending on inclusions
- Good for those without car, but less flexibility

### To Zion National Park, Utah

**By Air**:
- **Las Vegas (LAS)**: 160 miles, 2.5-hour drive (most common)
- **Salt Lake City (SLC)**: 300 miles, 4.5-hour drive
- **St. George (SGU)**: 45 miles, 1-hour drive (regional airport, limited flights)

**By Car**:
- **From Las Vegas**: I-15 North to UT-9 East, enter Springdale
- **From Salt Lake City**: I-15 South to UT-9 East

**Shuttle**:
- Zion Canyon Shuttle (mandatory March-November)
- Springdale town shuttle (free, connects to park shuttle)
- No private vehicles in canyon during shuttle season

**Cost**: $80 (valid 12 months from purchase date)

**Covers**:
- All national parks (60+)

- BLM land
- Wildlife refuges
- Many state parks (varies)

**Who Should Buy**:
- Visiting 3+ national parks in 12 months
- Grand Canyon + Zion + Bryce + Arches = $130 without pass

**Where to Buy**:
- Recreation.gov (online)
- Any national park entrance station
- REI, some sporting goods stores

**Types**:
- **Standard**: $80
- **Senior (62+)**: $20 annual or $80 lifetime
- **Military**: Free (active duty + dependents)
- **4th Grader**: Free (Every Kid Outdoors program)
- **Access**: Free (permanent disability)

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### Grand Canyon

**South Rim (Open Year-Round)**:
- **Best**: April-June, September-October
  - Mild weather: 60-75°F days, 30-45°F nights
  - Fewer crowds than summer
  - All facilities open
- **Summer (July-August)**:
  - Crowded: 5+ million annual visitors peak here
  - Hot: 70-85°F, inner canyon 100°F+
  - Afternoon monsoon storms (July-September)
  - All facilities open, shuttle running
- **Winter (November-March)**:
  - Few crowds, peaceful
  - Cold: 35-45°F days, can drop below 0°F
  - Snow common (100 inches/year average)
  - Some roads may close temporarily
  - North Rim closed

**North Rim (Mid-May to Mid-October Only)**:
- **Best**: June, September
  - Cooler than South Rim (8,000+ feet elevation)
  - Fewer crowds
  - All facilities open
- **Closed**: Mid-October to Mid-May (snow)

---

### Zion National Park

**Best**: March-May, September-November
- Mild weather: 60-80°F days
- Wildflowers in spring, fall colors
- Moderate crowds

**Summer (June-August)**:
- Hot: 90-105°F
- Very crowded
- Shuttle mandatory
- Afternoon thunderstorms possible
- The Narrows: warm water, but flash flood risk

**Winter (December-February)**:
- Cold: 50-60°F days, below freezing nights
- Possible snow, icy trails
- Few crowds
- Shuttle not running (private vehicles allowed)
- The Narrows: cold water, often closed

---

### Bryce Canyon National Park

**Best**: May-September
- Warm days: 70-80°F
- Cool nights: 40-50°F
- All facilities open
- Wildflowers June-July

**Summer (July-August)**:
- Crowded, especially July
- Afternoon thunderstorms common
- Pleasant temperatures due to elevation (8,000-9,000 feet)

**Winter (November-March)**:
- Snow: 100+ inches annually
- Cold: 25-40°F days
- Some roads close
- Cross-country skiing, snowshoeing
- Hoodoos dusted with snow: stunning photography

**Shoulder (April, October)**:
- Possible snow
- Fewer crowds
- Some facilities may have reduced hours

---

### Arches National Park

**Best**: April-May, September-October
- Mild: 60-80°F days
- Ideal hiking weather
- Wildflowers in spring

**Summer (June-August)**:
- Extremely hot: 95-105°F+
- Hike only early morning (before 8 AM) or evening (after 6 PM)
- Crowded
- Monsoon storms (July-August)

**Winter (November-February)**:
- Cold: 40-50°F days, below freezing nights
- Possible snow, ice on trails
- Few crowds
- Delicate Arch: magical with snow

**Timed Entry**: April-October, reserve 1-60 days in advance at Recreation.gov

## HIKING RECOMMENDATIONS

### Grand Canyon Hikes

**Easy**:
- **Rim Trail**: 5.5 miles paved, South Rim, any time
- **Bright Angel Point**: 0.5 miles, North Rim (seasonal)

**Moderate**:
- **Bright Angel Trail**: 9.5 miles to river, rest houses, water (seasonal)
  - Day hike: 1.5-3 miles down and back
  - **Warning**: Do NOT attempt rim-to-rim in one day
- **South Kaibab Trail**: 7 miles to river, steeper, no water
  - Better views, more exposed
  - Day hike: 1-2 miles down and back

**Difficult**:
- **Rim-to-Rim**: 21-24 miles, 1-2 days, permit required for overnight
- **Thunder River Trail**: Remote, technical, experienced hikers only

**Safety**:
- Carry 1 gallon water per person per day
- Hike down in morning, up before noon heat
- Turn around when halfway through water
- Do not attempt to hike from rim to river and back in one day

---

### Zion Hikes

**Easy**:
- **Pa'rus Trail**: 3.5 miles paved, bike-friendly
- **Emerald Pools (Lower)**: 1.2 miles, waterfalls (seasonal)
- **Canyon Junction Bridge**: 0.5 miles, Virgin River views

**Moderate**:
- **Angels Landing**: 5.4 miles, 1,488 feet elevation
  - **Permit required**: Reserve 3 months in advance or day-before lottery
  - Chains section: exposed, not for those with fear of heights
- **Observation Point**: 8 miles, 2,148 feet elevation
  - Better views than Angels Landing, fewer crowds
  - Currently accessible only from East Entrance (trail damage)

**Difficult**:
- **The Narrows**: Up to 16 miles, river hiking
  - Check flash flood forecast (NEVER hike if risk exists)
  - Rent dry suit, canyoneering shoes, walking stick
  - Best: July-September (warmest water)
- **West Rim Trail**: Top-down, multi-day, shuttle required

---

### Bryce Canyon Hikes

**Easy**:
- **Rim Trail**: 5.5 miles paved, connects viewpoints
- **Sunset Point to Sunrise Point**: 1 mile, hoodoo views

**Moderate**:
- **Navajo Loop**: 1.3 miles, through hoodoos, Wall Street section
- **Queens Garden**: 1.8 miles, easiest descent into canyon
- **Peek-a-Boo Loop**: 5.5 miles, combines with Navajo Loop

**Difficult**:
- **Fairyland Loop**: 8 miles, remote, fewer crowds
- **Under-the-Rim Trail**: 23 miles, multi-day, backcountry permit required

---

### Arches Hikes

**Easy**:
- **Park Avenue**: 1 mile, canyon wall walk
- **Balanced Rock**: 0.3 miles, loop
- **Double Arch**: 0.5 miles
- **The Windows**: 1 mile, four arches
- **Delicate Arch Viewpoint**: 0.5 miles, distant view

**Moderate**:
- **Delicate Arch**: 3 miles, 480 feet elevation
  - Go at sunrise or sunset (midday heat, crowds)
  - Bring headlamp if staying for sunset
- **Landscape Arch**: 1.6 miles, Devil's Garden
- **Skyline Arch**: 0.4 miles

**Difficult**:
- **Fiery Furnace**: 2 miles, maze, ranger tour or permit required
- **Devils Garden Primitive Loop**: 7.2 miles, seven arches

## SAFETY & CONSERVATION

### Desert Safety

**Heat**:
- Summer temperatures exceed 100°F in inner canyon
- Hike early morning or evening
- Rest in shade during midday
- Recognize heat exhaustion: headache, nausea, dizziness

**Water**:
- Carry 1 gallon (4 liters) per person per day
- Drink before you're thirsty
- Electrolyte supplements recommended
- Never rely on natural water sources without treatment

**Flash Floods**:
- Check weather forecast before entering slot canyons
- The Narrows: Never enter if flash flood risk exists
- Signs: Dark clouds, distant thunder, rising water, debris
- If flood comes: Move to high ground immediately

**Wildlife**:
- Elk, deer: Give space, do not feed
- Mountain lions: Rare, make noise, don't hike alone at dusk/dawn
- Rattlesnakes: Watch where you step, give space
- Ravens, squirrels: Do not feed (become aggressive)

---

### Leave No Trace

**Seven Principles**:
1. Plan ahead and prepare
2. Travel and camp on durable surfaces
3. Dispose of waste properly (pack it out)
4. Leave what you find (no artifacts, rocks, plants)
5. Minimize campfire impacts (use established fire rings)
6. Respect wildlife (observe from distance)
7. Be considerate of other visitors

**Specific to Canyon Country**:
- Stay on trails (cryptobiotic soil is fragile)
- Do not climb on arches or hoodoos
- Do not remove rocks, artifacts, or plants
- Pack out all trash (including toilet paper)
- Use established restrooms when available

---

### Permits & Reservations

**Required**:
- **Angels Landing (Zion)**: Permit required year-round
  - Seasonal lottery: 3 months in advance
  - Day-before lottery: 1 day in advance
  - Cost: $6 application + $3 per person if selected
- **Timed Entry (Arches)**: April-October
  - Reserve 1-60 days in advance: $2
  - Released at 8 AM MST on Recreation.gov
- **Backcountry Camping**: All parks require permits
  - Grand Canyon: 4 months in advance, competitive
  - Zion: Online, varies by trail
  - Bryce: First-come or reservable
  - Arches: Fiery Furnace, Devils Garden

**Not Required**:
- Day hiking (except Angels Landing)
- Regular campground reservations (but recommended)
- Park entrance (but America the Beautiful Pass recommended)

## ACCESSIBILITY

### Grand Canyon
- **South Rim**: Mostly accessible, paved Rim Trail
- **Viewpoints**: Mather, Yavapai, Hopi Point accessible
- **Visitor Centers**: Fully accessible
- **Lodging**: Accessible rooms available (request in advance)
- **Shuttle**: All buses wheelchair accessible

### Zion
- **Pa'rus Trail**: Paved, wheelchair accessible
- **Visitor Center**: Fully accessible
- **Shuttle**: All buses wheelchair accessible
- **Lodging**: Accessible rooms at Zion Lodge

### Bryce Canyon
- **Rim Trail**: Partially accessible (Sunrise to Sunset Points)
- **Visitor Center**: Fully accessible
- **Viewpoints**: Most accessible
- **Lodging**: Accessible rooms available

### Arches
- **Park Avenue, Balanced Rock, Windows**: Accessible trails
- **Delicate Arch Viewpoint**: Accessible (does not reach arch)
- **Visitor Center**: Fully accessible
- **Limited**: Many trails are not accessible due to terrain

## FINAL TIPS

1. **Book Early**: Lodging inside parks books 12-13 months in advance
2. **Arrive Early**: Parks fill by 9 AM in peak season
3. **Download Offline Maps**: Cell service is spotty to non-existent
4. **Carry Cash**: Some small vendors don't take cards
5. **Respect Native Land**: You're visiting someone's homeland
6. **Pack Layers**: Temperature swings of 30-40°F daily
7. **Break in Boots**: Don't wear new shoes on long hikes
8. **Leave No Trace**: Pack out everything you pack in
9. **Check Road Closures**: Weather can close roads unexpectedly
10. **Slow Down**: This isn't a checklist. It's a pilgrimage.
