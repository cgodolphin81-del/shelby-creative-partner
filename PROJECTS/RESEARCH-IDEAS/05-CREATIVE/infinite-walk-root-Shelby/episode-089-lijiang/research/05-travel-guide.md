## GETTING THERE

### International Travelers

**Step 1: Fly to China**
- Major hubs: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Kunming
- Visa: Apply in advance OR use 240-hour visa-free transit if eligible

**Step 2: Reach Kunming (Yunnan Province Capital)**
- **By Air:** Direct flights from most major Chinese cities, some international
- **By Train:** High-speed rail from other Chinese cities

**Step 3: Kunming to Lijiang**
- **By Air:** 1 hour, multiple daily flights, CNY 400-800
- **By Train:** High-speed rail, 3-3.5 hours, CNY 220-280 (RECOMMENDED)
- **By Bus:** Overnight bus, 8-9 hours, CNY 150-200 (not recommended)

### From Within China

**From Dali:**
- **Train:** High-speed rail, 1.5-2 hours, CNY 80-120 (BEST OPTION)
- **Bus:** 2.5-3 hours, CNY 50-80

**From Shangri-La:**
- **Bus:** 2.5-4 hours via new highway, CNY 80-120
- **Private Car:** CNY 400-600, more flexible
- **Train:** New railway, 1h 20min, check current schedules

## ALTITUDE SICKNESS PREVENTION

### Understanding the Risk

**Lijiang:** 2,400m (7,900 ft) - Moderate risk
**Shangri-La:** 3,280m (10,760 ft) - Significant risk
**Jade Dragon Snow Mountain:** Up to 5,600m (18,360 ft) - High risk

### Symptoms
- Headache
- Nausea/vomiting
- Dizziness
- Shortness of breath
- Fatigue
- Loss of appetite
- Difficulty sleeping

### Prevention Strategies

**Before You Go:**
- Consult doctor, especially if you have heart/lung conditions
- Consider Rhodiola (红景天) supplement - start 1-2 weeks before trip
- Arrive well-rested, not exhausted

**Upon Arrival:**
- **Day 1-2 in Lijiang:** Take it easy, no strenuous activity
- **Hydrate:** Drink more water than usual (3-4 liters/day)
- **Avoid Alcohol:** First 48 hours especially
- **Eat Light:** Carbohydrate-rich meals
- **Sleep:** Get plenty of rest
- **Don't Wash Hair:** Traditional advice, may help avoid cold first day

**Ascending to Shangri-La:**
- Spend at least 1-2 nights in Lijiang first
- Ascend gradually (bus journey allows some acclimatization)
- First day in Shangri-La: light activity only
- Consider spending night at intermediate elevation

**At High Altitude (Jade Dragon Snow Mountain):**
- Ascend slowly via cable car (don't rush)
- Limit time at highest elevations
- Descend if symptoms worsen
- Oxygen canisters available for purchase (CNY 50-100)

### If Symptoms Occur
- **Mild:** Rest, hydrate, don't ascend further
- **Moderate:** Descend 500-1,000m, rest
- **Severe:** Descend immediately, seek medical attention
- **Medication:** Diamox (acetazolamide) - consult doctor before trip

### Who's at Higher Risk?
- Previous altitude sickness
- Rapid ascent
- Strenuous exercise on arrival
- Alcohol consumption
- Respiratory conditions
- Sleeping at high altitude

**Important:** Altitude sickness can affect anyone, regardless of fitness level. Even Olympic athletes get it. Listen to your body.

## CULTURAL ETIQUETTE

### General Chinese Etiquette
- **Greetings:** Nod or slight bow; handshakes becoming common
- **Respect Elders:** Deference to older people
- **Saving Face:** Avoid public criticism or confrontation
- **Gift Giving:** Offer/receive with both hands
- **Chopsticks:** Don't stick vertically in rice (resembles funeral incense)

### Naxi Culture (Lijiang)
- **Dongba Culture:** Respect the ancient pictographic script and traditions
- **Photography:** Ask before photographing people, especially elders
- **Homes:** Remove shoes if entering traditional Naxi homes
- **Festivals:** Welcome to observe, ask before participating

### Tibetan Culture (Shangri-La)
- **Temples/Monasteries:**
  - Dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees)
  - Remove hats and sunglasses
  - Speak quietly
  - Ask permission before photographing monks or sacred objects
  - Don't point feet at Buddha statues or altars
  
- **Prayer Wheels:**
  - Spin clockwise only
  - Don't touch others' hands while spinning
  
- **Circumambulation (Kora):**
  - Walk clockwise around sacred sites
  - Don't cut across someone's path during prayer
  
- **Photography:**
  - Always ask permission before photographing people
  - Some monasteries prohibit photography inside
  - Offer small payment if someone poses for you (CNY 5-20)

- **Sacred Sites:**
  - Don't climb on religious structures
  - Don't touch religious artifacts without permission
  - Meili Snow Mountain is too sacred to climb

### Tipping
- **Restaurants:** Not expected, but 5-10% appreciated in upscale places
- **Guides:** CNY 50-100/day for good service
- **Drivers:** CNY 30-50/day
- **Hotel Porters:** CNY 10-20 per bag

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money
- **Currency:** Chinese Yuan (CNY/RMB)
- **Cash vs. Card:** Cash still king in smaller towns; WeChat Pay/Alipay ubiquitous
- **ATMs:** Widely available in Lijiang and Shangri-La
- **Exchange:** Better rates in China than abroad; bring USD/EUR as backup
- **Budget:** CNY 300-600/day for budget; CNY 600-1,200/day mid-range; CNY 1,200+/day luxury

### Connectivity
- **SIM Cards:** China Mobile/Unicom/Telecom; bring passport
- **WiFi:** Available in most hotels, cafes, restaurants
- **VPN:** Many Western apps blocked (Google, Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp); VPN recommended
- **Useful Apps:**
  - WeChat (messaging, payments)
  - Alipay (payments)
  - Didi (ride-hailing)
  - Pleco (Chinese dictionary)
  - Google Translate (download offline)
  - Maps.me (offline maps)

### Packing List

**Essentials:**
- Passport + visa + copies
- Travel insurance documents
- Cash (CNY) + credit/debit cards
- Phone + charger + power bank
- Camera + extra batteries
- Daypack for excursions

**Clothing (Layering is Key):**
- Lightweight base layers
- Fleece or sweater
- Waterproof/windproof jacket
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Hiking boots (if trekking)
- Warm hat and gloves (for Jade Dragon Snow Mountain)
- Sunglasses + sunscreen (high altitude UV)
- Scarf or buff (dust, wind)

**Health:**
- Personal medications + copies of prescriptions
- Altitude sickness medication (Diamox - consult doctor)
- Rhodiola supplement
- Pain relievers
- Stomach medication
- Bandages/blister treatment
- Hand sanitizer
- Face masks (still common in China)

**Miscellaneous:**
- Reusable water bottle
- Snacks for travel days
- Tissues/toilet paper (not always available)
- Small backpack for day trips
- Camera rain cover
- Power adapter (China uses Type A, C, or I; 220V)

### Health & Safety
- **Water:** Bottled or filtered only
- **Food:** Generally safe; busy restaurants = fresh food
- **Medical:** Hospitals in Lijiang and Shangri-La; serious issues require Kunming or evacuation
- **Travel Insurance:** Highly recommended; ensure covers high-altitude activities
- **Emergency Numbers:** 110 (police), 120 (ambulance), 119 (fire)
- **Embassy:** Know your country's embassy/consulate contacts

### Best Time to Visit

| Season | Months | Weather | Pros | Cons |
|--------|--------|---------|------|------|
| **Spring** | March-May | Mild, blooming | Flowers, comfortable temps | Can be crowded |
| **Summer** | June-August | Cool, rainy | Green landscapes, festivals | Rain, largest crowds |
| **Autumn** | September-November | Clear, mild | Best weather, clear mountain views | Peak season, higher prices |
| **Winter** | December-February | Cold, dry, sunny | Snow scenery, few tourists | Very cold, some closures |

**RECOMMENDED:** September-October (best weather) or May-June (flowers, fewer crowds)

## RESPONSIBLE TOURISM

### How to Travel Responsibly

**Environmental:**
- Don't litter (carry out trash in natural areas)
- Use reusable water bottles
- Respect wildlife (don't feed animals)
- Stay on marked trails

**Cultural:**
- Ask before photographing people
- Dress modestly at religious sites
- Learn a few local phrases
- Support local businesses

**Economic:**
- Bargain respectfully (it's expected, but don't be aggressive)
- Tip for good service
- Buy local handicrafts (but verify authenticity)

**Social:**
- Don't give money/candy directly to children (encourages begging)
- Support reputable organizations if you want to help
- Respect local customs even if they differ from yours
