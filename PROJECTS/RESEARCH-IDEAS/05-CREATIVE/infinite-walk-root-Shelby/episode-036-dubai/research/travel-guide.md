## GETTING THERE

### Flights
**Airport:** Dubai International Airport (DXB)
- One of the world's busiest international hubs
- 15+ minutes from downtown Dubai

**Major Airlines:**
- **Emirates:** Flag carrier, extensive global network
- **FlyDubai:** Budget option, regional focus
- **Qatar Airways:** Via Doha (often competitive pricing)
- **Etihad:** Via Abu Dhabi

**Flight Times (approximate):**
- London: 7 hours
- New York: 12-14 hours (often via Europe)
- Sydney: 14 hours
- Mumbai: 3 hours
- Singapore: 7 hours
- Frankfurt: 6 hours

**Best Deals:** Book 2-3 months ahead. January-February and September-October often have sales.

### Visas
**Visa-Free Entry (90 days):**
- EU citizens, UK, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, and 50+ other countries

**Visa on Arrival (30 days):**
- Many countries including India, Pakistan, Philippines, South Africa

**Pre-Arranged Visa Required:**
- Check UAE government website for your nationality

**Cost:** Free for visa-free countries. AED 350-500 ($95-135) for tourist visas.

### From Airport to City
**Metro:** AED 8-12, 45 minutes to downtown
- Red Line connects Terminal 3 to Burj Khalifa/Dubai Mall
- Clean, efficient, women's cars available (first carriage)

**Taxi:** AED 50-100 ($14-27), 20-30 minutes
- Metered, air-conditioned, widely available
- Careem/Uber apps work well

**Hotel Shuttle:** Many hotels offer complimentary pickup
- Confirm in advance

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### Peak Season (November - March)
**Weather:** 20-30°C (68-86°F) - Perfect
**Pros:**
- Ideal weather for outdoor activities
- All attractions open
- Desert safaris comfortable
- Outdoor dining pleasant

**Cons:**
- Highest prices (50-100% premium)
- Crowded attractions
- Book weeks/months ahead for popular experiences

**Best for:** First-time visitors, outdoor activities, events

### Shoulder Season (April-May, October)
**Weather:** 25-35°C (77-95°F) - Warm to hot
**Pros:**
- Lower prices than peak
- Fewer crowds
- Still manageable for most activities

**Cons:**
- Midday heat can be intense
- Some outdoor activities limited

**Best for:** Budget-conscious travelers, indoor attractions

### Low Season (June - September)
**Weather:** 35-45°C (95-113°F) - Extreme heat
**Pros:**
- Lowest prices (up to 60% off hotels)
- Minimal crowds
- Summer festivals and sales

**Cons:**
- Outdoor activities nearly impossible midday
- Desert safaris very hot
- Some outdoor attractions closed

**Best for:** Budget travelers, mall/indoor focus, business travel

### Special Events
- **Dubai Shopping Festival:** January (sales, fireworks, events)
- **Dubai Food Festival:** February-March
- **Ramadan:** Dates vary (month of fasting, special atmosphere)
- **Eid al-Fitr:** End of Ramadan (celebrations, family time)
- **Dubai Summer Surprises:** June-August (indoor events, sales)
- **National Day:** December 2 (fireworks, parades, patriotism)
- **New Year's Eve:** December 31 (world-famous Burj Khalifa fireworks)

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Understanding Emirati Culture

**Population Breakdown:**
- Emirati nationals: ~10%
- Expatriates: ~90% (from 200+ nationalities)
- Largest groups: Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi, Filipino, British

**Religion:**
- Islam is the official religion
- Call to prayer heard 5 times daily
- Respect mosque visiting hours and dress codes
- During Ramadan: No eating/drinking in public during daylight

**Language:**
- Arabic: Official language
- English: Widely spoken (business, tourism, signage)
- Hindi/Urdu/Tagalog: Common among expat communities

### Dress Code

**General Guidelines:**
- **Malls/Public Areas:** Shoulders and knees covered (both genders)
- **Beaches/Resorts:** Swimwear acceptable
- **Old Dubai/Souks:** More conservative dress appreciated
- **Mosques:** Women must cover hair, arms, legs (abayas often provided)
- **Fine Dining/Clubs:** Smart casual to formal

**Women:**
- Loose-fitting clothing recommended
- Carry a scarf for mosque visits
- Swimwear: One-piece or modest bikinis at resort beaches

**Men:**
- No sleeveless shirts in malls/government buildings
- Shorts acceptable in casual settings (knee-length)
- Long pants for fine dining, business, mosques

**What NOT to Wear:**
- Revealing clothing in traditional areas
- Clothing with offensive slogans/images
- See-through fabrics without underlayers

### Social Etiquette

**Greetings:**
- Handshakes common (men with men, women with women)
- Men wait for women to extend hand first
- "As-salamu alaykum" (peace be upon you) - traditional greeting

**Respect:**
- Ask before photographing people, especially Emirati women
- Public displays of affection: Keep minimal (holding hands OK, kissing not)
- Swearing/public anger: Can result in fines or arrest
- Drinking alcohol: Only in licensed venues (hotels, bars, clubs)

**Ramadan Specific:**
- No eating, drinking, smoking in public during daylight
- Many restaurants close during day, open after sunset
- Evening atmosphere festive and special
- Non-Muslims exempt from fasting but must respect public rules

**Photography:**
- Government buildings: Generally prohibited
- People: Always ask permission first
- Drones: Require special permit (apply through GCAA)

## GETTING AROUND

### Metro
**Lines:** Red Line (airport to Marina), Green Line (Deira/Bur Dubai)
**Hours:** 5 AM - midnight (extended on weekends)
**Cost:** AED 3-7.50 per trip (zones)
**Nol Card:** Reusable transit card (AED 25 minimum)
**Pros:** Clean, efficient, air-conditioned, women's cars available
**Cons:** Limited coverage, can be crowded during rush hour

### Taxi / Ride-Share
**Companies:** Dubai Taxi, Careem, Uber, RTA Taxi
**Cost:** AED 12 flag fall + AED 2/km
**Pros:** Door-to-door, air-conditioned, widely available
**Cons:** Traffic can be heavy, surge pricing on apps

### Bus
**Coverage:** Extensive network throughout city
**Cost:** AED 2-7.50 per trip
**Pros:** Cheap, reaches areas metro doesn't
**Cons:** Slower, can be crowded, less tourist-friendly

### Abra (Traditional Boat)
**Routes:** Dubai Creek (Deira to Bur Dubai)
**Cost:** AED 1 (public), AED 100+ (private tour)
**Pros:** Authentic experience, scenic, cheap
**Cons:** Limited routes, basic vessels

### Car Rental
**Cost:** AED 100-400/day depending on vehicle
**Requirements:** Valid license (international permit recommended), credit card, age 21+
**Pros:** Flexibility, good for desert/outside city
**Cons:** Traffic, parking can be challenging, Salik (toll) fees

**Note:** Driving is on the right side of the road

### Walking
**Feasible Areas:**
- Dubai Marina Walk
- JBR (Jumeirah Beach Residence)
- Downtown Dubai (Burj Khalifa area)
- Al Fahidi Historical District
- The Beach at JBR

**Not Walkable:**
- Most of the city is car-dependent
- Distances between attractions are large
- Summer heat makes walking dangerous

## ACCOMMODATION AREAS

### Downtown Dubai
**Best for:** First-time visitors, Burj Khalifa access, luxury
**Hotels:** Armani Hotel, Address Downtown, Palace Downtown
**Price Range:** AED 600-2,000+/night
**Pros:** Central location, walkable to Dubai Mall/Fountain, iconic views
**Cons:** Expensive, can be crowded

### Dubai Marina / JBR
**Best for:** Beach access, nightlife, young professionals
**Hotels:** Marina hotels, JBR beachfront resorts
**Price Range:** AED 400-1,500/night
**Pros:** Beach, walkable promenade, restaurants, metro access
**Cons:** Further from old Dubai, traffic

### Palm Jumeirah
**Best for:** Luxury resorts, honeymoon, relaxation
**Hotels:** Atlantis, Waldorf Astoria, One&Only
**Price Range:** AED 1,000-5,000+/night
**Pros:** Exclusive, beach, unique location, resort amenities
**Cons:** Expensive, requires transport to leave Palm

### Deira / Bur Dubai (Old Dubai)
**Best for:** Budget travelers, culture, authentic experience
**Hotels:** Heritage hotels, budget chains
**Price Range:** AED 150-500/night
**Pros:** Affordable, near souks, cultural sites, metro access
**Cons:** Older properties, less glamorous, crowded

### Business Bay
**Best for:** Business travelers, modern hotels, value
**Hotels:** Mid-range to upscale chains
**Price Range:** AED 300-800/night
**Pros:** Central, good value, modern properties, metro access
**Cons:** Less character, mostly business-oriented

### Jumeirah
**Best for:** Families, beach, upscale residential feel
**Hotels:** Jumeirah Beach Hotel, Madinat Jumeirah
**Price Range:** AED 800-3,000/night
**Pros:** Beach access, family-friendly, near Burj Al Arab
**Cons:** Expensive, requires transport

## SAFETY & HEALTH

### Safety
Dubai is one of the safest cities in the world.

**Low Crime:** Violent crime is extremely rare. Petty theft uncommon but possible in crowded areas.

**Women Traveling Alone:** Generally very safe. Standard precautions apply (avoid isolated areas late at night, be aware of surroundings).

**Emergency Numbers:**
- Police: 999
- Ambulance: 998
- Fire: 997
- General Emergency: 112

### Health
**Healthcare:** Excellent quality, but expensive for visitors.

**Travel Insurance:** Highly recommended. Many policies cover UAE.

**Vaccinations:** No special requirements for most travelers. Routine vaccines up to date.

**Heat Safety (Summer):**
- Stay hydrated (drink 3-4 liters/day)
- Avoid outdoor activities 11 AM - 4 PM
- Watch for heat exhaustion symptoms
- Air conditioning everywhere (bring light sweater)

**Medications:**
- Bring prescription medications in original packaging
- Some common medications (codeine, some ADHD meds) are controlled substances—check before traveling
- Pharmacies widely available

### Scams to Avoid
- **Taxi meters:** Ensure driver uses meter or agree on price beforehand
- **Gold/Souk purchases:** Verify weight and purity, get receipts
- **"Free" tours:** Often lead to high-pressure sales
- **Currency exchange:** Use authorized exchange offices, not street dealers

## CONNECTIVITY

### SIM Cards / eSIM
**Airport:** Counters in arrivals hall (Du, Etisalat, Virgin Mobile)
**Cost:** AED 50-150 for tourist packages (1-4 weeks, 5-20GB data)
**Coverage:** Excellent 4G/5G throughout city
**eSIM:** Available for compatible phones (download before arrival)

### WiFi
- **Free WiFi:** Malls, hotels, cafes, Dubai Metro, many public areas
- **Quality:** Generally excellent
- **Registration:** Some networks require UAE phone number for SMS verification

### Apps to Download
- **Careem:** Ride-hailing (like Uber, regional)
- **The Entertainer:** Discounts on dining and attractions
- **Dubai Now:** Government services, bill payments
- **RTA Dubai:** Public transport info, taxi booking
- **OpenTable / Zomato:** Restaurant reservations

## MONEY & SHOPPING

### Currency
**UAE Dirham (AED)**
- Pegged to USD: ~3.67 AED = 1 USD
- Coins: 25, 50 fils, 1 AED
- Notes: 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500, 1,000 AED

### Payment Methods
- **Credit Cards:** Widely accepted (Visa, Mastercard, Amex)
- **Cash:** Needed for souks, small vendors, tips, abra
- **ATMs:** Widely available, international cards work
- **Contactless:** Apple Pay, Google Pay common in chains

### Shopping
**Malls:**
- Dubai Mall (world's largest, 1,200+ stores)
- Mall of the Emirates (ski slope inside)
- Ibn Battuta Mall (themed courts)

**Souks:**
- Gold Souk (Deira)
- Spice Souk (Deira)
- Textile Souk (Bur Dubai)
- Perfume Souk (Deira)

**Bargaining:**
- Expected in souks (start at 50% of asking price)
- Not appropriate in malls/fixed-price stores
- Be respectful and friendly

**Tax-Free Shopping:**
- Tourists can claim VAT refund (5%) at airport
- Minimum purchase: AED 250 per receipt
- Look for "Tax-Free" stickers in stores

- Yes: Na'am
- No: La
- How much?: Kam?
- Water: Ma'

- Gulf Standard Time (GST)
- UTC +4 hours
- No daylight saving time

### Electrical Outlets
- Type G (British-style 3-pin)
- Voltage: 220-240V
- Adapters widely available

### Weekend
- **Official Weekend:** Saturday-Sunday (changed in 2022)
- **Some businesses:** Still closed Friday mornings (prayer time)
