# Episode 081: Jaipur, India - Location Research

## The Pink City: An Overview

**Founded:** 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II  
**Nickname:** The Pink City  
**Location:** Rajasthan, India (26.9124° N, 75.7873° E)  
**Best Time to Visit:** October to March (15-30°C / 59-86°F)  
**UNESCO World Heritage Sites:** Amber Fort, Jantar Mantar  

---

## Key Attractions

### 1. Amber Fort (Amer Fort)

**Built:** 1592 by Maharaja Man Singh I  
**Architecture:** Rajput-Mughal fusion  
**Location:** 11 km from Jaipur city center  

**Historical Significance:**
- Served as the capital of the Amber kingdom for centuries
- Built on a hilltop overlooking Maota Lake
- Combines Rajput strength with Mughal elegance
- UNESCO World Heritage Site (part of Hill Forts of Rajasthan)

**Key Features:**
- **Suraj Pol (Sun Gate):** Main entrance facing east
- **Diwan-e-Aam:** Hall of Public Audience
- **Diwan-e-Khas:** Hall of Private Audience
- **Sheesh Mahal:** Mirror Palace with intricate mirror work
- **Sukh Niwas:** Hall of Pleasure with water channels
- **Siladevi Temple:** Ancient Hindu temple within the fort

**Visitor Experience:**
- Elephant rides available (ethical concerns noted)
- Walking or jeep options to reach the fort
- Light and sound show in the evening
- Best visited early morning to avoid crowds
- Allow 2-3 hours for exploration

**Photography Notes:**
- Golden hour light on sandstone
- Reflections in Maota Lake
- Intricate mirror work interiors (no flash)
- Panoramic city views from ramparts

---

### 2. Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds)

**Built:** 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh  
**Architecture:** Rajput style with Mughal influence  
**Height:** 5 stories (50 feet / 15 meters)  
**Windows:** 953 small jharokhas (lattice windows)  

**Historical Significance:**
- Designed like Lord Krishna's crown
- Built for royal ladies to observe street life while remaining unseen (purdah system)
- No foundation - remarkable structural engineering
- Ramps instead of stairs for palanquin access

**Key Features:**
- Honeycomb façade in pink sandstone
- Intricate lattice work for ventilation
- Natural cooling system (Bernoulli's principle)
- Views over Johari Bazaar from upper floors

**Visitor Experience:**
- Best visited at sunrise for golden light
- Enter from the back (main façade is street-side only)
- Climb to upper floors for bazaar views
- Allow 1 hour for visit
- Museum inside showcasing royal artifacts

**Photography Notes:**
- Early morning light on pink façade
- Street-level shots with rickshaws
- Interior lattice patterns
- Aerial views from nearby cafés

---

### 3. City Palace

**Built:** 1727-1731 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II  
**Architecture:** Blend of Rajput, Mughal, and European styles  
**Status:** Partially still royal residence  

**Historical Significance:**
- Seat of the Rajput rulers of Jaipur
- Shows transition from Amber to Jaipur as capital
- Still home to the royal family (Chandra Mahal)
- Houses Maharaja Sawai Man Singh II Museum

**Key Features:**
- **Mubarak Mahal:** Now the museum, displaying royal costumes
- **Maharani's Palace:** Weapons collection and painted ceilings
- **Diwan-e-Khas:** Hall of Private Audience
- **Diwan-e-Aam:** Hall of Public Audience
- **Pritam Niwas Chowk:** Courtyard with four seasonal gates
  - Peacock Gate (South) - Autumn, dedicated to Vishnu
  - Lotus Gate (West) - Summer, dedicated to Lakshmi
  - Leheriya Gate (East) - Spring, dedicated to Ganesha
  - Rose Gate (North) - Winter, dedicated to Shiva

**Visitor Experience:**
- Audio guide or live guide recommended
- Allow 2-3 hours
- Photography restrictions in some areas
- Dress modestly (working palace)

**Photography Notes:**
- Ornate gate details (each season gate is unique)
- Courtyard symmetry
- Museum artifacts (where permitted)
- Palace guards in traditional attire

---

### 4. Jantar Mantar

**Built:** 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II  
**Type:** Astronomical observatory  
**UNESCO Status:** World Heritage Site (2010)  
**Name Origin:** Sanskrit "yantra" (instrument) + "mantra" (calculate)  

**Historical Significance:**
- One of five observatories built by Jai Singh II
- Largest stone observatory in the world
- Demonstrates advanced astronomical knowledge
- Instruments still accurate today

**Key Instruments:**
- **Samrat Yantra:** World's largest sundial (90 feet tall)
  - Tells time with 2-second accuracy
  - Casts shadow to indicate time
- **Jai Prakash Yantra:** Two hemispherical bowls for celestial mapping
- **Ram Yantra:** Measures altitude and azimuth of stars
- **Nadivalaya Yantra:** Equatorial sundial
- **Misra Yantra:** Compound instrument for various measurements

**Visitor Experience:**
- Guide highly recommended (instruments are complex)
- Allow 1-2 hours
- Best in morning or late afternoon light
- Evening light and sound show available

**Photography Notes:**
- Geometric shadows at different times
- Wide-angle shots of entire complex
- Close-ups of instrument details
- Sunset silhouettes

---

### 5. Jal Mahal (Water Palace)

**Built:** 18th century by Maharaja Jai Singh II  
**Location:** Middle of Man Sagar Lake  
**Architecture:** Rajput style in red sandstone  

**Historical Significance:**
- Used as royal summer resort and hunting lodge
- Four floors submerged underwater (only top floor visible)
- Not open to public (viewing from promenade only)

**Key Features:**
- Appears to float on the lake
- Beautiful at sunrise and sunset
- Surrounded by Aravalli hills
- Bird watching opportunities

**Visitor Experience:**
- View from lakeside promenade
- Photography stop (15-30 minutes)
- Nearby walking paths and seating
- Local vendors for snacks and souvenirs

**Photography Notes:**
- Reflection in still water (early morning)
- Golden hour glow
- Palace framed by hills
- Night illumination (limited)

---

### 6. Nahargarh Fort

**Built:** 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II  
**Location:** Aravalli hills overlooking Jaipur  
**Name Meaning:** "Abode of Tigers"  

**Historical Significance:**
- Built as royal retreat and hunting lodge
- Part of Jaipur's defensive ring (with Amber and Jaigarh)
- Less polished than Amber, more atmospheric

**Key Features:**
- **Madhavendra Bhawan:** Palace with rooms for queens
- **Padao Restaurant:** Rooftop dining with city views
- **Wax Museum:** Royal figures and celebrities
- **Sculpture Park:** Modern art installation

**Visitor Experience:**
- Best for sunset views
- Allow 1-2 hours
- Can combine with Amber Fort visit
- Café on-site

**Photography Notes:**
- Panoramic city views
- Sunset over Jaipur
- Fort architecture against sky
- Light trails of city below

## The Pink City: History & Significance

### Why Pink?

**The Story:**
- In 1876, Maharaja Ram Singh ordered the entire walled city painted pink
- Purpose: To welcome the Prince of Wales (future King Edward VII)
- Pink symbolized hospitality in Rajasthani culture
- The tradition continues today

**Preservation:**
- Jaipur Municipal Corporation enforces pink color regulations
- Buildings within historic walls must maintain pink hue
- City repainted every few years for uniformity
- Symbol of heritage preservation and city pride

### City Planning

**Vision:**
- Founded 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II
- Designed by architect Vidyadhar Bhattacharya
- India's first planned city
- Based on Vastu Shastra (ancient Indian architectural science)
- Nine-grid layout representing nine planets

**Seven Gates:**
1. Ajmeri Gate (West)
2. Sanganeri Gate (South)
3. Chandpole Gate (East)
4. Zorawar Singh Gate (North)
5. Ganga Pole (North-East)
6. Kishan Pole (East)
7. Dhruv Pole (North)

## Rajasthani Culture

### Traditional Attire

**Women:**
- **Ghagra:** Flowing embroidered skirt
- **Choli:** Fitted blouse
- **Odhni:** Veil/dupatta with mirror work
- **Jewelry:** Silver anklets, nathni (nose ring), borla (headpiece), lac bangles

**Men:**
- **Pagri:** Turban (style indicates region and status)
- **Angarkha:** Robe-like tunic
- **Dhoti:** Traditional lower garment
- **Mojari:** Embroidered leather shoes

### Folk Music & Dance

**Dances:**
- **Ghoomar:** Women's graceful swirling dance (Marwar region)
- **Kalbeliya:** Snake-charmer community dance (UNESCO recognized)
- **Bhavai:** Balancing pots while dancing
- **Chari:** Women balance brass pots with lit lamps

**Music:**
- **Instruments:** Sarangi, Ravanahatha, Khartal, Morchang, Dholak, Algoza
- **Communities:** Manganiyars and Langas (hereditary musicians)
- **Songs:** Maand (royal ballads), Panihari (water-fetching songs)

### Cuisine

**Signature Dishes:**
- **Dal Baati Churma:** Lentils, baked wheat balls, sweet crushed wheat
- **Laal Maas:** Spicy red meat curry (originally game meat)
- **Gatte ki Sabzi:** Gram flour dumplings in yogurt gravy
- **Ker Sangri:** Desert beans and berries (preserved for scarcity)
- **Mawa Kachori:** Sweet pastry filled with reduced milk
- **Ghewar:** Honeycomb-shaped sweet dessert

**Street Food:**
- Pyaaz Kachori (onion pastry)
- Mirchi Bada (stuffed chili fritter)
- Lassi (yogurt drink)

### Festivals

**Major Celebrations:**
- **Pushkar Camel Fair:** November (camel trading, folk performances)
- **Teej:** July-August (monsoon festival, women's celebration)
- **Gangaur:** March-April (Goddess Gauri, marital bliss)
- **Desert Festival:** February, Jaisalmer (desert culture showcase)
- **Jaipur Literature Festival:** January (global authors and thinkers)
- **Elephant Festival:** March, Jaipur (decorated elephants)

### Arts & Handicrafts

**Jaipur Specialties:**
- **Blue Pottery:** Distinctive turquoise-glazed ceramics
- **Block Printing:** Bagru and Sanganer prints
- **Bandhani:** Tie-dye textiles
- **Kundan & Meenakari:** Gemstone jewelry
- **Miniature Paintings:** Mughal-Rajput style
- **Leather Mojari:** Embroidered shoes
- **Kathputli:** String puppets

**Shopping Areas:**
- **Johari Bazaar:** Jewelry and gemstones
- **Bapu Bazaar:** Textiles, mojris, handicrafts
- **Tripolia Bazaar:** Lac bangles, brass items

## Practical Information

### Getting There

**By Air:**
- Jaipur International Airport (JAI)
- 1-hour flight from Delhi
- Direct flights from major Indian cities
- International connections from Middle East and Southeast Asia

**By Train:**
- Jaipur Junction (major rail hub)
- Shatabdi Express from Delhi (4-5 hours)
- Connections from Mumbai, Kolkata, Bangalore

**By Road:**
- NH48 connects Delhi (280 km, 4-5 hours)
- Regular bus services from nearby cities
- Private car hire available

### Getting Around

**Options:**
- Auto-rickshaws (bargain fares)
- App cabs (Uber, Ola)
- Cycle rickshaws (old city)
- Private car with driver
- Walking (old city areas)

### Accommodation

**Luxury:**
- Rambagh Palace (former royal residence)
- Raj Palace (heritage hotel)
- Samode Haveli (boutique heritage)
- Fairmont Jaipur (modern luxury)

**Mid-Range:**
- Heritage havelis in old city
- Boutique hotels near City Palace
- Chain hotels in newer areas

**Budget:**
- Guesthouses in old city
- Hostels near tourist areas
- Homestays with local families

### Safety

**General:**
- Generally safe for tourists
- Standard precautions in crowded markets
- Use trusted transportation
- Avoid isolated areas at night
- Keep valuables secure

**Solo Travelers:**
- Jaipur is considered safe for solo female travelers
- Dress modestly
- Stay in well-reviewed accommodations
- Share itinerary with someone

### Cultural Etiquette

**Do:**
- Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered)
- Remove shoes before entering temples/homes
- Use right hand for eating and passing items
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Respect religious sites

**Don't:**
- Don't touch religious artifacts without permission
- Don't photograph military installations
- Don't public displays of affection
- Don't litter (especially at monuments)
