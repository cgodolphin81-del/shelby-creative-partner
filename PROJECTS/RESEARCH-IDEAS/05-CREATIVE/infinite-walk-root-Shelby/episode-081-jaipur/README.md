## Episode Overview

### The Story
Episode 081 takes listeners on an audio walk through Jaipur, India—the Pink City. Founded in 1727 by an astronomer-king who designed his capital according to planetary positions, Jaipur is a city of intention, legacy, and radical hospitality.

### Key Themes
- **Legacy:** Building for generations you'll never meet
- **Welcome:** The color pink as a 150-year promise of hospitality
- **Ingenuity:** Hawa Mahal's 953 windows as a solution, not a prison
- **Precision:** Jantar Mantar's stone instruments, accurate since 1734
- **Continuity:** A living palace where royalty still resides

### Featured Locations
1. **Amber Fort** - Hilltop fortress, Mirror Palace
2. **Hawa Mahal** - Palace of 953 Winds
3. **City Palace** - Living royal residence
4. **Jantar Mantar** - Ancient astronomical observatory
5. **Jal Mahal** - Floating water palace
6. **Nahargarh Fort** - Sunset viewpoints
7. **Pink City Bazaars** - Johari, Bapu, Tripolia

## Key Statistics

### Historical Facts
- **Founded:** 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II
- **Pink Color:** Adopted 1876 to welcome Prince of Wales
- **Hawa Mahal:** Built 1799, 953 lattice windows
- **Jantar Mantar:** Built 1734, world's largest sundial (90 ft)
- **Amber Fort:** Built 1592, capital before Jaipur
