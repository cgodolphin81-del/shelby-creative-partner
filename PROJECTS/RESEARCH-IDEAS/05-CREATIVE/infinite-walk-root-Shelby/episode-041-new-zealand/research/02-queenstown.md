# Queenstown / Tāhuna

## Overview

Queenstown, known in Māori as Tāhuna ("shallow bay"), is New Zealand's adventure capital and one of the world's premier tourist destinations. Nestled on the shores of Lake Wakatipu and surrounded by the Remarkables mountain range, it offers an unparalleled combination of natural beauty and adrenaline-pumping activities.

## Geography & Setting

### Location
- **Region:** Otago, South Island
- **Coordinates:** 45.0312° S, 168.6626° E
- **Elevation:** 310 meters (1,017 feet) above sea level
- **Lake:** Lake Wakatipu (84 km / 52 miles long, Z-shaped)
- **Mountain Ranges:** The Remarkables, Cecil Peak, Ben Lomond

### Lake Wakatipu
- **Length:** 84 kilometers (52 miles)
- **Depth:** 380 meters (1,247 feet) — third deepest lake in New Zealand
- **Formation:** Glacial, formed during ice ages
- **Seiche Effect:** Water level rises and falls 20 cm every 5 minutes due to atmospheric pressure
- **Māori Legend:** Formed by the body of a giant burned by a tribal chief

## Climate

### Seasonal Overview
- **Summer (Dec-Feb):** 20-25°C (68-77°F), long daylight hours (until 10 PM)
- **Autumn (Mar-May):** 10-20°C (50-68°F), stunning golden colors, harvest season
- **Winter (Jun-Aug):** 0-10°C (32-50°F), snow on mountains, ski season
- **Spring (Sep-Nov):** 8-18°C (46-64°F), lambs in fields, flowers blooming

### Weather Characteristics
- **Four Seasons in One Day:** Rapid weather changes common
- **Wind:** Nor'wester winds can be strong
- **Rainfall:** Moderate, 750mm annually
- **Snow:** Winter snow on surrounding peaks, rare in town itself

## Adventure Activities

### Bungee Jumping
**Kawarau Bridge Bungy**
- **Height:** 43 meters (141 feet)
- **Significance:** World's first commercial bungy site (1988)
- **Operator:** AJ Hackett Bungy
- **Features:** Historic bridge, optional water touch, professional video

**Nevis Bungy**
- **Height:** 134 meters (440 feet)
- **Significance:** New Zealand's highest bungy
- **Location:** Nevis Valley, 30 minutes from Queenstown
- **Experience:** 8.5-second freefall, pod access via cable car

**Ledge Bungy**
- **Height:** 47 meters (154 feet)
- **Location:** Bob's Peak, accessible via Skyline Gondola
- **Unique Feature:** Night bungy available, harness-free (rope around ankles)

### Skydiving
- **Heights:** 9,000 ft, 12,000 ft, or 15,000 ft options
- **Freefall Time:** Up to 60 seconds at highest altitude
- **Views:** Lake Wakatipu, The Remarkables, surrounding peaks
- **Operators:** NZONE Skydive, Skydive Southern Alps

### Jet Boating
**Shotover Jet**
- **Location:** Shotover River canyons
- **Speed:** Up to 85 km/h (53 mph)
- **Signature Move:** 360-degree spins in narrow canyons
- **Duration:** 25 minutes
- **History:** Originally used by gold miners, now iconic tourist attraction

**Dart Jet**
- **Location:** Dart River (near Glenorchy)
- **Experience:** More scenic, less intense than Shotover
- **Setting:** Pristine alpine valleys, glacier-fed waters
- **Duration:** 55 minutes including nature tour

### Skiing & Snowboarding
**The Remarkables**
- **Terrain:** Mix of beginner, intermediate, advanced
- **Vertical:** 430 meters (1,411 feet)
- **Lifts:** 6 chairlifts
- **Features:** Terrain parks, night skiing, stunning views

**Coronet Peak**
- **Distance:** 18 km from Queenstown
- **Season:** June-October (longest season in region)
- **Terrain:** Wide open runs, night skiing
- **Facilities:** Restaurants, equipment rental, lessons

### Other Adventure Activities
- **White Water Rafting:** Shotover and Kawarau rivers (Grade 3-5 rapids)
- **Canyoning:** Rappelling, jumping, sliding through waterfalls
- **Paragliding:** Tandem flights from Bob's Peak
- **Zip Lining:** Ziptrek Ecotours through native forest
- **Mountain Biking:** World-class trails for all levels
- **Rock Climbing:** Multiple crags within driving distance
- **Via Ferrata:** Protected climbing routes on The Remarkables
- **Heli-Hiking:** Glacier walks with helicopter access

## Scenic & Relaxing Activities

### Skyline Gondola & Luge
- **Gondola:** 430 meters vertical rise to Bob's Peak
- **Views:** Panoramic vista over Queenstown and lake
- **Luge:** Gravity-powered carts on winding tracks (3 tracks available)
- **Restaurant:** Stratosfare Restaurant with revolving views

### TSS Earnslaw Steamship
- **Built:** 1912, one of the oldest tourist attractions
- **Experience:** Vintage coal-fired steamship cruise
- **Destination:** Walter Peak High Country Farm
- **Duration:** 1.5 hours each way
- **Features:** Engine room tours, farm demonstrations, barbecue dinner

### Wine Tours
- **Central Otago:** World's southernmost wine region
- **Varietals:** Pinot Noir (signature), Pinot Gris, Riesling, Chardonnay
- **Notable Wineries:** Amisfield, Rippon, Mt Difficulty, Chard Farm
- **Tours:** Half-day and full-day options available

### Glenorchy Day Trip
- **Distance:** 45 km (28 miles) from Queenstown
- **Drive:** 45 minutes through stunning scenery
- **Features:** Paradise, Lord of the Rings filming locations
- **Activities:** Hiking, horse riding, jet boating

## Māori History & Significance

### Tāhuna
- **Meaning:** "Shallow bay" or "shallow lagoon"
- **Traditional Use:** Seasonal fishing and gathering site
- **Ngāi Tahu:** The local iwi (tribe) with mana whenua (authority over the land)

### Māori Legends
- **Lake Wakatipu Formation:** Giant burned by chief, body became the lake
- **Tūte-wanawana:** Ancestral figure who carved the fiords and mountains
- **Sacred Sites:** Several locations around the lake hold spiritual significance

### Ngāi Tahu Presence Today
- **Cultural Tours:** Available through local operators
- **Place Names:** Increasing use of original Māori names
- **Partnership:** Collaborative management of natural resources

## Lord of the Rings Connection

### Filming Locations
Queenstown region was a major filming location for Peter Jackson's trilogy:

- **The Remarkables:** Isengard backdrop, various mountain scenes
- **Paradise (near Glenorchy):** Lothlórien, Amon Hen
- **Skippers Canyon:** Bridge of Khazad-dûm approach
- **Kawarau River:** Anduin River scenes
- **Deer Park Heights:** Various establishing shots

### Tourism Impact
- **LOTR Tours:** Multiple operators offer location tours
- **Fan Pilgrimage:** Significant portion of visitors are LOTR fans
- **Economic Impact:** Estimated $200+ million annually from film tourism

## Queenstown Gardens

### Overview
- **Location:** Peninsula in center of Queenstown
- **Size:** 8 hectares (20 acres)
- **Features:** Walking trails, lake views, historic buildings

### Attractions
- **Queenstown Gardens Track:** Easy 30-minute loop walk
- **Ice Arena:** Year-round ice skating
- **Golf Course:** 9-hole course with mountain views
- **Historic Buildings:** Former hospital, now community spaces

## Dining & Nightlife

### Cuisine Scene
- **Fine Dining:** Rata, Botswana Butchery, Amisfield
- **Casual:** Fergburger (legendary burgers), various food trucks
- **International:** Wide range of cuisines represented
- **Local Specialties:** Venison, salmon, lamb, Pinot Noir

### Nightlife
- **Bars:** Numerous cocktail bars and pubs
- **Clubs:** Several late-night venues
- **Live Music:** Regular performances throughout town
- **Casino:** Queenstown Casino for gaming

## Practical Information

### Getting There
- **Airport:** Queenstown International (ZQN), 8 km from town
- **Domestic Flights:** Direct from Auckland, Wellington, Christchurch
- **International:** Direct flights from Australia (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Gold Coast)
- **Road:** State Highway 6 connects to rest of South Island

### Getting Around
- **Walking:** Town center is compact and walkable
- **Bus:** Local bus network covers main areas
- **Car Rental:** Recommended for exploring region
- **Taxis/Rideshare:** Readily available

### Accommodation
- **Range:** From hostels to 5-star hotels
- **Peak Prices:** Summer and ski season (Dec-Feb, Jun-Aug)
- **Booking:** Advance booking essential for peak periods
- **Popular Areas:** Town center, Frankton, Fernhill, Kelvin Heights

### Visitor Statistics
- **Annual Visitors:** ~3 million per year (pre-pandemic)
- **Peak Season:** Summer (Dec-Feb) and Winter ski season (Jun-Aug)
- **Average Stay:** 3-4 nights
- **International:** ~60% of visitors are international

## Best Times to Visit

### For Adventure
- **Summer (Dec-Feb):** All activities available, long days
- **Winter (Jun-Aug):** Skiing, snowboarding, winter bungy

### For Scenery
- **Autumn (Mar-May):** Golden colors, clear air, fewer crowds
- **Spring (Sep-Nov):** Fresh green, lambs, flowers

### For Budget
- **Shoulder Seasons:** April-May, September-October
- **Avoid:** Christmas-New Year, school holidays

## Interesting Facts

1. Queenstown was the site of the world's first commercial bungy jump (1988)
2. Lake Wakatipu is one of the few lakes with a seiche effect (water rises/falls regularly)
3. The Remarkables mountain range was named by early surveyors for its "remarkable" appearance
4. Queenstown has more restaurants per capita than New York City
5. The Shotover Jet has been operating since 1965
6. Central Otago is the world's southernmost commercial wine region
7. Queenstown Airport has one of the most scenic approaches in the world
8. The TSS Earnslaw is older than the Titanic (1912 vs 1912)
9. Queenstown hosted the 2019 IRB World Masters Rugby Tournament
10. Fergburger sells over 1 million burgers per year
