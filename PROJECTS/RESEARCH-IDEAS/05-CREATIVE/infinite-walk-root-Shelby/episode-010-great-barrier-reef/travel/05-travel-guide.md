## GETTING THERE

### Gateway City: Cairns, Queensland

**Flights:**
- **From Sydney:** 2.5 hours, $200-500 AUD
- **From Melbourne:** 3 hours, $250-550 AUD
- **From Brisbane:** 2 hours, $180-400 AUD
- **International:** Direct flights from Singapore, Tokyo, Seoul, Auckland

**Airport:** Cairns International Airport (CNS)
- 7km from city center
- Taxi to CBD: $25-35 AUD
- Shuttle bus: $15-20 AUD
- Rental cars available

### Alternative Gateways

**Port Douglas** (60km north of Cairns)
- More upscale, quieter
- Closer to Agincourt Reef
- Transfer from Cairns Airport: 1 hour by shuttle

**Airlie Beach** (Whitsundays)
- Southern Great Barrier Reef access
- 90-min flight from Cairns or Brisbane
- Gateway to Whitehaven Beach

## WHEN TO VISIT

### Best Overall: June - October (Winter/Dry Season)

**Pros:**
- ✅ Best visibility (30-50 meters)
- ✅ Comfortable water temperature (22-25°C / 72-77°F)
- ✅ Minimal rainfall
- ✅ No stinger jellyfish
- ✅ Whale sightings possible (June-July)
- ✅ Calm seas

**Cons:**
- ❌ Peak season = higher prices
- ❌ More crowded
- ❌ Book well in advance

### Shoulder Season: September - November

**Pros:**
- ✅ Warming water
- ✅ Good visibility
- ✅ Coral spawning (November, after full moon)
- ✅ Fewer crowds than winter

**Cons:**
- ❌ Building humidity
- ❌ Stinger season begins November

### Summer: December - February

**Pros:**
- ✅ Warmest water (27-29°C / 81-84°F)
- ✅ Fewer crowds
- ✅ Lower prices
- ✅ Lush tropical landscape

**Cons:**
- ❌ Stinger season (box jellyfish, Irukandji)
- ❌ Stinger suits required
- ❌ Higher rainfall
- ❌ Runoff can reduce visibility
- ❌ Cyclone season (rare but possible)

### Autumn: March - May

**Pros:**
- ✅ Still warm water
- ✅ Decreasing rain
- ✅ Good visibility returning

**Cons:**
- ❌ Stinger season continues through April

## CERTIFICATION & DIVING

### Never Dived Before?

**Resort/Intro Dive:**
- No certification required
- Supervised by instructor
- 1-2 dives in shallow water
- Cost: $250-350 AUD
- Duration: Half day

**PADI Open Water Certification:**
- 3-4 days total
- Theory (online or classroom)
- Confined water (pool) training
- 4 open water dives
- Cost: $650-850 AUD
- Certification valid worldwide

**Referral Course:**
- Complete theory and pool work at home
- Do open water dives in Cairns
- Cost: $500-650 AUD
- Faster option for prepared students

### Already Certified?

**Bring:**
- Certification card (or digital)
- Logbook (some operators require proof of recent dives)
- If no dives in 6+ months: refresher course recommended ($150-200 AUD)

**Dive Packages:**
- 1 day, 2 dives: $280-350 AUD
- 2 days, 4 dives: $500-650 AUD
- 3 days, 6 dives: $700-900 AUD
- Multi-day passes offer better value

## HEALTH & SAFETY

### Diving Medical

**Required if:**
- Answered YES to any questions on dive medical form
- Over 45 years old (some operators)
- Have pre-existing conditions

**Common conditions requiring clearance:**
- Asthma
- Heart conditions
- Diabetes
- Previous ear surgery
- Respiratory issues

**Solution:** See a dive doctor before travel or in Cairns
- Cairns Dive Medical: Multiple clinics available
- Cost: $80-150 AUD
- Valid for 12 months

### Marine Life Safety

**Most creatures are harmless if not provoked:**

**Do NOT touch:**
- Coral (can cut, slow-growing)
- Fire coral (stings)
- Stonefish (venomous, camouflaged)
- Blue-ringed octopus (highly venomous, tiny)
- Cone shells (venomous)

**Sharks:**
- Present on outer reef
- Not aggressive toward humans
- Incidents extremely rare
- Follow guide instructions

**Stinger Season (November-May):**
- Box jellyfish and Irukandji present
- Stinger suits provided by operators
- Vinegar for first aid (available on boats/beaches)
- Most operators run year-round with protection

### Sun Protection

**Tropical sun is intense:**
- UV index regularly 11+ (extreme)
- Reef-safe sunscreen essential
- Reapply every 2 hours
- Wear rash guard or wetsuit
- Hat and sunglasses on boat
- Stay hydrated

### Seasickness

**Common on reef trips:**
- Take medication night before AND morning of trip
- Don't wait until you feel sick (too late)
- Stay on deck, look at horizon
- Avoid heavy meals before trip
- Ginger tablets can help

**Recommended:**
- Travel Calm (Phenergan) — available at pharmacies
- Take 1 tablet night before, 1 morning of

**Many operators accommodate:**

- Some pontoons have accessible facilities
- Private transfers can be arranged
- Notify operators in advance

**Recommended Operators:**

**Can still experience the reef:**

- Semi-submersible tours
- Helicopter/scenic flights

## ENVIRONMENTAL RESPONSIBILITY

### Choose Responsible Operators

**Look for:**
- Ecotourism Australia certification
- GBRMPA (Marine Park Authority) permits
- Environmental policies on website
- Educational briefings
- Citizen science programs

### What YOU Can Do

**On the Reef:**
- ❌ Don't touch or stand on coral
- ❌ Don't feed fish
- ❌ Don't collect shells or marine life
- ❌ Don't chase or harass wildlife
- ✅ Maintain good buoyancy
- ✅ Use reef-safe sunscreen
- ✅ Follow guide instructions
- ✅ Report damaged equipment (anchors, etc.)

**Beyond the Reef:**
- Reduce carbon footprint (biggest threat is climate change)
- Support conservation organizations
- Share what you learn
- Make sustainable choices at home

### Conservation Organizations to Support

- **Great Barrier Reef Foundation** — barrierreef.org
- **Australian Marine Conservation Society** — marineconservation.org.au
- **Reef Check Australia** — reefcheckaustralia.org
- **Citizens of the Great Barrier Reef** — citizensofthegbr.org

## EXTENDING YOUR TRIP

### Near Cairns

**Atherton Tablelands:**
- Waterfalls, rainforest, crater lakes
- 1-2 hours from Cairns
- Cool escape from tropical heat

**Daintree Rainforest:**
- World's oldest tropical rainforest
- Where reef meets rainforest
- Day trips or overnight stays

**Kuranda:**
- Scenic railway or skyrail
- Village markets
- Rainforest walks

### Further Afield

**Port Douglas:**
- Upscale resort town
- Four Mile Beach
- Closer to outer reef

**Mission Beach:**
- Cassowary sightings
- Access to Frankland Islands
- Quieter alternative

**Lady Elliot Island:**
- Southern Great Barrier Reef
- Manta ray capital
- Eco-resort, 2-3 days recommended
