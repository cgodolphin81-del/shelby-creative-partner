## TEASE SCRIPT (60 seconds)

**[SOUND: Episode 112 closing music fades. Temple bell from Bangkok lingers, then transforms into a different bell — deeper, older.]**

**NARRATOR:**
You leave Bangkok. The river flows on.

But the journey does not end.

**[SOUND: Plane taking off. Fades into wind, then... silence. A different kind of silence.]**

**NARRATOR:**
North. Past the mountains. Past the border. Into a country that closed itself to the world... and is now opening.

Myanmar.

**[SOUND: Distant temple bells. Many of them. Dozens. Hundreds. A chorus of bells across a vast plain.]**

**NARRATOR:**
Bagan.

Three thousand temples rise from the dust. Some golden. Some crumbling. Some reclaimed by the earth itself.

You arrive before sunrise. The air is cool. Dry. Nothing like Bangkok's embrace.

**[SOUND: Hot air balloon burner. Distant. Then silence again. First birds singing.]**

**NARRATOR:**
You wait.

The sky lightens. Purple. Pink. Orange.

And then...

**[SOUND: Sunrise. A single gong. Deep. Resonant. Holds for several seconds.]**

**NARRATOR:**
The sun rises over Bagan.

Three thousand temples. One moment. All of them golden. All of them silent. All of them waiting for you to understand something you cannot yet name.

**[MUSIC: Traditional Burmese saung (harp) — ancient, haunting, beautiful. Fades in gently.]**

**NARRATOR:**
Bangkok taught you impermanence.

Bagan will teach you something else.

**[SOUND: Wind across the plain. Temple bells. One more gong.]**

**NARRATOR:**
What survives when empires fall?

What remains when the builders are dust?

**[MUSIC: Swells slightly. Then begins to fade.]**

**NARRATOR:**
Episode 113.

The Temples of Bagan.

Coming soon to Infinite Walk.

**[SOUND: Final gong. Resonates. Fades to silence.]**

**[END TEASE]**

## KEY THEMES TO HIGHLIGHT

### From Bangkok to Bagan: The Journey
- **Bangkok:** Chaos, modernity, river, street life
- **Bagan:** Silence, ancient, plain, temples
- **Contrast:** Both Buddhist, vastly different expressions

### What Bagan Represents
- **Ancient civilization:** 9th-13th centuries
- **Spiritual devotion:** Thousands of temples built by kings and commoners
- **Impermanence:** Many temples crumbling, reclaimed by nature
- **Resilience:** Survived earthquakes, wars, time itself
- **Opening:** Myanmar emerging from isolation

### Visual Contrasts
| Bangkok | Bagan |
|---------|-------|
| Neon lights | Sunrise/sunset |
| Skyscrapers | Ancient temples |
| River flow | Dust plain |
| Street food | Meditation |
| Tuk-tuks | Hot air balloons |
| Crowds | Solitude |
| Noise | Silence |

- [ ] Hot air balloon tourism
- [ ] Current political situation (post-2021 coup)
- [ ] Travel logistics (visas, getting there)
- [ ] Local culture (Burmese Buddhism, differences from Thai)
- [ ] Sunset/sunrise viewpoints

### Sound Design
- **Ambient:** Wind across plain, temple bells, birds at dawn
- **Music:** Traditional Burmese saung (harp), patt waing (drums)
- **Contrast:** Silence is a key element (vs. Bangkok's chaos)
- **Transitions:** From Bangkok river to Bagan wind

### Narrative Arc
1. **Arrival:** Leaving Bangkok, arriving in Bagan
2. **Sunrise:** The iconic moment (3,000 temples)
3. **History:** Ancient kingdom, temple building
4. **Exploration:** Walking among temples
5. **Reflection:** What survives, what matters
6. **Departure:** Carry Bagan forward

### Sensitive Topics
- **Political situation:** Address carefully (2021 coup, ongoing conflict)
- **Tourism ethics:** Is it okay to visit?
- **Restoration controversy:** Some criticize reconstruction methods
- **Approach:** Acknowledge complexity, focus on timeless aspects
