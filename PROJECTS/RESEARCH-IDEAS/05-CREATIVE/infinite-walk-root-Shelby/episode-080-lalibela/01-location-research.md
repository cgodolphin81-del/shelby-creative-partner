# Episode 080: The New Jerusalem - Lalibela, Ethiopia
## Location Research

### The Eleven Rock-Hewn Churches

Lalibela is home to **11 monolithic churches**, carved from a single mass of red volcanic rock in the late 12th and early 13th centuries. These structures were built top-down, meaning artisans carved from the surface downward into the bedrock, creating freestanding structures still connected to the earth at their base.

---

### The Three Groups

#### **Northern Group (5 churches)**
1. **Biete Medhane Alem** (House of the Saviour of the World)
   - Largest monolithic church in the world
   - Approximately 8,000 square feet, four stories tall
   - Contains the Lalibela Cross

2. **Biete Maryam** (House of Mary)
   - Features vividly colored painted scenes
   - Exterior frieze of horsemen

3. **Biete Maskal** (House of the Cross)

4. **Biete Denagel** (House of Virgins)

5. **Biete Golgotha Mikael** (House of Golgotha Mikael)
   - Contains bas-relief carvings of human figures
   - Believed to house King Lalibela's tomb
   - **Women are not permitted entry**

#### **Southern Group (5 churches)**
6. **Biete Amanuel** (House of Emmanuel)
   - Exemplifies sculpted Aksumite architecture
   - Resembles layering of beams and stones
   - Currently at risk of structural collapse

7. **Biete Qeddus Mercoreus** (House of St. Mercoreos)
   - May have served as a royal residence

8. **Biete Abba Libanos** (House of Abbot Libanos)

9. **Biete Gabriel-Rufael** (House of Gabriel Raphael)
   - May have served as a royal residence

10. **Biete Lehem** (House of Holy Bread)

#### **Western Group (1 church)**
11. **Biete Ghiorgis** (House of St. George)
    - The most famous and best preserved
    - Carved in a perfect cruciform (cross) shape
    - Often called the "Eighth Wonder of the World"
    - Best photographed early morning (6-7 AM)

### Historical Context

**King Gebre Meskel Lalibela** (reigned ca. 1181–1221 AD)
- Member of the Zagwe Dynasty
- Ruled from the city then known as **Roha**
- According to Ethiopian Orthodox tradition, received a divine vision to build a "New Jerusalem"
- Construction said to have taken **24 years**
- Legend states angels assisted in the construction, working at night

**Why a New Jerusalem?**
- After Saladin's conquest of Jerusalem in 1187, pilgrimage to the Holy Land became dangerous for Ethiopian Christians
- Lalibela created an alternative pilgrimage destination
- The site's layout mirrors the topography of Jerusalem
- A river running through the complex is called the **River Jordan**

---

### UNESCO World Heritage Status

- **Inscribed:** 1978 (one of the first sites in Africa)
- **Criteria:** Unique artistic achievement, symbolic substitute for Jerusalem, testimony to medieval Ethiopian civilization
- **Altitude:** Approximately 2,480 meters (8,136 feet)
- **Area:** About 62 acres

---

### Current Status & Conservation

**Active Pilgrimage Site:**
- Still used for daily worship and religious festivals
- Tens of thousands gather for:
  - **Genna** (Ethiopian Christmas) - January 7
  - **Timkat** (Epiphany) - January 19-20
  - **Meskel** (Holy Cross Day) - September 27

**Conservation Challenges:**
- Water damage from seismic activity
- Filled drainage ditches causing degradation
- Biete Amanuel at risk of collapse
- Temporary shelters (erected 2008) now pose risks
- Tourism pressure (46,000+ international visitors in 2019)
- New construction encroaching on the site

**Management:**
- Joint responsibility of Church and State
- Authority for Research and Conservation of Cultural Heritage (ARCCH)
- World Monuments Fund training local priests and laymen

---

### The Faith

**Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church:**
- One of the earliest centers of Christianity
- Claims historical link to Queen of Sheba and King Solomon
- Ancient manuscripts, religious books, crosses, and icons housed in the churches
- Priests wear distinctive white robes
- Services include chanting, incense, and rhythmic prayer

**Pilgrimage Tradition:**
- Pilgrims travel hundreds of kilometers on foot
- Many arrive barefoot as an act of devotion
- The churches remain a living testament to faith, not merely historical monuments
