## The Oldest Living City

Varanasi, also known as Kashi and Benares, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Archaeological evidence suggests settlements dating back to 1000 BCE, though Hindu tradition places its founding over 3,000 years ago. Mark Twain famously wrote: *"Varanasi is older than history, older than tradition, older even than legend, and looks twice as old as all of them put together."*

### Key Facts:
- **Location**: Uttar Pradesh, Northern India
- **River**: Sacred Ganges (Ganga) River
- **Population**: ~1.2 million (metro area ~3.7 million)
- **Significance**: Holiest city in Hinduism, one of seven sacred cities (Sapta Puri)
- **UNESCO**: World Heritage Site candidate

## The Ghats of Varanasi

Varanasi has 84 ghats (stone steps leading to the river), each with its own character and purpose.

### Major Ghats:

#### 1. **Dashashwamedh Ghat** (Main Ghat)
- Most spectacular and crowded
- Site of daily Ganga Aarti ceremony
- Legend: Brahma created it to welcome Shiva
- Best visited at sunrise and sunset

#### 2. **Manikarnika Ghat** (Cremation Ghat)
- Primary cremation ground
- Hindus believe dying here grants moksha (liberation from rebirth)
- Funeral pyres burn 24/7
- Photography strictly prohibited
- One of the oldest continuously used cremation sites in the world

#### 3. **Harishchandra Ghat**
- Secondary cremation ghat
- Named after King Harishchandra, who worked here selling shrouds
- Less crowded than Manikarnika

#### 4. **Assi Ghat**
- Southernmost ghat
- Popular with pilgrims and students
- Morning aarti ceremonies
- More relaxed atmosphere

#### 5. **Tulsi Ghat**
- Named after poet-saint Tulsidas (Ramcharitmanas author)
- Cultural performances
- Less touristy

#### 6. **Panchganga Ghat**
- Where five rivers supposedly meet
- Aurangzeb Mosque built on Hindu temple ruins
- Architectural contrast

#### 7. **Man Mandir Ghat**
- Observatory built by Maharaja Jai Singh II (1710)
- Intricate stone carvings
- One of five observatories in India

## Cremation Ceremonies & Death Rituals

### The Hindu Philosophy:
Death in Varanasi is not an end but a transformation. The city is dedicated to Shiva, the destroyer and transformer. Hindus believe:

- **Moksha**: Liberation from the cycle of rebirth (samsara)
- **Kashi Labh**: The "fruit of Kashi" - spiritual merit gained from dying here
- **Agni**: Sacred fire purifies the soul for its journey

### The Cremation Process:
1. **Body preparation**: Washed in Ganges, wrapped in cloth (white for men, red for women)
2. **Pyre construction**: Sandalwood logs (expensive, sold by weight)
3. **Mukhagni**: Eldest son lights the pyre, often with sacred fire from a temple
4. **Kapal Kriya**: Ritual breaking of the skull to release the soul
5. **Asthi Visarjan**: Ashes collected and immersed in Ganges
6. **Pind Daan**: Offerings to ancestors on subsequent days

### Important Notes:
- Approximately 80-100 cremations daily
- Wood consumption: ~300-400 kg per body (sandalwood costs ₹30,000-60,000)
- Poor families may use fewer logs or bamboo
- Some bodies are not cremated: saints, children under 5, pregnant women, snake bites (believed already purified) - these are weighted and immersed directly

### Cultural Sensitivity:
- Photography forbidden at cremation ghats
- Maintain respectful distance
- No pointing or obvious gesturing
- Dress modestly
- Silence is appropriate

## Ancient Temples

### 1. **Kashi Vishwanath Temple** (Golden Temple)
- **Deity**: Lord Shiva (Vishwanath = "Ruler of the Universe")
- **Significance**: One of 12 Jyotirlingas (most sacred Shiva shrines)
- **History**: Destroyed and rebuilt multiple times; current structure 1780
- **Gold**: 800kg gold plating on spire (donated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh)
- **Access**: Non-Hindus cannot enter main sanctum (view from nearby viewpoints)
- **Security**: Strict (no phones, cameras, leather items)

### 2. **Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple**
- **Deity**: Lord Hanuman
- **Founded**: 16th century by Tulsidas
- **Significance**: Removes troubles (sankat = trouble, mochan = liberator)
- **Location**: Near Assi Ghat

### 3. **Durga Temple** (Monkey Temple)
- **Deity**: Goddess Durga
- **Architecture**: Nagara style, red stone
- **Monkeys**: Resident population (hence nickname)
- **18th century** construction

### 4. **Bharat Mata Temple**
- **Unique**: Dedicated to Mother India (personified as goddess)
- **Feature**: Marble relief map of India
- **Built**: 1936 by freedom fighter Shiv Prasad Gupta
- **Access**: Open to all religions

### 5. **Sarnath** (10km from Varanasi)
- **Buddhist significance**: Where Buddha gave first sermon
- **Dhamek Stupa**: 5th century CE, 43.6m tall
- **Ashoka Pillar**: 3rd century BCE (national emblem of India)
- **Multiple monasteries**: Thai, Tibetan, Chinese, Japanese

## Spiritual Significance

### In Hinduism:
- **Jyotirlinga**: Kashi Vishwanath is first among 12 sacred Shiva lingams
- **Shakta Pitha**: Site where Sati's earring fell (divine feminine power)
- **Moksha Puri**: City of liberation
- **Kashi Khand**: Section of Skanda Purana dedicated to Varanasi's glory

### In Other Traditions:
- **Buddhism**: Nearby Sarnath - birthplace of Buddhist sangha
- **Jainism**: Birthplace of four Tirthankaras
- **Islam**: Sufi traditions, historic mosques
- **Sikhism**: Guru Nanak visited in 1506

### The Ganges (Ganga):
- **Goddess Ganga**: Descended from heaven to earth
- **Purification**: Bathing washes away sins
- **Amrit**: Nectar of immortality during Kumbh Mela
- **Science**: Unusual bacteriophage content (historically noted for self-purifying properties)

## Festivals & Events

### Major Festivals:
1. **Dev Deepawali** (November)
   - "Diwali of the Gods"
   - 15 days after Diwali
   - Ghats illuminated with millions of earthen lamps
   - Celestial celebration when gods visit Earth

2. **Kumbh Mela** (Every 12 years, Ardh Kumbh every 6 years)
   - Largest human gathering on Earth
   - Millions of pilgrims
   - Shahi Snan (royal bath) by sadhus

3. **Maha Shivaratri** (February/March)
   - Night of Shiva
   - Special pujas at Kashi Vishwanath
   - All-night vigils

4. **Ganga Mahotsav** (October-November)
   - 5-day cultural festival
   - Music, dance, boat races
   - Culminates on Kartik Purnima

### Daily Rituals:
- **Ganga Aarti**: Dawn and sunset at Dashashwamedh Ghat
- **Morning baths**: Pilgrims in sacred river
- **Yoga sessions**: Along ghats at sunrise
- **Boat rides**: Traditional wooden boats

## Historical Layers

### Timeline:
- **1000 BCE**: Archaeological evidence of settlement
- **6th century BCE**: Buddha preached nearby at Sarnath
- **4th century CE**: Faxian (Chinese pilgrim) visited
- **7th century CE**: Xuanzang described prosperous city
- **1194 CE**: Qutb-ud-din Aibak destroyed temples
- **16th century**: Mughal period, temples rebuilt
- **17th century**: Aurangzeb destroyed temples again, built mosques
- **18th century**: Maratha reconstruction, current temple built
- **1791**: British East India Company control
- **1947**: Part of independent India
- **1987**: Varanasi declared cultural capital of India

### Colonial Era:
- **Mark Twain**: Visited 1896, wrote extensively
- **British architecture**: Cantonment area, railway station
- **Oriental Seminary**: 1791, Sanskrit scholarship

## Modern Varanasi

### Demographics:
- **Languages**: Hindi, Bhojpuri, Urdu, English (tourist areas)
- **Religions**: Hindu 84%, Muslim 15%, others 1%
- **Literacy**: ~80% (higher than national average)

### Economy:
- **Pilgrimage tourism**: Primary industry
- **Silk weaving**: Banarasi saris (geographical indication)
- **Handicrafts**: Brass, wood, carpets
- **Education**: Banaras Hindu University (1916), one of Asia's largest residential universities
- **Railways**: Diesel Locomotive Works

### Infrastructure:
- **Airport**: Lal Bahadur Shastri International (25km)
- **Railway**: Varanasi Junction, Mughal Sarai
- **Metro**: Under construction
- **Roads**: National Highway connectivity

## Practical Considerations for Visitors

### Best Times to Visit:
- **October-March**: Cool, pleasant (peak season)
- **April-June**: Very hot (35-45°C)
- **July-September**: Monsoon, humid
- **November**: Dev Deepawali (spectacular but crowded)

### Health & Safety:
- **Ganges water**: Do not drink (pollution concerns despite spiritual purity)
- **Food**: Eat at reputable places, avoid street food if sensitive
- **Air quality**: Can be poor, especially winter
- **Scams**: Common around tourist areas (fake guides, inflated prices)
- **Dress**: Modest clothing (shoulders, knees covered)

### Photography Ethics:
- **Cremation ghats**: Absolutely forbidden
- **Sadhus**: Ask permission, expect payment (₹50-200)
- **Temples**: Often prohibited inside sanctums
- **Locals**: Ask before photographing people
- **Sunrise boats**: Best for general ghat photography

### Accommodation Areas:
- **Near Dashashwamedh**: Central, noisy, convenient
- **Assi Ghat**: Quieter, backpacker-friendly, long-stay
- **Cantonment**: Modern, away from old city
- **Heritage hotels**: Converted havelis (expensive)
