## Color Usage Guidelines

### DO:
✅ Use Serengeti Gold as primary brand color
✅ Pair warm golds with cool blues for contrast
✅ Use sunset oranges sparingly for emphasis
✅ Maintain 60-30-10 rule (60% neutral, 30% primary, 10% accent)
✅ Ensure text has 4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum

### DON'T:
❌ Use bright neons or artificial colors
❌ Overuse sunset orange (loses impact)
❌ Mix too many earth tones (becomes muddy)
❌ Use pure black (#000000) — too harsh
❌ Ignore accessibility requirements

## Typography

### Primary Font: Headlines

**Playfair Display** (Serif)
- Usage: Episode titles, major headlines, quote overlays
- Weights: Regular (400), Semi-Bold (600), Bold (700)
- Style: Elegant, editorial, timeless
- Why: Evokes National Geographic, documentary feel

**Example:**
```
The Serengeti
Where the Land Runs On Forever
```

---

### Secondary Font: Body Text

**Lato** (Sans-Serif)
- Usage: Body copy, captions, descriptions
- Weights: Light (300), Regular (400), Bold (700)
- Style: Clean, readable, friendly
- Why: Highly legible, modern, works at all sizes

**Example:**
```
Episode 035 takes us to one of the most extraordinary 
places on Earth — Tanzania's Serengeti National Park.
```

---

### Accent Font: Special Elements

**Montserrat** (Sans-Serif)
- Usage: Numbers, statistics, data visualization
- Weights: Regular (400), Semi-Bold (600), Extra-Bold (800)
- Style: Geometric, modern, impactful
- Why: Great for stats, numbers pop

**Example:**
```
1.5 MILLION
Wildebeest
```

---

## Photography Style

### Composition

**Rule of Thirds:**
- Place horizon on lower third (emphasize sky)
- Place subjects at intersection points
- Leave negative space for text overlay

**Leading Lines:**
- Use acacia tree branches as frames
- Animal paths leading into distance
- Migration herds creating movement lines

**Scale:**
- Include human element for scale (Shelby, safari vehicle)
- Show vastness of plains
- Contrast small animals against large landscapes

---

### Lighting

**Golden Hour (Primary):**
- Shoot 1 hour after sunrise, 1 hour before sunset
- Warm, soft, directional light
- Long shadows add depth
- Ideal for: Landscapes, wildlife portraits, acacia trees

**Blue Hour (Secondary):**
- 30 minutes before sunrise, after sunset
- Cool, ethereal light
- Ideal for: Mood shots, silhouettes, night sky

**Midday (Avoid or Use Creatively):**
- Harsh shadows, high contrast
- If shooting: Use for abstract patterns, textures
- Ideal for: Kopje rocks, acacia bark, animal textures

---

### Color Grading

**Warm & Golden (Primary Look):**
- Temperature: +10 to +15
- Tint: Slightly toward magenta (+5)
- Highlights: Warm (orange/yellow)
- Shadows: Cool (blue/teal) for contrast
- Saturation: +10 to +15 (vibrant but natural)
- Vibrance: +15 (protects skin tones)

**Moody & Dramatic (Secondary Look):**
- Temperature: Neutral to slightly cool
- Contrast: +20
- Highlights: -20 (recover detail)
- Shadows: +10 (lift for detail)
- Saturation: -10 (muted, cinematic)
- Vignette: Subtle (-10 to -15)

**Natural & Documentary (Tertiary Look):**
- Minimal grading
- True-to-life colors
- Slight warmth (+5)
- Natural contrast
- Ideal for: Wildlife behavior, cultural documentation

## Accessibility

### Color Contrast

**Minimum Ratios:**
- Normal text: 4.5:1
- Large text (18px+): 3:1
- UI elements: 3:1

**Tested Combinations:**
✅ Night Plains on Bone White (12.5:1)
✅ East African Blue on Bone White (5.2:1)
✅ Serengeti Gold on Night Plains (8.1:1)
✅ Safari Sunset on Night Plains (6.3:1)
❌ Acacia Yellow on Bone White (1.8:1) — Do not use

---

### Text Readability

**Minimum Sizes:**
- Body text: 16px (web), 14px (mobile)
- Captions: 12px (absolute minimum)
- Headlines: 24px+

**Best Practices:**
- Avoid all-caps for long text
- Use sentence case for readability
- Adequate line spacing (1.5-1.6 for body)
- Limit line length (50-75 characters)

---

### Alt Text

**All Images Must Have:**
- Descriptive alt text
- Context for why image matters
- Not "image of" — describe the content

**Example:**
❌ "Image of lion"
✅ "Male lion with full mane resting in shade of acacia tree, golden hour light, Serengeti National Park"
