## Tease Copy (Long Form - Blog/Newsletter)

**Title:** After Angkor: Following the River

**Body:**

I'm still thinking about the stones.

About the jungle's patience. About the faces that saw me see them. About a country that chose to build anyway.

Episode 110: The Floating Temples was a return to something ancient. A meditation on impermanence carved in stone.

But rivers don't stop at temples. They flow.

And so do we.

---

**Episode 111 is coming.**

We're following the Mekong River from Angkor's gates to its delta. To where the water slows, splits, and meets the sea.

To Vietnam.

---

Here's what I'm exploring:

**The Floating Markets of the Mekong Delta**
Before dawn, hundreds of boats gather at the river's intersection. Not for ceremony. For commerce. For life. Vendors sell fruit, vegetables, fish, noodles—all from boats that have been their homes for generations.

What does it mean to live on water? To wake with the current? To sell your goods before the sun rises?

**The Ghosts of French Colonialism**
Saigon (Ho Chi Minh City) still bears the marks of French rule. Wide boulevards. Cathedral spires. Cafés serving cà phê sữa đá on crumbling balconies.

Empires fall. But their echoes remain. What do we do with the buildings, the food, the language they left behind?

**The War That Changed Everything**
I cannot visit Vietnam without walking the tunnels of Cu Chi. Without standing in the DMZ. Without listening to the stories of those who lived through it.

Cambodia taught me about choosing creation over bitterness. Vietnam will teach me... what?

I'm not sure yet. That's the point.

**The Food That Tells the Story**
Phở at dawn. Bánh mì on street corners. Fresh spring rolls wrapped in hands that have made them ten thousand times.

Food is memory. Food is resistance. Food is love that outlasts war.

---

**Why Vietnam? Why Now?**

Because Episode 52 took us through Cambodia. Because the Mekong doesn't respect borders. Because Vietnam is where the river meets the sea—and where one journey naturally becomes another.

Because I'm learning that the Infinite Walk isn't about destinations. It's about following the thread.

And the thread leads south. Downriver. Toward the delta. Toward the sea.

**Episode 111 Announcement: The Floating Markets**

I'm following the Mekong River from Cambodia's Angkor Wat to Vietnam's delta.

Here's why:

**1. Flow Over Foundation**
Temples are built to last (even if they don't). Rivers are built to move. What can we learn from water that temples can't teach?

**2. Commerce as Culture**
The floating markets of the Mekong Delta have operated for centuries. Before tourism. Before globalization. Just people, boats, and the rhythm of the river. What does that teach us about sustainable livelihoods?

**3. Colonial Echoes**
Vietnam still bears the marks of French colonialism—in architecture, food, language. Empires fall. But their echoes remain. How do societies integrate these layers?

**4. War and Recovery**
Like Cambodia, Vietnam endured devastating war. Unlike Cambodia, Vietnam has become an economic powerhouse. What's the difference? What can leaders learn?

**5. Following the Thread**
The Infinite Walk isn't about random destinations. It's about following the natural thread of geography, history, and human connection.

The Mekong connects Cambodia and Vietnam. So the journey continues.
