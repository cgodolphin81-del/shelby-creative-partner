## LinkedIn Post

**Headline:** What Ancient Temples Teach Us About Leadership, Resilience, and Impermanence

**Post:**

I just returned from Angkor Wat in Cambodia—not for the first time, but perhaps for the first time with eyes ready to see.

Episode 110 of The Infinite Walk explores what these 900-year-old temples teach us about:

🏛️ **Building for meaning, not permanence**
The Khmer builders knew their empire would fall. They built anyway. Not for glory, but to say: *We were here. We loved. We created.*

🌿 **Embracing change as teacher**
At Ta Prohm, jungle roots embrace ancient stone. The temple isn't being destroyed—it's being taught. Impermanence isn't tragedy. It's mercy.

🙏 **Choosing creation over bitterness**
Cambodia endured unspeakable suffering. Yet in these temples, there is no bitterness. Only the quiet wisdom of a people who chose to build, again and again.

💡 **The power of return**
You cannot know a place in one visit. You cannot know yourself in one lifetime. Come back. The stones will wait.

In leadership, in business, in life—we spend so much energy trying to make things permanent. What if we spent that energy making things *meaningful* instead?

What if we built knowing it would end, and built anyway?

20-minute meditation on these questions, out now. Link in comments.
