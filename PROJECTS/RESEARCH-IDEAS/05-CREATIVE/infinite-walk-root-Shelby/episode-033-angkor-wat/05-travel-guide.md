## Getting There

### Flights to Siem Reap

**Siem Reap International Airport (REP)** serves as the gateway to Angkor Wat.

**Major Routes:**
- Bangkok (Thailand) — 1 hour
- Ho Chi Minh City (Vietnam) — 1 hour
- Phnom Penh (Cambodia) — 45 minutes
- Singapore — 2 hours
- Kuala Lumpur (Malaysia) — 2 hours
- Seoul (South Korea) — 5-6 hours
- Taipei (Taiwan) — 4 hours

**Airlines:**
- Cambodia Angkor Air (national carrier)
- AirAsia
- Bangkok Airways
- Vietnam Airlines
- Singapore Airlines
- Multiple regional carriers

**Tip:** Book flights to arrive the day before your first temple visit. Jet lag + 5 AM sunrise = rough combination.

### From Airport to Siem Reap

**Distance:** Approximately 12 km from city center

**Options:**

1. **Hotel Pickup** (Recommended)
   - Many hotels offer complimentary airport transfer
   - Driver holds sign with your name
   - Cost: Free or $5-10 USD

2. **Tuk-Tuk**
   - Most authentic experience
   - Open-air, feel the tropical air
   - Cost: $5-7 USD
   - Time: 20-30 minutes

3. **Taxi/Private Car**
   - Air-conditioned comfort
   - Good for families or groups
   - Cost: $10-15 USD
   - Time: 20 minutes

4. **Ride-Hailing Apps**
   - Grab and PassApp operate in Siem Reap
   - Similar pricing to tuk-tuks
   - Convenient app-based booking

## Angkor Passes & Tickets

### Pass Options (2025-2026 Prices)

| Pass Type | Price | Validity | Best For |
|-----------|-------|----------|----------|
| **1-Day Pass** | $37 USD | Same day only | Tight schedules, first-time quick visit |
| **3-Day Pass** | $62 USD | Any 3 days within 10 days | **RECOMMENDED** — Most visitors |
| **7-Day Pass** | $72 USD | Any 7 days within 30 days | Temple enthusiasts, photographers |

**Important Notes:**
- Prices include $2 donation to Kantha Bopha Children's Hospital
- Children under 12 enter free (bring passport for age verification)
- Passes are non-transferable and include your photo
- Must carry pass at all times—random checks occur

### What's Included

Your Angkor Pass grants access to:
- ✅ Angkor Wat
- ✅ Angkor Thom complex (Bayon, Baphuon, terraces)
- ✅ Ta Prohm (Tomb Raider Temple)
- ✅ Banteay Srei (pink sandstone temple)
- ✅ Preah Khan
- ✅ East Mebon
- ✅ Pre Rup
- ✅ Ta Som
- ✅ Banteay Kdei
- ✅ Neak Pean
- ✅ Phnom Bakheng
- ✅ Beng Mealea (included since 2020)
- ✅ And 40+ other temples

**Separate Tickets Required:**
- ❌ Koh Ker Temple Complex — $15 USD
- ❌ Phnom Kulen — ~$20 USD
- ❌ Preah Vihear — Separate pricing

### Where to Buy

**Option 1: Online (HIGHLY RECOMMENDED)**
- Website: Official Angkor Enterprise portal
- Benefits: Skip ticket office queues, upload photo in advance
- Process: Receive QR code instantly, scan at entry
- Essential for sunrise visits (arrive at checkpoint by 5:15 AM)

**Option 2: Main Ticket Office**
- Location: Road 60, Krong Siem Reap
- Hours: 4:30 AM - 5:30 PM daily
- Payment: USD cash or credit cards
- Note: Can have long queues in peak season (Dec-Feb)

**Option 3: Self-Service Kiosks (New 2025)**
- Locations: Main ticket office, Angkor Wat entrance, Heritage Walk mall
- Accept credit cards
- Quick dispensing
- Good backup option

**Insider Tip:** Buy your pass after 5:00 PM the day before. You can enter the park to watch sunset without using a day from your pass. Perfect for orientation!

## Temple Hours

| Temple | Opening Hours | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| **Angkor Wat** | 5:00 AM - 6:00 PM | Sunrise access from 5:00 AM |
| **Phnom Bakheng** | 5:00 AM - 7:00 PM | 300-person capacity limit for sunset |

**Sunrise Strategy:**
- Arrive at Angkor Wat checkpoint by 5:15 AM
- Gates open at 5:00 AM for sunrise visitors
- Best viewing spots fill by 5:30 AM
- Sunrise occurs ~5:45 AM (varies by season)

**Sunset Strategy:**
- Phnom Bakheng: Arrive by 4:00 PM (capacity fills fast)
- Angkor Wat upper levels: Arrive by 3:30 PM
- Pre Rup: Less crowded, good alternative

## Best Time to Visit

### Seasonal Breakdown

**Peak Season: November - February**
- **Weather:** Cool and dry, 20-30°C (68-86°F)
- **Pros:** Perfect weather, clear sunrise views, comfortable exploration
- **Cons:** Largest crowds, higher hotel rates, booked tours
- **Best for:** First-time visitors, photography, comfort seekers
- **Crowd level:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)

**Hot Season: March - May**
- **Weather:** Very hot, up to 35-38°C (95-100°F)
- **Pros:** Fewer tourists, lower prices, dramatic sky colors
- **Cons:** Intense heat, humidity, midday exhaustion
- **Best for:** Budget travelers, heat-tolerant explorers
- **Crowd level:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Moderate)

**Rainy Season: June - October**
- **Weather:** Frequent rain, 25-32°C (77-90°F)
- **Pros:** Lush green landscapes, dramatic storm clouds, fewest tourists
- **Cons:** Muddy paths, some flooding, afternoon downpours
- **Best for:** Photographers, adventurous travelers, budget-conscious
- **Crowd level:** ⭐⭐ (Low)

**Shoulder Months: March, October**
- **Sweet spot:** Good weather + manageable crowds
- **Recommendation:** Target these months if possible

## Getting Around

### Transportation Options

**1. Tuk-Tuk (Most Popular)**
- **Cost:** $15-25 USD per day
- **Capacity:** 2-4 passengers
- **Pros:** Open-air experience, local drivers know best spots, affordable
- **Cons:** No AC, exposed to elements
- **Best for:** Most visitors, couples, small groups

**2. Bicycle**
- **Cost:** $2-6 USD per day rental
- **Pros:** Ultimate freedom, exercise, eco-friendly
- **Cons:** Very hot, long distances between temples, challenging
- **Best for:** Adventurous, fit travelers, solo explorers
- **Note:** E-bikes available for $5-8 USD/day (recommended over regular bikes)

**3. Private Car with Driver**
- **Cost:** $40-60 USD per day
- **Capacity:** 4-6 passengers
- **Pros:** Air-conditioned comfort, shelter from rain, storage space
- **Cons:** More expensive, less atmospheric
- **Best for:** Families, groups, hot weather, rainy season

**4. Motorbike/Scooter**
- **Cost:** $5-10 USD per day rental
- **Pros:** Independence, fun, affordable
- **Cons:** Traffic risks, not recommended for inexperienced riders
- **Best for:** Confident riders, solo travelers

**5. Guided Tour**
- **Cost:** $25-50 USD per day (guide only, excludes transport)
- **Pros:** Historical context, efficient routing, local insights
- **Cons:** Less flexibility, group pace
- **Best for:** History enthusiasts, first-time visitors

### Recommended Strategy

**Hire the same tuk-tuk driver for multiple days.**
- Build rapport
- They learn your preferences
- More reliable than finding new drivers daily
- Typical 3-day package: $45-60 USD total

## Cultural Etiquette

### Dress Code

Angkor Wat is an **active religious site**. Dress respectfully.

**Required:**
- ✅ Shoulders covered (no tank tops, spaghetti straps)
- ✅ Knees covered (shorts/skirts must be knee-length)
- ✅ Remove hats when entering upper levels

**Recommended:**
- Lightweight, breathable fabrics (linen, cotton)
- Comfortable walking shoes (lots of stairs, uneven surfaces)
- Hat and sunglasses for sun protection
- Light scarf (versatile for covering shoulders)

### Temple Behavior

- Speak quietly in sacred spaces
- Do not touch bas-reliefs or carvings (oils damage stone)
- Stay on marked paths
- Ask permission before photographing monks
- Remove shoes when required (signs posted)
- Do not climb on restricted structures

### General Cambodian Customs

- **Greetings:** Sampeah (hands pressed together, slight bow)
- **Head:** Considered sacred—don't touch anyone's head
- **Feet:** Considered low—don't point feet at people or Buddha images
- **Respect for Elders:** Deeply important in Cambodian culture
- **Tipping:** Not required but appreciated ($1-2 for tuk-tuk drivers, $2-5 for guides)

## Practical Tips

### What to Pack

**Essentials:**
- Passport (required for ticket purchase)
- Angkor pass (carry at all times)
- Sunscreen (SPF 50+)
- Insect repellent
- Reusable water bottle
- Hat or cap
- Sunglasses
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Light backpack

**Recommended:**
- Camera with extra batteries
- Power bank for phone
- Small flashlight or headlamp (for early morning)
- Hand sanitizer
- Wet wipes
- Cash in small USD bills ($1, $5, $10)
- Light rain jacket (rainy season)

### Health & Safety

**General Safety:**
- Angkor Wat is very safe for tourists
- Petty theft is the main concern—secure valuables
- Violent crime against tourists is rare

**Health Precautions:**
- Stay hydrated (drink 2-3 liters of water daily)
- Use mosquito repellent (dengue fever risk)
- Wear sunscreen (intense tropical sun)
- Take breaks during midday heat (11 AM - 2 PM)
- Consider travel insurance (recommended)

**Emergency Numbers:**
- Police: 117
- Ambulance: 115
- Fire: 118
- Tourist Police: +855 12 406 420

### Money Matters

- **Currency:** USD widely accepted; Cambodian Riel (KHR) for small purchases
- **Exchange Rate:** ~4,000 KHR = $1 USD
- **ATMs:** Widely available in Siem Reap
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted at hotels, upscale restaurants, some shops
- **Cash:** Essential for markets, street food, tuk-tuks, small vendors

**Tip:** Bring crisp, undamaged USD bills. Torn or old bills may be rejected.

## Sustainable Tourism

### How to Visit Responsibly

**Do:**
- ✅ Hire local guides (supports community)
- ✅ Buy from local vendors
- ✅ Respect dress codes and temple rules
- ✅ Stay on marked paths
- ✅ Use refillable water bottles
- ✅ Tip fairly for good service
- ✅ Learn basic Khmer phrases (hello, thank you)

**Don't:**
- ❌ Touch or lean on ancient carvings
- ❌ Give money or candy to begging children
- ❌ Climb on restricted structures
- ❌ Litter (carry out all trash)
- ❌ Purchase artifacts or antiquities
- ❌ Feed monkeys (they become aggressive)

### Supporting Local Community

- Choose locally-owned accommodations
- Eat at Cambodian restaurants
- Hire Cambodian guides and drivers
- Purchase from artisan cooperatives
- Consider visiting social enterprises (training vulnerable youth)

## Entry Requirements

### Visa

**Options:**
1. **E-Visa** (Recommended)
   - Apply online: evisa.gov.kh
   - Cost: $36 USD
   - Processing: 3 business days
   - Valid: 30 days

2. **Visa on Arrival**
   - Available at airport and land borders
   - Cost: $30 USD
   - Bring: Passport photo, exact change
   - Wait time: 15-45 minutes

3. **Visa-Free Entry**
   - Available for some ASEAN countries
   - Check your nationality's requirements

### E-Arrival Card

**Mandatory since September 2024:**
- Complete online before travel: arrival.gov.kh
- Free of charge
- Speeds up immigration processing
- Required for all visitors

### Passport Validity

- Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond entry date
- Ensure you have blank pages for visa stamps
