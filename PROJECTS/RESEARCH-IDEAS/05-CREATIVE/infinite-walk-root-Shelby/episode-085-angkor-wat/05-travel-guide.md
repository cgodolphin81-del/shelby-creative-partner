## GETTING THERE

### Flights to Siem Reap

#### Siem Reap-Angkor International Airport (SAI)
- **Opened:** October 2023 (replaced old REP airport)
- **Distance:** 50 km from Siem Reap city center (45-60 minutes by road)
- **Airlines:** 
  - Cambodia Angkor Air (regional hub)
  - AirAsia (Kuala Lumpur, Bangkok)
  - Bangkok Airways (Bangkok)
  - VietJet Air (Ho Chi Minh City, Hanoi)
  - Singapore Airlines (Singapore, seasonal)
  - China Eastern, China Southern (various Chinese cities)
  - Thai Airways (Bangkok)
  - Qatar Airways (via Bangkok/Doha)

#### Alternative: Fly to Phnom Penh
- **Phnom Penh International Airport (PNH):** Larger, more international connections
- **Ground Transfer to Siem Reap:**
  - Domestic flight: 45 minutes, $80-150
  - Bus: 5-6 hours, $12-25
  - Private car: 5-6 hours, $100-150

### From Thailand
- **Bangkok to Siem Reap:**
  - Flight: 1 hour, $80-200
  - Bus: 8-10 hours, $25-40 (border crossing included)
  - Private car: 6-7 hours, $150-200

### From Vietnam
- **Ho Chi Minh City to Siem Reap:**
  - Flight: 1 hour, $100-250
  - Bus: 6-7 hours, $20-35 (border crossing included)
  - Boat: Not currently operating regularly

### From Laos
- **Pakse to Siem Reap:**
  - Bus: 6-8 hours, $25-35
  - Via Stung Treng (border crossing)

## VISAS & ENTRY

### Visa Requirements
- **Visa on Arrival:** Available for most nationalities
- **E-Visa:** Available online before travel
- **Cost:** $30 USD (tourist visa), $35 USD (e-visa)
- **Validity:** 30 days, single entry
- **Extension:** Possible for additional 30 days ($45-60)

### E-Visa Application
- **Website:** www.evisa.gov.kh
- **Processing:** 3 business days
- **Requirements:**
  - Passport valid 6+ months
  - Digital passport photo
  - Credit/debit card for payment
  - Email for delivery

### Visa on Arrival
- **Available at:** Siem Reap Airport, Poipet border (Thailand), Bavet border (Vietnam)
- **Requirements:**
  - Passport valid 6+ months
  - One passport photo (or $2 extra for photo on-site)
  - $30 USD cash (exact change preferred)
  - Completed arrival card

### Important Notes
- **Passport Photos:** Bring 2-4 extras (needed for Angkor pass, SIM cards, etc.)
- **USD Currency:** Bring crisp, undamaged USD bills (Cambodia is dual-currency)
- **Vaccinations:** No required vaccinations, but Hepatitis A, Typhoid recommended

## ANGKOR PASSES

### Ticket Prices (2026)
- **1-Day Pass:** $37 USD
- **3-Day Pass:** $62 USD (valid 10 days, use any 3 days)
- **7-Day Pass:** $72 USD (valid 1 month, use any 7 days)
- **Children:** Under 12 free (passport required for verification)

### Where to Buy
1. **Online (Recommended):**
   - Website: Angkor Enterprise official site
   - App: "Angkor Ticket" (iOS/Android)
   - Instant download, QR code on phone
   - No photo needed (taken during first scan)

2. **Ticket Center (In-Person):**
   - Location: Charles de Gaulle Road, Siem Reap
   - Hours: 5:00 AM - 5:30 PM daily
   - Photo taken on-site
   - Can purchase day before visit

3. **At Temple Gates (Limited):**
   - Some gates have ticket booths
   - Open from 5:00 AM for sunrise visitors
   - May have longer lines

### Important Rules
- **Non-Transferable:** Photo on pass, checked at temples
- **Non-Consecutive:** 3-day pass can be used over 10 days
- **Checkpoints:** Random checks at temples, keep pass with you
- **Lost Pass:** No replacement, must buy new one

### Tips
- **Buy Day Before:** Save morning time, especially for sunrise
- **3-Day Best Value:** Most visitors need 2-3 full days
- **Keep Dry:** Laminate or use plastic sleeve (monsoon season)
- **Digital Backup:** Screenshot QR code in case of phone issues

## GETTING AROUND

### Tuk-Tuks (Most Popular)
- **Small Circuit (half-day):** $15-20
- **Grand Circuit (full-day):** $20-25
- **Angkor Wat Sunrise + Full Day:** $25-30
- **Multi-Day Discount:** $50-70 for 3 days
- **Capacity:** 2-4 passengers
- **Pros:** Open-air, affordable, experienced drivers
- **Cons:** No AC, slower than cars, weather exposure

### Private Cars
- **Full Day:** $35-50
- **Multi-Day Discount:** $100-140 for 3 days
- **Capacity:** 4-6 passengers
- **Pros:** Air-conditioned, faster, weather protection
- **Cons:** More expensive, less atmospheric

### Motorbikes/Scooters
- **Rental:** $10-15 per day
- **Requirements:** Valid motorcycle license, passport deposit
- **Pros:** Freedom, flexibility, affordable
- **Cons:** Hot, dangerous, police checkpoints, not recommended for inexperienced riders

### Bicycles
- **Rental:** $5-10 per day
- **E-Bikes:** $15-20 per day
- **Pros:** Eco-friendly, good exercise, flexible
- **Cons:** Very hot (35-40°C), hilly terrain, time-consuming
- **Best For:** Fit cyclists, dry season, short distances

### Grab App
- **Availability:** Siem Reap city only (not at temples)
- **Pricing:** Similar to tuk-tuks
- **Pros:** Fixed price, cashless option, reliable
- **Cons:** Can't wait at temples, need to book return

### Walking
- **Within Temples:** Extensive walking required (5-15 km per day)
- **Between Temples:** Not recommended (distances too far, no shade)

## BEST TIME TO VISIT

### Dry Season (November - April)
**Pros:**
- Clear skies, minimal rain
- Cooler temperatures (Nov-Feb: 25-30°C)
- Best photography conditions
- All temples accessible

**Cons:**
- Peak tourist season (Dec-Jan most crowded)
- Higher prices
- Need to book accommodations early
- Can be very hot (Mar-Apr: 35-40°C)

**Best Months:** November, February (balance of weather and crowds)

### Wet Season (May - October)
**Pros:**
- Fewer tourists (50-70% fewer)
- Lush green jungle
- Lower prices (30-50% off)
- Moats and pools full (better reflections)
- Dramatic cloud formations

**Cons:**
- Daily afternoon storms (usually 1-2 hours)
- High humidity (80-90%)
- Some dirt roads may be impassable
- Sunrise may be obscured by clouds

**Best Months:** May-June, September-October (shoulder seasons)

### Special Considerations
- **Khmer New Year:** Mid-April (very crowded, book early)
- **Chinese New Year:** January/February (crowded)
- **Harvest Season:** November (beautiful rice field colors)
- **Lotus Season:** August-October (lilies in full bloom)

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Dress Code
- **Temples:** Shoulders and knees must be covered
- **No:** Tank tops, short shorts, mini skirts
- **Yes:** T-shirts, long shorts, pants, skirts below knee
- **Scarf/Shawl:** Carry to cover up if needed
- **Shoes:** Remove before entering sacred areas

### Temple Etiquette
- **Voices:** Keep quiet, especially in prayer areas
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing monks/people
- **Touch:** Do not touch carvings, bas-reliefs, or Buddha images
- **Feet:** Never point feet at Buddha images or people
- **Heads:** Do not touch anyone's head (considered sacred)
- **Monks:** Women should not touch monks or hand objects directly
- **Prayer:** Observe respectfully, do not interrupt

### General Etiquette
- **Greetings:** "Chum reap suor" (hello), hands in prayer position (sampeah)
- **Respect:** Elders are highly respected in Cambodian culture
- **Anger:** Public displays of anger cause loss of face
- **Bargaining:** Expected at markets, do it politely with a smile
- **Photography:** Always ask first, especially of people

### Sacred Sites
- **Buddha Images:** Show respect, no climbing
- **Monks:** Ask before photographing, women maintain distance
- **Offerings:** Do not disturb flowers, incense, offerings
- **Prayer:** Observe quietly, do not walk in front of praying people

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money
- **Currency:** Dual currency - USD and Cambodian Riel (KHR)
- **Exchange Rate:** $1 USD ≈ 4,100 KHR
- **USD Bills:** Must be crisp, undamaged (no tears, marks, stamps)
- **ATMs:** Widely available, $5-7 fee per withdrawal
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted at hotels, upscale restaurants, not at markets
- **Small Change:** Keep $1, $5 bills and riel for tips, vendors

### Health & Safety
- **Water:** Do NOT drink tap water, bottled water only
- **Ice:** Generally safe (commercially made)
- **Food:** Street food usually safe, watch for hygiene
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, Typhoid recommended
- **Malaria:** Low risk in Siem Reap, consult doctor
- **Heat:** Stay hydrated, rest during midday (11 AM - 2 PM)
- **Sun:** High SPF sunscreen, hat, sunglasses essential
- **Insects:** Mosquito repellent (dengue risk)

### Connectivity
- **SIM Cards:** $5-10 at airport or city (Cellcard, Smart, Metfone)
- **Data:** Generous packages, 4G widely available
- **WiFi:** Most hotels, restaurants, cafes have free WiFi
- **Power:** 220V, Type A/C/G outlets (bring adapter)

### Safety
- **Crime:** Low violent crime, watch for petty theft
- **Scams:** Verify prices before services, agree on tuk-tuk fares
- **Landmines:** Stay on marked paths at remote temples
- **Monkeys:** Do not feed, secure belongings (Ta Prohm, Phnom Bakheng)
- **Traffic:** Chaotic, cross roads carefully
- **Emergency:** Tourist Police +855 12 942 484, Ambulance 115

### Packing List
- **Clothing:** Light, breathable, modest (shoulders/knees covered)
- **Footwear:** Comfortable walking shoes, sandals
- **Sun Protection:** Hat, sunscreen (SPF 50+), sunglasses
- **Hydration:** Reusable water bottle (many refill stations)
- **Camera:** Extra batteries, memory cards, tripod (for sunrise)
- **Daypack:** For water, sunscreen, guidebook
- **Light:** Headlamp/flashlight for sunrise visits
- **Medications:** Personal prescriptions, basic first aid
- **Documents:** Passport, visa, Angkor pass, travel insurance
- **Cash:** USD in small denominations
