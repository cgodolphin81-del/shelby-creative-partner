## ✈️ GETTING THERE

### By Air

**JFK International Airport**
- Location: Queens, 15 miles from Manhattan
- Best for: International flights, most major carriers
- To Manhattan: 
  - AirTrain + Subway (LIRR or E train): $10.75, 45-60 min
  - Taxi/Uber: $70-100 + tip, 45-90 min depending on traffic
  - Shared shuttle: $30-40, 60-90 min

**LaGuardia Airport (LGA)**
- Location: Queens, 8 miles from Manhattan
- Best for: Domestic flights
- To Manhattan:
  - Bus + Subway: $2.90, 45-60 min
  - Taxi/Uber: $40-60 + tip, 30-60 min
  - Shared shuttle: $30-40, 45-75 min

**Newark Liberty International (EWR)**
- Location: Newark, NJ (technically not NYC)
- Best for: United hub, sometimes cheaper flights
- To Manhattan:
  - AirTrain + NJ Transit: $15.50, 45-60 min to Penn Station
  - Taxi/Uber: $70-100 + tip, 45-90 min

**Pro Tip:** Book flights into JFK for best transit options. LGA is closest but has worst public transit connections.

### By Car

**Don't.**

Seriously. Unless you're planning to leave it parked for your entire stay:
- Traffic is brutal
- Parking is $50-75/day in Manhattan
- Street parking is nearly impossible
- Traffic cameras and tickets are everywhere

If you must drive:
- Use SpotHero or ParkWhistle for garage deals
- Expect to pay $40-80/day
- Never leave anything visible in the car

## 📅 BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### By Season

**Spring (April-June) ⭐ BEST**
- Weather: 50-75°F, pleasant
- Crowds: Moderate to high
- Pros: Cherry blossoms, outdoor seating, perfect walking weather
- Cons: Popular time, higher prices
- Events: Tribeca Film Festival, Cherry Blossoms (Brooklyn Botanic Garden)

**Summer (July-August)**
- Weather: 75-95°F, hot and humid
- Crowds: High (tourist peak)
- Pros: Outdoor concerts, Shakespeare in the Park, long days
- Cons: Heat, humidity, crowds, higher prices
- Events: Fourth of July fireworks, SummerStage concerts

**Fall (September-November) ⭐ BEST**
- Weather: 50-70°F, crisp and comfortable
- Crowds: Moderate
- Pros: Fall foliage, perfect weather, cultural season begins
- Cons: Popular time
- Events: NYC Wine & Food Festival, Halloween Parade, Thanksgiving Parade

**Winter (December-March)**
- Weather: 20-45°F, cold, possible snow
- Crowds: Low (except holidays)
- Pros: Fewer tourists, lower prices, holiday magic
- Cons: Cold, possible snow/ice, some outdoor attractions closed
- Events: Rockefeller Center tree, New Year's Eve, Restaurant Week

### By Month

**January-February:** Cheapest, coldest, fewest crowds
**March:** Still cold, early spring deals
**April-May:** Ideal weather, cherry blossoms
**June:** Great weather, summer begins, prices rise
**July-August:** Hot, crowded, expensive
**September-October:** Perfect weather, fall colors
**November:** Cool, Thanksgiving crowds
**December:** Holiday magic, very crowded, expensive

## 🚇 GETTING AROUND

### Subway (Best Option)

**Basics:**
- 24/7 operation
- $2.90 per ride (OMNY contactless payment)
- 7-day unlimited: $34 (worth it if 12+ rides)
- Use OMNY card or contactless credit card/phone

**Tips:**
- Download Citymapper or Google Maps
- Avoid empty cars late at night (ride where conductor is)
- Let passengers exit before boarding
- Stand right, walk left on escalators
- Express trains skip local stations (check carefully)

**Avoid:**
- Rush hour if possible (8-9:30 AM, 5-7 PM)
- Late night alone if unfamiliar
- L train during major construction (check MTA updates)

### Bus

- Same fare as subway ($2.90)
- Free transfers to/from subway within 2 hours
- Slower than subway but above ground (see the city)
- Good for crosstown routes

### Taxi/Uber

- Taxis: Hail on street (light on top = available)
- Uber/Lyft: App-based, surge pricing
- Tip: 15-20%
- Avoid during rush hour (you'll walk faster)

### Citi Bike

- $15/day or $30/month
- First 30 minutes included, then extra fees
- Great for parks and waterfront
- Wear a helmet (not provided)

### Walking

- Best way to experience the city
- Manhattan is very walkable (grid system)
- Comfortable shoes essential
- 20 blocks = roughly 1 mile

## 🏨 NEIGHBORHOODS TO STAY

### Manhattan

**Midtown (Times Square area)**
- Pros: Central, near attractions, many hotels
- Cons: Touristy, crowded, expensive, not "real" NYC
- Best for: First-time visitors, short stays

**Lower Manhattan (Financial District)**
- Pros: Quiet weekends, near 9/11, good value
- Cons: Dead on weekends, far from uptown
- Best for: Business travelers, history buffs

**Greenwich Village/West Village**
- Pros: Charming, great food, walkable
- Cons: Expensive, fewer hotels
- Best for: Repeat visitors, foodies

**Upper West Side**
- Pros: Near Central Park, museums, residential feel
- Cons: Far from downtown, pricier
- Best for: Families, museum lovers

**Upper East Side**
- Pros: Upscale, Museum Mile, quiet
- Cons: Expensive, less nightlife
- Best for: Luxury travelers, museum enthusiasts

**DUMBO/Brooklyn Heights**
- Pros: Manhattan views, Brooklyn Bridge access
- Cons: Expensive, touristy
- Best for: Views, romantic trips

- Pros: Hip, great food, nightlife
- Cons: Crowded, pricey, far from Manhattan

- Cons: Far from Manhattan, residential

## ⚠️ SAFETY & TIPS

### General Safety

**NYC is very safe for a major city**, but use common sense:

- Keep valuables secure (pickpockets in tourist areas)
- Don't leave bags unattended
- Stay aware in crowded places
- Use hotel safes for passports/extra cash
- Don't flash expensive jewelry/electronics

### Neighborhood Safety

**Very Safe:**
- Most of Manhattan (below 110th St)
- Brooklyn Heights, Park Slope, DUMBO
- Queens: Astoria, Long Island City
- Staten Island (suburban feel)

**Use Caution (especially at night):**
- Parts of the Bronx
- Parts of Brooklyn (East New York, Brownsville)
- Parts of Harlem (north of 125th)
- Parts of Queens (Jamaica, some areas)

**Rule of Thumb:** If an area feels sketchy, it probably is. Trust your instincts.

### Subway Safety

- Ride in cars with other people (especially late night)
- Stay near conductor's booth
- Don't fall asleep
- Keep bags/purses secure
- Avoid empty platforms late at night

### Scams to Avoid

- "Free" CDs/mixtapes (they'll demand money)
- Three-card monte (it's always a scam)
- Broken taxi meter (insist on meter or agree on fare)
- "Discount" Broadway tickets from street sellers
- People offering to take your photo (may steal phone)

### Emergency Numbers

- **911:** Emergency (police, fire, medical)
- **311:** Non-emergency city services
- **MTA Info:** 511 (transit info)

## 🎭 CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### New York Customs

**Walking:**
- Walk fast. Don't stop in the middle of the sidewalk.
- Stand right, walk left (especially on escalators)
- Don't block doorways or subway doors

**Subway:**
- Let passengers exit before boarding
- Move to center of car
- No music without headphones
- Offer seats to elderly/pregnant/disabled
- Don't put bags on seats during rush hour

**Tipping:**
- Restaurants: 18-22%
- Bars: $1-2 per drink or 18-20%
- Taxis/Uber: 15-20%
- Hotel housekeeping: $3-5/night
- Bellhop: $2-5 per bag
- Tour guides: 15-20%

**General:**
- New Yorkers are direct, not rude
- We're all in a hurry—don't take it personally
- Eye contact is brief, not confrontational
- Personal space is limited—accept it

### What NOT to Do

- Don't walk slowly in a group, blocking the entire sidewalk
- Don't stand on the left side of escalators
- Don't ask "Is it safe?" within earshot of locals
- Don't complain about prices (it's expensive for everyone)
- Don't say "New York City, New York" (just "New York City" or "NYC")
- Don't take photos of people without asking

## 💡 INSIDER TIPS

1. **Museum Pay-What-You-Wish:** The Met and Natural History Museum are technically pay-what-you-wish for NY residents, but tourists often aren't asked. You can pay less than suggested if needed.

2. **Free Staten Island Ferry:** Best free Statue of Liberty view. Round trip takes about 1 hour.

3. **TKTS Booths:** Same-day Broadway tickets at 20-50% off. Times Square location is iconic, but Downtown and Lincoln Center have shorter lines.

4. **Restaurant Week:** January and February, June and July. Fixed-price menus at top restaurants ($30-60).

5. **Free Summer Events:** Shakespeare in the Park (free tickets, line early), SummerStage concerts, outdoor movies.

6. **Happy Hours:** Many bars have 4-7 PM happy hours with discounted drinks and food.

7. **CityPASS:** If doing multiple attractions, CityPASS saves 40%+ on combination tickets.

8. **Bodega Breakfast:** Egg and cheese on roll ($5-8) is the real NYC breakfast.

9. **Avoid Times Square Restaurants:** Tourist traps with mediocre food. Walk 10 blocks in any direction for better options.

10. **Tip the Coat Check:** $1-2 per item, even if "free."
