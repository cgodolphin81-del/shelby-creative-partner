## 📷 PHOTOGRAPHY STYLE

### Lighting

**Golden Hour (Preferred)**
- Time: Sunrise (5:30-7 AM), Sunset (6-8 PM)
- Quality: Warm, soft, directional
- Mood: Hopeful, cinematic, nostalgic
- Best for: Brooklyn Bridge, Central Park, skyline

**Blue Hour**
- Time: Just before sunrise, just after sunset
- Quality: Cool, even, ethereal
- Mood: Contemplative, peaceful, mysterious
- Best for: Skyline shots, bridge silhouettes

**Midday (Use Sparingly)**
- Time: 11 AM - 3 PM
- Quality: Harsh, high contrast
- Mood: Energetic, overwhelming, real
- Best for: Times Square, street scenes, crowds

**Night**
- Time: 9 PM - 4 AM
- Quality: Artificial, mixed sources
- Mood: Intimate, electric, lonely
- Best for: Neon, empty streets, diners

---

### Composition

**Rule of Thirds**
- Place horizon on third lines
- Position subjects at intersection points
- Creates balance and interest

**Leading Lines**
- Subway tracks, bridge cables, street grids
- Draw eye through image
- Creates depth and movement

**Framing**
- Archways, windows, bridge cables
- Frame the subject within the subject
- Adds layers and context

**Scale**
- Tiny human against massive skyline
- Emphasizes city's immensity
- Evokes humility and wonder

**Reflections**
- Puddles, windows, water
- Doubles the visual interest
- Creates symmetry and depth

---

### Angles & Perspectives

**Low Angle (Looking Up)**
- Emphasizes building height
- Makes viewer feel small
- Best for: Skyscrapers, bridges

**High Angle (Looking Down)**
- From observation decks, bridges
- Shows city as organism
- Best for: Skyline, crowds, patterns

**Eye Level**
- Most natural, human perspective
- Creates intimacy
- Best for: Street scenes, portraits

**Dutch Angle (Tilted)**
- Slight tilt for unease
- Use sparingly
- Best for: Times Square chaos, subway

**POV (Point of View)**
- Viewer sees through walker's eyes
- Immersive, personal
- Best for: Walking sequences
