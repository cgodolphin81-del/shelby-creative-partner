# The Great Sphinx of Giza - Research

## Overview
- **Name:** The Great Sphinx (Arabic: أبو الهول, "The Terrifying One")
- **Built:** c. 2500 BCE (during reign of Khafre, 4th Dynasty)
- **Location:** Giza Plateau, east of the pyramids
- **Dimensions:** 
  - Length: 73 meters (240 feet)
  - Height: 20 meters (66 feet)
  - Width: 19 meters (63 feet)
  - Face: 4 meters (13 feet) wide
  - Eyes: 1 meter (3.3 feet) tall

## Construction
- Carved from a single massive limestone outcrop
- Body of a lion, head of a human (believed to be Pharaoh Khafre)
- Originally painted in bright colors (traces still visible)
- Part of Khafre's pyramid complex
- Guarded the Giza necropolis for 4,500+ years

## Historical Timeline
- **c. 2500 BCE:** Constructed during Old Kingdom
- **c. 1400 BCE:** Buried in sand up to neck, Thutmose IV cleared it (Dream Stele)
- **c. 1200 BCE:** Buried again, Ramses II restored
- **6th Century BCE:** Herodotus visited, made no mention (already buried?)
- **1817:** First modern excavation by Giovanni Caviglia
- **1925-1936:** Major restoration by Émile Baraize
- **1980s-Present:** Ongoing restoration and conservation

## The Mystery of the Nose
- Common myth: Napoleon's cannon shot it off (FALSE - he never did this)
- Reality: Damaged between 3rd-10th centuries CE
- Some attribute to Muhammad Sa'im al-Dahr (1378 CE), a Sufi Muslim who opposed idolatry
- Others suggest natural erosion or deliberate iconoclasm
- Nose fragments found during excavations, now in British Museum and Egyptian Museum

## Erosion Controversy
- **Traditional View:** Wind and sand erosion over millennia
- **Water Erosion Hypothesis:** Geologist Robert Schoch argues water erosion patterns suggest Sphinx is much older (10,000+ BCE)
- **Mainstream Response:** Limestone quality varies; different layers erode differently
- **Consensus:** Still debated, but mainstream Egyptology maintains Old Kingdom dating

## Modern Experience
- **Access:** Included in Giza Plateau ticket
- **Viewing:** Can walk around the Sphinx (except paws area, often restricted)
- **Best Light:** Morning light illuminates the face beautifully
- **Sound & Light Show:** Evening spectacle available
- **Photography:** Excellent from multiple angles

## Symbolism & Meaning
- **Lion Body:** Represents strength, power, royalty
- **Human Head:** Intelligence, wisdom, divine kingship
- **Guardian Role:** Protector of the Giza necropolis
- **Solar Connection:** Associated with sun god Ra-Horakhty
- **Dream Stele:** Tells of Thutmose IV's dream where Sphinx promised him kingship if he cleared the sand

## Cultural Impact
- Inspired countless works of art, literature, and film
- Symbol of mystery and ancient wisdom
- Featured in everything from Agatha Christie to The Mummy
- Continues to captivate millions of visitors annually
- UNESCO World Heritage Site (1979)
