# Episode 038: Pyramids of Giza - Complete Travel Guide

## Getting There

### Flights to Cairo
- **Airport:** Cairo International Airport (CAI)
- **Airlines:** EgyptAir, Emirates, Qatar Airways, Turkish Airlines, Lufthansa, British Airways, Air France
- **From US:** Typically 12-15 hours with one stop (Europe or Middle East)
- **From Europe:** 3-4 hours direct from major cities
- **Visa:** Available on arrival for most Western passports ($25 USD) or e-visa online before travel
- **Best Booking Time:** 2-3 months in advance for best prices

### From Airport to Giza/Cairo
- **Uber/Careem:** Most convenient, 45-60 minutes to Giza, $10-15 USD
- **Private Transfer:** Pre-booked hotel transfer, $20-30 USD
- **Taxi:** Available but negotiate fare beforehand, $15-25 USD
- **Public Bus:** Cheapest option, not recommended with luggage

### Getting to Giza Plateau
- **From Cairo Hotels:** Uber/Careem 30-45 minutes, $5-10 USD
- **Organized Tours:** Most include hotel pickup
- **Public Bus:** From Tahrir Square, very cheap but crowded
- **Tip:** Have your hotel write down the address in Arabic for taxi drivers

## Costs & Budget (2024 Estimates)

### Entry Tickets
- **Giza Plateau General Entry:** 240 EGP (~$8 USD)
- **Enter Great Pyramid (Interior):** Additional 440 EGP (~$14 USD)
- **Enter Khafre Pyramid:** Additional 100 EGP (~$3 USD)
- **Enter Menkaure Pyramid:** Additional 100 EGP (~$3 USD)
- **Solar Boat Museum:** 60 EGP (~$2 USD)
- **Sound & Light Show:** 400 EGP (~$13 USD)
- **Camel/Horse Ride:** 200-500 EGP (~$6-16 USD) - negotiate!

### Egyptian Museum (Tahrir)
- **General Entry:** 200 EGP (~$6.50 USD)
- **Royal Mummies Room:** Additional 180 EGP (~$6 USD)
- **Photography Pass:** 50 EGP (~$1.60 USD)

### Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)
- **Expected:** 300-500 EGP (~$10-16 USD) when fully open
- **Partial tours currently available**

### Saqqara
- **General Entry:** 150 EGP (~$5 USD)
- **Enter Step Pyramid:** Additional 100 EGP (~$3 USD)
- **Enter Tombs:** Some require additional small fees

### Daily Budget Estimates
- **Budget Traveler:** $30-50 USD/day (hostels, street food, public transport)
- **Mid-Range:** $75-150 USD/day (3-4 star hotels, restaurants, Uber/tours)
- **Luxury:** $250-500+ USD/day (5-star hotels, fine dining, private guides)

### Tipping (Baksheesh)
- **Expected everywhere:** Small change for most services
- **Guides:** 100-200 EGP/day ($3-6 USD)
- **Drivers:** 50-100 EGP/day ($1.50-3 USD)
- **Restaurants:** 10-12% if not included
- **Bathrooms:** 5-10 EGP ($0.15-0.30 USD)
- **Carry small bills:** Essential for tipping

## Tours & Guides

### Types of Tours
1. **Group Bus Tours:** Cheapest ($30-60 USD/day), least flexible
2. **Small Group Tours:** Better experience ($60-120 USD/day)
3. **Private Tours:** Most flexible ($150-300 USD/day for 1-4 people)
4. **Specialty Tours:** Photography, history, archaeology focused (premium pricing)

### Recommended Tour Companies
- **Emo Tours Egypt:** Highly rated, good value private tours
- **Egypt Tailor Made:** Custom itineraries, excellent guides
- **Memphis Tours:** Large operator, reliable quality
- **Ramasside Tours:** Well-reviewed, knowledgeable guides
- **GetYourGuide/Viator:** Book individual tours, read reviews

### Guide Tips
- **Licensed Guides Only:** Ask to see their license
- **Egyptologists:** Ensure they have proper archaeology/history training
- **Language:** Confirm English (or your language) proficiency
- **Tips:** 10-15% of tour cost for excellent service
- **Book in Advance:** Especially for peak season

### Self-Guided Option
- **Possible:** Yes, Giza is accessible independently
- **Audio Guides:** Available at major sites
- **Guidebooks:** Lonely Planet, Rick Steves, DK Eyewitness
- **Apps:** Google Maps works, download offline maps
- **Pros:** Flexibility, lower cost
- **Cons:** Less context, may miss hidden gems

## Best Times to Visit

### By Season

#### **High Season (October - April)**
- **Weather:** Pleasant, 20-25°C (68-77°F) daytime
- **Crowds:** Heavy, especially December-February
- **Prices:** Highest of the year
- **Pros:** Best weather, all sites fully open
- **Cons:** Crowds, higher prices, book well in advance

#### **Shoulder Season (May, September)**
- **Weather:** Warm to hot, 25-35°C (77-95°F)
- **Crowds:** Moderate
- **Prices:** Mid-range
- **Pros:** Good balance of weather and crowds
- **Cons:** Can be quite hot, especially May

#### **Low Season (June - August)**
- **Weather:** Very hot, 35-45°C (95-113°F)
- **Crowds:** Lightest
- **Prices:** Lowest
- **Pros:** Few tourists, best deals
- **Cons:** Extremely hot, limited midday activity possible

### By Time of Day
- **Opening Time (8 AM):** Best for avoiding crowds and heat
- **Morning (8-11 AM):** Ideal light for photography, cooler
- **Midday (11 AM-3 PM):** Hottest, most crowded, seek shade
- **Late Afternoon (3-5 PM):** Good light, cooling down
- **Sunset:** Magical but crowded, limited time before closing

### Best Months Overall
1. **November:** Perfect weather, manageable crowds
2. **February-March:** Great weather, spring blooms
3. **October:** End of heat, crowds building
4. **April:** Warm but good, Easter crowds possible

### Days of Week
- **Avoid:** Friday (Muslim holy day, locals off work)
- **Best:** Sunday-Thursday (standard work week)
- **Saturday:** Moderate crowds

## Cultural Essentials

### Dress Code
- **Conservative Dress Recommended:** Especially for women
- **Women:** Cover shoulders and knees, loose-fitting clothing
- **Men:** Shorts acceptable but long pants more respectful
- **Scarf:** Useful for covering hair when entering mosques
- **Shoes:** Comfortable walking shoes, easy to remove (for mosques)
- **Sun Protection:** Hat, sunglasses, sunscreen essential

### Customs & Etiquette
- **Greetings:** "As-salamu alaykum" (peace be upon you)
- **Right Hand:** Use right hand for eating, greeting, giving/receiving
- **Photography:** Always ask before photographing people
- **Mosques:** Remove shoes, women cover hair, dress modestly
- **Ramadan:** Eating/drinking in public during daylight hours discouraged
- **Friday:** Holy day, some sites may have reduced hours

### Tipping Culture (Baksheesh)
- **Expected:** For almost all services
- **Small Change:** Keep plenty of 5, 10, 20 EGP notes
- **Who to Tip:** Guides, drivers, bathroom attendants, porters, waiters
- **How Much:** Small amounts add up, budget $5-10/day for tips

### Safety & Scams
- **Generally Safe:** Violent crime against tourists rare
- **Common Scams:**
  - "Free" gifts then demand payment
  - Camel rides with inflated prices
  - "Closed" site redirects to shops
  - Overcharging in taxis (use Uber)
  - Fake guides without licenses
- **Prevention:** Book through reputable companies, agree on prices upfront, walk away from pressure

### Health & Safety
- **Water:** Drink bottled water only, avoid ice
- **Food:** Eat at reputable restaurants, avoid street food if sensitive
- **Sun:** Extreme sun protection necessary
- **Heat:** Stay hydrated, rest during midday heat
- **Insurance:** Travel insurance highly recommended
- **Vaccinations:** Check with doctor (Hepatitis A, Typhoid recommended)
- **Medications:** Bring prescriptions in original containers

### Money & Payments
- **Currency:** Egyptian Pound (EGP)
- **Exchange:** Better rates in Egypt than before travel
- **ATMs:** Widely available in Cairo/Giza
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted at hotels, larger restaurants, museums
- **Cash:** Essential for tips, small vendors, taxis
- **USD:** Some places accept USD but change may be in EGP

## Practical Tips

### What to Pack
- Comfortable walking shoes (essential!)
- Sun hat and sunglasses
- Sunscreen (high SPF)
- Light, breathable clothing
- Scarf (sun protection and mosque visits)
- Reusable water bottle
- Camera and extra batteries
- Portable phone charger
- Hand sanitizer
- Small backpack for day trips
- Cash in small denominations

### Connectivity
- **SIM Cards:** Available at airport (Vodafone, Orange, Etisalat)
- **Cost:** ~$10-20 USD for tourist SIM with data
- **WiFi:** Available at most hotels and cafes
- **Apps to Download:** Uber, Careem, Google Translate, Google Maps (offline)

### Language
- **Official:** Arabic
- **Tourist Areas:** English widely spoken
- **Useful Phrases:**
  - Hello: As-salamu alaykum
  - Thank you: Shukran
  - Yes: Na'am
  - No: La'a
  - How much?: Kam?
  - Too expensive: Ghali awi

### Photography
- **Allowed:** Most exterior areas
- **Restricted:** Inside some tombs (no flash), mummy rooms (no photos)
- **Tickets:** Photography passes required for some areas
- **Drones:** Generally not allowed without permit
- **Best Light:** Golden hour (sunrise/sunset)
- **Tripods:** May require special permission

### Accessibility
- **Limited:** Ancient sites not designed for accessibility
- **Giza:** Some areas accessible, but sand and uneven surfaces challenging
- **Museums:** Generally more accessible
- **Wheelchairs:** Can be difficult at archaeological sites
- **Mobility Issues:** Consider private tours with flexible pacing

## Suggested Itineraries

### One Day (Giza Focus)
- **8:00 AM:** Arrive at Giza Plateau (beat crowds)
- **8:30-10:30:** Explore Great Pyramid exterior and interior
- **10:30-11:30:** Visit Sphinx and Valley Temple
- **11:30-12:30:** Khafre and Menkaure pyramids
- **12:30-2:00:** Lunch with pyramid view
- **2:00-4:00:** Solar Boat Museum, more photography
- **4:00 PM:** Depart or stay for sunset

### Two Days (Giza + Cairo)
- **Day 1:** Full day at Giza Plateau (as above)
- **Day 2:** 
  - Morning: Egyptian Museum (Tahrir)
  - Afternoon: Khan el-Khalili bazaar, Islamic Cairo
  - Evening: Nile dinner cruise or Sound & Light Show

### Three Days (Giza + Saqqara + Cairo)
- **Day 1:** Giza Plateau full day
- **Day 2:** 
  - Morning: Saqqara (Step Pyramid, tombs)
  - Afternoon: Dahshur (Bent Pyramid, Red Pyramid)
  - Evening: Return to Cairo
- **Day 3:**
  - Morning: Egyptian Museum or Grand Egyptian Museum
  - Afternoon: Islamic/Coptic Cairo, bazaar
  - Evening: Nile felucca ride at sunset

### Five Days (Complete Experience)
- **Days 1-2:** Giza Plateau (leisurely exploration)
- **Day 3:** Saqqara and Dahshur
- **Day 4:** Cairo (museums, Islamic/Coptic sites, bazaar)
- **Day 5:** Nile cruise day trip or Alexandria excursion

## Food & Dining

### Must-Try Egyptian Dishes
- **Koshary:** Rice, pasta, lentils, chickpeas, tomato sauce (national dish)
- **Ful Medames:** Fava bean stew, breakfast staple
- **Ta'ameya:** Egyptian falafel (made with fava beans)
- **Shawarma:** Spiced meat in bread
- **Grilled Meats:** Kebab, kofta, shish tawook
- **Molokhia:** Green soup, often with rabbit or chicken
- **Mahshi:** Stuffed vegetables (eggplant, zucchini, peppers)
- **Fatta:** Rice, bread, meat, garlic vinegar sauce
- **Umm Ali:** Bread pudding dessert

### Where to Eat
- **Street Food:** Cheap, authentic, watch for cleanliness
- **Local Restaurants:** Better quality, reasonable prices
- **Hotel Restaurants:** Highest prices, reliable quality
- **Nile Restaurants:** Scenic, tourist-oriented
- **Recommended:** Abou Tarek (koshary), Sequoia (Nile view), Zooba (modern Egyptian)

### Dietary Restrictions
- **Vegetarian:** Many options (ful, ta'ameya, koshary, salads)
- **Vegan:** Possible but ask about ingredients
- **Halal:** All meat is halal
- **Kosher:** Very limited options
- **Allergies:** Communicate clearly, carry translation card

## Final Tips

1. **Start Early:** Beat crowds and heat
2. **Stay Hydrated:** More water than you think you need
3. **Negotiate:** Expected in markets and for services (not museums)
4. **Be Patient:** Things move at Egyptian time
