# Episode 067: Travel Guide - Angkor Wat & Siem Reap, Cambodia
## Practical Information for Your Visit

---

## GETTING THERE

### Flights to Siem Reap
**Siem Reap-Angkor International Airport (SAI)**
- New airport opened October 2023, replacing the old Siem Reap International Airport
- Located ~50km from Siem Reap city center (45-60 minute drive)
- Served by regional carriers: Bangkok Airways, AirAsia, Singapore Airlines, Vietnam Airlines, Qatar Airways (via connections)

### From Airport to City
- **Private Transfer:** $25-35 USD, arranged through hotel or booking platforms
- **Taxi:** Available at airport, $20-30 USD
- **Hotel Shuttle:** Many hotels offer pickup (check when booking)
- **Tuk-tuk:** Not recommended from airport (too far, safety concerns)

### Overland Options
- **From Bangkok:** Bus $25-35 USD, 8-10 hours (border crossing at Poipet)
- **From Phnom Penh:** Bus $8-20 USD, 5-6 hours; Flight 45 minutes
- **From Ho Chi Minh City:** Bus $25-35 USD, 6-8 hours (border crossing at Moc Bai/Bavet)

## ANGKOR PASSES

### Current Prices (2025-2026)
| Pass Type | Price | Validity |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| 1-Day Pass | $37 USD | Single day |
| 3-Day Pass | $62 USD | Valid over 10 days |
| 7-Day Pass | $72 USD | Valid over 1 month |

### Where to Buy
- **Angkor Enterprise Ticket Office:** Street 60, Siem Reap (opens 5 AM)
- **Online:** Official Angkor Pass portal (recommended for sunrise visits)
- **Kiosks:** Selected locations in Siem Reap town
- **At Temple:** Not available—must purchase before arrival

### Requirements
- Passport photo (digital or physical)
- Passport for verification
- Purchase in person (biometric photo taken on-site) OR online in advance

### Tips
- **Buy the day before** if planning sunrise visit (saves 30-45 minutes in morning queues)
- **3-Day Pass offers best value** for most visitors
- Passes are **non-transferable** (photo verification at temple entrances)
- Keep pass with you at all times (random checks throughout complex)

## GETTING AROUND

### Transportation Changes (2026 Update)
⚠️ **Important:** Tourists are NO LONGER ALLOWED to drive rental scooters/motorbikes around Angkor Wat complex. This rule was implemented for safety and traffic management.

### Available Options

#### Tuk-tuk with Driver
- **Cost:** $15-30 USD per day (depending on itinerary and negotiation)
- **Pros:** Affordable, open-air experience, local drivers know the temples well
- **Cons:** Slower than car, less protection from weather
- **Best for:** Small groups (1-3 people), budget travelers

#### Private Car with Driver
- **Cost:** $35-60 USD per day
- **Pros:** Air conditioning, faster, more comfortable, weather protection
- **Cons:** More expensive, less atmospheric
- **Best for:** Families, hot season visits, luxury travelers

#### Bicycle
- **Cost:** $3-8 USD per day rental
- **Pros:** Eco-friendly, flexible pace, good exercise
- **Cons:** Very hot midday, requires fitness, limited range
- **Best for:** Experienced cyclists, dry season, short distances

#### Guided Tours
- **Cost:** $40-100+ USD per person per day (depending on group size and inclusions)
- **Pros:** Expert knowledge, logistics handled, social aspect
- **Cons:** Less flexibility, fixed schedule
- **Best for:** First-time visitors, history enthusiasts, solo travelers

#### Grab / Ride-Hailing
- **Availability:** Limited within temple complex
- **Best for:** Getting to/from Siem Reap city

## WHEN TO VISIT

### Best Months

#### Dry Season (November - March) ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Weather:** 22-30°C (72-86°F), low humidity, minimal rain
- **Pros:** Clear skies, best for sunrise/sunset photography, comfortable temperatures
- **Cons:** Peak tourist season, larger crowds, higher accommodation prices
- **Crowd Level:** High (especially December-January and Chinese New Year)

#### Shoulder Season (April-May, October-November)
- **Weather:** 28-35°C (82-95°F), occasional rain
- **Pros:** Fewer crowds, lower prices, still good visibility
- **Cons:** Hotter, some rain risk
- **Crowd Level:** Moderate

#### Wet Season (June - October)
- **Weather:** 25-32°C (77-90°F), high humidity, frequent rain
- **Pros:** Lush jungle, dramatic skies, fewest crowds, lowest prices
- **Cons:** Rain can obscure sunrise, muddy paths, some areas may flood
- **Crowd Level:** Low

### Best Times of Day

#### Sunrise (5:00-7:00 AM)
- **Arrive:** 4:30 AM at temple gates
- **Pros:** Cool temperatures, magical light, fewer crowds (initially)
- **Cons:** Very early start, crowds build quickly at popular spots
- **Tip:** Buy pass the day before to skip ticket office queue

#### Morning (7:00-10:00 AM)
- **Pros:** Still cool, good light for photography, tour groups arriving
- **Cons:** Getting busier
- **Tip:** Visit popular temples (Angkor Wat, Bayon) early

#### Midday (10:00 AM-3:00 PM)
- **Pros:** Fewer tourists at major temples, time for lunch break
- **Cons:** Very hot (35-40°C/95-104°F), harsh lighting
- **Tip:** Visit smaller temples, take lunch break, swim at hotel pool

#### Afternoon (3:00-5:00 PM)
- **Pros:** Light softens, temperatures drop, good for photography
- **Cons:** Still warm, crowds returning
- **Tip:** Great time for Ta Prohm, Banteay Srei

#### Sunset (5:00-6:30 PM)
- **Best Spots:** Phnom Bakheng (limited to 300 visitors), Pre Rup, Srah Srang
- **Pros:** Dramatic light, fewer people than sunrise
- **Cons:** Some temples close before sunset
- **Tip:** Arrive early for Phnom Bakheng (capacity fills fast)

## ACCOMMODATION

### Budget ($5-20/night)
- **Dorm Beds:** $5-12 USD in backpacker hostels
- **Basic Guesthouses:** $12-20 USD for private room with fan/AC
- **Areas:** Old Market (Psar Chaa), Pub Street vicinity
- **Recommendations:** Onederz Siem Reap, Lub d Cambodia, The Siem Reap Hostel

### Mid-Range ($30-80/night)
- **Boutique Hotels:** $30-60 USD for comfortable double with pool
- **Features:** AC, private bathroom, breakfast included, often pool
- **Areas:** Wat Bo, Sala Kamreuk, near Old Market
- **Recommendations:** Golden Temple Retreat, The Privilege Floor, Borei Angkor

### Luxury ($100-400+/night)
- **Resorts & 5-Star:** $150-400+ USD
- **Features:** Multiple pools, spas, fine dining, temple shuttle service
- **Areas:** Road to Angkor, River Road, outskirts
- **Recommendations:** Amanjena, Park Hyatt Siem Reap, Raffles Grand Hotel d'Angkor, Belmond La Résidence

### Booking Tips
- **Book in advance** for dry season (November-February)
- **Negotiate** for longer stays (weekly/monthly rates available)
- **Check location**—proximity to Old Market vs. temples
- **Ask about tuk-tuk arrangements** (many hotels have preferred drivers)

## FOOD & DINING

### Local Khmer Cuisine
- **Amok:** Steamed fish curry in banana leaf ($4-8)
- **Lok Lak:** Stir-fried beef with rice ($3-6)
- **Nom Banh Chok:** Khmer noodles with fish curry ($1-3, breakfast)
- **Samlor Machu:** Sour soup with vegetables and fish ($3-5)

### Budget Eats ($1.50-4 per meal)
- **Street Food Stalls:** Throughout city, especially near markets
- **Local Restaurants:** Family-run establishments, authentic Khmer food
- **Night Markets:** Psar Chaa (Old Market), Angkor Night Market
- **Recommendations:** Khmer Kitchen Restaurant, Sister Srey Cafe, The Soup Dragon

### Mid-Range ($6-15 per meal)
- **Cafes & Restaurants:** Mix of Khmer and international cuisine
- **Areas:** Pub Street, Old Market, Wat Bo
- **Recommendations:** Temple Club, Cuisine Wat Damnak, Haven (training restaurant)

### Fine Dining ($20-50+ per meal)
- **Upscale Restaurants:** Contemporary Khmer, French, international
- **Recommendations:** Cuisine Wat Damnak (Michelin-recognized), The Tasting Room, Malis Restaurant

### Coffee Culture
- **Local Coffee:** $1.50-2.50
- **Artisanal Cafes:** $2.50-4 for lattes/cappuccinos
- **Recommendations:** The Little Red Fox Espresso, Brown Coffee, Starbucks (yes, there's one near Angkor Wat!)

### Dietary Considerations
- **Vegetarian/Vegan:** Widely available, many dedicated restaurants
- **Allergies:** Communicate clearly; English widely understood in tourist areas
- **Water:** Drink bottled water only (available everywhere)

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Dress Code (Temples)
- **Required:** Shoulders and knees must be covered
- **Not Allowed:** Tank tops, sleeveless shirts, short shorts, mini skirts
- **Solution:** Bring a sarong or light scarf to cover up
- **Enforcement:** Strict at Angkor Wat; guards will deny entry

### Behavior at Temples
- **Voice:** Speak quietly; these are sacred sites
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing monks
- **Climbing:** Respect restricted areas and barriers
- **Shoes/Hats:** Remove where indicated (some interior sanctuaries)
- **Pointing:** Don't point feet at Buddha images or monks

### Tipping
- **Restaurants:** 10-15% appreciated (check if service charge included)
- **Tuk-tuk Drivers:** $2-5 USD per day, more for exceptional service
- **Tour Guides:** $10-20 USD per day depending on group size
- **Hotel Staff:** $1-2 USD for porters, housekeeping

### General Etiquette
- **Greetings:** "Chum reap suor" (hello) with slight bow and hands together
- **Head:** Don't touch anyone's head (considered sacred)
- **Feet:** Don't point feet at people or Buddha images
- **Public Displays:** Modest affection in public; Cambodia is conservative
- **Photography of People:** Always ask permission first

## PRACTICAL INFORMATION

### Currency
- **Dual System:** US Dollars and Cambodian Riel (KHR) both accepted
- **Exchange Rate:** ~4,000 KHR = $1 USD (as of 2025-2026)
- **Small Purchases:** Often in Riel (under $1)
- **ATMs:** Widely available in Siem Reap ($5-8 withdrawal fee typical)
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted at hotels, larger restaurants, shops
- **Cash:** Essential for markets, tuk-tuks, small vendors
- **Tip:** Bring crisp, new USD bills (worn or torn bills may be rejected)

### Connectivity
- **SIM Cards:** Available at airport and throughout Siem Reap ($5-15 for tourist SIM with data)
- **eSIM:** Compatible phones can use international eSIMs
- **WiFi:** Widely available at hotels, cafes, restaurants
- **Roaming:** Turn off data roaming; use local SIM or eSIM instead

### Health & Safety
- **Water:** Do NOT drink tap water; bottled water only
- **Food:** Street food generally safe; choose busy stalls with high turnover
- **Vaccinations:** Consult travel doctor (Hepatitis A/B, Typhoid, Tetanus recommended)
- **Malaria:** Low risk in Siem Reap; consult doctor about prophylaxis
- **Sun Protection:** Essential—hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
- **Insects:** Mosquito repellent recommended (dengue fever present in Cambodia)
- **Medical Care:** Siem Reap has clinics; serious issues require evacuation to Bangkok

### Safety
- **Crime:** Generally safe; petty theft (bag snatching, pickpocketing) most common
- **Scams:** Be wary of gem scams, fake police, overly friendly strangers
- **Solo Travel:** Safe for solo travelers, including women
- **Night Safety:** Stick to well-lit, populated areas; use hotel tuk-tuk after dark

### Packing List
**Essentials:**
- Angkor Pass (purchase on arrival or online)
- Passport + copies
- Modest clothing (shoulders/knees covered)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Sunscreen and hat
- Insect repellent
- Reusable water bottle
- Power bank for phone/camera
- Headlamp/flashlight (for sunrise visits)
- Light rain jacket (wet season)
- Cash (USD in small denominations)
- Basic first aid kit

**Optional:**
- Camera with extra batteries/memory cards
- Binoculars (for wildlife, distant carvings)
- Travel journal
- Sarong (for temple coverage, beach, etc.)
- Quick-dry clothing
- Small backpack for temple days

## ITINERARY SUGGESTIONS

### One Day (Express)
- **Sunrise:** Angkor Wat
- **Morning:** Angkor Wat interior, Angkor Thom, Bayon
- **Lunch:** Break in town
- **Afternoon:** Ta Prohm, Banteay Kdei
- **Sunset:** Phnom Bakheng or Pre Rup

### Three Days (Recommended)
**Day 1:** Angkor Wat sunrise, Angkor Wat exploration, Angkor Thom, Bayon
**Day 2:** Ta Prohm, Banteay Kdei, Srah Srang, Preah Khan, sunset at Pre Rup
**Day 3:** Banteay Srei (pink temple), Landmine Museum, sunset at Phnom Bakheng

### Seven Days (Comprehensive)
- Days 1-3: Main circuit (as above)
- **Day 4:** Outer temples (Banteay Srei, Kulen Mountains, Beng Mealea)
- **Day 5:** Return visits to favorite temples, deeper exploration
- **Day 6:** Tonle Sap Lake, floating villages, bird sanctuary
- **Day 7:** Siem Reap town, museums, massage, shopping, farewell sunset

## BEYOND THE TEMPLES

### Museums
- **Angkor National Museum:** $12 USD, excellent context for temple visits
- **Landmine Museum:** $5 USD, important history, run by Aki Ra (former child soldier)
- **War Museum Cambodia:** $5 USD, Khmer Rouge history (heavy but important)

### Cultural Experiences
- **Apsara Dance Performance:** $25-45 USD including dinner
- **Cambodian Cooking Class:** $25-40 USD, market visit + cooking
- **Pottery Workshop:** Learn traditional Khmer ceramics
- **Circus Phare:** $18-36 USD, contemporary Cambodian circus with social mission

### Tonle Sap Lake
- **Floating Villages:** Chong Kneas, Mechrey, Kampong Phluk ($15-30 for boat tour)
- **Bird Sanctuary:** Prek Toal (November-April, best for migratory birds)
- **Note:** Some floating village tours are touristy; research ethical options

### Relaxation
- **Traditional Khmer Massage:** $8-15 USD per hour
- **Fish Spa:** $5-10 USD, fish nibble dead skin
- **Yoga Classes:** Multiple studios in Siem Reap ($8-15 per class)

### Shopping
- **Old Market (Psar Chaa):** Souvenirs, textiles, spices, local goods
- **Angkor Night Market:** Handicrafts, art, souvenirs (bargain expected)
- **Artisans Angkor:** High-quality traditional crafts, fair trade
- **Made in Cambodia Market:** Ethical shopping, supports local artisans

## FINAL TIPS

1. **Start early** to beat crowds and heat
2. **Buy your pass the day before** if doing sunrise
3. **Hire a guide** for at least one day—history comes alive
4. **Stay hydrated**—carry water at all times
5. **Take breaks**—temple fatigue is real
6. **Respect the sites**—these are sacred, not just tourist attractions
7. **Support local**—eat at local restaurants, hire local guides
8. **Be flexible**—weather, crowds, and energy levels change plans
9. **Leave no trace**—take only photos, leave only footprints
10. **Return if you can**—one visit is never enough
