### Long-Form Post

**Headline:** What 500-Year-Old Walls Can Teach Us About Leadership and Legacy

**Body:**

Last week, I recorded Episode 083 of The Infinite Walk in Beijing's Forbidden City. And I learned something about leadership that 20 years in business never taught me.

The Forbidden City was built by a million workers over 14 years (1406-1420). It housed 24 emperors across two dynasties. It was the center of an empire that believed itself to be the center of the world.

Every detail was intentional:
- North-south axis aligned with Polaris (the North Star)
- Yellow roof tiles reserved for the emperor alone
- Nine dragons everywhere (nine = imperial number)
- Perfect symmetry representing cosmic order

This wasn't just architecture. It was a statement: *We are the axis of the world.*

But here's what struck me:

The last emperor, Puyi, was evicted in 1924. He wrote: *"I was a god, and I was a prisoner. I owned everything, and I owned nothing."*

Five hundred years of power. Gone in three hours.

**Three lessons for leaders:**

1️⃣ **Power is temporary.** Even emperors fall. Build for impact, not permanence.

2️⃣ **The workers matter most.** A million unnamed laborers built the Forbidden City. History remembers the emperor. But without the workers, nothing exists. Honor your team.

3️⃣ **Legacy is what you leave in others.** The walls remember. Not the edicts. Not the battles. The *humanity*. What will your walls remember?

The full 20-minute episode explores imperial history, poetic reflection, and what it means to walk through memory made visible.
