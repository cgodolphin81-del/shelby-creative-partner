**Headline:** What Business Can Learn from Bhutan's Gross National Happiness

**Body:**
```
In 1972, while the business world obsessed over quarterly earnings, a small Himalayan kingdom made a radical declaration:

"Gross National Happiness is more important than Gross Domestic Product."

Fifty years later, Bhutan remains:
✅ The only carbon-negative nation on Earth
✅ 72% forest coverage (constitutionally mandated 60% minimum)
✅ Free healthcare and education for all citizens
✅ A thriving democracy with a constitutional monarchy

How? By measuring what matters.

Bhutan tracks nine domains of wellbeing:
• Psychological health
• Education
• Time balance
• Cultural resilience
• Community vitality
• Environmental health
• Living standards
• Good governance
• Overall life satisfaction

Notice what's absent: pure economic growth.

Now, I'm not suggesting companies abandon profitability. But what if we asked:

• What kind of organization do we want to be?
• What kind of wellbeing do we want to cultivate?
• What do we owe the future?

These are the questions Bhutan has been asking for five decades.

And the world is finally listening. New Zealand's "wellbeing budget." The UN's Happiness Index. B-Corp certification.

The conversation is happening.
```
