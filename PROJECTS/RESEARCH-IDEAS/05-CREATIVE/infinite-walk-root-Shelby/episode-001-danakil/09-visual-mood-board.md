## 🌡️ MOOD & ATMOSPHERE

### Primary Moods

**OPPRESSIVE HEAT**
- **Visual cues:** Heat shimmer/haze, sweat on skin, harsh shadows, washed-out colors at midday
- **Lighting:** Overhead, brutal, no mercy
- **Composition:** Tight frames that feel claustrophobic despite vast landscapes
- **Emotion:** Exhaustion, surrender, endurance
- **Reference:** War photography, survival documentaries

**ALIEN WONDER**
- **Visual cues:** Unusual colors, strange formations, steam, bubbles, textures unlike anywhere else
- **Lighting:** Side-lighting to show texture, or dramatic dawn/dusk
- **Composition:** Wide shots that emphasize scale and strangeness
- **Emotion:** Awe, disbelief, curiosity
- **Reference:** Sci-fi concept art, Mars rover photos, macro photography

**PRIMORDIAL POWER**
- **Visual cues:** Lava glow, volcanic steam, cracked earth, vast emptiness
- **Lighting:** Night (for lava), or harsh midday (for landscape)
- **Composition:** Human figure small against vast geological features
- **Emotion:** Humility, insignificance, reverence
- **Reference:** Ansel Adams, sublime landscape photography, religious art

**HUMAN ENDURANCE**
- **Visual cues:** Afar guides (unfazed), travelers (struggling), salt miners (working), sweat, protective gear
- **Lighting:** Natural, documentary-style
- **Composition:** Portraits, environmental portraits, action shots
- **Emotion:** Respect, admiration, connection
- **Reference:** National Geographic documentary photography, photojournalism

**SACRED SILENCE**
- **Visual cues:** Night sky over lava lake, solitary figures, still pools, vast emptiness
- **Lighting:** Low light, single source (lava, stars, headlamp)
- **Composition:** Minimalist, lots of negative space
- **Emotion:** Contemplation, spirituality, peace within harshness
- **Reference:** Spiritual photography, meditation imagery, fine art

## 📐 COMPOSITION GUIDELINES

### Rule of Thirds (Classic)
- Place horizon on upper or lower third (not center)
- Place key elements (person, volcano, pool) at intersection points
- **Use for:** Most landscape shots, establishing shots

### Central Symmetry
- Center the subject (especially for alien pools, crater shots)
- Creates sense of intention, design, sacredness
- **Use for:** Sulfur pools (they look designed), crater shots, portraits

### Leading Lines
- Use salt cracks, lava flows, paths to lead eye through frame
- Creates journey, movement, depth
- **Use for:** Salt flats, approach shots, caravan photos

### Scale Contrast
- Include human figure in vast landscape
- Emphasizes insignificance, scale, danger
- **Use for:** Establishing shots, Erta Ale rim, salt flats

### Tight Detail
- Fill frame with texture, color, pattern
- Abstract, intimate, sensory
- **Use for:** Sulfur formations, salt crystals, lava textures, bubbles

### Silhouette
- Backlit subjects against bright sky or lava glow
- Dramatic, mysterious, universal (could be anyone)
- **Use for:** Erta Ale at night, sunrise/sunset, dramatic moments

### Layering
- Foreground (texture), midground (subject), background (context)
- Creates depth, complexity, invitation to explore
- **Use for:** Dallol landscapes, camp scenes, caravan shots

## 🌅 LIGHTING SCENARIOS

### Harsh Midday Sun
- **When:** 11 AM - 3 PM
- **Quality:** Brutal, overhead, high contrast, deep shadows
- **Color temp:** Cool white (~5500-6000K)
- **Mood:** Oppressive, honest, unforgiving
- **Use for:** Heat scenes, salt flats (blinding white), "reality" moments
- **Tips:** Embrace the harshness; don't try to soften it

### Golden Hour (Dawn)
- **When:** Sunrise, ~5:30-6:30 AM
- **Quality:** Soft, warm, directional, long shadows
- **Color temp:** Warm gold (~3000-3500K)
- **Mood:** Hopeful, peaceful, temporary respite
- **Use for:** Opening shots, camp scenes, "before the heat" moments
- **Tips:** This is the most beautiful light; use sparingly for contrast

### Golden Hour (Dusk)
- **When:** Sunset, ~6:00-7:00 PM
- **Quality:** Warm, soft, dramatic skies possible
- **Color temp:** Warm orange (~3000K)
- **Mood:** Relief, reflection, end of trial
- **Use for:** Closing shots, camp scenes, "we survived" moments
- **Tips:** Skies can be spectacular; include sky in frame

### Blue Hour
- **When:** Just before sunrise or after sunset, ~20-30 minutes
- **Quality:** Soft, cool, even, no harsh shadows
- **Color temp:** Cool blue (~7000-8000K)
- **Mood:** Transitional, mysterious, quiet
- **Use for:** Camp scenes, transition moments, contemplative shots
- **Tips:** Short window; be ready

### Night (Lava Glow)
- **When:** After dark, Erta Ale crater
- **Quality:** Single source (lava), dramatic chiaroscuro, warm glow
- **Color temp:** Very warm from lava (~2000K), cool from stars (~10000K)
- **Mood:** Sacred, primordial, awe-inspiring
- **Use for:** THE money shots, climax of visual story
- **Tips:** Long exposure for stars, expose for lava glow (let sky go dark)

### Night (Headlamp/Artificial)
- **When:** Night climbing, camp at night
- **Quality:** Harsh, directional, limited throw
- **Color temp:** Cool white (LED, ~6000K) or warm (headlamp, ~3000K)
- **Mood:** Intimate, focused, survival
- **Use for:** Climbing shots, camp life, "in the moment" scenes
- **Tips:** Use practically; don't over-light

## 📱 PLATFORM-SPECIFIC VISUAL SPECS

### Instagram Feed
- **Aspect ratio:** 4:5 (vertical) or 1:1 (square) perform best
- **Resolution:** 1080x1350px (4:5) or 1080x1080px (1:1)
- **Color:** Vibrant but not oversaturated; Instagram compresses
- **Text:** Minimal on image; put text in caption

### Instagram Stories
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 (full vertical)
- **Resolution:** 1080x1920px
- **Safe zones:** Keep text away from top/bottom edges (UI covers)
- **Duration:** 15 seconds max per story; break longer content

### Instagram Reels
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 (vertical)
- **Resolution:** 1080x1920px
- **Length:** 30-90 seconds optimal
- **Text:** On-screen text for sound-off viewers

### TikTok
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 (vertical)
- **Resolution:** 1080x1920px
- **Length:** 30-60 seconds optimal
- **Text:** Large, bold, on-screen throughout

### YouTube (if making video)
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 (horizontal)
- **Resolution:** 1920x1080px (1080p) or 3840x2160px (4K)

- **Aspect ratio:** 2:3 (vertical) performs best
- **Resolution:** 1000x1500px minimum
- **Text:** Overlay text works well on Pinterest

### Twitter/X
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 (horizontal) or 4:3
- **Resolution:** 1200x675px (16:9) or 1200x900px (4:3)

### LinkedIn
- **Aspect ratio:** 1:1 (square) or 16:9 (horizontal)
- **Resolution:** 1200x1200px or 1200x627px

## 🎬 VISUAL STORYTELLING PRINCIPLES

### Show, Don't Tell
- Instead of saying "it was hot," show sweat, heat shimmer, exhausted faces
- Instead of saying "colors were alien," show extreme close-ups of pools
- Instead of saying "it was dangerous," show thin salt crusts, sharp lava

### Contrast is King
- Juxtapose neon colors with black lava
- Juxtapose human smallness with geological vastness
- Juxtapose traveler exhaustion with guide ease
- Juxtapose harsh day with sacred night

### Texture Matters
- Salt crystals (sharp, geometric, white)
- Sulfur deposits (smooth, layered, yellow)
- Lava rock (rough, twisted, black)
- Sweat on skin (wet, human, vulnerable)

### Movement Within Stillness
- Steam rising (constant motion)
- Bubbles forming and bursting (cyclical)
- Lava churning (slow, primordial)
- Heat shimmer (invisible but visible)

### The Human Element
- Always include people (even if small) for scale
- Show faces (emotion, reaction, transformation)
- Show hands (holding salt, drinking water, pointing)
- Show feet (boots on salt, on lava, walking)

## 🔧 POST-PROCESSING GUIDELINES

### Color Grading

**For Daytime Heat Scenes:**
- Slightly desaturate blues (sky is too pretty otherwise)
- Push yellows and oranges (emphasize heat)
- Increase contrast (harsh light = harsh image)
- Slight vignette (focus attention)

**For Dallol Colors:**
- Don't oversaturate (colors are real; trust them)
- Increase clarity/texture (show mineral detail)
- White balance: Slightly cool (counteracts yellow cast)
- Protect highlights (salt blows out easily)

**For Erta Ale Night:**
- Expose for lava (let sky go dark)
- Warm temperature (emphasize glow)
- Increase clarity on lava (show texture)
- Reduce noise carefully (night = noisy)

**For Salt Flats:**
- Protect highlights (white salt clips easily)
- Slight cool tint (counteracts potential yellow cast)
- Increase dehaze (heat shimmer can soften)
- Boost texture (show salt crystal detail)

### What NOT to Do

❌ Don't oversaturate to "make it pop" — the Danakil is already extreme
❌ Don't add fake lens flare or effects — keep it authentic
❌ Don't remove all imperfections — dust, sweat, struggle are part of the story
❌ Don't make it look like every other travel photo — this place is different
❌ Don't over-sharpen — natural softness from heat haze is real

### What TO Do

✅ Do trust the real colors — they're stranger than fiction
✅ Do embrace harshness — this isn't a resort
✅ Do show struggle — it's honest and relatable
✅ Do include imperfections — they prove authenticity
✅ Do create consistency — all Episode 001 images should feel cohesive
