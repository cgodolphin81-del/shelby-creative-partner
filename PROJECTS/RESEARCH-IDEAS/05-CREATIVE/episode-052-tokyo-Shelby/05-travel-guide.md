## ✈️ GETTING THERE

### Flights
**Major Airports:**
- **Narita (NRT)** - 60km east of Tokyo, international hub
  - Narita Express (N'EX): 60 min to Tokyo Station, ¥3,070
  - Keisei Skyliner: 41 min to Ueno, ¥2,570
  - Airport Limousine Bus: 60-85 min to major hotels, ¥1,300-3,200

- **Haneda (HND)** - 30km south, closer to city, growing international
  - Tokyo Monorail: 18 min to Hamamatsucho, ¥500
  - Keikyu Line: 30 min to Shinagawa, ¥300
  - Airport Limousine Bus: 30-50 min to hotels, ¥610-1,400

**Flight Tips:**
- Book 3-6 months ahead for best prices
- Red-eye flights from US arrive next day (helps with jet lag)
- ANA and JAL consistently rated world-class
- Budget: Scoot, Zipair, Peach (less legroom, fewer amenities)

### Visa Requirements
- **US/UK/Canada/Australia/EU:** 90 days visa-free for tourism
- **Other countries:** Check Japanese embassy requirements
- **Working Holiday Visa:** Available for 18-30 year olds from partner countries

## 🚃 GETTING AROUND

### JR Pass - Is It Worth It?

**Japan Rail Pass Prices (2024):**
- 7 days: ¥50,000 (~$330 USD)
- 14 days: ¥80,000 (~$530 USD)
- 21 days: ¥100,000 (~$660 USD)

**When JR Pass MAKES SENSE:**
- ✅ Tokyo → Kyoto round trip (¥27,000 each way = ¥54,000)
- ✅ Tokyo → Hiroshima round trip
- ✅ Multiple long-distance trips in 7 days
- ✅ Tokyo → Kanazawa → Takayama → Tokyo loop

**When JR Pass DOESN'T make sense:**
- ❌ Staying only in Tokyo
- ❌ One-way Tokyo to Kyoto (buy individual ticket)
- ❌ Slow travel with few long trips
- ❌ Flying between cities (budget airlines often cheaper)

**How to Use:**
1. Purchase exchange order BEFORE arriving in Japan
2. Exchange at airport or major JR stations
3. Activate on chosen start date
4. Show pass at manned gates (not automatic gates)
5. Covers most JR trains, some buses, Tokyo Monorail

**Alternative:**
- Buy individual Shinkansen tickets
- Use IC card (Suica/Pasmo) for local transit
- Often cheaper for one-way or Tokyo-only trips

### IC Cards (Suica/Pasmo/Icoca)

**What they are:**
- Reloadable contactless transit cards
- Work on trains, subways, buses nationwide
- Also work at convenience stores, vending machines, some shops

**Getting One:**
- **Welcome Suica:** For tourists, no deposit, expires in 28 days
- **Regular Suica/Pasmo:** ¥500 deposit + load amount
- **Mobile Suica:** Add to iPhone Apple Wallet (easiest option)

**Where to Buy:**
- Airport train stations
- Major JR stations
- Ticket machines (English available)

**Reload:**
- Ticket machines
- Convenience stores
- Apple Pay (for mobile Suica)

### Tokyo Metro & Trains

**Key Lines:**
- **JR Yamanote Line:** Circular line hitting all major areas (Shibuya, Shinjuku, Tokyo, Ueno, Akihabara, Harajuku)
- **Tokyo Metro:** 9 lines covering city center
- **Toei Subway:** 4 additional lines

**Apps to Download:**
- **Google Maps:** Excellent transit directions
- **Japan Transit Planner (Jorudan):** Detailed train info
- **Tokyo Subway Navigation:** Official app with maps

**Etiquette:**
- No phone calls on trains
- Quiet conversations only
- Priority seats: turn off phones, yield to elderly/pregnant
- No eating (drinking OK)
- Let passengers exit before boarding
- Women-only cars during rush hour (marked)

**Last Trains:**
- Most lines end around midnight
- Friday/Saturday: some run until 1-2 AM
- After midnight: taxis are expensive
- Plan accordingly!

## 📅 BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### Spring (March-May) ⭐ BEST
**Pros:**
- Cherry blossoms (late March-early April)
- Mild weather (10-20°C / 50-68°F)
- Golden Week holidays (late April/early May) - vibrant but crowded

**Cons:**
- Peak tourist season
- Higher prices
- Crowded everywhere
- Book months ahead

**Pack:** Light layers, light jacket, comfortable walking shoes

### Summer (June-August)
**Pros:**
- Long days, late sunsets
- Summer festivals (matsuri)
- Fireworks displays
- Lower accommodation prices (except Obon)

**Cons:**
- HOT and HUMID (25-35°C / 77-95°F)
- Rainy season (June-early July)
- Crowded during Obon (mid-August)
- Typhoon season begins

**Pack:** Light breathable clothes, umbrella, portable fan, sunscreen

### Autumn (September-November) ⭐ BEST
**Pros:**
- Fall foliage (November)
- Pleasant weather (15-25°C / 59-77°F)
- Less crowded than spring
- Food season (shokuyoku no aki)

**Cons:**
- Typhoon season (September)
- Can be unpredictable weather

**Pack:** Layers, light jacket, comfortable shoes

### Winter (December-February)
**Pros:**
- Fewest tourists
- Lowest prices
- Clear views of Mt. Fuji
- Winter illuminations
- Hot springs feel amazing

**Cons:**
- Cold (0-10°C / 32-50°F)
- Short days
- Some outdoor attractions less enjoyable
- New Year closures (Dec 29-Jan 3)

**Pack:** Warm coat, layers, gloves, scarf

### Special Events to Consider:
- **Cherry Blossoms:** Late March-early April (plan 6+ months ahead)
- **Golden Week:** April 29-May 5 (avoid - most crowded time)
- **Obon:** Mid-August (Japanese travel home, very crowded)
- **New Year:** Dec 29-Jan 3 (many closures, but unique experience)
- **Summer Festivals:** July-August (Gion Matsuri, Tenjin Matsuri)

## 🎌 ETIQUETTE & CULTURE

### Essential Do's ✅

- **Bow slightly** when greeting or thanking
- **Say "itadakimasu"** before eating (gratitude)
- **Slurp noodles** - shows appreciation
- **Remove shoes** when required (look for slippers, raised floors)
- **Use both hands** when giving/receiving important items
- **Queue properly** - Japanese line up for everything
- **Be punctual** - on time is late, early is on time
- **Speak quietly** on public transport
- **Sort trash** - complex system, follow local bins
- **Carry cash** - many places don't take cards

### Essential Don'ts ❌

- **Don't tip** - considered rude, service included
- **Don't eat while walking** (generally frowned upon)
- **Don't blow your nose** in public (use restroom)
- **Don't point** with one finger (use open hand)
- **Don't stick chopsticks upright** in rice (funeral ritual)
- **Don't pass food** chopstick-to-chopstick (funeral ritual)
- **Don't wear shoes** inside homes, some restaurants, temples
- **Don't take photos** of people without permission
- **Don't talk on phone** on trains
- **Don't enter onsen** without washing first

### Onsen (Hot Spring) Rules

1. **Remove all clothing** - no swimsuits
2. **Wash thoroughly** before entering (seated, use shower)
3. **No towels in water** - leave on side or small towel on head
4. **No tattoos** - many onsens ban visible tattoos (cover or find tattoo-friendly)
5. **No swimming** - soak quietly
6. **Dry off** before returning to changing room
7. **Hydrate** - bring water

### Temple & Shrine Etiquette

1. **Bow at torii gate** before entering
2. **Purify hands** at chozuya (water pavilion)
  - Left hand, right hand, mouth (don't swallow), ladle handle
3. **At shrine:** Bow twice, clap twice, bow once (prayer)
4. **At temple:** No clapping, just bow and pray
5. **Don't photograph** where prohibited
6. **Speak quietly**
7. **Dress modestly**

## 🍜 FOOD GUIDE

### Must-Try Dishes

**Sushi**
- High-end: Sukiyabashi Jiro (nearly impossible), Sushi Saito
- Mid-range: Sushi Zanmai (Tsukiji), local neighborhood shops
- Budget: Conveyor belt (Kura Sushi, Sushiro, ¥100-500/plate)

**Ramen**
- Tonkotsu (pork bone): Ichiran, Ippudo
- Shoyu (soy): Afuri (also has yuzu shio)
- Miso: Hokkaido style, rich and hearty
- Tsukemen: Dipping noodles, try at Rokurinsha

**Traditional**
- Tempura: Tempura Kondo (vegetables), local shops
- Yakitori: Omoide Yokocho (Shinjuku), tiny skewer shops
- Tonkatsu: Maisen, Tonki
- Soba: Buckwheat noodles, hot or cold
- Unagi: Grilled eel, summer specialty
- Okonomiyaki: Savory pancake, Osaka-style or Hiroshima-style
- Takoyaki: Octopus balls, street food

**Breakfast**
- Traditional: Rice, miso soup, fish, pickles, natto
- Modern: Toast, eggs, coffee
- Convenience store: Onigiri, sandwiches, bento

**Desserts**
- Matcha everything (ice cream, cake, lattes)
- Taiyaki: Fish-shaped cake with filling
- Mochi: Rice cake, various fillings
- Melon pan: Sweet bread
- Japanese cheesecake: Fluffy, light

### Dietary Restrictions

**Vegetarian/Vegan:**
- Challenging but possible
- Look for "shojin ryori" (Buddhist vegetarian)
- Many restaurants use dashi (fish stock) even in "vegetable" dishes
- Apps: HappyCow, Vegewel
- Areas: Shimokitazawa, Koenji (more vegetarian-friendly)

**Halal:**
- Growing options in Tokyo
- Okinawa soba, some ramen shops
- Look for halal certification
- Apps: Halal Gourmet Japan

**Gluten-Free:**
- Rice-based dishes naturally GF
- Soy sauce contains wheat (ask for tamari)
- Tempura batter usually has wheat
- Communicate clearly, use translation cards

### Food Translation Cards

Carry these phrases (or show on phone):

- "I am vegetarian" = Watashi wa bejitarian desu
- "No meat, please" = Niku nuki de onegaishimasu
- "No fish, please" = Sakana nuki de onegaishimasu
- "Does this contain meat?" = Kore wa niku ga haitte imasu ka?
- "Allergy to ___" = ___ ni arerugi ga arimasu

## 🏨 WHERE TO STAY

### Best Neighborhoods

**Shibuya** - Young, energetic, nightlife
- Pros: Great transport, shopping, dining, nightlife
- Cons: Crowded, can be noisy, expensive
- Best for: First-timers, young travelers, nightlife lovers

**Shinjuku** - Major hub, everything available
- Pros: Excellent transport, endless options, Kabukicho entertainment
- Cons: Overwhelming, crowded, some sketchy areas
- Best for: Convenience, nightlife, all budgets

**Tokyo Station/Ginza** - Upscale, central
- Pros: Central location, luxury shopping, high-end dining
- Cons: Expensive, less nightlife, business-oriented
- Best for: Luxury travelers, business, shopping

**Asakusa** - Traditional, older Tokyo
- Pros: Near Senso-ji, traditional atmosphere, cheaper
- Cons: Further from some areas, quieter nightlife
- Best for: Culture lovers, budget travelers, families

**Harajuku** - Youth culture, fashion
- Pros: Trendy, near Meiji Shrine, unique shopping
- Cons: Crowded weekends, touristy, limited transport
- Best for: Fashion, youth culture, families

**Akihabara** - Tech, anime, gaming
- Pros: Otaku paradise, unique experience, good transport
- Cons: Niche appeal, can feel overwhelming
- Best for: Anime fans, tech enthusiasts

**Shimokitazawa** - Hipster, vintage, local
- Pros: Vintage shops, live music, local vibe, affordable
- Cons: Further from center, less touristy infrastructure
- Best for: Repeat visitors, music lovers, hipsters

### Accommodation Types

**Hostels:**
- ¥3,000-5,000/night
- Clean, safe, social
- Many have private rooms
- Recommendations: K's House, Nui, Book and Bed

**Business Hotels:**
- ¥8,000-15,000/night
- Small but efficient
- Chains: APA, Toyoko Inn, Dormy Inn (has onsen)
- Good value, reliable

**Ryokan (Traditional Inn):**
- ¥20,000-50,000+/night
- Tatami floors, futons, kaiseki meals
- Often include onsen
- Experience traditional Japan
- Best outside Tokyo (Hakone, Kyoto)

**Capsule Hotels:**
- ¥3,000-5,000/night
- Tiny pod, shared facilities
- Novelty experience
- Good for late nights, solo travelers

**Airbnb:**
- Varies widely
- More space, kitchen
- Check regulations (some areas restricted)
- Good for longer stays, groups

## 🎯 DAY TRIP IDEAS

### Mt. Fuji Area
**Kawaguchiko** (2 hours from Tokyo)
- Best Fuji views
- Lake activities, hot springs
- Chureito Pagoda (iconic photo spot)
- Transportation: Direct bus from Shinjuku (¥1,800)

**Hakone** (2.5 hours)
- Hot springs, open-air museum
- Lake Ashi pirate ships
- Ropeway with Fuji views
- Use Hakone Free Pass (¥5,000-6,000)

### Kamakura (1 hour)
- Great Buddha (Daibutsu)
- Hasedera Temple
- Beaches (summer)
- Hiking trails
- Transportation: JR Yokosuka Line

### Nikko (2 hours)
- Toshogu Shrine (UNESCO)
- Stunning autumn colors
- Waterfalls, nature
- Transportation: Tobu Railway or JR

### Yokohama (30 minutes)
- Chinatown (largest in Japan)
- Cup Noodles Museum
- Waterfront, Ferris wheel
- Transportation: JR or Metro

### Disney Resort (30 minutes)
- Tokyo Disneyland
- Tokyo DisneySea (unique to Japan)
- Full day needed
- Transportation: JR Keiyo Line

## 💡 PRO TIPS

1. **Carry cash** - Many places still cash-only
2. **Get Suica on iPhone** - Easiest transit option
3. **Download offline maps** - Google Maps works offline
4. **Learn basic phrases** - Goes a long way
5. **Arrive early** - Popular restaurants book out
6. **Trash bags** - Few public bins, carry your trash
7. **Convenience stores are lifesavers** - 7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart
8. **Free Wi-Fi** - Available at stations, convenience stores
9. **Vending machines everywhere** - Hot and cold drinks
10. **Shoes off** - Always check, look for slippers
11. **No tipping** - Can be considered rude
12. **Quiet on trains** - No phone calls, quiet conversations
13. **JR Pass math** - Calculate before buying
14. **Cherry blossom timing** - Check forecasts, blooms vary yearly
15. **Respect the culture** - Observe, learn, adapt
