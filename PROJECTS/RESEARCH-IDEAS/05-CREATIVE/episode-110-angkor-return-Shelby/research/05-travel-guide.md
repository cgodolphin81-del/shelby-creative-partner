## Getting There

### Flights to Siem Reap

**Siem Reap-Angkor International Airport (SAI)**
- New airport opened October 2023 (replaced old REP)
- 45km from city center (50-minute drive)
- Airlines: Cambodia Angkor Air, Bangkok Airways, AirAsia, Vietnam Airlines, Singapore Airlines, Thai Airways

**Via Phnom Penh (Alternative)**
- Fly into Phnom Penh International Airport (PNH)
- Domestic flight to Siem Reap: 45 minutes, ~$80-120
- Bus from Phnom Penh: 5-6 hours, $15-25
- Private transfer: 5 hours, $80-120

**Via Bangkok (Budget Option)**
- Fly to Bangkok (often cheaper international fares)
- Connect to Siem Reap: 1 hour, $60-100
- Or bus: 8-9 hours, $25-35 (border crossing required)

### From Airport to City

**Taxi/Grab**
- Cost: $25-35
- Time: 45-60 minutes
- Available: 24/7 at arrivals

**Hotel Shuttle**
- Many hotels offer free/paid pickup
- Arrange in advance via email
- Cost: $15-30 (if not complimentary)

**Tuk-Tuk**
- Not recommended from new airport (too far)
- Better for city travel

## Temple Passes & Tickets

### Angkor Archaeological Park Passes

**1-Day Pass: $37**
- Valid for single day
- Best for: Rushed itineraries, budget travelers
- Not recommended: You'll exhaust yourself

**3-Day Pass: $62** ⭐ **RECOMMENDED**
- Valid over 10 days (use any 3 days within 10-day window)
- Best for: Most visitors, balanced pace
- Value: Excellent flexibility

**7-Day Pass: $72**
- Valid over 30 days (use any 7 days within month)
- Best for: Photographers, slow travelers, return visits
- Value: Best for dedicated temple explorers

### Where to Buy

**Angkor Ticket Office**
- Location: Vithei Charles de Gaulle Road
- Hours: 5:00 AM - 5:30 PM daily
- Payment: Cash (USD) or card
- Photo taken on-site (free)

**Online Pre-Purchase**
- Website: angkorenterprise.com
- Available: 3-day and 7-day passes
- Benefit: Skip ticket office queue
- Note: Still need to collect physical pass

### Important Notes
- Passes are **non-transferable** (photo verification at checkpoints)
- Children under 12: **Free** (bring passport for age verification)
- Pass required for **all** temple sites in the park
- Keep pass with you at all times (random checks)

## When to Visit

### Best Months: November-February (Dry Season)

**Weather:**
- Temperature: 25-32°C (77-90°F)
- Humidity: Lower, comfortable
- Rainfall: Minimal
- Skies: Clear, excellent for photography

**Crowds:**
- Peak: December-January (holiday season)
- Moderate: November, February
- Strategy: Start early, visit popular sites at sunrise

**Pros:**
- Comfortable temperatures
- Best light for photography
- All areas accessible
- Festivals and events

**Cons:**
- Highest prices (flights, hotels)
- Most crowded
- Book accommodations 2-3 months ahead

### Shoulder Season: March-May (Hot Season)

**Weather:**
- Temperature: 35-40°C (95-104°F)
- Humidity: Rising
- Rainfall: Rare

**Crowds:**
- Thinner than peak season
- Midday site visits challenging

**Pros:**
- Lower accommodation rates
- Fewer tourists
- Good for early morning/late afternoon visits

**Cons:**
- Intense heat (dangerous midday)
- Heat exhaustion risk
- Some sites feel oppressive

### Wet Season: June-October

**Weather:**
- Temperature: 25-35°C (77-95°F)
- Rainfall: Daily afternoon showers (1-2 hours)
- Humidity: High

**Crowds:**
- Minimal (lowest season)
- Best for solitude seekers

**Pros:**
- Lowest prices
- Lush, green jungle
- Full reflection pools
- Dramatic storm clouds
- Authentic local experience

**Cons:**
- Daily rain (usually afternoon)
- Mud at some sites
- Some areas may flood
- Humidity uncomfortable for some
- Photography challenging

### **Recommendation:** **January-February** or **September-October**
- January-February: Best weather, manageable crowds
- September-October: Green season, few tourists, dramatic skies

## Temple Itineraries

### 1-Day Express (For Rushed Travelers)

**Start: 5:00 AM**

- 8:30-10:00: Angkor Thom (South Gate entry)

- 1:30-3:30: Ta Prohm

**Pace:** Exhausting but covers highlights
**Best for:** One-day pass holders, cruise ship passengers

**Day 1: Angkor Wat & Immediate Area**
- 5:00-8:00: Angkor Wat sunrise

- 1:30-4:00: Angkor Wat bas-reliefs, libraries, outer galleries

**Day 2: Angkor Thom Circuit**
- 7:00-8:30: Angkor Thom South Gate
- 8:30-10:30: Bayon Temple
- 10:30-11:30: Baphuon
- 12:00-1:00: Lunch
- 1:30-3:00: Terrace of Elephants
- 3:00-4:00: Terrace of the Leper King
- 4:30-6:00: Preah Khan (less crowded late)

**Day 3: Ta Prohm & Outer Temples**
- 7:00-9:30: Ta Prohm (before crowds)
- 10:00-11:30: Ta Keo
- 12:00-1:00: Lunch
- 1:30-3:30: East Mebon OR Neak Pean

**Pace:** Comfortable, comprehensive
**Best for:** First-time visitors, photography enthusiasts

---

### 5-7 Day Deep Dive

**Add to 3-day itinerary:**
- **Preah Khan:** Larger, less crowded than Ta Prohm
- **Neak Pean:** Island temple, serene
- **East Mebon:** Pyramid temple, great sunset
- **Banteay Srei:** "Citadel of Women," pink sandstone, intricate carvings (25km from main complex)
- **Kbal Spean:** "River of a Thousand Lingas," jungle trek, carvings in riverbed
- **Beng Mealea:** Collapsed jungle temple, adventurous (70km from Siem Reap)
- **Return visits:** Revisit favorite temples at different times of day

**Pace:** Leisurely, immersive
**Best for:** Photographers, temple enthusiasts, slow travelers

## Getting Around

### Tuk-Tuk (Most Popular)
- **Cost:** $15-25/day for temple circuit
- **Pros:** Open-air experience, affordable, local drivers
- **Cons:** No AC, dusty, slower
- **Tip:** Negotiate price night before, arrange sunrise pickup

### Private Car with Driver
- **Cost:** $35-50/day
- **Pros:** AC, faster, more comfortable, rain protection
- **Cons:** Less atmospheric, more expensive
- **Tip:** Many drivers double as informal guides

### Bicycle
- **Cost:** $5-10/day rental
- **Pros:** Freedom, exercise, eco-friendly
- **Cons:** Hot, tiring, 40km+ circuit is challenging
- **Best for:** Fit travelers, dry season, shorter routes

### Grab (Ride-Sharing App)
- **Availability:** Siem Reap city only (not temples)
- **Cost:** Similar to tuk-tuk
- **Pros:** Fixed price, cashless, convenient
- **Cons:** Not available at all temple locations

### Walking
- **Within temples:** Yes, essential
- **Between temples:** Not practical (distances too great)
- **In Siem Reap town:** Very walkable

**Amok:** Steamed fish curry in banana leaf (national dish)
**Lok Lak:** Stir-fried beef with pepper sauce, rice, egg

## Culture & Etiquette

### Dress Code (Temples)
- **Shoulders must be covered** (no tank tops)
- **Knees must be covered** (no short shorts)
- **Light, breathable fabrics recommended** (linen, cotton)
- **Remove hats** inside sanctuaries
- **Remove shoes** in some upper levels (signs posted)

### Temple Behavior
- **Speak quietly** - temples are active religious sites
- **Don't touch carvings** - oils damage ancient stone
- **Ask before photographing people** - especially monks
- **Don't point feet at Buddha images** - sit respectfully
- **Clockwise circumambulation** - traditional direction

### General Cambodian Etiquette
- **Greet with "Chum Reap Suo"** (hello) and slight bow
- **Use right hand** for giving/receiving (left hand considered unclean)
- **Don't touch heads** - considered sacred
- **Remove shoes** when entering homes
- **Public displays of affection** - keep minimal
- **Losing temper** - causes loss of face, stay calm

### Tipping
- **Restaurants:** 10% if service charge not included
- **Tuk-tuk drivers:** $5-10/day
- **Tour guides:** $10-20/day
- **Hotel staff:** $1-5 for porters, housekeeping
- **Not mandatory but appreciated** - tourism is vital economy

## Practical Tips

### Health & Safety
- **Water:** Drink bottled water only (widely available)
- **Ice:** Generally safe in tourist establishments (filtered)
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A, Typhoid recommended; consult travel clinic
- **Malaria:** Low risk in Siem Reap, consider prophylaxis for rural areas
- **Heat:** Stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, take midday breaks
- **Insects:** Bring repellent (dengue fever present)

### Money
- **Currency:** USD widely accepted, Cambodian Riel for small change
- **Exchange rate:** ~4,000 Riel = $1 USD
- **ATMs:** Widely available, $4-5 withdrawal fee
- **Credit cards:** Accepted at hotels, nicer restaurants
- **Cash:** Essential for markets, tuk-tuks, small vendors
- **Condition:** USD bills must be crisp (no tears, marks)

### Connectivity
- **SIM cards:** Available at airport, $5-10 for tourist SIM
- **Providers:** Cellcard, Smart, Metfone
- **Coverage:** Good in Siem Reap, spotty at remote temples
- **WiFi:** Most hotels, cafes, restaurants have free WiFi

### Packing List
- **Lightweight, breathable clothing** (cover shoulders/knees)
- **Comfortable walking shoes** (sandals with straps OK)
- **Sun protection:** Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
- **Rain jacket** (even in dry season)
- **Daypack** for water, camera, sunscreen
- **Flashlight** for early sunrise visits
- **Power bank** for phone/camera
- **Insect repellent**
- **Hand sanitizer**
- **Copies of passport/visa** (keep separate from originals)

## Responsible Tourism

### How to Visit Responsibly

**Support Local:**
- Hire local guides (not just foreign-led tours)
- Eat at locally-owned restaurants
- Buy from local artisans (not mass-produced imports)
- Stay at locally-owned accommodations when possible

**Respect the Sites:**
- Stay on marked paths
- Don't climb on fragile structures
- Don't touch or lean on carvings
- Follow all posted rules

**Cultural Sensitivity:**
- Ask before photographing people
- Dress modestly (especially at temples)
- Learn basic Khmer phrases
- Be patient and smile

**Environmental:**
- Bring reusable water bottle (many hotels have refill stations)
- Minimize plastic use
- Don't litter (take trash with you)
- Respect wildlife (don't feed monkeys)

**Give Back:**
- Consider visiting social enterprises (Haven, Friends the Restaurant)
- Support organizations helping landmine victims
- Donate to temple conservation efforts
- Volunteer responsibly (research organizations first)

## Final Recommendations

### Do
- ✅ Start early (beat crowds and heat)
- ✅ Hire a local guide for at least one day
- ✅ Visit during shoulder season for best balance
- ✅ Stay hydrated and take midday breaks
- ✅ Respect dress codes and temple etiquette
- ✅ Support local businesses
- ✅ Allow more time than you think you need

- ❌ Visit only Angkor Wat (the complex is vast)

- ❌ Touch or climb on fragile structures
- ❌ Drink tap water
- ❌ Forget to bring cash (small bills)
- ❌ Be aggressive with bargaining (be fair)
