## HOOKS (First 15 Seconds)

### Hook 1: The Happiness Hook
**"This is the only country in the world that measures success by happiness, not money. And it's changing everything."**

*Why it works:* Challenges conventional thinking immediately. Creates curiosity about "how" and "why."

---

### Hook 2: The Visual Hook
**"Nine hundred meters above the valley, clinging to a sheer cliff face, sits a monastery that can only be reached by foot. This is the Tiger's Nest. And it's just the beginning."**

*Why it works:* Paints immediate visual. Creates sense of journey and discovery.

---

### Hook 3: The Contrarian Hook
**"What if I told you the world's most successful country has no skyscrapers, no traffic lights, and refuses to climb its own mountains? Welcome to Bhutan."**

*Why it works:* Subverts expectations. Makes viewer question assumptions about "success."

---

### Hook 4: The Environmental Hook
**"This nation absorbs more carbon than it produces. Seventy-two percent of its land is forest—by constitutional mandate. They're not just talking about climate action. They're living it."**

*Why it works:* Appeals to environmentally conscious audience. Shows tangible action vs. talk.

---

### Hook 5: The Mystery Hook
**"There's a place where the mountains touch the sky so intimately that the clouds rest upon their shoulders. A place the rest of the world almost forgot. This is the last Shangri-La."**

*Why it works:* Poetic, mysterious. Evokes the mythical. Creates longing.

---

### Hook 6: The Personal Hook
**"I came looking for a travel destination. I found something else entirely: a reminder of what I'd forgotten. This is Bhutan. And it's waiting to be remembered."**

*Why it works:* Personal journey framing. Relatable. Promises transformation.

---

### Hook 7: The Challenge Hook
**"Seven hundred forty steps. Carved into a cliff. No shortcuts. No cable cars. Just you, your breath, and the climb. This is what it takes to reach the Tiger's Nest."**

*Why it works:* Physical challenge creates tension. Viewer wants to see the payoff.

---

### Hook 8: The Wisdom Hook
**"The Bhutanese say some things are not meant to be conquered. Some things are meant to be revered. In a world obsessed with conquest, that's a radical idea."**

*Why it works:* Philosophical depth. Challenges dominant paradigm. Invites reflection.
