# Episode 086: Udaipur - Location Research

## The City of Lakes: Udaipur, Rajasthan, India

### Overview
Udaipur, founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, is known as the "Venice of the East" and the "City of Lakes." Located in the southern part of Rajasthan, it sits amidst the Aravalli Hills and is built around a series of artificial lakes. The city served as the capital of the Mewar Kingdom and is renowned for its royal grandeur, intricate architecture, and romantic lakeside settings.

---

## 1. City Palace (Udaipur City Palace)

**Built:** 1559 (construction continued for ~400 years)
**Builder:** Maharana Udai Singh II, founder of Udaipur
**Architecture:** Blend of Rajasthani, Mughal, European, and Chinese styles
**Construction Material:** Granite and marble

### History
- The Mewar dynasty originally ruled from Chittor (Chittorgarh)
- In 1537, Maharana Udai Singh II was advised by a hermit to establish his capital at a safer, naturally fortified site
- Construction began in 1559 on the eastern bank of Lake Pichola
- The palace complex was expanded over approximately 400 years by 22 generations of the Sisodia Rajput family
- 42 different rulers contributed to the palace's evolution
- A portion of the palace is still owned and used by the royal family of Udaipur

### Architecture Highlights
- **Facade:** 100 ft tall and 801 ft long
- **Elevation:** Built at 1,962 ft on a hill overlooking Lake Pichola
- **Complex:** 11 smaller palaces within the main complex, surrounded by fortified walls
- **Design:** Narrow zigzag corridors strategically designed for defense against enemies (particularly Mughal soldiers)
- **Interior:** Intricate mirror work, marble work, murals, wall paintings, silver work, inlay work, and colored glass

### Key Structures Within the Complex

1. **Ganesh Deodi** - Marble idol of Lord Ganesh with glass inlay work
2. **Rai Angan** - The oldest part of the palace, where the hermit advised Maharana Udai Singh II
3. **Badi Mahal (Garden Palace)** - Features a garden and Mughal-style corridor, offering Lake Pichola views; sits on an 89 ft high rock formation
4. **Amar Vilas Courtyard** - Highest point in Badi Mahal, ideal for photography; elevated garden with hanging gardens, fountains, towers, and terraces
5. **Badi Chitrashali Chowk** - Recreational space with blue Chinese tiles, colored glass, and murals
6. **Mor Chowk (Peacock Courtyard)** - Features exquisite glass mosaic peacock designs
7. **Kanch Ki Burj (Mirror Tower)** - Ornate chamber with glass inlay work, once the Maharanas' winter bedroom
8. **Moti Mahal (Pearl Palace)** - Maharana Karan Singh's former residence
9. **Manak Mahal (Ruby Palace)** - Chamber adorned with rubies and glass inlay work, used for formal audiences
10. **Surya Chopar (Sun Square)** - Features a large gold sun-face emblem
11. **Zenana Mahal (Women's Chamber)** - Where royal ladies spent their days
12. **Sheesh Mahal (Palace of Mirrors)** - Intricate mirror work throughout
13. **Krishna Vilas** - Houses miniature paintings of Lord Krishna and Radha
14. **Toran Pol (Crown Gate)** - Gate with cultural importance for wedding rituals

### Visitor Information
- **Timings:** 9:00 AM to 9:00 PM (daily)
- **Entry Fees (2025):**
  - Adults (18+): ₹400 (~$5 USD)
  - Children (5-18): ₹250 (~$3 USD)
  - Senior Citizens/Defence personnel: ₹250
  - Children below 5: Free
- **Camera:** No additional fees (previously ₹200 for still, ₹500 for video)
- **Time Needed:** 2-3 hours to explore properly
- **Best Time to Visit:** October to March; mornings or evenings to avoid heat
- **Accessibility:** Wheelchair accessible, elevators, ramps available
- **Audio Guides:** Available in English, French, German, Spanish

### Getting There
- **From Airport:** Maharana Pratap Airport (UDR) is 20-25 km from city center (~30 min drive)
- **From Railway Station:** Udaipur Railway Station is 3-4 km from the palace
- **Local Transport:** Auto-rickshaw, taxi, Uber available
- **Entry Gates:** Tripolia Gate (north, main entrance) or Ganesh Pol/Fateh Gate (south)

## 2. Lake Pichola

**Created:** 1387 by Laksh Singh (artificial freshwater lake)
**Type:** Artificial freshwater lake
**Significance:** Central feature of Udaipur's identity as "City of Lakes"

### Overview
- Lake Pichola is the crown jewel of Udaipur's lake system
- The City Palace is built on its eastern bank
- Contains two islands: Jag Mandir and Lake Palace (Taj Lake Palace)
- Offers stunning views of the City Palace, especially at sunset when golden sunlight reflects off the white marble
- Popular for boat rides, especially during sunset

### Activities
- **Sunset Boat Ride:** Most popular activity; tickets ~₹500 per person
- **Boat Ride Options:**
  - 30-minute ride: ₹250 adults, ₹125 children
  - 1-hour ride: ₹350 adults, ₹175 children
  - Ride typically ends at Jag Mandir Palace
- **Best Time:** Sunset (golden hour photography)
- **Views:** City Palace, Jag Mandir, Lake Palace, surrounding hills

## 3. Jag Mandir Palace (Island Palace)

**Built:** 1551-1652 (construction over ~100 years)
**Builders:** Maharana Amar Singh (started 1551), Maharana Karan Singh (1620-1628), Maharana Jagat Singh I (completed 1628-1652)
**Location:** Island in Lake Pichola
**Architecture:** Rajput with Mughal influences
**Material:** Yellow sandstone and marble
**Also Known As:** Lake Garden Palace

### History
- Construction began in 1551 by Maharana Amar Singh
- **Royal Refuge:** Prince Khurram (later Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan) sought refuge here in 1623-1624 during his rebellion against his father, Emperor Jahangir
  - Maharana Karan Singh provided sanctuary because Khurram's mother was of Rajput descent
  - Prince Khurram stayed in the Gul Mahal section
  - It's believed Khurram drew inspiration from Jag Mandir's pietra dura work for the Taj Mahal
- **1857 Revolt:** Maharana Swaroop Singh provided refuge to European families during the Sepoy Mutiny
- **Summer Resort:** Used by royal family as a summer retreat and for hosting parties
- **Film Location:** Featured in the 1983 James Bond film "Octopussy"

### Architecture & Key Structures

1. **Entry Pavilion**
   - White colonnade with cusped arches
   - Decorated with carved elephant statues facing Lake Palace
   - Serves as landing jetty for boats from Bansi Ghat

2. **Gul Mahal** (1551)
   - First structure built, originally a small sandstone palace with a dome
   - Built in Islamic architectural style to accommodate Prince Khurram's tastes
   - Features intricate marble work, paintings, and Muslim crescent motifs
   - Where Prince Khurram resided during his refuge

3. **Jag Mandir (Main Palace)**
   - Incorporates the Gul Mahal
   - Octagonal towers with cupolas
   - Reception halls, residential suites, internal courts
   - Blend of Rajput and Mughal styles

4. **Garden Courtyard**
   - Large courtyard with black and white tiles
   - Fountains, water pools, walkways
   - Plants: jasmine, moss rose, verbena, palm trees, frangipani, yew bushes, nasturtiums
   - Can be rented for events and weddings

5. **Darikhana**
   - Open-sided terrace with marble columns
   - Currently operates as a restaurant
   - Offers panoramic lake and hill views

6. **Bara Patharon ka Mahal** (Palace of the Twelve Stones)
   - Located in eastern wing
   - Constructed using twelve solid marble slabs
   - Twelve carved pillars representing zodiac signs

7. **Zenana Mahal** - Women's quarters
8. **Kunwar Pada ka Mahal** - Palace for the crown prince

### Visitor Information
- **Timings:** 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM (daily)
- **Entry Fee:** ~₹700 for Indians (includes jetty ride); higher for foreigners
- **Access:** Boat from Bansi Ghat jetty (City Palace) or Rameshwar Ghat
- **Best Time to Visit:** Around sunset for spectacular views over Lake Pichola
- **Time Needed:** 1-2 hours
- **Dining:** Restaurant available at Darikhana (premium pricing, book in advance)
- **Events:** Palace can be rented for weddings and lavish parties

## 4. Jagdish Temple

**Built:** 1651 AD (took ~3 years to complete)
**Builder:** Maharana Jagat Singh I
**Dedicated To:** Lord Vishnu (as Lord Jagannath)
**Architecture:** Indo-Aryan (Māha Māru / Māru-Gurjara style)
**Location:** Near City Palace, in the old city area

### History
- Originally named "Temple of Jagannath Rai"
- Completed in 1651 at an estimated cost of ₹1.5 million (1651 value)
- Has been in continuous worship since 1651
- **1680 Defense:** Charan warrior Naruji Barhat and 20 warriors martyred themselves defending the temple from Aurangzeb's Mughal army
- Largest temple in Udaipur
- Annual Rath Yatra (chariot festival) held here

### Architecture
- **Spire (Shikhara):** 79 feet tall - the tallest structure in Udaipur
- **Platform:** Built on a tall raised terrace
- **Steps:** 32 marble steps lead to the main shrine, with a brass image of Garuda at the end
- **Mandapa:** Double-storey main hall with 32 intricately carved pillars
- **Sanctum:** Double-storey saandhara sanctum
- **Main Idol:** Lord Vishnu carved from a single black stone (four-armed)
- **Outer Walls:** Detailed carvings depicting scenes from Hindu mythology, celestial dancers, elephants, musicians
- **Entrance:** Carved stone elephants guard the entrance
- **Ceilings:** Ornately carved with intricate designs

### Sub-Shrines
Four smaller shrines surround the main sanctum:
1. Lord Ganesha
2. Surya (Sun God)
3. Shakti (Goddess)
4. Lord Shiva

### Visitor Information
- **Timings:** Early morning to evening (aarti times vary)
- **Entry:** Free (donations accepted)
- **Dress Code:** Modest clothing required; remove shoes before entering
- **Best Time to Visit:** Early morning or evening aarti
- **Time Needed:** 30-45 minutes
- **Photography:** May be restricted inside the sanctum
- **Significance:** Major spiritual landmark and architectural marvel

## 5. Monsoon Palace (Sajjangarh Fort)

**Built:** 1884
**Builder:** Maharana Sajjan Singh of the Mewar dynasty
**Location:** Bansdara Hill (Bansdara Peak), Aravalli Hill Range
**Elevation:** Hilltop, approximately 10-12 km from Udaipur city center
**Architecture:** Rajput with Mughal and European influences
**Material:** White marble
**Also Known As:** Sajjangarh Palace, Sajjangarh Fort

### History
- **Original Purpose:** Intended as a nine-story astronomical center to track monsoon cloud movements
- **After Maharana Sajjan Singh's Death:** Plan abandoned; converted to monsoon retreat and royal hunting lodge
- **Royal Use:** Used as a summer palace and vantage point for rulers to view their kingdom
- **Film Location:** Featured in the 1983 James Bond film "Octopussy"
- **Current Status:** Part of Sajjangarh Wildlife Sanctuary

### Architecture
- **Style:** Blend of Rajput, Mughal, and European architectural elements
- **Features:** 
  - High turrets
  - Grand central court
  - Carved marble pillars
  - Large viewing balconies
  - High terraces
  - Open halls and marble arches
  - Small royal rooms and corridors
- **Design Focus:** Built for external views rather than as a grand residence; interior is relatively limited

### Views
The palace offers panoramic 360-degree views of:
- Lake Pichola
- City Palace
- Fateh Sagar Lake
- Udaipur city
- Aravalli hills
- Surrounding countryside
- Multiple lakes of Udaipur

### Sunset Experience
- **Primary Attraction:** Breathtaking sunset views
- **Best Viewing Time:** Arrive 5:30 PM - 6:30 PM
- **Western Terrace:** Provides dramatic backdrop as sun sets behind mountains
- **Experience:** Slow, calming sunset with fading light over the city; more about pausing and slowing down than dramatic spectacle
- **Wind:** Due to elevation, palace is windier and cooler than the city below

### Visitor Information
- **Timings:** Open until sunset (typically closes at sunset)
- **Best Time to Visit:**
  - **Winter (October-February):** Clear skies, comfortable weather, ideal for sunset
  - **Monsoon (July-September):** Magical misty atmosphere, clouds, greenery
  - **Summer (March-June):** Hot during day, but evenings are breezy
- **Getting There:**
  - 10-12 km from Udaipur city center
  - Auto-rickshaw, taxi, or two-wheeler
  - Drive passes through Sajjangarh Wildlife Sanctuary
  - Book round-trip taxi with waiting time
- **What to Bring:** Water, jacket (windy at top), camera, cash for tickets
- **Photography:** Best at sunset, after rain, during golden hour, and blue hour
  - Frame lakes through arches
  - Capture silhouettes against sky
  - Wide-angle exterior shots
- **Nearby Attractions:**
  - Sajjangarh Biological Park
  - Fateh Sagar Lake
  - Lake Pichola
  - Shilpgram
  - Badi Lake

## Additional Notable Locations

### Lake Palace (Taj Lake Palace)
- Floating palace hotel in the middle of Lake Pichola
- Built 1746-1751 by Maharana Jagat Singh II
- Now a luxury hotel operated by Taj Hotels
- Appears to float on the lake, especially magical at night

### Saheliyon Ki Bari (Garden of the Maidens)
- Ornamental garden built for royal ladies
- Features fountains, kiosks, marble art, and lotus pools

### Bagore Ki Haveli
- 18th-century haveli (mansion) on Lake Pichola
- Showcases Mewar architectural designs and royal history
- Hosts cultural performances

## Best Time to Visit Udaipur

### Peak Season (October - March)
- **Weather:** Pleasant, 10°C - 25°C (50°F - 77°F)
- **Pros:** Ideal for sightseeing, boat rides, outdoor activities
- **Cons:** Crowded, higher prices
- **Special Events:** Diwali, Mewar Festival, Holi (March)

### Shoulder Season (July - September)
- **Weather:** Monsoon season, humid, 20°C - 30°C (68°F - 86°F)
- **Pros:** Lush greenery, dramatic clouds, fewer tourists, lower prices
- **Cons:** Rain can disrupt plans, humidity
- **Special:** Monsoon Palace visits are especially atmospheric

### Off Season (April - June)
- **Weather:** Hot, 25°C - 40°C (77°F - 104°F)
- **Pros:** Least crowded, lowest prices
- **Cons:** Very hot, especially midday
- **Tip:** Visit early morning or evening; stay hydrated

## Cultural Notes

### Mewar Dynasty
- One of the oldest ruling dynasties in the world
- Known for valor, resistance against Mughal invasions
- Maharana Pratap is a legendary figure who never surrendered to Akbar
- Current royal family still owns portions of the palace and is involved in heritage preservation

### Rajasthani Culture
- **Music & Dance:** Traditional folk music, Ghoomar dance
- **Art:** Miniature paintings, intricate mirror work, blue pottery
- **Cuisine:** Dal Baati Churma, Gatte ki Sabzi, Laal Maas, Malpua
- **Textiles:** Bandhani (tie-dye), embroidered fabrics, leather goods
- **Crafts:** Miniature paintings, metalwork, stone carving

### Shopping
- **Markets:** Hathi Pol, Bada Bazaar, Jagdish Temple Road
- **Items:** Miniature paintings, embroidered fabrics, leather goods, jewelry, blue pottery, handicrafts
- **Tip:** Haggling and bargaining are common and expected

## Practical Travel Tips

### Getting to Udaipur
- **By Air:** Maharana Pratap Airport (UDR), 20-25 km from city; connected to major Indian cities
- **By Train:** Udaipur City Railway Station; well-connected to Delhi, Mumbai, Jaipur
- **By Road:** Well-connected by bus and car from Delhi (~12 hours), Jaipur (~6 hours), Mumbai (~14 hours)

### Local Transport
- **Auto-rickshaw:** Affordable, negotiate fare beforehand
- **Taxi/Uber:** Convenient, available from airport and railway station
- **Cycle Rickshaw:** Good for short distances in old city
- **Walking:** Old city areas are walkable

### Accommodation
- **Luxury:** Taj Lake Palace, Oberoi Udaivilas, The Leela Palace (₹15,000-50,000+ per night)
- **Mid-range:** Heritage havelis, boutique hotels (₹3,000-8,000 per night)
- **Budget:** Guesthouses, hostels (₹500-2,000 per night)

### Currency & Costs (2025-2026 estimates)
- **Currency:** Indian Rupee (₹)
- **Exchange Rate:** ~₹83 = $1 USD (varies)
- **Daily Budget (Budget Traveler):** ₹1,500-2,500 ($18-30 USD)
- **Daily Budget (Mid-range):** ₹4,000-8,000 ($48-96 USD)
- **Daily Budget (Luxury):** ₹15,000+ ($180+ USD)

### Safety & Etiquette
- Generally safe for tourists
- Dress modestly, especially at temples
- Remove shoes before entering temples
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Stay hydrated, especially in summer
- Use sunscreen and sunglasses
- Carry cash for small vendors and entry fees
