## GETTING THERE

### From Tokyo
**Shinkansen (Bullet Train)**
- **Duration:** 2 hours 15 minutes (Nozomi), 2 hours 30 minutes (Hikari)
- **Cost:** ¥14,000 one-way (unreserved), ¥14,500 (reserved)
- **Frequency:** Every 10-15 minutes
- **JR Pass:** Covered (except Nozomi on standard pass)
- **Stations:** Tokyo → Kyoto Station

**Overnight Bus** (Budget option)
- **Duration:** 7-8 hours
- **Cost:** ¥3,500-8,000 depending on class
- **Companies:** Willer Express, JR Bus, Kintetsu
- **Departure:** Various Tokyo locations
- **Arrival:** Kyoto Station or nearby

**Flight** (Not recommended)
- Tokyo (Haneda/Narita) → Osaka (Kansai/Itami)
- **Duration:** 1 hour flight + 2+ hours airport/transport time
- **Cost:** ¥10,000-20,000
- **Then:** Train to Kyoto (75 minutes from Kansai)
- **Verdict:** Shinkansen is faster door-to-door

---

### From Osaka
**JR Special Rapid**
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Cost:** ¥800
- **Frequency:** Every 15 minutes
- **Stations:** Osaka Station → Kyoto Station

**Hankyu Railway**
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Cost:** ¥400
- **Frequency:** Every 10 minutes
- **Stations:** Umeda → Kawaramachi (downtown Kyoto)
- **Note:** Cheaper, drops you closer to Gion

**Keihan Railway**
- **Duration:** 50 minutes
- **Cost:** ¥420
- **Stations:** Yodoyabashi → Sanjo/Gion-Shijo
- **Note:** Best for Fushimi Inari, Gion access

---

### From Kansai International Airport (KIX)
**Haruka Express**
- **Duration:** 75 minutes
- **Cost:** ¥2,000 (with discount ticket for tourists), ¥3,000 regular
- **Frequency:** Every 30 minutes
- **Stations:** KIX → Kyoto Station
- **Recommendation:** Best balance of speed and cost

**Airport Limousine Bus**
- **Duration:** 90-120 minutes (traffic dependent)
- **Cost:** ¥2,600
- **Frequency:** Every 30-60 minutes
- **Stops:** Major hotels and Kyoto Station
- **Note:** Good if staying near bus stop, door-to-door

**Taxi**
- **Duration:** 60-90 minutes
- **Cost:** ¥20,000-30,000
- **Note:** Only for groups or late arrivals

## WHEN TO VISIT

### Spring (March-May) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Pros:**
- Cherry blossoms (late March-early April)
- Mild temperatures (10-20°C / 50-68°F)
- Festivals: Aoi Matsuri (May 15)
- Long daylight hours

**Cons:**
- Peak tourist season
- Accommodation prices 2-3x higher
- Crowds at popular sites
- Need to book months in advance

**Best For:** First-time visitors, photographers, cherry blossom enthusiasts

**Tip:** Visit major sites at sunrise (6 AM) to avoid crowds

---

### Summer (June-August) ⭐⭐⭐
**Pros:**
- Fewer tourists (except Obon week)
- Lower accommodation prices
- Lush green bamboo and gardens
- Evening illuminations
- Gion Matsuri (July) - major festival

**Cons:**
- Hot and humid (25-35°C / 77-95°F)
- Rainy season (June-early July)
- Crowded during Obon (mid-August)
- Less comfortable for walking

**Best For:** Budget travelers, festival-goers, those who don't mind heat

**Tip:** Start early (6 AM), rest midday, resume evening

---

### Autumn (September-November) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Pros:**
- Fall foliage (mid-November peak)
- Pleasant temperatures (15-25°C / 59-77°F)
- Clear skies
- Seasonal food (matsutake, chestnuts)
- Jidai Matsuri (October 22)

**Cons:**
- Second peak season (especially November)
- Crowds at temples with famous maple trees
- Higher prices
- Book well in advance

**Best For:** Photographers, foodies, comfortable weather seekers

**Tip:** Arashiyama and Kiyomizu-ji are extremely crowded in peak foliage

---

### Winter (December-February) ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Pros:**
- Fewest tourists
- Lowest accommodation prices
- Chance of snow (rare but magical)
- Clear air, good visibility
- Special winter illuminations
- Hot springs feel amazing

**Cons:**
- Cold (0-10°C / 32-50°F)
- Some gardens less vibrant
- Shorter daylight hours
- Occasional rain/snow

**Best For:** Budget travelers, solitude seekers, winter photography

**Tip:** New Year (Jan 1-3) is very busy with Japanese visitors

## ACCOMMODATION

### Ryokan (Traditional Japanese Inn)

**What to Expect:**
- Tatami mat rooms with futon bedding
- Communal or private onsen (hot spring baths)
- Kaiseki dinners and traditional breakfasts included
- Yukata (casual kimono) provided
- Japanese-style service (omotenashi)
- Remove shoes at entrance

**Price Range:**
- **Budget:** ¥8,000-15,000/night (basic ryokan, shared bath)
- **Mid-range:** ¥20,000-40,000/night (private bath, meals included)
- **Luxury:** ¥50,000-100,000+/night (top-tier, all inclusive)

**Recommended Ryokan:**

**Luxury:**
1. **Tawaraya** - 300+ years old, legendary service, ¥80,000+
2. **Hiiragiya** - Historic, hosted many famous guests, ¥70,000+
3. **Yoshikawa Inn** - Michelin-starred kaiseki, ¥50,000+

**Mid-Range:**
1. **Ryokan Yachiyo** - Beautiful garden, good value, ¥25,000
2. **Gion Hatanaka** - In Gion district, geisha shows, ¥30,000
3. **Seikoro** - Historic, central location, ¥20,000

**Budget:**
1. **Len Kyoto** - Modern ryokan style, shared bath, ¥12,000
2. **Kyoto Machiya Stay** - Traditional townhouse, self-catering, ¥15,000

**Booking Tips:**
- Book 3-6 months in advance for peak seasons
- Many ryokan require dinner inclusion
- Check-in typically 3-4 PM, check-out 10-11 AM
- Some have curfews (midnight)
- English booking available on Rakuten Travel, Booking.com

## ETIQUETTE & CULTURE

### Temple & Shrine Etiquette

**Before Entering:**
1. **Purify hands and mouth** at chozuya (water pavilion)
   - Take ladle with right hand
   - Pour water over left hand
   - Switch ladle to left hand, pour over right hand
   - Pour water into left hand, rinse mouth (discreetly)
   - Tilt ladle vertically to let water clean handle
   - Return ladle

2. **Remove hats** as sign of respect

3. **Speak quietly** - these are active places of worship

**Inside Temple/Shrine:**
- **No photography** where prohibited (look for signs)
- **Remove shoes** where indicated (slippers may be provided)
- **Do not touch** sacred objects, statues, or offerings
- **Walk around** the outside of main halls (not through center)
- **Bow slightly** when passing through torii gates

**At the Altar:**
1. Toss coin into offering box (¥5 is lucky)
2. Bow twice deeply
3. Clap twice (shrines only, not temples)
4. Bow once more

**Photography:**
- Ask before photographing people (especially geiko/maiko)
- No tripods in crowded areas
- No flash inside buildings
- Respect "no photography" signs

---

### General Japanese Etiquette

**Greetings:**
- Bow slightly when greeting (15-30 degrees)
- Handshakes becoming more common with foreigners
- Say "arigatou gozaimasu" (thank you)

**Shoes:**
- Remove when entering homes, ryokan, some restaurants
- Look for shoe cubbies or slippers at entrance
- Separate toilet slippers often provided

**Public Behavior:**
- **Speak quietly** on public transport (no phone calls)
- **No eating while walking** (exceptions: festivals)
- **Queue properly** - Japanese are very orderly
- **No tipping** - can be considered rude
- **Trash:** Carry it with you, bins are rare

**Dining:**
- Say "itadakimasu" before eating
- Say "gochisosama deshita" after eating
- Do not stick chopsticks vertically in rice (funeral ritual)
- Do not pass food chopstick-to-chopstick (funeral ritual)
- Slurping noodles is acceptable (shows enjoyment)

**Onsen (Hot Springs):**
- Wash thoroughly before entering bath
- No swimsuits (naked is required)
- Keep small towel out of bath water
- No tattoos in many onsens (cover or use private)
- Quiet, contemplative atmosphere

**Gift-Giving:**
- Bring small gift when visiting someone's home
- Present and receive with both hands
- Modest refusal is polite before accepting

---

### What NOT to Do

❌ **Do not** tip (service included)
❌ **Do not** blow your nose in public (use restroom)
❌ **Do not** point with one finger (use open hand)
❌ **Do not** be loud on trains
❌ **Do not** eat while walking (generally)
❌ **Do not** wear shoes inside where removed
❌ **Do not** photograph geiko without permission
❌ **Do not** touch sacred objects
❌ **Do not** be impatient (queues, service)
❌ **Do not** refuse food/drink outright (try a bit)

## ESSENTIAL PHRASES

**Greetings:**
- Hello: Konnichiwa (koh-nee-chee-wah)
- Thank you: Arigatou gozaimasu (ah-ree-gah-toh goh-za-ee-mas)
- Excuse me: Sumimasen (soo-mee-mah-sen)
- Sorry: Gomen nasai (goh-men nah-sai)
- Yes: Hai (hai)
- No: Iie (ee-eh)

**Dining:**
- Before eating: Itadakimasu (ee-tah-dah-kee-mas)
- After eating: Gochisosama deshita (goh-chee-soh-sah-mah desh-tah)
- Delicious: Oishii (oh-ee-shee)
- Check please: Okaikei onegaishimasu (oh-kai-kay oh-neh-guy-shee-mas)

**Directions:**
- Where is...?: ...wa doko desu ka? (...wah doh-koh dess kah?)
- Station: Eki (eh-kee)
- Temple: Otera (oh-teh-rah)
- Shrine: Jinja (jeen-jah)
- Toilet: Toire (toh-ee-reh)

**Numbers:**
- 1: Ichi (ee-chee)
- 2: Ni (nee)
- 3: San (sahn)
- 4: Yon (yohn)
- 5: Go (goh)
- 1000: Sen (sen)
- 10,000: Man (mahn)

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money
- **Currency:** Japanese Yen (¥)
- **Cash is king:** Many places don't accept cards
- **ATMs:** 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards
- **Credit cards:** Accepted at hotels, department stores, chain restaurants
- **Exchange:** Better to withdraw in Japan than exchange at home

### Connectivity
- **SIM cards:** Available at airport (SoftBank, Docomo)
- **Pocket WiFi:** Rent at airport, unlimited data
- **Free WiFi:** Available at stations, convenience stores, some cafes
- **Google Maps:** Works excellently in Japan

### Language
- **English:** Limited outside tourist areas
- **Signs:** Often in English at stations, major sites
- **Translation apps:** Google Translate works well (download Japanese offline)
- **Gesture:** Japanese are helpful, will try to understand

### Safety
- **Very safe:** One of safest countries in world
- **Pickpockets:** Rare but possible in crowds
- **Emergency:** 110 (police), 119 (ambulance/fire)
- **Lost items:** Often returned (police have lost & found)

### Convenience Stores
- **7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart:** Everywhere
- **Open 24/7:** Food, drinks, ATM, copy machine
- **Quality:** Surprisingly good food
- **ATMs:** Accept foreign cards (7-Eleven best bet)

### Weather Prep
- **Spring:** Light jacket, layers
- **Summer:** Light clothing, umbrella, handkerchief (no AC everywhere)
- **Autumn:** Light jacket, layers
- **Winter:** Warm coat, gloves, scarf

## RECOMMENDED ITINERARY

### 3 Days (Essentials)

**Day 1: Eastern Kyoto**
- Morning: Fushimi Inari (arrive 6-7 AM)
- Late morning: Kiyomizu-dera
- Lunch: Soba noodles in Higashiyama
- Afternoon: Walk through historic streets to Yasaka Shrine
- Evening: Gion district, dinner

**Day 2: Northern & Western Kyoto**
- Morning: Arashiyama Bamboo Grove (arrive 7 AM)
- Late morning: Tenryu-ji Temple
- Lunch: Arashiyama area
- Afternoon: Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)
- Evening: Return to central Kyoto

**Day 3: Central Kyoto & Culture**
- Morning: Nijo Castle
- Lunch: Nishiki Market (food stalls)
- Afternoon: Ryoan-ji rock garden
- Late afternoon: Tea ceremony experience
- Evening: Departure or free exploration

### 5 Days (Recommended)
- Add: Philosopher's Path, Ginkaku-ji, Sanjusangen-do
- Add: Day trip to Nara (deer, giant Buddha)
- Add: More time in Gion, night photography
- Add: Sake brewery tour in Fushimi

### 7 Days (Immersive)
- Add: Day trip to Uji (tea culture)
- Add: Kurama-ji temple hike (north mountains)
- Add: Cooking class
- Add: More temple stays, deeper exploration
- Add: Time to just wander without agenda
