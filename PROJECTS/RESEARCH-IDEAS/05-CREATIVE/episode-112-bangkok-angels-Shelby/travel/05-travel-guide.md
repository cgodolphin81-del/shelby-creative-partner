## GETTING THERE

### By Air

#### Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK)
**Main international airport**
- **Distance:** 30 km east of Bangkok
- **Getting to city:**
  - **Airport Rail Link:** 45 THB, 30 min to Phaya Thai (connects to BTS)
  - **Taxi:** 350-500 THB + tolls, 45-60 min (use meter!)
  - **Grab:** 400-600 THB, app-based, reliable
  - **Airport bus:** 60 THB, multiple routes, slower
  - **Private transfer:** 800-1,500 THB, pre-booked

#### Don Mueang Airport (DMK)
**Budget airlines (AirAsia, Nok Air, etc.)**
- **Distance:** 25 km north of Bangkok
- **Getting to city:**
  - **Train:** 20 THB, 50 min to Hua Lamphong
  - **Taxi:** 250-400 THB + tolls, 30-45 min
  - **Bus:** 30 THB, multiple routes
  - **Grab:** 300-500 THB

### Visa Requirements

#### Visa Exemption (Most Countries)
- **30 days:** US, UK, EU, Australia, Canada, Japan, Singapore, and 60+ countries
- **Requirements:** Passport valid 6+ months, onward ticket, proof of funds
- **Extension:** Possible at immigration office (1,900 THB)

#### Visa on Arrival (Some Countries)
- **15 days:** India, China, Taiwan, and 20+ countries
- **Cost:** 2,000 THB
- **Requirements:** Passport, photo, onward ticket, 10,000 THB per person funds

#### Tourist Visa (Apply in Advance)
- **60 days:** Apply at Thai embassy/consulate
- **Cost:** Varies by country (~$40-80 USD)
- **Extension:** Possible for 30 days at immigration office

#### Long-Term Options
- **Education visa:** Study Thai, muay Thai, cooking
- **Retirement visa:** 50+ years old, financial requirements
- **Work visa:** Employer sponsorship required
- **Elite visa:** 5-20 years, expensive (600,000+ THB)

## GETTING AROUND

### BTS Skytrain ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Coverage:** Main tourist and business areas
- **Hours:** 6:00 AM - midnight
- **Cost:** 17-62 THB depending on distance
- **Payment:** Rabbit card (reloadable) or single tickets
- **Pros:** Fast, air-conditioned, avoids traffic
- **Cons:** Limited coverage, crowded at rush hour

### MRT Subway
- **Coverage:** Complements BTS, goes to different areas
- **Hours:** 6:00 AM - midnight
- **Cost:** 17-42 THB
- **Payment:** Single tickets or stored value cards
- **Pros:** Clean, efficient, air-conditioned
- **Cons:** Limited coverage

### Chao Phraya Express Boat ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Coverage:** River route, major temples
- **Hours:** 6:00 AM - 7:00 PM (varies by route)
- **Cost:** 15-30 THB (orange flag), 150 THB (tourist boat day pass)
- **Pros:** Scenic, cheap, avoids traffic
- **Cons:** Limited to river route, can be crowded

### Taxi
- **Availability:** Everywhere
- **Cost:** 35 THB flag fall + 7-8 THB/km
- **Tips:** 
  - **INSIST on meter** ("meter krub/ka")
  - Avoid drivers who refuse meter
  - Expect traffic delays
  - Tipping not expected but appreciated

### Grab (Southeast Asian Uber)
- **Availability:** App-based, reliable
- **Cost:** Similar to taxi + booking fee
- **Pros:** Fixed price, no language barrier, track your ride
- **Cons:** Surge pricing, pickup points sometimes awkward

### Tuk-tuk
- **Availability:** Tourist areas
- **Cost:** Negotiate first (usually 100-300 THB for short trips)
- **Tips:**
  - **Always negotiate price BEFORE getting in**
  - Expect to pay more than taxi
  - Fun for short trips, not practical for long distances
  - Beware of "temple closed" scams

### Motorbike Taxi
- **Availability:** Street corners, apps (GrabBike)
- **Cost:** 40-100 THB for short trips
- **Pros:** Fast in traffic, cheap
- **Cons:** Dangerous, no helmet often provided

### Public Bus
- **Coverage:** Extensive but confusing
- **Cost:** 8-25 THB
- **Pros:** Very cheap, goes everywhere
- **Cons:** No AC on some buses, confusing routes, slow in traffic

## FOOD & DINING

### Street Food ⭐ MUST-DO
- **Cost:** 40-100 THB per dish
- **Best areas:** Chinatown, Victory Monument, Silom Soi 38
- **Safety:** High turnover = fresh food, watch it cooked
- **Must-try:** Pad thai, som tam, grilled seafood, mango sticky rice

### Local Restaurants
- **Cost:** 100-300 THB per person
- **Types:** Thai, Chinese-Thai, regional specialties
- **Tipping:** Not expected, round up or leave 10%

### International Cuisine
- **Cost:** 300-1,000+ THB per person
- **Availability:** Everywhere (Japanese, Italian, Indian, etc.)
- **Quality:** Generally good, especially in expat areas

### Fine Dining
- **Cost:** 2,000-5,000+ THB per person
- **Michelin:** Bangkok has Michelin Guide (including street food!)
- **Reservations:** Recommended for top restaurants

### Dietary Restrictions
- **Vegetarian:** Say "gin jay" (Buddhist vegetarian), look for yellow flags
- **Vegan:** Increasing options, specify "mai sai nam pla" (no fish sauce)
- **Halal:** Available, especially in Muslim areas
- **Gluten-free:** Limited awareness, rice-based dishes are safe

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### The Thai Smile
Thailand is the "Land of Smiles" for a reason. Thais value:
- **Sanuk:** Fun, enjoyment in life
- **Mai pen rai:** No worries, it's okay
- **Saving face:** Avoid confrontation, embarrassment
- **Respect:** For elders, monks, royalty

### The Wai (Traditional Greeting)
- **How:** Press palms together at chest, slight bow
- **When:** Greeting, thanking, apologizing, saying goodbye
- **Hierarchy:** Lower hands = more respect (to monks, elders)
- **Tourists:** Not expected, but appreciated if done respectfully

### Dress Code
- **Temples:** Shoulders and knees covered (strictly enforced)
- **General:** Light, breathable clothing (it's hot!)
- **Upscale:** Smart casual for fine dining, rooftop bars
- **Beach:** Swimwear only at beach/pool

### Respect for Monarchy
- **Law:** Lèse-majesté laws are strict (criticism is illegal)
- **Currency:** King's image on all bills and coins (treat respectfully)
- **Anthem:** Played before movies, at events (stand respectfully)
- **Portraits:** King's portrait in government buildings, banks

### Temple Etiquette
- **Shoes:** Remove before entering temple buildings
- **Dress:** Modest (shoulders, knees covered)
- **Feet:** Never point feet at Buddha images or monks
- **Head:** Don't touch anyone's head (sacred)
- **Monks:** Women cannot touch monks or hand things directly
- **Photography:** Generally OK, but be respectful, no flash inside
- **Behavior:** Quiet, respectful, no PDA

### General Do's and Don'ts

#### DO:
- ✅ Smile and be friendly
- ✅ Learn basic Thai phrases (khob khun = thank you)
- ✅ Remove shoes when entering homes and some shops
- ✅ Dress modestly at temples
- ✅ Show respect to monks and Buddha images
- ✅ Bargain at markets (politely)
- ✅ Use your right hand for giving/receiving (left hand is "unclean")

#### DON'T:
- ❌ Touch anyone's head (sacred)
- ❌ Point feet at Buddha images, monks, or people
- ❌ Raise your voice or show anger in public
- ❌ Criticize the monarchy (illegal)
- ❌ Photograph monks without permission
- ❌ Public displays of affection (especially in temples)
- ❌ Stand when national anthem plays

## TEMPLES TO VISIT

### Must-See Temples

#### Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew
- **Admission:** 500 THB
- **Hours:** 8:30 AM - 3:30 PM
- **Time needed:** 2-3 hours
- **Dress code:** Strict (rentals available)
- **Highlight:** Emerald Buddha (most sacred in Thailand)

#### Wat Pho (Reclining Buddha)
- **Admission:** 200 THB
- **Hours:** 8:00 AM - 6:30 PM
- **Time needed:** 1-2 hours
- **Highlight:** 46-meter Reclining Buddha, Thai massage school

#### Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn)
- **Admission:** 100 THB
- **Hours:** 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM
- **Time needed:** 1 hour
- **Best time:** Sunset (cross river from Wat Pho)

#### Wat Trimit (Golden Buddha)
- **Admission:** 100 THB (includes museum)
- **Hours:** 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM
- **Time needed:** 1 hour
- **Highlight:** World's largest solid gold Buddha

### Other Notable Temples
- **Wat Saket (Golden Mount):** 50 THB, panoramic views
- **Wat Benchamabophit (Marble Temple):** 50 THB, beautiful architecture
- **Wat Mahathat:** Monk chats, meditation classes
- **Loha Prasat (Metal Castle):** Unique architecture, Old City

## SHOPPING

### Markets

#### Chatuchak Weekend Market
- **When:** Saturday-Sunday 9 AM - 6 PM
- **What:** 15,000+ stalls, everything imaginable
- **Budget:** 500-2,000+ THB
- **Tips:** Go early, bring cash, wear comfortable shoes

#### Floating Markets
- **Damnoen Saduak:** Most famous, touristy, 2 hours from Bangkok
- **Amphawa:** More authentic, weekends only
- **Khlong Lat Mayom:** In Bangkok, weekends
- **Budget:** 500-1,500 THB (including transport)

#### Night Markets
- **Ratchada Train Market:** Thu-Sun, trendy, Instagram-worthy
- **Jodd Fairs:** Multiple locations, trendy, food + shopping
- **Asiatique:** Riverside, mall + market, daily 4 PM - midnight

### Shopping Malls
- **Siam Paragon:** Luxury brands, aquarium, food court
- **Central World:** Huge, central location
- **MBK Center:** Bargains, souvenirs, electronics
- **ICONSIAM:** Ultra-luxury, riverside, indoor floating market

### Bargaining Tips
- **Markets:** Expected (start at 50% of asking price)
- **Malls:** Fixed prices (no bargaining)
- **Be polite:** Smile, be friendly, walk away if needed
- **Cash:** Better deals for cash payment

## HEALTH & SAFETY

### Health Precautions
- **Vaccinations:** Routine vaccines up to date, Hep A/B recommended
- **Mosquito-borne:** Dengue present (use repellent, especially rainy season)
- **Malaria:** Not in Bangkok (only in rural border areas)
- **Water:** Do NOT drink tap water (bottled water only)
- **Food:** Street food generally safe (high turnover = fresh)
- **Air quality:** PM2.5 can be bad Jan-March (bring mask if sensitive)

### Safety Tips
- **Crime:** Generally safe, violent crime rare
- **Scams:** Tuk-tuk "temple closed," gem scams, friendly strangers
- **Pickpockets:** Watch bags in crowds, on BTS, at markets
- **Traffic:** Dangerous (use crosswalks, be alert)
- **Emergency:** Tourist Police 1155 (English-speaking)

### Travel Insurance
- **Recommended:** Always get travel insurance
- **Coverage:** Medical, evacuation, trip cancellation
- **Providers:** World Nomads, SafetyWing, Allianz
- **Cost:** ~$50-100 USD for 2-week trip

## BEST TIME TO VISIT

### Cool Season (November-February) ⭐ BEST
- **Weather:** 25-32°C (77-90°F), low humidity, little rain
- **Crowds:** Peak tourist season
- **Prices:** Higher (book ahead)
- **Events:** Loy Krathong (November), Christmas/New Year

### Hot Season (March-May)
- **Weather:** 30-40°C (86-104°F), very hot, humid
- **Crowds:** Fewer tourists
- **Prices:** Lower
- **Events:** Songkran (Thai New Year, April 13-15) — water fights!

### Rainy Season (June-October)
- **Weather:** 25-35°C (77-95°F), afternoon storms, lush green
- **Crowds:** Low season
- **Prices:** Lowest
- **Events:** Vegetarian Festival (October)
- **Note:** Flooding possible (usually short-lived)

## FINAL TIPS

1. **Bring cash** — Street food and markets are cash-only
2. **Dress for temples** — Pack light scarf/long pants
3. **Download Grab** — Easier than negotiating with taxis
4. **Drink bottled water** — Never tap water
5. **Learn basic Thai** — "Khob khun" goes a long way
6. **Embrace the chaos** — Bangkok is overwhelming. That's the point.
7. **Eat street food** — It's safe, delicious, and cheap
8. **Respect the culture** — Temples, monks, monarchy
9. **Stay hydrated** — It's hot. Very hot.
10. **Leave time to get lost** — Some of the best moments are unplanned
