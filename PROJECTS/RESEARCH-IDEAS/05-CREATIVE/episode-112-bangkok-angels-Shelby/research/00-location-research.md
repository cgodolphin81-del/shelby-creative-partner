## KEY THEMES FOR THE EPISODE

### 1. Chaos and Serenity
Bangkok is loud, chaotic, overwhelming—and deeply peaceful. The monk meditates as the tuk-tuk honks. Both are real. Both are Bangkok.

### 2. Buddhist Impermanence (Anicca)
Everything changes. The temple stands for centuries; the street stall exists for a day. Both are expressions of impermanence.

### 3. The River as Lifeblood
The Chao Phraya is not just geography. It is history, economy, spirituality, identity. Bangkok floats.

### 4. Street Life as Theater
Every corner is a stage. The vendor cooks. The tourist photographs. The monk walks. All are performers. All are audience.

### 5. Golden Temples Amid Concrete
Sacred and secular do not compete in Bangkok. They coexist. The mall has a shrine. The skyscraper has a spirit house.

### 6. The Democracy of the Street
CEO and taxi driver sit on the same plastic stool. Street food is the great equalizer.

### 7. Old Siam Wearing Sneakers
Tradition does not die. It adapts. The monk has an iPhone. The spirit house has LED lights.

## CULTURAL SENSITIVITIES

### Do
- Dress modestly at temples
- Remove shoes when required
- Show respect to monks and Buddha images
- Ask before photographing people
- Learn basic Thai phrases (khob khun = thank you)
- Bargain respectfully at markets

### Don't
- Touch anyone's head (sacred)
- Point feet at Buddha or monks
- Photograph monks without permission
- Raise your voice in temples
- Criticize the monarchy (illegal)
- Assume all street food is safe (use judgment)

## PRACTICAL INFORMATION

### Best Time to Visit
- **Cool season:** November-February (best weather, most crowds)
- **Hot season:** March-May (very hot, fewer crowds)
- **Rainy season:** June-October (afternoon storms, lush green)

### Getting Around
- **BTS Skytrain:** Fast, air-conditioned, covers main areas
- **MRT Subway:** Complements BTS, goes to different areas
- **Chao Phraya Express Boat:** Scenic, cheap, avoids traffic
- **Taxi:** Metered, cheap, but traffic
- **Tuk-tuk:** Touristy, negotiate price first
- **Grab:** Southeast Asian Uber, reliable

### Budget (per day, excluding accommodation)
- **Budget:** 500-800 THB ($15-25) — street food, public transport
- **Mid-range:** 1,500-3,000 THB ($45-90) — mix of street food and restaurants
- **Luxury:** 5,000+ THB ($150+) — fine dining, private transport
