## Episode Overview

### The Story
Episode 087 takes listeners on an audio walk through Bagan, Myanmar—home to the largest and densest concentration of Buddhist temples, pagodas, stupas, and ruins in the world. Between the 9th and 13th centuries, over 10,000 monuments were built across a 41-square-kilometer plain. Today, approximately 2,200-3,500 temples remain, standing as testament to a civilization's devotion made visible in brick and stone.

At sunrise, these temples catch fire with golden light. And you understand: this was never just architecture. This was faith erected brick by brick, prayer by prayer.

### Key Themes
- **Devotion:** 10,000 temples as acts of merit-making
- **Legacy:** Building for futures you will never see
- **Continuity:** Living faith across 900+ years
- **Permanence:** What endures when empires fall
- **Purpose:** What does it mean to build for eternity?

### Featured Locations
1. **Ananda Temple (1105)** - Most beautiful, four standing Buddhas
2. **Shwesandaw Pagoda (1057)** - Best sunrise/sunset views
3. **Thatbyinnyu Temple (1144)** - Tallest in Bagan (61m)
4. **Dhammayangyi Temple (1170)** - Largest, legendary brickwork
5. **Shwezigon Pagoda (1059-1102)** - Most sacred, golden stupa
6. **Sulamani Temple (1183)** - "Crowning Jewel," ornate
7. **Htilominlo Temple (1218)** - Detailed carvings
8. **Mingalazedi Pagoda (1277)** - Last major temple
9. **Irrawaddy River** - Lifeblood of civilization
10. **Hot Air Balloons** - Aerial perspective (October-March)
