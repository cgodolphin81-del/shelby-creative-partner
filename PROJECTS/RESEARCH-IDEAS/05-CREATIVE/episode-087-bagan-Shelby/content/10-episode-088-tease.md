## Tease Copy (Long)

**"The kings of Pagan built their temples brick by brick, prayer by prayer, until the landscape itself became a cathedral.

But what if a city wasn't built at all?

What if it was carved—directly from the living rock of a canyon wall?

What if it was lost to the Western world for centuries, known only to local Bedouin tribes?

What if its greatest monument wasn't a temple, but a tomb? A treasury? A mystery?

From the temple plains of Bagan, we journey to Petra, Jordan.

The Rose-Red City. The lost city of the Nabataeans. One of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Walk through the Siq—a narrow gorge that opens suddenly to reveal the Treasury, carved from rose-red sandstone.

Explore the Royal Tombs, the Monastery, the ancient streets where traders once walked.

Understand what it means to carve your legacy not in brick, but in stone.

Episode 088: The Rose-Red City.

Coming soon to the Infinite Walk."**

### Thematic Connection

**Bagan → Petra:**
- Both: Ancient wonders
- Both: Devotion made visible (temples vs. tombs)
- Both: Rediscovered by modern world
- Both: UNESCO World Heritage Sites
- Both: "Must-see" bucket list destinations

**Contrast:**
- Bagan: Built (brick by brick)
- Petra: Carved (from living rock)
- Bagan: Buddhist faith
- Petra: Nabataean trade/burial
- Bagan: Open plain
- Petra: Hidden canyon

### Narrative Arc

**Episode 087:** "Build something that matters."
**Episode 088:** "Carve your legacy in stone."

**Continuing Theme:** What do we leave behind? How do we build (or carve) something that outlasts us?
