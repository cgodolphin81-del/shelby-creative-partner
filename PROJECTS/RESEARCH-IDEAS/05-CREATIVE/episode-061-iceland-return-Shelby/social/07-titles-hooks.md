We return to Iceland. But this time, we're walking the Westman Islands—where Surtsey was born from eruption (1963), where Eldfell buried a town overnight (1973), where 1.1 million puffins return every spring. 20 minutes on fire, return, and what the earth teaches.

### Apple Podcasts / Spotify (Long)
**Episode 061: The Fire Island - Westman Islands, Iceland**

Say it with me: *I am coming back to the fire.*

Episode 061 of The Infinite Walk returns to Iceland—but not the Iceland of glaciers and waterfalls. We're going to the Westman Islands (Vestmannaeyjar). The fire islands.

In this episode:
🌋 Surtsey: The island born from eruption in 1963, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site where zero humans are allowed to land
🏠 Eldfell: The volcano that woke at 1:55 AM in 1973, buried 400 homes, and was fought back with seawater pumps (it worked)
🐧 The Puffins: 1.1 million Atlantic puffins who return every spring to the same burrows after 8 months at sea—how do they know?
♨️ The Warm Ground: Standing on Eldfell today, 50 years later, feeling the heat through your boots

This is a story about return. About the spiral that brings us back to where we began—but higher, wiser, changed. About what the earth teaches when it splits open and shows you its molten heart.
