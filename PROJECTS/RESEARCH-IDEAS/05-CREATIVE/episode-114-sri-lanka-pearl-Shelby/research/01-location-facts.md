## Basic Facts

- **Official Name:** Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka
- **Former Names:** Ceylon (until 1972), Taprobane (ancient Greek), Serendib (Arab traders)
- **Location:** Indian Ocean, 31 km (18 mi) off India's southern coast
- **Shape:** Teardrop-shaped island
- **Area:** 65,610 km² (25,330 sq mi) - slightly larger than West Virginia
- **Population:** ~22 million
- **Capital:** Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte (legislative), Colombo (executive)
- **Largest City:** Colombo
- **Languages:** Sinhala (official), Tamil (official), English (link language)
- **Religions:** Buddhism 70%, Hinduism 12%, Islam 10%, Christianity 7%
- **Currency:** Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR)
- **Independence:** 1948 (from Britain)

## Sigiriya - The Lion Rock

### Overview
- **Type:** Ancient rock fortress and palace complex
- **Height:** 200 meters (660 feet) of sheer rock
- **Built:** 477-495 CE by King Kasyapa I
- **UNESCO World Heritage Site:** 1982
- **Name Meaning:** "Lion Rock" (Sīha + Giriya)

### History
- King Kasyapa built palace on top to protect from rival brother
- Originally had giant lion sculpture at entrance (only paws remain)
- Abandoned after Kasyapa's death, used as Buddhist monastery until 14th century

### The Frescoes
- **Sigiriya Ladies:** Ancient frescoes on rock face
- **Age:** 1,500+ years old
- **Subjects:** Celestial maidens (apsaras)
- **Style:** Unique Sri Lankan artistic tradition
- **Preservation:** Protected by sheltered pocket in rock

### The Mirror Wall
- Highly polished white plaster wall
- Once so reflective king could see himself
- Covered with ancient graffiti (6th-14th century)
- 1,800+ inscriptions preserved

### Water Gardens
- Sophisticated hydraulic engineering
- Fountains still work during rainy season
- Symmetrical pools, islands, causeways
- Among oldest landscaped gardens in world

### Access
- **Climb:** 1,200+ steps
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours round trip
- **Best Time:** Sunrise or late afternoon (avoid midday heat)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to challenging

## Kandy & The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic

### Kandy Overview
- **Location:** Central highlands
- **Elevation:** 500 meters (1,640 feet)
- **UNESCO World Heritage Site:** 1988
- **Last Kingdom:** Capital of last Sinhalese kingdom (until 1815)
- **Cultural Significance:** Heart of Sri Lankan Buddhism

### Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa)
- **Relic:** Buddha's left upper canine tooth
- **Arrival in Sri Lanka:** 4th century CE
- **Significance:** Whoever possesses relic holds governance of country
- **Architecture:** Kandyan style, gold roof, intricate carvings
- **Daily Puja:** 3 times daily (dawn, noon, evening)
- **Esala Perahera:** Grand festival (July/August) with 10 days of parades

### Temple Complex
- Multiple shrines and devales (dedicated to guardian deities)
- Museum with historical artifacts
- Sacred Bo Tree (descendant of original Bodhi Tree)
- Lake Kandy (artificial, built 1807)

### Cultural Shows
- Traditional Kandyan dance performances nightly
- Fire walking, whip cracking, acrobatics
- Authentic cultural experience for visitors

## Ella & The Hill Country

### Ella Overview
- **Location:** Southern edge of central highlands
- **Elevation:** 1,050 meters (3,440 feet)
- **Vibe:** Laid-back backpacker haven, stunning views
- **Climate:** Cooler than lowlands, misty mornings

### Nine Arches Bridge
- **Built:** 1921 (British colonial era)
- **Height:** 31 meters (100 feet)
- **Length:** 91 meters (300 feet)
- **Construction:** Stone, brick, cement - NO steel
- **Train Crossings:** Multiple daily (check schedules)
- **Photo Spot:** Iconic blue train crossing bridge

### Little Adam's Peak
- **Height:** 1,150 meters (3,773 feet)
- **Hike:** 3-4 hours round trip
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Sunrise/Sunset:** Popular times
- **Views:** Ella Gap, surrounding hills

### Ravana Falls
- **Height:** 25 meters (82 feet)
- **Legend:** Named after King Ravana (Ramayana epic)
- **Access:** Roadside viewing, swimming possible

### Ella Gap
- Dramatic rift in mountains
- Views stretch to southern coast (clear days)
- Sunset viewpoint popular

## Tea Plantations

### Overview
- **Introduction:** 1867 by James Taylor (British)
- **After:** Coffee blight destroyed coffee plantations
- **Current:** Sri Lanka = 4th largest tea producer globally
- **Ceylon Tea:** Protected geographical indication
- **Regions:** Nuwara Eliya, Ella, Kandy, Uva, Dimbula

### Nuwara Eliya
- **Elevation:** 1,868 meters (6,128 feet)
- **Nickname:** "Little England"
- **Climate:** Coolest in Sri Lanka
- **Colonial Architecture:** Tudor-style buildings, post office, golf course
- **Tea Factories:** Many offer tours and tastings

### Tea Production
- **Process:** Withering, rolling, oxidizing, drying, sorting
- **Types:** White, green, oolong, black (most common)
- **Grades:** Orange Pekoe, Pekoe, Broken Orange Pekoe, etc.
- **Harvesting:** Hand-picked (two leaves and a bud)

### Tea Pickers
- **Demographics:** Mostly Tamil women (descendants of British-era workers)
- **Daily Quota:** ~20 kg fresh leaves
- **Pay:** Per kilogram harvested
- **Conditions:** Improved but still challenging
- **Cultural Note:** Important to treat with respect, ask before photographing

### Factory Tours
- **What to Expect:** Processing demonstration, tasting, shop
- **Cost:** Usually free or small fee
- **Best Factories:** Pedro Estate, Mackwoods Labookellie, Handunugoda

## Sri Lankan Buddhism

### Overview
- **Introduction:** 3rd century BCE by Mahinda (son of Emperor Ashoka)
- **School:** Theravada Buddhism (oldest surviving branch)
- **Significance:** Integral to Sinhalese identity and culture
- **Temples:** 6,000+ across island

### Sacred Sites

#### Sri Maha Bodhi (Anuradhapura)
- **Tree:** Sacred fig tree
- **Age:** 2,300+ years (oldest planted tree with known date)
- **Origin:** Sapling from original Bodhi Tree (India)
- **Significance:** Buddha attained enlightenment under Bodhi Tree

#### Ruwanwelisaya Stupa (Anuradhapura)
- **Built:** 2nd century BCE
- **Height:** 103 meters (338 feet)
- **Significance:** One of world's tallest monuments (ancient)
- **Contains:** Buddha relics

#### Dambulla Cave Temple
- **Type:** Cave temple complex
- **Age:** 1st century BCE
- **Features:** 5 caves, 150+ Buddha statues, murals
- **UNESCO:** 1991

### Buddhist Practices
- **Alms Giving:** Monks collect food at dawn
- **Poya Days:** Full moon holidays (alcohol/shops closed)
- **Temple Etiquette:** Cover shoulders/knees, remove shoes/hats
- **Offerings:** Flowers, incense, lamps

### Vesak Festival
- **When:** May (full moon)
- **Significance:** Buddha's birth, enlightenment, death
- **Celebrations:** Lanterns, pandals, dansalas (free food stalls)

## Colonial History

### Portuguese Era (1505-1658)
- **Arrival:** 1505 (Lourenço de Almeida)
- **Control:** Coastal areas only
- **Legacy:** 
  - Catholicism (especially in fishing communities)
  - Place names (Dehiwala, Negombo)
  - Some surnames (Perera, Silva, Fernando)
  - Introduction of chili peppers (transformed cuisine)

### Dutch Era (1658-1796)
- **Control:** Coastal regions, cinnamon trade
- **Legacy:**
  - Galle Fort (UNESCO site)
  - Dutch architecture (canals, buildings)
  - Roman-Dutch law (still influences legal system)
  - Burgher community (mixed European-Sri Lankan)

### British Era (1796-1948)
- **Control:** Entire island (unified 1815)
- **Name:** Ceylon
- **Legacy:**
  - Tea plantations
  - Railways (Kandy-Ella line)
  - English language
  - Cricket (national sport)
  - Colonial buildings (Colombo Fort, Galle Face Hotel)
  - Education system

### Independence
- **Date:** February 4, 1948
- **Method:** Peaceful transition (Dominion status)
- **Republic:** 1972 (name changed to Sri Lanka)
- **Civil War:** 1983-2009 (see below)

## Civil War Context (1983-2009)

### Background
- **Ethnic Groups:** Sinhalese (74%), Tamils (18%, concentrated in north/east), Muslims (10%)
- **Roots:** Colonial divide-and-rule, post-independence language policies, discrimination
- **LTTE:** Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (Tamil Tigers)
- **Goal:** Independent Tamil state (Tamil Eelam) in north and east

### The Conflict
- **Duration:** 26 years (1983-2009)
- **Trigger:** Black July 1983 (anti-Tamil pogrom)
- **Casualties:** Estimated 80,000-100,000+ deaths
- **Displacement:** Hundreds of thousands
- **LTTE Tactics:** Suicide bombings, child soldiers, assassinations
- **Government Tactics:** Military offensives, human rights concerns

### Key Events
- **1987:** Indian Peace Keeping Force (IPKF) intervention
- **1991:** LTTE assassinates Indian PM Rajiv Gandhi
- **1993:** LTTE assassinates Sri Lankan President Premadasa
- **2001:** Ceasefire agreement
- **2005:** Ceasefire collapses
- **2009:** Military defeat of LTTE (May 18)

### Aftermath & Reconciliation
- **Current:** Government controls entire island
- **Challenges:** 
  - Disappeared persons
  - War crimes allegations (both sides)
  - Tamil political rights
  - Land mines (north)
  - PTSD and trauma
- **Tourism:** Northern areas (Jaffna) now accessible
- **Sensitivity:** Handle with care, acknowledge suffering on all sides

### Travel Considerations
- **North/East:** Safe for tourists, some military presence
- **Memorials:** Both government and LTTE memorials exist
- **Conversation:** Let locals bring up topic first
- **Respect:** Acknowledge complexity, avoid taking sides

## Wildlife

### Elephants
- **Population:** ~7,000 wild elephants
- **Best Viewing:** 
  - Minneriya National Park (Aug-Sept "The Gathering")
  - Kaudulla National Park
  - Udawalawe National Park (year-round)
  - Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage (captive, controversial)
- **Behavior:** Herds, bathing, feeding
- **Ethics:** Choose responsible safari operators

### Leopards
- **Subspecies:** Sri Lankan leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya)
- **Population:** ~800 in wild
- **Best Viewing:** Yala National Park (highest density globally)
- **Behavior:** Elusive, often rest in trees
- **Other Parks:** Wilpattu, Horton Plains

### Whales & Dolphins
- **Species:** Blue whales, sperm whales, Bryde's whales
- **Location:** Mirissa (south), Trincomalee (east)
- **Season:** November-April (south), May-October (east)
- **Success Rate:** High (80-90% in season)
- **Ethics:** Choose responsible operators (distance, time limits)

### Birds
- **Species:** 450+ (26 endemic)
- **Endemics:** Sri Lanka blue magpie, red-faced malkoha, Sri Lanka junglefowl
- **Best Birding:** Sinharaja Rainforest, Horton Plains, Bundala

### Other Wildlife
- **Sloth Bears:** Yala, Wilpattu
- **Water Buffalo:** National parks
- **Crocodiles:** Rivers, lagoons
- **Sea Turtles:** Nesting sites (south coast)
- **Monkeys:** Toque macaque, purple-faced langur

## Climate & Seasons

### Monsoon Patterns
- **Yala Monsoon:** May-August (southwest brings rain to south/west)
- **Maha Monsoon:** October-January (northeast brings rain to north/east)

### Regional Variations

#### South & West Coasts (Colombo, Galle, Mirissa)
- **Best Time:** November-April (dry, sunny)
- **Rainy:** May-October

#### East Coast (Arugam Bay, Trincomalee)
- **Best Time:** May-October (dry, sunny)
- **Rainy:** November-March

#### Hill Country (Kandy, Ella, Nuwara Eliya)
- **Year-Round:** Possible, cooler
- **Rain:** Can occur any time, mist common
- **Temperature:** 15-25°C (59-77°F)

#### North (Jaffna)
- **Best Time:** December-March
- **Hot:** March-September (can exceed 40°C/104°F)

### Temperature
- **Coastal:** 27-32°C (81-90°F) year-round
- **Hill Country:** 15-25°C (59-77°F)
- **Coldest:** Nuwara Eliya (can drop to 5°C/41°F at night)

## Tourism Practicalities

### Visas
- **ETA (Electronic Travel Authorization):** Required for most visitors
- **Type:** Tourist visa (30 days, double entry)
- **Cost:** ~$50 USD (varies by nationality)
- **Application:** Online (eta.gov.lk)
- **Processing:** Usually 24-48 hours
- **On Arrival:** Available but slower, higher fee

### Getting There
- **Main Airport:** Bandaranaike International (CMB), Colombo
- **Connections:** Major Middle Eastern hubs (Dubai, Doha, Abu Dhabi), Singapore, India
- **Flight Times:** 
  - From Dubai: ~4.5 hours
  - From Singapore: ~4 hours
  - From London: ~11 hours
  - From India: 1-4 hours (depending on city)

### Internal Transport

#### Train
- **Kandy-Ella Line:** Most scenic train journey in world
- **Classes:** 1st (reserved, AC), 2nd (reserved), 3rd (unreserved, cheap)
- **Booking:** Online (railway.gov.lk) or station
- **Tip:** Book 1st or 2nd class for popular routes

#### Bus
- **Coverage:** Extensive network
- **Types:** Public (cheap, crowded), Private AC (comfortable)
- **Booking:** Station or online (some routes)

#### Tuk-Tuk
- **Use:** Short distances, negotiating required
- **Cost:** Cheap for short trips
- **Apps:** PickMe, Uber (Colombo)

#### Domestic Flights
- **Routes:** Colombo to Jaffna, Trincomalee
- **Airlines:** Cinnamon Air, Helitours
- **Use:** Save time, scenic

## Health & Safety

### Vaccinations
- **Routine:** Up to date (MMR, polio, etc.)
- **Recommended:** Hepatitis A, Typhoid
- **Consider:** Hepatitis B, Rabies (if extended rural travel)
- **Malaria:** Low risk (consult doctor)
- **Dengue:** Present (use mosquito repellent)

### Food & Water Safety
- **Water:** Bottled water recommended (avoid tap)
- **Ice:** Generally safe in established venues
- **Food:** Street food usually safe (high turnover)
- **Fruit:** Peel it yourself
- **Hygiene:** Hand sanitizer, wash hands

### Common Health Issues
- **Traveler's Diarrhea:** Common, usually mild
- **Sunburn:** High UV, use sunscreen
- **Heat Exhaustion:** Stay hydrated
- **Mosquito-borne:** Dengue (day-biting), use repellent

### Safety
- **Crime:** Low violent crime, petty theft exists
- **Scams:** Tuk-tuk commissions, gem scams
- **Solo Travel:** Generally safe (especially for women)
- **Emergency:** 119 (police), 110 (ambulance)

### Swimming Safety
- **Rip Currents:** Strong on some beaches (check flags)
- **Monsoon:** Rough seas during monsoon
- **Lifeguards:** Limited (be cautious)

## Food & Cuisine

### Staples
- **Rice and Curry:** National dish (rice + multiple curries)
- **Hoppers (Appa):** Bowl-shaped pancakes
- **String Hoppers:** Steamed rice noodles
- **Kottu:** Chopped roti with vegetables/meat (street food)
- **Pol Roti:** Coconut flatbread

### Curries
- **Fish Curry:** Coastal specialty
- **Chicken Curry:** Common, flavorful
- **Dhal:** Lentil curry (vegetarian staple)
- **Vegetable Curries:** Eggplant, okra, beans, pumpkin

### Flavors
- **Spices:** Cinnamon (native), cardamom, cloves, nutmeg, pepper
- **Chili:** Sri Lankan food is SPICY (request "less spicy")
- **Coconut:** Milk, oil, grated (ubiquitous)
- **Tamarind:** Sour flavor base
- **Curry Leaves:** Aromatic herb

### Seafood
- **Fish:** Tuna, seer, snapper, pomfret
- **Crabs:** Mud crabs (Jaffna famous)
- **Prawns:** Large, fresh
- **Preparation:** Grilled, curried, fried

### Sweets & Snacks
- **Watalappam:** Coconut custard (Malay influence)
- **Kavum:** Oil cakes
- **Murukku:** Savory snack
- **Fresh Fruit:** Mango, papaya, pineapple, rambutan

### Drinks
- **Tea:** Ceylon tea (black, with milk and sugar)
- **King Coconut:** Fresh, sweet, hydrating (roadside)
- **Arrack:** Local spirit (coconut/toddy)
- **Beer:** Lion, Elephant (local brands)

### Dietary Notes
- **Vegetarian:** Widely available (Buddhist influence)
- **Vegan:** Possible (request no ghee, no egg)
- **Halal:** Available (Muslim population)
- **Allergies:** Communicate clearly (language barrier possible)

## Culture & Etiquette

### Greetings
- **Traditional:** Ayubowan (may you live long) + hands together
- **Handshake:** Common in business/urban
- **Respect:** Use "Sir" or "Madam"

### Dress Code
- **Temples:** Cover shoulders and knees (both genders)
- **Beaches:** Swimwear OK on beach, cover up in town
- **General:** Modest dress respected (especially rural)
- **Shoes:** Remove before entering temples, homes

### Behavior
- **Head:** Sacred (don't touch anyone's head)
- **Feet:** Low/status (don't point feet at people/Buddha)
- **Public Affection:** Minimal (holding hands OK, kissing not)
- **Photography:** Ask permission (especially people, religious ceremonies)
- **Buddha Images:** Respectful poses only (no climbing, inappropriate poses)

### Poya Days
- **When:** Full moon each month (12 per year)
- **Effect:** Alcohol sales prohibited, some shops closed
- **Temple:** Busy with worshippers
- **Travel:** Plan accordingly

### Tipping
- **Restaurants:** 10% if service charge not included
- **Tuk-Tuk:** Round up or small tip
- **Guides:** $5-10/day appreciated
- **Porters:** Small tip per bag

### Negotiation
- **Markets:** Expected (be friendly, fair)
- **Tuk-Tuk:** Agree price before departure (or use meter/app)
- **Shops:** Fixed prices in established stores

## Responsible Tourism

### Environmental
- **Plastic:** Minimize single-use (bring reusable bottle/bag)
- **Coral:** Don't touch or stand on coral
- **Wildlife:** Maintain distance, no feeding
- **Beaches:** Take trash with you

### Cultural
- **Temples:** Dress appropriately, follow rules
- **Photography:** Ask permission (people, ceremonies)
- **Donations:** Give to legitimate organizations, not directly to children
- **Respect:** Local customs, religious practices

### Economic
- **Local:** Buy local, eat local, stay local
- **Fair Pay:** Pay fair prices (don't over-negotiate to poverty)
- **Tips:** Appreciated in service industry
- **Community Tourism:** Support community-based initiatives

### Wildlife Ethics
- **Elephant Rides:** Avoid (animal welfare concerns)
- **Selfie with Animals:** Questionable ethics
- **Safaris:** Choose responsible operators
- **Whale Watching:** Reputable companies (distance, time limits)

### Voluntourism
- **Orphanages:** Avoid (often exploitative)
- **Teaching:** Ensure qualified, sustainable programs
- **Construction:** Skilled work only
- **Research:** Choose ethical organizations
