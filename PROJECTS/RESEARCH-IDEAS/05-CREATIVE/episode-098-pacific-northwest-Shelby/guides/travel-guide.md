## GETTING THERE

### From Seattle (Most Common Route)

**Distance:** 180 miles / 290 km  
**Drive Time:** 4.5 - 5 hours

**Route:**
1. Take I-5 South from Seattle to Olympia (60 miles)
2. Merge onto US-101 West toward Aberdeen (60 miles)
3. Continue on US-101 West through Aberdeen and Hoquiam (40 miles)
4. Follow US-101 North along the Olympic Peninsula (20 miles)
5. Turn onto Upper Hoh Road (signs for Hoh Rainforest) (20 miles)
6. Arrive at Hoh Rainforest Visitor Center

**Alternative Scenic Route:**
- Take Bainbridge Island Ferry from Seattle (35 min)
- Drive through Port Townsend and along the northern Olympic Peninsula
- More scenic, adds 1-2 hours

### From Port Angeles (Park Headquarters)

**Distance:** 85 miles / 137 km  
**Drive Time:** 2.5 hours

**Route:**
1. Take US-101 West from Port Angeles
2. Continue through Sequim and around the peninsula
3. Turn onto Upper Hoh Road near Forks
4. Follow to Hoh Rainforest Visitor Center

### From Forks (Closest Town)

**Distance:** 30 miles / 48 km  
**Drive Time:** 45 minutes

**Route:**
1. Take US-101 South from Forks
2. Turn onto Upper Hoh Road (clearly signed)
3. Follow 29 miles to the end

## ENTRANCE FEES (2025-2026)

### Park Entrance
- **Private Vehicle (7 days):** $30
- **Individual (hiker/biker, 7 days):** $15
- **Olympic National Park Annual Pass:** $55
- **America the Beautiful Pass:** $80 (covers all national parks)
- **Children 15 and under:** FREE

**Where to Purchase:**
- Entrance stations (self-pay envelopes available when unstaffed)
- Visitor centers
- Online at recreation.gov

**Important:** Keep your receipt visible on your dashboard. Rangers do check.

### Camping Fees
- **Hoh Rainforest Campground:** $24/night (standard), $48/night (group)
- **Other Park Campgrounds:** $15-40/night depending on location
- **Backcountry/Wilderness Permit:** $8/person/night (16+), plus $6 reservation fee
- **Annual Wilderness Pass:** $45/person

### Reservations
- **Hoh Rainforest Campground:** Reservations required June 12 - September 6 via Recreation.gov
- **Off-season:** First-come, first-served
- **Book Early:** Summer sites fill months in advance

## WHEN TO VISIT

### Year-Round Destination

#### Summer (July - September)
**Pros:**
- Warmest temperatures (60-75°F / 15-24°C)
- Longest days (sunset after 9pm)
- All facilities open
- Best for longer hikes and backpacking
- Clearest skies (relatively)

**Cons:**
- Most crowded (especially weekends)
- Moss may appear drier
- Campground reservations required
- Higher prices on lodging

**Best For:** First-time visitors, serious hikers, families

---

#### Fall (October - November)
**Pros:**
- Fewer crowds
- Moss at its lushest and greenest
- Dramatic light and atmosphere
- Good photography conditions
- Some facilities still open

**Cons:**
- More rain (but that's the point!)
- Shorter days
- Some services reduced
- Trails can be muddy

**Best For:** Photographers, solitude seekers, moss enthusiasts

---

#### Winter (December - February)
**Pros:**
- Least crowded (often alone on trails)
- Most atmospheric and moody
- Winter beauty (occasional snow at higher elevations)
- Lowest prices
- Deeply peaceful

**Cons:**
- Some facilities closed (check ahead)
- Shortest days (sunset ~4:30pm)
- Wettest season
- Some roads may have conditions
- Cold (35-45°F / 2-7°C)

**Best For:** Solitude, meditation, experienced visitors, moody photography

---

#### Spring (March - May)
**Pros:**
- Wildflowers emerging
- Increasing daylight
- Good balance of access and solitude
- Waterfalls at peak flow
- Baby animals

**Cons:**
- Variable weather
- Some trails may be muddy or damaged from winter
- Not all facilities open yet

**Best For:** Wildflower enthusiasts, waterfall chasers, shoulder season visitors

---

### RECOMMENDED

**For First Visit:** July-September (best weather, all access)  
**For Photography:** October-April (lush moss, dramatic light)  
**For Solitude:** November-March, weekdays  
**For Wildflowers:** April-May  
**For Salmon Runs:** September-October

- **Heart O' The Hills:** $24/night, near Port Angeles

- Historic lodge on beautiful Lake Crescent

## WHAT TO PACK

### Essential Gear (Year-Round)

#### Clothing
- **Rain jacket:** Non-negotiable, even in summer
- **Waterproof boots:** Trail runners get soaked
- **Layers:** Temperatures vary significantly
- **Wool or synthetic socks:** No cotton
- **Hat:** For rain or sun
- **Gloves:** In cooler months

#### Equipment
- **Backpack:** 20-30L for day hikes
- **Water bottle or hydration:** 2L minimum
- **Offline maps:** No cell service in the park
- **Headlamp:** Even for day hikes (forest is dark)
- **Trekking poles:** Helpful for muddy trails
- **Camera:** Obviously
- **Binoculars:** For wildlife viewing

#### Safety
- **Bear spray:** Recommended for deeper hikes
- **First aid kit:** Basic supplies
- **Emergency shelter:** Space blanket or bivy
- **Whistle:** For emergencies
- **Fire starter:** Waterproof matches or lighter

#### Comfort
- **Camp chair:** For sitting in the forest
- **Journal:** For reflection
- **Book:** For campground evenings
- **Snacks:** Trail mix, energy bars

### Seasonal Additions

#### Summer
- Sunscreen (yes, even in the forest)
- Insect repellent
- Lighter layers
- Sandals for camp

#### Winter
- Extra warm layers
- Waterproof gloves
- Warm hat
- Hand warmers
- Traction devices for trails (microspikes)

## TRAILS & HIKES

### Easy (Family-Friendly)

#### Hall of Mosses Trail
- **Distance:** 0.8 miles loop
- **Elevation:** 78 feet
- **Time:** 30-45 minutes
- **Highlights:** Moss-draped maples, most iconic Hoh views
- **Crowds:** High (most popular trail)
- **Accessibility:** Partially wheelchair accessible
- **Best For:** First-time visitors, photography, quick immersion

#### Spruce Nature Trail
- **Distance:** 1.2 miles loop
- **Elevation:** 16 feet
- **Time:** 45-60 minutes
- **Highlights:** Hoh River views, Olympic Mountains, mix of old/new growth
- **Crowds:** Moderate (less than Hall of Mosses)
- **Best For:** River views, wildlife spotting, peaceful walk

#### Rainforest Nature Trail
- **Distance:** 0.3 miles loop
- **Elevation:** Minimal
- **Time:** 15-20 minutes
- **Highlights:** Paved, accessible, quick introduction
- **Crowds:** Low to moderate
- **Best For:** Accessibility, quick visit, families with small children

---

### Moderate (Day Hikes)

#### Hoh River Trail to Five Mile Island
- **Distance:** 10.1 miles out-and-back (or turn around earlier)
- **Elevation:** Minimal gain, mostly flat
- **Time:** 4-6 hours (full distance)
- **Highlights:** Classic Hoh views, river following, old growth
- **Crowds:** Low (people thin out after 2 miles)
- **Best For:** Serious day hikers, solitude, wildlife

#### Hoh River Trail to Mineral Creek Falls
- **Distance:** 5.6 miles out-and-back
- **Elevation:** Moderate gain
- **Time:** 3-4 hours
- **Highlights:** Beautiful waterfall, rainforest immersion
- **Crowds:** Low
- **Best For:** Waterfall seekers, moderate challenge

#### South Snider-Jackson Trail
- **Distance:** 11.8 miles one-way (can turn around)
- **Elevation:** 2,700 feet gain
- **Time:** 6-8 hours
- **Highlights:** Connects to Bogachiel River, more primitive
- **Crowds:** Very low
- **Best For:** Experienced hikers, solitude, challenge

---

### Strenuous (Multi-Day Backpacking)

#### Hoh River Trail to Blue Glacier
- **Distance:** 34.6 miles roundtrip
- **Elevation:** 3,700 feet gain
- **Time:** 3-5 days
- **Highlights:** Glacier views, alpine terrain, wilderness experience
- **Permit:** Required (backcountry permit)
- **Best For:** Experienced backpackers, mountaineering access

#### South Fork Hoh River Trail
- **Distance:** Variable (primitive trail)
- **Elevation:** Variable
- **Time:** Multi-day
- **Highlights:** Solitude, pristine old growth, challenging
- **Permit:** Required
- **Best For:** Wilderness experts, solitude seekers

## WILDLIFE VIEWING

### Roosevelt Elk
- **Where:** Meadow areas, especially dawn and dusk
- **When:** Year-round, most active morning/evening
- **Distance:** Stay at least 25 yards away (23 meters)
- **Safety:** Do not approach, especially bulls during rut (September)

### Black-tailed Deer
- **Where:** Throughout the forest, edges of meadows
- **When:** Dawn and dusk most active
- **Distance:** 25 yards minimum

### Black Bears
- **Where:** Throughout park, less commonly seen
- **When:** More active spring-fall
- **Safety:** Make noise on trails, carry bear spray, store food properly

### Birds
- **Pacific Wren:** Listen for loud song, tiny bird
- **Steller's Jay:** Bold, blue and black, common
- **Bald Eagle:** Near rivers and coasts
- **Spotted Owl:** Old growth dependent, rare, twilight

### Salmon
- **When:** September-October (fall runs)
- **Where:** Hoh River, clear viewing areas
- **Note:** Bears may be fishing—give extra space

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Cell Service
- **Reality:** None in the Hoh Rainforest
- **Solution:** Download offline maps before arrival
- **Emergency:** Ranger station has radio communication

### Water
- **Potable Water:** Available at campground and visitor center
- **Trail Water:** Filter or treat before drinking
- **Recommendation:** Bring enough for your hike

### Food
- **In Park:** Very limited (campground store seasonal)
- **Forks:** Grocery stores, restaurants (30 miles)
- **Recommendation:** Bring food with you

### Weather Preparedness
- **Rain:** Expect it, even in summer
- **Temperature:** Can vary 20+ degrees in a day
- **Solution:** Layers, rain gear, flexibility

### Leave No Trace
1. Plan ahead and prepare
2. Travel and camp on durable surfaces
3. Dispose of waste properly (pack it out)
4. Leave what you find
5. Minimize campfire impacts (check regulations)
6. Respect wildlife (observe from distance)
7. Be considerate of other visitors

### Photography Tips
- **Best Light:** Early morning, late afternoon
- **Overcast:** Actually ideal for rainforest (no harsh shadows)
- **Tripod:** Helpful in low light
- **Polarizing Filter:** Reduces glare on wet surfaces
- **Protection:** Rain cover for camera essential

## SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS

### Wildlife
- **Elk:** Give 25 yards minimum, more during rut
- **Bears:** Rare but present, make noise, carry spray
- **Mountain Lions:** Extremely rare, same precautions as bears

### Weather
- **Hypothermia:** Risk year-round due to wet conditions
- **Lightning:** Rare but possible, avoid exposed areas in storms
- **Wind:** Falling branches possible in high winds

### Terrain
- **Mud:** Trails can be slippery, especially off-season
- **River Crossings:** Dangerous, do not attempt without experience
- **Roots:** Trip hazards on all trails

### Health
- **Water:** Filter or treat all backcountry water
- **Plants:** Learn to identify poison oak (less common in rainforest)
- **Allergies:** Bring medications, cell service is unavailable

### Emergency
- **Ranger Station:** Hoh Visitor Center (seasonal hours)
- **Emergency:** 911 (may not have cell service, use satellite device)
- **Recommendation:** Carry satellite messenger (Garmin inReach, SPOT)

## RESPECTFUL VISITATION

### Indigenous Lands
- This is ancestral Hoh territory
- Be respectful of cultural sites
- Learn about the Hoh people before visiting
- Support Indigenous businesses when possible

### Environmental Protection
- Stay on designated trails
- Do not pick plants or remove natural objects
- Pack out all trash (including biodegradable)
- Do not feed wildlife

### Other Visitors
- Keep noise levels down (this is a place of silence)
- Step aside for faster hikers
- Do not block trails for photos
- Respect others' experience

## BEYOND THE HOH

### Other Olympic National Park Rainforests

#### Quinault Rainforest
- **Location:** South side of park
- **Features:** Lake Quinault, big cedars, less crowded
- **Access:** Better for RVs

#### Queets Rainforest
- **Location:** South coastal area
- **Features:** Most remote, largest trees, primitive access
- **Access:** Forestry roads, high clearance recommended

#### Bogachiel Rainforest
- **Location:** Near Forks
- **Features:** Old growth spruce, wetlands, Ira Spring Loop
- **Access:** Good day hike option

### Other Park Highlights
- **Hurricane Ridge:** Alpine meadows, mountain views
- **Ruby Beach:** Dramatic coastal scenery
- **Lake Crescent:** Crystal clear alpine lake
- **Sol Duc Hot Springs:** Natural hot springs
- **Marymere Falls:** Beautiful waterfall near Lake Crescent
