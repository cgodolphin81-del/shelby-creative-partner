## RECOMMENDATION

**For Maximum Continuity:** Option A (Olympic Peninsula Continuation)
- Builds on Ep 098 research and momentum
- Establishes "Olympic Peninsula" as a mini-series
- Easier production (same trip, multiple locations)
- Clear narrative arc

**For Maximum Contrast:** Option B (Alpine Transition)
- Dramatic visual shift (green → grey/white)
- Thematic depth (forest floor → mountain peak)
- Shows range of The Infinite Walk
- Appeals to different audience segment

**For Maximum Impact:** Option C (Coastal Edge)
- Universally appealing (everyone loves coast)
- Stunning visuals (waves, sea stacks, driftwood)
- Natural progression (forest → coast)
- Strong emotional resonance

**My Recommendation:** **Option C (Coastal Edge)**

Why:
1. Natural geographic progression (Hoh → Coast is logical journey)
2. Visual variety while maintaining Pacific Northwest theme
3. Strong emotional hook (edge of continent, endings/beginnings)
4. Appeals to both forest lovers AND ocean lovers
5. Rich thematic material (boundaries, transitions, liminal spaces)

## EPISODE 099 PRELIMINARY OUTLINE
### (If pursuing Coastal Edge option)

**Working Title:** "The Edge of the Continent"

**Location:** Olympic Coast, Washington (Rialto Beach, Kalaloch, or similar)

**Key Themes:**
- Boundaries and transitions
- The power of the Pacific
- Driftwood as memory
- Tides as time
- Where the forest's story meets the ocean's story

**Key Visuals:**
- Waves crashing on ancient rocks
- Driftwood forests on beaches
- Sea stacks emerging from mist
- Tide pools teeming with life
- Sunset over the Pacific
- You walking the shoreline

**Key Sounds:**
- Waves (constant, rhythmic)
- Seabirds (gulls, puffins, cormorants)
- Wind through coastal grasses
- Your footsteps on sand/driftwood
- Silence between waves

**Poetic Angles:**
- The ocean as the ultimate destination (all rivers flow here)
- Driftwood as trees' final journey
- Tides as the planet's breath
- The edge as both ending and beginning
- Salt preserving memory

**Indigenous Connection:**
- Coastal tribes (Makah, Quileute, Quinault)
- Salmon's final journey ends here
- Canoe cultures, maritime traditions

**Runtime:** 18-22 minutes (similar to Ep 098)

**Tone:** Contemplative, awe-inspiring, slightly melancholic but hopeful
