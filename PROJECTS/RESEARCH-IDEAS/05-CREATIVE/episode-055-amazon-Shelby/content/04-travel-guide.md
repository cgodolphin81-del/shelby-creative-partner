## GETTING THERE

### Flights to Manaus

**International Connections:**
- **From Miami (MIA):** Seasonal direct flights (American Airlines), ~6 hours
- **From Europe:** Connect through São Paulo (GRU) or Brasília (BSB)
- **From South America:** Direct from Bogotá, Lima, Panama City

**Domestic Connections:**
- **São Paulo (GRU) → Manaus (MAO):** 4 hours, multiple daily flights
  - Airlines: LATAM, GOL, Azul
  - Cost: $150-400 USD roundtrip
- **Brasília (BSB) → Manaus (MAO):** 3 hours, frequent flights
  - Cost: $120-300 USD roundtrip
- **Rio de Janeiro (GIG) → Manaus (MAO):** 4.5 hours
  - Cost: $180-450 USD roundtrip

**Booking Tips:**
- Book 2-3 months in advance for best prices
- Dry season (July-September) = higher prices
- Use Google Flights or Skyscanner for comparison
- Consider multi-city: São Paulo → Manaus → Rio → home

### Alternative: The Boat Journey

**From Belém to Manaus:**
- **Duration:** 3-5 days depending on boat
- **Cost:** $50-200 USD (hammock to cabin)
- **Experience:** Authentic, adventurous, slow travel
- **Boats:** Cargo/passenger vessels (no luxury cruises)
- **What to expect:** Basic amenities, communal meals, deck sleeping or cabins
- **Book:** At Belém port or through hostels

**Pros:** Cheap, authentic, unique experience
**Cons:** Time-consuming, basic conditions, seasickness possible

## GETTING AROUND

### In Manaus

**From Airport to City:**
- **Taxi:** 45 minutes, $25-35 USD
- **Uber:** Available, $15-25 USD
- **Bus:** 90 minutes, $2 USD (not recommended with luggage)

**City Transport:**
- **Uber:** Reliable and affordable
- **Taxi:** Negotiate price before departure
- **Bus:** Extensive network, very cheap, confusing for visitors
- **Walking:** Safe in tourist areas during day

### River Transport

**To Lodges/Reserves:**
- Most lodges include boat transfer in package
- Speed boats: 1-4 hours depending on location
- Larger boats: Slower but more comfortable

**Public Boats:**
- Depart from Manaus port (Mercado Municipal)
- Destinations: Parintins, Santarém, various riverside towns
- Cost: $20-100 USD depending on distance and class
- Classes: Deck (cheapest), Cabin, First Class

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### High Water Season (December - May)

**Pros:**
- Access to flooded forests (igapó) by boat
- Cooler temperatures (26-28°C / 79-82°F)
- Birds nesting, active wildlife
- Lush, green vegetation

**Cons:**
- Heavy rainfall (especially March-April)
- Some hiking trails inaccessible
- Higher humidity (85-90%)
- Higher prices, more crowds

**Best For:** Boat exploration, bird watching, photography

### Low Water Season (June - November)

**Pros:**
- Less rainfall, more sunny days
- More hiking possible (trails exposed)
- Wildlife concentrates near rivers (easier to spot)
- Lower prices, fewer tourists
- River beaches accessible

**Cons:**
- Hotter (28-32°C / 82-90°F)
- Some areas inaccessible by boat
- More mosquitoes
- Afternoon thunderstorms still possible

**Best For:** Trekking, fishing, caiman spotting, budget travel

### Sweet Spot: **July - September**

- Balance of good weather and accessibility
- Peak wildlife viewing
- Moderate prices (book ahead)
- Ideal for first-time visitors

### Months to Avoid (if possible)

- **March-April:** Heaviest rainfall, flooding
- **December-January:** Peak crowds, highest prices
- **October:** Can be very hot

## MUST-DO EXPERIENCES

### In & Near Manaus

1. **Meeting of Waters (Encontro das Águas)**
   - See the Rio Negro and Solimões flow side by side
   - Half-day boat tours depart daily
   - Best viewed from boat, not shore
   - Cost: $30-60 USD

2. **Teatro Amazonas**
   - Stunning opera house from rubber boom era
   - Italian marble, French glass, Brazilian hardwood
   - Guided tours hourly
   - Cost: $10 USD

3. **Mercado Municipal Adolpho Lisboa**
   - Historic market on the waterfront
   - Local crafts, souvenirs, regional foods
   - Great for photos, less for shopping
   - Cost: Free entry

4. **Museu da Amazônia (MUSA)**
   - Natural history museum with canopy tower
   - Learn about Amazon ecology
   - Climb 42m tower for panoramic views
   - Cost: $15 USD including tower

5. **Ponta Negra Beach**
   - Local beach area on Rio Negro
   - Weekend gatherings, live music, food stalls
   - Visit on Saturday or Sunday afternoon
   - Cost: Free (pay for food/drinks)

### Jungle Experiences

6. **Anavilhanas National Park**
   - World's largest river archipelago (400+ islands)
   - Day trips or multi-day stays
   - Exceptional wildlife viewing
   - Cost: $100-300 USD day trip

7. **Indigenous Community Visit**
   - Learn about Tikuna, Dessana, or other cultures
   - Ethical, community-controlled tourism
   - Purchase crafts directly
   - Cost: $50-100 USD

8. **Night Safari**
   - Boat trip to spot caimans (eyes reflect light)
   - Hear nocturnal animals, see different ecosystem
   - Often includes piranha fishing
   - Cost: $40-80 USD

9. **Jungle Trekking**
   - Guided hikes through primary forest
   - Learn about medicinal plants, survival skills
   - Spot monkeys, birds, insects
   - Cost: $80-150 USD full day

10. **Canopy Walkway**
    - Walk among the treetops on suspension bridges
    - Unique perspective, bird's eye view
    - Several locations near Manaus
    - Cost: $25-40 USD

## ECO-TOURISM & RESPONSIBLE TRAVEL

### Choosing Responsible Operators

**Look For:**
- IBAMA certification (Brazilian environmental agency)
- Local guides employed (not just foreign guides)
- Small group sizes (under 12 people)
- Clear environmental policies
- Support for local communities
- Transparent pricing

**Ask Before Booking:**
- Where does my money go?
- Are guides local or brought from elsewhere?
- What is your environmental policy?
- Do you visit indigenous communities ethically?
- How do you minimize environmental impact?

### Red Flags
- Very cheap prices (exploitative practices likely)
- Large groups (20+ people)
- No clear environmental policy
- "Guaranteed" wildlife sightings (unethical practices)
- Visits to indigenous communities without consent
- Feeding wildlife or encouraging interaction

### Recommended Operators

**Luxury:**
- Iberostar Grand Amazon
- Aqua Amazon Cruises
- Delfin Amazon Cruises

**Mid-Range:**
- Amazon Clipper
- Jangada Amazon Cruise
- Uakari Lodge (community-owned)

**Budget/Backpacker:**
- Amazon Nature Tours
- Jungle City Tours
- Manaus Hostel tour desk (reputable operators)

## HEALTH & SAFETY

### Vaccinations (Required & Recommended)

**Required:**
- **Yellow Fever:** Certificate required for entry to some areas. Get 10 days before travel.

**Recommended:**
- **Typhoid:** Especially if eating street food
- **Hepatitis A:** Food and water-borne
- **Hepatitis B:** If any medical procedures possible
- **Rabies:** If spending extended time in remote areas
- **Tetanus:** Ensure up to date

**Malaria:**
- Risk in rural Amazon areas, low in Manaus
- Consult travel doctor for prophylaxis recommendation
- Common medications: Atovaquone-proguanil, Doxycycline
- Also use mosquito prevention (see below)

### Mosquito Prevention

**Essential:**
- DEET insect repellent (30-50% concentration)
- Permethrin-treated clothing (or treat before trip)
- Long sleeves and pants (lightweight, breathable)
- Mosquito nets (provided by most lodges)
- Plug-in repellent devices for rooms

**Tips:**
- Apply repellent after sunscreen
- Reapply every 4-6 hours
- Dawn and dusk = peak mosquito activity
- Wear light colors (mosquitoes attracted to dark colors)

### Water & Food Safety

**Water:**
- **Never drink tap water**
- Bottled water only (check seal is intact)
- Use bottled water for brushing teeth
- Avoid ice in drinks unless sure of source
- Boiled or filtered water at lodges is usually safe

**Food:**
- Eat thoroughly cooked food
- Avoid raw vegetables (washed in tap water)
- Peel fruits yourself
- Street food: choose busy stalls with high turnover
- Avoid buffet food that's been sitting out

### General Safety

**Manaus City:**
- Generally safe for tourists
- Avoid displaying expensive jewelry/electronics
- Use Uber/taxi at night, don't walk alone
- Keep copies of passport, leave original in safe
- Be aware of surroundings in crowded areas

**In the Jungle:**
- Always go with qualified guide
- Stay on marked trails
- Don't touch plants or animals (many are toxic)
- Listen to guide instructions carefully
- Inform someone of your itinerary

**Swimming:**
- Avoid swimming in rivers (piranhas, caimans, parasites)
- Some lodges have safe swimming areas
- Ask guide before entering any water

### Emergency Information

**Emergency Number:** 190 (Police), 192 (Ambulance)

**Hospitals in Manaus:**
- Hospital Universitário Getúlio Vargas
- Hospital Adventista de Manaus
- Private clinics available for tourists

**Travel Insurance:**
- **Essential:** Must cover medical evacuation
- Recommended providers: World Nomads, Allianz, SafetyWing
- Ensure coverage for adventure activities
- Keep insurance details accessible

## CULTURAL TIPS

### Language

- **Official language:** Portuguese (not Spanish!)
- **In tourist areas:** Some English spoken
- **Learn basic Portuguese phrases:**
  - Olá = Hello
  - Obrigado/a = Thank you (masc/fem)
  - Por favor = Please
  - Quanto custa? = How much?
  - Não falo português = I don't speak Portuguese
  - Ajuda = Help

### Etiquette

**General:**
- Brazilians are warm and friendly
- Greetings are important (handshake, sometimes kiss on cheek)
- Tipping: 10% in restaurants (often included on bill)
- Dress modestly when visiting communities

**Indigenous Communities:**
- Always visit with permission and guide
- Ask before taking photographs
- Respect sacred spaces and ceremonies
- Buy crafts at fair prices (bargaining inappropriate)
- Listen more than you speak
- Follow community guidelines strictly

**Photography:**
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Some indigenous communities restrict photography
- Don't use flash on wildlife
- Be respectful, not intrusive

### Money

- **Currency:** Brazilian Real (BRL, R$)
- **Exchange rate:** ~5 BRL = 1 USD (varies)
- **ATMs:** Available in Manaus, limited in rural areas
- **Credit cards:** Accepted in hotels, restaurants, larger shops
- **Cash:** Essential for markets, small vendors, tips
- **Tipping:** 10% in restaurants, $5-10/day for guides

## SAMPLE ITINERARIES

### 3 Days / 2 Nights (Quick Visit)

**Day 1:** Arrive Manaus, city tour (Teatro Amazonas, Mercado), overnight in city
**Day 2:** Full-day jungle excursion (Anavilhanas), return evening, overnight city
**Day 3:** Meeting of Waters morning tour, depart

**Cost:** $300-500 USD (mid-range)

### 5 Days / 4 Nights (Standard)

**Day 1:** Arrive Manaus, overnight city
**Day 2:** Transfer to jungle lodge, afternoon activities, night safari
**Day 3:** Full day jungle trekking, wildlife viewing, lodge activities
**Day 4:** Indigenous community visit, return to Manaus, overnight city
**Day 5:** Morning city tour, depart

**Cost:** $600-1,200 USD (mid-range)

### 7 Days / 6 Nights (Comprehensive)

**Day 1-2:** Manaus city exploration, acclimatization
**Day 3-5:** River cruise or jungle lodge, full immersion
**Day 6:** Return to Manaus, rest, souvenir shopping
**Day 7:** Final morning tour, depart

**Cost:** $1,000-2,500 USD (mid to luxury)

### 10+ Days (Deep Exploration)

- Combine Manaus with other Amazon regions
- Consider: Alter do Chão, Santarém, Rio Negro upstream
- Multiple lodges or extended cruise
- Time for slower travel, deeper experiences

**Cost:** $2,000-5,000+ USD

## FINAL TIPS

1. **Book ahead** for dry season (July-September)
2. **Pack light** - you'll live in casual clothes
3. **Bring binoculars** - wildlife viewing is 10x better
4. **Respect the forest** - take nothing but photos, leave nothing but footprints
5. **Support local** - choose locally-owned operators and lodges
6. **Be flexible** - weather and wildlife don't follow schedules
7. **Listen to guides** - they know the forest intimately
8. **Slow down** - this is not a place to rush
9. **Stay curious** - there's always more to learn
10. **Carry the Amazon home** - let it change how you see the world
