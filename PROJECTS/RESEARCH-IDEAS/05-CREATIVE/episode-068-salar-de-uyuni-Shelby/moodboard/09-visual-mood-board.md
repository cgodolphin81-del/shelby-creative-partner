## COMPOSITION GUIDELINES

### Rule of Thirds
- Place horizon on upper or lower third line
- Position subjects at intersection points
- Use for: Landscape shots, balanced compositions

### Central Symmetry
- Perfect symmetry for mirror effect shots
- Subject centered with identical reflection
- Use for: Mirror scenes, philosophical moments

### Leading Lines
- Salt polygon cracks as leading lines
- Path or footprints leading to horizon
- Use for: Journey themes, depth, perspective

### Negative Space
- Large areas of sky or salt
- Small human figure for scale
- Use for: Isolation, vastness, contemplation

### Low Angle
- Camera close to ground/water
- Emphasizes infinity and reflection
- Use for: Mirror effect, walking on clouds feeling

### Aerial/Drone
- Overhead perspective
- Shows scale and patterns
- Use for: Establishing shots, pattern emphasis

## DO'S & DON'TS

### DO
✅ Use the color palette consistently across all assets
✅ Maintain high contrast for readability
✅ Include human element for scale in landscape shots
✅ Use negative space for contemplative moments
✅ Match lighting to emotional tone
✅ Keep text minimal on images
✅ Use gradients for smooth transitions

### DON'T
❌ Use too many colors in one composition
❌ Place text over busy backgrounds
❌ Ignore the emotional impact of color
❌ Use harsh shadows for serene moments
❌ Overcrowd compositions
❌ Use low-resolution images
❌ Forget accessibility (contrast ratios)
