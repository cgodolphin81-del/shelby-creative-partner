## IMAGERY STYLE

### Photography Direction

#### Lighting
- **Golden Hour:** Warm, soft, directional light
- **Blue Hour:** Cool, mysterious, pre-dawn tones
- **Dappled Light:** Filtered through jungle canopy
- **High Contrast:** Silhouettes against sunrise/sunset

#### Composition
- **Symmetry:** Reflection pools, temple facades
- **Leading Lines:** Causeways, hallways, tree roots
- **Framing:** Doorways, windows, root archways
- **Scale:** Small human figures against massive temples
- **Detail:** Close-ups of carvings, textures, faces

#### Mood
- **Serene:** Peaceful, contemplative, meditative
- **Mysterious:** Shadow and light, hidden spaces
- **Awe-Inspiring:** Wide shots showing scale
- **Intimate:** Close details, human connection

### AI Image Generation Guidelines

**Prompt Keywords to Include:**
- Photorealistic, cinematic, National Geographic style
- Warm golden light, soft shadows
- Ancient, weathered, timeless
- Serene, peaceful, contemplative
- 8K, highly detailed, professional photography

**Prompt Keywords to Avoid:**
- Modern elements (cars, power lines, signage)
- Tourist crowds (unless specifically desired)
- Anachronistic elements
- Overly saturated or artificial colors

## GRAPHIC ELEMENTS

### Textures

#### Weathered Stone
- Use for backgrounds, overlays
- Adds authenticity and age
- Subtle, not overwhelming

#### Jungle Organic
- Leaf patterns, root textures
- Use as framing elements
- Connects to Ta Prohm theme

#### Water Reflection
- Subtle ripple effects
- Use behind text or as transitions
- Evokes reflection pool imagery

### Icons & Symbols

#### Lotus Bud
- Represents Angkor Wat's five towers
- Use as section dividers
- Symbol of enlightenment

#### Tree/Root
- Represents Ta Prohm, nature's reclamation
- Use for nature-themed sections
- Symbol of growth and impermanence

#### Face Silhouette
- Represents Bayon's watching faces
- Use sparingly, respectfully
- Symbol of wisdom and presence

#### Dancing Figure
- Represents apsara carvings
- Use for artistic/cultural sections
- Symbol of eternal beauty

### Layout Principles

#### Balance
- Symmetrical layouts for temple imagery
- Asymmetrical for jungle/nature content
- Mix of full-bleed and framed images

#### White Space
- Generous margins for contemplative feel
- Let images breathe
- Don't overcrowd

#### Hierarchy
- Clear visual hierarchy (title > subtitle > body)
- Use color and size to guide eye
- Consistent spacing throughout

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC GUIDELINES

### Instagram Feed (1:1 and 4:5)
- Use stone greys as base, accent with sunrise colors
- Large, bold typography for quote cards
- High-contrast images for scroll-stopping power
- Consistent filter/preset across all images

### Instagram Stories (9:16)
- Vertical gradients work well
- Leave space for interactive elements (polls, links)
- Use motion (subtle animations) when possible
- Keep text in safe zones (center 80%)

### TikTok/Reels/Shorts (9:16)
- Hook in first 3 seconds with strong visual
- Use sunrise/jungle colors for branding
- Text overlays in high-contrast colors
- End screen with episode info and CTA

### YouTube Thumbnail (16:9)
- High contrast, readable at small sizes
- Large, bold text (3-5 words max)
- Use sunrise orange/pink for CTAs
- Face or human element increases CTR

### Pinterest (2:3 or 9:16)
- Vertical format performs best
- Text overlay essential for context
- Use warm, inviting colors
- Include episode number and title

### LinkedIn (1:1 or 1.91:1)
- Professional, clean design
- Stone greys and deep greens primary
- Minimal text, maximum impact
- Thoughtful, not promotional

### Email Header (600px wide)
- Episode artwork or key visual
- Title and episode number clear
- Mobile-optimized (test at 320px)
- Include "Listen Now" CTA button

## DESIGN DON'TS

❌ Over-saturating colors (keep natural, authentic)
❌ Too many fonts (stick to 2-3 max)
❌ Cluttered layouts (embrace white space)
❌ Modern anachronisms (no cars, signs, etc. in imagery)
❌ Disrespectful use of sacred imagery
❌ Ignoring accessibility (ensure text is readable)
❌ Inconsistent branding across platforms

---

## DESIGN DOS

✅ Use natural, authentic colors from the location
✅ Embrace negative space for contemplative feel
✅ Maintain consistency across all platforms
✅ Test all designs at multiple sizes
✅ Ensure accessibility (contrast ratios, readable text)
✅ Honor the sacred nature of the sites
✅ Let imagery tell the story
✅ Keep the human element (Shelby/traveler) visible
