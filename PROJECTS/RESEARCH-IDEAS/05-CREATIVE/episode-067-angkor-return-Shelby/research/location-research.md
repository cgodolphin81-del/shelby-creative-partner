# Episode 067: Location Research - Angkor Wat Return, Cambodia

## The Temple Complex

### Angkor Wat Overview
- **Built:** 1113-1150 AD during reign of King Suryavarman II
- **Original Purpose:** Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, later transformed to Buddhist
- **Scale:** Largest religious monument in the world (162.6 hectares)
- **Architecture:** Classical Khmer style, representing Mount Meru (center of universe)
- **UNESCO Status:** World Heritage Site since 1992

### Key Temple Areas

#### Angkor Wat Main Temple
- **Sunrise Viewing:** Northern reflection pool (classic view), southern pool (less crowded)
- **Best Time:** Gates open 5:00 AM, arrive by 4:30 AM for prime spots
- **Sunrise Times:** 
  - Nov-Feb: 6:15-6:30 AM
  - Mar-May: 5:45-6:15 AM
  - Jun-Oct: 5:30-6:00 AM
- **Dress Code:** Shoulders and knees must be covered (sacred site)

#### Ta Prohm - The Jungle Temple
- **Built:** Late 12th century by King Jayavarman VII
- **Dedication:** Buddhist monastery dedicated to king's mother
- **Famous Feature:** Massive silk-cotton and strangler fig trees embracing stone ruins
- **Tree Species:** Tetrameles nudiflora and Ficus gibbosa
- **Vibe:** Left largely unrestored, showing nature reclaiming civilization
- **Tomb Raider Connection:** Featured in 2001 film, gained worldwide recognition
- **Visit Duration:** 1-2 hours recommended
- **Best Times:** Early morning or late afternoon for fewer crowds

#### Bayon Temple - The Face Temple
- **Location:** Center of Angkor Thom
- **Built:** Late 12th-early 13th century by King Jayavarman VII
- **Religious Shift:** Mahayana Buddhist (departure from Hindu tradition)
- **Famous Feature:** 200+ giant smiling stone faces on 54 towers
- **Face Interpretation:** Avalokiteshvara (Bodhisattva of compassion) OR King Jayavarman VII himself
- **Bas-Reliefs:** Daily life scenes (markets, cooking, childbirth, naval battles) - unique among Angkor temples
- **Symbolism:** Compassionate leadership, Buddhist cosmic view
- **Central Tower:** 43 meters high, originally housed Buddha statue protected by Mucalinda

#### Angkor Thom
- **Last Capital:** Final great city of Khmer Empire
- **Size:** 9 square kilometers
- **Gates:** Five monumental gates (four cardinal directions + Victory Gate)
- **Key Sites:** Bayon, Baphuon, Terrace of Elephants, Terrace of the Leper King

#### Banteay Srei
- **Distance:** ~25km from main complex
- **Material:** Pink sandstone (glows at sunrise/sunset)
- **Known For:** Intricate, well-preserved carvings
- **Nickname:** "Citadel of Women"

### Apsara Carvings
- **Quantity:** Over 1,500 across Angkor Wat alone
- **Description:** Celestial dancers/nymphs from Hindu-Buddhist mythology
- **Size:** Typically 30-40 cm
- **Attire:** Crown (3-5 points), braided hair, jewelry (earrings, necklaces, bracelets, belt, anklets), bare upper torso, patterned lower garment
- **Poses:** Usually standing, grasping swags or holding lotus stems
- **Unique Detail:** No two crowns are identical; one carving on western gopura shows visible teeth (rare)
- **Cultural Significance:** Inspired classical Apsara dance (UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage since 2003)

## Tourism Information (2025-2026)

### Angkor Pass Prices
- **1-Day:** $37 USD
- **3-Day:** $62 USD (valid over 10 days)
- **7-Day:** $72 USD (valid over 1 month)
- **Purchase:** Online portal, ticket office on Street 60, or kiosks (opens 5 AM)
- **Requirement:** Passport photo needed

### Transportation Changes (2026 Update)
- **Scooter Rentals:** NO LONGER ALLOWED for tourists around Angkor Wat
- **Options:** Tuk-tuk with driver, private car, guided tour, bicycle
- **Temple Transport:** $15-30 USD per day for tuk-tuk/car hire

### Best Times to Visit
- **Dry Season (Dec-Mar):** Clearest skies, peak crowds, pleasant temps (22-30°C/72-86°F)
- **Shoulder Seasons (Apr-May, Oct-Nov):** Fewer crowds, slight rain risk
- **Wet Season (Jun-Oct):** Lush jungle, fewer tourists, frequent rain, muddy paths
- **Daily Timing:** Sunrise (5 AM arrival), midday heat (explore early/late), sunset options at Phnom Bakheng/Pre Rup

### Siem Reap Costs (2025-2026)
- **Budget Daily:** $25-35 USD (dorms, street food, basic transport)
- **Mid-Range Daily:** $60-100 USD (private room, mix of dining, activities)
- **Luxury Daily:** $150-250+ USD (upscale hotels, fine dining, private tours)
- **Accommodation:** 
  - Dorm: $5-12/night
  - Budget guesthouse: $12-20/night
  - Mid-range boutique: $30-60/night
  - Luxury: $195+/night
- **Food:**
  - Street food/local: $1.50-4 per meal
  - Western restaurants: $6-12 per main
  - Coffee: $2.50-4
  - Beer (local draft): $0.50-1 (happy hour), $1.50-3 (restaurants)
- **Transport:**
  - Tuk-tuk short ride: $1.50-3
  - Airport shuttle: Available
  - Intercity bus (to Phnom Penh): $8-20

### Practical Tips
- **Currency:** Dual system - USD widely accepted, Cambodian Riel for small purchases
- **ATMs:** Widely available, watch for fees
- **Tipping:** 10-15% appreciated, check if service fee included
- **Connectivity:** Local SIM cards available, eSIMs work, turn off roaming
- **What to Bring:** 
  - Angkor Pass
  - Fully charged camera/phone + power bank
  - Headlamp/flashlight (for pre-dawn)
  - Water bottle
  - Insect repellent
  - Sunscreen, hat, sunglasses
  - Modest clothing (shoulders/knees covered)
  - Comfortable walking shoes
  - Light rain jacket (wet season)
  - Cash for tips/souvenirs

### Visitor Numbers
- **2024:** Over 2 million visitors (28% increase from 2023)
- **Trend:** Strong recovery post-pandemic, continuing growth into 2025-2026

### Cultural Notes
- **Sacred Site:** Speak quietly, respect religious practices
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing monks
- **Climbing:** Restricted areas must be observed
- **Shoes/Hats:** Remove where indicated
- **Local Guides:** Highly recommended for historical context, hidden spots, logistics
