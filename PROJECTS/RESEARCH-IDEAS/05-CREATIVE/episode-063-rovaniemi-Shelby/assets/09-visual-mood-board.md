## Typography

### Primary Font (Headings)
**Font:** Montserrat or similar geometric sans-serif
**Weights:** Bold (700), Semi-Bold (600)
**Use:** Episode titles, section headers, quote cards
**Why:** Clean, modern, readable, Nordic feel

### Secondary Font (Body)
**Font:** Lato or similar humanist sans-serif
**Weights:** Regular (400), Light (300)
**Use:** Body text, descriptions, captions
**Why:** Friendly, readable, pairs well with Montserrat

### Accent Font (Quotes/Special)
**Font:** Playfair Display or similar serif
**Weights:** Regular (400), Italic (400)
**Use:** Pull quotes, poetic passages, special moments
**Why:** Elegant, literary, emotional weight

## Image Style Guidelines

### Photography Style

**Overall Aesthetic:**
- Cinematic and atmospheric
- High contrast between light and dark
- Cool color temperature (blues, greens)
- Warm accents (cabin lights, fire, clothing)
- Moody but not depressing
- Sense of scale (vast landscapes, small humans)

**Exposure:**
- Slightly underexposed for night scenes
- Expose for highlights in snow scenes (+1 to +2 stops)
- Preserve detail in aurora (not blown out)

**Composition:**
- Rule of thirds for landscapes
- Leading lines (paths, aurora ribbons)
- Human element for scale
- Negative space for text overlays
- Vertical compositions for Stories/Reels

---

### Image Treatments

**Aurora Photos:**
- Enhance green/purple saturation (subtly)
- Increase contrast
- Reduce noise (high ISO cleanup)
- Sharpen stars (mask aurora)
- Add slight vignette

**Snow/Landscape:**
- Increase clarity/texture
- Boost blues in shadows
- Warm highlights slightly
- Preserve snow detail (not blown out)
- Add subtle dehaze

**Portraits (Shelby/People):**
- Warm skin tones (contrast with cold environment)
- Soft background blur
- Eye sharpness
- Protect highlights on snow
- Add catchlight if possible

**Santa Village:**
- Enhance warm lights
- Boost reds (Santa, decorations)
- Maintain magical atmosphere
- Not oversaturated (keep authentic)
- Add slight glow to lights

## Graphic Design Elements

### Shapes & Patterns

**Northern Lights Ribbons:**
- Flowing, organic curves
- Gradient: Purple → Green
- Use: Dividers, backgrounds, accents
- Opacity: 20-40% for overlays

**Snowflake Motifs:**
- Geometric, six-point symmetry
- Color: White or Ice Blue
- Use: Subtle patterns, corners, transitions
- Size: Small, not dominant

**Arctic Circle Line:**
- Clean, straight horizontal line
- Color: Aurora Green or White
- Use: Dividers, progress indicators, branding
- Weight: 2-4px

**Compass Rose:**
- Minimalist, geometric
- Color: Gold or White
- Use: Episode branding, navigation

## Visual Do's and Don'ts

### DO ✅
- Use high-contrast combinations for readability
- Maintain consistent color palette across all assets
- Include episode number for series recognition
- Use aurora imagery sparingly (it's powerful, don't overuse)
- Test all graphics on mobile devices
- Keep text minimal on images (especially for social)
- Use warm accents to contrast cold imagery
- Maintain brand consistency with previous episodes

### DON'T ❌
- Don't oversaturate aurora (looks fake)
- Don't use too many colors (stick to palette)
- Don't clutter designs (negative space is powerful)
- Don't use low-resolution images
- Don't forget accessibility (color contrast, alt text)
- Don't use copyrighted images without permission
- Don't make text too small for mobile viewing
- Don't deviate wildly from established brand

## Accessibility Considerations

### Color Contrast
- All text must meet WCAG AA standards (4.5:1 for normal text)
- Use contrast checker tools
- Don't rely on color alone to convey information

### Alt Text
- Describe images for screen readers
- Include key visual elements
- Mention text in images
- Keep concise but informative

### Examples:
**Good:** "Northern lights aurora borealis dancing over snow-covered forest in Finnish Lapland, green and purple ribbons across starry sky"
**Bad:** "aurora.jpg"

**Good:** "Quote card with text 'We are all magnetic creatures' in white serif font on dark blue aurora background"
**Bad:** "quote image"
