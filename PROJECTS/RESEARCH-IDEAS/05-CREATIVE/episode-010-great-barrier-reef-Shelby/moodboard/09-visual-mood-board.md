## COMPOSITION GUIDELINES

### Rule of Thirds
- Place horizon (surface) on upper third line
- Position key subjects at intersection points
- Leave negative space for breathing room

### Leading Lines
- Use reef walls as diagonal lines
- Light rays as vertical guides
- Fish schools as flow lines

### Scale Reference
- Include diver silhouette for vastness
- Show small fish against large coral
- Contrast human presence with nature's scale

### Depth Layers
- Foreground: Detailed coral or fish
- Midground: Main subject
- Background: Blue water gradient
- Creates three-dimensional feel

## TYPOGRAPHY PAIRINGS

### Primary Headings
**Font:** Playfair Display or similar serif
- Elegant, editorial feel
- Works with contemplative content
- Weights: Regular, Bold

### Body Text
**Font:** Lato, Open Sans, or similar sans-serif
- Clean, readable
- Modern but not cold
- Weights: Light, Regular

### Accent/Quotes
**Font:** Script or handwritten style
- Personal, intimate
- Use sparingly for pull quotes
- Example: "Coral Cathedral"

### Color Application
- Headings: Deep Navy (#001F3F) or Reef Blue (#0077BE)
- Body: Dark Grey (#333333) on light backgrounds
- Quotes: Living Coral (#FF6F61) or Purple Sea Fan (#9B59B6)
- Links: Reef Blue (#0077BE), hover to Turquoise (#40E0D0)

## IMAGE STYLING

### Photography Treatment

**Contrast:**
- Slightly elevated for underwater clarity
- Preserve shadow detail in deep areas
- Maintain highlight detail in sunlit areas

**Saturation:**
- Boost blues and teals moderately
- Enhance coral colors naturally
- Avoid oversaturation (looks artificial)

**Temperature:**
- Cool tones for deep water scenes
- Warm tones for shallow, sunlit areas
- Match mood to content

**Vignette:**
- Subtle, for focus
- Stronger for dramatic moments
- None for wide environmental shots

---

### Graphic Elements

**Overlays:**
- Gradient overlays for text readability
- Subtle water texture overlays
- Light ray overlays for ethereal moments

**Frames/Borders:**
- Organic, flowing shapes
- Avoid harsh geometric lines
- Consider coral-inspired organic borders

**Icons:**
- Simple line icons for travel info
- Marine life silhouettes
- Water drop, wave, coral shapes

---

## MOTION/VIDEO GUIDELINES

### Movement Style

**Pace:**
- Slow, contemplative for essay moments
- Moderate for travel guide sections
- Dynamic for social media clips

**Transitions:**
- Cross-dissolves for contemplative moments
- Quick cuts for energy
- Match cuts (similar shapes/colors)

**Camera Movement:**
- Smooth, floating (underwater aesthetic)
- Slow push-ins for intimacy
- Wide reveals for scale

### Color Grading

**Underwater Scenes:**
- Preserve natural blues
- Enhance coral colors slightly
- Maintain contrast for clarity

**Surface/Above Water:**
- Warm, tropical tones
- Golden hour for emotional moments
- Bright, vibrant for travel sections

---

## ACCESSIBILITY CONSIDERATIONS

### Color Contrast
- Ensure text meets WCAG AA standards
- Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for normal text
- 3:1 for large text (18pt+ or 14pt bold)

### Alt Text for Images
- Describe scene, colors, mood
- Include species names when known
- Note emotional content

### Motion Sensitivity
- Provide pause controls for video
- Avoid rapid flashing or strobing
- Respect reduced-motion preferences
