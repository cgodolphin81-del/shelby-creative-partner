## GEOGRAPHY & SCALE

**Location:** Coral Sea, off the coast of Queensland, Australia
**Coordinates:** Approximately 18°S 147°E (central reef)
**Length:** 2,300 kilometers (1,430 miles)
**Area:** 344,400 square kilometers (133,000 sq mi)
**Visibility:** Can be seen from space — the only living structure visible from orbit

**Composition:**
- 2,900 individual reefs
- 900 islands
- Extends from northern tip of Queensland (near Papua New Guinea) to just north of Bundaberg
- Spans tropical and subtropical waters

**Water Temperature:**
- Summer (Dec-Feb): 26-29°C (79-84°F)
- Winter (Jun-Aug): 22-25°C (72-77°F)
- Best diving: June-October (cooler, clearer water, less jellyfish)

---

## MARINE BIODIVERSITY

**Species Count:**
- 1,500+ fish species
- 400+ coral species (hard and soft)
- 4,000+ mollusk species
- 240+ bird species (on islands)
- 30+ whale and dolphin species
- 6 of the world's 7 marine turtle species
- 125+ shark and ray species

**Iconic Species:**
- **Green Sea Turtle** (Chelonia mydas) — herbivorous, can live 80+ years
- **Hawksbill Turtle** — critically endangered, eats sponges
- **Clownfish** (Amphiprioninae) — lives in anemone symbiosis
- **Maori Wrasse** — can grow to 2m, lives 30+ years
- **Giant Clam** (Tridacna gigas) — up to 1.2m, 200kg
- **Manta Rays** — wingspan up to 7m
- **Whale Sharks** — largest fish, visit June-July
- **Dugong** — "sea cow," vulnerable species
- **Minke Whales** — dwarf minke whales visit June-July

**Coral Types:**
- **Hard Corals:** Brain coral, staghorn, table coral, boulder coral
- **Soft Corals:** Sea fans, sea whips, leather coral
- **Coral Spawning:** Annual mass spawning (Nov, after full moon) — one of nature's greatest spectacles

## ECOSYSTEM ZONES

**1. Reef Flat (Shallow)**
- Depth: 0-5m
- High light, warm water
- Coral diversity lower, but abundant fish life
- Accessible for snorkeling

**2. Reef Slope**
- Depth: 5-30m
- Highest coral diversity
- Best for scuba diving
- Dramatic drop-offs and walls

**3. Lagoon**
- Protected inner reef areas
- Calm waters, seagrass beds
- Turtle grazing areas

**4. Outer Reef**
- Open ocean side
- Stronger currents, pelagic species
- Advanced diving

## ACCESS POINTS & GATEWAYS

**Primary Gateway: Cairns, Queensland**
- Population: ~153,000
- Airport: Cairns International (CNS)
- Distance from reef: 40-70km to outer reef
- Travel time by boat: 1.5-3 hours to outer reef

**Alternative Gateways:**
- **Port Douglas** (60km north of Cairns) — upscale, closer to Agincourt Reef
- **Airlie Beach** — gateway to Whitsundays (southern reef)
- **Townsville** — access to Magnetic Island and central reef
- **Lady Elliot Island** — southern reef, great for turtles and manta rays

## PERMITS & REGULATIONS

**Marine Park Authority:**
Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Authority (GBRMPA)
- Website: gbrmpa.gov.au
- All commercial operators require permits
- Environmental Management Charge (EMC) applies to all visitors

**Environmental Management Charge (EMC):**
- Day trip: $7.25 AUD per person
- Multi-day: $21.75 AUD per person
- Collected by tour operators

**Diving Regulations:**
- No touching or standing on coral
- No feeding fish
- No collecting shells, coral, or marine life
- Mandatory briefings before all dives
- Buoyancy control required to protect reef

**Protected Zones:**
- Green Zones (no-take): 33% of park
- Some areas closed seasonally for turtle nesting or coral spawning

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

**June-August (Winter/Dry Season):**
- ✅ Best visibility (30-50m)
- ✅ Cooler water (22-25°C)
- ✅ Minimal rain
- ✅ No stinger jellyfish
- ✅ Whale sightings possible
- ❌ Busiest season, higher prices
- ❌ Water may feel cool without wetsuit

**September-November (Spring):**
- ✅ Warming water
- ✅ Good visibility
- ✅ Coral spawning (November, after full moon)
- ❌ Building humidity
- ❌ Possible early stingers

**December-February (Summer/Wet Season):**
- ✅ Warmest water (27-29°C)
- ✅ Fewer crowds
- ✅ Lower prices
- ❌ Stinger season (box jellyfish, Irukandji) — stinger suits required
- ❌ Higher rain, runoff affects visibility
- ❌ Cyclone season (rare but possible)

**March-May (Autumn):**
- ✅ Still warm water
- ✅ Decreasing rain
- ✅ Good visibility returning
- ❌ Stinger season continues through April

**RECOMMENDED:** June-October for best overall conditions

## CLIMATE THREATS

**Coral Bleaching:**
- Mass bleaching events: 1998, 2002, 2016, 2017, 2020, 2022, 2024
- Caused by prolonged elevated water temperatures
- 2024 bleaching: most severe on record, affecting 70%+ of reef
- Bleached coral is not dead but stressed; can recover if temperatures normalize

**Climate Change Impacts:**
- Ocean acidification (CO2 absorption weakens coral skeletons)
- More frequent and severe bleaching
- Increased cyclone intensity
- Sea level rise affecting cays and islands

**Other Threats:**
- **Crown-of-Thorns Starfish:** Outbreaks devastate coral (natural predator but population explosions destructive)
- **Agricultural Runoff:** Sediment, pesticides, fertilizers from coastal farming
- **Coastal Development:** Port expansion, dredging
- **Overfishing:** Disrupts ecosystem balance

**Conservation Efforts:**
- Reef 2050 Long-Term Sustainability Plan
- $1 billion+ Australian government investment
- Coral restoration projects (coral gardening, larval reseeding)
- COTS (Crown-of-Thorns) control programs
- Tourism operators as reef guardians (citizen science, monitoring)

**What Visitors Can Do:**
- Choose eco-certified operators (Ecotourism Australia)
- Use reef-safe sunscreen (zinc oxide, no oxybenzone)
- Don't touch or stand on coral
- Reduce carbon footprint (climate change is the biggest threat)
- Support conservation organizations (Australian Marine Conservation Society, Great Barrier Reef Foundation)

## SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS

**Marine Life:**
- Most reef creatures are harmless if not provoked
- Do not touch anything (coral can cut, some creatures venomous)
- Sharks: present but not aggressive; incidents extremely rare
- Stonefish: venomous, camouflaged — wear booties when wading

**Stinger Season (November-May):**
- Box jellyfish and Irukandji present
- Stinger suits (full-body lycra) mandatory for most operators
- Vinegar stations on beaches (first aid for stings)
- Enclosures/netted areas at some beaches

**Diving Safety:**
- Always dive with a buddy
- Check equipment before every dive
- Monitor air supply and no-decompression limits
- Ascend slowly, safety stop at 5m for 3 minutes
- Don't dive if sick or congested

**Sun Protection:**
- Tropical sun is intense
- Reef-safe sunscreen essential
- Rash guards and wetsuits provide UV protection
- Hydrate frequently

## QUICK FACTS FOR NARRATION

- The reef is older than the Himalayas (formed over 25 million years)
- Current reef structure began growing 8,000 years ago after last ice age
- Coral polyps are animals, not plants — they're related to jellyfish and anemones
- Coral gets its color from symbiotic algae (zooxanthellae) living inside
- When stressed, coral expels algae → turns white (bleaching)
- The reef supports 64,000+ jobs and $6.4 billion AUD annually in tourism
- One coral colony can be hundreds of years old
- Parrotfish poop creates white sand beaches (they eat coral, excrete sand)
- The reef produces half of Australia's oxygen through marine plants
- Over 2 million visitors annually
