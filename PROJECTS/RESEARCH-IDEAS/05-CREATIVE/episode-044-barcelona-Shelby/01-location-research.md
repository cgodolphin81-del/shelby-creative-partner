## 🏛️ Sagrada Familia

**Overview:**
Antoni Gaudí's magnum opus, an unfinished basilica that has been under construction since 1882. Expected completion: 2026 (coinciding with the centenary of Gaudí's death).

**Key Features:**
- **Nativity Façade** (1894-1930): The only façade completed during Gaudí's lifetime, ornate and full of life
- **Passion Façade** (1954-1976): Stark, angular, depicting Christ's suffering
- **Glory Façade** (2002-present): The main façade, still under construction
- **Interior**: Forest-like columns, stunning light play through stained glass
- **Towers**: 18 planned, 8 completed as of 2024
- **Height**: Will reach 172.5 meters (566 feet)

**Visitor Information:**
- **Address**: Carrer de Mallorca, 401, 08013 Barcelona
- **Metro**: L2, L5 - Sagrada Família station
- **Tickets**: €26-40 (basic to guided tour with towers)
- **Best Time**: Early morning (9 AM) or late afternoon (after 4 PM)
- **Audio Guide**: Included with most tickets, available in 16 languages
- **Photography**: Allowed without flash

**Interesting Facts:**
- Gaudí dedicated the last 15 years of his life exclusively to this project
- The basilica can hold 13,000 people
- Gaudí is buried in the crypt
- UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2005
- First building to receive a building permit in 2019 (137 years after construction began!)

## 🌳 Park Güell

**Overview:**
A public park system composed of gardens and architectural elements, designed by Antoni Gaudí. Built between 1900-1914, originally conceived as a residential development.

**Key Features:**
- **El Drac (The Dragon)**: Famous mosaic salamander/lizard at the entrance
- **Sala Hipòstila**: Hall of 86 Doric columns
- **Plaça de la Natura**: Large terrace with serpentine bench covered in trencadís
- **Gaudí House Museum**: Where Gaudí lived from 1906-1925
- **Viaducts**: Stone pathways with leaning columns
- **Panoramic Views**: Overlooking Barcelona and the Mediterranean

**Visitor Information:**
- **Address**: 08024 Barcelona
- **Metro**: L3 - Lesseps or Vallcarca, then 15-20 min walk
- **Bus**: 24, 92, H6 from city center
- **Tickets**: €10 (general), €7 (residents), free for under 7
- **Timed Entry**: Required, book in advance
- **Best Time**: Sunrise or sunset for photography
- **Area**: 17 hectares (42 acres)

**Interesting Facts:**
- Named after Eusebi Güell, Gaudí's patron
- Only 2 of 60 planned houses were built
- The serpentine bench is 100 meters long
- Made using trencadís technique (broken tile mosaics)
- UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1984

## 🏰 Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic)

**Overview:**
The historic heart of Barcelona, with origins dating back to Roman times. A labyrinth of narrow medieval streets filled with hidden squares, historic buildings, and vibrant street life.

**Key Features:**
- **Barcelona Cathedral**: 13th-15th century Gothic cathedral
- **Plaça del Rei**: Medieval square, where Columbus was received by Catholic Monarchs
- **Las Ramblas**: Famous tree-lined pedestrian boulevard (edge of the quarter)
- **Plaça Sant Jaume**: City Hall and Generalitat (Catalan government)
- **Roman Walls**: Remnants of 4th-century fortifications
- **El Call**: Former Jewish quarter
- **Pont del Bisbe**: Neo-Gothic bridge (1928)

**Visitor Information:**
- **Metro**: L4 - Jaume I, Liceu (L3), or Cathedral area
- **Walking Tours**: Available daily, multiple languages
- **Best Time**: Early morning (before 10 AM) or evening (after 7 PM)
- **Free Entry**: The quarter itself is public
- **Cathedral**: €9 (free Sun 12:30-19:30)

**Interesting Facts:**
- One of the largest Gothic quarters in Europe
- Built on the site of ancient Roman Barcino
- Many buildings date from 14th-15th centuries
- Picasso had his first studio here
- Featured in numerous films including "Perfume" and "Vicky Cristina Barcelona"

## 🎨 Gaudí Architecture (Beyond Sagrada Familia & Park Güell)

### Casa Batlló
- **Location**: Passeig de Gràcia, 43
- **Year**: 1904-1906
- **Style**: Modernisme
- **Features**: Dragon-back roof, bone-like balconies, colorful mosaics
- **Tickets**: €29-35
- **Nickname**: "House of Bones"

### Casa Milà (La Pedrera)
- **Location**: Passeig de Gràcia, 92
- **Year**: 1906-1912
- **Features**: Wavy stone façade, rooftop chimneys, no load-bearing walls
- **Tickets**: €25-34
- **UNESCO**: World Heritage Site since 1984

### Palau Güell
- **Location**: Carrer Nou de la Rambla, 3-5
- **Year**: 1886-1890
- **Features**: Parabolic arches, central dome with 250 holes
- **Tickets**: €12

### Casa Vicens
- **Location**: Carrer de les Carolines, 20-26
- **Year**: 1883-1885
- **Features**: Gaudí's first major work, Moorish influence
- **Tickets**: €16-22

### Crypt of Colònia Güell
- **Location**: Santa Coloma de Cervelló (outside Barcelona)
- **Year**: 1898-1914 (unfinished)
- **Features**: Experimental structure, precursor to Sagrada Familia
- **Tickets**: €11

## 🚶 La Rambla

**Overview:**
Barcelona's most famous street, a 1.2-kilometer tree-lined pedestrian boulevard connecting Plaça de Catalunya to the Christopher Columbus Monument at Port Vell.

**Sections (from north to south):**
1. **Rambla de Canaletes**: Named after a fountain, meeting point for Barça fans
2. **Rambla dels Estudis**: Former site of a university
3. **Rambla de Sant Josep**: La Boqueria Market section
4. **Rambla dels Caputxins**: Site of the Gran Teatre del Liceu
5. **Rambla de Santa Mònica**: Art galleries and cultural centers

**Key Attractions:**
- **La Boqueria Market**: Historic food market (1836)
- **Gran Teatre del Liceu**: Opera house (1847, rebuilt 1999)
- **Plaça Reial**: Elegant square with Gaudí lampposts
- **Columbus Monument**: 60-meter tall statue (1888)
- **Street Performers**: Living statues, musicians, artists

**Visitor Information:**
- **Metro**: Multiple stations along the route
- **Best Time**: Early morning or evening
- **Caution**: Watch for pickpockets, especially in crowds
- **La Boqueria**: Open Mon-Sat 8 AM-8:30 PM

**Interesting Facts:**
- "La Rambla" comes from Arabic "ramla" (dry riverbed)
- Once a seasonal stream outside city walls
- George Orwell called it "the only street in the world I wish were longer"
- Featured in countless films, books, and songs

## 🏖️ Barcelona Beaches

### Barceloneta Beach
- **Length**: 1.1 km
- **Features**: Most popular, closest to city center
- **Amenities**: Showers, lifeguards, restaurants, beach bars
- **Vibe**: Lively, touristy, family-friendly

### Nova Icària Beach
- **Features**: Less crowded, water sports
- **Amenities**: Volleyball courts, playground, restaurants
- **Vibe**: Relaxed, local favorite

### Bogatell Beach
- **Features**: Wide, clean, Olympic legacy
- **Amenities**: Sports facilities, showers
- **Vibe**: Sporty, less touristy

### Mar Bella Beach
- **Features**: Nudist section, beach clubs
- **Vibe**: Alternative, LGBTQ+ friendly

## 📍 Additional Notable Locations

### Montjuïc
- **Montjuïc Castle**: 17th-century fortress with city views
- **Magic Fountain**: Light and music show (seasonal)
- **MNAC**: National Art Museum of Catalonia
- **Olympic Stadium**: 1992 Summer Olympics venue

### El Born
- **Santa Maria del Mar**: Stunning 14th-century Gothic church
- **Picasso Museum**: 4,251 works by Picasso
- **El Born Centre de Cultura**: Medieval ruins beneath modern building

### Gràcia Neighborhood
- **Vibe**: Bohemian, village-like atmosphere
- **Plaça del Sol**: Popular square for evening drinks
- **Park Güell**: Located in this neighborhood

### Tibidabo
- **Highest Point**: 512 meters above sea level
- **Tibidabo Amusement Park**: Oldest in Spain (1901)
- **Temple del Sagrat Cor**: Church overlooking the city
