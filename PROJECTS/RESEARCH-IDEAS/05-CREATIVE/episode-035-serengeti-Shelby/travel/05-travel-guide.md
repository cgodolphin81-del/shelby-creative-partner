## Getting There

### International Flights

**Primary Gateway: Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO)**
- Located between Arusha and Moshi, Tanzania
- 1 hour drive to Arusha (safari staging city)
- Airlines: KLM, Qatar Airways, Turkish Airlines, Ethiopian Airlines, Kenya Airways
- Most international travelers connect through Amsterdam, Doha, Istanbul, or Addis Ababa

**Alternative: Julius Nyerere International Airport (DAR) - Dar es Salaam**
- Tanzania's largest airport
- More flight options, but requires domestic connection to Arusha
- Add 2-3 hours for domestic transfer

**Via Nairobi, Kenya (NBO)**
- Often cheaper flights
- Cross border to Tanzania (visa required)
- 5-6 hour drive from Nairobi to Serengeti
- Consider if combining Kenya & Tanzania safari

### Regional Flights to Serengeti

**Domestic Airlines:**
- Coastal Aviation
- Auric Air
- Regional Air
- Flightlink

**Serengeti Airstrips:**
- **Seronera Airstrip** (central Serengeti) - year-round access
- **Kogatende Airstrip** (northern Serengeti) - best for migration crossings
- **Grumeti Airstrip** (western corridor) - luxury camps
- **Ndutu Airstrip** (southern Serengeti) - calving season access

**Flight Time:** 1-2 hours from Arusha
**Cost:** $350-600 USD per person, one-way
**Baggage:** 15-20kg limit in soft bags (strict!)

### Road Transfer

**Arusha to Serengeti:**
- Distance: 325 km (202 miles)
- Time: 7-8 hours (includes border post, rough roads)
- Route: Arusha → Karatu → Ngorongoro → Serengeti Gate
- Road conditions: Paved to Ngorongoro, rough gravel in park
- Recommended: Fly in, road out (or vice versa) to break up journey

**Vehicle Requirements:**
- 4WD Land Cruiser or similar essential
- Experienced safari driver-guide
- Park fees paid in advance

## When to Visit

### Peak Season: June-October (Dry Season)

**Pros:**
- ✅ Best wildlife viewing (animals congregate at water)
- ✅ Migration river crossings (July-September in north)
- ✅ Minimal rain, clear skies
- ✅ Roads passable throughout park
- ✅ Cooler temperatures

**Cons:**
- ❌ Highest prices (peak season rates)
- ❌ Most crowded (especially July-August)
- ❌ Dusty landscapes
- ❌ Book 6-12 months ahead

**Best For:** First-time visitors, photographers, migration crossings

### Calving Season: January-February

**Pros:**
- ✅ Baby animals everywhere (8,000 calves born daily!)
- ✅ Predator action at peak
- ✅ Southern Serengeti accessible
- ✅ Fewer tourists than mid-year
- ✅ Good photography light

**Cons:**
- ❌ Afternoon thunderstorms possible
- ❌ Some roads impassable after rain
- ❌ Hot temperatures

**Best For:** Wildlife enthusiasts, photographers, avoiding crowds

### Green Season: March-May (Long Rains)

**Pros:**
- ✅ Lush landscapes, beautiful photography
- ✅ Lowest prices (up to 50% off)
- ✅ Fewest tourists
- ✅ Bird watching excellent (migratory birds present)
- ✅ Green, vibrant scenery

**Cons:**
- ❌ Heavy rains, some areas inaccessible
- ❌ Wildlife more dispersed
- ❌ Some camps close
- ❌ Road conditions challenging

**Best For:** Budget travelers, birders, photographers wanting green landscapes

### Shoulder Season: November-December

**Pros:**
- ✅ Good balance of wildlife and value
- ✅ Short rains, landscapes green up
- ✅ Migration returning from north
- ✅ Moderate crowds

**Cons:**
- ❌ Some rain expected
- ❌ Variable conditions

**Best For:** Balanced experience, value seekers

## Safari Packages & Costs

### Budget Safari ($150-250 USD per person/day)

**Includes:**
- Basic camping or budget lodge
- Shared 4WD vehicle (6-7 passengers)
- Driver-guide
- All meals
- Park fees

**Example Operators:**
- Ultimate Safaris
- African Budget Safaris
- Kili Adventure

**Sample 5-Day Budget Itinerary:**
- Day 1: Arusha → Central Serengeti
- Day 2: Full day Central Serengeti
- Day 3: Serengeti → Ngorongoro
- Day 4: Ngorongoro Crater
- Day 5: Return to Arusha

**Total Cost:** $750-1,250 USD per person (excluding flights)

### Mid-Range Safari ($350-550 USD per person/day)

**Includes:**
- Comfortable tented camps or lodges
- Private or small group 4WD
- Experienced guide
- All meals, drinks
- Park fees

**Example Operators:**
- Asilia Africa
- Nomad Tanzania
- &Beyond

**Sample 7-Day Mid-Range Itinerary:**
- Day 1: Arusha → Tarangire
- Day 2: Tarangire → Central Serengeti
- Day 3-4: Central Serengeti game drives
- Day 5: Serengeti → Ngorongoro
- Day 6: Ngorongoro Crater
- Day 7: Return to Arusha

**Total Cost:** $2,450-3,850 USD per person

### Luxury Safari ($700-1,500+ USD per person/day)

**Includes:**
- Premium tented camps or luxury lodges
- Private vehicle and guide
- Bush meals, premium drinks
- All park fees
- Additional experiences (balloon safari, walking safaris)

**Example Operators:**
- Four Seasons Safari Lodge
- Singita
- &Beyond Grumeti
- Asilia Africa (premium camps)

**Sample 10-Day Luxury Itinerary:**
- Day 1-2: Tarangire (luxury camp)
- Day 3-5: Central Serengeti (premium camp)
- Day 6-7: Northern Serengeti (migration camp)
- Day 8-9: Ngorongoro Crater Lodge
- Day 10: Arusha departure

**Total Cost:** $7,000-15,000+ USD per person

### Park Fees (2024 Rates)

**Serengeti National Park:**
- Non-resident adults: $82 USD per person/day
- Children (5-15): $27 USD per day
- Vehicle: $40 USD per day
- Concession fees (private areas): Additional $60-100/day

**Ngorongoro Conservation Area:**
- Entry fee: $71 USD per person/day
- Crater service fee: $290 USD per vehicle (mandatory for crater descent)
- 24-hour maximum stay in crater

**Tips:**
- Fees must be paid in USD cash or credit card
- Keep receipts for checkpoint inspections
- Book through operator who handles fees

## Where to Stay

### Central Serengeti (Seronera Valley)

**Best For:** Year-round wildlife, leopards, lions
**Accessibility:** Excellent (airstrip, all-weather roads)

**Luxury:**
- Four Seasons Safari Lodge Serengeti ($1,200+/night)
- Serengeti Serena Safari Lodge ($600-800/night)

**Mid-Range:**
- Seronera Wildlife Lodge ($300-450/night)
- Kubu Kubu Tented Lodge ($400-600/night)

**Budget:**
- Public campsites ($30-50/night + park fees)
- Serengeti Simba Campsite

### Northern Serengeti (Kogatende/Lobo)

**Best For:** Migration crossings (July-October)
**Accessibility:** Seasonal (best June-October)

**Luxury:**
- Singita Mara River Tented Camp ($2,000+/night)
- &Beyond Kichwa Tembo ($1,000-1,500/night)
- Migration Camp ($800-1,200/night)

**Mid-Range:**
- Kati Kati Tented Camp ($400-600/night)
- Mobile migration camps (follow the herds)

### Western Corridor (Grumeti)

**Best For:** Grumeti River crossings (June-July)
**Accessibility:** Good (private concessions)

**Luxury:**
- Singita Grumeti (multiple camps, $2,000+/night)
- &Beyond Grumeti Serengeti Tented Camp

### Southern Serengeti (Ndutu)

**Best For:** Calving season (January-February)
**Accessibility:** Seasonal (best December-March)

**Mid-Range:**
- Ndutu Safari Lodge ($350-500/night)
- Mobile camps (seasonal)

## Health & Safety

### Vaccinations (Required & Recommended)

**Required:**
- **Yellow Fever:** Mandatory if arriving from endemic country (including Kenya transit >12 hours)
  - Get certificate 10 days before travel
  - Valid for life

**Highly Recommended:**
- **Hepatitis A & B:** 2 doses, start 6 months before
- **Typhoid:** Oral or injectable
- **Rabies:** If spending extended time outdoors
- **Meningitis:** Especially December-June
- **Tetanus:** Booster if >10 years

**Consult:** Travel medicine clinic 4-6 weeks before departure

### Malaria Prevention

**Risk:** Present year-round in Serengeti (moderate)

**Prophylaxis Options:**
- Atovaquone-proguanil (Malarone): Daily, start 1-2 days before
- Doxycycline: Daily, start 1-2 days before
- Mefloquine: Weekly, start 2-3 weeks before

**Additional Protection:**
- DEET insect repellent (30-50%)
- Long sleeves/pants at dusk and dawn
- Permethrin-treated clothing
- Mosquito nets (provided by most camps)
- Air-conditioned rooms when possible

### Other Health Considerations

**Traveler's Diarrhea:**
- Common but preventable
- Drink bottled or treated water only
- Avoid ice, raw vegetables, street food
- Bring antibiotics (azithromycin) from doctor

**Sun Protection:**
- Equatorial sun is intense
- SPF 50+ sunscreen
- Wide-brimmed hat
- Sunglasses with UV protection

**Medical Evacuation:**
- **Essential:** Travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage
- AMREF Flying Doctors: Most operators include this
- Nearest hospital: Arusha (Lucy Hospital)
- Serious cases evacuated to Nairobi or Johannesburg

### Safety in the Park

**Wildlife Safety:**
- Never leave vehicle except at designated areas
- Maintain safe distance from animals (guides know limits)
- Do not stand up in open vehicles during game drives
- No feeding wildlife
- Quiet voices near animals

**Camp Safety:**
- Always have escort at night (camps provide this)
- Keep tent/cabin locked
- Do not leave food in tent (attracts hyenas)
- Listen to safety briefings

**General:**
- Tanzania is generally safe for tourists
- Keep valuables secure
- Use hotel safes
- Avoid displaying expensive equipment
- Carry copy of passport

## What to Pack

### Clothing

**Colors:** Neutral, earth tones (khaki, green, brown, beige)
**Avoid:** Blue/black (attracts tsetse flies), bright colors, camouflage (reserved for military)

**Essentials:**
- Lightweight, breathable long sleeves (sun/insect protection)
- Long pants
- Fleece or sweater (mornings/evenings cold)
- Waterproof jacket (rainy season)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Sandals for camp
- Wide-brimmed hat
- Bandana or buff (dust)

**Layering:** Temperatures vary 15-30°C (60-85°F) daily

### Equipment

**Photography:**
- DSLR or mirrorless camera
- Telephoto lens (200-400mm minimum, 600mm ideal)
- Wide-angle lens (landscapes)
- Extra batteries (charge in camps)
- Large memory cards
- Bean bag (for vehicle window stability)
- Camera rain cover

**Other:**
- Binoculars (one pair per person ideal)
- Headlamp or flashlight (with red light setting)
- Power bank/battery pack
- Universal adapter (UK-style plugs, 220V)
- Reusable water bottle
- Daypack for game drives

### Documents

**Required:**
- Passport (valid 6 months beyond travel dates)
- Tanzania visa (available on arrival or e-visa)
- Yellow fever certificate (if applicable)
- Travel insurance documents
- Flight confirmations
- Safari booking confirmations

**Visa Information:**
- Single entry: $50 USD (most nationalities)
- Multiple entry: $100 USD
- US citizens: $100 USD (multiple entry only)
- Apply online: https://visa.immigration.go.tz/
- Or obtain on arrival at airport

### Money

**Currency:** Tanzanian Shilling (TZS), but USD widely accepted
**Exchange:** Better rates in Arusha than airport
**Cards:** Visa/Mastercard accepted at lodges (add 3-5% fee)
**Cash:** Bring USD for tips, small purchases, park fees
**ATMs:** Available in Arusha, not in park

**Tipping Guidelines:**
- Safari guide: $15-25 USD per person/day
- Camp staff: $10-15 USD per person/day
- General tip pool: Many camps have shared system
- Always tip in USD cash

## Booking Tips

**Book Early:**
- Peak season: 6-12 months ahead
- Migration camps: 9-12 months ahead
- Christmas/New Year: 12 months ahead

**Verify Operators:**
- Check TATO membership (Tanzania Association of Tour Operators)
- Read recent reviews (TripAdvisor, SafariBookings)
- Confirm vehicle condition, guide qualifications
- Ask about contingency plans (breakdowns, emergencies)

**Ask About:**
- Vehicle passenger capacity (max 6-7 for comfort)
- Guide experience and language skills
- Meal arrangements (dietary restrictions)
- Included vs. excluded fees
- Cancellation policy
- Travel insurance requirements

**Payment:**
- Deposit: 30-50% to secure booking
- Balance: 30-60 days before travel
- Use secure payment methods
- Get detailed invoice

## Responsible Tourism

**Choose Ethical Operators:**
- Employ local guides and staff
- Support community projects
- Follow park regulations
- Minimize environmental impact

**Respect Wildlife:**
- Maintain safe distances
- No off-road driving (damages ecosystem)
- Quiet voices near animals
- Never feed wildlife

**Support Local:**
- Buy from local markets (curios, crafts)
- Tip fairly
- Respect Maasai culture (ask before photographing)
- Learn basic Swahili greetings

**Environmental Impact:**
- Use reusable water bottles
- Minimize plastic
- Stay on designated tracks
- Take all trash with you
