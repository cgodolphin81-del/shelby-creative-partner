## Getting There

### International Flights
- **Main Gateway:** Christchurch International Airport (CHC) - largest South Island airport
- **Alternative:** Queenstown International (ZQN) - direct flights from Australia
- **From North Island:** Auckland, Wellington to Christchurch/Queenstown (1.5-2 hours)
- **From Australia:** Direct flights to Christchurch, Queenstown from Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Gold Coast

### From Christchurch to South Island Highlights
- **To Queenstown:** 1 hour flight OR 6-hour scenic drive (via SH1, SH8, SH6)
- **To Milford Sound:** 4.5 hours drive + cruise, OR fly Queenstown to Milford (25 min)
- **To Aoraki/Mt Cook:** 3.5 hours drive (via SH1, SH80)
- **To Franz Josef Glacier:** 5 hours drive (via SH6, scenic west coast route)

### From Queenstown (Recommended Base)
- **Milford Sound:** 4 hours drive (287 km) each way, OR 25-min flight
- **Aoraki/Mt Cook:** 3 hours drive (260 km)
- **Franz Josef Glacier:** 5.5 hours drive (560 km) via SH6
- **Doubtful Sound:** 4.5 hours to Manapouri + cruise

**Recommendation:** Fly into Christchurch, out of Queenstown (or vice versa) for one-way itinerary

## Road Trips

### Queenstown to Milford Sound (The Classic)
**Distance:** 287 km (178 miles)
**Time:** 4 hours without stops, 5-6 hours recommended
**Route:** State Highway 6 to Te Anau, then SH94 to Milford

**Stops Worth Making:**
1. **Kingston:** Historic town at southern tip of Lake Wakatipu (30 min from Queenstown)
2. **Te Anau:** Gateway to Fiordland, stock up on supplies (2 hrs from Queenstown)
3. **Mirror Lakes:** Perfect reflections on calm days (30 min before Milford)
4. **The Chasm:** Short walk to dramatic waterfall (20 min before Milford)
5. **Multiple Viewpoints:** Countless pullouts along SH94

**Important Notes:**
- SH94 has long sections with no cell service
- Limited fuel availability (fill up in Te Anau)
- Winter driving: chains may be required June-August
- Tunnel restrictions: oversize vehicles must use convoy system
- Parking at Milford: arrives early (before 9 AM) or book cruise with parking

### Christchurch to Queenstown (Inland Route)
**Distance:** 485 km (301 miles)
**Time:** 5.5-6 hours
**Route:** SH1 south, SH8 west, SH6 to Queenstown

**Highlights:**
- Canterbury Plains
- Rakaia Gorge
- Geraldine (cute town, coffee)
- Lake Tekapo (stunning turquoise lake, Church of the Good Shepherd)
- Twizel (gateway to Mt Cook)
- Lindis Pass (dramatic alpine scenery)
- Cromwell (fruit bowls, historic precinct)

**Overnight Option:** Lake Tekapo or Twizel (breaks up journey nicely)

### Queenstown to Franz Josef (West Coast Route)
**Distance:** 560 km (348 miles)
**Time:** 6-7 hours (long but spectacular)
**Route:** SH6 via Crown Range, Wanaka, Haast Pass

**Highlights:**
- Crown Range Road (highest sealed pass in NZ, steep!)
- Wanaka (lakeside town, #ThatWanakaTree)
- Makarora (gateway to Mt Aspiring)
- Haast Pass (UNESCO World Heritage, rainforest)
- Thunder Creek Falls (short walk, powerful falls)
- Lake Matheson (famous reflection walks)
- Fox Glacier (before Franz Josef)

**Recommendation:** Break into 2 days, overnight in Wanaka or Haast

## Adventure Activities by Season

### Summer (December-February)
**Best For:** Hiking, water activities, all-around weather

**Top Activities:**
- Milford Track (Great Walk season)
- Kayaking Milford Sound
- Bungee jumping (all locations open)
- Jet boating
- Mountain biking
- Via ferrata climbing
- White water rafting
- Paragliding

**Considerations:**
- Peak season = highest prices, biggest crowds
- Book accommodation and popular activities months in advance
- UV radiation extreme - sun protection essential
- Sandflies in Fiordland - bring repellent

### Autumn (March-May)
**Best For:** Photography, wine tours, fewer crowds

**Top Activities:**
- Central Otago wine tours (harvest season)
- Scenic drives (autumn colors)
- Hiking (stable weather, fewer people)
- Photography (clear air, golden light)
- Fishing (excellent in autumn)
- Mountain biking

**Considerations:**
- Weather still good, especially March-April
- Some activities reduce frequency after Easter
- Great value - shoulder season pricing
- Layers recommended - temperature varies

### Winter (June-August)
**Best For:** Skiing, snowboarding, winter landscapes

**Top Activities:**
- Skiing The Remarkables, Coronet Peak
- Snowboarding
- Heli-skiing (remote terrain)
- Winter bungy (still operates)
- Hot pools (Onsen Hot Pools, Queenstown)
- Stargazing (clear winter skies)
- Milford Sound cruise (dramatic weather)

**Considerations:**
- Some hiking tracks closed (avalanche risk)
- Milford Road may require chains
- Some activities limited (kayaking, some boat tours)
- Ski season = busy in Queenstown
- Cold but often sunny days

### Spring (September-November)
**Best For:** Waterfalls, wildlife, wildflowers

**Top Activities:**
- Viewing waterfalls (snowmelt = peak flow)
- Wildlife watching (penguins returning, seals with pups)
- Hiking (tracks opening)
- Photography (lambs, flowers)
- Fishing (season opens)
- Scenic flights

**Considerations:**
- Weather variable - can be excellent or stormy
- Some high-altitude tracks still closed
- Great shoulder season value
- Book Great Walks when bookings open (months ahead)

## Best Times to Visit

### For Specific Experiences

**Milford Sound at Its Best:**
- **Waterfalls:** May-August (peak rainfall)
- **Clear Views:** December-February (more stable weather)
- **Fewest Crowds:** June-August (winter)
- **Best Light:** Early morning, late afternoon

**Queenstown Adventures:**
- **All Activities Open:** December-March
- **Skiing:** June-October (best snow July-August)
- **Best Value:** April-May, September-October

**Glacier Viewing:**
- **Most Stable Weather:** December-March
- **Clearest Views:** Winter months (less cloud)
- **Heli-Hikes:** Year-round (weather dependent)

**Wildlife:**
- **Penguins:** October-March (breeding season)
- **Seals:** Year-round (pups born November-January)
- **Dolphins:** Year-round (more common in summer)
- **Birds:** Spring-summer (most active)

**Photography:**
- **Golden Hour:** Year-round (summer has latest sunsets)
- **Clear Air:** Winter months
- **Dramatic Weather:** Autumn-winter
- **Wildflowers:** November-December

## Practical Information

### Visas & Entry
- **Many Countries:** Visa waiver for up to 90 days (USA, UK, EU, Canada, Australia, etc.)
- **NZeTA Required:** Visa waiver travelers need NZeTA (online, ~$50 NZD)
- **Australia:** No visa required, indefinite stay
- **Check:** Always verify current requirements before travel

### Currency & Money
- **Currency:** New Zealand Dollar (NZD)
- **Credit Cards:** Widely accepted (Visa, Mastercard)
- **ATMs:** Readily available in towns
- **Tipping:** Not expected, but appreciated for exceptional service
- **Budget:** South Island is expensive - plan accordingly

### Connectivity
- **Cell Service:** Good in towns, limited in remote areas
- **Fiordland/Milford:** Very limited to no service
- **WiFi:** Available in most accommodations, cafes
- **SIM Cards:** Available at airports (Spark, Vodafone, 2degrees)
- **Download Offline Maps:** Essential for remote driving

### Safety
- **General:** New Zealand is very safe
- **Driving:** Left side of road, mountain roads can be challenging
- **Weather:** Changes rapidly - always check forecasts
- **Hiking:** Register intentions, carry proper gear, check track conditions
- **Water:** Tap water safe to drink everywhere
- **Sun:** Extreme UV - sunscreen, hat, sunglasses essential
- **Sandflies:** Fiordland and west coast - bring repellent

### Driving
- **License:** Valid overseas license acceptable for up to 12 months
- **Side of Road:** Left (same as Australia, UK)
- **Road Conditions:** Generally excellent, but mountain roads can be narrow/winding
- **Fuel:** Available in towns, sparse in remote areas (fill up when you can)
- **Speed Limits:** 100 km/h open road, 50 km/h urban (strictly enforced)
- **Winter:** Chains may be required (carry in trunk June-August)

### Packing List

**Essentials:**
- Waterproof jacket (high quality)
- Layers (fleece, merino)
- Sturdy walking shoes/hiking boots
- Swimwear (hot pools, some activities)
- Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
- Insect repellent (for sandflies)
- Daypack
- Reusable water bottle
- Camera + extra batteries
- Power adapter (Type I, 230V)

**Seasonal:**
- **Summer:** Light clothing, shorts, sandals (but still bring jacket)
- **Winter:** Warm layers, beanie, gloves, thermal underwear
- **Spring/Autumn:** Versatile layers, waterproof shoes

**Activities:**
- **Hiking:** Proper boots, hiking poles, map
- **Water Activities:** Quick-dry clothing, water shoes
- **Adventure:** Closed-toe shoes, secure long hair

## Accommodation Tips

### Booking Strategy
- **Peak Season (Dec-Feb, Jun-Aug):** Book 3-6 months ahead
- **Shoulder Season:** Book 1-2 months ahead
- **Milford Sound:** Very limited - book as early as possible
- **Great Walks:** Book when bookings open (months ahead)

### Types of Accommodation

**Luxury Lodges:** $400-1000+ NZD/night
- Eichardt's (Queenstown)
- The Rees (Queenstown)
- Aoraki Court (Mt Cook)
- Rainforest Retreat (Franz Josef)

**Hotels/Motels:** $150-350 NZD/night
- Wide range in Queenstown, Wanaka, Te Anau
- Good availability in larger towns
- Book ahead for peak periods

**Holiday Parks:** $50-150 NZD/night
- Powered sites for campervans
- Cabins available
- Good facilities (kitchens, bathrooms)
- JUCY, Top 10, Kiwi Holiday Parks

**Hostels:** $35-50 NZD/night (dorm)
- YHA, Base, Nomads
- Good for solo travelers
- Kitchen facilities

**Department of Conservation (DOC) Huts:** $15-70 NZD/night
- On hiking tracks
- Basic facilities
- Book Great Walks in advance
- Standard huts first-come, first-served

## Environmental Responsibility

### Tiaki Promise
New Zealand's care code for visitors:
- Care for land, sea, and nature
- Drive safely and respectfully
- Prepare for all weather and conditions
- Camp and light fires responsibly
- Keep NZ clean and pest-free
- Respect culture and heritage

### Practical Tips
- **Stay on Tracks:** Protect fragile vegetation
- **Take Rubbish:** No bins in many remote areas
- **Biosecurity:** Clean shoes between tracks (prevent kauri dieback spread)
- **Wildlife:** Observe from distance, do not feed
- **Fire:** Check fire restrictions, use designated areas only
- **Water:** Do not contaminate waterways
- **Drones:** Check restrictions (many areas prohibited)

### Conservation Contributions
- **DOC Conservation Fees:** Some tracks require fees
- **Volunteer:** Opportunities available for longer stays
- **Donate:** Supporting local conservation groups
- **Choose Responsible Operators:** Look for eco-certification
