# The Fiords of Fiordland

## Overview

Fiordland, in the southwestern corner of New Zealand's South Island, contains some of the most spectacular fiord scenery on Earth. With 14 true fiords (more than anywhere else globally), this UNESCO World Heritage landscape represents the finest example of glacial fiord formation anywhere on the planet.

## What is a Fiord?

### Definition
- **Fiord vs. Fjord:** "Fiord" is the New Zealand spelling, "fjord" is Norwegian
- **Formation:** Glacial valleys flooded by rising sea levels after ice ages
- **Characteristics:** Steep cliffs, deep water, U-shaped valleys
- **Difference from Sound:** Sounds are river valleys flooded by sea; fiords are glacial valleys

### Formation Process
1. **Glacial Carving:** Ice age glaciers carve deep U-shaped valleys (2 million+ years)
2. **Ice Retreat:** Glaciers melt and retreat (last ice age ended ~10,000 years ago)
3. **Sea Level Rise:** Ocean floods the carved valleys
4. **Result:** Deep, narrow inlets with steep cliffs on either side

## The 14 Fiords of Fiordland

### Northern Fiords

**1. Milford Sound / Piopiotahi**
- **Length:** 15 km
- **Most Famous:** Yes, most visited
- **Access:** Road, air, boat, Milford Track
- **Features:** Mitre Peak, Stirling Falls, Bowen Falls

**2. Bligh Sound**
- **Length:** 9 km
- **Access:** Boat only (no road)
- **Features:** Remote, pristine, wildlife-rich

**3. George Sound / Te Hapua**
- **Length:** 12 km
- **Access:** Boat only
- **Features:** Large, complex fiord system

**4. Caswell Sound / Taitetimu**
- **Length:** 10 km
- **Access:** Boat only
- **Features:** Doubtful Sound's northern neighbor

**5. Doubtful Sound / Patea**
- **Length:** 35 km (longest fiord)
- **Access:** Boat via Lake Manapouri
- **Features:** Three distinct basins, wilderness, silence

### Central Fiords

**6. Thompson Sound**
- **Length:** 8 km
- **Access:** Boat only
- **Features:** Connects to Doubtful Sound

**7. Breaksea Sound / Te Awa-o-Tū**
- **Length:** 10 km
- **Access:** Boat only
- **Features:** Research station, pristine

**8. Chalky Inlet / Te Kākahu-o-Tamatea**
- **Length:** 12 km
- **Access:** Boat only
- **Features:** Large, complex, rarely visited

**9. Preservation Inlet / Te Rāhui-a-Tamatea**
- **Length:** 16 km (southernmost fiord)
- **Access:** Boat only
- **Features:** Remote, historic gold mining sites

### Southern Fiords

**10. Nancy Sound / Te Ngaru-a-Tamatea**
- **Length:** 7 km

**11. Bauza Island area**

## Milford Sound / Piopiotahi (Detailed)

### Geography
- **Length:** 15 kilometers
- **Maximum Depth:** 291 meters below sea level
- **Cliff Height:** Up to 1,200 meters
- **Water Source:** Rain, waterfalls, ocean

### Key Features
- **Mitre Peak:** 1,692m, iconic pyramid-shaped mountain
- **Stirling Falls:** 146m, boat tours pass beneath
- **Bowen Falls:** 162m, tallest permanent waterfall
- **Lady Bowen Falls:** Another name for Bowen Falls
- **Pembroke Peak:** 1,120m, dramatic backdrop

### Waterfalls
- **Permanent:** Bowen Falls, Stirling Falls
- **Temporary:** 100+ appear after heavy rain
- **Best Flow:** During and after rainfall
- **Source:** Rainwater collected on cliff tops

### Marine Environment
- **Freshwater Layer:** 0-10m, stained dark by tannins
- **Saltwater Layer:** Below 10m, clear ocean water
- **Black Coral:** Grows at 10m (normally 100m+ depth)
- **Species:** Fur seals, dolphins, penguins, diverse fish

## Doubtful Sound / Patea (Detailed)

### Overview
- **Māori Name:** Patea ("place of silence")
- **Length:** 35 kilometers — longest fiord
- **Basins:** Three distinct sections
- **Depth:** Up to 421 meters (deepest fiord)
- **Access:** Only by boat via Lake Manapouri

### Why "Doubtful"?
- **Captain Cook:** Named it "Doubtful Harbour" in 1770
- **Reason:** He doubted it could be safely navigated
- **Later:** Became "Doubtful Sound"
- **Māori Name:** Patea is the original and preferred name

### Three Basins
1. **First Basin:** Near entrance, most accessible
2. **Second Basin:** Narrower, more secluded
3. **Third Basin:** Furthest, most remote

### Features
- **Wilderness:** No roads, minimal development
- **Silence:** One of the quietest places on Earth
- **Wildlife:** Crested penguins, seals, dolphins
- **Waterfalls:** Numerous, especially after rain
- **Rainforest:** Ancient temperate rainforest on cliffs

### Access
- **Journey:** 1 hour bus across Wilmot Pass + 2 hour boat cruise
- **Tours:** Full-day tours from Manapouri/Te Anau
- **Overnight:** Some operators offer overnight cruises
- **Kayaking:** Multi-day expeditions available

### Comparison to Milford
- **Milford:** More dramatic, iconic, accessible, crowded
- **Doubtful:** Larger, wilder, quieter, more remote
- **Choice:** Milford for iconic views, Doubtful for wilderness experience

## Climate & Weather

### Rainfall
- **Annual:** 6,000-9,000mm (among wettest places on Earth)
- **Rainy Days:** 180+ days per year
- **Waterfalls:** Flow varies dramatically with rainfall
- **Best Waterfalls:** During and after heavy rain

### Seasons
- **Summer (Dec-Feb):** Warmest, longest days, most visitors
- **Autumn (Mar-May):** Stable weather, fewer crowds
- **Winter (Jun-Aug):** Dramatic weather, snow on peaks, fewest visitors
- **Spring (Sep-Nov):** Waterfalls at peak, wildlife active

### Weather Characteristics
- **Unpredictable:** Changes rapidly
- **Rain:** Always possible, any time of year
- **Wind:** Can be strong in fiords
- **Cloud:** Often low cloud on mountain peaks

## Wildlife

### Marine Mammals
- **New Zealand Fur Seals:** ~3,000 in Milford Sound alone
- **Bottlenose Dolphins:** Regular visitors, often playful
- **Fiordland Crested Penguins:** Rare, endangered, breed in fiords
- **Little Blue Penguins:** Smallest penguin, return at dusk

### Birds
- **Kea:** Alpine parrot, often seen near Milford
- **Kākā:** Forest parrot
- **Tūī:** Abundant, beautiful song
- **Bellbird:** Melodious call
- **Kererū:** Native pigeon
- **Morepork:** Native owl (nocturnal)

### Fish & Marine Life
- **Black Coral:** Deep-water species in shallow water
- **Red Cod:** Common
- **Blue Cod:** Prized by anglers
- **Various Species:** 100+ fish species
- **Crustaceans:** Crayfish, various crabs

### Forest Wildlife
- **Deer:** Introduced, common in surrounding forest
- **Chamois:** Introduced mountain goat
- **Stoats, Rats:** Introduced predators (conservation threat)
- **Bats:** Native long-tailed bat (rare)

## Māori History & Significance

### Tamatea
- **Legendary Explorer:** Tamatea-pōkai-whenua traveled the fiords
- **Names:** Many fiords named after his actions
- **Significance:** Important ancestral figure for Ngāi Tahu

### Place Names
- **Piopiotahi:** Milford Sound (single thrush)
- **Patea:** Doubtful Sound (place of silence)
- **Te Hapua:** George Sound
- **Taitetimu:** Caswell Sound
- **Many Others:** Rich Māori naming tradition

### Traditional Use
- **Seasonal Visits:** Māori visited for resources
- **Resources:** Fish, birds, greenstone (pounamu)
- **Travel:** Waka (canoes) through fiords
- **No Permanent Settlement:** Too harsh for year-round living

### Ngāi Tahu Today
- **Mana Whenua:** Authority over the land
- **Partnership:** Co-management with DOC
- **Cultural Tours:** Available through operators
- **Place Names:** Increasing use of original names

## Tourism & Access

### Getting to Fiordland
- **Nearest Towns:** Te Anau (gateway), Manapouri
- **From Queenstown:** 2 hours to Te Anau, 4 hours to Milford
- **Flights:** Queenstown to Milford (25 minutes)
- **Cruise Ships:** Some visit Milford Sound

### Milford Sound Access
- **Road:** State Highway 94 from Te Anau (120 km, 2 hours)
- **Scenic Drive:** One of the world's great road trips
- **Air:** Scenic flights from Queenstown, Te Anau
- **Bus:** Coach tours from Queenstown, Te Anau
- **Boat:** Cruise boats, kayaks

### Doubtful Sound Access
- **No Road Access:** Only by boat
- **Journey:** Bus across Wilmot Pass + boat cruise
- **Tours:** Full-day from Manapouri/Te Anau
- **Overnight:** Some operators offer overnight experiences

### Activities
- **Cruise Boats:** 2-hour tours, multiple daily
- **Kayaking:** Single-day and multi-day expeditions
- **Scenic Flights:** Helicopter, fixed-wing
- **Underwater Observatory:** Milford Sound
- **Hiking:** Milford Track, day walks
- **Fishing:** Guided trips available

### Visitor Management
- **Milford Sound:** ~1.2 million visitors annually
- **Doubtful Sound:** ~100,000 visitors (more remote)
- **Peak Season:** December-February
- **Conservation:** Visitor numbers managed to protect environment

## Conservation

### Protected Status
- **Fiordland National Park:** Established 1952
- **Te Wāhipounamu:** UNESCO World Heritage Site (1990)
- **Marine Reserves:** Portions protected
- **Management:** Department of Conservation (DOC)

### Threats
- **Overtourism:** Especially at Milford Sound
- **Invasive Species:** Rats, stoats, possums, deer
- **Climate Change:** Affecting ecosystems, water temperatures
- **Water Quality:** Runoff, boat traffic

### Conservation Efforts
- **Predator Control:** Intensive trapping programs
- **Fiordland Marine Guardians:** Community-led protection
- **Sustainable Tourism:** Guidelines for operators
- **Research:** Ongoing monitoring of ecosystems

## Best Times to Visit

### For Photography
- **Best Light:** Sunrise, sunset
- **Dramatic:** Stormy weather (if safe)
- **Reflections:** Calm mornings
- **Waterfalls:** During/after rain

### For Wildlife
- **Seals:** Year-round, most active morning/evening
- **Dolphins:** Year-round, more common in summer
- **Penguins:** October-March (breeding season)
- **Birds:** Year-round, most active morning

### For Crowds
- **Least Crowded:** Winter (June-August)
- **Most Crowded:** Summer (December-February)
- **Best Balance:** Autumn (March-May), Spring (September-November)

### For Weather
- **Most Stable:** Summer (but still rainy)
- **Most Dramatic:** Winter (snow, storms)
- **Best Waterfalls:** Any rainy day

## Interesting Facts

1. Fiordland has 14 fiords — more than anywhere else on Earth
2. Milford Sound receives more rainfall than almost any inhabited place
3. Doubtful Sound is longer than Milford (35km vs 15km) but receives far fewer visitors
4. Black coral grows at just 10m depth in the fiords (normally 100m+ elsewhere)
5. The Milford Track is called "the finest walk in the world"
6. Captain Cook only visited one fiord (Doubtful Sound) and didn't enter it
7. No permanent human population lives in the fiords
8. The freshwater layer in fiords is stained dark by tannins from forest
9. Fiordland is part of Te Wāhipounamu UNESCO World Heritage Site
10. Over 100 temporary waterfalls appear in Milford Sound after heavy rain
