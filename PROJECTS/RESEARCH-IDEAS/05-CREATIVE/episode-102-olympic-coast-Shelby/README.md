## 📍 Episode Overview

There is a place where the continent gives up. Not in surrender, but in sacred exhaustion—the final breath of solid ground before the great Pacific expanse.

Episode 102 explores 73 miles of rugged Washington coastline where land meets sea in the most dramatic way possible. Through poetic narration, Indigenous history, geological wonder, and personal reflection, this episode asks: What do edges teach us about being temporary? About adaptation? About becoming?

## 🎙️ Production Notes

### Recording Environment:
- Quiet space with minimal reverb
- Consider recording near actual ocean for authenticity
- Layer additional ocean sounds in post-production

### Sound Design Layers:
1. **Base:** Constant gentle ocean ambience (varies by chapter)
2. **Accents:** Waves, gulls, foghorn, campfire, forest birds
3. **Music:** Minimal ambient pads, swelling at transitions
4. **Silence:** Strategic use for impact

### Voice Direction:
- Warm, contemplative, intimate
- Pauses are crucial—let atmosphere breathe
- Chapter transitions should feel like turning pages
- Build emotional arc: wonder → reflection → understanding → peace

## 🌊 Final Note

This episode is about edges—but it's also about what happens when we stand at them. When we let ourselves be temporary. When we accept that we are part of the cycle, not separate from it.

The Olympic Coast will be here long after this episode is released. Long after we're gone. The sea stacks will fall. New ones will form. The tide pools will teem with life, and that life will evolve, and change, and adapt.

This is the promise of the edge: it is always becoming.

And so are we.
