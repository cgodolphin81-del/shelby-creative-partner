## 🛂 Visa & Entry Requirements

### E-Visa (Recommended)
- **Availability:** Online e-visa for 90+ countries
- **Duration:** Up to 90 days, single or multiple entry
- **Cost:** $25 USD (single entry), $50 USD (multiple entry)
- **Processing Time:** 3-5 business days (apply 1 week before travel)
- **Website:** evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn (official government site)
- **Requirements:**
  - Passport valid 6+ months beyond entry date
  - Digital passport photo (4x6cm, white background)
  - Email address for delivery
  - Credit/debit card for payment

### Visa on Arrival (Alternative)
- **Availability:** Major airports only (not land borders)
- **Requires:** Pre-approval letter from visa agency
- **Cost:** $25-50 USD stamping fee + agency fee ($15-25)
- **Processing:** 2-3 business days for approval letter
- **Best For:** Last-minute travel, multiple entries

### Visa Exemptions
- **ASEAN Countries:** 14-30 days depending on nationality
- **Select European Countries:** 15 days (UK, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, etc.)
- **Check:** Always verify current exemptions before travel

### Entry Tips
- ✅ Print e-visa confirmation (have digital backup)
- ✅ Have hotel address ready for immigration form
- ✅ Declare cash over $5,000 USD
- ✅ No restricted medications (check before packing)
- ⚠️ Passport must have 2 blank pages
- ⚠️ Overstay fines are steep ($500+ USD)

## ✈️ Getting There

### International Flights
**Main Entry Points:**
- **Tân Sơn Nhất (SGN) - Hồ Chí Minh City:** Closest to Mekong Delta (3-4 hours)
- **Nội Bài (HAN) - Hà Nội:** Northern Vietnam, requires domestic connection
- **Đà Nẵng (DAD) - Central Vietnam:** Good for Hội An (Cao Lầu origin)

**Airlines:**
- Vietnam Airlines (full service)
- Vietjet Air (budget)
- Bamboo Airways (mid-range)
- International: Singapore Airlines, Thai Airways, Cathay Pacific, etc.

### From Hồ Chí Minh City to Mekong Delta

**By Bus (Most Common)**
- **Companies:** Phương Trang, Mai Linh, Kumho Samco
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours to Cần Thơ
- **Cost:** $8-12 USD
- **Frequency:** Every 30 minutes, 5 AM - 8 PM
- **Booking:** Online or at bus stations (Miền Tây Station)

**By Private Car/Transfer**
- **Duration:** 2.5-3 hours
- **Cost:** $80-120 USD (one way)
- **Best For:** Groups, comfort, flexibility
- **Booking:** Hotel concierge, Grab app, travel agencies

**By Motorbike (Adventure)**
- **Duration:** 4-5 hours with stops
- **Route:** QL1A or scenic route through rice fields
- **Best For:** Experienced riders, adventurous travelers
- **Caution:** Traffic is intense, license required

**By Boat (Scenic, Slow)**
- **Duration:** 6-8 hours
- **Cost:** $25-40 USD
- **Experience:** See river life up close
- **Availability:** Limited schedules, weather-dependent

### Within the Delta

**Local Buses:** Connect major towns, very cheap ($0.30-1)
**Taxis/Grab:** Available in cities, negotiate price for longer trips
**Motorbike Taxis (Xe Ôm):** Quick trips, negotiate fare first
**Bicycles:** Best for village exploration, many hotels rent
**Boats:** Essential for floating markets, islands, remote areas

## 🍜 Food & Drink Guide

### Must-Try Dishes

**Delta Specialties:**
1. **Hủ Tiếu Nam Vang:** Clear noodle soup with pork and shrimp (Phnom Penh-style)
2. **Cá Kho Tộ:** Caramelized fish in clay pot, sweet-salty
3. **Bún Nước Lèo:** Turmeric fish broth noodle soup, Khmer influence
4. **Lẩu Mắm:** Fermented fish hotpot, acquired taste, very local
5. **Chuối Nướng:** Grilled bananas with coconut cream, dessert

**National Dishes (Available Everywhere):**
1. **Phở:** Beef or chicken noodle soup, breakfast staple
2. **Bánh Mì:** Vietnamese sandwich, any time of day
3. **Cao Lầu:** Hội An specialty, thick noodles with pork
4. **Gỏi Cuốn:** Fresh spring rolls, healthy, delicious
5. **Bún Chả:** Grilled pork with noodles (Hà Nội specialty)

### Street Food Safety

**Green Flags:**
- ✅ High turnover (busy = fresh)
- ✅ Food cooked to order in front of you
- ✅ Local customers (they know what's safe)
- ✅ Clean preparation area
- ✅ Bottled water used

**Red Flags:**
- ❌ Food sitting out in heat for hours
- ❌ No customers (locals avoid it)
- ❌ Questionable water/ice
- ❌ Poor hygiene visible

**Tips:**
- Eat where locals eat
- Avoid raw vegetables in rural areas
- Peel fruits yourself
- Use bottled water for drinking and brushing teeth
- Ice in established venues is generally safe (commercial ice)

### Dietary Restrictions

**Vegetarian/Vegan:**
- Buddhism = many vegetarian options
- Look for "chay" (vegetarian) restaurants
- Buddhist temples serve vegetarian food on certain days
- Communicate clearly: "Tôi ăn chay" (I eat vegetarian)

**Allergies:**
- Learn key phrases: "Tôi dị ứng với..." (I'm allergic to...)
- Peanuts, shellfish common in Vietnamese cuisine
- Fish sauce (nước mắm) in almost everything
- Carry translation card with allergens

## 🏛️ Culture & Etiquette

### Basic Vietnamese Phrases

| Vietnamese | Pronunciation | English |
|------------|---------------|---------|
| Xin chào | sin CHOW | Hello |
| Cảm ơn | gahm UHN | Thank you |
| Xin lỗi | sin LOY | Excuse me / Sorry |
| Bao nhiêu? | bow NYOW? | How much? |
| Đắt quá | dat QWA | Too expensive |
| Tôi muốn... | toy MOON | I want... |
| Tính tiền | teeN teeN | Check, please |
| Ngon quá | ngon QWA | Delicious! |
| Tôi không hiểu | toy khong HIEU | I don't understand |
| Bạn có nói tiếng Anh? | ban co NOY teeN ang? | Do you speak English? |

### Do's ✅

- Greet with slight bow, hands together (namaste-style)
- Remove shoes before entering homes and temples
- Use both hands when giving/receiving (especially with elders)
- Dress modestly at temples (shoulders and knees covered)
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Learn and use basic Vietnamese phrases
- Support local businesses, pay fair prices
- Respect ancestor altars in homes
- Accept tea when offered (hospitality gesture)
- Tip modestly (not expected but appreciated)

### Don'ts ❌

- Touch someone's head (considered sacred)
- Point feet at people or altars (feet are lowest/dirtiest)
- Public displays of affection (conservative culture)
- Lose your temper (losing face is deeply shameful)
- Discuss war politics unless invited
- Photograph military installations or government buildings
- Give money directly to child beggars (encourages exploitation)
- Enter temples during prayer time without observing quietly
- Refuse food/drink without good reason (rude)

### Temple Etiquette

- Dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees)
- Remove shoes before entering
- Speak quietly
- Do not touch Buddha statues or religious objects
- Ask before photographing
- Follow local lead (observe what others do)
- Donations appreciated but not required

## 🌍 Responsible Tourism

### Environmental Considerations

**The Delta is Vulnerable:**
- Sea level rise threatens entire region
- Saltwater intrusion affecting rice fields
- Some areas may be underwater by 2050
- Upstream dams reducing sediment flow

**How to Help:**
- ✅ Choose eco-certified tours and accommodations
- ✅ Minimize plastic use (bring reusable bottle/bag)
- ✅ Don't litter (especially in waterways)
- ✅ Support conservation organizations
- ✅ Respect wildlife (don't feed or disturb)
- ✅ Use reef-safe sunscreen
- ✅ Choose boat tours with responsible operators

### Cultural Respect

**Support Local:**
- ✅ Stay in locally-owned accommodations
- ✅ Eat at local restaurants, not just tourist spots
- ✅ Buy from local artisans and markets
- ✅ Hire local guides (fair wages)
- ✅ Learn about history and culture before visiting

**Avoid:**
- ❌ Poverty tourism (don't treat people as photo ops)
- ❌ Exploitative attractions (animal shows, etc.)
- ❌ Overpaying at tourist traps (research fair prices)
- ❌ Cultural appropriation (wear traditional dress respectfully)

### War History Sensitivity

**Approach with Respect:**
- The war is called "American War" locally
- Listen more than you speak about this topic
- Acknowledge your outsider perspective
- Support museums and memorials that honor victims
- Understand reconciliation is ongoing
- Don't romanticize or trivialize the conflict

**Recommended:**
- War Remnants Museum (Hồ Chí Minh City)
- Reunification Palace (Hồ Chí Minh City)
- Củ Chi Tunnels (day trip)
- Agent Orange Memorial (various locations)

## 🏥 Health & Safety

### Vaccinations (Consult Travel Doctor)

**Recommended:**
- Hepatitis A & B
- Typhoid
- Tetanus (booster if needed)
- Japanese Encephalitis (for extended rural stays)
- Rabies (if spending time outdoors/with animals)
- Malaria prophylaxis (delta is low risk, but discuss with doctor)

**Required:**
- None for Vietnam (yellow fever only if arriving from infected country)

### Health Precautions

**Food/Water:**
- Drink bottled or filtered water
- Avoid ice in questionable establishments
- Eat thoroughly cooked food
- Peel fruits yourself
- Wash hands frequently

**Common Issues:**
- **Traveler's Diarrhea:** Pack Imodium, stay hydrated
- **Heat Exhaustion:** Stay hydrated, limit midday sun
- **Mosquito-Borne Illnesses:** Use repellent, wear long sleeves at dusk
- **Traffic Accidents:** Major risk—be extremely careful crossing streets

### Safety Tips

**Vietnam is Generally Safe:**
- Violent crime against tourists is rare
- Petty theft (pickpocketing, bag snatching) happens in cities
- Scams exist but are usually minor (overcharging, fake tours)

**Precautions:**
- Keep valuables secure (hotel safe for passports, excess cash)
- Use hotel safes
- Be aware of surroundings in crowded areas
- Use reputable taxi companies (Mai Linh, Vinasun) or Grab app
- Don't flash expensive jewelry/electronics
- Keep digital copies of important documents
- Have travel insurance (medical evacuation coverage)

**Emergency Numbers:**
- **Police:** 113
- **Ambulance:** 115
- **Fire:** 114
- **Tourist Police:** Available in major cities

## 📅 Best Time to Visit

### Mekong Delta Seasons

**Dry Season (December - April) - BEST TIME**
- ✅ Less rain, easier travel
- ✅ Cooler temperatures (25-32°C / 77-90°F)
- ✅ Clear skies, good photography
- ✅ Peak tourist season (more crowds, higher prices)
- ⚠️ Book accommodations in advance

**Wet Season (May - November)**
- ✅ Lush green landscapes
- ✅ Fewer tourists, lower prices
- ✅ Floating markets more active (higher water levels)
- ⚠️ Daily afternoon showers (usually brief)
- ⚠️ Some areas may flood (can be interesting or problematic)
- ⚠️ Humidity very high

**Monthly Breakdown:**

| Month | Temp (°C) | Rain | Crowds | Notes |
|-------|-----------|------|--------|-------|
| Jan | 25-32 | Low | High | Best weather |
| Feb | 26-33 | Low | High | Tết (Lunar New Year) - book early |
| Mar | 27-34 | Low | Medium-High | Great time |
| Apr | 28-35 | Low-Med | Medium | Getting hot |
| May | 27-34 | Medium | Medium | Wet season begins |
| Jun | 26-32 | High | Low | Daily showers |
| Jul | 26-32 | High | Low | Lush, green |
| Aug | 26-32 | High | Low | Flood season starts |
| Sep | 26-32 | High | Low | Maximum flooding |
| Oct | 26-32 | High | Low | Floods receding |
| Nov | 26-33 | Medium | Low-Med | Transition to dry |
| Dec | 25-32 | Low | High | Peak season begins |

### Special Events

**Tết (Lunar New Year) - January/February**
- Vietnam's biggest holiday
- Everything closes for 3-5 days
- Book months in advance
- Experience: Unique cultural immersion
- Challenge: Limited services, higher prices

**Ok Om Bok (Khmer Moon Festival) - November**
- Sóc Trăng, Trà Vinh (Khmer Krom areas)
- Boat races, moon offerings
- Unique cultural experience
- Less crowded than Tết

**Mid-Autumn Festival - September/October**
- Children's festival
- Lanterns, mooncakes, lion dances
- Family-oriented, fun to observe

## 📱 Practical Information

### Money

**Currency:** Vietnamese Đồng (VND)
- **Exchange Rate (2026):** ~25,000 VND = 1 USD (check current rate)
- **Bills:** 1,000, 2,000, 5,000, 10,000, 20,000, 50,000, 100,000, 200,000, 500,000₫
- **Tip:** Learn to count zeros (500,000₫ has 5 zeros)

**ATMs:**
- Widely available in cities and towns
- Withdrawal limit: 2-5 million VND per transaction ($80-200)
- Fees: 20,000-50,000₫ per transaction + your bank's fee
- **Banks:** Vietcombank, Techcombank, BIDV, Agribank

**Credit Cards:**
- Accepted at hotels, nicer restaurants, shops in cities
- Not accepted at markets, street food, rural areas
- Visa/Mastercard most common
- Notify your bank before travel

**Cash is King:**
- Always carry cash for markets, street food, small vendors
- Small bills useful for tips, small purchases
- Exchange money at banks or gold shops (better rates than airports)

### Connectivity

**SIM Cards:**
- **Providers:** Viettel (best coverage), Vinaphone, Mobifone
- **Cost:** $5-10 USD for tourist SIM with data
- **Where to Buy:** Airport, phone shops, convenience stores
- **Registration:** Requires passport (vendor handles)

**WiFi:**
- Available at most hotels, cafés, restaurants
- Quality varies (good in cities, spotty in rural areas)
- Free in most establishments

**Useful Apps:**
- **Grab:** Southeast Asian Uber (rides + food delivery)
- **Google Maps:** Navigation, translations
- **Google Translate:** Vietnamese camera translation
- **XE Currency:** Exchange rates
- **Agoda/Booking.com:** Accommodations

### Power & Electronics

**Voltage:** 220V, 50Hz
**Outlets:** Type A (2-pin), Type C (European 2-pin), Type F (Schuko)
**Adapters:** Universal adapter recommended
**Power Banks:** Useful for long days, boat trips

## 🙏 Final Notes

The Mekong Delta is not just a destination. It is a way of life—fluid, adaptive, resilient. The people here have learned to live with water, to farm it, to trade on it, to build their homes upon it. They have survived war, colonization, poverty, and now face the ultimate threat: a rising sea that may one day reclaim the delta entirely.

Visit with respect. Listen more than you speak. Support local businesses. Leave only footprints. Take only photographs (and even then, ask first).

And remember: you are a guest here. The river was here before you. It will be here after you. Treat it kindly.

May your journey be safe. May you be well.
