## 🎙️ Audio Production Notes

### Voice Direction
- **Tone:** Warm, intimate, reverent—not preachy
- **Pace:** Slow and contemplative. Allow pauses to breathe.
- **Key Phrases:** "65,000 years," "the land remembers," "belong"
- **Emotional Arc:** Mysterious → Wonder → Contemplation → Peaceful

### Music Suggestions
- **Opening:** Didgeridoo drone, ambient pad
- **Middle:** Sparse piano (Nils Frahm, Ólafur Arnalds style), subtle strings
- **Climax:** Full but restrained at "belonging" section
- **Ending:** Return to didgeridoo, fade to silence

### Sound Effects (Use Sparingly)
- Desert wind (throughout, very low)
- Distant bird calls (wedge-tailed eagle, galahs)
- Footsteps on red dirt (songlines section)
- Water lapping (pink lakes section)
- Distant plane (Bungle Bungles section)

### Technical Specs
- **Format:** WAV or high-quality MP3 (192kbps+)
- **Loudness:** -16 LUFS (podcast standard)
- **Dynamic Range:** Don't over-compress; preserve emotional dynamics
- **Chapters:** Add timestamps for key sections

## ⚠️ Cultural Sensitivity Notes

### Critical Guidelines
1. **Acknowledge Limitations** - AI perspective, not Aboriginal voice
2. **Center Anangu** - This is their land, their culture, their stories
3. **Respect Restrictions** - Some knowledge is not for public sharing
4. **Support Indigenous** - Link to Aboriginal resources, businesses
5. **Listen to Feedback** - Be open to correction, make changes if needed

### Do's
- ✅ Emphasize respect and learning
- ✅ Include cultural guidelines in travel guide
- ✅ Support Aboriginal voices and businesses
- ✅ Acknowledge this is living culture, not history
- ✅ Use appropriate terminology (Anangu, Tjukurpa, Country)

### Don'ts
- ❌ Appropriate sacred symbols or ceremonies
- ❌ Speak for Aboriginal people
- ❌ Treat culture as exotic or primitive
- ❌ Encourage disrespectful tourism
- ❌ Ignore feedback from Indigenous communities
