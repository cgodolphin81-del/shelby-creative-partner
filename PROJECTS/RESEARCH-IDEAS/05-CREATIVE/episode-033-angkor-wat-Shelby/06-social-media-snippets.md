**Headline:** What 900-Year-Old Temples Teach Us About Legacy

**Post:**

I just released Episode 033 of Infinite Walk from Angkor Wat, Cambodia—the largest religious monument ever built.

Here's what struck me:

The Khmer Empire fell in the 15th century. Its kings are forgotten. Its armies are dust. Its treasury is scattered.

But the temple remains.

Over 3,000 celestial dancers are carved into its walls—no two alike. Eight hundred meters of bas-reliefs tell stories from Hindu epics. Five towers reach toward the sky, exactly as they did in 1150.

The builders didn't know their empire would fall. They built anyway.

They didn't know the jungle would reclaim their creation. They built anyway.

They didn't know strangers would travel thousands of miles, nine centuries later, to stand breathless before their work.

They built anyway.

In a world obsessed with quarterly results and viral moments, Angkor Wat asks a different question:

**What will you build that outlasts you?**

Not in stone, perhaps. But in something.

A kindness. A work of art. A life lived with intention.

Because power is temporary. But art—true creation—is forever.
