# Episode 033: The Ancient Temple
## Location Research — Angkor Wat, Cambodia

---

## Overview

**Angkor Wat** is the largest religious monument in the world, covering 402 acres (162.6 hectares). Built in the early 12th century (1113-1150 CE) by King Suryavarman II, it was originally dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu before transforming into a Buddhist temple. The complex is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and appears on Cambodia's national flag.

**Location:** Siem Reap Province, Cambodia (6 km north of Siem Reap city)
**Coordinates:** 13.4125° N, 103.8670° E

---

## The Temple Complex

### Architecture & Design

- **Style:** Classical Khmer architecture (Angkor Wat style)
- **Layout:** Combines temple-mountain and galleried temple forms
- **Symbolism:** Represents Mount Meru, home of the gods in Hindu/Buddhist cosmology
- **Orientation:** Unusually faces west (suggests funerary purpose)
- **Central Tower:** 65 meters high, accessed by steep staircases
- **Five Towers:** Symbolize the five peaks of Mount Meru
- **Moat:** 200 meters wide, 1.9 km long on each side
- **Outer Wall:** 3.6 km long

### Construction

- **Materials:** Millions of sandstone blocks (average 1.5 tons each)
- **Method:** No modern machinery—likely used levers, rollers, rafts
- **Engineering:** Sophisticated understanding of hydraulics, geology, astronomy
- **Timeline:** Construction began 1122 CE, completed 1150 CE

### Key Structures

1. **Angkor Wat** — The main temple, largest religious monument on Earth
2. **Angkor Thom** — The royal city with Bayon Temple (216 massive stone faces)
3. **Ta Prohm** — "Tomb Raider Temple," jungle trees reclaiming stone
4. **Banteay Srei** — "Jewel of Khmer Art," pink sandstone, intricate carvings (25 km north)
5. **Preah Khan** — Sprawling monastic complex, Buddhist university
6. **Bayon Temple** — 54 towers with 216 serene faces of Avalokiteshvara
7. **Phnom Bakheng** — Hilltop temple, panoramic sunrise/sunset views (70m high)
8. **Terrace of the Elephants** — 300m long, used for public ceremonies
9. **Terrace of the Leper King** — Intricate carvings, secret passageways
10. **Neak Pean** — Small temple on artificial island, ancient hospital
11. **East Mebon** — 10th-century temple surrounded by reservoir
12. **Pre Rup** — Pyramid temple, believed funeral site
13. **Baphuon** — Massive temple-mountain, reconstructed
14. **Banteay Kdei** — Former Buddhist monastery, tranquil
15. **Ta Som** — Similar to Ta Prohm, tree growing atop eastern Gopura
16. **Srah Srang** — "Royal Baths," picturesque reservoir

## Sunrise Views

### The Experience

Watching sunrise at Angkor Wat is described as **spiritual** and **profoundly moving**. The first light illuminates the towers, reflecting in the moat. The atmosphere carries quiet reverence among observers.

### Best Viewing Locations

1. **Northern Edge of Reflecting Pool** — Iconic symmetrical view of five towers reflected in water
2. **Behind Central Sanctuary** — Alternative angle, fewer crowds
3. **Upper Tiers** — Accessible after 7:30 AM, panoramic views
4. **Phnom Bakheng** — Hilltop panoramic view (300-person capacity limit)

### Timing

- **Arrival:** 4:45-5:00 AM for good spots
- **Temple Opens:** 5:00 AM (sunrise access requires arrival by 5:15 AM)
- **Sunrise:** Approximately 5:45 AM
- **Best Months:** November to March (dry season, clearest skies)
- **Astronomical Alignment:** Sun rises directly over central tower during spring/autumn equinoxes

## The Khmer Empire

### Historical Context

- **Time Period:** 9th to 15th centuries CE
- **Territory:** Encompassed much of Southeast Asia
- **Capital:** Angkor (Yaśodharapura) — "City of Temples" or "Capital of Water"
- **Population:** Over 100,000 people in 112 historic settlements (still inhabited today)
- **Legacy:** Rich heritage of brick and stone cult structures

### King Suryavarman II

- **Reign:** Early 12th century
- **Achievement:** Commissioned Angkor Wat as state temple and mausoleum
- **Religious Devotion:** Dedicated to Vishnu (Hindu)

### King Jayavarman VII

- **Reign:** Late 12th century
- **Achievement:** Built Angkor Thom, Bayon, Ta Prohm, Preah Khan
- **Religious Devotion:** Mahayana Buddhist
- **Legacy:** Most prolific builder of Angkor monuments

### Religion & Transformation

- **Original:** Hindu (Vishnu)
- **Transformation:** After Cham sacking in 1177 CE, gradually converted to Buddhist
- **Current:** Active Buddhist worship center to present day
- **Synthesis:** Blends Hindu and Buddhist traditions

### Water Management

The Khmer Empire demonstrated sophisticated hydraulic engineering:
- **Barays:** Massive reservoirs (many still exist)
- **Canals:** Extensive network for irrigation and transport
- **Moats:** Symbolic and functional water management
- **Legacy:** Still influences modern rice cultivation in the region

## Jungle Reclaiming Stone

### Ta Prohm — The Tree Temple

**Built:** Late 12th century by Jayavarman VII
**Original Name:** Rajavihara (Royal Monastery)
**Purpose:** Buddhist temple and university
**Population:** 12,640 residents, serviced by 79,360 people from 3,140 villages

### The Trees

- **Silk-Cotton Trees** (Ceiba pentandra) — Khmer: ដើមស្ពង់
- **Strangler Figs** (Ficus gibbosa)
- **Growth Pattern:** Roots coil around sandstone structures, weave through crumbling galleries
- **Effect:** Both destructive (cracking walls, shifting foundations) and supportive (holding structures together)

### Conservation Philosophy

French archaeologists in the early 20th century made a deliberate decision to **leave Ta Prohm partially overgrown** to preserve the site as it was found. This allows visitors to witness the merger of jungle and ruins over centuries of abandonment.

**Modern Conservation:**
- Archaeological Survey of India works with APSARA National Authority and UNESCO
- Balance between restoration and preservation
- Careful decisions on which roots to leave for structural support
- Gentle removal of trees threatening collapse
- Maintaining the "wild magic" of the site

### Other Overgrown Temples

- **Beng Mealea:** Largely unrestored, maze of toppled stones and tangled roots
- **Preah Khan:** Jungle trees intertwining with temple gates, moss-covered carvings
- **Ta Nei:** Tree roots growing over ruins
- **Ta Som:** Notable tree growing atop eastern Gopura

### The Phenomenon

By the 15th century, after the fall of Khmer rule, the temples were abandoned. The jungle moved in. Thick tree roots caused significant damage, pushing through walls and walkways. This **synergy between ruins and forest** creates a haunting atmosphere, showcasing nature's power to both destroy and create, revealing the impermanence of human monuments.

## Apsara Carvings & Bas-Reliefs

### Apsaras (Devatas)

- **Count:** Over 1,796-3,000 documented depictions
- **Description:** Celestial dancers and deities
- **Uniqueness:** Each carved with different hair, headdresses, garments, stance, jewelry, flowers
- **Symbolism:** Based on actual practices of the Angkor period
- **Placement:** Adorn walls, doorways, columns at every level
- **Meaning:** Represent grace, beauty, divine presence; importance of dance and art in worship

### Bas-Relief Galleries

**Total Length:** Over 800 meters of intricate bas-reliefs

**Major Scenes:**

1. **West Gallery, South:** Battle of Kurukshetra (Mahabharata)
2. **West Gallery, North:** Battle of Lanka (Ramayana)
3. **South Gallery, West:** Procession of King Suryavarman II (historical)
4. **South Gallery, East:** Heavens and Hells (Puranas)
5. **East Gallery, South:** Churning of the Sea of Milk (Samudra Manthana)
6. **East Gallery, North:** Vishnu's Victory over Asuras
7. **North Gallery, East:** Krishna's Victory over Banasura
8. **North Gallery, West:** Battle between Gods and Asuras

### Symbolism

- **Mount Meru:** Central tower represents home of the gods
- **Five Towers:** Five peaks of Mount Meru
- **Moat:** Surrounding oceans
- **Outer Walls:** Mountains encircling the world
- **West Orientation:** Funerary symbolism (death, sunset)
- **Astronomical Alignments:** Solar and lunar time cycles, equinox alignments

## Cultural Significance

### Living Heritage

- **Inhabitants:** Over 100,000 people live within the park in 112 historic settlements
- **Ancestral Connection:** Many residents' ancestors date back to the Angkor period
- **Practices:** Agriculture (rice cultivation), ancestral traditions, archaic cultural practices
- **Worship:** Veneration of temple deities, ceremonies with prayers, traditional music and dance
- **Medicinal Plants:** Rich variety used by local population for healing

### National Identity

- **Flag:** Angkor Wat appears on Cambodia's national flag
- **Currency:** Featured on Cambodian riel
- **Pride:** Symbol of national identity and cultural achievement
- **Tourism:** Major economic driver for Cambodia

### Conservation Status

- **UNESCO:** World Heritage Site (inscribed 1992)
- **Danger List:** Removed in 2004 after successful conservation
- **Management:** APSARA National Authority
- **International Support:** ICC-Angkor coordinates efforts from multiple countries
- **Challenges:** Tourism pressure, local population growth, vegetation management
