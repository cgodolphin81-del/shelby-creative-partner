# St. Mark's Square (Piazza San Marco)

## Overview
The heart and soul of Venice, St. Mark's Square is the only public square in Venice called "Piazza" (all others are "Campi"). Napoleon called it "the drawing room of Europe."

## Key Landmarks

### St. Mark's Basilica
- **Built:** 828 AD (current structure 1063-1094)
- **Style:** Italo-Byzantine with Gothic and Renaissance elements
- **Features:** 
  - Five domes covered in golden mosaics
  - Pala d'Oro (golden altarpiece with 2,000+ gems)
  - Four bronze horses (originals inside, replicas outside)
  - Floor mosaics worn by 1,000+ years of footsteps

### Doge's Palace (Palazzo Ducale)
- **Built:** 9th century (rebuilt 1340-1420)
- **Style:** Venetian Gothic
- **Features:**
  - Pink and white Istrian stone facade
  - Porta della Carta (main entrance)
  - Chamber of the Great Council (largest room in Europe when built)
  - Bridge of Sighs connection to prisons
  - Secret Itineraries tours available

### Campanile (Bell Tower)
- **Height:** 98.6 meters (323 feet)
- **Built:** 9th century, collapsed 1902, rebuilt 1912
- **Views:** 360° panoramic views of Venice and lagoon
- **Cost:** €10-15 for elevator to top

### Clock Tower (Torre dell'Orologio)
- **Built:** 1496-1499
- **Features:**
  - Astronomical clock with zodiac signs
  - Two bronze Moors striking the bell hourly
  - Lion of St. Mark with book (open/closed based on Doge's reign)

## Practical Information
- **Best times:** Sunrise (6-7 AM) or after 7 PM for fewer crowds
- **Acqua alta:** Square floods first during high water (walkways provided)
- **Cost:** Free to enter square, paid attractions inside buildings
- **Crowds:** Peak 10 AM - 4 PM, especially cruise ship days
- **Cafes:** Historic but expensive (Caffè Florian, Quadri)

## Photography Tips
- Golden hour: Warm light on basilica facade
- Blue hour: Illuminated buildings, fewer people
- Reflections: After rain or acqua alta
- Wide angle needed for full square

## Hidden Details
- Look down: Different colored stones mark ancient canal boundaries
- Look up: Winged Lion of St. Mark appears throughout
- The "linea d'acqua" marks highest flood levels historically
