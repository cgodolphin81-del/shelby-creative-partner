### Post 2: The Vasa Story
```
In 1628, Sweden built the most expensive warship ever constructed.

The Vasa:
• 64 bronze cannons
• 3 gun decks
• Ornate carvings of lions, demons, Roman emperors
• A floating palace of war

On August 10, 1628, the Vasa sailed on its maiden voyage. Thousands watched from the shores. Cannons fired in salute.

Then came a gust of wind.

The ship heeled. Water rushed through the gun ports. It sank, taking 53 people with it.

The Vasa lay underwater for 333 years.

When divers found it in 1956, they found a miracle: 98% preserved. The carvings still showed paint. The bones of the dead still lay in the dark.

Today, the Vasa is Sweden's most visited museum. It teaches millions about ambition, failure, and humility.

Swedes have a word: "lagom" — not too little, not too much, just right.

The Vasa was not lagom. It was too tall, too heavy, too ambitious.

Sometimes, the things we build to show our strength become the things that teach us humility.

### Post 3: Fika Culture
```
It's 3pm. Time for fika. ☕

Fika is the Swedish coffee break. But calling it a "coffee break" is like calling the ocean "a lot of water."

Fika is:
• A ritual
• A pause
• A connection
• A recognition that life is not only about productivity

In Sweden, fika happens 2-3 times daily. You sit down. You take your time (30-60 minutes is normal). You talk. You look out the window.

You do NOT:
• Eat standing up
• Eat while working
• Rush

Fika is not optional. It's not indulgent. It's necessary.

You cannot know someone until you've shared fika with them. You cannot make decisions without fika. You cannot survive the long winter darkness without fika.

In a world that glorifies busyness, fika is revolutionary.

What would happen if you embraced fika today?
