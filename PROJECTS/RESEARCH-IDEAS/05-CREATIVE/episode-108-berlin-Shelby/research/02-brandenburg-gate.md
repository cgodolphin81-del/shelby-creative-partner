# Brandenburg Gate Research

## Historical Significance
- **Built:** 1788-1791
- **Architect:** Carl Gotthard Langhans
- **Style:** Neoclassical, inspired by Athens' Propylaea
- **Height:** 26 meters (85 feet)
- **Columns:** 12 Doric columns (6 on each side)
- **Quadriga:** Chariot statue by Johann Gottfried Schadow

## Symbolic Journey
- **18th century:** Symbol of peace (originally "Gateway of Peace")
- **Nazi era:** Used for propaganda parades
- **Cold War:** Stood in no-man's land, closed to all (1961-1989)
- **1989:** Center of celebrations when Wall fell
- **Today:** Symbol of German reunification and European unity

## Location & Access
- **Address:** Pariser Platz, 10117 Berlin
- **District:** Mitte (central Berlin)
- **Access:** Free, open 24/7, pedestrian zone
- **Best views:** From Pariser Platz side, also from Tiergarten
- **Nearby:** Reichstag, Holocaust Memorial, Tiergarten park

## The Quadriga
- **Description:** Chariot drawn by four horses, driven by Victoria (goddess of victory)
- **History:** Taken to Paris by Napoleon (1806), returned 1814
- **Orientation:** Faces east (toward Berlin center)
- **Symbolism:** Victory of peace (originally), now unity

## Historical Moments
- **1814:** Return from Paris, became symbol of victory over Napoleon
- **1933:** Nazi torchlight parade passed through
- **1963:** JFK's "Ich bin ein Berliner" speech nearby
- **1987:** Reagan's "Tear down this wall!" speech at the Wall nearby
- **1989:** Hundreds of thousands gathered when Wall fell
- **2002:** Euro introduction celebrations

## Photography & Visiting
- **Sunrise:** Fewest crowds, beautiful light
- **Sunset:** Dramatic silhouettes
- **Night:** Illuminated, atmospheric
- **Seasons:** Christmas markets (winter), outdoor cafes (summer)
- **Events:** New Year's Eve, marathons, celebrations

## Cultural Impact
- **On German euro coins:** Featured on 10, 20, 50 cent coins
- **In film:** Appears in countless movies about Berlin/Cold War
- **In art:** Painted, photographed, depicted endlessly
- **In protests:** Site of demonstrations, vigils, celebrations

## Interesting Facts
- Only survived WWII due to luck (surrounded by destroyed buildings)
- Underwent major restoration 2000-2002 (€6 million)
- Gates were closed during Cold War even to East German officials
- Today: Symbol of freedom, unity, and open borders

## Quotes
- "The Brandenburg Gate is not just a monument, it's a mirror of German history." - Historian
- "When I saw it open again in 1989, I wept. It meant we were one people again." - Berlin resident
