## ✈️ Getting There

### By Air

**International Airports:**
- **Paro International Airport (PBH)** - Only international airport
- One of the world's most challenging approaches (mountain valley)
- Only 8 pilots certified to land at Paro

**Airlines:**
- **Drukair** (Royal Bhutan Airlines) - www.drukair.com
- **Bhutan Airlines** - www.bhutanairlines.bt

**Routes:**
| From | Frequency | Flight Time |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Bangkok (BKK) | Daily | 3 hours |
| Delhi (DEL) | Daily | 2.5 hours |
| Kolkata (CCU) | 4x weekly | 1.5 hours |
| Kathmandu (KTM) | 4x weekly | 1 hour |
| Singapore (SIN) | 3x weekly | 4.5 hours |
| Dhaka (DAC) | 3x weekly | 2 hours |
| Gaya (GAY) | Seasonal | 1.5 hours |

**Tips:**
- Book flights well in advance (seats limited)
- Morning flights have better visibility (less cloud cover)
- Sit on left side for mountain views (when flying from Kathmandu/Delhi)
- Flights often delayed due to weather—build in buffer days

### By Land

**Entry Points from India:**
- **Phuentsholing** - Main border crossing (closest to Paro/Thimphu)
- **Gelephu** - Central Bhutan access
- **Samdrup Jongkhar** - Eastern Bhutan
- **Samtse** - Western Bhutan

**Requirements:**
- Indian nationals: Voter ID or passport
- Other nationals: Must fly (land borders restricted for tourists)

**Road Conditions:**
- Mountainous, winding roads
- Landslides possible during monsoon (June-August)
- Travel times longer than distances suggest

## 📋 Visa Requirements

### Who Needs a Visa?

**Visa Required:**
- All tourists EXCEPT citizens of India, Bangladesh, Maldives

**Indian Nationals:**
- No visa required
- Need permit (obtained at border or online)
- Bring voter ID card or passport

### Tourist Visa Process

**Step 1: Book with Licensed Tour Operator**
- All tourists (except India/Bangladesh/Maldives) must book through licensed Bhutanese tour operator
- Cannot apply for visa independently
- Operator handles all arrangements

**Step 2: Pay Tour Package**
- Full payment required before visa processing
- Includes: accommodation, meals, guide, transport, entry fees
- Does NOT include: flights, personal expenses, tips

**Step 3: Visa Processing**
- Operator submits visa application
- Processing time: 5-7 working days
- Visa clearance sent via email
- Print and present at check-in and arrival

**Step 4: Visa Stamping**
- Visa stamped in passport on arrival
- Paro airport or land border
- Bring 2 passport photos

### Costs (2026)

**Sustainable Development Fee (SDF):**
- **Adults:** $100 USD per person per night
- **Children (6-12):** $50 USD per night
- **Children (under 6):** Free

**Visa Fee:**
- $40 USD (one-time)

**Tour Package Rates (per person per night):**
| Season | Budget | Mid-Range | Luxury |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|
| High | $250-300 | $300-400 | $400-800+ |
| Low | $200-250 | $250-350 | $350-600+ |

**High Season:** March-May, September-November
**Low Season:** December-February, June-August

**What's Included:**
- 3-star hotel (budget) to 5-star (luxury)
- All meals
- Licensed guide
- Private transport
- All entry fees and permits
- Camping equipment (for treks)

**What's NOT Included:**
- International flights
- Bhutan visa fee ($40)
- SDF ($100/night)
- Travel insurance
- Tips for guide/driver
- Personal expenses
- Alcoholic beverages
- Laundry

## 📅 Best Time to Visit

### Spring (March-May) ⭐ RECOMMENDED

**Weather:**
- Temperatures: 15-25°C (59-77°F) in valleys
- Clear skies, mild temperatures
- Rhododendrons in bloom (April-May)

**Pros:**
- Best overall weather
- Paro Tshechu festival (usually March/April)
- Flowers in bloom
- Good visibility for mountain views

**Cons:**
- Peak season (more tourists)
- Higher prices
- Book 3-6 months in advance

### Autumn (September-November) ⭐ RECOMMENDED

**Weather:**
- Temperatures: 15-25°C (59-77°F)
- Clear skies after monsoon
- Excellent mountain visibility

**Pros:**
- Best mountain views
- Major festivals (Thimphu Tshechu, Punakha Drubchen)
- Harvest season
- Comfortable temperatures

**Cons:**
- Peak season
- Can be crowded at major sites
- Book well in advance

### Winter (December-February)

**Weather:**
- Temperatures: 0-15°C (32-59°F) in valleys
- Colder at night, can drop below freezing
- Snow at high elevations

**Pros:**
- Fewest tourists
- Clear skies (when not cloudy)
- Lower prices
- Good for cultural sites (not trekking)

**Cons:**
- Cold, especially at night
- Some high passes may be closed
- Shorter daylight hours
- Tiger's Nest hike can be icy

### Monsoon/Summer (June-August)

**Weather:**
- Temperatures: 20-30°C (68-86°F)
- Heavy rainfall, especially July-August
- High humidity

**Pros:**
- Lowest prices
- Fewest tourists
- Lush green landscapes
- Black-necked cranes arrive (November)

**Cons:**
- Frequent rain
- Landslides possible
- Leeches on treks
- Poor mountain visibility
- Some treks closed

## 🗺️ Major Destinations

### Paro Valley

**Highlights:**
- Tiger's Nest Monastery (Paro Taktsang)
- Paro Dzong (Rinpung Dzong)
- National Museum (Ta Dzong)
- Traditional farmhouses
- Kyichu Lhakhang (7th century temple)

**Time Needed:** 3-4 days
**Elevation:** 2,280m (7,480 ft)

**Must Do:**
- Hike to Tiger's Nest (full day, 5-6 hours round trip)
- Visit National Museum
- Explore Paro town
- Attend Paro Tshechu (if timing aligns)

### Thimphu

**Highlights:**
- Buddha Dordenma (51.5m statue)
- Memorial Chorten
- Weekend Market
- Takin Preserve
- Folk Heritage Museum
- Textile Museum

**Time Needed:** 2-3 days
**Elevation:** 2,320m (7,610 ft)

**Must Do:**
- Visit Buddha Dordenma at sunset
- Explore weekend market (Friday-Sunday)
- See takins (national animal)
- Visit textile museum

### Punakha

**Highlights:**
- Punakha Dzong (most beautiful in Bhutan)
- Suspension bridge (longest traditional bridge)
- Chimi Lhakhang (Fertility Temple)
- Rice terraces
- Warmer climate

**Time Needed:** 2-3 days
**Elevation:** 1,200m (3,940 ft)

**Must Do:**
- Tour Punakha Dzong
- Cross suspension bridge
- Hike to Chimi Lhakhang
- Enjoy warmer weather

### Dochula Pass

**Highlights:**
- 108 memorial chortens (Druk Wangyal Khang Zhang)
- Himalayan peak views (on clear days)
- Memorial to fallen soldiers
- Dochula Druk Wangyal Festival (December)

**Time Needed:** Half day stop
**Elevation:** 3,100m (10,170 ft)

**Must Do:**
- Stop for photos (weather permitting)
- Walk among chortens
- Visit temple
- Enjoy mountain views

### Bumthang

**Highlights:**
- Jambay Lhakhang (7th century)
- Kurjey Lhakhang (Guru Rinpoche's body imprint)
- Mebar Tsho (Burning Lake)
- Tamzhing Monastery
- Spiritual heartland

**Time Needed:** 3-4 days
**Elevation:** 2,600m (8,530 ft)

**Must Do:**
- Visit ancient temples
- Attend Jambay Lhakhang Drup (November)
- Explore quieter, less touristy valley
- Learn about Buddhist history

### Gangtey/Phobjikha Valley

**Highlights:**
- Black-necked cranes (October-February)
- Gangtey Monastery
- Pristine glacial valley
- Crane Information Centre
- Nature hikes

**Time Needed:** 2-3 days
**Elevation:** 2,900m (9,510 ft)

**Must Do:**
- Visit during crane season (Nov-Feb)
- Tour Gangtey Monastery
- Nature walk through valley
- Visit Crane Centre

## 🍽️ Food & Drink

### National Dishes

**Ema Datshi:**
- Chilies and cheese (national dish)
- Can be VERY spicy
- Served with red rice
- Must-try experience

**Other Traditional Dishes:**
- **Momos:** Dumplings (meat, cheese, vegetable)
- **Phaksha Paa:** Pork with red chilies
- **Jasha Maru:** Spicy chicken stew
- **Red Rice:** Bhutanese staple, nutty flavor
- **Suja:** Butter tea (salty, acquired taste)
- **Ara:** Local rice wine (homemade, potent)

### Dietary Considerations

**Vegetarian:**
- Widely available
- Bhutanese cuisine has many vegetarian options
- Cheese and vegetables prominent

**Vegan:**
- More challenging (dairy common)
- Can be arranged with advance notice
- Inform tour operator

**Allergies:**
- Inform tour operator in advance
- Carry medication
- Limited Western medical facilities outside Thimphu

### Water Safety

- **Do NOT drink tap water**
- Bottled water provided by tour operators
- Bring reusable bottle with filter
- Boiled water available at hotels

## 🚗 Getting Around

### Within Bhutan

**Private Vehicle:**
- Included in tour package
- Comfortable SUVs or minivans
- Experienced drivers
- Most common method

**Domestic Flights:**
- Limited availability
- Paro to Gelephu (eastern Bhutan)
- Weather dependent
- Book through tour operator

**Trekking:**
- Multi-day treks require permits
- Horses/porters available
- Must go with licensed guide
- Popular: Jomolhari Trek, Laya Gasa, Bumthang Cultural Trek

### Road Conditions

- Mountainous, winding roads
- Well-maintained but slow
- Landslides possible in monsoon
- Motion sickness medication recommended
- Scenic but can be nerve-wracking

## 🏛️ Culture & Etiquette

### Dress Code

**Temples & Dzongs:**
- Shoulders must be covered
- Pants/skirts below knee
- Remove shoes and hats
- No shorts or sleeveless tops

**General:**
- Modest dress appreciated
- Traditional dress (gho/kira) not required for tourists
- Comfortable walking shoes essential

### Photography

**Allowed:**
- Landscapes, architecture (exterior)
- Festivals (generally okay)
- Street scenes (ask permission)

**NOT Allowed:**
- Inside temple shrines (usually)
- Monks without permission
- Military installations
- Royal family (respectful distance)

**Tips:**
- Always ask before photographing people
- Offer small tip if they pose
- No flash in temples
- Drones require special permit

### Behavior & Respect

**Do:**
- Walk clockwise around stupas and temples
- Receive offerings with both hands
- Dress modestly at religious sites
- Ask permission before entering private areas
- Respect local customs

**Don't:**
- Point feet at people or sacred objects
- Touch religious artifacts without permission
- Public displays of affection
- Raise voice or show anger
- Distribute candy/money to children

### Tipping

**Guides:**
- $10-15 per day (good service)
- $20+ per day (exceptional)

**Drivers:**
- $8-12 per day

**Hotel Staff:**
- $2-5 per day (housekeeping)
- As appropriate for porters

**Restaurants:**
- Not expected (service included)
- Small tip for exceptional service

## 🏥 Health & Safety

### Vaccinations

**Recommended:**
- Hepatitis A
- Hepatitis B
- Typhoid
- Tetanus (routine)
- Japanese Encephalitis (for extended stays)

**Required:**
- None (unless arriving from yellow fever country)

**Consult:** Travel clinic 4-6 weeks before departure

### Altitude

**Considerations:**
- Most visits are 2,000-3,000m elevation
- Tiger's Nest: 3,120m
- Dochula Pass: 3,100m
- Altitude sickness possible but uncommon at these elevations

**Prevention:**
- Ascend gradually
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid alcohol first few days
- Rest if feeling unwell

**Symptoms:**
- Headache, nausea, dizziness
- Descend if symptoms worsen

### Medical Facilities

**Thimphu:**
- JDWNRH (Jigme Dorji Wangchuck National Referral Hospital)
- Private clinics available
- Basic to moderate care

**Paro:**
- Paro Hospital
- Limited facilities

**Elsewhere:**
- Very limited
- Serious issues require evacuation to Thailand or India

### Travel Insurance

**MUST HAVE:**
- Emergency medical evacuation coverage
- Minimum $200,000 coverage recommended
- Trip cancellation/interruption
- Lost luggage

**Recommended Providers:**
- World Nomads
- Allianz Travel
- Travel Guard
- IMG Global

## 🎭 Festivals & Events

### Major Festivals (Tshechus)

**Paro Tshechu:**
- When: March/April (varies by lunar calendar)
- Where: Paro
- Highlights: Mask dances, Tiger's Nest festival
- Crowds: Very busy

**Punakha Drubchen & Tshechu:**
- When: February/March
- Where: Punakha
- Highlights: Battle reenactment, most beautiful dzong

**Thimphu Tshechu:**
- When: September/October
- Where: Thimphu
- Highlights: Largest festival, most tourists

**Jambay Lhakhang Drup:**
- When: October/November
- Where: Bumthang
- Highlights: Naked dance (purification ritual), ancient temple

**Black-Necked Crane Festival:**
- When: November
- Where: Gangtey/Phobjikha
- Highlights: Crane conservation, cultural performances

### Planning Around Festivals

**Pros:**
- Unique cultural experience
- Colorful, photogenic
- Meet locals
- Special atmosphere

**Cons:**
- Crowded
- Hotels book out months ahead
- Higher prices
- Roads busy

**Recommendation:** Book 6-12 months in advance for festival travel

## 📱 Practical Information

### Money

**Currency:**
- Bhutanese Ngultrum (BTN)
- Indian Rupee (INR) widely accepted
- USD, EUR can be exchanged

**ATMs:**
- Available in Thimphu, Paro, Punakha
- Limited elsewhere
- Notify bank of travel

**Credit Cards:**
- Accepted at larger hotels, some shops
- Not widely accepted
- Carry cash

**Exchange:**
- Exchange at airport or banks
- Keep exchange receipts (may need for re-conversion)

### Communication

**Phone:**
- Buy local SIM at airport (Bhutan Telecom or TashiCell)
- Requires passport copy
- Good coverage in towns, limited in mountains

**WiFi:**
- Available at most hotels
- Speed varies (slow in rural areas)
- Often free for guests

**Internet:**
- Generally reliable in towns
- Can be slow/unreliable in mountains
- Some social media restrictions (rarely enforced for tourists)

### Time Zone

- **Bhutan Time:** UTC+6
- **No Daylight Saving Time**

### Language

- **Official:** Dzongkha
- **Widely Spoken:** English (educated Bhutanese)
- **Helpful Phrases:**
  - Kuzu zangpo = Hello
  - Kadrin chhe = Thank you
  - Ga de bai = Where is...?
  - Khe pa = How much?

## 🎯 Final Tips

### Before You Go
1. Book 3-6 months in advance (longer for festivals)
2. Verify tour operator is licensed
3. Purchase comprehensive travel insurance
4. Get vaccinations 4-6 weeks before departure
5. Notify bank of travel plans
6. Download offline maps
7. Inform family of itinerary

### On Arrival
1. Keep visa clearance accessible
2. Exchange some currency at airport
3. Buy local SIM card
4. Confirm itinerary with guide
5. Get emergency contacts
6. Note hotel addresses (for taxi returns)

### During Your Trip
1. Respect local customs
2. Stay hydrated
3. Pace yourself (altitude, walking)
4. Try the food (ema datshi!)
5. Be patient (things move slowly)
6. Be present (put camera down sometimes)
7. Remember: you're a guest

### Remember
Bhutan is not a typical tourist destination. It is a living culture, a sacred landscape, and a nation trying to preserve what matters in a rapidly changing world.

Travel with respect. Travel with curiosity. Travel with humility.

And remember the lesson of the Forbidden Kingdom:

*Happiness is not a destination. It is a practice.*
