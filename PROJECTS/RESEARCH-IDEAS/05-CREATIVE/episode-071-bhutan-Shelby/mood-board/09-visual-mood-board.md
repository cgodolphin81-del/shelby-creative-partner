### Color Usage Guidelines

**DO:**
- Use prayer flag colors together (they're meant to be seen as a set)
- Let white space breathe (like mountain peaks in sky)
- Use gold sparingly for sacred moments
- Grade landscapes cool (blues, greys) for majesty
- Warm interiors (golds, reds) for intimacy

**DON'T:**
- Oversaturate (Bhutan is vivid but not garish)
- Use neon or artificial-looking colors
- Make reds too aggressive (they should feel sacred, not alarming)
- Overuse filters (let natural beauty speak)

## 🖼️ Visual Style

### Composition

**Landscape Shots:**
- **Rule of Thirds:** Place Tiger's Nest at intersection points
- **Leading Lines:** Paths, rivers, prayer flag lines drawing eye
- **Scale:** Include human figures to show mountain vastness
- **Layers:** Foreground (flags), midground (valley), background (peaks)

**Portrait Shots:**
- **Eye Level:** For interviews, personal moments
- **Low Angle:** For temples, monuments (sense of awe)
- **High Angle:** For valleys, overview shots
- **Close-Ups:** Details (prayer wheels, hands, faces)

**Framing:**
- **Natural Frames:** Doorways, windows, prayer flag arches
- **Symmetry:** Dzong architecture, temple interiors
- **Negative Space:** Sky, mist, empty valleys (let image breathe)

### Lighting

**Golden Hour:**
- **When:** Sunrise (6-8 AM), sunset (5-7 PM)
- **Use For:** Tiger's Nest exteriors, mountain vistas
- **Mood:** Warm, hopeful, sacred
- **Grade:** Enhance warm tones, soft highlights

**Blue Hour:**
- **When:** Just before sunrise, just after sunset
- **Use For:** Contemplative moments, transitions
- **Mood:** Peaceful, mysterious, calm
- **Grade:** Cool blues, muted tones

**Midday:**
- **When:** 11 AM - 3 PM
- **Use For:** Valley shots, festivals, markets
- **Mood:** Vibrant, active, clear
- **Grade:** Maintain clarity, reduce harsh shadows

**Interior/low Light:**
- **When:** Temple interiors, candlelit scenes
- **Use For:** Sacred moments, meditation
- **Mood:** Intimate, reverent, mysterious
- **Grade:** Warm highlights, deep shadows

### Color Grading

**Overall Look:**
- **Contrast:** Medium-high (dramatic but not harsh)
- **Saturation:** Natural (don't oversaturate)
- **Highlights:** Slightly warm (golden)
- **Shadows:** Slightly cool (blue-grey)
- **Vignette:** Subtle (draw eye to center)

**LUT Recommendations:**
- **Landscapes:** Kodak 2383 emulation (filmic, natural)
- **Interiors:** Warm tungsten balance
- **Festivals:** Slightly increased saturation
- **Contemplative:** Desaturated, cooler

## 🎬 Motion & Animation

### Transitions

**Between Sections:**
- **Slow Fade:** 1-2 second crossfade
- **Dip to Black:** For major section breaks
- **Match Cut:** Similar shapes/movements (flags → flags)

**Within Scenes:**
- **Slow Push-In:** For reveals, emotional moments
- **Slow Pull-Out:** For conclusions, wide context
- **Gentle Pan:** Following movement (pilgrims, flags)

### Animation Style

**Text Animation:**
- **Entrance:** Slow fade + slight rise (0.5-1 second)
- **Exit:** Slow fade (0.5 second)
- **Emphasis:** Subtle scale up (105%, not more)
- **Timing:** Let text sit for 3+ seconds before animating out

**Graphic Elements:**
- **Prayer Flag Animation:** Gentle wave motion (loop)
- **Map Animations:** Slow draw lines, subtle glow
- **Icons:** Minimal, line-based, gentle fade

**DO:**
- Keep animations slow and intentional
- Match pacing to narration (contemplative)
- Use easing (ease-in, ease-out)
- Animate on meaning (not just for motion)

**DON'T:**
- Use flashy transitions (spins, zooms, wipes)
- Animate everything (let some things be still)
- Rush animations (Bhutan is about slowness)
- Overuse effects (subtlety is key)

## 📷 Image Style Guide

### Photography Style

**Landscape Photography:**
- **Orientation:** Mostly horizontal (16:9, 3:2)
- **Depth:** Deep focus (foreground to background sharp)
- **Time:** Golden hour preferred
- **Weather:** Clear skies OR dramatic clouds (not grey nothing)

**Portrait Photography:**
- **Depth of Field:** Shallow (subject isolated)
- **Lighting:** Natural, soft (open shade or window light)
- **Expression:** Contemplative, peaceful (not performative)
- **Context:** Show environment (not just faces)

**Detail Photography:**
- **Focus:** Macro or close-up
- **Subjects:** Prayer wheels, hands, textures, offerings
- **Lighting:** Side light for texture
- **Background:** Blurred, simple

### AI Image Generation Guidelines

**For Midjourney/DALL-E:**
- **Style:** Photorealistic, not illustrated
- **Quality:** High detail, sharp focus
- **Lighting:** Natural, cinematic
- **Composition:** Rule of thirds, leading lines
- **Avoid:** Cartoonish, oversaturated, artificial

## ✅ Do's & Don'ts

### DO:

✅ Show reverence for sacred spaces
✅ Include human scale in landscapes
✅ Use natural light when possible
✅ Let images breathe (white space, sky)
✅ Show modern Bhutan (not just ancient)
✅ Respect cultural accuracy
✅ Maintain series visual consistency
✅ Grade for emotion (not just beauty)
✅ Include prayer flag colors authentically
✅ Show the journey (not just destinations)

### DON'T:

❌ Exoticize or fetishize culture
❌ Use disrespectful imagery (feet pointing at sacred objects, etc.)
❌ Oversaturate or over-grade
❌ Show only "ancient kingdom" (show modernity too)
❌ Perpetuate stereotypes (Shangri-La fantasy only)
❌ Use inaccurate cultural elements
❌ Rush pacing (let moments land)
❌ Ignore accessibility (text size, contrast)
❌ appropriate sacred symbols
❌ Show tourism without context (SDF, responsible travel)
