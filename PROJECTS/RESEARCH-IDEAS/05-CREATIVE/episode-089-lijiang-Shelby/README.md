## EPISODE OVERVIEW

**Title:** Episode 089: The Tea Horse Road - Lijiang & Shangri-La, China

**Logline:** For 1,300 years, caravan porters carried tea bricks weighing 90kg through the mountains of Yunnan, trading for Tibetan horses at 10-15% mortality rate per journey. This is China's forgotten path. The vertical Silk Road. The road that connected worlds.

**Locations:**
- Lijiang Ancient Town (UNESCO World Heritage)
- Jade Dragon Snow Mountain (5,600m sacred peaks)
- Shangri-La (Tibetan culture, Songzanlin Monastery)
- Tiger Leaping Gorge (one of world's deepest)
- Ancient Tea Horse Road caravan paths

**Themes:**
- Ancient trade routes and cultural exchange
- Human endurance and sacrifice
- Memory, legacy, and forgotten history
- Mountains as guardians and barriers
- Connection between worlds
- What remains when the caravans stop

## CULTURAL SENSITIVITY NOTES

### Important Considerations

**Naxi Culture (Lijiang):**
- Respect Dongba heritage and script
- Ask before photographing people
- Understand tourism's impact on authenticity

**Tibetan Culture (Shangri-La):**
- Songzanlin Monastery is active religious site
- Dress modestly, speak quietly
- Spin prayer wheels clockwise only
- Ask before photographing monks

**Tea Horse Road History:**
- Acknowledge the human cost (10-15% mortality)
- Honor the porters whose names are lost
- Avoid romanticizing suffering

**Modern Context:**
- Yunnan sections don't require Tibet permits
- Distinguish between Yunnan and Tibet Autonomous Region
- Be sensitive to political complexities

### Guidelines Followed
✅ Extensive research from multiple sources
✅ Respectful language throughout
✅ Acknowledgment of cultural significance
✅ Practical etiquette guidance included
✅ Avoidance of stereotypes and appropriation
