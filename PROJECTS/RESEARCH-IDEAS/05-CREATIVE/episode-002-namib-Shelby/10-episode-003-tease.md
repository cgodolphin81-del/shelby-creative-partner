## LAKE NATRON: THE BASICS

### Location
- **Country:** Tanzania, East Africa
- **Region:** Great Rift Valley
- **Distance from Namib:** ~2,500 km northeast
- **Nearest Town:** Arusha (gateway to Serengeti)

### The Lake

**Size:**
- Length: ~57 km (35 miles)
- Width: ~22 km (14 miles)
- Depth: Shallow (less than 3 meters / 10 feet)
- Surface Area: ~880 km² (varies with season)

**Water Chemistry:**
- **pH:** 9-11 (highly alkaline — compare: bleach is pH 12)
- **Temperature:** Up to 60°C (140°F) at surface
- **Salinity:** Extremely high (salt and sodium carbonate)
- **Color:** Ranges from pink to red to orange (algae + minerals)

**Why Pink?**
- Microscopic algae (cyanobacteria) thrive in alkaline water
- Algae produce carotenoid pigments (same as carrots)
- Flamingos eat the algae → flamingos turn pink
- Millions of flamingos = pink shoreline

## THE FLAMINGOS

### Lesser Flamingos (*Phoeniconaias minor*)

**Population:**
- 1.5 to 2.5 million birds (varies by season)
- Up to 75% of world's lesser flamingo population
- One of largest bird gatherings on Earth

**Why Here?**
- Few predators can survive the alkaline water
- Abundant food (algae, small crustaceans)
- Isolated, safe breeding ground
- Shallow water perfect for filter-feeding

**Breeding:**
- **Season:** Varies with water levels (usually dry season)
- **Nests:** Mud mounds built on salt flats
- **Eggs:** 1 per pair (high investment)
- **Chicks:** Gray at birth, turn pink with diet
- **Survival:** Harsh — many don't make it

**The Spectacle:**
- Takeoff: Thunderous, coordinated, deafening
- In flight: Pink cloud against blue sky
- Feeding: Heads upside-down, synchronized
- Courtship: Elaborate group dances

## THE PARADOX

### "The Lake That Kills and Creates"

**What Dies in Lake Natron:**
- Animals that fall in are preserved (calcified)
- High alkalinity mummifies remains
- Photographer Nick Brandt's "Across the Ravaged Land" documented this
- Birds, bats, small mammals — turned to stone

**What Thrives in Lake Natron:**
- Lesser flamingos (millions)
- Alkali tilapia (fish that survive in corners with lower pH)
- Cyanobacteria (the algae that colors the water)
- Salt-loving microorganisms (extremophiles)

**The Lesson:**
Life doesn't just survive extremes. Sometimes, it *requires* them.

The very conditions that kill most creatures create a sanctuary for others.

Safe because it's deadly.
