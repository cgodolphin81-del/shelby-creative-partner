## 🖼️ VISUAL STYLE GUIDE

### Photography Style

#### Mood & Atmosphere
- **Dramatic:** High contrast, moody skies, epic scale
- **Atmospheric:** Mist, fog, soft light, ethereal quality
- **Authentic:** Real Ireland, not overly polished or touristy
- **Emotional:** Capture feeling, not just scenery

#### Composition Guidelines
- **Wide angles** for landscapes (show scale and drama)
- **Rule of thirds** for horizon placement
- **Leading lines** (roads, paths, coastlines)
- **Human element** for perspective (small figure, large landscape)
- **Golden hour** preferred (sunrise/sunset)
- **Blue hour** for mystical feel (dusk/dawn)

#### Color Grading
- **Cool tones** for cliffs, ocean, castles
- **Warm tones** for pubs, interiors, sunset
- **Desaturated greens** for moody landscapes
- **Enhanced greens** for vibrant countryside
- **High contrast** for dramatic skies
- **Soft contrast** for misty scenes

---

### Graphic Design Elements

#### Celtic Patterns & Motifs
- **Celtic knots:** Use as borders, dividers, backgrounds
- **Triskelion:** Episode branding, section markers
- **Tree of Life:** Connection themes, heritage sections
- **Claddagh:** Love/loyalty themes, community sections

#### Textures & Overlays
- **Stone texture:** Ancient sites, history sections
- **Parchment/paper:** Poetry, storytelling sections
- **Watercolor wash:** Soft transitions, dreamy sections
- **Fog/mist overlay:** Atmospheric sections, transitions

#### Iconography
- **Simple line icons** for travel guide sections
- **Celtic-inspired icons** for cultural sections
- **Minimalist approach** (avoid clutter)

## 🎯 VISUAL DO'S AND DON'TS

### DO ✅
- Use consistent color palette across all platforms
- Include episode number in all promotional art
- Show scale (human element in landscapes)
- Capture weather as character (rain, mist, storms)
- Use high-contrast thumbnails for YouTube
- Keep text minimal and readable
- Maintain brand consistency with previous episodes
- Use authentic, not overly polished imagery

### DON'T ❌
- Use cliché Irish stereotypes (leprechauns, shamrocks everywhere)
- Overuse green (vary the palette)
- Make text too small (especially on mobile)
- Use low-resolution images
- Over-edit photos (keep it natural)
- Ignore accessibility (contrast ratios for text)
- Inconsistent branding across platforms
- Use copyrighted images without permission

---

## 🌈 ACCESSIBILITY

### Color Contrast
- **Text on background:** Minimum 4.5:1 ratio (WCAG AA)
- **Large text:** Minimum 3:1 ratio
- **Test with:** WebAIM Contrast Checker

### Alt Text
- **Describe images** for screen readers
- **Include context** (not just "cliffs" but "Cliffs of Moher at sunset with dramatic orange sky")
- **Keep it concise** (1-2 sentences)

### Readable Fonts
- **Minimum size:** 16px for body text
- **Line height:** 1.5-1.6 for readability
- **Avoid:** All caps for long text, decorative fonts for body
