## ✈️ GETTING THERE

### International Flights
**Guilin Liangjiang International Airport (KWL)**
- Direct flights from: Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, Seoul, Tokyo
- Connecting flights via: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Hong Kong
- Airport to city: 45 minutes by taxi ($25-35) or airport bus ($5)

## 🌦️ BEST TIME TO VISIT

### Spring (March-May)
**Temperature:** 15-25°C (59-77°F)

**Pros:**
- Comfortable temperatures
- Blooming flowers
- Misty mountains (photogenic)
- Fewer crowds than autumn

**Cons:**
- Occasional rain
- Variable weather
- Some activities may be limited by rain

**Best For:** Photography, hiking, comfortable touring

### Summer (June-August)
**Temperature:** 25-35°C (77-95°F)

**Pros:**
- Lush green landscapes
- Full rivers (good for rafting)
- Long daylight hours
- Rice terraces at greenest

**Cons:**
- Very hot and humid
- Peak tourist season (crowds)
- Heavy rainfall
- Typhoon risk (July-August)

**Best For:** River activities, early morning/evening tours

### Autumn (September-November) ⭐ BEST
**Temperature:** 18-28°C (64-82°F)

**Pros:**
- Best weather (dry, comfortable)
- Clear skies, good visibility
- Golden rice terraces (harvest)
- Pleasant temperatures

**Cons:**
- Peak tourist season
- Higher prices
- Crowded attractions

**Best For:** All activities, photography, festivals

### Winter (December-February)
**Temperature:** 8-15°C (46-59°F)

**Pros:**
- Few tourists
- Lowest prices
- Dramatic mist and fog
- Unique moody atmosphere

**Cons:**
- Cool/cold mornings
- Some activities closed
- Less vibrant colors
- Possible road closures to terraces

**Best For:** Budget travel, moody photography, avoiding crowds

## 🍜 FOOD & DINING

### Guilin Specialties

**Guilin Rice Noodles (桂林米粉)**
- The city's signature dish
- Rice noodles with pork, peanuts, pickled vegetables
- Cost: $2-4
- Best at: Local street stalls, Chongshan Rice Noodles

**Beer Fish (啤酒鱼)**
- Li River fish cooked with beer
- Yangshuo specialty
- Cost: $15-30 per dish
- Best at: Master Beer Fish (Yangshuo)

**Stuffed Snails (田螺酿)**
- Snail meat mixed with pork, stuffed back in shell
- Unique local delicacy
- Cost: $8-15

**Oil Tea (油茶)**
- Traditional Yao minority drink
- Bitter taste, acquired flavor
- Cost: $3-5

### Where to Eat

#### Guilin
- **Chongshan Rice Noodles** - Best Guilin noodles
- **Small Noodle Shop** - Authentic local
- **Guilin Food Street** - Variety of options
- **Zhengyang Pedestrian Street** - Tourist-friendly

#### Yangshuo
- **Master Beer Fish** - Famous beer fish
- **Café West** - Western & Chinese
- **The Local** - Good fusion
- **Street Food Stalls** - Cheap, authentic

#### Longji Terraces
- **Village Guesthouses** - Home-cooked meals
- **Local Restaurants** - Simple, fresh
- **Bring Snacks** - Limited options on trails

### Dietary Considerations
- **Vegetarian:** Possible but challenging; learn key phrases
- **Vegan:** Very difficult outside major cities
- **Gluten-Free:** Soy sauce contains wheat; communicate carefully
- **Allergies:** Carry translation card
- **Water:** Bottled water only (boiled water provided in hotels)

## ⚠️ HEALTH & SAFETY

### General Safety
- Guilin/Yangshuo is very safe for tourists
- Violent crime is rare
- Petty theft exists (watch belongings)
- Scams target tourists (be aware)

### Common Scams
- **"Tea Ceremony" scam** - Invited to tea ceremony, charged hundreds
- **"Art School" scam** - Invited to see student art, pressured to buy
- **Taxi commission** - Drivers take you to shops for commission
- **Fake guides** - Unlicensed guides charging high fees

**Prevention:**
- Book through hotels or official sites
- Agree on prices in advance
- Use ride-hailing apps (Didi)
- Be skeptical of "too good to be true" offers

### Health Considerations
- **Water:** Drink bottled or boiled water only
- **Food:** Generally safe, street food okay if busy
- **Altitude:** Low elevation, no altitude sickness
- **Mosquitoes:** Bring repellent (dengue risk in summer)
- **Sun:** Strong in summer, use sunscreen
- **Pollution:** Can be moderate, sensitive individuals take note

### Medical Facilities
- **Guilin:** Guilin No. 181 Hospital (international department)
- **Yangshuo:** Yangshuo County Hospital (basic care)
- **Pharmacies:** Widely available
- **Emergency:** 120 (ambulance), 110 (police)

### Travel Insurance
**Recommended coverage:**
- Medical evacuation
- Trip cancellation
- Lost luggage
- Adventure activities (if climbing)

## 🎭 CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Basic Etiquette
- **Greetings:** Nod or slight bow; handshakes acceptable
- **Gifts:** Give/receive with both hands
- **Chopsticks:** Don't stick vertically in rice (resembles funeral ritual)
- **Tipping:** Not customary but appreciated in tourist areas
- **Photos:** Ask permission, especially of ethnic minorities

### Language Tips
**Essential Phrases:**
- Hello: Nǐ hǎo (你好)
- Thank you: Xièxiè (谢谢)
- How much?: Duōshǎo qián? (多少钱)
- I don't understand: Wǒ bù dǒng (我不懂)
- Bathroom: Xǐshǒujiān (洗手间)
- Delicious: Hǎo chī (好吃)

**Apps:**
- Pleco (dictionary)
- Google Translate (download Chinese offline)
- WeChat (essential for communication/payment)

### Cultural Sensitivity
- Dress modestly at temples
- Remove shoes when entering homes
- Don't touch people's heads
- Avoid sensitive political topics
- Respect ethnic minority traditions

## 💳 MONEY & PAYMENT

### Currency
- **Chinese Yuan (CNY/RMB)**
- **Exchange Rate:** ~7.2 CNY = 1 USD (varies)
- **ATMs:** Widely available (Bank of China, ICBC)
- **Credit Cards:** Accepted at hotels, some restaurants
- **Cash:** Still king for small vendors, markets, rural areas

### Mobile Payment (Essential)
**WeChat Pay & Alipay:**
- Dominant payment methods
- Link international credit card
- Accepted everywhere (even street vendors)
- Download and set up BEFORE arriving

### Budget Tips
- Withdraw larger amounts (fewer fees)
- Carry small bills for vendors
- Bargain at markets (not restaurants)
- Street food is cheap and good
- Public transport is very affordable

## 🚨 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Not getting visa in advance** - Apply before travel
2. **Only relying on credit cards** - Cash and mobile payment essential
3. **Visiting only in summer** - Autumn is better
4. **Skipping Yangshuo** - Don't just stay in Guilin
5. **Missing sunrise at Xianggong Hill** - Worth the early wake-up
6. **Not trying local food** - Guilin noodles are amazing
7. **Overpacking** - Laundry available everywhere
8. **Not learning basic Chinese** - A few phrases go far
9. **Ignoring scams** - Be aware but not paranoid
10. **Rushing** - This region deserves slow travel

## ♿ ACCESSIBILITY

### Mobility Challenges
- **Guilin City:** Relatively accessible, paved sidewalks
- **Li River Cruise:** Accessible boats available
- **Yangshuo:** Old town has some cobblestones
- **Rice Terraces:** Not accessible, extensive hiking required
- **Caves:** Reed Flute Cave has stairs, not wheelchair accessible

### Recommendations
- Book accessible hotel rooms in advance
- Use private transport for flexibility
- Focus on Guilin city and Li River cruise
- Yangshuo countryside cycling may be challenging

## 🌱 SUSTAINABLE TRAVEL

### How to Travel Responsibly
- **Support local:** Eat at local restaurants, stay in guesthouses
- **Reduce plastic:** Bring reusable water bottle
- **Respect culture:** Ask before photographing people
- **Stay on trails:** Protect rice terraces and natural areas
- **Wildlife:** Don't feed or disturb animals
- **Cormorant fishermen:** Tip fairly for photos

### Environmental Concerns
- Li River pollution from tourism
- Rice terrace maintenance requires community effort
- Overtourism in peak season
- Choose responsible tour operators
