## ✈️ GETTING THERE

### By Air
**Copenhagen Airport (CPH)**
- One of Europe's major hubs
- 12 km from city center
- **Train:** Most convenient—Øresundståg to Central Station, 15 minutes, ~36 DKK ($5 USD)
- **Metro:** M2 line to city center, 15 minutes, ~36 DKK
- **Taxi:** 20-30 minutes, 250-350 DKK ($35-50 USD)
- **Copenhagen Card:** Includes airport transfer on public transport

### By Train
- **From Germany:** Direct trains from Hamburg (4.5 hours)
- **From Sweden:** Øresund Bridge connection, Malmö 35 minutes, Stockholm 5 hours
- **Central Station:** København H, in city center

### By Ferry
- **From Oslo, Norway:** DFDS Seaways, overnight ferry, 16 hours
- **From Kiel, Germany:** Color Line ferry
- **Terminal:** Oceankaj, connected by bus/metro

### By Car
- **Øresund Bridge:** From Sweden, ~55 EUR one-way
- **Germany:** Easy autobahn access
- **Note:** Parking expensive, cycling/public transport recommended

### Copenhagen Card (HIGHLY RECOMMENDED)
**Includes:** 80+ attractions + unlimited public transport + airport transfer

**2026 Prices:**

| 120 hours | 1,339 DKK (~$210) | 719 DKK (~$113) | FREE |

**Children:** Up to 2 children (ages 3-11) travel FREE with each paying adult

**Where to Buy:** Online (GetYourGuide, Viator) or Copenhagen Card app

**Is It Worth It?** YES, if you plan to visit 3+ attractions. Breaks even quickly with Tivoli (145 DKK) + Canal Tour (150 DKK) + one museum (100+ DKK) + transport.

### Public Transport
**Zones:** Copenhagen is divided into zones; most city travel is zones 1-4

**Tickets:**
- Single ticket (2 zones): 24 DKK with Rejsekort, 36 DKK single-use
- 24-hour pass: 80 DKK
- 72-hour pass: 200 DKK
- **Copenhagen City Pass:** Similar to Copenhagen Card but fewer attractions

**Apps:**
- **DOT Tickets:** Official public transport app
- **Rejsekort:** Reloadable travel card (better rates)
- **Google Maps:** Excellent for routing

**Metro:**
- Runs 24/7
- Driverless, clean, efficient
- Connects airport, central station, major areas

**Buses:**
- Extensive network
- Night buses available
- Accept same tickets as metro

**Harbor Buses:**
- Line 991/992
- Scenic route along harbor
- Included in Copenhagen Card

### Cycling
**The Copenhagen Way:**
- 390+ km of bike lanes
- Safest cycling city in the world
- Locals bike year-round, in all weather

**Rental Options:**
- **Donkey Republic:** App-based, ~100 DKK/day (~$15), discounts for multi-day
- **Swapfiets:** Subscription, ~450 DKK/week, includes maintenance
- **Bycyklen:** Electric city bikes, ~40 DKK/hour, app-based
- **Hotels:** Many offer free/cheap bikes for guests

**Cycling Tips:**
- Stay in bike lanes (don't wander onto sidewalks)
- Use hand signals for turns
- Don't cycle side-by-side in busy lanes
- Use bell to alert pedestrians
- Lock properly (bike theft is common)
- Cycling under influence is illegal

### Walking
- City center is very walkable
- Most attractions within 30-min walk of each other
- Pedestrian-friendly streets
- Strøget: 1.1 km car-free shopping street

## 🍽️ FOOD & DRINK

### Danish Specialties to Try

**Smørrebrød:**
- Open-faced sandwiches on rye bread
- Toppings: herring, roast beef, shrimp, egg, remoulade
- **Where:** Aamanns, Schønnemann, Torvehallerne
- **Price:** 50-120 DKK per piece

**Frikadeller:**
- Danish meatballs
- Served with potatoes, gravy, red cabbage
- **Where:** Traditional restaurants, cafés
- **Price:** 100-180 DKK

**Stegt Flæsk:**
- Fried pork belly with parsley sauce
- Official national dish (since 2014!)
- **Where:** Traditional Danish restaurants
- **Price:** 150-220 DKK

**Wienerbrød:**
- What Danes call "Danish pastries"
- Cinnamon snails, cardamom buns, fruit Danishes
- **Where:** Any bakery (bageri)
- **Price:** 25-45 DKK

**Flødeboller:**
- Chocolate-covered marshmallow treats
- Classic Danish confection
- **Where:** Bakeries, supermarkets
- **Price:** 20-40 DKK for pack

### Where to Eat

**Budget (Under 100 DKK):**
- **Reffen:** Street food market, international, 70-120 DKK
- **Torvehallerne:** Food market, various vendors, 80-150 DKK
- **Pølsevogn:** Sausage stands, classic Danish fast food, 40-80 DKK
- **Supermarkets:** Rema 1000, Netto, Irma for groceries

**Mid-Range (150-300 DKK):**
- **Kødbyen (Meatpacking District):** Trendy restaurants
- **Nørrebro:** Diverse, authentic, good value
- **Warung:** Indonesian, multiple locations, 150-200 DKK

**Fine Dining (500+ DKK):**

### Coffee Culture

- **Coffee, Cappuccino:** 35-45 DKK

### Drinks

## 🎯 TOP ATTRACTIONS

### Must-See (Free with Copenhagen Card)

**1. Tivoli Gardens**
- **Time:** 3-5 hours
- **Cost:** 145 DKK adults (included in Card), rides extra
- **Hours:** Varies by season
- **Tip:** Visit evening for magical lights

**2. Nyhavn**
- **Time:** 1-2 hours
- **Cost:** FREE (canal tours 150 DKK)
- **Hours:** Always open
- **Tip:** Early morning for photos, evening for atmosphere

**3. The Little Mermaid**
- **Time:** 15-30 minutes
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Hours:** 24/7
- **Tip:** Combine with Kastellet fortress walk

**4. Rosenborg Castle**
- **Time:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Cost:** 125 DKK (included in Card)
- **Hours:** 10 AM - 4 PM (varies by season)
- **Tip:** See Crown Jewels, beautiful gardens

**5. Christiansborg Palace**
- **Time:** 2-3 hours
- **Cost:** 175 DKK (included in Card)
- **Hours:** 10 AM - 5 PM
- **Tip:** Free tower access for views

**6. National Museum of Denmark**
- **Time:** 2-3 hours
- **Cost:** FREE (always)
- **Hours:** 10 AM - 5 PM, closed Mondays
- **Tip:** Viking history, Danish culture

**7. Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek**
- **Time:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Cost:** 115 DKK (FREE on Tuesdays)
- **Hours:** 10 AM - 5 PM
- **Tip:** Ancient art, beautiful winter garden

### Other Notable Attractions

**8. Round Tower (Rundetårn)**
- Spiral ramp to 360° views

**9. Amalienborg Palace**
- Royal residence, changing of guard 11:30 AM

**10. Freetown Christiania**
- Alternative community

**11. Designmuseum Danmark**
- Scandinavian design
- 100 DKK (included in Card)

**12. Copenhagen Zoo**
- One of Europe's oldest zoos
- 269 DKK (included in Card)

**13. Canal Tour**
- See city from water

**14. Strøget**

## 📅 BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### Spring (April-May) ⭐ RECOMMENDED
**Pros:**
- Blooming gardens
- Fewer crowds
- Good value
- Tivoli opens for Easter

**Cons:**
- Unpredictable weather
- Some attractions limited hours

**Temperatures:** 8-15°C (46-59°F)

### Summer (June-August)
**Pros:**
- Best weather
- Longest days (17+ hours daylight)
- All attractions open
- Harbor baths open

**Cons:**
- Most expensive
- Most crowded
- Accommodation books out early

**Temperatures:** 18-22°C (64-72°F)

### Autumn (September-October) ⭐ RECOMMENDED
**Pros:**
- Beautiful fall colors
- Fewer tourists
- Better prices
- Hygge season begins

**Cons:**
- Shorter days
- Some outdoor activities close

**Temperatures:** 10-15°C (50-59°F)

### Winter (November-March)
**Pros:**
- Christmas markets (magical!)
- Lowest prices (except Christmas)
- Authentic hygge experience
- Cozy atmosphere

**Cons:**
- Cold, short days
- Some attractions closed
- Limited daylight (4 PM sunset in December)

**Temperatures:** 0-5°C (32-41°F)

**Special Events:**
- **Easter in Tivoli:** March 27 - April 6, 2026
- **Copenhagen Jazz Festival:** July
- **Distortion:** Street party, June
- **Christmas Markets:** Mid-November - December 31

## 🎒 PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money
- **Currency:** Danish Krone (DKK)
- **Cards:** Widely accepted (Visa, Mastercard)
- **Cash:** Rarely needed, but good to have some
- **ATMs:** Widely available, use bank ATMs (avoid Euronet)
- **Tipping:** Not expected, service included. Round up or 10% for exceptional service.

### Language
- **Danish** is official language
- **English** widely spoken (90%+ fluency)
- **German** also common
- Learn: *Tak* (thank you), *Hej* (hello), *Farvel* (goodbye)

### Safety
- **Very safe** city
- Pickpocketing in tourist areas (watch bags in crowded places)
- Bike theft common (lock properly!)
- Emergency: 112
- Police: 114

### Connectivity
- **WiFi:** Widely available (cafés, hotels, public spaces)
- **SIM Cards:** Telenor, Telia, 3 Denmark
- **EU Roaming:** Works if you have EU SIM
- **eSIM:** Airalo, Holafly available

### Weather Prep
- **Layers** essential (weather changes quickly)
- **Waterproof jacket** year-round
- **Comfortable walking shoes**
- **Umbrella** (wind can be strong)
- **Sunglasses** (summer days are long)

### Etiquette
- **Punctuality** valued (be on time)
- **Queuing** respected
- **Cycling rules** strictly followed
- **Photography** in Christiania: Ask first, no photos of Pusher Street
- **Tipping** not expected
- **Shoes off** when entering Danish homes

### Accessibility
- **Excellent** accessibility
- Metro and buses wheelchair accessible
- Most attractions accessible
- Bike trailers for children available
- Service animals welcome

## 🚨 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Eating at Nyhavn restaurants** — Tourist traps, overpriced. Walk 2 blocks inland for better value.

2. **Not getting Copenhagen Card** — If visiting 3+ attractions, it pays for itself.

3. **Expecting Little Mermaid to be big** — She's small. Manage expectations.

4. **Not trying cycling** — You'll miss the authentic Copenhagen experience.

5. **Visiting Tivoli only during day** — Evening lights are magical.

6. **Forgetting to lock bike** — Bike theft is rampant.

7. **Only visiting in summer** — Copenhagen is beautiful year-round (Christmas markets are incredible).

8. **Not embracing hygge** — Slow down. Sit. Enjoy the moment.
