# Vintage Sound Effects Library
## Authentic 1950s-60s Sound Effects for Springbok Radio

**Created for:** Springbok Radio for Mom  
**Goal:** Source and organize period-accurate sound effects  
**Philosophy:** Less is more - suggest, don't overwhelm

---

## SOUND EFFECTS PHILOSOPHY

### Golden Age Radio Principles

**The 10% Rule:**
- 75% Dialogue/Narration
- 15% Music
- **10% Sound Effects** ← This is your budget

**Why So Little?**
- Radio drama lives in the listener's imagination
- Too many SFX distract from the story
- Suggest action, don't recreate it perfectly
- One well-placed sound is worth ten random noises

**Vintage Approach:**
- Manual SFX created live in studio
- Imperfect is authentic (perfect sounds modern)
- Layer 2-3 sounds max per scene
- Let silence breathe

## DIY SFX RECORDING

### Why Record Your Own?
- **Authentic:** Your own kettle, your own doors
- **Free:** No licensing worries
- **Fun:** Part of the creative process
- **Unique:** Sounds no one else has

### Equipment Needed:
- **Microphone:** Any USB mic (Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica ATR2100)
- **Recorder:** Audacity (free) or smartphone
- **Environment:** Quiet room, soft furnishings (reduce echo)

### Recording Tips:
1. **Get Close:** 6-12 inches from sound source
2. **Record Room Tone:** 30 seconds of silence in each location
3. **Multiple Takes:** Record 5-10 versions, choose best
4. **Label Immediately:** "kettle_whistle_take3.wav"
5. **Normalize:** Bring to -6dB peak

### Easy DIY SFX:
- ✅ Kettle whistling (your kitchen)
- ✅ Teacup on saucer (your china)
- ✅ Door open/close (your house)
- ✅ Paper rustling (any paper)
- ✅ Chair scraping (your dining room)
- ✅ Clock ticking (your wall clock)
- ✅ Footsteps (your floors)

### Hard DIY SFX (Better to Download):
- ❌ Steam train (find recording)
- ❌ 1950s car (find recording)
- ❌ Vintage radio static (find recording)
- ❌ Thunder (find recording, safer)

## SFX EDITING TIPS

### In Your Audio Editor (Audacity/Reaper):

#### 1. **Trim Silence**
- Remove dead air before/after sound
- Leave 0.5 seconds of room tone

#### 2. **Fade In/Out**
- 50-100ms fade prevents clicks
- Essential for loopable ambience

#### 3. **EQ for Vintage Sound**
- Cut lows below 100Hz (remove rumble)
- Cut highs above 8kHz (remove modern crispness)
- Boost mids at 1-3kHz (telephone/radio effect)

#### 4. **Layering**
- Don't layer more than 3 sounds
- Each should occupy different frequency range
- Example: Rain (low) + Thunder (mid) + Wind (high)

#### 5. **Volume Balancing**
- Dialogue: 0dB (reference)
- Music: -15 to -20dB under dialogue
- SFX: -10 to -25dB under dialogue
- Ambience: -25 to -30dB (barely audible)

## SFX PLACEMENT GUIDE

### When to Use SFX:

✅ **DO Use SFX For:**
- Scene transitions (door close, train whistle)
- Important actions (phone ringing, letter opening)
- Establishing location (birds for garden, train for journey)
- Emotional emphasis (thunder for drama, silence for tension)
- Period authenticity (rotary phone, vintage car)

❌ **DON'T Use SFX For:**
- Every footstep (distracting)
- Every object movement (overwhelming)
- Dialogue underneath (competes with voices)
- Modern sounds (cars, phones, computers)
- Realistic perfection (suggest, don't recreate)

### SFX Timing:
- **Before dialogue:** Establish scene first
- **During pauses:** Don't talk over important SFX
- **After dialogue:** Let SFX resonate
- **Crossfade:** Smooth transitions between scenes

## FINAL TIPS

1. **Start with ambience** - sets the scene
2. **Add key SFX** - phone, door, letter (story-critical)
3. **Layer carefully** - don't overcrowd
4. **Test with dialogue** - ensure clarity
5. **Get feedback** - does it sound authentic?
6. **Trust your ears** - if it feels right, it is

**Remember:** Springbok Radio succeeded with minimal SFX. Your mother will remember the **stories**, not the sound effects. Use them wisely, sparingly, and with love.
