# Marketing Plan - Springbok Radio for Mom

**Personal Project Distribution Strategy**  
**Note:** This is a personal gift, not a commercial product

---

## Project Positioning

**What:** Vintage radio drama collection (10 episodes)  
**Who:** Charmaine's mother (primary), family members (secondary)  
**Why:** Nostalgic gift celebrating mom's memories of Springbok Radio

---

## Distribution Strategy (Non-Commercial)

### Option 1: Private Spotify Playlist (Recommended)

**Steps:**
1. Create private Spotify account (or use existing)
2. Upload episodes as "podcast" episodes
3. Set playlist to private
4. Share link with mom and family
5. Mom can listen anytime, anywhere

**Pros:** Easy access, familiar platform, mobile-friendly  
**Cons:** Requires audio production first

**Timeline:** 1-2 days after audio is ready

---

### Option 2: Private YouTube Channel

**Steps:**
1. Create unlisted YouTube channel
2. Upload episodes with static images or simple visuals
3. Set all videos to "Unlisted"
4. Share playlist link with family

**Pros:** Free, easy to share, can add visuals  
**Cons:** Less audio-focused, requires video creation

**Timeline:** 2-3 days after audio is ready

---

### Option 3: USB Drive / Physical Gift

**Steps:**
1. Produce all audio files (MP3 format)
2. Create custom USB drive or CD
3. Design simple cover art
4. Package as physical gift

**Pros:** Tangible, personal, no tech required for mom  
**Cons:** Can't update easily, physical item to manage

**Timeline:** 1 week after audio is ready

---

### Option 4: Private Website / Archive

**Steps:**
1. Set up simple password-protected website
2. Upload all episodes
3. Add photos, historical context, family notes
4. Share login with family

**Pros:** Fully customizable, can include photos and stories  
**Cons:** Requires web hosting, more technical

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks

---

## Family Sharing Plan

### Phase 1: Initial Gift (Week 1)
- Present to mom as surprise
- Include printed episode guide
- Show her how to access

### Phase 2: Family Sharing (Week 2-4)
- Share with siblings and close family
- Collect their memories and reactions
- Consider adding their voice messages as bonuses

### Phase 3: Preservation (Ongoing)
- Archive all files in multiple locations
- Consider memorial/heritage documentation
- Preserve for future generations
