# EPISODE 2: "Mrs. Van Der Merwe's Garden"
**Genre:** Gentle Mystery / Community Drama  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Theme:** Community, kindness, neighbors helping neighbors  
**Setting:** Pretoria suburb, 1958  

---

## CHARACTERS

- **ELSIE VAN DER MERWE** (70s) - Widow, tends her famous garden, Afrikaans accent
- **PIETER** (12) - Young boy from next door, curious and helpful
- **MRS. THOMPSON** (50s) - English neighbor, initially suspicious
- **CONSTABLE KRUGER** (40s) - Local policeman, friendly
- **ANNOUNCER**

---

## SOUND EFFECTS NEEDED

- Birds chirping (morning ambience)
- Garden shears snipping
- Watering can pouring
- Bicycle bell and wheels
- Gate creaking
- Footsteps on gravel path
- Teapot and cups
- Police car (distant, vintage)
- Light accordion or piano music

---

## SCRIPT

**[MUSIC: Cheerful accordion theme - 10 seconds, fade under]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Springbok Radio presents... "Mrs. Van Der Merwe's Garden." A tale of suspicion, friendship, and the flowers that brought a neighborhood together.

**[MUSIC: Out]**

**[SFX: Morning birds, garden shears snipping rhythmically]**

**ELSIE:**  
*(humming Afrikaans folk song, then to herself)*  
Ja, ja... you need a little trim, don't you, my pretty roses? There now.

**[SFX: More snipping, then watering can pours]**

**ELSIE:**  
*(contented sigh)*  
My Henry would be proud. This garden... it's our baby, isn't it? Even though he's been gone five years now.

**[SFX: Bicycle bell rings, bicycle brakes squeak]**

**PIETER:**  
*(excited, out of breath)*  
Mevrou Van Der Merwe! Mevrou!

**ELSIE:**  
*(warmly)*  
Ah, Pieter! Good morning, young man. What brings you to my garden on this beautiful Saturday?

**PIETER:**  
I was riding past, and I saw... well, I saw someone last night. In your garden!

**[SFX: Shears stop suddenly]**

**ELSIE:**  
*(concerned)*  
Someone? In my garden? At night?

**PIETER:**  
Ja! I was walking my dog—well, my mother's dog—and I saw a shadow moving between the rose bushes. I thought maybe... maybe a thief?

**ELSIE:**  
*(worried)*  
A thief? But what would anyone steal from an old woman's garden? My roses?

**PIETER:**  
That's what I thought too. But then I remembered—Ouma always says you hide your jewelry in the garden!

**ELSIE:**  
*(laughing)*  
Oh, Pieter! You and your stories. No, my jewelry is safely in the bank. But... thank you for telling me. You're a good boy to look out for your neighbors.

**[SFX: Gate creaks open]**

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
*(stern, English accent)*  
Good morning, Mrs. Van Der Merwe. Pieter, your mother is looking for you.

**PIETER:**  
Yes, Mrs. Thompson. Goodbye, Mevrou!

**[SFX: Bicycle rides away]**

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
*(lowering voice)*  
Mrs. Van Der Merwe, I couldn't help but overhear. Pieter mentioned someone in your garden last night?

**ELSIE:**  
Ja, Mrs. Thompson. It seems we may have had a visitor.

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
*(alarmed)*  
This is serious! I've been saying for months that this neighborhood isn't as safe as it used to be. We should call Constable Kruger immediately!

**ELSIE:**  
*(calmly)*  
Perhaps, perhaps. But first, let me show you something. Come, come.

**[SFX: Footsteps on gravel path]**

**ELSIE:**  
Look here, at my prize roses. The ones I enter in the Pretoria Fair every year. See anything unusual?

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
*(examining)*  
Well... they look magnificent, of course. But I don't see—wait. These tags. They've been... rearranged?

**ELSIE:**  
*(pleased)*  
Exactly! And look at this lavender bush. Someone's been harvesting it. Carefully, respectfully. Not like a thief at all.

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
I don't understand.

**[SFX: Gate opens again, footsteps approach]**

**CONSTABLE KRUGER:**  
*(friendly)*  
Morning, ladies! Pieter stopped by the station. Said there might be trouble here?

**ELSIE:**  
Constable! So good to see you. Come, let me make you some rooibos tea. And I'll explain.

**[SFX: Transition - teacups, teapot pouring]**

**ELSIE:**  
You see, Constable, I've known about our nighttime visitor for three weeks now.

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
*(shocked)*  
Three weeks?! And you didn't say anything?

**ELSIE:**  
Because, my dear Mrs. Thompson, our visitor isn't a criminal. Look at this.

**[SFX: Paper rustling]**

**ELSIE:**  
A note. Left this morning. "Dear Mevrou Van Der Merwe, thank you for the lavender. My wife is ill, and it helps her sleep. I leave money in the bird bath. —A Friend."

**CONSTABLE KRUGER:**  
*(reading)*  
Hmm. Well, that's unusual.

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
*(softening)*  
Oh... oh my. His wife is ill?

**ELSIE:**  
Ja. And you know what I discovered? The money in the bird bath? It's always more than the lavender is worth. This isn't theft, Mrs. Thompson. This is... a quiet arrangement between neighbors.

**CONSTABLE KRUGER:**  
*(thoughtful)*  
You know, there was a family that moved into the old Anderson place three weeks ago. Man works at the mine, wife's been poorly. I drove past yesterday—saw lavender growing by their front door.

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
*(emotional)*  
I... I misjudged. I'm so sorry, Mrs. Van Der Merwe.

**ELSIE:**  
*(kindly)*  
No apology needed, dear. We all jump to conclusions sometimes. That's why I wanted you both to know the truth.

**CONSTABLE KRUGER:**  
So what will you do, Mevrou?

**ELSIE:**  
*(smiling)*  
Tomorrow, I'll leave a bigger bunch of lavender by the gate. With a note: "For your wife. From your neighbor. No payment needed."

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
*(touched)*  
That's... that's very generous, Mrs. Van Der Merwe.

**ELSIE:**  
Henry always said, "Elsie, a garden isn't just for looking at. It's for sharing." Besides... *(pause)* we're neighbors, aren't we? That's what neighbors do.

**[SFX: Birds chirp, gentle breeze]**

**CONSTABLE KRUGER:**  
*(standing)*  
Well, I'll be on my way. Nothing to investigate here except... *(warmly)* the kindness of strangers becoming friends.

**MRS. THOMPSON:**  
Mrs. Van Der Merwe, I'd like to help. I bake excellent scones. Perhaps we could all have tea sometime? You, me, and... well, when our new neighbor is ready to meet everyone.

**ELSIE:**  
*(delighted)*  
I'd like that very much, Mrs. Thompson. Very much indeed.

**[SFX: Garden shears resume, contented humming]**

**ELSIE:**  
*(to herself)*  
There now, my roses. Our neighborhood is blooming again.

**[MUSIC: Accordion theme returns, warm and cheerful]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
"Mrs. Van Der Merwe's Garden" was written for Springbok Radio by [Your Name]. Next week: "The Last Train to Durban." Good afternoon, South Africa.

**[MUSIC: Fade out]**

**[END - 15 minutes]**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Character Voices:**
- Elsie: Warm Afrikaans accent, elderly, wise and kind
- Pieter: Young boy, enthusiastic, respectful
- Mrs. Thompson: English accent, initially stern, then softens
- Constable Kruger: Friendly Afrikaans accent, authoritative but warm

**Cultural Notes:**
- "Mevrou" = Mrs./Madam in Afrikaans
- "Ouma" = Grandma
- "Rooibos" = South African red bush tea
- Reflects 1950s suburban community values

**Music:**
- Light accordion or concertina (Afrikaans folk influence)
- Cheerful, community-oriented theme

**Moral:**
- Don't judge too quickly
- Kindness builds community
- Sharing makes us all richer
