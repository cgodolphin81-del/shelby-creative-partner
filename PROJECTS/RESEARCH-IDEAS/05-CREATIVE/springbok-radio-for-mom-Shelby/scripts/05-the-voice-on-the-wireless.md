# EPISODE 5: "The Voice on the Wireless"
**Genre:** Gentle Supernatural / Memory  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Theme:** Memory, loss, connection beyond time  
**Setting:** Living room, Cape Town, 1961 (evening)  

---

## CHARACTERS

- **SARAH VAN NIEKERK** (55) - Widow, lives alone, misses her husband
- **HENRY'S VOICE** (50s) - Her late husband, warm and loving (possibly supernatural, possibly memory)
- **RADIO ANNOUNCER** (30s) - Springbok Radio broadcaster
- **DORIS** (50s) - Sarah's neighbor and friend
- **ANNOUNCER** (narrator)

---

## SOUND EFFECTS NEEDED

- Radio static and tuning
- Vintage radio dial clicking
- 1950s music (big band or light orchestral)
- Kettle boiling
- Teacup and saucer
- Clock ticking (grandfather clock)
- Rain outside (gentle)
- Wind chimes
- Fire crackling (fireplace)

---

## SCRIPT

**[MUSIC: Mysterious but gentle theme - music box or celesta - 10 seconds, fade under]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Springbok Radio presents... "The Voice on the Wireless." A story of love, memory, and the voices that never truly leave us.

**[MUSIC: Out]**

**[SFX: Evening ambience - clock ticking, fire crackling, rain outside]**

**[SFX: Radio static, dial turning]**

**RADIO ANNOUNCER:**  
*(filtered through radio)*  
...and now, for all you night owls and lonely hearts, Springbok Radio brings you "Melodies in the Moonlight." This is James Peterson, keeping you company through the Cape Town evening...

**[SFX: Radio plays 1950s romantic song briefly, then Sarah turns it down]**

**SARAH:**  
*(to herself, sighing)*  
Another evening alone. Henry would have hated this rain. Always said it made his arthritis act up.

**[SFX: Kettle begins to whistle]**

**SARAH:**  
There we go. Tea time. Just like always.

**[SFX: Kettle lifted, water poured into teapot]**

**SARAH:**  
*(softly)*  
One cup. Though I suppose I could make two... out of habit.

**[SFX: Teacup placed on saucer, chair creaks]**

**[SFX: Radio static increases slightly, then clears]**

**RADIO ANNOUNCER:**  
*(filtered)*  
And now, a dedication. This one comes from... *(slight pause, as if reading)* ...a listener in Observatory who'd like to send a song to his wife, Sarah. For their twenty-fifth anniversary. Even though... *(pause)* ...even though he's watching from somewhere else now. This is "As Time Goes By."

**[SFX: Radio plays "As Time Goes By" or similar romantic song, very quiet underneath]**

**SARAH:**  
*(gasps, startled)*  
What... how...?

**[SFX: Sarah stands quickly, chair scrapes]**

**SARAH:**  
*(whispering)*  
Henry?

**[SFX: Brief silence, then radio volume seems to increase on its own]**

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
*(warm, loving, slightly filtered as if through radio)*  
Hello, my love.

**SARAH:**  
*(breathless)*  
Henry? Is that... am I dreaming?

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
*(gentle laughter)*  
You're not dreaming, Sarah. Or maybe you are. Does it matter?

**SARAH:**  
*(emotional)*  
Where... where are you? How can I hear you?

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
I'm where I've always been. In the music we danced to. In the rain you hate but I loved. In the tea you make too strong. I'm everywhere we built together, my love.

**[SFX: Sarah sits back down, trembling]**

**SARAH:**  
I miss you so much, Henry. Every day. The house is so quiet.

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
I know, darling. I know. But listen to me. I'm not gone. Not really.

**SARAH:**  
*(tearfully)*  
The doctors said you'd suffer. But you didn't, did you? You just... went to sleep.

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
And I woke up somewhere beautiful. No pain. No worry. Just... peace. And from there, I can still see you. Still love you.

**SARAH:**  
*(crying softly)*  
I planted your roses. The ones you loved. They're blooming again.

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
*(warmly)*  
I know. I smell them every morning. Red ones, yes?

**SARAH:**  
*(laughing through tears)*  
Yes. Always red. Your favorite.

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
Our favorite, my love. You know what I want you to do?

**SARAH:**  
What?

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
Live. Really live. Not just... exist. Go to the seaside with Doris. Dance at the community hall. Let young Francois teach you how to use that new television everyone's talking about.

**SARAH:**  
*(uncertain)*  
Would... would that make you unhappy? Me moving on?

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
*(firmly, loving)*  
Sarah van Niekerk, I loved you too much to want you to be lonely. I want you happy. I want you laughing. I want you living the life we planned, even if I'm not there to share it.

**[SFX: Radio music swells slightly]**

**SARAH:**  
*(softly)*  
I don't know how to be without you.

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
You already are, my love. You're the strongest person I know. You've just forgotten. But I haven't.

**[SFX: Door knock]**

**DORIS:**  
*(calling from outside)*  
Sarah? Sarah, are you home? I brought you some scones!

**SARAH:**  
*(to Henry's voice)*  
That's Doris.

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
*(smiling in his voice)*  
Go on. Let her in. Eat the scones. Drink your tea. Live your life, Sarah. I'm not going anywhere.

**SARAH:**  
*(standing)*  
Henry... I love you. I'll always love you.

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
*(fading slightly, but warm)*  
And I love you, my darling. Now... *(playfully)* ...turn up the radio. They're about to play our song.

**[SFX: Sarah turns radio dial, music becomes clearer]**

**RADIO ANNOUNCER:**  
*(filtered)*  
And now, for all the lovers out there, young and old... "Unforgettable."

**[SFX: Music plays - "Unforgettable" or similar]**

**SARAH:**  
*(whispering)*  
Unforgettable... that's what you are, Henry van Niekerk.

**[SFX: Door opens]**

**DORIS:**  
*(entering)*  
Sarah! There you are! I thought you might be lonely on such a rainy—oh, you've been crying. What's wrong?

**SARAH:**  
*(wiping tears, smiling)*  
Nothing, Doris. Nothing at all. I was just... having a conversation with an old friend.

**DORIS:**  
*(concerned)*  
Are you sure?

**SARAH:**  
*(genuinely happy)*  
Yes. More sure than I've been in months. Doris... remember that dance at the community hall next Saturday?

**DORIS:**  
Yes?

**SARAH:**  
*(determined)*  
I'm going. And you're going with me. And we're going to dance until our feet hurt.

**DORIS:**  
*(delighted)*  
Sarah! That's wonderful!

**SARAH:**  
*(looking toward radio)*  
Someone reminded me... life is for living.

**[SFX: Music continues, warm and romantic]**

**HENRY'S VOICE:**  
*(faintly, almost part of the music)*  
That's my girl...

**SARAH:**  
*(softly, to herself)*  
Thank you, Henry. For everything.

**[SFX: She sits, picks up teacup]**

**SARAH:**  
Doris, tell me about your son's new job. I want to hear everything.

**[MUSIC: Swells emotionally, then becomes gentle]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
"The Voice on the Wireless" was written for Springbok Radio by [Your Name]. Next week: "Tea at Four O'Clock." Goodnight, South Africa. Sleep well.

**[MUSIC: Fade out with radio static underneath, then silence]**

**[END - 15 minutes]**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Supernatural Ambiguity:**
- Is Henry's voice real, or Sarah's memory/grief?
- Leave it ambiguous - let listeners decide
- The emotional truth matters more than the literal truth

**Henry's Voice Treatment:**
- Add slight radio filter/ EQ (cut highs, boost mids)
- Gentle reverb to suggest "otherworldly"
- But keep it warm, not spooky

**Emotional Arc:**
- Sarah's loneliness (opening)
- Shock and disbelief (hearing Henry)
- Acceptance and healing (closing)
- Hope for the future

**Music:**
- 1950s romantic standards
- "As Time Goes By" (Casablanca reference)
- "Unforgettable" (Nat King Cole era)
- Use royalty-free versions or similar style

**Cultural Notes:**
- Observatory is a Cape Town suburb
- Reflects 1960s transition (radio to television)
- Community dances were common social events
