# EPISODE 6: "Tea at Four O'Clock"
**Genre:** Romance / Gentle Drama  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Theme:** Elderly love, second chances, courage  
**Setting:** Tea room, Pietermaritzburg, 1962  

---

## CHARACTERS

- **ARTHUR BROWN** (68) - Retired schoolteacher, shy, widowed 5 years
- **GLADYS WINTER** (65) - Retired nurse, warm, widowed 3 years
- **MRS. PATEL** (40s) - Tea room owner, kind matchmaker
- **WALTER** (70) - Arthur's friend, encouraging
- **ANNOUNCER**

---

## SOUND EFFECTS NEEDED

- Tea room ambience (quiet chatter, cups clinking)
- Teapot pouring
- Spoon stirring tea
- Cake plate being set down
- Door bell tinkling
- Napkin unfolding
- Gentle piano music (tea room background)
- Clock chiming 4 PM

---

## SCRIPT

**[MUSIC: Gentle piano tea room music - 10 seconds, fade under]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Springbok Radio presents... "Tea at Four O'Clock." A story proving it's never too late for love.

**[MUSIC: Continues softly underneath]**

**[SFX: Tea room ambience - gentle chatter, cups and saucers]**

**MRS. PATEL:**  
*(warm, welcoming)*  
Right this way, Mr. Brown. Your usual table by the window?

**ARTHUR:**  
*(nervous, shy)*  
Yes, thank you, Mrs. Patel. Though... actually, I was wondering if... that is, if it's not too much trouble...

**MRS. PATEL:**  
*(knowing)*  
If you could sit at the table for two instead? The one in the corner?

**ARTHUR:**  
*(embarrassed)*  
How did you...?

**MRS. PATEL:**  
*(laughing)*  
Mr. Brown, I've owned this tea room for fifteen years. I've seen every kind of customer. And I know a man in love when I see one. Even if he hasn't told the lady yet.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(flustered)*  
I'm not... that is... we're just... having tea.

**MRS. PATEL:**  
*(gently)*  
Of course you are. Tea at four o'clock. Very proper. Very... innocent.

**[SFX: Door bell tinkles]**

**MRS. PATEL:**  
*(brightly)*  
Ah! Speak of the angel! Mrs. Winter! Over here, dear!

**GLADYS:**  
*(warm, approaching)*  
Hello, Mrs. Patel. Hello, Arthur. You're already here!

**ARTHUR:**  
*(standing, nervous)*  
Gladys! Yes, I... I wanted to make sure I got the good table. The one with the... the view.

**GLADYS:**  
*(sitting)*  
The view of the rose bush? Yes, it is lovely. Mrs. Patel's roses are magnificent this year.

**MRS. PATEL:**  
I'll leave you two to your tea. And Arthur? *(whispering)* Just be yourself. She already likes you.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(whispering back)*  
How do you know that?

**MRS. PATEL:**  
Because she's been coming here every Thursday for a month. Always asking, "Is Mr. Brown here yet?"

**[SFX: Mrs. Patel walks away, amused chuckle]**

**GLADYS:**  
*(lightly)*  
What was that about?

**ARTHUR:**  
*(flustered)*  
Nothing! Nothing at all. Tea? I mean, shall I order tea?

**GLADYS:**  
*(laughing)*  
Arthur, relax. We're just having tea. Two friends. That's all.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(disappointed but hiding it)*  
Yes. Of course. Just friends.

**[SFX: Teapot pouring, cups filling]**

**GLADYS:**  
Though... I must admit, I've been looking forward to these Thursdays.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(hopeful)*  
You have?

**GLADYS:**  
Mmm. After Harold passed, I thought... I thought my social life was over. Nurses don't make many friends outside the hospital, you know? Everyone's always working shifts.

**ARTHUR:**  
I understand. After Mary died, I... I stopped going out altogether. Walter had to practically drag me to the senior center.

**GLADYS:**  
Walter's a good friend.

**ARTHUR:**  
He is. Though he's been rather... insistent lately. About me "getting back out there."

**GLADYS:**  
*(amused)*  
Is that what he calls it?

**ARTHUR:**  
*(embarrassed)*  
His words, not mine.

**[SFX: Clock chimes four times]**

**GLADYS:**  
Four o'clock. Right on time. You know, Arthur, Harold and I had a tradition. Tea at four o'clock, every day. No matter what.

**ARTHUR:**  
Mary and I did the same. She called it "our daily date."

**GLADYS:**  
*(softly)*  
Do you ever talk to her? Mary?

**ARTHUR:**  
*(pause, thoughtful)*  
Sometimes. When I'm in the garden. I tell her about the roses. About the books I'm reading. About... *(pause)* ...about a lovely woman I've met at the tea room.

**[SFX: Gladys's teacup clinks slightly]**

**GLADYS:**  
*(quietly)*  
What does she say?

**ARTHUR:**  
*(smiling)*  
She says... "Arthur, don't be a fool. Ask her to dance."

**GLADYS:**  
*(surprised)*  
Dance?

**ARTHUR:**  
There's a dance at the community center. Next Saturday. Walter bought me a ticket. Said I was going, whether I liked it or not.

**GLADYS:**  
*(lightly)*  
Walter sounds like a man who gets results.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(gathering courage)*  
Gladys... would you... that is, if you're not busy... would you consider... going with me? To the dance?

**[SFX: Brief pause, tea room ambience continues softly]**

**GLADYS:**  
*(softly)*  
Arthur... I'd like that very much.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(relieved, happy)*  
You would? Oh, that's... that's wonderful!

**GLADYS:**  
*(warmly)*  
But on one condition.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(alarmed)*  
Condition?

**GLADYS:**  
*(laughing)*  
No condition! I'm teasing, Arthur. Relax!

**ARTHUR:**  
*(laughing with relief)*  
Oh, you're terrible!

**GLADYS:**  
*(gently)*  
Arthur... Harold would want me to be happy. I know he would. And Mary... I think she'd want you to be happy too.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(moved)*  
Yes. Yes, she would. She always said life was for living.

**MRS. PATEL:**  
*(approaching)*  
More tea, anyone? Or perhaps... some cake? On the house, for special customers.

**ARTHUR:**  
Mrs. Patel, you're too kind.

**MRS. PATEL:**  
*(setting down cake plate)*  
Nonsense. Celebrations call for cake. And this looks like a celebration.

**GLADYS:**  
*(to Arthur)*  
She knows, doesn't she?

**ARTHUR:**  
*(resigned)*  
She knows everything. She's owned this tea room for fifteen years.

**MRS. PATEL:**  
*(proudly)*  
And I've matched forty-seven couples in that time. You two will be number forty-eight. Mark my words.

**[SFX: Everyone laughing]**

**GLADYS:**  
Mrs. Patel, you're impossible.

**MRS. PATEL:**  
Impossible at matchmaking, perhaps. Now, I'll leave you to your cake. And Arthur?

**ARTHUR:**  
Yes, Mrs. Patel?

**MRS. PATEL:**  
Saturday night. Seven o'clock. Don't be late. And wear the blue tie. It brings out your eyes.

**[SFX: Mrs. Patel walks away]**

**ARTHUR:**  
*(to Gladys)*  
The blue tie?

**GLADYS:**  
*(laughing)*  
Definitely the blue tie.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(gently)*  
Gladys... thank you. For saying yes.

**GLADYS:**  
*(softly)*  
Thank you, Arthur. For asking.

**[SFX: They sit in comfortable silence, tea room ambience]**

**ARTHUR:**  
*(after a moment)*  
You know... Mary used to say that life begins again when you think it's over.

**GLADYS:**  
*(warmly)*  
Harold said something similar. "The best chapters," he said, "are the ones you don't see coming."

**ARTHUR:**  
*(raising teacup)*  
To... unexpected chapters?

**GLADYS:**  
*(clinking her cup against his)*  
To new beginnings, Arthur. At four o'clock.

**ARTHUR:**  
*(happily)*  
To new beginnings.

**[MUSIC: Piano swells warmly, romantic but restrained]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
"Tea at Four O'Clock" was written for Springbok Radio by [Your Name]. Next week: "The Children's Hour." Until then, remember—it's never too late.

**[MUSIC: Fade out]**

**[END - 15 minutes]**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Tone:**
- Gentle, warm, not sappy
- Elderly romance handled with dignity
- Humor through Mrs. Patel's matchmaking

**Character Voices:**
- Arthur: Shy, nervous, but genuinely kind
- Gladys: Warm, confident, but tender
- Mrs. Patel: Maternal, knowing, cheerful
- Walter: Mentioned only, boisterous friend

**Pacing:**
- Slow, relaxed (like a tea room afternoon)
- Pauses for emotional moments
- Let silences breathe

**Music:**
- Light piano (tea room style)
- 1950s/60s easy listening
- Romantic but not overwhelming

**Cultural Notes:**
- Community dances were common for seniors
- Tea rooms were social hubs
- Reflects respectful 1960s courtship
