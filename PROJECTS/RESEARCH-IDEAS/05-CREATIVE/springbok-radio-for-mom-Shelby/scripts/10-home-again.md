# EPISODE 10: "Home Again" (FINALE)
**Genre:** Family Drama / Finale  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Theme:** Coming home, love, family reunion, legacy  
**Setting:** Family home, Durban, 1966  

---

## CHARACTERS

- **MARGARET** (from Episode 1, now 62) - Matriarch, waiting for family
- **DAVID** (from Episode 1, now 37) - Her son, returned home
- **SUSAN** (from Episode 1, now 32) - David's wife
- **LITTLE MICHAEL** (4) - David and Susan's son, Margaret's grandson
- **TANT GEESIE** (from Episode 9, cameo) - Elderly friend
- **MRS. VAN DER MERWE** (from Episode 2, cameo) - Neighbor
- **ANNOUNCER**

---

## SOUND EFFECTS NEEDED

- Car pulling up to house
- Car doors opening/closing
- Front door opening
- Family chatter and laughter
- Suitcase being set down
- Hugs (fabric rustling)
- Tea service (multiple cups)
- Children laughing and playing
- Garden birds (morning)
- Camera shutter (old-fashioned)
- Piano music (family gathering)

## SCRIPT

**[MUSIC: Full orchestral theme - all previous episode motifs combined - 15 seconds, fade under]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Springbok Radio presents... "Home Again." The final episode of our Springbok Radio collection. A story of coming home, of family, and of love that endures.

**[MUSIC: Softens to gentle underscore]**

**[SFX: Car pulling up to house, engine stops]**

**DAVID:**  
*(from outside car)*  
We're here, Michael. This is where Daddy grew up.

**LITTLE MICHAEL:**  
*(excited)*  
Really, Daddy? Did you have a swing in the garden?

**SUSAN:**  
*(laughing)*  
He did, darling. And he fell out of it more than once.

**DAVID:**  
*(mock-offended)*  
Susan! Don't tell him that!

**[SFX: Car doors open, family gets out]**

**DAVID:**  
*(taking deep breath)*  
Home. After all these years... it still smells the same.

**SUSAN:**  
*(gently)*  
Come on, my love. She's waiting.

**[SFX: Footsteps to door, suitcase]**

**DAVID:**  
*(knocking, then pausing)*  
I'm nervous.

**SUSAN:**  
*(warmly)*  
Don't be. She's been waiting for this moment for three years.

**[SFX: Door opens]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(from inside)*  
Coming! Just a moment—

**[SFX: Door opens fully]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(gasping)*  
David...

**DAVID:**  
*(emotional)*  
Hello, Mother.

**[SFX: Long embrace, fabric rustling]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(crying happily)*  
My boy. My boy, you're home.

**DAVID:**  
*(tearfully)*  
I'm home, Mother. I'm home.

**LITTLE MICHAEL:**  
*(shyly)*  
Ouma?

**MARGARET:**  
*(pulling back, seeing grandson)*  
Oh... oh, David. Is this...?

**DAVID:**  
Mother, this is Michael. Your grandson.

**MARGARET:**  
*(kneeling to child's level)*  
Hello, Michael. I'm your Ouma Margaret.

**LITTLE MICHAEL:**  
*(curious)*  
Ouma... do you have cookies?

**[SFX: Everyone laughing]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(delighted)*  
Do I have cookies? Michael, I have the best cookies in all of Durban! Come, come!

**[SFX: They enter house, door closes]**

**[SFX: Interior - family gathering sounds, teacups]**

**SUSAN:**  
Margaret, the house looks beautiful.

**MARGARET:**  
Thank you, dear. I've been preparing for weeks. Though I suppose I've been preparing for three years. Since David called from the train station.

**DAVID:**  
*(warmly)*  
Mother, I want you to know... coming back was the best decision I ever made.

**MARGARET:**  
*(softly)*  
And keeping you away was the worst mistake I ever made. I was so angry when you left. So proud and stubborn.

**DAVID:**  
We both were, Mother. But we're here now. That's what matters.

**[SFX: Child laughing in background]**

**LITTLE MICHAEL:**  
Ouma! Look what I found!

**MARGARET:**  
*(going to child)*  
What is it, my darling?

**LITTLE MICHAEL:**  
A photograph! Of you and Oupa!

**[SFX: Photo being handled]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(emotional)*  
Oh... yes. That was taken on our wedding day. 1928. Look how young we were.

**DAVID:**  
*(looking at photo)*  
Father looks so happy.

**MARGARET:**  
He was. He loved this house. Loved this family. *(pause)* He would have loved you, Michael. So much.

**LITTLE MICHAEL:**  
Where is Oupa, Ouma?

**MARGARET:**  
*(gently)*  
He's in the garden, darling. In the roses he planted. In the stories we tell. In the love we share. He's... everywhere that matters.

**[SFX: Door knock]**

**MARGARET:**  
Ah! They're here!

**[SFX: Door opens, multiple people entering]**

**MRS. VAN DER MERWE:**  
Margaret! We brought the koeksisters!

**TANT GEESIE:**  
And I brought the melktert! Can't have a homecoming without dessert!

**MARGARET:**  
*(delighted)*  
Mrs. Van Der Merwe! Tant Geesie! You didn't need to bring anything!

**MRS. VAN DER MERWE:**  
Nonsense! David's home! This is a celebration!

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(noticing David)*  
David Harris! Look at you! A grown man with a family!

**DAVID:**  
*(laughing)*  
Tant Geesie! You haven't changed a bit!

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(wisely)*  
Oh, I've changed, boy. I've just gotten better at hiding it.

**[SFX: Everyone laughing]**

**SUSAN:**  
Margaret, you have wonderful friends.

**MARGARET:**  
They're more than friends, Susan. They're family. The family you choose.

**[SFX: Piano music begins - someone starts playing]**

**MRS. VAN DER MERWE:**  
Who's at the piano?

**DAVID:**  
*(surprised)*  
That's... that's Michael.

**LITTLE MICHAEL:**  
*(playing simple tune)*  
Ouma, I'm playing for you!

**MARGARET:**  
*(tearfully proud)*  
He's playing... the song your father used to play.

**DAVID:**  
*(remembering)*  
"Home Sweet Home." He played it every Sunday evening.

**MARGARET:**  
*(softly)*  
Yes. And now his grandson plays it... while his son sits beside him... in the house where he was happy.

**[SFX: Piano continues softly underneath]**

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(raising teacup)*  
A toast! To David and Susan and little Michael! To coming home!

**ALL:**  
To coming home!

**[SFX: Teacups clinking]**

**DAVID:**  
*(standing)*  
Mother... I want to say something.

**MARGARET:**  
David, you don't need to—

**DAVID:**  
No. I do. For three years, I thought I was running toward something. A new life. A fresh start. But I was running away. From grief. From anger. From... from you.

**[SFX: Pause, piano continues softly]**

**DAVID:**  
And all that time, home was here. Waiting. Just as you were.

**MARGARET:**  
*(tearfully)*  
Always, my boy. Always.

**DAVID:**  
I'm not leaving again, Mother. This is where we belong. Where Michael will grow up. Where... where Father's memory lives.

**MARGARET:**  
*(embracing him)*  
Oh, David. My heart is so full.

**[SFX: Child giggling]**

**LITTLE MICHAEL:**  
Ouma? Can we plant flowers in Oupa's garden?

**MARGARET:**  
*(pulling back, smiling)*  
Yes, my darling. Yes, we can. What would you like to plant?

**LITTLE MICHAEL:**  
Roses! Red ones! Like in your garden!

**MARGARET:**  
*(to David)*  
Red roses. Just like Henry's.

**DAVID:**  
*(smiling)*  
Just like home.

**[SFX: Piano music swells slightly]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(voiceover, reflective)*  
They say you can't go home again. That once you leave, everything changes. But they're wrong. Home isn't a place. It's the people who wait for you. The love that never leaves. The memories that shape you.

**[SFX: Family laughter, garden sounds blend in]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(voiceover continues)*  
And sometimes... going home means starting again. With new generations. New stories. New love. But the same heart.

**[SFX: Camera shutter clicks - family photo being taken]**

**MRS. VAN DER MERWE:**  
Everyone together! Say "Springbok Radio"!

**ALL:**  
Springbok Radio!

**[SFX: Laughter]**

**TANT GEESIE:**  
There! A memory for the recipe box!

**[SFX: Piano plays final chords of "Home Sweet Home"]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(softly, to herself)*  
Welcome home, my family. Welcome home.

**[MUSIC: Full orchestral finale - all episode themes woven together - 20 seconds]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
"Home Again" concludes our Springbok Radio collection. Ten stories of love, family, courage, and home. Created with love for the mothers, grandmothers, and families who keep our stories alive.

**[MUSIC: Continues]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Thank you for listening to Springbok Radio. May your homes be warm, your families be close, and your hearts be full. Goodnight, South Africa.

**[MUSIC: Swells to emotional climax, then gently fades]**

**[SFX: Final sound - gentle cricket chirping, peaceful night]**

**[END OF COLLECTION - 15 minutes]**
