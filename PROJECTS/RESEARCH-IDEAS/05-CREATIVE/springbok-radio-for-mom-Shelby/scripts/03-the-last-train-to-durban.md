# EPISODE 3: "The Last Train to Durban"
**Genre:** Adventure / Romance  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Theme:** Second chances, unexpected love, destiny  
**Setting:** Train from Johannesburg to Durban, 1959  

---

## CHARACTERS

- **CATHERINE "KATIE" PRETORIUS** (28) - Schoolteacher, returning home after broken engagement
- **MICHAEL STEYN** (32) - Doctor, traveling to Durban for new position
- **CONDUCTOR** (50s) - Friendly, grandfatherly
- **MRS. BOTHA** (60s) - Fellow passenger, chatty widow
- **ANNOUNCER**

---

## SOUND EFFECTS NEEDED

- Train wheels on tracks (rhythmic clacking)
- Train whistle (steam locomotive)
- Train station ambience (crowd, announcements)
- Suitcase being placed on rack
- Teacup and saucer (dining car)
- Paper rustling (newspaper, letter)
- Rain on train windows
- Steam hiss (train stopping)

---

## SCRIPT

**[MUSIC: Romantic orchestral theme with train rhythm underneath - 10 seconds, fade under]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Springbok Radio presents... "The Last Train to Durban." A story of two strangers, one journey, and the destination neither expected to find.

**[MUSIC: Out, train sounds fade in]**

**[SFX: Train wheels clacking rhythmically, steam whistle in distance]**

**MRS. BOTHA:**  
*(chatty)*  
...and I said to my daughter, "Gertie, if you're going to marry that boy, you'd better make sure he has steady employment!" But would she listen? No, no. Young people never do.

**[SFX: Teacup clinks on saucer]**

**CATHERINE:**  
*(polite but distant)*  
No, they don't, Mrs. Botha.

**MRS. BOTHA:**  
You're not married, are you, dear? I can tell. You have that... independent look about you.

**CATHERINE:**  
*(slightly uncomfortable)*  
I was engaged. Not anymore.

**MRS. BOTHA:**  
*(gasps softly)*  
Oh! Oh, I'm so sorry. Did he... was there another woman?

**CATHERINE:**  
*(quietly)*  
No. There was another city. Cape Town. And a job that mattered more to him than I did.

**[SFX: Train whistle blows loudly, then fades]**

**MRS. BOTHA:**  
Well! His loss, I say. A young woman like you—educated, pretty, good manners—there are plenty of fish in the sea, as my Henry used to say. Rest his soul.

**CATHERINE:**  
*(genuinely)*  
Thank you, Mrs. Botha. That's kind of you.

**[SFX: Compartment door slides open]**

**CONDUCTOR:**  
*(friendly)*  
Tickets, please! Evening, ladies. Beautiful night for a journey.

**MRS. BOTHA:**  
Evening, Conducter. My friend here is heading to Durban. First time!

**CATHERINE:**  
*(correcting gently)*  
Returning home, actually. I grew up there.

**CONDUCTOR:**  
Ah! Then you're going home. That's different from visiting. Home is where the heart knows the way, eh?

**CATHERINE:**  
*(softly)*  
Yes. I suppose it is.

**[SFX: Conductor moves to next compartment, door slides]**

**MICHAEL:**  
*(from doorway, apologetic)*  
Excuse me—is this compartment three? The sign says...

**CATHERINE:**  
Yes, it is. Are you...?

**MICHAEL:**  
Michael Steyn. And you must be Catherine Pretorius. My fellow traveler! The conductor mentioned we'd be sharing the compartment.

**CATHERINE:**  
*(surprised)*  
Oh! I... yes, I'm Catherine. Please, come in.

**[SFX: Michael enters, places suitcase on rack]**

**MICHAEL:**  
Thank you. I hope I'm not intruding. First-class compartments are rather scarce on the evening train.

**MRS. BOTHA:**  
*(whispering to Catherine)*  
Handsome young man. A doctor, by the look of his hands.

**MICHAEL:**  
*(overhearing, chuckles)*  
Guilty as charged. I'm heading to Durban to start at Addington Hospital.

**CATHERINE:**  
*(interested despite herself)*  
A doctor? That must be... rewarding.

**MICHAEL:**  
Some days. Other days, exhausting. But yes, rewarding. And you, Miss Pretorius? The conductor mentioned you're a teacher?

**CATHERINE:**  
Yes. I teach at a small school in the Berea district. Heading home for the holidays.

**MICHAEL:**  
Berea! That's where I'll be living. Small world, isn't it?

**[SFX: Train continues, rain begins on windows]**

**MRS. BOTHA:**  
*(standing)*  
Well! I think I'll visit the dining car. Anyone care to join?

**CATHERINE:**  
I'm rather tired, Mrs. Botha. But thank you.

**MICHAEL:**  
I'd love some coffee, if you don't mind my company, Mrs. Botha?

**MRS. BOTHA:**  
*(knowingly)*  
Not at all, Dr. Steyn. Not at all.

**[SFX: Mrs. Botha exits, door slides shut]**

**[SFX: Rain intensifies on windows]**

**CATHERINE:**  
*(after pause)*  
She's a lovely woman. Rather... enthusiastic.

**MICHAEL:**  
*(laughing)*  
She reminded me of my grandmother. The way she sized me up—doctor, single, eligible. Very thorough.

**CATHERINE:**  
*(laughs despite herself)*  
Yes, well. Some things never change.

**MICHAEL:**  
*(gentle)*  
You know, when the conductor told me I'd be sharing with a young woman, I was concerned. Proper etiquette and all that. But I'm rather glad it's you.

**CATHERINE:**  
*(surprised)*  
Why's that?

**MICHAEL:**  
Because you have kind eyes. And you look like someone who understands that sometimes, the journey matters more than the destination.

**[SFX: Train slows slightly]**

**CATHERINE:**  
*(quietly)*  
That's a lovely sentiment, Dr. Steyn.

**MICHAEL:**  
Michael, please. "Dr. Steyn" is what my patients call me. When I'm trying to escape, I prefer Michael.

**CATHERINE:**  
*(smiling)*  
Catherine, then. Or Katie. My father calls me Katie.

**MICHAEL:**  
Katie. I like that. So, Katie... tell me about this school of yours. What do you teach?

**[SFX: Train continues, rain softens]**

**CATHERINE:**  
*(warming up)*  
Everything, really. It's a small school—reading, writing, arithmetic. But also... I teach them to dream. To imagine places beyond our little neighborhood.

**MICHAEL:**  
*(impressed)*  
That's the most important subject of all.

**CATHERINE:**  
*(thoughtful)*  
You know, I almost didn't take this job. After... after my engagement ended, I thought about moving to Johannesburg. Starting over.

**MICHAEL:**  
But you didn't.

**CATHERINE:**  
No. Because those children need me. And... Durban is home. The sea, the warmth, the way the light hits the harbor in the morning. I realized I was trying to run from myself, not from Cape Town.

**MICHAEL:**  
*(softly)*  
That takes courage, Katie. To recognize that. Most people spend their whole lives running.

**[SFX: Train whistle, beginning to slow]**

**CONDUCTOR:**  
*(over loudspeaker)*  
Approaching Durban Station! All passengers prepare to disembark!

**CATHERINE:**  
*(standing)*  
Well... this is it.

**MICHAEL:**  
*(also standing)*  
Katie... I know this is rather forward. But I'll be at Addington Hospital starting Monday. And you're in Berea. That's... what, two miles apart?

**CATHERINE:**  
*(smiling)*  
About that.

**MICHAEL:**  
Would you... might I take you to dinner? Sometime this week? No pressure, no expectations. Just... two people who shared a train ride, having a meal.

**[SFX: Train brakes hiss, coming to stop]**

**CATHERINE:**  
*(pause, then warmly)*  
I'd like that, Michael. Very much.

**MICHAEL:**  
*(relieved, happy)*  
Excellent! I'll... I'll find you. At the school?

**CATHERINE:**  
*(laughing)*  
Or I could give you my number. Since you've already met Mrs. Botha, I imagine the whole train knows our business by now.

**MICHAEL:**  
*(laughing)*  
Probably!

**[SFX: Train stops, crowd noises, doors open]**

**CONDUCTOR:**  
Durban! All aboard who's coming off!

**CATHERINE:**  
*(gathering her things)*  
Michael... thank you. For making this journey... memorable.

**MICHAEL:**  
*(gently)*  
The pleasure was mine, Katie. Until Monday, then?

**CATHERINE:**  
Until Monday.

**[SFX: They exit train, crowd ambience]**

**MRS. BOTHA:**  
*(calling out)*  
Katie! Dr. Steyn! There you are! Oh, you must exchange addresses! I insist!

**CATHERINE & MICHAEL:**  
*(laughing together)*  
Mrs. Botha!

**[MUSIC: Romantic theme swells]**

**CATHERINE:**  
*(voiceover, reflective)*  
Sometimes, the last train isn't an ending at all. Sometimes, it's the beginning of everything. And sometimes... home isn't just a place. It's a person you haven't met yet.

**[MUSIC: Full orchestral swell]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
"The Last Train to Durban" was written for Springbok Radio by [Your Name]. Next week: "Uncle Piet's Secret." Good evening, South Africa.

**[MUSIC: Fade out]**

**[END - 15 minutes]**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Romantic Tone:**
- Subtle chemistry between Catherine and Michael
- Not overly dramatic—restrained 1950s romance
- Mrs. Botha provides comic relief and warmth

**Train Ambience:**
- Continuous train rhythm underneath dialogue
- Vary intensity (louder in stations, softer in conversation)
- Rain adds intimacy to compartment scenes

**Music:**
- Romantic orchestral theme (strings, piano)
- 1950s cinema romance style
- Not melodramatic—gentle and hopeful

**Cultural Notes:**
- Addington Hospital is real (Durban)
- Berea is a Durban suburb
- Reflects 1950s train travel (steam locomotives, dining cars)
