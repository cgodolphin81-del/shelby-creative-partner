## Production Notes

**Voice Direction:**
- Warm, intimate delivery (imagine speaking to family)
- Clear, theatrical diction (radio requires perfect enunciation)
- Gentle South African/British accent influence
- Not rushed - allow pauses for effect
- Smile while recording warm scenes (it comes through in the voice!)

**Sound Effects Philosophy:**
- Less is more (10% of production)
- Suggest action, don't overwhelm dialogue
- Use vintage sounds: rotary phones, doorbells, kettle whistles
- Ambient beds: distant traffic, birds, room tone

**Music:**
- Piano or light orchestral underscoring
- Theme tune at open/close
- Emotional stings for transitions
- Available royalty-free options included

**Recording Tips:**
- Record in a quiet, soft-furnished room
- Use pop filters for plosives
- Maintain consistent distance from microphone
- Record room tone (30 seconds of silence) for editing

## How to Use These Scripts

1. **Read through** each script to understand the story
2. **Cast voices** - assign characters to different voice actors (or use AI voices)
3. **Mark sound effects** - note where SFX are needed
4. **Record dialogue** - can be done separately and edited together
5. **Add music and SFX** - layer in post-production
6. **Master** - apply vintage broadcast EQ and slapback delay

**Estimated Recording Time:** 2-3 hours per episode (including retakes)
**Total Project Time:** 20-30 hours for all 10 episodes
