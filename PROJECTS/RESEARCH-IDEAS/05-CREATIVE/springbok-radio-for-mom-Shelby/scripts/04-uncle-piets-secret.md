# EPISODE 4: "Uncle Piet's Secret"
**Genre:** Comedy / Family  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Theme:** Family humor, secrets, acceptance  
**Setting:** Family home, Johannesburg, 1960  

---

## CHARACTERS

- **UNCLE PIET** (65) - Lovable rogue, Afrikaans accent, always has a "scheme"
- **TANT Anna** (60) - His sister, practical, no-nonsense
- **FRANCOIS** (16) - Their nephew, curious teenager
- **OOM SJAAL** (70) - Piet's oldest friend, equally mischievous
- **ANNOUNCER**

---

## SOUND EFFECTS NEEDED

- Front door opening/closing
- Teacups and saucers (lots of them)
- Newspaper rustling
- Match striking, pipe lighting
- Laughter (warm family laughter)
- Car horn (vintage)
- Kitchen sounds (pots, pans)
- Radio static and music (1950s tune)

---

## SCRIPT

**[MUSIC: Bouncy, comedic accordion theme - 10 seconds, fade under]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Springbok Radio presents... "Uncle Piet's Secret." A comedy of family, friendship, and the secret that wasn't.

**[MUSIC: Out]**

**[SFX: Front door opens, closes, family chatter in background]**

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(calling out)*  
Piet! Piet, where are you? It's almost time for lunch!

**[SFX: Footsteps, door to study opens]**

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(slightly guilty)*  
Ja, ja, Anna! I'm coming! Just... finishing some important business.

**FRANCOIS:**  
*(whispering)*  
Tannie Anna, Oom Piet's been in his study every morning for three weeks. He says it's "important business," but I think—

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(interrupting, knowing)*  
Francois, my boy, your uncle has had "important business" for forty years. Usually involving cards, horses, or schemes that end with him hiding from bookmakers.

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(offended)*  
Anna! That's not fair! I only hid from bookmakers twice. And both times were entirely justified!

**[SFX: Family laughter]**

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(fondly exasperated)*  
Come to the table, Piet. And bring whatever it is you're hiding. I can hear it rustling from here.

**[SFX: Paper rustling, footsteps]**

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(reluctant)*  
It's... it's nothing, really. Just... ah, here. Look.

**[SFX: Paper placed on table]**

**FRANCOIS:**  
*(reading)*  
"Certificate of... Completion?" Oom Piet, what's this?

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(proud but trying to be casual)*  
Well, you see... your Tannie Anna has been complaining for years that I don't have any "proper qualifications." Just my charm and business sense.

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(suspicious)*  
Piet... what kind of certificate is this?

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(beaming)*  
I completed a course! "Advanced Accounting and Business Management!" From the Johannesburg Technical College!

**[SFX: Shocked silence, then explosion of laughter]**

**OOM SJAAL:**  
*(entering, laughing)*  
Piet! You old dog! You've been studying? Since when do you study anything thicker than a racing form?

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(indignant)*  
Since I realized my beloved sister thinks I'm a complete incompetence!

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(emotional, trying not to cry)*  
Piet... you never told me.

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(softly)*  
I wanted it to be a surprise. For your birthday next week. I know you worry about me, Anna. Always have. Since Mother and Father passed, you've been my keeper.

**FRANCOIS:**  
Oom Piet... this is actually brilliant!

**UNCLE PIET:**  
Ja? You think so?

**FRANCOIS:**  
Definitely! Now you can help me with my mathematics homework!

**[SFX: More laughter]**

**OOM SJAAL:**  
Wait, wait. Piet, my friend... does this mean you're giving up the "import-export business"?

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(sheepish)*  
Ah. Well. About that...

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(stern)*  
Piet. What import-export business?

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(quickly)*  
It's completely legitimate! I import... goodwill. And I export... problems! Very profitable!

**[SFX: Everyone laughing]**

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(wiping tears, laughing)*  
You impossible man. Forty years old and still a rascal.

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(taking her hand)*  
But I'm your rascal, Anna. And I wanted you to know... I do try. Even if I'm not very good at the trying.

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(emotional)*  
You're better than you think, brother. You've given us laughter. You've given Francois stories to tell his grandchildren. You've given me... a life filled with adventure, even if half of it was trying to keep you out of trouble.

**OOM SJAAL:**  
*(raising teacup)*  
A toast! To Uncle Piet! The only man I know who can get a certificate for studying, and still not know how to balance his checkbook!

**[SFX: Teacups clinking, laughter]**

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(mock-offended)*  
I'll have you know, Sjaal, my checkbook is perfectly—Anna, stop laughing!

**FRANCOIS:**  
Oom Piet? Can I ask you something?

**UNCLE PIET:**  
Anything, my boy.

**FRANCOIS:**  
Why did you really do the course? The certificate is lovely, but... you've never cared about qualifications before.

**[SFX: Brief pause, thoughtful music underneath]**

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(gently)*  
Because I saw the way you look at your textbooks, Francois. The way you want to make us proud. And I thought... if your old Oom Piet can learn something new, then you can do anything.

**FRANCOIS:**  
*(quietly)*  
Oh.

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(tearfully)*  
Piet...

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(lightly)*  
Also, the college has a excellent canteen. The meat pies are extraordinary.

**[SFX: Everyone laughing]**

**OOM SJAAL:**  
There he is! The Piet we know and love!

**UNCLE PIET:**  
Now! Who wants to hear about my next business venture? I've been thinking about ostrich farming...

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(immediately)*  
No.

**UNCLE PIET:**  
But Anna—

**TANT ANNA:**  
No ostriches, Piet. No emus. No exotic birds of any kind. Remember the peacocks?

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(defensive)*  
The peacocks were a temporary setback!

**FRANCOIS:**  
The peacocks escaped and lived in Mrs. Van Wyk's garden for six months!

**UNCLE PIET:**  
She loved them!

**TANT ANNA:**  
She called the police, Piet!

**[SFX: Sustained laughter]**

**OOM SJAAL:**  
*(standing)*  
Well, friends, I must be going. Piet, tomorrow we have golf?

**UNCLE PIET:**  
Of course! Though I must warn you, I've been practicing my swing.

**OOM SJAAL:**  
*(dryly)*  
Somehow, that terrifies me more than your accounting.

**[SFX: Door opens and closes]**

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(softly)*  
Piet... thank you. For the certificate. For... being you.

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(gently)*  
Anna... I may be a fool. But I'm your fool. And I love you more than all the schemes in the world.

**FRANCOIS:**  
*(groaning)*  
Oom Piet, that was terribly sentimental!

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(laughing)*  
Ja, ja. Don't tell anyone. I have a reputation to maintain!

**[SFX: Radio in background plays 1950s tune]**

**TANT ANNA:**  
*(contented)*  
Lunch is ready. Wash your hands, both of you. And Piet?

**UNCLE PIET:**  
Ja, Anna?

**TANT ANNA:**  
I'm proud of you.

**UNCLE PIET:**  
*(quietly, touched)*  
...Dankie, Anna. Dankie.

**[MUSIC: Warm, comedic theme returns]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
"Uncle Piet's Secret" was written for Springbok Radio by [Your Name]. Next week: "The Voice on the Wireless." Until then, keep smiling, South Africa!

**[MUSIC: Fade out with laughter underneath]**

**[END - 15 minutes]**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Comedy Style:**
- Warm family humor, not mean-spirited
- Piet is lovable, not a buffoon
- Physical comedy through sound (rustling, teacups, etc.)

**Afrikaans Touches:**
- "Tant/Tannie" = Aunt
- "Oom" = Uncle
- "Ja" = Yes
- "Dankie" = Thank you
- Keep authentic but understandable for all listeners

**Pacing:**
- Quick back-and-forth dialogue
- Pauses for laughter (leave room in recording)
- Build to emotional moment, then release with comedy

**Music:**
- Light accordion or concertina
- Bouncy, cheerful theme
- Underscore for emotional moments only
