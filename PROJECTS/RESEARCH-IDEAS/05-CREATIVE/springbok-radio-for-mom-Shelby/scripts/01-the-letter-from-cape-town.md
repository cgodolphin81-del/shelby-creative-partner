# EPISODE 1: "The Letter from Cape Town"
**Genre:** Family Drama  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Theme:** Reconnection, forgiveness, family bonds  
**Setting:** Johannesburg, 1957  

---

## CHARACTERS

- **MARGARET HARRIS** (60s) - Warm, gentle matriarch, lives alone in Johannesburg
- **DAVID HARRIS** (35) - Her son, successful businessman in Cape Town, estranged
- **SUSAN** (30s) - David's wife, kind-hearted, the peacemaker
- **ANNOUNCER** - Classic Springbok Radio announcer voice

---

## SOUND EFFECTS NEEDED

- Rotary telephone dial and ring
- Kettle whistling
- Teacup and saucer
- Paper rustling (letter)
- Door opening/closing
- Light rain outside
- Train whistle (distant)
- Soft piano music (underscoring)

---

## SCRIPT

**[MUSIC: Gentle piano theme - 10 seconds, then fade under]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Springbok Radio presents... "The Letter from Cape Town." A story of family, forgiveness, and the courage to come home. Starring Margaret Harris as Margaret, and David Harris as David.

**[MUSIC: Swell briefly, then out]**

**[SFX: Light rain outside window, kettle beginning to whistle]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(to herself, humming)*  
There now... almost time for tea.

**[SFX: Kettle whistle grows louder, Margaret moves to turn it off]**

**MFX: Sound of kettle being lifted, water pouring into teapot]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(softly)*  
One cup. Just one cup, as always. Though I suppose I could make two... if anyone were here to drink it.

**[SFX: Teacup placed on saucer, chair creaks as she sits]**

**[SFX: Paper rustling - she picks up a letter]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(reading, voice trembling slightly)*  
"Dear Mother... I hope this letter finds you well. It has been three years, hasn't it? Three years since we last spoke. Susan and I have been talking, and we feel it's time..."

*(pause, emotional)*

Time. How strange that word sounds now. Time keeps moving forward, whether we're ready or not.

**[SFX: Telephone rings - old rotary phone, loud ring]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(startled)*  
Oh my! Who could that be?

**[SFX: Chair scrapes, footsteps across room, phone picked up]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(into phone)*  
Hello? Harris residence.

**DAVID:**  
*(nervous, on phone)*  
Mother? It's... it's David.

**[SFX: Margaret's teacup clatters slightly on saucer]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(breath catches)*  
David? Oh, David... hello, my boy.

**DAVID:**  
I know it's been a long time. Too long. I received your letter last week—the one about Father's garden.

**MARGARET:**  
Yes, well... I wasn't sure if you'd write back. Or call. The roses are blooming beautifully this year. Your father would have loved to see them.

**DAVID:**  
*(emotional)*  
I know, Mother. I know he would have. And I know... I know I should have been there. For the funeral. For you. For everything.

**[SFX: Rain continues softly outside]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(gentle)*  
You were grieving in your own way, David. We all grieve differently. Your father understood that.

**DAVID:**  
Did he? I'm not so sure. We argued so much in those last years. About the business, about moving to Cape Town, about... about everything.

**MARGARET:**  
*(softly)*  
Fathers and sons. It's an old story, my love. But you know what he told me, the last time we spoke about you?

**DAVID:**  
*(quiet)*  
What?

**MARGARET:**  
He said, "David's a good boy. He's just finding his own path. When he's ready, he'll come home."

**[SFX: David takes a deep breath on the other end]**

**DAVID:**  
I'm ready, Mother. Susan and I... we're coming to Johannesburg next week. We'd like to see you. If that's alright.

**[SFX: Margaret's hand covers the phone, emotional breathing]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(tearfully)*  
Of course it's alright. Of course. Your room is exactly as you left it. I... I make sure to air it out every week. Just in case.

**DAVID:**  
*(choked up)*  
Mother...

**MARGARET:**  
*(recovering, warm)*  
Now, you tell Susan to pack her warm clothes! Johannesburg winter is nothing like Cape Town. And I'll make my famous scones. Your father's recipe.

**DAVID:**  
*(laughing through tears)*  
I'd like that. We'd both like that very much.

**[SFX: Paper rustling - David picks up something]**

**DAVID:**  
Oh, and Mother? I found something in Father's study. A letter he wrote to you. Never sent. I think... I think you should have it.

**MARGARET:**  
*(whispered)*  
A letter?

**DAVID:**  
I'll bring it with me. It's... it's beautiful, Mother. He loved you very much. We all do.

**[SFX: Long pause, rain continues]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(softly)*  
Come home, David. Just come home.

**DAVID:**  
We will, Mother. We will.

**[SFX: Phone hangs up gently]**

**[SFX: Margaret stands, walks to window]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(to herself)*  
Home. Yes.

**[SFX: She opens window, rain sound increases slightly]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(looking out)*  
The garden's ready, Henry. Our boy's coming home.

**[SFX: Train whistle in far distance]**

**MARGARET:**  
*(smiling)*  
And this time, he's staying.

**[MUSIC: Piano theme returns, emotional swell]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
"The Letter from Cape Town" was written for Springbok Radio by [Your Name]. Next week: "Mrs. Van Der Merwe's Garden." Until then, good evening, South Africa.

**[MUSIC: Theme continues, then fades out]**

**[END - 15 minutes]**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Emotional Beats:**
- Margaret's loneliness (opening scene)
- The phone call revelation (midpoint)
- David's apology and return (climax)
- Margaret's hope (resolution)

**Voice Direction:**
- Margaret: Warm, elderly, slightly fragile but strong
- David: Middle-aged, regretful, seeking redemption
- Both should sound genuinely emotional but not overacted

**Music Cues:**
- Opening: Gentle piano, nostalgic
- Phone call: Minimal underscoring (let dialogue carry)
- Ending: Full emotional theme

**Vintage Touch:**
- Add slight slapback delay to voices (75-100ms)
- EQ: Boost midrange, cut lows below 100Hz
- Add subtle room tone throughout
