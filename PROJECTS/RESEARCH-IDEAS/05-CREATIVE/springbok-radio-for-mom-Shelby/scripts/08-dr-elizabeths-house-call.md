# EPISODE 8: "Dr. Elizabeth's House Call"
**Genre:** Medical Drama / Inspiration  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Theme:** Dedication, care, breaking barriers  
**Setting:** Rural Transvaal, 1964  

---

## CHARACTERS

- **DR. ELIZABETH NDLOVU** (35) - Black female doctor, pioneering, dedicated
- **MRS. KRUGER** (60) - White Afrikaner farm wife, initially prejudiced
- **LITTLE PIETER** (8) - Mrs. Kruger's son, sick with pneumonia
- **MR. KRUGER** (65) - Farm owner, gruff but worried
- **NURSE THANDIWE** (28) - Elizabeth's assistant, brave and loyal
- **ANNOUNCER**

---

## SOUND EFFECTS NEEDED

- Car engine (vintage, struggling on dirt road)
- Rain and wind (stormy night)
- Car door opening/closing
- Footsteps on wooden porch
- Door knocking
- Medical bag opening
- Stethoscope sounds
- Kettle boiling
- Child coughing (sick)
- Thunder and lightning

---

## SCRIPT

**[MUSIC: Dramatic but hopeful orchestral theme - 10 seconds, fade under]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Springbok Radio presents... "Dr. Elizabeth's House Call." A story of courage, compassion, and the healing that transcends all boundaries.

**[MUSIC: Out]**

**[SFX: Car engine struggling, rain and wind, tires on mud]**

**NURSE THANDIWE:**  
*(concerned)*  
Sister Ndlovu, the roads are terrible. Maybe we should turn back and try again in the morning.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(determined)*  
No, Thandiwe. That boy has pneumonia. Every hour matters. Keep going.

**NURSE THANDIWE:**  
But these people... they called you "girl" on the phone. They don't want a Black doctor.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(calmly)*  
They don't want to call me, perhaps. But they want their son to live. That's what matters.

**[SFX: Car stops, engine off]**

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
We're here. Help me with the bag.

**[SFX: Car doors, footsteps through mud, rain continues]**

**NURSE THANDIWE:**  
*(shivering)*  
It's freezing! How do these farmers stand it?

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(knocking on door)*  
They stand it because it's their home. Now hush—someone's coming.

**[SFX: Door opens]**

**MR. KRUGER:**  
*(gruff, suspicious)*  
Ja? What do you want?

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(professional, calm)*  
Mr. Kruger? I'm Dr. Ndlovu. We spoke on the phone. Your son needs medical attention.

**MR. KRUGER:**  
*(hesitant)*  
You're... you're the doctor?

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
I am. May we come in? It's rather wet out here.

**[SFX: Door opens wider, they enter]**

**MRS. KRUGER:**  
*(from inside, worried)*  
Hendrik? Who is it? Is it the doctor?

**MR. KRUGER:**  
*(conflicted)*  
Ja, Elisabet. It's... the doctor.

**[SFX: Door closes, wind and rain muffled]**

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(removing wet coat)*  
Mrs. Kruger? I'm Dr. Ndlovu. This is Nurse Thandiwe. Where is your son?

**MRS. KRUGER:**  
*(relieved, emotional)*  
In here, quickly! He's been coughing all night. The fever won't break!

**[SFX: Quick footsteps to bedroom]**

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(immediately professional)*  
Thandiwe, set up the examination kit. Mrs. Kruger, tell me everything. When did it start?

**MRS. KRUGER:**  
Two days ago. Just a cough at first. But last night, the fever came. He's so hot, Doctor! And he can barely breathe!

**[SFX: Medical bag opens, instruments]**

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(examining)*  
Little Pieter, can you hear me? My name is Dr. Elizabeth. I'm going to help you feel better.

**LITTLE PIETER:**  
*(weak, coughing)*  
...Mummy?

**MRS. KRUGER:**  
*(tearfully)*  
I'm here, my boy. The doctor is here.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(using stethoscope)*  
Deep breath, Pieter. In... and out. Good boy. Again.

**[SFX: Child coughing violently]**

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(concerned)*  
His lungs are congested. This is serious pneumonia. He needs penicillin, immediately.

**MR. KRUGER:**  
*(from doorway)*  
Penicillin? Is that... is that expensive?

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(firmly)*  
Mr. Kruger, your son needs this medicine. Money doesn't matter right now.

**[SFX: Brief pause, tension]**

**NURSE THANDIWE:**  
*(quietly, to Elizabeth)*  
Sister... the injection.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(preparing syringe)*  
Pieter, this will pinch for just a moment. You're very brave, you know that?

**LITTLE PIETER:**  
*(small voice)*  
Are you... are you a real doctor?

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(gently)*  
Yes, little one. I studied very hard to become a doctor. So I could help children like you.

**LITTLE PIETER:**  
My... my friend says Black people can't be doctors.

**[SFX: Awkward silence]**

**MRS. KRUGER:**  
*(sharply)*  
Pieter! Don't be rude!

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(calmly, continuing work)*  
Your friend is wrong, Pieter. Anyone can be a doctor. Anyone can be anything they want to be. If they work hard and never give up.

**[SFX: Injection administered]**

**LITTLE PIETER:**  
*(small yelp)*  
Ow!

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
All done! You were very brave. Now, the medicine will make you feel better. But you must rest. Lots of sleep.

**MRS. KRUGER:**  
*(grateful)*  
Thank you, Doctor. Thank you so much.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(standing)*  
I'll leave instructions with Nurse Thandiwe. He needs to stay warm, drink plenty of fluids, and take his temperature every four hours. If the fever doesn't break by morning, call me immediately.

**MR. KRUGER:**  
*(from doorway, gruff)*  
Doctor... wait.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(turning)*  
Yes, Mr. Kruger?

**MR. KRUGER:**  
*(struggling with words)*  
I... I owe you an apology. When you called, I... I asked for a different doctor.

**[SFX: Pause]**

**MR. KRUGER:**  
But you came. In this storm. To help my son. When you had every reason not to.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(softly)*  
I'm a doctor, Mr. Kruger. Your son was sick. That's all that matters.

**MR. KRUGER:**  
*(emotionally)*  
No. No, that's not all that matters. You're... you're a better person than I am, Doctor.

**[SFX: Pause, emotional]**

**MRS. KRUGER:**  
*(tearfully)*  
Doctor... please. Stay. Have some coffee. At least warm up before you drive back.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(checking watch)*  
It's late. We should get back. There are other patients.

**LITTLE PIETER:**  
*(weakly)*  
Dr. Elizabeth? Will you... will you come back?

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(softening)*  
Yes, Pieter. I'll come back tomorrow to check on you. I promise.

**LITTLE PIETER:**  
*(sleepy)*  
Thank you... for helping me...

**[SFX: Child's breathing becomes easier]**

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(to parents)*  
He's resting now. The medicine is working. Keep him warm.

**MRS. KRUGER:**  
Doctor... how much do we owe you?

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(gathering her things)*  
Send payment to the clinic. The standard fee.

**MR. KRUGER:**  
*(insistent)*  
No. I mean... thank you. For more than the medicine.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(at door)*  
Mr. Kruger, Mrs. Kruger... I know this country has... difficulties. But in this room, tonight, we were just people. A sick child. Parents who love him. And a doctor who could help.

**[SFX: Pause]**

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
Remember that feeling. Hold onto it.

**[SFX: Door opens, rain and wind return]**

**NURSE THANDIWE:**  
*(impressed)*  
Sister... you amaze me sometimes.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(tired but satisfied)*  
Come, Thandiwe. Let's get back. I want to check on Mrs. Mbeki's baby in the morning.

**[SFX: Footsteps through mud, car door]**

**NURSE THANDIWE:**  
*(as they drive away)*  
Do you think they'll remember? What you said?

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(thoughtful)*  
Tonight, they will. Tomorrow... I don't know. But if we keep showing them, keep being doctors, keep being human... maybe someday, they won't see color at all. Just people.

**[SFX: Car drives away, rain continues]**

**NURSE THANDIWE:**  
*(quietly)*  
You're a hero, Sister Ndlovu.

**DR. ELIZABETH:**  
*(humbly)*  
No, Thandiwe. I'm just a doctor. Doing my job.

**[MUSIC: Hopeful orchestral theme returns, building]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
"Dr. Elizabeth's House Call" was written for Springbok Radio by [Your Name]. Next week: "The Recipe Box." Until then, remember: healing has no color.

**[MUSIC: Fade out]**

**[END - 15 minutes]**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Historical Context:**
- Set during apartheid era (1964)
- Black doctors were rare pioneers (Dr. Elizabeth Ndlovu is fictional but represents real pioneers)
- Reflects courage required to serve across racial lines

**Sensitive Handling:**
- Doesn't preach, but shows through action
- Mr. Kruger's transformation is gradual, realistic
- Elizabeth's dignity and professionalism are central

**Character Voices:**
- Dr. Elizabeth: Calm, professional, compassionate, strong
- Nurse Thandiwe: Loyal, supportive, occasionally outspoken
- Mrs. Kruger: Worried mother, initially prejudiced but grateful
- Mr. Kruger: Gruff Afrikaner farmer, learns humility
- Pieter: Sick child, innocent questions

**Sound Design:**
- Storm outside mirrors social turbulence
- Warm interior contrasts with cold outside
- Medical sounds authentic but not graphic

**Message:**
- Compassion transcends prejudice
- Professionalism and humanity
- Small acts of courage matter
