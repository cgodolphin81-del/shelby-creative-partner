# EPISODE 9: "The Recipe Box"
**Genre:** Family Drama / Heritage  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Theme:** Heritage, tradition, passing down wisdom  
**Setting:** Kitchen, Stellenbosch, 1965  

---

## CHARACTERS

- **TANT GEESIE** (72) - Elderly Afrikaans woman, matriarch, wise
- **MARIA** (25) - Her granddaughter, newly married, learning to cook
- **LITTLE ANNA** (5) - Maria's daughter, curious
- **NEIGHBOR MRS. JOOSTE** (68) - Friend, fellow recipe collector
- **ANNOUNCER**

---

## SOUND EFFECTS NEEDED

- Kitchen ambience (pots, pans, stirring)
- Recipe cards shuffling
- Pen writing on paper
- Oven timer dinging
- Mixing bowl and whisk
- Flour being sifted
- Kettle whistling
- Chair scraping
- Laughter (warm, family)

---

## SCRIPT

**[MUSIC: Warm Afrikaans folk theme - guitar or accordion - 10 seconds, fade under]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
Springbok Radio presents... "The Recipe Box." A story of flour, family, and the recipes that hold our hearts.

**[MUSIC: Out]**

**[SFX: Kitchen sounds - mixing, whisking, pots clinking]**

**MARIA:**  
*(frustrated)*  
Tant Geesie, I don't understand! I followed your recipe exactly, but the koeksisters don't taste like yours!

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(calm, amused)*  
Maria, my child. Come here. Sit.

**[SFX: Chairs scraping, sitting]**

**TANT GEESIE:**  
Show me the recipe.

**MARIA:**  
*(rustling paper)*  
Here. "Tant Geesie's Famous Koeksisters." Your handwriting. Your measurements. Everything.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(chuckling)*  
Ah, yes. The recipe. You know what this is, Maria?

**MARIA:**  
Your recipe for koeksisters?

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(gently)*  
No. This is a map. But a map is not the journey, is it?

**LITTLE ANNA:**  
*(curious)*  
Ouma, what's a journey?

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(picking up little girl)*  
A journey, my little flower, is how you get somewhere. The recipe tells you where to go. But the journey... *(pause)* ...the journey is in your hands.

**MARIA:**  
*(confused)*  
I don't understand, Tant Geesie.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(standing, moving to old wooden box)*  
Maria, come. Look at this.

**[SFX: Old box opening, hinge creaking]**

**TANT GEESIE:**  
This is my mother's recipe box. From before I was born. From before my mother was born.

**MARIA:**  
*(amazed)*  
It's... it's so old.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
Ja. And do you know what's inside? Not just recipes. Look.

**[SFX: Recipe cards shuffling]**

**TANT GEESIE:**  
This card—your great-grandmother's bobotie. See the stain at the corner?

**MARIA:**  
Yes...

**TANT GEESIE:**  
That's from 1923. She was making dinner for your great-grandfather, who had just come home from the war. Her hands were shaking. She spilled the turmeric.

**LITTLE ANNA:**  
*(touching card)*  
Ouma, why is this one crinkled?

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(softly)*  
That one, little flower, is from the day your Oupa was born. I was making his favorite—melktert. I was so excited to tell him it was ready, I ran too fast. Dropped the recipe in my tea.

**MARIA:**  
*(emotional)*  
You kept it anyway?

**TANT GEESIE:**  
Of course! Every stain, every wrinkle, every tear... it's a memory. This recipe isn't just flour and sugar, Maria. It's love. It's history. It's who we are.

**[SFX: Maria quietly emotional]**

**MARIA:**  
*(tearfully)*  
I just... I want to cook like you, Tant Geesie. I want Anna to remember my cooking the way I remember yours.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(wisely)*  
Then stop following the recipe. Start feeling it.

**MARIA:**  
Feeling it?

**TANT GEESIE:**  
Ja. When you make koeksisters, you're not just mixing ingredients. You're making memories for Anna. You're creating something she'll remember when she's grown. When she has her own children.

**LITTLE ANNA:**  
Will I make koeksisters too, Mummy?

**MARIA:**  
*(emotional)*  
Yes, darling. Yes, you will.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
Here. Take this.

**[SFX: Recipe card handed over]**

**MARIA:**  
Tant Geesie... this is your original card. I can't take this.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
You must. Because now it's your turn to add to it.

**MARIA:**  
What do you mean?

**TANT GEESIE:**  
When Anna helps you cook, and she spills a little flour—don't wipe it away. Leave it. When you make this recipe on her birthday, write the date on the card. When she grows up and makes it for her children, she'll see your handwriting. Your love. Your life.

**[SFX: Maria crying softly]**

**MARIA:**  
*(tearfully)*  
I never thought of it like that.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(hugging her)*  
My sweet girl. Cooking isn't about perfect measurements. It's about passing down who we are. Every dish tells a story. Every meal is a memory.

**[SFX: Oven timer dings]**

**TANT GEESIE:**  
Ah! The melktert! Perfect timing.

**LITTLE ANNA:**  
Melktert! My favorite!

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(laughing)*  
Ja, ja. Maria, help me take it out. And this time—don't measure the cinnamon. Smell it. Taste it. Feel how much your heart wants to give.

**[SFX: Oven opening, dish being removed]**

**MARIA:**  
*(sniffing)*  
It smells... like childhood. Like coming home.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(proudly)*  
That's the smell of love, my child. That's what we're really cooking.

**[SFX: Door knock]**

**MRS. JOOSTE:**  
*(calling from outside)*  
Geesie! Are you home? I brought you some fresh figs!

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(calling back)*  
Come in, Martha! The door is open!

**[SFX: Door opens]**

**MRS. JOOSTE:**  
*(entering)*  
Ah! I smell melktert! You're making the family recipe!

**TANT GEESIE:**  
Maria is. With my help.

**MRS. JOOSTE:**  
*(noticing Maria's tears)*  
Oh, dear. What's wrong?

**MARIA:**  
*(laughing through tears)*  
Nothing, Mrs. Jooste. Everything. I just... I finally understand.

**MRS. JOOSTE:**  
*(knowing)*  
Ah. The recipe box.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
She's ready, Martha. Our girls are always ready. They just need to know it's alright to make it their own.

**MRS. JOOSTE:**  
*(to Maria)*  
My mother taught me the same thing. Her koeksister recipe has my daughter's handwriting on it now. And someday, my granddaughter's.

**LITTLE ANNA:**  
Can I write on the recipe too, Ouma?

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(picking up child)*  
Yes, my little flower. You can draw a flower on it. So everyone who makes these koeksisters will know that Anna helped, on this beautiful day in 1965.

**LITTLE ANNA:**  
*(excited)*  
Really? Can I use the red pen?

**MARIA:**  
*(laughing)*  
Yes, darling. The red pen.

**[SFX: Child scribbling on card]**

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(softly, to Maria)*  
You see? The recipe grows. Just like the family.

**MARIA:**  
*(grateful)*  
Tant Geesie... thank you. For everything.

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(wisely)*  
No, my child. Thank you. For carrying it forward. For keeping us alive.

**[SFX: Family laughter, kitchen sounds continue]**

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(voiceover, reflective)*  
Someday, I'll be gone. But I won't really be gone. I'll be in every koeksister Maria makes. In every stain on every recipe card. In every child who tastes the sweetness and wonders, "Who made this first?"

**[MUSIC: Warm Afrikaans folk theme returns]**

**TANT GEESIE:**  
*(voiceover continues)*  
And the answer will be: Love. Love made this first. And love will make it last.

**[MUSIC: Swells emotionally]**

**ANNOUNCER:**  
"The Recipe Box" was written for Springbok Radio by [Your Name]. Next week: "Home Again"—the final episode. Until then, cook with love, South Africa.

**[MUSIC: Fade out]**

**[END - 15 minutes]**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Cultural Elements:**
- Koeksisters: Traditional Afrikaans sweet pastry
- Melktert: South African milk tart
- "Tant": Aunt (respectful term for elder woman)
- Reflects Afrikaans family traditions

**Emotional Core:**
- Recipes as family heritage
- Imperfection as memory
- Passing down traditions through generations

**Character Voices:**
- Tant Geesie: Wise, warm, Afrikaans accent, elderly matriarch
- Maria: Young mother, eager to learn, emotional
- Little Anna: Curious 5-year-old
- Mrs. Jooste: Friendly neighbor, same generation as Geesie

**Sound Design:**
- Kitchen sounds create warmth and authenticity
- Recipe box creaking adds age and history
- Scribbling, stirring, baking—all intimate sounds

**Message:**
- Heritage lives through tradition
- Love is the secret ingredient
- Imperfections make memories
