# The Great Pyramid of Giza - Research

## Overview
- **Official Name:** Pyramid of Khufu (Cheops)
- **Built:** c. 2580-2560 BCE (4,500+ years ago)
- **Location:** Giza Plateau, Cairo, Egypt
- **Original Height:** 146.6 meters (481 feet)
- **Current Height:** 138.5 meters (454 feet)
- **Base:** 230.4 meters (756 feet) per side
- **Weight:** Estimated 6 million tons
- **Blocks:** ~2.3 million limestone blocks (average 2.5 tons each)

## Historical Context
- Built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom
- Commissioned by Pharaoh Khufu (Cheops in Greek)
- Designed by architect Hemiunu (Khufu's vizier)
- Construction took approximately 20-27 years
- Workforce: 20,000-100,000 skilled workers (not slaves)

## Architectural Marvels
- **Precision:** Aligned to true north within 3/60th of a degree
- **Internal Chambers:** King's Chamber, Queen's Chamber, Grand Gallery
- **Shafts:** Narrow shafts extending from chambers (possible astronomical alignment)
- **Capstone:** Originally covered in polished Tura limestone (now lost)
- **Mortar:** Unknown composition, still holding after 4,500 years

## Modern Experience
- **Entry:** Limited tickets daily (300 per day, split morning/afternoon)
- **Interior:** Can enter the King's Chamber (extra ticket required)
- **Temperature:** Inside remains constant ~20°C (68°F)
- **Best Time:** Early morning (8-10 AM) or late afternoon
- **Photography:** No flash inside, exterior photography allowed

## Interesting Facts
- Only surviving wonder of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World
- Remained the tallest man-made structure for 3,800+ years
- Base covers 13 acres (5.3 hectares)
- Blocks fit so precisely that a credit card cannot fit between many of them
- Contains approximately 5.5 million tons of limestone
- Originally stood 146.6m, now 138.5m due to erosion and loss of capstone

## Spiritual Significance
- Built as a tomb and resurrection machine for the pharaoh
- Part of a larger complex including mortuary temple, causeway, valley temple
- Aligned with Orion's Belt (Osiris in Egyptian mythology)
- Solar boat pit discovered 1954 (now in museum on-site)
- Represents the primordial mound from which Egyptians believed earth was created
