# Saqqara - Research

## Overview
- **Location:** 30 km south of Cairo, Egypt
- **Time Period:** Used for 3,000+ years (Early Dynastic to Ptolemaic)
- **Significance:** Egypt's oldest complete stone building complex
- **UNESCO Status:** Part of Memphis and its Necropolis World Heritage Site (1979)

## The Step Pyramid of Djoser

### Key Facts
- **Built:** c. 2670 BCE (3rd Dynasty)
- **Architect:** Imhotep (first named architect in history)
- **Height:** 62 meters (203 feet)
- **Structure:** 6 mastabas stacked on top of each other
- **Base:** 121 × 109 meters (397 × 358 feet)
- **Significance:** First pyramid ever built, prototype for all later pyramids

### Innovation
- First large-scale cut stone construction in history
- Imhotep later deified as god of medicine and wisdom
- Complex includes colonnaded entrance, Heb Sed court, temples
- Underground labyrinth of tunnels and chambers (5.7 km of shafts)
- Blue faience tiles once decorated underground chambers

## Other Notable Structures

### Pyramid of Unas
- **Built:** c. 2350 BCE (5th Dynasty)
- **Significance:** Contains oldest known Pyramid Texts
- **Texts:** Spells and incantations for pharaoh's afterlife journey
- **Art:** Beautiful reliefs showing food preparation, daily life

### Pyramid of Teti
- **Built:** c. 2320 BCE (6th Dynasty)
- **Features:** Well-preserved Pyramid Texts
- **Access:** Can enter interior (less crowded than Giza)

### Mastaba Tombs
- **Mastaba of Ti:** Exquisite reliefs of daily life, farming, crafts
- **Mastaba of Ptahhotep:** Wisdom literature, beautiful paintings
- **Mastaba of Mereruka:** Largest non-royal tomb, 32 chambers
- **Mastaba of Kagemni:** Vibrant colors, hunting scenes

### Serapeum
- **Purpose:** Burial place of sacred Apis bulls
- **Discovery:** Auguste Mariette, 1851
- **Sarcophagi:** Massive granite boxes (up to 100 tons each)
- **Mystery:** How were these moved and placed?

## Modern Experience
- **Getting There:** 1 hour from Cairo by car/taxi
- **Tickets:** Less expensive than Giza, fewer tourists
- **Best Time:** Weekday mornings (avoid Friday, Muslim holy day)
- **Duration:** Plan 3-4 hours minimum
- **Facilities:** Basic, bring water and snacks

## Why Visit Saqqara?
- Less crowded than Giza (more intimate experience)
- See evolution of pyramid construction
- Better preserved interior reliefs and colors
- Understand 3,000 years of burial practices
- Walk where ancient Egyptians walked for millennia
- See the FIRST pyramid ever built

## Practical Tips
- Wear comfortable walking shoes (extensive site)
- Bring sun protection (limited shade)
- Hire a guide for context (highly recommended)
- Combine with nearby Dahshur (Bent Pyramid, Red Pyramid)
- Photography allowed in most areas (no flash inside tombs)

## Historical Significance
- Named after Sokar, ancient Egyptian god of the necropolis
- Served as cemetery for Memphis, ancient capital of Egypt
- Over 15 pyramids within the complex
- Thousands of mastaba tombs of nobles and officials
- Continuous use from 1st Dynasty (c. 3100 BCE) to Ptolemaic period
