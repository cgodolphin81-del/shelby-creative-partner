## Platform-Specific Strategies

### Instagram
**Frequency:** Daily for first week, then 3-4x/week
**Content Mix:**
- 40% Episode highlights (quotes, clips, images)
- 30% Educational (history facts, travel tips)
- 20% Behind-the-scenes (creation process)
- 10% Community (listener feedback, Q&A)
**Stories:** Daily during launch week
**Reels:** 3-5 during launch week, then 1-2/week
**Hashtags:** 15-20 per post (mix of popular and niche)

### Twitter/X
**Frequency:** 3-5 tweets/day during launch week
**Content:**
- Episode facts and insights
- Thread on launch day
- Engagement with history/travel community
- Retweet listener reactions
**Strategy:** Use threads for longer content, engage with Egyptology accounts

### LinkedIn
**Frequency:** 2-3 posts/week
**Content:**
- Leadership/legacy lessons from pyramids
- Professional reflection on building lasting work
- Article: "What 4,500-Year-Old Stones Teach Us About Legacy"
**Tone:** Professional, insightful, thought leadership
**Goal:** Reach professional audience, establish authority

### Facebook
**Frequency:** Daily during launch week, then 3-4x/week
**Content:**
- Episode announcements
- Longer-form reflections
- Community discussion posts
- Share blog posts
**Groups:** Share in travel/history podcast groups (follow group rules)

### TikTok/Reels
**Frequency:** 1-2/day during launch week, then 3-5/week
**Content:**
- Quick facts ("3 things you didn't know about pyramids")
- Emotional hooks ("Standing before 4,500 years of history")
- Travel tips ("Visiting the pyramids? Watch this first")

**Strategy:** Hook in first 3 seconds, strong CTA, trending audio when relevant

### YouTube
**Frequency:** 1 main video + 2-3 Shorts during launch week
**Content:**

**SEO:** Optimize title, description, tags for "pyramids," "Egypt," "ancient history"

1. T-7 days: "Coming Soon" teaser

## Crisis Management

### Potential Issues & Responses

**Issue:** Historical inaccuracy claims
**Response:** "We based our research on [sources]. We welcome respectful dialogue and will correct any errors. Thank you for engaging."

**Issue:** Negative reviews/comments
**Response:** Acknowledge, don't argue. "Thanks for your perspective. This episode resonated differently with different listeners, and that's okay."

**Issue:** Low initial engagement
**Response:** Don't panic. Continue promotion schedule. Engage authentically. Some episodes take time to find their audience.

**Issue:** Technical issues (audio quality, platform problems)
**Response:** Acknowledge quickly, fix if possible, move forward. Don't over-apologize.
