# Stockholm Travel Guide - Episode 107 Companion

## Getting There

### By Air
**Stockholm Arlanda Airport (ARN)**
- Main international airport, 42 km north of city
- **Arlanda Express**: Fastest option, 20 minutes to Central Station, 320 SEK (~$30)
- **Flygbussarna**: Airport coach, 45 minutes, 129 SEK (~$12)
- **Taxi**: 40-50 minutes, 450-550 SEK (~$45-55), use official taxi companies
- **Public transport**: SL bus + metro, cheapest but slowest, ~60 minutes, 50 SEK

**Stockholm Bromma Airport (BMA)**
- Domestic and some European flights, 8 km west of city
- **Flygbussarna**: 20 minutes to Cityterminalen, 129 SEK
- **Taxi**: 20 minutes, 300-350 SEK
- **Bus**: SL bus 152, connect to metro

**Stockholm Skavsta (NYO) & Västerås (VST)**
- Budget airline airports (Ryanair, Wizz Air)
- 100+ km from city
- **Flygbussarna**: 80-90 minutes, 159-179 SEK
- Plan extra travel time

### By Train
**Stockholm Central Station**
- **From Copenhagen**: SJ high-speed train, 5.5 hours, from 300 SEK
- **From Oslo**: SJ/Norwegian, 6 hours, from 400 SEK
- **From Gothenburg**: SJ, 3 hours, from 250 SEK
- Book in advance at sj.se for best prices
- Night trains available from southern Sweden and Germany

### By Ferry/Cruise
- **From Helsinki**: Viking Line, Tallink Silja (overnight, 16-17 hours)
- **From Turku**: Tallink Silja (overnight, 11 hours)
- **From Baltic capitals**: Various cruise lines (day trips and overnight)
- Arrive at Stadsgården or Frihamnen terminals, both close to city center

### By Car
- Well-connected via E4, E18, E20 highways
- **Congestion tax**: 7-18:30 weekdays, 11-35 SEK per passage, max 135 SEK/day
- **Parking**: Expensive in city center, 30-60 SEK/hour
- Consider park-and-ride at metro stations outside center

## Getting Around

### Public Transport (SL)
**Stockholm Public Transport (SL)** is excellent and comprehensive.

**Tickets:**
- **Single ticket**: 49 SEK, valid 75 minutes on all transport
- **24-hour pass**: 165 SEK
- **72-hour pass**: 360 SEK
- **7-day pass**: 580 SEK
- **30-day pass**: 970 SEK
- Children (7-17): Half price
- Children under 7: Free

**Where to buy:**
- SL app (easiest)
- Ticket machines at metro stations
- Pressbyrån/7-Eleven convenience stores
- SL Center (Central Station)

**Metro (Tunnelbana)**
- 3 lines: Red, Green, Blue
- 100 stations, many are "art galleries" with unique designs
- Runs 5 AM - 1 AM (weekends until 2:30 AM)
- Clean, safe, efficient

**Buses**
- Extensive network covering entire region
- Night buses (N-prefix) on weekends
- Same tickets as metro

**Trams**
- Line 7 to Djurgården (museums, ABBA, Vasa)
- Tvärbanan (cross-city tram)
- Nockebybanan (western suburbs)

**Commuter Trains (Pendeltåg)**
- Connect outer suburbs and nearby towns
- Useful for day trips (Sigtuna, Uppsala)
- Same SL tickets within Stockholm county

**Ferries**
- **Djurgårdsfärjan**: Line 82, Central Station to Djurgården, included in SL pass
- **Waxholmsbolaget**: Archipelago boats, separate tickets
- **Stringferry**: Free ferry between Slussen and Djurgården

### Biking
- **City Bikes (Stadshjulen)**: Seasonal (April-October), 220 SEK/week, 650 SEK/season
- **Donkey Republic**: App-based bike share, year-round
- **Bike rental shops**: 200-400 SEK/day
- Dedicated bike lanes throughout city
- Flat terrain, very bike-friendly

### Walking
- Stockholm is extremely walkable
- Most attractions within 30 minutes of each other
- Gamla Stan best explored on foot
- Comfortable shoes essential (cobblestones!)

### Taxis/Rideshare
- **Uber**: Available, reliable pricing
- **Bolt**: Often cheaper than Uber
- **Official taxis**: Look for "Taxi Stockholm" or "Taxi Kurir"
- **Avoid**: Unmarked cars, fixed-price scams at tourist areas
- Always agree on price or ensure meter is running

- ATMs available but rarely needed
- 1 USD ≈ 10-11 SEK (varies)

- 1 GBP ≈ 13-14 SEK (varies)

## Best Times to Visit

### Summer (June-August)
**Weather:** 18-25°C (64-77°F), occasional days up to 30°C
**Daylight:** 18+ hours, midnight sun effect
**Pros:**
- Best weather, warmest temperatures
- Long days, maximum sightseeing time
- Outdoor cafes, parks, archipelago accessible
- Festivals and events
- All attractions open
**Cons:**
- Peak tourist season, crowded
- Highest prices
- Some locals away on vacation (July)
- Can be surprisingly hot

**Best for:** First-time visitors, archipelago, outdoor activities, festivals

### Autumn (September-October)
**Weather:** 8-15°C (46-59°F), crisp air
**Daylight:** Decreasing, but still reasonable
**Pros:**
- Fewer tourists
- Beautiful fall colors
- Cultural season begins (theater, concerts)
- Lower prices than summer
- Pleasant walking weather
**Cons:**
- Shorter days
- More rain
- Some outdoor activities close
- Archipelago less accessible

**Best for:** Culture, photography, city exploration, budget travelers

### Winter (November-March)
**Weather:** -5 to 5°C (23-41°F), snow common Dec-Feb
**Daylight:** Very short (6 hours in December)
**Pros:**
- Christmas markets (late Nov-Dec)
- Cozy atmosphere (mysig)
- Lowest prices (except Christmas/New Year)
- Fewest tourists
- Ice skating, winter activities
- Chance of northern lights (rare)
**Cons:**
- Very short days
- Cold, can be depressing
- Some attractions reduced hours
- Archipelago mostly inaccessible
- Gray, dark November especially bleak

**Best for:** Christmas markets, cozy cafes, budget travel, winter sports nearby

### Spring (April-May)
**Weather:** 5-15°C (41-59°F), unpredictable
**Daylight:** Rapidly increasing
**Pros:**
- Nature blooming
- Fewer tourists
- Lower prices
- Longer days by May
- Spring festivals (Walpurgis Night, April 30)
**Cons:**
- Unpredictable weather
- Can still be quite cold in April
- Some seasonal attractions not yet open

**Best for:** Budget travelers, avoiding crowds, spring celebrations

### Special Events
- **Walpurgis Night (April 30)**: Bonfires, spring celebration
- **National Day (June 6)**: Parades, free museum entry
- **Midsummer (late June)**: Traditional celebrations, city quieter
- **Stockholm Culture Festival (August)**: Free concerts, performances
- **Nobel Week (December)**: Nobel Prize ceremonies, special events
- **Christmas Markets (late Nov-Dec)**: Gamla Stan, Skansen

## Swedish Culture & Etiquette

### Social Norms

**Punctuality**
- Swedes value punctuality highly
- Arrive on time (or 5 minutes early)
- Being late is considered rude
- If delayed, always call/text

**Personal Space**
- Swedes value personal space
- Don't stand too close in queues
- Avoid unnecessary physical contact with strangers
- Handshakes for greetings, hugs for friends

**Queueing**
- Always queue properly (take a number if system exists)
- Never cut in line
- Wait your turn patiently

**Volume**
- Speak quietly in public
- Loud conversations frowned upon
- Public transport is typically very quiet

**Tipping**
- Not expected but appreciated
- Service charge usually included
- Round up or leave 5-10% for good service
- No tip needed for poor service

### Fika Culture

**What is Fika?**
- Coffee break with something sweet
- Social institution, not just a drink
- Happens 2-3 times daily
- Business meetings often conducted over fika

**Fika Etiquette**
- Accept fika invitations (it's relationship-building)
- Sit down, don't rush
- Typical fika bread: kanelbulle (cinnamon bun), kardemummabulle (cardamom bun)
- Coffee is usually included, but you can order tea

**Fika Spots**
- Vete-Katten (classic, since 1928)
- St. Jakob's Bakery (excellent pastries)
- Drop Coffee (specialty coffee)
- Café Saturnus (vegan options)

### Alcohol Culture

**Systembolaget**
- State monopoly on alcohol sales
- Beer >3.5%, wine, spirits only sold here
- Limited hours (closed Sundays, early evenings)
- Higher prices than other countries
- ID required (20+ for spirits, 18+ for medium beer)

**Restaurants/Bars**
- Alcohol served with meals
- Higher prices than buying at Systembolaget
- Wine by glass or bottle
- Craft beer scene excellent

**Drinking Customs**
- Skål! (cheers) - make eye contact when toasting
- Drinking songs at traditional gatherings
- Moderate drinking preferred over excess
- Public drinking technically illegal (enforcement varies)

### Language

**Swedish Basics**
- **Hej** (hay) = Hello/Hi
- **Tack** (tahck) = Thank you
- **Ja** (ya) = Yes
- **Nej** (nay) = No
- **Ursäkta** (oor-shek-ta) = Excuse me
- **Pratar du engelska?** = Do you speak English?
- **Jag förstår inte** = I don't understand
- **Hej då** (hay doh) = Goodbye

**English Proficiency**
- 90%+ of Swedes speak English fluently
- All signage in both Swedish and English
- No need to learn Swedish for tourism
- Swedes appreciate effort but will switch to English quickly

## Practical Tips

### Safety
- Stockholm is very safe
- Violent crime rare
- Watch for pickpockets in tourist areas
- Normal city precautions sufficient
- Emergency number: 112
- Non-emergency police: 114 14

### Connectivity
- **SIM cards**: Telia, Tele2, Telenor, 3
- **Tourist SIM**: Available at airport, 200-400 SEK
- **WiFi**: Widely available, free in most cafes, hotels, public spaces
- **EU roaming**: Works if you have EU SIM

### Electricity
- **Voltage**: 230V, 50Hz
- **Plug type**: C, F (two round pins)
- **Adapters**: Needed for US/UK visitors
- USB charging widely available

### Water
- **Tap water**: Excellent quality, drink freely
- **Restaurants**: Can ask for tap water (may charge small fee)
- **Public fountains**: Available in parks, summer months

### Shopping
- **Stores**: Typically open 10-18 (weekdays), 10-16 (Saturday), closed Sunday
- **Grocery**: ICA, Coop, Willys (open later, some 24/7)
- **VAT refund**: Non-EU residents can claim VAT refund on purchases over 200 SEK
- **Credit cards**: Accepted everywhere, even small purchases
- **Cash**: Rarely needed, but good to have 200-500 SEK emergency cash

### Weather Preparedness
- **Summer**: Light layers, rain jacket, comfortable walking shoes
- **Winter**: Warm coat, layers, hat, gloves, waterproof boots
- **Spring/Autumn**: Layers, rain gear, unpredictable weather
- **Always**: Comfortable walking shoes (cobblestones!)

### Accessibility
- Stockholm is generally accessible
- Metro stations have elevators (check SL app)
- Most museums accessible
- Cobblestones in Gamla Stan challenging for wheelchairs
- Accessible toilets widely available

## Recommended Itineraries

### 1 Day (Express)
- Morning: Gamla Stan (Stortorget, Palace, Cathedral)
- Lunch: Traditional Swedish in Gamla Stan
- Afternoon: Vasa Museum
- Evening: Fika, waterfront walk
- Dinner: Södermalm

### 2 Days (Essentials)
**Day 1:** Gamla Stan, Royal Palace, Vasa Museum, evening fika
**Day 2:** ABBA Museum, Skansen, archipelago boat tour, Södermalm views

### 3 Days (Complete)
**Day 1:** Gamla Stan full exploration, City Hall, Nobel Museum
**Day 2:** Djurgården (Vasa, ABBA, Skansen), evening concert or show
**Day 3:** Archipelago day trip (Vaxholm or Grinda), Fotografiska

### 5 Days (In-Depth)
**Days 1-3:** As above
**Day 4:** Day trip to Uppsala or Sigtuna, Nordiska Museet
**Day 5:** Design shopping (Svenskt Tenn, Östermalm), Moderna Museet, farewell dinner

### 7 Days (Immersive)
**Days 1-5:** As above
**Day 6:** Extended archipelago exploration (Sandhamn overnight or full-day kayaking)
**Day 7:** Local neighborhoods (Vasastan, Kungsholmen), last-minute shopping, long fika
