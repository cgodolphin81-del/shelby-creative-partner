## Getting There

### By Air

**Istanbul Airport (IST)**
- **Location:** European side, 40 km from city center
- **Opened:** 2019 (main international hub)
- **Airlines:** Turkish Airlines, Pegasus, all major international carriers
- **To City Center:**
  - **Havaist Bus:** $3–5 USD, 60–90 minutes, multiple routes
  - **Metro (M11):** $1 USD, 40 minutes to Kağıthane, transfer to city
  - **Taxi:** $25–40 USD, 45–60 minutes depending on traffic
  - **Private Transfer:** $35–60 USD, pre-booked

**Sabiha Gökçen Airport (SAW)**
- **Location:** Asian side, 45 km from city center
- **Airlines:** Pegasus (hub), budget carriers, some international
- **To City Center:**
  - **Havabus Shuttle:** $3 USD, 60–90 minutes to Taksim
  - **Metro (M4 + transfer):** $1 USD, 70 minutes
  - **Taxi:** $30–50 USD, 60–90 minutes

**Tip:** Istanbul Airport (IST) is more convenient for most tourists. Book flights into IST if possible.

### By Train

**International Routes:**
- **Bucharest to Istanbul:** Daily, ~12 hours (border crossing can be slow)
- **Sofia to Istanbul:** Daily, ~10 hours
- **Thessaloniki to Istanbul:** Seasonal, ~8 hours

**Station:** Halkalı Station (European side), then connect via Marmaray or bus

**Note:** Train travel to Istanbul is adventurous but slow. Flying is usually more practical.

### By Bus

**Long-Distance Bus:**
- **From Europe:** Multiple daily buses from Greece, Bulgaria, Romania
- **From Asia:** Buses from Georgia, Iran, Syria (check current safety advisories)
- **Terminal:** Büyük İstanbul Otogarı (Bayrampaşa), European side
- **To City:** Metro (M1A) or taxi from bus station

**Note:** Bus travel is budget-friendly but time-consuming. Best for regional travelers.

### By Cruise Ship

**Ports:**
- **Galataport:** New cruise terminal in Karaköy (opened 2021), walking distance to Sultanahmet
- **Port of Istanbul:** Older terminal, shuttle to city center

**Major Lines:** Royal Caribbean, MSC, Norwegian, Celebrity (seasonal, mainly April–October)

## Getting Around

### Public Transportation

**Istanbulkart:**
- **What:** Reloadable transit card for all public transport
- **Cost:** $2 USD for card + credit (available at airports, metro stations, kiosks)
- **Discount:** 30–50% cheaper than single tickets
- **Where:** Metro, tram, bus, ferry, funicular, cable car

**Metro:**
- **Coverage:** Extensive network, expanding rapidly
- **Key Lines:**
  - **M1A/M1B:** Airport to city, western suburbs
  - **M2:** Hacıosman to Yenikapı (crosses Golden Horn)
  - **M4:** Asian side metro
  - **Marmaray:** Undersea rail tunnel connecting Europe and Asia
- **Cost:** ~$0.50–1 USD per ride with Istanbulkart
- **Hours:** 6:00 AM–midnight (varies by line)

**Tram:**
- **T1 Line:** Most useful for tourists
  - **Route:** Bağcılar to Kabataş
  - **Stops:** Sultanahmet (Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque), Grand Bazaar, Eminönü (ferries), Karaköy, Galata Bridge
- **Cost:** ~$0.50 USD with Istanbulkart
- **Hours:** 6:00 AM–midnight

**Ferry:**
- **Operator:** Şehir Hatları (public), Turyol, Dentur (private)
- **Key Routes:**
  - Eminönü to Kadıköy (Asian side)
  - Eminönü to Üsküdar
  - Karaköy to Kadıköy
  - Bosphorus tour ferries (longer scenic routes)
- **Cost:** $0.50–2 USD depending on route
- **Experience:** Best value sightseeing in Istanbul
- **Tip:** Take the sunset ferry for spectacular views

**Bus:**
- **Coverage:** Extensive, reaches areas metro doesn't
- **Challenge:** Can be crowded, routes confusing for tourists
- **Tip:** Use Moovit or Google Maps for real-time routing

**Taxi & Ride-Sharing:**
- **Yellow Taxis:** Metered, relatively inexpensive
  - **Base fare:** ~$0.50 USD
  - **Per km:** ~$0.70 USD
  - **Tip:** Ensure meter is running; agree on approximate fare beforehand
- **Ride-Sharing:** BiTaksi app (local), Uber (calls yellow taxis)
- **Caution:** Traffic can be severe; public transport often faster

### Walking

**Walkability:**
- **Sultanahmet:** Very walkable, most sites within 10–15 minutes
- **Beyoğlu/Galata:** Walkable, hilly
- **Bosphorus:** Waterfront promenades in many sections
- **Challenge:** Hills, cobblestones, crowds

**Tip:** Wear comfortable shoes. Istanbul involves more walking than you expect.

## Where to Stay

### Sultanahmet (Historic Peninsula)

**Best For:** First-time visitors, history lovers, proximity to major sites

**Pros:**
- Walking distance to Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, Topkapı Palace
- Numerous hotels at all price points
- Safe, tourist-friendly
- Tram access (T1 line)

**Cons:**
- Touristy, can feel like a museum after dark
- Fewer local restaurants and nightlife
- Quieter at night

**Price Range:**
- **Budget:** $30–60 USD/night (hostels, basic hotels)
- **Mid-range:** $80–150 USD/night (3–4 star hotels)
- **Luxury:** $200–500+ USD/night (historic properties, 5-star)

**Recommended Hotels:**
- **Budget:** Sultanahmet Palace Hotel, Cheers Hostel
- **Mid-range:** Hotel Amira, White House Hotel
- **Luxury:** Four Seasons Sultanahmet (former prison), Sirkeci Mansion

### Beyoğlu/Galata/Karaköy

**Best For:** Nightlife, dining, shopping, trendy neighborhoods

**Pros:**
- Vibrant, cosmopolitan atmosphere
- Excellent restaurants, bars, cafés
- Galata Tower, Istiklal Street, Taksim Square
- Ferry and tram access

**Cons:**
- Hilly (Galata especially)
- Can be noisy
- Further from historic sites (but well-connected)

**Price Range:**
- **Budget:** $25–50 USD/night
- **Mid-range:** $70–140 USD/night
- **Luxury:** $180–400+ USD/night

**Recommended Hotels:**
- **Budget:** Galata Hill Hotel, Hostel One
- **Mid-range:** Witt Istanbul, Galata Bridge Hotel
- **Luxury:** The Galata Istanbul Hotel, CVK Park Bosphorus

### Kadıköy (Asian Side)

**Best For:** Local experience, foodies, longer stays, budget travelers

**Pros:**
- Authentic, less touristy
- Outstanding food scene
- Lower prices
- Ferry ride to European side (scenic commute)

**Cons:**
- Further from major tourist sites
- Fewer luxury hotels
- Nightlife more low-key

**Price Range:**
- **Budget:** $20–40 USD/night
- **Mid-range:** $50–100 USD/night
- **Luxury:** Limited options

**Recommended Hotels:**
- **Budget:** Kadıköy Hostel, Mingle Hotel
- **Mid-range:** Hotel Kadıköy, Suadiye Hotel

### Beşiktaş/Ortaköy (Bosphorus)

**Best For:** Bosphorus views, upscale experience, ferry access

**Pros:**
- Beautiful waterfront location
- Ortaköy Mosque, weekend market
- Ferry connections
- Upscale dining

**Cons:**
- Higher prices
- Further from Sultanahmet
- Can be crowded on weekends

**Price Range:**
- **Mid-range:** $100–180 USD/night
- **Luxury:** $250–600+ USD/night

**Recommended Hotels:**
- **Luxury:** Çırağan Palace Kempinski (former Ottoman palace), Swissôtel The Bosphorus

## When to Visit

### Spring (April–May) ★ BEST

**Weather:** 15–22°C (59–72°F), mild and pleasant
**Crowds:** Moderate, increasing toward May
**Pros:**
- Tulips bloom throughout the city (Istanbul Tulip Festival, April)
- Ideal walking weather
- Outdoor cafés open
- Good light for photography

**Cons:**
- Some rain possible
- Prices rising toward peak season

**Events:**
- Istanbul Tulip Festival (April)
- Istanbul Film Festival (April)
- National Sovereignty and Children's Day (April 23)

### Summer (June–August)

**Weather:** 22–30°C (72–86°F), can be hot and humid
**Crowds:** Peak tourist season, very crowded
**Pros:**
- Long daylight hours (sunset ~8:30 PM)
- Bosphorus cruises most pleasant
- All attractions open
- Vibrant street life

**Cons:**
- Crowds at major sites (arrive early!)
- Higher prices
- Can be uncomfortably hot
- Traffic worse

**Events:**
- Istanbul Music Festival (June)
- Istanbul Jazz Festival (July)
- Rock'n Coke Music Festival (August, some years)

### Autumn (September–November) ★ BEST

**Weather:** 15–25°C (59–77°F), pleasant, especially September–October
**Crowds:** Decreasing after October, more manageable
**Pros:**
- Ideal weather (especially September–early October)
- Fewer tourists after mid-October
- Cultural season begins (theater, concerts)
- Golden light for photography
- Good value (prices drop in late October)

**Cons:**
- Rain increases in November
- Some outdoor activities close late season

**Events:**
- Istanbul Biennial (alternate years, fall)
- Istanbul Design Week (October)
- Republic Day (October 29, fireworks)

### Winter (December–March)

**Weather:** 5–12°C (41–54°F), can be cold and rainy, occasional snow
**Crowds:** Minimal, except for New Year's
**Pros:**
- Fewest tourists
- Lowest prices (except New Year's)
- Museums and indoor sites ideal
- Cozy atmosphere (tea houses, hammams)
- Possible snow (magical, but rare)

**Cons:**
- Cold, can be rainy
- Shorter daylight hours
- Some outdoor attractions less enjoyable
- Bosphorus cruises cold (but still operate)

**Events:**
- New Year's Eve (major celebrations, Taksim Square)
- Istanbul Short Film Festival (February–March)

## Culture & Etiquette

### Greetings

- **Handshake:** Common in business and formal settings
- **Cheek kisses:** Two kisses (right-left) among friends and family
- **Greetings:** "Merhaba" (hello), "Günaydın" (good morning), "İyi akşamlar" (good evening)
- **Respect:** Use "Bey" (Mr.) and "Hanım" (Ms.) with first names for respect

### Dress Code

**General:**
- Istanbul is cosmopolitan and relatively liberal
- Dress modestly when visiting mosques (covered shoulders, knees; headscarf for women)
- Beachwear only at beaches/pools
- Smart casual for upscale restaurants

**Mosques:**
- Shoulders and knees must be covered (both genders)
- Women: Headscarf required (often available at entrance)
- Remove shoes before entering prayer areas
- Avoid prayer times if visiting as tourist (check schedule)

### Dining Etiquette

- **Tea/Coffee:** Offered as hospitality; accepting is polite
- **Bread:** Never cut with knife; tear with hands
- **Eating:** Wait for host to begin; keep hands visible
- **Paying:** Offering to pay is polite; host often insists
- **Tipping:** 10% customary if service not included

### Social Customs

- **Hospitality:** Turks are famously hospitable; accept offers of tea/food
- **Bargaining:** Expected in bazaars, not in shops/restaurants
- **Shoes:** Remove when entering homes (and mosques)
- **Ramadan:** Respect fasting Muslims; eating/drinking in public during daylight hours can be seen as insensitive (though tolerated in tourist areas)
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing people, especially in mosques or rural areas

### Taboos & Sensitivities

- **Atatürk:** Mustafa Kemal Atatürk (founder of modern Turkey) is highly revered; criticism is offensive and illegal
- **Politics:** Avoid heated political discussions, especially regarding Kurdistan, Armenia, Cyprus
- **Religion:** Respect Islamic practices; don't enter mosques during prayer
- **National Pride:** Turks are proud of their country and culture; genuine interest is appreciated

## Food & Drink

### Must-Try Dishes

**Breakfast (Kahvaltı):**
- **Menemen:** Scrambled eggs with tomatoes, peppers
- **Simit:** Sesame bread rings
- **Peynir:** Various Turkish cheeses
- **Zeytin:** Olives (green and black)
- **Kaymak & Bal:** Clotted cream with honey
- **Çay:** Turkish tea (unlimited refills)

**Main Dishes:**
- **Kebabs:** Adana, Urfa, Şiş, Döner (many varieties)
- **Köfte:** Turkish meatballs
- **Lahmacun:** Thin Turkish "pizza" with minced meat
- **Pide:** Boat-shaped flatbread with toppings
- **İskender:** Döner kebab over pita with tomato sauce and yogurt
- **Balık Ekmek:** Fish sandwich (Eminönü specialty)

**Meze (Appetizers):**
- **Humus:** Chickpea dip
- **Haydari:** Thick yogurt with garlic and herbs
- **Patlıcan Salatası:** Eggplant salad
- **Dolma:** Stuffed grape leaves or vegetables
- **Calamari:** Fried squid (coastal specialty)

**Desserts:**
- **Baklava:** Layers of phyllo with nuts and syrup
- **Künefe:** Shredded phyllo with cheese, soaked in syrup
- **Sütlaç:** Rice pudding
- **Lokum:** Turkish delight (many flavors)
- **Dondurma:** Turkish ice cream (stretchy, theatrical serving)

**Drinks:**
- **Çay:** Black tea, served in tulip glasses
- **Türk Kahvesi:** Turkish coffee, strong and unfiltered
- **Ayran:** Yogurt drink, salty and refreshing
- **Şalgam:** Fermented turnip juice (spicy, acquired taste)
- **Raki:** Anise-flavored spirit, "lion's milk" when mixed with water
- **Wine:** Turkish wines improving rapidly (Kalecik Karası, Öküzgözü, Narince)

### Food Neighborhoods

**Sultanahmet:** Touristy but convenient; seek out side streets
**Beyoğlu:** Diverse, from street food to fine dining
**Kadıköy:** Foodie paradise, local favorites, lower prices
**Beşiktaş:** Fish restaurants, local scene
**Nişantaşı:** Upscale dining, international cuisine
**Karaköy:** Trendy cafés, brunch spots

### Dietary Restrictions

- **Vegetarian:** Widely available (meze, pide, lahmacun without meat, vegetable dishes)
- **Vegan:** Increasing options, especially in Kadıköy and Beyoğlu
- **Halal:** Almost all food is halal
- **Gluten-Free:** Limited awareness, but naturally GF options exist (kebabs, rice, grilled fish)
- **Allergies:** Communicate clearly; English menus often list ingredients

## Safety & Practical Tips

### Safety

**Overall:** Istanbul is generally safe for tourists

**Common Issues:**
- **Pickpocketing:** Watch bags in crowded areas (bazaars, public transport, Taksim)
- **Scams:** Overly friendly strangers, shoe shine "drops," overpriced carpets
- **Taxi scams:** Ensure meter is running; use BiTaksi app when possible
- **Protests:** Avoid political gatherings; check news

**Emergency Numbers:**
- **Police:** 155
- **Ambulance:** 112
- **Fire:** 110
- **Tourist Police:** +90 212 527 4503 (Sultanahmet)

### Health

- **Tap Water:** Not recommended for drinking; bottled water cheap and available
- **Vaccinations:** None required for Turkey (check current requirements)
- **Healthcare:** Good quality private hospitals; travel insurance recommended
- **Pharmacies:** Widespread; pharmacists can advise on minor ailments
- **Sun:** Summer sun strong; use sunscreen

### Connectivity

- **SIM Cards:** Available at airports and phone shops; bring passport
  - **Providers:** Turkcell, Vodafone, Türk Telekom
  - **Cost:** ~$20–30 USD for tourist package (data + calls)
- **WiFi:** Widely available in hotels, cafés, restaurants
- **Apps to Download:**
  - **BiTaksi:** Taxi hailing
  - **Moovit:** Public transport routing
  - **Google Maps:** Works well in Istanbul
  - **Google Translate:** Turkish offline pack
  - **XE Currency:** Currency conversion

### Money

- **Currency:** Turkish Lira (TRY)
- **ATMs:** Widespread; use bank ATMs (Garanti, İş Bankası, Yapı Kredi)
- **Credit Cards:** Widely accepted; Visa/Mastercard preferred
- **Cash:** Needed for bazaars, small shops, street food
- **Exchange:** Avoid airport exchanges (poor rates); use ATMs or city exchange offices

### Language

- **Turkish:** Official language
- **English:** Widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, restaurants
- **German, Russian, Arabic:** Common in tourist zones
- **Tip:** Learn a few Turkish phrases—appreciated by locals
  - Merhaba (hello)
  - Teşekkür ederim (thank you)
  - Lütfen (please)
  - Evet/Hayır (yes/no)
  - Ne kadar? (how much?)

## Final Tips

1. **Arrive early** at major sites (Hagia Sophia, Topkapı) to avoid crowds
2. **Get an Istanbulkart** immediately—saves money and time
3. **Take the ferry**—best value sightseeing in the city
4. **Wear comfortable shoes**—you'll walk more than expected
5. **Learn basic Turkish phrases**—goes a long way
6. **Bargain in the bazaar**—it's expected and part of the experience
7. **Try everything**—the food scene is world-class
8. **Respect mosque etiquette**—dress modestly, check prayer times
9. **Stay hydrated**—carry water, especially in summer
10. **Embrace the chaos**—Istanbul is overwhelming and that's part of its charm
