## WHEN TO GO

### Best Months by Location

| Location | Season | Peak Months | Why |
|----------|--------|-------------|-----|
| **Iceland** | Sep - Mar | Oct - Feb | Dark nights, accessible roads |
| **Norway (Tromsø)** | Sep - Mar | Dec - Feb | Polar night, reliable activity |
| **Finland (Lapland)** | Sep - Mar | Dec - Feb | Clear skies, winter activities |
| **Canada (Yellowknife)** | Aug - Apr | Nov - Mar | 240+ aurora nights/year |
| **Alaska (Fairbanks)** | Aug - Apr | Sep - Mar | Clear sky capital |

### Avoid These Times
- **May - July**: Midnight sun = no darkness
- **August**: Often too early for reliable darkness
- **Late March**: Increasing daylight hours

### Ideal Trip Length
- **Minimum**: 5 nights (allows for 2-3 clear nights)
- **Recommended**: 7-10 nights (maximizes chances)
- **Extended**: 14+ nights (combine multiple locations)

**Pro Tip**: Stay at least 5 nights in ONE location rather than splitting time. Weather is unpredictable. Patience is rewarded.

## UNDERSTANDING THE KP INDEX

### What Is It?
The KP Index measures geomagnetic activity on a scale of 0-9. Higher numbers = more aurora activity.

### What You Need
| Your Location | Minimum KP | Notes |
|---------------|------------|-------|
| Arctic Circle | KP 2-3 | Often visible even at low activity |
| Iceland | KP 3-4 | Moderate activity needed |
| Southern Norway | KP 4-5 | Further south = need more activity |
| UK/Northern US | KP 6-7 | Rare, requires strong storm |
| Southern Europe/US | KP 8-9 | Extremely rare events only |

### Current Solar Cycle
We're approaching **Solar Maximum (2024-2026)** — the best time in 11 years to see auroras! Solar activity is high, meaning more frequent and intense displays.

## WHAT TO PACK

### Clothing (Layer System)

**Base Layer** (against skin)
- Merino wool thermal top and bottom
- Avoid cotton (retains moisture)
- Brands: Icebreaker, Smartwool, Patagonia

**Mid Layer** (insulation)
- Fleece jacket or down vest
- Wool sweater
- Should trap heat but allow movement

**Outer Layer** (protection)
- Waterproof/windproof jacket (Gore-Tex or similar)
- Insulated winter parka (-20°C rated minimum)
- Snow pants (if standing still for long periods)

**Extremities** (critical!)
- Insulated waterproof boots (Baffin, Sorel, Muck)
- Wool socks (2 pairs, different thicknesses)
- Insulated gloves + thin liner gloves (for camera operation)
- Warm hat (covers ears)
- Neck gaiter or balaclava
- Hand warmers (disposable or rechargeable)

### Photography Gear

**Essential**
- Camera with manual mode (DSLR or mirrorless)
- Wide-angle lens (14-24mm, f/2.8 or faster)
- Sturdy tripod (carbon fiber for cold)
- Extra batteries (cold drains them fast)
- Memory cards (bring more than you think)
- Headlamp with red light mode

**Optional but Helpful**
- Remote shutter release (or use timer)
- Lens warming strips (prevent frost)
- Camera rain cover
- Backpack for gear
- Portable power bank

**Camera Settings Cheat Sheet**
- Mode: Manual
- Aperture: f/2.8 or widest available
- Shutter: 5-25 seconds (start at 15s)
- ISO: 1600-3200 (adjust as needed)
- Focus: Manual, set to infinity
- Format: RAW for editing flexibility
- White Balance: 3500-4000K

### Other Essentials
- Power bank (cold drains phones too)
- Thermos for hot drinks
- Snacks (high energy)
- First aid kit
- Hand sanitizer
- Cash (some remote places don't take cards)
- Portable phone charger
- Emergency blanket

## AURORA TOURS vs. SELF-GUIDED

### Join a Tour If...
- First time chasing aurora
- Don't want to drive in winter conditions
- Want expert guides and photography help
- Prefer not to worry about forecasts
- Traveling solo and want to meet people

**Pros**: Expert knowledge, warm buses, hot drinks, no driving stress
**Cons**: Less flexibility, fixed schedule, more expensive

### Go Independent If...
- Experienced winter driver
- Have your own transport
- Want flexibility on timing/location
- On a tighter budget
- Prefer solitude

**Pros**: Complete flexibility, cheaper, personal experience
**Cons**: All responsibility on you, cold wait alone, need own gear

### Hybrid Approach (Recommended)
- Rent a car for flexibility
- Book 1-2 tours for expert guidance
- Self-guide on other nights
- Best of both worlds!

## PHOTOGRAPHY TIPS

### Before You Go
- Practice manual settings in dark conditions
- Learn to focus at infinity in the dark
- Test your gear in cold if possible
- Download offline maps

### On Location
1. **Arrive before dark** to scout locations
2. **Set up tripod** on stable ground
3. **Compose shot** before aurora appears
4. **Focus manually** using live view and bright star
5. **Take test shots** and adjust settings
6. **Be patient** — aurora comes in waves
7. **Stay warm** — cold hands make mistakes

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Using autofocus (will fail in dark)
- Shutter too long (stars will trail)
- Not bringing extra batteries
- Forgetting to turn off image stabilization on tripod
- Breathing on viewfinder (causes fog)

## SAFETY & ETIQUETTE

### Safety First
- Never chase aurora in dangerous road conditions
- Tell someone where you're going
- Carry emergency supplies in vehicle
- Watch for ice on roads and paths
- Don't wander far from vehicle in remote areas
- Check weather forecasts before heading out

### Aurora Etiquette
- Respect private property
- Don't shine lights at other photographers
- Keep noise down in remote areas
- Pack out all trash
- Follow local guidelines and restrictions

### Cultural Respect
- Learn about indigenous cultures (Sami, Inuit, etc.)
- Support indigenous-owned businesses
- Ask before photographing people
- Respect sacred sites and traditions
