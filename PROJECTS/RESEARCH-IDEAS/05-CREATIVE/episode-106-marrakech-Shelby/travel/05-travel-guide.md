# Episode 106: Marrakech - Travel Guide

**Destination:** Marrakech, Morocco  
**Episode:** 106 - The Desert Jewel  
**Best For:** First-time visitors, culture seekers, photographers, foodies  
**Recommended Stay:** 4-7 days minimum

---

## Getting There

### By Air

**Marrakech Menara Airport (RAK)**
- **Distance from medina:** 6 km (3.7 miles)
- **Airlines:** Royal Air Maroc, EasyJet, Ryanair, Transavia, British Airways, Lufthansa
- **Major hubs:** London, Paris, Amsterdam, Frankfurt, Dubai, Casablanca
- **Flight time from Europe:** 3-4 hours
- **Flight time from US:** 7-9 hours (usually via European connection)

**Airport Transfer Options:**

| Method | Cost (MAD) | Cost (USD) | Time | Notes |
|--------|------------|------------|------|-------|
| **Taxi (petit taxi)** | 50-100 | $5-10 | 15-20 min | Negotiate fare upfront |
| **Pre-arranged riad pickup** | 100-150 | $10-15 | 15-20 min | Most reliable, recommended |
| **Bus (Line 19)** | 30 | $3 | 30 min | Budget option, limited luggage space |

**Pro Tip:** Arrange airport pickup through your riad. They will meet you at arrivals with a sign, help with luggage, and guide you through the medina to the property. Worth every dirham.

---

### By Train

**ONCF (Moroccan National Railways)**
- **From Casablanca:** 2.5-3 hours, 120-180 MAD ($12-18)
- **From Rabat:** 3.5 hours, 150-220 MAD ($15-22)
- **From Fes:** 6-7 hours, 250-350 MAD ($25-35)
- **Station:** Marrakech Ville (Gueliz, new city)
- **Transfer to medina:** 20 min taxi, 30-50 MAD ($3-5)

**Train Classes:**
- **First class:** Comfortable, air-conditioned, recommended
- **Second class:** Budget option, can be crowded

**Booking:** Book online at oncf-voyages.ma or at station. Book in advance for peak season.

### By Bus

**CTM / Supratours**
- **From Casablanca:** 3-4 hours, 80-120 MAD ($8-12)
- **From Agadir:** 3 hours, 100-150 MAD ($10-15)
- **From Essaouira:** 2.5-3 hours, 80-100 MAD ($8-10)
- **Station:** Near medina (walking distance to most riads)

**Budget option, but less comfortable than train.**

## Getting Around

### Within the Medina

**On Foot:** The medina is best explored on foot. Most riads are within 10-15 minutes walk of Jemaa el-Fnaa.

**Petit Taxis:** Small red taxis for short distances within city. Insist on meter or negotiate fare upfront.
- **Starting fare:** 1.50 MAD
- **Per km:** 1.50-2 MAD
- **Medina to Gueliz:** 30-50 MAD ($3-5)

**Grand Taxis:** Larger taxis for longer distances or airport trips. Negotiate fare.

**Note:** Motorbikes and donkey carts navigate medina alleys. Listen for "Balak!" (Watch out!) and step aside.

### Seasonal Breakdown

| Season | Months | Temperature | Crowds | Recommendation |
|--------|--------|-------------|--------|----------------|
| **Spring** | Mar-May | 23-30°C (73-86°F) | High | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Ideal |
| **Summer** | Jun-Aug | 36-40°C+ (97-104°F+) | Moderate | ⭐⭐ Too hot |
| **Fall** | Sep-Nov | 25-32°C (77-90°F) | High | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Ideal |
| **Winter** | Dec-Feb | 12-20°C (54-68°F) | Low-Moderate | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Good value |

### Best Time for Specific Activities

- **Medina exploration:** Early morning (8-10 AM) or late afternoon (4-6 PM)
- **Jardin Majorelle:** First entry (8 AM) to avoid crowds
- **Atlas Mountains hiking:** Spring (March-May) or fall (September-October)
- **Photography:** Golden hour (sunrise/sunset), blue hour (dusk)
- **Shopping:** Weekday mornings (less crowded)
- **Jemaa el-Fnaa:** Evening (7-9 PM) for food stalls and performers

### Religious Holidays

- **Ramadan:** Dates vary (Islamic lunar calendar). Many restaurants closed during daylight. Evening festivities are special. Respectful dress essential.
- **Eid al-Fitr / Eid al-Adha:** Major holidays. Some attractions may close. Book accommodation early.

| **Rugs/carpets** | Souk Sebbaghine | 500-5,000+ | Berber, wool, negotiation essential |
| **Ceramics** | Souk Fekharine | 100-500 | Tagine pots, decorative tiles |
| **Argan oil** | Various | 80-200 per 100ml | Women's cooperatives, pure oil |
| **Textiles** | Souk Semmarine | 100-600 | Kaftans, scarves, fabrics |

### Haggling Guide

**The Rules:**
1. **Start low:** Offer 30-40% of asking price
2. **Be friendly:** It's a conversation, not a confrontation
3. **Take your time:** Tea may be offered. Accept it.
4. **Be prepared to walk away:** This is your strongest negotiating tool
5. **Know when to stop:** If you've reached a fair price, buy it
6. **Cash only:** Most souk vendors don't accept cards

**Sample Negotiation:**
- Vendor asks: 500 MAD
- You offer: 150 MAD
- Vendor counters: 400 MAD
- You counter: 200 MAD
- Vendor: 350 MAD
- You: 250 MAD, walk away slowly
- Vendor: "Okay, okay, 280 MAD, final price!"
- You: (Consider if fair) Buy or continue walking

**Fixed-Price Shops:** Some modern boutiques have fixed prices. Usually marked. Less negotiation, but fair pricing.

## Cultural Etiquette

### Dress Code

- **Women:** Cover shoulders and knees. Loose-fitting clothing respected. Scarf useful for mosque visits.
- **Men:** Shorts acceptable, but long pants more respectful. No shirtless walking.
- **General:** Modest dress shows respect. Especially important in medina and religious sites.

### Greetings & Interactions

- **Greeting:** "Salam alaykum" (peace be upon you), response "Wa alaykum salam"
- **Right hand:** Use right hand for eating, passing items, handshakes
- **Photography:** Ask permission before photographing people. Some expect payment.
- **Mosques:** Non-Muslims cannot enter most mosques (Koutoubia exterior only)
- **Ramadan:** Do not eat, drink, or smoke in public during daylight hours

### Tipping

| Service | Tip |
|---------|-----|
| **Restaurant** | 10% if service not included |
| **Riad staff** | 20-50 MAD/day |
| **Tour guide** | 100-200 MAD/day |
| **Driver** | 50-100 MAD/day |
| **Hammam attendant** | 50-100 MAD |
| **Porter** | 10-20 MAD per bag |

## Practical Information

### Currency

- **Currency:** Moroccan Dirham (MAD)
- **Exchange rate (approx):** 1 USD = 10 MAD, 1 EUR = 10.5 MAD, 1 GBP = 12 MAD
- **ATMs:** Widely available in Gueliz and medina edges
- **Credit cards:** Accepted in hotels, upscale restaurants, some shops
- **Cash economy:** Souks, small restaurants, taxis, tips require cash

**Money Tip:** Withdraw larger amounts to minimize fees. Keep small bills (20, 50 MAD) for tips and small purchases.

### Language

- **Official:** Arabic, Berber (Amazigh)
- **Common:** French (widely spoken), English (tourist areas), Spanish (some)
- **Useful phrases:**
  - Shukran = Thank you
  - Salam alaykum = Hello / Peace be upon you
  - Bshhal? = How much?
  - Ghali = Expensive
  - Maalsh = No problem / It's okay

### Connectivity

- **WiFi:** Most riads, cafés, restaurants have WiFi
- **SIM cards:** Maroc Telecom, Orange, Inwi. Available at airport, shops. 10GB ~100 MAD ($10)
- **Coverage:** Good in city, variable in mountains

### Safety

**Marrakech is generally safe,** but use common sense:

- **Petty crime:** Pickpocketing in crowded areas (souks, Jemaa el-Fnaa). Keep valuables secure.
- **Scams:** "Guide" offers, fake shops, inflated prices. Politely decline unsolicited help.
- **Solo female travelers:** Generally safe. Dress modestly. Avoid dim areas at night. Walk confidently.
- **Emergency numbers:** Police (19), Ambulance (15), Fire (15)

### Health

- **Water:** Drink bottled water. Avoid tap water.
- **Food safety:** Eat freshly cooked food. Peel fruits. Avoid raw vegetables if sensitive.
- **Sun protection:** High SPF, hat, sunglasses (especially summer)
- **Travel insurance:** Recommended for all travelers

## Final Tips

1. **Learn basic Arabic/French phrases** - Locals appreciate the effort
2. **Carry small bills** - Essential for tips, small purchases
3. **Drink lots of water** - Especially in summer
4. **Embrace getting lost** - Best discoveries happen off the map
5. **Say yes to mint tea** - It's hospitality, not just a drink
6. **Haggle with a smile** - It's a game, not a battle
7. **Respect the call to prayer** - Pause, listen, appreciate
8. **Book riad airport transfer** - Stress-free arrival
9. **Visit Jardin Majorelle early** - 8 AM entry avoids crowds
10. **Trust your instincts** - If something feels off, walk away
