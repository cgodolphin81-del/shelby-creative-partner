## Overview

**Location:** Great Barrier Reef Marine Park, Queensland, Australia  
**Coordinates:** Spans 14 degrees of latitude (approximately 10°S to 24°S)  
**Size:** 348,700 square kilometers (134,600 sq mi)  
**Length:** Over 2,300 kilometers (1,400 miles)  
**Width:** 60 to 250 kilometers (37 to 155 miles)  
**Visibility from Space:** Yes - the world's largest living structure visible from orbit

---

## The Reef System

### Structure
- **2,900+ individual coral reefs**
- **1,050+ islands** (continental and coral cays)
- **600 continental islands**
- **150 inshore mangrove islands**
- **300 coral cays**
- **70 bioregions** (30 reef, 40 non-reefal)
- **Average depth:** 35 meters near shore, 2,000+ meters on outer reefs

### Three Main Zones

#### 1. Inshore Marine Area
- Shallow waters near the mainland
- Seagrass meadows and coral reefs
- Critical nursery habitat for juvenile fish, turtles, and dugongs
- Higher sediment and nutrient levels from land runoff

#### 2. Mid-Shelf Area
- Located between inner and outer reef zones
- Patch reefs and isolated coral formations ("bommies")
- Seagrass meadows
- Habitat for juvenile fish and diverse marine species

#### 3. Outer Shelf Area
- Along the continental shelf edge
- Deeper waters with expansive coral reef systems
- Large barrier reefs and atolls
- Pristine coral formations with excellent visibility (30-50+ meters)
- Highest biodiversity

## Marine Biodiversity

### Coral Species
- **450+ species of hard coral**
- **Soft corals, sea pens, and sea fans** throughout
- **Fast-growing Acropora** - crucial for reef recovery but highly susceptible to bleaching
- **Massive corals** (Porites, etc.) - slower growing but more resilient

### Fish Species
- **1,625 species of fish** documented
- **Notable species:**
  - Clownfish (Amphiprioninae) - living among sea anemones
  - Parrotfish - produce up to 5 tonnes of sand per year through digestion
  - Cleaner wrasse - can clean up to 2,300 fish daily
  - Coral reef pygmy goby - shortest vertebrate lifespan (3.5 weeks)
  - Reef sharks (grey reef, whitetip, blacktip)
  - Maori wrasse (Napoleon wrasse) - can grow to 2+ meters
  - Giant groupers and potato cod (especially at Cod Hole)

### Marine Megafauna
- **6 of the world's 7 marine turtle species:**
  - Green turtle
  - Loggerhead turtle
  - Hawksbill turtle
  - Flatback turtle (endemic to Australian waters)
  - Olive ridley turtle
  - Leatherback turtle

- **Dugongs** - one of the world's most important populations
- **Dolphins** - multiple species including bottlenose and spinner dolphins
- **Whales** - humpback whales migrate along the reef (June-September)
- **Manta rays** - especially at Lady Elliot Island (best in winter)

### Other Marine Life
- **Molluscs** - thousands of species including giant clams (Tridacna)
- **Sponges** - diverse and colorful filter feeders
- **Marine algae** - essential for reef ecosystem health
- **Sea snakes** - highly venomous but generally docile
- **Crocodiles** - saltwater crocodiles in northern inshore areas
- **Seabirds** - 41 shorebird species, 20 nesting seabird species

---

## Coastal Ecosystems

### Mangroves
- **2,000+ square kilometers** of mangrove forests
- **54% of the world's mangrove diversity** represented
- Critical nursery habitat for fish and crustaceans
- Protect coastline from erosion and storm surge

### Seagrass Beds
- **6,000+ square kilometers**
- Primary food source for dugongs and green turtles
- Carbon sequestration ("blue carbon")
- Stabilize sediment and improve water quality

### Wetlands and Islands
- Provide nesting sites for seabirds and marine turtles
- Freshwater input zones
- Cultural significance for Traditional Owners

## Best Locations for Diving & Snorkeling

### From Cairns

#### Outer Reef Sites
- **Moore Reef** (50km offshore) - coral gardens, giant clams, diverse fish
- **Hastings Reef** (50km offshore) - vibrant corals, occasional reef sharks
- **Norman Reef** (70km offshore) - unique formations, abundant marine life
- **Flynn Reef** (60km offshore) - excellent for beginners, sea turtles common
- **Milln Reef** (56km offshore) - clear waters, 10-20m depth, night diving available

#### Islands
- **Green Island** (27km, 45-min ferry) - coral gardens, turtles, northern side best
- **Fitzroy Island** (29km, 45-min ferry) - Nudey Beach, Shark Fin Rock, White Rock
- **Frankland Islands** (45km) - healthiest fringing reef near Cairns, less crowded

#### Cays
- **Michaelmas Cay** (40km) - seabird sanctuary, soft corals, clear water
- **Vlasoff Cay** (33km) - secluded sandy cay, pristine corals

### From Port Douglas

- **Agincourt Reef** (65km northeast) - 16 dive sites, caves, wall dives, Barracuda Bommie
- **The Low Isles** (15km, 30-min boat) - sheltered lagoon, perfect for families
- **Ribbon Reefs** (200km stretch north of Cairns) - biodiversity hotspot

### Premier Dive Sites

#### Cod Hole (Ribbon Reefs)
- Famous for friendly potato cod (up to 1.5m)
- Depth: 15-30 meters
- Access: Liveaboard from Cairns or Port Douglas (3+ days)

#### S.S. Yongala Wreck (near Townsville)
- Considered one of the world's best wreck dives
- Sunk in 1911, now an artificial reef
- Marine life: bull sharks, eagle rays, sea snakes, turtles
- Depth: 14-28 meters
- Suitable for intermediate to advanced divers

#### Lizard Island
- Northernmost resort on the Great Barrier Reef
- Walk-in beach snorkeling
- Access to Cod Hole and Ribbon Reefs
- Charter flights from Cairns (1 hour)

#### Lady Elliot Island (Southern GBR)
- Manta ray encounters (best in winter)
- Dolphins and turtles year-round
- Coral gardens visible from beach
- Access by air from Bundaberg or Brisbane

## Climate & Best Times to Visit

### Seasons

#### Dry Season (May - November) ⭐ BEST
- Stable weather conditions
- Cooler temperatures (22-26°C / 72-79°F)
- Best underwater visibility (30-50m)
- Lower humidity
- Minimal rainfall
- **Peak tourist season**

#### Wet Season (December - April)
- Hot and humid (26-31°C / 79-88°F)
- Afternoon thunderstorms common
- Reduced visibility due to runoff
- **Stinger season** (November-May) - box jellyfish and Irukandji
- Cyclone season (January-March)
- **Lower prices, fewer crowds**

### Water Temperatures
- **Summer (Dec-Feb):** 28-30°C (82-86°F)
- **Autumn (Mar-May):** 26-28°C (79-82°F)
- **Winter (Jun-Aug):** 22-24°C (72-75°F)
- **Spring (Sep-Nov):** 24-26°C (75-79°F)

### Marine Stinger Information
- **Season:** November to May (peak Dec-Feb)
- **Protection:** Stinger suits provided by most tour operators
- **Risk:** Low with proper protection
- **Box jellyfish** and **Irukandji** are the primary concerns

## Conservation Status & Threats

### Current Condition (2024-2025)

#### Coral Bleaching Events
- **Six mass bleaching events since 2016** (1998, 2002, 2016, 2017, 2020, 2022, 2024, 2025)
- **2024:** Largest spatial footprint ever recorded
- **2025:** Second consecutive year of mass bleaching
- **First time:** Both Great Barrier Reef and Ningaloo Reef bleached simultaneously (2025)

#### Coral Cover Decline (2024-2025)
- **Northern GBR:** 39.8% → 30.0% (24.8% decline)
- **Central GBR:** 33.2% → 28.6% (13.9% decline)
- **Southern GBR:** 38.9% → 26.9% (30.6% decline - largest regional decline on record)
- **Some individual reefs:** Up to 70.8% decline

#### Primary Threats

1. **Climate Change** (CRITICAL)
   - Rising sea temperatures cause bleaching
   - Ocean acidification weakens coral skeletons
   - More frequent and severe bleaching events
   - Recovery periods shortening (now 3-5 years between events vs. 10-15 years needed)

2. **Crown-of-Thorns Starfish (COTS)**
   - Coral-eating starfish outbreaks
   - Culling program showing some success in Central GBR
   - No severe outbreaks recorded in Central GBR in 2025

3. **Land-Based Runoff**
   - Agricultural pollutants and sediment
   - Urban sewage
   - Excess nutrients harm coral and disrupt ecosystems
   - Reef 2050 Water Quality Improvement Plan in effect

4. **Coastal Development**
   - Dredging and construction
   - Habitat loss and fragmentation
   - Port expansions

5. **Fishing Impacts**
   - Overfishing
   - Illegal fishing practices
   - Destructive gear (gillnets being phased out)

### Conservation Efforts

#### Government & Management
- **Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Act 1975** (and amendments)
- **Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999**
- **Reef 2050 Plan** - long-term sustainability framework
- **Multiple-use Zoning Plan** - regulates activities
- **Gillnet bans** - protecting vulnerable species
- **Crown-of-thorns starfish culling program**

#### Research & Restoration
- **AIMS Long-Term Monitoring Program** (since 1986)
- **Reef Restoration and Adaptation Program (RRAP)**
- **Indigenous Futures Project** - training Traditional Owners in reef restoration
- **Coral larval restoration** - collecting spawn slicks, deploying larvae
- **ReefScan and ReefCloud** - technology-based monitoring

#### Traditional Owner Involvement
- **70+ Aboriginal Traditional Owner groups** with Sea Country management authority
- **Torres Strait Islander groups** connected to northern Reef
- **Traditional Use of Marine Resource Agreements (TUMRA)**
- **Reef 2050 Traditional Owner Implementation Plan**
- **Indigenous Rangers** trained in restoration techniques

#### UNESCO Status
- World Heritage Area (inscribed 1981)
- Currently listed as "in danger" due to warming seas and pollution
- Ongoing monitoring and reporting requirements

## Aboriginal & Torres Strait Islander Culture

### Traditional Owners
- **70+ Aboriginal Traditional Owner groups** along the Queensland coastline
- **Torres Strait Islander groups** connected to northern Reef
- Connection spans **7,000+ years** (before sea level rise formed the current Reef)
- **Sea Country** - spiritual and cultural connection to marine environment

### Notable Traditional Owner Groups
- **Yirrganydji people** - Cairns to Port Douglas
- **Eastern Kuku Yalanji** - Cedar Bay to Port Douglas
- **Guugu Yimidhirr** - Lizard Island to Hopevale region
- **Djiru** - Mission Beach region
- **Ngaro** - Whitsunday Islands
- **Woppaburra** - Keppel Islands
- **Gidarjil** - Gladstone region

### Cultural Practices

#### Sea Country Management
- Sustainable harvesting for thousands of years
- Seasonal knowledge of marine resources
- Traditional ecological knowledge passed through generations
- Taking only what is needed, selective harvesting

#### Art & Expression
- **Rock art** - marine creatures, contact stories, spiritual beings
- **Masks** (Torres Strait) - wood and turtle shell, ceremonial use
- **Dance** - imitating marine creatures and natural phenomena
- **Music** - clap sticks, didgeridoos, contemporary fusion
- **Storytelling** - Dreaming and Creation stories

#### Creation Stories
- **Dhui Dhui story** (Bandjin People)
- **Creation of Lizard Island (Jiigurru)** (Dingaal people)
- **Story of Nageg and Geigi** (Tig Dowareb Clan of Mer)

#### Totems
- Natural objects adopted as family/clan emblems
- Examples: Diamond stingray (Wuthathi tribe), sharks (Meriam people)
- Some clans forbid eating their totem animal

#### Language
- Many traditional languages actively being revived
- Examples: Guugu Yimidhirr, Meriam Mir, Kuuku Ya'u, Wulguru
- Language names for marine life (e.g., dugong = Girrbithi in Guugu Yimidhirr)

### Indigenous Futures Project
- Led by AIMS (Australian Institute of Marine Science)
- Training Indigenous Rangers in reef restoration techniques
- Coral larval restoration, monitoring, technology use
- Certificate III in Conservation and Ecosystem Management
- Empowering Traditional Owners to lead restoration on their Sea Country

## Economic & Tourism Impact

### Tourism Statistics
- **2+ million visitors annually** (pre-pandemic)
- **Major gateway cities:** Cairns, Port Douglas, Airlie Beach (Whitsundays), Bundaberg
- **Economic value:** Approximately $6.4 billion AUD annually
- **Employment:** 64,000+ jobs supported

### Tour Types
- **Day trips** - snorkeling and diving ($209-$330 AUD)
- **Liveaboards** - multi-day diving expeditions ($635-$3,000+ AUD)
- **Island resorts** - Green Island, Fitzroy Island, Lizard Island, Lady Elliot Island
- **Sailing tours** - Whitsundays region
- **Scenic flights** - helicopter and seaplane tours
- **Semi-submarines and glass-bottom boats** - for non-swimmers

### Tour Costs (Cairns, 2025 estimates)
- **Half-day island tours:** $89-$129 AUD
- **Full-day outer reef (pontoons):** $219-$289 AUD
- **Snorkel & dive day trips:** $239-$330 AUD
- **Introductory scuba diving:** +$129-$180 AUD
- **Certified diving:** +$95-$160 AUD
- **Liveaboards:** from $635 AUD (2 day/1 night)
- **Luxury liveaboards:** $1,500-$3,000+ AUD
