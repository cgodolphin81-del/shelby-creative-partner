## GETTING THERE

### Flights

**International Airports:**
- **Tashkent (TAS)** - Main international gateway
- **Samarkand (SKD)** - Limited international flights
- **Urgench (UGC)** - Closest to Khiva (30km)

**Major Routes:**
- From Europe: Turkish Airlines (via Istanbul), Aeroflot (via Moscow), Uzbekistan Airways (direct from some cities)
- From Asia: Korean Air (via Seoul), Air India, China Southern
- From Middle East: flydubai, Qatar Airways (via Doha)

**Typical Costs:**
- Europe to Tashkent: $400-800 roundtrip
- US to Tashkent: $800-1,500 roundtrip (usually 2+ stops)
- Asia to Tashkent: $300-600 roundtrip

**Tip:** Book 2-3 months in advance for best prices. Istanbul is often the most convenient connection point.

### Overland (Adventurous)

**From Kazakhstan:**
- Train: Almaty to Tashkent (overnight, ~$30-50)
- Border: Closed periodically, check current status

**From Turkmenistan:**
- Possible but complicated, requires Turkmen visa
- Not recommended for most travelers

**From Kyrgyzstan:**
- Shared taxis from Osh to Andijan
- Border crossings can be slow

## VISA INFORMATION

### Visa-Free Countries (30-60 days)
Many countries can enter visa-free, including:
- Most EU countries
- UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand
- Japan, South Korea, Singapore
- UAE, Israel, Turkey

### E-Visa (Most Travelers)
**Process:**
1. Apply online at e-visa.gov.uz
2. Upload passport scan and photo
3. Pay fee (~$20 USD)
4. Receive e-visa in 2-3 business days
5. Print and present on arrival

**Validity:** 30 days, single entry
**Processing:** 2-3 business days (sometimes faster)

**Required Documents:**
- Passport (6+ months validity)
- Digital passport photo
- Email address
- Credit/debit card for payment

### Visa on Arrival
Available at Tashkent airport for some nationalities
- Fee: ~$30 USD
- Bring exact cash
- Can have long queues

**Important:** Always check current visa requirements before travel. Rules change frequently.

## GETTING AROUND

### Between Cities

**Train (Recommended)**
- **Afrosiyob**: High-speed train Tashkent-Samarkand-Bukhara
  - Tashkent to Samarkand: 2 hours, ~$25
  - Samarkand to Bukhara: 1.5 hours, ~$15
  - Comfortable, air-conditioned, reliable
  - Book in advance: ticket.uzrailway.uz

- **Regular trains**: Slower, cheaper, more local experience
  - Available on all major routes
  - Multiple classes (2nd, 3rd, 4th)
  - 3rd class is fine for most travelers

**Shared Taxis**
- Common for shorter routes
- Negotiate price before departure
- Samarkand to Bukhara: ~$15-20 per person
- Faster than train but less comfortable

**Domestic Flights**
- Tashkent to Urgench (for Khiva): 1.5 hours, ~$80-120
- Book through Uzbekistan Airways
- Limited schedule, book in advance

### Within Cities

**Taxis**
- **Yandex Go**: Uber-like app, works well
- Very affordable: most rides $1-3
- Agree on price if no meter
- Hotel can call reliable drivers

**Walking**
- Historic centers are compact
- Samarkand Registan area: very walkable
- Bukhara old city: pedestrian-friendly
- Khiva Itchan Kala: pedestrian only

**Public Transport**
- Buses available but not tourist-friendly
- Routes in Uzbek/Russian only
- Very cheap (~$0.20) but challenging

## BEST TIMES TO VISIT

### Spring (April-May) ⭐ BEST
**Weather:** 20-28°C (68-82°F), mild and pleasant
**Pros:**
- Perfect temperatures
- Gardens in bloom
- Less crowded than autumn
- Good light for photography

**Cons:**
- Some rain possible
- Popular time, book ahead

### Autumn (September-October) ⭐ BEST
**Weather:** 22-30°C (72-86°F), warm days, cool nights
**Pros:**
- Harvest season, fresh fruit
- Clear skies
- Festivals and events
- Best overall conditions

**Cons:**
- Most popular time
- Higher prices
- Crowds at major sites

### Summer (June-August)
**Weather:** 35-45°C (95-113°F), very hot
**Pros:**
- Fewer tourists
- Lower prices
- Long days

**Cons:**
- Extremely hot, especially July-August
- Harsh midday sun
- Can be uncomfortable for sightseeing
- Need to start very early

**Tips if visiting in summer:**
- Sightsee 6am-11am, rest during midday
- Stay hydrated
- Wear sun protection
- Plan indoor activities for afternoon

### Winter (November-March)
**Weather:** 0-10°C (32-50°F), can be cold, occasional snow
**Pros:**
- Very few tourists
- Lowest prices
- Unique photo opportunities (snow on domes)
- Authentic local experience

**Cons:**
- Cold, especially January-February
- Some sites have reduced hours
- Shorter days
- Possible travel disruptions

## CULTURE & ETIQUETTE

### Greetings
- **Assalomu alaykum**: Peace be upon you (formal hello)
- **Vaalaykum assalom**: And upon you peace (response)
- Handshake common (men)
- Hand over heart gesture shows respect
- Remove shoes when entering homes/mosques

### Dress Code
**General:**
- Modest dress appreciated
- Cover shoulders and knees (especially women)
- Lightweight, breathable fabrics
- Comfortable walking shoes

**Religious Sites:**
- Women: Headscarf recommended (carry one)
- Men: Long pants, covered shoulders
- Remove shoes before entering
- Quiet, respectful behavior

**Photography:**
- Ask permission before photographing people
- Some sites prohibit photography (observe signs)
- No flash in museums/mausoleums
- Drones require permits

### Dining Etiquette
- Wash hands before meals (traditional)
- Eat with right hand only (left is considered unclean)
- Bread (non) is sacred, never place upside down
- Wait for eldest to start eating
- Finish food on plate (shows appreciation)
- Toasts are important at formal meals

### Tea Culture
- Tea (chai) is central to hospitality
- Poured from traditional teapot (dastarkhan)
- Never fill cup completely (shows you want more)
- Accept at least one cup (refusal can offend)
- Sip slowly, enjoy conversation

### Tipping
- Not mandatory but appreciated
- Restaurants: 10% if service charge not included
- Guides: $5-10/day
- Drivers: $3-5/day
- Hotel staff: $1-2 for porters

## FOOD & DRINK

### Must-Try Dishes

**Plov (Osh)**
- National dish: rice, carrots, onions, lamb/beef
- Regional variations
- Best at local oshkhona (plov centers)
- $3-5 per plate

**Shashlik**
- Grilled meat skewers (lamb, beef, chicken)
- Served with onions and non
- Street food staple
- $2-4 per skewer

**Lagman**
- Hand-pulled noodles with meat and vegetables
- Soup or stir-fry style
- Hearty and filling
- $4-6

**Manti**
- Steamed dumplings with meat filling
- Served with sour cream or yogurt
- $4-6

**Samsa**
- Baked pastries with meat or pumpkin
- From tandoor oven
- Perfect snack
- $1-2

**Non (Bread)**
- Round, flat bread with decorative patterns
- Sacred, never waste
- Sold everywhere
- $0.50

### Drinks

**Chai (Tea)**
- Green or black tea
- Served constantly
- Often with sweets
- $0.50-1

**Ayran**
- Salty yogurt drink
- Refreshing in summer
- $1-2

**Local Beer**
- Uzbecko, Baltika
- Widely available
- $2-3

**Vodka**
- Local brands available
- Part of toast culture
- Drink responsibly

### Dietary Restrictions
- **Vegetarian**: Possible but challenging
  - Plov often made with meat broth
  - Specify "go'shtsiz" (without meat)
  - Fresh fruits, vegetables, bread widely available
  
- **Vegan**: Difficult
  - Dairy common
  - Learn key phrases
  - Stick to fruits, nuts, bread
  
- **Halal**: All meat is halal
- **Gluten-free**: Challenging (bread central to meals)

## SAFETY

### Overall Safety
Uzbekistan is **very safe** for travelers:
- Violent crime against tourists: rare
- Police presence: visible
- Scams: minimal compared to other destinations
- Solo female travelers: generally safe

### Common Sense Precautions
- Keep valuables secure
- Use hotel safes
- Don't flash expensive items
- Be aware in crowded markets (pickpockets rare but possible)
- Use registered taxis or Yandex Go
- Keep copies of passport/visa

### Health
- **Water**: Bottled water recommended
- **Food**: Generally safe, street food okay
- **Vaccinations**: Routine vaccines up to date
- **Medical care**: Adequate in cities, limited in rural areas
- **Travel insurance**: Recommended

### Political Situation
- Stable government
- Tourism actively encouraged
- Avoid political discussions
- Photography of government buildings: restricted

### Emergency Numbers
- **Police**: 102
- **Ambulance**: 103
- **Fire**: 101
- **Tourist Police**: Available in major cities

## SHOPPING

### What to Buy

**Ceramics**
- Rishtan: Blue patterns (most famous)
- Gijduvon: Traditional styles
- Prices: $20-200+ depending on size/quality

**Textiles**
- Suzani: Embroidered wall hangings
- Ikat (Abrband): Dyed silk fabrics
- Prices: $50-500+

**Carpets**
- Hand-knotted wool
- Traditional patterns
- Prices: $200-2000+

**Metalwork**
- Copper engravings
- Jewelry (silver, semi-precious stones)
- Prices: $20-200+

**Food**
- Dried fruits and nuts
- Spices
- Sweets (halva, navat)
- Prices: Very reasonable

### Where to Shop
- **Siab Bazaar** (Samarkand): Tourist-friendly, good variety
- **Trading Domes** (Bukhara): Authentic, covered markets
- **Itchan Kala** (Khiva): Inside fortress, higher prices
- **Local Markets**: Better prices, less English

### Bargaining
- Expected in bazaars
- Start at 50-60% of asking price
- Be friendly, it's a social interaction
- Fixed prices in modern shops
- Don't bargain if you're not serious about buying

## PRACTICAL TIPS

### Money
- **Currency**: Uzbek Som (UZS)
- **ATMs**: Widely available in cities
- **Cards**: Visa/Mastercard accepted in hotels, some restaurants
- **Cash**: Still king for markets, small shops, taxis
- **Exchange**: Banks and official exchange offices
- **Tip**: Bring USD/EUR in good condition (no tears, marks)

### Packing List
**Essentials:**
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
- Light scarf (for women, religious sites)
- Modest clothing
- Power adapter (Type C/F)
- Reusable water bottle
- Hand sanitizer
- Basic first aid kit

**Seasonal:**
- Spring/Autumn: Light layers, light jacket for evenings
- Summer: Very light clothing, extra water, cooling towel
- Winter: Warm layers, coat, gloves, warm hat

### Photography
- **Best light**: Golden hour (sunrise/sunset)
- **Blue hour**: Domes glow after sunset
- **Interiors**: Low light, bring tripod or use high ISO
- **People**: Always ask permission
- **Drones**: Require permits (apply in advance)

### Respectful Travel
- Ask before photographing people
- Dress modestly at religious sites
- Learn basic greetings in Uzbek
- Support local businesses
- Don't touch or climb on monuments
- Follow posted rules at all sites

2. **Carry cash** - Many places don't take cards
