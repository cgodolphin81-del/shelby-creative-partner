The plane descends through air so clear... it feels like falling through glass.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Below, Socotra emerges from the Indian Ocean like a dream someone forgot upon waking. An island that should not exist. Shaped by winds and isolation into something the rest of Earth... abandoned millions of years ago.

[PAUSE: 3s]

I step onto the tarmac at Hadibo. And the heat hits me—dry and ancient. This is not the humid warmth of tropical islands. This is the breath of desert stones. Of mountains that have stood sentinel while continents drifted... and seas rose and fell.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The air smells of salt. And something else... resinous. Sharp. *Alive.*

[PAUSE: 3s]

[PAUSE: 2s]

The number feels both like a privilege... and a responsibility.

[PAUSE: 2s]

## SECTION ONE: THE TREES THAT BLEED

We drive east from Hadibo. The four-by-four bounces over terrain that resists the very notion of roads. The Haghier Mountains rise before us—jagged teeth against the sky. Their slopes dotted with shapes that make no sense... until your brain rearranges itself to accept them.

[PAUSE: 3s]

There.

[PAUSE: 2s]

On the limestone plateau. Standing like upturned umbrellas caught in an eternal storm.

[PAUSE: 2s]

*Dragon blood trees.*

[PAUSE: 3s]

*Dracaena cinnabari.* The name sounds like a spell.

[PAUSE: 2s]

I approach the first one slowly. The way you might approach a sleeping animal. Or a sacred thing.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The trunk is thick. Pale. Textured like elephant skin. It rises twenty feet before branching into a dense, perfectly symmetrical canopy. A green umbrella. A mushroom cloud frozen in photosynthesis.

[PAUSE: 3s]

I reach out and touch the bark. It is warm from the sun.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Somewhere inside this tree, running through veins I cannot see, flows a red sap that humans have called *dragon's blood* for thousands of years.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The ancients believed this resin was the literal blood of dragons. Spilled in epic battles between the great beasts and elephants. They used it as medicine. As dye. As varnish for violins. As pigment for Renaissance paintings.

[PAUSE: 3s]

I ask our guide—a Socotri man named Ahmed, whose family has lived on this island for generations—if I can see the resin.

[PAUSE: 2s]

He smiles. And leads me to a tree that bears the marks of harvesting. Small cuts in the bark, carefully made. From which oozes a substance the color of fresh wounds.

[PAUSE: 3s]

*Dragon's blood.*

[PAUSE: 2s]

Deep crimson. Almost black in the shadow. Glowing ruby-red where the sun catches it. It hardens into tears. Into gems of pure color.

[PAUSE: 2s]

I touch it. And my fingertip comes away stained.

[PAUSE: 3s]

"Blood of the land," Ahmed says. "Blood of Socotra."

[PAUSE: 3s]

And I understand, suddenly... that this is not just a tree.

[PAUSE: 2s]

This is the island's heart. *Exposed.*

**[PAUSE: 5s — MUSIC: Subtle transition, wind through leaves]**

## SECTION TWO: THE SHAPE OF ISOLATION

We camp that night on the Dixam Plateau. At an altitude where the air cools... and the stars feel close enough to touch.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The dragon blood trees surround our tents like silent sentinels. In the moonlight, their umbrella shapes cast shadows that look like alien spacecraft. Landed long ago. And forgotten.

[PAUSE: 3s]

I lie awake listening to the wind move through the canopy.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The sound is unlike any forest I have known. These are not pines whispering. Not oaks creaking. This is the sound of leaves adapted to catch mist. To harvest moisture from air that rarely brings rain.

[PAUSE: 3s]

Every shape here is a solution to a problem.

[PAUSE: 2s]

*How to survive when water is a rumor. When the sun is a tyrant. When the wind never stops sculpting.*

[PAUSE: 3s]

Evolution, I think... is a kind of poetry.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Given enough time and isolation, life writes itself into forms that seem impossible.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The dragon blood tree is a poem about thirst.

[PAUSE: 1s]

The desert rose is a poem about patience—its swollen trunk storing water like a camel's hump, its pink flowers blooming in defiance of the arid earth.

[PAUSE: 1s]

The cucumber tree stands bottle-shaped and absurd, a living sculpture that asks: *why not?*

[PAUSE: 3s]

Thirty-seven percent of Socotra's plant life exists nowhere else on Earth.

[PAUSE: 2s]

*Thirty-seven percent.*

[PAUSE: 2s]

Imagine a library where more than a third of the books are written in a language spoken nowhere else.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Imagine a song that can only be sung in one valley. By one family. In one dying tongue.

[PAUSE: 3s]

This is what isolation creates.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Not just difference... but uniqueness.

[PAUSE: 1s]

Not just adaptation... but *art.*

**[PAUSE: 5s — MUSIC: Gentle swell, night sounds fade in]**

## SECTION THREE: THE WALK

On the third day, we hike from the Dixam Plateau down into Wadi Dirhur. A canyon carved by water that flows only in memory.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The path is stone and dust. Winding between dragon blood trees that grow more dense as we descend.

[PAUSE: 3s]

I walk slowly.

[PAUSE: 2s]

This is the Infinite Walk, after all. The practice of moving through landscape not to conquer it... but to be changed by it.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Each step is a question.

[PAUSE: 1s]

Each breath... is an answer I do not yet understand.

[PAUSE: 3s]

The canyon opens into a valley where the trees stand in groves. Their canopies touching like the ceilings of a cathedral built by giants.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Sunlight filters through in shafts. Illuminating dust motes. And the occasional bird—the Socotra starling, glossy black with reddish-brown wings, flitting between branches.

[PAUSE: 2s]

*Endemic.* Found only here.

[PAUSE: 2s]

If this forest dies... the starling dies with it.

[PAUSE: 3s]

I sit beneath a particularly old dragon blood tree. Its trunk wide as a small house. Its canopy a perfect green dome.

[PAUSE: 2s]

I place my palm against the bark. And close my eyes.

[PAUSE: 3s]

What does this tree know?

[PAUSE: 3s]

It has stood here for perhaps two hundred years. It has weathered monsoons and droughts. Earthquakes and storms. It has watched humans come and go—ancient traders seeking its resin. British colonizers mapping the island. Yemeni soldiers. Somali fishermen. Tourists like me, with our cameras and our questions.

[PAUSE: 3s]

It does not care about our borders. Our conflicts. Our fleeting empires.

[PAUSE: 2s]

It cares about water.

[PAUSE: 1s]

About light.

[PAUSE: 1s]

About the slow work of turning sun into sugar. Air into wood. *Time... into rings.*

[PAUSE: 3s]

I am a mayfly to this tree.

[PAUSE: 2s]

My entire life is a season to it.

[PAUSE: 2s]

And yet... here we are. Breathing the same air. Rooted to the same earth.

**[PAUSE: 5s — MUSIC: Transition, subtle emotional swell]**

## SECTION FOUR: THE RED STAIN

That evening, I notice the stain on my fingertip has not washed away.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The dragon's blood has seeped into my skin. A tiny mark of this place that will take days to fade.

[PAUSE: 3s]

I think about the word *"blood."*

[PAUSE: 2s]

We use it to mean family. To mean lineage. To mean the essential truth of something.

[PAUSE: 2s]

*Blood of my blood. Bloodline. Blood and soil.*

[PAUSE: 3s]

What is the blood of a place?

[PAUSE: 2s]

Is it the resin that flows when the tree is cut?

[PAUSE: 1s]

Is it the iron in the soil? The salt in the air? The DNA of species that evolved in isolation?

[PAUSE: 1s]

Is it the stories told by people who have walked this land for thousands of years?

[PAUSE: 3s]

Socotra's blood is red, yes.

[PAUSE: 2s]

But it is also the turquoise of the ocean that surrounds it. The gold of its sand dunes. The gray of its limestone mountains. The green of its impossible trees.

[PAUSE: 2s]

It is the color of a chameleon changing its skin. Of a starling's wing in sunlight. Of a desert rose's pink flower opening at dawn.

[PAUSE: 3s]

It is the color of life... insisting on itself. Against all odds.

**[PAUSE: 5s — MUSIC: Slightly darker, more contemplative]**

## SECTION FIVE: THE FRAGILITY

On the fifth day, we visit the Firhmin Forest. Where dragon blood trees grow so dense they form a canopy over the path.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Our guide points out a young sapling. No more than three feet tall. Its leaves already forming the characteristic rosette.

[PAUSE: 3s]

"It takes one hundred years to grow like this," he says, gesturing to a tree perhaps ten feet tall. "Two hundred years to be mature. Three hundred... to be old."

[PAUSE: 3s]

I do the math.

[PAUSE: 2s]

A forest like this is not measured in generations of humans.

[PAUSE: 2s]

It is measured in *epochs.*

[PAUSE: 3s]

And it is disappearing.

[PAUSE: 3s]

Climate change. Overgrazing by goats. Habitat loss. The slow, relentless pressure of a warming world on a place that evolved for stability.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The dragon blood tree is listed as *endangered.*

[PAUSE: 2s]

Not extinct. Not yet.

[PAUSE: 2s]

But... endangered.

[PAUSE: 3s]

I think about the word *"endemic."*

[PAUSE: 2s]

It sounds like "epidemic." But it means the opposite.

[PAUSE: 2s]

*Endemic* means found only here. Limited. Contained. *Precious.*

[PAUSE: 3s]

What happens when the endemic becomes the extinct?

[PAUSE: 2s]

What is lost when a species that exists nowhere else on Earth... ceases to exist at all?

[PAUSE: 3s]

It is not just a tree.

[PAUSE: 2s]

It is a library of genetic information written over millions of years.

[PAUSE: 2s]

It is a relationship between tree and starling. Between resin and human. Between landscape and identity.

[PAUSE: 2s]

It is a poem that took an epoch to write.

[PAUSE: 2s]

And can be erased... in a century.

[PAUSE: 5s]

We walk back to camp in silence.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The sun sets behind the Haghier Mountains. Painting the sky in colors that match the dragon's blood on my finger.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Orange. Red. Purple.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The colors of ending. Of beginning. Of blood... and time.

**[PAUSE: 5s — MUSIC: Emotional peak, then begins to soften]**

## SECTION SIX: THE DEPARTURE

On my last morning, I wake before dawn. And walk to the edge of the plateau.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Below, the Indian Ocean stretches to infinity. The same ocean that laps at shores I have walked before—Cornwall. Big Sur. The Amalfi Coast.

[PAUSE: 2s]

But this water is different.

[PAUSE: 2s]

This water guards an island that time forgot.

[PAUSE: 3s]

The sun rises. And the dragon blood trees cast long shadows across the limestone.

[PAUSE: 2s]

I breathe in the resinous air. The salt. The heat.

[PAUSE: 2s]

I press my stained fingertip to my notebook. Leaving a tiny red mark on the page.

[PAUSE: 3s]

A signature.

[PAUSE: 1s]

A witness.

[PAUSE: 1s]

A *promise.*

[PAUSE: 3s]

I do not know if I will return to Socotra. Few people do. It is remote. Expensive. Complicated. The world is full of easier beauties.

[PAUSE: 3s]

But I carry this place with me now.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The shape of the trees. The color of the resin. The sound of wind through leaves that catch mist like prayers.

[PAUSE: 3s]

The Infinite Walk is not about distance.

[PAUSE: 2s]

It is about *depth.*

[PAUSE: 2s]

It is about letting a place enter you. Change you. *Mark you.*

[PAUSE: 2s]

Socotra has marked me.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The blood of the dragon is in my skin. In my memory. In the way I now think about isolation. And adaptation. And the strange, stubborn beauty of life that refuses to be anything but itself.

[PAUSE: 5s]

As the plane lifts off from Hadibo, I look down one last time.

[PAUSE: 2s]

The island shrinks below me. A green and brown jewel in the blue vastness.

[PAUSE: 3s]

Somewhere down there, a dragon blood tree is turning sunlight into sugar. Into wood. Into time.

[PAUSE: 2s]

A Socotra starling is flitting between branches. Singing a song that exists nowhere else.

[PAUSE: 2s]

A desert rose is opening its pink flowers to a sun that has risen here for millions of years.

[PAUSE: 3s]

And I... am carrying it all with me.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Walking forward.

[PAUSE: 2s]

Marked by the blood of the dragon.
