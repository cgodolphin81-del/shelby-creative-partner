## 🎒 What to Pack for Japan

### Seasonal Packing

**Spring (March-May):**
- Light jacket (chilly mornings)
- Layers (temperature swings)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Umbrella (spring showers)

**Summer (June-August):**
- Lightweight, breathable clothes
- Hand towel (very humid!)
- Portable fan
- Insect repellent

**Fall (September-November):**
- Light sweater/jacket
- Layers
- Comfortable shoes (lots of walking)

**Winter (December-February):**
- Warm coat
- Gloves, scarf, beanie
- Thermal layers
- Moisturizer (very dry)

## 🍜 Food & Dining

### Must-Try Japanese Food

**Sushi & Sashimi:**
- Tsukiji/Toyosu Market (Tokyo)
- Sushi Dai, Sukiyabashi Jiro (Tokyo)
- Local conveyor belt sushi (cheap & fun!)

**Ramen:**
- Ichiran (tonkotsu, nationwide)
- Ippudo (rich broth)
- Afuri (light yuzu broth)

**Other Specialties:**
- Wagyu beef (Kobe, Matsuzaka)
- Tempura (light, crispy)
- Yakitori (grilled skewers)
- Okonomiyaki (savory pancake)
- Takoyaki (octopus balls)
- Kaiseki (multi-course traditional)

### Dietary Restrictions

- **Vegetarian:** Limited but growing (try shojin ryori - Buddhist cuisine)
- **Vegan:** Challenging, research ahead
- **Gluten-free:** Soy sauce contains wheat, ask for tamari
- **Allergies:** Learn key phrases, carry allergy card

## 🌸 Best Time to Visit

### Spring (March-May) ⭐ BEST
- **Pros:** Cherry blossoms, mild weather, festivals
- **Cons:** Crowded, expensive, book ahead
- **Pack:** Layers, light jacket

### Summer (June-August)
- **Pros:** Festivals, fireworks, hiking
- **Cons:** Hot, humid, rainy season (June)
- **Pack:** Light clothes, umbrella, fan

### Fall (September-November) ⭐ BEST
- **Pros:** Fall foliage, comfortable weather
- **Cons:** Popular season, typhoon risk (Sept)
- **Pack:** Layers, light jacket

### Winter (December-February)
- **Pros:** Skiing, fewer crowds, illuminations
- **Cons:** Cold, some closures
- **Pack:** Warm coat, thermal layers
