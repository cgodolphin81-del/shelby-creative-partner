## 🎒 What to Pack for Iceland

### Essential Gear

**Clothing:**
- Waterproof jacket (essential!)
- Waterproof pants
- Warm layers (fleece, wool)
- Thermal underwear
- Warm hat, gloves, scarf
- Waterproof hiking boots
- Quick-dry socks (multiple pairs)

**Tech:**
- Universal Adapter - Iceland uses Type C/F plugs
- Power Bank 20000mAh - Cold drains batteries
- Camera with cold-weather protection
- Tripod (for Northern Lights)
- Waterproof camera bag

**Health:**
- Lip balm with SPF
- Moisturizer (air is very dry)
- Sunglasses (glare from snow/ice)
- Basic meds

## ⚠️ Important Tips

### Do's:
- ✅ Check weather before heading out
- ✅ Respect road closures (F-roads)
- ✅ Stay on marked trails
- ✅ Carry cash for remote areas
- ✅ Fill up gas tank when you see a station

### Don'ts:
- ❌ Don't drive on F-roads without 4x4
- ❌ Don't swim in ocean (dangerous currents)
- ❌ Don't approach wildlife (seals, birds)
- ❌ Don't step on moss (takes decades to grow)
- ❌ Don't leave trash anywhere

### Emergency Numbers:
- Emergency: 112
- Police: 444-1000
- Mountain Rescue: 499-0000

## 🌟 Best Time to Visit

### Summer (June-August)
- **Pros:** Midnight sun, all roads open, warmest weather
- **Cons:** Most expensive, most crowded
- **Best for:** Road trips, hiking, wildlife

### Shoulder Season (May, September)
- **Pros:** Fewer crowds, lower prices, still accessible
- **Cons:** Some F-roads closed
- **Best for:** Northern Lights (Sept), photography

### Winter (November-March)
- **Pros:** Northern Lights, snow landscapes, fewer tourists
- **Cons:** Limited daylight, some roads closed, cold
- **Best for:** Northern Lights, ice caving, cozy vibes
