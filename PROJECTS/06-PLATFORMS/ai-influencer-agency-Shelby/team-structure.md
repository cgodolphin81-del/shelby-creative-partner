# Team Structure & Organizational Design
## Building a Scalable AI Influencer Agency

---

## Overview

This document outlines the organizational structure, roles, and hiring plan for building a scalable AI influencer management agency from startup to enterprise scale.

---

## 1. Organizational Phases

### Phase 1: Startup (0-6 months)
**Revenue:** $0-50K/month
**Team Size:** 1-5 people
**Focus:** Prove business model, build first clients

### Phase 2: Growth (6-18 months)
**Revenue:** $50K-200K/month
**Team Size:** 5-20 people
**Focus:** Scale operations, systematize processes

### Phase 3: Scale (18-36 months)
**Revenue:** $200K-500K+/month
**Team Size:** 20-50 people
**Focus:** Market expansion, multiple revenue streams

### Phase 4: Enterprise (36+ months)
**Revenue:** $500K-2M+/month
**Team Size:** 50-200+ people
**Focus:** Market dominance, acquisitions, global expansion

### 2.2 Key Roles

**Founder/CEO**
- **Responsibilities:** Vision, strategy, sales, key client relationships, fundraising
- **Time Allocation:** 40% sales, 30% strategy, 20% operations, 10% other
- **Skills Needed:** Sales, leadership, industry knowledge, fundraising
- **Compensation:** Equity + minimal salary initially

**Creative Lead**
- **Responsibilities:** Character design, content quality, creative direction
- **Skills:** AI image generation, design, creative direction, prompt engineering
- **Tools:** Midjourney, Photoshop, Figma, Adobe Suite
- **Compensation:** $60K-100K + equity (2-5%)
- **Hire:** Month 1-2

**Operations Lead**
- **Responsibilities:** Client management, project coordination, process documentation
- **Skills:** Project management, client communication, organization
- **Tools:** Notion, Asana, Slack, Google Workspace
- **Compensation:** $55K-90K + equity (1-3%)
- **Hire:** Month 2-3

**Sales/Marketing (can be Founder initially)**
- **Responsibilities:** Lead generation, outreach, closing deals, marketing content
- **Skills:** Sales, copywriting, social media, relationship building
- **Compensation:** $50K-80K + commission (10-15% of deals) + equity (1-2%)
- **Hire:** Month 3-4 (or Founder handles initially)

## 7. Hiring Process

### 7.1 Interview Framework

**Stage 1: Phone Screen (30 min)**
- Basic qualifications check
- Culture fit assessment
- Salary expectations
- Why this role/company?

**Stage 2: Skills Assessment**
- Role-specific test or assignment
- Examples:
  - Designer: Create character from brief
  - Writer: Write 5 captions
  - Sales: Mock cold call or pitch
  - CSM: Handle escalation scenario

**Stage 3: Team Interview (60 min)**
- Meet potential teammates
- Deep dive on experience
- Scenario-based questions
- Culture add assessment

**Stage 4: Executive Interview (30 min)**
- Meet department head or executive
- Vision alignment
- Career goals discussion
- Final assessment

**Stage 5: Reference Checks**
- 2-3 professional references
- Verify claims
- Get outside perspective

### 7.3 Onboarding Program

**Week 1: Orientation**
- Company overview and vision
- Team introductions
- Tools and systems training
- Product/service deep dive
- Initial assignments

**Week 2-3: Role Training**
- Shadow team members
- Complete training modules
- Begin taking on responsibilities
- Regular check-ins with manager

**Week 4: First Deliverables**
- Complete first independent project
- Present to team
- 30-day review
- Set 60/90 day goals

**90-Day Milestones:**
- Fully ramped in role
- Meeting performance expectations
- Integrated into team
- 90-day review and goal setting

## 10. Remote vs. Office

### 10.1 Remote-First Approach

**Benefits:**
- Access to global talent
- Lower overhead costs
- Employee flexibility
- Better work-life balance

**Challenges:**
- Communication overhead
- Culture building
- Collaboration friction
- Time zone coordination

**Best Practices:**
- Over-communicate
- Document everything
- Regular video meetings
- Intentional culture building
- Annual in-person gatherings

---

### 10.2 Hybrid Model

**Structure:**
- 2-3 days in office (for local team)
- Rest remote
- Optional office space
- Team gatherings quarterly

**Benefits:**
- Balance of flexibility and collaboration
- Culture building opportunities
- Access to office amenities
- Reduced real estate costs

## Summary: Team Building Principles

1. **Hire slow, fire fast** - Take time to find right fit
2. **Culture add, not culture fit** - Seek diverse perspectives
3. **Invest in development** - Grow people internally
4. **Compensate fairly** - Pay at or above market
5. **Communicate openly** - Transparency builds trust
6. **Document everything** - Systems enable scale
7. **Measure what matters** - Track people metrics
8. **Adapt as you grow** - Structure evolves with company

---

*Team structure should evolve with business needs. Review and adjust quarterly.*
