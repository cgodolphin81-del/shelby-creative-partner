## DETAILED PLATFORM COMPARISON

### 1. SUBSTACK

#### Overview
Substack is the simplest way to start a paid newsletter. Built for writers who want to focus on content, not tech.

#### Pricing
- **Free tier:** Unlimited subscribers, free newsletters
- **Paid tier:** 10% revenue share on subscriptions (you keep 90%)
- **No monthly fee** - they only make money when you do

#### Features
✅ Built-in payment processing
✅ Simple email editor
✅ Basic analytics
✅ Recommendation network (cross-promotion)
✅ Mobile app for readers
✅ Podcast hosting (beta)
✅ Chat feature for subscribers

❌ Limited customization
❌ No automation sequences
❌ Basic landing pages
❌ No A/B testing
❌ Limited integrations
❌ Can't export email list easily (intentional)

#### Best For
- First-time newsletter creators
- Writers who want simplicity
- Those leveraging Substack's network effect
- People who don't want to manage tech

#### Revenue Potential
- Substack takes 10% of subscription revenue
- Payment processing (~2.9% + $0.30) is additional
- **You keep ~87% of subscription revenue**

#### Real Examples
- Lenny's Newsletter (tech/product)
- Matt Taibbi (journalism)
- Heather Cox Richardson (politics)

#### Verdict: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Choose Substack if:** You want the easiest possible start and believe in the network effect. Accept the 10% cut as the price of simplicity.

---

### 2. BEEHIIV

#### Overview
Beehiiv is built for growth. It's the most feature-rich platform for creators serious about scaling and monetizing.

#### Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 2,500 subscribers, all core features
- **Growth:** $42/mo (up to 10K subs) - advanced analytics, A/B testing
- **Pro:** $84/mo (up to 100K subs) - priority support, custom HTML
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing

**Zero revenue share** - you keep 100% of your subscription revenue

#### Features
✅ Advanced analytics (attribution, cohorts)
✅ A/B testing (subject lines, send times, content)
✅ Referral program built-in
✅ Ad network (Beehiiv places sponsors for you)
✅ Custom landing pages
✅ Full email template customization
✅ Advanced segmentation
✅ Automation sequences
✅ Website builder included
✅ Easy list export
✅ API access
✅ Priority support (paid tiers)

❌ More complex than Substack
❌ No built-in network effect
❌ Learning curve for advanced features

#### Best For
- Growth-focused creators
- Those who want full control
- Creators who want 100% revenue ownership
- People planning to scale past 10K subscribers

#### Revenue Potential
- Keep 100% of subscription revenue
- Beehiiv Ad Network takes ~30% of sponsorship deals (optional)
- Payment processing (~2.9% + $0.30) applies
- **You keep ~97% of subscription revenue**

#### Real Examples
- Morning Brew (acquired, started on similar model)
- The Hustle (before acquisition)
- Many top tech newsletters

#### Verdict: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Choose Beehiiv if:** You're serious about building a business. Best balance of features, pricing, and control.

---

### 3. CONVERTKIT (now Kit)

#### Overview
ConvertKit is the most powerful for creators who have (or plan to have) multiple products - courses, coaching, digital products.

#### Pricing
- **Free:** Up to 10,000 subscribers (limited features)
- **Creator:** $15/mo (unlimited subs) - all features
- **Creator Pro:** $30/mo - advanced features, priority support

**Zero revenue share** on subscriptions

#### Features
✅ Best-in-class automation (visual builder)
✅ Landing pages & forms
✅ Digital product sales (courses, ebooks)
✅ Member areas
✅ Advanced tagging & segmentation
✅ Email sequences
✅ A/B testing
✅ Integrations (100+)
✅ Easy migrations from other platforms
✅ Commerce features (sell directly)

❌ More expensive at scale
❌ Overkill for simple newsletters
❌ No built-in discovery/network
❌ Ad network not as developed

#### Best For
- Creators with existing audiences
- Those selling courses/products
- People who need complex automation
- Multi-product creators

#### Revenue Potential
- Keep 100% of subscription revenue
- Payment processing applies
- Can sell products directly (they take small %)
- **You keep ~97% of subscription revenue**

#### Real Examples
- Pat Flynn (Smart Passive Income)
- Many course creators
- YouTubers with email lists

#### Verdict: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
**Choose ConvertKit if:** You have (or plan) multiple revenue streams beyond the newsletter itself.

## MIGRATION GUIDE

### Moving TO Beehiiv (Recommended)
1. Export from current platform (CSV)
2. Import to Beehiiv (preserves emails)
3. Set up custom domain
4. Recreate landing pages
5. Set up paid subscription product
6. Send migration announcement
7. Redirect old URL (if possible)

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks
**Risk:** Low (Beehiiv has good import tools)

### Moving TO Substack
1. Export from current platform
2. Import to Substack
3. Set up publication
4. Enable paid subscriptions
5. Announce move to subscribers

**Timeline:** 3-5 days
**Risk:** Medium (harder to leave Substack later)

### Moving TO ConvertKit
1. Use ConvertKit's migration tool (they help)
2. Map tags/segments
3. Recreate automations
4. Set up landing pages
5. Test everything
6. Switch DNS

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks
**Risk:** Low-Medium (more complex setup)
