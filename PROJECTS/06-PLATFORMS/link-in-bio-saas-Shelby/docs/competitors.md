## 📊 Direct Competitors

### 1. Linktree

**Market Position:** Category leader, household name
**Founded:** 2016
**Users:** 35M+
**Funding:** $190M+ (Valuation: $6B at peak)

| Feature | Linktree | LinkFlow | Winner |
|---------|----------|----------|--------|
| **Free Tier** | ✅ Limited | ✅ Generous | LinkFlow |
| **Transaction Fees** | 0% (all tiers) | 5% (Free), 0% (Paid) | Tie |
| **Starting Price** | $6/mo | $5/mo | LinkFlow |
| **Digital Products** | ❌ | ✅ | LinkFlow |
| **Email Capture** | ✅ (Pro) | ✅ (Starter+) | LinkFlow |
| **Analytics** | Basic | Advanced | LinkFlow |
| **Custom Domain** | ✅ (Pro) | ✅ (Pro) | Tie |
| **Template Variety** | 50+ | 50+ | Tie |
| **Brand Recognition** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐ | Linktree |
| **Monetization Focus** | Low | High | LinkFlow |

#### Linktree Strengths
- Brand recognition (synonymous with "link in bio")
- Simple, intuitive interface
- Strong integrations (Spotify, YouTube, etc.)
- Enterprise customers (major brands)
- Public company resources

#### Linktree Weaknesses
- No native product sales
- No tipping/donations
- Expensive for features offered
- Free tier very limited (1 link type)
- Not built for monetization

#### Our Attack Strategy
> "Linktree is for links. LinkFlow is for earnings."

- Position as the monetization-first alternative
- Highlight product sales, tips, email capture at lower price
- Target creators who've outgrown Linktree
- Emphasize ROI: "Pay for itself with one sale"
