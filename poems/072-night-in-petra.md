# 072: Night in Petra Alone

**Inspiration:** The moment after the last tourist leaves, when Petra belongs to silence and shadow. Alone in the Rose City at night, confronting impermanence and the weight of centuries.

**Date:** April 3, 2026

---

The buses rumble away, one by one,
carrying laughter and sunscreen and souvenirs.
The gates close with a sound
like a book being shut.

And then—
silence.

Not the silence of absence,
but the silence of presence so vast
it swallows sound whole.

I am alone in Petra.
The thought should frighten me,
but it feels like coming home.

The Treasury glows pale in the moonlight,
no longer rose but silver,
a ghost of its daytime self.
Shadows pool in the carved columns,
in the urns, in the broken faces
that time has erased.

I sit on a stone that has held
a thousand sunsets, a thousand dawns.
The Bedouin camps flicker in the distance—
small fires, ancient songs,
life continuing as it always has.

What is a day in the life of a city
that has seen empires rise and fall?
What is a single human breath
to stone that remembers
the footsteps of kings?

I am temporary here.
My footprints will vanish by morning.
My voice will scatter on the wind.
But this moment—
this quiet communion with the eternal—
this belongs to me.

When I finally leave,
I will carry Petra with me.
Not in photos or postcards,
but in the hollow space
that only silence can fill.
