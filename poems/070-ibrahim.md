# 070: Ibrahim the Bedouin Guide

**Inspiration:** Meeting Ibrahim again after five years—a Bedouin guide whose family has lived in Petra for generations. The ancestral connection that runs deeper than tourism, deeper than time.

**Date:** April 3, 2026

---

Ibrahim remembers my face
before I remember his name.
Five years is nothing to a man
whose ancestors carved homes
into these cliffs two thousand years ago.

His grandfather's grandfather
walked these same paths,
tended goats in the wadis,
found water in stone that looked dry.
The Nabataeans are dust,
but the Bedouin remain.

He pours tea from a blackened pot,
three cups, always three—
one for the guest, one for the host,
one for the silence between them.
The cardamom smells like forgiveness.

"You come back," he says,
not a question but an observation,
like noting that rain follows cloud,
that sun warms the high place at noon.

I want to tell him I've been searching—
for what, I don't know.
Something the city holds
that I left here last time.

But Ibrahim already knows.
He gestures toward the trail,
toward the tombs glowing rose-gold in the light.
"Your feet remember," he says.
"That is enough."

And I understand:
some connections don't need words.
They live in the soil, in the stone,
in the hands that pour tea
for a stranger who is no longer strange.
