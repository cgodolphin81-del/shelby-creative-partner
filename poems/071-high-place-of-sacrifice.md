# 071: The High Place of Sacrifice

**Inspiration:** Climbing to the High Place of Sacrifice, where ancient rituals were performed. Understanding that memory lives in stone, and that every generation leaves its mark—some in blood, some in breath.

**Date:** April 3, 2026

---

The climb is steep, unforgiving—
a staircase cut by hands
that turned to dust centuries ago.
I climb anyway, drawn by something
older than curiosity.

At the top, the altar waits.
Flat stone, worn smooth
by knees that begged, by hands that offered,
by blood that soaked into rock
and vanished into memory.

What did they sacrifice here?
Lambs? Grain? Promises?
Did they believe the gods lived in the sky,
or in the stone beneath their feet,
or in the space between heartbeats?

I place my palm on the altar.
It is neither warm nor cold.
It simply is—
as it was, as it will be,
long after I am gone.

The view stretches endlessly:
canyons, tombs, the winding Siq,
the modern road where buses carry tourists
who will never know this silence.

I understand now:
sacrifice was never about loss.
It was about giving something up
to make room for what comes next.

I leave nothing on the altar.
I take nothing away.
But something has shifted—
in the stone, in me,
in the thin air between us.

We are all carving our high places,
one breath, one choice, one step at a time.
