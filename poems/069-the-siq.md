# 069: The Siq

**Inspiration:** Walking the narrow canyon toward Petra, where anticipation builds with each step—but recognition only comes when the city reveals itself. Walking with new eyes, five years later.

**Date:** April 3, 2026

---

The walls close in, red stone rising
like guardians who've forgotten their names.
I've walked this path before,
but memory is a liar who smooths the edges.

Five years ago, I rushed through here—
camera ready, treasure ahead,
the famous face waiting at the end.
Today, my feet know something my mind forgot:
the journey is the destination wearing a disguise.

Sandstone breathes against my palms,
warm from the morning sun.
The canyon narrows, then widens,
then narrows again—
a heartbeat measured in stone.

I am not the same person who walked here before.
The Siq knows this.
It does not rush to reveal its secret.
It makes me wait, makes me wonder,
makes me small beneath its towering silence.

And when the opening finally comes,
when Petra blooms in the distance—
rose-colored, impossible, ancient—
I do not gasp.
I do not reach for my phone.

I simply stand,
and let the city recognize me first.
