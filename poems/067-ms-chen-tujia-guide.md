# 067: Ms. Chen, Tujia Guide

**Inspiration:** Episode 034: Zhangjiajie, China — Ms. Chen, a Tujia guide, indigenous connection to the land  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

---

Ms. Chen does not walk *on* the mountain.
She walks *with* it.

Her feet know the old paths,
the ones not marked on any map,
the trails her grandmother's grandmother
wore into the stone
when this was not a park,
not a destination,
but home.

She points to a tree
and tells us its name in Tujia,
a language that lives in her mouth
like a river finding its bed.
The Mandarin translation comes after,
an afterthought,
as if the mountain itself
prefers the older word.

*My people have lived here*
*twenty generations,*
she says, not with pride,
but with the quiet certainty
of someone stating the weather.
*Before the tickets, before the buses,
before the elevators cut through stone.*

I watch her hands as she speaks —
they move like branches in wind,
like roots finding water.
She is not performing culture.
She is inhabiting it,
the way moss inhabits rock:
completely, inevitably,
without apology.

When we reach the overlook,
the other tourists raise their phones.
Ms. Chen closes her eyes.

*I am listening,*
she says.
*The mountain is speaking.*

We wait, awkward in our sneakers,
our synthetic jackets,
our cameras loaded with memory cards
that will forget us in a decade.

What does the mountain say?
I want to ask, but don't.
Some questions are not for visitors.
Some knowledge is not transferable,
cannot be packaged into a tour,
a souvenir,
a Instagram caption.

Later, she shows us the shrine —
a small stone altar
tucked into a crevice
where incense burns
and offerings of rice are left.

*For the guardians,*
she explains.
*They keep the paths safe.
They remember the old names.*

I bow, though I do not know
if I am allowed to.
She nods, once.
A small permission.
A bridge between worlds.

When we descend,
I realize I have been holding my breath
the entire day,
as if the altitude
had stolen something from my lungs.

Ms. Chen walks ahead,
light-footed, breathing easy.
She is not a visitor here.
She is returning.
