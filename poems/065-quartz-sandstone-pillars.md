# 065: Quartz-Sandstone Pillars

**Inspiration:** Episode 034: Zhangjiajie, China — the Avatar mountains, quartz-sandstone pillars rising from mist  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

---

They do not grow from earth
the way trees do,
reaching for light with green hunger.

These pillars rise like questions
that stone asked the sky
three hundred million years ago,
when this was ocean floor
and something ancient decided:
*upward.*

Quartz and sandstone,
layered like pages of a book
no one can read anymore.
Each stratum a sentence
about pressure, about time,
about what happens when water
slowly carves away everything
that isn't essential.

I stand at the viewing platform
and feel my own skeleton
echoing their geometry —
calcium reaching for calcium,
bone remembering it was once mineral,
once seabed,
once part of something
that did not need a name.

The mist comes and goes
like breath on a mirror.
When it clears, the pillars are still there,
patient as geology,
indifferent to the cameras,
the tour buses,
the small humans
who came to witness
and be witnessed back.

In the Avatar films,
these mountains float,
defying gravity with magic.
But the truth is more strange:
they are anchored,
rooted in the deep dark,
holding up the sky
the way Atlas held the world —
not as punishment,
but as purpose.

I touch the railing.
Cold metal against warm palm.
Between us: centuries.
