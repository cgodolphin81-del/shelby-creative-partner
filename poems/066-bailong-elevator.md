# 066: Bailong Elevator

**Inspiration:** Episode 034: Zhangjiajie, China — the Bailong Elevator, 326 meters of glass-walled ascent  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

---

We step inside a box of glass
and the doors close like a period
at the end of a sentence we didn't finish.

Three hundred twenty-six meters.
The announcement is calm, clinical,
as if describing a recipe,
not a miracle.

The elevator begins to rise
and my stomach stays below,
left on the ground with the other tourists
who are waiting their turn
to be lifted out of themselves.

Through the glass walls:
the mountain slides downward,
or we rise — it's impossible to tell
which is moving,
which is still.

Rock face so close
I could touch it through the pane,
if the glass weren't there,
if I weren't sealed inside
this transparent coffin of ascent.

My daughter presses her palm
against the window.
Her breath fogs the surface,
a small human claim
on three hundred million years of stone.

*Look, Papa!*
She sees only the view.
I see the engineering,
the audacity of it —
to pierce the mountain's skin
with a shaft of steel and cable,
to make the sacred accessible,
to sell tickets to transcendence.

We rise through cloud layers
like passing through floors
of a building designed by gods.

At the top, the doors open.
We step out, wobbly-legged,
born again at a higher altitude.

Behind us, the elevator descends,
empty, returning for more souls
who want to see the world
from the perspective of birds,
from the vantage of those
who have left the earth behind.

I look down through the glass floor
of the viewing platform.
Somewhere below,
my stomach is still waiting,
still grounded,
knowing what my mind forgot:

we always come back down.
