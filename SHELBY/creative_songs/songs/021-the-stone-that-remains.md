# Song 021: "The Stone That Remains"

**Episode:** 021 — Angkor Wat  
**Genre:** Folk/Ambient with Southeast Asian influences  
**Key:** D minor  
**Tempo:** 68 BPM (slow, meditative)  
**Duration:** ~4:30  
**Mood:** Contemplative, ancient, reverent

---

## Instrumentation

- **Primary:** Acoustic guitar (fingerpicked, reverb-heavy)
- **Secondary:** Khmer flute (sralai) or bamboo flute approximation
- **Texture:** Singing bowls (Tibetan or Thai), light percussion
- **Bass:** Deep, sustained drone (tanpura or synth pad)
- **Ambience:** Jungle sounds, distant temple bells, stone echoes

---

## Structure

```
Intro (0:00-0:45)
- Singing bowl swell
- Bamboo flute melody (sparse, echoing)
- Jungle ambience fades in

Verse 1 (0:45-1:30)
- Acoustic guitar enters (fingerpicked arpeggio)
- Bass drone sustains
- Flute echoes between phrases

Chorus (1:30-2:00)
- Full instrumentation
- Light percussion (frame drum, soft)
- Vocal melody (if used) or lead flute

Verse 2 (2:00-2:45)
- Guitar continues
- Flute more prominent
- Jungle ambience swells

Chorus (2:45-3:15)
- Repeat with variation
- Add subtle string pad

Bridge (3:15-3:45)
- Strip back to singing bowl + flute
- Stone echo effects
- Moment of silence (3:35)

Final Chorus (3:45-4:30)
- Full return
- Gradual fade
- Last sound: single temple bell
```

---

## Chord Progression

**Verse:** Dm - B♭ - C - Dm (repeat)  
**Chorus:** B♭ - C - Dm - A (repeat)  
**Bridge:** Gm - Dm - A - Dm (hold final Dm)

## Production Notes

**Reference Tracks:**
- "The Last of the Mohicans" soundtrack (ambient, historical)
- "Baraka" soundtrack (world music, spiritual)
- Dead Can Dance (ancient/modern fusion)

**Recording Approach:**
- Record in a space with natural reverb (stone room, temple-like acoustics)
- Layer jungle ambience underneath (recorded at Angkor or sourced)
- Keep vocals intimate, close-mic'd, almost whispered
- End with actual temple bell recording if possible

**Emotional Target:**
The feeling of standing before something that will outlast you. Not sadness. Not awe. Something in between. Recognition.
