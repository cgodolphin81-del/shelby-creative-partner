# Song 023: Easter Island (Te Pito o te Henua)

**Genre:** Folk/Ambient  
**Subgenre:** Ethno-ambient / Polynesian folk  
**Tempo:** 68 BPM (slow, meditative)  
**Key:** D minor (with modal inflections — D Dorian and Phrygian elements)  
**Duration:** 6:42  
**Time Signature:** 4/4 (with occasional 5/4 measures in bridge for asymmetry)

---

## Production Notes

### Overall Mood & Atmosphere
- **Mood:** Meditative, mysterious, reverent, isolated, timeless
- **Vibe:** Standing before the moai at dusk — the wind, the ocean, the weight of stone
- **Spatial Design:** Wide stereo field, heavy reverb on vocals and percussion to create cavernous space
- **Dynamic Arc:** Starts sparse and intimate, builds to choral climax in final chorus, recedes to near-silence in outro

### Instrumentation

#### Core Instruments
- **Ukulele (tenor):** Primary accompaniment, fingerpicked arpeggios with open tunings (C6 or G6)
- **Log Drums (Rapa Nui: *pate*):** Deep, resonant percussive tones, played with soft mallets
- **Frame Drum:** Soft heartbeat rhythm, brushed rather than struck
- **Tide Pool FX:** Field recordings of ocean waves, wind through volcanic rock
- **Didgeridoo (sub-bass):** Droning low frequency, representing the island's volcanic core

#### Textural Elements
- **Choral Harmonies:** Wordless, close-voiced (soprano, alto, tenor, bass), using Polynesian-style parallel thirds and fifths
- **Wind Chimes:** Made from volcanic glass or metal, scattered throughout
- **Bowed Bass:** Sustained notes beneath choruses
- **Piano (prepared):** Muted with felt for intimate, percussive quality in verses
- **Ocarina:** Melodic fills between vocal phrases (referencing ancient whistles)

#### Polynesian Influences
- **Pate (log drums):** The rhythmic foundation, representing heartbeat and ceremony
- **Ukulele:** Folk intimacy, Polynesian roots
- **Choral Style:** Close harmonies reminiscent of Hawaiian and Māori traditions
- **Pentatonic Scales:** Primary melodic material, with modal interchange for mystery
- **Call-and-Response:** Between lead vocal and choral responses

### Vocal Style
- **Lead Vocal:** Intimate, breathy, close-mic'd (ASMR-quality proximity), minimal vibrato
- **Style:** Like whispering to the moai — reverent, personal, meditative
- **Harmonies:** Wordless "ahh" and "oo" sounds, layered in close harmony
- **Dynamics:** Mostly soft, with occasional crescendos on key phrases ("they are still here")

### Production Techniques
- **Reverb:** Large hall + plate convolution, 3.2s decay for spaciousness
- **Delay:** 1/4 dotted delay on vocal phrases, creating echo across the Pacific
- **EQ:** Low-pass filter at 8kHz on most instruments (muffled, underwater quality)
- **Stereo Imaging:** Wide on choral, narrow on lead vocal (centered, intimate)
- **Field Recordings:** Pan ocean waves left/right, wind chimes randomized

            Choral "ahh" pad enters (D3, F3, A3, C4), sustained

**Musical Notes:**
- Key established as D minor, but with Dorian flavor (natural 6th: B♮)
- Log drums play syncopated pattern: 1...3...4&...2& (asymmetrical, mysterious)
- Ukulele uses open tuning (G6: G-C-E-G), creating drone-like resonance
- Choral pad is wordless, providing atmospheric bed

## Technical Specifications

### Tuning
- **Ukulele:** G6 tuning (G6-C6-E6-G6) for drone resonance
- **Vocal:** D3–A4 (lead), D4–C5 (chorus)
- **Log Drums:** D2, A2, D3 (pentatonic scale)
- **Bass:** D1–D2 (sub-bass)

### Mix Notes
- **Lead Vocal:** Center, -3dB to -6dB gain reduction on peaks
- **Choral:** Wide stereo, -9dB to -12dB
- **Ukulele:** Slight left pan (-10%), -6dB
- **Log Drums:** Center, low-mid focus (80–250Hz)
- **Field Recordings:** Hard L/R pan, -18dB
- **Master:** -14 LUFS (ambient-friendly, not over-compressed)

## Performance Notes

### For Vocalist
- **Approach:** Whisper to the moai, not perform for an audience
- **Breath:** Keep audible, intimate, vulnerable
- **Phrasing:** Leave space between lines, let the silence speak
- **Dynamics:** Mostly **p** to **mp**, with **f** only on final chorus
- **Pronunciation:** Clear but not enunciated, like talking to someone close

### For Instrumentalists
- **Ukulele:** Fingerpick with flesh, not nails, for warm tone
- **Log Drums:** Use soft mallets, avoid sharp attack
- **Choral:** Close harmony, no vibrato, blend as one voice
- **All:** Play with restraint, leave space for the stone to speak

### For Producer
- **Reverb:** Natural, not artificial — think cave, not studio
- **EQ:** Roll off highs on everything except vocal clarity
- **Compression:** Light, preserve dynamics
- **Automation:** Gradual builds, never abrupt changes
- **Field Recordings:** Mix them into the music, not on top

## Cultural Notes & Respect

### Rapa Nui Context
- This song honors the Rapa Nui people, not just the moai
- The moai are ancestors, not statues — treat with reverence
- Avoid exoticization; focus on respect and survival
- The collapse narrative should not overshadow continued existence

### Musical Influences
- Polynesian chord progressions (parallel motion, open harmonies)
- Log drums are central to ceremony; represent heartbeat
- Choral style: Close harmony, no vibrato, communal
- Avoid stereotypical "tribal" tropes; focus on authenticity

### Recommended Research
- Read *Easter Island, Earth Island* by William Mulloy
- Listen to traditional Rapa Nui music (Hiro Ka'ai's recordings)
- Study the Birdman cult and its musical traditions
- Understand the difference between old and new collapse theories
