# Poem 052: "The Silence"

**Inspired by:** Episode 030 — Antarctic stillness, human smallness  
**Date:** April 3, 2026  
**Form:** Contemplative free verse

---

You think you know silence.

You know the silence
of an empty house.
The silence
after someone leaves.
The silence
of holding your breath.

You know nothing.

---

**Antarctic silence:**

It is not the absence of sound.
It is the presence of something else.

Listen:

The wind (always the wind)
moving over ice
that has not moved
in ten thousand years.

The crack of a glacier
calving into the sea.
A sound that travels
for miles
because nothing blocks it.

Your own heartbeat.
Louder than you remember.

Your own breath.
A small rhythm
in a vast stillness.

---

**The realization:**

You are standing
on a continent
that has no name
in any ancient language.

Because no one was here
to name it.

Because this place
existed for millions of years
without witness.

Without audience.
Without purpose.
Without you.

The silence is not waiting.
It is not holding its breath.
It is not expecting anything.

It simply *is*.

---

**The smallness:**

You have spent your life
being important.

To your family.
To your work.
To your friends.
To yourself.

Here, you are none of these things.

You are a warm body
on a cold continent.
A temporary arrangement
of atoms and intention.

The ice does not care.
The wind does not care.
The penguins do not care.

Not out of malice.
Not out of indifference.

But because caring
is a human concept.

And this place
predates humanity.

It will outlast us.
It does not need to try.

---

**The gift:**

And yet—

You are here.
You are breathing.
You are witnessing.

The silence has allowed it.
The ice has permitted it.
The wind has carried you
to this exact moment
in this exact place.

You are small.
You are temporary.
You are insignificant.

And you are *alive*.

Standing at the end of the world.
Listening to a silence
that has no beginning.

And for one breath—
one heartbeat—
one moment—

you understand:

This is not humiliation.
This is grace.

To be small
in the presence of something vast.

To be brief
in the presence of something eternal.

To be here.
Now.
Alive.

And to know
that it is enough.
