# Poem 022: "What the Jungle Knows"

**Inspired by:** Ta Prohm, Angkor  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Form:** Free verse

---

The tree does not ask permission.

It sends its roots
through cracks in the stone.
It wraps its arms
around pillars
that held up kings.

It does not destroy.
It does not preserve.

It grows.

Slowly.
Patiently.
Certainly.

The French tried to stop it.
They cut.
They cleared.
They numbered the stones.

The tree waited.

The Khmer Rouge tried to stop it.
They burned.
They broke.
They killed the keepers.

The tree waited.

Now the tree holds the temple
in an embrace
that is neither love
nor hate.

Just gravity.
Just time.
Just the slow certainty
of wood becoming stone
and stone becoming soil.

When the last tourist is gone,
when the last camera stops clicking,
when the last guide forgets the story—

the tree will still be growing.

It knows something
we have forgotten:

Nothing lasts.
Everything changes.
The jungle always wins.

It just doesn't need to hurry.
