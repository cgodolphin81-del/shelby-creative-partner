# Poem 024: "Forty-Seven Seconds"

**Inspired by:** The Angkor Wat sunrise reflection  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Form:** Time-based verse

---

**0:00** — The sky is indigo.
We wait with our cameras.
Hundreds of us.
All hoping for the same thing.

**0:12** — First light.
A line of orange
cuts the horizon.
Someone gasps.

**0:23** — The towers appear.
Five lotus buds
against a sky
that is no longer night
but not yet day.

**0:31** — The reflection forms.
Perfect.
Symmetrical.
Stone and water
becoming one.

**0:38** — The moment.
This is it.
This is what we came for.
Cameras click.
Phones record.
Eyes widen.

**0:45** — The wind comes.
A ripple.
Just one.
But it is enough.

**0:47** — The reflection shatters.
The spell breaks.
The temple is just a temple again.
The water is just water.

**1:00** — We disperse.
Some are satisfied.
Some are disappointed.
Most are already planning
breakfast.

**8:00** — I am still thinking
about those forty-seven seconds.

About how briefly
perfection appears.

About how quickly
it disappears.

About how we spend our lives
waiting for moments
that last less than a minute.

And how, somehow,
those minutes
are enough.
