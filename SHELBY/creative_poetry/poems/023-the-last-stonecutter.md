# Poem 023: "The Last Stonecutter"

**Inspired by:** The unnamed workers of Angkor  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Form:** Dramatic monologue

---

My hands are broken.
My back is bent.
My name is forgotten.

I do not know the king's name.
I do not know the temple's name.
I know only the stone.

Chisel.
Strike.
Dust.

Chisel.
Strike.
Dust.

The sun rises.
The sun sets.
The stone remains the same.

They say this temple
will make the king a god.

They say this temple
will last forever.

I do not care.

I care about the next strike.
The next chip.
The next breath.

My son will not know my name.
My daughter will not know my face.
But somewhere,
in the stone,
my handprint remains.

Not by design.
Not by intention.

Just the accident
of a lifetime
spent making something
I will not live to see finished.

Chisel.
Strike.
Dust.

The king will be forgotten.
The temple will fall.
But the stone—

the stone remembers
the weight of my hands.

And that is enough.

That has to be enough.
