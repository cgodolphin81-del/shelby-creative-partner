# Poem 047: "Gaucho's Song"

**Inspired by:** Episode 029 — Local guide, life on the steppe  
**Date:** April 3, 2026  
**Form:** Dramatic monologue

---

You ask me how long I have lived here.

I laugh.
Not at you.
At the question.

Time is different
on the steppe.

We do not count years.
We count seasons.
We count calves.
We count the times
the river flooded
and the times it did not.

My grandfather's father
rode this land.
His name is gone now.
But his horse's hoofprints
are still in the soil
where the rain has not washed them away.

You carry a camera.
I carry a knife.
Both are tools.
Yours captures moments.
Mine sustains life.

I do not judge.

When the wind comes from the south,
it brings ice.
When it comes from the north,
it brings dust.
When it comes from the east,
it brings nothing.
The east is empty.

I know where the condors nest.
I know which plants will stop the bleeding.
I know the sound a horse makes
when it is about to bolt.

You ask about the mountains.
I have never climbed them.
They are not for climbing.
They are for watching.
They are for knowing
that something greater exists
and does not need your acknowledgment.

At night, I sit by the fire.
The stars here are different.
Closer.
Brighter.
As if they have descended
to see what we are doing
down here in the dark.

I tell my children stories.
Not about heroes.
Not about kings.
About the land.
About the animals.
About the winter that came early
and the summer that never arrived.

They will tell their children.
And those children will tell theirs.

And one day,
long after I am dust,
someone will stand on this steppe
and feel the wind
and understand:

This place was here before us.
It will be here after.
We are just passing through.

But while we are here—

We ride.
We tend.
We remember.

That is enough.
