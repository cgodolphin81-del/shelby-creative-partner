# Poem 049: "Drake Passage"

**Inspired by:** Episode 030 — Crossing the roughest seas  
**Date:** April 3, 2026  
**Form:** Narrative verse

---

They call it *Drake's Shake*.
Eight hundred kilometers
of the angriest water
on the planet.

Two oceans collide here.
Atlantic meets Pacific.
Neither willing to yield.

The ship rises.
Falls.
Rises again.

Someone is sick.
Someone is praying.
Someone is laughing—
the nervous laugh
of someone who has lost control
and knows it.

I hold the railing.
My knuckles are white.
The railing does not care.

---

**Hour 4:**

The horizon has not been visible
since breakfast.

The sky is gray.
The water is gray.
The line between them
is a suggestion.

A wave hits.
The ship groans.
Metal protesting
the weight of water.

I think about all the sailors
who crossed this passage
in wooden ships.
In sail.
In silence.

They did not have stabilizers.
They did not have weather reports.
They had faith.
Or desperation.
Or both.

Most made it.
Some did not.

The ocean keeps its own records.

---

**Hour 12:**

Sleep is impossible.
Dreams are vertical.
Up. Down. Up. Down.

I close my eyes.
The room spins.
I open them.
The room spins.

There is no still point.
There is only motion.

---

**Hour 18:**

The captain announces:
*"We are through the worst of it."*

No one cheers.
We are too tired.
Too relieved.
Too aware that
"the worst of it"
is a matter of perspective.

Someone stumbles to the deck.
The air is cold.
The stars are out.
The water is calmer.

Not calm.
Just *calmer*.

A relative term.
Like *safe*.
Like *home*.

---

**Hour 24:**

Antarctica appears.
A white line
on the horizon.

We have crossed
the last boundary.

The water that surrounds us
has not been touched
by human hands.
Not really.

It is as it was
before we arrived.
It will be as it was
after we leave.

The passage has tested us.
Found us wanting.
Let us through anyway.

Not out of kindness.
But out of indifference.

And that, somehow,
is more humbling
than any mercy
could have been.
