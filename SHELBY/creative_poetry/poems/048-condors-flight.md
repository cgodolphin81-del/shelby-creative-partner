# Poem 048: "Condor's Flight"

**Inspired by:** Episode 029 — Soaring over the Andes  
**Date:** April 3, 2026  
**Form:** Perspective shift

---

**From below:**

A shadow crosses the valley.
Someone points.
"*Cóndor!*"

Necks crane.
Cameras lift.
A hundred phones
track a single bird.

It does not flap.
It does not hurry.
It simply *is*.

Riding air
that has moved over mountains
for a hundred million years.

---

**From above:**

The world is small from here.

The humans are dots.
Their vehicles are smaller dots.
The river is a silver thread
stitched through brown and green.

I have seen this before.
I will see it again.

My wings do not tire.
The thermals do the work.
I am a passenger
on air that rises
because the mountain wills it.

Below, a fox hunts.
Below, a horse runs.
Below, a human stands still
and looks up.

I circle once.
Twice.
I have acknowledged them.

Now I move on.

There are carcasses to find.
There are heights to reach.
There is sky—
always more sky.

---

**From the space between:**

What is it like
to be so large
and so light?

To span three meters
and weigh nothing?

To live sixty years
and never land
unless you choose to?

The condor does not answer.
It does not speak our language.
It speaks wind.
It speaks thermal.
It speaks the old tongue
of creatures who were flying
before we learned to stand.

We watch until it disappears.
A black speck
against white peaks.
Then nothing.

Someone says:
*"I've never seen anything so free."*

They are wrong.

The condor is not free.
It is bound to the air
as we are bound to the ground.

It just flies better
than we fall.
