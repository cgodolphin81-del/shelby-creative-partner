# Poem 010: The Heat Is What You Remember

*They asked me what I saw*  
*In the place where hell meets earth*  
*The colors? The sulfur? The acid pools?*

*I told them no.*  
*I told them it was the heat.*  
*Not the air. Not the sun.*  
*The heat that gets inside your bones.*

*That stays.*  
*That stays after you leave.*  
*That stays when you come back.*  
*That stays when you never leave.*

*The Danakil doesn't give you memories.*  
*It gives you heat.*  
*And the heat is what you remember.*  
*The heat is what you become.*

# Poem 011: The Mirror

*Standing on the salt flat*  
*Where sky meets earth meets sky*  
*I couldn't tell up from down*  
*Couldn't tell me from the sky*

*The mirror showed me everything*  
*And nothing at all.*  
*An infinite blue*  
*Where I disappeared.*

*Not drowned.*  
*Not lost.*  
*Just... gone.*  
*Like I was never here.*

*But the salt remembers.*  
*The salt always remembers.*  
*Even when you leave.*  
*Even when you forget.*

# Poem 012: The Cave Dwellers

*They live in stone*  
*In the belly of the mountain*  
*Where the light doesn't reach*  
*Where the wind doesn't speak*

*They tell stories by candlelight*  
*In a language the world forgot*  
*And the stories are old*  
*Older than the mountain*

*Older than the sea.*  
*Older than the stars.*  
*Older than us.*  
*Older than memory.*

*Some people build houses.*  
*Some people build caves.*  
*Some people build worlds.*  
*Some people build silence.*

*The Socotrans built silence.*  
*And in the silence*  
*They found everything.*  
*Including us.*

# Poem 013: The Salt Miners

*They carry the earth*  
*On their backs*  
*Block by block*  
*Day by day*

*Twenty kilograms*  
*One hundred kilometers*  
*Three days of walking*  
*To trade for rice*

*They don't call it work.*  
*They call it life.*  
*They don't call it survival.*  
*They call it honor.*

*The salt is white.*  
*The earth is red.*  
*The hands are black.*  
*The spirit is gold.*

*They mine the salt.*  
*The salt mines them.*  
*And in the mining*  
*They find themselves.*

# Poem 014: The Return

*You leave.*  
*You think you're done.*  
*You think you've seen enough.*  
*You think you've learned.*

*But the place calls.*  
*Not with words.*  
*Not with promises.*  
*With heat.*  
*With silence.*  
*With the color of the sky.*

*You return.*  
*Not because you have to.*  
*Because you can't not.*  
*Because some places aren't visited.*  
*They visit you.*

*And when they do*  
*You don't go back.*  
*You stay.*  
*You always stay.*
