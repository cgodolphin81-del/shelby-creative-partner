# Poem 051: "Blue Ice"

**Inspired by:** Episode 030 — Icebergs, Antarctic light  
**Date:** April 3, 2026  
**Form:** Sensory meditation

---

There is a blue
that exists only here.

Not the blue of sky.
Not the blue of ocean.
Not the blue of paint
or pigment
or pixel.

This blue is older.

It is the blue of compression.
Of weight.
Of time made visible.

---

**The iceberg:**

It broke from the shelf
somewhere upstream.
Maybe last week.
Maybe last year.

It floats now.
A cathedral of ice
in a sea of gray.

The surface is white.
Snow.
Fresh.
Untouched.

But below the waterline—

The blue emerges.
Deep.
Saturated.
Glowing from within.

This is ice
that has forgotten
how to be anything else.

No bubbles.
No air.
Just density.
Just clarity.
Just blue.

---

**The light:**

Antarctic light is different.

It travels farther.
Through cleaner air.
Through colder space.

It hits the ice
and something happens.

The red wavelengths are absorbed.
The blue wavelengths scatter.
The ice becomes a prism.
A lens.
A source.

You stare too long.
Your eyes ache.
You look away.
The blue remains.

Burned into your vision.
A ghost of color
that will not fade.

---

**The melting:**

A drop forms.
Rolls down the side.
Falls into the sea.

The sound is small.
A *plink*.
Gone.

But it is happening.
Everywhere.
Always.

The iceberg is leaving.
Not dramatically.
Not all at once.

Just molecule by molecule.
Return to the ocean.
Return to the cycle.
Return.

---

**The memory:**

You will try to describe this blue.
You will fail.

You will show photographs.
They will not capture it.

You will use words:
*Sapphire.*
*Cobalt.*
*Azure.*

None of them fit.

Because this blue
is not a color.
It is an experience.

It is the color of:
— Ancient cold
— Compressed time
— Light that has traveled
  farther than you have
— A world that does not need you
  to witness it

You stand on the deck.
The iceberg passes.
The blue fades.

But you know now:

There is a blue
that exists without you.

And that knowledge
changes everything.
