# Poem 021: "The Apsaras Dance"

**Inspired by:** Angkor Wat bas-reliefs  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Form:** Free verse

---

They carved her into stone
and called her celestial.

Eight hundred years
she has danced
without moving.

Her fingers curl
in gestures older than memory.
Her hips sway
in a rhythm no one remembers.
Her eyes look past you
toward something
you cannot see.

Who carved her?

A man with tired hands.
A man who would die
before the temple was consecrated.
A man whose name
was never written down.

He chiseled her smile
stroke by stroke.
He gave her jewelry
he would never afford.
He made her eternal
while knowing
he was not.

Now tourists photograph her.
Flashes pop.
Shutters click.
They capture her image
and move on.

She does not mind.

She has watched
emperors fall.
She has watched
jungles grow.
She has watched
stone crack
and weather
and warm
in the Cambodian sun.

She will watch you
become dust.

And she will dance on.
