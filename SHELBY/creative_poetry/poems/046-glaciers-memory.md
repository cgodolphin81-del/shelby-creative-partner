# Poem 046: "Glacier's Memory"

**Inspired by:** Episode 029 — Perito Moreno, ice as time capsule  
**Date:** April 3, 2026  
**Form:** Time-lapse verse

---

**Year 1** — The snow falls.
It does not melt.
It becomes ice.

**Year 100** — The ice is thick.
It flows, slowly as thought.
It carries air from another century
in frozen bubbles.

**Year 500** — The glacier moves.
One meter per day.
A snail's pace.
A continent's pace.

**Year 1000** — The ice remembers:
— The eruption that darkened the sky
— The ship that passed in the night
— The wolf that howled on the shore
— The child who pressed her palm
  against the blue wall
  and felt the cold
  seep into her bones

**Year 2000** — The calving begins.
A piece breaks away.
The size of a building.
The size of a neighborhood.
It crashes into the lake.
The sound travels for miles.

Someone records it on their phone.
Posts it with a caption.
Gets 847 likes.

The glacier does not notice.

**Year 2025** — A tourist stands before the wall.
She is twenty-four years old.
The ice she touches
is older than her country.

She thinks: *How temporary I am.*
The ice thinks: *How temporary this moment is.*

**Year 2050** — The glacier has retreated.
Two kilometers.
Three kilometers.
The lake is larger.
The memories are melting.

The air bubbles rise to the surface.
They pop.
They release breaths
from a world that no longer exists.

**Year 3000** — What remains?

A story.
A photograph.
A memory of blue
so deep it hurt to look at.

And the knowledge
that some things hold time
better than others.

That ice is not frozen water.
It is frozen history.

And it is telling us something
we are only beginning to understand.
