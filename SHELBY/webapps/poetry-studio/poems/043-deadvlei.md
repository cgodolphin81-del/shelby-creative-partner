# Poem 043: Dead Vlei

**Inspired by:** Episode 028 — The dead pan, 900-year-old trees  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Theme:** The trees died but did not fall

---

Nine hundred years ago
This was a lake

The Tsauchab River flowed here
The trees grew
The birds came
The animals drank

Then the river changed course
The water disappeared
The trees died

But they did not fall

They stand
Blackened by the sun
In a white clay pan
Surrounded by red dunes

It is too dry
There is no moisture
No insects
No bacteria

The trees are mummified
They will stand
For another thousand years
Perhaps longer

Johannes's wife died five years ago
Cancer
She was forty-five
Too young

He brought her ashes here
Scattered them on the pan
The wind took them
To the dunes
To the desert
To the vast place

*Now she is part of the Namib*
*Part of the fifty-five million years*
*Part of the memory*

The trees are dead
But they are still here

And so is she

In the wind
In the sand
In the memory
Of the vast place
