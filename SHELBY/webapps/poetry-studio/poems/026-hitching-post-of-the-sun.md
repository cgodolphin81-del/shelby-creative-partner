# Poem 026: Hitching Post of the Sun

**Inspired by:** Episode 022 — Machu Picchu, the Intihuatana Stone  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Theme:** The stone that anchors the sun to the earth

---

The Incas called it
*Intihuatana*
The Hitching Post of the Sun

They believed the stone
could anchor the sun to the earth
prevent it from drifting too far
during the winter

I place my hand on its surface
cool
rough
solid

I close my eyes

At first: nothing
Then: everything

The weight of centuries
The prayers of thousands
The hands that touched this stone before mine

Inca priests
Quechua villagers
Pilgrims
Tourists

All of them leaving something behind
All of them taking something away

A French woman told me
*Places hold memory*
*Stones remember the hands that shaped them*
*Prayers leave echoes*

She comes every five years
Each time she is different
Each time the stone is the same

It anchors her
To the earth
To herself
To whatever waits beyond

I remove my hand
Open my eyes

The woman is gone
The stone remains

It has seen empires rise
It has seen empires fall
It has seen four hundred years of silence

And it has remained

Still anchoring the sun
Still anchoring us
To whatever waits beyond
