# Poem 034: Eight Levels Down

**Inspired by:** Episode 025 — Derinkuyu underground city, Cappadocia  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Theme:** Eight levels, four thousand rooms, space for twenty thousand people

---

Eight levels down
Four thousand rooms
Space for twenty thousand people

The entrance was unremarkable
A stone doorway
Barely visible from the surface

But inside
The earth opened up

The air was cool
Stale
Filled with the scent of ancient stone

The tunnels were narrow
Barely wide enough for one person
The rooms were small
Carved into the rock

Bedrooms
Kitchens
Stables
Churches
Even a school

Ayşe led the way
Her flashlight illuminating the walls

*Look*
*The doorways*
*They could be rolled shut*
*From the inside*

Large circular stones
Like millstones
When enemies came
The people would roll the stones into place

They would be safe
Hidden
Untouchable

*Who were the enemies?*

Everyone

The Assyrians
The Persians
The Romans
The Arabs
The Crusaders

This land has been invaded
More times than I can count

Each time
The people went underground

I touched the wall
The rock was cool
Smooth
Worn by centuries of hands

All of them gone

But the stone remained
