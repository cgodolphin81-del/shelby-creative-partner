# Poem 027: The Navel of the World

**Inspired by:** Episode 023 — Easter Island, Rapa Nui  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Theme:** The most isolated place on Earth where humans have lived

---

Five hours from Santiago
Four thousand kilometers from mainland Chile
Two thousand kilometers from the nearest inhabited island

The most isolated piece of land
where humans have lived continuously
for over a thousand years

They call it *Te Pito o te Henua*
The Navel of the World

I arrive at 6 AM
after a midnight flight
after crossing time zones
after crossing the International Date Line

The runway is carved into volcanic rock
The terminal is a small building
A sign says *Welcome to Rapa Nui*

I have arrived at the edge of the world
And the world is waiting

Seven moai guard the airport
Facing inward
Watching the planes arrive and depart

They are not gods
They are ancestors

A man named Mateo says
*The stones do not speak to outsiders*
*But they speak to us*

Nine hundred moai were carved
Eight hundred eighty-seven have been counted
Some stand on platforms
Some lie fallen
Some remain half-finished in the quarry

The carvers walked away mid-stroke
They did not plan to abandon their work
They did not know they were creating mysteries
for tourists to puzzle over
a thousand years later

They were trying to honor their ancestors

And they did

The moai survived
The slave raids of the 1860s
The diseases that killed most of the population
The missionaries who burned the knowledge
The tablets
The texts

They survived

And they are still teaching
