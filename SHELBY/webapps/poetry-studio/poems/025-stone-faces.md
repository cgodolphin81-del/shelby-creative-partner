# Poem 025: Stone Faces

**Inspired by:** Episode 021 — Angkor Wat, the Bayon temple  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Theme:** The 216 faces of the Bayon, watching across eight centuries

---

Two hundred and sixteen faces
carved into fifty-four towers
gazing in all directions

They say they are Avalokiteshvara
the bodhisattva of compassion
They say they are Jayavarman VII
the builder king
They say they are both

I stand in the central courtyard
turning slowly
trying to meet every gaze

The faces wear the same expression:
serene
knowing
slightly amused

As if they understand something
I have not yet learned

Eight hundred years ago
a king believed in something
larger than himself

He built a temple of faces
so that long after his empire fell
long after his religion changed
long after his name was forgotten

The stones would still watch
The stones would still remember
The stones would still ask:

*What will you build*
*that outlasts you?*

I have no answer
Only the faces
Watching
Waiting
Knowing
