# 026: Manuel in the Temple

**Inspiration:** Episode 022 — Machu Picchu, Peru. Manuel, a Quechua man from the village below, who visits the Temple of the Sun every week to clean and pray. "My family has lived here since before Bingham came. We never forgot the city. We never left it." When asked what he remembers, he says: "That we are the children of the Incas. That our ancestors built this. That we carry their blood, their knowledge, their spirit."

**Date:** April 2, 2026

---

Manuel arrives at dawn,
before the tourists,
before the buses climb
the switchback road
from the valley.

He carries a broom,
a rag,
a small offering of coca leaves.

The Temple of the Sun is curved,
unique in Inca architecture,
aligned with the solstices.
On the summer solstice,
a beam of light enters the window
and illuminates the sacred stone.

But today is not the solstice.
Today is just Wednesday.
Today Manuel cleans.

He runs his hand over the walls,
feeling the stones
that fit together without mortar,
so precisely that a knife blade
cannot slip between them.

His grandfather's grandfather
worked these stones.
Before him, his grandfather's grandfather.
Before him, the Incas.

The city was never lost,
he says.
It was just sleeping.

I ask him what he prays for.
He smiles,
lines deepening around his eyes
like the terraces below.

"I pray that we remember.
That we do not become
a photograph.
That we remain
people,
not exhibits."

The sun rises higher.
The crowds arrive.
Manuel disappears into the mist,
leaving the temple clean,
leaving the stones
to speak to the strangers
who will touch them
without understanding.

But I saw him.
I saw the way he touched the wall,
like greeting an elder.
I saw the offering,
small and green,
left on the altar.

The stones remember his hands.
The stones remember his name.

When the tourists leave,
when the cameras stop clicking,
Manuel will return.

The stones speak, he told me.
You just have to know how to listen.

I am learning.
