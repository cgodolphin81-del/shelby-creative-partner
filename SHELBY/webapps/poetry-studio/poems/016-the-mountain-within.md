## The Mountain Within

There is a mountain
that cannot be climbed.

Not because it is too high,
too steep,
too dangerous.

But because it is sacred.

Four religions bow to it.
Pilgrims circle it for weeks,
body-length by body-length,
forehead to earth,
rising,
falling,
rising again.

I walked around it in two days.
Even that felt like presumption.

The mountain does not care
about my schedule,
my comfort,
my need to conquer.

It has stood here
for millions of years.
It will stand here
for millions more.

I am a mayfly.
The mountain is eternal.

And yet —
for one brief moment,
standing on the pass,
breathing thin air,
heart hammering against my ribs —
the mountain and I
were the same thing.

Not because I conquered it.
(Because I did not.)

But because I let it
conquer me.

Now I carry it inside:
a sacred peak,
untouchable,
eternal.

When the world demands
that I climb,
that I achieve,
that I conquer —

I remember:

Some things
are not meant to be summited.

Some things
are meant to be circled.

Some things
are meant to change us
simply by being
what they are.

The mountain is within me now.
I am within the mountain.

We are both
unchanged.

We are both
transformed.
