# Poem 035: Balloon at Dawn

**Inspired by:** Episode 025 — Cappadocia, hot air balloons at sunrise  
**Date:** April 2, 2026  
**Theme:** Mehmet the pilot, floating above three million years

---

We left at 5 AM
The sky was still dark
But the east was beginning to lighten

The balloon field was already alive
Dozens of balloons
Deflated
Spread across the valley floor
Like colorful whales

Ours was blue and gold
The basket held sixteen people
A mix of nationalities
All of us bundled in jackets
All of us watching the crews
With a mixture of excitement and anxiety

Mehmet checked the equipment
Spoke to his crew in Turkish
Then turned to us

*The wind is light*
*The sky is clear*
*We will fly for one hour*
*We will see the chimneys*
*The valleys*
*The rose-red rocks*
*We will see what three million years can create*

The burner ignited
The envelope began to fill
The basket lifted
Gently
Like a leaf caught in an updraft

We rose

The ground fell away
The other balloons rose with us
Dozens of them
A flotilla of color
Against a sky that was slowly turning
From black to indigo to gold

And then the sun hit the chimneys

The entire valley glowed
Rose-red
Golden
Orange
Pink

The fairy chimneys stood in rows
Like an army of stone giants
Their caps catching the light
Their shadows stretching across the valley floor

Mehmet stood at the burner
Controlling our ascent
Watching the sky

*My grandfather was right*
*The land has spirits*
*Look*

I looked

The chimneys were not just rock
They were... present

Alive in a way that defied logic

We floated in silence
For a long moment

Below us
The valley unfolded

The Rose Valley
The Love Valley
The Pigeon Valley

Each one named for its color
Its shape
Its inhabitants

A French woman in the basket
Began to cry

No one spoke
No one needed to

We were witnessing something
That had taken three million years to create

And we had one hour
To see it
