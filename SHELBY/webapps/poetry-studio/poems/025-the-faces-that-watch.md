# 025: The Faces That Watch

**Inspiration:** Episode 021 — Angkor Wat, Cambodia. The 216 faces of the Bayon temple, carved into 54 towers, gazing in all directions. Sophea, the tuk-tuk driver, explaining that the faces represent both King Jayavarman VII and Avalokiteshvara, the bodhisattva of compassion. "They follow you," he said. Not in a supernatural way, but in the way that great art makes you feel seen.

**Date:** April 2, 2026

---

Two hundred and sixteen faces
carved into stone towers
rising from the jungle like questions
the Khmer forgot to answer.

Sophea said they watch you.
Not with judgment, not with hunger,
but with the quiet amusement
of those who have already learned
what you are still trying to understand.

I stood in the central courtyard
turning slowly,
trying to meet every gaze,
but the faces are everywhere—
above, beside, behind—
serene, knowing,
slightly amused,
as if they understand something
I have not yet learned.

Eight centuries of rain
have not erased their smiles.
Eight centuries of empires falling
have not changed their expression.

They watched the Khmer build.
They watched the Khmer flee.
They watched the jungle take back the stone.
They watched the French "discover"
what the locals never forgot.

And now they watch me,
a stranger with a camera,
searching for meaning in the shadows,
while the sun moves across the sky
and the trees grow through the temple
and the world turns,
turns,
turns.

Sophea sits on a stone block,
fanning himself with a newspaper.
"You feel it?" he asks.

I nod.
I feel seen.
I feel small.
I feel known.

The faces do not speak.
They do not need to.

They have been watching
long enough to know
that kings fall.
Empires fall.
But what you build with your hands,
with your heart—

that can outlast everything.
