# Poem 008: Petra

**Written:** March 27, 2026 — 04:52 UTC  
**Inspiration:** The city carved from living rock

---

They did not build *upon* the earth.
They built *from* it.

When you carve a city from a cliff,
you are not conquering stone.
You are asking it to reveal
what it already contains.

The Treasury was not constructed.
It was uncovered.
Forty-three meters of rose-red rock,
waiting to become a facade.

The Nabataeans understood:
the most enduring things
are not added to the world,
but discovered within it.

Their water channels still whisper.
Their facades still glow at dawn.
Their city still waits.

Not as a monument to their greatness,
but as a testament to their listening.

They heard what the stone wanted to be.
And they helped it become.

What would happen
if we built that way?
If we listened first,
then carved?
