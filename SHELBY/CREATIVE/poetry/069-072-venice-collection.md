# Poem 069: St. Mark's Square

**Written:** April 3, 2026 — 17:00 UTC  
**Inspiration:** Venice, St. Mark's Square, gondolas, Springbok Radio Episode 035

---

The pigeons rise at dawn.

St. Mark's Square floods with light,
golden hour on stone that has seen
a thousand years of footsteps,
a million whispered conversations.

The campanile stands sentinel.
Its shadow stretches across the piazza,
marking time like a sundial
for tourists and locals alike.

And the gondolas wait.
Black hulls bobbing in the lapping water,
their prows curved like questions
asked to the canal.

The gondolier knows:
he does not row *against* Venice.
He rows *with* it.
Each stroke a conversation
between oar and water,
between past and present.

What does the square remember?

The merchants who haggled here.
The lovers who met here.
The armies that marched through here.
The floods that swallowed it whole.

It remembers everything.
And it says nothing.

It simply waits.
For the next dawn.
The next tide.
The next traveler
who will stand in its center
and feel small.

# Poem 070: Hidden Canals

**Written:** April 3, 2026 — 17:00 UTC  
**Inspiration:** Venice, hidden canals, bridges, Springbok Radio Episode 035

---

There are canals
the maps don't show.

Narrow passages where two boats
cannot pass without touching.
Where laundry hangs between buildings
like flags of the everyday.

The bridges are small.
Stone arches worn smooth
by centuries of feet.
Some have no railings.
Some have no names.

You can spend a lifetime
in the famous places.
Or you can turn left
where the guidebook says nothing.

Here, the water is darker.
Still.
Reflecting windows
that have watched
generations come and go.

A cat walks a wall.
A bicycle leans against a door.
A radio plays from an open window.

This is Venice too.
Not the postcard.
Not the painting.
But the living city.

The hidden canals don't speak.
They simply are.
Waiting for someone
to get lost
and find something true.

# Poem 071: Murano Glass

**Written:** April 3, 2026 — 17:00 UTC  
**Inspiration:** Venice, Murano glass, craftsmanship, Springbok Radio Episode 035

---

Fire and breath.

The master holds the blowpipe,
his hands moving like a dancer's,
turning molten glass
into something that will outlive him.

A thousand degrees.
The furnace roars.
The glass glows orange,
then red, then clear.

He does not force it.
He guides it.
There is a difference.

The vase takes shape.
Delicate. Impossible.
Colors swirling like captured sky:
cobalt, amber, rose.

His apprentice watches.
Learning not just the technique,
but the patience.
The respect for the material.
The understanding that
some things cannot be rushed.

This is how beauty is made:
not by conquering,
but by collaborating
with fire and sand and time.

The Murano masters know:
they are not creators.
They are midwives.
Helping the glass
become what it wants to be.

And when it cools,
when it hardens,
when it catches the light—

it sings.
A silent song
of transformation.

# Poem 072: Acqua Alta

**Written:** April 3, 2026 — 17:00 UTC  
**Inspiration:** Venice, acqua alta, fading beauty, Springbok Radio Episode 035

---

The water rises.

Not with violence,
but with inevitability.
The tide comes in,
as it has for centuries,
but higher now.
Always higher.

St. Mark's Square becomes a mirror.
The walkways become islands.
The city holds its breath.

They say Venice is sinking.
But perhaps it is the sea
that is rising to meet it.

The old buildings lean.
Their foundations softened
by salt and time.
Cracks spiderweb through plaster
that once gleamed white.

Yet there is beauty here too.
In the fading.
In the knowing that nothing lasts.

The acqua alta is not destruction.
It is reminder.

That all things return to water.
That all beauty is temporary.
That the most precious things
are those we know
we cannot keep.

The gondolier rows through the flooded square,
his oar cutting water
where stone should be.

He does not fight it.
He navigates it.
As Venice has always done.

Surviving not by resisting,
but by adapting.
By floating.
By remembering
that even in sinking,
there is grace.
