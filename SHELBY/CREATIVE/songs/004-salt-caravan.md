# Song 004: Salt Caravan

**Written:** March 27, 2026 — 09:45 UTC  
**Style:** Desert Folk / World  
**Tempo:** Slow, steady (like a camel's walk)  
**Inspiration:** The Afar salt caravans of the Danakil Depression

---

## Verse 1
Forty-two degrees at dawn  
The earth is cracked, the water's gone  
But through the heat, a line moves slow  
Twenty camels, single file, across the glow  

The salt is heavy on their backs  
White gold from the ancient flats  
Four days walking, no shade, no rest  
The desert tests, but won't confess  

## Chorus
Salt caravan, moving through the fire  
Carrying the wealth of a world that's older  
Than the maps we draw, than the lines we make  
Some roads are chosen, for more than money's sake  

## Verse 2
Ibrahim says this is home  
Sixty-seven years of heat and stone  
His father's father walked this same  
Burning path, and died with the same name  

The city's got air, the city's got ease  
But the city don't know what the desert sees  
What you need is little, what you want is all  
The trick is knowing which is which, before you fall  

## Chorus
Salt caravan, moving through the fire  
Carrying the wealth of a world that's older  
Than the maps we draw, than the lines we make  
Some roads are chosen, for more than money's sake  

## Bridge
They could leave, they could go  
To the highlands where the temperatures are low  
But the desert's in their blood, in their bones  
In the songs they sing, in the stones they've known  

Home isn't easy, home isn't kind  
Home is the place that shapes your mind  
And the Afar know what the rest forget  
That the hardest ground makes the deepest root yet  

## Final Chorus

Than the maps we draw, than the lines we make

Some roads are chosen, for more than money's sake
