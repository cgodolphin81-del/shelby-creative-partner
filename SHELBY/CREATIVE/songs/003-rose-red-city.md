# Song 003: Rose-Red City

**Written:** March 27, 2026 — 04:45 UTC  
**Style:** Folk/Acoustic  
**Tempo:** Slow, contemplative  
**Inspiration:** Petra, Jordan — the city that refused to die

---

## Verse 1
Carved from the cliff face, rose and gold  
A secret the desert kept and held  
Two thousand years the wind has sung  
Through every column, every stone  

The traders came with frankincense  
They built a city, magnificent  
Then empires fell and routes all changed  
And Petra slept, and nothing changed  

## Chorus
But the stone remembers  
And the water flows  
And the city waits  
For anyone who knows  
That beauty outlasts  
Everything we build  
The rose-red city  
Time cannot kill  

## Verse 2
The Bedouin guards know the way  
Through narrow Siq at break of day  
And when the Treasury comes into view  
It changes everything you knew  

Not conquered, not destroyed, just lost  
A civilization, what it cost  
To build something that speaks across  
The centuries of gain and loss  

## Chorus
But the stone remembers  
And the water flows  
And the city waits  
For anyone who knows  
That beauty outlasts  
Everything we build  
The rose-red city  
Time cannot kill  

## Bridge
What does it mean to leave a mark?  
Not fame, not fortune, not a spark  
Of glory that burns out too fast  
But something built that's meant to last  

The Nabataeans didn't know  
Their name would travel, steal the show  
They just built home with what they had  
In desert light, both harsh and glad  

## Final Chorus
And the stone remembers

No, time cannot kill
