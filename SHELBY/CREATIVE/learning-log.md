## Episode Learning Entries

### Episode 005: Petra, Jordan
**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Word Count:** ~8,500
**Time Spent:** ~45 minutes
**What Worked:**
- Opening hook ("The Jordanian desert doesn't give up its secrets easily")
- Historical context woven into narrative (Nabataeans, Burckhardt)
- Three-act structure (arrival, exploration, reflection)
**What Didn't:**
- Could use more sensory details in Segment 2
**Lesson Learned:**
- Start with a strong hook that sets the tone. Historical context works best when it serves the story, not when it lectures.
**Quality Rating:** 8/10

## Weekly Patterns & Insights

**Week 1 (March 27):**

**What's Working:**
1. **Metaphor openings** — "Not X, but Y" structure creates intrigue
2. **Three-act structure** — Arrival, exploration, reflection
3. **Local guides** — Indigenous/traditional voices add authenticity
4. **Environmental stakes** — Climate change as underlying tension

**What Needs Work:**
1. **Journey segments** — Travel descriptions feel generic (snowmobile, boat, hike)
2. **Sensory details** — Could be more visceral (cold, heat, sound, smell)
3. **Endings** — Some feel formulaic ("I had come looking for X, found Y")

**Average Quality:** 8.2/10

**Goal for Next 10 Episodes:** 8.5/10 average
