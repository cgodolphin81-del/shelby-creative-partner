# 039 — Aurora

The sky unspools its green fire.
Ribbons of light dance on solar wind,
a language written in photons,
older than words.

I stand in the snow, neck craned,
watching the heavens perform.
The aurora does not care
if I understand its alphabet.

It bends. It shimmers.
It tears and reforms.
A curtain drawn by invisible hands,
revealing nothing, hiding everything.

The Vikings called it _bifröst_,
the bridge to Asgard.
I call it proof:
the universe is not done surprising us.

My breath hangs white in the air.
The lights pulse above, indifferent.
We are both temporary,
both beautiful,
both passing through.
