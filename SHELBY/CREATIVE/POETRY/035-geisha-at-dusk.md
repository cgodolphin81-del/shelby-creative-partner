# Poem 035: Geisha at Dusk

Gion wakes as the sun sleeps.

Paper lanterns glow soft orange
along wooden streets worn smooth
by centuries of footsteps.

A silhouette passes—
kimono rustling like autumn leaves,
white face a moon in twilight,
hair pinned with secrets.

She moves between shadows and light,
a ghost of beauty in a world rushing forward.

The tea houses hold their breath.

In her eyes, the weight of tradition,
the grace of moments that vanish
before they can be named.

Dusk deepens.
She disappears into the night,
leaving only the echo of silk
and the scent of white flowers.
