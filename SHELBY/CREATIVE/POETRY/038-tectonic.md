# 038 — Tectonic

I stand with one foot on America,
one foot on Europe,
the earth splitting beneath me
like a wound that will not close.

This is Silfra's throat,
where continents divorce at two centimeters per year.
The crack fills with glacier melt,
clear as truth, cold as separation.

What grows in the space between?
Nothing. Everything.
A fissure is also a bridge.
A rift is also a meeting place.

I think of all the things that pull apart
slowly, inevitably,
and how we stand in the gap
pretending we are not also breaking.

The rock remembers when we were one.
The water forgets nothing.
I am small on this ancient fracture,
learning to balance on the edge of becoming two.
