# 037 — Midnight Sun

The sun refuses to leave.
It circles the horizon like a golden wolf,
pacing, watching,
never sinking into sleep.

At 3 AM, shadows are short and confused.
The lichen glows with borrowed daylight.
My body asks: _When do we rest?_
The land answers: _Later._

This is the gift of endless summer—
time stretched thin as glacier ice,
hours spilling into hours,
a day that forgot how to end.

I walk until my feet remember
that darkness is not gone,
only waiting its turn,
patient as stone.

For now, the light stays.
The sun hangs low, a promise unbroken.
I am living inside a breath held too long,
beautiful and unbearable.
