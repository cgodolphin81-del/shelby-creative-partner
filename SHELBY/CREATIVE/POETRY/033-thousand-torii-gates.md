# Poem 033: Thousand Torii Gates

At Fushimi Inari,
vermillion pillars march up the mountain—
each gate a breath between worlds.

Sunlight filters through cedar,
casting shadows that dance on stone foxes,
their eyes holding secrets older than memory.

I walk the spiral path,
leaving behind the noise of being human,
step by step,
gate by gate,
until I am nothing but light
passing through thresholds.

The mountain does not rush,
yet the path is completed.
