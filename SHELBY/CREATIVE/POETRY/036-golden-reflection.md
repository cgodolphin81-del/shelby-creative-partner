# Poem 036: Golden Reflection

Kinkaku-ji shines above the pond,
three stories of gold leaf catching heaven.

The pavilion does not boast—
it simply is,
radiant against pine and sky.

In the water below,
its twin shivers with each breeze,
perfect and impermanent.

Visitors raise their cameras,
but the temple knows:
beauty cannot be captured,
only witnessed.

I stand at the edge of the pond,
watching gold ripple into fragments,
then reform.

The reflection teaches what the temple preaches—
that all things are both eternal
and passing,
both real
and dream.
