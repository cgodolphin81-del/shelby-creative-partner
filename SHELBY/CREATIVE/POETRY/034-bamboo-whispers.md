# Poem 034: Bamboo Whispers

Arashiyama breathes in green.

Tall stalks reach for sky,
swaying in conversations I cannot hear—
ancient language of wind through leaves.

I stand still among them,
a stranger in their cathedral,
listening to the creak and rustle
of a thousand stems bending but not breaking.

Sunlight pierces the canopy in shards,
painting the forest floor with gold.

The bamboo does not speak to me,
but in their whispering,
I remember how to be quiet.
