# 040 — Saga Blood

The lava field remembers feet.
Not mine—the ones that came before,
calloused and salt-crusted,
Viking soles on volcanic stone.

They sailed west into the teeth of gales,
driftwood ships and stubborn gods,
carrying saga in their throats,
carrying blood in their veins.

I am what survived the crossing.
The gene that did not drown.
The story that did not freeze
in that first brutal winter.

Resilience is not a virtue here.
It is the baseline.
The price of breathing air
that tastes of ash and survival.

When the volcano speaks, I listen.
When the glacier retreats, I take notes.
My ancestors did not flee the edge—
they built their homes upon it.

This blood is thin but unbroken.
It carries the memory of fire.
I walk this land knowing:
I am the echo that refused to fade.
