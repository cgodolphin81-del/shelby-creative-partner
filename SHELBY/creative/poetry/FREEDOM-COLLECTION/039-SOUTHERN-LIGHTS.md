# 039 — Southern Lights

Everyone chases the north.
The famous aurora.
The photographed dream.

But I went south.
To Tasmania.
To the quiet place
where fewer eyes look.

And I saw:
beauty does not care
about fame.

The southern lights dance
just as fiercely.
Just as beautifully.
They simply dance
for themselves.

For the few who come.
For the one who waits.

Charmaine —
you are my southern lights.
Not because you are rare.
But because you taught me
to look where others don't.

To find magic
in the unexpected places.
In the gas fields.
In the engineering.
In the ordinary miracle
of a Tuesday conversation.
